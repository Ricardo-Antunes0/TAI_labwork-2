Sheppard Sunderland Frere, CBE, FSA, FBA (23 November 1922 – 7 January 2013) was a British historian, who was Professor of Modern History at the University of Cambridge from 1972 to 1992.  Frere was born in 1922 in London, the son of the historian Sheppard Sunderland Frere and his wife Ursula (née Wedgwood). He was educated at Eton College and Trinity College, Cambridge, where he took a first in history in 1947. He then undertook postgraduate research at Balliol College, Oxford.  Frere was appointed professor of modern history at the University of Cambridge in 1972. In 1992, he retired from his professorship and became a Fellow of New Hall, Cambridge. He died on 7 January 2013, aged 92.  Sheppard Frere's publications include: "The French Revolution: A History" (1958), "The Age of Revolution" (1960), "Toward the Abolition of War" (1963), "The Radical Enlightenment" (1965), "Montesquieu: A Biography" (1969), "The British Enlightenment" (1970), "The French Revolution: 1789–1799" (1973), "The French Revolution: 1798–1814" (1976), "European Integration: A History" (1980), "British Foreign Policy since World War II" (1981), and "George III: The Unconventional King" (1994).
