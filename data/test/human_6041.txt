Description Hibbertia obtusifolia is a prostrate, often mat-forming shrub with more or less glabrous branches up to  long. The leaves are linear to lance-shaped with the narrower end towards the base,  long and  wide with a rounded end and usually a groove along the upper surface. The flowers are arranged on the ends of branches and are sessile, surrounded by a cluster of up to six leaves. The sepals are  long and of unequal lengths. The petals are bright yellow, egg-shaped with the narrower end towards the base, and  long. There are eighteen to twenty-five stamens arranged in groups around usually four glabrous carpels. Flowering occurs from October to December.
