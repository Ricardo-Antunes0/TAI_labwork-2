Lake Chamo (Amharic: ቻሞ ሐይቅ) is a large freshwater lake in Ethiopia. It is located in the Eastern lowlands of the Tigray Region, about 100 km northeast of the city of Mekele. At its widest point, it is about 50 km wide. The lake has a surface area of 5,540 km², making it the second largest lake in Ethiopia after Lake Tana.  The lake has a very diverse aquatic fauna including six species of crocodiles and more than eighty species of fish. There are also many bird species that can be found living in or around the lake. The main tourist attraction around Lake Chamo is the Gondar Royal Palace which can be seen from a distance.
