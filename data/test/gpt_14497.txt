Frank Wallace Notestein (August 16, 1902 - October 28, 1984) was an American zoologist, ethologist, and professor of biology at the University of California, Berkeley.  Notestein was born in Morristown, New Jersey. He obtained his B.A. from Swarthmore College in 1923 and his M.A. from Columbia University in 1925. He received his Ph.D. from the University of Chicago in 1929.  Notestein's early career was spent teaching at the University of California, Berkeley (1929-1940), and then at the University of Texas (1940-1962). In 1962 he became a professor emeritus at Berkeley. In 1961 he was elected a Fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.  He died in 1984 in Oakland, California.
