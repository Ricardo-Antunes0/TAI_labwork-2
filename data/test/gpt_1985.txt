Peace Pagoda, Darjeeling or Darjeeling Peace Pagoda, is a Buddhist pagoda in Darjeeling, West Bengal, India. It is situated in the middle of the Bagrupo Tea Estate. The pagoda is a popular tourist destination and is also one of the oldest and most prominent Buddhist temples in Darjeeling.  The pagoda was built in 1861 by the then-governor of Darjeeling, Sir Richard Temple. The construction was funded by the local community and the contributions of monks from nearby Sikkim.
