Boghos Bey Yusufian (1775 - 1844) was an Armenian writer, translator and public figure who served as the governor of Der Zor (1827-1844) during the early years of the Ottoman Empire.  Born in 1775 in the town of Yeghvard, Boghos Bey Yusufian studied at the Yeghvard Seminary and then at the University of Constantinople before becoming a teacher. In 1810 he moved to Der Zor, where he served as the governor from 1827 to 1844. Yusufian was known for his translations of French, Russian and Turkish literature into Armenian, as well as his own works which focused on Armenian history, folklore and mythology. He died in Der Zor in 1844.
