Ronald Wayne "Ron" Davis (born July 17, 1946) is an American politician who served as the 38th Governor of Florida from 1995 to 2003. He also served as the 36th Lieutenant Governor of Florida from 1989 to 1995.  Davis was born in Bainbridge, Georgia and moved to the Miami area as a child. He graduated from Coral Gables Senior High School and the University of Miami with a Bachelor's degree in political science. Davis began his career as a city planner in Miami before becoming involved in politics.  Davis first ran for office in 1978 as a Democrat for the Florida State Senate, but was unsuccessful. He ran for lieutenant governor on the ticket of incumbent Lawton Chiles in 1986 and won, serving from 1989 to 1995. In 1994, Davis was elected governor of Florida, becoming the first Republican to hold that office since Reconstruction. Davis was re-elected in 1998 and 2002, becoming one of the longest-serving governors in Florida history.  Davis left office after two terms, citing health concerns. In 2003, he was convicted on federal corruption charges and was sentenced to five years in prison. He was released in 2006 after serving two years of his sentence and continues to campaign for political reform. Davis is currently a professor at Rollins College in Winter Park, Florida.
