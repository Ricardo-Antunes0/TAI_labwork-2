Hedvig Mollestad Thomassen (born 4 February 1982) is a Norwegian architect, who is known for her work with organic architecture.   Hedvig Mollestad Thomassen was born in Bergen, Norway. She completed her undergraduate studies at the University of Bergen in 2002, and her graduate studies at the Royal Danish Academy of Fine Arts in 2005.   In 2006, she completed her first architectural project, a house in the town of Vik in Vest-Agder, Norway. Her subsequent projects have been more experimental, incorporating organic materials and structures into traditional architecture. Her work has been exhibited at venues such as the Venice Biennale of Architecture, the São Paulo Bienal de Arte Contemporânea, and the Berlinale International Film Festival. In 2017 she was named one of the 100 most important people in the world by Time magazine.
