After graduating from Binghamton High School in 1952, Quinlan studied at Georgetown University and then transferred to the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, where he received his undergraduate degree in 1955. While at UNC, Quinlan began writing short stories for the school magazine.  After graduating from UNC, Quinlan served in the United States Air Force for two years. He then moved to New York City and worked as a advertising copywriter. In 1963, Quinlan published his first book, a novel called "The Deep." The book was well-received and won the Edgar Award for Best Novel. From then on, Quinlan wrote prolifically, publishing more than twenty books over the next thirty years.  Quinlan's work often features detectives or law enforcement officers working cases that are fraught with danger and suspense. His novels have been translated into more than thirty languages and have been popular worldwide. In 2002, he was awarded the Grand Master Award by the Mystery Writers of America. He died on October 26, 2010 at his home in New York City after a long illness.
