Martin Rowan "Marty" Holah (born 10 September 1967) is an Irish retired hurler who played as a midfielder for the Cork senior team.  Born in Cork, Holah first arrived on the inter-county scene at the age of seventeen when he first linked up with the Cork minor team. He made his senior debut in the 1986 championship. Holah went on to play a key role for Cork during a successful era, and won three All-Ireland medals (1991, 1992, 1993). He also won four Munster medals (1987, 1988, 1989, 1990) and two National Hurling League medals (1991, 1992).  At club level Holah was a three-time county club champion with St. Finbarr's. He also won one championship medal with West Cork.  Holah retired from inter-county hurling following the conclusion of the 1997 championship. He later became involved in coaching and was appointed manager of the Cork under-21 team in 2006.  In retirement from playing Holah became involved in coaching and was appointed manager of the Cork under-21 team in 2006. He remained in charge until 2009 when he was replaced by Darragh MacAnthony. In 2011 he took over as manager of the Westmeath senior team and led them to their first Leinster crown since 1978.
