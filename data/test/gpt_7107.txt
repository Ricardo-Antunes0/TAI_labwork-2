Peter Brimelow (born 13 October 1947) is an American conservative political commentator and editor-in-chief of the website VDARE.com. He is a critic of what he sees as the harmful effects of mass immigration to the United States, and has authored or co-authored several books on the topic.  Brimelow was born in New York City, the son of May (n√©e Korth) and Irving Brimelow, who was a physicist at IBM. He was raised in Teaneck, New Jersey. He graduated from Columbia University in 1969 with a B.A. in English Literature.  He began his career as a copywriter at BBDO New York in 1969, before becoming a journalist at "The Wall Street Journal" in 1975, where he worked until 1984. In 1984 he became editor-in-chief of "The American Conservative", a magazine founded by William F. Buckley Jr.. In 1990 he founded VDARE.com, which he continues to edit and manage.  Brimelow is married to Jeanine Pirro, the former district attorney for Westchester County, New York (1977-1983).
