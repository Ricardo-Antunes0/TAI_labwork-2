He studied piano with Olga Samaroff and composition with Darius Milhaud at the Paris Conservatory. Returning to the United States in 1935, he taught at Princeton University from 1945 to 1959 and at Yale University from 1959 until his retirement in 1984. He also served on the faculties of the Juilliard School (1949-1950), Columbia University (1964-1965), and the Royal Academy of Music in London (1968-1970).  Raymond's compositions include chamber music, orchestral works, choral works, piano pieces, and solo pieces. His best known works are the opera "The Deer Hunter" (1978), the symphonic concertos "Symphony No. 3" (1953), "Symphony No. 1" (1951), and "Tales of Hoffmann" (1968), and the string quartet "The Four Seasons" (1957). In addition, he wrote a number of choral works, including "Prelude and Fugue on a Theme by Thomas Tallis" (1966), "Procession from the Temple" (1970), "Creation" (1976), and "The Dream of Gerontius" (1988).
