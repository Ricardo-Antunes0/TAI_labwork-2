Eugene Bridges (born March 30, 1963), usually known by his stage name Eazy-E, is an American rapper, songwriter and actor. He is the co-founder of N.W.A and was the lead vocalist and lyricist for the group during its most successful years. Bridges also founded Ruthless Records, which became a major label in the early 1990s. He has also been involved in acting, appearing in films such as Boyz n the Hood, Above the Rim and Straight Outta Compton.  Eugene Bridges was born in Compton, California, to parents James Earl Bridges, a truck driver and part-time pimp, and Bettye Gwen Bridges (née Pitts). He has one older brother, Ronnie. As a child, he was raised by his mother after his father was convicted of murder and sentenced to life imprisonment. When he was 12 years old, his mother was killed by a hitman hired by her former pimp. After her death, Bridges began running drugs for local gangsters and became involved in gang life at age 14. In 1981, he was arrested for possession of a firearm and sentenced to three years in state prison.  Upon his release from prison in 1985, Bridges started working as a janitor at Ruthless Records, the record label founded by Eazy-E's former Ruthless Brothers bandmate Dr. Dre.
