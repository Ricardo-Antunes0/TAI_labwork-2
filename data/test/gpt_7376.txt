Asad Ghanma was born in the town of Neelum, located in the Rawalpindi District of Punjab, on February 14, 1930. He received his early education in a religious school and then pursued his studies at the Government College, Lahore. He later moved to Karachi and completed his B.A. in English Literature from the Karachi University in 1953.  In 1954, he married Zakia Soman, with whom he had four children: Hassan, Hira, Yasmeen and Ayesha.  Ghanma's first collection of poems, "Bazaar-e-Hijaz", was published in 1958. He followed it with "Noor e-Zaman" (1969), "Quran e-Shams" (1976), and "Sajda e-Watan" (1987).   In 1992 he received the prestigious Hilal-e-Imtiaz Award for his work in poetry. He died on September 18, 2007, in Karachi after a long illness.
