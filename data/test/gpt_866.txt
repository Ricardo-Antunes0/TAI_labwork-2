He served as a United States Representative from Pennsylvania from 1774 to 1779 and as the tenth Governor of Pennsylvania from 1778 to 1780.  Gerrish was born in Philadelphia, the son of John Gerrish and Sarah (Lane) Gerrish. He studied law and was admitted to the bar in 1741. He served as a member of the Pennsylvania House of Representatives from 1747 to 1753 and as a justice of the peace from 1753 to 1757. He was elected to the Continental Congress in 1774 and served until his resignation in February 1779. In November 1778, he was elected Governor of Pennsylvania, serving until his resignation in March 1780.  Gerrish died in Philadelphia at the age of 78. He was buried in Christ Church Burial Ground.
