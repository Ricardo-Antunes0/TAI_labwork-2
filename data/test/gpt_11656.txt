Eric Fenson (born May 6, 1971 in Geneva, New York) is an American composer and musician. Fenson grew up in Connecticut and studied composition at the University of Southern California with Christopher Rouse and at the University of Pennsylvania with Leon Kirchner. He also studied with Olivier Messiaen and participated in the Pierre Boulez Conducting Course at the Paris Conservatoire.  Fenson's music is often performed by ensembles such as the Arditti Quartet, the Los Angeles Philharmonic, and the Cleveland Orchestra. He has won several awards, including a MacArthur Fellowship in 2002.
