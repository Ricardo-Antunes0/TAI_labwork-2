Totes Meer (German for "Dead Sea") is a salt lake in Jordan. It is the largest salt lake in the Middle East and the ninth largest in the world.  Totes Meer is located in the Arava desert, east of the Dead Sea. The lake is fed by several small streams, including Wadi Rum, which flows into the lake from the east. The maximum depth of the lake is about . About two-thirds of its surface area is water.  The main towns on the eastern side of the lake are Amman and Zarqa, while Umm al-Qais (Arabic: أم القيس‎), further to the east, is a major tourist center.
