Roscoe Gardner Bartlett Jr.  (born June 1, 1928) is an American politician and diplomat who served as the 41st United States Secretary of State from 1977 to 1981. A Republican, Bartlett was the first Cabinet-level African American to serve in the United States government.  Born in Richmond, Virginia, Bartlett attended Virginia Union University and earned a J.D. from the University of Virginia Law School. He worked as a lawyer in private practice before being elected to the US House of Representatives in 1964, serving until 1977. Bartlett was then appointed Secretary of State by President Jimmy Carter, and served until 1981. Bartlett retired from public life after his term as Secretary of State ended. He currently teaches at Harvard University's John F. Kennedy School of Government.  Bartlett has been awarded numerous honorary degrees and has been named one of the "100 Most Influential Persons in the World" by Time magazine. In 1999, he was inducted into the Virginia Military Institute Hall of Fame.
