John Marion Riddle (born 1937) is an American author, lecturer, and former intelligence officer. He is best known for his work on the subject of espionage and national security.  Riddle was born in 1937 in New York City. He received a bachelor's degree from Holy Cross College in 1959 and a master's degree from the Georgetown University School of Foreign Service in 1961. Riddle served as an intelligence officer in the United States Army from 1961 to 1965, reaching the rank of lieutenant colonel. He then worked for the Central Intelligence Agency until 1969, when he left to become a professor at the United States Naval War College.  Riddle has written several books on espionage and national security, including The Spy Who Loved Me (1977), which was adapted into a movie starring Roger Moore and Barbara Bach in 1979. He also wrote The Riddle of the Sands (1950), which was later made into a movie starring David Niven and Deborah Kerr.
