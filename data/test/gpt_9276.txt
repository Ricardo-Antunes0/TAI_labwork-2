Paulina Susana Rubio Dosamantes  (; born October 17, 1984) is a Cuban-American singer, songwriter and actress. After starting her career in the late 1990s as a member of the Cuban-American pop group Nadia, Rubio achieved international success as a solo artist with her debut album Paulina (2005). Rubio has since released two further studio albums, El Dorado (2007) and Dónde Están Los Ladrones? (2009), both of which received critical acclaim. Rubio has since starred in the Spanish-language telenovela La Reina del Sur (2012–2014), for which she received an Emmy Award nomination. She has also lent her voice to Spanish versions of American animated films The Incredibles (2007) and Ratatouille (2007), as well as the English-language Spanish animated film Monster University (2013).  Born in Havana, Cuba, Rubio's parents fled to the United States when she was three years old. She grew up in Miami, Florida, where she began her musical career in the late 1990s as a member of the Cuban-American pop group Nadia. After signing with Universal Music Latino, Rubio released her debut album Paulina in 2005. The following year, she starred in the Spanish-language telenovela La Reina del Sur, for which she won an Emmy Award nomination.
