He was the first American journalist to be knighted by the British Empire.  Eugene O'Dunne was born in County Monaghan, Ireland on June 5, 1863. He came to the United States in 1884 and settled in New York City. He began writing for magazine publications in 1886 and became a columnist for the New York World in 1898. He also wrote novels, plays, and articles for newspapers and magazines.  O'Dunne was awarded the Pulitzer Prize for his novel, The Great God Brown (1903). He was made a Knight Commander of the British Empire in 1922. O'Dunne died in 1932 in Manhattan.
