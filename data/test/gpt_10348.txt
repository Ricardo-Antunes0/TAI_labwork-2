He is known for his work on economic growth, public finance, and development economics.  Pedro Magallanes was born in Santiago, Chile, on June 14, 1956. After completing his undergraduate studies at the Universidad de Chile in 1977, he received his doctorate in economics from the University of Chicago in 1982. From 1982 to 1984 he was a research associate at the National Bureau of Economic Research (NBER), and from 1984 to 1987 he was a research associate at the World Bank. He then became an assistant professor at the University of Chile in 1987 and a professor there in 1993.  Magallanes has been a visiting fellow at the National Bureau of Economic Research (NBER) and at the World Bank. He has also been a guest professor at universities in Argentina, Brazil, Chile, Colombia, Mexico, Peru, Uruguay, and Venezuela. He has been a member of the Board of Directors of the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) since 2006 and of the Economic Commission for Latin America and the Caribbean (CEPAL) since 2009.
