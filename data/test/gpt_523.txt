Shiu-Ying Hu, BBS (; 22 February 1910 â€“ 2 September 1988) was a Chinese educator, writer and politician.  Hu was born in Changsha, Hunan province, in 1910. After graduating from the Department of Education of Hunan Normal University in 1934, he worked as a teacher in various schools. In 1937, he became an editor of the journal "School Journal". In 1944, Hu was appointed president of the Hunan Teachers College. From 1949 to 1951, he served as the deputy commissioner of education for Hunan province. In 1951, Hu moved to Beijing and became a professor at the National School of Political Science and Law. He retired from his teaching position in 1972.  Hu's works include the novels "The Return of the Condor Heroes" (1954), "The Flaming Sword" (1956), and "The Hero with a Thousand Faces" (1957); the novella "The Journey to the West" (1961); and the short stories "Old Mr. Wu" (1947), "In Memory of My Grandfather" (1950), "The White-headed Eagle" (1953), and "Snow on the Yangtze" (1976). He also wrote essays on literature, history, politics and education. Hu died in Beijing in 1988.
