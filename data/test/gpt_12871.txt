Born in 1902 in Austro-Hungarian Empire, Shapira studied law at the universities of Vienna, Prague and Berlin. He served in the Israeli Army during World War II, where he received the Bronze Star. He joined the Israeli Bar in 1947 and became a professor at Hebrew University of Jerusalem in 1965. Shapira served as Attorney General of Israel from 1965 to 1970. He died in 1987.
