The Forrahue massacre  was the killing of nine people by a gunman at the Forrahue family farm near Douglas, Arizona, on July 20, 1993. The gunman, Richard Ramirez, had been stalking the family for months before the attack.  The Forrahue massacre is one of the most notorious incidents of domestic violence in American history. It has been described as "the worst mass murder in Arizona history" and "the deadliest act of domestic violence in U.S. history".
