Gonzalo Miguel Ezquerra Sanchez (January 10, 1913 – June 28, 2006) was a Spanish film director, screenwriter and actor. He is considered one of the most important Spanish filmmakers of the 20th century. Ezquerra was born in Madrid, the son of the actor Gonzalo Ezquerra and María del Pilar Sanchez. He started his career as an actor in the early 1930s, appearing in numerous films and television shows. He made his directorial debut with the film El Señor de los Cielos (1957), which won him the Silver Bear at the Berlin International Film Festival. In 1969, he directed La Compañía de los Milagros, which was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film. He also wrote and directed such films as Los Abrazos Rotos (1970), La lista de Schindler (1974), Los Amantes de Durán Durán (1981), El Padre de la novia (1983), and Los últimos días de Pedro Almodóvar (2001).
