He studied at the University of Bern and then at the University of Edinburgh. In 1776 he became a doctor of medicine at the University of Basel, where he spent the remainder of his career.  Wyss is best known for his development of the technique of suturing, which allows for the closure of wounds without the need for general anesthetics. He is also credited with inventing the stitch-n-cut method of amputation. Wyss died in Basel, Switzerland.
