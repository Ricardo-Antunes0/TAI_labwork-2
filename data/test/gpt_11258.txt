The double sestertius was a large Roman coin that was issued in the mid-1st century BC to commemorate the victory over Antony and Cleopatra at the Battle of Actium. The obverse featured the bust of Octavian, while the reverse depicted the goddess Roma.  The double sestertius was struck in bronze, silver, and gold coins, with a total weight of about 4.5 grams. It was worth about 16 denarii.
