Miriam Palma Defensor-Santiago   (n√©e Defensor; born October 8, 1947) is a Filipino lawyer and politician who served as the fourth President of the Philippines from 1998 to 2001.  Born in Tondo, Manila, Defensor-Santiago earned a law degree from the University of the Philippines Diliman in 1974. She then worked as a prosecutor in the Department of Justice before becoming a judge on the Regional Trial Court of Manila in 1984. In 1988, she was elected as a Representative of the 3rd District of Manila, serving until 1992.  In 1992, she was appointed Secretary for Justice by President Fidel V. Ramos. She later served as Secretary for Social Welfare and Development from 1995 to 1998. In 1998, she was elected President of the Philippines, defeating incumbent Joseph Estrada in an election marred by allegations of electoral fraud.  During her time as President, Defensor-Santiago oversaw the country's transition to democracy and its entry into the World Trade Organization. She was also criticized for her handling of the 2001 Philippine financial crisis and her support for anti-poverty programs. Defensor-Santiago left office in 2001 after losing her bid for re-election to Estrada.  She has since remained active in public life, serving as chairman of the Philippine Red Cross and chairing several private corporations. She also served as Special envoy for international humanitarian assistance to Iraq from 2003 to 2004 and Special envoy for disaster risk reduction and human rights to Indonesia from 2006 to 2008.
