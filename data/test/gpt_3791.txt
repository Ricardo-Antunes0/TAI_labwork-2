Pramod P. Khargonekar is the Vice Chancellor of the University of Mumbai, one of the largest universities in India. He has also served as the Vice Chancellor of Pune University and the Chairman of National Centre for Biological Sciences (NCBS), both in Maharashtra, India.  Kargonekar was born in 1956 in a small town in Maharashtra, India. After completing his undergraduate studies at the Mumbai University, he completed his postgraduate studies at the Indian Institute of Technology Bombay (IITB) in 1978. He then went on to obtain a doctorate from the University of Cambridge in 1988, on the topic of plant physiology.  Kargonekar began his career as a research scientist at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc) in Bangalore. He later became an assistant professor at IITB and an associate professor at the University of Mumbai. In 1995, he was appointed as the Vice Chancellor of Pune University, a post he held until 2003. From 2003 to 2008, he served as the Vice Chancellor of NCBS, a position he held until his appointment as the Vice Chancellor of the University of Mumbai in 2008.  As a university administrator, Khargonekar has been responsible for numerous significant achievements. He is known for his efforts to improve university infrastructure and for fostering a culture of innovation and creativity among students and staff.
