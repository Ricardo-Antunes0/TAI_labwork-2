John Collier was born on 18 December 1708 in Westminster, London, England, the son of John Collier, a draper, and his wife Ann. He was educated at Westminster School and Christ Church, Oxford.   Collier became a caricature artist in the late 18th century. His early works were political cartoons, but he is best known for his caricatures of famous people, particularly royalty. He produced around 1,500 drawings and paintings in his lifetime. Collier died on 14 December 1785 in London.
