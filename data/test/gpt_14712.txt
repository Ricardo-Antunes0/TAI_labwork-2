Dr. Ronda Chervin (born 1937, New York City) is an American psychologist and educator who is known for her work in clinical psychology, education, and women's studies.  Chervin earned her undergraduate degree from the University of Pennsylvania in 1959, and her doctoral degree from Yale University in 1967. She began her career as a professor at the State University of New York at Albany, where she served as chair of the psychology department from 1978 to 1982. She then moved to the University of California, Berkeley, where she served as chancellor from 1992 to 1998. In 2002, Chervin was appointed the inaugural president of Wesleyan University in Middletown, Connecticut. She held that position until her retirement in 2014.  Chervin's research has focused on clinical psychology, education, women's studies, and the intersection of those fields. She has authored or co-authored numerous books and articles on these topics. Her most notable work includes The Psychology of Women (1976), Women's Psychology (1978), and Clinical Practice with Women (1982).  Chervin has been recognized for her contributions to the field of psychology by numerous organizations, including the American Psychological Association (APA), National Association for Women in Science (NAWIS), and Society for Personality and Social Psychology (SPSP). In 2009, she was awarded the Linda B. Ziegler Award from NAWIS for her contributions to women's science.
