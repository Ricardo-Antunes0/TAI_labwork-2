Benjimen Daniel Schwimmer (born January 18, 1984) is an American actor and comedian. He is best known for his roles as the title character in the NBC sitcom New Girl and as Neil Goldman on the CBS sitcom The Big Bang Theory.  Schwimmer was born in Los Angeles, California, to Jewish parents who divorced when he was two years old. Schwimmer's mother, Leslie (née Friedman), is a writer and television producer, and his father, Daniel, is a psychotherapist. He has two sisters. Schwimmer attended Beverly Hills High School and then the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA), where he studied theatre.  Schwimmer began his career performing stand-up comedy in comedy clubs. He made his acting debut in the 2004 film Mean Girls and went on to appear in several short-lived television series, including Happy Endings, Don't Trust the B---- in Apartment 23, and The O.C.. In 2011, he starred as Nick Miller on the Fox comedy series New Girl. The show became a hit and ran for six seasons. Schwimmer won a Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Television Series Musical or Comedy for his performance on New Girl.   In early 2018, Schwimmer joined the cast of the CBS sitcom The Big Bang Theory as Neil Goldman, replacing Howard Stern who departed after twelve seasons. Schwimmer's tenure on The Big Bang Theory ended after ten seasons on May 17th, 2019.
