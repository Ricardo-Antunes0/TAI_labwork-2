Thomas Earl Grettenberg (died 1989) was a United States economist who wrote extensively on topics related to public finance and economic theory.  Thomas Earl Grettenberg was born in 1922 in Milwaukee, Wisconsin. He received his B.A. from the University of Wisconsin in 1943 and his M.A. from the University of Chicago in 1945. He served in the United States Army during World War II, achieving the rank of lieutenant colonel. After the war, he returned to Chicago and completed his Ph.D. in economics in 1950.  Grettenberg began his academic career at the University of Wisconsin where he was a professor from 1953 until his retirement in 1989. He was also the director of the university's graduate program in public finance from 1978 until his retirement. In 1984, he was awarded the prestigious John von Neumann prize by the American Economic Association for his work on public finance theory.  Thomas Earl Grettenberg died in 1989. He was survived by his wife, Barbara; two daughters; and three grandchildren.
