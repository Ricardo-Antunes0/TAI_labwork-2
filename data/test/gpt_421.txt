The emperor penguin (Aptenodytes forsteri) is the largest of all penguins and the heaviest. Adult males can weigh up to 2.3 kg (5.1 lb), while females weigh about 1.8 kg (4.0 lb). At 1 m (3.3 ft) tall and 85 cm (33 in) long, these birds are some of the largest terrestrial vertebrates on Earth.   The emperor penguin is a tropical and subtropical bird found in the Antarctic region around the South Pole. The emperor penguin is a marine bird that feeds on krill, a type of small shrimp-like animal. These birds breed on sub-Antarctic islands, laying two eggs per clutch. Young emperor penguins fledge in about three months and are capable of swimming, diving and flying.
