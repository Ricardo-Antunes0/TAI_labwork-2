Earl of Dunfermline was a title in the Peerage of Scotland. It was created on 2 May 1660 for Alexander Stewart, 2nd Lord Dunfermline. He had previously been created Baron Stewart of Menteith and Earl of Fife on 8 March 1651. The titles became extinct on the death of the fourth Earl in 1721.   The Dunfermline title was revived in 1784 for George Ramsay, 1st Duke of Atholl. His grandson, the second Duke, also succeeded as ninth Earl of Dunfermline and sixth Duke of Atholl. He was succeeded by his son, the third duke, who was also styled Marquess of Tullibardine until he succeeded to the Dukedom in 1892. The fourth duke, who succeeded his father in 1907, was childless and the title became extinct on his death in 1942.   The Honourable Alexander Stewart, second son of Sir Alexander Stewart, 3rd Baronet (see Stewart Baronets), was created a Baronet in 1696. The first Earl was his grandson, the second Baronet. He represented Dunfermline Burghs in the Scottish Parliament and served as Lord Lieutenant of Forfarshire from 1728 to 1739. On his death the title passed to his eldest son, the third Baronet.
