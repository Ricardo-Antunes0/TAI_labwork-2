Poco is an American country rock band formed in Austin, Texas in 2001. The group consists of vocalist and lead guitarist Richie Furay, lead guitarist and backing vocalist Timothy B. Schmit, bassist Jim Messina, and drummer Randy Meisner.  Formed in 2001 by lead singer and guitarist Richie Furay, Poco was initially a trio consisting of Furay, lead guitarist and backing vocalist Timothy B. Schmit, and bassist Jim Messina. The band signed to Dualtone Records that year and released their debut album "Poco" in 2002. The album was commercially successful, peaking at number four on the "Billboard" 200 chart. Poco went on to release three more albums with Dualtone before the label disbanded in 2004.  Furay and Schmit founded their own label, Furay/Schmit Productions, in 2005 and released the album "The Unforgiven" that year. The album was well-received by critics, peaking at number six on the "Billboard" 200 chart. Poco toured extensively in support of the album and won a Grammy Award for Best New Artist at the 54th Grammy Awards in 2006. In 2007, the band released their fifth album "The Revival" which reached number two on the "Billboard" 200 chart.
