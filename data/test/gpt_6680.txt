Art Rascon (born December 4, 1962) is an American artist who creates paintings, sculptures and installations that explore the boundaries of painting, sculpture and installation.  Rascon was born in 1962 in San Francisco, California. He received his BFA from the California Institute of the Arts in 1985 and his MFA from Yale University in 1988. His work has been exhibited at the Whitney Museum of American Art, the Museum of Modern Art, the Guggenheim Museum and other venues around the world. Rascon is the recipient of numerous awards, including a MacArthur Fellowship (2002), a National Endowment for the Arts Fellowship (1991) and a Pollock-Krasner Foundation Grant (1989).
