Born in 1758 in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, Billingsley studied art under the guidance of his father, a portrait painter. He moved to New York City in 1784 and became one of the most successful painters in America. His paintings depict American life at a time when it was rapidly changing, from Revolutionary War to early republic to early industrial age.  Billingsley died in 1828 in New York City. His works are in the collections of the Museum of Fine Arts in Boston, the Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York City, and many other museums around the world.
