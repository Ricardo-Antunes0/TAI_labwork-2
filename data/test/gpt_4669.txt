Monsters vs. Aliens is a 2009 American science fiction action comedy film directed by Peter Jackson and produced by his company Wingnut Films. It is the third film in the Monsters, Inc. franchise and the first sequel to 2002's Monsters, Inc.  The film follows the return of the monsters from their exile on Earth to stop an alien threat. They are opposed by a new generation of monsters, made by combining human and alien DNA, led by a shape-shifting alien queen.  Development of Monsters vs. Aliens began in 2007, after the release of The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King. Jackson was approached to direct by producer Kathleen Kennedy, who wanted to continue the Monsters, Inc. franchise. The film's development was delayed by two years while Jackson completed The Hobbit.  The film was shot in New Zealand from December 2008 to March 2009 with a budget of $150 million. It was released on July 16, 2009, in theaters worldwide and grossed over $418 million worldwide. It received generally positive reviews from critics, who praised its humor and action sequences, but criticized its loose plot.
