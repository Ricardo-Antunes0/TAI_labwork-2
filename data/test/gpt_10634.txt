Kupres , also known as Kupres Republike Srpske, is a municipality located in the Central Banja Luka District of Bosnia and Herzegovina. As of 2013, it has a population of 10,014 inhabitants.  Kupres is located in the Central Banja Luka district, at the foothills of the Velebit Mountains. The municipality covers an area of . The main ethnic groups are Bosniaks and Serbs. The municipality has a rich history dating back to the Middle Ages. It was mentioned for the first time in written sources in 1374. In 1462, Kupres was captured by Ottoman forces and remained under Turkish rule until 1527. It was subsequently seized by the Habsburgs, who ruled it until 1878. In 1918, after World War I, it became part of the Kingdom of Yugoslavia. After World War II, it was included in the Socialist Republic of Bosnia and Herzegovina. In 1992, after the Bosnian War, it became part of Bosnia and Herzegovina again.  The municipality is subdivided into ten villages: Bistrica, Dragišići, Kupres, Ljubinje, Lovćenac, Malo Polje, Novi Kupres, Orahovac, Prijedorac and Stolac.
