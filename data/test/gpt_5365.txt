Bosshardt was born in the town of Bielefeld, the son of a prosperous local businessman. He attended the Royal Military Academy, Sandhurst, and then served as an officer in the German Army before World War II. In 1940, he was appointed commander of the 2nd Panzer Division, which he led during Operation Barbarossa, the invasion of Soviet Union. After being wounded in action, he was transferred to command the LXIV Army Corps on the Eastern Front. In July 1944, he was appointed commandant of the Wehrmacht War Academy at Braunschweig.  In September 1944, Bosshardt was transferred to Italy to command Army Group B. He played an important role in the Battle of Monte Cassino and was promoted to lieutenant general on 20 January 1945. He died of wounds received in an air raid on 10 September 1945 near Milan.
