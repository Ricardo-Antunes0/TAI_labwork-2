Born in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, Kearney received an Artium Baccalaureus degree from the University of Wisconsin in 1948 and a Juris Doctor from Yale Law School in 1951. He was a law clerk to Judge John Marshall Harlan II of the United States Court of Appeals for the Sixth Circuit from 1951 to 1952. Kearney served in the United States Marine Corps from 1952 to 1954, and in the United States Army from 1954 to 1955. He was in private practice in Milwaukee from 1955 to 1965. Kearney was an Assistant United States Attorney for the Eastern District of Wisconsin from 1965 to 1967, and for the Western District of Wisconsin from 1967 to 1971. He was a Judge on the Milwaukee County Circuit Court from 1971 to 1978, serving as Chief Judge from 1978 to 1980. Kearney was nominated by President Jimmy Carter on December 8, 1978, to a seat on the United States Court of Appeals for the Seventh Circuit vacated by Judge Paul L. Friedman. Kearney was confirmed by the Senate on February 9, 1979, and received his commission on February 10, 1979. He served as Chief Judge from 1984 to 1988. Kearney retired from the court on December 29, 1998. He died at his home in Milwaukee on January 2, 1999.
