Dan A. Gwadosky (February 16, 1954 – October 21, 2009) was an American author and journalist. He wrote about politics, business, and sports for a variety of publications.  Born and raised in Philadelphia, Gwadosky attended the University of Pennsylvania where he received his B.A. in political science in 1976. He then worked as a reporter for The Philadelphia Inquirer from 1976 to 1978, covering both city and state government. In 1978 Gwadosky moved to New York City and became a staff writer at The Wall Street Journal, where he covered a wide range of subjects including business, finance, and sports. Gwadosky also wrote columns for the Journal’s Weekend Review section.  Gwadosky left the Journal in 1992 to become a contributing editor at Fortune magazine, where he wrote about business and finance. He also served as a senior editor at Forbes. Gwadosky left Forbes in 2006 to focus on his writing full-time.  Gwadosky’s first book, The Outsiders: Eight Unconventional CEOs and Their Radically Rational Blueprint for Success (published in 2000), was a biography of eight successful CEOs who broke from convention. His second book, The Outsiders: Eight Unconventional CEOs and Their Radically Rational Blueprint for Success Again (published in 2003), was a sequel to the first book and focused on eight more successful CEOs.  Gwadosky died from pancreatic cancer on October 21, 2009 at the age of 58.
