Stephen Christopher Spagnuolo (; born December 21, 1965) is an American football coach who is the defensive coordinator for the New Orleans Saints of the National Football League (NFL). He previously served as head coach of the Philadelphia Eagles from 2009 to 2013.  Spagnuolo played college football at Temple University, where he was a two-time All-American linebacker. He was selected by the New York Giants in the first round of the 1988 NFL draft, and spent eleven seasons with the team, including four as a starter. He also played for the Baltimore Ravens and Houston Texans.  Spagnuolo served as defensive coordinator for the New York Jets from 2007 to 2008 and for the Baltimore Ravens from 2009 to 2013. He was hired as head coach of the Eagles in October 2009, replacing Andy Reid, and led them to a playoff appearance in his first season but was fired after a poor performance in his second year. Spagnuolo was hired by the Saints in January 2014 and has led them to three consecutive playoff appearances.
