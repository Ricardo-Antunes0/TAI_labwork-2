Brants's whistling rat or Brants' whistling rat-tailed macaque is a species of monkey in the family Cebidae.  The Brants's whistling rat-tailed macaque is endemic to the north-east of Sulawesi, Indonesia. It is currently listed as Endangered on the IUCN Red List, as its population has declined by over 50% over the past two decades. The primary threat to this monkey is habitat loss and degradation, as well as hunting for meat, which is considered a delicacy in some parts of Indonesia.
