Rishi Sunak (; born 12 May 1980) is an Indian film director and actor.  Sunak was born in Mumbai on 12 May 1980 to a Parsi family. He attended the Jogeshwari Vidyalaya, and then went to the Stella Maris College, where he completed a degree in business administration. He then moved to London to study film making at the London Film School, graduating in 2003.  His first feature film, "Koyla", was screened at the Montreal World Film Festival and won the Best Screenplay Award at the UK Film Awards. It was also selected as the Indian entry for the Best Foreign Language Film at the Academy Awards, but was not nominated.  Sunak's second feature film, "Bhoothnath" (2008), also won awards at international film festivals, including the Golden Globe Award for Best Foreign Language Film and Best Screenplay Award at the British Independent Film Awards. It was also selected as the Indian entry for the Academy Awards, but was not nominated.  Sunak's third feature film, "3 Idiots" (2009), was a major commercial success, breaking all records set by his previous films. The film became one of India's highest-grossing films of all time and won numerous awards, including five Filmfare Awards (including Best Director) and three National Film Awards (including Best Feature Film).
