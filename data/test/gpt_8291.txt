Reva Zilpha Beck Bosone (April 2, 1895 – October 17, 1978) was an American editor, writer, and publisher. She was the first woman to be president of the New York Public Library (1937–1941), and the first woman to serve as a trustee of the Metropolitan Museum of Art.  Born in Brooklyn, New York, Beck Bosone graduated from Barnard College in 1918 and worked as an editorial assistant at Scribner's Magazine. In 1926 she became editor-in-chief of Harpers Magazine, a position she held until 1937. That year she was elected president of the New York Public Library, and served in that capacity until 1941. She was also a trustee of the Metropolitan Museum of Art from 1937 to 1967. Beck Bosone died in 1978 in Manhattan.
