The Brookhaven Rail Terminal (BRT) is a rail station in the New York City borough of Brooklyn. It is served by the Metro-North Railroad's Port Washington Branch, and is located on the east side of the Long Island Expressway in the Park Slope neighborhood. The station opened on April 24, 1904, and is the second-oldest active station on the Metro-North system.  The BRT is served by six Metro-North trains per hour in each direction, during rush hours. The station also has a small parking lot and a small bike docking station. Nearby pedestrian and bicycle routes include the Prospect Park West and Gowanus Expressways.
