Tapi district is one of the 33 districts of the Indian state of Gujarat. The district occupies an area of 2,827 km² and has a population of 1,167,541 (2011 census).  The district is located in the western region of Gujarat. The districts are bordered by Rajkot district to the north, Kutch district to the east, Jamnagar district to the south and Madhya Pradesh state to the west.  Tapi district has five tehsils: Tapi, Amreli, Bhavnagar, Morvi and Surat. The administrative headquarters of the district is Tapi town.  The district is divided into two sub-divisions: Tapi sub-division and Amreli sub-division.  Tapi is a riverine district located on the banks of two major rivers – Tapi and Dahod. It stretches from north to south for about 240 km and from east to west for about 50 km. The total area is 2,827 km². The eastern part of the district drains into Dwarka River while the western part drains into Sabarmati River.  The main crops grown in the district are cotton, rice, sugarcane and wheat. Other important crops include pulses (chickpeas, lentils), oilseeds (soybean, rapeseed) and fruits ( oranges, guavas).
