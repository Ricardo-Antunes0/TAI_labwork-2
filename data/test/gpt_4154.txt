Until We're Dead is the debut studio album by American rock band Fall Out Boy. It was released on October 20, 2003, by Fueled by Ramen.  The album was produced by Pete Wentz and the band's former drummer Andy Hurley. The album has since been certified platinum in the United States.  "Until We're Dead" debuted at number two on the US "Billboard" 200 chart with first-week sales of 154,000 copies. The album has since been certified platinum in the United States.  The album spawned four singles: "Sugar, We're Goin Down", "This Ain't a Scene, It's an Arms Race", "Thin Lizzy" and "I Don't Care". The first three singles reached number one on the US "Billboard" Hot 100 chart, while "I Don't Care" peaked at number two.
