Two Thoroughbreds is a 1939 American drama film directed by Howard Hawks and written by Leigh Brackett. The film stars Spencer Tracy, Katharine Hepburn, and Humphrey Bogart. Tracy and Hepburn play two horse trainers who fall in love but are unable to resolve their differences.  The film was shot at the racetrack at Balboa Park in San Diego, California. It was released on July 2, 1939, by 20th Century Fox. The film was nominated for three Academy Awards: Best Picture, Best Director, and Best Actress for Katharine Hepburn.
