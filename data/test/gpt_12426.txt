The Melanau calendar  is a calendar used by the Melanau people of Southeast Asia. It is a 12-month lunar calendar, with each month divided into 31 days. Each day has two names, one for the morning and one for the evening. The month begins with the first day of the new moon, and ends with the last day of the month.   The Melanau calendar is used to maintain traditional religious and social ceremonies. It is also used to calculate important dates such as births, marriages, and funerals. The Melanau calendar is also used to record historical events.
