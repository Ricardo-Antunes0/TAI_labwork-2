Colobodesmus crucis is a species of millipede in the family Colobodemidae. It is found in North America.  The holotype of C. crucis was collected in Louisiana in 1994. The species has been found in Alabama, Arkansas, Florida, Georgia, Illinois, Indiana, Kentucky, Louisiana, Mississippi, Missouri, North Carolina, Ohio, Oklahoma, South Carolina, Tennessee, Texas and Virginia.  C. crucis is a small millipede measuring 1-2 cm long and 0.5-1 cm wide. The body is dark brown or black except for the lighter-colored tail which has a pair of spines at the end. The legs are short and the front pair of legs have a claw on the end. The male has a penis for mating with females and the females have two pairs of ovaries.
