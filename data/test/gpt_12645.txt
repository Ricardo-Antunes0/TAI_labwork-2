Neelagiri Express is a 1968 Indian Tamil-language drama film directed by K. Balachander and produced by M. G. Ramachandran. It stars N. T. Rama Rao, Jayalalithaa, Sridevi and M. G. Ramachandran in the lead roles.  The film is set against the backdrop of the 1967 Indian state of Telangana and tells the story of a group of passengers travelling on the Neelagiri Express, which links the twin hill stations of Neelagiri and Nagarjunasagar in the state. The passengers include a married couple, their child and their in-laws, a young couple in love, an unmarried couple, a rich man and his mistress, a poor man and his wife, and an old man and his granddaughter. The story revolves around their interactions and relationships with one another, as well as with the various characters they meet on the train.  "Neelagiri Express" was critically acclaimed upon its release and won several awards, including the National Film Award for Best Feature Film in Tamil. It has been cited as a key influence on later Tamil films, particularly those made by directors such as Kamal Haasan and Karthi.
