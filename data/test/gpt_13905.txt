Taubenberg is a mountain with a height of 2,960 meters above sea level, located in the Austrian federal state of Tyrol. It is the highest mountain in the eastern Alps and is part of the Hohe Tauern range.  The mountain has two peaks, the "Süd" (South) and the "Nord" (North) Taubenberg. The "Süd" summit is 2,960 meters high and the "Nord" summit is 3,130 meters high.   The first ascent of the "Nord" summit was made on September 22nd, 1865 by Adolf Hempel and Jakob Anderegg. The first ascent of the "Süd" summit was made on October 11th, 1870 by Wilhelm Zille and Jakob Anderegg.
