Abram Salmon Benenson (1914 – December 15, 1992) was an American lawyer and politician who served as the 36th Attorney General of the United States from January 20, 1969, to January 20, 1973.  Born in 1914 in Omaha, Nebraska, Benenson graduated from the University of Nebraska–Lincoln law school in 1937. He was a member of the Nebraska State Senate from 1941 to 1945 and the United States House of Representatives from 1947 to 1969. Benenson was appointed Attorney General by President Lyndon B. Johnson on January 20, 1969, and served until his resignation on January 20, 1973. Benenson died in 1992 at the age of 78.
