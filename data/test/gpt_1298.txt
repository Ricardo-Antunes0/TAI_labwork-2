Rock Rest is a historic house and gardens in the town of Stroud, Gloucestershire, England. The house and gardens are open to the public and are Grade II* listed. The gardens were designed by Capability Brown in 1764 for Henry Thrale, 3rd Baronet.   The house is a two-storey, stone-built structure with a slate roof. It has a three-bay frontage and a single-storey rear extension. The front entrance has an elliptical fanlight and leaded glass window. The garden features an Italian garden with clipped boxwood hedges, an herbaceous border, fountains and statues, as well as several formal flower beds.   The house was extensively restored in the late 1990s by English Heritage.
