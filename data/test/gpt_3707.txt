The Selangor water works provides water supply and sanitation services to the state of Selangor in Malaysia. The works was established in 1896 and is one of the oldest water supply and sanitation utilities in Malaysia.  The Selangor water works supplies water to an area of 1,841 square kilometres, providing around 2,000 million litres of water a day to over 1.5 million people. It also provides sanitation services to around 1.5 million people, with a total population of over 3 million people.  The Selangor water works is operated by the Selangor Regional Water Board (SRWB). The SRWB is a statutory body under the Ministry of Federal Territories that was established on 1 January 1997 to administer and manage the regional water resources in Selangor.
