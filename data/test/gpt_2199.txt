The Davey O'Brien Award, officially the Davey O'Brien National Quarterback Award, is an annual award given to the best American football quarterback in college football. The award was created in 1978 by the Davey O'Brien Foundation, and is given by the Jim Thorpe Awards Board of Directors.  The Davey O'Brien Award has been given annually to the best quarterback in college football since 1978. The award is named for Davey O'Brien, a former quarterback and national champion who died in a plane crash in 1962. Past recipients of the Davey O'Brien Award include Johnny Unitas, Dan Marino, Troy Aikman, Peyton Manning, and Cam Newton.
