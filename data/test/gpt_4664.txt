He received his B.S. from UCLA in 1935 and his M.S. from the University of Chicago in 1936. He worked at the American Museum of Natural History from 1937 to 1969, where he became the Curator of Palaeontology in 1962 and the Chairman of the Division of Vertebrate Paleontology in 1969. He was a research associate at the Carnegie Museum of Natural History from 1956 to 1958 and at Harvard University from 1958 to 1959.
