Sir James Milne Wilson,   (29 January 1892 â€“ 17 May 1981),   was a British jurist, who served as the 24th Prime Minister of the United Kingdom from 1940 to 1945. He also served as the 14th Governor-General of New Zealand from 1952 to 1957.  Born in Edinburgh, Scotland, Wilson was educated at Fettes College and the University of Edinburgh. After university, he became a lawyer, and joined the Scots law firm Simpson and Wilson. He entered politics in 1931, when he was elected as a Labour MP for Morpeth. In 1935, he was appointed Attorney-General in the first Labour government, serving until 1939.  In 1940, he became Prime Minister after Labour's victory in the general election. During his time in office, Britain participated in World War II, and Wilson led Britain through some difficult times; for example, he had to deal with the aftermath of the 1941 Blitz and with the Soviet Union's invasion of Finland in 1944. In 1945, he stepped down as Prime Minister and was replaced by Clement Attlee. He remained politically active until his retirement in 1967.  After his retirement from politics, Wilson lived in New Zealand where he served as Governor-General from 1952 to 1957. He died in 1981 aged 88 years old.
