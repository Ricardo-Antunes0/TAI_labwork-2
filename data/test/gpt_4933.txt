William "Bill" Mason Gray (9 October 1929 – 5 September 2003) was an American geologist who specialized in the study of Precambrian and Proterozoic rocks.  Gray was born in Terre Haute, Indiana, on October 9, 1929. He received his B.S. from Indiana University in 1951 and his M.S. from Yale University in 1952. He then received his Ph.D. from Yale in 1957 under the guidance of Harry Lillie.  Gray began his career as a research associate at the University of California, Berkeley, in 1957. He moved to the University of Utah in 1960 and became a full professor there in 1972. He retired from the University of Utah in 1998 and was emeritus professor until his death in 2003.  Gray's research focused on Precambrian and Proterozoic rocks, particularly their geology, mineralogy, and petrology. He is best known for his studies of Archean rocks, which he published extensively throughout his career. Gray was also known for his teaching abilities, having taught at both the graduate and undergraduate levels for more than 40 years.  Gray died on September 5, 2003, after a long battle with leukemia.
