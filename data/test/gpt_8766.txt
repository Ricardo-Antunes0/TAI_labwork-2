The Pöschel Equator was a single engine, high-wing monoplane designed and built in Germany in the early 1920s.  The Pöschel Equator was designed by Wilhelm Pöschel, who also designed the Pöschel Parasol. The Equator was a single engine, high-wing monoplane with twin booms and a fixed conventional landing gear. It had a wooden frame and was powered by a Renault engine.
