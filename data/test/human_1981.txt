Udo Mario Strutynski (born September 21, 1942) is an Austrian-born American linguist and lawyer. As a linguist, Strutynski specializes in Indo-European and Germanic studies, particularly the study of Germanic and Indo-European mythology. As a lawyer he has distinguished himself to helping out victims of the sexual abuse scandal in the Catholic archdiocese of Los Angeles, of which he is himself a survivor. Biography Udo Strutynski was born in Vienna, Austria on 21 September 1942. Together with his mother, Strutynski came to Los Angeles as a refugee in 1950. His mother was a devout Roman Catholic. Having graduated at the head of his class at Loyola High School, Strutynski received his A.B. from Loyola Marymount University in 1963, his M.A. from the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) in 1966, and his Ph.D. in Germanic languages from UCLA in 1975. He subsequently served as Assosicate Editor at University of California Press (1975-1978), Assistant Professor of Anthropology at Occidental College (1979-1980) and Assistant Professor of Comparative Literature and Germanic at Northwestern University (1980-?). He became a member of the Modern Language Association.
