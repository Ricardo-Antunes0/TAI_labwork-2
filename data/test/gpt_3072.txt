Mark Seidenfeld is an American legal academic who specializes in intellectual property law. He is the William J. Brennan, Jr. Professor of Law at Yale Law School, where he has also served as Dean of the Law School since 2002.  Born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, Seidenfeld received his B.A. from Princeton University in 1981 and his J.D. from Yale Law School in 1985. He clerked for Judge John M. Walker Jr. of the United States Court of Appeals for the Third Circuit and for Justice Sandra Day O'Connor of the United States Supreme Court.  Seidenfeld began his academic career at Yale Law School, where he was an Assistant Professor from 1987 to 1992 and then a Professor from 1992 to 2002, when he became Dean of the Law School. He served on the boards of directors of eBay and Google Inc., and was a member of the board of trustees of Yale University from 2004 to 2009. In February 2009, Seidenfeld joined the faculty at Columbia Law School as a Professor of Legal Studies and Intellectual Property Law and Director of its Innovation Lab. He became a William J. Brennan, Jr. Professor at Yale Law School in 2014.  Seidenfeld has written extensively on intellectual property law, including articles on software patents, trade dress protection, copyright law, trademark law, and internet commerce law.
