Professor Raphael Owor, MBChB, MD, FRCPath, ScD, FRCP is a medical doctor, researcher and academic who currently serves as the Vice-Chancellor (Research) at the University of Aberdeen.  Raphael Owor was born in Ghana in 1969. He attended the Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology (KNUST), where he obtained his MBChB in 1993 and his MD in 1997. He then undertook postgraduate studies at the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine, where he obtained his ScD in 2002.  After completing his postgraduate studies, Owor joined the staff of the University of Aberdeen as a lecturer in 2002. He rose through the ranks, becoming a professor of medical science in 2009 and vice-chancellor (research) in 2015. During his time at Aberdeen, Owor has been involved in a number of research projects, including work on malaria, HIV and tuberculosis. In 2017, he was elected a fellow of the Royal College of Physicians (FRCP).
