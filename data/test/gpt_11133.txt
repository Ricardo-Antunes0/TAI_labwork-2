Ethlie Ann Vare (born March 8, 1953) is an American actress, singer and dancer.  Vare was born in Cleveland, Ohio, the daughter of Esther (n√©e Hirsch) and Benjamin Vare. She is Jewish. She attended the Cleveland School of the Arts and then studied at the University of California, Los Angeles.  Vare's early acting roles included a lead role in the off-Broadway production of "The Glass Menagerie" and a role in the ABC television movie "The Burning Bed". In 1984, she starred as Kelly in the Broadway revival of "Damn Yankees". She also starred in the original Broadway cast of "A Chorus Line" from 1987 to 1988.  In 2009, Vare became a judge on season six of "America's Got Talent".  Vare has also appeared in several films, including "Pretty Woman", "National Lampoon's Animal House", "A League of Their Own", and "My Cousin Gravity". Vare starred as Velma Kelly in the 2017 Broadway revival of "Damn Yankees".
