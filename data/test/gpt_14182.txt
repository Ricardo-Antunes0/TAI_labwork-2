Garson Romalis (October 23, 1937 – January 16, 2013) was a Canadian composer and pianist.  Born in Montreal, Romalis studied at the Royal Conservatory of Music (1956–1959), with Nadia Boulanger and Maurice Ravel. He made his debut as a pianist in 1960 and went on to become one of Canada's most acclaimed composers. His works have been performed by such leading musicians as Glenn Gould, Daniel Barenboim, Isaac Stern, Alan Gilbert, and André Previn.  Romalis was made an Officer of the Order of Canada in 1984 and a Companion of the Order of Canada in 2001. He died in Montreal at the age of seventy-seven.
