Julia Mary Black  (born 29 January 1967) is the strategic director of innovation and a professor of law at the London School of Economics and Political Science (LSE) She was the interim director of the LSE, a post she held from September 2016 until September 2017, at which time Minouche Shafik took over the directorship. She is the president of the British Academy, the UK's national academy for the humanities and social sciences, and will become the academy's second female president in July 2021 for a four-year term. Early life and education Black was born on 29 January 1967 in Waterloo, Lancashire, England. She graduated with a First Class undergraduate degree in jurisprudence from the University of Oxford in 1988. In 1994, she completed her DPhil with a thesis on Conduct of Business Rules in the financial sector. Her primary research interests are regulation of the financial services sector and financial risk.
