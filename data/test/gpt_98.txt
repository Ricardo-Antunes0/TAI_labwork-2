Sir Lynden Oscar Pindling, NH, KCMG, PC, GCMG, GCVO (25 December 1901 – 5 January 1965) was a New Zealand politician who served as the tenth Prime Minister of New Zealand from 1951 to 1957.  Born in Auckland, Pindling was educated at Auckland Grammar School and the University of Auckland. He served in the New Zealand Army during World War II and was wounded in action. After the war, he became a barrister.  Pindling entered politics in 1946, winning the Papakura electorate in the general election that year. He served as Minister of Agriculture from 1952 to 1957, during which time he introduced major agricultural reforms. In 1954, he became Deputy Prime Minister, and in 1957 he succeeded James Fraser as Prime Minister. Pindling's term as Prime Minister was marked by strong economic growth, but his government was also plagued by allegations of corruption. He retired from politics in 1959 after losing the electorate of North Shore to Labour's Harry Holland.  Pindling died in 1965 at the age of 66. He was posthumously appointed a Companion of the Order of St Michael and St George and a Knight Commander of the Order of St Michael and St George. He is buried at Point Chevalier Cemetery in Auckland.  Lynden Oscar Pindling was born on 25 December 1901 in Auckland, New Zealand to Harry Pindling and his wife Elsie (née Mason).
