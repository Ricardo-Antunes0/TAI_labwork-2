John Moores Jr., CBE, DL, (22 November 1922 â€“ 9 October 2014) was a British businessman, who was the son of John Moores, the founder of the J. M. Smucker Company.  Moores Jr. succeeded his father as chairman and chief executive officer (CEO) of the J. M. Smucker Company in 1978 and served in that position until his retirement in 1992. During his tenure, the company became a leading worldwide producer and distributor of food products, including jams, jellies, fruit juices, ice cream, nuts, and seeds. He was also a director of several other companies, including British Petroleum and British Aerospace.  In 1985, Moores Jr. was appointed a CBE for his services to business. He died at the age of 92 on 9 October 2014.
