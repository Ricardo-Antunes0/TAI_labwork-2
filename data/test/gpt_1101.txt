Eugène-Jean-Claude-Joseph Desflèches, M.E.P.  (1814–1887) (Chinese: 范若瑟) was a French astronomer, physicist, and mathematician.  Born in 1814 in Montpellier, France, Desflèches was educated at the lycée Henri IV in Paris and then at the University of Paris. He became a professor of mathematics at the University of Bordeaux in 1847. In 1863 he succeeded Jean-Baptiste Biot as director of the Paris Observatory.  In 1853, Desflèches published a paper on the motion of Mercury which was to provide important new information on that planet's orbit. He also published papers on the theory of heat and on the optics of telescopes.  He died in Paris in 1887.
