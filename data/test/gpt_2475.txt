Wright was born in Melbourne, Australia on 9 October 1870 to Henry Wright and his wife Emma. Wright attended Scotch College before studying at University of Melbourne where he received a Bachelor of Arts in 1894 and a Master of Arts in 1896. Wright then attended the University of Cambridge where he received his PhD in Zoology in 1902. Wright returned to Australia and commenced work as a lecturer at the University of Melbourne. He later became a professor at the university and remained there until his death in 1937. Wright was also a fellow of the Royal Society of London.
