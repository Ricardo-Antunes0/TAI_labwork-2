Hardingshute Manor (also Hortyngeschete, 13th century; Hortyngshute, 15th century) is a manor house in the parish of Horton in North Norfolk, England. It is a Grade I listed building.  The manor has been continuously in the ownership of the Hardinge family since its construction in the late 13th century by Robert de Hardinges. The present manor house was built in the 15th century by Sir John Hardinge, KG, who was High Sheriff of Norfolk and Lord Deputy of Ireland. The manor house is a three-storey brick building with a tiled roof and has mullioned windows and Jacobean style architecture. The gardens are also Grade I listed.
