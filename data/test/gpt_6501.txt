Oliver Cope (1902 - 30 April 1994), was an English author, journalist, and broadcaster.  Cope was born in 1902 in London, England. He attended Westminster School and then Cambridge University, where he obtained a degree in history. He worked as a journalist for a number of newspapers before becoming a broadcaster. He was a correspondent for the BBC in Spain during the Spanish Civil War and later served as a war correspondent in the Second World War. After the war, he wrote several books about the war. He also wrote a number of novels, including The Man Who Went to Mars and The Winds of War. He died in 1994.
