Sir Robert Joseph Phillimore, 1st Baronet   Sir Robert Phillimore, 1st Baronet (18 December 1811 – 15 October 1892) was an English jurist and Liberal politician who sat in the House of Commons between 1847 and 1892.  Born at Selborne, Hampshire, Phillimore was son of Robert Phillimore of Selborne and his wife Mary, daughter of John Lewis of Salford. He was educated at Winchester College and at Trinity College, Cambridge. He was called to the bar in 1830 and began practice at Northampton.   In 1847 Phillimore was elected Member of Parliament for Northampton, a seat he held until his resignation in 1892. In parliament he served as Parliamentary Secretary to the Treasury (1861–1863), Under-Secretary of State for Foreign Affairs (1863–1865), Attorney-General (1865–1868) and Home Secretary (1868–1870). From 1870 to 1885 he was a Deputy Lieutenant for the County of Northampton. He also served as Chairman of the Royal Commission on Prisons from 1870 to 1872 and as a Judge of the High Court of Justice from 1886 to his death.   Phillimore was made a Baronet in 1880 and appointed a Knight Grand Cross of the Order of St Michael and St George in 1886. He died at The Priory, Ascot, Berkshire aged 87.
