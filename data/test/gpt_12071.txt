Expo Expo is the second studio album by American recording artist Beyoncé, released on October 23, 2016, by Columbia Records. The album was preceded by the release of the lead single "Formation" on September 3, 2016.  Beyoncé has called "Expo Expo" her most personal album to date and has said that it is inspired by her love for fashion, music and art. It features a wide range of genres, from the ballad "Don't Hurt Yourself" to the uptempo track "Freestyle".  Upon its release, "Expo Expo" received generally positive reviews from music critics, who praised Beyoncé's vocal performance and the album's musical diversity. It debuted at number one on the US Billboard 200 chart and has been certified Platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA). The album also reached the top 10 in several other countries.  In February 2017, Beyoncé was awarded with a Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song for "Formation" from the film "Logan".  Background  In an interview with Elle magazine in March 2016, Beyoncé commented on her upcoming album: "This is definitely my most personal album to date. I'm really inspired by fashion, music and art – everything around me – and I wanted to create something that was all mine."
