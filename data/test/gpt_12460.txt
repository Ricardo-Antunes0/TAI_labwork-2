is Hiroshi Teshigahara's film about the 16th century Zen Buddhist monk and gastronome, Rikyu.  Rikyu (1573-1652) was a famous Zen Buddhist monk and gastronome of Japan's Sengoku period. He is celebrated for his innovative cooking techniques and his mastery of the art of tea ceremony. Teshigahara's film tells the story of Rikyu's journey from a young boy to one of Japan's most renowned monks and gastronomes.
