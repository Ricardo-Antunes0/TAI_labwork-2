Eucalyptus rugulata is a species of mallet or tree that is endemic to a restricted area of Western Australia. It has smooth bark, lance-shaped adult leaves, ribbed flower buds in groups of seven, creamy white flowers and shortened spherical fruit. Description Eucalyptus rugulata is a mallet or tree that typically grows to a height of  but does not form a lignotuber. It has smooth grey bark that is shed in strips. Adult leaves are the same dark, glossy green on both sides, lance-shaped,  long and  wide, tapering to a petiole  long. The flower buds are arranged in leaf axils in groups of seven on an unbranched peduncle  long, the individual buds on pedicels  long. Mature buds are an elongated oval shape,  long and  wide with a ribbed floral cup and a smooth, conical operculum. Flowering has been recorded in November and the flowers are creamy white. The fruit is a woody, shortened spherical capsule with the valves protruding strongly but fragile.
