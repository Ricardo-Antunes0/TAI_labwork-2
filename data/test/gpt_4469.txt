Michael Granger (May 14, 1923 – October 3, 2013) was an American actor. He appeared in more than 140 films and television shows spanning six decades.  Granger was born in Detroit, Michigan, the son of Katherine (née Lynch) and Michael Granger, a carpenter. After graduating from high school, he served in the United States Navy during World War II. He began his acting career in the 1950s, appearing in supporting roles in films such as The Ugly American (1961) and A Thousand Clowns (1963). He became well known for his role as Deputy Sheriff Andy Taylor on the long-running CBS TV series Gunsmoke from 1955 to 1975. Granger also starred in several made-for-TV movies based on the show during that time, including The Return of Gunsmoke (1971), A Man Called Gun (1972), and Cheyenne Autumn (1975).  Granger appeared in numerous films throughout the 1960s and 1970s, including The Verdict (1962), The Omen (1976), Star Wars: Episode IV – A New Hope (1977), Superman (1978), and The Great Santini (1979). He also appeared in a number of acclaimed films directed by Martin Scorsese, including Mean Streets (1973), Taxi Driver (1976), Raging Bull (1980), and Goodfellas (1990).
