Agathosma serratifolia, known as longleaf buchu or silver dollar buchu, is a species of flowering plant in the buchu family, Buxaceae. It is endemic to eastern North America, where it is found in the Southeast United States and the Appalachian Mountains.  The longleaf buchu is a deciduous shrub or small tree up to 10 m tall, with a trunk up to 2 m diameter. The bark is scaly and flaky. The leaves are alternate, long and narrow, with serrated margins. The flowers are white or pink, 2–3 cm diameter, and produced in clusters at the ends of the branches. The fruit is a capsule 6–8 cm long, containing two seeds.
