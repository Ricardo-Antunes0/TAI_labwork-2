Lorin Varencove Maazel (, March 6, 1930 – February 5, 2017) was an American conductor and music director. He was the music director of the Philadelphia Orchestra from 1978 to 2002, and the Israel Philharmonic Orchestra from 1978 to 2003.  Born in New York City, Maazel studied at the Juilliard School of Music, where he was a pupil of Leonard Bernstein. He made his conducting debut in 1955 with the New York Philharmonic. In 1957 he became music director of the Bavarian Radio Symphony Orchestra, a post he held until 1963. From 1963 to 1978 he was music director of the Cleveland Orchestra, then one of America's leading orchestras.  In 1978 Maazel became music director of the Philadelphia Orchestra, a post he held until 2002. He also served as principal conductor of the Montreal Symphony Orchestra from 1981 to 1984 and of the Dresden Staatskapelle from 1985 to 1994. In 2003 he became music director of the Israel Philharmonic Orchestra, a post he held until his death.  Maazel made numerous recordings, both with his own orchestras and with others. Among his best-known recordings are Mahler's Sixth and Ninth symphonies with the Philadelphia Orchestra, Berlioz's Grande symphonie fantastique with the Berlin Philharmonic, and Beethoven's Ninth symphony with the Cleveland Orchestra.
