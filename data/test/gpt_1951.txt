John William Boldeman   is an American author, filmmaker, and artist. He is best known for his novel Thirteen Reasons Why and the Netflix series based on the book.  Boldeman was born in 1961 in Boston, Massachusetts. He graduated from Brown University in 1983 with a degree in English. After college, Boldeman moved to San Francisco, where he worked as a graphic designer. In 1990 he published his first book, a collection of short stories called The Painter's Room. Two years later he wrote and directed his first film, the dark comedy The Adventures of Buckaroo Banzai Across the 8th Dimension.  In 2003 Boldeman published Thirteen Reasons Why, a novel about teenage suicide which was adapted into the successful Netflix series released in 2017. His latest novel is The Last Full Measure, which was released in 2018. Boldeman also directs and produces films and television shows.
