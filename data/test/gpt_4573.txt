Geb was the Egyptian god of the Earth, the underworld and the dead. He was a powerful deity who was able to create mountains and valleys, and he was also known as the "Lord of the Underworld".  Geb was one of the oldest gods in Egyptian mythology, and he was often associated with the sun god Ra. He was also associated with the Nile River, which is why he was often called "the Lord of the Nile".  Geb was often worshiped in temples built near water sources, and he was also considered to be a god of fertility. He was also known as the "Lord of the Dead", and he was believed to be able to help people who were dead return to life.
