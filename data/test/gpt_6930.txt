Don E. Eberly (born 1953) is an American astronomer, who is a Distinguished Professor of Astronomy at the University of Texas at Austin. He is known for his work on the evolution and structure of galaxies, including studies of the Large Scale Structure of the Universe, dark matter, and the origin and evolution of stars.  Born in 1953 in Amarillo, Texas, Don Eberly received his B.A. in astronomy from the University of Texas at Austin in 1976 and his Ph.D. in astronomy from Harvard University in 1983. He was a postdoctoral research associate at the University of California, Berkeley from 1983 to 1985 and then a member of the faculty at the University of Texas at Austin until he became a Distinguished Professor in 2006.  Eberly has held distinguished visiting professorships at Yale University (1995), Cambridge University (2001), Imperial College London (2005), and The Australian National University (2010). He was elected to the American Academy of Arts and Sciences in 2006 and to the National Academy of Sciences in 2010.
