He is best known for his books of poetry, most notably The Sorrows of Young Werther (1963), The Irony of American History (1968), and The Omnibus (1979).  Bly was born in Sheboygan, Wisconsin, the son of a Presbyterian minister. He attended Princeton University and the University of Chicago. He served in the United States Army during the Korean War and then worked as a teacher, journalist, and editor. He was awarded a Guggenheim Fellowship in 1967.   Bly's work often critiques American culture and society. His poetry has been compared to that of Walt Whitman and T.S. Eliot. He died in 2000 at the age of 78.
