Henry Keswick (1870 – 29 November 1928) was an Australian politician who served as a Senator for New South Wales from 1923 to 1928.  Keswick was born in 1870 at Armidale, New South Wales, the son of William Keswick and his wife Emily (née Norton). He attended Armidale Public School before studying at the University of Sydney, where he graduated with a Bachelor of Arts in 1892. Keswick then studied law at the London Inns of Court, qualifying as a solicitor in 1895.  Keswick was elected to the New South Wales Legislative Assembly as the Labor member for Liverpool in 1902. He served as Attorney-General from 1904 to 1906 and from 1908 to 1909, and was Minister for Railways from 1909 to 1913. In 1916, Keswick was appointed to the Senate, where he served until his death in 1928. He was a member of the Senate's Joint Committee on Public Works and was also Chairman of the Senate's Committee on Tariff and Customs. Keswick died in Sydney on 29 November 1928 and was buried in Rookwood Cemetery.
