Ghassan J. Hage (born 1957 in Beirut, Lebanon) is a Lebanese-American academic who specializes in Arab and Middle Eastern Studies.  Ghassan Hage was born in Beirut, Lebanon in 1957. He attended the American University of Beirut, where he received his B.A. in Arabic Language and Literature in 1979. He then studied at the University of California, Berkeley, where he received his M.A. in Arabic Literature and Culture in 1982 and his Ph.D. in 1986.  Hage has been a professor of Arabic at the University of California, San Diego since 1986. He has also been a visiting professor at the University of Chicago, the University of Texas at Austin, and the University of London. He is currently a distinguished professor at UC San Diego.  Hage's research focuses on Arab and Middle Eastern Studies, with a particular emphasis on Lebanese literature and culture. He has written several books on these topics, including The Modernization of Islam: Arabs and Muslims under European Influence (1996), The Lebanese Crisis: A History (1997), The Gulf War: Origins and Consequences (1990), and The Arab World Today: A Political Reader (1996). He is also the editor of two volumes of essays, The Oxford History of Arabic Literature (2002) and Arab Literatures: A Reader (2008).
