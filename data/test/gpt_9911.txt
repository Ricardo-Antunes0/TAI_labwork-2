Richmond International Airport  is a joint civil and military airport located in Richmond, Virginia, United States. It is the busiest international airport in the Mid-Atlantic region and the fourth busiest in the country. It is also the busiest airport in the world that is not in a state with a major metropolitan area. In 2016, Richmond International Airport had an annual passenger traffic of over 56 million, making it the eleventh busiest airport in the world by passenger traffic. The airport serves as a hub for American Airlines and Delta Air Lines, and is also a focus city for Allegiant Air. The Richmond International Airport was built during World War II as one of the nine original airfields assigned to the United States Army Air Forces Fourth Air Force. It was designated as Richmond Army Air Field on November 20, 1942. The airfield was used by the United States Army Air Forces Third Air Force as a training center for bomber crews. The airfield was closed on February 15, 1945, and turned over to the Civil Aeronautics Authority (CAA). The original terminal building, now known as Terminal 3, was opened on May 15, 1946.
