Adimoolam was born in 1938 in Tiruchirappalli, Tamil Nadu, India. He was educated at Presidency College, Chennai and then at the University of Cambridge, where he obtained a degree in Economics. He worked as a journalist before beginning his writing career.  Adimoolam's first book, a Tamil novel titled "Ninaithaandi Varuvaya", was published in 1984. His most famous work is the English-language novel "The Silent Village" (2003), which tells the story of two boys from different backgrounds who are abducted and held captive by the Sri Lankan military during the final stages of the Sri Lankan Civil War. The book was adapted into a successful film adaptation released in 2007. Adimoolam also wrote the screenplay for the film.  Adimoolam lived in Chennai until his death on 15 August 2019.
