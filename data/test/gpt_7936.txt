Richard Henry "Harry" Goodhew AO (born 19 September 1931) is an Australian-born British academic, philosopher and broadcaster. He is the current Chancellor of the University of Bristol, England.  Goodhew was born in Sydney, Australia, to parents of English descent. He attended the University of Sydney, where he graduated with a Bachelor of Arts (BA) with honours in 1951. He then attended the University of Cambridge, where he completed a Master of Arts (MA) in 1952 and a Doctor of Philosophy (DPhil) in 1954.  Goodhew began his academic career as a fellow at Trinity College, Cambridge from 1954 to 1956. He then became a lecturer at Birmingham University from 1956 to 1958, and then a senior lecturer at Oxford University from 1958 to 1970. He was Head of the Department of Philosophy at Oxford from 1970 to 1973, and then Vice-Chancellor of the University of Edinburgh from 1973 to 1978.  Goodhew was made a Fellow of the Royal Society of Edinburgh in 1978, and he served as its President from 1984 to 1986. He was made an Honorary Fellow of Trinity College, Cambridge in 1987. In 1990 he was appointed Chancellor of the University of Bristol, where he served until his retirement in 2001.  Goodhew has written extensively on philosophy and other subjects, including ethics, religion, art, literature and history.
