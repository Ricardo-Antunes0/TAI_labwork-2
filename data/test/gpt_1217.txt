Jules Guérin (November 18, 1866 – June 8, 1924) was a French painter and printmaker who is best known for his Symbolist works.  Guérin was born in Lille, Nord, and studied at the Académie Julian in Paris. He began painting in the 1890s, often depicting rural scenes and landscapes. In 1904, he exhibited at the Salon d'Automne, where he was awarded a medal. He later moved to the south of France and concentrated on painting Mediterranean scenes.  In 1913, Guérin exhibited at the Armory Show in New York City. His work was later featured in the 1941 exhibition "French Painting from the Twentieth Century", which toured several American museums. He died in 1924 in Nice.
