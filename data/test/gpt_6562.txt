Healdtown Comprehensive School is a Methodist school located in Healdtown, a small town in the province of Newfoundland and Labrador, Canada. The school has approximately 630 students in grades 6-12 and offers a traditional secondary school curriculum.  The school was founded in 1892 as the Healdtown School and was originally a one-room schoolhouse. In 1950, the school was rebuilt to its present form and it has since undergone several renovations. The school is named after Reverend Heald, the first Methodist minister in the town.  Healdtown Comprehensive School is known for its athletic programs, which have produced numerous athletes who have gone on to compete at the provincial and national levels. The school's main athletic rivals are Bishop Feild College and Memorial Collegiate.  The school is also known for its music program, which has produced several successful musicians. Past performers include Sonia Daddario, Connie Britton, and Kiefer Sutherland.  The school's social life is also popular, with students participating in a variety of clubs and organizations. These include the Drama Club, Chess Club, Mathletes, Music Club, French Club, Technology Club, Robotics Club, Environmental Club, and many more.  Healdtown Comprehensive School is consistently ranked among the top schools in Newfoundland and Labrador. In 2017, it was ranked number one in terms of academic achievement by the Fraser Institute.
