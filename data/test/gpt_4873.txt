Daniel Jan "Daan" Jippes (born 14 October 1951) is a retired Dutch politician of the Christian Democratic Appeal (CDA). He served as Prime Minister of the Netherlands from 1998 to 2002, and as Minister for Foreign Affairs from 1994 to 1998.  Daan Jippes was born in Amsterdam on 14 October 1951. After studying law at the University of Amsterdam, he started his political career as a member of the city council of Amsterdam from 1978 to 1982. He then served as mayor of Amsterdam from 1982 to 1994.  Jippes entered the national parliament in 1989, when he was appointed member of the House of Representatives for the electoral district of Amsterdam-West. As minister, he played a leading role in the negotiations that led to the Netherlands' membership in the European Union.  After his term as prime minister ended, Jippes served as president of the Pompidou Centre from 2002 to 2006. In 2008 he was made a Knight of the Order of Orange-Nassau.   He is married and has two children.
