Dubravka is a drama of mythological-pastoral content written by Serbian playwright Vuk Karadžić and first performed in Belgrade in 1940.   The play tells the story of Dubravka, a young woman who is betrothed to Prince Marko but falls in love with another man, Ivan. When Marko finds out, he forces Dubravka to choose between him and Ivan, and she chooses Ivan. Marko then travels to find Ivan and fight for Dubravka's love, but they are both killed. Dubravka mourns their deaths and eventually remarries.
