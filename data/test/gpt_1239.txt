Hessenford  is a small village in Wiltshire, England, about 3 miles south of Salisbury. The village has a population of just over 100.  The village is mentioned in the Domesday Book as Henford. It is thought that the name means 'hill ford'. The village was originally an agricultural settlement and was used as a crossing point for the Wiltshire Way. The first recorded mention of a church in the village is in the 12th century.  The village has a number of listed buildings, including the 16th century terrace houses on Church Street and the 17th century George Inn. There is also a windmill located in the village.
