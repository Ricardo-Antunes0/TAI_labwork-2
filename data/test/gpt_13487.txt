"Follow the Wind" is a song by the Eagles. The song was written by Don Henley and Bernie Leadon. It was released in 1971 as the second single from the Eagles' album of the same name.  "Follow the Wind" is a slow ballad about a man who is looking for a way out of his life. The song's lyrics describe how the man is unable to escape his problems, even when he tries to follow the wind. The song was commercially successful, peaking at number 10 on the US Billboard Hot 100 chart. It has since been certified Platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA).  The song has been covered by a number of artists, most notably by Irish singer Enya who released a version in 1984 which reached number one on the UK Singles Chart. American country singer Kenny Rogers also covered the song in 1984, and Canadian rock band Rush recorded a version in 1990 which reached number two on the US charts. British rock band Muse performed a version of "Follow the Wind" at their 2003 debut concert at Wembley Stadium.
