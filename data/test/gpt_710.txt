Vincent Norman Colbert (born December 20, 1945) is an American comedian, actor, writer, and political commentator. He is the host of the Comedy Central show The Colbert Report and the former host of the Fox News show The Colbert Report.  Colbert was born in Shreveport, Louisiana. He attended Tulane University in New Orleans, graduating with a B.A. in history in 1967. After graduation, he worked as a writer for the local CBS affiliate in New Orleans. Colbert then moved to Washington D.C., where he became a correspondent for Comedy Central's The Daily Show with Jon Stewart. In 1996, he debuted his own half-hour comedy show on Comedy Central, which became one of the network's most popular programs. He also appeared as a guest star on various TV shows, including Late Night with Conan O'Brien and The Simpsons.  In 2006, Colbert announced he was leaving The Daily Show to create a new political satire show on cable television, The Colbert Report. The show became one of Comedy Central's most popular programs and won two Peabody Awards in 2006 and 2007. In 2008, Colbert returned to The Daily Show as its host. In 2010, he left The Daily Show to focus full-time on his new show.
