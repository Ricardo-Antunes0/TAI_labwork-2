Robert Paul Ruwe (born 1941) is a British historian who specializes in the history of the Cold War.   Born in 1941 in Bournemouth, England, Ruwe graduated from the University of Oxford with a degree in history. He subsequently worked as a researcher at the London School of Economics, before becoming a lecturer at the University of Sussex.  Ruwe's work has focused on the history of the Cold War, particularly its origins and development. He is particularly noted for his book The Origins of the Cold War: A History of Soviet-American Relations, published in 1988. This book won the 1989 John L. Snell Award for best book on international relations from the American Political Science Association.
