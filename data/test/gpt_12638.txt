Egge Church  is a parish church in Aker municipality in Rogaland county, Norway. It is located in the village of Eggevoll, just northeast of the town of Egge. The church is part of the Egge parish in the Haugaland deanery in the Diocese of Stavanger. The white, wooden church was built in a long church style in 1892 by the architect Johannes Henrik Fauskanger. The church seats about 350 people.   The first church on this site was probably built around 1100. The present church was built in 1892 by the architect Johannes Henrik Fauskanger. The construction was done in a long church style, with a white wooden exterior. The church was consecrated on 25 November 1892 by Bishop Johan Christian Eckhoff.
