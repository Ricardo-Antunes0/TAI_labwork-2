Jaime Francisco Sebastián Castillo Petruzzi, known as Jaime Castillo Petruzzi (born February 8, 1984 in Rosario, Santa Fe), is an Argentine professional footballer who plays for Club Atlético River Plate as a left back.   Jaime Castillo Petruzzi was born in 1984 in Rosario, Santa Fe, Argentina. He started his career at Club Atlético River Plate, where he made his professional debut in 2002. After impressing with River Plate, Jaime joined English club Bolton Wanderers on loan for the 2003-2004 season. He made his Bolton debut on 11 February 2004 in a 1-1 draw against Aston Villa. Bolton Wanderers decided not to make the loan permanent and Jaime returned to River Plate in the summer of 2004.  Jaime continued to play for River Plate until 2006, when he joined Racing Club de Avellaneda. He spent two seasons with Racing Club de Avellaneda before joining Club Brugge KV in Belgium. He spent two seasons with Club Brugge KV before returning to River Plate in 2010.  Jaime Castillo Petruzzi has played for Argentina at under-20 level, and made his senior international debut on October 10, 2006 in a 2-0 win over Paraguay.
