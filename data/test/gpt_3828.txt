Johann Christian Bauer (1802–1867) was a German historian.  Bauer was born in Lübeck, the son of a locksmith. He studied at the universities of Göttingen and Berlin, where he was a student of Georg Friedrich Hegel and Karl August von Stein. Bauer became a professor at the University of Königsberg in 1844, and in 1851 became a professor at the University of Halle. He died in Halle.  Bauer is most famous for his work on history of the German people, particularly his treatise "Geschichte des deutschen Volkes" (1851–1854). Bauer also wrote works on medieval history, comparative history, and German literature.
