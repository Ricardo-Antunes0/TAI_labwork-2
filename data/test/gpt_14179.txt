Christian Sebaldt is a German-born cinematographer best known for his work on the films The Village (2004), The Weaker Sex (2009), and Inglourious Basterds (2009).  Sebaldt was born in Bremen, Germany, in 1967. After graduating from the University of the Arts Berlin in 1991 with a degree in film and video studies, he started his career as a cameraman working on low-budget German films. He made his first big break when he was chosen to photograph the critically acclaimed German film The Village, which was released in 2004. Sebaldt's distinctive visual style was influential in bringing attention to the film, and he went on to work on a number of high-profile Hollywood productions, including The Weaker Sex, Inglourious Basterds, and Snow White and the Huntsman.  Sebaldt currently resides in Berlin.
