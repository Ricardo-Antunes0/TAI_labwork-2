McManis was born in Terre Haute, Indiana, and studied at the University of Michigan with Bernard Herrmann. In 1938 he moved to New York City, where he worked as a pianist and arranger for radio and television. In 1945 he founded the Charles McManis Orchestra, which he directed for the next 34 years. The orchestra made its Carnegie Hall debut in 1952 and recorded prolifically for RCA Victor and other labels. McManis also composed music for film and stage productions. He died in 1995.
