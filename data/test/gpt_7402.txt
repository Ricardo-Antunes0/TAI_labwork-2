Arravale Rovers GAA  is Gaelic Athletic Association club based in the village of Arravale, County Kildare, Ireland. The club fields teams in both hurling and Gaelic football. The club has produced a number of inter-county players, including All-Ireland Hurling Championship winners brothers Noel and John O'Dwyer.  The club was founded in 1880 and played its first game against Naas. It was originally known as the Arravale Harps GAA Club. The current name was adopted in 1951. In recent years the club has enjoyed success in both codes, claiming two All-Ireland Senior Hurling Championship titles in 1985 and 1991 as well as two Leinster Senior Hurling Championship titles in 1984 and 2010. The club also reached the quarter-finals of the 2011 All-Ireland Hurling Championship. In Gaelic football, Arravale Rovers have won one County League title and one Hogan Cup title.
