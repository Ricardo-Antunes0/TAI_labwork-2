Paul Thek (November 2, 1933 – August 5, 2007) was an American artist and sculptor, who is best known for his sculpture of the human form.  Born in Chicago, Illinois, Thek studied at the School of the Art Institute of Chicago and the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. He also studied at the Université de Grenoble in France.  Thek's sculpture has been exhibited at the Whitney Museum of American Art, the Museum of Modern Art in New York City, and the Tate Gallery in London. He has been awarded several awards, including a National Medal of Arts in 1998.  Thek died from lymphoma at his home in Woodstock, New York, aged 75.
