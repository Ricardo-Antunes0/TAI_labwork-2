Aziz-Ur-Rahman Ibrahim (born 16 March 1964) is a Nigerian politician who has been the President of Nigeria since 14 May 2015. Ibrahim was re-elected for a second term as president in March 2019.  Ibrahim was born on 16 March 1964 in Zaria, Nigeria. He holds a B.Sc. degree from the University of Ibadan, and an M.B.A. from the University of London.   He started his career in banking, working at the First Bank of Nigeria, before moving to pursue a career in politics. In 1991, he was elected to the Senate, and served until 1999.   Ibrahim became vice president of Nigeria in 2007, and was elected president in 2014, defeating long-time incumbent Goodluck Jonathan by more than two million votes. He took office on 14 May 2015.
