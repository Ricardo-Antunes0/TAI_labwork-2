Sungaya is a monotypic genus of stick insects in the family Phasmidae. There are only two species in the genus, Sungaya bipunctata and Sungaya nigricornis. The genus is endemic to the New World, with S. nigricornis being found from Panama to Costa Rica.  The genus is known for its brightly coloured hindwings, which are often patterned with stripes or spots. These insects are nocturnal, and feed on plant juices.
