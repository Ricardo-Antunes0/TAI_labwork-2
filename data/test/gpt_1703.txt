Amar'e Carsares Stoudemire (; ; born November 28, 1984) is an American professional basketball player for the Memphis Grizzlies of the National Basketball Association (NBA). He played college basketball for the Syracuse Orange, where he was a two-time unanimous first-team All-American.  Stoudemire was drafted by the Phoenix Suns with the second pick in the 2003 NBA draft. He won two championship titles with the Suns before being traded to the New York Knicks in 2011. He then joined the Memphis Grizzlies in 2013. He has also played for the Phoenix Suns, Knicks, and Golden State Warriors. In 2017, Stoudemire was named an NBA All-Star for the sixth time in his career.  Stoudemire is known for his high scoring ability, powerful dunks, and long arms. He is a four-time All-Star and was named to the All-NBA First Team twice. He is also a two-time Olympic gold medalist and a two-time FIBA Americas Player of the Year.  Stoudemire was born in Springfield, Massachusetts on November 28, 1984. He attended Springfield Central High School where he played basketball under coach Rick Majerus. As a senior, Stoudemire averaged 26 points and 10 rebounds per game en route to being named Massachusetts Player of the Year.
