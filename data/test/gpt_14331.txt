Payanam is a village in kanyakumari district, Tamil Nadu, India. The village is located about 7 km from Thiruvannamalai and about 240 km from Chennai.   The economy of the village is largely dependent on agriculture. The main crops grown are paddy, sugarcane, and maize. There is also a small scale cottage industries in weaving and making pots and pans. There is also a small scale cooperative dairy farm. The village has a library and a school. There are also a few temples in the village.  The village has a population of about 1400 people. Most of the people are Hindus, with a few Muslims and others living in the village. The language spoken in the village is Tamil.
