The 340th Weapons Squadron is a United States Air Force unit. The squadron was first activated on 3 December 1943, as the 340th Bombardment Squadron, a B-24 Liberator heavy bomber squadron. Inactivated on 15 September 1945, it was reactivated on 1 January 1951 as the 340th Fighter-Bomber Squadron, flying F-86A Sabres. It was inactivated on 1 July 1957 and reactivated again on 1 October 1965 as the 340th Tactical Fighter Squadron, flying F-4 Phantom IIs. The squadron was inactivated on 1 October 1991.  The squadron's current mission is to provide air defense of United States Air Force installations worldwide. The squadron operates from Joint Base Myer-Henderson Hall, Virginia.
