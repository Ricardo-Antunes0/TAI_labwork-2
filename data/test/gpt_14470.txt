Nino Burjanadze (Georgian:  ნინო ბურჯანაძე , born 26 February 1970) is a Georgian politician who served as the first female Prime Minister of Georgia from 2007 to 2008.  Burjanadze served as the Minister of Justice from 2002 to 2004, and as President of the Parliament of Georgia from 2004 to 2007. She was appointed Prime Minister on 18 May 2007, following the resignation of Giorgi Margvelashvili. Burjanadze lost her office on 10 November 2008, after a political crisis that followed the 2008 South Ossetia War. She was arrested on charges of treason and abuse of power, but was later released and fled to Russia.  Burjanadze was born in Tbilisi, Georgia, into a family with a military background. Her father, General Giorgi Burjanadze, was a prominent Soviet-era military officer who later served as Minister of Defense for several years in the late 1980s and early 1990s. Nino Burjanadze attended the Tbilisi State University, where she graduated with a degree in law in 1992. After graduation, she worked as an assistant at the Ministry of Justice from 1992 to 1994.  Burjanadze began her political career in 1994, when she was elected to the Parliament of Georgia as a member of the Democratic Party of Georgia (DPG).
