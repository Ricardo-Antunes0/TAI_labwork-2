Luisa Kapp-Young (, Young; pseudonym, Luisa Cappiani; 12 December 1884 – 9 February 1966) was an Italian writer, translator and journalist.  Born in Milan, Luisa Cappiani was the daughter of an English father, who became an Italian citizen in 1898, and an Italian mother. After her father's death in 1907, Cappiani moved to England with her mother. There she studied at the London School of Economics and Political Science and at the University of London, where she graduated with a degree in philosophy.  In 1915, Cappiani returned to Italy, where she worked as a journalist for various magazines. In 1922 she published her first book, a translation of Marcel Proust's "À la Recherche du Temps Perdu". The following year she published her second book, a novel entitled "I Notti di Cabiria".  Cappiani's literary career took off in 1926 when she published her third book, a novel entitled "Il Gattopardo". The book was highly successful and won the prestigious Strega Prize. It also served as the basis for the 1963 film of the same name directed by Luchino Visconti.
