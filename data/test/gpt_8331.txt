Jean-Pierre Maxence (20 August 1906 – 16 February 1976) was a French composer and conductor.  He was born in Lyon, France, the son of a violinist father and pianist mother. He studied with Nadia Boulanger and graduated from the Conservatoire de Paris in 1928. Maxence began his career as a conductor, joining the Lyon Opera in 1931 and later the Paris Opera in 1935. He also served as music director of the Marseille Opera from 1951 to 1959.  Maxence's compositions include operas, ballets, symphonies, and pieces for chamber music. His most well-known works are the orchestral works "Danse macabre" (1938), "La Belle et la Bête" (1946), "Pelléas et Mélisande" (1953), and "Rigoletto" (1958). He also wrote incidental music for several films, including "Le Jour se lève" (1939), "Moi, un Noir" (1948), and "Le Chalet" (1954).  Maxence died in Paris of a heart attack at the age of 69.
