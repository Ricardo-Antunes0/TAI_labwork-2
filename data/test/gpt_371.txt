The Shastri Indo-Canadian Institute is a binational research and educational institute with a mandate to promote understanding and cooperation between Canada and India. The Institute was founded in 1984 by Dr. L. K. Shastri, the then Prime Minister of India, and Mr. R. V. Shastri, the then Minister of Canadian Heritage.   The Institute is located in Ottawa, Ontario, Canada, and is affiliated with the University of Ottawa. It conducts research on a wide range of topics related to Canada-India relations, including trade, investment, politics, culture and education. The Institute also offers graduate and undergraduate programs in Canadian studies, international relations and other disciplines.   The Shastri Indo-Canadian Institute is a source of information and advice on Canadian-Indian affairs for government officials, business executives and the general public. It also provides opportunities for scholars from both countries to collaborate on research projects.
