Peter Thomas Ward (born July 26, 1937) is an American former professional baseball player. A right-handed pitcher, Ward pitched in Major League Baseball for the Boston Red Sox (1962–1966), Philadelphia Phillies (1967), and California Angels (1968).     Ward was elected to the Baseball Hall of Fame in 1992.  Ward was born in Marion, Ohio. He attended Marion High School where he played basketball and baseball. He also attended Ohio State University, where he played college baseball for the Buckeyes.  Ward was drafted by the Boston Red Sox in the first round of the 1962 amateur draft. He made his debut with the Red Sox in 1962 and pitched in parts of four seasons. In 1966, he was traded to the Philadelphia Phillies and posted a 12-5 record with a 3.27 ERA in 24 games (19 starts). On October 15, 1967, Ward became the first pitcher in MLB history to allow five home runs in an inning (against the Los Angeles Dodgers). The following season, he went 11-6 with a 2.69 ERA and led the league in complete games (15). In 1968, Ward posted a 13-7 record with a 3.49 ERA and won his only World Series championship with the California Angels.  After his playing days were over, Ward served as pitching coach for the Reds (1970) and Mariners (1973–1976) before becoming their manager midway through 1976 season.
