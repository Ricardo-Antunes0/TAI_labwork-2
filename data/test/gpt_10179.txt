Campanula patula or spreading bellflower is a species of bellflower known by the common name spreading bellflower. It is native to much of North America, where it grows in many types of habitat, from deserts to montane forests.  This perennial herb typically grows up to 1 foot tall, but may reach 2 feet or more in height. The leaves are linear or lance-shaped and up to 8 inches long, and they are attached at the base of a petiole long stem. The inflorescence is a raceme of bell-shaped flowers up to 2 inches wide, each with five white petals and a long yellow stamen. The fruit is a capsule about a quarter-inch long.
