The Edmund Barton Building (known originally as the Federal Buildings) is a prominent skyscraper in Melbourne, Australia. It was completed in 1972 and has since been the tallest building in the city for over thirty years. The building is named after Edmund Barton, the first Prime Minister of Australia.  The Edmund Barton Building is located in the central business district of Melbourne, Australia. It stands at a height of 174 m (574 ft), making it the tallest building in Melbourne and the fourth-tallest building in Australia. The building was designed by architect Harry Seidler and is made from a combination of polished granite, glass and steel. The building has a total floor area of 1,095,000 square metres (11,000,000 sq ft).
