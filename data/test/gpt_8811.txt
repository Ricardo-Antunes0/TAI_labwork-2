A' Mhòine  is a peninsula in the Inner Hebrides, Scotland. The peninsula is located on the north-west coast of the mainland of Scotland. It is separated from the mainland by the Loch Moidart.     The peninsula is characterised by steep-sided hills and lochs, and is home to a variety of wildlife including otters, deer and seals. A' Mhòine also has a number of ancient castles, churches and distilleries including Ardbeg, Glenmorangie and Glenrothes.
