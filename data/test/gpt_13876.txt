John Lee Hancock Jr. (born December 15, 1962) is an American screenwriter, director, and producer. He has written and directed films including The Blind Side (2009), Hancock (2008), and The Best Exotic Marigold Hotel (2015).  Hancock was born in Boston, Massachusetts. He studied at the University of Southern California School of Cinematic Arts. Hancock began his career as a television writer and producer, working on series such as ER, Monk, and The West Wing. Hancock's first feature film screenplay was the drama The Great Santini (1999). His second film, the comedy-drama The Blind Side, which he wrote and directed, received widespread critical acclaim and made Hancock a household name. Hancock has since written and directed the drama Hancock (2008), the action thriller Battle: Los Angeles (2011), and the romantic comedy-drama The Best Exotic Marigold Hotel (2015).
