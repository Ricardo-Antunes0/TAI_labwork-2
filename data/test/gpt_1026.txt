Adam Whitney Savage (born July 15, 1967) is an American television and radio personality, scientist, and author. He is the co-host of the television show MythBusters and the author of several books on science and technology. Savage attended Germantown Academy, a private boarding school in suburban Philadelphia. He then attended the University of California, Berkeley, where he earned a bachelor's degree in engineering in 1989.  Savage started his professional career as a product tester for 3M Company. In 1992, he started working as a technical producer for the television show Star Trek: The Next Generation. He was promoted to technical consultant for the show in 1995. Savage left Star Trek in 1997 to pursue a career in television and writing.  In 1998, Savage co-created the television show MythBusters with Jamie Hyneman. The show aired on the Discovery Channel from 1999 to 2017, becoming one of the most popular science programs on television. Savage has also written four books based on MythBusters: The Complete Collection (2000), MythBusters: The Explosive Truth (2002), The Science of MythBusters (2006), and MythBusters: The Unofficial Science of MythBusters (2013).
