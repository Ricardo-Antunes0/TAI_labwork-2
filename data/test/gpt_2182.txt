Walter Kaaden (1 September 1919 – 3 November 2006) was a Swiss artist, sculptor and graphic artist.  Kaaden was born in Nidau, in the Canton of Bern. He studied at the School of Applied Arts in Zürich and later at the Academy of Fine Arts in Vienna. He worked as an assistant to Otto Neurath and Joseph Beuys. Kaaden is best known for his large-scale abstract sculptures, which are often based on the principles of symmetry and repetition.
