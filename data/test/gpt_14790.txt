Fiona Stewart (born 15 May 1966) is a Scottish author. She is the author of the novel, The Girl in the Photograph, and the memoir, My Life in Pictures.  Stewart was born in Glasgow, Scotland, to a Scottish father and English mother. She was educated at Edinburgh Academy and St Andrews University, where she studied English literature. After graduating from university, Stewart worked as a journalist for a number of newspapers before becoming a full-time writer in 2001.  Stewart's first novel, The Girl in the Photograph, was published in 2006. It tells the story of a young woman named Fiona who is forced to relive her childhood through flashbacks after being diagnosed with post-traumatic stress disorder. The novel was well received by critics, and won several awards including the prestigious Scottish Book Awards and the Betty Trask Award.  In 2008, Stewart published her memoir, My Life in Pictures. The book tells the story of her life from childhood to adulthood, including her experiences as a journalist, author, and mother. It was well received by critics and sold over 100,000 copies worldwide.  Stewart currently lives in Edinburgh with her husband and two children.
