Maxwell V L Barclay FRES is a British mathematician and computer scientist, who works in the areas of combinatorics and graph theory. He is the co-inventor of the algorithm for finding maximum flows in graphs and was awarded the Fields Medal in 1988.  Barclay was born on 19 November 1946 in North London, England. After attending school in North London, he studied at Trinity College, Cambridge, graduating with a Bachelor of Arts degree in 1967. He then attended the University of Oxford, where he received his Master of Science degree in 1970 and his Doctor of Philosophy degree in 1975.  Barclay began his career at the University of Cambridge as a research fellow at Trinity College from 1970 to 1973. From 1973 to 1978 he was a lecturer at Imperial College London; from 1978 to 1984 he was a lecturer at Birkbeck, University of London; and from 1984 to 1988 he was a professor at the University of Edinburgh. Since 1988 he has been a professor at the University of Cambridge.  In 1988 Barclay was awarded the Fields Medal, one of the highest honors in mathematics. He has also received the Shaw Prize for Mathematical Sciences (1984), the Copley Medal (1985), and the Wolf Prize (1988). In 2006 he was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society.
