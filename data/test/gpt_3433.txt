KO One Re-call (; literally: "The Ultimate KO") is a professional wrestling event produced by the Japanese promotion New Japan Pro-Wrestling (NJPW). The event, which is traditionally held in December, is typically billed as the final show of the year and features championship matches or other high-profile matches.  KO One Re-call was first held on December 10, 2006 at the Tokyo Dome in Tokyo, Japan. The inaugural event was won by then-NJPW heavyweight champion Tetsuya Naito who defeated Shinya Aoki in the main event. As of 2008, KO One Re-call has been held at the Saitama Super Arena in Saitama, Japan.  As of 2018, KO One Re-call has been held at the Sapporo Dome in Sapporo, Japan.
