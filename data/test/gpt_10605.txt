Musk deer can refer to any one, or several, of the four species of deer found in Eurasia and North America. The four species are the European musk deer, the North American musk deer, the Caspian musk deer, and the Bactrian musk deer. All of these species are classified within the family Moschidae.   The European musk deer is the most widespread of the four species. It is found in central and eastern Europe, as well as parts of Asia and North Africa. The North American musk deer is found in North America, from central Canada to northern Mexico. The Caspian musk deer is found in Central Asia, and the Bactrian musk deer is found in parts of Central and South Asia.
