Lander County is a county in the U.S. state of Nevada. As of the 2010 census, the population was 18,462. The county seat is Lander.  Lander County comprises the Gardnerville, NV Micropolitan Statistical Area.  Lander County was created on February 4, 1864, from parts of Lincoln and Elko counties. It was named for Col. William Lander, a Union officer killed in action at the Battle of Gettysburg. The county was originally much larger than it is today, including portions of what are now White Pine and Eureka counties.  According to the U.S. Census Bureau, the county has a total area of , of which is land and (0.8%) is water.       As of the census of 2000, there were 16,538 people, 6,097 households, and 4,526 families residing in the county. The population density was 7 people per square mile (3/km²). There were 6,664 housing units at an average density of 3 per square mile (1/km²). The racial makeup of the county was 94.11% White, 0.19% Black or African American, 2.02% Native American, 0.68% Asian, 0.04% Pacific Islander, 1.48% from other races, and 2.90% from two or more races. 4.27% of the population were Hispanic or Latino of any race.
