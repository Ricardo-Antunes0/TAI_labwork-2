Mohamed Lamine Sanha (died 6 January 2007) was a Senegalese diplomat who served as the Permanent Representative of Senegal to the United Nations from 2003 to 2007.  Sanha was born on 6 January 1946 in Dakar, Senegal. After completing his secondary education at the Lycée Sarrasin in Dakar, he studied law at the University of Paris I Panthéon-Sorbonne, where he graduated with a degree in law in 1971. He then practiced law in Senegal.  He served as Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs and Cooperation from 1987 to 1991, and as Minister of Foreign Affairs from 1991 to 1993. He also served as Ambassador to Spain from 1993 to 1997, and Ambassador to the United States of America from 1997 to 2003. He was appointed Permanent Representative of Senegal to the United Nations on 30 March 2003 and served until his death on 6 January 2007.
