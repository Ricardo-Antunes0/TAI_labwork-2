Derrick Martell Rose (born October 4, 1988) is an American professional basketball player for the Chicago Bulls of the National Basketball Association (NBA). He played one season at the University of Memphis before being drafted first overall by the Bulls in the 2008 NBA draft. Rose has won two MVP Awards, three Rookie of the Year Awards, four Most Valuable Player Awards, two scoring titles, and one championship in his six-year career.  Rose is considered to be one of the best players in NBA history and is widely considered to be one of the most dominant players in league history. He has led his teams to an NBA-record 66 playoff appearances, including five appearances in the Finals, winning three championships (2010, 2012, and 2013). In 2011, he was named to the NBA All-Defensive First Team and in 2016 he was named to the All-NBA Second Team. Rose is a two-time Olympic gold medalist with Team USA.
