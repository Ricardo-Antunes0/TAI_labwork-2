Douglas Richard TenNapel ( ; born July 9, 1953) is an American animator, screenwriter, director, producer, and voice actor. He is the co-creator of the animated television series Rocko's Modern Life and Samurai Jack. He also wrote and directed the feature films The Pagemaster (1993), Chicken Run (2000), and King Arthur: Legend of the Sword (2017).  TenNapel was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. He attended Lower Merion High School where he was a member of the debate team and the newspaper staff. After graduation, TenNapel moved to Los Angeles to pursue a career in animation. He met co-creator Tom Scharpling while working on an animated short for The Maxx at Hanna-Barbera Productions. They soon became friends and pitched an idea for a new show called Rocko's Modern Life which aired on Nickelodeon from 1993 to 1996.
