The Chewa regiments were a feudal noble warrior class of the Chewa people of present-day southeastern Zimbabwe. They were divided into small units called "murena", each commanded by a "murena" or war chief. The regiments were used as shock troops to attack neighbouring villages and were also used in large-scale cattle raids. The regiments were also involved in ceremonial life, such as hunting and dancing.
