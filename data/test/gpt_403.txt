Melvyn Reginald Knight (born July 30, 1944) is an American actor, comedian, and writer. He is best known for his work as a cast member on the sketch comedy show Saturday Night Live from 1975 to 1980, and as a writer and performer on the show's subsequent incarnations.  Knight was born in Newark, New Jersey. After graduating from high school, he attended the University of California, Santa Barbara, where he studied theatre. He left UCSB before completing his degree to pursue a career in comedy.  Knight's first job in comedy was as a bongo player with the improvisational comedy group The Players Workshop. He then performed with the National Lampoon Radio Hour and appeared on the TV show The Steve Allen Show.  Knight joined SNL in 1975 as a cast member and stayed until 1980. During his time on SNL, Knight was known for his impressions of celebrities such as Richard Nixon and John Lennon. He also wrote and performed many of the show's sketches, including "The Mel Knight Show", an adaptation of the children's television series Arthur. Knight left SNL to pursue other opportunities, but returned for a brief period in 1985. In addition to his work on SNL, Knight has appeared in several films and television shows, most notably The Blues Brothers (1980), Tootsie (1982), The Birdcage (1996), and Mad TV (1999).
