Gregg De Lorto (born October 12, 1950) is a Canadian orchestral conductor and music educator. He is currently the Music Director of the Edmonton Symphony Orchestra.  Gregg De Lorto was born in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. He began his musical education at the age of eight, studying piano with Marjorie Norris and cello with Joyce Yellen. He then attended the University of Toronto, where he obtained his Bachelor of Arts degree in musicology in 1971.  De Lorto continued his studies at the University of Southern California, where he received his Master of Music degree in 1974 and his Doctor of Musical Arts degree in 1979. During his time at USC, he was a pupil of Leonard Bernstein and Otto Klemperer.  De Lorto began his conducting career with the Edmonton Symphony Orchestra (ESO) in 1979. He has since led the ESO in a number of critically acclaimed concerts and recordings, including works by Beethoven, Brahms, Bruckner, Dvořák, Mahler, Mendelssohn, Ravel and Shostakovich. In addition to his work with the ESO, De Lorto has also served as the conductor of the Alberta Chamber Orchestra (ACO), the Toronto Symphony Orchestra (TSO), and the Calgary Philharmonic Orchestra (CPO).
