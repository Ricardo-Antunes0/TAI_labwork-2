Michael James Nazir-Ali (; born 19 August 1944) is a British Labour Party politician who has served as Member of Parliament for Richmond ( Yorkshire and the Humber) since 1997, and as shadow Foreign Secretary from 2007 to 2010.  Born and brought up in Yorkshire, Nazir-Ali was educated at Haberdashers' Aske's School for Boys in London and at Corpus Christi College, Cambridge. He worked as a journalist before entering politics.  Nazir-Ali was first elected to Parliament in the 1997 general election, defeating the Conservative MP Edward Leigh by 5,500 votes. He has been re-elected with increasing majorities since then, taking his total vote tally to almost 20,000. In 2010 he was appointed Shadow Foreign Secretary, a position he held until the Labour Party's defeat in the general election that year.  He is openly gay and has been an advocate for human rights and minority rights within the Labour Party. He is also a co-founder of the All-Party Parliamentary Group on HIV/Aids. In addition to his political career, Nazir-Ali has been a broadcaster and author.
