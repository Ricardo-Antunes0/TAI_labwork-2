James “Martin” Vinnicombe (born 5 December 1964) is an English musician and songwriter. He is the founder, lead singer, and main songwriter of the alternative rock band Radiohead.  Born in Oxford, England, Vinnicombe was educated at the Dragon School in Oxfordshire. He studied at the University of Exeter, where he met then Radiohead guitarist Jonny Greenwood. The two formed a band called The Moon and helped to launch the career of singer/songwriter Colin Greenwood.  After leaving Radiohead, Vinnicombe released two solo albums: "Martin Vinnicombe" (1998) and "The Unforgiving" (2002). In 2006 he formed the band P J Harvey with Harvey's former partner Paul Ryder and Fleet Foxes drummer Robin Pecknold. They released their debut album "Let England Shake" in 2007. The album was nominated for the Mercury Prize and won the 2009 Grammy Award for Best Alternative Music Album.
