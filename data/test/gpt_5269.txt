Nils Ohlsen (born 1967 in Oldenburg, Germany), is a German artist and sculptor.  Nils Ohlsen was born in 1967 in Oldenburg, Germany. He studied at the Academy of Fine Arts, Hamburg from 1987 to 1991. After graduation, Ohlsen moved to Berlin and worked as an assistant to the art historian Jörg Immendorf. In 1994, he completed his first solo exhibition at the Kunstverein Schwerin.  Ohlsen's work is characterised by its poetic use of materials, such as plastic and metal, as well as its emphasis on the physicality of sculpture. His pieces often explore the relationship between form and function, or between art and everyday life.  Ohlsen has exhibited throughout Germany and abroad, including at the Musée d'Orsay in Paris (2003), the Centre Pompidou in Paris (2002), and the Museum of Modern Art in New York City (2000). He has also been awarded numerous prizes, including the Ernst-Ludwig-Jahn-Preis (2005) and the Heinrich-Heine-Preis (2008).
