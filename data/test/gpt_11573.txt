Aura Cristina Geithner (born March 9, 1967) is an American economist, banker and politician who is the 32nd Treasurer of the United States. She was nominated to the post by President Barack Obama on January 25, 2009, and was confirmed by the United States Senate on March 10, 2009. She has also served in the administrations of both Bill Clinton and George W. Bush.  Geithner is a graduate of Brown University, where she earned her B.A. in 1987 and her M.A. in 1989. After graduation, she worked as a research economist at the Federal Reserve Bank of New York from 1989 to 1993. From 1993 to 1997, she worked for Drexel Burnham Lambert as a vice president and associate chief financial officer. Geithner then served as deputy Treasury secretary from 1997 to 1999, under then-Treasury Secretary Robert Rubin.  Geithner was nominated for secretary of the Treasury by President George W. Bush on November 20, 2005, but withdrew his nomination on February 12, 2006, after reports surfaced that he had overcharged taxpayers for travel expenses while he served as CEO of Bear Stearns Cos., and that he had failed to report a $200 million loss on his taxes in 2001. Geithner was subsequently reappointed to the post by Bush on May 15, 2006.
