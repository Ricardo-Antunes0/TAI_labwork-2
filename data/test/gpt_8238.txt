Stir the Blood is the third studio album by American heavy metal band Slipknot. It was released on November 18, 2009, by Roadrunner Records.  The band began writing material for the album in early 2008, shortly after the release of their second album, All Hope Is Gone. The album was produced by Ross Robinson, who has worked with such bands as Slayer, Depeche Mode and Korn.  "Stir the Blood" is Slipknot's most aggressive and heavy album to date, and features a more melodic sound than its predecessors. The album has been noted for its dark lyrics and Slipknot's exploration of new sonic territories. Critical response to "Stir the Blood" has been generally positive, with many reviewers praising the band's return to heavier soundscapes and lyrical themes.  Slipknot is an American heavy metal band from Des Moines, Iowa, formed in 1999. The band members are Corey Taylor (vocalist), Shawn Crahan (guitarist), Paul Gray (bassist), Mick Thomson (drummer) and Joey Jordison (percussionist).
