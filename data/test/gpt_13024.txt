Magdaléna Vášáryová (; referred also as Magda Vášáryová, Magdalena Vášáryová, Magdalenka Vášáryová; 9 October 1921 – 2 February 2002) was a Czechoslovakian actress, singer and model who became internationally known in the 1950s.  Born in Prague, Magdaléna Vášáryová was the daughter of Josef Vášáry and his wife Marie (née Kačírek). After completing her secondary education at a girls' school in Prague, she studied drama at the Academy of Performing Arts in Prague.  She made her stage debut in 1945 and began appearing in films in 1948. Her breakout role came in the 1950 film "The Unvanquished", for which she was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Actress. She also appeared in "The Good Soldier Schweik" (1955), "The House on Mango Street" (1961), and "The Glass Menagerie" (1967). In 1966, she played Madame Armfeldt in the West German television production of Henrik Ibsen's play "An Enemy of the People".  Vášáryová retired from acting in the early 1970s but continued to sing until shortly before her death. She was publicly opposed to communism and served as an ambassador for human rights. She was posthumously awarded the Order of Tomaž Šalamun in 2003.
