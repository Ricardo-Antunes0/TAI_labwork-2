Vladimir Abdualievich Vasilyev (; born August 11, 1966) is a Russian politician who has been the Governor of Tomsk Oblast since December 2013. Previously, he served as the Deputy Prime Minister of Russia from May to December 2012, and as the Minister of Transport from December 2011 to May 2012.  Vasilyev was born in 1966 in the town of Dmitrov, Tomsk Oblast. He graduated from the Law Faculty of Saint Petersburg State University in 1990 and started his career as a lawyer. In 1998, he became a member of the Saint Petersburg City Duma. From 2003 to 2007, he was the mayor of Saint Petersburg.  In 2007, Vasilyev was appointed as the Minister of Transport in Dmitry Medvedev's first cabinet. He held this position until May 2012, when he was replaced by Maxim Sokolov. In December 2011, Vasilyev was appointed Deputy Prime Minister in charge of social policy and youth policy under Putin. On 16 December 2012, Putin dismissed Vasilyev from his position and appointed him Minister of Energy instead.  Vasilyev is married and has two children.
