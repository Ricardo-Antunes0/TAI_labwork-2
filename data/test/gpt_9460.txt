She received her B.A. from Barnard College in 1959 and her M.A. from Columbia University in 1962. She taught at The New School for Social Research from 1967 to 1998, where she also served as the dean of faculty from 1994 to 1998.  Tiberino's first book, "The Female Persuasion: American Women in the 1920s" (1978), won the National Book Award for History and was a finalist for the Pulitzer Prize. Her second book, "The American Woman: Her Nature and Destiny" (1981), won the Pulitzer Prize for History.  Tiberino died on February 28, 2018, at her home in Manhattan after a long illness. She was 86 years old.
