José "Coquito" López Rosario (1976 – July 3, 2003) was a Puerto Rican professional boxer who competed from 1996 to 2003. He was the IBF Super Featherweight Champion from 1999 to 2001 and the WBC Featherweight Champion from 1999 to 2002.  López Rosario was born in San Juan, Puerto Rico and raised in the barrios of Carolina and Roosevelt. He began boxing at age 8 and became a professional at the age of 19. Lopez Rosario fought as an amateur for Puerto Rico at the 1996 Summer Olympics in Atlanta, Georgia, where he won a silver medal in the men's featherweight division.  López Rosario turned professional in 1996 and won his first championship, the IBF Super Featherweight Championship, in 1999. He retained his title with a fourth-round knockout of Fernando Vargas. In 2001, he won the WBC Featherweight Championship with a seventh-round knockout of Héctor Camacho. Lopez Rosario then defended his titles twice before losing them both to Marco Antonio Barrera. Lopez Rosario retired in 2003 with a record of 38 wins (18 by knockout), six losses and one draw.  José López Rosario died on July 3, 2003 after collapsing during a training session in Las Vegas. He was 31 years old.
