The Ridgewood Historic District is a residential historic district in Ridgewood, Queens, New York City. The district was designated a New York City landmark in 1966 and listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1972.  The Ridgewood Historic District is located in the northwestern corner of Ridgewood, bounded by Woodhaven Boulevard to the north, Metropolitan Avenue to the east, Waverly Place to the south, and Myrtle Avenue to the west. It includes 1,609 homes and apartments, most of which were built between 1885 and 1925. The district is notable for its well-preserved late Victorian and Edwardian architecture, including fine examples of Queen Anne, Richardsonian Romanesque, and Tudor Revival styles.  The district also includes a number of significant civic buildings, including Ridgewood Presbyterian Church (1892), Ridgewood Public Library (1904), Ridgewood High School (1912), and Ridgewood Armory (1911).
