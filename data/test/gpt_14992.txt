Aurélien Barrau (born 19 May 1973, in Montpellier, France) is a French journalist and author.  Aurélien Barrau was born on 19 May 1973 in Montpellier, France. He studied at the University of Montpellier, where he received his degree in political science in 1997.  From 1997 to 2001, Barrau worked as a journalist for the newspaper "Le Monde". In 2001, he moved to Paris to work as a correspondent for "Le Figaro". From 2002 to 2003, he served as the newspaper's editorial director.  In 2003, Barrau published his first book, "L'Ombre d'un président". The book tells the story of François Mitterrand's last years in office. The following year, he published "Le Grand Soir", a history of the French Fifth Republic.  In 2007, Barrau published "Aurélien Barrau et les secrets du Prince". The book tells the story of Prince Andrew and Marc Dutroux. That year, he also published "Le Roi est mort", a biography of Charles de Gaulle.  In 2010, Barrau published "Les Mondialistes", a history of the French left. That year also saw the release of his second book, "L'Étoile du Nord". The book tells the story of Jean-Paul II's visit to France and his relationship with François Mitterrand.
