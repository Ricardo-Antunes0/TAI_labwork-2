Ely Mound is a historic burial mound in Preble County, Ohio, United States. It is one of the largest mounds in North America and was once thought to be the largest prehistoric earthwork in the world.  The mound is located near the town of Eaton in Preble County, Ohio. It is about in circumference at its base and high. It is composed of a series of concentric ridges and basins, with a central citadel or plaza. The mound was built between 5000 BC and 4000 BC by an unknown culture, and is one of the most enduring symbols of the American Midwest.  The mound was designated a National Historic Landmark in 1965. The surrounding area is now a protected archaeological district and a National Natural Landmark.
