Ferdinand Faivre (1860–1937) was a French sculptor.  Ferdinand Faivre was born in 1860, in Saint-Mandé, near Paris. After studying at the École des Beaux-Arts, he worked in collaboration with Alexandre Falguière before establishing his own studio in 1892. His most famous works include the statue of Joan of Arc at Orléans and the Grande Arche de la Défense, both in Paris. Faivre died in 1937.
