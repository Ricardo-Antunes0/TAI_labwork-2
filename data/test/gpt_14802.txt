The Cypress–Emerson Historic District encompasses a residential area in southwest Houston that was developed in the early 20th century. It is notable for its collection of bungalows and rowhouses, as well as its high quality of construction. The district was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1983.  The Cypress–Emerson Historic District is located in southwest Houston, just east of downtown. It covers an area of about . The district is bounded on the north by Buffalo Bayou, on the south by White Oak Drive, and on the west by Studewood Avenue.  The earliest known development in the district was a small cluster of houses that were built between 1905 and 1910. This cluster was followed by a much larger development, which began to be built in the 1920s and continued until the 1940s. The majority of the houses in the district are Bungalows or Rowshows, two-story wood frame structures with Tudor Revival, Colonial Revival, or Moderne styling. Most have front porches and were built for middle-class families. There are also a few three-story brick structures, most notably the former home of Houston oilman H.L. Hunt.
