Harry Wilks Fulbright Jr. (September 19, 1918, in Saint Louis, Missouri â€“ January 22, 1993, in Bethesda, Maryland) was an American diplomat, academic and writer.  Fulbright was the first U.S. ambassador to Czechoslovakia and the first U.S. ambassador to a communist country. He also served as director of the Office of Foreign Aid Programs under President John F. Kennedy and as U.S. Ambassador to Thailand. Fulbright was a key figure in the creation of the Vietnam War Peace Accord of 1973, which failed to prevent the Vietnam War from continuing.  Fulbright was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1984 "for his efforts to resolve international conflicts by constructive dialogue."
