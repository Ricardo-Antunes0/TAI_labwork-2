Captain Robert Henry Dundas Bolton OBE (13 May 1917 â€“ 8 January 1990) was a British naval officer and Conservative politician.  Bolton was born in 1917, the son of the 3rd Baron Bolton of Bolton, and educated at Eton College and Trinity College, Cambridge. He served in the Royal Navy during World War II, reaching the rank of lieutenant.  Bolton was elected to Parliament as the Member of Parliament (MP) for Bolton North East in 1950, serving until he retired in 1983. He was Minister of State for Overseas Development from 1970 to 1972, Minister of State for Employment and Productivity from 1972 to 1974, and Secretary of State for Foreign and Commonwealth Affairs from 1974 to 1975. He served as Leader of the Conservative Party from 1975 to 1983.  Bolton married Cynthia Asquith, daughter of former Prime Minister H. H. Asquith, in 1952. They had two sons and two daughters. He died in 1990, aged 76.
