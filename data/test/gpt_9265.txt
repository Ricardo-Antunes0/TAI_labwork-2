Marie Françoise Gilot (born 26 November 1921) is a French actress, singer, and former model. She has appeared in more than 100 films and television shows since 1949.  Gilot was born in Paris, the daughter of Elsie (née Hodes) and Rene Gilot. Her father was a cinematographer and director, and her mother was an actress. She attended the Lycée Français de New York City, where she was classmates with Brigitte Bardot. After returning to France, Gilot started her career as a model and appeared in several commercials.  In 1949, Gilot made her acting debut in the film "Le coeur est un oiseau rare". For the next few years, she appeared in a number of roles in French films. Her breakthrough came in 1952 with the role of Jeanne in Jean Renoir's film "La Grande Illusion". In the same year, she also starred in Fritz Lang's "M" and sang the song "Non, je ne regrette rien" for the film's soundtrack.  Gilot's international fame grew with her performances in a number of American films throughout the 1950s and 1960s, including "To Catch a Thief" (1951), "The Sun Also Rises" (1957), "An American In Paris" (1951), "The Nun's Story" (1959), and "The Fall of the Roman Empire" (1964).
