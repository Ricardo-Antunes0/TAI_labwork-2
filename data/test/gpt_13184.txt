Obninsk  is a city in Kaluga Oblast, Russia, located on the left bank of the Volga River about 158 kilometers east of Moscow. Population: 312,500 (2002 Census); 318,000 (2010 Census).    Obninsk is the administrative center of the oblast. The city was founded in 1586 by Ivan the Terrible as a fortress on the right bank of the Volga River to protect Moscow against Swedish incursions. As the administrative center of a large and important province, it rapidly developed into a major trade center and an important cultural center. The city's industries include food processing, metallurgy, machinery production, and chemical production.     Obninsk is a cultural and educational center of Kaluga Oblast. The city has a number of museums and theaters.
