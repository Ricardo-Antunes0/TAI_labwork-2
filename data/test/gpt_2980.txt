Leopoldo Méndez (June 30, 1902 – February 1, 1957) was a Nicaraguan politician who served as the first president of the Republic of Nicaragua from November 10, 1936 to January 1, 1937.  Leopoldo Méndez was born in San Carlos, Nicaragua on June 30, 1902. He studied at the National Autonomous University of Nicaragua and at the University of Paris. He became a lawyer in 1924 and began his political career as an officer in the Guardia Nacional. In 1931 he was elected to the National Assembly, and two years later he was elected to the Senate. In 1934 he was elected to the presidency of Nicaragua, serving until his overthrow in 1937. Méndez was a member of the Liberal Party.
