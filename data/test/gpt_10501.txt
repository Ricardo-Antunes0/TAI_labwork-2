He studied at the Moscow Art Theatre School and made his stage debut in 1967 at the age of 23.  Agapov's breakthrough came in the early 1970s with leading roles in films such as "The Unforgiven" and "War and Peace". He went on to appear in a wide range of roles in both Russian and international films, often playing villains or semi-villains. His most famous roles include that of Viktor Chernomyrdin in "Election" and Ivan the Terrible in "Tsar".  Agapov also appeared in several successful theatrical productions, including "King Lear" and "Hamlet". He was made an honorary member of the Moscow Art Theatre in 2001.  Boris Agapov died from a heart attack on 6 November 2006 at the age of 65.
