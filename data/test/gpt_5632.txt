Born in London, Wormald was the son of the actor Leslie Wormald and his wife, the actress Lilian Baylis. He made his stage debut in 1909 and became a regular performer on British stage and television in the 1930s and 1940s. He also appeared in a number of films, including "The Ladykillers" (1955), "A Matter of Life and Death" (1946), "The Nun's Story" (1959), and "Doctor Zhivago" (1965).  Wormald was a leading member of the Old Vic Company from 1934 to 1965, serving as its artistic director from 1945 to 1965. He also directed productions at the Royal Shakespeare Company, the National Theatre, and at Broadway's Westside Theatre. He was made a Commander of the Order of the British Empire in 1958.
