He attended the University of Ibadan, where he earned his Bachelor of Arts in 1961 and his Master of Arts in 1965. He also attended the University of Amsterdam, where he earned his Doctor of Philosophy in 1978.  Afigbo taught at the universities of Ibadan and Amsterdam before moving to the United States in 1984. He served as a professor at the University of Texas at Austin from 1984 until his retirement in 2003.  Afigbo's works include the novels "The Daughters of Oyo" (1987) and "The Marriage Counselor" (1994), and the plays "The Slave Girl" (1982), "The Immortal Beloved" (1985), and "The Interrupters" (1991). He also wrote poetry, including collections such as "The Book of Questions" (1992) and "In Memory of My Fathers" (2007).  Afigbo died on 12 February 2018 at the age of 80.
