Shivgarh is a village and corresponding community development block in Purba Bardhaman district in the Indian state of West Bengal.  The area around Shivgarh has been settled since the Neolithic period. The earliest archaeological evidence of human habitation in the area is from the Mehrgarh culture, which is dated to 7000 BCE. Shivgarh was part of the kingdom of Kalinga during the Vedic period. The region was conquered by the Muslim Sultanate of Bengal in 1204 and came under the control of the Mughal Empire in 1576. Shivgarh was part of East Bardhaman district until 1974, when it was transferred to Purba Bardhaman district.  Shivgarh is located in the southeastern part of Purba Bardhaman district, on the border of Uttar Dinajpur and Paschim Medinipur districts. The village is located approximately south of Bankura and east of Purulia.  According to the 2011 census, Shivgarh had a total population of 5,528, of which 2,814 (51%) were males and 2,708 (49%) were females. Population below 6 years was 527. The total number of literates in Shivgarh was 3,936 (86.08% of the population over 6 years).  Shivgarh is one among 108 villages from Purba Bardhaman district which have been selected for developmental intervention by the Government of India's JAM
