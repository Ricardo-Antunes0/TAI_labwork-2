Mary Shirley Walters (née Harrison; 31 August 1918 – 10 September 2002) was an English stage, film, and television actress.  Walters was born in 1918 in the town of Wigan, Lancashire, the daughter of Arthur Walters, a coal miner, and his wife Bertha (née Harrison). She began her theatrical career in repertory theatre in the 1930s. Her first major role came in the film The Bells of St. Mary's (1945), for which she received a BAFTA Award nomination. She went on to appear in such films as The Ladykillers (1955), Alfie (1966), and The Return of the Pink Panther (1975).  Walters starred in the television series Shirley (1967–1972), for which she received Emmy and Golden Globe nominations. She also appeared in the BBC TV series Midsomer Murders (1987–1993), playing the role of Nora Batty for seven seasons. In 2003, Walters received an Honorary Academy Award for her contribution to stage and screen. She died at the age of 86 on 10 September 2002, survived by her second husband, actor Denis Quilley.
