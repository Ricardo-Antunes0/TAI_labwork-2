Stephen Julius Masele (MP)  (born October 20, 1957) is a South African politician who has served as the Minister of Higher Education and Training since May 24, 2014. He previously served as the Minister of Energy from 2009 to 2014.  Stephen Masele was born on October 20, 1957 in Johannesburg, South Africa. He matriculated at Soweto's Orlando High School in 1974. He then studied at the University of Cape Town, where he obtained his BA degree in 1979 and his MA degree in 1982. He then attended the London School of Economics, where he obtained his MA degree in 1984.   Masele worked as a lecturer at the University of Cape Town from 1984 to 1987. He also served as an advisor to the Department of Higher Education and Training from 1988 to 1989. In 1990, he was appointed Deputy Director-General of the South African Broadcasting Corporation (SABC). He was promoted to Director-General in 1995, serving in that capacity until 1999.  Masele was elected to the National Assembly of South Africa in the 1999 general election, representing the constituency of Alexandra. He was re-elected in 2004 and 2009. In May 2009, he was appointed Minister of Energy by President Thabo Mbeki. In May 2014, Masele was appointed Minister of Higher Education and Training by President Jacob Zuma.  Masele is married and has two children.
