The Jewish–Roman wars were a series of three wars fought by the Jewish people and the Roman Empire between 66 BCE and 4 CE.  The first war, fought in 66 BCE, resulted in the destruction of the Second Temple in Jerusalem. The second war, fought in 70 CE, resulted in the destruction of the city of Jerusalem. The third war, fought in 134–135 CE, resulted in the relocating of the Jews to Europe.
