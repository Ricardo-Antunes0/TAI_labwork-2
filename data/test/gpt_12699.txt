Alexandr Vasilievich Kolobnev (; born 4 May 1928) is a Russian politician who was the General Secretary of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union from 1985 to 1991. He served as the Premier of the Soviet Union from 1978 to 1985.  Kolobnev was born in the village of Kuntsevo, near Moscow, into a poor peasant family. After completing secondary school in 1945, he worked in a number of positions in the agricultural sector. He joined the Communist Party in 1949, and rose through its ranks to become a member of its Central Committee in 1969.  In 1975, Kolobnev became Deputy Prime Minister and Minister for Agriculture in Leonid Brezhnev's government. In February 1978, he was made First Deputy Chairman of the Council of Ministers, and in November 1978 he succeeded Nikolai Tikhonov as Premier of the Soviet Union.
