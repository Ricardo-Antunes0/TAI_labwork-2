Cala Bona is a small fishing town in the Province of Alicante, in the Valencian Community, Spain. It is located on the Costa Blanca, about 10 km south of Alicante and about 1 km from the Mediterranean Sea.  Cala Bona has a population of about 2,000 people. The main economic activity is fishing. There is also a small tourism sector. The town is known for its white sand beaches and crystal clear waters.
