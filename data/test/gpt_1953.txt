The Fox is a novella by D. H. Lawrence. Published in 1922, it tells the story of a country farmer whose life is turned upside down when he is seduced and abandoned by a woman who represents all that is corrupt in his society.  The Fox is one of D. H. Lawrence's most famous works, and has been frequently anthologized and studied. It has been described as a Bildungsroman, a story of the protagonist's growth, and as an exploration of the consequences of sexual temptation. The novella has been praised for its powerful writing, its evocative images, and its exploration of the theme of morality in a society corrupted by materialism.
