Mariah Lopez (born 1985) is an American singer and songwriter. She rose to prominence in 2008 with the release of her debut album, Mariah's World, which peaked at number one on the US Billboard 200 chart and spawned the successful singles "Fantasy" and "Honey". Lopez has since released two more albums, Unapologetic (2010) and The Emancipation of Mimi (2014), as well as a string of singles.  Born in Miami, Florida to Cuban parents, Lopez began singing at an early age, first taking up the ukulele before progressing to the guitar and then to the piano. She began writing her own songs at the age of 16, and after graduating from high school in 2003 she moved to New York City to pursue a music career. Lopez signed a recording contract with Columbia Records in 2006 and released her debut album Mariah's World that year. The album reached number one on the US Billboard 200 chart and spawned the successful singles "Fantasy" and "Honey". Lopez has since released two more albums, Unapologetic (2010) and The Emancipation of Mimi (2014), as well as a string of singles.
