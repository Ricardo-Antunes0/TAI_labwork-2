John J. O'Meara (18 February 191512 February 1997) was an American politician who served as the 44th Governor of California from 1967 to 1971. O'Meara was a Democrat who defeated incumbent Republican Governor Ronald Reagan in the 1966 gubernatorial election. He was the first Irish-American governor of California.  O'Meara was born in San Francisco, California, to John Joseph O'Meara (1878–1966), a hotel worker, and his wife, the former Catherine Hall (1882–1966). He attended Holy Cross College in Worcester, Massachusetts, and then transferred to the University of California, Berkeley, where he graduated with a B.A. in 1934. He then attended Harvard Law School and was admitted to the bar in 1937.  O'Meara began his political career as a member of the Berkeley City Council from 1941 to 1943. He also served as chairman of the California Democratic Party from 1952 to 1954. O'Meara entered the political arena by defeating incumbent Republican Governor Ronald Reagan in the 1966 gubernatorial election. As governor, O'Meara promoted economic development and liberal policies such as public employment programs and increased funding for education. In 1969, he signed legislation that created the California State University system. O'Meara left office in 1971 after losing re-election to Republican George Deukmejian.
