Manuel de Odriozola (August 11, 1804 in San Sebastián, Basque Country – October 15, 1876 in Madrid) was a Basque painter and sculptor.  Manuel de Odriozola was born on August 11, 1804 in San Sebastián, Basque Country. He moved to Madrid in 1825 to study at the Royal Academy of Fine Arts. He soon became known for his portraits and historical scenes. Odriozola won the Gold Medal at the Paris World's Fair in 1867. He died on October 15, 1876 in Madrid.
