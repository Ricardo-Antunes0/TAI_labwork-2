Tommy Roland Shaw (born September 11, 1953) is an American musician and singer-songwriter, who was a member of the rock band Tommy Shaw and the Shaws. Shaw also starred in the short-lived Fox television series The X-Files.  Born in Seattle, Washington, Shaw was raised in Newport Beach, California. He began his musical career as a singer and guitarist in the band Tommy Shaw and the Shaws, which achieved regional success in the early 1970s. The band released two albums before breaking up in 1976. In 1994, Shaw founded the rock band Sons of Champlin, which released two albums before disbanding in 1998. Since then, Shaw has released two solo albums and toured extensively.  Shaw made his television debut in 1993 with a guest appearance on the Fox series The X-Files. He starred as himself on the short-lived Fox television series The Tommy Shaw Show from 1998 to 2000. In 2003, he appeared as a guest star on the CBS show NUMB3RS. He has also appeared on various television shows and documentaries, including VH1's Behind the Music and A&E's Biography.  Shaw was inducted into the Pacific Coast Rock Hall of Fame in 2007.
