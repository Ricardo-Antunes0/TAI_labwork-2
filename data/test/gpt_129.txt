Cullen was born in Melbourne, Victoria, on 13 February 1912. He attended Melbourne Grammar School before joining the Australian Army in 1932. After serving with the 2/10th Battalion and the 3rd Battalion, Royal Australian Regiment, Cullen served in World War II as commander of the 6th Division. He was wounded during the fighting at Monte Cassino in 1944 and later served as Chief of Staff to Lieutenant General Sir Thomas Blamey.  After the war, Cullen served as Director-General of Military Training and then as Inspector-General of Military Forces. In this role, he played a significant role in reforming the Australian Army. He retired from the Army in 1968 and later died in Canberra on 25 November 1988.
