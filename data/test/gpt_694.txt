Zygmunt Gorazdowski (1 November 1845 – 1 July 1909) was a Polish writer, poet and thinker. He is considered one of the most important Polish poets of the 19th century. Gorazdowski is also known for his philosophy and his writing on aesthetics.  Gorazdowski was born in the town of Nowy Sącz, Austrian Galicia, in 1845. He studied at the universities of Vienna and Heidelberg, where he became interested in philosophy and poetry. He moved to Paris in 1868, where he continued his studies at the Sorbonne. In 1875 he returned to Poland and settled in Kraków.  In 1879 Gorazdowski published his first book of poetry, "Poems". The following year he founded the magazine "Młoda Polska", which he directed until 1895. In 1892 he was elected a member of the Polish Academy of Sciences. Gorazdowski died in 1909 in Kraków.
