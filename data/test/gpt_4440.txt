Michael Dylan Foster is a professor of philosophy at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. He specializes in the philosophy of mind, ethics, and religious belief.  Foster was born on October 15, 1967, in Chapel Hill, North Carolina. He received his B.A. from Duke University in 1989 and his M.A. and Ph.D. from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill in 1992 and 1995, respectively.  Foster has been a professor of philosophy at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill since 1995. He also teaches at the graduate level at Elon University and UNC-Asheville. His main areas of research are the philosophy of mind, ethics, and religious belief.  Foster is the editor-in-chief of "The Journal for Philosophy and Religion" (since 2006), and he has also been the editor of "Philosophy Compass" (since 2009). He has been a fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences since 2002, and he is a member of the Society for Phenomenology and Existential Philosophy (since 1999).
