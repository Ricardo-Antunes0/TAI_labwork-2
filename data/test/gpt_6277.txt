Marie Margaret McMillin (July 1, 1902 – December 10, 1987) was an American actress and singer.  Born in Sacramento, California, McMillin began her career as a singer and actress in vaudeville. After appearing in several films in the 1930s, she achieved national prominence in the 1940s and 1950s for her roles in the television series "The Adventures of Ozzie and Harriet", "The Waltons", and "The Andy Griffith Show". McMillin also appeared in films such as "Brother Rat" (1936), "The Lady Eve" (1941), "The Glenn Miller Story" (1954), and "The Poseidon Adventure" (1972). She received three Emmy Awards for her work on "The Adventures of Ozzie and Harriet", five Golden Globe Awards, and two Screen Actors Guild Awards. McMillin retired from acting in the 1970s.  Marie McMillin was born on July 1, 1902, in Sacramento, California, to Helen (née McMillin) and Patrick McMillin. Her father was an Irish immigrant who operated a grocery store. Marie McMillin's early musical training came from her father; he taught her how to play the piano at a young age. She began her performing career as a singer and actress in vaudeville with her siblings.  In 1935, McMillin appeared in her first film role opposite Dick Powell in the comedy Brother Rat.
