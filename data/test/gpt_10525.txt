Susan Barbara Gyankorama Ofori-Atta also de Graft-Johnson, (born November 5, 1966, Accra, Ghana) is a Ghanaian-British businesswoman and philanthropist.  She is the founder and CEO of the Ofori Atta Group, a Ghanaian conglomerate with interests in mining, agricultural production, and health and social services. The Group's activities have made it one of Ghana's most successful businesses.  Ofori-Atta has also been involved in a number of charitable causes in Ghana and abroad. She is a patron of the International Aids Vaccine Initiative (IAVI), and has donated more than $5 million to the charity. She has also donated money to help rebuild victims of the 2010 Haiti earthquake.   In December 2017, Ofori-Atta was named one of the world's 100 most influential women by Forbes magazine.
