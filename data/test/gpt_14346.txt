Cedar Creek is a  stream in the U.S. state of Wisconsin. It is a tributary of the Wisconsin River.  The Cedar Creek watershed drains an area of in northeast Wisconsin. The stream rises in the town of Cedarburg and flows southeastwardly through Cedarburg, Waukesha, and Washington Counties, passing through the villages of Cedar Creek and Lyons before joining the Wisconsin River near Kiel.   The watershed has a population of about 4,000 people. The main uses of the watershed are for municipal water supplies, agricultural production, and recreation.   The name "Cedar Creek" is derived from the cedar trees that grow along its course.
