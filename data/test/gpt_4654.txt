Scott Klement, born January 28, 1969 in San Francisco, is an American artist and musician. He is best known as the frontman and primary songwriter of the band Scott Klement and the Liberty Bells.  Klement grew up in the Bay Area of Northern California and attended De Anza High School. He started his musical career in the late 1980s as a drummer in a number of local San Francisco punk bands, including Dead Kennedys, The Fartz, and The Lookouts. In 1992, he joined Scott Klement and the Liberty Bells, with whom he released two albums before splitting up in 2000.  Since then, Klement has pursued a solo career, releasing two albums and touring extensively throughout the United States and Europe. He has also collaborated with a number of artists, including Elvis Costello, John Lee Hooker, Del McCoury, Lucinda Williams, and Ry Cooder. In 2013, he released his first album of new material in over a decade, entitled "The Great Unknown".
