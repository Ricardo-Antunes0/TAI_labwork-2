Kate Brousseau (April 24, 1862 – July 21, 1925) was a French artist and sculptor.  Born in Paris, Brousseau studied at the École des Beaux-Arts and won the Prix de Rome in 1889. She returned to France and became a sought-after figure in Parisian art circles, exhibiting her works at the Salon and elsewhere. In 1902 she moved to the United States, where she continued to exhibit her work and teach at various schools. Brousseau died in 1925 in New York City.
