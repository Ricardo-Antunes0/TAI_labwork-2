The Evinos  is a  river in the central part of Greece. Its source is in the mountains of Parnassus, and it flows through the regions of Central Macedonia and Thessaly before emptying into the Gulf of Corinth.  The Evinos river is a significant waterway in central Greece. It is a tributary of the River Corinth, and flows through the regions of Central Macedonia and Thessaly before emptying into the Gulf of Corinth. The Evinos is formed by the confluence of the rivers Kalogerones and Pindus, in the municipality of Tsaritsani, Parnassus Mountains. The Evinos has a length of 174 km, a width of 9 km at its widest point, and a drainage area of 1,812 km2.
