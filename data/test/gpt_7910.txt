Emily Fortune Feimster (; born July 1, 1984) is an American actress, comedian, radio personality and writer.  Born in Scottsdale, Arizona, Feimster attended Chaparral High School in Scottsdale, Arizona. After high school, she studied at New York University's Tisch School of the Arts but dropped out after two years to pursue an acting career.  Feimster made her acting debut in a small role in the independent film "The Business of Strangers" (2005). Her first major film role came that year in "The Sisterhood of the Traveling Pants 2", where she played character T-Bag. She had a supporting role in the comedy-drama "Take Me Home Tonight" (2009), and starred as the title character in the comedy-drama "Fortune Feimster" (2012), for which she was nominated for an Independent Spirit Award for Best Female Lead. In 2018, she starred as Diane Chambers in the ABC sitcom "The Good Place".
