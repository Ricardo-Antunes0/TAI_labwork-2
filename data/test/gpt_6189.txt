Regina Sullivan is an American developmental behavioral pediatrician and the medical director of the pediatric intensive care unit at Children's Hospital of Philadelphia.  Regina Sullivan was born on May 20, 1972, in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. She is a developmental behavioral pediatrician and the medical director of the pediatric intensive care unit at Children's Hospital of Philadelphia. Sullivan has been named one of "America's Top Doctors" by "The Huffington Post" and "Philadelphia magazine" and has been featured in "The New York Times", "The Wall Street Journal", and other media outlets. She is an Associate Professor of Pediatrics at the University of Pennsylvania and a member of the National Academy of Medicine.  Sullivan's work focuses on the care of critically ill children, particularly those with severe brain injuries or congenital heart conditions. She is well known for her work in developing innovative treatments for these patients, including a procedure that uses fetal stem cells to help heal brain damage. Sullivan also leads the hospital's pediatric burn unit and is recognized as a leading expert in pediatric burn care.  Sullivan lives in Philadelphia with her husband, Dr. David Sullivan, and their two children.
