Hans-Joachim Schulze (born 3 December 1934) is a German composer, pianist, and keyboardist. He is best known for his electronic and classical music, as well as his collaborations with other musicians.  Schulze was born in Berlin, Germany. He studied at the Berlin University of the Arts and the Royal College of Music in London. He has worked as a composer, pianist, and keyboardist. His music has been performed by leading orchestras and ensembles around the world, including the Berlin Philharmonic, London Symphony Orchestra, Tokyo Symphony Orchestra, and the Boston Symphony Orchestra. He has also collaborated with other artists, including David Bowie, U2, and Lou Reed. Schulze has been awarded several prizes, including the Georg Solti Prize and the Edison Award. He has lived in Berlin since 1967.
