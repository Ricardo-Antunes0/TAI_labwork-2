Alan Musgrave (; born 1940) is an English economist and academic. He is currently the Professor of Economics at the University of Cambridge, and was formerly the Director of the Cambridge Centre for Economic Policy Research.  Musgrave was educated at Winchester College and Trinity College, Cambridge, where he was a member of the Footlights. He received his PhD from Cambridge in 1965. He has been a Fellow of Trinity since 1973, and a Fellow of King's College since 1986.  Musgrave has been a professor at the University of Cambridge since 2000, where he also served as director of the Cambridge Centre for Economic Policy Research from 2002 to 2006. He has also been a visiting professor at Stanford University and the London School of Economics.  Musgrave is a member of the Royal Economic Society, the Econometric Society, and the International Economic Association.
