Léon Motchane (19 June 1900 – 17 November 1967) was a French writer, journalist and screenwriter.  Born in Marseille, Motchane worked as a journalist and screenwriter before becoming one of France's most important novelists in the 1940s and 1950s. He is best known for his post-war novels, which explore the effects of the war on French society. Motchane died in Paris in 1967.
