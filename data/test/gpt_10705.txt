David J. Thomson is a Professor in the Department of History at McGill University.   Thomson has written extensively on Canadian history, the history of journalism, and the history of American journalism. He is the author of The Rise and Fall of the Canadian Media Dynasty (2003), which won the prestigious Guggenheim Fellowship, as well as The Media Monopoly (2008), a book widely considered to be one of the definitive works on the media industry. Thomson has also written extensively on Europe and its history, most notably his book The Renaissance: A History (2009).
