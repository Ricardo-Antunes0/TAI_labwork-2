SERVIR-Mekong project is a joint initiative by the governments of Cambodia, Laos, Thailand and Vietnam to jointly develop the Mekong River Basin, which covers an area of approximately 1.3 million square kilometers. The goal of the project is to improve the living standards of people in the region through coordinated development projects, and to promote regional integration. The project will provide access to essential services for people in the Mekong River basin, including education, health care, infrastructure development, and environmental protection.
