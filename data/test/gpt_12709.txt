Culbertson's Path was a Native American trail that connected the Great Lakes and the Mississippi River. It was used by the Potawatomi, Ottawa, and Mississauga peoples. The path was named after George Culbertson, an American fur trader who explored the region in the early 1800s.  Culbertson's Path was a major transportation route linking the Great Lakes and the Mississippi River. It was used by the Potawatomi, Ottawa, and Mississauga peoples. The path was named after George Culbertson, an American fur trader who explored the region in the early 1800s. The trail ran from present-day Green Bay, Wisconsin to near Saint Paul, Minnesota.
