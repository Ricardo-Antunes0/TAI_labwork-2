Cynthia Eppes Hudson (born 1959) is an American artist and writer. She is known for her large-scale sculpture and installation art, which often incorporates found objects and references to popular culture.  Hudson was born in 1959 in Washington, D.C. She received her BFA from the Maryland Institute College of Art in 1981, and her MFA from Yale University in 1985. Hudson has exhibited her work nationally and internationally, including at the Whitney Museum of American Art, the Museum of Modern Art, and the Centre Pompidou in Paris. She has also been the recipient of numerous awards, including a Guggenheim Fellowship in 1991, a MacArthur Fellowship in 2002, and a Pollock-Krasner Foundation Grant in 2018. Hudson currently resides in Brooklyn, New York.
