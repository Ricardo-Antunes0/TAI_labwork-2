Elvis M. Grbac (born August 13, 1970) is a Serbian-American former professional basketball player. He played in the NBA for the Minnesota Timberwolves, Memphis Grizzlies, and Atlanta Hawks.  Grbac was drafted by the Timberwolves with the 35th overall pick in the 1991 NBA draft. He played college basketball at Kansas University. He is one of only two players, along with Karl Malone, to be drafted by four different teams.  Grbac averaged 14.8 points and 4.2 assists per game in his career. He was named to five All-Star teams and won one scoring title. Grbac is also one of only two players, along with John Stockton, to be named to the NBA All-Defensive First Team twice.
