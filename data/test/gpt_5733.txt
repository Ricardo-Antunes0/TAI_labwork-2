Dann James Bilardello (born May 26, 1959) is an American former professional ice hockey player who played in the National Hockey League (NHL) for the Philadelphia Flyers, Quebec Nordiques, and Toronto Maple Leafs. He is currently the head coach of HC Slovan Bratislava in the Kontinental Hockey League (KHL).  Bilardello was born in Trenton, New Jersey. He played junior hockey with the Ottawa 67's of the Ontario Hockey Association (OHA), then went on to play college hockey at Boston University, where he was drafted by the Philadelphia Flyers in the eighth round of the 1979 NHL Entry Draft.  Bilardello made his NHL debut with the Flyers in 1981 and played parts of six seasons with the team. He was traded to Quebec Nordiques in 1987 and spent two seasons with them before being traded to Toronto Maple Leafs in 1990. He played his final season with Toronto in 1992–93.  Bilardello retired from professional hockey after playing his last game for Toronto. He then became a coach, working as an assistant coach with Flyers affiliate Reading from 1994 to 1995 and as head coach of their AHL affiliate Hershey Bears from 1996 to 1997.  In 1998, Bilardello was hired as head coach of HC Slovan Bratislava in Slovakia.
