Milan Orlić (Serbian-Cyrillic: Милан Орлић; born 15 September 1981) is a Serbian professional footballer who plays as a midfielder for Serie A club Lazio and the Serbia national team.  Orlić started his career with Partizan Belgrade, playing in their first team from 2000 to 2002. He then moved to Chelsea, where he made only one appearance before being loaned to Newcastle United. In 2006, he joined Real Madrid, where he won two Champions League titles and two Spanish La Liga titles. In 2013, he signed for Serie A club Lazio.  Orlić has played for Serbia at the under-21 and senior levels. He made his full international debut in 2004, and has since earned over 60 caps. He represented his country at the 2004 Summer Olympics, the 2006 FIFA World Cup, the 2007 FIFA World Cup, the 2008 European Championship, the 2010 FIFA World Cup and the 2014 FIFA World Cup.  Milan Orlić was born on 15 September 1981 in Kragujevac, SR Serbia and Montenegro to a father of Bosnian Serb descent and a mother of Croatian origin. Growing up in Kragujevac, Orlić started playing football with local side FK Sloga Kraljevo before signing with Partizan Belgrade in 2000.
