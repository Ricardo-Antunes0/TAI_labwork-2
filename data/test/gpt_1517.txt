Martin Hellberg (also known as Martin Heinrich, Martin Hellberg Hübner and Martin Hellberg-Hübner) (born 16 July 1971) is a Swedish artist who lives and works in Berlin.  Hellberg was born in Borås, Sweden, and studied at the Konstakademi in Stockholm. He moved to Berlin in 2000. His work spans painting, sculpture, video and installation. It often deals with the relationship between humans and technology, exploring ideas of control, surveillance and self-awareness.  Hellberg has exhibited at the Museum of Modern Art in New York; the Centre Pompidou in Paris; the Serpentine Gallery in London; the Kunstmuseum Basel; the Ludwig Museum in Cologne; and the Whitney Museum of American Art in New York. In 2018 he was awarded the prestigiousorden ofthe Polar Star by King Carl XVI Gustaf of Sweden.
