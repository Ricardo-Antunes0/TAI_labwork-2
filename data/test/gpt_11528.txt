Cala Vadella (Cala Vedella in Catalan) is a small fishing village on the north coast of Mallorca, in the province of Palma, Balearic Islands, Spain.  The village is located at the mouth of the river Vedella, a tributary of the river Palma. It is accessible by car via the A-7 motorway from Palma de Mallorca or through a 10-minute walk from the Llucmajor beach. The village has a population of about 140 people.  The economy of Cala Vadella is based on tourism and fishing. The village has a small harbour and a few small shops. There is also a restaurant and a bar. The village has a few beaches, including Cala Vadella beach which is wide and sandy.
