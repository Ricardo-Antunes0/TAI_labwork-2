Hanna was born in Houston, Texas, to Peter Hanna (1901-1977), a boxer and vaudeville entertainer, and his wife, the former Dorothy Pembroke. Hanna's family moved to Los Angeles when he was young and he began performing at age 12 with his father. He began his career as a member of the Western swing band The Wild Cats before signing with Capitol Records in 1957.  Hanna had several hit singles throughout the 1960s and 1970s, including "The More I See You", "The Song Goes On", "Walking On Sunshine", and "Put It All On Me". He also starred in several motion pictures, including "The Big Country" (1978), "The Defiant Ones" (1980), "The Last American Hero" (1981), and "The Appaloosa" (2008). Hanna received two Golden Globe Award nominations for his work on the television series "M*A*S*H" (1972-1975) and for his role in the film "Annie" (1982). He was also inducted into the Songwriters Hall of Fame in 1984. Hanna died from leukemia at age 73 on November 9, 2006.
