Laetitia du Couëdic de Kerérant (born 6 September 1966) is a French journalist, author and public speaker.  Born Laetitia du Couëdic in Paris, France, into a family of journalists, she started her career in 1986 as a reporter at the magazine L'Express. She then worked at the French television channel TF1, where she became a political correspondent and, later, a political analyst. In 2002, she founded the think tank La République en Marche! (LRM), which she chaired until 2009.  Since 2009, she has been a member of the National Assembly of France, representing the department of Seine-Saint-Denis. She has also been president of the Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe (PACE) since 2017.   In addition to her parliamentary work, she has written several books, including L'État de siège (2007), Un homme à part (2011), and L'avenir d'un monde (2018).
