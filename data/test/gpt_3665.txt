Leucorchestris is a genus of African huntsman spiders, with only one species, Leucorchestris dumicola. It is found in Angola, Benin, Burkina Faso, Cameroon, Central African Republic, Chad, Democratic Republic of the Congo, Ivory Coast, Kenya, Liberia, Mali, Niger, Nigeria, Senegal, Sierra Leone, Togo, and Uganda.  The genus is characterised by its small size (10-15 mm body length), dark colouration and simple chelicerae (2 pairs of appendages on the head). The webs are typically built in trees or on low vegetation. The species chiefly hunts small prey such as insects and other spiders.
