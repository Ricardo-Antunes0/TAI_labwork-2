Charles Januarius Acton was born in London, the son of Edward Acton, a barrister. He was educated at Eton and at Trinity College, Cambridge, where he became a fellow in 1825.   He began his career as a schoolmaster, but in 1833 he became secretary to the Royal Commission on the Iron Mines and Works. He went on to be secretary to the Board of Works from 1835 to 1841, and then secretary to the Treasury from 1841 to 1846. In 1846 he was elected to Parliament as MP for Northampton, a seat he held until his retirement in 1868. In parliament he was a strong supporter of John Stuart Mill's liberalism.   Acton also wrote historical works, the most famous of which is "The History of the French Revolution" (1849). He also edited the collected works of John Stuart Mill. He died at Haslemere in Surrey.
