The Via Domitia was the first Roman road in Britain. It was built in the 1st century AD, linking Hadrian's Wall to the port of Dubris on the south coast. It is now known as the A1 road.  The Via Domitia was a Roman road that connected Hadrian's Wall with the port of Dubris on the south coast of Britain. The road is now known as the A1 road. The Via Domitia was built in the 1st century AD, and is thought to have been constructed to improve trade and communication along the border between Roman Britain and the Pictish kingdom.
