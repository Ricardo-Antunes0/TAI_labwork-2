Nikolai Yefimovich Andrianov (; 14 October 1952 – 8 January 1998) was a Russian politician who served as the Prime Minister of Russia from 1991 to 1993 and as the last head of the Soviet Union, from 1991 to its dissolution in 1991. He was also the first President of Russia, serving from 1991 until his death in 1998.  Andrianov was born in Moscow into a family of nobility. He graduated from the Moscow State Institute of International Relations in 1976. Andrianov began his political career as a member of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union. He rose through the ranks, becoming a Deputy Prime Minister in 1985 and the Minister of Foreign Affairs in 1988. In December 1989, he became Chairman of the Supreme Soviet, a position he held until its dissolution in 1991.  Andrianov played an active role in Soviet Union's dissolution and transition to democracy, serving as the first President of Russia (1991–1998). As Russia's first ever democratically elected leader, Andrianov implemented sweeping economic and social reforms during his time in office. Despite some initial concerns over his rule, Andrianov's legacy remains largely positive in Russian society. He is also widely considered to be one of Russia's most successful Prime Ministers. Andrianov died from a heart attack while on holiday in Italy just two years after leaving office as president. He was buried at the Novodevichy Cemetery in Moscow.
