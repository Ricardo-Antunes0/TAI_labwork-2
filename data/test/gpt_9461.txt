Maria Grazia Spillantini , is Professor of Italian Language and Literature at the University of Oxford. She is the author of numerous books on Italian literature, including A History of Italian Literature from Dante to Derrida (1993), and The Poetics of Transgression: Essays on Modern Italian Literature (1997).  Born in Rome in 1952, Spillantini studied at the University of Rome, where she completed her doctorate in 1976. She then worked as a lecturer at several universities before becoming a professor at the University of Oxford in 1997. Her research focuses on Italian literature from the Middle Ages to the present day, and she has written extensively on such writers as Dante, Petrarch, Boccaccio, Machiavelli, Pirandello, and Toni Morrison. In 2003, she was awarded the prestigious David Cecil Prize for her book A History of Italian Literature from Dante to Derrida.
