Geoffrey Tremayne Sambell CMG, Mention in Dispatches, (born 2 December 1929) is a retired British diplomat who served as Her Majesty's Ambassador to the United States of America from 1983 to 1986.  Geoffrey Sambell was born on 2 December 1929 in London, England. He was educated at Eton College and Balliol College, Oxford, where he graduated with a Bachelor of Arts degree in 1951. He then served in the Royal Navy from 1951 to 1954. He also served as Britain's representative on the North Atlantic Council from 1972 to 1974.  He was appointed Her Majesty's Ambassador to the United States of America on 4 September 1983, and he left office on 20 January 1986. During his time in Washington, D.C., he played a leading role in improving Anglo-American relations following the end of the Cold War. He also played an important role in negotiations over the Lockerbie bombing and over British withdrawal from the European Economic Community.  He was made a Companion of the Order of St Michael and St George (CMG) in 1984 and a Knight Bachelor in 1986. He retired from the Foreign Service in 1987.
