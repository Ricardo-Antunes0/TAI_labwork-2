The climate of Massachusetts is mainly  a temperate climate, with hot summers and cold winters. The state has a  variety of topography, including hills, mountains, and coastal plains. Massachusetts has a   coastline that is mostly sandy. The state has many rivers and streams  that flow through it, including the Charles River, the Merrimack River, and the Nashua River.   The highest point in Massachusetts is Mount Washington, which is located in the White Mountains. The lowest point in Massachusetts is sea level at the coast.   The weather in Massachusetts varies greatly depending on location. In the northeast part of the state, temperatures can range from below freezing to over 100 degrees Fahrenheit during summer months. In the southern part of the state, temperatures can range from below freezing to around 50 degrees Fahrenheit during winter months.
