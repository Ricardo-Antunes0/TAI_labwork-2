Jezki (foaled 8 March 2008) is an Irish Thoroughbred racehorse and sire. After finishing third in his only race as a juvenile in 2010 he emerged as a top-class performer in 2011, winning four races including the Group 1 Prix du Jockey Club. He was retired from racing at the end of the year after sustaining an injury in the Japan Cup.  Jezki is a bay horse with a white star and a black sock on his left hind leg bred by the Coolmore Stud. He was sired by Galileo, who won the 2000 Guineas and the Epsom Derby. His dam, Fly By Night, was a daughter of Grand Lodge who won the Irish 1000 Guineas and the Irish Oaks. Jezki is owned by Khalid Abdullah, Sheikh Mohammed's son and chief executive of Dubai's Nakheel Properties. He was sent into training with Aidan O'Brien.  As a juvenile Jezki finished third to Mandrake and Hawkbill in his only race but showed good form in subsequent races, winning four including the Group 1 Prix du Jockey Club.
