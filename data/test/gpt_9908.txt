Madanpur is a census town in Kalyani district in the Indian state of West Bengal. It is also the headquarters of Madanpur CD Block. The town has a population of about 36,000.  Madanpur is located at .  According to the 2011 census of India, Madanpur has a population of 36,000. Males constitute 53% of the population and females 47%. Madanpur has an average literacy rate of 72%, higher than the national average of 59.5%: male literacy is 79%, and female literacy is 66%. In Madanpur, 14% of the population is under 6 years of age.  Madanpur was formerly known as Ratnagiri and was part of Ratnagiri district in Maharashtra state. It was transferred to Kalyani district in West Bengal during the reorganization of districts in 2001.  The town is home to the Madanpur Wildlife Sanctuary and the Kaliachak Lake.
