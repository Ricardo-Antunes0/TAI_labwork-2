He is considered one of the founders of the study of deviance.  Born in 1919 in Auxerre, France, Rapport migrated to the United States with his family in 1930. He obtained his bachelor's degree from City College of New York in 1941 and his master's degree from Columbia University in 1943. He then served in the U.S. Army during World War II, returning to Columbia University after the war to complete his doctoral studies. Rapport received his doctorate from Columbia University in 1948.  Rapport began his academic career at Rutgers University, where he served as a professor from 1948 until 1970. He then moved to the University of Chicago, where he served as a professor until his retirement in 1995. During his career, Rapport published more than 150 papers and books on criminology and deviance. He was twice awarded the Distinguished Scientific Award from the Society for Criminology. Rapport died on November 21, 2011 at the age of 88
