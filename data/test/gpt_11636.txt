Ram Upendra Das (born 18 November 1967) is an Indian actor and director known for his work in Telugu cinema. He has acted in over 150 films and directed over 50 films. He is popularly known for his roles in romantic dramas, typically playing the role of a sensitive and caring husband or father.  Das was born in Hyderabad, Andhra Pradesh, India. After completing his schooling from Loyola School, Hyderabad, he pursued an undergraduate degree in commerce from Osmania University. He made his acting debut with the role of a petty thief in the 1989 film "Agnidevam". He then started appearing in supporting roles in films, before gaining prominence with the role of a police officer in the blockbuster film "Krishna!". He then moved to Tollywood to act in more romantic dramas. In 2003, he founded his own production company, Ram Productions.  Das has received numerous awards and nominations for his work in film and television. He has been awarded the Nandi Award for Best Actor two times (for "Agnidevam" and "Sri Krishna"), and the Nandi Award for Best Director once (for "Sri Krishna"). He also won the Filmfare Award for Best Performance by an Actor in a Supporting Role for "Agnidevam".
