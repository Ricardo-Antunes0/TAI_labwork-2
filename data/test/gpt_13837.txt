Capromeryx minor, sometimes known as the dwarf jay, is a small Old World jay. It is found in subtropical and tropical forests from Senegal to northern Argentina.  The dwarf jay is a small, brown bird, about the size of a sparrow. It has a short tail and short wings. The male has a red head and neck, and a black breast. The female is duller in colour, with a mainly brown plumage. The dwarf jay feeds mainly on insects, but it also eats small fruits.
