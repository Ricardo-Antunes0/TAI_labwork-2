The Research Works Act, 102 H.R. 3699, was introduced into the United States House of Representatives by Representative James Sensenbrenner on October 30, 2002. The bill would have created a new copyright law that would have granted copyright protection to any research paper, regardless of its original source. The bill was never brought to a vote and did not make it past the committee stage.  The Research Works Act was introduced in 2002 as H.R. 3699 by Representative James Sensenbrenner.
