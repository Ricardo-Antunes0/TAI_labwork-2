Phrynobatrachus njiomock  is a species of frog in the family Phrynobatrachidae. It is endemic to Cameroon and known from the Cross River and its tributaries, including the Nzé River.  This species was first described in 1923 by Albert Günther and Hans Hirschfeld. It was later redescribed in 1998 by François Grange and colleagues.  It is a medium-sized frog, with a snout-vent length of about . The colouration is variable, but generally it is greenish or brownish, with some darker spots on the dorsal surface. The throat is white. The tympanum is distinct. The male has a vocal organ on the base of the tail.
