William Francis Ganong, M.A.,  Ph.D., LL.D., was a Canadian educator, university administrator and historian.  Ganong was born in 1875 in Kingston, Ontario, the son of Samuel Ganong, a Presbyterian minister. He attended the University of Toronto, where he received his Bachelor of Arts degree in 1898 and his Master of Arts degree in 1900. He then attended the University of Edinburgh, where he received his Doctor of Philosophy degree in 1905.  Ganong began his career as an educator at the University of Toronto. From 1906 to 1911, he was the principal of the Victoria College School for Boys in Toronto. From 1911 to 1916, he was the director of education at the University of Toronto. From 1916 to 1923, he was the president of McMaster University. He served as the chancellor of Queen's University from 1923 to 1928. He also served as the chairman of the Ontario Library Board from 1923 to 1928.  Ganong was a historian and author. He wrote books on Canadian history, including The Universities and Ourselves (1924), A History of Education in Canada (1927), and A History of Canadian Politics (1931). He also wrote articles for various journals, including The Canadian Journal of History and The Canadian Historical Review.  Ganong died in 1956.
