Araul (Hindi:अरौल, also known as Aroul) is a town and a municipality in the Araul District of Uttar Pradesh, India. It is located on the banks of the Ganges River, about west of state capital Lucknow.  The town has a population of around 41,000 people and is known for its pilgrimage site (Araul Ghat) to the east of the town on the bank of the river. The site is said to be where the Hindu god Shiva danced a dance called "Navratri". Araul is also home to a Hindu temple dedicated to goddess Durga.  The Araul Ghat is used by Hindus during Navratri, a nine-day festival celebrated in honor of the Hindu goddess Durga. The festival culminates on the ninth day with a grand parade through the town.
