Britten's Children is a scholarly 2006 book by musicologist and critic Christopher Hailey, which investigates the life and work of the composer Benjamin Britten.  Hailey's book is a comprehensive study of Britten's life and work, tracing his development as a composer from the early years of his career to his later years. Hailey discusses Britten's relationships with fellow composers such as Peter Pears, William Walton, and Olivier Messiaen, as well as his stage works. He also examines Britten's influence on later composers, including those who worked with him at the Aldeburgh Festival.  The book has been praised for its exhaustive research and its focus on the composer's own works.
