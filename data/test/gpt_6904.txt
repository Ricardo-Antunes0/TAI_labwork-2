The 16th Punjab Regiment was an infantry regiment of the British Indian Army. The regiment was raised in 1857, during the time of the Indian Rebellion of 1857. The regiment fought in the Second Anglo-Afghan War and in World War I. In 1947, the regiment was transferred to the Pakistan Army. In 1971, the regiment was amalgamated with the 1st Punjab Regiment to form the 1st Punjab Regiment (16th Punjab Regiment).  The 16th Punjab Regiment was raised on 10 January 1857 at Meerut, in northern India, as one of the first infantry regiments of the British Indian Army. The regiment served in various wars, including the Second Anglo-Afghan War (1878-1880), World War I (1914-1918) and World War II (1939-1945). In 1947, after independence from Britain, the 16th Punjab Regiment transferred to Pakistan Army. During the Indo-Pakistani War of 1965, the 16th Punjab Regiment fought in East Pakistan and helped to liberate that province from Bengali control. After 1971, when East Pakistan became Bangladesh, the 16th Punjab Regiment amalgamated with 1st Punjab Regiment to form 1st Punjab Regiment (16th Punjab Regiment).
