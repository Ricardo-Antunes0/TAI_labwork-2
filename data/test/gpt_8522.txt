Lasse Daniel Efskind (born 13 February 1944) is a Danish poet, essayist, and translator. He was born in Copenhagen and educated at the University of Copenhagen and the University of California, Berkeley.  Efskind's poems are often characterized by their wit, irony, and delicate sense of observation. He has been nominated for the Academy Awards for Best Foreign Language Film twice, for his films "The Hunt" (1983) and "Witness" (1985). Efskind has also received the BAFTA Award for Best Screenplay and the Nordic Council Film Prize.  In addition to his poetry and film work, Efskind is also a translator. He has translated works by Michel Houellebecq, Raymond Carver, and Pablo Neruda.
