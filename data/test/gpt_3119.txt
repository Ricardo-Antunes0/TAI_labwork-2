Gondi  is a South-Central Dravidian language, spoken by about 1.5 million people in the state of Tamil Nadu, India.  Gondi is the most divergent Dravidian language and has the smallest number of speakers. It is also among the most endangered languages of India. Gondi is spoken by a people known as the Gonds, who are traditionally herding nomadic livestock in the hills of southern Maharashtra and northern Karnataka.  Gondi has about 40% lexical similarity with Kannada, but no mutual intelligibility. The two languages are mutually unintelligible with each other and with Marathi.
