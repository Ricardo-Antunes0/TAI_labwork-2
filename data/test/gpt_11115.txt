Kopparberg is a locality and the seat of Kopparberg Municipality in Värmland County, Sweden with 9,816 inhabitants in 2010.  The municipality was created on 1 January 1973 through the merger of the two former municipalities of Brunnsberg and Hultsfred.  Kopparberg is located in north-central Värmland, on the banks of the Koppar river. The town has a population of around 9,000 people. The town is well known for its granite quarries and its production of slate, which is exported worldwide. The town also has a small ski resort and a hydroelectric power plant.
