Ratu Boko  or Ratu Boko Palace is a royal residence in the city of Lautoka, Fiji. The palace is situated on a small hill overlooking the city and is surrounded by gardens.  The Ratu Boko Palace was originally built in 1873 by Ratu Sir Kamisese Mara, the first king of Fiji. It was originally used as the king's residence and was later used as the main palace of the queen regent. The palace was damaged by an earthquake in 1965 and has since been restored.   Today, the Ratu Boko Palace is a tourist attraction and is used as a guest house. The palace is also home to the National Museum of Fiji, which contains artifacts from all of Fiji's history.
