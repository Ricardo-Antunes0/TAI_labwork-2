Harpinder Singh Chawla is an Indian dental surgeon, who is known for his work in endodontics. He has also been a professor of dental surgery at the All India Institute of Dental Sciences (AIIDS) in New Delhi since 1984.  Harpinder Singh Chawla was born on 4 January 1931 in the Indian state of Punjab. He completed his dental education at the Dental College and Hospital, Chandigarh, in 1955, and then trained in endodontics at the University of Michigan, United States of America, from 1962 to 1964. After returning to India, he worked as a lecturer at the AIIDS until 1984. In that year, he was appointed professor of dental surgery at AIIDS.  Chawla is the founder-director of the Department of Endodontics and Prosthodontics at AIIMS. He is also the editor-in-chief of the "Journal of Endodontics", and a member of the editorial boards of "Dentistry Today" and "Endodontics International". He has received several awards, including the Ramon Magsaysay Award for Public Service in 1986, and the Padma Shri award from the government of India in 2001.  Chawla married Pratibha Kaur in 1959; they have two daughters. He died on 5 July 2018 at the age of 86.
