Evdokimos Tsolakidis (; born 1962) is a Greek contemporary artist who has worked in a variety of media, including painting, sculpture, installation, video and performance.  Born in Athens in 1962, Evdokimos Tsolakidis studied painting at the Academy of Fine Arts of Athens from 1980 to 1984. He then moved to London and continued his studies at the Royal College of Art, where he graduated with a BFA in 1988. Returning to Athens, he exhibited his first solo exhibition in 1989 at the Cretan Foundation Gallery. Since then he has had numerous solo and group exhibitions throughout Greece and abroad, including at the Guggenheim Museum Bilbao (1996), the Museum of Modern Art (New York City, 1998), the Museum of Contemporary Art (Los Angeles, 2002), the Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum (New York City, 2002), the Museo Nacional Centro de Arte Reina Sof√≠a (Madrid, 2007) and the Centre Pompidou (Paris, 2009). He was awarded the National Prize for Painting in 1995 and was a member of the Venice Biennale in 1997 and 2003. Tsolakidis is currently Professor of Painting at the University of Athens.
