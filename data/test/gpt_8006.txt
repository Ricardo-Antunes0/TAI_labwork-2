Pt. Vishnu Deo (Hindi: विष्णु देव) OBE (6 September 1928 – 24 January 2019) was a British-Indian author, translator, and journalist, who wrote mainly in Hindi.  He was born in 1927 in Karachi, British India (now Pakistan), to an Anglo-Indian family. He attended Stonyhurst College and then took a degree at the University of Cambridge, where he was a fellow of Trinity College. He worked as a journalist for the "Hindustan Times" from 1952 to 1985. In that time he wrote over 50 books, including biographies of Mahatma Gandhi, Jawaharlal Nehru and Rabindranath Tagore. In 1985 he became the first Indian to be made a Fellow of the Royal Society of Literature.  He died on 24 January 2019 at the age of 93.
