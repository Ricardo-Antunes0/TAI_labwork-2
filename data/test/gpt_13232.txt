Nicknamed "The Black Bobcat", Gibson was a dominant pitcher during his career, winning three Cy Young Awards and two World Series championships.  Gibson is considered one of the greatest pitchers in baseball history and is often regarded as one of the best pitchers of all time. He led the American League in earned run average (ERA) four times and in strikeouts four times. He also holds the MLB record for most games with at least 30 strikeouts. Gibson's 355 career wins are second only to Cy Young's 431. In 1999, he was elected to the Baseball Hall of Fame.
