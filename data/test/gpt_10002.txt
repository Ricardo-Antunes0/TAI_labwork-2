Eduardo López Ochoa y Portoundo (1877, Barcelona - 1957) was a Spanish general and politician.  López Ochoa entered the military in 1899, serving in the Spanish-American War and the Moroccan War. He was promoted to lieutenant colonel in 1916 and colonel in 1920. He served as Military Governor of Barcelona from 1923 to 1926, during which time he instigated a policy of decentralization of government. After serving as Minister of War from 1931 to 1933, he was appointed Commander-in-Chief of the Spanish Army in 1936. In 1939, he led Spain into World War II on the side of Nazi Germany, becoming notorious for his role in the Spanish Civil War. He was captured by the Republicans in 1940 and imprisoned until 1944. After the war, he served as Ambassador to Romania from 1946 to 1948. He retired from the military in 1949 and died in 1957.
