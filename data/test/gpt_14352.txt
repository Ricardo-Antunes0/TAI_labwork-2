Nick Meyer is an American film producer, known for his work on films such as The Meyerowitz Stories (New and Selected), A Serious Man, and The Post.  Meyer was born in New York City, the son of Esther (née Weintraub) and Nicholas Meyer. He was raised in suburban Long Island. Meyer graduated from the University of Wisconsin–Madison with a degree in English in 1984.  Meyer's first producing credit was as co-producer on the 1990 Sundance Film Festival hit drama Dead Man Walking. He then produced the films The Meyerowitz Stories (New and Selected), A Serious Man, and The Post. He also produced HBO's The Normal Heart, which won an Emmy for Outstanding Drama Series in 2014. Meyer is also a director, having directed the films Dead Man Walking, A Serious Man, and The Post.
