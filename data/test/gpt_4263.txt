Knight Foundry, also known as Knight's Foundry, is a historic industrial site located in the Near West Side of Chicago, Illinois. The site was originally settled in 1835 and became known as the location of a foundry that produced cannon for the United States Army during the American Civil War. The foundry was later used to produce bells and other metal objects. The site was added to the National Register of Historic Places in 1975. The acreage encompasses a complex of buildings, including a factory, administration building, and storage sheds. The factory is a three-story brick structure with an Italianate facade. The interior features cast iron columns and archways, and a hammer and anvil are still in use.
