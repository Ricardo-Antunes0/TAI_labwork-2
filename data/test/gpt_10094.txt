Scott Andrew Brister (born January 8, 1955) is an American former professional baseball pitcher who played in Major League Baseball (MLB) for the Texas Rangers, Toronto Blue Jays, Boston Red Sox, and Los Angeles Dodgers.  Scott Brister was drafted by the Texas Rangers in the first round of the 1977 amateur draft. He made his MLB debut with the Rangers in 1978. Brister was traded to the Toronto Blue Jays in 1984, where he played until 1990. He then signed with the Boston Red Sox and played for them until 1993. He then signed with the Los Angeles Dodgers and retired after the 1998 season.  Brister is a three-time All-Star and won two Gold Gloves (1984 and 1985) while playing for the Blue Jays. He was also named to two All-Star teams while playing for the Rangers, Red Sox and Dodgers. In 1988, he led the majors with a 2.92 earned run average (ERA).
