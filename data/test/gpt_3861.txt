Doo Hoi Kem (; born 27 November 1966) is a Cambodian-born Australian film and television actor.  Kem was born in Phnom Penh, Cambodia, to a Cambodian mother and an Australian father. He moved to Australia with his family at the age of six and attended the Canberra School of Art. Kem first came to prominence in the Australian television series "Neighbours" (1985–1991), playing the role of Thong Zee. He also appeared in the film "The Adventures of Priscilla, Queen of the Desert" (1994), as well as the Australian miniseries "Matilda" (1996). Kem has since starred in a number of television series, including "Sea Change" (2004), "Kings" (2006), "State Coroner" (2007), "Offspring" (2009), "Rush" (2013–2014) and "The Code". He has also appeared in films such as "The Adventures of Tintin" (2011), "The Book Thief" (2013), "Rogue One: A Star Wars Story" (2016) and "First Man" (2018).
