Yogurt (; , from )  also known as chia pudding, is a fermented milk product typically made from cow, goat, or sheep milk, or a combination thereof. It is a thick and creamy food that can be enjoyed for its natural sweetness and sourness, or flavored with fruits, sweeteners, or spices.  The modern yogurt industry began in the late 18th century in England and the United States. Yogurt reached mass production in the early 20th century. Today, yogurt is popular around the world and is available in various flavors and formulations. Yogurt is often an important part of the diet of people of all ages, especially in East and South Asia, Europe, North America, and parts of South America.
