Kelly Ingram Park, formerly West Park, is a public park located in the West End of Indianapolis, Indiana. The park occupies a total of 1,253 acres and includes a variety of recreational activities such as baseball, football, soccer, tennis, and basketball.  The park was originally purchased in 1909 by John Kelly and was then donated to the City of Indianapolis in 1924. The park was renamed West Park in 1954 after Indianapolis Mayor Kelly Ingram. It has since been renamed Kelly Ingram Park in honor of the Mayor's wife.
