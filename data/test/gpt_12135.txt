Baker Botts L.L.P. is an American multinational law firm headquartered in Dallas, Texas. The firm has more than 1,000 lawyers in 22 offices in the United States, Europe, and Asia.  Baker Botts was founded in 1892 by Frank Baker and W.A. Botts. The firm's main practice areas are corporate law, litigation, real estate, and tax law. The firm is one of the largest law firms in the United States and has been ranked as one of the best law firms in the world by several publications.  The firm has been involved in a number of high-profile cases, including representing tobacco companies during the 1990s lawsuits related to the health effects of smoking, representing ExxonMobil in its legal battles with Argentina over the claim that its drilling activities had damaged the environment there, and representing UBS in its dispute with the U.S. government over international tax evasion. In 2016, Baker Botts was selected as the global law firm of the year by Chambers & Partners.
