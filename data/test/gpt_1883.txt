Joan M Rodker (1 May 1915, Kensington, London – 25 October 2003, Kensington, London) was an English actress.  Rodker was born to a Jewish family in Kensington, London, the daughter of Lillian (née Levy) and Morris Rodker. Her father was a businessman who founded the clothing company Charles Tyrwhitt Ltd. She attended the prestigious Cheltenham Ladies' College before studying at RADA.  Her stage debut came in 1934 in a production of "The Merchant of Venice" at the Old Vic theatre. She made her West End debut in 1938 in a production of "The Royal Family". In 1942 she appeared in the Broadway production of "The Philadelphia Story". She also appeared in productions in London's West End and on Broadway.  Rodker first became known to American audiences when she starred as Blanche Dubois opposite Katharine Hepburn and Spencer Tracy in the 1951 Broadway production of "The Philadelphia Story". The following year she starred as Hetty Winkle in the London premiere of Lerner and Loewe's stage adaptation of Charles Dickens' "The Adventures of Oliver Twist" opposite Peter Cushing. Rodker subsequently starred in several successful Hollywood films, including "Bus Stop" (1956), "The Nun's Story" (1958), "Gentlemen Prefer Blondes" (1955), and "The Pleasure Seekers" (1965).
