Maurizio Serra (born 3 June 1955 in Palermo) is an Italian architect.  Serra studied at the Politecnico di Milano and the Royal College of Art in London, England. He has worked as an architect in Milan, Turin, Venice, and London. He was head of the School of Architecture at the University of Milan from 2002 to 2006. In 2006 he became a professor at the same school.   Serra's work often emphasizes simplicity and functionality in architecture. His designs have been featured in magazines such as "The New York Times", "Architectural Review", and "House & Home". Serra has been awarded several prestigious awards, including the Pritzker Prize (2001), European Prize for Contemporary Architecture (2005), and the Stirling Prize (2006).
