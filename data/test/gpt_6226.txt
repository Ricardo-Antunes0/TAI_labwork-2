Jonathan Sprout (born John Wells Sprout, Jr. on October 14, 1949) is an American singer-songwriter and musician. He is best known as the frontman of the folk rock band Fleetwood Mac.  Sprout was born in Chicago, Illinois, the son of John Wells Sprout, Sr., an advertising executive, and his wife, Betty. Sprout attended Phillips Academy in Andover, Massachusetts, and then the University of California at Berkeley. After graduation, he moved to London to join the British folk rock band Pentangle.  In 1975, Sprout and drummer Mick Fleetwood traveled to Los Angeles to audition for John McVie's new band, Fleetwood Mac. Impressed with Sprout's voice and songwriting talents, McVie offered him a position in the band. Sprout joined Fleetwood Mac in 1975 and has been with them ever since.  Fleetwood Mac released their self-titled debut album in 1976 and went on to become one of the most popular and successful rock bands of all time. They have sold over 150 million records worldwide and are still touring today.
