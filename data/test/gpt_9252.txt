The William Prager Medal is an award given annually by the IEEE History Center to an individual or organization that has made significant contributions to the history of IEEE.  Created in 1978, the William Prager Medal is the highest honor that IEEE can bestow. The Award is named for William Prager, a longtime IEEE officer and historian who served as the organization's first Director of History from 1951 until his death in 1965.  The William Prager Medal is awarded annually to an individual or organization that has made significant contributions to the history of IEEE.  The recipient(s) receives a bronze medal and a $1,000 cash prize.  The award is given at the Annual Awards Dinner, which is held during the IEEE International Conference on Electrical and Electronic Engineers (IEEE EEE).
