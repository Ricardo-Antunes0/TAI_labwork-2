Pricilla Jean "P.J." Cowan is an American singer, songwriter, and record producer. She started her music career as the lead singer of the rock band P.J. Cowan and the All Stars, which she formed in 1993. The band released one album, which was certified gold by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA). Cowan's solo career began in 2001 with the release of her debut album, Shine. The album was certified platinum by the RIAA and produced two singles, "Foolish Games" and "I Will Always Love You". Cowan has since released six more albums, including most recently Soaring (2015).  Cowan has sold over 10 million records worldwide and has been nominated for several awards, including two Grammy Awards. She has also won a Billboard Music Award and a Brit Award. In 2009, she was awarded an honorary doctorate from Berklee College of Music.
