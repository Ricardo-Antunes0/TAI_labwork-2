Khawla bint al-Azwar  was an Arab poet who lived in the late 9th century AD. She was born in al-Azwar, a province of the Abbasid Caliphate and died in Baghdad.  Khawla bint al-Azwar was one of the most famous Arab poets of her time. She wrote romantic and lyric poetry, which is characterized by its refined and elegant language. Her poems are often about love and beauty, and she is considered to be one of the founders of the tradition of love poetry in Arabic literature.  Khawla bint al-Azwar was also known for her scholarship. She was a polymath, whose interests ranged from poetry to history to philosophy. She is credited with being the first poet to write a comprehensive history of the Abbasid dynasty, which was one of the most powerful dynasties in Islamic history.
