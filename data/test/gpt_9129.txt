Sir Tasker Watkins  (18 November 1918 – 11 January 2002) was a Welsh-born British actor and director.  Tasker Watkins was born in Cardiff, Wales, the son of Elsie (née Edwards) and Benjamin Watkins. His father was a coal miner, and his mother was a laundress. Watkins was educated at the local school in Pontcanna before attending the Central School of Arts and Crafts in London.  Watkins began his acting career in 1936, appearing in small roles in various productions. In 1939, he made his stage debut in the role of Rufus in a production of Shakespeare's play The Two Gentlemen of Verona at the Royalty Theatre. He went on to appear in many productions throughout the 1940s and 1950s, including roles in such plays as A Man for All Seasons, Cat on a Hot Tin Roof, and The Crucible.  In 1960, Watkins made his directorial debut with the play The Father Christmas Show at the Royalty Theatre. He went on to helm productions of such plays as A Midsummer Night's Dream, Othello, Macbeth, and Antony and Cleopatra.  Watkins retired from acting in 1984, but continued to work as a director until his death from heart failure on 11 January 2002 at the age of 87.
