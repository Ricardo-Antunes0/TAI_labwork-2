Tlaloc  is a member of the Aztec pantheon of gods and goddesses. He is the god of rain, thunder, lightning, water, and war.  Tlaloc is a powerful god who commands respect. He is often depicted as a fearsome creature with horns and a fish's tail, wielding a mace and a club. He also has wings, which he uses to fly into the sky.  Tlaloc is believed to have created the world and all that exists in it. He is also responsible for regulating the weather and ensuring that crops grow. In some myths, Tlaloc is said to be able to turn himself into any liquid or solid needed for sustenance.  Tlaloc is often represented by a cactus or a fish. His feast day is January 20th.
