Bakary Sereme (born June 4, 1981) is a Senegalese professional basketball player who last played for the Tunisian club Étoile du Sahel. Standing at 6 ft 9 in (2.06 m), he plays at the power forward position.  Sereme was born in Dakar, Senegal and raised in Paris, France. He played college ball at the University of Texas at Austin, where he was teammates with future NBA player Kevin Durant. He was drafted by the Utah Jazz in the second round of the 2003 NBA draft and played for them for four seasons before being traded to the Orlando Magic in 2006. After one season with Orlando, Sereme was traded to the Houston Rockets, where he spent four more seasons before being waived by them in 2010.  Sereme then signed with Étoile du Sahel of Tunisia for the 2010–11 season and helped them win their first Tunisian League Championship. He then signed with Turkish club Kayseri Erciyesspor for the 2011–12 season and helped them win their first Turkish League Championship. After one season with Kayseri Erciyesspor, Sereme returned to Étoile du Sahel for the 2012–13 season. In July 2013, he signed with Lithuanian club Lietuvos Rytas.
