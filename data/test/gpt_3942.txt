The Stronger Vow is a 1919 American silent drama film directed by Hal Roach and produced by his company, Hal Roach Studios. The screenplay by Harry Chandlee is based on the novel of the same name by Marion Harland.  The film is about a young woman (Nina Foch) who marries a wealthy man (Paul C. Brinegar) but soon discovers that he is abusive. She decides to leave him and returns to her childhood home to live with her mother (Mae Busch). She eventually decides to remarry and this time finds a kind man (Eddie Quillan) who helps her rebuild her life.   "The Stronger Vow" was released on December 12, 1919, by Paramount Pictures. The film was well received by critics, with particular praise given to Nina Foch's performance. It has been noted as one of the first films to deal realistically with domestic violence.  The film is currently available on DVD from the Warner Archive Collection.
