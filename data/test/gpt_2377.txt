Bishop Miguel Mykycej, F.D.P. (; 17 October 1915 â€“ 24 January 2003) was a Ukrainian Greek Catholic hierarch who served as the third Archbishop of Lviv from 1972 until his death in 2003.  Miguel Mykycej was born in the town of Zakarpattia, then part of the Austro-Hungarian Empire, on 17 October 1915. He was ordained to the priesthood on 25 December 1939. After World War II he served as a parish priest in Lviv, and from 1961 to 1972 he served as the Auxiliary Bishop of Lviv. He was appointed Archbishop of Lviv by Pope Paul VI on 7 July 1972, and he received his episcopal consecration on the following 10 July from Metropolitan Andriy Shevchuk.  Miguel Mykycej died on 24 January 2003. He is buried in the Cathedral of Saint Vladimir in Lviv.
