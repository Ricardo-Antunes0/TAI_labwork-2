Elliott Waters Montroll (May 4, 1916 in New York City – December 5, 1984 in Woodstock, New York) was an American composer and ethnomusicologist.  Montroll began his musical education at the age of six when he began studying piano with his father. He continued his studies at the Juilliard School of Music where he graduated in 1939. During World War II he served in the U.S. Navy and composed music while stationed in the South Pacific.  Montroll returned to Juilliard in 1946 to teach composition and ethnomusicology. He also served as the director of the Eastman School of Music’s ethnomusicology program from 1969 until his death. Montroll was a pioneer in the field of ethnomusicology and composed works that explore world music traditions. His compositions have been performed around the world and have been featured on television shows such as The Lawrence Welk Show and 60 Minutes.
