Hobhouse was born in Rugeley, Staffordshire, the son of a farmer. He attended the local school and then worked on his father's farm before becoming a clerk in a chemist's. He became active in local politics and served as a councillor on the Rugeley town council from 1913 to 1921.   Hobhouse was elected to Parliament in 1935, taking the seat vacated by his father. He served as Parliamentary Private Secretary to the Minister of Agriculture from 1936 to 1938 and to the Minister of Transport from 1938 to 1940. In 1940, he was appointed Parliamentary Under-Secretary of State for India, a position he held until his death two years later.   Hobhouse died in 1942, aged 58. He had married Marie-Louise Catherwood in 1907, with whom he had two children.
