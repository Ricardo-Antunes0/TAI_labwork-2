Ali Bhai  is a 2007 Indian biographical film directed by Ramesh Sippy and produced by Aditya Chopra. It is a fictionalized account of the life of Ali Bhai Khan, one of the most influential figures in the history of the Indian independence movement.  Ali Bhai (Agha Ali Mirza) was born in 1857 in the town of Kalanaur in what is now Rajasthan, into a noble Muslim family. He began working as a child to help support his family, and at age 16 he began working as a clerk for a land agent. In 1876, he married Sayyida Khadija (Khadija Khatoon), the daughter of a wealthy landed family.  Bhai joined the Indian National Congress in 1885 and quickly became one of its most influential members. He played an instrumental role in organizing the 1885 Ghadar conspiracy, a failed attempt to overthrow British rule in India. In 1906, he was elected president of the Congress Party, and served in that position until his death in 1936.  Ali Bhai was a tireless campaigner for Indian independence, and played a critical role in organizing the Non-Cooperation Movement and the Salt March. During World War I, he served as chairman of the All-India War Committee, and was instrumental in persuading Gandhi to boycott British goods.
