Jeffrey David Ullman (born November 22, 1942) is an American economist, currently the Harry Frank Guggenheim Professor of Economics and Management at the University of Pennsylvania. He is a member of the National Academy of Sciences.  Ullman was born in Baltimore, Maryland, to Jewish parents. He received his B.A. from Yale University in 1964 and his Ph.D. from Harvard University in 1969. He has been a professor at the University of Pennsylvania since 1971.  Ullman is known for his work on decision theory, game theory and economic analysis of law. His best-known publication is The Theory of Economic Regulation (1981), which has been cited more than 2,000 times. He has also written extensively on entrepreneurship, public finance and taxation, and has co-authored a book with Nobel laureate Herbert Simon on the economics of organizations.  In 2003, Ullman was awarded the John Bates Clark Medal, the highest honour bestowed by the American Economic Association, for his "distinguished contributions to economic theory and analysis". In 2009 he was awarded an honorary DSc from Heriot-Watt University. In 2013 he was elected to the National Academy of Sciences.
