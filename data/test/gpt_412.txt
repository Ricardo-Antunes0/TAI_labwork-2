He is best known for his work on the history of Indonesia, which earned him a national reputation.  Lukman was born in 1920 in Semarang, Central Java, Dutch East Indies (now Indonesia). After finishing high school in 1938 he studied at the University of Leiden in the Netherlands, where he obtained his doctorate in history in 1951. Between 1951 and 1954 he taught at the University of Indonesia, where he also served as rector from 1969 to 1971. He then served as a professor at the University of the Philippines from 1974 until his death in 2006.  Lukman's work on the history of Indonesia has won him a national reputation. He was awarded the National Order of Culture by President Suharto in 1998.
