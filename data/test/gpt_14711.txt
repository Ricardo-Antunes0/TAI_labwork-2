Netherlands American Cemetery and Memorial  is a United States military cemetery located in Margraten, Netherlands. It is the final resting place of more than 3,000 American military personnel who died in World War II, the Korean War, and the Vietnam War.  The Netherlands American Cemetery and Memorial was established on a site near Margraten in the Netherlands in 1951. The cemetery was dedicated on July 12, 1952. The cemetery was originally intended to be a temporary burial ground for American military personnel who had died in Europe during World War II. However, as more and more American military personnel died in Europe during and after the war, the cemetery became permanently established.  The cemetery is operated by the United States Department of Defense and is open to the public. The cemetery is home to a number of memorials and monuments, including a bronze statue of an American soldier by sculptor James Earle Fraser. The cemetery is also home to the National WWII Memorial.
