George Town (formerly known as Tanjung Penaga) is a town located on the east coast of Penang Island, Malaysia. It is the capital of the state of Penang. The town had a population of around 210,000 in 2016.  George Town is a multicultural town with a history dating back to 1786. Originally known as Tanjung Penaga, the town was named after Prince George of Denmark who was the first European to set foot on Penang Island. George Town became the capital of British Malaya in 1867 and attained its current name in 1925. The town is noted for its architecture, including the iconic Star Fort and Christ Church. The Penang International Airport is also located within George Town.
