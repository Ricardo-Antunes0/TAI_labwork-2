He studied art under Washington Allston at the Maryland Institute College of Art in 1824-1825, and under Benjamin West at the Royal Academy of Arts in London in 1829-1830.   Rains first exhibited at the National Academy of Design in 1832, and he continued to exhibit there until 1859. He also exhibited at the American Academy of Arts and Sciences from 1837 to 1860. Rains died in New York City on March 24, 1873.  Rains is best known for his portraits, including those of John Quincy Adams and Andrew Jackson. He also painted landscapes and seascapes.
