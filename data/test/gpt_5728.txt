Bo Gunnar Almqvist (5 May 1931 - 31 August 2003) was a Swedish author, journalist and screenwriter.   Almqvist was born in Umeå, Sweden, the son of a teacher. He studied at the University of Umeå and the Sorbonne in Paris. Almqvist worked as a journalist for several newspapers before writing his first novel, The Girl Who Kicked the Hornet's Nest, in 1975. It was not until after its publication that he learned he had won the prestigious Nobel Prize for Literature. Almqvist wrote several more novels, including The Horseman, The Demonologist and Crime and Punishment, before his death from a stroke at the age of seventy-five.
