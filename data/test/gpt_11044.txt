"General" Morton Matthew McCarver (January 14, 1807 – October 22, 1877) was an American soldier who served in the Mexican-American War and the American Civil War. He achieved national prominence as a Union general during the latter conflict, and later served as Confederate governor of Arkansas.  Morton M. McCarver was born in 1807 in Preble County, Ohio, to John and Sarah (Powell) McCarver. He attended the common schools and then learned the carpenter's trade. In 1827 he enlisted in the United States Army as a private, and served in the Mexican-American War. Afterwards he served in the United States Army during the Florida War of 1838–1839. From 1847 to 1849 he served as colonel of the 2nd Regiment Ohio Volunteer Cavalry, which participated in the California Gold Rush. In 1850 he received a promotion to brigadier general of volunteers, and he fought in the Mexican–American War under General Winfield Scott.  In 1861 McCarver was appointed colonel of the 25th U.S. Infantry, which he led during the American Civil War. He distinguished himself at First Bull Run and in subsequent campaigns, including Chancellorsville, Gettysburg, and Appomattox Court House. In 1865 he was promoted to major general of volunteers, and he served until his death in 1877.  Morton M. McCarver is buried at Hollywood Cemetery in Richmond, Virginia.
