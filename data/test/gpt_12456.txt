Chadderton Town Hall is a municipal building located in the town of Chadderton, within the Metropolitan Borough of Oldham, in Greater Manchester, England.  The building was opened in 1888 and is located on Market Street, just outside the town centre. It serves as the town's main civic and administrative centre, housing the town's mayor and council chambers, as well as a library and a branch of the Oldham County Museum. The building is also used for weddings and other events.  The town hall was built by J. W. Gaunt at a cost of £6,000 (£ in 2019). It was designed by the Manchester architect Alfred Waterhouse and features a stone exterior with a slate roof. The building was officially opened on 8 June 1888 by the Lord Mayor of Manchester, Harry Alderdice.
