Jean Résal (22 October 1854, in Besançon – 10 March 1916, in Paris) was a French painter.  Résal was the son of a watchmaker. He studied at the Besançon Academy and then at the École des Beaux-Arts in Paris. He exhibited at the Salon from 1881 to 1914. His work is characterized by its realism and its use of light and shadow. He is particularly noted for his portraits, including that of Émile Zola.
