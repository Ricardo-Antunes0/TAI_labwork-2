Lieutenant General Richard Wardlaw,  is a retired Australian Army officer who served as Chief of the General Staff from 1990 to 1992.  Richard Wardlaw was born in Perth, Western Australia, on 13 December 1936. After attending public schools, he entered the Royal Military College, Duntroon, in 1953. Wardlaw graduated with a Bachelor of Arts (BA) degree in 1959 and was commissioned into the Australian Army as a second lieutenant.  Wardlaw served in the Second Australian Imperial Force (2nd AIF) during the Malayan Emergency and the Vietnam War. He was promoted to lieutenant colonel in 1977 and appointed deputy commander of the 1st Cavalry Division. He later served as commander of the 1st Brigade and then the 3rd Division. In 1988, Wardlaw was promoted to brigadier general and appointed deputy chief of the general staff (DGSE). He became Chief of the General Staff in 1990 and retired in 1992.  After retiring from the army, Wardlaw became a consulting senior fellow with the Australian Strategic Policy Institute (ASPI). He has also been a visiting professor at Monash University and Macquarie University. In 2006, he was made an Officer of the Order of Australia (AO) for his services to defence policymaking and to the army.
