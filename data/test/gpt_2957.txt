Żyleta  is a common name of a type of Polish folk dance. Żyleta is a lively, fast-paced dance with a distinctive step. The rhythm is typically syncopated, with quick steps and vigorous arm movements.  The Żyleta is believed to have originated in the region of Greater Poland in the late Middle Ages. It was popular among peasants and was used as a way to pass long hours in the fields during summertime. The dance was also used as entertainment by the nobility.   Today, the Żyleta is still popular in eastern Poland and other parts of Central and Eastern Europe. It is often featured at festivals and competitions.
