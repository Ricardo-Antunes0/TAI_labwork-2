Sartain began her writing career in 1866, when she published a volume of poetry entitled "The Flowering of the Rod". The following year she published her first novel, "Aunt Jane's Nieces". She wrote over 60 books during her lifetime, many of which were based on her experiences as a minister's wife and mother.   Sartain was a tireless advocate for women's rights and social reform. She served on the boards of several organizations, including the National Woman Suffrage Association and the American Woman Suffrage Association. In 1892 she was awarded the honorary degree of D.D. from Columbia University.  Sartain died in 1902 at the age of 66. Her body was buried in Oakwood Cemetery in Greensboro.
