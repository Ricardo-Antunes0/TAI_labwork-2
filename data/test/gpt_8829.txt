Marie-Claude Felton (born 1981), is a French-Canadian artist. She is a multimedia artist who uses video, sound, performance and installation art.  Marie-Claude Felton was born in 1981 in Montreal, Quebec. She has a BFA from Concordia University in Montreal and an MFA from the University of California at Los Angeles.  Felton's work often explores the intersections of art, technology and society. Her videos, sound installations and performance pieces often explore themes of identity, gender, power and sexuality. Felton's work has been exhibited at museums and galleries around the world, including the Museum of Modern Art in New York City, the Centre Pompidou in Paris and the Vancouver Art Gallery. Felton is also a recipient of several awards, including the prestigious Guggenheim Fellowship.
