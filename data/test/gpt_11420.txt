Willie Larry Randolph (born July 6, 1954) is an American former professional basketball player who played in the American Basketball Association (ABA) and National Basketball Association (NBA). He played for the Boston Celtics, Houston Rockets, and Los Angeles Lakers.  Randolph was a prolific scorer and rebounder who was a three-time ABA Most Valuable Player (MVP) and two-time NBA All-Star. He is one of only four players in NBA history to score over 30,000 points and grab over 13,000 rebounds. He is also one of only two players in NBA history to record over 20,000 points and 10,000 rebounds in a career. Randolph was inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame in 1998.
