Emmanuel Bez Idakula, known as Bez (born 17 January 1971), is a Tanzanian musician, composer, and producer. He is best known as the frontman and songwriter of the Tanzanian supergroup Makali'i.  Bez was born in 1971 in Dar es Salaam, Tanzania, where he started his musical training at an early age. He later moved to Germany to study music production at the Berlin School of Music and Dramatic Art, graduating in 1997. Upon graduating, he returned to Tanzania and formed Makali'i with his former Berlin School classmate Sefa Mochoni. In 2001, they released their self-titled debut album, which earned them critical acclaim and established them as one of the most popular musical acts in Tanzania. The album spawned four hit singles, including "Ngoni" (which peaked at number 2 on the Tanzanian music charts) and "Ndona".  Bez has since released four more albums with Makali'i: "Majestic" (2002), "Soulful" (2003), "Sensational" (2005), and "Revelation" (2007). He has also collaborated with a number of other artists, including Machakos Boys Choir, Miriam Makeba, and Khaled. He has also performed at a number of international festivals, including the Montreux Jazz Festival, the North Sea Jazz Festival, and the London Jazz Festival.
