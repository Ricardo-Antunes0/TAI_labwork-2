Farwell is a city in and the county seat of Kenedy County, Texas, United States. The population was 3,824 at the 2010 census. It is part of the Houston–The Woodlands–Sugar Land metropolitan area.  Founded in 1878, Farwell is the oldest city in Kenedy County. The city is named for Colonel John S. Farwell, a Civil War hero and delegate to the Texas Constitutional Convention. A post office has been in operation at Farwell since 1878.  Farwell is located on the eastern edge of Kenedy County at (29.197439, -97.730315). It is bordered to the northeast by the city of Rosenberg and to the southeast by Fort Bend County. The county line forms the southern border of Farwell while a small portion of the city extends into Brazoria County.
