Rain dust or snow dust, traditionally known as "snow ice", is a fine-grained powder that forms from water droplets that fall as rain or snow. The size and shape of the individual droplets determine the size and shape of the resultant rain dust or snow dust particle. Rain dust typically contains a higher concentration of suspended material than snow dust.
