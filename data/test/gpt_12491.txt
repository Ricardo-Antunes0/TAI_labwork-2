Al-Burdayni Mosque  is a mosque in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia. It is situated in the eastern part of the city, in the vicinity of the Al-Burdayni Palace.  The mosque was commissioned by Muhammad bin Saud, the founder of modern Saudi Arabia, in 1892 and it was completed in 1894. The mosque is named after Hussein ibn Ali, the grandson of Muhammad, who was killed at the Battle of Karbala. The mosque is made of red sandstone and marble.
