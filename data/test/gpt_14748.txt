James Hugh Calum Laurie  (; born 26 February 1962) is an English actor, comedian, and writer. He is best known for his roles as Dr. Gregory House on the Fox medical drama series House and as Max Black in the BBC television series Black Mirror. Laurie has also starred in films such as The World's End, Dead Poet's Society, and The Omen.  Laurie was born in Chichester, West Sussex, to a Scottish father, James Alexander Laurie (1922-2008), and an English mother, Elizabeth Calum (n√©e Cook; 1925-2007). He has two sisters and one brother. His family moved to Bath when he was young and he attended the John Batchelor School before studying law at Bristol University. Laurie began his career by performing in local comedy clubs. After several years of stand-up work, Laurie made his first television appearance in 1986 with a guest appearance on the cult programme The Young Ones. In 1987, he starred in his own sitcom, A Bit of Fry and Laurie.  Laurie's breakthrough role came in 1994 when he was cast as Dr. Gregory House in the Fox medical drama series House. He played the role for eight seasons from 2004 to 2013. Laurie has since appeared in films such as The World's End (2013), Dead Poet's Society (1989), and The Omen (1976). In 2018, he starred as Winston Smith in the Netflix series Fahrenheit 451.
