The rosy boa (Charina trivirgata) is a snake found in the Amazon rainforest. It is the only member of its genus.  The rosy boa is a large snake, growing to a length of 2.5 meters (8.2 feet). It has a creamy-colored body with bright pink markings, and a bright red band around its neck. The snake is active during the day, hiding under tree bark or in crevices during the night. It feeds on small animals, and can kill prey by constriction.
