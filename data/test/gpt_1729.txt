Anne Bryson Sutherland (7 January 1922 - 12 February 2017) was a Scottish actress and author. She was best known for her roles in the television series Minder and Doc Martin.  Anne Bryson Sutherland was born on 7 January 1922 in Glasgow, Scotland. She was educated at the Royal Scottish Academy of Music and Drama, graduating in 1943. She began her career as an actress in the early 1950s, appearing in television programmes such as The Adventures of Robin Hood and The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes. In 1964, she appeared in the film version of The Railway Children, for which she was nominated for a BAFTA Award for Best British Actress.  In 1969, she starred in the television series Minder, for which she won a BAFTA Award for Best Actor. She also appeared in the television series Doc Martin, for which she won a BAFTA Award for Best Actress in a Television Drama. Sutherland retired from acting in 1992. She later wrote two memoirs, A Life in Letters (2000) and An Evening with Anne Bryson Sutherland (2005). She died on 12 February 2017 at the age of 94.
