Anthony Arthur "Tony" Cucolo (born August 22, 1959) is an American former professional ice hockey player and current coach. He played in the National Hockey League (NHL) with the Philadelphia Flyers, Boston Bruins, Hartford Whalers and Vancouver Canucks. He also played in the World Hockey Association (WHA) with the Quebec Nordiques and Calgary Flames.  Cucolo was born in 1959 in Levittown, New York, to immigrant parents from Calabria, Italy. As a youth, he played hockey for Levittown Skating Center. He attended Siena College, where he played for the Saints men's ice hockey team from 1978 to 1981. He then transferred to Boston University, where he played for the Terriers men's ice hockey team from 1982 to 1984.  Cucolo was drafted by the Philadelphia Flyers in the fourth round of the 1983 NHL Entry Draft. He made his NHL debut with the Flyers in 1984–85 and established himself as a regular player over the next several seasons. In 1988–89, he helped lead Philadelphia to its first Stanley Cup championship. Cucolo also played for the Bruins and Whalers in the NHL before joining the Canucks in 1991–92. In Vancouver, he helped lead the team to its first Stanley Cup championship in 1994. After playing one season with HC Davos of Switzerland, Cucolo retired from professional hockey in 1995.
