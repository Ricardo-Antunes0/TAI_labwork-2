In 1939 he enlisted in the United States Army Air Forces and served in World War II as a bombardier on a B-17 Flying Fortress bomber. After the war he returned to Berkeley and completed his undergraduate studies in 1947.  Childs taught creative writing at Yale University from 1965 until his retirement in 1981. He was a visiting professor at Columbia University, Brown University, the University of Texas at Austin, and the University of California, Irvine.  He died in 1991 in Santa Barbara, California. His body was cremated and his ashes scattered at sea.  Childs's novels explore the effects of violence on individuals and society. Childs also wrote novels about working-class Americans during the Great Depression, such as "The Street" (1951) and "Elbow Room" (1956).
