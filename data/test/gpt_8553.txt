"Can the Can" is the second solo album by American rapper and singer-songwriter Mos Def. It was released on August 14, 2002, by Def Jam Recordings.  The album was produced by Kanye West, J Dilla, and Mos Def himself. Featured guests include Talib Kweli, Q-Tip, Pharoahe Monch, and Lauryn Hill.  "Can the Can" garnered critical acclaim from music critics and peaked at number three on the US "Billboard" 200 chart. It has been certified platinum by the RIAA. The album also won a Grammy Award for Best Rap Album in 2003.  Mos Def released his first solo album "Black on Both Sides" in 1998 to critical acclaim and commercial success. He followed it up with "Music & Me" in 2000, but feeling creatively unfulfilled, he took a break from music. In 2002, Mos Def decided to record a new album and enlisted producers Kanye West, J Dilla, and himself.   The album features guest appearances from Talib Kweli, Q-Tip, Pharoahe Monch, Lauryn Hill and others. Mos Def described the sound of the album as "raw hip-hop", saying "I wanted it to be gritty sounding [...] like you're in the room with me."   "Can the Can" was released on August 14, 2002.
