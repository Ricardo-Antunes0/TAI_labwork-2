Heikki Ritavuori was born on 23 March 1880 in Turku, Finland. He studied architecture at the Helsinki University of Technology from 1898 to 1902, graduating with a degree in architecture. From 1902 to 1907 he worked as an architect in Helsinki. In 1907 he moved to Paris, France, where he worked as an architect until 1913. He then returned to Finland and resumed his practice in Helsinki.  Ritavuori was one of the leading architects in Finland during the first half of the 20th century. His work is characterized by its Functionalist style, which emphasized the use of industrial materials and simple forms. His notable projects include the Helsinki Central Railway Station (1914), the University of Helsinki (1916), and the Finnish National Museum (1929). Ritavuori died on 11 March 1945 in Helsinki.
