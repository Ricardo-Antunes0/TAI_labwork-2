Peer Smed (1878 - November 17, 1943) was a Norwegian painter.  Peer Smed was born in the town of Lillehammer, in Oppland, Norway. He was the son of a carpenter and began his artistic career painting landscapes in the traditional Norwegian style. In 1904, he moved to Copenhagen, Denmark where he exhibited his paintings and became friends with the painters Aksel Lundgaard and Edvard Munch. He returned to Norway in 1912 and settled in Oslo. In 1916, he was appointed professor at the Norwegian National Academy of Fine Arts, a position he held until his death. Peer Smed is best known for his paintings of rural Norway, often depicting scenes from the rural life of the early 20th century.
