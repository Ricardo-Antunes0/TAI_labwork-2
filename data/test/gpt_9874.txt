Sailing  is the third studio album by American singer and songwriter Taylor Swift. It was released on October 25, 2010, by Big Machine Records. The album was produced by Nathan Chapman, Shellback, and Grey.  The album debuted at number one on the US Billboard 200, selling over 542,000 copies in its first week. "Sailing" has sold over 1.287 million copies in the United States as of August 2015. The album received generally positive reviews from critics, who complimented Swift's experimentation with new sounds and themes.  Following the release of her second album, Fearless (2008), Swift took a two-year break from music to focus on her education. She wrote most of the material for Sailing during this time, with some of the songs dating back to before Fearless was released. "Sailing" is a departure from the country pop sound of her previous album, incorporating elements of electronica, tropicalia, and world music into her music.  The title track is a ballad about the loss of a relationship. "Shake It Off" is a dance-pop song about combating negative self-talk. "I Knew You Were Trouble" is a R&B song about a one-night stand. "We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together" is a love ballad written about an ex-boyfriend of Swift's sister. "22" is a song about Swift's birthday and her struggles with anxiety and depression.
