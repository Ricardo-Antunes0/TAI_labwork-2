Lord Reay, of Reay in the County of Caithness, KT, PC (born 8 October 1936) is a British Conservative Party politician. He was the Member of Parliament (MP) for Thurrock from 1983 to 1997, and has been the Member of the Scottish Parliament for Caithness, Sutherland and Easter Ross since 1999.  Reay was educated at Eton College and Trinity College, Cambridge. He served in the Royal Navy from 1956 to 1959. He was called to the Bar at the Inner Temple in 1961.  Reay was elected MP for Thurrock in the 1983 general election, defeating the incumbent Labour MP David Owen. He held the seat until it was abolished in 1997, when he was elected for Caithness, Sutherland and Easter Ross. He served as Parliamentary Private Secretary to Home Secretary Douglas Hurd from 1990 to 1992 and then as Minister of State at the Department of Social Security from 1992 to 1994.  Reay is a member of the Conservative Party and sits on its frontbench team as Shadow Secretary of State for Scotland. In this role he has been responsible for issues such as Scottish devolution, education reform and welfare reform. He also serves on the Conservative Party's National Executive Committee.  Reay married Jane Armstrong in 1969. They have two daughters and one son.
