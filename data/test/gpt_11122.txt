The Niger stingray or smooth freshwater stingray, Potamotrygon niger, is a species of ray in the family Potamotrygonidae. It is found in the drainage systems of the Niger River and its tributaries, in western Africa.  The Niger stingray has a relatively elongate body with a flattened snout and a pointed tail. It has a wide range and is common in some parts of its range, although it is considered vulnerable by the IUCN. The meat is considered to be edible, but it is not commonly eaten.
