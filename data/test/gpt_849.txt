Archer "Archie" Parr (December 25, 1860 or 1861 – January 15, 1934) was an American actor, best known for his work in silent films. He appeared in over 160 films and was the father of actress Veronica Parr.  Archie Parr was born in Detroit, Michigan, the son of John and Margaret (née Broughton) Parr. He began his acting career in the late 1880s, appearing in a number of plays before moving to Hollywood in 1911. He quickly became known for his work in silent films, appearing in such classics as The Birth of a Nation (1915) and The Hunchback of Notre Dame (1923). He retired from acting in 1928, but continued to make occasional appearances until his death two years later.
