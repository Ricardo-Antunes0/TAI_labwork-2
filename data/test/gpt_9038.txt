Lieutenant General Henry Vijaya Athukorale, VSV, psc, is a retired senior military officer and a former Commander of the Special Forces Group (SFG) in the Sri Lanka Army.   Henry Vijaya Athukorale was born on the 10th of February, 1956 in a well-to-do family in Kandy, Sri Lanka. After completing his education at the Royal Military Academy, Sandhurst, he was commissioned into the Sri Lanka Army as a Second Lieutenant on the 1st of December, 1977. He subsequently attended the United States Army Command and General Staff College at Fort Leavenworth, Kansas, USA where he graduated with distinction in 1981.  After successful completion of his basic training at the Sri Lanka Infantry Regiment (SLIR), he was posted to the 5th Battalion, The Royal Sri Lanka Artillery (5 RSLA) as a Battery Commander. He later served as a Brigade Major with the 7th Division and then as the Deputy Commander of the 37th Infantry Brigade.   In September 2003, he was appointed Commander of the Special Forces Group (SFG) and held this position until his retirement on the 30th of December 2004. During his tenure as SFG Commander, he successfully spearheaded several operations including Operation Jayaweera which resulted in defeat of the Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam (LTTE) in May 2002.
