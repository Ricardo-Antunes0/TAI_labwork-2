Frank Daniel Garofalo (born April 1, 1944), also known as Frank Gari, is an American musician and songwriter. He is best known as the founder, vocalist, and guitarist of the punk rock band the Dead Kennedys.  Frank Daniel Garofalo was born in San Francisco, California on April 1, 1944. He first became interested in music at an early age, learning to play the guitar from his father. In 1962, Garofalo and his friend Jello Biafra formed the short-lived band the Fear. The following year, they formed the Dead Kennedys, a punk rock band which achieved international notoriety in the early 1970s. The band released several albums and singles before disbanding in 1980. In 1987, Garofalo formed the Bad Brains, a hardcore punk band which released two albums before disbanding in 1992.  Since the early 1990s, Garofalo has been a music teacher at San Francisco State University. He has also released several solo albums and contributed to various projects including the Lollapalooza festival and the soundtrack to the film Natural Born Killers. In 2017, he released his first solo album in over 25 years, entitled Frank Garofalo Sings for President!.
