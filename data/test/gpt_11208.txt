Pedro Lucio Cuadra Luque (14 April 1842 – 21 February 1920) was a Chilean politician and jurist who served as the 5th President of Chile from 1912 to 1920.  Cuadra was born in Valparaíso, son of Pedro Lucio Cuadra y Araujo and Manuela Sánchez de Luque. He studied law at the University of Chile, where he gained a law degree in 1865. He later worked as a lawyer.  Cuadra was a member of the Liberal Party and served as its president from 1899 to 1902. He was elected to the National Congress in 1892 and served until 1912, when he was elected President of Chile. As President, he initiated sweeping social reforms, including land redistribution, labour laws, and education reform. He also played a role in the country's defense during World War I. Cuadra retired from office in 1920 and died two years later at the age of 81.
