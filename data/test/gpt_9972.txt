Fulton Lewis III (January 25, 1936 - January 1, 1988) was an American journalist and author.  Fulton Lewis III was born in West Palm Beach, Florida on January 25, 1936. He attended the University of Florida, where he was a member of the Phi Delta Theta fraternity, and later the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, from which he received his B.A. in 1957. He then served in the United States Marine Corps from 1958 to 1960.   Lewis began his journalism career as a staff writer for the "Jacksonville Journal" in Jacksonville, Florida, and later became its managing editor. In 1971 he joined the staff of "The Washington Post", where he spent the remainder of his career. He was a member of the Pulitzer Prize jury for 1973 and 1984. Lewis wrote a number of best-selling books, including "The Education of a Politician" (1972), "The Making of the President 1972" (1974), "The Final Days" (1976), "Rising Star: The Pre-Presidency Years of George H. W. Bush" (1987), and "A Man in Full: The Life of John F. Kennedy" (1999). Lewis died on January 1, 1988 after a prolonged illness at the age of 54.
