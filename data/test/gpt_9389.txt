Thomas Tate (born February 26, 1956) is an American singer-songwriter, musician and actor.  Tommy Tate was born in 1956 in Hickory, North Carolina. His parents were both musicians, and Tate started playing the guitar at age six. After graduating from high school, Tate moved to Nashville, Tennessee, and started working as a songwriter. He released his first album in 1984 and quickly became a successful country music singer. In the 1990s, Tate began to experiment with other genres, and his music began to incorporate elements of rock and alternative rock. He has released several albums since then, including the platinum-selling album "Life" in 2006.  Tommy Tate has acted in several films and television shows over the years, most notably starring roles in the films "The Perfect Storm" (2000) and "The Help" (2011). He has also written songs for several movies and television shows, including "The Omen" (1976), "Days of Thunder" (1990), "Dumb & Dumber" (1994), "The Shawshank Redemption" (1994), " Forrest Gump" (1994), "The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring" (2001), "The Lord of the Rings: The Two Towers" (2002), "Flags of Our Fathers" (2006), and "Captain Phillips" (2013).  In 2018, Tommy Tate was awarded the prestigious Kennedy Center Honors for his contributions to American culture.
