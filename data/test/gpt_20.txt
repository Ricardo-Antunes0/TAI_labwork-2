Edward Charles "Whitey" Ford (October 21, 1928 – August 9, 2006) was an American professional baseball player, manager, and coach. He played in Major League Baseball (MLB) for the Detroit Tigers (1951–1954), Los Angeles Dodgers (1955–1957), and New York Yankees (1958). Ford served as the manager of the Yankees (1971–1973) and Oakland Athletics (1976–1978). He was a member of the Baseball Hall of Fame, inducted in 1982.  Ford was born in West Virginia coal country to a family of six children. His father, Charles Ford, was a miner. As a youth, Ford played baseball and football in his home town. He attended high school at Pineville High School before enrolling at West Virginia University on a football scholarship. Ford left college after one year to sign with the Tigers.  Ford made his debut with the Tigers in 1951 and spent five seasons with Detroit. In 1954 he was traded to the Dodgers, where he played two more seasons before retiring at the end of 1957. In 1958 he became manager of the Yankees and led them to their first championship in 38 years. He then spent three seasons as manager of the Dodgers before returning to manage the Yankees again from 1971 to 1973. Ford retired from playing after the 1973 season and served as a coach with the Yankees for two more years before retiring from coaching at the end of 1975.
