The Smithsonian Institution Building, located near the National Mall in Washington, D.C., is the largest museum in the world, housing over two million items in its collection. It was founded in 1846 by American architect James Renwick, Jr., with the goal of promoting American art and history. The building was designed in the Beaux-Arts style and was completed in 1881.  The Smithsonian Institution Building is a large neoclassical structure built on a raised platform to allow for views of the National Mall from all sides. The building is composed of a central core flanked by two wings, with an arched portico at the front entrance. The building is decorated with marble and bronzes from American art and history, including pieces from the Museum of American History, the National Museum of African American History and Culture, and the National Museum of Natural History.  The Smithsonian Institution Building has been used for a wide variety of purposes over the years, including as a museum, library, office building, and exhibition space. It has been designated a National Historic Landmark and is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Washington, D.C.
