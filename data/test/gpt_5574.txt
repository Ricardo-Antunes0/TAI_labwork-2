Robert William Pressley (born April 8, 1959) is an American politician and jurist who is the current United States Attorney for the District of Massachusetts. He was previously a Republican member of the Massachusetts House of Representatives from 2007 to 2013, representing the district that includes parts of Boston and its surrounding suburbs.  Pressley was born in Arlington, Virginia, and moved to Boston as a teenager. He earned his A.B. from Boston College in 1981 and his J.D. from Harvard Law School in 1985. He worked as a law clerk for Judge Robert Bork of the United States Court of Appeals for the District of Columbia Circuit from 1985 to 1986. Pressley was then a litigation associate at Arnold & Porter from 1986 to 1988, when he became an assistant district attorney in Middlesex County, Massachusetts.  Pressley won election to the Massachusetts House of Representatives in 2006, defeating Democrat James Phelan by almost 20 percentage points. He was re-elected in 2008 and 2010 with more than 60% of the vote. In 2013, Pressley was appointed United States Attorney by Governor Deval Patrick, replacing John W. Kaczmarek who had been appointed to serve as U.S. Ambassador to Portugal by President Barack Obama. Pressley began serving in that capacity on October 1, 2013.
