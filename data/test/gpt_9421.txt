Born in Denver, Colorado, Nelson first came to prominence as one of the vocalists for the folk-rock group The Byrds, alongside Roger McGuinn, David Crosby and Chris Hillman. He subsequently achieved solo success with his 1972 debut album "Gasoline Alley". After a hiatus of several years, Nelson returned with new material in 1980 and released his final album, "The Path", in 1983.  Nelson died from a drug overdose at the age of 52. He was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 1992.
