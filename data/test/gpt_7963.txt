José Antonio Price (1890–1951) was a prominent Cuban author, journalist, and politician. He was the first president of the Republic of Cuba after its establishment in 1902.  José Antonio Price was born in Matanzas, Cuba in 1890. He studied at the University of Havana, where he earned a law degree in 1912. After graduating, Price worked as a lawyer and journalist. In 1902, he helped to establish the Republic of Cuba after a long period of colonial rule by Spain. Price served as its first president until 1909. He later served as the country's ambassador to Mexico and the United States. Price died in Havana in 1951.
