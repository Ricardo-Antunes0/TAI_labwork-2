Brigadier Tristram Hugh "Tim" Massy-Beresford, DSO, MVO, (born 21 November 1947) is a retired British Army officer who served as a deputy commanding general (DG) in the British Army.  Massy-Beresford was educated at Wellington College and the Royal Military Academy, Sandhurst. He was commissioned into the Grenadier Guards in 1971. He served in Northern Ireland, Cyprus, and Germany during the 1970s before becoming a staff officer at the Staff College, Camberley in 1981. He was made a colonel in 1995 and served as deputy commanding general for training from 1998 to 2000. He became Commander of 3rd Infantry Division in 2000 and was made a brigadier in 2001. He was appointed Deputy Commander of Land Forces in Britain in 2006 and became Deputy Commanding General (Land) on 1 October 2007. On 1 September 2009, he retired from the British Army after 40 years of service.
