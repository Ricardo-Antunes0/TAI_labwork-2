Organic-rich sedimentary rocks are a specific type of sedimentary rock that is rich in organic matter. They are typically formed from mud, sand, and gravel that was deposited by rivers, lakes, or estuaries. The organic matter in these rocks can be sourced from plants, animals, or both.  Organic-rich sedimentary rocks can be found across the globe. They are most commonly found in areas that have a warm climate and a lot of rainfall. There are a few examples of organic-rich sedimentary rocks that are found in North America, Europe, and Asia.  Organic-rich sedimentary rocks can be used for a variety of purposes. They can be used as a source of construction materials, such as bricks and concrete. They can also be used for landscaping purposes, such as creating gardens or parklands.
