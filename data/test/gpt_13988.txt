Natural, Everyday Degradation is the second studio album by American post-hardcore band Chiodos. It was released on September 25, 2010, through Rise Records.  The album was produced by former Deftones and current Scar Symmetry producer Frank Delgado. The album's lyrical themes deal with personal struggles and the general deterioration of society. To promote the album, the band embarked on a headlining tour with labelmates Saosin and Pianos Become the Teeth in early 2011.   The album received generally positive reviews from critics, who praised the band's return to their heavier sound and lyrical depth. It debuted at number nine on the Billboard 200 chart, selling over 22,000 copies in its first week.
