Born to Defence  is a 1986 Indian Bollywood film directed by Ram Gopal Varma. The film stars Naseeruddin Shah and Madhuri Dixit in the lead roles.  The story is set in the backdrop of the Indo-Pakistani War of 1971. Raaj Kumar (Naseeruddin Shah) is a young man who has just graduated from college and wants to join the Indian Army to fight for his country. He is refused entry on the grounds that he is not tall enough, but his determination and strong will help him overcome all obstacles and eventually become a part of the army. During the war, Raaj Kumar helps protect a group of women and children from being captured by the Pakistani army. For his bravery, Raaj Kumar is awarded the Indian Military Medal (IMM). Years later, Raaj Kumar is now a colonel and has retired from the army. He lives a quiet life with his wife (Madhuri Dixit) and daughter until one day, they receive a call from the Indian government asking them to come back to serve their country once again. Raaj Kumar returns to duty as commander of a unit of soldiers stationed in Kashmir. The unit is tasked with protecting civilians from terrorist attacks orchestrated by Pakistan. While trying to protect civilians, Raaj Kumar also has to face his old enemy, Captain Khan (Rishi Kapoor), who still wants revenge for what Raaj Kumar did to him during the war years.
