Sławomir Jan Dębski (born 23 May 1971) is a Polish politician who has been the President of the Republic of Poland since 6 August 2015. Previously, he served as Minister of Justice from 2005 to 2007, Minister of Regional Development from 2007 to 2010 and Minister of Education from 2010 to 2015.  Dębski was born on 23 May 1971 in the city of Poznań in western Poland. He completed his secondary education at a Mercy High School in Poznań and subsequently studied law at the University of Warsaw. Dębski is a member of the Polish Academy of Sciences and Humanities.  Dębski has been a member of the Law and Justice party since its foundation in 2001. He served as deputy minister of justice from 2005 to 2007, minister of regional development from 2007 to 2010 and minister of education from 2010 to 2015. In that role, he was responsible for the reform of Poland's educational system, which saw high school attendance rates increase from 83% to 91%.   On 6 August 2015, Dębski was elected as President of Poland by the Sejm. He succeeded Andrzej Duda, who had been elected in 2013.
