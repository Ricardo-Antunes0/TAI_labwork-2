Raymond St. John is an English songwriter, musician and record producer. He has been a member of the rock band Jethro Tull since 1968, and has been the band's principal songwriter and frontman since then.  Ray St. John was born in London, England in 1948. He began his musical career as a member of the folk-rock band Humble Pie, which he formed with Ian Anderson, Mick Ralphs and John Lodge in 1967. After leaving Humble Pie in 1972, St. John formed Jethro Tull with bassist and fellow Humble Pie alumni Martin Barre and drummer Ian Anderson. Jethro Tull enjoyed international success throughout the 1970s and 1980s, with their albums Folk Denied, Thick as a Brick, Stand Up Tall and Aqualung achieving widespread critical acclaim. In 1998, St. John retired from Jethro Tull to spend more time with his family.  Since retiring from Jethro Tull, St. John has continued to write songs and produce records for other artists. His most recent album is the folk-rock album The Raven That Refused to Sing (2012).
