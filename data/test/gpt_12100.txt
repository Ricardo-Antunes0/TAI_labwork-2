Dr. Pratapsinh Ganpatrao Jadhav is an Indian medical doctor and scientist who has made significant contributions to the understanding of tuberculosis and its treatment. He is also the founder-director of the Indian Institute of Chest Diseases, Pune.  Born on 17 March 1951 in a Marathi-speaking family in Satara district, Maharashtra, Jadhav completed his undergraduate studies in medicine from the University of Bombay in 1974 and subsequently completed his postgraduate studies at the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine, obtaining a MD in 1978. He then worked as a junior doctor at the Yerwada General Hospital in Mumbai before joining the National Tuberculosis Control Programme as an assistant director in 1983.  Jadhav's work on tuberculosis has been highly influential, particularly his identification of Mycobacterium bovis as the causative agent of tuberculosis. His work on this topic has led to the development of new treatments for tuberculosis, including the use of isoniazid and rifampicin. Jadhav is also responsible for establishing the Indian Institute of Chest Diseases, Pune, which is now one of India's leading centres for research into chest diseases.  Jadhav has been awarded several prestigious awards, including the Padma Bhushan (1984), the Ramanujan Medal (1986), and the IEEE Medal (1991). He was also elected as a fellow of both the Indian Academy of Sciences and the Royal Society of London in 2003.
