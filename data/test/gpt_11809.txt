He studied law with his father and was admitted to the bar in 1823. He began practice in Plymouth, Massachusetts.  Borden served as a member of the Massachusetts House of Representatives from 1830 to 1832 and as a member of the Massachusetts Senate from 1832 to 1834. He was appointed as Secretary of the Commonwealth in 1834, serving until 1841. Borden was elected Governor of Massachusetts in 1840 and served from 1840 to 1844. During his term, Borden promoted education reform, including the establishment of state universities. Borden returned to the practice of law after leaving the governorship and died in Plymouth, Massachusetts, on September 19, 1864. He was buried in Plymouth Cemetery.
