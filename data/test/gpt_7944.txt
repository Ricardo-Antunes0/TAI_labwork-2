Hill-Murray School is a coeducational private Catholic preparatory school in Saint Paul, Minnesota, United States. It enrolls students in grades 9-12 and has a current student body of 1,500 students.  Hill-Murray School was founded in 1891 by the Sisters of Mercy and is named after the school's first headmistress, Mother Mary Augusta McNeill Hill. The school is a member of the Upper Great Lakes Association of Independent Schools and the National Association of Independent Schools.  Hill-Murray has a history of producing successful leaders and professionals. Alumni include 34 Rhodes Scholars, 49 Marshall Scholars, 55 Fulbright Scholars, 39 astronauts (including both American and Russian astronauts), and 29 U.S. Senators.
