Burl Stevens Watson Sr. (7 November 1893 – 20 October 1988) was an American mathematician and statistician known for his work in combinatorics, probability theory, and statistics. He served as the president of the Institute for Mathematical Statistics (IMS) from 1951 to 1958.  Watson was born in 1893 in Worcester, Massachusetts, United States. He graduated from Harvard University in 1916 with a Bachelor of Arts degree in mathematics. He then attended the University of Göttingen, where he received his Doctor of Science degree in 1918. After returning to the US, he started working at Union College as an instructor in mathematics, but soon moved to Columbia University, where he became a full professor in 1936. He served as the president of IMS from 1951 to 1958. Watson died on 20 October 1988 at the age of 96.
