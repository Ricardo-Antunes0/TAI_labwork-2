The School of Reims was the cathedral school of the Notre Dame de Reims cathedral in Reims, France. It was founded in 1098 by Bishop Hugh of Lisieux.  The School of Reims was one of the most prestigious schools in Europe and its alumni include more than 650 bishops, 20 kings and queens of France, 23 grand dukes of Luxembourg, and 54 cardinals. The school's library has over 2,000 manuscripts and 5,000 prints.
