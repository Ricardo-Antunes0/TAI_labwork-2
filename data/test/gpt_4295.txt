Mount Timpanogos, often referred to as Timp, is a large peak in the Wasatch Range of Utah, United States. With an elevation of 12,801 feet (3,886 m), it is the fifth-highest peak in the state. The mountain is located within the Mount Timpanogos National Forest and is managed by the United States Forest Service.  The mountain is composed of Precambrian granite and is a popular destination for hikers, climbers, and skiers. It has been sighted from many parts of the world and has been the backdrop for many movies and television shows. Mount Timpanogos is also a popular destination for BASE jumping.
