Robin Pogrebin (pron. ro-bin POG-re-bin, born May 5, 1951) is an American journalist and author who has written extensively about the arts.  Pogrebin was born in New York City, the son of actress and playwright Paula Vogel and actor and playwright Robin Pogrebin. He received his B.A. from Wesleyan University in 1973, and his M.F.A. from Columbia University in 1976.  Pogrebin has been a contributing writer to "The New Yorker" since 1978, and has written books on the theater, dance, opera, and classical music. He served as theater critic for "The New York Times" from 1984 to 1995, and as arts editor of "The New York Review of Books" from 1995 to 2003. He currently writes a column for "The Daily Beast".  Pogrebin has been married to actress Leslie Cheung since 1986. They have two children.
