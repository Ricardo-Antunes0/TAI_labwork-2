Michael Vincent Curran (born April 14, 1944) is a retired American professional basketball player who played in the National Basketball Association (NBA) for the Philadelphia 76ers, Baltimore Bullets, and Boston Celtics. He was selected by the Boston Celtics in the first round of the 1966 NBA draft.  Curran played college basketball for the University of Notre Dame. He was a two-time All-American and led Notre Dame to the 1965 NCAA Final Four.  Curran was drafted in the first round by the Philadelphia 76ers. He played with them from 1966 to 1969, and then joined the Baltimore Bullets in 1970. He won an NBA championship with Boston in 1974. Curran finished his career with the Utah Stars of the American Basketball Association (ABA) in 1975.  Curran was named to both the NBA All-Defensive First Team and Second Team, and he was also selected to play in three consecutive All-Star Games, from 1967 to 1969. He was inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame in 1980.
