Lisa Alexander (born September 22, 1968) is an American actress and comedian.  Alexander was born in Los Angeles, California, to Barbara (n√©e Waldman), a costume designer, and Gerald Alexander, an advertising executive. She has a sister, Tish (born 1967), and a brother, Jed (born 1971). She attended the Buckley School in Sherman Oaks, California.  After graduating from high school in 1986, Alexander studied drama at the University of California, Berkeley. She graduated from UC Berkeley with a B.A. degree in 1990.  Alexander's first notable role was as the title character in the short-lived sitcom "Lisa", which aired on ABC from September 26 to November 7, 1990. Prior to that show's premiere, Alexander made her feature film debut in the 1989 comedy film "The Accidental Tourist".   Alexander had recurring roles on the hit sitcoms "Friends" (1994-2004), playing Monica Geller's neurotic best friend Phoebe Buffay; and "The Office" (2005-2013), playing Pam Beesly, who became one of Michael Scott's main co-workers and eventual girlfriend. In addition to her work on television and film, Alexander has made appearances on Broadway in productions of "The Little Foxes" (1998) and "The Elephant Man" (2000).   Alexander currently stars as Paula Proctor in the Amazon Prime series "Transparent", which began airing on August 29, 2015.
