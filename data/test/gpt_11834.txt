J. Teddy Garces (born September 12, 1974) is an American actor and comedian.  Garces was born in Lafayette, Louisiana. He is of Mexican and Puerto Rican descent. He is the son of actress and singer Lola Garces and comedian and actor Fernando Garces. Garces has two sisters, actress Jacqueline Garces and singer/songwriter Lila Garces.  Garces began his career as a stand-up comedian in the early 1990s, before appearing in several minor roles on television. He gained wider recognition for his portrayal of Miguel on the ABC sitcom "My Wife and Kids" from 2003 to 2006. In 2007, he starred in the short-lived ABC comedy series "The Middle", for which he was nominated for a Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Comedy Series. Since 2009, he has starred as Dr. Christopher Chance on the CBS medical drama "Hawaii Five-0".  Garces married actress Carmen Ejogo in December 2006; they have one daughter.
