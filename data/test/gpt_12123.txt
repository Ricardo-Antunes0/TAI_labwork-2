Daniel Radosh (born 23 March 1969) is an American historian and expert on the history of the Soviet Union and the Cold War. He is the author of several books on the topic, including The Russian Revolution: A History (2007), which was selected as a Best Book of the Year by The New York Times and The Washington Post, and The Cold War: A History (2011).  Radosh was born in 1969 in Massachusetts. He received his bachelor's degree from Franklin & Marshall College in 1991 and his master's degree from Yale University in 1993. He completed his doctorate at Harvard University in 2000.  Radosh has been a professor at Yale since 1997, where he is the Sterling Professor of History. He has also been a visiting professor at Princeton University, Columbia University, and the London School of Economics.  Radosh has written for publications including The New York Review of Books, The New Republic, Slate, and The Atlantic. He is a regular contributor to The Nation, where he has also served as senior editor for opinion. In 2013 he was elected a fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.
