The Ryde police station is a heritage-listed former police station at 172-174 Military Road, Ryde, City of Sydney, New South Wales, Australia. It was designed by NSW Government Architect Walter Liberty Vernon and built in 1936. It is also known as Ryde Police Station and Ryde Watch House. The property is owned by the City of Sydney. It was added to the New South Wales State Heritage Register on 2 April 1999. It was designed by NSW Government Architect Walter Liberty Vernon and built in 1936. It is also known as Ryde Police Station and Ryde Watch House. The property is owned by the City of Sydney. It was added to the New South Wales State Heritage Register on 2 April 1999.  TheRyde Police Station was designed by Walter Liberty Vernon and built in 1936 as a Works Progress Administration project. The site had been used as a police station since 1885, when the first police barracks were erected on the present site. In 1938-39, a new substation was constructed to house the expanding police force on Military Road and in 1963-64 an annexe to the main building was erected to house the morgue and evidence rooms.
