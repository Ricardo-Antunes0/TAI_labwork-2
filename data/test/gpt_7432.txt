Andre Nolan Dawson (born July 10, 1954), nicknamed "The Hawk," is a former American professional baseball outfielder who played in Major League Baseball (MLB) for the Philadelphia Phillies, Chicago Cubs, Montreal Expos, and the Minnesota Twins. He was a nine-time All-Star and won three Gold Glove Awards. Dawson also played for the Oakland Athletics, Baltimore Orioles, and Toronto Blue Jays.  Dawson was born in Harlem, New York City. He attended Don Bosco High School in Brooklyn and played college baseball at the University of Maryland. Dawson was drafted by the Philadelphia Phillies in the third round of the 1975 Major League Baseball Draft. He made his MLB debut with the Phillies in 1977. After playing for Philadelphia and the Cubs, he joined the Montreal Expos in 1991. Dawson played for the Twins from 1992 to 1995 before returning to Oakland in 1996. He retired after playing for the Blue Jays in 1997. Dawson finished his career with a record of 3,060 hits, 426 home runs, 1,406 RBIs, and a .302 batting average.  Dawson has been involved with numerous humanitarian projects throughout his career, including work with Operation Smile and The Andre Dawson Foundation for Cancer Research. He has also been involved with various anti-violence efforts. In 2001, Dawson was named an MLB Commissioner's Ambassador for Youth Development and Community Affairs.  Dawson was diagnosed with liver cancer in March 2015; he underwent surgery to remove a tumor on March 25 and began chemotherapy treatment on April 5
