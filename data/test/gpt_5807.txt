Samuel Atkinson Waterston (born November 15, 1940) is an American actor, director and producer. He has been nominated for eight Academy Awards, including two for Best Actor, for roles in The Silence of the Lambs and The Town.  Born in New York City, Waterston attended the Juilliard School in New York City before making his stage debut in the 1967 production of A View from the Bridge. He first came to prominence as a supporting actor in films such as The Great Santini (1979), Ordinary People (1980), and Arthur Penn's The Crossing Guard (1982). His breakthrough role came in 1984 playing Hannibal Lecter in Jonathan Demme's Academy Award-winning film The Silence of the Lambs. In 1988, he directed and starred in the film Hearts of Darkness, based on Joseph Conrad's novel of the same name. For his performance as Tom Hagen in the 1992 film version of Arthur Miller's play The Crucible, Waterston received an Academy Award nomination for Best Actor.  Since then, Waterston has appeared in a wide range of films, including Unforgiven (1992), Fargo (1996), The Homecoming (1997), You Can't Take It With You (1938) and Big Little Lies (2017). In addition to acting and directing, he has produced several films, including Lost in Translation (2003), Babel (2006), Night Moves (2014) and Sam Waterston's Margin Call (2011).
