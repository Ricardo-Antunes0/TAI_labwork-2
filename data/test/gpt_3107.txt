Moyúa, or Elliptic Square, is a public artwork by Mexican artist José Clemente Orozco, located in the courtyard of the Palacio de Bellas Artes in Mexico City. The sculpture is made up of three tiers of ellipses, each measuring approximately 25 feet by 16 feet.  The sculpture was inaugurated on November 11, 1976. It is one of Orozco's most famous sculptures, and has been featured in several books and articles about his work.
