4-H is a U.S.-based network of youth organizations that promote human and animal health. 4-H programs are open to youths aged 7 to 18, and offer a variety of activities, including farming, home economics, conservation, recreation, and leadership.  4-H programs provide youths with opportunities to learn about their environment, develop skills in hands-on activities, and connect with others who share their interests. These programs also help prepare youths for future careers and life experiences.  4-H is a nonprofit organization with more than 1 million members in the United States. 4-H programs are offered in more than 50 counties nationwide, and there are nearly 2,000 clubs across the country.
