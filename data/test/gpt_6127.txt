War/Dance is a 2007 American documentary film about the art of traditional African dance and its connection to war. The film was directed by Anthony King and produced by Laurie David.  The film is about the afro-brazilian martial art capoeira and its connection to African slave dances. The film features interviews with choreographers, historians, and practitioners of capoeira, as well as dramatic reenactments of traditional capoeira battles.  The film has been praised for its beautiful cinematography, its historical context, and the strength of its emotional connection to its subject matter. It has won several awards, including an Academy Award for Best Documentary Feature in 2008.
