Cossacks  is a sculpture composition on the surface of the pond in the Gardens of Versailles. It is made of bronze and is 63.5 cm high, 58 cm wide and 10 cm deep. The composition is divided into three panels, the first of which is in the form of a horseshoe. The second panel depicts a group of riders on horseback, while the third panel shows a group of Cossacks on foot.   The work was created by the French sculptor Jean-Baptiste Pigalle in 1785 and was originally located in the gardens' labyrinthine Pond Garden. It was moved to its current location in 1827.
