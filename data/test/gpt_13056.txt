Thomas Edward Hulce (born December 6, 1953) is an American actor and musician. He is known for his roles as Michael Scott in the NBC sitcom The Office, as Kevin Spacey's boss Bobby Cannavale in the HBO series Boardwalk Empire, and as Dr. John Watson in the BBC's Sherlock.  Hulce was born in New York City, the son of Margaret (née Crowley), a teacher, and Edward Hulce, a stockbroker. He has two sisters, actress Connie Hulce and author Susan Hulce. After attending Fiorello H. LaGuardia High School of Music & Art and Performing Arts in New York City, he studied at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art in London, graduating in 1975.  Hulce's first role was in the 1977 British television film Ask a Policeman. He made his Broadway debut in 1978 in Neil Simon's Play It Again, Sam. He also appeared on Broadway in Simon's The Odd Couple (1979), Arthur Miller's The Crucible (1980), and William Inge's Picnic (1982).  Hulce began his television career on the soap opera Another World in 1979. In 1980, he starred opposite Andy Kaufman in the short-lived comedy series Taxi. Hulce played Deputy Sheriff Roy DeSoto on the CBS series Matlock from 1986 to 1991.
