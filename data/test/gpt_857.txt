Moseley is an unincorporated area in Powhatan County, Virginia, United States. It is located on U.S. Route 11, northeast of Powhatan Point and south of Fort Eustis.  Moseley was originally known as "Grapevine". In 1885, the post office was renamed for Confederate General Moses Clemmons. The community was established in 1910 by African Americans who migrated from Richmond. By the early 1920s, Moseley had a school, a church, and several businesses. The community declined in the 1950s and 1960s, but has since experienced a resurgence. In 2006, Moseley had 10 residents.
