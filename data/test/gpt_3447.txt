Konstantinos Papadakis (; born October 2, 1972) is a Greek pianist.  Konstantinos Papadakis was born in Athens, Greece on October 2, 1972. He began his musical studies at the age of four with his mother and then continued his studies at the Athens Conservatoire, where he graduated with honours in 1995. In the same year he won first prize at the prestigious International Tchaikovsky Competition in Moscow.  Papadakis has appeared as a soloist with many of the world's leading orchestras, including the Berlin Philharmonic, the Chicago Symphony Orchestra, the Cleveland Orchestra, the Moscow Symphony Orchestra, the Royal Concertgebouw Orchestra Amsterdam, and the Sydney Symphony Orchestra. He has also performed with many of today's leading conductors, including Daniel Barenboim, Riccardo Chailly, Antonio Pappano, Gustavo Dudamel, and Valery Gergiev.  Konstantinos Papadakis has released several critically acclaimed albums of classical music, including The Piano Quintet (2002), The Piano Sonatas (2004), and The Piano Concerto (2009). In 2009 he was awarded an honorary doctorate by the University of Sydney.
