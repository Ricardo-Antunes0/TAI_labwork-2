The Avco World Trophy, also known as the Shell World Cup, is a biennial international motorsport event for Formula One racing cars and sports cars. The event is currently held at the Circuit of the Americas in Austin, Texas, United States.  The first World Trophy was held at Silverstone in 1971 and was won by Ferrari. The event moved to Brands Hatch in 1974 and then to Monza in 1978. It returned to Silverstone in 1984 and has been held at the Circuit of the Americas since 1995. The World Trophy has been jointly organised by the Automobile Club de l'Ouest (ACO) and the Royal Automobile Club (RAC) since 2003. In 2017, it was announced that the World Trophy would be discontinued after the 2018 edition.
