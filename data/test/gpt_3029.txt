Dinas Cromlech or Dinas y Gromlech is a large prehistoric megalithic monument in southwestern Wales, United Kingdom. It is located near the village of Llansantffraid-ym-Mochnant and within the boundaries of the Brecon Beacons National Park.  Dinas Cromlech consists of a series of aligned stone circles, each measuring up to 20 metres in diameter and up to 3 metres high. The monument was built between 4000 and 3000 BC, and may have been used for religious ceremonies or as a place of burial. Some of the stones at Dinas Cromlech have been dated to between 4000 and 3000 BC, making it one of the oldest megalithic sites in Europe.  The site is protected under UK law as a scheduled monument.
