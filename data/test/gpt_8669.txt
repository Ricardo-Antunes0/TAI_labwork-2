Kfar Fila , is a town in the northern West Bank, located 25 kilometers south of Nablus. According to the Palestinian Central Bureau of Statistics, it had a population of 10,988 in 2007.  Kfar Fila is known locally for its olive trees, and has been designated as an agricultural zone. It is also known for its grapes, which are used to produce wine. The town has a number of mosques and churches.  The town is home to the Fila family, who have played an important role in Palestinian politics.
