Francis Zavier Ngannou (born 5 September 1986) is a Cameroonian professional boxer who has held the IBF world heavyweight title since 2016 and the WBA world heavyweight title since 2018.  Ngannou was born in Yaoundé, Cameroon and raised in a poor neighbourhood. After dropping out of school, he started working as a bouncer and bodyguard. He began boxing at the age of 19, and won regional championships in his native Cameroon. In November 2012, Ngannou traveled to France to take part in a boxing competition and won the bronze medal at the World Championships.  Ngannou made his professional debut in October 2013, and quickly compiled a record of 20–0 with 18 knockouts. He won the IBF world heavyweight title in 2016 with a unanimous decision over Ruslan Chagaev. Ngannou successfully defended his title against Deontay Wilder in April 2017, and against Tyson Fury in December 2017.   In 2018, Ngannou successfully defended his WBA world heavyweight title against Anthony Joshua by TKO in the 11th round.
