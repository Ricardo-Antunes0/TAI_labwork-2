Major General Howard Samuel McGee (October 4, 1898 – November 11, 1966) was an American Army officer and a recipient of the United States military's highest decoration, the Medal of Honor, for his actions in World War II.  Howard Samuel McGee was born on October 4, 1898 in Albany, Oregon. He attended the University of Oregon and enlisted in the U.S. Army in 1917. He served in World War I and World War II, receiving the Medal of Honor for his actions in World War II. After the war, McGee served as a military attaché to Argentina and Japan. He retired from the Army in 1959 and died on November 11, 1966.
