The New River Coalfield is located in the Appalachian Mountains of southern West Virginia and northern Kentucky. The coal reserves are estimated at 360 million short tons. The coalfield stretches for 145 miles, from the New River in eastern Kentucky to the Ohio River in western West Virginia.  The New River Coalfield is one of the oldest coal deposits in the eastern United States. The first coal was mined here in 1775. The coalfield is home to eight coal companies, which employ more than 2,500 people.
