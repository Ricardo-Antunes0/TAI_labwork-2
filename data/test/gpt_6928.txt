Rosie Goldsmith is a journalist specializing in investigative reporting. She has worked for the Guardian, the Independent, and Vice News, where she was the first female bureau chief in their history.  Goldsmith has covered a range of stories, from exposing government corruption in Cambodia to investigating the use of child soldiers in Africa. She is also known for her work on the Panama Papers, which revealed the offshore holdings of politicians and celebrities around the world.  Goldsmith was born in London to Jewish parents. She attended university in the United States, where she studied journalism at New York University. After returning to England, she worked as a reporter for the Guardian before joining Vice News as their first bureau chief in 2016.
