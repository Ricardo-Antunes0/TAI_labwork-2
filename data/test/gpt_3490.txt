Gladys Maria Knight (born May 28, 1944), known professionally as Gladys Knight, is an American singer, songwriter, and actress. She is a five-time Grammy Award winner and has sold over 150 million records worldwide. Knight has been honored with five American Music Awards, four Soul Train Awards, two NAACP Image Awards, and two Kennedy Center Honors.  Knight began singing gospel at the age of 12 and joined the Pentecostal Church of God in Christ as a teenager. After signing with the soul record label Verve in 1969, Knight became a prominent R&B performer of the 1970s, achieving commercial success with her albums I've Got to Get Out of This Place (1971), Spirit in the Dark (1973), Back to Basics (1975), and Lady Sings the Blues (1976). In 1988, she released her first album of new material in over 10 years, entitled Heart Full of Soul. The album achieved platinum sales worldwide and was later inducted into the Grammy Hall of Fame. Knight continued to tour throughout the 1990s and 2000s, making her last live performance in December 2012.  Since her retirement from touring in 2012, Knight has focused on philanthropic work and she has been particularly devoted to cancer research. She has received several honorary doctorates, including one from Yale University in 2013. As of 2016, Knight continues to make occasional public appearances and has released two new albums since her retirement from touring.
