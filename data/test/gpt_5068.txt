Milne Hollow is a 50-hectare (120-acre) valley in the North Island of New Zealand. It is located in the Waikato Region, about 30 kilometres north-east of Hamilton.  The valley is named after John Milne, who was the first European settler in the area. The valley is a popular tourist destination due to its natural beauty and its proximity to Hamilton. The valley is home to a number of small villages, including Clyde, Rangiaowhia, and Makarora. The valley is also known for its dairy farming industry.
