HMS Conflict was the lead ship of the first British armoured cruiser class. She was laid down in 1892, launched in 1894 and completed in 1896. After serving in the Mediterranean Fleet she was transferred to the Channel Fleet in 1899. In 1905 she was transferred to the China Station and was present at the Battle of Tsushima. She was decommissioned in 1907 and sold for scrap in 1908.  HMS Conflict was an armoured cruiser of the first British armoured cruiser class. She was laid down in 1892 at the yards of Barclay Curle & Co, Glasgow, Scotland, launched on 27 September 1894, and completed on 30 September 1896 at Devonport Dockyard.  Commissioned into the Mediterranean Fleet on 10 October 1899, she was transferred to the Channel Fleet on 3 April 1905. In 1907 she was decommissioned and sold for scrap on 17 September 1908.
