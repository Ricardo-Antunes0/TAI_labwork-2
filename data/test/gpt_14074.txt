Anders Börje Salming (; born 17 April 1942) is a retired Swedish ice hockey player who played for the Detroit Red Wings, Toronto Maple Leafs, and Philadelphia Flyers in the National Hockey League (NHL). He was the first European-born player to win the Stanley Cup, which he accomplished with the Red Wings in 1979. He was also named the NHL's Most Valuable Player (MVP) twice, in 1977 and 1978. Salming was inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame in 1991.  Born in Helsingborg, Sweden, Salming played for his hometown team Härnösand IF before being drafted by the Detroit Red Wings in the first round of the 1961 NHL Amateur Draft. He made his NHL debut with the Red Wings during the 1965–66 season and became a regular player over the next few seasons, helping Detroit to their first Stanley Cup championship in 1967. He also played for Toronto Maple Leafs and Philadelphia Flyers before retiring in 1980.  Salming is one of only six players to have won both the Hart Memorial Trophy as NHL MVP and the Stanley Cup as league champion. He is also one of only four players to have won both awards twice. Salming was inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame in 1991. In 2006, he was named one of the 100 Greatest NHL Players of all time by "The Hockey News".
