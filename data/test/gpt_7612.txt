Thomas Commuck (January 18, 1804 – November 20, 1854) was a United States Representative from New York.  Born in the town of Canajoharie, Montgomery County, New York, Commuck attended the common schools. He engaged in agricultural pursuits and also in milling. He was appointed postmaster of Canajoharie in 1827 and served until 1830.  Commuck was elected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-third Congress (March 4, 1837 – March 3, 1839). He was not a candidate for reelection in 1838 to the Twenty-fourth Congress.  He served as clerk of Montgomery County from 1840 to 1853. He then resided in Canajoharie until his death there on November 20, 1854. He was interred in the Old Dutch Reformed Church Cemetery.
