Tony Langston was best known as"a tastemaker," who was credited with helping to bring hip hop music into the mainstream. Langston was also an accomplished musician, and he released several albums throughout his career.  Langston was born in Harlem, in New York City, on February 16, 1965. He started out as a drummer, playing in local groups before becoming a DJ and producer. In the early 1990s, Langston became known as the "Tastemaker" for his work with artists like Run-DMC, Beastie Boys, and LL Cool J. Langston helped to bring hip hop music into the mainstream, and his productions often reflected this shift in popular culture. He also released several albums of his own throughout his career. Langston died on October 14, 2011, at the age of 47 from a heart attack.
