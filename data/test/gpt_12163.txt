Klosterneuburg Abbey or Monastery  is a Benedictine monastery in Klosterneuburg, Lower Austria, southeast of Vienna. It is the largest Benedictine monastery in Austria, and one of the largest in Europe.  The monastery was founded in 976 by Saint Otto I, Holy Roman Emperor and Queen Adelaide of Italy as a Carthusian monastery. The abbey grew rapidly and became one of the most influential monasteries in the Holy Roman Empire. It was dissolved during the Reformation, but was restored by Emperor Joseph II in 1783. The monastery has been continuously inhabited since then.  The Klosterneuburg Monastery is one of Austria's most important tourist attractions. It is home to the famous Romanesque Altar of Saint Remigius from 1140 and a number of other outstanding artworks.
