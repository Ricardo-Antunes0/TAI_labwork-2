Anna "Anno" O'Rahilly (née Rahilly) (17 February 1876 – 10 September 1943) was an Irish neurologist who was a professor of psychiatry at the University of Dublin.  Born in County Clare, Rahilly studied medicine at Trinity College, Dublin, graduating with a Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Science in 1901. She then undertook postgraduate studies at the University of Edinburgh and the University of Bern, before returning to Dublin to take up a lectureship in psychiatry in 1904. Rahilly remained in this position for the rest of her career, becoming a full professor in 1932. She was also the president of the Royal College of Psychiatrists from 1937 to 1939.  Rahilly is best known for her work on schizophrenia, which she studied extensively during her career. She also made significant contributions to neuropsychiatry and neurology, publishing over 150 papers and books on these topics. She died in Dublin aged 70 years.
