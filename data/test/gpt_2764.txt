Baron Greville, of Clonyn, County Westmeath, was an Irish peer and politician.  Baron Greville was the second son of the Reverend George Greville, Dean of Cashel. He was educated at Trinity College, Dublin, and became a Fellow of Trinity in 1876. He succeeded his father in 1877.  Greville was elected to the House of Commons for Westmeath in 1882, a seat he held until 1886. He served as Under-Secretary for Ireland in the first Gladstone ministry from 1885 to 1886, and as Colonial Secretary in the second Gladstone ministry from 1886 to 1892. He was made a Knight Bachelor in 1889.  Greville also served as Lord Lieutenant of Westmeath from 1893 to 1898, and as President of the Royal Irish Academy from 1898 to 1902. He died at his home, Clonyn, near Mullingar, County Westmeath, on 17 January 1902.
