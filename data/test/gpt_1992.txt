Zaynaddīn Ibn al-ʿAjamī, also known as ʿAbdalmalik al-ʿAjamī, was an Arabic poet and historian. He was born in Damascus around 860 CE and died there in 932 CE.  Zaynaddīn Ibn al-ʿAjamī is best known as a historian and poet. He wrote a number of works on a variety of topics, including history, biography, geography, and mythology. His poetry is characterized by its delicate and evocative language.  Zaynaddīn Ibn al-ʿAjamī was also a scholar of Islamic theology. He was one of the leading proponents of the Sufism school of thought, which emphasized the mystical aspects of Islam. Zaynaddīn Ibn al-ʿAjamī is credited with introducing the concept of the marifa, or Perfect Man, into Islamic theology.
