Cope India Exercise are a series of bilateral exercises between India and NATO that began in 2007. The drills are held every two years and are aimed at enhancing cooperation and interoperability between the two organisations.  Since its establishment in 1947, India has been an active member of the United Nations and has played a vital role in promoting international peace and security. The country is also a member of the Commonwealth of Nations, the South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation, the Shanghai Cooperation Organisation, and the World Trade Organization. India is also a member of the G-20 major economies and the BRICS grouping of emerging economies.  India is a nuclear-armed state and is ranked as the sixth-largest defence exporter in the world. The Indian military has undergone significant modernization efforts over the past decade, with plans to increase its troop strength to 1.3 million by 2021. In 2016, India announced plans to purchase 36 Rafale fighter jets from France at a cost of $8 billion.
