Satara  (ISO: Sātārā) is a city in the Indian state of Maharashtra. It is the administrative headquarters of the Satara district and also serves as the capital of the Marathwada region. The city is located on the banks of the Tapi River, about west of Mumbai.  Satara was originally known as Sapta-Rājya (Seven Rājas). It was founded by the Raja Shahu in 1641 as a fortified town to protect his territory from attacks by the Mughals. The city was renamed Satara in 1729 after his son and successor, Pratap Singh.  Satara is home to many historical temples, including the Bhavani Temple, Jain Temple and Hanuman Temple. The city is also popular for its handloom fabrics, which are used in regional decoration and clothing. The city has a number of colleges and universities, including Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Marathwada University, DY Patil College of Engineering, Wadia Institute of Technology and Design, and Deccan College of Arts and Commerce.
