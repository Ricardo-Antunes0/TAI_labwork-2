Leonard "Lenny" Lipton (born May 18, 1940) is an American comedian, actor, writer, producer and television personality.  Lipton was born in Brooklyn, New York to Jewish parents. He began his career as a stand-up comedian in the early 1960s and made his first appearance on television in 1965. In the 1970s and 1980s, Lipton became known for his work on television variety shows, including The Tonight Show Starring Johnny Carson and The David Letterman Show. In the 1990s, he began appearing in films again and has since starred in several successful films, including The Birdcage (1996), What Women Want (2000), and Big Daddy (2003).  Lipton has also been involved in writing and producing television series, including The Ellen DeGeneres Show and Curb Your Enthusiasm. He was a judge on the reality show America's Got Talent from 2011 to 2015. Lipton is currently a correspondent on the talk show The Ellen DeGeneres Show.
