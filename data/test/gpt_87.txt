Stokesay Castle is one of the finest examples of a medieval Scottish castle. It is located in the village of Stokesay, on the north coast of Scotland. The castle was originally built by Alexander II of Scotland in 1275, and was later expanded by his son, David I of Scotland. The castle has been used as a residence by the Scottish monarchs and their consorts since the 14th century.  Today, Stokesay Castle is a popular tourist destination, and is open to the public for tours. The castle is also used for functions such as weddings and conferences. The interior of the castle has been decorated in a number of different ways, including with murals and furnishings from the 16th century.
