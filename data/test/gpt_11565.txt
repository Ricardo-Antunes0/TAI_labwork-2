The Access Linux Platform (ALP) is a x86-64 Linux distribution made for devices with limited resources. It is designed to provide a platform for developers and system administrators to build, test and deploy applications on small, low-power devices.  The ALP uses a modular architecture that allows for easy installation, configuration and updates of the underlying software. It includes a variety of lightweight tools and utilities that make it easy to develop, deploy and use applications on small devices. The ALP also includes a wide range of pre-installed software to make it easy to get started with computing on small devices.  The ALP is free and open source software released under the GNU General Public License (GPL).
