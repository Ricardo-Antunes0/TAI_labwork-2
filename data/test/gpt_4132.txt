Anthony 'Tony' David Owen is an economist and the current Governor of the Bank of England. Owen started his career at HSBC in 1988 and was appointed deputy governor of the Bank of England in 2006. He was named as the new governor in July 2013.  Anthony David Owen was born in 1958 in Cardiff, Wales. He studied at the University of Cambridge, where he received a B.A. in economics in 1980 and an M.A. in 1982. Owen then worked for HSBC for eighteen years, where he became deputy governor in 2006 and governor in 2013. In this role, he has played a leading role in the bank's response to the global financial crisis, notably as chairman of the Financial Stability Board from 2009 to 2012.  Owen has also been a visiting professor at universities around the world, including Harvard University, the London School of Economics, and Oxford University. He was knighted in the 2013 New Year Honours for services to banking and economic policymaking.
