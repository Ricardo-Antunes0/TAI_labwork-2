The third series of the British spy thriller television series Spooks begins airing on BBC One on 10 September 2007 and concludes on 23 December 2007. The series was produced by Kudos Film and Television, the production company created by the creators of the original television series Midsomer Murders, which is based on the novels of the same name by Agatha Christie.  The series features a new cast and focuses on a new team of MI5 officers who are investigating a series of terrorist attacks in London. The episodes are written by Jed Mercurio, who wrote the first two series.  The title sequence is a montage of images from the first two series with updated titles.  The three main cast members are David Tennant as James Bond-style secret agent John Steed; Keeley Hawes as Emma Peel; and Michael Gambon as retired MI6 officer Arthur Seaton. Recurring cast members include Denis Lawson as Seaton's assistant, Roger Allam as head of MI5's counter-terrorism unit and Lucy Davis as Steed's secretary.
