The 12th Chief (or "Main") Directorate of the General Staff of the Armed Forces of the Russian Federation, also known as the "KGB Directorate", is a directorate of the General Staff responsible for military intelligence.  The 12th Chief Directorate was founded on July 1, 1992, after the dissolution of the Soviet Union and the establishment of the Russian Federation. The first deputy director was Colonel-General Anatoliy Kolesnikov, who had been deputy head of the KGB.  As of December 1, 2010, its personnel numbered 1,078 officers and 7,000 civilian employees. The 12th Chief Directorate has its headquarters in Moscow and regional branches throughout Russia.
