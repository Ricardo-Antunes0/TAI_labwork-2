Jean Baptiste Marius Augustin Challamel (March 18, 1815 – September 25, 1885) was a French painter.  He was born in Lyon, France, the son of Jean Baptiste Challamel and Marie-Louise Duchâtel. He began his artistic training at the Lycée Louis-le-Grand in Paris, and then studied at the Royal Academy of Painting and Sculpture. He won the Prix de Rome in 1838, and traveled to Italy, where he studied under Giuseppe Bellini and Rafaela Longhi. Upon his return to France, he became a successful painter of portraits.   Challamel also exhibited his work at the Salon from 1841 to 1883. He died in Paris at the age of 73.
