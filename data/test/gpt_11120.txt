Aloysia is a genus  of flowering plants in the daisy family, Asteraceae. It is distributed mainly in North America, but also in Eurasia, Australia, and South Africa.  The genus is characterised by its small, finely divided leaves, a short stem, and large heads of white or pale blue flowers. The plants are herbaceous or subshrubby and grow from a single rootstock. The species are mostly annuals or perennials, but some are biennial or short-lived.  There are about 120 species of Aloysia, most of which are found in the eastern and central United States and in eastern Canada. A few species extend into Mexico and Central America. One species extends into South Africa.
