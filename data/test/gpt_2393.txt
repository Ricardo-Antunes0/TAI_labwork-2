He is most noted for his service as Chief of the General Staff (CGS) from 1951 to 1954 and as Chief of the Imperial General Staff (CIGS) from 1954 to 1955.  Born in 1901, May was educated at Eton and Sandhurst, before being commissioned into the Royal Engineers in 1922. He served in the Second World War, initially as a platoon commander in the 1st Battalion, Royal Scots Fusiliers. He later commanded the 5th Infantry Brigade, serving in North-West Europe and Italy. After the war he was appointed Deputy Chief of Staff at Allied Land Forces Europe, and then Chief of Staff at British Military Administration in Germany.  In 1951 May became Chief of the General Staff, a post he held until 1954 when he was made Chief of the Imperial General Staff. In this capacity he played a significant role in planning Operation Veritable, the Allied invasion of West Germany. He retired from the Army in 1955.  May died in 1985.
