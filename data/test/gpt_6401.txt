Olympique Lyonnais Féminin (; commonly referred to as OLF or simply Lyon) is a professional football club based in Lyon, France, that competes in the French Division 1 Féminine. Lyon plays its home matches at the Gerland stadium.  OLYMPIQUE LYONNAIS FÉMININ was formed on 15 May 1987 as a result of the merger of Olympique Lyonnais and AS Lille Oissel. The team made its debut in the top flight on 11 August 1988 with a 3–1 win over FC Nantes Atlantique. Lyon has finished in the top three of the Division 1 Féminine every season since then except for 2006, when they finished fourth. The team reached the UEFA Women's Champions League semi-finals in 2009 and 2013, losing to eventual champions Wolfsburg and Olympique Lyonnais respectively. In 2016, Lyon reached the final of the UEFA Women's Champions League, where they lost to Paris Saint-Germain.  Lyon has also won numerous domestic titles, including seven Coupe de France trophies (1993, 1994, 1995, 1996, 1997, 2002, 2016), four Ligue 1 titles (1995, 1997, 2002, 2016) and two Coupe de la Ligue titles (2002, 2016). The team has also won the French Cup twice (1994, 2002).
