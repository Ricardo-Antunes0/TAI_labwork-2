Accident Prone is Ira Losco's second studio album. It was released in 2002 on Barsuk Records.  Losco is a musician and composer from Brooklyn, New York. He is the founder and lead singer of the band Tapes 'n Tapes, as well as a member of the bands The Fiery Furnaces and The Gossip. Accident Prone is Losco's only album as a solo artist.  The album was recorded over a period of two years in Losco's home in Brooklyn. It features 10 tracks, all of which were written by Losco himself. Accident Prone is an atmospheric and introspective album, with Losco exploring themes of loss, love and self-doubt.  Reviews for Accident Prone have been positive. AllMusic writer Ned Raggett awarded the album 4 out of 5 stars, describing it as "a beautifully written, hypnotic album that rewards repeated listens". The Guardian's John Harris called it "a deeply affecting record, full of delicate beauty and intense emotion" and praised its "elegant melodies and understated but resonant lyrics".
