Al Ain  is a city in the United Arab Emirates. It is located in the north-west of the country on the Gulf of Oman. The city has a population of over 1 million people. Al Ain is the second largest city in the UAE after Dubai and is also one of the wealthiest cities in the world.  Al Ain was founded in 1871 by Sheikh Abdullah bin Zayed Al Nahyan, then ruler of Abu Dhabi. The settlement was initially called "Bani Yas" after Sheikh Abdullah's father, but it was renamed "Al Ain" in 1919 after Sheikh Zayed's accession to the throne of the UAE. Al Ain served briefly as the capital of the UAE in 1996 while Abu Dhabi was undergoing a major redevelopment, but Abu Dhabi regained its status as capital in 1998.  Al Ain is a trade centre, with a large number of businesses involved in oil refining and tourism. The city has several universities, including Al Ain University and Emirates University. Al Ain is also home to several archaeological sites, including the ancient towns of Madinat as-Sultan and Khor Fakkan.
