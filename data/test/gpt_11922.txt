Born in London, England, Francis appeared in stage productions before making her screen debut in 1911. She achieved prominence for her performances in the films of Alfred Hitchcock, notably in "The 39 Steps" (1935), "Notorious" (1943), and "The Lady Vanishes" (1938). She continued to work in films throughout the 1950s, often playing supporting roles. Francis died at the age of eighty-eight in 1957.
