Ēriks Ešenvalds (born January 26, 1977) is a Latvian professional ice hockey goaltender who is currently playing for the HC Sochi of the Kontinental Hockey League (KHL).  Ešenvalds was drafted in the first round, 23rd overall, by the Boston Bruins in the 1996 NHL Entry Draft. He played eight seasons in the Bruins organization, three with the Providence Bruins of the American Hockey League (AHL), and five with Boston. After his release by Boston in 2002, he spent two seasons in Europe before signing with HC CSKA Moscow of the KHL. He has since played for HC Sochi, HC Spartak Moscow, and Atlant Moscow Oblast.  Ešenvalds has represented Latvia at three World Championships, two World Junior Championships, two Winter Olympics, and one European Championship.
