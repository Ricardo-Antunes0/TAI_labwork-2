Salisbury Woodland Gardens is an open space and garden in the city of Salisbury, Wiltshire, England.  The gardens were designed by the architect Frederick Law Olmsted, Jr. between 1875 and 1879 for the 3rd Earl of Clarendon. They are situated in the southwest corner of Salisbury Cathedral close to the River Avon. The gardens are open to the public and have been described as "one of the great English landscape gardens".  The gardens occupy an area of and contain a variety of plants, including conifers and rhododendrons, as well as lawns and flower beds. The gardens are surrounded by a wall and contain a number of follies, including an octagonal greenhouse, a stone fountain, and a number of sculptures. The entrance to the gardens is located on Salisbury Plain Road.  The gardens were designated a Grade I listed building in 1954.
