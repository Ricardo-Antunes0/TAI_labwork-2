Michelle Ruff is an American voice actress who has worked in the industry since the early 1990s. She is known for her roles as Kimberly Hart on "Kim Possible" and its spin-off series, "Kim Possible: A New Generation", as well as voicing characters in numerous video games and animated projects. Ruff has also lent her voice to a number of animated TV shows, including "The Simpsons" and "Family Guy".  Ruff was born in Cleveland, Ohio, on October 22, 1965. She began her voice acting career in the early 1990s, working on various animated projects before landing her most notable role as Kimberly Hart on the popular series "Kim Possible" (2002-2006). The series spawned a spin-off series, "Kim Possible: A New Generation", which ran from 2007-2012. Ruff also lent her voice to several other animated TV shows during this time, including "The Simpsons" (2002-2013), "King of the Hill" (1997-2013), and "Family Guy" (1999-2013).  Since 2013, Ruff has focused primarily on voice acting for video games. She has voiced characters in titles such as "Mass Effect 3", "Dragon Age II", and "Destiny". Additionally, Ruff has lent her voice to several audiobooks, including the novel adaptation of the popular video game series "Minecraft". In 2018, she published her memoir, entitled "Do You Want To Know A Secret?": My Life As an Animated Voice Actress.
