Robert Daniel Morris (born October 19, 1943) is an American composer, music theorist, and author. He is a leading figure in the postmodern movement and has been described as one of the "most influential American composers of our time".  Morris was born in New York City, the son of a corporate lawyer. He studied music at SUNY Purchase, Yale University, and the University of Chicago. He has worked as a college professor, as a composer-in-residence with several orchestras, and as a music theorist. Morris is also a published author, having written books on music theory, postmodernism, and jazz.  Morris has been awarded several honorary degrees from universities around the world. In 2003 he was awarded an honorary doctorate from the University of Edinburgh. In 2006 he was awarded an honorary doctorate from Bard College. In 2009 he was awarded an honorary doctorate from Rutgers University-New Brunswick.
