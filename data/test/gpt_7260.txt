Gary J. Tunnicliffe (born 27 February 1968) is a British television presenter, journalist and author.  Gary Tunnicliffe was born in London on 27 February 1968. He attended the independent Wimbledon School and then studied at Pembroke College, Cambridge, where he graduated with a degree in History.  In 1988, Tunnicliffe began his career as a trainee journalist at the BBC Television Centre. He then worked as a reporter for BBC Radio 5 Live and BBC Radio 4 before being appointed the network's political correspondent in 1997. In 1999, he became the BBC's North America editor, based in Washington D.C. He left the BBC in 2003 to become a correspondent for ITN News Channel, based in London.  In 2004, Tunnicliffe co-founded the news website The Daily Telegraph with his former ITN colleague Christopher Hope. The website became one of the most popular news websites in the UK, with a weekly readership of over 1 million people. In 2006, he was awarded an OBE for services to journalism.  In 2008, Tunnicliffe returned to the BBC as a presenter and political analyst for the flagship current affairs programme Newsnight. He has also presented programmes such as The Andrew Marr Show and The Big Questions on BBC Two. In 2014, he was named by The Guardian as one of Britain's most influential journalists.
