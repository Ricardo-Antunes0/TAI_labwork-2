He was one of the most popular and successful rock musicians of the 1970s, with several Billboard Hot 100 top 10 hits. He also had several successful albums in the UK and Europe.  Palmer started his career as a pianist and singer in Boston, Massachusetts. He then moved to New York City, where he began a career as a session musician and worked with artists such as Lou Reed, Carly Simon, Elton John and Barbra Streisand. He released his first album in 1976, which yielded three top 10 hits in the U.S.: "Love is a Battlefield", "Simply Irresistible" and "Doctor's Orders". In 1978, Palmer achieved international success with the album "Crazy Little Thing Called Love", which topped the charts in more than 20 countries and sold over 20 million copies worldwide. His subsequent albums (including "Head Over Heels" (1980), "Private Dancer" (1982), "Born to Die" (1986), "Innocent Eyes" (1991), and "Ghost Dance" (1995)) also received widespread critical acclaim.   Palmer's late-1970s and 1980s work largely revolved around music videos and live appearances; he was known for his elaborate stage costumes and showmanship.
