USS Cimarron (AO-22) was a  oiler that served in the United States Navy from 1943 to 1945.  USS Cimarron was laid down on 8 February 1943 at Kearny, New Jersey, by the Federal Shipbuilding and Drydock Company; launched on 25 March 1943; sponsored by Mrs. A.L. Jones; and commissioned on 30 June 1943, Lt. Comdr. J.L. MacNamara in command.  Following shakedown off Bermuda, Cimarron departed Norfolk on 6 September 1943 for the Pacific theater. She reached Pearl Harbor on 15 September and commenced operations with Service Squadron (ServRon) 12 in support of the forces operating in the Central Pacific Area. Her first duty was to provide fuel and supplies to ships operating in the area.  Cimarron sailed from Pearl Harbor for Eniwetok on 4 October 1943, then continued her support of naval operations in the Central Pacific Area until departing Ulithi on 9 December for the invasion of Iwo Jima. She arrived off that island two days later and began providing fuel, ammunition, and supplies to ships engaged in the assault and subsequent fighting ashore.  Cimarron departed Iwo Jima on 16 February 1944 to rendezvous with Task Group 58.2 which was preparing for the invasion of Okinawa. The oiler arrived off Okinawa on 26 February and began supplying ships engaged in the amphibious assault and Bloody Sunday ashore.
