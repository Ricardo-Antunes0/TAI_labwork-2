Géza Lóczi is a Hungarian American car designer and engineer. He is the co-founder and president of Lóczi Automotive, a manufacturer of luxury sports cars in the United States.  Lóczi was born in Budapest, Hungary in 1960. He studied mechanical engineering at the Budapest Technical University.  Lóczi began his career as a designer at the Hungarian automakers Gyron and Magna Steyr. In 1988, he moved to Germany to work for the Audi company, where he was responsible for the design of their high-performance sports cars.  In 1996, Lóczi founded Lóczi Automotive with his wife, Monika. The company specializes in producing luxury sports cars such as the Audi R8 and Aston Martin Vantage. In 2006, Lóczi Automotive was acquired by the Canadian luxury car maker Fairmont Hotels & Resorts.  Lóczi has been involved in a number of motorsport events, including the 24 Hours of Le Mans and the Pikes Peak International Hill Climb. He has also designed racing cars for drivers such as Stefan Johansson and Jan Magnussen.  Lóczi is a member of both the American Society of Automotive Engineers and the Automobile Club de l'Ouest. He is also a member of the boards of directors of both the Hungarian American Chamber of Commerce and the Hungarian American Educational Foundation.
