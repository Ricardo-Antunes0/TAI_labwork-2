Joel Parker (November 24, 1816January 2, 1888) was an American politician who served as the 14th Governor of Iowa from 1874 to 1876 and as a United States Senator from Iowa from 1881 to 1885.  Joel Parker was born in Sullivan County, New York on November 24, 1816. He moved with his family to Iowa in 1833 and settled in Jones County. He studied law and was admitted to the bar in 1841. He commenced practice in Jones County, but moved to Audubon County in 1850 and continued the practice of law.  Parker was elected as a Republican to the Forty-fourth Congress (March 4, 1869 â€“ March 3, 1871). He served as chairman of the Committee on Expenditures in the Department of the Interior ( Forty-fifth Congress). He was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1870 to the Forty-fifth Congress.  He served as Governor of Iowa from 1874 to 1876. In 1880, he was elected as a United States Senator from Iowa and served until his death on January 2, 1888. He is interred in Forest Hill Cemetery in Des Moines, Iowa.
