Baron Acton, of Aldenham in the County of Hertford, was a British Liberal politician who served as Home Secretary from 1905 to 1905 and as Chancellor of the Exchequer from 1910 to 1912.  Baron Acton was born in 1834, the son of the Reverend Henry Acton, vicar of Aldenham. He was educated at Eton and Trinity College, Cambridge, where he was a member of the Cambridge Apostles.  Acton entered politics in 1868, being elected as MP for Hertfordshire. He held several ministerial posts during the Liberal administrations of Henry Campbell-Bannerman and H. H. Asquith, serving as Secretary at War from 1892 to 1895 and Secretary at the Treasury from 1895 to 1905. In 1905, he was appointed Home Secretary in Asquith's second administration. He held this position until the Liberal coalition government fell in December 1912.  Acton remained active in Liberal politics after leaving office, serving as President of the Liberal Party from 1912 to 1914 and as Chairman of the Imperial War Cabinet Committee from 1915 to 1916. He also served on many royal commissions and committees, including those that investigated trade unions and electoral fraud. Baron Acton died in 1933.
