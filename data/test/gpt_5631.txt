Ahmed Hussein Harkan  (born 10 December 1984) is an Egyptian football player who plays for Egyptian club Zamalek and the Egypt national team.  Harkan began his career with Al-Masry, before joining Zamalek in 2006. He has won five Egyptian Premier League titles, one Egyptian Cup, and one Confederations Cup with Zamalek. He made his international debut in 2005, and has since represented Egypt at the 2006 FIFA World Cup, the 2010 FIFA World Cup, the 2012 Africa Cup of Nations, and the 2013 Confederations Cup.  Harkan was born in Alexandria to an Egyptian father and a Sudanese mother. He is of Arab descent. After spending his early years in Sudan and Egypt, he moved to Germany with his family at the age of six to pursue his footballing dreams. He played for various German clubs before returning to Egypt to join Al-Masry in 2002.  Harkan made his professional debut for Al-Masry on 2 August 2002 against Ismaily. He appeared in 20 league matches for Al-Masry that season before being transferred to Zamalek on 6 January 2006 for a fee of EGP 2 million. At Zamalek, Harkan became a regular starter and helped the club win five Egyptian Premier League titles (2006–08, 2009–10, 2011–12, 2013–14, 2014–15) as well as one Egyptian Cup (2008) and one Confederations Cup (2013). He also scored 29 goals
