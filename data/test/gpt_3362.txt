Collinsville is a rural locality in the Australian state of South Australia located about north-west of the state capital Adelaide and about north-east of the town of Ceduna.  Collinsville was proclaimed a village in 1875 and was renamed Collinsville North in 1887. The name was changed back to Collinsville in 1897. It became a town in 1912. The town was surveyed and laid out by W. H. Collins and J. A. Margetts, and named after the local Collins family.     The town had a population of 4,845 at the 2016 census.
