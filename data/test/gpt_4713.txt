De Wet Basson is a retired South African rugby union player who played as a flanker. Basson is a two-time IRB World Player of the Year, winning the award in 2006 and 2007. He was also a member of the South Africa national team, playing 85 international matches and scoring 33 tries.  Basson was born on 13 February 1981 in Johannesburg, South Africa. He played for the Blue Bulls in the Currie Cup and Vodacom Cup before joining French side Racing Métro 92 in 2002. He helped Racing Métro reach the final of the European Champions Cup in 2003 and 2004. In 2005, Basson joined English side Northampton Saints, helping them reach the final of the Premiership in his first season.  Basson made his international debut for South Africa against Wales on 15 November 2001 at Twickenham Stadium. He gained recognition for his performances during the 2003 Rugby World Cup, where he was named in the squad as a replacement for Jean de Villiers. Following South Africa's victory over England in the semi-final, Basson helped his country to their first world title since 1995. He played all of South Africa's matches during their successful campaign and was man of the match in their victory over Australia in the final.  Basson announced his retirement from international rugby on 7 August 2007 after playing 85 matches for South Africa.
