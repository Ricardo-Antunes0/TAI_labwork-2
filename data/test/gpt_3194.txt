Brookes was born in London, England on 16 August 1945. After studying at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art, he began his career in 1966 with a role in the play The Homecoming by Alan Bennett. He soon moved to television, appearing in episodes of such programmes as Z-Cars, Dixon of Dock Green and The Sweeney. In 1975 he starred in the BBC2 medical drama series St. Elsewhere as Dr. David Hunter, which ran for eight seasons until 1988. From 1985 to 1988 he starred as Colonel Richard Sharpe in the historical drama series Sharpe, which earned him two BAFTA Awards and two Golden Globe Awards. From 1992 to 1994 he starred as Arthur Dent in the BBC science fiction sitcom Doctor Who, which earned him a Saturn Award nomination for Best Actor on Television.
