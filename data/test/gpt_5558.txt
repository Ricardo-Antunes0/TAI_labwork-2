The Chínipas is river of northwestern Mexico. It is a left tributary of the Lerma River.  The Chínipas River originates in the Sierra Madre Oriental, near the village of San Andrés de las Casas in the Mexican state of Michoacán. It flows generally northwestward through Chilpancingo, Morelia, and Toluca before entering the Lerma River in Mexico City.  The Chínipas River basin has an area of . Its drainage basin covers parts of Michoacán, México State, Guerrero, Puebla, and Hidalgo States. The river is used for irrigation and for power generation.
