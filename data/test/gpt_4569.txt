Cleve Rugby Football Club is an English rugby union team based in Cleve, near Coventry. The club was formed in 1875 and plays in Division One North of the Rugby Football Union.  Cleve RFC is one of the oldest rugby clubs in England, and has played at its current ground in Cleve since 1878. The club's first recorded match was against Warwickshire on 15 October 1875, and it became a founding member of the Rugby Football Union in 1895. Cleve RFC has won the Coventry Senior Cup twice (1903 and 1909), and the Ricoh Cup once (2002).  The club also fields teams in women's rugby union, junior rugby, touch rugby and Sunday rugby.
