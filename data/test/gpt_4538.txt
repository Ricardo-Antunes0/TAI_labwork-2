Julian Gregory Klymkiw (born July 16, 1933) is a Canadian historian, specializing in the history of Ukraine and Russia.  Klymkiw was born in Winnipeg, Manitoba to Ukrainian parents. He attended the University of Toronto where he received his B.A. in 1954 and his M.A. in 1955. He then taught at the University of Alberta from 1955 to 1960, before becoming a research fellow at the University of Cambridge from 1960 to 1962. He then moved to Yale University where he became a professor in 1966 and served as chairman of the history department from 1987 to 1993. In 1998, he was awarded an honorary D.Litt from the University of Western Ontario.  Klymkiw has written extensively on Russian and Ukrainian history, and is considered one of the most respected historians of his generation. He has also published articles on European history, comparative history, and social history. His books include The Soviet Revolution (1972), The Russian Revolution (1990), Ukraine: A History (1996), and The Soviet Union Since 1917 (2007). He has also edited or co-edited several volumes on Russian and Ukrainian history, including The Cambridge History of Russia (1988), The Cambridge History of Ukraine (1993), and A Companion to Russian and Soviet History (2005).
