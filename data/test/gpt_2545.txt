Rannvijay Singh Singha is an Indian actor, producer and politician who is the current Chief Minister of Himachal Pradesh. He is also a member of the Indian National Congress.  Rannvijay Singh Singha was born on 26 September 1951 in Shimla, Himachal Pradesh to Rameshwar Singh and Raj Kumari. He completed his early education at Shimla Public School and then studied at the Mount St. Mary's College, Shimla. After completing his graduation in 1972, he worked as an accountant with the State Bank of India.  In 1978, Singh Singha joined the Indian National Congress and was appointed as a district secretary in the Kullu district. He became president of the Kullu District Congress Committee in 1981 and remained in that position until 1984. In 1984, he was elected to the Himachal Pradesh Legislative Assembly as an Indian National Congress candidate from the Mandi constituency. He served as speaker of the Himachal Pradesh Legislative Assembly from 1988 to 1990 and from 1994 to 1998.  Singh Singha was elected as the Chief Minister of Himachal Pradesh on 14 February 2001, defeating incumbent Prem Kumar Dhumal by a margin of over one million votes. He was re-elected as Chief Minister on 7 February 2006, defeating Rajinder Singh Bajwa by a margin of over one million votes.
