Léa Garcia (born March 11, 1933) is an Iranian-born French actress, singer, and dancer. She has appeared in more than sixty films and television shows since 1955.  Garcia was born in Tehran, Iran, to a Spanish father and an Iranian mother. When she was five years old, her family fled the country during the Iranian Revolution and moved to France. Garcia began her career in the French theater, appearing in such plays as La Cage aux Folles and A Streetcar Named Desire. She came to prominence in the 1960s with roles in films such as La Baie des Anges and The Marseillaise. In 1974, she starred in the Academy Award-winning drama The Exorcist. Garcia has since appeared in several Hollywood films, including The Towering Inferno (1974), The Sting (1973), Airport (1970), Star Wars: Episode IV – A New Hope (1977), The Return of the Jedi (1983), and Ocean's Eleven (2001).
