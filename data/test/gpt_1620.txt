The Scotia Mine is located in the Fraser Canyon, approximately 55 kilometers south of Prince George. The mine is a former open pit operation that operated from 1962 to 1983. The mine produced over 1 million ounces of gold, making it one of the largest gold mines in northern British Columbia. The mine closed due to decreasing gold prices and environmental concerns. Today, the property is owned by the Government of British Columbia and is used for forestry and other resource extraction activities.
