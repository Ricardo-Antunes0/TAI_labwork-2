Pterostylis lingua, commonly known as the large-lipped pterostylis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Orchidaceae. It is endemic to the south-west of Western Australia.  This orchid grows as a terrestrial herb with a single smooth, leathery leaf and up to twenty white or pink flowers with long, narrow sepals and petals. The labellum is heart-shaped, with a long, recurved tongue and a prominent notch in its centre.  This species is widespread in the southern parts of Western Australia where it grows in open woodland and heath. It is threatened by habitat loss.
