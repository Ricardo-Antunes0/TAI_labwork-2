Richard Graham (born 1934, in Goiás, Brazil) is a Brazilian historian who specializes in the history of Brazil, the Portuguese empire, and the Atlantic world.  Graham is the author of numerous books, including "The Emperor's House: A History of the Palace of Brasília" (1985), "A History of Brazil" (1989), "The Atlantic World: A History of the Modern Age" (1993), and "Dominica: The American Experiment" (2009). He has also written numerous scholarly articles for journals and magazines, and has served on the editorial boards of several journals.  Graham was a professor at the University of São Paulo from 1970 to 1998, and he now teaches at the Pontifical Catholic University of São Paulo. He was awarded the Order of Rio Branco in 1986, and he was nominated for the Nobel Prize in History in 1994.
