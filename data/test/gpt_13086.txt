He was best known for his work in quantum field theory, statistical mechanics, and condensed matter physics. Crowther shared the 1981 Nobel Prize in Physics with Richard Feynman and Murray Gell-Mann "for their contributions to the theory of the strong interaction".  Crowther was born in 1939 in Houston, Texas. After graduating from high school, he enrolled at the University of Texas at Austin where he received his bachelor's degree in 1961 and his doctorate in physics in 1965. He then taught at Texas for 34 years before retiring in 2006. Crowther died on January 16, 2016, aged 78.
