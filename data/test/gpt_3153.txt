The Augustus Post House, also known as the Augustus Post Museum, is a historic house located at 10 Post Road in Westport, Connecticut. It is a 2-1/2 story wood frame structure, with a gable roof and clapboarded exterior. The house was built in 1847 for Augustus Post, who was a prominent local merchant and politician. It was later used as a museum by the town. The house was added to the National Register of Historic Places in 1973.  Augustus Post House is significant as one of the few surviving examples of Italianate architecture in Westport. The house is also significant as a rare example of a town-owned museum, which served as a repository for artifacts related to local history.
