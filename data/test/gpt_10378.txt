Alvin Leonardo Harrison (born January 20, 1974) is an American retired professional basketball player. A 6'8" center, Harrison played 12 seasons in the National Basketball Association (NBA) from 1997 to 2007. He was a two-time All-Star, and won an NBA championship with the Miami Heat in 2006.  Harrison attended Frederick Douglas High School in Frederick, Maryland and played college basketball for the University of Michigan. He was drafted by the Chicago Bulls with the fourth pick of the 1997 NBA draft. After playing four seasons with the Bulls, he was traded to the Seattle SuperSonics in 2001. He returned to Chicago in 2003, where he played for two more seasons before being traded again, this time to the Miami Heat. He won his first championship with Miami in 2006. After playing one season with the New Orleans Hornets, Harrison retired from professional basketball in 2007.  Harrison is active in business ventures, and is a minority owner of the Baltimore Thunder of the NBA Development League. In September 2016, Harrison announced his candidacy for Mayor of Baltimore in the 2017 election.
