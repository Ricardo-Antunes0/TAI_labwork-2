Julio César Arana del Águila, (1864–1952) was a Chilean historian and politician. After studying at the University of Chile, he taught at the school of history and political economy before becoming a professor at the University of Chile. He served as Minister of Education in the governments of Eduardo Frei Montalva and Salvador Allende. In 1937 he was elected to the Senate, where he served until his death.  Arana was a prolific writer, publishing several works on Chilean history and politics. He is best known for his study of the Chilean War of Independence, La guerra de independencia de Chile (1955), which has been described as "the classic work on the subject".
