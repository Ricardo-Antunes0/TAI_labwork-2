Constance H. Williams (born June 27, 1944) is an American author, journalist and social critic. She is the author of numerous books, including The Autobiography of an Ex-Black Woman (1981), Ain't I a Woman (1987), and most recently, The Soul of Black Folk (2010). Williams has also written for The New York Times, The Guardian, and other publications.  Williams was born in Baltimore, Maryland to Ella Baker and Fenton Williams. She was raised in Baltimore's Mount Vernon-Belvedere neighborhood. Her father was a professor of English at Morgan State University and her mother was a civil rights activist and secretary-treasurer of the NAACP. Williams attended the University of Maryland, where she received her B.A. in 1966. She then attended the University of California at Berkeley, where she received her M.A. in 1968 and her Ph.D. in 1972.  Williams began her career as a journalist with The Baltimore News-American in 1966. From 1970 to 1972, she was a reporter for The New York Times. In 1973, she became a columnist for Essence magazine; she held that position until 1987. From 1987 to 2003, she was a contributing writer for The New York Times Magazine. She has also written for The Guardian, Harper's Magazine, Ms., and other publications.
