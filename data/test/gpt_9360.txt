Abu'l-Hasan ( 1569 - 12 June 1641) was an Iranian Muslim scholar and Sufi poet.  He was born in 1569 in the city of Qazvin, Iran, the son of Hasan Asaf Khan and grandson of Husayn ibn Ali. He studied Islamic jurisprudence and mathematics at the Topkapi Palace School in Istanbul, where he became acquainted with the poetry of Rumi and Hafiz. He also studied philosophy and theology under such scholars as Abd al-Qadir Jilani and Mir Damad.  In 1610, he returned to Iran and became a professor at the Isfahan University. He also served as the governor of Fars Province for a time. He died in Isfahan in 1641 at the age of 68.
