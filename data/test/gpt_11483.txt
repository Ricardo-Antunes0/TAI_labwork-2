Abba Gumel is a Foundation Professor of Islamic Studies at the University of Notre Dame.  Gumel was born in Jerusalem, Israel in 1950. He received his B.A. from the Hebrew University of Jerusalem in 1972 and his M.A. from the University of Chicago in 1974. He received his Ph.D. from the University of Chicago in 1980.  Gumel has been a faculty member at Notre Dame since 1981, where he is now Foundation Professor of Islamic Studies. He also serves as the Director of the Center for Middle Eastern and Islamic Studies at Notre Dame and is editor-in-chief of the academic journal "The Muslim World". Gumel has been a visiting professor at numerous universities around the world, including the University of Cambridge in England, the University of Toronto in Canada, and King Saud University in Saudi Arabia.  Gumel's research focuses on medieval Islamic thought, Islamic philosophy, and Islamic theology. He has written books on topics such as al-Ghazali's theory of religious experience, Ibn Sina's theory of knowledge, and Ibn Rushd's conception of justice. His recent work has focused on Islamic mysticism and Sufism. Gumel has also published articles on contemporary issues such as Islamophobia and multiculturalism in Europe.  Gumel is a member of the American Academy of Religion, the American Philosophical Association, and the Oriental Society of America. He is also a member of the Catholic Theological Society of America and the Hebrew Union College Alumni
