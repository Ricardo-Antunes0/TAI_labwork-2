Born in Tokyo, Inoue made her acting debut in 1941 and quickly became a leading lady in many popular dramas. Her most famous roles were in "The Setting Sun" (1957), for which she was awarded the Best Actress prize at the 1957 Cannes Film Festival, and "Tokyo Story" (1953), for which she won the Best Actress award at the 1954 Academy Awards. Inoue also received acclaim for her work in "An Actor's Life" (1958), "The Face of Another" (1965), and "The Thief of Baghdad" (1959).  Inoue retired from acting in 1971 but continued to make occasional appearances until her death from cardiac arrest on 25 February 2007 at the age of 88. Inoue remains one of Japan's most respected and admired actors.
