He played in Major League Baseball (MLB) for the Detroit Tigers and Philadelphia Phillies from 1921 to 1936.  Gautreau was a shortstop and third baseman who batted left-handed and threw right-handed. He was born in Poughkeepsie, New York, and attended Georgetown College in Georgetown, Kentucky.  Gautreau made his MLB debut with the Detroit Tigers in 1921. He was traded to the Philadelphia Phillies in 1931 and played for them until 1936. In a 12-year career, Gautreau posted a .287 batting average with 225 home runs and 824 RBIs in 2,323 games. He led the National League (NL) in home runs twice (1929 and 1934), and ranked fourth in RBIs seven times. He also finished second in the NL MVP voting in 1929 and 1934.  After his playing career ended, Gautreau served as a baseball coach at Georgetown University from 1937 to 1946, then at Louisiana State University from 1947 to 1955. He was inducted into the Baseball Hall of Fame in 1956.
