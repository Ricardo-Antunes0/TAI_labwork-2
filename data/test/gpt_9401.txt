Jasenovac – istina (English: Jasenovac – The Truth) is a Croatian documentary film directed by Mladen Božić and produced by the Croatian Film Institute. The film premiered at the 2013 Toronto International Film Festival.  The documentary film tells the story of Jasenovac concentration camp, located near the town of Jasenovac, Croatia. The camp was active from 1941 to 1945, during World War II, and was one of the largest concentration camps in Europe. It was operated by the Ustaše regime, a fascist militant organization which aimed to create a Greater Croatian State. More than 100,000 people were killed in Jasenovac, most of them Serbs.
