Amt Bilstein was an administrative district in the Prussian province of West Prussia.  The district was located in the south of the province, bordered by the districts of Oppeln and Thorn. The district consisted of the following five counties: Oppeln, Thorn, Bromberg, Trier-Saarburg and Marburg.  The district was founded in 1815 as part of the restructuring of West Prussia after its defeat in the Napoleonic Wars. The original name of the district was "Oberamt Bilstein". In 1817 it was renamed "Amt Bilstein", after Prince Georg von Bilstein.
