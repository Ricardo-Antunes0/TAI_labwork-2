Winston Raymond Peters  (born 11 April 1951) is a New Zealand politician who has been a Member of Parliament (MP) for the Labour Party since 1975, and was the Leader of the Labour Party from 2003 to 2007.  Peters is one of New Zealand's most experienced and successful politicians, having served in a number of Cabinet positions over the years. He was Deputy Prime Minister (holding the post from 1996 to 1999) and Minister of Foreign Affairs (1999 to 2003) in the first two terms of the Labour Government led by Helen Clark. In December 2007, he announced his retirement from politics, effective at the next election.  Peters was born in Auckland, New Zealand on 11 April 1951. He was educated at Mt Albert Grammar School and at Victoria University of Wellington where he achieved a Bachelors degree in law in 1975. Peters then worked as a lawyer before being elected to Parliament in 1975 as MP for Petersfield. He served on various Parliamentary committees before being appointed Deputy Leader of the Labour Party in 1992.  In 1996, Peters became Deputy Prime Minister under Prime Minister Jim Bolger and held that position until 1999 when Labour was re-elected and he became Minister of Foreign Affairs. In this role, Peters played an important role in negotiations which led to New Zealand's membership of the United Nations Security Council for the first time in 2002.  In December 2007, Peters announced his retirement from politics effective at the next election.
