Peter H. Duesberg (born December 2, 1936) is an American physician, cancer researcher, and AIDS denialist who has made significant contributions to the field of cancer research. He is also a member of the National Academy of Sciences and a fellow of the American Association for the Advancement of Science.  Duesberg was born in Los Angeles, California. He received his medical degree from the University of California, Berkeley in 1960. He completed his residency at the University of California, San Francisco in 1964. Duesberg then undertook a post-graduate fellowship at the Karolinska Institute in Stockholm, Sweden.  Duesberg began his career as a cancer researcher at the University of California, San Francisco. In 1971, he was appointed professor of oncology at the University of Southern California School of Medicine. He remained at USC until his retirement in 1998.  Duesberg is best known for his work on the genetics of cancer. In particular, he has contributed to the understanding of how different cancers develop and how they can be treated.  Duesberg is also known for his work on AIDS denialism. He has argued that there is no evidence that HIV causes AIDS and that it is instead a self-inflicted disease caused by drug abuse and promiscuity.
