Samuel C. Hughes (August 28, 1829 – December 7, 1902) was a U.S. Representative from Illinois.  Born near Corydon, Randolph County, Indiana, Hughes attended the common schools and graduated from Transylvania University in Lexington, Kentucky, in 1851. He studied law. He was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Peoria, Illinois, in 1852.  Hughes served as member of the State house of representatives in 1859 and 1860. He served as member of the State senate in 1861 and 1862.  Hughes was elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-third Congress (March 4, 1873 – March 3, 1875). He was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1874 to the Thirty-fourth Congress.  He resumed the practice of law. He died in Peoria, Illinois, December 7, 1902. He was interred in Oak Hill Cemetery.
