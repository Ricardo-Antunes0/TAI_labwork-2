Steven Ross Smith (born June 25, 1945) is an American philosopher and Professor of Philosophy at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill.  Smith is known for his work on ethics, political philosophy, and legal theory. He has written extensively on topics such as the role of reason in ethical decision making, the nature and justification of political authority, and the relationship between law and morality. He is also known for his work on the philosophy of language, most notably his book The Language of Morals.  Smith was born in Ossining, New York, and attended Yale University where he received his B.A. in 1967 and his M.A. in 1969. He then attended the University of Chicago where he received his Ph.D. in 1973. After completing his doctoral studies, Smith served as a postdoctoral fellow at the University of California at Berkeley before joining the faculty at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill in 1974. He has been a member of the philosophical faculty at UNC since that time.
