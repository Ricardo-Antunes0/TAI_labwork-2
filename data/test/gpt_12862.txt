Madeleine Monette is a Canadian novelist, short story writer, and poet who was born on October 11, 1958 in Montreal, Quebec.  Madeleine Monette's writing has been described as "distinctive and poetic" and she has been awarded the Governor General's Award for English-language fiction twice, in 2003 for her novel The Wolf Road and in 2015 for her collection of short stories, The Light Between Oceans. She is also the recipient of numerous other awards, including the Scotiabank Giller Prize, the France-Canada Literary Prize, and the Quebec Writers' Federation Award.  Monette was born in Montreal on October 11, 1958 and grew up in Sainte-Marie-de-Beauce. After studying at Collège Lionel-Groulx and Université de Montréal, she worked as a teacher before begining to write full-time. She has lived in various parts of Canada and France over the years but currently resides in Saint John's, Newfoundland and Labrador.
