After Five is a 1915 American silent comedy film produced by Famous Players-Lasky and distributed by Paramount Pictures. It was directed by James Cruze and starred Mary Pickford and Douglas Fairbanks.  The film is set in New York City and tells the story of the complications that ensue when five friends decide to celebrate their last day of school before summer break by going out on a double date. The men end up getting drunk, while the women are left to fend for themselves. The resulting chaos is hilarious as the friends deal with their misadventures.
