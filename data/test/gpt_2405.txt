He attended Grafton Public School and then the University of Sydney, where he studied law. Nevin was admitted to the New South Wales bar in 1916 and commenced practice in Grafton.  In 1917, Nevin enlisted in the Australian Imperial Force and served in France and Belgium with the 2nd Field Ambulance, later transferring to the Australian Machine Gun Corps. He was discharged as a lieutenant in 1919 and returned to his legal practice. In 1924, Nevin entered politics as a member of the Nationalist Party of New South Wales. He was elected to the New South Wales Legislative Assembly as the member for Grafton in 1925 and served as Attorney-General from 1939 to 1940. In 1942, he was appointed Minister for Defence and held this position until 1945. Nevin was also Minister for Railways from 1942 to 1945.  In 1947, Nevin was appointed Ambassador to the United States and served until his retirement in 1953. During his time in Washington, D.C., he played a leading role in Australia's alliance with the United States during the Cold War. Nevin also served as Australian Ambassador to France from 1956 to 1958 and Ambassador to Spain from 1958 to 1961. He died at Sydney on 6 September 1966.
