Richard Keith Armey (; born July 7, 1951) is an American politician and lobbyist who was the Majority Whip of the United States House of Representatives from 1995 to 2003. He was also a member of the Republican Party.  Born in Houston, Texas, Armey graduated from Baylor University in 1973 with a Bachelor of Science degree in Political Science. He then earned his Juris Doctor from the University of Houston Law Center in 1977. After law school, Armey clerked for U.S. District Court Judge William Wayne Justice of the United States District Court for the Southern District of Texas and then became a lawyer with the Houston law firm Houser, Armey, Johnson, and Keefe.  Armey served as a legislative aide to U.S. Congressman Jack Kemp from 1978 to 1980 and then became an aide to Republican National Committee chairman Lee Atwater. In 1984, he was elected to the Texas House of Representatives as a Republican, and he served until 1988.  In 1988, Armey was recruited by Majority Leader Tom DeLay to become an aide on the House Judiciary Committee where he worked on issues such as impeachment proceedings against President Bill Clinton and campaign finance reform. In 1992, Armey was elected as majority whip of the House of Representatives, becoming one of the most powerful members of Congress.
