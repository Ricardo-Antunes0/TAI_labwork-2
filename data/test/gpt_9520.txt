The Wekiva River (sometimes spelled Wekiwa, a plural form) is a river in Florida. It is a tributary of the St. Johns River.  The Wekiva River originates in the Ocala National Forest, about southeast of Ocala, in Marion County. It flows generally southward through central Florida, passing through Orlando and then entering the St. Johns River near Palm Coast.  The Wekiva River has a drainage basin of . About 46% of its water comes from rainfall and 54% from groundwater recharge. The river is not designated as a wild or scenic river, but part of the river's natural history is protected by state and federal law.
