Clarence Reginald Jenkins (January 10, 1898 - November 21, 1987) was an American Negro League baseball player and manager. Jenkins played first base and was the manager of the Newark Eagles from 1931 to 1936.  Jenkins was born in 1898 in Wilmington, Delaware. He played baseball in the Negro Leagues from 1924 to 1936, mostly with the Newark Eagles. In 1931, Jenkins became the manager of the Eagles, a position he held until 1936. His teams won five Negro League championships (1929, 1933, 1934, 1935, and 1936). Jenkins also led the Eagles to a second-place finish in the 1937 Negro League season. Jenkins was elected to the Baseball Hall of Fame in 1971. He died in 1987.
