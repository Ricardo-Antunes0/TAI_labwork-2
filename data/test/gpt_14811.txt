Frederick John Kern (September 2, 1864 – May 23, 1937) was an American lawyer and politician from the state of New York.  Kern was born in the town of West Monroe, Monroe County, New York, the son of John Kern (1827–1901) and Matilda (Lundy) Kern (1839–1909). He attended the common schools and graduated from Yale University in 1886. He studied law with Hon. John B. Frye and was admitted to the bar in 1888.  Kern commenced practice in Buffalo, New York, and later in Rochester, New York. He served as a member of the New York State Assembly (Rochester County, 2nd D.) from 1895 to 1898; as a justice of the peace from 1899 to 1902; and as a deputy attorney general from 1902 to 1905. Kern was elected as a Republican to the Sixty-seventh Congress (March 4, 1911 – March 3, 1915). He was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1914 to the Sixty-eventh Congress. Kern resumed practicing law in Rochester. Kern died in Rochester at the age of seventy-eight years. He was interred in Mount Hope Cemetery.
