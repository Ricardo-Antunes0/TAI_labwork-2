Typhoon Lee, known in the Philippines as Typhoon Sanba, was a powerful typhoon that affected the Philippines and China in 1981. The storm developed on September 2 in the Philippine Sea and moved towards the south-east. On September 5, Lee made landfall in Luzon as a strong typhoon, killing at least 134 people and leaving over 1.5 million people homeless. After weakening over land, Lee made a second landfall in China on September 11 as a moderate typhoon, killing an additional 58 people and leaving another 1.5 million people homeless.
