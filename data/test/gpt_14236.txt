Melbury Bubb is a small village and civil parish in the North Somerset district of Somerset, England. The village is on a small hill overlooking the River Brue, about south-west of Yeovil.  The parish includes the hamlets of Bubb's Green and West Bubb.  The name of the village is derived from the Old English word "bubb", meaning a bubbling spring or stream.  The parish has a population of 269 people. The population density is 1 person per square kilometre.  There are two churches in the parish, both of which are Grade II* listed: St Peter's Church and St Mary Magdalene's Church.  There is also a primary school, which was founded in 1849 and which had 96 pupils as of September 2012.
