Toshifumi (Toshi) Yokota  is a medical doctor and researcher who is known for his work on the genetic causes of cancer. Yokota was one of the co-founders of the Institute of Genetics, Health and Medicine (IGHM), a nonprofit research institute in Tokyo, Japan. He is also the founder and president of the OncoGenesis Foundation, which funds cancer research.  Toshifumi Yokota was born on October 25, 1954, in Tokyo, Japan. He received his medical degree from Kyoto University in 1978 and completed his residency at Keio University’s Department of Internal Medicine in 1984. From 1985 to 1990, he served as a postdoctoral fellow at the Rockefeller University in New York City.  Yokota returned to Japan in 1990 and began working at the Institute of Genetics, Health and Medicine (IGHM), a nonprofit research institute he co-founded with Haruko Obokata in 1992. At IGHM, Yokota conducted pioneering research on the genetic causes of cancer. In 2003, he was awarded the Japan Academy Prize for Medical Science for his work on identifying genes that cause inherited forms of cancer.  In 2006, Yokota founded the OncoGenesis Foundation, which has funded cancer research at institutions such as Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center and Yale University. He is also the president of IGHM’s board of directors.  Yokota has published more than 350 articles in peer-reviewed journals and has given more than 600 presentations around the world
