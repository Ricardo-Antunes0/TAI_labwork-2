The Green Line (Arabic: الخط الأخضر) was a proposed light rail line in Lebanon. The line was proposed in 2009 by the Beirut-based engineering firm AECOM and would have run from Beirut's southern suburbs to the southern city of Metn. The project was cancelled in 2013 due to lack of funding.  The Green Line would have been the longest light rail line in Lebanon, running for . It would have had a total of 26 stations, including two termini. The first terminus would have been located in the southern suburbs of Beirut, near the Saudi embassy.
