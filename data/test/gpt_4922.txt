Carlisle United Football Club  is a professional football club based in Carlisle, Cumbria, England. The club was formed in 1877 and play in League One, the third tier of English football. They were founder members of the Football League in 1888 and have spent their entire history in the top flight of English football. The club's home ground is the Brunton Park Stadium.  Carlisle United were formed on 15 October 1877, and played their first ever game on 17 November against North Western Railway. The club entered the Football League in 1888, and have played in every division since then. They reached the Second Division in 1951, but were relegated the following season. They reached the First Division again in 1974, but were relegated again two years later. The club returned to the top flight in 1986, and stayed there for thirteen seasons. In 2002, they reached the FA Cup Final for the first time ever, but lost 3–1 to Manchester United. Carlisle were relegated from League One in 2006, but returned to the division two years later. In 2010, they reached the Championship play-offs for the first time ever, but lost 4–3 on aggregate to Blackpool. The following season they finished fourth in League One, and were promoted to Premier League for the first time in their history.  The club's home ground is Brunton Park Stadium.
