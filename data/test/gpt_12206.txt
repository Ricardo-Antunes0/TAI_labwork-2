Joseph J. "Sport" Sullivan (November 2, 1870 – October 3, 1947) was an American professional baseball player. He played in Major League Baseball (MLB) as a pitcher from 1892 to 1897 for the Buffalo Bisons, Boston Beaneaters, New York Giants, and Cincinnati Reds.  Sullivan compiled a career record of 57–49 with a 3.49 ERA in 809 innings pitched. He led the National League in innings pitched in 1896 and 1897. He also led the league in losses each year. Sullivan was one of the early stars of baseball and is considered one of the best pitchers in MLB history.  Sullivan was born in Buffalo, New York, on November 2, 1870. He played baseball for Amherst College from 1889 to 1891 and then for the Rochester Bronchos of the Western League from 1892 to 1893. He joined the Buffalo Bisons of MLB in 1892 and finished his career with the Cincinnati Reds in 1897.
