Steven J. Erlanger (born October 14, 1952, in Worcester, Massachusetts) is an American journalist and foreign correspondent who has written for The New York Times, The Wall Street Journal, and other publications.  Erlanger began his journalistic career as a reporter for the Worcester Telegram & Gazette. He moved to The New York Times in 1984, where he has been a foreign correspondent and senior editor. He has also written for The Wall Street Journal, Newsweek, and other publications.  Erlanger is the author of nine books, including two bestsellers: Poison Spring: The Untold Story of the Iraq War's Deadly Chemical Weapons (2004) and Churchill: A Life (2010). He has also won numerous journalism awards, including the Pulitzer Prize for International Reporting in 2006.
