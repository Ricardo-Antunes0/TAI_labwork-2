Richard Sale (4 October 1919 – 3 December 2001) was an English footballer who played as a goalkeeper in the Football League for Stockport County, Chester, Rochdale, Manchester City and Stafford Rangers.  Born in Stockport, Sale played football for Stockport County before signing for Chester in 1941. He made his debut in the Football League on 17 August 1941 in a 2–1 victory over Rochdale. He spent three seasons with Chester before moving to Rochdale in 1945. He made 134 appearances for Rochdale, helping them to the Third Division North title in 1947–48 and the Second Division title the following season.  He joined Manchester City in 1950 and made 125 appearances for them over the next four seasons. He was part of the City team that won the FA Cup in 1952 and finished as runners-up in the First Division in 1953–54. He then moved to Stafford Rangers in 1955 where he made 45 appearances.  Sale later worked as a goalkeeper coach with Manchester City, Stockport County, Chester and Northwich Victoria. He died on 3 December 2001 at the age of 84.
