Relations between Uganda and the People's Republic of China date back to the 1960s, when both countries were part of the United Nations. Over the years, bilateral ties have grown stronger, with China providing assistance to Uganda in a number of areas, including agriculture, infrastructure development, and health. In recent years, both countries have cooperated in various multilateral fora, including the United Nations and the African Union.  The relationship between China and Uganda is marked by close economic ties. China is Uganda's largest foreign investor and provides loan assistance totaling some US$436 million since 2004. The two countries have also strengthened cooperation in trade and investment fields, with bilateral trade reaching US$3.4 billion in 2016. The Chinese government has also been supportive of Uganda's bid for membership in the East African Community (EAC).
