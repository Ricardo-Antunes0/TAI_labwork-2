Marjorie Ryerson is an American editor, photographer, and writer.  Ryerson was born in Cleveland, Ohio, in 1931. She attended the University of Michigan, where she majored in English and graduated with a degree in 1953. Ryerson then worked as an editor for magazines such as "Reader's Digest" and "House & Garden".  Ryerson is best known for her work as an editor and photographer for "The New York Times Magazine", where she served from 1984 to 2007. During her tenure at the "Times", Ryerson was responsible for creating and running the magazine's photo department, which won several awards, including a Pulitzer Prize in 2002. She has also written several books, including a memoir, "In Spite of Myself: A Life" (2004), and a book of photography, "Pictures at an Exhibition" (2009).
