Valook Weir is a small diversion dam on the River Valox in the Scottish Borders. It is about 2 kilometres upstream of the village of Darnick and about 1 kilometre downstream of the village of Ponteland. The Weir was constructed in 1792 by Alexander Geddes and provides water for the mills at Ponteland.  The Weir is a Scheduled Ancient Monument and a Grade II listed building.
