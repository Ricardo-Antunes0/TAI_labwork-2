He also served as governor of Siam from 1575 to 1599.  Chao Phraya Bodindecha was born in Si Satchanalai, present-day Uttaradit Province, on 13 January 1527. He was the son of Chao Sri Jaya Waddhanathon, a minor nobleman and courtier of King Phra Ngama of Lan Na. Bodindecha's mother died when he was young and he was raised by his father and elder brother.  Bodindecha studied at the Temple of the Emerald Buddha in Pattani and then at Wat Arun in Bangkok before entering the service of King Phra Ngama. In 1556, he became a royal secretary and in 1560 became regent to the young king of Ayutthaya, who was only six years old at the time. After ruling for two years as regent, Bodindecha returned to Ayutthaya in 1562. He served as governor of Siam from 1575 to 1599.  Bodindecha is best known for his poetry and scholarship.
