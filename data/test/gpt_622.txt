Jacques Pellegrin (born 17 June 1944- 7 January 2014) was a French painter and sculptor.  Jacques Pellegrin was born on 17 June 1944 in Saint-Étienne, France. After studying at the School of Fine Arts in Lyon, he went on to complete his training at the École Nationale Supérieure des Beaux-Arts in Paris, where he was a student of Jean Dubuffet.   Pellegrin's early work focused on painting and sculpture, but he soon broadened his palette to include performance art and video art. In the 1980s, he began to focus more on painting and created ensembles of paintings that were often combined with environmental installations. Pellegrin's work is noted for its use of light and color, as well as its playful and experimental approach. He died on 7 January 2014 after a long battle with cancer.
