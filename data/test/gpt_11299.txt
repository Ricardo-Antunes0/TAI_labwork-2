Natalia Viktorovna Konopleva (; 23 November 1944 â€“ 3 May 2016) was a Russian operatic soprano. She was known for her dramatic voice, and often portrayed dramatic roles in opera.  Konopleva was born in Leningrad, Soviet Union, into a musical family. Her mother, Viktorina Konopleva, was a pianist and her father, Viktor Konopleva, was a conductor and music teacher. She began her vocal training at the Leningrad Conservatory at the age of six, and made her professional debut in 1968 as Nina in Sergei Prokofiev's War and Peace. In 1973 she won the prestigious International Grand Prix du Disque for her performance of the title role in Giacomo Puccini's Tosca.  Konopleva later made appearances at major opera houses around the world, including La Scala in Milan, the Metropolitan Opera in New York City, Covent Garden in London, and the Vienna State Opera. Her career spanned more than three decades and she notably created roles in two operas by Dmitry Shostakovich: Lady Macbeth of Mtsensk (1981) and The Nose (1997). Konopleva also performed extensively as a recitalist and appeared as guest soloist with major orchestras around the world. In 2007 she received an Honorary Doctorate from the University of Hull.  On 3 May 2016, Natalia Konopleva died at her home in Moscow from undisclosed causes at the age of 71.
