Professor Richard Allan Bryant  (born 17 October 1951) is a British psychologist who specialises in the area of cognitive science. He is the current Regius Professor of Psychology at the University of Cambridge.  Bryant's research focuses on the cognitive processes underlying human reasoning and decision-making, with a particular focus on how people use information to guide their decisions. His work has had a significant impact on fields such as engineering, law, marketing, and management.  Bryant was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society in 2007, and he was awarded the Copley Medal in 2015. He has also been awarded honorary degrees from several universities, including the University of Cambridge, University of Oxford, and University of Edinburgh.
