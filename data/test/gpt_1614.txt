Lenka Reinerová  (17 May 1916 – 1 November 1988) was a Czechoslovak dramatic soprano.  Born in Prague, Reinerová studied with Marie Saal and Emmy Destinn at the Prague Conservatory. She made her debut in 1939 and became a leading figure in the Czech Republic's operatic scene during the 1950s and 1960s. She appeared at major opera houses throughout Europe and the Americas, notably making her Metropolitan Opera debut in 1957. Her most acclaimed roles include Violetta in La traviata, Amina in La sonnambula, Azucena in Don Giovanni, Cio-Cio-San in Madame Butterfly and the title role in Verdi's Rigoletto.
