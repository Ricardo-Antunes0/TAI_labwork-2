A number of ships of the Royal Navy have been named Diligence. The first ship to bear the name was a 74-gun third rate launched in 1741. The most recent ship was a Type 23 frigate, decommissioned in 2016.  The HMS Diligence was a 74-gun third rate launched in 1741. She served in the French and Indian War, the Seven Years' War, and the American Revolutionary War. In 1778 she was renamed HMS Falcon and then HMS Vengeance. In 1801 she was sold to the East India Company and became HMS Phoenix. She served in the First Anglo-Burmese War and the Second Anglo-Burmese War before being sold to the Portuguese Navy in 1810. She served as a storeship before being broken up in 1818.
