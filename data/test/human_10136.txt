Shaquille Harrison (born October 6, 1993) is an American professional basketball player for the Delaware Blue Coats of the NBA G League. Harrison played college basketball at Tulsa, and has played for the Phoenix Suns, Chicago Bulls, Utah Jazz, Denver Nuggets, and Brooklyn Nets in the NBA. College career A  guard from Kansas City, Missouri, Harrison played college basketball at Tulsa, turning down an offer from Kansas to play football. He was a four-year starter for the Golden Hurricane. As a senior, he was named American Athletic Conference Men's Basketball Scholar-Athlete of the Year after averaging 14.8 points, 5.5 rebounds and 4.1 assists per game while maintaining a 3.135 grade-point average. He is the only player in Tulsa history to register 1,300 points, 400 assists, and 200 steals in his career. However, he struggled with his shooting, hitting 19.5 percent of shots from behind the arc and 63 percent of his free throws.
