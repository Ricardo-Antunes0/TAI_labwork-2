Wylie's Baths is a heritage-listed tidal swimming pool and spa at Haymarket, Sydney, New South Wales, Australia. It was added to the New South Wales State Heritage Register on 2 April 1999.  The tidal pool and spa complex was designed by the Sydney architect John Wylie in 1859 for his client, the wealthy Sydney businessman and politician, John Wylie. The baths were opened in 1861, and are one of the earliest examples of a purpose-built tidal pool in Australia. The baths are now managed by the Sydney Harbour Federation Trust and are open to the public free of charge.  The Wylie's Baths tidal pool complex is located on the eastern side of Haymarket, adjacent to the Royal Prince Alfred Hospital. The site is bounded by Sussex Street, Macquarie Street, Cumberland Street and Elizabeth Street. The complex comprises a tidal pool ( rectangular in plan), a children's pool ( square), a spa area ( square), a kiosk and toilets. The tidal pool is fed by an artificial stream from the nearby Sydney Harbour. The complex is built of brick and sandstone with slate roofs.  The Wylie's Baths tidal pool complex was listed on the New South Wales State Heritage Register on 2 April 1999 having satisfied the following criteria.  The place is important in demonstrating the course, or pattern, of cultural or natural history in New South Wales.
