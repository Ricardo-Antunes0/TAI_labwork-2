Ann Noreen Widdecombe  (born 4 October 1942) is a retired British Conservative Party politician who served as the Member of Parliament (MP) for South West Devon from 1987 to 2005. She was also a Minister in the Conservative government of John Major.  Widdecombe was born in 1942 in Dartington, Devon, the daughter of Mary (n√©e Richards), a nurse, and Sir William Widdecombe, 3rd Baronet. She was educated at Dartington Hall School and the University of Oxford, where she read Philosophy, Politics and Economics. She worked as a journalist before being elected to Parliament in 1987.  Widdecombe served as Parliamentary Private Secretary to both Margaret Thatcher and Michael Howard. She was promoted to the Cabinet in 1997 as Minister for Social Security, where she made controversial cuts to benefits and introduced the means-tested personal independence payment. In 2001, she was promoted to Minister for Women, where she became known for her outspoken views on feminism and her opposition to gay marriage.  After the Conservative Party lost power to Labour in 2005, Widdecombe retired from politics. She has since written books on political subjects and appeared on television programmes discussing politics.
