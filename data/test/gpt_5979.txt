Russian state theater "Satyricon" named after Arkady and Boris Strugatsky science fiction novel of the same name.  Satyricon (Russian: Сатирикон) is a Russian state theater named after the science fiction novel of the same name by Arkady and Boris Strugatsky. The theater was opened in 1967 in Leningrad, USSR, and closed in 1991 during the Soviet Union's collapse. It reopened in 2002 in Saint Petersburg, Russia.
