John Jacob (J.J.) Malone (August 20, 1935 – May 26, 1997) was an American film and television actor. He is most noted for his roles as Michael Landon's father on the television series "The Landon Family" (1966–1971) and as Sheriff Tom Brennan on the series "The Virginian" (1962–1971).  Malone was born in New York City, the son of Marie (née Ponzi), an Italian immigrant, and John Jacob Malone, Sr., an Irish-American elevator operator. He had one brother, William P. Malone.  Malone began his acting career appearing in various Broadway productions, before making his feature film debut in the role of Jerry in the 1961 thriller "The Night of the Iguana". He subsequently appeared in a number of films, including "Posse" (1967), "The Towering Inferno" (1974), "Murder on the Orient Express" (1974), and "Dune" (1984), for which he was nominated for a Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actor.  Malone also had a long-running role on the television series "The Landon Family", playing Michael Landon's father from 1966 to 1971. He also played Sheriff Tom Brennan on the television series "The Virginian" from 1962 to 1971.
