Mark Andrew O'Connor (born 10 March 1963) is an English former professional footballer who played as a midfielder. He played in the Football League for Leicester City, Plymouth Argyle, Torquay United and Burnley, and in the Scottish Premier League for Celtic and Aberdeen.  Born in Leicester, O'Connor began his career with local club Leicester City. He made his professional debut in 1981 and spent six seasons with the club, helping them to win the Football League Second Division title in 1984–85 and the FA Cup in 1986. He then moved to Plymouth Argyle, where he played for two seasons before joining Torquay United. He spent three seasons with Torquay, winning the First Division title in 1990–91. He then moved to Burnley, where he played until his retirement in 1997. He made over 280 appearances in the Football League and Scottish Premier League.  O'Connor later worked as a football coach with several clubs, most notably Celtic and Aberdeen. He has also been a commentator for BBC Radio Lancashire and BBC Northern Ireland.
