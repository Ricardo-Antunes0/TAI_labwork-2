The Penal Code of Singapore sets out the criminal law of Singapore. It comprises a main part, the Penal Code, and four supplements, the Drug Trafficking Act, the Mental Health Act, the Sexual Offences Act, and the Terrorism Act. The main part of the Penal Code deals with crimes against persons, while the four supplements deal with crimes against property, crimes against the state, and terrorism. The Penal Code is split into five sections: Part I (Crimes Against Persons), Part II (Crimes Against Property), Part III (Crimes Against the State), Part IV (Crimes Against Sexuality), and Part V (Terrorism).
