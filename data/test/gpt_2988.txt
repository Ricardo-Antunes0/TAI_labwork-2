Parteniy Pavlovich (, "Parthenius, son of Paul") was a Russian writer, translator and critic.  Born in 1892 in Odessa, Ukraine, Parteniy Pavlovich studied at the Odessa Conservatory and then at the University of Berlin. He served in World War I and then worked as a journalist. In 1923 he moved to Moscow, where he became a leading figure in the Russian avant-garde. He wrote poetry, novels, and essays, and was also a translator of French and German literature. He died in 1973.
