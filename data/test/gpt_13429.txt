Born in Yan'an, Shaanxi province in August 1919, Wang Guangying attended the University of Beijing and obtained a doctorate in physics in 1949. He then worked at the Chinese Academy of Sciences and became an associate professor at Beijing University in 1956. In 1966 he became a full professor at Beijing University and served as its vice president from 1971 to 1978.  Wang Guangying entered politics in the early 1960s, when he was appointed deputy head of the propaganda department of the Communist Youth League of China. He rose through the ranks during the Cultural Revolution, becoming head of the propaganda department of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China in 1971. In 1978 he was made an honorary president of Tsinghua University.  Wang Guangying was elected to the National People's Congress in 1984 and served as its deputy chairman from 1986 to 1993. He was elected General Secretary of the Communist Party of China in 1993 and served until his retirement in 1998. During his tenure, Wang presided over the country's transition from a centrally planned economy to a market-based one, and led China into membership in the World Trade Organization.  Wang Guangying died on 29 December 2006 at the age of 87.
