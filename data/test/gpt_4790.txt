Peter Franklin Paul (born September 2, 1948) is an American politician and author who is the senior Republican member of the House of Representatives from Kentucky. He was first elected to Congress in 1994, and has been re-elected six times, most recently in November 2014.  Peter Paul was born in Lexington, Kentucky on September 2, 1948. After graduating from high school, he attended the University of Kentucky, where he earned a B.A. in political science in 1970.  In 1971, Peter Paul began working as a legislative aide for then-Representative John J. Ashmore of Kentucky's 4th congressional district. Ashmore would later become Speaker of the United States House of Representatives. In 1978, after three years as Ashmore's chief of staff, Paul was elected to the Kentucky House of Representatives as a Republican; he served two terms before being elected to the U.S. House of Representatives in 1994.  In Congress, Peter Paul has been a strong advocate for fiscal responsibility and limited government. He has authored more than two dozen pieces of legislation, including bills to repeal and replace Obamacare, to reduce the federal deficit by billions of dollars, and to reform taxation by reducing tax rates across the board. In October 2013, he was one of only nine members of Congress to vote against the government shutdown that resulted from partisan disagreements over funding for Planned Parenthood.
