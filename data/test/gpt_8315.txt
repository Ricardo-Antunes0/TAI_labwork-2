Brigitte Eisenmann (10 April 1942 – 26 February 2010) was a German artist and sculptor. She is considered one of the most significant artists of her generation, and is known for her monumental sculptures, as well as for her paintings and drawings.   Brigitte Eisenmann was born in Essen, North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany in 1942. She studied sculpture at the Kunstakademie Düsseldorf from 1962 to 1965, receiving her diploma in 1965. From 1965 to 1969 she studied at the Staatliche Akademie der Bildenden Künste in Berlin, receiving her diploma in 1969. Eisenmann then moved to Paris, where she worked with Joseph Beuys and André Masson.  In 1973 Eisenmann returned to Germany and founded the studio Brigitte & Jörg Eisenmann with her husband Jörg Eisenmann. The studio has since been based in Essen. Eisenmann has exhibited her work throughout the world, including at the Museum of Modern Art in New York (1987), the Guggenheim Museum in Bilbao (1995), and the Tate Gallery in London (1998). She was awarded the Georg-Küng-Preis in 2008.  Brigitte Eisenmann died on 26 February 2010 in Essen after a long battle with cancer.
