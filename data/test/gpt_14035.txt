Born in London on 8 October 1908, Carlos Blacker was the son of a doctor. He attended Westminster School and then studied medicine at the University of Cambridge, graduating with his MB BCh in 1934. After a period of postgraduate study at the University of Edinburgh, Blacker began his career as a physician at the Royal Free Hospital in London.  In 1942, Blacker was appointed Professor of Pediatrics at the University of Birmingham, where he served until his retirement in 1978. During his time at Birmingham, Blacker also served as Chairman of the British Cardiac Society and President of the International Pediatric Cardiology Association.  Blacker died on 25 October 1990.
