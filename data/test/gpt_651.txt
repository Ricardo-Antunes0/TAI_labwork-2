Streptanthus breweri is a species of flowering plant in the mustard family known by the common name Brewer's rocket.  This plant is native to western North America from British Columbia to California, where it grows in sandy and gravelly soils in areas with moderate to high rainfall. It is a perennial herb producing a branching stem up to a meter tall. The leaves are oppositely arranged, each divided into many narrow leaflets. The inflorescence is an open array of many flowers crowded together at the end of a long stem. Each flower is about a centimeter wide and has five white petals which curve inward. The fruit is a capsule filled with brown seeds.
