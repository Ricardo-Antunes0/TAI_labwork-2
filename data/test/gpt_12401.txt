Moszczenica  is a sołectwo in the administrative district of Gmina Jastrzębie-Zdrój, within Zamość County, Lublin Voivodeship, in eastern Poland.  Moszczenica is a small village situated in the southern part of Jastrzębie-Zdrój. It is the only inhabited locality in the sołectwo. The population consists of about 100 people. The village is located on the edge of a forest, on the banks of the Biebrza River. There is a small chapel in the village. The sołectwo has an area of 2.5 square kilometers and is bordered by the villages of Bystrzyca Kłodzka and Sieniawa to the north, Biała Podlaska to the east, and Żabno to the south.  Moszczenica was first mentioned in documents in 1392. The sołectwo was created in 1944, when it was split off from Biała Podlaska. The village has a population of Polish people, as well as Ukrainians, Belarusians and Jews.
