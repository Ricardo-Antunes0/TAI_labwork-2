Jason White (born November 11, 1973) is an American musician and songwriter. He is the former lead singer and rhythm guitarist of the rock band White Zombie.  White began his musical career as the lead singer and rhythm guitarist for the alternative rock band White Zombie. The band achieved mainstream success with their 1995 album, Hellbilly Deluxe, which produced the singles "More Human Than Human" and "Superman (It's Not Easy)." White left White Zombie in 1998 to focus on his solo career.  White has released four solo albums: 2001's Spiritual Healing, 2003's The Great American Nightmare, 2006's The Return of White Zombie, and 2009's ZombiU. He has also released two compilation albums: 2004's Greatest Hits & Unreleased Recordings and 2007's Anthology: The Best of White Zombie. White has also contributed music to films, including 2002's House of 1000 Corpses and 2008's Drag Me to Hell.  In 2010, White rejoined White Zombie for a reunion tour. The band released their fourth album, World War III, in 2013.
