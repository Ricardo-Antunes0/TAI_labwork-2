Lawrence Golan (born 1966) is an American artist and sculptor.  Golan was born in 1966 in the Bronx, New York City. He received his BFA from the School of Visual Arts in New York City in 1988, and his MFA from Yale University in 1991.  Golan's art is often associated with Conceptual art, though he has also been associated with Land Art and Performance Art. His work typically focuses on the use of materials and objects as sculptural elements, and often includes elements of ritual and performance.  Golan's work has been exhibited in galleries and museums around the world, including the Museum of Modern Art (MoMA), the Whitney Museum of American Art, the National Gallery of Victoria in Melbourne, Australia, and the Centre Pompidou in Paris. He has also been awarded a number of prizes, including a MacArthur Fellowship in 2002.
