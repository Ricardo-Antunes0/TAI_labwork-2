The Hawker Cygnet was a British ultralight aircraft designed and produced by Hawker Siddeley Aviation.  The aircraft was designed as a cheaper and easier to operate alternative to the more expensive Hawker Siddeley Harrier. The Cygnet was first flown on 9 October 1978.  The Cygnet was built primarily of aluminium, with a wingspan of only . Despite its small size, the Cygnet was able to carry up to two passengers and had a cruising speed of 190 km/h (118 mph).  In 1983, Hawker Siddeley Aviation ceased production of the Cygnet, and the aircraft was subsequently replaced by the Harrier II.
