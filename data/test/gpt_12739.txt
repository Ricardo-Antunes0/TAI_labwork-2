Paul Lansky (born June 18, 1944, in Newark, New Jersey) is an American composer, performer and music educator. He is the founder and director of the Paul Lansky Music Institute and the Paul Lansky Composer-In-Residence program at Brooklyn College.  Lansky's compositions have been performed by such ensembles as the Kronos Quartet, the Arditti Quartet, the string quartets of David Zinman and Howard Shore, the New York Philharmonic, the Cleveland Orchestra, and many others. He has also recorded extensively, both as a soloist and with various ensembles.  Lansky has taught at Tufts University, Yale University, Brandeis University, Queens College (CUNY), and Brooklyn College. He was appointed Distinguished Professor of Music at SUNY Purchase in 2003. In 2007 he received an honorary doctorate from City University of New York.   Lansky has been a music critic for The New York Times since 1982.
