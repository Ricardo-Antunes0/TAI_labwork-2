Saffron Walden (foaled 5 March 1996) was a retired Thoroughbred racehorse who is best known for winning the Champagne Stakes in 2002.  Saffron Walden was a chestnut horse with a white blaze and three white socks. He was bred in the United States by Margaret A. Hamburg and raced under the ownership of her son, Michael T. Hamburg.  Saffron Walden began his racing career in 1999, finishing fourth in a maiden race at Gulfstream Park. The following year he finished second in a maiden race at Monmouth Park, before being transferred to race in England and finishing fourth in the Group 2 Listed Champagne Stakes at Ascot Racecourse. In 2002, Saffron Walden won the Champagne Stakes at Newmarket Racecourse, beating stablemate Elusive Quality by one and a half lengths.  Saffron Walden retired from racing at the end of 2002 and was pensioned off by his owner. He died in September 2016.
