Pigpile is a live album by the English rock band Radiohead, released on 10 July 1997 through Parlophone. The album was recorded during the band's 1996 tour in support of their debut album, Pablo Honey (1996).  The album was recorded during the band's 1996 tour in support of their debut album, Pablo Honey. Recorded at EMI's Abbey Road Studios in London, the album was produced by Nigel Godrich and Radiohead.  The album marked a stylistic change for the band, with slower, more introspective songs, and critical acclaim followed. It peaked at number two on the UK Albums Chart and has since been certified gold by the British Phonographic Industry. In 2003, "Pigpile" was ranked number 423 on "Rolling Stone" magazine's list of the 500 greatest albums of all time.  In March 2017, "Pigpile" was reissued as a 40th anniversary edition with a bonus disc including alternate versions and outtakes.
