Reel Moments (sometimes Glamour Reel Moments or just Reel Moments) is a Canadian documentary film. It was directed by Michel Rabagliati and produced by his wife, Monique Rabagliati. The film was released in 2006 and won the Academy Award for Best Documentary Feature.  The film follows a group of Canadian actors, directors and filmmakers as they prepare and shoot their short films for the 2006 Reel Moments competition. The films are all based on true stories and explore different aspects of Canadian culture and identity.  The film has been praised for its intimate and personal look at Canadian filmmaking, its richly diverse range of films, and its portrayal of the creative process.
