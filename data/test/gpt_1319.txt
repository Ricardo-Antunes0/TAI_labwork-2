She was the first African-American woman to earn a doctorate from the University of Pennsylvania. Dresang specialized in the history of women in America, slavery, and the African diaspora.  Eliza Timberlake Dresang was born on October 21, 1941, in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. Her parents were of African American and Cherokee descent. Dresang earned a doctorate from the University of Pennsylvania in 1977. She taught at Temple University and Brown University before becoming the John P. McGovern Professor of American History at Rutgers University in 1993. Dresang died on January 13, 2010, in New Brunswick, New Jersey, aged 70.
