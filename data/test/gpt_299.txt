Typhoon Yancy, known in the Philippines as Typhoon Ruby, was a powerful Category 5 storm that affected the Philippines in August 1993. The storm formed from a monsoon system over the South China Sea on August 10th and rapidly intensified to winds of 205 mph (330 km/h). It made landfall on the island of Leyte on August 14th as a Category 5 storm, killing over 1,000 people and injuring over 3,000.
