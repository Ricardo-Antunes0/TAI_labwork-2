Li Qiang (; born 23 July 1959) is a Chinese film director, screenwriter and producer. He has directed over thirty films since the early 1990s, most notably the acclaimed films The Warlords and Riding Alone for Thousands of Miles.  Li was born in 1959 in Jiading District, Shanghai. After graduating from Shanghai Film Academy in 1981, he began his career as a screenwriter. His early films, such as the crime thriller A Touch of Sin (1986) and the war drama Riding Alone for Thousands of Miles (1999), were well-received by audiences and critics. Li's later films have also been widely acclaimed, including the critically acclaimed drama The Warlords (2003), which won the Golden Bear at the Berlin Film Festival, and the historical epic The Emperor's New Clothes (2009), which won Best Director and Best Picture at the Golden Globe Awards. In 2012, Li directed his first animated feature film, The Monkey King.
