Dana Dornsife (born September 21, 1961, n√©e Dana Dorn) is an American actress, director, and screenwriter.  Dana Dornsife was born on September 21, 1961 in Los Angeles, California. She is the daughter of television producer and director Fred Silverman and actress-director Susan Kohner. After attending Beverly Hills High School, she studied theater at the University of Southern California.  Dana made her acting debut in 1982 with a small role in the television series "Miami Vice". From there she appeared in a number of films and television series, including "The Golden Girls", "Murphy Brown", "ER", "NYPD Blue", "Charmed", "7th Heaven", and "Desperate Housewives". She also directed two episodes of "Charmed" and starred in the HBO film "Watchmen". She has also written episodes of "Miami Vice" and "ER".  In 2006, Dana married screenwriter David E. Kelley. They have one child.
