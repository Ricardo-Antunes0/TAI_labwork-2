Elizabeth "Lizzie" Mahoney was an American screenwriter, film director, and producer.  Born in Buffalo, New York, Mahoney graduated from Barnard College in 1929 and worked as a journalist before turning to writing. Her first screenplay, for the 1936 film The Road to Morocco, was based on the novel by E. Phillips Oppenheim. She went on to write films including Johnny Eager (1949), In the Cool of the Evening (1953), and The Middle of the Night (1956).  Mahoney also directed three films, including Two for the Seesaw (1962), which was nominated for an Academy Award. She produced several films, including Wait Until Dark (1967), Baby Boom (1975), and Cocoon (1985). Mahoney died in Woodland Hills, California in 1998.
