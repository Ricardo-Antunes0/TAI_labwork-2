Christopher Hoag is a composer best known for his orchestral and choral works. He has written music for a variety of media, including opera, ballet, film and television. Hoag studied at the University of Southern California with composition professors Milton Babbitt and David Diamond, and at the University of Michigan with Carl Orff.  Hoag's music has been described as "beautiful, lyrical, and often moving", and he has been praised for his "striking originality" and "expert skill" in manipulating traditional orchestral instruments. His works have been performed by orchestras around the world, including the Berlin Philharmonic, the London Symphony Orchestra, the Los Angeles Philharmonic, and the Munich Philharmonic. He has also won several awards for his work, including a Guggenheim Fellowship in 1982 and a Grammy Award in 1993.
