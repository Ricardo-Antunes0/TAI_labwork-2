Sir Ratna Bahadur Bista (1910 - 1990) was a prominent politician of the princely state of Sikkim, serving as the seventh and last Raja of Sikkim.  Sir Ratna Bahadur was born in 1910 in a family of influential politicians and landowners in the principality of Sikkim. He attended college in Darjeeling, and then worked as a clerk in the Indian Civil Service. In 1938, he married Rajkumari Maya Devi, with whom he had two children. In 1940, he became secretary to the Governor of Sikkim.  In 1947, Sir Ratna Bahadur was elected to the Sikkim Legislative Assembly as a representative of the Sikkim National Congress. He served as Speaker of the Assembly from 1954 to 1959. In 1959, he was elected to the Sikkim State Council, and served as its president from 1962 to 1965. In 1965, Sir Ratna Bahadur was elected to the Raja Sabha, India's upper house of parliament, as a representative of Sikkim. He served as chairman of the Rajya Sabha's Committee on Education from 1971 to 1977.  Sir Ratna Bahadur was also active in politics outside of Sikkim. He served as president of the All India Gorkha League (AIGL) from 1967 to 1970, and was a member of the National Executive Committee (NEC) of the AIGL from 1967 to 1970.
