Denise Kanstrup Dupont (born 24 May 1984) is a Danish professional athlete, who specializes in the 400 metres. She has represented Denmark at the 2008, 2012 and 2016 Olympics and won a silver medal in the 4 × 400 metres relay at the 2012 Olympics.  Dupont is the daughter of former Danish long-distance runner and world record holder Jens Dupont. She was born in Copenhagen, Denmark. Dupont began running at an early age, and won her first Danish national title at the age of 16. She also competed in the European Junior Championships in 2003 and 2004, finishing fourth in the 400 metres.  Dupont made her international debut at the 2005 World Championships in Helsinki, Finland, where she ran a personal best of 51.48 seconds to finish sixth in her heat. She also competed at the 2006 European Championships in Gothenburg, Sweden, but failed to reach the final. In 2007, Dupont became the Danish national champion in both the 400 metres and 4 × 400 metres relay.  At the 2007 World Championships in Osaka, Japan, Dupont finished fourth in her heat of the 400 metres with a time of 51.75 seconds. In addition, she helped Denmark to win their second consecutive 4 × 400 metre relay gold medal with a time of 3:51.11 minutes.
