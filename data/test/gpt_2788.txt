Born in Los Angeles, California, Denmark began her career as a backing singer for artists such as Neil Diamond and Barbra Streisand before releasing her self-titled debut album in 1985. The following year she released her second album, which produced the hit singles "I Feel Free" and "The Boy Is Mine".  Denmark's third album, Room to Dream (1989), was nominated for a Grammy Award. Her fourth album, The Return of Leila (1992), was certified platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA). In 1996 Denmark released her fifth album, Forever Yours, which produced the singles "I Believe in a Thing Called Love" and "When You're Near Me". She resumed her acting career with roles in the films Soul Food and Hope Floats.  Denmark was married to musician Michael McDonald from 1990 to 1995. They have three children: daughter Lillie Rose born in 1991, son Noah Emmett born in 1993, and son Silas Aaron born in 1997.
