Richard Edgar Skeen (March 15, 1906  – January 3, 1984) was an American character actor who appeared in over 200 films and television shows during a career spanning more than 50 years. He was known for his roles as ditzy, lovable ne'er-do-wells, most notably the title role in the TV series The Dick Van Dyke Show.  Skeen was born in Newark, New Jersey, to Irish immigrant parents. After dropping out of school at the age of 15, Skeen worked as a garbage truck driver and a meatpacker. In the early 1930s, Skeen began appearing in small roles on Broadway before moving to Hollywood in 1935. He initially found work in B-movies and low-budget horror films, but gained notice for his comic roles in the 1940s and 1950s. He starred in several popular sitcoms, including The Dick Van Dyke Show (1961–1966), The Andy Griffith Show (1961–1968), and The Beverly Hillbillies (1962–1971). Skeen also appeared in such feature films as It's a Wonderful Life (1946), You Again! (1948), and Kiss Me Deadly (1955). He retired from acting in the early 1970s after suffering a stroke.  Skeen was married four times and had three children. He died of a heart attack at the age of 83.
