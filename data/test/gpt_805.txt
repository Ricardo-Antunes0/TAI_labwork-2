William Morgan, FRS (26 May OS? 1750 – 4 June 1826) was an English mathematician, actuarial scientist and Fellow of the Royal Society.  Morgan was born in Hull, Yorkshire, in 1750, the son of a prosperous draper. He was educated at the Hull Grammar School and then at the University of Edinburgh, where he became a friend of Adam Smith. He then moved to London, where he worked as an actuary for the South Sea Company. In 1802 he was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society. Morgan died in London in 1826.
