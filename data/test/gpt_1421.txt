Martin Werner Walter Horn (née Hoffmann; born 12 October 1951) is a German politician who has been the leader of the Christian Democratic Union (CDU) in the Bundestag since 2008. He was also the Foreign Minister of Germany from 2005 to 2009, serving in the cabinets of Chancellor Angela Merkel. Horn is considered a frontrunner for the CDU's nomination for Chancellor in the 2021 federal election.  Horn was born in Bremen, West Germany, to a Protestant family. He studied law at the University of Göttingen and completed his doctoral studies at the University of Freiburg. Horn worked as a lawyer before being elected to the Bundestag in 1987 as a member of the Christian Democratic Union (CDU). From 1990 to 1994, he served as Parliamentary Secretary for Justice and Consumer Protection. From 1994 to 2005, Horn served as Minister for Foreign Affairs in the cabinet of Chancellor Angela Merkel. He played a leading role in German reunification and was one of the architects of the European Union's Common Foreign and Security Policy. In 2009, he left office as Minister for Foreign Affairs to become Leader of the CDU in the Bundestag.
