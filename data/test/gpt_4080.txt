Gary Ablett Sr. (born 1 October 1961), nicknamed "Ablett the Great" and "The Gazelle", is a former Australian rules footballer who played with Geelong in the Victorian Football League (VFL). He is the father of Gary Ablett Jr., also a professional footballer.  Gary Ablett Sr. was born in Melbourne to parents Norm and Pat Ablett. He attended Wesley College, where he played football for the Wesley football club in the Victorian Football Association (VFA). Ablett played with Geelong from 1981 to 1992, winning three VFL premierships (1981, 1985, 1988) and a wooden spoon in 1984. He was awarded the Brownlow Medal in 1988 and was a member of the Australian national football team that competed at the 1988 Olympic Games. Ablett was also selected as one of the fifty delegates to attend the World Congress of Sports Science held in Vienna in 1990.  Ablett retired from playing football at the end of 1992 and began coaching Geelong's reserves team. He later served as an assistant coach at Essendon, North Melbourne and Carlton. In 2007, he was appointed head coach of Gold Coast in the Australian Football League (AFL), but resigned after just two weeks due to personal reasons. In 2009, he was appointed head coach of Collingwood in the AFL, but resigned after one season due to health reasons.
