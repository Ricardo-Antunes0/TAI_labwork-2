In 1959, Ocran joined the Ghana Armed Forces and served in various capacities including that of an officer in the infantry. He also held various positions within the military including that of Deputy Chief of Defence Staff (1981-1982) and Chief of Staff (1982-1985). From 1985 to 1995, he served as the Director-General of Defence Services.  In 1995, Ocran was elected president of Ghana, defeating incumbent Jerry Rawlings by a narrow margin. During his time as president, Ocran oversaw significant economic growth and reforms, including the privatization of several state-owned enterprises. He also initiated a number of social programs, including a massive literacy campaign.  Ocran left office in 1999 after being defeated in a presidential election by John Kufuor. He subsequently retired from public life and died on 22 May 2002 at the age of 70.
