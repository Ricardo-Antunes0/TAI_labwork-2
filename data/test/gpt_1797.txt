Born in Laurinburg, North Carolina, Black was a lawyer before being elected to the North Carolina General Assembly in 1912. He served in the North Carolina Senate from 1915 to 1923, and was elected governor in 1932. Black served as governor until he resigned in 1935 to become the 15th Chief Justice of the United States. He held this position until his death in 1953. Black is buried at Elmwood Cemetery in Laurinburg.
