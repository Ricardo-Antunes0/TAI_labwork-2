Merrill attended Johns Hopkins University and then spent two years at Oxford University before returning to the United States to attend Yale University. After Yale, he served in the United States Army from 1948 to 1950 and then attended Harvard University where he received his M.A. and Ph.D. in 1955 and 1958, respectively. Merrill taught at the University of Notre Dame from 1959 until his death in 2004. In 1984, he won the Pulitzer Prize for his book Thirteen Ways of Looking at a Blackbird.
