Vincent Alfred Simmons (born February 17, 1952) is an American basketball coach and former player. A graduate of the University of Utah, Simmons was a two-time All-American and an NCAA champion with the Utes in 1974. He has been head coach at San Diego State University since 1988 and has led the Aztecs to three NCAA tournament appearances, including a Final Four appearance in 2006.  Simmons played for the Utah Jazz from 1975 to 1977 and was selected by the Boston Celtics in the first round (16th overall) of the 1977 NBA draft. He played for the Celtics, Philadelphia 76ers, Phoenix Suns and Milwaukee Bucks over nine seasons. He retired from playing in 1982.  Simmons began his coaching career at Weber State University in 1988, where he was named Mountain West Coach of the Year in 1992. He moved on to San Diego State University in 1994, where he led the Aztecs to their first NCAA tournament appearance in 18 years. Simmons was named Pacific-10 Conference Coach of the Year in 1998. In 2006, Simmons led San Diego State to their first Final Four appearance and national championship game appearance since 1963.
