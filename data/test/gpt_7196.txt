Sampadananda Mishra (, born 17 November 1971) is an Indian author, singer, and film actor.  Mishra was born in Kolkata, West Bengal, India. He is the son of writer and poet Ashok Mishra and his wife, the actress and Hindi film producer Asha Bhosle. Mishra graduated in Economics from St. Stephen's College, Delhi and obtained a master's degree in Business Administration from the Indian Institute of Management, Ahmedabad.  Mishra has worked as a marketing executive for several multinational companies in India before starting his writing career with the novel "A Fine Balance" in 1998. The book was adapted into the Academy Award-nominated film of the same name directed by Sanjay Leela Bhansali and produced by Bhansali and Rakesh Roshan. His second book, "The English Teacher" was also made into a successful film directed by Karthik Subbaraj and produced by Gautam Ghose. His third novel, "Sacrifice", was released in October 2009 to rave reviews.   Mishra made his acting debut with a supporting role in the drama "Lakshya" (2003). He has since appeared in several commercially successful Hindi films including "Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge", "Happy New Year", "Zindagi Na Milegi Dobara", "MS Dhoni: The Untold Story" and "Dangal".
