The fifth USS Truxtun (DLGN-35/CGN-35) was a Gearing-class guided missile cruiser in the United States Navy. Named for Commodore David Porter Truxtun, she was commissioned on 16 December 1972 and decommissioned on 1 July 2001.  In early 2002, Truxtun was reactivated for service in the Iraq War. She served as the flagship of Task Force 50, which was responsible for air strikes against targets in Baghdad and throughout southern Iraq. Truxtun was decommissioned again on 1 July 2004 and stricken from the Naval Vessel Register on 23 March 2005. She was sunk as a target off the coast of California on 10 May 2006.
