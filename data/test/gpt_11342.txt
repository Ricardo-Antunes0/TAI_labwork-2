Vermont State Hospital, alternately known as the State Hospital at Montpelier or simply The State Hospital, is a psychiatric hospital located in Montpelier, Vermont, United States. Opened in 1855 as the Vermont State Asylum for the Insane, it was renamed the Vermont State Hospital in 1892 and became a state-owned facility in 1949. It ceased operations as a psychiatric hospital in 1996, and became the Vermont State Museum.  The hospital is situated on a campus that includes the Governor's Mansion and the State House. It has a total of 296 beds and employs 1,700 people. The museum features exhibits on mental health and history of mental institutions in the United States.
