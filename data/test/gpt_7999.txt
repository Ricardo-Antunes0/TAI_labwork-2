Nicole Nadra Baukus (born April 2, 1989) is an American actress, comedian and writer. She is best known for her work as a cast member on the Comedy Central show, The Daily Show with Jon Stewart, and as a writer on the show.  Baukus was born on April 2, 1989, in Kansas City, Missouri. She is the daughter of Brenda (n√©e Herzog), a registered nurse, and Michael Baukus, an attorney. She has two sisters. Baukus attended Shawnee Mission East High School in Overland Park, Kansas.  Baukus began her career as a stand-up comic in 2009. That year, she won the prestigious Just for Laughs festival in Montreal.  In 2010, Baukus joined the cast of the Comedy Central show The Daily Show with Jon Stewart. Her tenure on the show lasted until 2014. During that time, she co-wrote several episodes of the show with her then-husband, Rob Corddry.  Since leaving The Daily Show, Baukus has appeared in several films and TV shows. Her roles include appearances on Parks and Recreation as Toni Topaz and Wet Hot American Summer: First Day of Camp as Megan McCarthy. She also appeared in the films Neighbors and The Five-Year Engagement. In 2017 she starred in the film I Think We're Alone Now alongside John Cusack and Amanda Seyfried.
