Rev. Brian F. Linnane, S.J. (born August 7, 1943) is an American Jesuit priest and academic who served as president of Georgetown University from 1990 to 2000.  Linnane was born in Providence, Rhode Island, to parents who were Irish immigrants. He studied at the Jesuit-run Mount St. Mary's College in Emmitsburg, Maryland, graduating summa cum laude in 1966. He then completed his undergraduate studies at Boston College, where he received a B.A. in 1967 and an M.A. in 1968. He then attended the University of Notre Dame, where he received a J.D. in 1970 and a S.T.D. in 1973.  Linnane entered the Society of Jesus in 1970 and was ordained a priest in 1974. He served as an associate professor at the Jesuit School of Theology at Berkeley from 1974 to 1978, and as a professor at Georgetown from 1978 to 1990. He served as president of Georgetown from 1990 to 2000, during which time the university's endowment increased by $350 million and its budget grew by 25%. In 2001, Linnane was honored with the Presidential Medal of Freedom by George W. Bush.  Linnane is also a member of the National Academy of Sciences and the American Philosophical Society, both of which he has served as president.
