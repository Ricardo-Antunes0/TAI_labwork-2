Lawrence "Larry" Russell Brown  (born June 9, 1935) is an American philosopher and Emeritus Professor of Philosophy at the University of California, Riverside. He is known for his work on epistemology, metaphysics, philosophy of language, and philosophy of religion.  Brown was born in Little Rock, Arkansas, and grew up in the small town of Maumelle. After graduating from high school, Brown attended the University of Arkansas, where he received his B.A. in 1957 and his M.A. in 1959. He then attended Yale University, where he received his Ph.D. in 1967.  After completing his doctoral studies, Brown was a Research Associate at the University of California at Berkeley from 1967 to 1970, a Junior Fellow at Harvard University from 1970 to 1971, and an Assistant Professor at the University of California at Riverside from 1971 to 1977. He then became the Arthur Lasker Professor of Philosophy at the University of California, Riverside in 1977 and retired in 2009.  Brown's main areas of philosophical research are epistemology (particularly concerning knowledge acquisition), metaphysics (particularly concerning the nature of reality), philosophy of language (particularly concerning meaning and reference), and philosophy of religion (particularly concerning the nature of God). He has also written extensively on moral issues (especially concerning free will and determinism), aesthetics (particularly concerning art and beauty), and social issues (especially concerning responsibility and justice).
