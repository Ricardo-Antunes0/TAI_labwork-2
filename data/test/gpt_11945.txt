Stony Man Mountain, also known as Stony Man or the Adam's Peak, is a mountain located in the White Mountains of New Hampshire, in the United States. With an elevation of 5,288 feet (1,587 m), it is the fourth-highest peak in the White Mountains and the 75th highest peak in the United States. The mountain is named for its stony cliffs, which are a popular hiking destination.  The first ascent of Stony Man was made by Gideon O. Martin and Bradford Washburn on July 17, 1892. The first recorded use of the name "Stony Man" was by Nathaniel Hawthorne in his novel "The Blithedale Romance" (1852).
