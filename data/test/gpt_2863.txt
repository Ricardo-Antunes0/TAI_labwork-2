Robert White Creeley (May 21, 1926 – February 7, 2004) was an American poet and essayist who published in both traditional and experimental forms. He was associated with the Beat Generation and the New York School of poets.  Creeley was born in Portland, Oregon, the son of Veneta (née White) and Robert Creeley Sr., a World War I veteran and blue-collar worker. Creeley's parents divorced when he was young, and he and his brother were raised by their mother. In 1942 Creeley married the artist Elizabeth McNeill; they divorced in 1962. Creeley had two daughters, Caren and Jennifer.  Creeley attended public schools in Oregon, then graduated from Clackamas High School in Portland in 1944. He then entered the University of Oregon, where he studied English under Allen Tate and became involved in the campus poetry movement. He served in the United States Navy from 1945 to 1946. After his discharge, he returned to the University of Oregon, where he continued his studies under William Carlos Williams and Robert Frost.  In 1948 Creeley moved to New York City, where he became part of the Beat Generation. He published his first book of poems, "First Light", in 1952. In 1955 Creeley moved to Paris, where he worked as a translator for UNESCO and wrote articles for "The Paris Review".
