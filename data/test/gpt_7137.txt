Cleo Manago is an African American activist and writer. She is the co-founder of the Organization for Black Struggle (OBS), and served as its Director from 1991 to 2003. Born in Cleveland, Ohio, Manago is a graduate of Case Western Reserve University and the University of California, Berkeley. She has written extensively on race, politics and culture in America, and has been a critic of the African American political establishment.  Manago has also been active in the struggles for civil rights and social justice in her hometown of Cleveland. In 1971 she helped organize the Cleveland branch of the Congress of Racial Equality (CORE), and later served as its director. She also played a leading role in the successful effort to desegregate Cleveland's public schools in 1971-72.  Manago is currently a Professor of African American Studies at Case Western Reserve University.
