John Dilg (born 1945) is an American artist who has worked in various media, including painting, sculpture, installation, and video. He is known for his paintings of everyday objects and environments, which often explore the Hasidic Jewish community in Williamsburg, Brooklyn.  John Dilg was born in 1945 in New York City. He studied at the School of Visual Arts and the Rhode Island School of Design, where he received his BFA in 1967.  Dilg's early paintings were abstract expressionist-influenced abstractions of landscape and cityscapes. He began to focus on painting everyday objects and environments in the early 1980s, when he began living in Williamsburg, Brooklyn. His paintings often explore the Hasidic Jewish community there.  Dilg has exhibited his work throughout the United States and abroad, including at the Whitney Museum of American Art (1986), the Museum of Modern Art (1989), and the Tate Gallery (2003). He currently lives and works in Brooklyn.
