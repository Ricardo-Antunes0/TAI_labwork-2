W. C. Mackey is an American author and speaker on leadership and strategy. He is the chairman and CEO of The Mackey Group, a private equity firm. He is also a trustee of the Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts and a member of the Council on Foreign Relations.  Mackey was born in Birmingham, Alabama in 1952. He graduated from the University of Alabama in 1974 with a B.S. in business administration. In 1978, he graduated from the Harvard Business School with an M.B.A.  Mackey began his career at Price Waterhouse in 1978, where he worked in management consulting. He then joined Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meagher & Flom LLP as a partner in 1984. In 1992, he founded The Mackey Group, a private equity firm. The firm has since raised more than $6 billion in committed capital.  Mackey is also a trustee of the Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts and a member of the Council on Foreign Relations. He has served on several boards, including those of Lockheed Martin Corporation, Merck & Co., and National Grid plc. In 2006, he was named one of America's 25 Most Influential Business Leaders by Fortune magazine.  Mackey is married to Jan Mackey and they have two daughters.
