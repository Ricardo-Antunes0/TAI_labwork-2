Málaga  is a municipality of Spain, located in the province of Málaga. The city has a population of approximately 144,000 people.  Málaga is located in the south of the Iberian Peninsula, on the east coast of the Gulf of Malaga. The municipality covers an area of and has a population of 144,000 (INE 2006).  The city was founded by Phoenicians in the 8th century BC and became a Roman municipality in 95 BC. After a short period as part of Granada, it came under the dominion of the Nasrid dynasty in 1090 and later that of the Almoravid dynasty. Málaga passed to the Crown of Castile in 1492 and was given city status in 1501.  Málaga was occupied by French troops during World War II, and was bombed several times. The city was liberated by Allied forces on 17 August 1945, becoming part of the Spanish Republic. Málaga experienced rapid economic growth after 1980, as a result of its strong tourism industry. It is one of Spain's most important cultural and tourist destinations.  The municipality includes the coastal towns of Playa del Malecón and Marbella, as well as Los Banos and Estepona to the northwest.
