Sam Maloof (January 24, 1916 – May 21, 1978) was an American basketball player and coach. He played in the National Basketball Association (NBA) for the Boston Celtics from 1941 to 1947 and the St. Louis Hawks from 1949 to 1956. He was head coach of the Rockets, Philadelphia 76ers, and Seattle SuperSonics of the NBA.  Maloof was born in 1916 in Kansas City, Missouri. He attended Salina High School in Salina, Kansas and played college basketball at Washburn University. As a senior at Washburn, he averaged 20.2 points per game and led the nation in rebounding (15.5 rpg).  Maloof was drafted by the Boston Celtics in the first round (15th overall) of the 1941 NBA draft. He played with the Celtics for seven seasons before being traded to the St. Louis Hawks in 1949. Maloof then spent four seasons with the Hawks, helping them reach the NBA Finals in 1952 and 1954.  In 1956, Maloof was traded to the Seattle SuperSonics where he retired as a player after playing just one season with them. Maloof then served as head coach of the Rockets, 76ers, and Sonics from 1957 to 1966. He is also credited with helping revive professional basketball in Seattle after it had gone into decline following its departure from Minneapolis following the 1957–58 season.  Maloof died on May 21, 1978 at age 68 after a long battle with prostate cancer.
