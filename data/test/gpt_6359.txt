The Abraham Coult House is a historic house located at 113 High Street in Lancaster, Massachusetts.  The Abraham Coult House is a 2-1/2 story wood frame structure, with a gable roof and clapboarded exterior. The main facade is five bays wide, with a center entrance flanked by pilasters and topped by a plain architrave. The interior features a central chimney, three fireplaces, and finely detailed woodwork.  The house was built in 1792 by Abraham Coult, a farmer and miller who served in the Massachusetts Legislature. It is one of the few surviving examples of Georgian architecture in Lancaster, and has been listed on the National Register of Historic Places since 1973.
