49 Cassiopeiae is a binary star system in the constellation Cassiopeia. The primary component, 49 Cephei, is a white-hued A-type main sequence star. It has a average apparent magnitude of 4.79 and is about 99% the size of the Sun. The secondary component, 49 Cassiopeiae B, is an orange-hued K-type dwarf star.  49 Cassiopeiae was first noticed by British astronomer John Goodricke in 1784 and was named by him after his wife's mother, Lady 49 Cassiopeiae. The system is located about 44 light-years from Earth and is visible to the naked eye with an amateur telescope.
