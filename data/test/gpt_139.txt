Thomas William Hiddleston (born 9 February 1981) is an English actor. He is best known for his roles as Loki in the Marvel Cinematic Universe, and James Bond in the Skyfall and Spectre films. His father is of Welsh and Irish descent and his mother is English. Hiddleston attended Westminster School before going on to study Drama at the University of Manchester.  Hiddleston began his career in 2001 with a guest appearance on the BBC's "Doctor Who". In 2005, he appeared in the feature film adaptation of Mark Haddon's novel "Atonement". He played Jed Hargreaves, a role for which he was praised by several critic groups. Later that year, he appeared in the Royal Shakespeare Company's production of Anthony Burgess's "The Clandestine Marriage", for which he was also praised. In 2007, he appeared in the independent film "The Imaginarium of Doctor Parnassus", directed by Terry Gilliam and starring Colin Farrell and Johnny Depp.  In 2009, Hiddleston starred in the ITV series "Life on Mars".
