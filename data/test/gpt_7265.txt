Sparsholt Roman Villa was a Roman villa in Sparsholt, Hampshire, England. It was excavated in the early 19th century and is now a Scheduled Ancient Monument.  The villa was first excavated by Henry De la Beche in 1819, who discovered the remains of a bathhouse, mosaic floors and walls, an atrium, and a hypocaust. The villa was further excavated by James Peake between 1852 and 1854, who discovered the remains of a dining room, a granary, and wine cellars.  The villa is now a Scheduled Ancient Monument and is open to the public.
