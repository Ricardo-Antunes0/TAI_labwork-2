Born on October 10, 1940, in Montgomery, Alabama, Bryant Wright is a Baptist pastor and author who has been active in the civil rights movement since the early 1960s. Wright is best known for his work as president of the Southern Christian Leadership Conference (SCLC) from 1971 to 1973, during which time he helped to organize and lead the successful voter registration drives in Alabama and Mississippi during the early 1970s.  Wright has also written several books about the civil rights movement, including A Time to Speak (1973), Strength to Love (1976), and Journey from Protest to Power (1982). In addition, he has been a frequent guest commentator on television and radio programs about race relations in America.
