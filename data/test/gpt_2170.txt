Marc-Vivien Foé (1 May 1975 – 26 October 2002) was a French actor and director.  Born in Paris, Foé began his acting career in 1994 with a minor role in the television film Les Liaisons dangereuses. He later gained recognition for his performance in Jacques Audiard's 1998 film The Fifth Element, which earned him a César Award for Best Actor and an Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actor. Foé's subsequent films include the 2001 drama Zazie dans le métro, the 2002 drama The Unbearable Lightness of Being, and the 2005 drama L'Enfant. Foé also directed the film My Father's Glory (2001) and the short film La Ballade des gueux (2002).  Foé was killed on 26 October 2002 in a car accident in the south of France. He was 34 years old.
