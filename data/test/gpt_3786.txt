Selected Essays, 1917-1932 is a collection of essays by the British writer and philosopher Bertrand Russell. The essays were originally published in several magazines between 1917 and 1932. The book was first published in 1948.  The essays cover a wide range of topics, including philosophy, religion, mathematics, logic, education, and social and political issues. Some of the most well-known essays in the collection are "Why I Am Not a Christian", "The Ethics of Liberty", and "Political Ideals".  The collection has been praised for its philosophical depth and for its lyrical writing. However, it has also been criticized for its narrow focus on certain topics, and for its outdated views on some issues.
