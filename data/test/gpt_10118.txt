Nate Barnes is an American country music singer and songwriter. He has released six studio albums, two of which have been certified gold by the RIAA. Barnes has also charted nine singles on the Billboard Hot Country Songs charts, including three number ones.  Barnes was born in Fort Worth, Texas, and raised in Jacksonville, Arkansas. He moved to Nashville, Tennessee, in 2001 to pursue a career in country music. Barnes signed to Arista Nashville in 2003 and released his debut album that year. The album reached number five on the Billboard country albums chart and was certified gold by the RIAA. Barnes followed up his debut album with another gold-certified effort in 2006 titled "Lonesome Road". His third album, "Real Life", was released in 2009 and reached number one on the Billboard country albums chart.  In 2011, Barnes moved to RCA Records Nashville where he released his fourth album, "I'm Not a Problem". The album reached number one on the Billboard country albums chart and was certified platinum by the RIAA. His fifth album, "Take a Little Time", was released in 2013 and reached number one on the Billboard country albums chart. His sixth album, "Sing It Loud", was released in 2016 and reached number one on the Billboard country albums chart.
