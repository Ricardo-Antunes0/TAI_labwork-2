2Wire, Inc., was (between 1998 and 2010) a telecommunications company that developed and sold a variety of telecommunications products. The company was headquartered in Springboro, Ohio. It was acquired by Cisco Systems in 2010.  2Wire was founded in 1998 by Curt Richardson and Scott Robertson, who had both worked at Nortel Networks. Richardson served as CEO until his departure in 2006, after which Robertson took over. In 2007, the company bought out its main competitor, Nortel's Embarq division, and became the second-largest provider of broadband services in the United States.  In 2010, Cisco Systems acquired 2Wire for $2.9 billion. At the time, it was the largest acquisition in Cisco's history. 2Wire's products included broadband modems, routers, switches, and wireless access points.
