Doug Grean is an American record producer, songwriter, and musician. He has produced albums for artists such as Sheryl Crow, Nickelback, and Maria Menounos. Grean has also written songs for artists including the Dixie Chicks, Alanis Morissette, and Seal.  Doug Grean was born in Champaign, Illinois in 1967. After graduating from high school, he attended the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign where he studied music composition. During his time at Illinois, Grean met future collaborator Sean O'Keefe and the two started a band called Sunny Day Real Estate.  After Sunny Day Real Estate disbanded in 1990, Grean moved to Los Angeles to pursue a career in music. He began working as a session musician and producer for artists such as Sheryl Crow, Nickelback, and Maria Menounos. In 2002, Grean produced the album "The Very Best of Sheryl Crow" which sold over 4 million copies worldwide.  In 2006, Grean produced the album "The Best of Nickelback" which sold over 10 million copies worldwide. The same year he produced the song "Far Away" for the soundtrack to the film "The Omen". The song became Nickelback's first number one hit in Canada and the United States. In 2007, Grean produced the album "Here and Now" which was nominated for a Grammy Award for Best Pop Vocal Album.
