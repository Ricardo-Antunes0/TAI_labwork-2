Dean Vetrock (born November 5, 1954) is an American politician who served as a United States Senator from Nevada from 2001 to 2013. A Republican, he previously served as the Lieutenant Governor of Nevada from 1999 to 2001.  Vetrock was born in Reno, Nevada, in 1954. He received his B.A. from the University of Nevada, Las Vegas in 1976 and his J.D. from the University of Nevada, Reno in 1980. He practiced law in Reno before being elected lieutenant governor in 1998.  In 2001, Vetrock was elected to the United States Senate, defeating Democratic incumbent Ted Stevens by a wide margin. He was reelected in 2006 and 2010. After six terms in the Senate, Vetrock retired from politics at the end of his term in 2013.  Since leaving office, Vetrock has resumed his legal career, serving as a partner at law firm Cowan & Wood. In 2017 he was named Chair of the Board of Trustees for the University of Nevada, Las Vegas Foundation.
