Montrose W. Morris (March 20, 1861 – April 1, 1937) was an American educator and author who specialized in the history of the Negro in America.  Born in LaPorte County, Indiana, Morris attended the University of Michigan and graduated from the University of Chicago in 1889 with a A.B. degree. He then taught at Hampton Institute and Fisk University before becoming director of Negro studies at Howard University. He served as president of the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People from 1915 to 1922.  Morris wrote "The Story of American Negroes" (1902), "The Negro in America" (1910), "The Negro in White America" (1915), "The Education of the Negro Prior to 1865" (1918), "The Negro after 1865" (1920), and "A History of Afro-American Education" (1927). He also edited "The Papers of W. E. B. Du Bois" (1948–1963).
