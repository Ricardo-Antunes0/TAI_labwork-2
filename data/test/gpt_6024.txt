Cluny Brown is a 1946 American romantic drama film directed by Lewis Milestone and written by Cecile B. DeMille and Michael Kanin. The film stars Rita Hayworth, Don Ameche, and José Ferrer.  Set in the fictional French Riviera town of Saint-Tropez, the film tells the story of American expatriate Carol Stevens (Rita Hayworth) and French naval officer Pierre Beaumarchais (José Ferrer). After a chance meeting on the beach, they fall in love but are thwarted by their differences in class, education, and culture.   Cluny Brown was nominated for five Academy Awards including Best Picture.
