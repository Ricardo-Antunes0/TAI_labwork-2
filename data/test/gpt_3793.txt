Born in 1922 in Cleveland, Ohio, Thayer attended Oberlin College and the University of California, Berkeley. He became a professor of history at Amherst College in 1957 and a professor emeritus in 1991. He also served as the college's president from 1978 to 1982.  Thayer is best known for his biographies of American writers Ralph Waldo Emerson and Nathaniel Hawthorne. His other books include "The Life of Henry Adams" (1949), "The Liberal Tradition in America" (1958), "The American Mind" (1973), "James Madison" (1981), and "The Harvard Classics: The Works of James Freeman Clarke" (1986).  Thayer was awarded the Pulitzer Prize for History in 1981 for his biography of James Madison. He also received the Bancroft Prize in History in 1978, the National Book Award for Biography in 1984, and the Arthur M. Schlesinger Award for History in 1988. He was elected to the American Academy of Arts and Sciences in 1973.  Thayer died on October 3, 2002, at the age of 85.
