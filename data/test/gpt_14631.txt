The Bronx–Whitestone Bridge (colloquially referred to as the Whitestone Bridge) is a bridge in New York City that spans the Harlem River. The Bronx–Whitestone Bridge is the only bridge in New York City that is open to both vehicular and pedestrian traffic.  The Bronx–Whitestone Bridge was originally built in 1918 and was designated a National Historic Landmark in 1961. The bridge was repaired and reconstructed between 1994 and 1998 and reopened to traffic on November 11, 1998. The cost of the reconstruction was $465 million.
