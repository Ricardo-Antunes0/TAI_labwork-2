Charles Dudley "Charlie" Daly (October 31, 1880 â€“ January 11, 1963) was an American politician and jurist who served as the 78th Governor of Massachusetts from 1927 to 1931, as a U.S. Senator from Massachusetts from 1931 to 1949, and as the 6th Chief Justice of the United States from 1951 to 1959.  Daly was born in Boston, Massachusetts, on October 31, 1880. He graduated from Boston Latin School in 1898 and from Harvard Law School in 1903. He was admitted to the bar in 1903 and commenced practice in Boston. Daly was assistant district attorney of Suffolk County, Massachusetts, from 1906 to 1909, and district attorney of Suffolk County from 1909 to 1911. He served in the United States Army during World War I, 1917-1918.  Daly was a Republican member of the Massachusetts House of Representatives from 1919 to 1921 and of the Massachusetts Senate from 1921 to 1927. He was governor of Massachusetts from 1927 to 1931. Daly unsuccessfully sought reelection to the Senate in 1930 and was elected again in 1934. He served as U.S. Senator from Massachusetts from 1931 to 1949. Daly was nominated by President Harry S Truman to be Chief Justice of the United States, but he declined due to poor health. Daly died on January 11, 1963, in Washington D.C.
