Marcus L. Rowland (born 1953) is an American philosopher, and the current James B. Duke Professor of Philosophy at Duke University.  Rowland's research focuses on the philosophy of mind, the philosophy of language, and the philosophy of science. He is also known for his work on epistemology and philosophy of religion.  Rowland has authored or edited more than 20 books, including The Nature of Mind: Philosophical Essays (Oxford University Press, 1996), The Search for Certainty: A Reassessment of the Foundations of Metaphysics (Oxford University Press, 2002), and The Well-Tempered Mind: Philosophical Essays on Thinking, Feeling, and Reasoning (Oxford University Press, 2009). He has also published more than 300 articles in professional journals.  Rowland is a fellow of the American Association for the Advancement of Science and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.
