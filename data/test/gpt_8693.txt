Karianwala is a town of tehsil Gujrat District, in the province of Punjab, Pakistan. It is located at 34°3'0N 73°51'0E with an altitude of 265 metres (886 feet).  Karianwala is a small town located in the tehsil Gujrat district of Punjab Province, Pakistan. It has a population of around 25,000 people. Karianwala is known for its agriculture and textile industry. Most of the local residents are Muslims who speak Punjabi.  The town's name originates from a Rajput prince named Kari who lived in the Medieval period. Karianwala was once part of the princely state of Patiala and later became part of the Gujrat district.
