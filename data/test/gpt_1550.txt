Azam Khan  Swati  (born 8 December 1951) is an Indian actress who has appeared in over 150 films in Hindi, Telugu, Malayalam and Kannada. She has received four National Film Awards and five Filmfare Awards.  Swati was born on 8 December 1951 in the city of Nagpur, Maharashtra, to a Muslim family. She began her career as a child actress, appearing in supporting roles in films such as "Chandni" (1968) and "Kuch Kuch Hota Hai" (1997). Her breakthrough came in the early 1990s with roles in commercially successful films such as "Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge" (1995) and "Kabhi Khushi Kabhie Gham" (1996). In 2002, she received the National Film Award for Best Actress for her role in the critically acclaimed film "Mirzya". Swati has since appeared in several successful films, including "Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge 2" (2003), "Shootout at Wadala" (2007), "Housefull 2" (2011), and "Aarakshan" (2015).
