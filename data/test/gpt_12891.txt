Saving Lincoln is a 2013 American historical drama film directed by Steven Spielberg and written by Tony Kushner. The film is based on the book Team of Rivals: The Political Genius of Abraham Lincoln by Doris Kearns Goodwin. It stars Daniel Day-Lewis, Sally Field, Joseph Gordon-Levitt, and Tommy Lee Jones.  The film tells the story of Abraham Lincoln's attempt to save the Union during the American Civil War. It was released in the United States on November 16, 2013, to generally positive reviews. It was nominated for seven Academy Awards, including Best Picture and Best Director, and won three: Best Actor for Day-Lewis, Best Supporting Actor for Gordon-Levitt, and Best Adapted Screenplay for Kushner.
