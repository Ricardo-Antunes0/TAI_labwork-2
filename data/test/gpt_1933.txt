Pat Jabbar is a producer, musician and actor. He is the founder and owner of SoulFire Productions and has acted in over 40 films and television shows.  Pat Jabbar was born on January 20, 1951, in Akron, Ohio. He attended the University of Michigan where he played basketball for the Wolverines under coach John Wooden. Jabbar was named an All-American in 1972 and won the Naismith Memorial Basketball Award for Player of the Year that same year.  Jabbar was drafted by the Milwaukee Bucks in the first round of the 1972 NBA Draft and went on to play with them for six seasons. He was traded to the Seattle Supersonics in 1978 and then to the Los Angeles Lakers in 1982. Jabbar retired from playing in 1988 after winning five NBA Championships with the Lakers.  Jabbar began his producing career in 1990 with the release of his debut album, Who's Afraid of Virginia Woolf? He has since produced over 20 albums and won several Grammy Awards. He has also appeared in over 40 films and television shows, most notably as himself on Saturday Night Live from 1975 to 1980 and as Dr. Zach Braff on Scrubs from 2001 to 2006.
