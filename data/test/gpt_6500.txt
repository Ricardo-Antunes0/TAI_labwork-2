Josiah Masters (November 22, 1763 â€“ June 26, 1845) was an American lawyer and politician from New York.  Masters was born in Albany, New York to Josiah and Olive Masters. He attended the local schools and then studied law with James Kent and George Woodbridge. He was admitted to the bar in 1787 and commenced practice in Albany. In 1802, he moved to Oswego County, New York, where he continued the practice of law.  Masters was a member of the New York State Assembly (Oswego Co.) in 1806, 1809, and 1810. He was elected as a Federalist to the Fifteenth Congress, serving from March 4, 1817 until his resignation on December 2, 1817. He resumed the practice of law in Oswego County. Masters died in Oswego County on June 26, 1845. He was interred in the Masonic Cemetery in Corinth.
