"(I'm) Stranded" is the debut single released by the American rock band Thirty Seconds to Mars, released on November 3, 2002. The song was written by Thirty Seconds to Mars frontman Jared Leto and produced by the band's then-guitarist Shannon Larkin.  The song is a ballad, with a heavy guitar riff and piano accompaniment. It is about Leto's character, stranded on a deserted island, and his feelings of isolation and loneliness.  "I'm Stranded" was well received by music critics. "NME" awarded it 8 out of 10, praising its "powerful chorus" and "gorgeous production". "CMJ New Music Monthly" named it one of the best singles of 2002.  The song peaked at number 30 on the U.S. "Billboard" Hot 100 chart, and reached number 5 on the U.S. Mainstream Rock Tracks chart. It also reached number 7 on the UK Singles Chart and number 1 on the Australian ARIA Singles Chart. In 2013, "NME" ranked it at number 72 in their list of The Top 100 Tracks of All Time.   In August 2017, Thirty Seconds to Mars announced that they would be playing a special one-off performance of "I'm Stranded" at London's Royal Albert Hall as part of their worldwide trek in support of their sixth album "This Is War".
