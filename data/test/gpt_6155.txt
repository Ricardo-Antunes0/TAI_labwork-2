She was known for her distinctive abstract paintings and her use of colour and light.  Buttfield was born in 1926 in Holden, Northumberland, the daughter of a coal miner. She studied at the Newcastle School of Art from 1945 to 1949, and then at the Royal College of Art in London. From 1951 to 1953 she was a student at the Accademia di Belle Arti di Roma. After returning to England, she exhibited with the group the New Generation at the Institute of Contemporary Arts in London from 1954 to 1956. From 1957 to 1961 she was Professor of Painting at Newcastle Polytechnic.  Buttfield's work is held in several national collections including that of the Tate Gallery and the National Gallery of Scotland. She was made a Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire (DBE) in 1992.  Dame Nancy Eileen Buttfield died on 25 November 2009, aged 84.
