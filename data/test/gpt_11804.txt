Andre' Chandler Woodson, Jr. (born April 25, 1978) is an American former professional basketball player who played in the National Basketball Association (NBA). He played college basketball for the Duke Blue Devils, and was selected third overall in the 1999 NBA draft.  Woodson played for the Golden State Warriors, Cleveland Cavaliers, Dallas Mavericks, and Philadelphia 76ers from 2005 to 2014. He was a member of the United States national team at the 2004 Summer Olympics and at the 2008 Summer Olympics. Woodson was also named to the NBA All-Defensive Second Team in 2007, 2008, and 2009.  Woodson was born in Charlotte, North Carolina. He attended Pine Forest High School in Forest City, North Carolina. As a senior, he averaged 21.5 points, 10.0 rebounds and 4.8 assists per game while leading Pine Forest to a 32–2 record and a state championship.  Woodson played college basketball for Duke University from 1997 to 1999. During his three seasons with the Blue Devils, he was a two-time consensus first-team All-American and led Duke to back-to-back NCAA Tournament appearances (1998–1999). He was also a two-time Naismith College Player of the Year (1998 and 1999).  On June 27, 1999, Woodson was drafted third overall by the Golden State Warriors in the 1999 NBA draft.
