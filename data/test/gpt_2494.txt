Hangleton Manor Inn, the adjoining Old Manor House and the Swan Inn are all Grade II* listed buildings.   The Manor dates back to the 12th century when it was granted to the de Hangleton family. The present building was built in 1603 by Sir John Hangleton of Hangleton Hall, Huntingdonshire. The Manor was later acquired by the Duke of Westminster who sold it in 1995 to the current owners.  The Manor is now a hotel with 25 bedrooms, a restaurant and a bar. The hotel has been awarded two AA Rosettes and is recognised as a nationally recognised four-star hotel. The Old Manor House is also a listed building, dating from the 15th century, and is now used as a conference centre. The Swan Inn is a Grade II* listed building dating from circa 1700 and is now a public house.
