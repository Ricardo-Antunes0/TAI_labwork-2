The Life Chain is an anti-abortion social movement founded in the United States in the early 1990s. It is a pro-life movement that uses the visual and textual arts to communicate its message.  The Life Chain movement was founded in the early 1990s by Marianna De Marco and Catherine Glenn Foster. The movement uses the visual and textual arts to communicate its message of pro-life activism. Life Chain artists create public artworks, such as murals, billboards, and T-shirts, that create awareness of the Life Chain message. Their works have been displayed in many states across the U.S., as well as in Canada, Europe, and Asia.  Life Chain activists work to oppose abortion through educational programs, lobbying efforts, and social media campaigns. They also work to support pregnant women and new mothers by providing resources and support services.
