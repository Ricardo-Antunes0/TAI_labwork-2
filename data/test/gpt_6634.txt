Kumar Bishwajit (born 1 June 1963) is an Indian film actor and producer who appears in Hindi-language films. He has acted in over 150 films in a career spanning over three decades. He is known for his comic timing and for his portrayals of dashing, cocky, and charming villains.  Kumar Bishwajit was born on 1 June 1963 in Muzaffarnagar, Uttar Pradesh, India. His father, Late Shri Jagjivan Ram Bishwajit was a well-known journalist andwriter. Kumar Bishwajit did his schooling from DAV Public School, Muzaffarnagar. After completing his graduation from St. Stephen's College, Delhi University in 1987, he worked as an advertising executive before making his acting debut in the 1988 Hindi-language film "Aagman".  Kumar Bishwajit has appeared in over 150 Hindi-language films and has won several awards, including the Filmfare Award for Best Villain for his performance in the 2000 film "Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge". He also won critical acclaim for his performances in the 2012 films "Queen" and "The Lunchbox". In 2017, he starred in the critically acclaimed crime drama "Rangeela Raja".
