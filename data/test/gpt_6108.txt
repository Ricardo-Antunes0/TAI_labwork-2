She received her B.A. from the University of Pennsylvania in 1923 and her LL.B. from the University of Pennsylvania Law School in 1925.  Eisenberg was admitted to the bar in Pennsylvania in 1925 and commenced practice in Philadelphia. In 1937 she became a member of the District of Columbia Bar. During World War II, Eisenberg served as a judge advocate general in the army and was awarded the Bronze Star Medal for her service.  In 1961 Eisenberg became an Associate Justice of the United States Supreme Court, serving until her retirement on May 26, 1981. Eisenberg was a member of the Judicial Conference of the United States from 1970 to 1971 and served as president of that body from 1970 to 1971.  Eisenberg died on February 3, 1996, in Washington, D.C., at age 95.
