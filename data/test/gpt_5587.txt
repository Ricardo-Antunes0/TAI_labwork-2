Kunduru Jana Reddy (born 20 June 1946) is an Indian politician and Member of Parliament from Andhra Pradesh. He is a member of the All India Congress Committee and belongs to the Telugu Desam Party.  Kunduru Jana Reddy was born on 20 June 1946 in Dharmavaram, Andhra Pradesh. He completed his schooling from St. John's School, Dharmavaram. He then studied at the Madras Hindu College and graduated with a B.A. degree in 1967. After completing his college education, Reddy started his career as a teacher in a school in Dharmavaram.  In 1977, Reddy was elected to the Andhra Pradesh Legislative Assembly as a candidate of the Congress party. He served as Minister of Forests and Wildlife from 1978 to 1980, Minister of Rural Development from 1984 to 1985, and Minister of Industries and Commerce from 1985 to 1987. In 1989, he was elected to the Rajya Sabha, where he served until 1996.  In 1996, Reddy was elected to the Lok Sabha as a candidate of the Congress party. He served as Minister of Chemicals and Fertilizers from 1998 to 2000, Minister of Petroleum and Natural Gas from 2000 to 2002, and Minister of Textiles from 2002 to 2004. In 2004, he was re-elected to the Lok Sabha as a candidate of the Telugu Desam Party.  In 2009, Reddy was arrested on charges of bribery and corruption while he was serving as the Minister of Petroleum
