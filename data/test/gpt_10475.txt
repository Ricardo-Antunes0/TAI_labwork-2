Karnam Chengalvaraya Pillai (1900–1970) was a bishop, educator and social reformer in the Indian state of Tamil Nadu. He is best known for his work in the fields of education and social welfare. Pillai was also a prolific writer, publishing several books and articles on a wide range of topics.  Born in 1900 into a conservative Tamil Brahmin family in Kurinji village, near Thanjavur, Pillai received his early education at the Thanjavur Hindu School and later at the Madras Christian College, where he graduated with a BA degree in 1923. After completing his studies, he served as a teacher at the Hindu College, Thanjavur from 1923 to 1925. In 1925, he began his theological studies at the Theological College, Madras, where he completed his BD degree in 1928. Pillai then served as an assistant professor at the Madras Christian College from 1928 to 1929 and as the headmaster of the Hindu School, Kumbakonam from 1929 to 1931.  In 1931, Pillai was appointed the dean of the newly formed Madras Christian College Law College, a post he held until 1935. During this time, he also served as the secretary of the Indian National Congress's Tamil Nadu unit (1932–1935). In 1935, Pillai was appointed the bishop of the newly created Diocese of Thanjavur.
