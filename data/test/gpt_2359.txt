Nicholas Tarrier, FBA, FBPsS (born 1 May 1966) is a British historian and academic. He is Professor of Modern British History at the University of Cambridge and Director of the Cambridge History Centre.  Nicholas Tarrier was born on 1 May 1966 in London, England. He was educated at Westminster School and Trinity College, Cambridge, where he graduated with a Bachelor of Arts (BA) in 1987 and a Master of Arts (MA) in 1990. He then undertook postgraduate studies at King's College, Cambridge, where he completed his Doctor of Philosophy (DPhil) in 1995.  Tarrier has been a Fellow of Trinity College since 1993 and a Fellow of King's College since 1999. He became a Professor of Modern British History at the University of Cambridge in 2006. He has also been Director of the Cambridge History Centre since 2010.  Tarrier's research focuses on the history of Britain during the 20th century. His publications include books on the history of Labour Party politics, the history of the British Empire, and the history of twentieth-century British society. He has also written articles for various journals, including The Times Literary Supplement, The Guardian, and The Historical Journal.  Tarrier was elected a Fellow of the British Academy (FBA) in 2010 and a Fellow of the British Association for the Study of Social Problems (FBPsS) in 2013.
