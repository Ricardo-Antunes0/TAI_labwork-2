She is best known for her work as editor of The Feminist Review from 1923 to 1951.  Johnson grew up in a Quaker family in Bangor, Maine. After graduating from Wellesley College in 1911, she taught school for a year before moving to New York City to study at the Sorbonne. There she met and married Reginald Johnson, with whom she had two children.  In 1923 Johnson became the editor of The Feminist Review, a journal she would lead until 1951. During her tenure, the journal became one of the leading voices of feminism in America. Johnson also wrote extensively on feminist topics, publishing such books as The Feminine Mystique (1962) and Woman's Place (1963). She was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President John F. Kennedy in 1963.
