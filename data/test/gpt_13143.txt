She is known for her work in social cognition and social psychology, as well as for her contributions to feminist theory and activism.  Born in Chicago, Illinois, Harackiewicz earned her B.A. from the University of Michigan in 1976, and her M.A. and Ph.D. from the University of California, Berkeley in 1979 and 1982, respectively. She has been a professor at Yale University since 1984, where she is also the Director of the Yale Center for Social and Emotional Learning.  Harackiewicz's research has focused on social cognition and social psychology, with particular focus on the role of emotions in social interaction. Her work has been published in journals such as "Journal of Personality and Social Psychology", "Personality and Social Psychology Review", "Psychology of Women Quarterly", and "The Journal of Social Psychology". Harackiewicz has also written a number of books, including "The Emotional Life of Girls" (1998), "The Female Brain" (2002), and "Feminist Perspectives on Politics" (2008).
