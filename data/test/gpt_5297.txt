Nomonde Mbusi (born May 29, 1976) is a Congolese singer and songwriter. She is the lead vocalist of the Congolese band, Zouk Machine.  Mbusi was born in Bukavu, Democratic Republic of the Congo on May 29, 1976. She started her music career as a backing vocalist for the Congolese band, Faure. After leaving Faure, she started working with producer Clarence "Fally Ipupa" Mpundu, who helped her develop her songwriting skills. Along with Fally Ipupa and her former Faure bandmates, Mbusi formed Zouk Machine in 2002. The band's first album, "Nomonde", was released in 2004. They have since released two more albums, "Django" (2006) and "Carnaval" (2008).  Mbusi has received several awards for her work as a musician, including the Kora Awards for Best Female Artist in 2006 and 2007, and the African Music Awards for Artist of the Year in 2006 and 2007. In 2009, she was nominated for a Grammy Award for Best Contemporary World Music Album for her album "Django".
