Born near Lexington, Kentucky, Naper was educated in the public schools and then attended Transylvania University, where he was a member of the Phi Delta Theta fraternity. He studied law and was admitted to the bar in 1849, commencing practice in Lexington. He served as circuit attorney for the eighth judicial district of Kentucky from 1850 to 1853.  In 1857, Naper moved to Missouri and commenced practice in St. Louis. He served as prosecuting attorney for the fourth judicial district of Missouri from 1865 to 1867, and as judge of the fifth judicial district of Missouri from 1867 to 1871. In 1886, he was elected governor of Missouri, serving until his resignation in 1891. During his administration, he enacted several measures designed to promote industrial development in the state. Naper died in St. Louis at the age of seventy-eight. He was buried in Bellefontaine Cemetery.
