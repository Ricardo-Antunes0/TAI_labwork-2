Wuppertal (;  "Wupper Dale"; named Barmen-Elberfeld until 1921) is a city in the federal state of North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany. It has a population of over 500,000 and is the administrative seat of the Wuppertal district. The city is located in the Ruhr Area and is known for its steel and engineering industries.  The city was founded in 1872 as a merger of two villages, Barmen and Elberfeld. It became a town in 1888 and a city in 1920. Wuppertal was heavily bombed during World War II, but was rebuilt after the war. The population of Wuppertal grew from 38,000 in 1950 to over 500,000 in 2010.
