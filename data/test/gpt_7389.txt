Anne Malherbe Gosselin (born Anne Malherbe on October 10, 1952) is a Canadian actress and director.  Gosselin was born in Montreal, Quebec, the daughter of Anne (n√©e Gosselin), a homemaker, and Raymond Gosselin, a lumberjack. Gosselin's early theatre appearances were in children's productions with the Theatre du Nouveau Monde. She made her feature film debut in the 1984 drama film "Le Boucher". Gosselin has appeared in films such as "The Sweet Hereafter", "The English Patient", "The House of Mirth", "The Notebook", "Chicago", and "The Descendants".  In 2002, Gosselin directed her first feature film, "A Good Marriage". The film was selected for the Toronto International Film Festival. Gosselin has also directed episodes of the Canadian television series "Heartland" and the American television series "Nurse Jackie".
