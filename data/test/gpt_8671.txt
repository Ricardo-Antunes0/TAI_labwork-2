Antipater (; ; c. 400 BC319 BC) was son of Philip II of Macedon and father of Alexander the Great.  Antipater was one of the most influential and powerful men in Alexander's court, second only to his father. He was responsible for the administration of the kingdom while Alexander was campaigning and played a major role in the decision to invade India. After Alexander's death, he attempted to take control of the empire himself, but was assassinated by one of his own officers.
