Tyrrellspass (, IPA:[ˈbʲaləxˈanˠˈtʲɪɾʲiəliː]) is a Georgian village in the Samtskhe-Javakheti region, located northeast of Tbilisi.  The village is named after the Tyrrell family, which owned a large estate in the area in the 18th century. The family's home, known as Tyrrellspass Manor, is now a museum.  The village is situated on the banks of the Alazani River, a tributary of the Kura River. It has a population of about 400 people.  Notable people from Tyrrellspass include: Gogi Vasadze, Prime Minister of Georgia from 1992 to 1993; Iaia Tsereteli, a Georgian writer and politician; and Tamar Berdikashvili, an actress and former model.
