Sydney John Kearney (18 August 1870 – 9 January 1948) was an Australian politician who served as a Senator for New South Wales from 1925 to 1941 and as Leader of the Opposition from 1934 to 1941.  Kearney was born in Orange, New South Wales, son of Michael Kearney, a farmer, and his wife Catherine (née O'Neill). He attended local school before becoming a clerk in the Lands Department. In 1895 he married Kathleen McNamara and they had four children.   In 1915 Kearney was elected to the New South Wales Legislative Assembly as the Labor member for Liverpool. He served as Minister for Mines and Minister for Labour in the First McGirr Ministry from 1920 to 1923. In 1925 Kearney was elected to the Senate as a Labor Senator for New South Wales. He held the seat until 1941, when he resigned to take up a position with the Australian Broadcasting Commission.   Kearney died in 1948 at the age of 79.
