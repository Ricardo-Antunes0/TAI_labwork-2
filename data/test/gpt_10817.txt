Edward Louis "Ned" Keenan, Jr.  (May 17, 1929 â€“ January 10, 2016) was an American economist who specialized in the economics of development and organization. He was a professor at the University of California, Irvine from 1967 until his retirement in 1997.  Born in New York City, Keenan received his A.B. from Brown University in 1951 and his Ph.D. from the University of Chicago in 1957. He served as a research associate at the National Bureau of Economic Research from 1957 to 1959 and as a research associate and then an assistant professor at Yale University from 1959 to 1967. He was then appointed professor of economics at the University of California, Irvine where he remained until his retirement.  Keenan is most widely known for his work on development economics, which emphasized the role of institutions in economic progress and how these institutions can be improved through market-based mechanisms. He also wrote extensively on the economics of organization, especially in the areas of mergers and acquisitions, technological change, and global competition.  Keenan was a fellow of both the American Academy of Arts and Sciences and the Econometric Society. He died on January 10, 2016 at the age of 88 after a long battle with leukemia.
