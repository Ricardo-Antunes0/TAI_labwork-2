Manchu Pallaki  is a 1982 Indian Hindi-language drama film directed by Ramesh Sippy and produced by Sippy and Raj Kapoor. It is a remake of the Tamil film "Pallavi" (1981), which was itself based on the novel "The Narrow Margin" by Sharadindu Bandyopadhyay.  The film stars Dilip Kumar, Rekha, Sanjeev Kumar, Jaya Prada and Jeetendra in lead roles. The screenplay was written by Salim-Javed, with music composed by Laxmikant-Pyarelal.  The movie is about two brothers, one of whom is a successful lawyer, while the other is a struggling artist. The lawyer brother marries a rich woman and has a son, but the artist brother falls in love with her and they get married. They have a daughter together, but the artist brother's career fails and he is forced to leave his family. The artist brother dies in a car accident, leaving his wife and daughter to fend for themselves.
