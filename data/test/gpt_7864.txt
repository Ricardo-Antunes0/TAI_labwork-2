Ibrahim ibn Ahmad (died 10th-century), was the founder of the Twelver Shi'a school of Islamic theology.  Ibrahim ibn Ahmad was born in Baghdad in 809 CE, to a family of Shi'a jurists. He studied under his father and other Shi'a scholars, and eventually developed his own theological views. He died in 1037 CE, leaving behind a body of work that has had a significant impact on Twelver Shi'a theology.
