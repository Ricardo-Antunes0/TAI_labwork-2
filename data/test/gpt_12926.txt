Samuel Woodford Holbrook (born July 7, 1965) is an American actor, director, and writer.  Holbrook was born in Houston, Texas, the son of actress Patty Duke and actor John Holbrook. His maternal grandparents were the vaudeville entertainer Eddie Dowling and his wife, Lillian (n√©e Dolan). Holbrook's father was of English, Irish, Scottish, Welsh, and German descent and his mother had some distant Native American ancestry. Holbrook has stated that he grew up mainly in Newport Beach, California.  Holbrook began his acting career in the early 1990s with guest appearances on television series such as "Murder She Wrote" and "The Wonder Years". He made his feature film debut in the 1994 comedy-drama "Liar Liar". In 1996, he starred in the thriller "The Craft", which received critical acclaim and earned him a nomination for an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor. He followed this with roles in the dramas "The Mist" (1997), "The Crossing Guard" (1998), and "Patriot Games" (2000).  In 2003, Holbrook starred as Dr. Richard Kimble in the NBC drama series "The Closer". The show ran for six seasons until May 19, 2011.
