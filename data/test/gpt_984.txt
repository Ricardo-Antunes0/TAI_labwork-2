Li Sijian  (died 908), probably né Li Sijing (李斯靖), was an official of the Chinese dynasty Tang Dynasty, serving as the chancellor during the reign of Emperor Zhenzong.  Li Sijian was born in 779, during the reign of Emperor Daizong. He entered government service in 806, serving as a county magistrate in Shannan Prefecture (仙annan, in modern Hanzhong, Shaanxi). In 815, he became a low-level official at the ministry of civil service affairs (吏部侍郎, Libu Sheng). In 820, he was made an imperial censor (司徒, Shiyuan).  In 827, Li Sijian was made the deputy minister of civil service affairs ( deputy head of the legislative bureau of government (立法部侍郎, Lijibu Sheng)). In 829, he was made the minister of civil service affairs. He served in that position until he became chancellor in 836.   As chancellor, Li Sijian carried out a number of important reforms. For example, he eased restrictions on government salaries and allowed officials to purchase land and live outside their postings.
