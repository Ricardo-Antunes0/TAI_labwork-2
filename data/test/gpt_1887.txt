Kathryn Ann Moler (born  1966) is an American film and television actress. She is known for her roles as Dr. Anna Delaney on the medical drama series "Chicago Hope" (1996–1998), as well as recurring roles on the sitcoms "The Office" (2005) and "Parks and Recreation" (2009).  Kathryn Moler was born in 1966 in Cleveland, Ohio, to Barbara (née Gulley) and Jerry Moler, who was a truck driver. She has two sisters, one of whom is actress Lindsay Wagner. After graduating from John F. Kennedy High School in 1985, Moler earned her bachelor's degree from Eastern Illinois University in 1989.  Moler began her acting career in regional theater before making her television debut on the series "Heartland" in 1992. She then guest-starred on episodes of various series before landing her first major role as Dr. Anna Delaney on the medical drama series "Chicago Hope" from 1996 to 1998. Moler also appeared in the film "The Whole Nine Yards", which starred Samuel L. Jackson and Bruce Willis, and had a recurring role on the sitcom "The Office" from 2005 to 2006. In 2009, she reprised her role as Dr. Anna Delaney on the short-lived spinoff series "Chicago Med".  Since 2017, Moler has played recurring character Jeanine Pirro on the political thriller series "House of Cards".
