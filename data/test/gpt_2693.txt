EV Lacertae (EV Lac, Gliese 873, HIP 104827) is a red supergiant star located about 163 light-years away in the constellation Lacerta. It is a probable member of the Upper Scorpius subcluster of the Milky Way, and is estimated to be around 13,000 times as luminous as the Sun. It is thought to have an age of about 250 million years and may have already exhausted its nuclear fuel.
