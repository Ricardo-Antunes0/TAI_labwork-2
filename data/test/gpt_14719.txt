The Nebraska Cornhuskers football team competes as part of the National Collegiate Athletic Association Division I Football Bowl Subdivision (FBS). The team is coached by Mike Riley and play their home games at Memorial Stadium in Lincoln, Nebraska.  The Cornhuskers have been playing football since the 1890s. The first recorded game occurred in 1892 and the team played its first game in 1894 against the University of Nebraska-Lincoln. The team was not officially sanctioned by the NCAA until 1898. Nebraska won their first conference championship in 1910 and their first national championship in 1961. The Cornhuskers have appeared in nine bowl games, winning four. The team has had five head coaches since its inception, most recently Mike Riley who has led the Cornhuskers to three bowl games in his four years as coach.
