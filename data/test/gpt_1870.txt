Sevnica  is a town  on the left bank of the River Sava in the Municipality of Sevnica in eastern Slovenia. It is the seat of the Municipality of Sevnica. The town has a population of about 4,000 people.  Sevnica was first mentioned in written sources in 1348 as "Sevinza". The name may come from the Slavic word "sev" meaning 'peaceful'. Sevnica was part of the County of Gorizia until it became part of the Kingdom of Yugoslavia in 1918. It was captured by Italian forces on 10 September 1943 and liberated by Yugoslav partisans on 5 May 1945. Sevnica became part of the Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia and was renamed "Sevnice" in 1974.  Sevnica became an independent municipality in 1994. It is now a popular tourist destination, with many old buildings and churches.
