Vegårshei Church (, locally: ) is a parish church in the municipality of Eidfjord in Hordaland county, Norway. It is located in the village of Vegårshei. The church is part of the Eidfjord prosti (deanery) in the Diocese of Bjørgvin. The white, wooden church was built in a long church style in 1868 by the architect Ole Wilhelm Kaland. The church seats about 350 people.  The first documented mention of a church at Vegårshei dates back to the year 1124 when it was mentioned as ""Vegarhei kirke"" in a charter from King Inge I of Norway. The present church was built in 1868 by the architect Ole Wilhelm Kaland using designs by the sculptor Carl August Moser. The church was consecrated on 14 October 1868 by Bishop Andreas Grimstad.
