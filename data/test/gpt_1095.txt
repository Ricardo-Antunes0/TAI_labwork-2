Lynn Benesch, born Lynn Benish in Westchester, NY, is an American singer and actress.  Benesch's mother, Elinor Benish, is a music teacher, and her father, Walter Benish, was a dentist. She has two sisters, Gail and Shelley. Benesch was raised in the town of Harrison, New York and attended Harrison High School.  After graduating from high school, she studied music at the State University of New York at Purchase. Benesch began her career in 1990 as a backing vocalist for the rock band The Cardigans. She later released her own solo album in 1996.  In 2001, Benesch starred in the Broadway production of "The Vagina Monologues". The show won her a Tony Award for Best Actress in a Play. She reprised her role in the 2005 film adaptation of the show.  In 2006, Benesch released her second album, "This Is Me Now". The album features duets with country singer Brad Paisley and pop singer Justin Timberlake. In 2007, she starred in the Broadway production of "The Producers". The show won her a Tony Award for Best Actress in a Musical.  Benesch has also appeared in films such as "George Washington" (2000), "Alpha Dog" (2007), and "The Lucky One" (2012). In 2016, she starred in the Broadway production of "The Music Man".
