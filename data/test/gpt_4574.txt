Randy Lamar Ingram Schoenwetter (born October 27, 1963) is an American former professional baseball player, who played as an outfielder in Major League Baseball (MLB) for the Chicago Cubs, Houston Astros, Kansas City Royals and Philadelphia Phillies.  Schoenwetter attended the University of Oklahoma, where he played college baseball for the Sooners. He was selected by the Cubs in the 14th round of the 1985 amateur draft. Schoenwetter made his MLB debut with the Cubs in 1988 and played for them until 1992. He was traded to the Astros in 1993 and played for them until 1997. He was traded to the Royals in 1998 and played for them until 2000. Schoenwetter was then traded to the Phillies in 2001 and played for them until 2004. He finished his MLB career with a .269 batting average, 90 home runs and 492 RBIs.  Schoenwetter has been a coaching staff member with the Astros, Royals and Phillies since retiring as a player. In 2006, he became manager of the Phillies' Class A affiliate, the Lakewood BlueClaws.
