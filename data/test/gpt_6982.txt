Saruq Al Hadid  is an archaeological site located in Kuwait. It is the type site for the Neolithic culture of Kuwait. The site was first discovered by Hossein Nasr in 1937 and excavated by G. S. Sayce and S. D. Woolley from 1938 to 1945.  The Saruq Al Hadid site is located on a small hill overlooking the Gulf of Kuwait, about inland from Kuwait City. The site has an area of and is divided into four parts: an inner area with large, circular houses; an outer area with smaller houses; a central area with a temple and wells; and an outer periphery with outbuildings.  The houses at Saruq Al Hadid were built between 3800 and 3200 BC, during the Neolithic period. They are made of mud-bricks and have round or oval shapes. There are also several small temples at the site, as well as well-built reservoirs that may have been used for irrigation.  Saruq Al Hadid was excavated between 1938 and 1945 by G. S. Sayce and S. D. Woolley, who identified it as the type site for the Neolithic culture of Kuwait. The site was added to the UNESCO World Heritage List in 2005.
