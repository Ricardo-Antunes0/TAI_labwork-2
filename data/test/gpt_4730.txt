Theodore Ginn Jr. (born April 12, 1985) is an American football cornerback for the Carolina Panthers of the National Football League (NFL). He played college football at Notre Dame.  Ginn was drafted by the Panthers in the third round of the 2008 NFL Draft. He has also played for the New Orleans Saints.  Ginn was born in Chicago, Illinois, and grew up in Harvey, Illinois. He attended Harvey-Decatur High School, where he played football and basketball. As a senior, he was named to the Class 3A All-State first team on offense and defense. He also played receiver on the school's varsity team.  Ginn enrolled at Notre Dame in 2003, where he played for the Fighting Irish football team under head coach Charlie Weis. As a freshman, he appeared in all 13 games and made five starts at cornerback. In 2004, he started all 13 games at cornerback and finished third on the team with 82 tackles (60 solo), including six for loss, two sacks, and two interceptions. As a sophomore in 2005, he started all 13 games and led Notre Dame with 107 tackles (76 solo), including 11 for loss, four sacks, three interceptions, and one touchdown. In 2006, he started all 13 games and recorded 106 tackles (77 solo), including 10 for loss, five sacks, four interceptions, and one touchdown. In 2007, he started all 13 games and recorded 95 tackles (67 solo), including seven for loss, one sack, two interceptions, and one touchdown
