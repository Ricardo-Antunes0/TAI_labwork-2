Dhanraj Pillay Yadav (born 16 July 1968) is a retired Indian cricketer who played in 46 Tests and 103 One Day Internationals. He is the cousin of former Indian cricket captain Sourav Ganguly.  Dhanraj Pillay was born in Calcutta, West Bengal, on 16 July 1968. He made his first-class debut for Bengal in 1987 and played for the team until 1997. He also played for Tamil Nadu and Orissa between 1998 and 2001.   Pillay made his Test debut for India against Sri Lanka in February 1994. He played 46 Tests and 103 ODIs for India, averaging 34.79 with the bat and taking 46 wickets at an average of 32.27. He was part of the Indian team that won the 1998 World Cup and the 2000 World Cup.   Pillay announced his retirement from international cricket in February 2002. He later returned to play one match for Orissa in December 2003.   Pillay has since worked as a cricket commentator, mainly for Indian network TV channels, and is currently working as a television commentator for the Indian Premier League (IPL) on Ten Sports.   Dhanraj Pillay is married to Sheeba Yadav and they have two children - a girl (Aaradhya) and a boy (Aryan).
