Jill Lepore is an American historian and journalist, who has written extensively on American history. She is the author of seven books, including "The New Yorker"s "Great American History Quiz Book".  Jill Lepore was born in Albany, New York in 1967. She graduated from Princeton University in 1988 with a degree in English. Lepore then worked as a newsroom reporter for the "Boston Globe". In 1993, she moved to New York City and became a staff writer at "The New Yorker". Lepore has been a staff writer at "The New Yorker" since 1993.  Lepore's work has been praised for its insight and ability to make complex history accessible to readers. Her first book, "The Name of War: A History of Two Words", was published in 2001 and was shortlisted for the National Book Award. Her other books include "The Great American History Quiz Book" (2004), "East of Eden" (2005), "War of Dreams: A History of the Vietnam War, 1945-1975" (2007), "Life of Leon Trotsky" (2010), and "Homesick: A Memoir of Place" (2015).  Lepore has also written for the magazine "The Atlantic", where she is a contributing editor. She has also appeared on various TV shows, including "Charlie Rose", "This American Life", and "Real Time with Bill Maher". Lepore was appointed professor of history at Harvard University in 2016.
