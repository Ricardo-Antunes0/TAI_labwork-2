The Mariana fruit bat (Pteropus mariannus), also known as the flying fox, is a species of megabat in the family Pteropodidae. It is endemic to the Mariana Islands in the western Pacific Ocean.  The Mariana fruit bat is a medium-sized bat with a wingspan of about . It has long, slender limbs and a long tail. The fur on its body is soft and silky, and it has a reddish-brown abdomen. It feeds principally on fruit, but it also eats insects. The Mariana fruit bat is endangered because of habitat loss and hunting, and its population has declined by more than 90% since the 1970s.
