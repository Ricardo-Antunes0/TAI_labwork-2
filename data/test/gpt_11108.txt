The Volcae were a Gallic tribal confederation that dwelt in the region of the Volcaean Mountains in the late Iron Age. The Volcaean Mountains are situated in the southeast of France, between the rivers Loire and Garonne. The Volcae were a powerful tribal confederation that dominated their neighbours, the Aedui, Sequani and Arverni. They were also in conflict with the Etruscan city-state of Veii. The Volcaean Mountains were a natural barrier to trade and warfare, and enabled the Volcae to maintain their independence.
