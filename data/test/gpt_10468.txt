Brady Seals (born March 29, 1969) is an American singer, songwriter and record producer. He is a founding member of the band Seal.  Brady Seals was born in San Francisco, California, to parents who were both music teachers. He began playing the piano at the age of four and guitar at the age of thirteen. After graduating from high school in 1987, he studied at Berklee College of Music in Boston, Massachusetts.  Seals formed the band Seal in 1989 with his college friend and future bandmate Bono, who had recently left U2. The band released their debut album, Seal, in 1991 and went on to release six more albums before splitting up in 2002. In 2006, Seals reunited with Bono and the rest of U2 for their Elevation Tour. Seals also released his first solo album, The Road, in 2006.
