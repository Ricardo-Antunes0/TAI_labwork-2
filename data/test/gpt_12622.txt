Born in 1844 in the village of Kunka, Congress Poland, Stefan Drzewiecki was the son of a weaver. He began his artistic career as a draftsman and lithographer, working for various publications. In 1878 he participated in the Vienna Secession show, and two years later he moved to Paris. He exhibited at the Salons from 1881 until his death in 1913. Drzewiecki is best known for his portraits and historical scenes.
