"Young Girls" is a song by American singer Britney Spears. It was written by Spears and Max Martin, with production handled by the former. "Young Girls" was initially conceived as the lead single from Spears' fourth studio album, In the Zone (2003). However, its release was cancelled due to the 2003 Columbine High School shooting.  "Young Girls" is a pop ballad with a neo soul and R&B influence. The song is written in the key of G major and features a downtempo beat. Lyrically, "Young Girls" discusses the protagonist's feelings for a young man and her admiration for girls her age. The song received positive reviews from music critics, who praised its composition and vocals. It reached number one on the US Billboard Hot 100 chart and became Spears' fourth number-one single in the country. The accompanying music video, directed by Dave Meyers, features Spears performing in various locations around Los Angeles.
