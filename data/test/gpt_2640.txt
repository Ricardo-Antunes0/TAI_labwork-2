Rachel Alexandra (foaled January 29, 2006) is an American Thoroughbred racehorse who was the leading money winner of the 2013 racing season.  Rachel Alexandra was bred in Kentucky by her owner, Sheikh Mohammed. She was sired by the stallion Amsterdam, who won the 2000 U.S. Triple Crown and the 2002 British Triple Crown. Rachel Alexandra's dam, Rachel Alexandra, is a daughter of the 1990 U.S. Triple Crown champion, Seattle Slew.  Rachel Alexandra made her racing debut in a maiden race on February 2, 2007 at Gulfstream Park Racetrack in Hallandale Beach, Florida. She finished second behind her future stablemate Sweet Nothings. On February 25, Rachel Alexandra started in a maiden race at Woodbine Racetrack and won by lengths over her only opponent. In her second start of the year, on March 16 at Santa Anita Park, Rachel Alexandra started as the 4-5 favorite and won by four lengths over a field of seven horses after being restrained early in the race by jockey Mike Smith.   Rachel Alexandra's third start came on March 30 at Keeneland Race Course in Lexington, Kentucky where she started as a 10-1 underdog against seven other horses. She won easily by four lengths over Sweet Nothings with jockey Mike Smith aboard.
