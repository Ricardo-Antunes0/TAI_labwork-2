He studied at the Kyoto School of Fine Arts and the Tokyo National University of Fine Arts and Music, where he received his Bachelor's degree in 1944. In 1951, he moved to the United States, where he began his professional career as a painter and sculptor.  In 1964, Kasai began creating ceramics; his early works were influenced by Japanese culture and traditional pottery techniques. He gained international recognition for his work in the 1970s, when he began creating innovative pieces that blended traditional techniques with modern design concepts. Kasai's later works are characterized by their minimalist designs and use of bright colors. He died on January 15, 2009 at the age of 87.
