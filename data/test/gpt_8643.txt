There is a population of Uyghurs in Kyrgyzstan numbering in the thousands, making it one of the largest Uyghur communities in Central Asia. The Uyghur community in Kyrgyzstan has been historically marginalized and subjected to systematic discrimination and violence.   The Uyghurs are a Turkic ethnic group who live primarily in China, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Russia, and Turkmenistan. They are a Muslim minority group that practices IslamVariant of Sunni denomination. The Uyghurs are descended from the ancient people of the oasis city-states of Turfan and Khotan in Xinjiang province.
