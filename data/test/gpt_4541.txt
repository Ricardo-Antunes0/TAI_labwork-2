Gerald Cranston's Lady is a 1924 American silent comedy film directed by King Vidor and starring Dolores del Rio. The screenplay by Vidor and Noel Langley is based on the 1922 play of the same name by Fred Thompson.  Gerald Cranston (Dolores del Rio) is a rich, idle playboy who meets and falls for Lady (Alice Brady). When Lady discovers Gerald's true character, she realizes she can never marry him and leaves him for a more respectable man. Meanwhile, Gerald's friends and family do their best to help him win Lady back.
