Villiersdorp is a town of approximately 10,000 people located in the Western Cape province of South Africa. The town is situated on the banks of the Vaal River, about halfway between the city of Cape Town and the port of Simon's Town. The town was founded by Jan van Villiers in 1878 as a stop on the railway line from Cape Town to Kimberley.  Villiersdorp is known for its wine grape production and for its natural attractions including a variety of forested hills, fertile valleys and lakes. The town is also known for its annual flower show, held in late April or early May. The show attracts tourists from around the country and has been described as one of the best in Africa.
