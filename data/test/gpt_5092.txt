Neuraminic acid (5-amino-3,5-dideoxy-D-glycero-D-galacto-non-2-ulosonic acid) is an acidic, organic compound with the chemical formula NH2C(=O)NH. It is a colorless, water-insoluble solid with a slightly sweet taste. It is found in nature in the form of neuraminidase, an enzyme that catalyzes the hydrolysis of the N-acetyl neuraminidase complex in influenza virus.
