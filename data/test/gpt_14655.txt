Asian Dub Foundation (ADF) is an English-language non-profit organisation that promotes the appreciation and promotion of Asian music. It was founded in 1998 by musician and cultural activist Richard Southerland.  ADF provides education and outreach programs to help promote Asian music and culture, and organizes concerts and festivals featuring Asian performers. The organisation also maintains a online catalog of Asian music recordings, as well as an online archive of texts, audio, and video about Asian culture.  ADF has worked with a wide range of artists, including Osamu Kitajima, Tha Eastsidaz, KRS-One, and Ravi Shankar. The organisation has also collaborated with various institutions, including the Smithsonian Institution's National Museum of American History and the Victoria & Albert Museum in London.
