Light After Dark is the debut album by American rock band The Used. It was released on September 24, 2004, through RCA Records.  The album was recorded at The Blasting Room in Fort Worth, Texas with producer James Iha. It was mixed at The Blasting Room and at Sony Music Studios in New York City.  The Used announced the album's release date on their MySpace page on August 12, 2004. "The Ghost of You" was the first single from the album and it was released on July 9, 2004.  Light After Dark debuted at number two on the Billboard 200 with first-week sales of 107,000 copies. It has since sold over 245,000 copies in the United States.  The album received mixed reviews from music critics. At Metacritic, which assigns a normalized rating out of 100 to reviews from mainstream critics, the album received an average score of 53, based on 12 reviews.
