The discography of Welsh rock and roll artist Shakin' Stevens comprises twelve studio albums, one live album, three compilation albums and forty-six singles.  Shakin' Stevens was born as Harry James Stevens on 17 July 1951 in Pontypridd, Wales. He started his career in the early 1970s as a backing singer for the Welsh group The Sweet, who released two singles before splitting up in 1973. In 1974, he released his first album, which reached number one in the UK chart. His follow-up album, "A Lover's Question" (1975), also topped the chart and contained the UK number one hit single "I Love You Too Much".  In 1976, Stevens released his third album, "Shakin' All Over". The album reached number two in the UK and spawned several hit singles including "I Can't Help It (If I'm Still In Love With You)", "That's What Friends Are For" and "The Middle Of The Road". In 1978, Stevens released his fourth album, "I'm Alright", which became his first UK number one album and yielded the UK number one hit single "I'm Alright". The album also included the UK number one hit single "Whiter Shade of Pale".  During 1980, Stevens took a break from recording to tour with Elton John.
