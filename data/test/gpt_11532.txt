Ahmed H. Tewfik is an Egyptian-American electrical engineer, and the president and CEO of the Institute for Science and International Security (ISIS). He has also served as a professor at the University of Maryland, Baltimore County, and as a distinguished research scientist at the Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Laboratory.  Ahmed Tewfik was born in Cairo, Egypt in 1952. He received his B.S. in Electrical Engineering from Cairo University in 1974, and his M.S. and Ph.D. from Stanford University in 1977 and 1981, respectively. He began his career at AT&T Bell Laboratories, where he worked on digital signal processing and telecommunications systems. In 1984, he moved to the University of Maryland, where he served as a professor of electrical engineering until 2003.   Since 2003, Ahmed Tewfik has served as the president and CEO of ISIS, a nonpartisan research institute based in Washington, D.C.. In this role he has been responsible for directing ISIS's research programs in nuclear security, energy security, terrorism prevention, cyber security, and arms control. Ahmed Tewfik also serves on the boards of directors of several technology companies and nonprofit organizations.   Ahmed Tewfik is an active member of both the American Engineering Association (AEA) and the Institute for Electrical & Electronics Engineers (IEEE). He has served on the AEA's Board of Directors since 2002, and on the IEEE's Board of Governors since 2007. He is also a fellow of both organizations.
