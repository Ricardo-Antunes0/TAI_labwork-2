Elizabeth Gibson was a Union nurse during the American Civil War. She served as a nurse in the United States Army from 1861 to 1865. Gibson was one of the first women to serve in a U.S. military hospital, and one of the first women to receive a medical degree in the United States.   Gibson's work during the war earned her national recognition and acclaim. She was awarded the Medal of Honor for her work at the Battle of Gettysburg. After the war, Gibson continued to work as a nurse and doctor, and became a leading advocate for women's rights. She served as president of the National Council of Women and co-founded the National Women's Political Caucus. Gibson died in 1934 at the age of 87.
