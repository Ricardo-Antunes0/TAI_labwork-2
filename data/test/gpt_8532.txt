Janice Sue Meghan Myers (born October 6, 1978) is an American actress and singer.  Meg Myers was born in Austin, Texas, to a mother of Irish descent and a father of Norwegian descent. Myers has two sisters, one older and one younger. Myers was raised in Edmond, Oklahoma, and attended Edmond North High School. She then studied at the University of Oklahoma, but dropped out after two years to pursue an acting career.  Myers made her acting debut in the 1999 film "The Faculty". Her subsequent roles have included appearances in films such as "Catch Me if You Can", "The Straight Story", "The Family Stone", "Charlie's Angels: Full Throttle", "The Sisterhood of the Traveling Pants 2", "Buffalo Soldiers" and "The Proposal". Myers has also appeared on television shows such as "House", "Grey's Anatomy", "Private Practice", "Criminal Minds" and "Nurse Jackie".  In 2007, Myers released her debut album, entitled "Speak Only to God". The album was produced by David Foster and featured contributions from Diane Warren and Cee Lo Green. The album peaked at number 20 on the US Billboard 200 chart. In 2009, Myers starred in the Broadway revival of the musical Cabaret. She reprised her role in the show's successful 2011 London run. In 2013, she starred in the Broadway play Fun Home.  Myers has been married to actor Taylor Kinney since September 24, 2010
