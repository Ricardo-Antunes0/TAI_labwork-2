Pedino  is a village in the municipal unit of Kilkis, Thessaly, Greece. It is located about 16 km southeast of the city of Kilkis.   The village Pedino was first mentioned in an Ottoman tax register in 1453 as "Pedin". In the 1881 census it was recorded with the population of 114 inhabitants. During the Greco-Turkish War (1919-1922) it was occupied by the Turkish army and suffered damage. The population reached its peak during the 1930s with 297 inhabitants. The population has since declined, reaching a low point of only 21 inhabitants in the 2001 census.  The main economic activity in Pedino is agriculture. There is also a small tourism sector, mainly consisting of visitors who come to visit the nearby archaeological sites.
