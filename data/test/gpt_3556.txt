Haughley is a village and civil parish in the East Riding of Yorkshire, England. The population at the 2011 census was 1,856. The village is situated on the A1039, about 8 miles north of Bridlington and about 12 miles south-east of Northallerton.  Haughley is mentioned in the Domesday Book as Hachelei. The village probably takes its name from Hacheletta, a Saxon landowner who had a manor here. In 1212, Haughley was part of the manor of Hornby and in 1257 it was part of the manor of South Dalton. In 1553, it became part of the manor of Knaresborough.  The village has a Grade II listed church, Holy Trinity, which was built in 1879. It has an octagonal spire and an Arts and Crafts interior.  The village has a pub, The Royal Oak, which is situated on the A1039.   There is also a cricket ground and a football pitch.
