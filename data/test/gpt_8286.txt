The Uman–Botoșani offensive or Uman–Botoshany offensive  was a World War I offensive launched by the Romanian Army in October 1916 against the Austro-Hungarian Army in the Carpathian Mountains. The offensive was a success, leading to the Treaty of Bucharest which ended World War I on November 11, 1918.  The offensive was launched on October 24, 1916, by the Romanian Army with the goal of advancing towards Transylvania and breaking through into Austria-Hungary. The offensive was successful, leading to the Treaty of Bucharest which ended World War I on November 11, 1918.
