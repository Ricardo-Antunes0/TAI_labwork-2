Craig Arend Haupt (born 25 June 1972) is a retired Australian rules footballer who played with Hawthorn and the Sydney Swans in the Australian Football League (AFL).  Haupt was originally drafted by Hawthorn with their first selection, and first pick overall, in the 1992 AFL Draft. He made his debut in 1993 and played for Hawthorn for eleven seasons between 1993 and 2000. In 2001, Haupt retired from the AFL at the age of 29 after playing 153 games and kicking 161 goals. He was a member of Hawthorn's premiership team in 1998.  After retiring from football, Haupt moved to Sydney to become a football coach and was appointed head coach of the Sydney Swans' NEAFL side in 2002. He remained in that role until 2006, when he was appointed senior coach of the Swans' VFL side. In 2009, Haupt was sacked as senior coach of the Swans after a poor start to the 2009 season. He later became an assistant coach at Carlton in 2011.  Haupt has also coached Australia's under-18 and under-19 national teams.
