Brandon Astor Jones (February 13, 1943 – September 6, 2016) was an American film and stage actor. He was known for his roles in the 1970s and 1980s in films such as The Great Santini, The Apprenticeship of Duddy Kravitz, and The War of the Roses.  Jones was born in Miami, Florida, to David Astor Jones and his wife, the former Judith Harris. He attended the University of Miami before making his Broadway debut in the 1967 production of A View from the Bridge. For his work on the play, Jones won a Tony Award for Best Actor in a Play.  Jones subsequently appeared in a number of feature films, including The Great Santini (1970), The Apprenticeship of Duddy Kravitz (1973), and The War of the Roses (1984). His television appearances included an appearance on the soap opera Peyton Place in 1978. In 1985, he appeared as grand wizard Cornelius Fudge in the film adaptation of Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone. Jones reprised his role for the sequels, Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (2002) and Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows – Part 2 (2011).  Jones died from cancer on September 6, 2016 at his home in Los Angeles at age 74.
