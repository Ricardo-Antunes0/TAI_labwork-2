Kelly David McCullough (born 1967) is a United States District Judge of the United States District Court for the Eastern District of Pennsylvania.  Born in 1967, McCullough received a Bachelor of Arts degree from Dickinson College in 1989 and a Juris Doctor from the University of Pennsylvania Law School in 1992. He was a law clerk to then-Judge Joseph H. Greenfield of the United States District Court for the Eastern District of Pennsylvania from 1992 to 1993. He then served as an assistant district attorney for the Eastern District of Pennsylvania from 1993 to 1998, during which time he was an associate deputy attorney general.  McCullough was nominated by President Bill Clinton on January 20, 1998, to a seat on the United States District Court for the Eastern District of Pennsylvania vacated by Judge Arlene Bloch. He was confirmed by the United States Senate on March 27, 1998, and received his commission on March 28. He served as Chief Judge from January 1, 2009, to December 31, 2016.
