Richard Parkyn (c. 1772 - May 28, 1851) was a New York merchant and politician who served as the sixth Mayor of New York City.  Parkyn was born in 1772 in what is now Rye, West Sussex, England. After emigrating to the United States, Parkyn became a successful merchant. In 1824, he was elected Mayor of New York City, serving until his death in 1851. Parkyn was a proponent of municipal reform and was known for his outspokenness. He is buried at Green-Wood Cemetery in Brooklyn.
