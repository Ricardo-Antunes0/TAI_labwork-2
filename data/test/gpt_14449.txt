The Engadin  or Engadine ( , , ; ; ; ) is one of the four main valleys of the Swiss Alps. It is located in the canton of Graubünden, between the Bernese Alps to the north and the Ticino Alps to the south. The Engadin is long, wide and has a total area of about . It is drained by the River Inn and its tributaries, including the Isère.  The Engadin is dotted with villages and small towns, many of which are linked by a network of ski descents, hiking trails and mountain passes. The highest point in the Engadin is Piz Bernina at . The valley has a population of about 30,000 people.
