Richard Anthony Hudson FBA (born 18 September 1947) is a British linguist and Professor of Linguistics at University College London. He specialises in Modern English syntax and semantics, with a focus on pragmatic aspects.  Hudson was educated at Winchester College and then at Trinity College, Cambridge, where he received his PhD in 1975 for a thesis on the syntax of conditional statements in English. He joined University College London in 1977, becoming Professor of Linguistics in 2001.  Hudson has been a Visiting Professor at the University of California, Berkeley, the University of Michigan, and the University of Texas at Austin. He is also a Fellow of the British Academy and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.  He has written or edited over 60 books, including works on syntax, semantics, pragmatics and discourse analysis. His most recent book is Pragmatics: From Language to Interaction (Cambridge University Press, 2012).
