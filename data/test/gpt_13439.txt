Tash-Kömür (,  Tash-Kumyr) is one of the most important Turkish wines. It is a dry red wine made from the grape Tashkömür, which grows in the districts of Konya and Isparta in central Turkey.  The wine is named after the town of Tash in Konya Province, where the grapes are usually grown. It is usually made from a blend of around 60% Tashkömür and 40% Pinot noir.
