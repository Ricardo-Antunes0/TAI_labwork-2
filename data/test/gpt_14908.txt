Maurine Whipple (January 20, 1903 – April 2, 2002) was an American actress and singer.  Whipple was born in Los Angeles, California, the daughter of Harry and Lillian (née Savin) Whipple. She began her career as a child actress in silent films. In 1933, she made her Broadway debut in the original production of Rodgers and Hammerstein's Oklahoma! She went on to appear in more than fifty Broadway shows, including South Pacific, The Sound of Music, Gypsy, and The King and I. She also made appearances in films throughout her career, most notably as Lady Macbeth in the 1941 film version of Macbeth.  Whipple married actor Rex Harrison in 1942. They had one child, actress Liza Minelli. After Harrison's death in 1985, Whipple retired from acting. She died at the age of 98 in 2002.
