Harstad Church  is a parish church in the municipality of Harstad, in Hordaland county, Norway. The church is located in the village of Harstadvåg. The white, wooden church was built in a long church style in 1892 by the architect Ole Bull. The church seats about 350 people.   The first church on this site was probably built around the year 1000. The present church was built in 1892 by Ole Bull, who also designed the nearby Harstad Folk High School. The church is located in the village of Harstadvåg, about east of the village of Harstad and about south of the municipal center of Bergen. The white, wooden church was built in a long church style.
