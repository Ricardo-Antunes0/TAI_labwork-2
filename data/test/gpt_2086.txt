Goodenia heppleana is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family. It is endemic to Ecuador and known from only a few localities in the Oriente region.  Goodenia heppleana is a small, inconspicuous annual herb growing up to 15 centimeters tall, with woolly-textured, egg-shaped leaves and showy inflorescences of five or six red or pink flowers. The fruit is a dry capsule.
