The east–west Santa Tecla Freeway, RN-21, is a proposed toll road in the Mexican state of Veracruz. The estimated cost of the project is US$5.1 billion. The tollway will connect the state capital, Xalapa, with the port of Veracruz. The planned route runs through the municipalities of Teapa, Tehuacán, Acayucan, Coatzacoalcos and Xalapa.  The RN-21 project was first proposed in 2001 by then-governor Héctor Astudillo Flores. Construction began in 2007 and was completed in 2011. The tollway opened to traffic on July 1, 2016.
