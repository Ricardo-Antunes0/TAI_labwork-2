Li Yiji was born in 268 BC in the State of Qin. He studied at the State of Qin Academy and later at the State of Zhao Academy. In 222 BC he became a tutor to the crown prince of Wei. He retired in 204 BC.  Li Yiji's works include the mathematical treatise "The Method of Loci" and the astronomical text "Precautions for Observing Lunar Eclipses". He is also credited with developing the Liouville theorem.
