The cinema of Iquitos, also known as the Iquitos film festival, is the largest film festival in South America. Held annually in Iquitos, Peru, since 1978, the festival has presented a wide variety of films from around the world.  The cinema of Iquitos was born on September 4, 1978, when a group of friends decided to organize a movie night at the Casino Municipal. The first edition of the Iquitos Film Festival featured six films from different Latin American countries. The event grew and soon became known as the "Iquitos Film Festival."  Today, the Iquitos Film Festival is one of the most important cultural events in Peru and one of the most important festivals in South America. It features a wide variety of films from around the world and has become one of the main platforms for introducing new filmmakers to international audiences.
