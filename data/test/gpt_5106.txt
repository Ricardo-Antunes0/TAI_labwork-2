Yankee Hotel Foxtrot is the fourth studio album by American rock band The Eagles. It was released on March 10, 1976, by Asylum Records.  The Eagles' fourth album, Yankee Hotel Foxtrot, marked the beginning of a new era in the band's history. Founding member Don Henley had left the band in 1971 to form the Eagles with Bernie Leadon and Randy Meisner, and was replaced by Mike Newfield. The album yielded three Top Ten singles – "Hotel California", "Life in the Fast Lane", and "The Long Run" – as well as two number one hits on the U.S. country charts: "Take It Easy" and "Heartache Tonight".  The album received widespread critical acclaim and is considered one of the greatest albums of all time. It has sold over 25 million copies worldwide, making it one of the best-selling albums of all time.
