The Mississauga Steelheads are a junior ice hockey team based in Mississauga, Ontario, Canada. They are members of the Ontario Hockey League (OHL) and play their home games at the Hershey Centre.  The Steelheads are the only junior hockey team in Mississauga and were founded in 1973 as a member of the Mid-Western Junior Hockey League. They moved to the OHL in 2001 and have been a consistent playoff team ever since. In 2009, they advanced to the OHL Finals, but were defeated by the Windsor Spitfires. In 2010 and 2011, they reached the OHL Finals again, but were unable to win their first championship. In 2012, they reached the Memorial Cup semi-finals but were defeated by the Portland Winterhawks. In 2013, they won their first OHL championship by defeating the Kingston Frontenacs 4-2 in the finals.
