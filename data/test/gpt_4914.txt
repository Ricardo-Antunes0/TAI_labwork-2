Tennessee Pulleybone was a band formed in the late 1990s in Memphis, Tennessee. The group's sound was a mix of psychedelic, country, and bluegrass. The band's lineup consisted of John Jennings (guitar and vocals), Jason Isbell (guitar and vocals), Mike Cooley (banjo and vocals), and Doyle Lawson (fiddle and vocals).  Tennessee Pulleybone released two albums, "Tennessee Pulleybone" (1999) and "The Tennessee Pulleybone Sessions" (2001). The band broke up in 2002. Jennings went on to form the band the John Jennings Band with Isbell, Cooley, and guitarist David Ball. Isbell went on to form the band Drive-By Truckers with guitarist Patterson Hood. Lawson died in 2006.
