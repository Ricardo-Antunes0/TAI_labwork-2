Andrew Joshua Hargrett, Sr. (1879–1932) was a U.S. Army general who served in World War I, World War II and the Korean War.  Andrew Hargrett was born on September 16, 1879, in Nashville, Tennessee. He attended the United States Military Academy at West Point, New York, graduating in 1903 with a commission as a second lieutenant in the Infantry.  Hargrett served in World War I with the 26th Infantry Regiment of the 3rd Division. He was promoted to first lieutenant in 1916 and to captain in 1918. He was awarded the Distinguished Service Cross for his actions at the Battle of Cantigny on July 30, 1918.  Hargrett later served with the 31st Infantry Regiment of the 8th Infantry Division during World War II and participated in campaigns in North Africa and Europe. He was promoted to major in 1943 and to lieutenant colonel in 1945. He was awarded the Bronze Star for his actions at the Battle of Bastogne on December 24–25, 1944.  Hargrett served as a deputy commander of the 24th Infantry Division during the Korean War. He was promoted to colonel in 1951 and retired from the Army in 1952.  Hargrett died on September 2, 1932, in Nashville.
