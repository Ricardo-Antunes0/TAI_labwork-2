Ajahn Pasanno (born Reed Perry, Manitoba, Canada, June 10, 1954) is a Thai Buddhist monk and teacher. He is the abbot of the Abhayagiri Monastery in North Carolina, United States.  Ajahn Pasanno was born on June 10, 1954 in Manitoba, Canada to Reed and Shirley Perry. He has been a Buddhist monk since he was 18 years old. In 1982 he traveled to Thailand to study with Ajahn Sumedho and became a full-time monk at the Wat Pah Nanachat monastery in Chiang Mai. He was made abbot of the Abhayagiri Monastery in North Carolina, United States in 2001.  Ajahn Pasanno's teachings focus on the Three Jewels of Buddhism: the Buddha, the Dhamma (the teachings of the Buddha), and the Sangha (the community of monks and nuns). He teaches meditation and mindfulness practices, emphasizing that these are tools for living a good life in this present moment. Ajahn Pasanno's teachings have been featured on PBS's "Nova" and "Frontline".
