Lawrence Hajioff is a British-American rabbi, educator, author, and international speaker on religious pluralism and interfaith dialogue. He is the rabbi of Congregation B'nai Israel in Newton, Massachusetts, and a professor of religious studies at Brandeis University.  Rabbi Hajioff was born in London in 1950 to a Jewish family. He attended the University of Cambridge, where he received his BA in 1971 and his rabbinic ordination from the Jewish Theological Seminary of America in 1973. Hajioff served as rabbi of both the London Beth Elohim Synagogue and the Westminster Synagogue in London before moving to the United States in 1987.  Rabbi Hajioff's work focusses on interfaith dialogue, religious pluralism, and the promotion of mutual understanding between different religions. He is a frequent speaker at conferences around the world on these topics, and has also written several books on the subject. He has also been involved in a number of humanitarian projects, most notably the creation of an interfaith youth center in Sarajevo after the Bosnian War.
