Trevor Richardson Glover (born 26 November 1951) is a British actor and comedian.  Glover was born in London, England, the son of Betty (née Richardson) and Trevor Richardson Glover. He was educated at the independent Westminster School in London. After leaving school he travelled to South America, where he worked as a barman and waiter. He then studied at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art, where he won the Bancroft Gold Medal in 1975.  He made his professional stage debut in 1973 with the National Theatre Company. From 1975 to 1978, he appeared in several television series, including "The New Avengers", "The Return of Sherlock Holmes" and "Randall and Hopkirk (Deceased)". In 1978, he starred in the film "The Abominable Dr. Phibes", which was a critical and commercial success.  Glover's first major role on television was as PC Andy Tennant in the successful police sitcom "Not The Nine O'Clock News" (1979–1986). He also appeared as Algernon Shellard in the BBC sitcom "Keeping Up Appearances" (1989–1995), for which he was nominated for both a BAFTA Award and an Emmy Award. He guest starred on many other programmes, including "Murder She Wrote", "The Simpsons", "Doctor Who" and "Monkey News".
