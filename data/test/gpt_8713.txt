Humble Gods is the debut studio album by American rapper Jayceon Taylor, released on December 18, 2010, by Roc-A-Fella Records. The album was preceded by the singles "All I Know" and "In God's Country".  The album was largely produced by American record producer Kanye West, with additional production from Mike Dean, Noah "40" Shebib and Hit-Boy. Guest appearances include Jay Electronica, Mos Def, Pusha T, Swizz Beatz, and 2 Chainz.  Upon its release, Humble Gods received generally positive reviews from music critics. At Metacritic, which assigns a normalized rating out of 100 to reviews from mainstream critics, the album received an average score of 71, based on 21 reviews.  "Humble Gods" debuted at number two on the US "Billboard" 200 chart with first-week sales of 133,000 copies. It also debuted at number one on the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums chart and number two on the Top Rap Albums chart. As of January 2014, the album has sold 447,000 copies in the United States.
