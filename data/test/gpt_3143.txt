Robert David Gamble (born 9 March 1937) is an American mathematician, known for his work in the theory of probability and statistics.  Gamble was born in Boston, Massachusetts on 9 March 1937. He received his B.A. from Boston University in 1959, and his Ph.D. from Yale University in 1963. After a year of postdoctoral research at the University of California, Berkeley, he became an assistant professor at the University of Rochester in 1965. He was promoted to associate professor in 1967, and full professor in 1978. He served as chair of the mathematics department from 1981 to 1984. Gamble is a fellow of the American Mathematical Society, and a member of the National Academy of Sciences.
