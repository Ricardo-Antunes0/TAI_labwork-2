Chen Tze-chung (; born 24 June 1958) is a Taiwanese politician who currently serves as the President of the Legislative Yuan.  Chen Tze-chung was born in 1958 in Changhua, Taiwan. He finished his primary and secondary education at Changhua Commercial High School. He then studied at National Taiwan University, where he obtained a bachelor's degree in Business Administration in 1981. In 1984, he obtained a master's degree in Business Administration from George Washington University.  Chen began his career as an accountant with the Taipei City Government in 1980. He served as the Deputy Director-General of the Directorate-General of Budget, Accounting and Statistics from 1990 to 1991 and Deputy Mayor of Taipei from 1991 to 1995. Chen then served as the Minister of Economic Affairs from 1995 to 1998 and Minister of Transportation and Communications from 1998 to 2000. Chen was elected to the Legislative Yuan in 2002 as a member of the Democratic Progressive Party (DPP). He has been re-elected twice since then, serving until 2016. In 2008, Chen was nominated by DPP leader Ma Ying-jeou to run for president in the 2008 presidential election, but he lost to Tsai Ing-wen.
