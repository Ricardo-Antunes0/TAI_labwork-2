Peter L. Gluck (born November 19, 1939) is an American composer and conductor. He is the founder and artistic director of the New York Philharmonic's Festival of New American Music, which he founded in 1976.  Gluck was born in Newark, New Jersey. He started his musical career at the age of 10 as a pianist and violinist. In 1961, he entered Yale University, where he studied with Vincent Persichetti and Milton Babbitt. He graduated in 1967 with a Bachelor of Music degree.  Gluck's professional career began in 1967 when he became the resident composer for the Cincinnati Symphony Orchestra. In 1970, he became the music director of the Atlanta Symphony Orchestra, a position he held until 1976. During his tenure in Atlanta, Gluck made his first recordings for the Telarc label.  In 1976, Gluck founded the Festival of New American Music (FOM) with support from Columbia Records. The festival has since become one of the most prestigious contemporary music festivals in the United States. The FOM presents new works by composers from all over the world, as well as performances of classical and contemporary music by the New York Philharmonic.  In 1987, Gluck was appointed chairman of the board of directors for the Metropolitan Opera Association. He served in this capacity until 1995.
