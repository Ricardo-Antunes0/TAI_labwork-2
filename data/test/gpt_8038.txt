Hypercallia loxochorda is a moth in the Depressariidae family. It was described by Edward Meyrick in 1919. It is found in Peru.  The wingspan is about 18 mm. The forewings are light brown, suffused with whitish anteriorly and with a deep purple-blackish costal streak from the base to one-fourth, attenuated anteriorly and edged posteriorly by white, extending beyond the fold before the middle. There is also a slender broad purple-blackish marginal streak from the apex to three-fourths, edged posteriorly by white. The hindwings are light ochreous-yellow, with a slender purple-blackish marginal streak from the apex to three-fourths, edged posteriorly by white.
