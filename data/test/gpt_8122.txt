Patricia Cronin (born in 1963 in Beverly, Massachusetts) is an American author of contemporary fiction.  Cronin was born in Beverly, Massachusetts, and raised in Newton, Massachusetts. She received her B.A. from Emerson College in 1986, and her M.F.A. from the University of Massachusetts Amherst in 1990.  Cronin's first novel, A Tale for the Time Being, was published in 1998 and won the National Book Award for Fiction. Her other novels include The View from the Cheap Seats (2003), The Passage (2007), and The Master's Son (2013). Cronin has also written short stories and a memoir, This Is My Life Now: A Memoir of Loss and Gain (2017).   Cronin has been a Professor of Creative Writing at Boston University since 2003.
