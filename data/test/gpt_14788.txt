Bizarre is the tenth and final  album by the American funk band Parliament. It was released on November 17, 1981, by Casablanca Records.  After the success of their previous album Funkentelechy vs. the Placebo Syndrome (1980), Parliament members George Clinton and P-Funk decided to take a break. They recruited a new drummer, Armando "Pato" Ortiz, and recorded Bizarre at Clinton's studio in Clinton Township, Michigan. The album features funk and soul songs with experimental elements, including electronic music and samples from old horror films.  The album received mixed reviews from critics, who praised its experimental sound but found its lyrical content unpalatable. It peaked at number 134 on the US Billboard chart, and was Parliament's last album for Casablanca Records.
