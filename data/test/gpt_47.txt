South Uist  is the second-largest island in the Inner Hebrides of Scotland. The island is located about 30 kilometres east of Lewis and about 160 kilometres west of Skye. The island has an area of 373 square kilometres and a population of about 1,100 people.   South Uist is known for its dramatic coastline and its many lochs, which are home to many fish and wildlife species. The island also has a wide variety of natural habitats, including mountains, forests, and heaths. South Uist has several settlements, including Lochmaddy, which is the largest town on the island. The island has a significant cultural heritage, including several ancient castles and monasteries.
