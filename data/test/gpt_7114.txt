Éric Caritoux (born August 16, 1960 in Montreal, Quebec) is a Canadian television and film actor and comedian.  Éric Caritoux was born in Montreal, Quebec on August 16, 1960. He is the son of actress France Caritoux and theatre director René Caritoux. His older brother is the actor and comedian Michel Caritoux.  Éric studied at the Université de Montréal where he obtained a degree in English Literature. He then moved to Los Angeles to study at the American Academy of Dramatic Arts.  Éric began his professional career in Canadian theatre, appearing in productions such as The Rake's Progress, The Cherry Orchard, and The Seagull. In 1992, he made his American television debut on the series Northern Exposure as Dr. Mark Stern.   Since then, Éric has appeared in numerous television and film roles, including episodes of ER, NCIS, The West Wing, and Charmed. His feature film credits include roles in The Sweet Hereafter, I Love You to Death, and The Squid and the Whale.   In 2006, Éric released his first comedy album entitled Éric Caritoux: Unplugged. The album was well-received by critics and fans alike and helped him win the 2007 Gemini Award for Best Comedy Performance.   Éric currently resides in Los Angeles with his wife and two children.
