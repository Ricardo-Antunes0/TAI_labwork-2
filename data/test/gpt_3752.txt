Gregory was born in Bromley, Kent, the son of a solicitor. He became a barrister in 1885 and practised in London. He entered politics in 1892, and was elected to the House of Commons as Member of Parliament (MP) for South Norfolk. He held this seat until he was unseated by Conservative candidate Charles James Hargrave-Parker in 1906.  Gregory returned to Parliament as MP for Norwich West in 1910. He held this seat until he retired from the House of Commons at the general election of 1918. In 1920 Gregory was appointed Solicitor General for England and Wales, a position he held until 1923.  After his retirement from Parliament, Gregory served as Chairman of the East Anglian Railway from 1925 to 1930. He died at the age of 79 years old.
