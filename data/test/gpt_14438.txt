Sulgrave Manor is a large Jacobean-style manor house in Sulgrave, Northamptonshire, England, dating from the 12th century. The present building is largely from the late 16th century, with additions and alterations from the 17th and 18th centuries. The house and its contents are open to the public.  The house was first mentioned in about 1194, when it belonged to Ranulf de Gernon. The de Gernon family held it until 1447, when it passed to the Botelers. The Botelers were succeeded by the De La Warr family in 1590, who held it until 1635. The De La Warr family were connected with both the court and military circles of Elizabeth I and James I. From 1635 to 1702, it was held by Sir John Berkeley, 1st Baron Berkeley of Stratton. In 1702, it passed to his son, Sir George Berkeley, 2nd Baron Berkeley of Stratton. From him it descended to his grandson, George Berkeley (the 3rd Baron), who died unmarried in 1756.
