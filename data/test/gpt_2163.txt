U Sports football is the highest level of football sanctioned by the United States Collegiate Athletic Association (USCAA). The top eight teams in the country compete in a 16-team postseason tournament.  U Sports football began in 1978 as an eight-team divisional format. In 1991, the format was expanded to 10 teams and in 2002, it was raised to 12 teams. The 16-team postseason tournament was established in 2006.  The most successful program in U Sports football is the University of Oregon, which has won nine national championships. Other programs with multiple national championships include the University of Nebraska, the University of Michigan, and the University of Southern California.
