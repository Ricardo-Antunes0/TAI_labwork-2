François-Benoît Hoffman (11 July 1760 – 25 November 1842) was a French historian and philosopher.  Hoffman was born in Montauban, Tarn-et-Garonne, France, the son of a notary. He studied law at Toulouse and then at the University of Paris, where he became interested in the history of philosophy. In 1789, he became a professor at the University of Metz and then at the University of Strasbourg. In 1827, he succeeded Jean-Baptiste Say as director of the National Library of France. Hoffman died in Paris.
