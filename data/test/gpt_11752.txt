Ararat College is a public, co-educational high school located in Ararat, Texas, United States. It is a part of the Ararat Independent School District. As of the 2009-10 school year, the school had an enrollment of 554 students and 44.1 classroom teachers (on an FTE basis), for a student–teacher ratio of 10.0.  Ararat College offers a variety of courses and programs to its students, including Advanced Placement (AP) courses, dual enrollment with colleges and universities, and a variety of vocational and technical programs. The college also has a strong athletic program, with teams in football, soccer, basketball, baseball, softball, track and field, and wrestling.  The Ararat College Campus is located on a 100-acre site in the heart of Ararat. The campus has a large gymnasium/auditorium with a stage and sound system for performances and events. There are also two arts and crafts buildings; one for ceramics and one for painting/drawing; a library; three computer labs; two science labs; three music labs; two gymnasiums; three cafeterias; two main offices; two nurse’s offices; one guidance counselor’s office; one president’s office; four classrooms with audio/visual support and six classrooms without audio/visual support.   Ararat College is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges to award associate degrees (two-year)
