Anne Emily Bergl (born 25 April 1975) is an Australian actress, model and author.  Bergl was born in Melbourne, Victoria. She is the daughter of film producer and director John Bergl and actress Anne-Marie McEvoy. She has two sisters, Georgina and Hayley, and a brother, Luke. Bergl attended Scots College in Melbourne before studying at the National Institute of Dramatic Art (NIDA) in Sydney.  Bergl's early work includes appearances in television shows such as "Neighbours" (1994-1997), "Heartbreak High" (1998), "The Secret Life of Us" (2003) and "Packed to the Rafters" (2009). She made her feature film debut in the Australian independent film "The Human Element" (2002). Her breakthrough performance was as the title character in the Australian drama series "Wildcard" (2003-2006), for which she received an award nomination at the AACTA Awards. Bergl has since appeared in a number of high-profile Australian films, such as "Happy Feet Two" (2011), "The Lucky One" (2012), "The Railway Man" (2014) and "The Dressmaker" (2015).  In 2014, Bergl was cast as the lead role of Detective Emily Bishop in the ABC crime drama series "Revenge". The show premiered on 25 September 2014 and ended on 15 May 2016.
