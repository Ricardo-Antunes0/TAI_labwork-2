Gwilym Owen Williams (23 March 1913 – 31 October 1993) was an English actor, who appeared in more than sixty films from the 1930s to the 1970s.  Williams was born in Cardiff, Wales, the son of Gwilym Owen Williams and Ada (née Griffiths). He made his stage début at the age of sixteen with a performance in Shakespeare's "Richard III". He also appeared in productions of "Hamlet" and "Macbeth".  Williams' film debut was in 1934's "The Blue Lamp", followed by appearances in films such as "The Battle of Britain" (1940), "The Seventh Veil" (1946), "The Ladykillers" (1955), "The Dirty Dozen" (1967), "Dirty Harry" (1971), and "Shampoo" (1975). He was also featured in the television series "Boots of Leather, Slippers of Gold" and "The Avengers".  Williams married actress Peggy Ashcroft on 15 November 1940; they had one child, actor Gareth Williams. Williams died from a heart attack on 31 October 1993 at the age of 80.
