DJ Fuze, is an American Hip-Hop DJ and producer from Queens, New York. Fuze is known for his turntablism and production skills, having released several albums and singles on various labels throughout the years.  Fuze started out as a breakdancer and later turned to DJing in the late 1990s. His first break came in 2001 when he was featured on the Busta Rhymes album The Great Indian Rap Album. Fuze has also released several solo albums, including 2005's The Revival and 2006's Unstoppable. He has collaborated with a range of artists, including Jadakiss, Styles P, The LOX, 50 Cent, and Pharrell Williams. In recent years he has focused on production work, releasing the album The Prologue in 2010 and the mixtape In Memory of Death in 2011.  Fuze is also known for his involvement in the hip-hop community. He has been involved in charity work and has performed at numerous events such as the Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade and the BET Awards.
