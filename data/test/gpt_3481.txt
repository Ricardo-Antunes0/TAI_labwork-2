József Vészi, (born: Joseph Weiss; November 6, 1858, Budapest, Hungary—died: October 27, 1935, Budapest), Hungarian mathematician who solved the Fermat equation in a single stroke.  József Vészi was born in Budapest on November 6, 1858. After studying at the universities of Vienna and Göttingen, he became a professor at the University of Budapest in 1892. In 1897 he solved the Fermat equation in a single stroke. This achievement made him one of the most famous mathematicians of his time and earned him the title of "the Hungarian Mozart." Vészi died on October 27, 1935, in Budapest.
