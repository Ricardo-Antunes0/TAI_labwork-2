Étienne Decroux (19 July 1898 in Paris, France – 2 October 1970 in Paris, France) was a French economist and Professor of Economics at the University of Paris. He was also the co-founder of the Institut d'Études Politiques (IP), which became one of the most influential think tanks in France.  Decroux is best known for his work on general equilibrium theory, which has been influential in the development of modern economics. He also made important contributions to economic theory and to macroeconomic policymaking, and is considered one of the founders of the Keynesian school of economics.
