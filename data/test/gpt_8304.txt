Kavita Bala was born in Mumbai (formerly Bombay), India, in 1965. She is a sculptor and printmaker.  Kavita Bala studied at the National Institute of Design, Ahmedabad from 1982 to 1986, where she was influenced by sculptors such as Anish Kapoor and Vivan Sundaram. She then went to the Rhode Island School of Design, in Providence, USA, where she studied sculpture with Tony Smith and printmaking with Gary Hill.  Bala's first solo exhibition was held at the Mumbai-based gallery Paragon in 1998. Her work is in the collections of the MFA Museum, Boston; the Art Institute of Chicago; and the Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum, New York. In 2012 she was awarded the Padma Shri by the Government of India.  Kavita Bala's sculptures are often site-specific and involve a collaboration between artist and natural environment. She has made works using concrete, metal, wood, and glass, as well as textile materials. Her prints are often screenprints or silkscreen prints made on large sheets of paper.
