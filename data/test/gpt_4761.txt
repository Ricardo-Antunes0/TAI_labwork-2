Phrynocephalus vlangalii, also known as the Qinghai giant squirrel, is a species of giant squirrel found in the Qinghai province of China. It is the largest known member of the family Phrynocephalidae and can weigh up to 18 kilograms (40 pounds).  The Qinghai giant squirrel is primarily a herbivore, but will opportunistically eat small amounts of meat. It inhabits temperate forests and grasslands, and is mostly nocturnal. The species has a wide distribution, but is threatened by habitat loss.
