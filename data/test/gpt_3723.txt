Saint Illtud (also spelled Illtyd, Eltut, and, in Welsh, Illtud) was a 6th-century saint and early Christian missionary in north-west Wales. He is venerated as a patron saint of the town of Holywell, Flintshire, and of the Diocese of St Asaph.  Saint Illtud is said to have been born in the region around present-day Bangor, Gwynedd, in the early 6th century. He is believed to have died in the year 589.  Illtud is best known for his missionary work in north-west Wales. He is said to have travelled extensively throughout the region, preaching the gospel and converting many people to Christianity.  Illtud is also venerated as a patron saint of Holywell, Flintshire, and of the Diocese of St Asaph. His feast day is celebrated on 1 October.
