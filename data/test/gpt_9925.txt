Joshua Siegel is an American Mechanical Engineer, Professor and Director of the Center for Energy and Environmental Policy at the University of California, Berkeley. He has also been a visiting professor at the University of Cambridge, the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, and the University of Tokyo.  Siegel received his B.S. from Cornell University in Mechanical Engineering in 1973 and his M.S. from Stanford University in 1975. He received his Ph.D. from Stanford University in 1978.  Siegel has been a Professor at UC Berkeley since 1978, where he also served as Director of the Center for Energy and Environmental Policy from 1995 to 2002. From 2002 to 2006 he was a visiting professor at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. In 2006 he returned to UC Berkeley as Professor and Director of the Center for Energy and Environmental Policy.  Siegel's research focuses on energy policy, environmental policy, energy efficiency, transportation energy policy, and renewable energy technology. He is one of the authors of The Economics of Energy Efficiency (CEE), which has been translated into 10 languages and has been cited more than 2,000 times. He is also the author of The Moral Case for Fossil Fuels (Oxford University Press, 2009), a book that has been praised by Pulitzer Prize-winner Paul Krugman as "the most important book on climate change in years."  In August 2009, Siegel was elected to serve on the National Academy of Engineering (NAE).
