William Morgan Beckner (June 19, 1841 – December 8, 1915) was an American lawyer, politician and jurist who served as the 26th Governor of Missouri from 1885 to 1889. He also served in the United States House of Representatives from 1883 to 1885.  Born in Richmond, Kentucky, Beckner attended the common schools and then studied law. He was admitted to the bar in 1864 and commenced practice in Charleston, Missouri. Beckner served as prosecuting attorney for Jefferson County from 1870 to 1872 and was elected as a Democrat to the Forty-seventh Congress (March 4, 1883 – March 3, 1885). He served as governor of Missouri from 1885 to 1889. Beckner died in Charleston, Missouri, and is buried there in Forest Hill Cemetery.
