Black Sabbath were an English rock band formed in Birmingham in 1968. The band consisted of Ozzy Osbourne (vocals, guitar), Geezer Butler (bass guitar), Bill Ward (drums) and Tony Iommi (guitar). They are often considered the world's first heavy metal band.  Black Sabbath are one of the most successful rock bands of all time, having sold over 150 million records worldwide. They have had five number-one albums in the UK, seven number-one albums in the US, and ten number-one singles in the US. They have won three Grammy Awards, four Brit Awards, three American Music Awards, two MTV Video Music Awards and two NME Awards. They were inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2006.
