Patricia Goosey O’Connor (born 2 October 1940) is an Irish educator who was the first female president of Trinity College, Dublin, from 1996 to 2002. She is also the first woman to serve as President of the National College of Ireland.  Born in Dublin, O’Connor was educated at the Christian Brothers school in Rathfarnham and at Trinity College, Dublin (B.A., 1962). She subsequently undertook post-graduate studies at the University of California, Berkeley (M.A., 1966), and at Harvard University (Ph.D., 1971).  O’Connor began her career as a lecturer at Trinity College, Dublin, in 1966. She became its first female president in 1996, and served until 2002. During her time as president, she oversaw major changes to the college, including the establishment of a School of Education and Social Work. O’Connor has also been involved in a number of political organisations, including Fianna Fáil and the Progressive Democrats. She was awarded an honorary doctorate by Trinity College in 2002.
