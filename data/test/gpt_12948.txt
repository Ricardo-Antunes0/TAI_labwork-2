Beatrice Dixon was born in Glasgow, Scotland, on 17 August 1916. She was educated at the Royal High School of Edinburgh and the University of Glasgow, where she received her B.A. in 1938. After graduation, Dixon worked as a teacher before beginning to write fiction in the early 1950s. Her first novel, The House on the Strand, was published in 1952 and won the Somerset Maugham Award. Dixon wrote several more books over the next several years, but her greatest success came with her 1976 novel, The Duffield Girls. She died in 1992 at the age of 78.
