Big Muskie was a coal mining Bucyrus-Erie Corporation (BEC) locomotive. It was designed and built in 1938 by the Baldwin Locomotive Works and was one of the largest locomotives ever built. The locomotive was powered by a single 750 horsepower coal-fired engine.  Big Muskie saw limited service after it was built, mainly due to the decline of the coal industry. It was retired in 1966 and scrapped in 1971.
