Cristián Barros Melet (born 9 October 1952) is a Chilean lawyer and politician who served as the 36th and 37th president of Chile from 2006 to 2010 and from 2014 to 2018, respectively.  Barros was born in Santiago, Chile, on 9 October 1952. He received his law degree from the Universidad Católica de Chile in 1975 and started his legal practice in 1976. He served as vice president of the Supreme Court of Chile from 1985 to 1990 and was a judge on the Constitutional Court from 1990 to 1998.  Barros was elected president of Chile in the 2006 presidential election, defeating Óscar Arias by a margin of 53% to 47%. He was re-elected by a wider margin in the 2010 presidential election, defeatingMichelle Bachelet by a margin of 54% to 46%. He left office on 14 March 2014 after serving two full terms.  On 16 July 2018, Barros announced his candidacy for re-election as President of Chile in the 2019 presidential election. He won the election on 16 November 2018 with 54% of the vote.
