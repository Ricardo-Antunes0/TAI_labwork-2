Gerd Lüdemann (July 5, 1946–May 23, 2021) was a German philosopher.  Lüdemann was born in 1946 in the village of Nieder-Olm, Duchy of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha. He studied philosophy at the Universities of Erlangen and Freiburg, where he received his doctorate in 1976. He then taught at the universities of Tübingen, Innsbruck and Heidelberg, where he became a full professor in 1993.  Lüdemann's work focuses on philosophy of religion, philosophy of science, and philosophy of history. His most famous book is The Miracle of the Market: A Theological Interpretation of Financial Democracy (2003).  Lüdemann died on May 23, 2021 at the age of 68.
