Fran Harris (born March 12, 1965 in Waco, Texas) is a retired American soccer player who played professionally in the United States, Germany and Japan.  Fran Harris attended Waco High School, where he played soccer for the Cardinals. He then attended the University of Virginia, where he played for the Cavaliers from 1983 to 1986. Harris was drafted by the Los Angeles Galaxy in the first round of the 1986 National Soccer League Draft. He spent two seasons with the Galaxy before moving to Germany to play for TSV 1860 Munich. In 1990, Harris returned to the United States and signed with the San Jose Earthquakes. He played two seasons with San Jose before moving to Japan to play for JEF United Chiba. In 1994, Harris returned to the United States and signed with the Tampa Bay Mutiny. He retired after playing three seasons with Tampa Bay.  Harris was a member of the U.S. national team that competed in the 1990 FIFA World Cup. He also played in two CONCACAF Gold Cups and one Copa America.
