Edward Sapiano was a Canadian defence lawyer, known for his work on high-profile murder and terrorism cases. Sapiano was born in Montreal, Quebec on June 14, 1954. He received his law degree from McGill University in 1977. Sapiano began his career as a prosecutor, working in the Crown Prosecution Service of Canada. He later moved to the defence department, where he served as a counsel to the RCMP and the Canadian Security Intelligence Service. In 2002, Sapiano was appointed as a judge of the Federal Court of Canada. He retired from the bench in 2016. Sapiano has written several books on legal subjects, including The Lawyer's Survival Guide: A Comprehensive Guide to Your Rights and Responsibilities as a Client (2002), and Guilty Minds: Inside the Minds of Serial Killers and Other Dangerous Criminals (2008).
