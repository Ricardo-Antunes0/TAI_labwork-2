Numerous ships of the British Royal Navy have borne the name HMS Portsmouth, dating back to 1690 when HMS Portsmouth was commissioned. The current vessel, built in 1982, is the tenth ship to bear the name. The ship's primary role is as a training flagship for the Royal Navy's School of Maritime Warfare.  The ship was involved in several operations during the Iraq War in 2003 and 2004, including the invasion of Iraq and the Battle of Basra. In 2006, she was deployed to the Gulf of Aden in response to the Iranian hostage crisis. The ship returned to Portsmouth in late 2007. She was again deployed to the Gulf of Aden in 2009 and 2010. The ship made a port visit to Thailand in late 2010 and early 2011.  In 2012, HMS Portsmouth participated in the NATO-led operation "Unified Protector" off the Libyan coast. She returned to Portsmouth in early 2013. In early 2014, she deployed to the Gulf of Aden as part of Operation Atalanta. The ship returned to Portsmouth in late 2014.  The tenth HMS Portsmouth was built by BAE Systems at their shipyard in Govan, Scotland, and launched on 14 April 1982. The ship entered service with the Royal Navy later that year and has since participated in a number of operations including during the Iraq War in 2003 and 2004.
