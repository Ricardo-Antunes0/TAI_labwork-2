Gary Lawrence Francione (born May 1954) is an American philosopher, law professor, and animal rights activist.  Francione was born in Syracuse, New York, to a Jewish family. Francione has a B.A. from the University of Rochester and a J.D. from the University of Pittsburgh School of Law, where he was an editor of the Pittsburgh Journal of Law and Ethics.  He taught at Rutgers University, the University of Utah, and Rutgers University–Newark before joining Rutgers Law School in 1992 as a full professor and chair of the philosophy department. He has also been a visiting professor at Yale Law School, the London School of Economics and Political Science, and the University of Orléans in France.  Francione is the author or editor of more than 20 books, including "Animals as Persons" (1995), "Law's Empire" (2000), "The Case for Animal Rights" (2003), "Terrorism and Torts" (2005), "The Conquest of Liberty" (2007), "Signs on the Plain" (2009), "The Myth of Human Superiority" (2010), "Liberty for All: A Defense of Equality and Human Rights" (2013), and "Free Will: An Anthology" (2015). He has also written extensively on animal rights law, philosophy, and terrorism.  In 2013 Francione was awarded an honorary doctorate by Monmouth University in West Long Branch, New Jersey.
