Patricia Roberts Harris (May 31, 1924March 23, 2006) was an American actress, singer and dancer. Born in San Francisco, Harris began her career in the 1930s as a Broadway and nightclub performer. In 1951, she made her Hollywood debut in the film noir thriller The Blue Dahlia. Over the next four decades, Harris appeared in a number of popular films, including The Prince and the Showgirl, Gidget, The Three Musketeers, and An Affair to Remember. In 1976, Harris won a Golden Globe Award for her performance in the musical/drama film The Best Exotic Marigold Hotel. She retired from acting in 1986.  Harris was married to Dick Van Dyke from 1945 to 1961. They had three children: Jerry Van Dyke (born 1947), Cynthia Van Dyke (born 1949) and Robyn Van Dyke (born 1953). Harris later married actor Greg Morrisett and had one son with him, Adam Morrisett (born 1984). She died from ovarian cancer at the age of 82 on March 23, 2006 in Los Angeles County.
