Deer Medicine Rocks (Siouan: literal translation "Antler Medicine Rocks") are a group of granite outcrops located in the town of Deer Lodge, in the eastern section of the U.S. state of Montana.  The Deer Medicine Rocks are a popular tourist destination, with numerous trails providing scenic views of the outcrops and surrounding countryside. The rocks have been used for centuries by the local Blackfeet and Gros Ventres tribes as a place to make medicinal items, such as teas and powder medicines.  The Deer Medicine Rocks are part of the Rocky Mountains National Park, and are accessible only by foot or horseback.
