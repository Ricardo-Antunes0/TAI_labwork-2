The Calvin Coolidge House is a historic home located at Plymouth, Massachusetts.   The Coolidge House was built in 1895 for President Calvin Coolidge and his wife, Grace. The Coolidges were prominent Plymouth residents, and the house is representative of the popular Second Empire style of architecture popular during the late 19th century. The Coolidge House is a two-story wood frame structure, with a hip roof and bracketed eaves. It features a wraparound porch with turned posts and balustrade, and a large glass-enclosed sunroom on the second floor. The Coolidge House was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1984.
