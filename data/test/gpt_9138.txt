Born in Milan, Minervini studied at the Royal Conservatory of Brussels with Olivier Messiaen and at the University of Rome with Goffredo Petrassi. He won first prize in the prestigious Prix de Rome competition in 1941, and worked as a composer and conductor in Italy until his death. His works include operas, ballets, chamber music, choral works and orchestral pieces. He has been called "one of the most influential Italian composers of the 20th century".
