Nu Horologii, Latinized from Î½ Horologii, is the name given in modern Greek to a constellation located in the Southern Hemisphere. The name means "New Watch" in reference to the fact that this constellation was first catalogued by the Dutch astronomer Jacobus Kapteyn in 1922.  The constellation Nu Horologii is located in the Southern Hemisphere, specifically in the southern sky. It consists of seven stars and is typically visible from February to May. The brightest star in the constellation is Alpha Nu Horologii, which is magnitude 3.5.
