The Zike, or Sinclair Zike, is a breed of small horse originating in the United States. They are most commonly seen in western states, where they are used as ranch horses and pleasure horses.  The Zike is a small horse, typically measuring between 14 and 16 hands high, and weighing between 900 and 1,200 pounds. They are usually bay or black with a white blaze and tail, but can also be sorrel, roan, or chestnut. They have short hair, which is usually kept short or brushed over the ears.  The Zike is a versatile horse, well-suited for ranching and pleasure riding. They are strong and sure-footed, making them good ranch horses and trail riders. They are also known for their gentle temperament, making them good mounts for children.
