Baron Franjo Jelačić Bužimski (English: Franz Jellacic, born 4 November 1876, died 10 July 1946) was a Croatian lawyer, diplomat and politician.  He served as the Prime Minister of Croatia from 1929 to 1931 and again from 1937 to 1939. In 1941, he was appointed the Deputy Prime Minister of Austria in the government of Engelbert Dollfuss. After the Anschluss of Austria to Nazi Germany in 1938, he fled to Italy and then Switzerland, where he lived until his death in 1946. Jelačić is remembered for his role in helping to negotiate the Treaty of Rapallo between Italy and the Soviet Union.  Jelačić was born in Bužim, a small town near Zagreb, on 4 November 1876. He attended the University of Vienna, where he obtained a law degree in 1899. He worked as a lawyer in Zagreb from 1899 to 1907. He then served as the prosecutor for Zagreb County from 1907 to 1911. He also served as a member of the Croatian parliament (Sabor) from 1911 to 1918 and from 1919 to 1921. Jelačić became involved in Croatian politics after the breakup of Austria-Hungary in 1918. He served as Minister without Portfolio in the first cabinet of Prime Minister Nikola Pašić from 1918 to 1919 and again from 1921 to 1924.
