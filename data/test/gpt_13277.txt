Roberto Dalla Vecchia (born 5 January 1968) is an Italian football manager and former player who is currently the manager of Serie C club Cosenza.  Dalla Vecchia began his career as a midfielder with hometown club Campobasso, before being signed by Serie A side Lazio in 1988. He spent six seasons with the "Blucerchiati", winning the Coppa Italia in 1992 and making 113 appearances in all competitions. He then moved to S.S.C. Napoli, winning the Serie A title in 1995 and becoming the first player to captain two different clubs to European glory, as Napoli reached the 1996 UEFA Cup Final. He retired from playing at the end of the 1996–97 season, subsequently joining Napoli's coaching staff.  In 2001, Dalla Vecchia was appointed head coach of Serie B side Cesena, guiding them to promotion to Serie A in his first season and finishing in fifth place in his second season. He left Cesena in 2003, subsequently joining Parma where he spent two seasons as head coach, leading them to a fourth-place finish in 2004–05 and a third-place finish in 2005–06. In 2006–07, he was appointed head coach of league rivals Cagliari, but resigned after only nine games into the season due to poor results.
