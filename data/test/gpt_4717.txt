Crinkley Bottom, also popularly referred to as the Crinkley Bottom Wildlife Preserve, is a nature preserve located in the town of Union, South Carolina. The preserve is located on the banks of the Congaree River and covers an area of 1,800 acres (7.3 km2). The preserve is managed by the South Carolina Department of Natural Resources.  The preserve was named after the crinkles in the bottom of the river where it flows over large boulders. The bottom of the river is a rich natural environment with many varieties of plants and animals. The preserve features a variety of habitats including lowland hardwood forest, several types of wetlands, and a pine barrens. The preserve is home to white-tailed deer, bobcats, raccoons, and several other species of wildlife.
