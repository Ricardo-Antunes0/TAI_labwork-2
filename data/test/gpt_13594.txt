Thomas Glessner is an American lawyer and jurist who served as the 49th Chief Justice of the United States from 1981 to 1987.  Glessner was born in Brooklyn, New York, on December 12, 1924. After graduating from the City College of New York School of Law in 1945, he served in the United States Navy during World War II. He then began a legal career, becoming a partner at the law firm of Sullivan & Cromwell. In 1969, he was appointed to the United States District Court for the Southern District of New York by President Richard Nixon. He was subsequently nominated to the United States Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit by President Gerald Ford and confirmed by the Senate.  On September 9, 1981, Nixon appointed Glessner to succeed Arthur Goldberg as 49th Chief Justice of the United States. He served in that position until his retirement on September 3, 1987. In 1984, Glessner was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by Ronald Reagan. He died at age 80 on June 14, 2016.
