Berea–Midpark High School (BMHS) is a public high school located in Berea, Ohio, United States. It is the only high school in the Berea City Schools district.  The Berea–Midpark High School mascot is the Bear. The school colors are blue and gold. BMHS has a total enrollment of 1,854 students.  Berea–Midpark High School was founded in 1922 and was originally known as Berea High School. In 1949, Midpark High School was merged with Berea High School and the school became Berea–Midpark High School. The school underwent a $1 million renovation in 2007 that added a new academic wing and improved the aesthetics of the building.   The Berea–Midpark Bears compete in the Ohio Valley Athletic Conference (OVAC). They have won 11 state championships: boys' basketball (1951, 1957, 1959, 1960, 1967, 1968, 1971, 1972), girls' basketball (1974, 1982, 1991), wrestling (1974, 1976), baseball (1974), softball (1998), volleyball (2007), track and field (1978), and football (1970).
