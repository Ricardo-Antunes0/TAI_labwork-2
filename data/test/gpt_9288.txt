Char Satiner Ghar (Eng : Four Wive's House) is a 1954 Indian Bollywood film, directed by K. Asif and produced by M. J. Akbar. The film stars Dilip Kumar, Madhubala, and Mehmood in the lead roles. The story is based on the Urdu short story "Dil Ki Chahat" by Munshi Premchand.  The film is set in the 1930s during the British Raj, and concerns the conflict between two families vying for control of a house - one of which is headed by an ambitious woman. The film has been critically acclaimed, and was awarded the President's Silver Medal at the 1955 Filmfare Awards.  Char Satiner Ghar was remade in Telugu as "Ramudu" with Akkineni Nageswara Rao in the lead role and released in 1957. It was also remade in Hindi as "Sawan" with Sanjeev Kumar and Rekha in the lead roles, released in 1966.
