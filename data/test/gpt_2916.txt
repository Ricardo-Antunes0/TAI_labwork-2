He attended Scotch College and studied law at the University of Melbourne, from where he graduated with first-class honours. Malkin practiced law for two years before joining the Melbourne Herald as a reporter in 1947. He rose rapidly through the ranks, becoming managing editor in 1958 and editor-in-chief in 1967.  Malkin left the Herald in 1972 to become publisher of the Sydney Daily Telegraph. He held that position until his retirement in 1986. In retirement, Malkin wrote a number of books, including an autobiography and a history of Australian journalism. He died in 2009 at the age of 89.
