Raymond Auguste Quinsac Monvoisin (May 31, 1790 – September 5, 1871) was a French aristocrat, businessman and politician. He was the son of Auguste Monvoisin and the grandson of Jean-Baptiste Monvoisin, both wine merchants. In 1815, he married Louise Crespi de La Pagerie, with whom he had six children.   Raymond Monvoisin was born in 1790 in the town of Moulins in the Allier department of central France. After completing his secondary education at the Collège Louis-le-Grand in Paris, he entered the family wine business in 1806. In 1815, Monvoisin married Louise Crespi de La Pagerie, with whom he had six children. He became a director of his father-in-law's wine firm in 1827.  Monvoisin served as a deputy for Allier in the Chamber of Deputies from 1831 to 1840 and from 1848 to 1851. He also served as a senator from 1850 to 1859 and from 1863 to 1870. Monvoisin died on September 5, 1871 in Bordeaux.
