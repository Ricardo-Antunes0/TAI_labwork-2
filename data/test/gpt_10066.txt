Mount Crickmer is one of the southernmost peaks in the Tasman Mountains. It is located in the Australian state of Tasmania. The mountain rises to an elevation of 1,825 metres above sea level.  Crickmer is composed of Precambrian granite and quartzite, and is the highest peak in the area. The surrounding countryside is covered in temperate rainforest, with pockets of snow on the higher slopes. The mountain is part of the Crickmer Range, which also includes Mount Field and Mount Arthur.  The peak is accessible by car via a unsealed road from the town of Crickmer. The walk to the summit takes approximately two hours, and there are several camping spots along the way.
