The Manor of Worksop is a feudal manor in the town of Worksop, Nottinghamshire, England. It is a Grade I listed building.  The Manor of Worksop was originally built in the 12th century by the de Ferrers family. The manor was bought by the Duke of Northumberland in 1551 and passed down through the Northumberland family. In 1882, it was sold to the Earl of Iddesleigh who used it as a country estate. The manor house was damaged in World War II and has since been renovated. Today, it is used as a hotel and conference centre.
