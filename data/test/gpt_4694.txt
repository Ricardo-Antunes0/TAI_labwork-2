The Dusit Thani Bangkok, often referred to simply as Dusit, is a five-star hotel in Bangkok, Thailand. It is the largest hotel in Thailand by floor area, with 1,004 rooms. The hotel has been awarded five stars by the Forbes Travel Guide.     The Dusit Thani Bangkok was opened on 9 November 1988 by then Crown Prince Maha Vajiralongkorn and his wife Princess Sirindhorn. The hotel was originally designed as a luxurious weekend getaway for Bangkok's business and royal elite. It quickly became one of Bangkok's most popular luxury resorts and today remains one of the city's most luxurious hotels.    The hotel's facilities include a casino, a spa, a golf course, an 18-hole championship golf course, a swimming pool, a tennis court, and a fitness center. It also has a convention center with capacity for 1,500 delegates and an amphitheater that can hold up to 3,000 people. The hotel is home to the Dusit Thani Bangkok restaurant which has been awarded three Michelin stars.
