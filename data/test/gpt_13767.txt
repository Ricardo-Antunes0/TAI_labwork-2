Yuliya Aleksandrovna Saltsevich (; born May 12, 1983) is a Russian singer and actress. She debuted as a solo artist in 2003 with the song "Darling".  Saltsevich has released six albums: "Darling" (2003), "Believe" (2005), "Innocent" (2007), "Secrets" (2009), "Unforgettable" (2011), and "Brand New Day" (2015). She has also appeared in several films, including "The Pianist" (2002), "Warrior's Way" (2010), and "The Keeper of Lost Causes" (2017).  Saltsevich was born in Leningrad, Russian SFSR, Soviet Union, to a musical family. Her father, Aleksandr Saltsevich, is a jazz saxophonist and her mother, Yuliya Shcherbakova-Saltsevich, is a pianist. She has two older sisters and an older brother. When she was six years old, her family moved to Moscow.  Saltsevich started taking singing lessons at the age of four. In 1998, she joined the National Youth Theatre of Russia. In 2003, she released her debut solo album, "Darling". The album reached number one on the Russian Albums Chart and was certified platinum by the RIA Novosti certification agency.
