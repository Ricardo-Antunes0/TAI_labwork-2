He is most notable for his command of the 1st Airborne Division in the Italian Campaign during the Allied invasion of Europe in 1944, and his subsequent command of the U.S. XVIII Airborne Corps during Operation Neptune's Spear, the airborne invasion of Japan in 1945.  Born in Toronto, Canada, Keyes entered the United States Military Academy at West Point in 1907 and graduated in 1911. He served in World War I as an artillery officer with the 45th Infantry Regiment of the US Army before transferring to the infantry and serving with various units on the Western Front. He was awarded a Distinguished Service Cross for his actions at Saint-Mihiel, France.  After World War I, Keyes attended the Command and General Staff School at Fort Leavenworth, Kansas and then served on the staff of the American Expeditionary Force (AEF) in France. Upon his return to the United States he became an instructor at West Point and then served on General John J. Pershing's General Staff during World War II. He was appointed Deputy Chief of Staff for Operations at Army General Headquarters in London in 1943 and later assumed command of III Corps on the European Theater of Operations.
