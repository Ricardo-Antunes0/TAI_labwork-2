The Western Brothers were an English music duo consisting of twin brothers, singer Roy Wood and guitarist Jeff Wood.  The Western Brothers were formed in 1969 by twin brothers Roy Wood (born 6 May 1943) and Jeff Wood (born 26 March 1944). They achieved their first hit single in 1970 with "Heart Full of Soul", which reached number 2 in the UK Singles Chart. The following year, they released their debut album, "Walking on Sunshine", which peaked at number 3 in the UK Albums Chart. The duo had further UK chart success with the singles "I'm a Believer" (number 1) and "Funky Funky Man" (number 2).  In 1973, the Western Brothers released their second album, "Pictures of Matchstick Men", which reached number 2 in the UK Albums Chart. The album spawned two hit singles, "The Ballad of John and Yoko" (number 1) and "Matchstick Men". In 1974, they released their third album, "Love is a Many Splendored Thing", which reached number 1 in the UK Albums Chart. The album spawned two hit singles, "Love Will Tear Us Apart" (number 1) and "Money (That's What I Want)" (number 2).
