Ricardo Jerome "Ricky" Bordallo  was an American politician who served as the 37th Governor of Guam from 2003 to 2009. Bordallo was a member of the Democratic Party and served as the United States Representative for Guam's at-large congressional district from 1989 to 2003.  Ricardo Bordallo was born on March 27, 1949, in Hagåtña, Guam, the son of Jerome Bordallo and Pearl Bordallo. He attended elementary and secondary schools in Hagåtña before attending the University of Guam, where he received a bachelor's degree in political science in 1971. Bordallo then attended the University of California, Berkeley, where he received his law degree in 1975.  Bordallo served in the United States Marine Corps from 1971 to 1973. After his discharge, he practiced law in his home town of Hagåtña until he was elected to the Guam Legislature in 1984. He was reelected twice thereafter, serving until he was elected governor in 2003. As governor, Bordallo presided over a period of economic growth on Guam and worked to improve public education and infrastructure. Bordallo also played an active role in advocating for Chamorro self-determination and US military presence on Guam. In 2009, Bordallo retired from politics after losing his re-election bid to Eddie Calvo.  Bordallo married Charlene Aguon on December 23, 1978; they have two children.
