Alice Kuipers (born 29 June 1979) is a Dutch journalist and author. Kuipers has worked for several Dutch newspapers, including De Telegraaf, NRC Handelsblad, and Het Parool. She became known for her work as a foreign correspondent for the BBC World Service, where she reported from Iraq, Syria, Egypt, Libya, Pakistan and Afghanistan.  Kuipers was born in The Hague, Netherlands. After graduating from high school in 1997, she studied political science at the University of Amsterdam. During her studies she worked as a reporter for the student newspaper Varsity. After completing her degree in 2002, Kuipers worked as a reporter for the Dutch newspaper De Telegraaf. She later moved to NRC Handelsblad and Het Parool, before joining the BBC World Service in 2013.  Kuipers has been a foreign correspondent for the BBC World Service since 2013. She has reported from Iraq, Syria, Egypt, Libya, Pakistan and Afghanistan. In 2017 she was appointed as a BBC World Service special correspondent on global media freedom.
