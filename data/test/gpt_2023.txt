Monasypov was born in 1925 in the village of Novaya Sloboda, Volyn Oblast, Ukrainian SSR. He studied at the Kiev Conservatory with Vassily Mykolayovych and Dmitry Shostakovich. After graduating in 1948, he served as a conductor at the Yekaterinburg Symphony Orchestra. In 1957, he emigrated to the United States and became professor of composition at the Eastman School of Music, where he remained until his retirement in 1993. He also served as chairman of the composition department and president of the Eastman School's trustees. Monasypov was awarded several honorary degrees from universities in the United States and Europe. He died in 2008 in Ithaca, New York.  Monasypov's compositions are characterised by their lyricism and expressiveness. He is perhaps best known for his symphonic poems "Requiem" (1966) and "The Seasons" (1973), both of which were highly acclaimed by classical critics. His other works include sonatas for cello and piano, string quartets, operas, chamber music, and choral works.
