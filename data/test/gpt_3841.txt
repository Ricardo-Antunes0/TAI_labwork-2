Dioncounda Traoré (born 23 February 1942) is a Malian singer and songwriter.  Traoré was born in Bamako, Mali, and began her musical career in the early 1960s. She gained prominence as a singer in the 1970s, and released her first album in 1974. Her most successful period as a performer came in the 1980s and 1990s, when she became one of Mali's best-known singers. Traoré has recorded more than 30 albums, and has won several awards, including the César Award for Best African Act in 1987. She has also been nominated for a Grammy Award.
