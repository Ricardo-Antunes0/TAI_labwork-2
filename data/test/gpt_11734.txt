Richard Leo Simon was born in 1899 in Newark, New Jersey. He received his B.A. from the University of Michigan in 1921 and his M.A. from the same institution in 1923. He then attended the University of Chicago, where he received his Ph.D. in 1928. After completing his doctoral studies, Simon became an instructor at the University of Chicago and then a professor there in 1938. He served as chairman of the economics department at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign from 1955 to 1969 and as chancellor of that institution from 1970 to 1974. Simon died on December 20, 1984.
