Lajo Jose is an Indian writer of children's books.  Lajo Jose was born in the city of Udaipur, in Rajasthan, India, on October 18, 1938. He is the author of more than fifty books for children, many of which have been translated into numerous languages. He has been awarded a number of prestigious literary awards, including the Hans Christian Andersen Award (1981), the prestigious Hans Christian Andersen Medal (1996), and the prestigious Balzan Prize (2002). He has also been nominated for the prestigious World Bank Children's Book Award several times.   Lajo Jose's books are full of humor and warmth, and are often based on folk tales from Rajasthan. His work has been praised for its beautiful illustrations and for its sensitive exploration of Indian culture and traditions. Lajo Jose lives in Udaipur with his wife and four children.
