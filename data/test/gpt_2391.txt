Capt Harry Waldo Yoxall OBE MC JP (15 May 1907 – 1 October 1984) was a British soldier, author and journalist, who served in the Royal Artillery during World War II. After the war he became a journalist, writing for the Sunday Times, and later for The Observer. He is best known for his autobiography, A Gun for Hire (1954), which tells the story of his life as a professional contract killer.  Harry Waldo Yoxall was born on 15 May 1907 in Belgravia in London, England to Alfred Yoxall, a stockbroker, and his wife Lillian (née Waldo). In 1911, the family moved to Hampshire where Harry attended The Kings School, Southampton. He left school in 1923 and began working as a clerk at the London Stock Exchange.  In 1928, Yoxall joined the Royal Artillery and served during World War II in North Africa and Europe. After the war he became a journalist and wrote for the Sunday Times, later for The Observer. He is best known for his autobiography, A Gun for Hire (1954), which tells the story of his life as a professional contract killer. In 1978 he published his memoirs of World War II in collaboration with Geoffrey Jones.  Yoxall was married twice: firstly to Patience Vaughan from 1929 until their divorce in 1937; and secondly to Daphne Guinness from 1957 until her death in 1984.
