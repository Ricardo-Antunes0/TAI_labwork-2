Laraba Gambo Abdullahi (born 1951) is a Somali academic and politician. He has served as the Minister of Higher Education in the Federal Republic of Somalia since 2007.  Abdullahi was born in 1951 in the city of Hargeisa, in what was then the British Somaliland protectorate. After completing his secondary education at Tarmo School, he studied at the Somali National University (then known as the Somali National University College), where he earned a bachelor's degree in political science in 1974. He then went on to study at Columbia University, where he earned a doctorate in political science in 1983.  Abdullahi has been a professor at the Somali National University since 1984, and has also served as its vice chancellor since 2003. He has also been a visiting professor at several universities around the world, including Harvard University, Columbia University, and King's College London.  Abdullahi has been involved in politics since the late 1970s, when he became active in the Somali National Revolutionary Party (SNRP). He was elected to the Somalia National Assembly in 1988 and to the Federal Parliament in 1993, serving as its deputy Speaker from 1994 to 1996. In 2007, Abdullahi was appointed Minister of Higher Education by Prime Minister Ali Mohamed Gedi. In this role, he has helped to expand access to education for students across Somalia and establish new universities. Abdullahi has also played a role in developing Somaliland into an autonomous region within Somalia.
