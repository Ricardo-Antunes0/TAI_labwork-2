The Church of St Margaret, Westminster, is an Anglican parish church in the City of Westminster, London. The church is dedicated to Saint Margaret of Antioch, the patron saint of England.  The church was built in 1837–38 to a design by George Gilbert Scott and designed in the Decorated style. It was consecrated on 25 November 1838 by Archbishop Trench. The architects were Scott and Peddle. The church cost £15,000 (£ as of 2019) to build and had seating for 1,000 people. The stained glass windows were designed by Edward Burne-Jones and his son John Burne-Jones.  The parish is part of the Central London Benefice which includes St George's Hanover Square, St George's Bethnal Green, and St George's Westminister.
