Born in the Bronx, New York City, Silverman was the daughter of Russian Jewish immigrants, who operated a delicatessen. She learned to sing and play the guitar at an early age. After graduating from high school in 1951, she began her career performing in nightclubs and on the radio. In 1960, she released her first album, which included the hit single "I Can't Stop Loving You".  Silverman's subsequent albums yielded successful singles including "The First Time Ever I Saw Your Face", "The Love I Lost (Has Found Me Again)", and "Wonderful World". Silverman also won a Golden Globe Award for Best Actress for her performance in the television movie The Second Hundred Years (1982).  Silverman retired from the entertainment industry in the early 1980s to raise her five children. She died from cancer at her home in Beverly Hills, California, at the age of 80.
