She received her bachelor's degree from the University of Michigan in 1936 and her Juris Doctor from Harvard Law School in 1940.  Brown clerked for Supreme Court Justice James M. Douglas from 1940 to 1941. She then served in the United States Army during World War II, first as a captain and then as a lieutenant colonel. After the war, she returned to Harvard Law School, where she became a professor in 1951. Brown served as the Dean of the Law School from 1978 to 1981.  Brown was appointed to the United States District Court for the Southern District of New York by President Jimmy Carter in 1979. She served on that court until her retirement in 1991.  Anna V. Brown died on November 12, 1993, in Boston, Massachusetts.
