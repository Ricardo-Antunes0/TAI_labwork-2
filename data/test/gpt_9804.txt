Stevens' Building is a historic commercial building located at 212-214 Chestnut Street in the Center City section of Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. The three-story, masonry building was built in 1852 and is one of the few surviving examples of a Richardsonian Romanesque commercial building in the city. It was added to the National Register of Historic Places in 1982.  Stevens' Building is significant as one of the few remaining examples of a Richardsonian Romanesque commercial building in Philadelphia. It features a projecting central bay with round arches, and is distinguished by its heavy rustication and polychrome brick veneer. The building served as the regional headquarters for several major Philadelphia businesses during its heyday, including the Wharton Bank and Trust Company and the Richards, Kline and French department store.
