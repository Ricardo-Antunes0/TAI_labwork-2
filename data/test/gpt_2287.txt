The Lumber Champ is an animated short film produced by Walt Disney Productions and released on October 16, 1937. The film is about a logger named Champ who falls in love with a mill girl named Sue. Champ tries to impress Sue by logging faster than anyone else, but he eventually fails. Sue loves Champ for who he is, not for what he can do.  The Lumber Champ was released to critical acclaim and won the Academy Award for Best Animated Short Film in 1938. The film has been considered a classic of animation and is often cited as one of the best examples of the Disney animated style.
