Tahtaköprü (literally "Wooden bridge") is a town and district in the province of Konya, Turkey. At , it is the largest town in the province, and the provincial capital. The population is 97,524 as of 2011.  Tahtaköprü was first settled by the Hittites in the Late Bronze Age and was known as Hattusha. The town was captured by the Persians in 546 BC and remained part of their empire until the arrival of Alexander the Great in 333 BC. After his death, it became part of the Seleucid Empire. The town passed into Roman hands after their victory over Antiochus III the Great in 190 BC, and remained under Roman rule until its dissolution in 395 AD. After being sacked by the Visigoths in 410 AD, Tahtaköprü came under Muslim rule. It was conquered by the Seljuks in 1071 AD and became part of their empire. The town passed into Ottoman hands after their victory over the Seljuks at Battle of Manzikert in 1071 AD, and remained under Ottoman rule until its proclamation as a province of Turkey in 1938.
