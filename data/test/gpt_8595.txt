Donald Tai Loy Ho (August 13, 1930 – February 25, 2002) was an American singer, actor and comedian.  Ho was known for his deep voice, sense of humor, and flashy dress. He performed in a wide range of genres, including rock and roll, rhythm and blues, pop and Cantonese opera. Ho was one of the first Asian Americans to achieve mainstream success in the United States. He has been called "the King of Cantopop" and "one of the most influential entertainers in history".  Ho was born in San Francisco, California, to Taiwanese parents. His father, Tai-Loi Ho, worked as a cook and chauffeur. Ho's mother, Kuan-Yin Ho (née Sun), was a homemaker. The family moved to Honolulu when Ho was three months old. In 1944, his family moved again to Seattle. There, Ho attended Roosevelt High School.  Ho began his entertainment career as a teenager in Seattle nightclubs. He made his recording debut in 1955 with the song "The Witch Doctor". Ho's first major U.S. hit came in 1963 with the song "Funny Girl", which reached number one on the "Billboard" Hot 100 chart and became one of the best-selling singles of all time.
