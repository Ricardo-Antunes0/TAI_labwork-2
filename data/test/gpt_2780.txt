Russell City (also known as Russell) was a town in Butte County, California. It was founded in 1876 and became a ghost town in the early 1970s. It is located southeast of Chico, at an elevation of 903 feet (273 m).  Russell City was named for Alfred Russell, a Canadian mining engineer who discovered gold in the area in 1875. The population was 577 at the 2010 census.  Today, the only remains of Russell City are a few foundations and a cemetery. In 1971, it was listed on the National Register of Historic Places as the Russell City Historic District.
