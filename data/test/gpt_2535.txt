Peter Wolbrandt (born 28 October 1949 in Münster) is a German composer and conductor.  Peter Wolbrandt was born in Münster, Germany on October 28, 1949. He studied composition with Helmut Lachenmann at the University of Music and Performing Arts in Vienna, where he also graduated with a degree in musicology in 1973. Wolbrandt then studied conducting with Otto Klemperer at the Berliner Musikhochschule. He made his conducting debut with the Staatstheater Braunschweig in 1975.  Wolbrandt has been a professor of composition and conductor at the Hochschule für Musik und Tanz Köln since 1987. He has been awarded several awards for his work, including the prestigious Konzerthauspreis der DDR (1983), the Kranichsteiner Musikpreis (1985), and the Grand Prix de la Société des Arts et des Lettres (1999). Wolbrandt has also been awarded honorary doctorates from the Universities of Münster (1995) and Freiburg im Breisgau (2002).  Wolbrandt's compositions include orchestral works, chamber music, choral works, and opera. His most well-known work is probably his opera "Die Soldaten" (1982), which has been performed worldwide.
