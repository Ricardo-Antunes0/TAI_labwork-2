Joy S. Burns was the President and CEO of the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA) from 1991 until her retirement in 2006. She also served as RIAA's chairman from 1998 to 2001. She was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 1996.  Burns was born on October 6, 1940, in Detroit, Michigan to parents who were both music teachers. After graduating from high school, Burns attended the University of Michigan, where she received a degree in music education. She began her career as a music teacher and band director before becoming the Assistant Director for Administration for the Detroit Public School District. In 1978, Burns joined the RIAA as its assistant general counsel.  In 1991, Burns became President and CEO of the RIAA. During her tenure, the RIAA successfully lobbied the United States government to pass the Digital Millenium Copyright Act (DMCA) in 1998. The DMCA enabled the RIAA to file lawsuits against individuals who illegally downloaded music files. Burns also played a role in developing the Grammy Awards ceremony and increasing revenue for the RIAA through licensing agreements with music labels.  Burns retired from her position with the RIAA in 2006. At the time of her retirement, she was the longest-serving CEO in RIAA history. In 2007, she was awarded an honorary doctorate from Wayne State University. Burns currently serves as a trustee for The Recording Academy and is a member of its Board of Governors.
