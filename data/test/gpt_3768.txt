Eduard Bargheer (25 December 1901 – 1 December 1991) was a Swiss artist who specialized in portrait painting.  Bargheer was born in Zürich, Switzerland, the son of a wine merchant. He attended the Zurich School of Art from 1919 to 1921, and then the Académie Julian from 1922 to 1924. He studied at the Royal Academy of Brussels from 1924 to 1928, where he was taught by Paul Signac and Fernand Léger.   Bargheer lived in Paris from 1928 to 1933, where he was associated with the Surrealist movement. After returning to Switzerland, he became one of the most successful portrait painters in the world. His works are in the collections of museums around the world, including the Museum of Modern Art in New York City and the Kunstmuseum Basel. He died in Zürich at the age of 91.
