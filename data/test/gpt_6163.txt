Klaus Detlef Sierck (30 March 1925 – 8 February 2013) was a German linguist, considered one of the leading experts on the German language.  Klaus Detlef Sierck was born on 30 March 1925 in Frankfurt, Germany. He died on 8 February 2013 in Bad Honnef, Germany.  Sierck undertook his undergraduate studies at the universities of Münster and Cologne, earning his doctorate from Cologne in 1951. From 1951 to 1954 he was a research assistant at the University of Cologne. He then worked as a professor at Heidelberg University from 1954 to 1978, and at the University of Tübingen from 1978 to 2003.  Sierck's work focused on the German language, its grammar and syntax, with a particular focus on historical linguistics. He was also an expert on German dialectology and lexicography.  In 1992, Sierck received the Goethe Prize for his contributions to German linguistics. In 2003, he was awarded the Order of Merit of the Federal Republic of Germany.
