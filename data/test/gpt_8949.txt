Alexander George Neill (born 22 July 1950) is a New Zealand former cricketer. He played first-class cricket for Auckland from 1969 to 1981, and was captain of the team from 1974 to 1977.  Neill was born in Auckland, New Zealand. He attended Saint Kentigern's College, where he played cricket for the school team. He made his first-class debut for Auckland in 1969, and played regularly for the team until 1981. During that time, he was also captain of Auckland from 1974 to 1977. After leaving Auckland, Neill played three seasons for Canterbury. He retired from cricket in 1984.  Neill later became an umpire, and served in that role until 1995. In 2001, he was named as one of the twelve inaugural inductees into the New Zealand Cricket Hall of Fame.
