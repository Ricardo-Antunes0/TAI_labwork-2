Tsakonia  or the Tsakonian region  is a mountainous region in northwestern Greece. The region is bounded by the Greek mainland to the west, by the Ionian Sea to the north, by Albania to the east and by Bulgaria to the south. Tsakonia is part of the regional unit of Central Greece.  The region is home to Mount Olympus, one of the highest peaks in Europe, and has a temperate climate with heavy rainfall. The area is rich in mineral resources, including coal, iron ore, lead and silver. Tourism is a major industry in Tsakonia, with popular destinations including Mount Olympus, Meteora and Skiathos.
