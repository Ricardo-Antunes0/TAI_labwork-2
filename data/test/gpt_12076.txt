William Thomas Pettit (April 23, 1931 â€“ October 7, 2003) was an American philosopher.  Pettit was born in Berkeley, California, and educated at the University of California, Berkeley where he studied with W. V. Quine and Willard Van Orman Quine. He then went on to study with Karl Jaspers at the University of Freiburg, receiving his doctorate in 1955. Pettit taught at Yale from 1965 until his retirement in 1999. He died in 2003.  Pettit's philosophical work focuses on the problem of knowledge, specifically the relationship between reason and belief. His best known book is The Quest for Certainty (1979), which has been highly influential both within philosophy and in the wider cognitive sciences.
