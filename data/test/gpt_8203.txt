Howard Graham Buffett (born December 16, 1954) is an American business magnate, investor, and philanthropist. He is the chairman and CEO of Berkshire Hathaway, and was ranked as the third richest person in the world as of 2013, with a net worth of $44.5 billion.  Buffett was born in Omaha, Nebraska, to Howard Buffett, Sr., a stockbroker, and Doris Buffett. He attended the private Phillips Exeter Academy in New Hampshire and then earned his undergraduate degree from University of Nebraska at Omaha. He then went to Harvard Business School, where he was a member of the Delta Kappa Epsilon fraternity.  Buffett began his career at Salomon Brothers in 1976. He rose through the company's ranks to become its chief executive officer in 1990. In 1997, he merged Salomon Brothers with investment firm Berkshire Hathaway and took over as chairman of Berkshire Hathaway. As of 2013, he is also the head of Berkshire's investment subsidiary, Berkshire Hathaway Mid-Cap Value Fund.  Buffett has donated more than $30 billion to various charitable causes throughout his life. In 2011, he gave $3 billion to the Gates Foundation and committed himself to giving away 95 percent of his wealth during his lifetime. He is also a trustee of the Rockefeller Foundation and the University of Chicago Board of Trustees.  Buffett married Susan Thompson Buffett in 1975; they have three children: Howard Graham Buffett, Jr., Howard Howard Buffett III, and Isabella May Buffett.
