Lancelot du Lac (French for Lancelot of the Lake) was an Arthurian knight who achieved legendary status in medieval romances. He was the son of King Ban of Benwick and Lady Elaine, and the father of Guinevere.  Lancelot's life is generally portrayed as one of great adventure, with many notable feats of arms. He was known for his great strength and courage, as well as his skill with the lance. Lancelot was also renowned for his courtly manners and for his deep love for Queen Guinevere. In the end, Lancelot was mortally wounded in the battle against Agravain, and died soon afterwards.
