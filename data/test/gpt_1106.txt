Alexey Germanovich Lushnikov (; born June 10, 1954) is a Russian professional ice hockey coach and a former player. He is currently the head coach of HC Sochi of the Kontinental Hockey League (KHL).  Lushnikov played in the Soviet Union for CSKA Moscow, and played in the National Hockey League (NHL) with the Boston Bruins and Detroit Red Wings. He was also a member of the Soviet national team, winning a gold medal at the 1984 Winter Olympics. He has coached several teams in Russia and Europe, most notably HC Sochi.
