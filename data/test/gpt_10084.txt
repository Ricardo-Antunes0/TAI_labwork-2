Karen Yuzbashyan was born on January 6, 1987, in Los Angeles, California. She graduated from the University of Southern California with a degree in journalism in 2009.  Yuzbashyan began her career as a reporter for Agos newspaper in Yerevan, Armenia. She later became the paper's bureau chief in Washington D.C. In 2006 she co-authored the book "The Armenians: A History from Ancient to Modern", which was published by Harvard University Press.  In 2007, Yuzbashyan moved to New York City and worked as a freelance journalist for various publications including The New Republic, The Armenian Weekly and The International Herald Tribune. She also served as a visiting fellow at Columbia University's School of Journalism.  On October 2, 2009, Yuzbashyan was killed in a car crash while driving on Highway 101 in Santa Rosa, California. She was 27 years old. Her death received national attention and led to the creation of the Karen Yuzbashyan Memorial Fund at the Columbia Journalism Review Foundation.
