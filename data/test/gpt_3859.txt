Steve Bowman (born January 14, 1967) is an American former professional ice hockey player who played in the National Hockey League for the Hartford Whalers, Calgary Flames, and Philadelphia Flyers. He is currently an assistant coach for the Flyers.  Bowman was drafted by the Hartford Whalers in the second round, 47th overall, of the 1987 NHL Entry Draft. He made his NHL debut with Hartford in 1988. The following season, he was traded to the Calgary Flames. In 1991–92, Bowman led all NHL defensemen in scoring with 76 points (26 goals and 50 assists) and was named to the NHL First All-Star Team. He also played for Philadelphia from 1995 to 2000. In 2000–01, he led all Flyers defensemen in scoring with 61 points (17 goals and 46 assists). After playing his final season with Philadelphia in 2001–02, Bowman retired from professional hockey.
