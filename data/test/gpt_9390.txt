Chandrakant Shah, OOnt, is a Canadian doctor, scientist, and professor. He is a world-renowned expert on the pathology of cancer and has made significant contributions to the field of oncology.  Born in India in 1957, Shah moved to Canada with his family in 1970. He obtained his medical degree from the University of Toronto in 1980 and subsequently completed a residency at Toronto Western Hospital. He then completed a fellowship at the Royal Marsden Hospital in London, England.  Shah returned to Canada in 1985 and was appointed assistant professor at the University of Toronto's Department of Medicine. He became associate professor in 1992 and full professor in 1997. In 2000, he was appointed vice-president (research) at the University of Toronto and president of the university's Health Sciences Institute. In 2003, he was appointed chairman of the Department of Medicine at the University of Toronto. In 2008, he was appointed chancellor of the University of Toronto.  Shah has published more than 300 articles in peer-reviewed journals and has been awarded numerous awards, including the Guyton Award (1998), the Royal Society of Canada's Adamson Chair (1999), and the Michael Smith Prize (2006). He is also a Fellow of both the Royal Society of Canada and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.  In 2010, Shah was made an Officer of the Order of Canada for his contributions to cancer research.
