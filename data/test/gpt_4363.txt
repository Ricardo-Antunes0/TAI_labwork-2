Tombodu is a relatively small provincial town in the Western Region of Ghana. The town is located about 205 kilometers from the regional capital of Takoradi. Tombodu has a population of around 14,000 people and is the administrative center of Tombodu District.   The town is known for its traditional weaving and embroidery, which is carried out by a small number of women. The town also has a small market and a number of small businesses. Tombodu is also home to a number of schools, including a junior secondary school and a University of Science and Technology.
