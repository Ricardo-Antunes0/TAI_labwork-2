Rusudan Bolkvadze (born November 6, 1959) is a Georgian writer and journalist. She is the author of the novel Tbilisi (1996), the story collection In the Fog (1997), and the memoirs Father, Son, and Holy War (2004).  Rusudan Bolkvadze was born in Tbilisi, Georgia on November 6, 1959. She graduated from the Tbilisi State University with a degree in journalism in 1981. Bolkvadze began her career as a journalist at the Rustavi-2 TV channel. In 1984 she moved to Moscow and worked as a correspondent for various Russian newspapers and magazines. In 1988 she moved back to Georgia and became deputy editor-in-chief of the independent newspaper Zguli. From 1990 to 1992 she was editor-in-chief of the journal Kavkazsky Uzel.  Bolkvadze's first novel, Tbilisi, was published in 1996. The story tells the story of a young woman who travels to Tbilisi after escaping from Stalin's Russia. The novel was awarded the Lev Zhdanov Prize in 1997. Bolkvadze's second novel, In the Fog, was published in 1997. The story is set during Stalin's time and tells the story of a family forced into exile. The novel was awarded the Georgian National Book Prize in 1998.
