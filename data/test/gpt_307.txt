Operation Rolling Thunder was the title of an American military campaign in the Vietnam War, officially known as Operation Commando Hunt, that lasted from March 17, 1965 to March 27, 1968. The objective of the operation was to destroy the Viet Cong Infrastructure, principally their command and control structures and their supply lines.  The original name for the operation was "Operation Tiger Hound", but due to objections by the Department of Defense's General William Westmoreland, the name was changed to Operation Rolling Thunder. The operation was code-named after Rolling Thunder Rolling Thunder was a metaphor for the thundering sound made by artillery shells as they travelled through the air.
