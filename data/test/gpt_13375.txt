Swarup Singh Karki  or Swaroop Singh Karki, also spelt Sarkar Singh Karki (born 1 January 1954) is a serving Indian Army general and has served as the Chief of Army Staff (CAS) of the Indian Army since 2 December 2018.  Swarup Singh Karki was born on 1 January 1954 in the Nepalese town of Biratnagar. He graduated from the National Defence Academy in 1978 and was commissioned into the Indian Army as a Second Lieutenant. He has held a number of staff and command appointments, including Commandant of the Army War College, Director General Military Training and Deputy Chief of General Staff. He has also served as Chief of the General Staff (CGS), the military advisor to the Prime Minister of India, and as Commander-in-Chief of the Eastern Command. He has been awarded the Padma Bhushan, India's third highest civilian honour, and the Param Vir Chakra, India's second highest military award.
