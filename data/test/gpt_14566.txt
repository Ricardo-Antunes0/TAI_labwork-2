Natalie Cecilia Elphicke  (n√©e Ross; born 10 March 1974) is a British Conservative MP who has been the Member of Parliament (MP) for Dover and Deal since 2010.  Elphicke was born in London and educated at the University of Edinburgh, where she graduated with an MA in European Studies. She worked as a civil servant from 1997 to 2003, serving in the Scottish Government and then the Department for Constitutional Affairs before becoming a Conservative Party councillor in Dover in 2003. She was elected to Parliament at the 2010 general election, replacing the retiring Patrick Mercer.  Elphicke is a supporter of Brexit and has been outspoken on the issue. She served as a Deputy Chief Whip from May 2015 to November 2016, and was appointed Minister of State for Women and Equalities in November 2016. In October 2018, it was announced that she would be resigning her seat to become Secretary of State for Defence, succeeding Michael Fallon.
