Darkest Africa (1936) is a Republic movie directed by Michael Curtiz, and starring Gary Cooper, Ruth Roman, and Humphrey Bogart. The film is about a group of people who travel to the Congo to help a Belgian mining company in its fight against the natives.  The film is a classic adventure movie, full of action and suspense. It is also noteworthy for its social commentary, which criticizes the exploitation of the Congo by the Belgians.
