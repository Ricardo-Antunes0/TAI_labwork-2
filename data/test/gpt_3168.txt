Universal  is a historic term that means “of or pertaining to all people.” The term has been used in many legal contexts to describe laws or principles that are applicable to everyone, regardless of nationality or religion. Universal laws are based on the principle of general application and are thus binding on all nations.  Universal suffrage is the right of all adult citizens in a given country to vote. Universal suffrage is often seen as one of the fundamental rights of a democracy. It is also a key principle of human rights.
