Alexander "Sasha" Olegovich Kaun (, born May 9, 1988) is a Russian professional ice hockey forward who is currently playing with HC Sochi of the Kontinental Hockey League (KHL).  Kaun was drafted in the third round, 81st overall, by the Atlanta Thrashers in the 2006 NHL Entry Draft. He began his professional career in 2007 with HC Sochi of the Kontinental Hockey League (KHL). In his first full season in the KHL in 2009–10, he set career highs in goals (33) and points (68) while playing for HC Sochi. In 2010–11, he played for Metallurg Magnitogorsk of the Kontinental Hockey League (KHL) where he recorded career highs in goals (38) and points (80).  Kaun signed with the Winnipeg Jets on July 1, 2013. After playing out the remainder of the 2013–14 season with Manitoba Moose of the American Hockey League (AHL), he was recalled by Winnipeg on March 6, 2014. He made his NHL debut that night against the Nashville Predators and recorded an assist. Kaun was traded to the Calgary Flames on December 10, 2014. He was assigned to AHL affiliate, the Stockton Heat on January 5, 2015. Kaun was recalled by Calgary on January 11 and made his Flames debut that night against the Columbus Blue Jackets.
