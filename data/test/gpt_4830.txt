He attended the University of Pennsylvania and graduated with a Bachelor of Arts degree in 1949. He then attended Columbia Law School, where he received his Juris Doctor in 1952.  Rudin started his legal career as a law clerk for Judge Learned Hand of the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit. He then worked for the law firm of Sullivan & Cromwell, where he became a partner in 1957. Rudin left Sullivan & Cromwell in 1967 to become the president and CEO of the brokerage firm Bear, Stearns. He served in that role until 1985, when he was appointed to the United States Court of Appeals for the Third Circuit. Rudin served on the court until his retirement in 2001.  After retiring from the bench, Rudin resumed his work as a consultant and board member for several companies, including Goldman Sachs and American Express. He also served on the board of trustees for New York University and Columbia College. Rudin died at his home in Southampton, New York, at the age of 83.
