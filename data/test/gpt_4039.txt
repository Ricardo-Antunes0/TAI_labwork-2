Joseph Hensley (12 June 1824 – 11 November 1892) was an American lawyer and politician who served as the 33rd Governor of Arkansas from 1881 to 1883.  Hensley was born in Clark County, Kentucky, on June 12, 1824. He moved with his parents to Arkansas in 1835 and attended the common schools. He studied law and was admitted to the bar in 1850. Hensley began practice in Arkansaw and became a successful lawyer.  Hensley served as the prosecuting attorney for Clark County from 1850 to 1853. He also served as a member of the Arkansas House of Representatives from 1860 to 1861 and from 1865 to 1869. Hensley was elected as a Democrat to the Forty-ninth Congress (March 4, 1865 – March 3, 1869). He was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1868.  Hensley was elected governor of Arkansas in 1880. He served until he resigned on January 10, 1883, due to ill health. Hensley died on November 11, 1892, at his home in Little Rock, Arkansas. He is buried in Rose Hill Cemetery in Little Rock.
