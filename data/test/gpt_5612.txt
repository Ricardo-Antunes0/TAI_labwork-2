Ibrahim Amir (born 9 December 1967) is a Pakistani-born British businessman and the founder and chairman of the private equity firm, Xtreme Capital.  Ibrahim Amir was born in Pakistan in 1967 to a father who was a doctor and a mother who was a teacher. He attended English schools in Pakistan and India before moving to the United Kingdom in 1984.  He started his career as a financial analyst with Price Waterhouse Coopers before becoming a senior manager at the investment bank, Lazard. In 2001, he founded Xtreme Capital, a private equity firm that has since invested in companies such as Tesco, Marks & Spencer, and Virgin Media.  Ibrahim Amir has been involved in a number of philanthropic causes including supporting educational initiatives in Pakistan and sponsoring young talent from disadvantaged backgrounds into the business world.
