She studied at the Conservatorio di Milano with Carlo Alberto Magni and Giuseppe Verdi, and at the Royal College of Music in London with Dame Ethel Smyth. Returning to Italy, she taught at the Milan Conservatory from 1928 to 1964. She also composed chamber music, orchestral music, choral works and songs. Her most famous works include the opera "L'amore vietato" (1939), the ballet "Il ritorno di Ulisse in patria" (1957) and the Requiem mass (1958). Giuseppina Masotti Biggiogero died in Milan on 29 October 1964.
