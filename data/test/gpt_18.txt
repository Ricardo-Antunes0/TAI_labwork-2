The Palestine pound (, ; , , ; , ; ) was the currency of the British Mandate of Palestine between 1923 and 1948. It was subdivided into 100 cents.  The Palestine pound was issued by the British authorities in 1923, replacing the Ottoman pound. The first coins were issued in 1925, with 1 pence, 2 pence, 5 pence, 10 pence and 20 pence denominations. The Palestine pound was replaced by the Israeli shekel in 1948.
