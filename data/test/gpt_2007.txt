Vyacheslav Alekseyevich Nikonov (; born 5 June 1966 in Leningrad, Soviet Union) is a Russian professional football coach and a former player.  Nikonov started his professional career in 1985 with PFC Leningrad. He played for the club until 1989, when he was transferred to FC Spartak Moscow. With Spartak, Nikonov won the Russian Premier League in 1990 and 1991. He also played for FC Dynamo Moscow and FC Rostov.  After leaving playing football, Nikonov became a coach. He started his coaching career with FC Rostov in 2004. In 2006, he was appointed as head coach of PFC CSKA Moscow. He stayed with the club until 2009, when he was sacked after a poor start to the 2009–10 season. He then spent a year out of coaching before being appointed as head coach of FC Krylia Sovetov Samara in 2011. In 2013, Nikonov was sacked by Krylia Sovetov Samara after a poor start to the 2013–14 season. He then spent two years out of coaching before being appointed as head coach of FC Terek Grozny in 2015. With Terek Grozny, Nikonov won the Russian Premier League in 2017 and 2018.
