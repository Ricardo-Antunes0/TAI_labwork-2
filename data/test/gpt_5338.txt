Henry Dodge was born in Kingston, New York on October 12, 1782. He attended the common schools and graduated from Yale College in 1803. He studied law with John Young and was admitted to the bar in 1806.  Dodge commenced practice in Kingston, but soon moved to Rochester, where he became one of the leading lawyers. He served as assistant district attorney for Monroe County from 1811 to 1815, and as county attorney from 1815 to 1820.  In 1820 Dodge was elected as a Democrat to the United States Senate, where he served until his resignation in 1837. He was chairman of the Committee on Public Buildings and Grounds from 1830 to 1833 and of the Committee on Expenditures in the Department of State from 1833 to 1837. He was also a member of the Committee on Commerce from 1830 to 1834 and of the Joint Committee on Printing from 1836 to 1837.  Dodge returned to Rochester in 1837 and resumed practicing law. He died there on October 5, 1852. He was interred in Mount Hope Cemetery.
