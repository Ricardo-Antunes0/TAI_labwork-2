West Kyo (also known locally as Old Kyo, Hangul: 왕국; Hanja: 遼宮, romanized: Wangguk) is a city located in the western part of the Korean Peninsula. It is the capital of the Yeongnam Province and has a population of over 1 million people.  West Kyo is located in the heart of Chungcheongbuk-do, approximately south of Seoul, and north of Busan. The city has an elevation of above sea level. The city is surrounded by mountains on three sides, with the Yeongnam River flowing through it. The city has a temperate climate with a rainy season from October to March, and a dry season from May to September.  West Kyo was founded in 1876 as a small fishing village on the banks of the Yeongnam River by Yesung-gun, the fourth son of Gyeonggi-do's last king, Gongmin. The town was named after West Kyo-guk (노서국), one of the Eight Provinces of ancient Korea. In 1895, West Kyo became an independent county and was elevated to city status in 1910. The city experienced rapid industrialization in the 1960s and 1970s under the leadership of then mayor Kim Dae-jung.
