The second Balkenende cabinet was the executive branch of the Dutch government from 3 November 2002 to 22 March 2006. The cabinet was a coalition of the Labour Party (PvdA), the Christian Democratic Appeal (CDA), and the Liberal Party (PVV). The prime minister was Wim Kok (PvdA).  The cabinet was defeated in the 2006 general election, which led to the formation of the third Balkenende cabinet.  The second Balkenende cabinet was formed after the general election on 3 November 2002. The coalition consisted of the Labour Party (PvdA), the Christian Democratic Appeal (CDA), and the Liberal Party (PVV). The prime minister was Wim Kok (PvdA).  The cabinet initially consisted of fifteen ministers: three from the PvdA, two from the CDA, seven from the PVV, and two independents. The three PvdA ministers were Jan Peter Balkenende, Rita Verdonk, and Fleur Agnew; the two CDA ministers were Frits Bolkestein and Hans Dijkstal; the seven PVV ministers were Geert Wilders, Pim Fortuyn, Alexander Pechtold, Hans Janmaat, Edwin van der Sar, Rita Verdonk, and Piet Hein Donner.
