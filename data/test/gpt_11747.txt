Jean-Marie-Théodore Baudouin also d’Aubigny, born in Paris on 11 October 1784, was a French general.  He entered the French army in 1803 and fought in the Napoleonic Wars, serving in Spain and Portugal. In 1809 he was made a brigadier general and fought in Switzerland. He was made a major general in 1813 and fought in Russia, where he was wounded at Borodino.  In 1815 he became governor of the Illyrian Provinces and, in 1818, of Algeria. He returned to France in 1827 and was made a lieutenant general. In 1830 he was made a marshal of France.   He died on 27 January 1841 in Paris.
