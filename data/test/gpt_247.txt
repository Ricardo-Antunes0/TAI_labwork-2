Lucius Lyon (February 26, 1800September 24, 1851) was an American lawyer and politician who served as a U.S. Representative from New York and Secretary of the Treasury under President James K. Polk. Born in Geneseo, New York, Lyon studied law and was admitted to the bar in 1825. He commenced practice in Batavia, New York and served as probate judge of Genesee County from 1830 to 1833. He resumed the practice of law in Batavia until 1850, when he moved to Washington, D.C., and resumed the practice of his profession. Lyon served as Secretary of the Treasury under President James K. Polk from March 4, 1845, to January 17, 1850. He died in Washington, D.C., on September 24, 1851.
