William Esco Moerner (born June 24, 1953) is an American physician and professor of reproductive medicine and surgery at the University of California, San Francisco (UCSF). He is a specialist in infertility and assisted reproduction.  Moerner is best known for his work on assisted reproduction technologies, including in-vitro fertilization (IVF), donor sperm use, and preimplantation genetic diagnosis (PGD). He has been a pioneer in the field of mitochondrial genetics, which has led to significant advances in assisted reproductive technologies. Moerner is also the director of the UCSF In Vitro Fertilization Program, one of the largest IVF programs in the world.  Moerner was born in San Francisco, California. He received his bachelor's degree from Stanford University in 1975 and his medical degree from UCSF in 1979. He completed his residency at UCSF and his fellowship at the University of Pennsylvania. Moerner joined the faculty at UCSF in 1984. He is a member of the American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists, the American Society for Reproductive Medicine, and the American Association for Theoretical Biology.
