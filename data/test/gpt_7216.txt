Jennifer Rice is a Canadian politician, who is currently the Minister of Foreign Affairs in the Liberal government of Justin Trudeau. Rice has also been the Member of Parliament for the riding of Mississauga Centre since 2006.  Rice was born in Toronto, Ontario, on February 15, 1968. She is the daughter of Barbara and Bruce Rice, and has two brothers. Rice attended Etobicoke School of the Arts and York University, where she received a Bachelor of Arts in political science in 1990.  Rice worked as a researcher for then-Liberal MP Lynne Yelich from 1990 to 1992. From 1992 to 1994, she worked as an assistant to then-Liberal MP John Manley. Rice then served as a policy analyst for then-Liberal MP Sheila Copps from 1994 to 1997.  Rice was first elected to Parliament in the 2006 federal election, defeating incumbent Conservative Party MP Jay Aspin by over 12,000 votes. In Parliament, Rice has served on the House of Commons Standing Committee on International Trade and on the House of Commons Standing Committee on Canadian Heritage. She also served as Parliamentary Secretary to the Minister of Transport from 2009 to 2010 and Parliamentary Secretary to the Minister of Industry from 2010 to 2011.  On November 4, 2013, it was announced that Rice would be appointed Canada's Ambassador to Japan. She assumed her post on December 12, 2013. On September 29, 2017, it was announced that Rice would be appointed Minister of Foreign Affairs in the Liberal government of Justin Trudeau.
