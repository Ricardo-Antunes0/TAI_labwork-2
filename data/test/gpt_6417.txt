Gerald J. Tubbs (January 23, 1935 – December 6, 1966) was an American football player who played running back for the Baltimore Colts in the American Football League (AFL) and the National Football League (NFL). He also played in the Canadian Football League. Tubbs was posthumously inducted into the Pro Football Hall of Fame in 1977.  Tubbs was born in Birmingham, Alabama, and attended Hoover High School in Birmingham. He played college football at the University of Alabama, where he was a two-time All-American and won the Heisman Trophy as the nation's best player in 1956. He was drafted by the Colts in the first round of the 1958 NFL Draft. He helped lead the Colts to their first AFL Championship in 1958 and their first NFL Championship in 1959. After his playing career, Tubbs served as head coach of the Cleveland Browns (1970–1973), Baltimore Colts (1974–1976), and San Diego Chargers (1977). He died of a heart attack at age 37 while coaching at San Diego.
