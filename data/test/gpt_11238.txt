The Hudsucker Proxy is a 1994 comedy-drama film written and directed by Joel and Ethan Coen. The story follows the tribulations of a small-time advertising executive, his philandering boss, and their employees in the fictitious company of Hudsucker Industries.  The Hudsucker Proxy was acclaimed by critics, who praised its humor, complex plot, and performances by its cast. It was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Screenplay and won the Golden Globe Award for Best Screenplay - Motion Picture. The film has been cited as an influence on later works by the Coen brothers, including Fargo and True Grit.
