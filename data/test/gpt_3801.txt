Robert Joseph Thorpe (June 12, 1935 – October 8, 1978) was an American professional baseball player. He played in Major League Baseball (MLB) for the Boston Red Sox and Philadelphia Phillies. Thorpe was a two-time All-Star, winning the American League (AL) Most Valuable Player (MVP) Award in 1966 and 1967.  Thorpe enjoyed a successful 12-year career, posting a record of 158–108 with a 3.30 ERA in 377 appearances, including 346 starts. He was recognized as one of the best pitchers of his era and is considered by many to be one of the finest pitchers in MLB history. Thorpe was inducted into the National Baseball Hall of Fame in 1979.  Thorpe was born on June 12, 1935, in Indianapolis, Indiana. He attended Weir High School in Indianapolis, where he played baseball and football. Thorpe was drafted by the Boston Red Sox in the third round of the 1951 Major League Baseball draft.  Thorpe made his debut with the Red Sox in 1953, and spent nine seasons with the team before being traded to the Philadelphia Phillies in 1961. In 1966 and 1967, Thorpe led the AL in wins and strikeouts while earning MVP Awards each season. Thorpe also won two Cy Young Awards (1965 and 1967), becoming one of only four pitchers to win both awards.
