Bridget Kathleen Archer (born 18 May 1975) is an English actress. She is known for her roles as Siobhan Clarke in the British soap opera, Coronation Street, and for her role as the titular character on the FX drama series Archer.  Archer was born in Manchester, England, to parents Kathleen (n√©e Butler) and Tim Archer. She has two older sisters, Karen and Claire. Archer attended Stretford High School for Girls and graduated in 1993. She then studied at Drama Centre London. Archer made her professional stage debut in 1998 playing the role of Juliet in William Shakespeare's play Romeo and Juliet.  In 2001, she was cast as Siobhan Clarke on the British soap opera, Coronation Street. The character was introduced as a love interest for Tony Capstick (John Michie). After four years on the show, she left in 2003 to pursue other acting opportunities.  In 2004, Archer starred in the short-lived Fox series Threshold. The show was canceled after one season. In 2006, she appeared on the BBC One soap opera EastEnders as Lauren Branning. In 2007, she starred in the British independent film The Big White as Julie.
