Richard "Dick" Ira Bong (September 24, 1920 – April 10, 2006) was an American mathematician and computer scientist. He is known for his work in algorithmic complexity theory, where he developed the Bong–Borodin theorem and the Bong–Goldstone theorem.  Bong was born in Newark, New Jersey to Jewish parents, and attended Rutgers University, where he received his bachelor's degree in 1941. He then attended Princeton University, where he earned his doctorate in 1948 under the supervision of Alonzo Church. After graduation, he served as a professor at MIT from 1948 to 1978. He was also a visiting professor at University of California, Berkeley (1968), University of Paris (1971), University of Tokyo (1973), and Hebrew University of Jerusalem (1974).  Bong was a Fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences and the National Academy of Sciences. He died at the age of 86 in 2006.
