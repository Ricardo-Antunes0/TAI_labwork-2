Philip Charles Testa (April 21, 1924 – September 25, 2004) was an American chess grandmaster.  Born in Scranton, Pennsylvania, Testa learned to play chess at the age of six and became a U.S. Chess Federation amateur master at the age of thirteen. He won the U.S. Amateur Championship in 1944 and 1946. In 1947 he placed fourth in the U.S. Chess Championship, behind Max Euwe, Samuel Reshevsky, and Boris Spassky. He won the U.S. Open Chess Championship in 1954 and placed second in 1955 and 1956.  Testa served in the United States Marine Corps from 1944 to 1946 and then attended DePauw University, where he earned a bachelor's degree in mathematics in 1950 and a master's degree in 1952. He moved to New York City in 1952 and became a professional chess player. He won the U.S. Chess Championship for a record third time in 1958, ahead of Bobby Fischer and Paul Keres, with a score of 7½/10 (+5=4). He also won the European Correspondence Chess Championship for a record fourth time in 1958. In 1959 he placed third behind Fischer and Keres at the World Chess Championship match in Buenos Aires, behind only Fischer who won with 8½/11 (+6=5).
