Mucuna gigantea is a species of large legume in the Fabaceae family. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, where it grows as a tree up to 30 m tall. The leaves are large, up to 150 cm long and 20 cm broad, and are covered in a dense coat of coarse hairs. The flowers are pink or purple, and are pollinated by bees. The pods are large, up to 2.5 kg, and contain up to 400 seeds.
