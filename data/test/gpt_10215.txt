Vincent Edward Moncrief is an American mathematician and computer scientist who is best known for his work in the theory of algorithms. He is a Fields Medalist and a Fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.  Moncrief was born in Chicago, Illinois, on October 10, 1954. After completing his undergraduate studies at the University of Notre Dame in 1976, he received his Ph.D. from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 1981 under the supervision of John Hopcroft.  Moncrief has held positions at several universities, including the University of California at Berkeley, where he is currently a Professor of Mathematics and Computer Science. He also served as the inaugural holder of the J. W. Clements Chair in Algorithms and Computation at Rice University from 2000 to 2002.  Moncrief has authored or co-authored more than two hundred papers in a variety of fields, including algorithms, computational complexity, graph theory, and mathematical logic. He has also written two books: Algorithms: Theory and Practice (2nd edition, 2009), which has been translated into Chinese, French, Italian, Russian, Spanish, and Turkish; and Graph Theory: An Introduction (2nd edition, 2009), which has been translated into Chinese, French, Italian, Russian, Spanish, Portuguese, and Turkish.  In 2006 Moncrief was awarded an honorary doctorate by the University of Notre Dame. In 2013 he was awarded an honorary doctorate by Rice University.
