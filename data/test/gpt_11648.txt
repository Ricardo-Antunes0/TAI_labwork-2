The Banai is a sub-tribe of the Qiang people. They are located in Qinghai and Sichuan provinces of China. The Banai speak a Tibeto-Burman language.  The Banai live in the gorge of the Yarlung Tsangpo River in Qinghai and Sichuan provinces. They are hunter-gatherers, and their main occupation is livestock herding. The Banai have a diverse religious tradition, including Buddhism, Daoism, and Shamanism.
