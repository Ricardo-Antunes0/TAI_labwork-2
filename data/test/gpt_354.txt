Cornelio Saavedra Rodríguez (1821 – April 7, 1861) was a Chilean poet and patriot.  Saavedra Rodríguez was born in Santiago, Chile, in 1821. He studied at the University of Chile, where he first showed an interest in poetry. He fought in the War of the Pacific against Peru and Bolivia, and later served as Chile's Minister of Foreign Affairs. He died in 1861, at the age of 36.  Saavedra Rodríguez is best known for his poems about Chile's history and culture. His most famous work is the poem "Nuestra patria", which commemorates the Chilean War of Independence.
