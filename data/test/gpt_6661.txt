Born in Norwich, Bright began her career as a backing singer for artists including Joe Cocker, David Bowie, and Elton John. She released her debut album, "Bloody Sunday", in 1981. The album reached number four on the UK Albums Chart and spawned the singles "Satellite" and "A New Day". Bright's second album, "The Wanderer", was released in 1983 and reached number three on the UK Albums Chart. Her third album, "Fire Over England", was released in 1985 and reached number two on the UK Albums Chart. Bright retired from music in 1988 to focus on her family. She died of cancer aged just 38.
