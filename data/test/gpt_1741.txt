Typhoon Nanmadol, known in the Philippines as Typhoon Ketsana, was a powerful typhoon that caused widespread damage to the Philippines during the 2004 Pacific typhoon season. The storm originated from a tropical depression on September 10 and intensified into a typhoon on September 12. Nanmadol made landfall in the Philippines on September 14 as a strong typhoon, causing catastrophic damage to the island nation. The typhoon rapidly weakened as it moved over open waters and dissipated on September 17.  Nanmadol was the strongest typhoon to hit the Philippines in over a decade, causing $1.5 billion in damage and dozens of deaths. The storm destroyed more than 1,000 homes and left over 100,000 people homeless. Hundreds of schools were also destroyed, leaving over 100,000 students without access to education. In addition, Nanmadol caused significant damage to infrastructure, including power plants and roads. Overall, the storm killed eight people and injured more than 1,500 others.
