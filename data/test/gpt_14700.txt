The Western Album is an album by American singer and musician Elvis Presley, released on RCA Victor in mono and stereo, LPM/LSP 3470, in July 1956. It was certified Gold on March 27, 1992 by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA).  The album was recorded at Radio Recorders in Hollywood on July 15 and 16, 1955. It featured a selection of songs that had been successful on the charts for Elvis Presley during the first half of 1955. Most of the selections were released as singles before the album's release. Two new songs by Presley were also included, "Heartbreak Hotel" and "I Believe in You (She Believes in Me)." The single "Heartbreak Hotel" reached number one on the American pop chart and number two on the British pop chart.  The album was reissued on CD in 1997 and again in 2002 with additional material. In 2007, the original masters were remastered for release on a two-disc set by Sony BMG. The master tapes were transferred from Graceland by Presley's son Chad.
