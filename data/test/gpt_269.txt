Tigray Province (Amharic and ) is a province of Ethiopia. The province occupies an area of 5,610 square kilometers and has a population of 1,536,000 as of the 2007 census. Its capital and largest city is Addis Ababa.  The province has a long and rich history. Tigray was the home of the first Ethiopian emperor, Menelik I. Tigray Province is also known for its natural resources such as coffee, sugar cane, and gold. The province also has a number of tourist attractions such as the Aksumite (First Dynasty) ruins at Hawi Tsegaye and the spectacular Amana National Park.
