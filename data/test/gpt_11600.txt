Born in 1856, James William Olive was educated at Rugby and Trinity College, Cambridge. After spending two years as a Rhodes Scholar at Oxford, he joined the Foreign Office in 1879. He served in various posts in Africa and Asia, becoming Director of the Foreign Office's African Department in 1895. He was made a Knight Bachelor in 1903.  Olive served as the Governor of the Gold Coast from 1898 to 1902 and as the Governor of Mauritius from 1903 to 1908. In both positions he made significant improvements to the administration of the colonies. He also played an important role in encouraging increased trade between Britain and the colonies. Olive retired from active service in 1911, but continued to serve on the Colonial Council until his death in 1924.
