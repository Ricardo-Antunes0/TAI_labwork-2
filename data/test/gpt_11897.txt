Pinellas Park is a city located in Pinellas County, Florida, United States. As of the 2010 census, the population was 31,015. It is part of the Tampa–St. Petersburg–Clearwater Metropolitan Statistical Area.  The city was founded in 1909 as a planned community by the developer George E. Pate and named for his friend and former business partner, James A. Pinellas, who served as Florida's sixth governor from 1907 to 1911. The city's downtown district was designated a historic district by the City of St Petersburg in 1986.  Pinellas Park is located at 29°14′N 82°37′W (29.25,-82.62). It is bordered to the northeast by Clearwater, to the east by Dunedin, to the south by Tarpon Springs, and to the west by Largo.
