Trochenbrod or Trohinbrod, also in Polish: Zofiówka, is a traditional dish from the Polish cuisine. It is a type of soup that originated in the Podlasie region, in the 17th century. The dish consists of beef, vegetables, and sour cream, and is served with rye bread. Trochenbrod is also known as Żurek, and both dishes are variations of the same dish.
