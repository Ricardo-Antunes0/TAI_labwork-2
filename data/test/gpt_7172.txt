The player controls a car on a race track and must avoid obstacles while racing other cars. The game includes two modes: a time trial mode where the player races against the clock, and a race mode where the player competes against other drivers.  "Speed Race" was ported to various home systems including the Atari 2600, Atari 7800, Commodore 64, MS-DOS and Nintendo Entertainment System. A sequel, "Speed Race 2", was released in 1983.  "Speed Race" was followed by "Super Speed Race" in 1984, "Racecar GP" in 1986, and "Turbo Racing" in 1991. A spin-off game, "Turbo Circus", was released for the Sega Saturn in 1996. A remake of "Speed Race", titled "Super Turbo Racing", was released for the PlayStation 4 and Xbox One in 2019.
