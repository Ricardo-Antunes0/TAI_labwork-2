Irene Hickson (August 14, 1915 – November 3, 1994) was an American actress and singer.  Born in Norfolk, Virginia, Hickson began her career as a vaudeville singer before making her Broadway debut in 1938 in the musical "The Boys from Syracuse". She went on to make appearances in various Broadway productions including "Oklahoma!" (1943), "The Unsinkable Molly Brown" (1964), and "The Sunshine Boys" (1975). Her film career began in 1950 with roles in "The Outlaw" and "The Lady from Shanghai". She starred in the television series "The Wonderful World of Disney" (1955–1957) and appeared in several films including "Harlem Nights" (1989). Hickson was also a successful songwriter, penning the hit songs "Put On A Happy Face" and "I Can't Give You Anything But Love". She died of natural causes at her home in Los Angeles at the age of 80.
