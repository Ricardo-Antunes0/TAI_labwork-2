Hattersley railway station serves the Hattersley housing estate in the Metropolitan Borough of Oldham, Greater Manchester, England. The station is also the terminus of the Hattersley railway line, which is operated by Northern.  The station was opened by the Manchester, Sheffield and Lincolnshire Railway on 4 October 1859. It became part of the Great Central Railway in 1875. The line was nationalised in 1948 and became part of British Railways in 1948. The station was closed to passengers on 3 January 1966 and to freight on 5 November 1967. It was reopened by British Railways as a stopping point for passengers on 18 May 1969. The station was closed again on 27 September 2006 and reopened by Transport for Greater Manchester (TfGM) as a light rail stop on 8 December 2006.  Hattersley railway station is served by two trains an hour in each direction Monday to Saturday, and two trains an hour on Sundays.
