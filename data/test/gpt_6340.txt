"Rosso Malpelo" is a short story by the Argentinian writer Jorge Luis Borges. It was first published in the magazine Sur in 1945.  The story is set in the fictional country of Isla Negra, which is located on a certain part of the Atlantic Ocean. The story's main character is a man named Santiago, who is travelling there on a boat. Santiago meets a woman called Malpelo, who has a strange and beautiful color called rosso malpelo. Santiago falls in love with Malpelo and eventually marries her.  The story is notable for its use of mythological references, its exploration of the concept of infinity, and its surrealism.
