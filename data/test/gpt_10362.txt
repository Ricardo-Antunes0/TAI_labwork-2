The Rhabanus-Maurus-Gymnasium St. Ottilien is a church-funded, state-run secondary school in the city of Cologne, Germany. It was founded in 1792 as the Kölner Stadtschule and is one of the oldest schools in Germany. The Rhabanus-Maurus-Gymnasium has around 1,600 students and 50 teachers. The school offers a comprehensive range of subjects, including mathematics, natural sciences, social sciences, arts, music and sports.   The Rhabanus-Maurus-Gymnasium is known for its excellent academic results, with students regularly achieving top positions in national and international examinations. In 2009, the school was awarded the prestigious "Excellence" label by the German Education Excellence Foundation (Bildungs Excellence-Förderungsverein).
