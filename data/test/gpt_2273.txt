Walkerana leptodactyla is a species of frog in the family Hylidae.  The generic name "Walkerana" honors American herpetologist Otto Wilhelm Walker (1893-1978). The specific name "leptodactyla" is derived from the Ancient Greek words "lepto-" meaning "thin" and "dactylos-" meaning "finger".  This frog is endemic to the Atlantic Forest of northeastern Brazil. Its natural habitats are subtropical or tropical moist lowland forests, rivers, and intermittent freshwater marshes.
