Puffins are any of three species of seabird in the genus Fratercula, with a worldwide distribution. They are the smallest of the puffins, and have a distinctive black head and neck, white body, and black wings and tail.  The three puffin species are the northern fulmar, the common puffin, and the Iceland puffin. All three are found in cold seas around the world, with the northern fulmar being the most widespread.puffins feed on fish and other aquatic creatures. They nest on cliffs or in rock crevices.
