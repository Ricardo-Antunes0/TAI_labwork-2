Antawn Cortez Jamison (; born June 12, 1976) is an American retired professional basketball player who played for the Golden State Warriors, Dallas Mavericks, Washington Wizards and Cleveland Cavaliers of the National Basketball Association (NBA). He played collegiately at the University of Maryland.  Jamison was originally drafted by the Golden State Warriors with the sixth overall pick in the 1996 NBA draft. He played for the Warriors, Mavericks, Wizards and Cavaliers before retiring in 2013. He is one of only four players in NBA history to score 20,000 points, 10,000 rebounds and 5,000 assists. He was a Six-time All-Star and won two MVP Awards (2005–06 and 2006–07). He also led the Warriors to their first championship in 40 years in 2015.
