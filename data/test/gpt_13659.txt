Downright Dencey is a 1927 children's historical novel by A.A. Milne. It is about a young boy who goes to live with his grandparents in a Welsh valley during the industrial revolution.  The novel is based on the life of Milne's father, who worked in the coal mines of Wales. Milne wrote the novel in just six weeks, during a period of illness in which he was confined to bed.
