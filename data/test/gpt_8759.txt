Petronel Malan (born in 23 February 1977 in Cape Town, South Africa) is a South African cricketer who played in Tests and One Day Internationals (ODIs). He was the first black player to represent South Africa in Tests.  Malan made his Test debut for South Africa against England in 2001 and played four Tests, scoring 116 runs at an average of 21.66. He also played ODIs for South Africa, appearing in 98 matches and scoring 2,611 runs at an average of 34.92. Malan represented his country at the 2003 Cricket World Cup, where he scored 229 runs in the tournament, including a century against Namibia. He retired from international cricket after the 2007 World Cup.
