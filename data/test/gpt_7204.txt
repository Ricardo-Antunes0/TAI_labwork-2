The Molly Pitcher Inn is a privately-owned inn located in downtown Saratoga Springs, New York. It is named after the nineteenth-century woman known as Molly Pitcher, who helped keep troops hydrated during the American Revolutionary War.  The inn has been in the same family for over 150 years, and includes eleven guest rooms, a restaurant, and a bar. The inn is known for its cuisine, which includes traditional American dishes as well as French and Italian dishes. The inn also offers a variety of wine and beer selections, as well as a selection of cigars.  The inn is a popular tourist destination, and it is often used as a filming location for movies and television shows. In 2008, the Inn was used as the setting for the movie "Saratoga", starring Michael Douglas and Helen Mirren.
