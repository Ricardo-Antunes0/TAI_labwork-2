Ghazar Parpetsi  was a 5th to 6th century Sassanian Era Zoroastrian monastery located in present-day Iran. The monastery is known for its frescoes, the finest of which are thought to date from the 6th century.  The monastery was founded by the Sassanian general Shapur I in the 5th century and was named after his father, Ghazan. It was one of the most important Zoroastrian monasteries of its time, and is thought to have been one of the most important centres of learning for Zoroastrian priests. The frescoes at Ghazar Parpetsi are some of the finest examples of Sassanian era art.
