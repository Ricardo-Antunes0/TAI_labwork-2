Peter Douglas Feaver (born December 17, 1961) is an American political scientist and expert on counterinsurgency. He is currently the Director of the Center for International Security and Cooperation at the RAND Corporation.  Feaver has written extensively on counterinsurgency, democratic transition, and conflict resolution, and has been a consultant to several U.S. government agencies, including the State Department and the Central Intelligence Agency. He is also a regular commentator on foreign policy issues for U.S. media outlets.  Born in Washington, D.C., Feaver graduated from Dartmouth College in 1983 with a degree in political science, and later earned his doctorate from Duke University in 1988. From 1988 to 1992 he served as a Peace Corps volunteer in the Dominican Republic, where he worked on development projects related to agriculture and health.  Feaver began his career as a research associate at the Center for International Security and Cooperation at the University of Maryland in 1992, later serving as its director from 1999 to 2004. From 2004 to 2006 he was a Research Fellow at the London School of Economics and Political Science, where he was also deputy director of LSE's Middle East Centre.  In 2006 Feaver joined RAND Corporation as its Director of the Center for International Security and Cooperation. At RAND he has been responsible for overseeing RAND's work on a broad range of issues related to international security, including counterterrorism, nuclear deterrence, arms control, energy security, climate change adaptation, humanitarian assistance/response, and peacekeeping operations.
