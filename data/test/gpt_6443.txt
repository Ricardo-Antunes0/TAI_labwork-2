Robert Jan Mrazek (born November 6, 1945) is a retired American professional ice hockey defenseman.  Born in Detroit, Michigan, Mrazek played college hockey at the University of Michigan where he was team captain and led the Wolverines to the NCAA Championship in 1967. He was drafted by the Boston Bruins in the first round, 16th overall, of the 1967 NHL Amateur Draft.  Mrazek played nine seasons in the NHL with the Bruins and Philadelphia Flyers. He also played one season in the World Hockey Association with the Buffalo Braves. Mrazek was a member of the Bruins team that won the Stanley Cup in 1972. He retired after playing one season with the Flyers in 1976-77.  After his playing career ended, Mrazek served as an assistant coach for the Flyers for two seasons (1978-79 and 1979-80). In 1981, he became head coach of the WHL's Swift Current Broncos, leading them to their first WHL title in 1982. He then served as head coach of EC Red Bull Salzburg of Austria from 1984-1989 before returning to Swift Current as head coach for two more seasons (1991-93). In 1995, he became head coach of the US national team, leading them to a bronze medal at the IIHF World Championship in Sweden that year. Mrazek was fired after coaching Team USA at the 1996 Olympics where they were eliminated in the quarterfinals by Canada.  In 1997, Mrazek became coach and general manager of HC Slovan Brat
