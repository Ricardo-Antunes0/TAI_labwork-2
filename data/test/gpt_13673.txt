Lee Harris Pomeroy (November 19, 1932 – March 6, 2019) was an American historian and professor emeritus at the University of California, Davis.  Pomeroy was born in 1932 in Boston, Massachusetts to Harry and Ruth (née Harris) Pomeroy. After graduating from high school in 1950, he attended the University of California, Berkeley, where he received his bachelor's degree in 1953 and his master's degree in 1954. He then taught at the University of Oregon from 1954 to 1956, before joining the faculty at the University of California, Davis in 1956. Pomeroy remained there until his retirement in 1998.  Pomeroy's research focused on American history, with a particular emphasis on the history of the American West. He has written extensively on topics such as the Mexican-American War, American imperialism, and the American Civil War. His work has been praised for its insight into historical events and its ability to make complex subjects accessible to nonspecialists.  Pomeroy died on March 6, 2019 at the age of 87.
