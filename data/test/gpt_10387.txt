Tuanigamanuolepola Tagovailoa ( ; born March 2, 1997) is an American football quarterback for the Alabama Crimson Tide of the National Collegiate Athletic Association (NCAA) Division I Football Bowl Subdivision (FBS). He played college football for the University of Alabama.  Tagovailoa was born in Honolulu, Hawaii, to parents Tongan and Samoan heritage. He was raised in both Tuaoloa, a small town on the island of Kauai, and Huntsville, Alabama. He attended Madison Academy in Huntsville before enrolling at the University of Alabama in 2013.   As a true freshman in 2015, Tagovailoa started all 13 games for the Crimson Tide, completing 267 of 451 passes for 3,665 yards with 26 touchdowns and six interceptions. The following year, he became Alabama's starting quarterback as a sophomore and led the team to a College Football Playoff semifinal appearance. He was named second-team All-American by "The Associated Press" and "USA Today", and won the Maxwell Award as the nation's best player. In 2018, he became Alabama's fourth quarterback to win three national championships and first since Bear Bryant in 1963. He was also named first-team All-American by "The Sporting News", "The Walter Camp Football Foundation", and "The Associated Press".  Tagovailoa announced on May 29, 2019, that he would forgo his final year of eligibility and enter the 2019 NFL draft.
