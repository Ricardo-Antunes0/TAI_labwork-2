Ruth Lyons (born Ruth Evelyn Reeves October 24, 1931) is an American broadcast journalist and author. She is best known for her work as a news anchor, reporter, and commentator for ABC News from 1966 to 1998.  Lyons was the first female news anchor on national television. She was also the first female correspondent for ABC News. After leaving ABC News, Lyons served as a senior fellow at the Corporation for Public Broadcasting and was a columnist for The New York Times.  Lyons was born in Mound Ridge, Tennessee, on October 24, 1931. She graduated from Fisk University in Nashville with a degree in English literature.  Lyons began her broadcasting career as a reporter and news anchor at WSM-TV in Nashville in 1959. In 1966, she became the first female correspondent for ABC News. In that role, she covered major events such as the Vietnam War, the Watergate scandal, and the assassination of President John F. Kennedy.  Lyons left ABC News in 1998 to become a senior fellow at the Corporation for Public Broadcasting. In that capacity, she wrote articles for The New York Times and served as a columnist. Lyons retired from writing in 2006.  Lyons has written two books: A Woman's Place: Reclaiming the American Dream (1998) and Don't Forget Your Hat: One Woman's Search For The Perfect Hat (2005).
