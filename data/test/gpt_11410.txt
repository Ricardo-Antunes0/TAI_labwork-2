Access Software, Inc. was an American video game software company, founded in 1986 by brothers Rick and Scott Stein. The company developed and published video games for home computers, consoles, and mobile devices. After selling its console game division to Sega in 2001, the company changed its name to 2K Games in 2003.  Access Software was best known for its adventure games, which often starred well-known actors such as Tim Curry and John Rhys-Davies. It also created the Point & Click series of adventure games, the first of which, "The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes", was published in 1993. Other popular titles from Access Software include "Myst", "Riven", "The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion", "Fallout 3", and "Star Wars: The Old Republic".
