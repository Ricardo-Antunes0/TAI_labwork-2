Born in Liverpool, Shaw attended Liverpool School of Art from 1879 to 1882, then studied at the Royal Academy Schools in London under Sir John Everett Millais and Anthony Farrant. Shaw exhibited with the Royal Academy from 1889 to 1933. In 1907 he became a Fellow of the Royal Society. Shaw lived in Kent and died in 1931.  Shaw's major works include illustrations for the writings of Alfred Russel Wallace, studies of birds of the British Isles and North Africa, and paintings of natural scenes. Many of his paintings are based on his own travels and observations.
