A utility cooperative is a type of cooperative or mutual organization whose members share the ownership or use of a common resource, such as electricity, water, or gas. Utility cooperatives typically provide their members with lower prices for their services than would be available from private sector suppliers. They rely on the cooperative principle to ensure that the benefits of collective action are passed on to all members.  Utility cooperatives are common in North America and Europe, but are less common in Asia and Latin America. They have been especially successful in rural areas and among smaller businesses. Some utility cooperatives have also developed into multinationals.
