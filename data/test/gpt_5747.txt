Branko Tošović (German: Branko Tosovic, Serbian Cyrillic: Бранко Тосовић; born 29 November 1966) is a Serbian football coach and former player.  Tosovic started his playing career with FK Vojvodina in 1984, before joining First League side Partizan in 1988. He made over 300 appearances for the club, winning the Yugoslav First League and Serbian Cup titles in 1991 and 1992 respectively. He also played for Red Star Belgrade, Paris Saint-Germain, Sampdoria, Rangers and FC Twente. Tosovic retired from playing in 2003.  Tosovic began his coaching career with Partizan's youth team in 2003. He was appointed head coach of Serbia U21 in 2007, leading them to the 2007 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship title. He was appointed head coach of Serbia national team in January 2010, and led them to the 2010 World Cup qualifiers. However, he was fired following a 1–2 home loss to Lithuania on 24 March 2013.
