Leonard Freed (October 23, 1929 – November 9, 2009) was an American painter and sculptor. He was born in New York City. Freed is known for his abstract expressionist paintings and sculptures.  Leonard Freed was born on October 23, 1929, in New York City. He received his BFA from the Pratt Institute in 1955 and his MFA from Yale University in 1959. After graduating from college, Freed worked as a commercial artist before beginning his career as a painter and sculptor.  Freed's work is characterized by its abstract expressionist style. His paintings and sculptures often depict simple shapes and figure, with bright colors and bold brushstrokes. Freed's work has been exhibited in museums and galleries around the world, including the Museum of Modern Art in New York City, the Whitney Museum of American Art in New York City, the Tate Gallery in London, and the Musée National d'Art Moderne in Paris. He died on November 9, 2009, in Ithaca, New York.
