Thirumeninathar temple (also called Boominathar temple or Kandanathar temple) is a Hindu temple located in the town of Kandanathar, near Poovar in the Thanjavur district of Tamil Nadu, India. The temple is dedicated to the god Vishnu and is considered one of the most important shrines in southern India.  The presiding deity of the temple is Thirumeninathar, an incarnation of Vishnu. The temple complex also includes a gopuram (gateway tower), a koil (chapel), and several other associated shrines. The temple is considered one of the most important pilgrimage sites in southern Tamil Nadu. The annual festival associated with the temple, Thirukalyana Vaigasi, is one of the most important festivals in the region.
