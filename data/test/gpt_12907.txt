Eloise at Christmastime is a 2003 American-Canadian family comedy-drama film directed by Bob Nelson and written by Rita Wilson and her daughter, Alexandra Paul. It is based on the novel of the same name by Eleanor Estes.  The film follows the story of Eloise (Wilson), an adult daughter who returns home for Christmas to find that her mother (Paul) has remarried and has three children with her new husband (John Travolta). Eloise tries to reconnect with her estranged family and must deal with her mother's new family while still honoring her own Christmas traditions.   The film received mixed reviews from critics but was a box office success, grossing over $107 million worldwide. It was nominated for a Golden Globe Award for Best Motion Picture – Musical or Comedy and won the Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Motion Picture Musical or Comedy for Wilson.
