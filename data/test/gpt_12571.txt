The Matthew Bowen Homestead, also once known as the Bowen-Bowen House, is a historic residence located in Hawkins County, Tennessee.  The house was built in 1809 by Matthew Bowen, one of the early settlers of Hawkins County. It is a two-story log cabin with a gable roof and clapboard siding. It is currently used as a museum and research center and is open for tours during the summer.  The Matthew Bowen Homestead was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1978.
