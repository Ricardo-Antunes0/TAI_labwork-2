She was the author of eleven novels and five short story collections, as well as more than sixty essays and reviews, many of which appeared in The New York Times.  Born in 1916 in Brooklyn, New York, Norris attended Barnard College and Columbia University before moving to Paris in the late 1930s. There she worked as a journalist for The New Yorker and became friends with Truman Capote. Returning to the United States in 1948, she settled in Greenwich Village and began writing novels. Her first book, The Glass Menagerie (1944), won the Pulitzer Prize. Her most famous novel is The Help (2011), which was adapted into a successful film by Tate Taylor.  Norris died at her home in 2006.
