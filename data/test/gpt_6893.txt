Michael David Palance (born March 27, 1970) is an American actor and director. He is known for his roles in the films Dune, The Untouchables, Armageddon, and Syriana.  Born in Buffalo, New York, Palance moved to Los Angeles as a teenager to pursue a career in acting. He made his feature film debut in the role of Paul Atreides in David Lynch's Dune (1984). He went on to appear in such films as The Untouchables (1987), Armageddon (1998), and Syriana (2007). Palance has also directed several films, including the drama Saving Private Ryan (1998) and the comedy-drama Keeping Up with the Joneses (2016).
