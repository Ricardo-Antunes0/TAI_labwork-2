Anya Kamenetz (born September 15, 1980) is an American journalist, author and television personality. She is the co-founder and executive editor of independent news website BuzzFeed.  Kamenetz was born in New York City, to an Orthodox Jewish family. Her father is a doctor, and her mother is a social worker. She has a younger brother. Kamenetz graduated from Brown University in 2002 with a degree in political science and anthropology.  After college, Kamenetz worked as an associate at a law firm and then as a freelance journalist for "The New York Observer". In 2006, she moved to Los Angeles to work as a reporter for "Showtime News".  In 2007, Kamenetz and her writing partner Ben Smith founded BuzzFeed, an online news and entertainment website which quickly became one of the most widely read and discussed sites on the internet. Kamenetz served as the site's co-founder and executive editor until December 2016, when she resigned to join NBCUniversal as its head of digital media.   Kamenetz has written for "The New York Times", "The Washington Post", "Slate", "The Atlantic", "GQ", and other publications. Her first book, "Hi Mom: A Love Story", was published in July 2018 by HarperCollins Publishers.
