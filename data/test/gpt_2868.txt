Hiralal Sen (, Hiralal Shen; 2 August 1899 â€“ 24 October 1977) was an Indian philosopher and Professor Emeritus at the University of California, Berkeley. He was born in a Brahmin family in a small town in the state of Bihar in British India.   Sen was educated at Deoband, and then at Presidency College, Calcutta, where he obtained his bachelor's degree in 1918 and his master's degree in 1921. After two years of study at the University of Cambridge, England, he returned to India and took up a lectureship at the University of Delhi. In 1928 he moved to the United States to take up a post as Professor of Philosophy at the University of California, Berkeley. He held this position until his retirement in 1972.   Sen's philosophical work is concerned with questions such as reason, understanding, truth, knowledge, and reality. His most well-known work is The Idea of Justice (1961), which has been widely influential both inside and outside philosophy. He also published books on philosophy of language, epistemology, metaphysics, and aesthetics.   Sen was awarded the Nobel Prize in Economics in 1998 for his work on contract theory and economic governance.
