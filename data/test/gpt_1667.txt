He was drafted by the Milwaukee Bucks in the fourth round of the 1974 NBA draft. Webster played for the Bucks, Detroit Pistons, and Philadelphia 76ers in his career. Webster also played in Turkey for Galatasaray S.K. from 1975 to 1977.  Webster averaged 10.4 points per game and 4.5 rebounds per game in his career. He was a six-time All-Star and won two championships with the Pistons in 1989 and 1990. Webster also played in the 1988 Summer Olympics. Webster died of a heart attack at the age of 41 after playing in a game for the 76ers on December 10, 1998.
