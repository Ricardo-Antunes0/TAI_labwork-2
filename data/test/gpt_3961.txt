Nalin Jayawardena (Sinhala : නලින් ජයවර්ධන) (born 1 January 1966) is a Sri Lankan journalist, author and rights activist. He is the founder and editor-in-chief of the online magazine "Dinamina". He is also a board member of the International Federation of Human Rights (FIDH).  Nalin Jayawardena was born on 1 January 1966 in Gampaha, Western Province. He completed his primary and secondary education at Gampaha Royal College. After completing his university studies at the University of Peradeniya, he worked as a journalist for the "Daily Mirror" newspaper in Colombo. In 1998, he founded the online magazine "Dinamina", which he continues to edit and publish.  Jayawardena has been a vocal critic of the Sri Lankan government. He has been arrested several times for his political activities, most notably in 2006 for publishing articles critical of the government. In October 2006, he was sentenced to three years' imprisonment for sedition, but this sentence was later overturned on appeal. In March 2009, he was again arrested and charged with sedition after writing articles critical of the government's handling of the civil war in northern Sri Lanka. In December 2009, he was sentenced to three years' imprisonment, but this sentence was also later overturned on appeal. In February 2010, he was again arrested and charged with sedition after writing articles critical of the government's handling of the civil war in northern Sri Lanka; he was released on bail two months later.
