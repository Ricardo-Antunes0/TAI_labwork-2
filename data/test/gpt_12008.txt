"Crazy Girl" is a song recorded by American singer Demi Lovato for her third studio album, "Don't Forget" (2012). It was written by Lovato, Savan Kotecha, and Max Martin, with Martin also serving as the album's producer. The song was released on September 25, 2012, by Hollywood Records as the album's lead single. "Crazy Girl" is a mid-tempo ballad about a love that is out of control. Lovato described the song as being about a "crazy girl" who is "always in a hurry", and who "doesn't take life seriously".  The song received generally positive reviews from music critics, who praised its production and Lovato's vocals. It became her fourth number-one single on the US "Billboard" Hot 100 chart, and also topped the charts in Australia, Canada, Ireland, New Zealand, and the United Kingdom. The accompanying music video was directed by Anthony Mandler and filmed in Los Angeles in August 2012. Lovato performed the song at the 2012 MTV Video Music Awards and at the 2013 iHeartRadio Music Festival.
