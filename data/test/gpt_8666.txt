Joseph William Knowland (July 26, 1930 – May 12, 1999) was an American politician who served as a United States Senator from California from 1967 to 1981.  Knowland was born in Los Angeles, California, the son of Hazel (née Griffiths) and Joseph William Knowland, Sr., a salesman. He attended Carroll School in Pasadena and then served in the United States Marine Corps during the Korean War. After his discharge, he attended the University of California, Berkeley, where he received his B.A. in 1953 and his J.D. in 1955. He practiced law in Pasadena and became active in Republican Party politics.  In 1962, he was elected to the California State Assembly, where he served two terms. In 1964, he was elected to the United States Senate, defeating incumbent Richard M. Nixon by a wide margin. He served as chairman of the Senate Intelligence Committee during the 1970s, and was a key figure in the Senate's investigation of Watergate in 1973. He was also one of only four senators to vote against the Gulf of Tonkin Resolution that authorized military action in Vietnam.  In 1980, Knowland lost his reelection bid to Edward M. Kennedy by a wide margin. After leaving office, he practiced law again and served on the board of directors of BankAmerica Corporation. He died of cancer at age 73 in Los Angeles.
