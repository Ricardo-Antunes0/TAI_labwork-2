Kent Ninomiya is the first male Asian American to be nominated for an Emmy Award, and the first to win one. He has also been nominated for a Golden Globe and a Screen Actors Guild Award.  Kent Ninomiya was born in Seattle, Washington, on November 7, 1961. He is the son of Japanese immigrants. Ninomiya began his acting career in regional theater before making his television debut in the series China Beach in 1987. In 1992, he starred in the film The Last Days of Disco. That same year, he was cast as Dr. Daniel Jackson on the NBC science fiction series Stargate SG-1. Ninomiya remained with the show for nine seasons, from 1994 to 2003. During that time he also appeared in several feature films, including The Sum of All Fears (2002), The Kingdom (2009), and Race to Witch Mountain (2009).  In 2004, Ninomiya starred in the ABC drama Lost as Ken Leung. He left the show after its fourth season to pursue other opportunities. In 2006, he guest-starred on Law & Order: Special Victims Unit and appeared in the films Mission: Impossible III and Chicken Little. In 2007, he joined the cast of Heroes as Masato Kato. That same year he also starred in an episode of 24 as Yuji Okumura. In 2011, he reprised his role as Dr. Jackson on Stargate Atlantis for three seasons.
