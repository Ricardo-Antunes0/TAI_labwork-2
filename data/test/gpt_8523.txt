Castle of the Pomeranian Dukes - a castle in Szczecinek, Poland.  Szczecinek Castle is a castle in Szczecinek, Poland. It was built by the Pomeranian Dukes in the early 16th century as a residence for their court and later used as a military base. The castle was damaged during the Polish–Swedish War (1626–29) and the Swedish invasion of Poland (1656). It was subsequently rebuilt by the Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth. The castle was seized by the Russian Empire in 1772 during the Great Northern War, and remained in Russian hands until 1918. It was subsequently used as a military hospital and barracks until 1978.
