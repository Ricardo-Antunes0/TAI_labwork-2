T. G. Venkatesh (born 16 May 1950) is an Indian economist, author and public policy analyst. He is currently Professor of Economics at the University of California, Berkeley, and a senior fellow at the Hoover Institution.  Venkatesh was born in 1950 in Tiruchirappalli, Tamil Nadu, India. He received his B.A. from Madras University in 1971 and his M.A. and Ph.D. from the University of Chicago in 1974 and 1977 respectively.  Venkatesh has held academic positions at Yale University (1977-1979), the University of California, Berkeley (1979-1984), and the National Bureau of Economic Research (1984-1994). He was a visiting professor at the London School of Economics in 1994-1995 and a research associate at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey, in 2007-2008.  Venkatesh's research focuses on economic development, public policy analysis, labor economics, international economics, and economic history. He is the author or editor of three books: The Making of Economic Development (1981), Illicit Money (1986), and India's Dangerous Demography (2005). Venkatesh has also published more than 150 articles in scholarly journals and edited volumes.  Venkatesh served as a member of the President's Council of Economic Advisers under President Clinton from 1994 to 1997. In 2006 he was elected fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.
