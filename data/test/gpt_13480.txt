Herbert L. Peterson (May 16, 1876 - December 21, 1965) was an American economist, best known for his work on Keynesian economics and for his contributions to economic theory.  Peterson was born in 1876 in West Amesbury, Massachusetts, and received his B.A. from Amherst College in 1898 and his M.A. from Yale University in 1901. He taught at Wesleyan University from 1902 to 1904, and then at the University of Chicago from 1904 to 1941. He served as president of the American Economic Association in 1941 and 1942. He died in 1965.
