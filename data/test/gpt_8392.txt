Helena Legido-Quigley (born 1977) is a Spanish-born American artist who lives and works in New York City. She is known for her paintings, drawings, and sculptures that explore themes of gender and sexuality.  Legido-Quigley was born in 1977 in Madrid, Spain. She received her BFA from the University of California, Davis in 2000. From 2001-2002, she studied at the Royal College of Art in London. Legido-Quigley has exhibited her work in solo and group exhibitions throughout the United States, Europe, and Asia. Her work has been featured in publications such as "The New York Times", "The Huffington Post", "The Guardian", and "The Independent".  Legido-Quigley's paintings, drawings, and sculptures explore themes of gender and sexuality. She has said that she is inspired by the dichotomy between the physical and emotional aspects of human existence.
