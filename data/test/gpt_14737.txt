Radio Boys was the title of three British teenage pop groups active from the early 1960s to the early 1970s.  The first Radio Boys group was formed in 1963 in Liverpool by Vince Clarke (of The Beatles) and Pete Best (of The Beatles). They were soon followed by a second Liverpool group, also featuring Clarke and Best, in 1964. The groups disbanded in the early 1970s.  The third Radio Boys group was formed in London in 1971 by John Lennon, Paul McCartney, and George Harrison. The group was initially known simply as "The Beatles", but soon changed their name to "The Beatles' Radio Stars". The group disbanded in 1973.
