Kazimierz Serocki (3 March 1922 – 9 September 2002) was a Polish writer, translator and editor, who worked in the field of literary criticism. He is best known for his critical study of the works of Witold Gombrowicz.  Kazimierz Serocki was born in 1922 in Tarnów, Galicia, then part of the Austro-Hungarian Empire. He studied at the University of Krakow and the University of Warsaw, where he received his doctorate in 1954. From 1954 to 1966 he was professor of Polish literature at the University of Warsaw. From 1966 to 1988 he was professor at Jagiellonian University in Krakow. He died in 2002 in Krakow.  Serocki's major works include: "Witold Gombrowicz" (1959), "The Poetics of Witold Gombrowicz" (1963), "The Novels of Władysław Reymont" (1965), "The Drama of Władysław Reymont" (1968), "The Short Stories of Witold Gombrowicz" (1971), and "The Letters of Witold Gombrowicz" (1988). He also edited several anthologies and wrote articles on various authors and movements in Polish literature.
