Qashani or Kashani is a Persian decorative art form that evolved in the late 17th and 18th centuries. It is a type of calligraphy that uses a brush to write ornate patterns and designs on paper.  The word qashani comes from the Persian word qasaneh, meaning "to decorate". The art form of qashani developed in the late 17th and 18th centuries, in part due to the influx of new techniques from Europe. Qashani is a type of calligraphy that uses a brush to write ornate patterns and designs on paper. The designs can be simple or complex, and can be used for decorative purposes or to create a more formal document.
