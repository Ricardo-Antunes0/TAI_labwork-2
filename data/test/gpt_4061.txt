Jackson (March 12, 1883, Montgomery, Alabama â€“ November 25, 1959, Mobile, Alabama) was an American jurist who served as a United States District Judge of the United States District Court for the Southern District of Alabama.  Born in Montgomery, Alabama, Jackson received an Artium Baccalaureus degree from the University of Alabama in 1906 and a Bachelor of Laws from the University of Alabama Law School in 1909. He was in private practice in Montgomery from 1909 to 1916. He was a delegate to the 1916 Democratic National Convention. He was an assistant attorney general of Alabama from 1916 to 1918. He was judge advocate general of the United States Army in 1918 and 1919. He was a judge on the Montgomery Circuit Court from 1919 to 1933, serving as chief judge from 1929 to 1933.  Jackson was nominated by President Franklin D. Roosevelt on March 12, 1933, to a seat on the United States District Court for the Southern District of Alabama vacated by Judge John H. Bankhead. Jackson was confirmed by the United States Senate on March 16, 1933, and received his commission on March 17, 1933. He served as Chief Judge from 1949 to 1959. He retired on November 25, 1959. He died on November 26, 1959, in Mobile, Alabama.
