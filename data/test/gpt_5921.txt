Konrad Rupf (23 May 1929 – 29 December 2017) was a German artist and sculptor.  Rupf was born in Würzburg, Bavaria, the son of the painter Fritz Rupf. After attending the School of Applied Arts in Munich, he studied sculpture at the Kunstakademie Düsseldorf from 1949 to 1953. He then moved to Paris, where he worked with Jean Dubuffet and Charles Dupee. He returned to Germany in 1957 and began teaching at the Hochschule für Bildende Künste (now the Academy of Fine Arts Mainz). In 1970 he co-founded the ZKM Center for Art and Media in Karlsruhe. From 1985 to 1994 he was president of the Academy of Fine Arts Vienna.  Rupf's sculptures are characterized by their use of bronze, stone and wood. His work has been exhibited in museums around the world, including the Museum of Modern Art in New York City, the Centre Pompidou in Paris, and the Tate Modern in London. He has also been awarded several honorary doctorates, including those from the universities of Freiburg, Münster and Vienna.  Rupf died on 29 December 2017 at his home in Mainz, aged 91.
