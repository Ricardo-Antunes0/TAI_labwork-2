He played with several bands in the San Fernando Valley before moving to Los Angeles in the early 1970s.  Robb played with many influential bands during the punk and new wave era, including the Sid Vicious-fronted Sex Pistols, the Television Personalities, The New York Dolls, and The Dead Kennedys. He was also a member of Elvis Costello's backing band for most of his early career.  In the 1980s and 1990s, Robb played with artists including J.D. Souther, Michael Bolton, Dionne Warwick, Rick Nielsen, and Sammy Hagar. He also played on albums by John Lee Hooker and Joe Bonamassa.  Robb died of a heart attack on March 2, 2018 in Malibu, California at the age of 70.
