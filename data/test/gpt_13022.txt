Major-General Sir Frederic John Goldsmid  KCSI, GCMG, GCVO, DL (1850-1917) was a British soldier, civil servant and philanthropist.  Born in 1850 in India, Goldsmid was educated at Harrow and Trinity College, Cambridge. He served in the Indian Army and then in the Egyptian Army before joining the British civil service in 1875. He served as secretary to the viceroy of India and then as inspector of schools in Ireland. In 1904 he was appointed Governor of the Bank of England, a position he held until his retirement in 1911.  Goldsmid was a major benefactor of numerous charities, including The Royal London Hospital for Sick Children, which he founded in 1904 and which is now known as The Royal London Hospital for Children NHS Foundation Trust. He also donated money to hospitals in London and Birmingham, to the University of Cambridge, to Trinity College, Dublin and to Jewish charities throughout Europe.  Goldsmid died in 1917. He was knighted in 1902 and appointed a Companion of the Order of St Michael and St George (CMG) in 1907.
