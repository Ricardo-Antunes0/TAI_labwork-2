Simon Feil is an American actor. He is known for his roles as Tony in the Broadway musical Jersey Boys, as Hank in the Broadway revival of Grease, and as Nathan Detroit in the Broadway revival of The Phantom of the Opera.  Simon Feil was born on December 10, 1971 in New York City. He is the son of actress Gail Feil and actor, director and producer Simon Feil. His father is Jewish, and his mother is from a Catholic background. Feil has two sisters and one brother. He attended the Fiorello H. LaGuardia High School of Music & Art and Performing Arts, where he was a theater major.  Feil's professional acting career began in 1995 when he appeared in three episodes of the NBC drama series Law & Order: Special Victims Unit. In 1996, he made his Broadway debut in the role of Tony in the musical Jersey Boys. The following year, he appeared in the Off-Broadway production of Grease at the New World Stages. In 1998, he appeared in a minor role as Richie Valens in Quentin Tarantino's film Kill Bill: Volume 2. In 2004, Feil made his big screen debut as Hank in the Broadway revival of Grease. That same year, he reprised his role as Tony in the national tour of Jersey Boys. In 2006, Feil starred as Nathan Detroit in the Broadway revival of The Phantom of the Opera, which won him a Tony Award for Best Performance by a Leading Actor in a Play
