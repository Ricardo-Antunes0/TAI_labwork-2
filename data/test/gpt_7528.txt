Jimmie's Chicken Shack is an American alternative rock band from Gainesville, Florida. The band was formed in 1995 by singer-guitarist Jimmie Fadden and drummer Davey Latterman.  The band's first release, the self-titled EP, was released in 1996 on Fadden's own label, Effigy Records. The EP caught the attention of major record labels and the band signed to Atlantic Records in 1997. The band's second album, "Two Weeks", was released in 1999 and reached number 4 on the Billboard 200 chart. The album produced two singles: "Where the Streets Have No Name" and "I Will Follow You Into the Dark".  The band's third album, "Chamber Music", was released in 2002 and reached number 5 on the Billboard 200 chart. The album produced one single: "Home".
