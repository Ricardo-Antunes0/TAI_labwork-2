The Treasons Act 1571 (13 Eliz.1 c.1) was an Act of the Parliament of England. It made it treason to commit high treason, and provided for the punishment of those convicted.  The Treason Act 1571 was a landmark piece of legislation in English history. It made it treason to commit high treason, and provided for the punishment of those convicted. High treason consisted of levying war against the Crown, or aiding and abetting foreign enemies of the state. Conviction carried a penalty of death.
