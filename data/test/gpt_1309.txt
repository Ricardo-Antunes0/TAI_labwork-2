Lott is a city in Falls County, Texas, United States. The population was 2,839 at the 2010 census. It is the county seat of Falls County.  Lott was founded in 1859 by Jasper Lott and his brother-in-law, Dr. John W. Moore. The city was named for Colonel Thomas Lott, a Confederate soldier and local landowner. The first post office opened in 1860.  In 1875, the Texas and Pacific Railway reached Lott, and the town began to grow rapidly. In 1885, a fire destroyed much of the town, but it was rebuilt with new brick buildings. In 1886, Lott became the county seat of Falls County.
