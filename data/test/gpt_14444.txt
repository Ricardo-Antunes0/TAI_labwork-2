Géza Zichy (23 July 184914 January 1924) was a Hungarian painter, art critic and teacher.  Géza Zichy was born in Pest, Hungary on 23 July 1849. He was the son of János Zichy and Katalin Weisz. He showed an early interest in art, studying at the Academy of Fine Arts in Budapest under Károly Kisfaludi and János Hildebrandt.  Zichy taught at the Academy of Fine Arts from 1879 to 1907, serving as its director from 1892 to 1907. He was a member of the Hungarian Academy of Sciences from 1897 to 1904 and a member of the Royal Academy of Painting, Sculpture and Architecture in Budapest from 1892.  Zichy is best known for his portraits, which display a realistic, almost photographic quality. His subjects include members of the Hungarian aristocracy, politicians and writers. His most famous portrait is that of György Konrád, which was exhibited at the Paris World's Fair in 1900.  Zichy died in Budapest on 14 January 1924.
