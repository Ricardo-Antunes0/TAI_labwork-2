Johnny Weissmuller (born Johann Peter Weißmüller; June 7, 1879 – November 6, 1966) was an Austrian-born American actor, swimmer, and seal hunter who became world-famous as Tarzan.  Weissmuller was born in Mönchengladbach, Germany, to Jewish parents, Rosa (née Pincus; 1848–1933) and Jacob Weissmüller (1841–1922). He emigrated with his family to the United States in 1892 and settled in New York City. Weissmuller began his theatrical career in vaudeville, making his Broadway debut in 1902. In 1911 he began appearing in films and became a leading man. He appeared in more than eighty movies over the course of his career, often playing the role of a hero or leading man. He was also a successful swimmer and athlete, winning a silver medal in the 1904 Summer Olympics in St. Louis and a gold medal in the 1932 Summer Olympics in Los Angeles. Weissmuller retired from acting in 1957 and later became an animal rights advocate. He died at the age of eighty-six from heart failure at his home in Los Angeles.
