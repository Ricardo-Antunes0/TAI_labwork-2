David William Ricketts (July 12, 1935 â€“ May 8, 2012) was an American businessman and investor. He was the founder and former CEO of the Chicago Cubs baseball team.  Ricketts was born in Chicago, Illinois, to William Ricketts and the former Helen Wallace. His father was the founder of the Wrigley Company, one of the world's largest chewing gum companies. Ricketts attended Phillips Exeter Academy and then received a bachelor's degree from Yale University in 1957.  Ricketts began his career as a sales representative for Wrigley in 1956. He became president of Wrigley in 1974 and CEO in 1984. During his tenure, the company grew from $200 million to $2 billion in sales.  Ricketts purchased the Chicago Cubs baseball team in 1989 for $8 million. He led the team to its first World Series appearance in 108 years in 2003, and repeated as champions in 2005. The Cubs were unable to retain their championship title in 2007, but won again in 2016 under manager Joe Maddon. Ricketts retired as chairman of the Cubs after the 2016 season.   Ricketts also served on the boards of directors for Walmart and J.C. Penney, and was a director of Motorola and United Airlines. He was also a member of the Council on Foreign Relations, and served on the board of trustees for Northwestern University.
