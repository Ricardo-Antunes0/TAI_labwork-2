Stiliyan Kovachev  (born on 26 February 1975) is a Bulgarian former professional footballer who played as a goalkeeper.  Kovachev started his career with local side Chernomorets Burgas, before moving to Litex Lovech in 1995. He spent four seasons with Litex, before moving to Russian side FC Terek Grozny in 1999. Kovachev spent three seasons with Terek, before joining Lokomotiv Plovdiv in 2002. He stayed with Lokomotiv for six seasons, helping the club to win the Bulgarian Supercup and Bulgarian Cup in 2005 and 2006. Kovachev then moved to Scottish Premiership side Rangers in 2007, but failed to make an impact and was released at the end of the 2007–08 season. He then returned to Bulgaria and played for Botev Plovdiv until his retirement at the end of the 2009–10 season.  Kovachev made his international debut for Bulgaria in a 1998 FIFA World Cup qualification match against Romania, but did not feature again until 2006. He played 56 matches for Bulgaria, keeping 38 clean sheets.
