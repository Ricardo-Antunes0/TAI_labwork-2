Takvam Chapel  is a parish church located in the village of Takvam, about 2 km east of the town of Ystradgynlais in the county borough of Swansea, south Wales. The church is a Grade I listed building.  The first mention of a chapel in Takvam is from 1294, when it is recorded as belonging to the Premonstratensian order. It was rebuilt in the early 16th century, and further alterations were made in the late 17th century. The present building was consecrated in 1875.  The church has a cruciform plan with a west tower. The roof is tiled, and the walls are rendered. The windows have pointed arches and are divided by mullions and transoms. The west door is flanked by two fluted Ionic columns surmounted by pediments with urns on either side. The pulpit is Screened in Arts and Crafts style with carved work including a relief of St Michael slaying the dragon.
