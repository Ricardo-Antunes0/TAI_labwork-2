Mount Elkins, also known as Jökelen (which means "The Jökel"), is a peak in the Rocky Mountains of North America, located in the Elk Mountains of central Colorado. The peak is part of the Continental Divide and has an elevation of 14,259 feet (4,345 m).  Mount Elkins is one of the fourteeners, peaks over 14,000 feet in height. It was named for General William Tecumseh Sherman's Chief of Staff, Major General John Pope. Mount Elkins was first climbed on July 2, 1876 by J.B. Long and H.M. Bowers, members of the Hayden Geological Survey.
