Lewis Milton Goldwasser (1885 Łódź, Congress Poland – 1950 Hollywood, California), commonly known as Lou Gold, was an American film producer and director. He was best known for producing films such as Casablanca and For Whom the Bell Tolls.  Goldwasser was born in 1885 in Łódź, Congress Poland, then part of the Russian Empire. He emigrated to the United States in 1912, and started working as a film producer in Hollywood in the 1920s. He is most famous for his production of Casablanca (1942), which won the Academy Award for Best Picture. Goldwasser also directed For Whom the Bell Tolls (1943), which was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Director. He died in 1950 in Hollywood.
