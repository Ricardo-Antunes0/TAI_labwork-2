Daniel Coulter Reynolds (born July 14, 1987) is an American singer, songwriter and record producer. He is the lead singer and main songwriter for the rock band Linkin Park.  Reynolds was born in Santa Monica, California. He has two younger sisters, Alyssa and Emily. His father, Bill Reynolds, is a music journalist and his mother, Lynne, is an art teacher. Reynolds attended Santa Monica High School, where he met future Linkin Park bandmate Mike Shinoda.  After high school, Reynolds studied art at Full Sail University in Winter Park, Florida, but dropped out after two semesters to focus on Linkin Park. He moved to Los Angeles to pursue a career in music and started working with producer Rob Bourdon. The two collaborated on the tracks "Numb" and "In My Place" for Linkin Park's debut album " Hybrid Theory".  Linkin Park's second album "Meteora" sold over 15 million copies worldwide and produced five successful singles: "Crawling", "In the End", "Breaking the Habit", "Hybrid Theory" and "Numb". The album won a Grammy Award for Best Rock Album in 2006.  Linkin Park's third album "Minute of Silence" was released in 2007 and featured the international hit singles "Shadow of the Day", "Leave Out All The Rest" and "Papercut". The album achieved platinum status in several countries and won a Grammy Award for Best Hard Rock Performance in 2008.
