Olumide  Edwards Adegbulu (born September 1976) is a Nigerian author and journalist.  Maintain is a Yoruba word meaning "to keep or preserve". It is the title of Edwards' first novel, published in 2007. It tells the story of a family struggling to keep their farm and their traditions alive during Nigeria's military dictatorship of General Sani Abacha.  Edwards is also the founder and editor-in-chief of the literary magazine, "Majeed".  Born in Ibadan, Nigeria, in 1976, Olumide Edwards grew up in a family of farmers. He attended Methodist Boys' High School before studying English Literature at the University of Ibadan. Edwards began his career as a journalist, working for several publications before founding the literary magazine "Majeed" in 2001. His first novel, "Maintain", was published in 2007.
