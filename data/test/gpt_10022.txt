Gu Ruzhang or Ku Yu-cheung (; 1894–1952) was a Chinese painter and sculptor.  Born in Wuchang, Hubei Province, Gu Ruzhang studied at the Beijing Normal University before moving to France in 1922. He studied at the Academie Julian with Henri Matisse and André Derain, and then at the École des Beaux-Arts in Paris. He returned to China in 1931 and became a professor at the Beijing Arts Institute in 1935. He was one of the founders of the Chinese National School of Painting, and served as its director from 1945 until his death. He is best known for his abstract paintings and sculptures, which are characterised by their use of geometrical shapes and bold colours.
