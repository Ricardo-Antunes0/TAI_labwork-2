John Robert Isner (born April 26, 1985) is an American professional tennis player. He reached a career-high singles ranking of world No. 4 in May 2013, and is the first American man to reach a career-high ranking in the top 10 since Jimmy Connors reached No. 3 in 1985.  Isner has won eighteen ATP singles titles, including three ATP World Tour Masters 1000 events: the Monte Carlo Masters in 2013, the Rome Masters in 2014, and the Barcelona Masters in 2016. He also reached the semifinals of the 2016 Australian Open and the final of the 2017 French Open. Isner has also been runner-up at the US Open three times (in 2011, 2013, and 2017), and has won two doubles titles at the French Open (with Ryan Harrison).  Isner was born in Augusta, Georgia. He attended Clemson University, where he played for the Clemson Tigers men's tennis team. He was a member of Clemson's 2004 NCAA Division I men's tennis championship team.  Isner made his ATP main draw debut at the 2005 Los Angeles Indoor Championships, where he lost in the first round to Hungarian player Zsolt Erdei. He made his Grand Slam debut at Wimbledon that year, where he lost in the first round to sixth seed Marcos Baghdatis.
