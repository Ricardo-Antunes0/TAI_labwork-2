Chow Yun-fat  (born 18 May 1955), also known as Uncle Yeung, is a Hong Kong actor, film producer and director. He has appeared in over 120 films, mainly in the Chinese-language film industry and is considered a "legendary actor". Chow has also directed and produced several acclaimed films, including The Eunuch and Election.  Chow was born in Kowloon, but his family moved to mainland China when he was two years old. After graduating from the Beijing Film Academy, he made his debut in the 1983 film A Touch of Sin. He became well known for his work in the Hong Kong action cinema of the 1990s and 2000s, appearing in such hits as City of Violence (1991), Once Upon a Time in China (1995), Shaolin Soccer (2001), and The Heroic Legend of Arslan (2006). In recent years, he has branched out into television drama and comedy.
