The High Plains Reader is an alternative newspaper that circulates in the High Plains region of the United States. It was founded in 1989 and is currently published every other week.   The High Plains Reader is a nonprofit organization that is staffed entirely by volunteers. The paper is distributed free of charge to residents in nine counties in western Kansas and eastern Nebraska. It publishes a mix of news, opinion, arts and culture, and advertising.  The High Plains Reader has been described as "an eclectic read," "a unique voice," and "a window into the lives of its readers."
