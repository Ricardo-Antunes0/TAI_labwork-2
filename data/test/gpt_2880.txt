Mariàngela Vilallonga Vives (Girona, 3 April 1952) is a Catalan artist who lives and works in Barcelona. Vilallonga’s body of work features a wide range of media, including painting, sculpture, installation, and video. Her work has been exhibited in museums and galleries throughout the world, including the Museum of Modern Art in New York City and the Centre Pompidou in Paris.  Mariàngela Vilallonga was born on 3 April 1952 in Girona, Catalonia. She studied at the School of Fine Arts of the University of Barcelona (1972-1976), where she met Pau Casals and Joan Miró. In 1978 she participated in the Ibero-American Biennial in Mexico City and then moved to New York City, where she continued her studies at the School of Visual Arts (1978-1979).  Vilallonga’s first solo exhibition was held at the Centre d'Art Contemporani de Girona in 1981. Since then, she has had numerous solo exhibitions both in Spain and abroad, including at the Museum of Modern Art (MOMA) in New York City (1998), the Centre Pompidou in Paris (2002), and the Palau de la Música Catalana in Barcelona (2007).
