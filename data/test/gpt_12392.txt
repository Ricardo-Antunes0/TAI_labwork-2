Dún Laoghaire–Rathdown  is a county in Ireland. It is part of the Dublin Region and is located on the eastern coast of the island of Ireland. The population of the county was 201,771 according to the 2016 census.  The county is named after two Irish towns in County Wicklow, Dún Laoghaire (the largest town) and Rathdown (the smallest town). The county has an area of 1,212.68 km² and is divided into six baronies: Ardee, Baltinglass, Castlebar, Dun Laoghaire–Rathdown, Glanmire and Killinarden. The county council is based in Rathdown.  Dún Laoghaire–Rathdown is home to a number of important tourist destinations, including Cliffs of Moher, Dalkey Island and Salthill. The largest employer in the county is the Department of Social Protection. The main agricultural products are beef, dairy, pork and cereal. There are also a number of small businesses and manufacturing plants in the county.      Dún Laoghaire-Rathdown occupies a central place in Irish history. It was here that James II attempted to flee from England after the Battle of the Boyne in 1690, and it was also here that Daniel O'Connell led the campaign for Catholic emancipation in the mid-19th century. Since then, Dún Laoghaire-Rathdown has continued to play an important role in Irish politics.
