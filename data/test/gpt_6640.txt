Mir Jafar Abbas oghlu Baghirov ( (Russian: Мир Яфарь Абсас Багиров) (October 11, 1914 – November 25, 1978) was a Soviet Azerbaijani politician who served as the Prime Minister of the Azerbaijan SSR from 1956 to 1967.  Baghirov was born in the village of Bash Qala, near Ganja, in 1914. He graduated from the Baku Technical School in 1936 and worked as a metalworker and an engineer. In 1937 he joined the Communist Party of the Soviet Union. He served in the Soviet military from 1941 to 1945 and rose to the rank of lieutenant colonel. He returned to civilian life and became a deputy chairman of the Ganja Regional Committee of the Communist Party. Baghirov was appointed head of the Ganja regional party organization in 1949 and became a member of the Central Committee of the Communist Party in 1951.  In 1954 Baghirov was appointed Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Heavy Industry in the government of Geidar Alizade. He served as Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Transport in the government of Hasanmammad Kerimov from 1957 to 1961. Baghirov was appointed Prime Minister of Azerbaijan on August 12, 1956, succeeding Yusif Aybarov. He served as Prime Minister until his death on November 25, 1978.
