Wardell Joseph Quezergue ( ; March 12, 1916 – March 2, 1991) was an American singer and songwriter. He is best known for his 1957 recording of "Wonderful World" which reached number one on the Billboard Hot 100 chart. Quezergue also wrote the song's lyrics.  Wardell Joseph Quezergue was born in 1916 in the city of Newark, New Jersey, to Marie (née Pascarella) and Wardell Quezergue. His father was of French descent and his mother was of Italian descent. He had three elder sisters and one younger brother, Raymond. Wardell Quezergue grew up in Newark, where he attended St. Benedict's Preparatory School and Newark College.  Wardell Quezergue began his music career as a singer with the band the Ravens in 1935. In 1942, he formed his own band, the Wardells, which became one of the most popular jazz groups in the United States. In 1957, he recorded "Wonderful World" with producer Phil Spector. The song reached number one on the Billboard Hot 100 chart and sold more than two million copies. Quezergue also wrote the song's lyrics. He retired from music in 1975 after a series of illnesses.  Wardell Quezergue died on March 2, 1991 at age 76 after a long illness.
