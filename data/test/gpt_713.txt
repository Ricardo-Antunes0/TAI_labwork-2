Viktor Aleksandrovich Zinger (, 29 October 1941 â€“ 25 January 2017) was a Russian and Soviet ballet dancer, choreographer, and educator.  Zinger was born in Leningrad, Soviet Union, to a Jewish family. He began his ballet training at the age of six with Tamara Karsavina. After five years of study with Konstantin Sergeyevich Liberman at the Leningrad Ballet School, he joined the Kirov Ballet in 1962. In 1968, he defected to the West and joined the Royal Ballet, where he became a principal dancer. He retired from the Royal Ballet in 1987.  Zinger created over thirty ballets, including "The Pharaoh's Daughter" (1970), "Journey to the West" (1975), "The Dream of Gerontius" (1978), and "The Nutcracker" (1984). He also choreographed numerous ballets for the Royal Ballet, including "Swan Lake" (1976), "The Sleeping Beauty" (1979), "Coppelia" (1986), and "Giselle" (1993). He was made an Officer of the Order of British Empire in 1987.  Zinger died on 25 January 2017, aged 80.
