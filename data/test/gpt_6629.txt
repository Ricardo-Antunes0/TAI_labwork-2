A passenger train is a train used for carrying passengers, as opposed to a freight train. Passenger trains operate on fixed routes and may be operated by a public railway, railway operator, or private railway. The most common type of passenger train is the train passenger, which carries passengers in coaches or compartments.  Passenger trains began operation in the early 19th century. The first passenger trains ran between Liverpool and Manchester in 1830. Over time, passenger trains have increased in capacity, speed, and popularity. Today, passenger trains are commonly used to transport people between major cities throughout the world.
