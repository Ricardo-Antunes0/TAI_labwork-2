Södertörn University (, abbreviated as SH) is a university in Sweden, located in Gothenburg. SH was founded in 1827 as the Royal Academy of Technology, and later renamed the Royal School of Technology in 1886. It became an university in 1990. SH is one of Sweden's largest universities with over 27,000 students.  SH offers undergraduate and graduate programmes in engineering, science, mathematics and business. The university has a strong focus on research with over €130 million spent on research in 2014. SH is also home to the Södertörn Research Centre, which is one of Europe's largest centres for research in renewable energy.
