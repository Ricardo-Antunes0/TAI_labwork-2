He served as the 36th Governor General of Canada, from December 2, 1992 to November 30, 1995.  Pinto was born in 1932 in Toronto, Ontario. He joined the Canadian Army as a private in 1951 and rose through the ranks to become a lieutenant general. He served as the 36th Governor General of Canada, from December 2, 1992 to November 30, 1995. Pinto also served as Vice-Chief of the Defence Staff from 1984 to 1985 and as Commander of Canadian Forces Europe from 1985 to 1988. In retirement, Pinto served as a member of the Security Council of Canada and as President of the Royal Military College of Canada. He died on March 11, 2003 in Ottawa after a long illness.
