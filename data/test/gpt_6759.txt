Hodges was born in 1912 in Oxford, England, the son of Geoffrey Hodges, a Classics scholar, and his wife, Dora. He had two brothers, Christopher and Anthony. He was educated at Eton College and then at Balliol College, Oxford, where he read classics.  Hodges made his first-class debut for Oxford University against Northamptonshire in the 1933 season. He played Minor counties cricket for Berkshire from 1933 to 1937 before joining Hampshire in 1938. He played Minor counties cricket for Hampshire until 1954, making a total of 375 appearances in first-class and Minor counties cricket. His final appearance for Hampshire came against Surrey in the 1954 County Championship.  Hodges worked as a cricket correspondent for "The Sunday Times" from 1948 to 1978. He also served on the ECB's Cricket Committee from 1959 to 1966. Hodges died 29 July 1990 at the age of 80 in Hampshire.
