Tasmin Olajuwon Mitchell (born June 25, 1986) is an American professional basketball player for the Atlanta Dream of the Women's National Basketball Association (WNBA). A center, Mitchell attended the University of Maryland, where she played for the Terrapins women's basketball team from 2004 to 2007.  Mitchell was drafted by the Dream in the second round of the 2007 WNBA draft. In her rookie season in 2007â€“08, Mitchell helped lead the Dream to their first playoffs appearance. The following season, Mitchell averaged 13.1 points, 7.5 rebounds and 1.8 blocks per game and was named to the WNBA All-Rookie First Team. In 2010, Mitchell became only the third player in WNBA history to record at least 1,000 points, 700 rebounds and 300 blocks in a career.   In 2013, Mitchell won her first WNBA Championship with the Dream. She was also a part of the United States women's national basketball team that won bronze medals at the 2012 and 2016 Olympic Games.
