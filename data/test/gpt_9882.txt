Kelly David Brownell (born October 31, 1951) is an American psychologist and the Dean of the Yale School of Medicine. Previously he was the president of the American Psychological Association from 2003 to 2009.  Brownell was born in Springfield, Massachusetts, to a father who was a retired physician and a mother who was a retired schoolteacher. He received his B.A. from Yale University in 1973 and his M.D. from Harvard Medical School in 1977. After completing residency training at Beth Israel Hospital, he worked as an assistant professor of psychiatry at the University of Michigan from 1978 to 1984. He then moved to Stanford University where he served as the Helen Wills Neuroscience Institute's first director (1984-1989).  In 1989, Brownell became the founding president of the Yale Center for Prevention Research (now known as the Brownell Center for Public Health Education). During his tenure, Brownell led numerous initiatives aimed at reducing health disparities and promoting healthy behaviors across Yale's campus and beyond. In 2003, Brownell was elected president of the American Psychological Association, serving until 2009.   During his time as president, Brownell oversaw major policy changes, including revamped Ethics Codes and revamped structures for Board Governance and Presidential Appointments. He has also been outspoken on issues related to climate change and health promotion, appearing frequently on national television and radio programs to discuss these topics.
