Portsmouth City Council is the local authority for the city of Portsmouth in Hampshire, England. It is a metropolitan borough council and is one of nine local authorities in the City of Portsmouth district. The council has 105 elected members, representing 39 wards.  The council was created on 1 April 1974 as a successor to the former Portsmouth Corporation. The first mayor of Portsmouth City Council was Sir Harry Stonehouse. The current mayor is Peter Butler, who was elected in May 2016.  Portsmouth City Council operates the main civic buildings in the city, including the town hall, library and art gallery. It also owns and manages a number of public parks and green spaces, including Gunwharf Quays park, Southsea Common and Fort Nelson Park. In addition, the council is responsible for providing social care services for the elderly and vulnerable, street cleaning and waste disposal services.
