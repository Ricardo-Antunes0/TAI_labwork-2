Grand Sentinel is an 8,518-foot-elevation (2,596Â meter) pillar in the Sentinel Range of the Rocky Mountains, in Arapaho National Forest, Colorado. The pillar is the highest point in Arapaho National Forest and is one of the most popular hiking destinations in Colorado. The trail to the summit is rated as moderate to difficult and takes about 3 hours to complete.  The Grand Sentinel was first climbed on July 15, 1898 by a group of 11 men led by Dr. C.D. Broadhead. The first woman to summit the Grand Sentinel was Nellie McClung on July 5, 1934.
