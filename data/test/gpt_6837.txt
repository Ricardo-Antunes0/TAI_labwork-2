Bruce Obomeyoma Onobrakpeya (born 30 August 1932) is a Nigerian musician and composer who has been a leading figure in the development of African popular music.  Onobrakpeya was born in 1932 in the town of Oguta in Lagos State. He began his musical training at the age of six, learning to play the violin and later the pianoforte. At the age of sixteen he joined a traditional band, the Egbejim Band, and began to perform on radio broadcasts. In 1956 he formed his own band, the Bruce Onobrakpeya Orchestra, which became one of Nigeria's most popular ensembles.  Onobrakpeya has composed music for stage and screen, including scores for films such as The Lion King, Shaft and Ghost Dog: The Way of the Samurai. He has also recorded more than thirty albums, many of which have been acclaimed for their innovative melodies and rhythms. In 2006 he was awarded an honorary doctorate from the University of Lagos.
