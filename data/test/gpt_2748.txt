Western American Art includes artistic work which has been produced in the United States, Canada, and Mexico since the late 18th century. The term can also be used to describe contemporary art from these regions.  The history of Western American Art is closely intertwined with the history of the United States, Canada, and Mexico. The first settlers in these regions brought with them their culture and art, and over time these influences have been mixed together to create a unique regional art style. Western American Art is often characterized by its bright colors and expressive brushstrokes, and it is often used to express the joys and passions of its creators.  Today, Western American Art is still being produced by a variety of talented artists. It is often featured in national exhibitions, and it has been used to create works of art for public display. There is a great deal of diversity within the genre, and it is constantly evolving to reflect the changing trends in American culture.
