Elizaveta Sergeyevna Tuktamysheva ( ; ; born 25 January 1966) is a Russian actress and model.  Born in Moscow, Tuktamysheva made her stage debut in 1978 at the age of six. She began her modeling career in the early 1980s and appeared in numerous advertisements. Her breakthrough role came in 1992 with her role as Lyudmila in the film adaptation of Leo Tolstoy's War and Peace. She has since appeared in roles in a number of acclaimed films, including The Master (2002), Anna Karenina (2009), and Leviathan (2014). She has also starred in several successful television series, including The Return (2013–14) and War and Peace (2017–18).  Tuktamysheva was one of the recipients of the prestigious St. Petersburg Film Award for Best Actress for her performance as Katya in the film adaptation of Leo Tolstoy's War and Peace (2002). In 2018, she was awarded an honorary doctorate from Saint Petersburg State University for her work in film.
