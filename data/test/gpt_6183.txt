Khan Sahib, Qazi Zafar Hussain came from a very illustrious family of lawyers and judges. He was born on 10th of January, 1883 in the city of Lahore, Punjab. After completing his early education from local schools, he moved to England to study law at the University of Cambridge. He started his legal career with a small practice in Lahore.   He rose through the ranks of the legal profession, becoming a judge in 1936. In 1941, he was appointed Chief Justice of the Punjab and Rajasthan High Courts. In 1946, he was appointed Judge of the Supreme Court of India, a post he held until his retirement in 1959. During his time as a judge, Qazi Zafar Hussain played an important role in reforming the Indian judiciary. He is also credited with introducing modern methods of case management into the Indian judiciary.   Qazi Zafar Hussain died on 20th of January, 1971. He is considered one of the most influential judges in Indian history.
