Philip Hawford alias Ballard (died 11 August 1643) was an English judge.  Hawford was born in 1592, the son of Sir John Hawford, of Cheshire, and his wife, Margaret, daughter of Sir John Gresham of Norfolk. He was educated at the University of Cambridge and the Middle Temple. He was called to the bar in 1616. He became a bencher of Middle Temple in 1638 and a serjeant-at-law in 1640.  On 25 January 1621 he was appointed recorder of Bristol, a post which he held until his death. In March 1629 he was made commissioner for assessment for Warwickshire. He became a justice of the peace for Warwickshire and Worcestershire in 1634. He was knighted on 26 January 1639.  Hawford died in London on 11 August 1643 and was buried in Westminster Abbey.
