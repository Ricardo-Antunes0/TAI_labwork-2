Melissa Susanne Henderson (born August 23, 1989) is an American singer, songwriter and actress. Henderson was born in Orange County, California, to father John Henderson and mother Susanne (n√©e Foster). She has two sisters, Emily and Madeline. Henderson's family moved to Nashville, Tennessee when she was six years old. She attended Franklin Road Elementary School in Franklin, TN and then moved to Brentwood Academy in Brentwood, TN before attending The Collegiate School in Nashville. Henderson later relocated to Los Angeles, California.  Henderson began her music career in 2007 when she auditioned for the seventh season of American Idol as a solo artist and made it to the top 12. She finished in sixth place on the show. In 2008, she released her first album, "Melissa", which peaked at number 25 on the "Billboard" 200. The album spawned three singles: "How Far I'll Go", "I Believe in a Thing Called Love" and "The Way You Loved Me". In 2009, she released her second album, "Here Comes the Sun". The album reached number 5 on the "Billboard" 200 and spawned the singles "Here Comes the Sun" and "Don't Tell Me What to Do". Henderson starred as Erica Kane on the Fox sitcom New Girl from 2013 to 2015. From 2016 to 2019 she starred as Carrie Mathison on the Showtime series Homeland.
