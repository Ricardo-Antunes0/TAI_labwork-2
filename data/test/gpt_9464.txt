Ladycross was a Catholic preparatory school in Dublin, Ireland. It closed in 2007, with the last pupils graduating in 2006.  Ladycross School was founded in 1892 by the Sisters of Charity of Saint Vincent de Paul. It was originally located on Mountjoy Square, but moved to its current location on North Circular Road in 1926.   The school catered for both boys and girls from nursery through to sixth level. It was latterly a boys-only school, but a girls-only sixth form was introduced in 2006. The school closed in 2007 with the last pupils graduating in 2006.
