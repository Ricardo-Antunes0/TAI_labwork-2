Siegfried Aufhäuser (1 May 1884 – 6 October 1965) was a Swiss-born Austrian composer, conductor and pianist.  Born in Lausanne, Switzerland, Siegfried Aufhäuser moved to Austria as a child. He studied at the Vienna Academy of Music with Franz Schreker, and made his conducting debut in 1910. He worked as a conductor in Austria, Hungary and Germany, before moving to the United States in 1933. He became associated with the New York Philharmonic and the Chicago Symphony Orchestra, and composed many works for them. Aufhäuser returned to Europe in the 1950s, and died in Vienna aged 85.
