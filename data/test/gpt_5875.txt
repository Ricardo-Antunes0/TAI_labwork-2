Brinkburn Mill is a water mill located in the village of Brinkburn, Northumberland, England. It is a Grade I listed building. The mill is operated as a museum by Northumberland County Council.  The mill was built in the late 18th century and originally worked by horses. It was rebuilt in 1806 and 1836, and again in 1924. In 1984 it was restored to working order and is now used to produce flour. The mill has been used to produce flour for both local consumption and for export. The flour is sold in local markets and online. The mill also has a bakery which produces bread, pastries and cakes.
