He served as the Foreign Minister of Bangladesh from 1972 to 1978 and as the eighth Prime Minister of Bangladesh from 1978 to 1981. He was the first Bangladeshi to hold the post of Prime Minister.  Malik was born in Chittagong on September 21, 1919. He obtained his early education at home before enrolling at the Dhaka University in 1937. He earned a LLB degree from the university in 1941 and was subsequently appointed an assistant professor at Dhaka University.  In 1946, Malik joined the civil service and served as a district magistrate, deputy commissioner, and additional secretary in various government offices. In 1962, he became the principal secretary to the President of Bangladesh. In 1965, he was appointed ambassador to India and served in that position until 1971.  In 1972, Malik was appointed foreign minister of Bangladesh and served in that position until 1978. In 1978, he was elected prime minister of Bangladesh and served until 1981. During his time as prime minister, he initiated a number of economic reforms in Bangladesh which helped to improve the nation's economy. Malik also played a role in regional diplomacy during his tenure as prime minister and helped to improve relations between Bangladesh and its neighbors India and Sri Lanka. He retired from politics in 1981 and died on October 10, 2006 at the age of 87.
