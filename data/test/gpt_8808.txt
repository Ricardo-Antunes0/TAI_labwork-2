Timothy Carl Means (born February 20, 1984) is an American mixed martial artist and former professional wrestler who is currently signed to the Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC) organization. He competes in the welterweight division.  Born and raised in Columbus, Ohio, Means began wrestling at the age of six and then transitioned to mixed martial arts in 2003. After compiling a record of 10-2 while competing for various organizations, including the Ohio wrestling powerhouse Team Quest and the now-defunct IFC promotion, Means signed with the UFC in 2010. He made his debut that same year against Mike Pierce and won via first-round TKO. Means has since gone on to compile a record of 19-7 and hold championships in both the UFC and WEC.  Means was recently featured on an episode of The Ultimate Fighter: Nations, where he was eliminated by eventual champion Johnny Case.
