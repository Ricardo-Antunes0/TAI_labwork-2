Eaton is a village and civil parish in the English county of Leicestershire. It is located about 8 miles north-west of Leicester and about 2 miles east of the A6 road.  The village has a population of about 820 people, including the hamlet of North Eaton. The parish has an area of 9.4 square miles.  The village is mentioned in the Domesday Book as "Eaton" and was part of the estate of Earl Hugh of Mercia. The parish church, dedicated to Saint John the Baptist, dates from the 14th century and has been restored several times. The ruins of a mediaeval manor house are also open to visitors.  The village has a pub, the Red Lion, which has been open since 1729 and was granted its current license in 1892. There is also a small shop, a post office, and a primary school.
