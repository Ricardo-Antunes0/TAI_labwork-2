HMCS Curlew was a commissioned minesweeper and patrol vessel of the Royal Canadian Navy. She was built in Scotland by William Denny and Brothers Ltd. at Dumbarton and launched on 8 November 1928. The ship was initially named "Dundee" after the city in Scotland, but was renamed "Curlew" in April 1930.  Curlew served with the RCN during the Second World War and participated in operations in the Battle of the Atlantic, including Convoy PQ-18, the Battle of the Saint Lawrence and the Battle of the Atlantic Patrol. The ship was placed out of service on 15 September 1945 and sold for scrapping in December that year.
