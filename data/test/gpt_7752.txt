Dallas Frazier (October 27, 1939 – January 4, 2014) was an American basketball player who competed in the 1960s and 1970s. He played college basketball for the University of Kentucky and then played professionally in the American Basketball Association (ABA) and National Basketball Association (NBA).  Frazier was born in Louisville, Kentucky. He attended Eastern High School in Louisville and was a two-time state champion in basketball. Frazier then enrolled at the University of Kentucky, where he played for the Kentucky Wildcats men's basketball team from 1959 to 1962. In his freshman season, Frazier was a member of the Wildcats' 1963 NCAA championship team.  In 1964, Frazier joined the ABA's Cincinnati Royals, where he played with future NBA players Jerry Lucas and Nate Thurmond. The Royals won the ABA championship that season. The next year, Frazier joined the Philadelphia 76ers, where he played with Wilt Chamberlain. The 76ers won the ABA championship in 1966 and 1967.  Frazier was drafted by the Boston Celtics in the first round of the 1968 NBA draft. However, he did not play with them until after he had been suspended by the NBA for refusing to participate in a physical exam. He played with the Celtics from 1968 to 1972, winning two championships (1969 and 1972). After leaving Boston, Frazier played for the Atlanta Hawks (1973–74), Detroit Pistons (1974–76), New York Knicks (1977–78), and Utah Jazz (1978–80). He retired after playing one season
