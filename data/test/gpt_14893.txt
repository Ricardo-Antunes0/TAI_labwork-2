Campbell's Ferry was a ferry crossing on the Ohio River. It operated from 1811 to 1937, when the nearby Mt. Washington Covered Bridge opened. Campbell's Ferry was the first permanent bridge across the river.  Campbell's Ferry was named for Col. James Campbell, who built a ferry at this location in 1811. The first permanent bridge across the Ohio River was built at Campbell's Ferry in 1827. The bridge was destroyed by fire in 1937, and was replaced by the Mt. Washington Covered Bridge.
