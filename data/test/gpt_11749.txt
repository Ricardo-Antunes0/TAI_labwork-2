"Thanks for Leaving" is a song recorded by the British rock band the Beatles. Written by John Lennon and credited to Lennonâ€“McCartney, it was released as a single in the United Kingdom on 31 May 1967 and in the United States on 7 June 1967.  The song was recorded during the sessions for their album "Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band" in late 1966, and features backing vocals from Ringo Starr. It was released as the fourth and final single from the album in Britain and the United States, respectively.  Lennon said of the song: "It's about a guy who's been dumped, you know. He thanks his lucky stars that he's getting out of this relationship." The song has been described as an autobiographical lament, with Lennon singing about his struggles with self-confidence.  "Thanks for Leaving" became one of the Beatles' most popular singles, peaking at number two on the UK Singles Chart and number one on the US "Billboard" Hot 100. The song played a significant role in their transition from a 1960s pop band to a 1970s Jukebox act. It has been covered by numerous artists, including Elvis Presley, The Monkees, The Kinks, and Joe Cocker.  In 2017, "Thanks for Leaving" was voted as the best Beatles song by readers of Q Magazine.
