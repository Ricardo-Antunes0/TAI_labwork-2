Rakesh "Raj" Kumar Bhala (born 1962) is an Indian film actor and producer who works in Hindi cinema. He has appeared in more than 250 Hindi films, mostly in supporting roles.  Bhala was born in Pune, Maharashtra, India to a Hindu father and Christian mother. He did his schooling at St. Xavier's High School, Pune, and later studied Economics at the University of Mumbai.  Bhala started his career as an assistant director in 1984 with the film "Naya Daur", before making his debut as an actor with the 1987 film "Char Dil Char Kiya". He has since appeared in supporting roles in many of Hindi cinema's biggest hits, including "Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge", "Pyar Ka Punchnama", "Aa Paar" and "Dil Chahta Hai". In 2000, he produced and starred in the critically acclaimed mystery thriller film "Dhadkan". He has since produced several more films, including the commercially successful romantic comedy "Tum Se Hi".
