Manchester Collieries was a coal mining company in Manchester, England. It was founded in 1892 and became one of the largest coal producers in the United Kingdom. In 1923 it merged with the Lancashire Coalfield Co Ltd to form the National Coal Board.  The Manchester Collieries was founded in 1892 by William Seddon and his brother-in-law, John Wright. The company became one of the largest coal producers in the United Kingdom, with mines at Ashton-under-Lyne, Chorlton-cum-Hardy, Didsbury, Fallowfield, Heaton Mersey, Manchester, Newton Heath and Rochdale. In 1923 it merged with the Lancashire Coalfield Co Ltd to form the National Coal Board. The company closed in 1984.
