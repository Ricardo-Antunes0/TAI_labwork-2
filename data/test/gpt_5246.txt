Born in Kyoto, Hamasaki started her career as a vocalist in the late 1990s, and achieved mainstream success in the early 2000s with her debut album A Place for My Head. Her subsequent studio albums have sold over 40 million copies worldwide, making her one of the best-selling artists in Japan.  Hamasaki has released five English language albums— debut album "A Place for My Head" (2001), "Love Songs" (2002), "Colors" (2003), "The Best of Ayumi Hamasaki" (2005), and "Distance" (2009)— and has sold over 20 million records worldwide. She has won numerous awards, including five Grammy Awards, seven MTV Video Music Awards, two Brit Awards, an Emmy Award, and a Golden Globe Award. In 2005, she was appointed a UNESCO Goodwill Ambassador for young people's music.  In June 2019, Hamasaki announced her retirement from music after a decade-long career.
