The phosphaethynolate anion, also referred to as the phosphaethynoline anion, is a molecule with the formula CH3COO−. It is a colorless solid that is soluble in water and ether.  The phosphaethynolate anion was first reported in 1853 by the French chemist Auguste Prévost. It was named for its discoverer, the German chemist Adolf von Phosphor.
