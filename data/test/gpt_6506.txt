Sadāparibhūta Bodhisattva, Never Disparaging Bodhisattva, (Ch: 常不輕菩薩) is a Mahāyāna Buddhist deity who represents the ideal of patience.  The Sadāparibhūta Bodhisattva is one of the most popular and well-known bodhisattvas in Mahayana Buddhism. He is often depicted as wearing a simple white robe, with his hands in prayer position. He is said to be very kind and compassionate, and he is always willing to help others.  The Sadāparibhūta Bodhisattva is especially venerated in countries with a large Buddhist population, such as China, Japan, and Tibet. His popularity has led to him being featured in many religious paintings and sculptures.
