Musallam bin Nufl, was a key insurgent strategist in the Saudi Arabian-led intervention in Yemen, fighting against Houthi rebels and their allies, including Saleh's forces.  Born in 1952 in Najran, Musallam bin Nufl fought in the Yemeni Civil War on the side of the government of President Ali Abdullah Saleh, eventually becoming a leading insurgent strategist. He is most notable for his role in the Saudi Arabian-led intervention in Yemen, which began in March 2015. As an insurgent commander and strategist, he is credited with helping to regain some territory from the Houthis and their allies, and he has been described as a "master tactician". He was killed in an air strike on 15 October 2017.
