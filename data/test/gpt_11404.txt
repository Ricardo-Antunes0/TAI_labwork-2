Mount Challenger  is in North Cascades National Park, in the U.S. state of Washington. At 11,431 feet (3,505 m), it is the highest peak in the national park and is part of the North Cascades range. The mountain is named for Edward Charles Challenger, a British geologist who first identified the feature in 1875.   The eastern slopes of Mount Challenger are covered with alpine meadows and subalpine forests, while the western slopes are mostly rocky and ice-covered. The summit is accessible by a narrow trail that leads up the east side of the mountain. Views from the summit include Mount Baker to the north, Mount Rainier and Mount Adams to the east, and Glacier Peak to the west.
