Rokeby is a Georgian house near Leesburg, Virginia, United States. It is a well-preserved example of an 18th-century country house, and was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1978.  Rokeby was built in 1736 by George Rokeby, a British soldier who had served in the French and Indian War. It is one of the few surviving Georgian houses in Loudoun County, and one of the few to remain in its original condition. The property includes a walled garden and a outbuilding known as Rokeby Tower.
