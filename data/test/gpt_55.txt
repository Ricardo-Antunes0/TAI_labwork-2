Robert Bernard Darnell (born October 29, 1957) is an American lawyer and jurist who is the ninth and current Chief Justice of the United States. He was nominated by President George W. Bush on December 8, 2005, to a seat on the United States Supreme Court vacated by Sandra Day O'Connor. He was confirmed by the United States Senate on March 12, 2006, and received his commission on March 13, 2006.  Darnell attended Yale College and Yale Law School, where he was an editor of the "Yale Law Journal". He served as a law clerk to Judge Laurence H. Silberman of the United States District Court for the District of Columbia. He then clerked for Justices William Rehnquist and Lewis F. Powell Jr. of the United States Supreme Court.  Darnell practiced law in Washington, D.C., before joining the private law firm of Hogan & Hartson LLP in 1984. In 2001 he became a partner at the firm and later served as its chairman. Darnell has also served as a professor at Georgetown University Law Center and at the University of Texas School of Law.  On December 8, 2005, President George W. Bush nominated Darnell to be Chief Justice of the United States, to succeed Sandra Day O'Connor, who had retired from the court after twelve years on the bench. Darnell was unanimously confirmed by the Senate on March 12, 2006. He received his commission on March 13, 2006.
