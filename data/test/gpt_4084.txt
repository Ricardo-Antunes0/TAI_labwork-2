Operation Deadlight was the code name for a British Army offensive in Afghanistan in 2006. The objective of the operation was to clear the Taliban from the district of Musa Qala, which was considered one of their key strongholds.  The offensive began on 4 November 2006 and lasted until 9 December 2006. The operation was a success, with the Taliban being pushed out of the district. This victory helped to improve security and stability in the area, and allowed reconstruction to begin.
