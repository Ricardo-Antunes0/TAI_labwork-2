Marketing engineering is currently defined as "a multidisciplinary field of study that deals with the application of engineering principles and practices to the development, management, and control of marketing programs." In addition to its application to marketing, marketing engineering is also related to such disciplines as operations research, quantitative methods, and marketing research.  The purpose of marketing engineering is to create efficient and profitable marketing programs by applying engineering principles and practices. Marketing engineers work with a variety of stakeholders, including businesses, product managers, advertising agencies, and product development teams. They use a variety of techniques, including mathematical modeling, forecasting, and scenario analysis.  Marketing engineering is an important field because it helps businesses create products that meet customer needs while minimizing costs. In addition, marketing engineering can help businesses improve their marketing strategy by providing them with accurate information about customer needs and preferences.
