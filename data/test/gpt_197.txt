Born in 1903 in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, Ross attended Bryn Mawr College, where she graduated with a B.A. in 1925. Afterward, she studied at the University of London and the University of Paris, earning a Ph.D. in 1933. She then taught at Bryn Mawr and Wellesley College before joining the faculty at Barnard College in 1938.  Ross is best known for her work on medieval history, which focused on women's roles in society and culture. She also wrote extensively on women's education and feminist issues, as well as on medieval literature and art. In 1964, she was elected a fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences. She retired from Barnard in 1978 but continued to teach part-time until her death in 1993.
