Paul Leser studied at the universities of Freiburg and Heidelberg. In 1922 he became a professor at the University of Heidelberg, where he remained until his retirement in 1965. He was a member of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences and the German Academy of Sciences Leopoldina.  Leser was best known for his studies of the French Revolution, which he undertook with an empirical approach. He was also interested in intellectual history and the history of ideas.
