Herman Thomas Davis Jr. (born March 21, 1951), better known by his stage name Tommy Davis, is a retired Major League Baseball outfielder. He played in the American League from 1973 to 1983 for the Baltimore Orioles and Detroit Tigers.  Davis was born in Portsmouth, Virginia, and attended Portsmouth High School. He was drafted by the Orioles in the fourth round of the 1971 amateur draft. Davis made his big league debut with Baltimore on July 3, 1973. He played for the Orioles for eleven seasons before being traded to the Tigers in August 1983. In his twelve seasons in the majors, Davis hit .271 with 219 home runs and 981 RBI. After his playing career ended, Davis served as a hitting coach for the Tigers from 1984 to 1988, and then as a scout for the team from 1989 to 1993. He also served as an assistant baseball coach at Hampton University from 1995 to 2001. Davis was inducted into the Virginia Sports Hall of Fame in 2004.  Davis has two children, daughter Kari and son Tyler, both of whom play college baseball. Davis is currently a color commentator with MLB Network.
