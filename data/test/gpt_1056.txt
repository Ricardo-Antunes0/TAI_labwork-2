Born into a prominent family in Greensboro, North Carolina, Brown was the youngest of four daughters of attorney and judge Orlando Brown and socialite Lavinia Beall. She attended the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, where she was a member of the Kappa Alpha Theta sorority.  After graduating in 1935, Brown worked as a teacher in New York City before moving to Los Angeles in 1942 to work as a journalist. She became one of the city's leading feminists, writing for publications such as "The Nation" and "The Los Angeles Times". She also served on numerous boards and committees, including the National Committee for a Sane Nuclear Policy and the National Advisory Commission on Civil Disorders.  In 1988, Brown received the Eleanor Roosevelt Award from the National Women's Political Caucus. She died in 2002 at the age of 90.
