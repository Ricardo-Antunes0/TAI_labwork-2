Born in London, Harvey started her music career as a backing vocalist for the British soul group, The Brand New Heavies. She released her solo debut album, "Liza", in 1995, which spawned the singles "I Believe in You" and "The Chain". In 1997, she released her second album, "The Soul Sessions", which featured the hit singles "Since You've Been Gone" and "I'll Be There for You".  In 2000, Harvey released her third album, "Voice of the Heart". The album peaked at number two on the UK Albums Chart and spawned the hit singles "A Hundred Miles" and "When You Say Nothing at All". In 2002, she released her fourth album, "The Whole World". The album was preceded by the hit single "Cost of Living".  In 2004, Harvey released her fifth studio album, "The Truth About Love", which spawned the singles "Every Little Thing She Does Is Magic" and "It Ain't Over Yet". In 2006, she released her sixth studio album, "Living Proof", which was preceded by the hit single "How Deep Is Your Love".  In 2013, Harvey released her seventh studio album, "Brave".
