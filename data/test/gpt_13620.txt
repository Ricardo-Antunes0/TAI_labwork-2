Andrew Edward Left  (born July 9, 1957) is an American political commentator and author who served as the senior advisor to U.S. Senator Bernie Sanders (I-VT) during his 2016 presidential campaign. Left is a former political reporter and columnist for The Huffington Post, where he was a founding editor-in-chief.  Andrew Left was born in 1957 in Dorchester, Massachusetts. He attended Dartmouth College and the University of California, Berkeley, where he received his B.A. in political science in 1979.  After college, Left worked as a journalist for The Boston Globe and then as a political reporter for The Washington Post. In 1992, he became the editorial director of The Huffington Post, where he served until his departure in early 2017. He was a founding editor-in-chief of the website.  Left has written extensively on U.S. politics, including articles for The Huffington Post and books on topics such as health care reform and the 2008 financial crisis. He has also appeared as a political commentator on cable news networks such as MSNBC and CNN, and on radio shows such as NPR's "All Things Considered" and "The Diane Rehm Show".  In January 2017, Left announced that he was leaving The Huffington Post to join Sen. Bernie Sanders' (I-VT) presidential campaign as its senior advisor. Left served in this role until Sanders' defeat in the 2016 election.  Left has been married to Jill Stein since 1984; they have two children together.
