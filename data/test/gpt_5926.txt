Hawthorne was born in Concord, Massachusetts, to Nathaniel and Sophia (Peabody) Hawthorne. He had two sisters, Elizabeth and Margaret. His father was a Unitarian minister and his mother a philosopher and author. After the death of his father in 1864, Hawthorne moved with his mother and sisters to The Old Manse in Concord. He attended the Phillips Academy in Andover, Massachusetts, from 1908 to 1910. He then attended Harvard University, where he was a member of the Psi Upsilon fraternity and the Latin Club. After graduating from Harvard in 1913, he spent a year traveling in Europe and then entered the Law School of Harvard University, from which he graduated in 1917.  Hawthorne began his writing career as a journalist for the "Boston Evening Transcript". He then wrote articles for magazines such as "The Atlantic Monthly" and "The Nation". In 1919, he published his first book, "The House of the Seven Gables". He married Elizabeth Peters on October 14, 1924; they had two children before divorcing in 1942. The following year he married Lillian Hellman. They remained married until his death.  Hawthorne's first novel, "The Scarlet Letter", was published in 1850.
