Journeys of Frodo: An Atlas of J.R.R. Tolkien's Legendarium is a comprehensive reference work on the fictional world of J.R.R. Tolkien, written by Raymond E. Feist and David Day.  Frodo was the protagonist of Journeys of Frodo, one of the most popular and acclaimed works of fantasy fiction ever written, and the subject of an atlas-style reference work, Journeys of Frodo: An Atlas of J.R.R. Tolkien's Legendarium. Published in 2003, the book is lavishly illustrated with maps, diagrams, and photographs tracing Frodo's journeys from his departure from his home in the Shire to his ultimate confrontation with the evil Lord Sauron at Mount Doom.  The book is divided into three parts: The Road Goes On Forever (Chapters 1-9), The Taming of the Shire (Chapters 10-13), and The Journey to Mount Doom (Chapters 14-25). Each chapter begins with a map that shows Frodo's route from point to point as he travels across Middle-earth. There are also detailed descriptions of all the major places and characters encountered along the way, as well as full-page illustrations for each chapter.
