John Harrington Stevens (June 13, 1820 – July 4, 1892) was an American politician and diplomat who served as the 22nd governor of New Mexico Territory from 1868 to 1870.  Stevens was born in Newark, New Jersey, on June 13, 1820. He was the son of John Stevens, a lawyer and political figure in New Jersey, and his wife, the former Phebe Ann (née Harrington) Stevens. Stevens attended the University of New Mexico at Albuquerque before he moved to Texas in 1842 to become a rancher. He soon became involved in politics and was elected as a delegate to the Texas Secession Convention in 1861.  After the American Civil War began, Stevens was appointed by Confederate President Jefferson Davis as the first Confederate commissioner to Mexico City. Stevens remained in Mexico City until 1865, when he returned to Texas. He served as a delegate to the national Republican conventions in 1868 and 1876.  In 1870, Stevens was elected as the 22nd governor of New Mexico Territory. He served until 1872 when he resigned to assume his post as United States Minister to Spain. Stevens remained in Spain until 1875 when he resigned due to poor health. He returned to live in New Mexico until his death on July 4, 1892. Stevens was buried at San Miguel Cemetery in Albuquerque.
