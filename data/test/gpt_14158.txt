Albert Edmunds Cahlan was born in 1908 in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, the eldest of three children of Charles and Julia Cahlan. After attending the Archbishop Ryan School and Villanova University, he studied at the Pontifical Gregorian University in Rome, where he received a licentiate in theology.  On August 5, 1929, Cahlan was ordained a priest by Archbishop John J. Myers at the Cathedral of Saint John the Evangelist. He served as a curate at Saint Charles Borromeo Church in Philadelphia from 1934 to 1938. From 1938 to 1949 he was secretary to Cardinal Francis Spellman. In 1949 Cahlan became rector of Saint Francis de Sales Cathedral in Philadelphia.  On March 12, 1969, Cahlan was appointed by Pope Paul VI as the fourth Bishop of Philadelphia, replacing James Edward McCarthy. Cahlan had been considered for the position since October 1968; McCarthy had announced his intention to retire on May 1 of that year. Cahlan was consecrated on April 30 by Cardinal John J. O'Connor at Saint Peter's Basilica. As bishop, Cahlan advocated for social justice and interfaith understanding. He also worked to improve the diocese's schools and hospitals.
