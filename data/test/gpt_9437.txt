Matthias Eberhard (born 15 November 1815, at Tübingen – died 8 October 1881, in Munich) was a German philosopher and theologian.  Eberhard was born into a wealthy and well-connected family. After completing his studies at Tübingen, he became a Privatdozent at the University of Heidelberg in 1842. In 1846, he became an associate professor at the University of Freiburg and in 1852, a full professor at the University of Munich. He was also rector of the University of Munich from 1865 to 1868.  Eberhard is best known for his work on philosophy and theology, but he also wrote on pedagogy, history, and philology. His most famous work is "The History of Philosophy in Three Volumes" (1859).
