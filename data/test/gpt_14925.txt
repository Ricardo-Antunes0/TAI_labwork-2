Richard P. Condie (July 5, 1898 – October 9, 1976) was an American historian and professor. Condie is best known for his work on colonial America and the American Revolution.  Condie was born in 1898 in Wausau, Wisconsin. He received his bachelor's degree from the University of Wisconsin–Madison in 1920 and his doctorate from Harvard University in 1932.  Condie began his teaching career at the University of Wisconsin–Madison, where he taught until his retirement in 1968. He then served as a visiting professor at Brown University and the University of California, Berkeley. He died in 1976.
