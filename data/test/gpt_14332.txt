The Brazilian monitor Ceará was the fifth ship of her name to serve in the United States Navy.  The Brazilian monitor Ceará was designed and built in Brazil in 1859, and commissioned into the United States Navy in 1872. She served in both the American Civil War and the Spanish–American War, and was decommissioned in 1899. She was sold for scrap in 1902. Her armament consisted of two smoothbore cannon mounted on sponsons fore and aft, four rifled cannons mounted in casemates at the bow and stern, and six torpedoes.
