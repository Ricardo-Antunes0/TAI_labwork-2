Do It Records was a British independent record label founded in 1976 by Tony Wilson and Bernard Sumner. The label was notable for its association with the Manchester music scene of the late 1970s and early 1980s, and for releasing recordings by The Smiths, Joy Division, The Happy Mondays, New Order and The Charlatans.
