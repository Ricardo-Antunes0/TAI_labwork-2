Hooveria purpurea is a species of flowering plant in the daisy family known by the common name purple coneflower.  This plant is native to much of North America, where it can be found in many types of habitat, from open areas such as deserts and woodlands to rocky slopes and canyon walls. It is a perennial herb growing up to 30 centimeters tall. The leaves are oppositely arranged on the stem, each one divided into several narrow lobes. The inflorescence is a large array of pink or purple ray florets surrounded by disc florets.
