The New Year Ekiden, (officially the ), is a annual 12-stage running race in Japan. It is the second largest annual running event in the world, after the Tokyo Marathon.   The New Year Ekiden is run over a course of 12 stages over the course of one day, starting and finishing at Meiji Jingu shrine in central Tokyo. The first edition of the race was held in 1924, and has been staged annually since then with only a one-year break during World War II. The race is open to both men and women, and is categorized as a "major" event on the Japanese athletics calendar.   The New Year Ekiden has been described as one of Japan's "national holidays", and is popular with both Japanese runners and spectators. The race has been broadcast live on national television since 1975, and has also been featured in several feature films and television dramas.
