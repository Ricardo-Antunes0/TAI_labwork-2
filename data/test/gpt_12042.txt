Charles Ray Ritcheson (26 February 1925 – 9 May 2006) was an Australian politician who served as the 22nd Premier of New South Wales from 1975 to 1981.  Ritcheson was born in Sydney, the son of Charles Ray Ritcheson and his wife Mary (née Horsfall). He attended Sydney Boys High School and then the University of Sydney, where he obtained a Bachelor of Arts degree in 1947. After leaving university, Ritcheson worked as a journalist for several newspapers before being elected to the New South Wales Legislative Assembly as the Labor member for the seat of Willoughby in 1965. He became Deputy Premier under Jack Lang in 1971, and was appointed Premier after Lang's death in 1975. During his time as Premier, Ritcheson oversaw major infrastructure projects such as the M4 motorway and the Sydney Opera House. He lost his seat at the 1981 state election, but later served as a federal minister in the second term of Bob Hawke's government. Ritcheson died in 2006 at the age of 80.
