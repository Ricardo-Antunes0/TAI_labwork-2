Rafael Minaskanian (, born 19 December 1941) is an Iranian-born Armenian composer, conductor and pianist.  Rafael Minaskanian was born on December 19, 1941 in Tehran, Iran to Armenian parents. He began his musical training at the age of six with his mother, a pianist and composer. In 1957, he moved to Armenia with his family and continued his musical education under the guidance of prominent Armenian composers such as Vahram Papazian and Gevorg Kocharian.   In 1963, Minaskanian made his debut as a conductor with the Yerevan Opera House Orchestra. He has since served as the conductor of several major orchestras, including the Yerevan State Symphony Orchestra (1966-1977), the Yerevan Radio Symphony Orchestra (1977-1984), and the Tehran Symphony Orchestra (1984-1991).   As a composer, Minaskanian has written over 100 works, including symphonies, operas, ballets, and chamber music. His most well-known works include the symphony "The Adventures of Baron Munchausen" (1976), the opera "The Poet and the Painter" (1985), and the ballet "The Golden Age" (1998). In 1992, he was awarded the State Prize of Armenia for his contributions to Armenian music.   Minaskanian currently resides in Yerevan, Armenia.
