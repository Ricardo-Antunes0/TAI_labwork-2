Jennifer Kate Foster is an American singer-songwriter, musician, and actress. Foster was born in Orlando, Florida, to Kathy and Jerry Foster. She was raised in a devoutly Christian family and has two sisters, Tasha and Tamara. Foster's early musical experiences were with church choir and playing the violin. At age thirteen, she discovered her love for songwriting when she wrote a song for her church's talent show. After high school, Foster attended the University of Central Florida, where she studied musical theater. While at UCF, Foster formed a band with friends and began to play shows in the Orlando area. In 2006, Foster released her first album, Jen Foster. The album was produced by Dave Cobb and featured guest appearances from Norah Jones, John Mayer, and John Petrucci of Dream Theater. The album received positive reviews from music critics and reached No. 17 on the Billboard Top Heatseekers chart. In 2007, Foster released her second album, The Heart of Everything. The album was produced by Tom Schuck (Wilco, The National) and featured guest appearances from Ben Harper, John Mayer, and Sheryl Crow. The album received mixed reviews from music critics but reached No. 26 on the Billboard Top 200 chart. In 2009, Foster released her third album, Bird on a Wire. The album was produced by T-Bone Burnett (Bonnie Raitt, Emmylou Harris) and featured guest appearances from KT Tunstall, Elvis Costello, and Doyle Bramhall II.
