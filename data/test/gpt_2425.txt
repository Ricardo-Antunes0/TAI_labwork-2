German submarine U-969 was a Type VIIC U-boat of Nazi Germany's Kriegsmarine during World War II. She was laid down on 23 October 1943 at the Blohm & Voss yard in Hamburg, launched on 4 December 1943 and commissioned on 1 March 1944 under the command of Oberleutnant zur See Claus-JÃ¼rgen Jost.  U-969 served with 3rd U-boat Flotilla from 1 March 1944 until her destruction on 17 May 1944. She sank one merchant ship, one tanker and one warship for a total of 29,000 tons (28,000 tons after the loss of one ton of oil).  U-969 was sunk by depth charges from a British Liberator aircraft on 17 May 1944. All 28 crew members were killed.
