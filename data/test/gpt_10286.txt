Denis Laurence Dutton (9 February 1944 – 20 January 2017) was an Australian philosopher and law professor. He was the Dean of the Faculty of Law at the University of New South Wales from 2001 to 2006 and a Fellow of the Australian Academy of Law and a member of the Order of Australia.  Born in Sydney, Dutton attended The King's School, Parramatta before studying law at the University of Sydney, from which he graduated with First Class Honours in 1967. Dutton then undertook postgraduate studies at Oxford University, where he obtained his Doctor of Philosophy in 1970.  Dutton commenced his teaching career at the University of Melbourne in 1970, where he became professor in 1984 and dean of the faculty of law in 2001. He subsequently held appointments at Harvard Law School (1995–1996), Yale Law School (1997), and the University of Oxford (2001).  A prolific writer, Dutton has authored or co-authored more than sixty books, including texts on legal philosophy, criminal law, contract theory, property law, torts, and constitutional law. He has also edited or co-edited thirteen books, including works on legal philosophy by John Austin and Ronald Dworkin.  Dutton died on 20 January 2017 after a long battle with multiple myeloma. He was 71 years old.
