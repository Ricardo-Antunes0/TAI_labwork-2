Lynn Zelevansky (born 1947) is an American artist who has worked in various media, including painting, sculpture, installation, performance, and video. Zelevansky is known for her use of appropriated images and materials in her art, as well as her interest in the history and philosophy of art.  Zelevansky was born in 1947 in New York City. She earned a BFA from Hunter College in 1967 and an MFA from Yale University in 1971. Zelevansky has been the recipient of numerous awards, including a Guggenheim Fellowship (1979), a Pollock-Krasner Foundation Award (1991), and a National Endowment for the Arts Fellowship (1996). She currently lives and works in Brooklyn, New York.
