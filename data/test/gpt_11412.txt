Kloss's gibbon (Hylobates klossii), also known as the Kloss's monkey, is a gibbon species from the genus Hylobates. It is found in eastern and central Vietnam, as well as Laos, Cambodia and Thailand. Its natural habitats are subtropical or tropical moist lowland forests and subtropical or tropical moist montane forests.  The Kloss's gibbon is a small, slender gibbon that grows to a shoulder height of about . It has a long tail and bushy hair on its head, body and arms. The face is round with large eyes. The gibbon is mainly red or orange in color, with black markings on its face and a white stripe down its back. It is omnivorous, eating fruits, insects and other small animals.
