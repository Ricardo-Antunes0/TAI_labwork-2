Wood County is a county located in the U.S. state of Texas. As of the 2010 census, the population was 9,827. Its county seat is Woodville.  Wood County is part of the Tyler, TX Metropolitan Statistical Area.  The county was created in 1876 and organized in 1879. It was named for Major General James A. Wood, a hero of the Mexican-American War.  According to the U.S. Census Bureau, the county has a total area of , of which is land and (1.3%) is water.       As of the census of 2000, there were 10,458 people, 3,872 households, and 2,914 families residing in the county. The population density was 34 people per square mile (13/km²). There were 4,123 housing units at an average density of 14 per square mile (5/km²). The racial makeup of the county was 97.89% White, 0.51% Native American, 0.08% Asian, 0.02% Pacific Islander, 0.82% from other races, and 1.87% from two or more races. 2.21% of the population were Hispanic or Latino of any race.
