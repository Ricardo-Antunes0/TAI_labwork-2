Darkest Days is the third album released by American heavy metal band Slipknot. The album was released on November 3, 2009, in the United States and February 24, 2010, in Europe. It is the follow-up to the band's 2006 album Vol. 3: The Subliminal Verses and the first to not feature bassist Paul Gray and drummer Joey Jordison.  Darkest Days was produced by Slipknot and Rick Rubin and recorded at Fort Minor Studios in Los Angeles and NRG Studios in North Hollywood, California. The album features guest appearances from Corey Taylor of Slipknot and Stone Sour, Tim Lambesis of Lamb of God, Santigold, Killswitch Engage's Jesse Leach and Randy Blythe of Black Label Society.  Upon its release, Darkest Days received generally positive reviews from music critics. At Metacritic, which assigns a normalized rating out of 100 to reviews from mainstream critics, the album has received an average score of 78, which indicates "generally favorable reviews".
