Joseph S. Suliga (December 25, 1957 â€“ ) is an American historian, author and professor emeritus at the University of Texas at Austin.  Suliga was born in Chicago, Illinois. He received his B.A. from DePaul University in 1978 and his M.A. and Ph.D. from the University of Michigan in 1981 and 1984, respectively. Suliga has been a professor at the University of Texas at Austin since 1984, where he currently holds the position of Professor Emeritus of History. Suliga's research focuses on the history of Polish America and the history of Polish communism in America.  Suliga has authored or co-authored six books, including "Polish America: A History" (1998), "The Polish Communist Party in America: 1919-1939" (2003), "The Warsaw Uprising 1944" (2012), "Red Star Over Poland: The Polish Left After World War II" (2017), and "Poles and Jews in American Life: A History" (forthcoming). He has also published more than seventy articles in scholarly journals, including "Wisconsin Magazine of History", "The Journal of American History", "The Polish Review", and "History Compass".  Suliga has been awarded a Guggenheim Fellowship (1993), a fellowship from the American Council of Learned Societies (1994), an honorary doctorate from Concordia University (2000), and the George Polk Award for Historical Writing (2004).
