Rodney Laurence Ferguson II (born August 25, 1966) is an American former professional baseball player who played in Major League Baseball (MLB) for the St. Louis Cardinals, Philadelphia Phillies, New York Mets, and Tampa Bay Devil Rays. He was a three-time All-Star and won the National League (NL) Most Valuable Player (MVP) Award in 2003.  Ferguson attended high school at Meridian High School in Meridian, Mississippi. He played college baseball at the University of Mississippi, where he was a two-time All-American and led the Ole Miss Rebels to their first NCAA College World Series appearance in 1988.  Ferguson was selected by the Cardinals in the first round of the 1988 amateur draft. He made his MLB debut with the Cardinals in 1990 and became a key player on the team's championship teams in 1995 and 1997. Ferguson was traded to Philadelphia in 2001, and then to New York in 2003. In 2004, he signed with Tampa Bay as a free agent. Ferguson retired after the 2006 season.  In 2006, Ferguson became head coach of Ole Miss' baseball program, which he led to its first NCAA Division I tournament appearance in over a decade in 2013. The Rebels were eliminated from competition by Florida State in the first round.
