Ophelia Mensah Hayford is a Ghanaian Politician. She is the current Minister of Gender, Children and Social Protection in the Ghanaian Cabinet.  Hayford was born in Kumasi, Ghana on 2 July 1974. She studied political science at the University of Ghana, Legon, where she graduated with a BSc in 1996. Hayford then continued her education at the University of Edinburgh, where she obtained an MSc in Development Studies in 1999.  After completing her studies, Hayford worked as a research assistant at the Centre for African Studies at the University of Edinburgh. In 2001, she became a research fellow at the Centre for Development Research in Accra, Ghana. In 2003, Hayford was appointed director of the National Youth Service Corps (NYSC), a position she held until 2007.  Hayford entered politics in 2007 when she was elected to parliament as a member of the New Patriotic Party (NPP). She served as deputy parliamentary leader for youth affairs and gender issues until her appointment as Minister of Gender, Children and Social Protection in the cabinet of Prime Minister Nana Akufo-Addo on 12 December 2018.
