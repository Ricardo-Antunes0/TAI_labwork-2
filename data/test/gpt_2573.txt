102 Boulevard Haussmann is a 1991 British-French crime film directed by Neil Jordan and written by Peter Morgan. The story is based on the true story of the French serial killer, Marcel Petiot.  The film tells the story of a serial killer, Marcel Petiot (Yves Montand), who is arrested in 1944 after killing 37 people in the Parisian district of Haussmann. The film also focuses on the detectives investigating the crimes, as well as Petiot's wife and daughter.
