Monolith is the sixth studio album by American rock band Kansas, released in 1988 on Wind-Up Records. The album peaked at No. 85 on the "Billboard" 200 chart.  After leaving MCA Records, the band signed with Wind-Up Records and recorded Monolith in 1988. The album was produced by the band's longtime collaborator Bob Ezrin. It features the singles "Carry On Wayward Son" and "Dust in the Wind".  The album received mixed reviews from critics. However, it has been certified platinum by the RIAA. In 2009, "Rolling Stone" placed it at No. 53 on their list of the 100 Greatest Albums of the 1980s.   In a 2012 interview with "Classic Rock", drummer Vinnie Moore said that while he thought the album was okay, he was not happy with it and preferred their earlier work.   Kansas formed in 1984 in Wichita, Kansas. The band's original lineup consisted of vocalist/guitarist Kerry Livgren, drummer Vinnie Moore, bassist Tim Collins, and guitarists Donnie Moore and Steve Walsh. After releasing two independent albums, "Leftoverture" (1984) and "Point of Know Return" (1985), the band signed with MCA Records and released their debut album, "Kanaduh" (1986).
