Nico Castel (August 1, 1931 – May 8, 2016) was a Swiss composer and pianist.  Castel was born in Lausanne, Switzerland, to a musical family. After studying at the University of Geneva, he began his career as a pianist. He later composed music for stage, film and television. Castel won many awards for his work, including the Grand Prix du Disque from the Académie Charles Cros and the Silver Lion at the Venice Film Festival. He died in Lausanne at the age of 86.
