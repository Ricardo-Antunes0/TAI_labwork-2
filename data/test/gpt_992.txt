Théophile Charles Gilles Ferré (6 May 1845 – 12 March 1912) was a French composer, pianist, and conductor. He is considered among the most important composers of the early 20th century.  Born in Sète, France, Ferré studied at the Paris Conservatoire with Etienne-Louis De Lausanne and won first prize in 1867. He worked as a pianist and conductor, mainly in France and Belgium. He also wrote songs, chamber music, and orchestral works. His best-known pieces include the opera "La Bohème" (1896), the ballets "Giselle" (1892) and "Swan Lake" (1905), and the orchestral suite "Le Carnaval des Animaux" (1910).
