The Chinese alligator (Alligator sinensis, ), also known as the Taiwan alligator, is a species of alligator found in Taiwan and the People's Republic of China.  The Chinese alligator grows to a length of and has a wide head, short neck, and small body. The lower jaw is slightly longer than the upper jaw, giving the alligator a distinctive "jaw gap" appearance. The skin is smooth and green, with darker bands running along its back. It has yellow eyes and a broad black stripe on its chin. The Chinese alligator is a timid animal that feeds on fish and other aquatic creatures.
