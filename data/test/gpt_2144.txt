Otto Friedrich von Gierke, born Otto Friedrich Graf von Gierke, (6 October 1809 – 16 April 1892) was a German jurist and political philosopher. He is considered one of the founders of the legal positivism movement.  Gierke was born in Göttingen, the son of a Prussian statesman. After studying law at the universities of Göttingen, Heidelberg and Berlin, he became an advocate at the supreme court in Berlin in 1837. In 1851 he was appointed professor at the University of Berlin, where he remained until his retirement in 1882.  Gierke is best known for his work on political philosophy, which includes books on the theory of the state, political economy and social philosophy. He was also a leading theorist of legal positivism.
