Guna Vasantha Pai was a Senior Advocate of India who was a part of the Indian National Congress, and later the Communist Party of India (Marxist). She was also a recipient of the Padma Shri, India's fourth-highest civilian award.  Guna Vasantha Pai was born on 2 February 1924 in the town of Kancheepuram, in the South Indian state of Tamil Nadu. She received her early education at home before attending college in Madras (now Chennai), where she studied law. After completing her legal training, Pai worked as an advocate for a number of years, before joining the Indian National Congress (INC) in 1948.  Pai became a part of the INC's rural wing, the All India Kisan Sabha, and fought for the rights of farmers and labourers. She also became involved in politics outside of INC's traditional spheres, joining the Communist Party of India (Marxist) in 1965.  Pai held several positions within CPI(M), including secretary of its women's wing, the Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (DMK). She was also a member of the state legislative assemblies of Kerala and Tamil Nadu. In 1985, she was elected to the Rajya Sabha (the upper house of India's Parliament), serving until 1990.
