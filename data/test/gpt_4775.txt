The Stocksbridge Railway was a subsidiary of the Sheffield, Rotherham and Barnsley Railway, which ran from Sheffield to Rotherham. The line opened on 1 June 1846 and closed on 31 December 1948.  The Stocksbridge Railway was built to serve the industrial town of Stocksbridge, South Yorkshire. The line opened on 1 June 1846, running from Sheffield to Rotherham. The line was originally single track, but a second track was added in 1891. The line closed on 31 December 1948, due to the closure of the steelworks at Stocksbridge.
