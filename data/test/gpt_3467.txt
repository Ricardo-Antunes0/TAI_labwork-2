Loup Verlet (; 24 May 1931 – 2 July 1978) was a French mountaineer and climber.  Verlet was born in the Ardennes region of France, and began climbing in childhood. He made his first ascent of a mountain in 1951, and became a professional mountain climber in 1954. His first major climb was the Grandes Jorasses, a mountain in the Alps which he climbed with René De Filippi and André Bideau in 1955. Verlet was also the first person to ascend the Eiger, a Swiss mountain peak.  Verlet made many significant climbs throughout his career, including the Matterhorn, Aconcagua, and Mount Kenya. Loup Verlet died in a climbing accident on Mount Kenya in 1978.
