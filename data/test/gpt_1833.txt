Alva Earl Campbell Jr. (April 30, 1948 – December 9, 1999) was an American football player who was a running back in the National Football League for fourteen seasons. He played for the Houston Oilers (1968–1973), the Oakland Raiders (1974–1976), and the Minnesota Vikings (1977–1981). He was a three-time Pro Bowler and was named to the All-Pro First Team in 1978 and 1979. In 1980, he became the first player in NFL history to rush for over 2,000 yards in a season. He also played for the Los Angeles Rams (1982) and the New Orleans Saints (1983). Campbell was posthumously inducted into the Pro Football Hall of Fame in 2001.  Born in Huntsville, Alabama, Campbell grew up in Jacksonville, Florida, and attended Northeast Florida State College. He played college football at Northeast Florida State College as a running back and linebacker. He was drafted by the Houston Oilers in the fifth round of the 1968 NFL Draft. Campbell played for the Oilers from 1968 to 1973. He then played for the Oakland Raiders from 1974 to 1976, and for the Minnesota Vikings from 1977 to 1981. In 1978 and 1979, he was named to the All-Pro First Team. In 1980, he became the first player in NFL history to rush for over 2,000 yards in a season. Campbell also played for the Los Angeles Rams from 1982 to 1983 and for the New Orleans Saints from 1984 to 1985. He retired from playing in 1986 after playing fourteen seasons in the NFL
