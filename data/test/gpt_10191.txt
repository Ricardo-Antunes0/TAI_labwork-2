William B. Evans (born February 23, 1958) is an American jurist who has served on the United States Court of Appeals for the Tenth Circuit since 2006.  Evans was born in Pueblo, Colorado, and received a B.A. from Brigham Young University in 1980 and a J.D. from the University of Utah Law School in 1983. He was a law clerk to Judge Bruce D. Jenkins of the United States District Court for the District of Utah from 1983 to 1984. He was an associate at the Salt Lake City law firm of Olsen, Olsen, & Olson from 1984 to 1988, and was a partner at that firm from 1988 to 2006. He served as a deputy attorney general for the State of Utah from 1988 to 1990, and as an assistant attorney general for the State of Utah from 1990 to 1992.  He served as a judge on the Utah Supreme Court from 1992 to 1998, and on the United States District Court for the District of Utah from 1998 to 2006. He was nominated by President George W. Bush to the Tenth Circuit on November 1, 2005, and was confirmed by the Senate on December 14, 2005.
