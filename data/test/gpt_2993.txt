François Eugène Alfred Évanturel (August 31, 1846 – 1919) was a French composer and pianist.  Born in Paris, Évanturel studied at the Paris Conservatoire with François-Joseph Fétis and Jules Massenet. He won first prize in 1867 for his cantata "Le départ", and second prize in 1872 for his piano concerto. He also composed chamber music, songs, orchestral works, and operas. Évanturel collaborated with several leading composers of his time, including Ambroise Thomas, Camille Saint-Saëns, Hector Berlioz, and Georges Bizet.  Évanturel's most famous works are the operas "L'Enfant et les sortilèges" (1896) and "Les Huguenots" (1902). He also wrote several ballets, including "Le Corsaire" (1883), "Pas de deux" (1892), and "Les Sylphides" (1896). Évanturel was awarded the Légion d'honneur in 1901. He died in Paris in 1919.
