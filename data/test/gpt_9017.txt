The Altar is the second studio album by American rock band Alt-J. It was released on 25 January 2015 through Infectious Records.  The album was produced by Alt-J's previous producer, Paul Epworth, who also mixed the album alongside Toby Jones. The band wrote and recorded the album in various studios in London and Durham over a period of 18 months. The album's lyrical themes include religion, love, and loss.  "The Altar" received generally positive reviews from music critics. At Metacritic, which assigns a normalized rating out of 100 to reviews from mainstream publications, the album received an average score of 77, which indicates "generally favorable reviews".
