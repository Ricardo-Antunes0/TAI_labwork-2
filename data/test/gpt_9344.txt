The Ebert test gauges whether a computer-based film review is a fair and objective assessment. It was created by Roger Ebert in 1985.  The Ebert test gauges whether a computer-based film review is a fair and objective assessment. The test consists of 10 questions, with the answers being either "true" or "false." The questions are as follows:  1. Was the film well made? 2. Was the film interesting? 3. Was the film worth seeing? 4. Was the acting good? 5. Was the cinematography good? 6. Was the plot interesting? 7. Was the director skillful? 8. Were the themes well developed? 9. Was the ending satisfying? 10. Was the film appropriate for its genre?
