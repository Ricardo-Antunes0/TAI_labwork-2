Bertha Bay-Sa Pan is a Taiwanese-American Director, Producer, and Actor.  Bertha Bay-Sa Pan was born on October 9, 1966, in Taichung, Taiwan. Growing up, Bay-Sa Pan excelled in both academics and athletics. He was a talented high school student and captain of the varsity soccer team.  Bay-Sa Pan attended the University of California, Berkeley, where he studied Political Science and Economics. After graduating in 1988, he moved to New York City to pursue a career in acting.  Bay-Sa Pan's first major role came in the television series "New York Undercover". He played a character named Chi Chen. He also appeared in the television series "Hill Street Blues" and "The X-Files".  Bay-Sa Pan's breakthrough role came in the 1995 film "The Hand That Rocks the Cradle". He played the role of Johnny Wang. The film was awarded the Grand Jury Prize at the 1995 Sundance Film Festival. Bay-Sa Pan went on to appear in several successful films, including "The American President", "The Talented Mr. Ripley", and "The Fast and The Furious".  Bay-Sa Pan has also directed several films, including "Naked Truth" (2000), "Redemption" (2002), and "To All the Boys I've Loved Before" (2018).
