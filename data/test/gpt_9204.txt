Claudi Arimany i Barceló (born in Granollers, Catalonia, on October 19, 1971) is a Spanish singer, songwriter, and actor. He rose to prominence in the early 1990s as the lead singer of the pop group La Oreja de Van Gogh. Arimany has since released several solo albums and starred in several successful films.  Born into a musical family, Arimany began his career as a singer with La Oreja de Van Gogh in 1991. The group enjoyed success in Spain and throughout Latin America, but their biggest achievement came when they were chosen to represent Spain in the Eurovision Song Contest 1998 with the song "El pueblo", which finished in third place. Arimany subsequently pursued a solo career, releasing several successful albums. He has also appeared in several successful films, most notably the 2002 adaptation of Pedro Almodóvar's film Volver, for which he received critical acclaim. Arimany is currently pursuing a acting career, appearing in the 2016 drama series El juego de la vida.
