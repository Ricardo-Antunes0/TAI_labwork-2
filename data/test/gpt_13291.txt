Frederick Tibbott was born on December 11, 1885, in Troy, New York. He received his undergraduate degree from Columbia University in 1907 and his doctorate from the University of Leipzig in 1912. Tibbott taught at Clark University for a year before joining the faculty at Princeton University in 1915. He served as the chair of the Psychology Department at Princeton from 1942 to 1956.  Tibbott is considered to be the father of Gestalt psychology. His work in this area focused on the concept of pattern and how it can be seen in everyday life. Tibbott also made significant contributions to cognitive psychology and developmental psychology. He died on November 12, 1965.
