Hedyscepe canterburyana, the big mountain palm or Wellingtonia, is a species of palm native to New Zealand.  The Wellingtonia tree grows to a height of around 30 m and has a trunk diameter of up to 2 m. The trunk is fluted and covered in short, sharp spines. The leaves are large, up to 30 cm long and 10 cm broad, and are arranged in decussate pairs at the base of the stem. The inflorescence is a panicle of up to 30 flowers, each about 5 cm long and 3 cm wide. The flowers are white with a reddish tinge, and have five petals which are joined at their bases to form a tube. The fruit is a spherical maturing brown fruit which measures up to 8 cm in diameter.
