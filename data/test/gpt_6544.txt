Kathleen O'Meara, also known under her pen name of K.O. McCarthy, is an American author and essayist. She has written three books of essays, one novel and several short stories. O'Meara's work has been featured in The New York Times, The Boston Globe, The Guardian, NPR, and many other publications.  Kathleen O'Meara was born in San Francisco in 1964 to an Irish-Catholic family. She attended Notre Dame High School in San Francisco. After graduating from high school, she studied at Bryn Mawr College before transferring to the University of California, Berkeley, where she received her BA in English in 1986.  O'Meara's first book of essays, "The Things They Carried" (2002), won the National Book Critics Circle Award for Nonfiction. Her second book of essays, "Less Than Zero" (2005), was also a National Book Critics Circle Award nominee and won the Los Angeles Times Book Prize for Criticism. Her third book of essays, "Bad Blood" (2009), was also a National Book Critics Circle Award nominee and won the Chicago Tribune Book Prize for Criticism.  O'Meara's novel, "The Girl with All the Gifts" (2013), was published to wide acclaim and became a worldwide bestseller. It was later made into a feature film starring Noomi Rapace and Glenn Close.
