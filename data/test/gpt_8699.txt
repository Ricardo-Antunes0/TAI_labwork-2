Michael Danan Henry (born 1939) is an American author, poet, and translator.  Henry was born in 1939 in New York City. After graduating from the City College of New York in 1961, Henry worked as a research analyst for the Federal Communications Commission. He began writing poetry in the early 1960s and has since published eleven collections of poetry. His work has been published in numerous magazines and anthologies, including The Best American Poetry, The Norton Anthology of Poetry, The Best American Short Stories, and The Pushcart Prize: Best of the Small Presses. In addition, Henry has been awarded a Guggenheim Fellowship and a Lannan Foundation Award. He lives in New York City.
