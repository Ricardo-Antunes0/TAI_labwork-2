Henri La Fontaine (; 22 April 1854 – 9 November 1913) was a French author, poet, dramatist and journalist. He is best known for his Fables (1881), which are among the most widely read works of French literature.  Henri La Fontaine was born in Châtillon-sur-Seine, Essonne, on 22 April 1854. He was the son of a watchmaker. He received a basic education and then studied law at the University of Paris. In 1876, he published his first work, a collection of poems entitled "Le Romancier".  In 1880, La Fontaine published his most famous work, the Fables. The book is made up of short stories grouped according to animals, with a moral or lesson to be learned at the end. The Fables have been translated into dozens of languages and have sold over 150 million copies worldwide.  La Fontaine died on 9 November 1913 in Paris.
