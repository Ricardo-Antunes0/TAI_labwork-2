The Montana State Library Is the official repository for the history, culture, and natural sciences of the state of Montana. The Library collects, preserves, and provides access to materials that document Montana's history, its people and their culture, and the natural world.  The Montana State Library was founded in 1885 as the territorial library of Montana. In 1893, it became a branch of the Montana State University Library. In 1909, the library became an independent department within the state department of education. In 1921, it was renamed the Montana State Library and Archives. In 1957, it was transferred to the newly created state department of culture, arts and heritage. In 1975, it became an autonomous department within the state government.
