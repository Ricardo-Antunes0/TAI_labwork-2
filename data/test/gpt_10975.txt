Nekade Vo Nokta (in Macedonian Cyrillic: „Некаде Во Нокта“, meaning "The Night Falls") is a 2004 Macedonian drama film directed by Goran Markovski. The film was selected as the Macedonian entry for the Best Foreign Language Film at the 77th Academy Awards, but was not accepted as a nominee.  The film tells the story of a young couple, Ljube and Maja, who are trying to make their way in life. Maja is pregnant and they are living in poverty with their young son. Ljube starts working at a construction site, but his salary is not enough to support them. Maja becomes increasingly desperate and starts prostituting herself to make money. Their lives spiral out of control and they eventually lose everything. The film ends with Ljube and Maja committing suicide.   Nekade Vo Nokta received mostly positive reviews from critics. Review aggregator website Rotten Tomatoes reports that 79% of critics gave the film a positive review, based on 26 reviews.
