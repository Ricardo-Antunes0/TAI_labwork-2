The  or CPS-3 is an arcade system board from Sega, first released in 1988. It is a modified version of the CP System II arcade board. The CPS-3 features a Motorola 68000 processor and a new graphics chip, the Sega Graphics Processor (SGP). The Sega Mega Drive version of Sonic the Hedgehog 2 was the first game to use the CPS-3.  The CPS-3 was popular in Japan, where it was known as the Sega Mark III. It was also popular in Europe, where it was known as the Mega Drive III. In North America, it was known as the Sega Genesis.
