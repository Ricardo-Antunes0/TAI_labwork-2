Kathryn Felicia Day (born June 28, 1979) is an American actress, writer, producer and director. She is best known for her roles as Charlie Bradbury on the Fox sitcom Hell's Kitchen, Dr. Horrible's Sing-Along Blog and its spin-off Doctor Horrible's Sing-Along Blog: The Series, as well as for her role as Belle on the Disney Channel sitcom The Suite Life of Zack & Cody.  Kathryn Felicia Day was born in Newport Beach, California, to Kathryn (née Cavanaugh) and Christopher Day. She has two sisters, actress Sarah Chowning and writer Zoe Kravitz, and a brother, actor Beau Chowning. Kathryn's father is of English and Irish descent and her mother is of half-Irish and half-Italian ancestry. She was raised in San Francisco, where she attended the American Conservatory Theater.  Day began her acting career at the age of six, appearing in a television commercial for Coca-Cola. She made her feature film debut in the independent film The Education of Charlie Bradbury (1998), for which she received a nomination for Best Young Actress from the Independent Spirit Awards. Her breakout role came in the Fox sitcom Hell's Kitchen, for which she appeared from 2003 to 2007. Day also starred in the short-lived but critically acclaimed sitcom Dr. Horrible's Sing-Along Blog (2008–09), for which she received an Emmy Award nomination for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Comedy Series.
