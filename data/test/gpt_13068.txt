Apoch'Waal was an 8th-century Maya lakam, or political organization. It was founded in 785 and lasted until 810.  Apoch'Waal was one of the largest lakams in the Maya Lowlands, with a membership of around 1,500. Its capital was at Palenque.   The lakam controlled a number of important temples, including the El Castillo at Chichén Itzá, and managed trade routes between the Maya cities. Its members were mainly nobles and warriors, and it was responsible for defending the kingdom against its enemies.
