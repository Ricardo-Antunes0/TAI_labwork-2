Michael A. Hicks (born 1948) is an American historian and academic. He is the John W. Pope Distinguished Professor of History at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill.  Hicks specializes in the history of the American South, with a focus on the Civil War and Reconstruction. He has written numerous books, including "The Confederate Republic: A History of the South during Reconstruction" (2008), "Lincoln's Emancipation Proclamation" (2012), and "A New South? The Emergence of a Southern Middle Class, 1865-1900" (2016).  Hicks was elected a Fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences in 2013.
