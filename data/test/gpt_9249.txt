Braith Anastasakis , commonly known as Braith, (born November 11, 1989) is an American soccer player who currently plays as a midfielder for the Vancouver Whitecaps in Major League Soccer.  Anasta was born in Santa Monica, California and attended Santa Monica High School. He played college soccer at UCLA, where he was a two-time All-American and was a member of the NCAA Men's Soccer Championship team in 2008.  Anasta was drafted by D.C. United in the first round (6th overall) of the 2009 MLS SuperDraft. He made his debut for United on June 6, 2009 against Chicago Fire, coming on as a substitute for Jaime Moreno in the 71st minute. Anasta scored his first professional goal on September 2, 2009 against Houston Dynamo.  On December 10, 2012, Anasta was traded to Portland Timbers in exchange for allocation money and a conditional draft pick in the 2013 MLS SuperDraft.  On March 6, 2016, Anasta was traded to Vancouver Whitecaps FC in exchange for Andrew Jacobson and a third-round pick in the 2017 MLS SuperDraft.
