Viliame Waqaseduadua (born 23 April 1983 in Levuka, Fiji) is a Fijian rugby union player. He played for the Fiji national team in the 2007 Rugby World Cup and has also played for the New Zealand national team.  Waqaseduadua was born in 1983 in Levuka, Fiji. He attended Viti Levu College before moving to New Zealand to further his rugby career. Waqaseduadua played for the Wellington Saints in the ITM Cup and made his debut for the New Zealand national team in 2002 against Canada. He then went on to play for the Crusaders and the Highlanders in Super Rugby, as well as representing Fiji at international level. Waqaseduadua made his World Cup debut for Fiji in their opening game of the 2007 tournament against Romania, and went on to play in all of their matches during the tournament.
