The Teatro San Cassiano (or Teatro di San Cassiano) is a theatre in the city of Pisa, Italy.   The theatre was built in 1598 by the architect Agostino Cornacchia. It is one of the oldest surviving theatres in Italy. The theatre underwent a restoration in the early 1990s. The theatre can seat around 800 people.  Theatre productions at the Teatro San Cassiano include Shakespeare's plays "Romeo and Juliet" and "Hamlet", as well as works by Carlo Goldoni, Pietro Mascagni, and Giuseppe Verdi.
