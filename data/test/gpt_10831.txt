is a disaster novel by Japanese writer Haruki Murakami, originally serialized in Japan's "Asahi Shimbun" newspaper from 2006 to 2007. The novel tells the story of a group of Japanese tourists who journey to the town of Ishinomaki, located on the northeast coast of Japan, in the aftermath of a massive earthquake and tsunami that has caused widespread destruction.  The novel was published in Japan in three volumes between October 2006 and January 2007 by Kodansha. A French translation by Jacques Roubaud was published in January 2008 by Gallimard. A Spanish translation by José Joaquín Fernández was published in November 2009 by Tusquets.  The English translation by Philip Gabriel was published by Knopf Canada in September 2010.  A film adaptation directed by Takashi Miike and starring Tadanobu Asano, Koyuki Arima, Masahiro Motoki, and Rinko Kikuchi was released in Japan on September 1, 2013.
