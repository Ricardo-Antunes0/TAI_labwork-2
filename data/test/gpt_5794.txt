Santosh Saroj is a Bollywood screenwriter and film director.  Saroj was born in Mumbai, Maharashtra, India in 1967. He studied at the University of Mumbai, where he received a B.A. in English Literature and Literature from 1987 to 1990.  Saroj's writing career began in the early 1990s with the short story collection "The Invisible Hand". His first screenplay was for the 1997 Bollywood film "Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge", which won him the National Film Award for Best Screenplay. He went on to write screenplays for several successful films, including "Kuch Kuch Hota Hai" (1998), "Lagaan" (2001), "Happy New Year" (2006), and "3 Idiots" (2009).  In 2006, Saroj made his directorial debut with the romantic drama "Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge". The film was a critical and commercial success, and won Saroj the National Film Award for Best Director. He followed up the success of "Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge" with the 2009 comedy-drama "3 Idiots", which also received critical acclaim and became one of the highest-grossing Indian films of all time. Saroj has since directed several other critically acclaimed films, including "Queen" (2012) and "Tubelight" (2016).  Saroj is married to actress Shruti Haasan. They have two sons.
