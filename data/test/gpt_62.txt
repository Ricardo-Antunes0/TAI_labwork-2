Polyhymno intorta is a moth of the family Gelechiidae. It was described by Meyrick in 1913. It is found in India (Assam).  The wingspan is about 10Â mm. The forewings are light grey, with a narrow elongate dark streak along the costa from one-fourth to three-fourths, reaching two-thirds across the wing, and a short dark dash near the base before the tornus. There is an irregularly placed dark spot on the costa before the middle, and a suffused cloudy dark dot on the costa beyond it. The hindwings are light grey, with a broad irregularly shaped dark area from two-thirds of the costa to the dorsum before the tornus, and a suffused cloudy darker dot on the dorsum beyond it.
