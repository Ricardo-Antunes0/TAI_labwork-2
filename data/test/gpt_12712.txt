Chuan Leekpai (, , ; ; born 12 March 1952) is a Thai business magnate and politician who has been the Prime Minister of Thailand since 2014.  Chuan was born in 1952 in Khon Kaen Province, northeastern Thailand. He is the son of Chuan Leekpai, a former Prime Minister of Thailand. Chuan completed his undergraduate studies at Khon Kaen University in 1974 and his graduate studies at the University of London in 1976.  Chuan started his business career in 1976, working for Thai Airways International. He later founded the state-owned CPP Group, which he led until he was appointed as Deputy Prime Minister and Finance Minister in 2001 under Prime Minister Thaksin Shinawatra. In 2006, Chuan was appointed as Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Industry and Commerce. He was then made Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Energy in 2007. In 2009, he was appointed as Deputy Prime Minister and Minister for Public Utilities and Transport. He replaced Yingluck Shinawatra, who had been dismissed by the military after months of protests against her government.  Chuan is a member of the United Front for Democracy Against Dictatorship (UDD) political party.  Chuan is married to Pao Lekpai and has two children.
