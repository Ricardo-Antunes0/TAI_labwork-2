Arthur Lewis Nathan Walter (27 September 1873 – 12 December 1953) was an Australian politician, who represented the Labor Party in the Australian House of Representatives from 1922 to 1943. He was Minister for Defence in the First and Second Curtin Governments.  Walter was born on 27 September 1873 at Taree, New South Wales, to farmer Arthur Walter and his wife Elizabeth (née Lewis). He attended public schools in Taree and Sydney before becoming a clerk in the Department of Lands and Works. In 1899 he married Caroline Mayhew; the couple had one son and one daughter. In 1906 he became secretary of the Taree branch of the Labor Party.  From 1909 to 1922, Walter was a member of the New South Wales Legislative Assembly for the seat of Hunter. In 1922 he was elected to the Australian House of Representatives as the Labor member for Newcastle, defeating sitting Nationalist MP Herbert Vere Harcourt. He held Newcastle until 1943, when he retired from politics due to ill health. Walter died on 12 December 1953 at Sydney's Royal Prince Alfred Hospital.
