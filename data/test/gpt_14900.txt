The Lucille Lortel Theatre is an off-Broadway theatre located at 315 West 43rd Street in Manhattan, New York City. Named after the actress Lucille Lortel, it is one of the oldest and most prestigious Off-Broadway theatres.  Founded in 1927 by producer George Abbott and director Harold Clurman, it was originally known as the Lafayette Theatre. It opened with a production of Lillian Hellman's "The Children's Hour". The theatre has been home to a variety of productions, including plays by George Bernard Shaw, Tennessee Williams, and Eugene O'Neill. In 1981, the Lucille Lortel Theatre was designated a New York City Landmark.
