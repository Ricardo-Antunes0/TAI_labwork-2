James W. Kincaid (October 23, 1934 – July 2, 2017) was an American journalist and author who was a national correspondent for the Los Angeles Times.  Kincaid was known for his investigative reporting and his work on the Iran–Contra affair. He also wrote a number of books on U.S. history, including "The Unfinished Revolution: The Reagan Legacy" (2000) and "A Time to Speak: The Untold Story of Martin Luther King Jr." (2003). Kincaid was a finalist for the Pulitzer Prize in 1991 for his reporting on the Iran–Contra affair.  Kincaid was born in 1934 in New York City to James Kincaid, a printer, and his wife, the former Frances Reid. He attended DeWitt Clinton High School in the Bronx and then earned a bachelor's degree from Harvard University in 1957. After serving in the U.S. Air Force from 1955 to 1957, he began his career as a journalist at The Providence Journal in Rhode Island before moving to Los Angeles in 1960.  Kincaid was a national correspondent for the Los Angeles Times from 1967 to 1995, when he retired from journalism. He also wrote a number of books on U.S. history, including "The Unfinished Revolution: The Reagan Legacy" (2000), "A Time to Speak: The Untold Story of Martin Luther King Jr." (2003), and "The Boys on the Bus: Nine Americans Who Made History" (2006).
