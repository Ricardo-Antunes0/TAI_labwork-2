The Bartel BM.4 was a Polish biplane fighter aircraft that saw service during World War II. It was a development of the Bartel BM.3, which had been produced in small numbers by the Bartel factory.  The Bartel BM.4 was a single-seat biplane fighter aircraft. It was armed with a forward-firing machine gun and had a maximum speed of 305 km/h (190 mph). The Bartel BM.4 served with the Polish Air Force in the early stages of World War II, but saw little action due to its low speed and poor performance.
