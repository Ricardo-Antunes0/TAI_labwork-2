Folklore is the body of culture shared by a group of people, typically through oral tradition. Folklore encompasses the traditions of storytelling, music, dance, art, cuisine and other cultural expressions.  Folklore is a repository of traditional knowledge and a vital source of historical information. It has been used to study social interaction, religion, culture and society. It is also used as a resource for creating new cultural expressions. Folklore is often an oral tradition, and can be passed down through generations without being written down.  Folklore is dynamic and evolves over time. It can be used to reflect upon the past and to create new traditions. Folklore can also be used to explore various aspects of the human experience. Folklore is often associated with specific geographical areas or cultures, but it can be found anywhere there are people who share a common culture.
