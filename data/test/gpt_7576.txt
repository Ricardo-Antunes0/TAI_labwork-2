The Ramsay Scallop is a young adult sea snail, a marine gastropod mollusk in the family Pectinidae.  The Ramsay Scallop was first described by J.D. Ramsay in 1892. The type locality is off the Isle of Skye. It is a common and widespread species found in European waters and the Mediterranean Sea.  The Ramsay Scallop feeds on algae and sponges. It can be found on muddy or sandy bottoms at depths of between 0 and 100 metres.
