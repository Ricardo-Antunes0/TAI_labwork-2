In Greek mythology, Diocles (Ancient Greek: Διοκλῆς) was a statesman, orator, and general who flourished in the reign of Philip II of Macedonia. He was the son of Damon and Pythias.  Diocles was noted for his wisdom and eloquence. He served as a general during the Macedonian Wars and fought against the Persians at the Battle of Plataea. After the death of Philip II, Diocles unsuccessfully attempted to seize power. He died in exile in Thebes.
