Adam Benjamin Jr. (August 6, 1935 – May 6, 2016) was an American actor and singer.  Born in Brooklyn, New York, Benjamin began his career in the late 1950s as a child actor on Broadway. He made his motion picture debut in the 1963 comedy film The Interns. Over the following decades, he appeared in such films as The Great Santini (1971), The Fortune Cookie (1972), The Sunshine Boys (1975), and Coming to America (1988). He also had recurring roles on television series such as M*A*S*H (1970–1983), Cheers (1982–1993), The Golden Girls (1985–1992), and Sisters (1989–2000). In later years, Benjamin toured the United States performing stage shows. He died at the age of 82 in Beverly Hills, California.
