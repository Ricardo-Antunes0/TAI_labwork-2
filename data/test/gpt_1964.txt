Trinant is a village located in the commune of Saint-Martin-des-Champs, in the Aisne department of northern France. It lies on the D921 road, about 1 km north of the village of Saint-Martin-des-Champs and about 3 km south of the village of Péronne.  The village of Trinant is part of the ancient commune of Saint-Martin-des-Champs. The commune was formed in 1801 by merging the two villages of Trinant and Péronne.   The village is located in the middle of a cornfield, at an elevation of 135 metres above sea level. The commune has a population of around 270 people.   The economy is based on agriculture, with a significant part devoted to cereal crops. There is also a small livestock sector, notably pigs and poultry. There are also a number of small businesses involved in trade and services.
