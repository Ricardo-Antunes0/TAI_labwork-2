Eight ships of the Royal Navy have been named Newcastle, after the city of Newcastle upon Tyne in North East England.  The first HMS Newcastle was a 74-gun third-rate ship of the line of the Royal Navy, launched on 13 October 1759 at Pembroke Dockyard. She was wrecked on the coast of Cartagena, Spain, in 1762.  The second HMS Newcastle was a 64-gun fourth-rate ship of the line launched on 12 June 1776 at Pembroke Dockyard. She was wrecked off the coast of Gibraltar on 30 April 1782.  The third HMS Newcastle was a 74-gun third-rate ship of the line launched on 5 January 1793 at Portsmouth Dockyard. She was wrecked on 10 May 1805 off the coast of Portugal.  The fourth HMS Newcastle was a 64-gun fourth-rate ship of the line launched on 26 January 1814 at Portsmouth Dockyard. She served in the Mediterranean and participated in the Battle of Waterloo on 18 June 1815. The fifth HMS Newcastle was a 90-gun fifth-rate ship of the line launched on 2 February 1831 at Chatham Dockyard. She collided with HMS Alacrity during her maiden voyage and sank with loss of life on 22 July 1831.
