The Remington Model 798 is a bolt-action rifle manufactured by Remington Arms Company in the United States from 1962 to 2000. It was Remington's flagship rifle for over 20 years and is still popular among hunting and target shooters.  The Remington Model 798 was designed as a cost-effective replacement for the Remington Model 700. It features a simpler design, a shorter barrel, and is available in a variety of calibers. The Model 798 is also popular among law enforcement and military users for its durability and accuracy.
