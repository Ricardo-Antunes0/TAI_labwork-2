Eucalyptus longirostrata, commonly known as grey gum, is a eucalypt that is endemic to the south-west of Western Australia. It is a medium-sized tree that can reach up to 30 metres in height and has smooth bark. The leaves are spirally arranged, long and narrow, and are covered in sticky resin. The flowers are white and fragrant, and are produced in clusters at the ends of the branches. The fruit is a globular pod that contains several black seeds.
