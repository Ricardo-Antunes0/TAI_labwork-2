Julius LeVonne Chambers (October 6, 1936 – October 1, 1998) was an American author and philosopher. He was the author of Philosophical Papers (1971), The Quest for Certainty (1978), and The Mind of God (1987).  Chambers was born in Greensboro, North Carolina, the second of four children of Julius Chambers, Sr., an insurance agent, and the former Belle LeVonne Fletcher. Chambers attended the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, where he received a B.A. in 1958. He then attended Yale University, where he received his M.A. in 1961 and his Ph.D. in 1964.  Chambers taught philosophy at the University of California at Irvine from 1966 to 1998. He also served as president of the American Philosophical Association (1996–1997).  Chambers died from pancreatic cancer in Irvine, California, aged 66 years, five months and eleven days
