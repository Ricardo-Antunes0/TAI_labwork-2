Orlando Gregorio Peña Guevara (born November 17, 1981) is a Nicaraguan professional baseball outfielder who is currently a free agent. He has played in Major League Baseball (MLB) for the Tampa Bay Rays, Kansas City Royals, and Oakland Athletics.  Peña was drafted by the Tampa Bay Devil Rays in the first round of the 2000 Major League Baseball Draft. He made his MLB debut with the Rays in 2003. In 2005, he was traded to the Kansas City Royals, where he played until 2007. He then signed with the Oakland Athletics before the 2008 season. He has also played for the Nicaragua national baseball team.  Peña was born in Managua, Nicaragua, to Orlando Peña and Carmen Guevara. He has two brothers, Orlando and Roberto, and a sister, Evelyn. Peña played for José Ramón Sandoval's youth team and then attended Pedro Martínez High School in Managua.  In 2001, while playing for José Ramón Sandoval's youth team, Peña hit .433 with six home runs in 33 games. He then attended Pedro Martínez High School and hit .449 with 16 home runs and 76 RBIs in 53 games his senior year. In 2000, he was drafted by the Tampa Bay Devil Rays in the first round of the Major League Baseball Draft.  Peña made his MLB debut with the Rays on May 6, 2003 at age 21. He recorded his first hit that day against Tampa Bay's Dan Wheeler.
