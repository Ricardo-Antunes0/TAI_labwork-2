Corinna Schnitt (born in 1964) is a German artist and art critic who lives and works in Berlin. Schnitt is best known for her paintings, which are typically colorful and abstract, and her performance art, which often focuses on the body as medium.  Schnitt was born in the city of Wuppertal in 1964. She studied painting at the Kunstakademie Düsseldorf from 1984 to 1988, and at the University of Applied Arts Vienna from 1988 to 1990. Schnitt has exhibited her work in Germany, Austria, Poland, Italy, Spain, Switzerland and Belgium. She has been the recipient of numerous awards, including the Berlin Prize (1996), the Künstlerhaus Schloss Solitude Prize (2001), and the Friedrich-Naumann-Preis (2006).
