Pegula is the widow of Terry Pegula, who co-founded the Buffalo Sabres of the National Hockey League (NHL). The Pegulas are the owners of the Buffalo Sabres, and also own the Buffalo Bills of the National Football League (NFL).  Pegula was born in Lewiston, Maine. After her mother died when she was nine, Pegula was raised by her father. She attended Maine South High School and then transferred to Bates College in Lewiston. After she graduated from Bates in 1973 with a degree in economics, she moved to Buffalo to work as an assistant manager at a local department store.  In 1978, Pegula married Terry Pegula. Together they founded WESCO Energy, a regional energy company that became one of the largest electric utilities in central and southern New York State. The Pegulas sold WESCO Energy to First Niagara Corporation in 2002 for US$2.3 billion.  Since 2002, Kim Pegula has been involved in a number of philanthropic endeavors. These include donations to community organizations such as Make-A-Wish Foundation, Second Harvest Food Bank, and United Way of Western New York; support for women's sports; and investments in renewable energy sources, such as wind and solar power.
