The Probationer's Catechism, also called The Probationer's Creed, is a catechism published in 1648 by the English Puritan clergyman and theologian John Owen. It is a concise exposition of Protestant Christian doctrines, based on the Westminster Confession of Faith.  The Catechism is divided into five sections:   The first section contains a summary of the Ten Commandments. The second section covers the doctrine of justification by faith alone. The third section covers the doctrine of sanctification. The fourth section covers the doctrine of redemption. The fifth and final section covers the doctrine of the atonement.
