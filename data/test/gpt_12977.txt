Winfield Scott Mattraw (October 19, 1880 – March 17, 1966) was an American football and baseball player, coach, and college athletics administrator. He served as the head football coach at the University of Kansas from 1912 to 1919, compiling a record of 18–19–2. Mattraw was also the head baseball coach at Kansas from 1912 to 1917, tallying a record of 93–59.  Mattraw played football for the University of Michigan from 1899 to 1901 and 1903 to 1905, and was captain of the 1903 team. He played professional football for the Canton Bulldogs in 1906 and 1907. Mattraw was the head football coach at Washburn University in 1908 and 1909, tallying a record of 4–5–1. He served as the athletic director at Kansas from 1915 to 1966.
