Darryl "Slip" Sly (April 3, 1939 – November 4, 1996) was an American jazz pianist and composer.  Darryl Sly was born in New York City on April 3, 1939. He grew up in the Bronx and began playing the piano at the age of six. Sly studied at the Juilliard School of Music, where he was a pupil of Rosina Lhévinne and Darius Milhaud.  Sly's first album, "Piano Dreams", was recorded in 1964 and received critical acclaim. He toured extensively throughout the 1970s and 1980s, often with his own trio or quartet. His later recordings were more experimental, incorporating electronica and world music into his compositions. Sly died in New York City on November 4, 1996.
