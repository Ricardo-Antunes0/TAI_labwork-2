The Beaver River is an intermittent river, located in Oklahoma. It is a tributary of the Canadian River.  The Beaver River originates from the eastern slopes of the Wichita Mountains in Kiowa County, and flows generally southward for about before turning westward and then southward again, joining the Canadian River about southwest of Altus. The river has been stocked with trout by the Oklahoma Department of Wildlife Conservation since the early 1900s. The Beaver River also supports a large population of wild turkeys.
