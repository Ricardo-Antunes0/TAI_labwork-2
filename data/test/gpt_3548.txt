The Olympic Tower (German: ) in the Olympic Park in Berlin, Germany, is the world's tallest free-standing structure. It was built for the 1936 Summer Olympics and is 194.34 metres (619.4 feet) high, making it the highest point in Berlin and the fourth-highest building in Europe.  Construction of the tower began on 1 May 1935 and it was completed on 12 August 1936. The tower was originally named Reichssportpalast (Reich Sports Palace), but was renamed Olympic Tower after the 1936 Summer Olympics.
