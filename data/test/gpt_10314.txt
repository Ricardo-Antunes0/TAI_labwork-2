Kathputli (; ; translation: Puppet) is a 1957 Hindi-language Indian feature film directed by B.R. Chopra and produced by Ratna Pathak Shah. It is considered to be the first Indian movie with an all-Puppet cast and was also the first Indian animated feature film. The story is about a family of puppeteers who are forced to leave their home due to financial difficulties, and travel to different parts of India in search of work.   The film was released on 15 December 1957 and was a major success, becoming one of the highest-grossing films of that year. It won the National Film Award for Best Feature Film in Hindi and the Filmfare Award for Best Feature Film.  Kathputli was followed by two sequels, Kathputli (1960) and Kathputli (1963).
