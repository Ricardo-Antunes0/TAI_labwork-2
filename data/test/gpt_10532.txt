Oscar Elton Sette (March 29, 1900 - March 16, 1979) was an American jazz bassist and composer.  Sette was born in Omaha, Nebraska, and studied music at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln. He played in local bands before moving to New York City in 1922. There he played with a range of musicians, including Benny Goodman and Duke Ellington. He recorded prolifically as a leader, and his compositions include the tunes "Woody 'n' You" and "The Maiden's Prayer". Sette died in 1979.
