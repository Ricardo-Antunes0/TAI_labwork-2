John Rockefeller Prentice (December 17, 1902 – March 9, 1977) was an American economist and political scientist.  He was born in Cleveland, Ohio, to John D. Rockefeller Jr. and Abby Aldrich Rockefeller. He received his A.B. from Harvard University in 1923 and his Ph.D. from the University of Chicago in 1928. After teaching at the University of Chicago and Yale University, he became a professor of economics at Princeton University in 1948. He served as president of the American Economic Association (1955–1956) and the National Academy of Sciences (1967–1968).  Prentice was also a prolific author on economic topics, writing more than twenty books and numerous articles. He is most famous for his work on general equilibrium theory and for his development of the theory of public goods.
