George McWhirter (born September 26, 1939 in Kansas City, Missouri) is an American jazz bassist and composer. He is perhaps best known for his work with the Jazz Crusaders and for his collaborations with Miles Davis.  George McWhirter was born in 1939 in Kansas City, Missouri. He began playing bass at the age of fourteen, studying with local bassists such as Howie Smith and Johnny Griffin. In the early 1960s, McWhirter moved to New York City, where he joined the Jazz Crusaders. He played with the group until 1971, during which time they recorded several acclaimed albums.  McWhirter then moved to California, where he played with a variety of groups including the Miles Davis Quintet and the Bill Evans Trio. He also collaborated with a number of prominent jazz artists, including John Coltrane, Sonny Rollins, and Stan Getz. McWhirter retired from music in the early 1990s, but has since resumed performing. He is currently based in Los Angeles.  McWhirter has received numerous awards and accolades throughout his career, including a Grammy Award nomination for Best Jazz Performance by a Duo or Group with Vinnie Colaiuta in 1989. He has also been cited as one of the most influential bassists of his generation.
