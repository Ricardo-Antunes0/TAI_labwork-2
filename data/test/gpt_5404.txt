Avoth Yeshurun (1904–1992; Hebrew אבות ישורון, born Abraham Auerbach) was a major Israeli poet and one of the founders of the Israeli Poetry Movement.  Avoth Yeshurun was born in 1904 in the town of Sremski Karlovci, then part of the Austro-Hungarian Empire. He studied law at the University of Vienna and then moved to Palestine in 1933, where he worked as a lawyer. In 1935, he published his first book of poetry, Be-Shalom Aleichem (In Peace, Aleichem). In 1947, he co-founded the Kibbutz Ein Harod with Yitzhak Rabin and Yehuda Amichai. He served as its first chairman and remained a member of its board until his death.  Yeshurun was one of the founders of the Israeli Poetry Movement and served as its first chairman. His work is characterized by its close connection to Hebrew culture and its use of traditional Hebrew forms and rhythms. He also wrote plays and children's books. Yeshurun died in 1992.
