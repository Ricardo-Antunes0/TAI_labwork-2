Robert John McGhee (born 1941) is a Canadian archaeologist who has made significant contributions to the understanding of pre-Columbian cultures in the Americas. McGhee is particularly noted for his work on the Mississippian culture, which he has written about extensively.  McGhee was born in 1941 in New Glasgow, Nova Scotia. He received a B.A. from Acadia University in 1963 and a Ph.D. from the University of Pennsylvania in 1968. After completing his doctoral studies, he worked as an archaeologist with the Provincial Museum of Newfoundland and Labrador until 1974, when he became an assistant professor at the University of South Carolina. In 1984, he moved to the University of Arizona, where he remains a professor emeritus.  McGhee's work on the Mississippian culture has been particularly influential. He is the author of several books on the subject, including "The Mississippian Culture" (1976), "The Ancient Peoples of Middle America" (1981), and "The Ancient Peoples of North America" (1992). He has also written numerous articles for archaeological journals, and has been a contributor to various encyclopedias and other reference works.  McGhee has twice been nominated for the prestigious AIA Award for Architecture, and he has been awarded honorary degrees from several universities. In 2002, he was appointed a Fellow of the Royal Society of Canada.
