Jean Jacques Émile Cheysson (Nîmes, 18 May 1907 – Paris, 6 October 1977) was a French composer, pianist, and conductor.  Cheysson was born in Nîmes, Gard, the son of a music teacher. He studied with his father and at the Conservatoire de Paris. His early works, including orchestral pieces and chamber music, were influenced by Claude Debussy and Maurice Ravel. He became known internationally for his film scores in the 1940s, including La Belle et la Bête and The Loneliness of the Long Distance Runner. In the 1960s he began writing choral works and opera. He died in Paris in 1977.
