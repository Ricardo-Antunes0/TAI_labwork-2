Gretchen Lynn Kalonji (born April 13, 1953) is an American astronomer. She is a professor of astronomy at the University of California, Berkeley, where she also holds the William Mather and Sally Ride chairs in space sciences and technology. Kalonji is best known for her work on infrared and submillimeter astronomy, particularly on the study of galaxies and the early universe.  Born in Los Angeles, Kalonji received her undergraduate degree from Yale University in 1975 and her graduate degree from UC Berkeley in 1979. After postdoctoral study at MIT, she joined the faculty at UC Berkeley. In 2006, she was elected to the National Academy of Sciences.
