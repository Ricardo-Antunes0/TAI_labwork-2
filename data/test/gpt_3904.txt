Jeong Hyeon-Cheol (hangul: 정현철; born February 21, 1968), better known by his stage name Seo Taiji, is a South Korean rapper, songwriter and record producer. He is considered one of the most influential and respected figures in the Korean hip hop industry, having helped to pioneer and define the genre with his influential early work in the early 1990s. He is also known for his innovative use of sampling in his music.  Born in Seoul, South Korea, Seo began rapping at the age of 15 and formed the duo Deux with rapper Park Kyung-Jae. After Deux disbanded in 1992, Seo began working with producer JYP Entertainment and released his debut album, Life Is Good, in 1993. The album was a critical and commercial success, selling over a million copies and spawning several hit singles including "I Just Wanna Dance With Somebody (Who Loves Me)", "Crazy Crazy" and "Dancing on My Own". Seo became one of Korea's most popular rappers and was frequently featured on TV shows and in magazines.  In 1995, Seo released his second album Come into My Room, which was even more successful than Life Is Good. The album spawned the successful singles "Go Go" and "Touch My Body", both of which became staples of Korean pop music.
