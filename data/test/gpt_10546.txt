Baarle is a village whose territory is situated in the Dutch province of North Brabant. It has a population of about 2,000 people. Baarle is situated on the river Meuse, about 10 kilometers east of the city of Antwerp.  Baarle was first mentioned in a charter of Emperor Otto I dated 955. At that time it belonged to the Abbey of Saint-Vincent in Tournai. In 1256, Baarle passed to the Bishopric of Li√®ge. In 1352, it came under the rule of the Duchy of Burgundy. In 1477, it became part of the County of Hainaut.  In 1678, Baarle passed to the Netherlands as part of the Treaty of Nijmegen. In 1795, it was annexed by France as a result of the War of the First Coalition. The Dutch retook Baarle in 1798 and it remained part of the Dutch Republic until 1814. Between 1830 and 1833, it was part of Belgium.  Baarle is home to two museums: Museum De Meuse and Museum De Prinsengracht.
