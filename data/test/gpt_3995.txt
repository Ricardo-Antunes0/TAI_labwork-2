Peter Joseph "Pete" Shellem (6 October 1960, Cleveland, Ohio) is an American composer and musician.  Shellem was born in Cleveland, Ohio to a musical family. His father, Joseph Shellem, was a classical violinist and his mother, Paula (n√©e Pasternack), was a pianist. Shellem began composing at the age of six and studied composition with Daniel Pemberton at the Cleveland Institute of Music. He also studied with Michael Daugherty and David Diamond at the University of Michigan.  Shellem's compositions have been performed by ensembles such as the Cleveland Orchestra, New York Philharmonic, National Symphony Orchestra, Boston Symphony Orchestra, Stuttgart Radio Symphony Orchestra, and Rotterdam Philharmonic. He has also recorded works for the Nonesuch Records label.  Shellem has been a professor of composition at the University of Michigan since 1995.
