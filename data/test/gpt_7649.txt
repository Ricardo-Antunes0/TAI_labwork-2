Michael J. Harder (born February 8, 1973) is an American politician and lawyer who is the U.S. Representative for California's 50th congressional district, serving since 2019. A Democrat, he previously served as the California State Attorney General from 2011 to 2019.  Harder was born in Sacramento, California, the son of Kathleen (née O'Connor) and John Harder, who both worked as attorneys. He earned a B.A. in political science from the University of San Francisco in 1996 and a J.D. from Stanford Law School in 2000. He then worked as an attorney with the Sacramento law firm of Morrison & Foerster before entering politics.  In 2002, Harder was elected to the Sacramento City Council where he served until 2008. In 2008, he was elected to the State Senate, serving until 2011 when he was elected California State Attorney General by defeating incumbent Bill Lockyer. As Attorney General, Harder led the Department of Justice’s civil rights division and oversaw all criminal prosecutions by the state’s attorney general’s office.   In 2018, Harder announced his candidacy for Congress in California's 50th congressional district, which is currently held by Republican Jeff Denham. He won the Democratic primary on June 5, 2018, defeating musician John Cleese and business executive Ami Bera.
