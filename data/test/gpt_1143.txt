Anatoly Ilyich Bibilov (; , ; born 6 May 1956) is a Russian politician who was the Prime Minister of Russia from 2012 to 2014. He also served as the Chairman of the State Duma from 2007 to 2012.  Bibilov was born in Leningrad into a working-class family. After completing secondary school in 1975, he worked as an electrician and later as a technical officer at a plant. He married in 1984 and has two children.  Bibilov was elected to the State Duma in 1999, and served as its Chairman from 2007 to 2012. Bibilov was appointed Prime Minister of Russia by President Vladimir Putin on 7 December 2012 following Putin's re-election to another term in office. Bibilov resigned from office on 25 March 2014 following allegations of corruption.
