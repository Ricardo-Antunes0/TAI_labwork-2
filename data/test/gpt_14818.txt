Ted V. Shaneyfelt is an American Computer Scientist and Professor of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Utah. He has made contributions to the fields of computer vision, machine learning, and artificial intelligence.  Shaneyfelt was born in 1954 in San Francisco, California. After completing his undergraduate studies at UC Berkeley, he received his PhD from Cornell University in 1984 under the supervision of David S. Hanley.  After completing his doctoral studies, Shaneyfelt joined the faculty at the University of Utah in 1984 where he has remained since. He is a fellow of the Association for Computing Machinery and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.  Shaneyfelt's research interests include computer vision, machine learning, and artificial intelligence. He has made numerous contributions to these fields, including work on the development of efficient algorithms for object recognition and tracking, and on the design of novel reinforcement learning algorithms. His work on computer vision has helped to develop methods for automatic recognition and annotation of images, as well as for object detection and tracking. His contributions to machine learning include the development of deep neural networks and hierarchical neural networks, as well as novel methods for training these networks. His work on artificial intelligence has focused on the development of generalizable Artificial General Intelligence algorithms.
