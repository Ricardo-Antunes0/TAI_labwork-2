Flavio Ambrosetti (October 8, 1919 in Lugano – February 17, 2000 in Rome) was a Swiss composer and conductor.  Ambrosetti studied violin at the Geneva Conservatory with Marcel Pape and composition with Antal Doráti. After World War II he began his career as a conductor, first with the Zurich Opera and later with the La Scala Opera. In 1958 he became the music director of the Royal Opera House, Covent Garden, a post he held until 1967. He then founded his own opera company, the Teatro dell'Opera di Roma, where he served as music director until 1988. Ambrosetti also made important contributions to symphonic music, chamber music and choral works.  Ambrosetti was awarded the 1996 Nobel Prize in Music for his "unique achievement in contemporary classical music".
