Alice Elizabeth Anderson, born Alecia Elizabeth Foley on July 20, 1984 in Omaha, Nebraska, is an American singer-songwriter. After graduating from high school in 2002, she moved to Austin, Texas and began to play shows.   Her first album, titled Alice Elizabeth Anderson, was released in 2007 and produced by Nathan Chapman. She toured extensively throughout the United States and Europe in support of the album, appearing at festivals such as Coachella and SXSW.   In 2009, Anderson released her second album entitled "The Fall". The album was produced by John Congleton and recorded at his studio in Georgetown, Texas. The album received positive reviews from music critics and featured appearances from notable musicians such as Kurt Vile and St. Vincent.   In 2011, Anderson released her third album entitled "The Dreamer". The album was produced by Dave Cobb and recorded at his studio in Nashville, Tennessee. The album received positive reviews from music critics and featured appearances from notable musicians such as Emmylou Harris and Lucinda Williams.   Anderson's fourth album, "The Voyager", was released in February of 2018. The album was produced by Dave Cobb again, and was recorded at his studio in Nashville, Tennessee. The album has been described as a "return to her roots" and features a more stripped down sound compared to her previous albums.   Anderson has been nominated for two Grammy Awards: Best New Artist in 2009 and Best Country Album in 2013.
