Godfrey Gitahi Kariuki, known as GG Kariuki, was a Kenyan political leader and independence activist who played a leading role in the struggle for Kenyan independence. He served as the first Prime Minister of Kenya from 1963 to 1964, and was a key figure in the country's early years as an independent state.  Kariuki was born in 1922 in the Masai Mara, one of the most remote and rugged parts of British East Africa. He attended school in Nairobi, where he became involved in politics, joining the African National Union (ANU) in 1942. He rose through the ranks of the ANU, becoming its Youth League Secretary in 1946 and its General Secretary in 1949. He was arrested in 1952 for his involvement in riots protesting British colonial rule and sentenced to four years imprisonment.  After his release from prison, Kariuki resumed his political work, becoming the Chairman of the ANU Kisumu branch in 1954. In 1959 he was elected to the Kenyan Legislative Assembly, serving as its Deputy Leader from 1960 until 1962. In 1962 he was appointed Prime Minister after winning a general election that saw his party, Kenya African National Union (KANU), win a majority of seats. As Prime Minister, Kariuki worked to break down British colonial rule and secure Kenya's independence from British rule. He played a leading role in negotiations that led to Kenya's independence on December 12, 1963.  Kariuki served as Prime Minister until 1964, when he resigned due to health problems.
