Wilson was born in Chicago, Illinois, on September 24, 1929. She received her undergraduate degree in philosophy from the University of Chicago in 1952. She then earned her doctorate in linguistics from the University of Pennsylvania in 1957.  After completing her doctorate, Wilson joined the faculty at the University of Connecticut. She became a full professor there in 1978 and remained there until her death in 2006.  Wilson's research focused on language acquisition and cognitive science. She is particularly known for her work on grammar and vocabulary development, as well as for her contributions to the theory of dual processing.  Wilson was also active in the field of education. In 1986, she founded and served as the president of The Barbara Ker Wilson Center for Learning at the University of Connecticut. The center is dedicated to improving educational programs throughout the world.  Wilson was married to philosopher Paul David Wilson from 1957 until his death in 2000. They had two children together.
