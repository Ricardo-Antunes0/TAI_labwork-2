The Caspian lamprey, Caspiomyzon wagneri, is a member of the family Myctophidae. This lamprey is a parasite that lives in the intestines of various fish. It has a long, cylindrical body with a pointed snout and large, protruding eyes. The Caspian lamprey is a common inhabitant of rivers and lakes in western Eurasia, including the Caspian Sea.
