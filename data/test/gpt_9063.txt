The Geelong West Giants, nicknamed the Giants, are an Australian rules football club based in Geelong, Victoria. The club was formed in 2000 as an amalgamation of the Geelong Falcons and the North Ballarat Rebels, and played its first season in the Victorian Football League (VFL).   The Giants have since competed in the Australian Football League (AFL) and Victorian Football League (VFL) competitions, as well as the National Football League (NFL) and United States Football League (USFL) competitions. In 2012, they reached the AFL Grand Final, but were defeated by the Melbourne Storm.   The Giants play their home games at Simonds Stadium, which has a capacity of 22,500 spectators.
