She is the first woman to be elected as both Governor and Senator from Arkansas.  Born in Hope, Arkansas, Huckabee graduated from Hope High School in 1978 and then attended Ouachita Baptist University in Arkadelphia, Arkansas, where she received her baccalaureate degree in 1982. After graduation, she worked as a teacher and then moved to Little Rock, where she became a news reporter for KTHV-TV (NBC), working her way up to become the station's managing editor. Huckabee then became the first female news anchor in the television market.  Huckabee served as the chair of the Arkansas Republican Party from 2004 to 2006. In 2007, she was elected Governor of Arkansas, becoming the first woman to serve as governor of a U.S. state. She was re-elected in 2011, becoming the first woman to be elected to two consecutive terms as governor of Arkansas. As Governor, Huckabee worked on improving the economy by increasing jobs, lowering taxes, and cutting government spending. She also focused on education by reforming public schools and increasing funding for higher education.  In 2013, Huckabee was elected to the United States Senate as a Republican. She is currently serving her first term in office. In 2016, she announced her candidacy for President of the United States in the 2016 election but withdrew from the race
