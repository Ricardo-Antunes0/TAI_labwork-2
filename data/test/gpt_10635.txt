He also won a gold medal at the 1986 World Artistic Gymnastics Championships. Uchimura retired from competition in 2003.  Uchimura was born in Osaka on September 21, 1966. He started gymnastics when he was three years old and became a national champion by the age of eleven. He attended Komazawa University where he studied physical education and sports science.  In 1984, Uchimura made his international debut at the World Artistic Gymnastics Championships in Moscow. He won all of his preliminary events and placed fourth in the all-around final behind Kim Young-nam, Vytautas Šalčiūnas and Paul Hamm. At the 1984 Summer Olympics in Los Angeles, Uchimura became Japan's first ever male Olympic gymnastics medalist when he won silver in the all-around competition behind United States' Greg Louganis.   At the 1988 Summer Olympics in Seoul, Uchimura made history again as Japan's first ever male gymnastics gold medalist when he won the all-around competition ahead of Belarus' Yauheni Hutarovich and Russia's Sergei Bubka. It was also Uchimura's fourth consecutive appearance at an Olympic Games and his final Olympics until the 2000 Summer Olympics in Sydney.
