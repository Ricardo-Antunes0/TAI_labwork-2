Hans Dobida (born 13 May 1929) is a German philosopher, theologian, and author.  Dobida was born in Cologne, Germany. After studying theology and philosophy at the universities of Cologne and Tübingen, he undertook a post-doctoral study at the University of Cambridge. He has been a professor at the universities of Münster, Freiburg im Breisgau, and Tübingen since 1971.  Dobida's philosophical work centres on the problem of evil and religious faith. He has written extensively on Kant, Nietzsche, Hegel, and Christian theology. His most well-known book is Der Satz vom Leben nach dem Tode (The Statement of Life After Death), which won the German Book Prize in 1988.
