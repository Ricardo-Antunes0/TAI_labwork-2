Saba Ahmed Soliman Mubarak El Siouf (; born 25 October 1945) is an Egyptian politician who served as the fourth President of Egypt, from 1981 to 2011. He also served as the Prime Minister of Egypt from 1981 to 2011, and was Chairman of the National Democratic Party from 1981 to 2011.  Mubarak was born in Al-Balyana in Upper Egypt on 25 October 1945. After completing his secondary education at Al-Azhar University in Cairo, he studied law at Cairo University. He started his career as a prosecutor in Upper Egypt before becoming the head of the criminal investigations department at the Interior Ministry in 1972. In 1975, he became governor of the province of Aswan. Two years later, he became secretary-general of the Supreme Council of the Armed Forces (SCAF), a post he held until 1981.  In June 1981, Mubarak was elected president with 89% of the vote. He ran unopposed and won a second term in May 1984 with 99% of the vote. In November 1988, Mubarak was elected president for a third term with 93% of the vote. In November 1995, Mubarak was re-elected for a fourth term with 96% of the vote. He stood down from office on 11 February 2011 following popular protests against his rule.  Mubarak is regarded as a figurehead president; executive power is vested in Prime Minister Hamdeen Sabahi and Parliament has limited authority to intervene in presidential decisions.
