The Cavalry Division Zaza was a cavalry division of the French Army during World War I. The division was formed on 28 September 1914, in Metz, and was commanded by General Charles Lanrezac.  The division fought on the Western Front throughout the war, seeing action at the First Battle of the Marne, the Second Battle of Ypres, the Third Battle of Ypres, the Aisne-Marne Offensive, and the Champagne-Marne Offensive. In November 1918, it was dissolved and its infantry and artillery Divisions were combined to form the 8th Army Corps.
