African nightshades are several species of plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae. They are native to Africa, the Middle East, and southwestern Asia, and have been naturalized in other parts of the world.  African nightshades are a diverse group of plants that share a number of common characteristics. Most African nightshades are small to medium-sized shrubs or small trees that grow up to 3 meters tall. They have fleshy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and can be green, black, or a mixture of both colors. The flowers are usually yellow or orange and are borne in clusters on short stalks. African nightshades have a strong flavor and can be used to make food products, including spices, sauces, and medicines.
