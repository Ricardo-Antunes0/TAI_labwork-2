Eleanor Torrey Powell (November 21, 1912 – October 12, 1998) was an American actress and singer. She was considered one of the leading Hollywood actresses of her era. Powell was nominated for five Academy Awards, winning two. She also received a Golden Globe Award and a Tony Award.  Powell began her career in the late 1920s as a dancer and chorus girl. In the 1930s, she began appearing in small roles in films. Her performance in the musical film The New Moon (1937) led to her first starring role in the 1939 film The Great Ziegfeld. After a successful period in the 1940s and 1950s, Powell retired from acting in the 1960s. She later made occasional appearances on television and in films. Powell died of lung cancer at age 85.  Eleanor Torrey Powell was born on November 21, 1912, in Jamestown, North Dakota, to Florence Torrey (née Rutherford) and Torrey Powell, a cattle rancher and politician. Her mother was of English ancestry and her father had Scottish, Irish, and Dutch ancestry. When she was six months old, her family moved to the town of Pueblo, Colorado. They later moved to Los Angeles where her father had been elected mayor.   Powell attended Hollywood High School where she participated in many school plays and became interested in acting.
