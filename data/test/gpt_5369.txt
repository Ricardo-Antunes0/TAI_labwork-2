Champlin Library, at 12154 Ensign Avenue North in Champlin, Minnesota, is a historic library building. It was designed by the Minneapolis architect Louis H. Sullivan and built in 1915. It is a high-style example of early 20th century library design and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.  The Champlin Library is one of only two surviving examples of the work of Louis H. Sullivan, Minnesota's most famous architect. The other is the Masonic Temple in Minneapolis. The library was built as a memorial to Champlin's war dead and as a center for community life. It features a distinctive octagonal reading room with a domed roof, marble floors, and elaborately carved woodwork. The exterior features an elaborate mural by Emil Nolde depicting American Indian scenes.
