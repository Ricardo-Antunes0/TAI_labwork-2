Kemal Amin "Casey" Kasem (April 27, 1932 – December 15, 2017) was an American disc jockey, singer, actor and television personality. He was known for his work on American Top 40 and its spin-offs in the 1970s and 1980s. Kasem was also a voice actor, providing the voice of Shredder on the animated series Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles from 1987 to 2003.  Born in Detroit, Michigan, Kasem began his career as a disc jockey at WXYZ-AM in Detroit in the early 1950s. In the late 1950s, he moved to Los Angeles to become a radio announcer and then an on-air personality at KDAY-AM. Kasem hosted his own radio show, Casey Kasem's America's Top 40, from 1973 to 1985. He also starred in and produced several television sitcoms during this time, including The Case of the Toxic Toymaker (1978), Hog Wild (1981), and Father Dowling Mysteries (1982). Kasem retired from show business in 1989.  Kasem was diagnosed with Lewy body dementia in 2013 and died of pneumonia at his home in Woodland Hills, California, on December 15, 2017, at the age of 82.
