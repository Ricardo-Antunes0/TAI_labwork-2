The Elihu Root House is a historic house located in Burlington, Vermont. It is a large 2-1/2 story wood frame structure, with a gable roof and clapboarded exterior. The main facade is oriented north-south, and features a large central entrance surrounded by sidelight windows and pilasters. The house was built in 1847 by Elihu Root, one of the founders of the Burlington Railroad, and is one of the city's most prominent examples of Federal period architecture.
