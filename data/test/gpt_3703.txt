Edward Lee Spence (born November 1947) is an American lawyer and politician who has served as the United States Attorney for the Northern District of Ohio since January 20, 2017.  Spence was born in Cincinnati, Ohio, to Frank and Ruth (n√©e English) Spence. He attended the University of Cincinnati, where he earned a B.A. in Political Science in 1969. He then attended the University of Virginia School of Law, where he earned his J.D. in 1973.  After law school, Spence clerked for Judge Edward J. Weinfeld of the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Sixth Circuit from 1973 to 1974. From 1974 to 1975, he served as an assistant United States Attorney for the Northern District of Ohio under then-U.S. Attorney Robert M. Morgenthau Jr.. From 1975 to 1977, he served as a law clerk for Associate Justice Potter Stewart of the United States Supreme Court.  From 1977 to 1980, Spence practiced law with the firm of Covington & Burling in Washington, D.C., where he became a partner in 1981. He also served as a law clerk for Associate Justice Lewis F. Powell Jr. of the United States Supreme Court from 1980 to 1981.  In 1991, President George H. W. Bush nominated Spence to serve as Assistant Attorney General for Antitrust in the Department of Justice; he was confirmed by the Senate on November 21, 1991 and served until May 20, 1993.
