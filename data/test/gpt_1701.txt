James Horace Alderman (in some sources Aldermon) (August 25, 1876 â€“ February 20, 1971) was an American jurist who served as a United States District Judge for the Southern District of New York.  Born in White Plains, New York, Alderman received a Bachelor of Laws from Columbia Law School in 1898. He was in private practice in New York City from 1898 to 1915. He was a special assistant United States Attorney for the Southern District of New York from 1915 to 1917. He was a judge on the New York City Civil Court from 1917 to 1933.  Alderman was nominated by President Franklin D. Roosevelt on February 9, 1933, to a seat on the United States District Court for the Southern District of New York vacated by Judge Learned Hand. He was confirmed by the United States Senate on February 12, 1933, and received his commission the same day. He served as Chief Judge from 1947 to 1961. His service terminated on his retirement on February 20, 1971.
