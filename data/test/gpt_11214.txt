Sterling Pictures was a film company headed by producer Hal Wallis that produced and distributed films from the late 1940s to the early 1970s. The company was best known for producing films with a liberal and progressive political agenda, often critical of American culture and politics.  Sterling Pictures was founded by producer Hal Wallis in 1948 as Wallis Productions. The company's first release was "The Defiant Ones" (1958), which starred James Dean and Marlon Brando. The company became most well-known for its films with a liberal and progressive political agenda, often critical of American culture and politics. These films include "Bonnie and Clyde" (1967), "The Graduate" (1967), " Guess Who's Coming to Dinner? (1967)", "The Wild Bunch" (1969), "The Godfather" (1972), and "The Godfather, Part II" (1974).  In 1970, Sterling Pictures merged with 20th Century Fox Film Corporation to form 20th Century-Fox Film Corporation. The company ceased operations in 1973.
