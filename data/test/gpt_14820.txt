Albert Sidney Thomas (February 6, 1873 – October 5, 1918) was an American soldier and statesman who served as the 26th Governor of Oklahoma from 1911 to 1913. He also served as a United States Senator from Oklahoma from 1913 to 1917.  Albert Sidney Thomas was born in Muskogee, Oklahoma Territory, on February 6, 1873. He was the son of Albert Sidney Thomas and the former Lillie (née Beck) and attended public schools in Muskogee and then the University of Oklahoma, from which he graduated with a B.S. in 1897. He served in the Spanish–American War with Company I of the 3rd Regiment Infantry and was wounded in action at Santiago de Cuba.  Thomas returned to Muskogee and became a lawyer. In 1907 he married Fannie Belle Hester (1875–1947), with whom he had two daughters: Fannie Belle Thomas (1908–2000) and Alice Thomas (1911–2003).  In 1911, Thomas was elected governor of Oklahoma by a large margin. As governor, he promoted public education and economic development, including construction of new roads and bridges. He also fought corruption in government, although he faced opposition from powerful interests. In 1913, he was elected to the U.S. Senate, serving until his death in 1918.   Albert Sidney Thomas is buried at Greenwood Memorial Park in Muskogee, Oklahoma.
