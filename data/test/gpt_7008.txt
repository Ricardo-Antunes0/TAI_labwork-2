Sung Hwan Kim (born 1975 in Seoul, South Korea) is a contemporary Korean artist. Kim is known for his installations and videos, which often explore the connections between culture, memory, and the physical world.  Kim was born in Seoul in 1975. He received his BFA from the University of California at Santa Cruz in 1998, and his MFA from Yale University in 2003. After graduation, he worked in New York City as an assistant to Douglas Gordon. In 2006, he moved to Berlin, where he has lived since.  Kim's work is often rooted in historical and cultural references. For example, many of his installations reference traditional Korean architecture or paintings. His videos often explore the connections between culture, memory, and the physical world. Recent projects have focused on the way that memories are passed down from one generation to the next.
