DELAG, acronym for Deutsche Luftschifffahrts-Aktiengesellschaft (German for "German Airship Transport Company"), was a German airline company. It was founded in 1912 as Flugzeug- und Segel-Lloyd Aktiengesellschaft (FLA), and changed its name to DELAG in 1920. In 1933, it was nationalized as part of the Nazi regime. DELAG ceased operations in 1945 after the war ended.  DELAG was founded in 1912 as Flugzeug- und Segel-Lloyd Aktiengesellschaft, or FLA for short. The company's original purpose was to provide air transport services for the shipping industry. In 1920, the name was changed to Deutsche Luftschifffahrts-Aktiengesellschaft, or DELAG for short.  In 1933, DELAG became part of the Nazi regime and was nationalized as part of the process of Gleichschaltung Germany. From that point on, all operations were dedicated to the government's military objectives. DELAG ceased operations in 1945 after the war ended.
