Ushuaia  is the capital of Tierra del Fuego, a province in the southernmost part of Argentina. Situated on the shores of Isla de los Estados at the southern entrance of Beagle Channel, Ushuaia is a thriving city with a population of over 7,000.  Ushuaia was founded on December 14, 1884, as a military post by Colonel Domingo Sarmiento y Erasmo de San Martín and named after the Ushuaia Island group which was discovered by Pedro Sarmiento de Gamboa on July 22, 1579. The city was officially declared a municipality on March 12, 1885. Ushuaia attained city status on December 10, 1907.   The city is situated on Isla de los Estados, which houses the world's largest reserve of fresh water ice. The climate is mild with average temperatures ranging from −24 to −5 °C (−13 to 23 °F). The average annual rainfall is 1,800 mm (70 in).   Ushuaia is noted for its clean air and clear skies and its natural beauty including the Tierra del Fuego National Park, Mount Acheron and Palena Peninsula. It is also home to the Ushuaia Airport and Naval Base which support tourism activities in the area.
