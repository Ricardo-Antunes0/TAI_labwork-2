Clement Edson Warner (February 23, 1836May 20, 1902) was an American jurist and politician who served as a U.S. Senator from New York and as the 34th Governor of New York.  Born in Hudson, New York, Warner studied law and was admitted to the bar in 1859. He practiced law in Hudson until 1872, when he was elected as a Republican to the U.S. Senate, serving from March 4, 1875 to March 3, 1881. Warner was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1880, but was appointed U.S. Attorney for the Southern District of New York by President James A. Garfield in 1881, serving until his resignation in 1884.  Warner returned to practicing law in New York City until his death there on May 20, 1902. He was interred in Hudson Cemetery.
