Colonel Juan Blas Hernández (January 20, 1879 – December 4, 1954) was a Spanish general during the Spanish Civil War and the Second World War.  Born in Madrid, Hernández joined the Army in 1898 and fought in the Spanish–American War. He was promoted to captain in 1921 and to major in 1931. He served as military attaché in Paris from 1933 to 1935. In 1936 he was appointed commander of the XI Military Zone in Andalusia.  In July 1937 he became commander of the II Military Region of Madrid, a position he held until October 1938. From October 1938 to January 1939 he served as Inspector General of Infantry. In February 1939 he was appointed Inspector General of Armaments.  In May 1939 he became Secretary-General of the Ministry of War. He played an important role in the decision to declare war on Britain and France in September 1939, and was appointed Deputy Minister for War on December 1, 1939. He served as Minister for War from December 10, 1940, to April 17, 1941.   He participated in the Battle of Teruel from March 19 to April 23, 1944, where his troops were defeated. On April 24 he was captured by Franco's forces and executed two weeks later for his part in the Battle of Bilbao (Bilbao Offensive).
