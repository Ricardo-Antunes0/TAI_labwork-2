Zeina Hashem Beck (Arabic: زينة هاشم بيك) (born May 10, 1951) is a Palestinian writer and journalist.  Zeina Hashem Beck was born on May 10, 1951 in the town of Beit Sahour, located in the West Bank. She grew up in the village of al-Khalil, located northeast of Bethlehem. She attended a local school and then studied at the University of Jerusalem, where she earned a degree in journalism.  Beck began her career as a journalist with the Palestinian news agency Maan. In 1984 she moved to Cairo, where she worked for various publications, including Al-Ahram and El-Hayat. In 1990 she returned to Palestine and continued to work as a journalist for various outlets, including Al-Quds al-Arabi and Al-Hayat al-Jadida. She also served as the editor-in-chief of Al-Quds al-Arabi's women's magazine Zanan.  Beck has written several books, including the memoirs I Saw Bethlehem (1995) and The Lost Daughter of Palestine (1998). She has also published articles in magazines and newspapers around the world, including The New York Times, The Guardian, and Le Monde diplomatique.  Zeina Hashem Beck is a member of the Palestinian Writers Union and the PEN Center USA.
