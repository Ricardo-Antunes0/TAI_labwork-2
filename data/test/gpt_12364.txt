Dodd was born in London, the son of Josiah Dodd, an accountant, and his wife Sarah. He was educated at Harrow School and Christ Church, Oxford, where he was a member of the Pitt Club and achieved first-class honours in law. He became a barrister in 1879 and Queen's Counsel in 1899.  In 1901 he was appointed Professor of International Law at the University of Cambridge, a post he held until his retirement in 1932. During the First World War he served as Deputy Director of Military Intelligence. He was made a Commander of the Order of the British Empire in 1919 and a Fellow of the Royal Society in 1921.  Dodd died in Cambridge, England, aged 87.
