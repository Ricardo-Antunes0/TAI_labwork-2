The Irwin Consolidated School in Irwin, Iowa, was a historic school that served the students of Irwin and neighboring towns from 1892 to 1965. The school was designated a National Historic Landmark in 1966.  The Irwin Consolidated School was built in 1892 on a site donated by Josiah Irwin, one of the town's founders. The school served the students of Irwin and neighboring towns until it closed in 1965. The school is now used as a community center.  The Irwin Consolidated School is a two-story brick building with a gabled roof. It has a hipped roof and features an elaborate entrance porch with turned posts and balustrade. The building has been restored and is used as a community center.
