Calluna vulgaris, common heather, ling, or simply heather, is a species of flowering plant in theEricaceae family. It is native to Europe and North Africa, and has been introduced to many other parts of the world.  Heather grows in moist, fertile soils near bodies of water, and can be found in lowlands, hillsides, and mountains. It is a perennial herb with erect branches and small leaves. The flowers are small and white, and the fruits are small black capsules.  Heather is used as a ground cover, for landscaping, and as a spice for cooking. It has been used for medicinal purposes, especially in Germany and Scandinavia, where it is known as ling or heather.
