Digimon Masters (Korean: 디지몬 마스터즈), also known as Digimon World, is a video game for the Nintendo DS. It is the first installment in the Digimon World franchise, and was released in Japan on March 8, 2006, in North America on September 26, 2006, and in Europe on October 3, 2006.  The game is set in a virtual world where players must raise and train creatures called Digimon to fight evil forces. Players can choose from a variety of Digimon Masters to assist them in their quest, including characters from various anime series. The game features Digimon battles against computer-controlled opponents, as well as cooperative multiplayer mode for up to four players.  Development for the game began in 2002, and was overseen by Ken Sugimori, the creator of the Digimon anime series. The story for the game was written by Kazuki Nakashima, who also wrote the story for the first two seasons of the anime. The music for the game was composed by Yoko Shimomura, who also composed the music for many of the episodes of the anime.
