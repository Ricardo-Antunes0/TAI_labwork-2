Godfrey Ho (Chinese language: 何志强 or 何致强, Pinyin: Hé zhìqín, born 6 April 1944) is a Hong Kong-born, Mainland China-based film director and screenwriter.  Ho was born in Hong Kong. He is a graduate of the University of California, Los Angeles with a degree in theater and film. He began his career as an actor, appearing in roles in several American films before moving to China in the early 1980s to make documentary films. His first feature-length film, Rice Cooker (1984), was nominated for the Golden Bear at the Berlin Film Festival.  Ho has directed a number of acclaimed films, including The Return of the Secaucus Seven (1991), City of Sadness (1993), and The Fortune teller (1997). He has also written screenplays for many of his films, including The Return of the Secaucus Seven and City of Sadness. Ho has been nominated for the Golden Bear at the Berlin Film Festival and the Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay for The Return of the Secaucus Seven.
