Lax-Kw'alaams , previously called Port Simpson, is a First Nations community located on the north coast of Vancouver Island, in the Canadian province of British Columbia. It has a population of 1,371, as of the Canada 2016 Census.  The community is located on the northeast coast of Lax Kw'alaams Peninsula, and is bordered by the City of Port Simpson to the south and east, and by the Village of Tofino to the north. The community is connected to Tofino by the Tofino-Lax Kw'alaams Highway.  The name Lax Kw'alaams means "place where bass are found". The community was first settled in 1892 by the Lax Kw'alaams band, who were displaced from their traditional territory in what is now near modern-day Victoria, British Columbia.  Lax Kw'alaams is home to the only commercial salmon fishing operation on Vancouver Island, operated by Indigenous Nations Fisheries Limited. The community also has a small commercial clamming fleet. There are also a number of small businesses in the community catering to local needs such as fishing supplies, car repairs, and tourism services.  The primary economic activity in Lax Kw'alaams is fishing. The community is home to two commercial salmon fishing operations - Indigenous Nations Fisheries Limited (INFL) and Haida Salmon Restoration Corporation (HSRC).
