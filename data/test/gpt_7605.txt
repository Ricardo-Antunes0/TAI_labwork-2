Coylton  is a village and civil parish in the East Riding of Yorkshire, England. It is situated approximately east of Beverley and west of Hull.  The civil parish has an area of and had a population of 291 at the 2011 Census.  Coylton is mentioned in the "Domesday Book" as "Culiton". It was part of the manor of Beverley, and the land was held by Roger de Poitou. The village has a 17th-century church which is Grade II* listed.
