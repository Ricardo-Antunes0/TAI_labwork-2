Christopher John Carpenter (born April 27, 1975) is an American film director and producer. He began his film career in the late 1980s, directing the low-budget slasher film Halloween (1978) and its sequels. He then achieved critical and commercial success with his second film, the horror film The Fog (1980). Carpenter went on to direct such mainstream films as The Thing (1982), Escape from New York (1981), Starman (1984), The Terminator (1984), and The Thing II (1982). In the early 1990s, he directed two cult classics, Christine (1990) and Halloween II (1981). Carpenter has been nominated for five Academy Awards, including Best Director for Escape from New York and The Terminator.
