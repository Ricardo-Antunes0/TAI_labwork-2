Mouda, (or Mauda) is a town and union council in Mianwali District of Punjab, Pakistan. It is located at 32°1′N 73°21′E. According to the 1998 census, the population of the town was 19,000. Mouda's name is derived from a Pashtun word meaning 'gravel'. The town was first settled by the Pathans in the 18th century.  Mouda is located in Mianwali District of Punjab Province. It has an altitude of 5,000 feet (1,500 meters).  The town is on the Grand Trunk Road which links Lahore with Peshawar. Mouda has a total area of . The soil is sandy and the climate is hot and dry during summer and cold and wet during winter. The main crops are wheat, barley, cotton, peas and lentils. There is also a small dairy industry.
