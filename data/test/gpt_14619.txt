The David Ogden House is a historic house at 243 Broadway in the West Village of Manhattan, New York City. It was built in 1837 and is a good example of Federal period architecture. It was designated a National Historic Landmark in 1965.  The David Ogden House is a two-story, five-bay house with a hipped roof in the Federal style. It has a front facade that is three bays wide and two deep, with a center entrance flanked by sidelight windows and pilasters. The roofline is adorned with modillions and dentils, and there are bracketed cornices on the first and second floors. The interior features Federal period woodwork, including hand-carved mantelpieces and paneled walls.
