Archbishop Basil Volodymyr Ladyka, O.S.B.M. (; 2 September 1927 – 2 November 2017) was a Ukrainian Greek Catholic hierarch who served as the first Chancellor of the National University of Kyiv-Mohyla Academy (1977–1981). He was also the founder and abbot of the Basilian Fathers, a monastic community.  Basil Ladyka was born in 1927 in Kyiv, Ukrainian SSR, Soviet Union. He studied at the Kyiv Theological Academy and at the Pontifical Gregorian University in Rome. He was ordained to the priesthood in 1951. Ladyka then served as a teacher at the Kyiv Theological Academy.  In 1977, Ladyka became Chancellor of the National University of Kyiv-Mohyla Academy. During his tenure, he helped to revive the university and improve its reputation. He also founded the Basilian Fathers, a monastic community. Ladyka retired from his position as chancellor in 1981.  Ladyka died on 2 November 2017 at the age of 94.
