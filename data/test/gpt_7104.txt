The snare drum or side drum is a percussion instrument used in many popular styles of popular music. It is a drum that is played by striking its skin with a thin drumstick, or by hitting it with a mallet.  The snare drum is an important part of traditional and contemporary drumming, and is used in genres such as rock, pop, funk, metal, and reggae. It is also used in dance and marching bands, as well as in jazz and Latin music.
