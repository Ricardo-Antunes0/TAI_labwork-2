Lieutenant General Sir Kenneth Morley Loch, KCIE, CB, DSO, MC (15 October 1894 â€“ 21 December 1984) was a senior British Army officer who served in both World War I and World War II. He is best known for his service as Chief of the Imperial General Staff (CIGS) during the Second World War, where he was responsible for the coordination of defence in the United Kingdom and overseas.  Loch was born on 15 October 1894 in Cheltenham, Gloucestershire. He was educated at Eton College and the Royal Military College, Sandhurst. Loch was commissioned into the Queen's Royal Regiment (West Surrey) in 1913. He served on the Western Front during World War I, becoming commanding officer of 103rd Infantry Brigade in 1917. He then served as adjutant general to the British Expeditionary Force (BEF) from 1918 to 1919. After the war, Loch served in Palestine and Iraq before being appointed Deputy Assistant Adjutant-General (DAAG) at the War Office in 1923.   Loch transferred to the Grenadier Guards in 1927 and became Commanding Officer of 2nd Battalion in 1930. He went on to be Deputy Assistant Adjutant-General at Headquarters Northern Command in 1934, General Officer Commanding 3rd Infantry Division in 1935 and Commander of London District from 1938 to 1940. Loch served as Chief of Staff to the Commander-in-Chief Home Forces from 1940 to 1941 before being appointed Deputy Chief of Staff (DCS)
