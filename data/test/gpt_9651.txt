Robert Capa (born Endre Ernő Friedmann; October 18, 1895 – August 16, 1954) was a Hungarian-American photographer and war correspondent. He was one of the most renowned photojournalists in history, and is best known for his photographs of World War II.  Capa was born in Budapest, Hungary in 1895 to Jewish parents. His family fled to Vienna after the October Revolution of 1918 and then to America in 1921. Capa began his photographic career in the early 1920s, working as a freelance photographer for various newspapers and magazines. He became a staff photographer for "Life" magazine in 1936, and during World War II he covered conflicts in Europe and the Middle East for the magazine. His photographs have been exhibited worldwide and are some of the most famous images of the 20th century. Capa died from leukemia at the age of 57 in 1954.
