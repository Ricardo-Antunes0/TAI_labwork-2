He received his undergraduate degree from the University of Cambridge in 1954 and his doctoral degree from the University of Chicago in 1958.  Sarma joined the faculty of Cornell University in 1958, where he became a full professor in 1966. He served as the Chairman of the Department of Economics from 1984 to 1989 and Dean of the Faculty of Arts and Sciences from 1986 to 1993. He also served as a visiting professor at Harvard University, Stanford University, and the London School of Economics.  Sarma was appointed Governor of the RBI by Prime Minister Rajiv Gandhi on 23 July 1993 and served until 27 September 1997. During his tenure, Sarma oversaw a period of economic reform at the RBI, which included liberalizing currency and interest rates, deregulating the banking sector, and encouraging private investment. He also played a role in introducing new financial products such as derivatives and credit default swaps.  Sarma retired from academic life in 1998 and returned to his native India to serve as Chairman of IDFC Ltd., a leading financial services company. He died on 27 September 2009 at the age of 80 after a long illness.
