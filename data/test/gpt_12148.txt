Jerrald King Goldsmith (February 10, 1929July 21, 1992) was an American composer, conductor, and music producer. He is best known for his work in film and television, but also composed music for stage and concert productions.  Goldsmith was born in San Francisco, California, to Jewish parents. He attended the San Francisco Conservatory of Music and the University of California, Berkeley. After serving in the United States Army in Germany, Goldsmith began his career as a composer and conductor for television and film. He co-wrote the score for the original Star Trek television series and produced the first two films in the Indiana Jones franchise. He also composed music for the television series M*A*S*H, The Streets of San Francisco, and The Six Million Dollar Man. Goldsmith's notable works include the score for the film The Omen (1976), which won him a Golden Globe Award, and the Academy Awards-nominated score for The Towering Inferno (1974).
