The Wawel Cathedral , formally titled the Cathedral of the Virgin Mary, is a Roman Catholic cathedral located in Krakow, Poland. It is the mother church of the Archdiocese of Krakow, and one of Poland's major tourist attractions.  The first cathedral on the site was built in 992 by Duke Mieszko I and his wife, Princess Dobrawa. The present cathedral was started in 1290 and completed in 1433. It is one of the largest cathedrals in Europe, with a total floor area of 134,000 square metres (14 acres).
