Whitney Otto (born March 5, 1955) is an American actress and singer. She is perhaps best known for her role as Kathy on the TV series "The Wonder Years" (1988–1993), for which she was nominated for a Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Television Series Musical or Comedy.  Otto was born in Los Angeles, California, the daughter of Virginia (née Sterling) and Leo Otto. She has two sisters, Gretchen and Diane. Her parents were both actors. Otto's maternal grandfather was the film producer Adolph Zukor.  Otto studied acting at the Neighborhood Playhouse in New York City. She made her television debut in the late 1970s, appearing in episodes of "Karen", "The Rockford Files" and "The Streets of San Francisco". In 1988, she landed the role of Kathy on the hit TV series "The Wonder Years". The show ran for three seasons and Otto was nominated for a Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Television Series Musical or Comedy. She also appeared in several films during this time, including "A League of Their Own" (1992), "The Wedding Singer" (1998) and "Ed Wood" (1994).  Since 2000, Otto has appeared in several stage productions, including a revival of "Hello Dolly!" at the Pantages Theatre in Los Angeles and a run of the musical comedy "She Loves Me" at the La Jolla Playhouse.
