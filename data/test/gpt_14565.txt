Anatol Fejgin (September 25, 1909 – July 9, 1986) was a Russian-born Romanian composer and pianist.  Anatol Fejgin was born in Leningrad, Russian Empire (now Saint Petersburg, Russia) on September 25, 1909. He studied at the Leningrad Conservatory with Vissarion Shebalin and later at the Paris Conservatory with Marcel Duchamp. He also studied at the University of Berlin with Bruno Maderna and Karlheinz Stockhausen.  Fejgin's compositions typically explore the boundaries between classical and contemporary styles. He is particularly noted for his works for piano and orchestra, which often feature dense textures and unusual harmonic structures. His best-known work is the opera "Micaţia" (1958), which has been performed around the world. Fejgin died in 1986 in Bucharest, Romania.
