Lucien Paul Victor Febvre (, ; 22 October 1878 – 5 September 1954) was a French historian and anthropologist who specialised in the history of the ancient world. He was also a noted philologist and historian of classical literature.  Febvre was born in Paris on 22 October 1878. He studied at the École Normale Supérieure and then at the University of Paris, where he obtained his doctorate in 1903. Febvre then taught at various universities, including the Sorbonne, before becoming a professor of history at the University of Paris in 1932.   Febvre is well known for his work on ancient history, and is particularly noted for his studies of classical culture and literature. He was chairman of the council for historical research from 1939 to 1944. Febvre died on 5 September 1954.
