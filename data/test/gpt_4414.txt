Boris Rosenthal (1881–1938) was a character actor, playwright, and screenwriter. He is best remembered for his work in Hollywood films of the 1930s and 1940s, particularly for his roles in the films The Adventures of Robin Hood (1938), The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes (1939), and The Third Man (1949).  Rosenthal was born in Osnabrück, Germany, to Jewish parents. He began his career in theater in Germany before moving to the United States in 1912. He first appeared in Hollywood films in the early 1930s, and became a popular character actor. He died in 1938.
