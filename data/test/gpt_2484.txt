Carnabuci was born in Ferrara, Emilia-Romagna, on 6 September 1893. After studying at the Accademia di Belle Arti in Florence, he moved to Rome in 1919. There he met Giorgio de Chirico and together they founded the movement of Metaphysical Painting.  In 1933 Carnabuci directed his first film, "The Return of Martin Guerre". He went on to make over forty films, many of which were acclaimed for their poetic and atmospheric qualities. He also wrote several novels and poems, and exhibited his sculptures both in Italy and abroad. Carnabuci died in Rome on 13 December 1963.
