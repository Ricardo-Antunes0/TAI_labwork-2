Pendik is a district of Istanbul, Turkey. The district is located in the northwest of the city, on the European side. Pendik is bordered by the districts of Şişli to the east, Kartal to the south, Sultangazi to the southwest, and Emirgan to the west.  Pendik was founded as a village in 1885 and became a district of Istanbul in 1923. It has a population of about 184,000 people. The district is mostly residential, with a small commercial center in the northern part of the district. Pendik is well known for its parks, including Pendik Park and Çamlıca Park. The district is served by two metro stations: Pendik Çarşı and Pendik Köyü.
