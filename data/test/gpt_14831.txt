Christopher Grant Champlin (April 12, 1768March 18, 1834) was an American physician, anatomist, and zoologist. He is considered one of the founders of comparative anatomy and zoology.  Born in Groton, Massachusetts, Champlin received his medical education at the University of Pennsylvania, where he was a member of the Philadelphia Medical Society. He subsequently practiced medicine in Pennsylvania and later in New York City. In 1815 he moved to West Point, New York, where he served as professor of natural philosophy and anatomy until his death. Champlin was also a noted collector of fossils and vertebrates.
