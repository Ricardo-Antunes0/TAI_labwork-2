The CVV1 Pinguino  was a single-seat, high-wing, single-engine advanced trainer aircraft designed in Italy in the early 1960s.  The CVV1 Pinguino was designed as a advanced trainer aircraft, intended to replace the ageing Cessna 172 and 182. The aircraft was designed by the Italian company Aermacchi, and first flew in 1963.  The CVV1 Pinguino was a conventional high-wing monoplane design, with a single engine in pusher configuration mounted in the nose. The aircraft was fitted with a fixed tricycle landing gear, and a single cabin door was located on the forward fuselage.
