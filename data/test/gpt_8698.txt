Size Isn't Everything is the twentieth studio album by the English rock band Queen, released in March 1987. It was the last album to be released during the group's original line-up, with Freddie Mercury deceased by December 1991.  The album is a departure from Queen's heavy metal roots, incorporating funk and soul influences into their music. It debuted at number one on the UK Albums Chart and spawned three singles: "I Want to Break Free", "Innuendo" and "The Show Must Go On".  In 2003, "Size Isn't Everything" was remastered and reissued with bonus tracks. The album has since been certified Platinum by the British Phonographic Industry (BPI).
