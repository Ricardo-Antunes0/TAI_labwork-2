Judith O. Becker (born September 3, 1932) is an American historian who specializes in the history of women in America.  Born in 1932 in New York City, Judith O. Becker graduated from Barnard College in 1954 with a degree in history. She taught at Barnard from 1964 to 1998, and was its first female president. She is now a professor emerita at Barnard.  Becker is the author or editor of fourteen books, including "The Female Persuasion: The History of Women in American Politics" (1977), "Theaunders and the Quest for Equality: Women and Politics in Nineteenth-Century America" (1984), and "The Politics of Woman Suffrage: The American Movement from Property to Citizenship" (1985). She has also written numerous articles for journals and anthologies, and she has been a guest lecturer at universities around the world.  In 1990, Becker was awarded the Eleanor Roosevelt Award for Distinguished Civil Rights Leadership. In 2001, she received the Presidential Medal of Freedom from President George W. Bush.
