He was born in London, and studied medicine at the University of Edinburgh from 1790 to 1794. He then moved to London and began to practise as a physician. In 1814 he was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society.  Bevan wrote extensively on medical subjects, publishing works on diseases such as tuberculosis, smallpox, yellow fever and cholera. He also wrote on other medical topics such as mental health and diet. His most famous work is "A System of Medicine" (1820), which became the standard medical text in Britain for many years. He also wrote "A Practical Treatise on the Diseases of Children" (1824). Bevan died in London in 1839.
