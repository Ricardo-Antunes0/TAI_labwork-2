Nat(h)an Friedland was a rabbi and member of the Akiva religious movement. Friedland was born in Warsaw, Poland, in 1894. He was ordained as a rabbi in 1916 and served as rabbi of the city of Lodz, Poland, from 1923 to 1936. Friedland immigrated to the United States in 1938 and became a rabbi at the Reform Congregation Emanu-El in New York City. He served as president of the Rabbinical Assembly of America from 1951 to 1953. Friedland died in 1957. A noted scholar and author, Friedland was also highly influential in the field of halakhah (Jewish law). He served as president of the Rabbinical Assembly of America from 1951 to 1953 and was a member of numerous American Jewish organizations, including the World Jewish Congress and the American Jewish Committee.
