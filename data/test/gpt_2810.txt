Trevor Henry Worthy (born 3 January 1957) is an English musician, songwriter, and record producer. He is best known as the founder and frontman of the progressive rock band Porcupine Tree.  Worthy was born in Bromsgrove, Worcestershire, England, on 3 January 1957. He began playing the guitar at the age of 11 and soon developed a reputation in his local area as a talented guitarist and songwriter. In 1979, Worthy met former King Crimson and Yes bassist John Wetton and formed Porcupine Tree with him.  Porcupine Tree's debut album, "In the Wake of Poseidon", was released in 1984 to critical acclaim. The album featured some of Worthy's most recognizable compositions, including the hits "The Incident" and "Lazarus". Following the release of "In the Wake of Poseidon", Porcupine Tree toured extensively throughout Europe and North America.  Over the next several years, Porcupine Tree released two more albums, "Fear of a Blank Planet" (1988) and "Deadwing" (1992). Both albums received widespread critical acclaim and cemented Porcupine Tree's status as one of progressive rock's leading bands. In 1995, Porcupine Tree released their fourth album, "The Sky Moves Sideways", which marked a turning point in Worthy's songwriting career.
