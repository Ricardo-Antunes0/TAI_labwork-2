Toxicity is the second studio album by American nu metal band Slipknot. It was released on October 20, 1999, through Roadrunner Records. Toxicity was produced by Slipknot and Ross Robinson, who also served as the album's engineers.  The album is considered a departure from the band's earlier work, featuring a more aggressive sound that fused elements of death metal and thrash metal with their nu metal influences. The lyrical content of the album is dark and violent, dealing with themes of drug addiction and depression. Toxicity has been cited as one of the key albums in the development of nu metal and is often cited as one of the greatest albums of all time.  Slipknot achieved mainstream success with Toxicity, becoming one of the most popular heavy metal bands in the world. The album has sold over 15 million copies worldwide, making it one of the best-selling albums ever released by an American act. It has been certified platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA) and has received widespread critical acclaim, being praised for its technical musicianship and dark lyrics. Toxicity is often considered one of the greatest albums of all time.
