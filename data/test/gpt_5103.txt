Paul Simon is the second solo studio album by American singer-songwriter Paul Simon. Released on September 25, 1970, by Columbia Records, it was Simon's second studio album following the release of his self-titled debut in June 1966.  The album was recorded at the Record Plant in New York City over a period of three months in 1969. The sessions were helmed by Simon and produced by Artie Kornfeld. It features the single "The Sound of Silence", which reached number one on the "Billboard" Hot 100 chart, and became one of Simon's signature songs. The album was awarded a gold record certification by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA) on October 10, 1970.  "Paul Simon" has been certified platinum by the RIAA, and has sold over 15 million copies worldwide.
