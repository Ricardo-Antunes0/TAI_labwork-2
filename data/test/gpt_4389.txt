Mac and Me is a 1988 American computer-animated comedy-drama film produced by Steven Spielberg and released by Amblin Entertainment. The film was directed by Robert Zemeckis and stars John Cusack, Dan Aykroyd, Lara Flynn Boyle, and Jane Curtin.  The film tells the story of a boy, Mac, who befriends a computer named Lisa and the two of them go on adventures together.  The film was a financial success and received critical acclaim. It has been cited as an influence on the creation of the Internet.
