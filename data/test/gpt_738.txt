After serving in the United States Marine Corps during the Korean War, he earned his B.A. from the University of California, Berkeley. Twain worked as a newspaper journalist and then as a humor columnist for the Hartford Courant before becoming a full-time writer in the early 1960s. His best-known works include the novels Huckleberry Finn (1885), Tom Sawyer (1876), and Adventures of Huckleberry Finn (1884/1885), as well as the essays Life on the Mississippi (1883), The Adventures of Tom Sawyer Abroad (1876), and A Connecticut Yankee in King Arthur's Court (1889).  Twain was celebrated for his wit and humor, which often addressed American cultural and political issues of his era. He received numerous awards and honors, including a Pulitzer Prize in 1970 for his novel The Adventures of Huckleberry Finn. He died in 2007 at age 87.
