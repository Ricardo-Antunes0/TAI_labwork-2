Lebanon is a parliamentary democratic republic within the Middle East. The president is directly elected by universal suffrage and is the head of state. The prime minister is appointed by the president and responsible to the parliament. Lebanon is bordered by Syria to the north, Israel and the Palestinian territories to the east, Iraq and Iran to the south, and Turkey to the west. It has a population of approximately 4 million people.  Lebanon has been divided into parliamentary districts since 1993, with a total of 128 seats in the parliament. The president nominates a prime minister who must be approved by parliament. Lebanon's political system is based on sectarianism, with power concentrated in the hands of Shiite Hezbollah and Sunni Muslim groups. The country has been plagued by civil war since 1990, which has caused millions of refugees and killed over 150,000 people.
