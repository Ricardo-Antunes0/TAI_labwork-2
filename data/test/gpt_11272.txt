Frederick Carlyle Boucher (; born August 1, 1951) is a Canadian former ice hockey center who played 18 seasons in the National Hockey League (NHL) for the St. Louis Blues, Philadelphia Flyers, and Boston Bruins. He was awarded the Conn Smythe Trophy as the most valuable player of the playoffs in 1978 and 1979.  Boucher was born in Sainte-Anne-de-Bellevue, Quebec. As a youth, he played in the 1970 Quebec International Pee-Wee Hockey Tournament with a minor ice hockey team from his hometown.  Boucher was drafted by the St. Louis Blues in the fourth round of the 1970 NHL Entry Draft. After playing two seasons with the junior Windsor Spitfires of the Ontario Hockey League (OHL), Boucher made his NHL debut with the Blues during the 1974–75 season. He spent most of his first ten seasons with St. Louis, but was traded to Philadelphia in September 1980.  In 1981–82, Boucher helped lead Philadelphia to its first playoff appearance in six years. The Flyers lost to Montreal in six games in the first round. In 1982–83, Boucher led all NHL players in points (84), assists (53), and goals (37) while helping Philly reach the playoffs again, this time defeating Boston in six games.
