Taylor played for the Birmingham Black Barons, Homestead Grays, and Cincinnati Reds in the Negro Leagues. He is most notable as the manager of the Homestead Grays, who won three consecutive Negro League championships from 1940 to 1942. He also coached the Newark Eagles in the Negro American League from 1951 to 1958.  Taylor was born in Tuskegee, Alabama, and died in Uniontown, Pennsylvania. He was posthumously inducted into the National Baseball Hall of Fame in 1976.
