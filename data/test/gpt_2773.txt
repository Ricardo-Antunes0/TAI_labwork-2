The York Mystery Plays, more properly the Play of the Sacrament, are an annual medieval mystery play performed in York, England, on the first Sunday of Lent. The plays are believed to have been written between 1350 and 1400 by an unknown author or authors, and were first performed in 1455.  The plays are based on the legend of the murder of Thomas Becket and the subsequent Quest for his killers. The play is divided into four acts, with a prologue and epilogue. The central event of the play is the revelation of Becket's murderers, who are then punished.  The Play of the Sacrament is one of the most popular mystery plays in England and has been performed by many different theatre companies and directors over the years.
