Cape Palmas is a headland on the east coast of Africa, at the southernmost extremity of the African mainland. The cape is located about 340 km south-southwest of the city of Malabo, the capital of Equatorial Guinea.   The cape is a popular tourist destination because it is situated in an area of outstanding natural beauty, with clear ocean waters and rugged cliffs. It has a wide variety of beaches, including white-sand beaches and some with black sand. Cape Palmas is also noted for its lush rainforest and diverse wildlife.
