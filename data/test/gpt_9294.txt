Britannia, published in 2015 is the fourteenth novel in the Harry Potter series by J.K. Rowling. It is set two years after the events of Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows and follows the lives of Harry, Ron, and Hermione as they finish their education at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry and set off on their own adventures.  The story begins with the trio travelling to France to visit Fleur Delacour and her family, who have been distant since the war ended. While there, they are attacked by a group of Death Eaters led by Bellatrix Lestrange, who wants to bring Voldemort back to life. Hermione is killed in the attack, but Ron and Harry manage to escape with their lives.  Harry returns to England to start his new life as an Auror, but he is soon called back to assist the Ministry of Magic in investigating the disappearances of several important people, including Delacour's father. It is eventually revealed that Voldemort has returned and is trying to rebuild his army. Harry, Ron, and Hermione work together to stop him, culminating in a final showdown on top of the Tower of London.
