Mian Mikaal Patras Zulfiqar (born 5 September 1983) is a Pakistani cricketer. A right-handed batsman and wicket-keeper, he has played for several teams in first-class and international cricket.  Zulfiqar made his One Day International debut for Pakistan against Sri Lanka in the 2007 Cricket World Cup, and his Test debut for the country against India in December 2009. He competed in the 2010 ICC World Twenty20 as Pakistan's second-choice wicket-keeper, behind Asif Ali. He also played in the 2012 ICC World Twenty20, where he was Pakistan's leading run scorer with 218 runs in seven matches.  Zulfiqar was named as captain of Pakistan's squad for the 2015 Cricket World Cup. Pakistan lost to India in the semi-final, after which Zulfiqar announced his retirement from international cricket.
