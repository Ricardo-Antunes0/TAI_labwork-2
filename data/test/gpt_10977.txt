Kent David Jensen, Sr. (born March 31, 1951) is an American politician who is the Republican nominee for Governor of Missouri in the 2018 election.  Jensen was born in Kansas City, Missouri and graduated from Shawnee Mission South High School in 1969. He then attended Southwest Missouri State University and the University of Missouri, where he earned a B.S. degree in political science in 1973 and an M.A. degree in 1976.  Jensen began his political career as a city councilman for Shawnee Mission, Missouri from 1978 to 1980. He then served as executive director of the Missouri Republican Party from 1980 to 1984, and as state chairman from 1984 to 1988. Jensen was elected to the Missouri House of Representatives from the 75th district (St. Charles County) in 1988, and served two terms until he was elected to the United States House of Representatives in 1992, defeating incumbent Democrat Jim Talent by nine percentage points in one of the most Republican victories in House history.  Jensen was re-elected to a second term with 71% of the vote, defeating Democratic nominee Tom Schweich by a wide margin. He served as Chairman of the House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure from 1995 to 1999, during which time he authored and helped pass the National Highway System Improvement Act of 1998, which authorized $50 billion for highway projects over ten years.
