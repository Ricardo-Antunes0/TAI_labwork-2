Plants belonging to the genus Pachypodium vary in size from a few centimeters to several meters in height. The leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, and are simple, lance-shaped, and entire. The flowers are arranged in terminal spikes, and consist of five white petals which are fused at the base. The fruit is a dry capsule.  The morphology of Pachypodium is quite diverse, with plants ranging from small, lance-shaped leaves to large, terminal spikes of flowers. The fruit is also quite varied, ranging from a dry capsule to a fleshy fruit resembling a watermelon. All of these morphological features contribute to the genus' diverse taxonomy.
