Miramax Books was a publishing company started in 1983 by brothers Harvey and Bob Weinstein. The company was headquartered in New York City and specialized in producing and distributing independent films. Miramax produced or distributed many critically acclaimed films, including Sex, Lies, and Videotape, The Crying Game, Pulp Fiction, and Shakespeare in Love. In 1997, Miramax was acquired by The Walt Disney Company.
