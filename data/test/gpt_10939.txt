Cass Bird (born 1974) is an artist, curator, and writer who has worked in a variety of mediums, including painting, sculpture, photography, video and installation.  Bird's practice investigates the boundaries between art and life, often exploring the psychological and physical effects of violence. Her work has been exhibited internationally, most notably at the Museum of Modern Art in New York City, the Hirshhorn Museum and Sculpture Garden in Washington D.C., and the Museum of Contemporary Art in Chicago. She is also the recipient of numerous awards, including a Guggenheim Fellowship and a MacArthur Fellowship.
