Gregory Wendell  McMurtry (born October 15, 1948) is an American novelist and short story writer. He is the author of twelve novels, including "Lonesome Dove" (1985), "The Last Picture Show" (1971), and "Buffalo Bill and the Indians" (1984).  Greg McMurtry was born on October 15, 1948, in Wichita, Kansas. He attended Wichita Heights High School and then obtained a B.A. from Washburn University in 1969. McMurtry then moved to Austin, Texas, where he began writing. His first novel, "Lonesome Dove", was published in 1985 and won the 1985 Pulitzer Prize for Fiction. His novel "The Last Picture Show" was published in 1971 and won the National Book Award for Fiction. McMurtry's other novels include "The Great Santini", "Buffalo Bill and the Indians", "The Reivers", "The Road", and "Catcher in the Rye". He has also written short stories and essays, as well as a comic book adaptation of his novel "Buffalo Bill and the Indians". McMurtry currently lives in Austin, Texas.
