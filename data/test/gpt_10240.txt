Mikhail Innokentyevich Semyonov (, 26 November 1938 – 10 December 1996) was a Russian pianist and conductor.  Semyonov was born in 1938 in Leningrad, Soviet Union. He studied at the Leningrad Conservatory with Lev Oborin and Dmitri Shostakovich, graduating in 1958. He then continued his studies with Arturo Benedetti Michelangeli and Leonard Bernstein, completing his diploma in 1963.  Semyonov made his debut as a pianist at the age of 17, and quickly gained prominence as one of the leading performers of the 20th century. He appeared as a soloist with major orchestras around the world, including appearances with the Berlin Philharmonic Orchestra, the Vienna Philharmonic Orchestra, the Royal Philharmonic Orchestra, the London Symphony Orchestra, and the New York Philharmonic Orchestra. He also served as principal conductor of the Tbilisi Philharmonic Orchestra and guest conductor of many other orchestras around the world.  Semyonov was also active as a conductor. He made his conducting debut in 1967, and became chief conductor of the Leningrad Philharmonic Orchestra in 1984. He also served as chief conductor of the Orquesta Sinfónica de Galicia from 1986 to 1990, and was named Music Director of the Dresden Staatskapelle in 1990.
