Charlotte Zwerin (born Charlotte Mitchell, August 15, 1978) is an American actress, film director, and writer.  Zwerin was born in Washington, D.C., the daughter of Leslie Mitchell, an artist and sculptor, and Michael Mitchell, a musician.   She attended the University of Maryland, where she studied drama. Zwerin's acting career began in 2001 with a small role in the film Donnie Darko. She later appeared in the films The Omen (2006), 28 Days Later (2007), and Watchmen (2009). In 2008 she directed her first feature film, A Single Man, which was screened at the Sundance Film Festival. Her second feature film, Youth (2011), which she also wrote and directed, was awarded the Grand Jury Prize at the 2011 Sundance Film Festival.
