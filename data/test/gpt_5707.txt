The GC5 (Grady Coffee Five) was a five-piece American R&B vocal group, best known for their 1965 hit single "I Hear a Symphony".  The GC5 were formed in Detroit, Michigan, in 1964. The group consisted of brothers Grady Clay (born November 11, 1941), Leon Clay (born October 5, 1942), Leroy Clay (born January 10, 1945), and Reggie Young (born December 12, 1946). After signing with the Gordy Records label, the GC5 released their debut single "I Hear a Symphony" in early 1965. The song reached number one on the "Billboard" Hot 100 chart and stayed at the top spot for seven weeks. It also reached number one on the "Cash Box" chart and was certified gold by the Recording Industry Association of America. The GC5 toured extensively throughout 1965 and 1966 and released further singles including "A Thousand Miles" and "Love Is All Around". In 1967, Leon Clay left the group and was replaced by Clarence Reid. The GC5 disbanded in 1969.
