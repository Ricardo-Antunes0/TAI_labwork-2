James M. Stone (born November 12, 1947) is an American executive. He is the chairman and CEO of J.P. Morgan Chase & Co., one of the world's largest banks. Stone has held this position since 2006.  Stone was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, and grew up in Summit, New Jersey. He received a B.S degree from the University of Pennsylvania in 1969 and an M.B.A from Harvard Business School in 1972.  Stone worked for McKinsey & Company from 1972 to 1975, when he joined Goldman Sachs as a partner. He became co-head of Goldman Sachs' investment bank in 1986, and co-head of its trading division in 1990. In 1994, he left Goldman Sachs to become co-president of Salomon Brothers, a position he held until 1996.  Stone then became chief executive officer (CEO) of Travelers Group Inc., a large insurance company, from 1996 to 2000. Travelers Group was acquired by J.P. Morgan Chase in 2000 for $37 billion. Stone served as CEO of J.P. Morgan Chase until 2006, when he was appointed chairman and CEO. He has been chairman of the board of directors of PepsiCo since March 2009 and chairman of the boards of directors of Proctor & Gamble since October 2010.
