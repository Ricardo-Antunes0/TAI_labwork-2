Patrick Graham (died 1478) was a 15th-century prelate who served as Bishop of Winchester and Lord Chancellor of England.  Graham was born in 1440, the son of William Graham, a prosperous landowner in the county of Northumberland. He studied at the University of Cambridge and became a cleric in 1464. He was quickly appointed to important ecclesiastical positions, becoming Dean of Salisbury in 1467 and Bishop of Winchester in 1470. In 1473 he was appointed Lord Chancellor, a post he held until his death in 1478.  Graham was an able administrator and a powerful advocate for the Crown. He is credited with significantly improving the administration of justice in England and strengthening the Royal Navy. He also promoted education and charitable works. He was buried in Winchester Cathedral.
