Anatolie Nosatîi (born 12 September 1972, in Chişinău) is a Moldovan composer who specializes in contemporary classical music.  Nosatîi was born into a family of musicians. His father, Moldovan trombonist and composer Vadim Nosatîi, is also a prominent figure in the Moldovan music scene. After completing his secondary education at the Chişinău Music School and the Moscow Tchaikovsky Conservatory, Nosatîi continued his studies at the University of Southern California, where he received his Master's degree in 2001.  Nosatîi's compositions have been performed by leading ensembles and orchestras around the world, including the Berlin Philharmonic, the Cleveland Orchestra, the Moscow Symphony Orchestra, and the Saint Petersburg Philharmonic. In 2009, he was awarded the prestigious Koussevitzky Prize for his orchestral work "Symphony No. 2".
