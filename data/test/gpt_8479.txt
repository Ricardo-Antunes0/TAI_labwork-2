A hippie, also spelled hippy, especially in the early 1960s and 1970s, was a young person who followed a lifestyle associated with the hippie movement. Originating in the United States during the early 1960s, the hippie movement was a reaction against the conventional values of society and the materialism of American society. The hippie lifestyle typically involved a disregard for conventions, relaxed dress and hairstyles, free love, and a strong belief in peace, love, and freedom. Many hippies also participated in various forms of activism and communitarianism.
