Tomaž Kavčič  (born 28 November 1953) is a Slovenian philosopher, mathematician and academic.  Kavčič is currently Full Professor at the University of Ljubljana, where he also served as rector from 2007 to 2013. He is a member of the Slovenian Academy of Sciences and Arts, and the European Academy of Sciences and Arts. He has been a visiting professor at several universities worldwide, including Princeton University, the University of California at Berkeley, the University of Oxford and the École Normale Supérieure in Paris.  Kavčič's research spans many areas of philosophy and mathematics, but has been particularly noted for his work in the areas of set theory, mathematical logic, and philosophical logic. He is also known for his work on the foundations of mathematics, philosophy of mathematics, mathematical physics, probabilistic epistemology, and philosophy of science.  Kavčič was born in Celje in central Slovenia on 28 November 1953. After completing his undergraduate studies at the University of Ljubljana in 1975, he completed his Phd at the University of Texas at Austin in 1980.   In 1981 he became a research associate at the Institute for Mathematical Sciences at the University of California at Berkeley.
