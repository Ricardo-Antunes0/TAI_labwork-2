Mohamed Khaled bin Nordin (Jawi: محمد خالد بن نordin; born 14 November 1975) is a Malaysian footballer who currently plays for Selangor in the Malaysian Premier League.  Nordin started his career with hometown club Negeri Sembilan in 1994 before moving to Pahang in 1998. He also played for Terengganu, Kelantan and Selangor between 1998 and 2008. Nordin has won two Malaysia Cup titles with Selangor in 2006 and 2007, as well as three Super League titles with the club. He also played in the 2006 AFC Champions League and the 2007 AFC Cup.  Nordin made his international debut for Malaysia in 2000 and has since earned over 50 caps, scoring six goals. He represented Malaysia at the 2002 FIFA World Cup, the 2004 Summer Olympics, the 2005 ASEAN Football Federation Championship, the 2006 World Cup qualifiers, the 2007 AFC Asian Cup, and the 2010 World Cup. Nordin is also a member of the Malaysia national team coaching staff.
