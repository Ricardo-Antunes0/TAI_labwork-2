Heriberto Morales Jr. (born January 24, 1975) is an American former professional boxer who competed from 1999 to 2017. He is a two-time world champion, having held the IBF junior welterweight title from 2002 to 2004 and the WBC welterweight title from 2006 to 2007. Morales also held the WBO welterweight title from 2009 to 2013, and the IBO welterweight title from 2007 to 2009. He was a two-time winner of The Ring magazine's Fighter of the Year award, and was named ESPN The Magazine's Fighter of the Year in 2007.  Morales made his professional debut in 1999, and won his first eighteen fights by knockout or technical decision. He captured the IBF junior welterweight title by defeating Randall Bailey on September 9, 2002. Morales defended his title six times before losing it to Ricky Hatton on November 22, 2003. He rebounded with a victory over Carlos Baldomir on February 25, 2004, to win his second world championship. In 2006 he defeated Shane Mosley to win the WBC welterweight championship, and defended it twice before losing it to Manny Pacquiao on November 13, 2007. Morales regained the WBO welterweight title in 2009 by defeating Paul Williams. He defended his title three times before losing it to Timothy Bradley on July 12, 2013. Morales regained the IBO welterweight title in 2014 by defeating Devon Alexander. He defended his title once before losing it to Devon Alexander on June 18, 2017.
