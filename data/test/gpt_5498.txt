The Via Delphi Institute for Research on the Ancient World is a scholarly research institute located in Rome, Italy. It is dedicated to the study of the ancient world, especially Greece and Rome.  The Via Delphi Institute was founded in 1990 by the Italian historian and archaeologist Massimo Pallottino. The Institute's first director was the classical philologist Pietro Battaglini. The Institute currently has around sixty employees, including fifteen professors. The Institute's main library is located in the Palazzo Massimo alle Terme.  The Institute publishes two journals, "Atti della Via Delphi" (abstracts of proceedings) and "Quaderni di Via Delphi" (reviews of books and articles on classical studies).
