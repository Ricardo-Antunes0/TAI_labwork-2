Le Chef  is a 2012 French-Belgian drama film directed by Jean-Pierre and Luc Dardenne. It was screened in the Directors' Fortnight section at the 2012 Cannes Film Festival.   The film tells the story of Christian (Julien Deroubaix), a young chef who is struggling to keep his restaurant afloat in the face of mounting competition. When Christian's girlfriend (Cécile De France) leaves him, he sinks into a deep depression. However, he is inspired to come back to life when he is offered the chance to work with a prestigious Michelin-starred chef (Thierry Frémaux) at his new restaurant.   Critical response to Le Chef has been positive, with many reviewers praising the Dardenne brothers' direction and Deroubaix's performance. At the 86th Academy Awards, the film was nominated for Best Foreign Language Film.
