Mark Raymond Woodforde, OAM (born 23 September 1954) is a former Australian rules footballer who played with Melbourne in the Victorian Football League (VFL). He also played for Essendon in the Australian Football League (AFL).   Woodforde, a versatile player, was used as both a key forward and on the wing. He was Melbourne's leading goalkicker for four consecutive seasons from 1977 to 1980. He finished his career with 301 goals from 174 games, which placed him third on Melbourne's all-time list behind Norm Smith and Wayne Carey. Woodforde was inducted into the Australian Football Hall of Fame in 2003.  Since retiring from football, Woodforde has worked as a television commentator, most notably for the AFL on Fox Footy.
