Michael Netzer (born 9 October 1955) is a Swiss film director and screenwriter. He has directed more than twenty films since 1984. He is also a screenwriter and has written the screenplays for many of his films.  Netzer was born in Zürich, Switzerland, to a Sephardi Jewish family. He studied film at the Zurich Film School and then worked as an assistant director on several feature films before making his debut as a director with the short film "Kindergarten" (1981). His first feature film, "Das verlorene Paradies" (1987), was critically acclaimed and won Netzer the Swiss Film Award for Best Director.  Since then, Netzer has directed numerous other feature films, including "Der Schatten des Kreuzes" (1991), "Die letzten Tage der Menschheit" (2004), "Schweizer Familie" (2006), and "Land der Kühe" (2015). He has also written the screenplays for many of his films, including "Das verlorene Paradies", "Der Schatten des Kreuzes", "Die letzten Tage der Menschheit", and "Schweizer Familie". Netzer has been nominated for the German Film Award for Best Screenplay twice, for "Das verlorene Paradies" in 1987 and for "Die letzten Tage der Menschheit" in 2005.
