Lionel Frederick Marson  (15 June 1895 â€“ 16 October 1918) was an English composer, arranger and pianist.  Marson was born in Hampstead, London, the son of the music publisher George Marson. He was educated at Westminster School and the Royal College of Music, where he studied piano with Arthur Benjamin and composition with Arnold Bax. He embarked on a musical career in 1915, arranging and composing music for silent films. He was killed in action during the First World War.  Marson's compositions include orchestral works, film scores, solo piano pieces, songs and choral works. His best-known works are the orchestral suite "The Planets" (1938), the song cycle "Three Songs from an Unfinished Opera" (1935), and the piano piece "The Leaves That Fall" (1915).
