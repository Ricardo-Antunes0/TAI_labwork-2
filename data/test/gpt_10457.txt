Melinda Lopez is an actress, playwright, and singer. Lopez was born and raised in East Harlem, in New York City. She is of Puerto Rican and Dominican descent.  Lopez began her career as a child actress, appearing in television shows and films. She made her Broadway debut in the play "A Soldier's Play" in 1998. Lopez had a successful career as an actress, appearing in films such as "Boys Don't Cry", "Garden State", and "The Cell". She also had recurring roles on television shows such as "Ugly Betty", "The O.C.", and "Desperate Housewives". In 2011, Lopez released her first album, entitled "The Voice".  Lopez is also a playwright. Her plays include "In the Heights" and "The Mambo Kings Play Songs of Love". Lopez has also released two albums of salsa music: "Melinda" (2005) and "Mi Vida" (2009).
