Ernest Keith Smith, known professionally as Ernest (musician), is an American singer, songwriter, and record producer. He first came to prominence as the lead vocalist and songwriter of the rock band Poco, before going on to found his own solo career in the early 1980s.  Ernest has released six studio albums, two compilations, and numerous singles and EPs over the course of his career. He has sold over 20 million records worldwide, making him one of the most successful rock artists of all time. In 2003, he was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.  Ernest was born in Butte, Montana in 1951. He formed Poco with drummer Billy Talbot and bassist Bob Moore in 1975, after meeting while working at a local gold mine. After releasing their debut album in 1978, Poco achieved mainstream success with their follow-up album, 1979's The Unforgiven. The album spawned three hit singles—"Precious metals", "I Can't Tell You Why", and "Mr. Tambourine Man"—and established Poco as one of the leading rock bands of the early 1980s.  In 1981, Poco disbanded following the death of Moore from a drug overdose. Ernest subsequently founded the band Ernestina with childhood friend Ray Kennedy on guitar and lead vocals. The band released one album before disbanding in 1984. In 1985, Ernest formed the band Ernest which released two albums before disbanding in 1990.
