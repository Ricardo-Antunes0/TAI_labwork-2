Fool the World: The Oral History of the Global Dance Music Revolution is a book by David Sheff, an American journalist and author. It is a comprehensive oral history of the global dance music revolution, covering the period from the late 1980s to the early 2000s.  The book was first published in 2006 and revised and updated in 2008. It was named one of the Best Books of 2006 by "The New York Times".  Fool the World tells the story of a decade-long musical revolution, during which dance music became one of the most popular genres in the world. Drawing on interviews with artists, promoters, journalists and others involved in the scene, Sheff captures the excitement, romance and chaos of this transformative time. He describes how dance music stormed into popular consciousness, fuelling a global craze for electronic dance music (EDM). From its roots in Chicago's acid house scene and early UK rave culture, Fool the World follows EDM's ascent to global domination. Along the way we meet pioneering DJs such as Paul Oakenfold and Sasha and John Digweed; pioneering labels such as Ministry of Sound and A&M Records; and iconic venues like The Paradise Garage and Ministry of Sound in London, The Mayan in Chicago, Ultra Music Festival in Miami Beach and Coachella Valley Music And Arts Festival in Indio, California.
