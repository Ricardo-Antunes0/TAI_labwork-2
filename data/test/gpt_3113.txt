Michael Joseph O'Higgins (1 November 1917 – 14 November 1987) was an Irish politician who served twice as Prime Minister of Ireland, first from 1973 to 1977, and again from 1981 to 1982. He also served as Minister for Foreign Affairs, Trade and Industry, and Defence.  O'Higgins was the leader of the Irish Labour Party from 1962 to 1975 and Leader of the Opposition from 1975 to 1977. He served as the eighteenth President of Ireland from 1979 to 1981.  Born in Clonmel, County Tipperary, O'Higgins was educated at Clonmel CBS and Trinity College, Dublin. He became involved in politics as a young man and became a member of the Irish Labour Party in 1939. He served as Minister for Posts and Telegraphs (1945–1946) and Minister for Economic Development (1946–1947). He was elected to Dáil Éireann as a TD for Tipperary North in 1948. He became Minister for Finance (1948–1950) and Minister for Industry (1950–1951).  In 1951, O'Higgins was elected leader of the Irish Labour Party following the death of Eamon de Valera. O'Higgins led the party until his resignation in 1975. During his time as leader, he led Labour to two general election victories – in 1954 and 1957.
