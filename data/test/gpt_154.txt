The Plymouth Pinelands, also known as the Pinelands National Reserve, is a protected area located in southern New Jersey, United States. The reserve is located in the lower sections of the Raritan Valley and encompasses an area of about 295 square miles.  The reserve was created in 1978 as part of the Pinelands National Forest and includes portions of Morris, Sussex, and Warren counties. The reserve is managed by the United States Forest Service. The reserve is a popular spot for hiking, biking, birdwatching, fishing, and hunting.
