He was a co-founder of the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 1934 with Paul Ehrlich, Konrad Lorenz, and Gerhard Domagk.  Nijland was born on 26 February 1880 in The Hague, Netherlands. He studied at the University of Amsterdam where he received his doctorate in 1903. In 1907 he became a professor at the University of Amsterdam. In 1928 he became a professor at the University of Leiden. He died on 14 May 1967 in Leiden.  Nijland is best known for his work on metabolism and the role of enzymes in biological reactions. He shared the Nobel Prize in 1934 with Paul Ehrlich, Konrad Lorenz, and Gerhard Domagk for their work on enzyme research.
