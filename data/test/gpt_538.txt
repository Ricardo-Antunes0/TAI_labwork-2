Hilda Lindley, born Hilda Livingston, (5 October 1907 â€“ 20 January 1992) was a British actress.  Lindley was born in London, England, the daughter of actors Richard Livingston and Enid Mason. She made her stage debut in 1926 in a production of "The Heiress". She went on to appear in such plays as "The Importance of Being Earnest", "The Vortex", "A Streetcar Named Desire", "Cat on a Hot Tin Roof", and "Death of a Salesman".  In 1935, Lindley won the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress for her performance as Mama Morton in the film version of Tennessee Williams' play "The Glass Menagerie". She also received nominations for her roles in "The Animal Kingdom", "A Place in the Sun", and "The Nun's Story".  Lindley retired from acting in the early 1960s. She died in London, England, aged 88.
