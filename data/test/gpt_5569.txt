Szentpétery Ádám (; born 24 February 1956) is a Hungarian football manager and former player who played as a goalkeeper.  Szentpétery was born in Budapest, Hungary. He began his career with local side Vasas SC, before moving to Bundesliga side 1. FC Köln. After two seasons in Germany, Szentpétery moved back to his homeland to join MTK Hungária FC. After two seasons in Hungary he returned to Germany and signed for Hamburger SV. Szentpétery spent six seasons at Hamburger SV before retiring at the end of the 1989–90 season.  Szentpétery began his managerial career with Hungarian side Puskás Akadémia FC in the early 1990s. He later managed German side VfB Stuttgart and Spanish side Racing Santander. In 2006, Szentpétery was appointed manager of English side Liverpool F.C., but was sacked after just 18 months in charge.  In 2007, Szentpétery was appointed manager of Australian side Melbourne Victory FC, but he resigned after just one season in charge. In January 2010, Szentpétery was appointed manager of Turkish side Fenerbahçe S.K., but he resigned after just one season in charge. In March 2011, Szentpétery was appointed manager of Hungarian side Debrecen FK, but he resigned after just 11 games in charge.
