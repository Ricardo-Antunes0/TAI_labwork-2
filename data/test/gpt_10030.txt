Berlin Bound is a World War I propaganda film directed by Frank Capra and released in 1941. The film is about a group of American soldiers who are sent to Berlin to help guard the city against possible attack by the Nazis.  The film was produced on a very small budget and received mixed reviews from critics. However, it has been noted for its excellent propaganda value and its effective use of cinematic techniques to convey its message.  Berlin Bound was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Writing, Story and Screenplay, but lost to Casablanca.
