He is considered one of the most eminent Islamic scholars and leaders in Pakistan, where he is also credited with helping to revive the country's Muslim faith during a time of great turmoil.  Muhammad was born in 1893 in a village near Multan in British India to Muhammad Ismail and Khadija Bibi. He studied Islamic theology at Aligarh Muslim University, where he earned his bachelor's degree in 1916 and his master's degree in 1919. In 1923, Muhammad earned his doctorate from the same institution with a thesis on "The Principles of Islamic Jurisprudence".  After completing his education, Muhammad began teaching at the mosque school in his hometown. He soon became known as an eminent scholar, and in 1933, he was appointed chief judge of Rawalpindi district. In 1942, he was appointed the mufti (grand mufti) of Pakistan, a position he held until 1958. During that time, he helped to revive Islam in a time of great turmoil, helping to quell religious violence and establish Islamic law as the official law of Pakistan. Muhammad died on 15 November 1977 at the age of 83.
