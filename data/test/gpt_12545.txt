John Walter Heselton (March 17, 1900 – July 4, 1966) was an American football player and coach. He played college football for the University of Michigan from 1918 to 1919 and professionally for the Canton Bulldogs of the National Football League (NFL) from 1920 to 1922. Heselton served as the head football coach at the University of California, Berkeley from 1923 to 1926, compiling a record of 10–21–2. He was also the head basketball coach at Berkeley from 1923 to 1925, tallying a mark of 8–15. Heselton died in 1966 at the age of 72.
