Peter Charles McCarthy Robinson (9 November 1951 – 6 May 2009) was an Irish Fine Gael politician who served as Taoiseach of Ireland from 1997 to 2002.  McCarthy was first elected to the Dáil as a Teachta Dála (TD) for the Dublin North-West constituency at the 1987 general election. He was appointed Minister for Health and Children in 1987, and became Minister for Foreign Affairs in 1989. He was appointed Taoiseach on 14 May 1997, following the resignation of John Bruton. He served until his resignation on 5 November 2002 following the party's defeat in the 2002 general election.
