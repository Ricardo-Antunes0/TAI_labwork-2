Club Fonograma was a United States-based daily radio show that aired from 1949 to 1971. The show was broadcast on the Mutual Broadcasting System and featured music from the 20th century.  Club Fonograma was one of the earliest radio programs to focus exclusively on music from the 20th century. The program was hosted by Lowell Thomas and featured performances by such musicians as Ella Fitzgerald, Louis Armstrong, and Benny Goodman. Over the course of its run, Club Fonograma helped to introduce new listeners to some of the most popular music of the era.
