Pensacola International Airport , formerly Pensacola Gulf Coast Regional Airport, is an international airport in Escambia County, Florida, United States. It is the principal airport for the Pensacola metropolitan area, which has a population of almost 410,000 people. The airport is located about 8 miles (13Â km) northwest of downtown Pensacola.  Pensacola International Airport was built in the 1940s and opened in 1948 as a military airfield. It served as a stop on the inaugural Pan American World Airways route to South America. In the late 1990s the airport underwent a major renovation that included new runways, an expanded passenger terminal, and additional aircraft facilities. The airport is now operated by the Pensacola Metropolitan Airport Authority. In 2014 it was ranked as the sixth busiest airport in the United States by passenger traffic.  The Pensacola International Airport covers an area of 2,903 acres (1,163 ha) at an elevation of 24 feet (7.3 m). It has two runways: 7/25 is 6,002 by 150 feet (1,829 x 46 m) with an asphalt surface and 17/35 is 4,801 by 150 feet (1,433 x 46 m) with a concrete surface. In January 2017 the FAA announced it would be adding a new runway to Pensacola International Airport which will be 5,000 by 150 feet (1,524 x 46 m).
