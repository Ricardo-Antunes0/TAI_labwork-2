Felton Jeffrey Capel II (January 6, 1953 – January 29, 2019) was an American basketball coach and player. He was the head men's basketball coach at Temple University from 2001 to 2013 and at the University of Richmond from 2014 to 2019. Capel was also a player at Temple from 1970 to 1973.  Capel was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. He played college basketball for the Temple Owls under head coach John Chaney and earned All-American honors in 1973.  After his playing career, Capel began his coaching career as an assistant coach at his alma mater, Temple. He served as Temple's head men's basketball coach from 2001 to 2013, compiling a record of 187–130. Capel left Temple after compiling an overall record of 186–130. He then became head coach of the University of Richmond Tigers in 2014. In his one season at Richmond, he led the Tigers to the American Athletic Conference Tournament championship game, where they lost to Memphis.  Capel died on January 29, 2019, at the age of 71 after a long battle with leukemia.
