I) was an act of the Parliament of England that imposed a tax on the colonies of America. The act levied a fixed rate of 2 shillings per pound sterling on all goods exported from the colonies, with a further duty on imports. This represented a significant increase in taxation over previous years, and proved unpopular with colonists.
