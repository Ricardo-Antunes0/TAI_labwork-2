Jesse Dee (born March 10, 1980) is an American singer and songwriter. He is the lead vocalist and primary songwriter for the American post-hardcore band From First to Last.  Jesse Dee was born in Riverside, California, on March 10, 1980. He was raised in nearby Norco and graduated from Norco High School in 1998. Dee formed his first band, Malice, in high school with friends. The band released a self-titled EP in 1998 and a full-length album in 2000.  In 2001, Jesse Dee met fellow Norco resident and future From First to Last guitarist Austin Carlile at a local show. The two became good friends and started jamming together. In 2002, they formed the band With Confidence and recorded an eight-song demo tape. The band toured throughout 2002 and 2003 as support for popular metalcore bands such as Converge and Killswitch Engage.  In 2003, Jesse Dee left With Confidence to focus on From First to Last full-time.   From First to Last released their debut album "First Step" in 2004 to critical acclaim. The album was recorded at producer Toby Wright's (The Used) studio in Hollywood, California. The album featured the singles "Lift Me Up" and "Welcome to the Family."
