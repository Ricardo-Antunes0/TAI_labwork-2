The PAST Foundation, Partnering Anthropology with  Science, Technology and Society (PAST), is an independent, non-profit research and educational institution that focuses on the application of anthropology to contemporary issues in science, technology and society.  The PAST Foundation was founded in 2001 with the goal of fostering collaborative cross-cultural research and education. The Foundation’s programs focus on the application of anthropology to contemporary issues in science, technology and society. PAST’s work has been supported by grants from the National Science Foundation, the John D. and Catherine T. MacArthur Foundation, the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, the Ford Foundation, and others.  The PAST Foundation is home to a wide range of research projects, including studies of biotechnology and genetic engineering, climate change, digital media and social networks, information technology and globalization, human rights and conflict resolution, natural resources management, and more.  In addition to its own research projects, PAST works with a number of partner institutions around the world to advance cross-cultural understanding and collaboration. These partnerships include universities in the United States (including Arizona State University, Brown University, Columbia University, Cornell University, Dartmouth College, Duke University, Georgetown University, Massachusetts Institute of Technology), Europe (including the University of Cambridge in England), Asia (including the National Taiwan University in Taiwan), Australia (the University of Melbourne), South America (the Pontifical Catholic University of Chile), and Africa (the University of Ghana).
