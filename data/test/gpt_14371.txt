Yuanyang County (; Hani: Yeiqyaq) is located in Yunnan Province, China. The county is bordered by the provinces of Sichuan and Guizhou to the east, and the Tibet Autonomous Region to the north. The county has an area of 2,023 square kilometers. The county seat is Yuanyang City.  Yuanyang County is located in the eastern part of Yunnan, bordering Sichuan to the east and Guizhou to the north. The county has an area of 2,023 square kilometers and a population of 1,826,000 as of 2010. The county seat is Yuanyang City.  The county's economy is based on agriculture and forestry. The major crops are rice, maize, potatoes, sugar beets, cotton and tea. There are also a number of animal husbandry and fisheries enterprises in the county. The major manufactures include textiles, electrical equipment and furniture. There are several hydroelectric dams in the county, providing power to local industries.  The cultural heritage of Yuanyang County includes ancient temples such as Baishiyun Temple and Longmen Temple as well as more recent tourist attractions such as the Jade Dragon Snow Mountain National Park and Kunming Zoo. There are also many traditional villages in the county with distinctive cultural practices still practiced today.
