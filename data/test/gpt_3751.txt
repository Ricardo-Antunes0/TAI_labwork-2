Born in 1946 in Oklahoma City, Oklahoma, O'Neil began her career as a backing vocalist for country singer Johnny Cash. She recorded her first album in 1971 and continued to release albums throughout the 1970s and 1980s. Her most successful period came in the early 1980s, when she had several Top 40 hits including "I Can't Quit You Baby", "I'll Always Love You", and "Ain't That a Shame". O'Neil died of breast cancer in 1995.
