Douglas Alan Racine (born October 7, 1952) is a Canadian retired professional ice hockey defenceman who played in the National Hockey League (NHL) for the Philadelphia Flyers, Quebec Nordiques, and Calgary Flames. He was also a member of the Canada national ice hockey team.  Racine was born in Toronto, Ontario. He played junior hockey for the Peterborough Petes of the Ontario Hockey League (OHL). He was drafted by the Philadelphia Flyers in the fifth round of the 1970 NHL Amateur Draft. Racine spent three seasons with the Flyers, playing in 80 games and scoring nine goals and adding 22 assists. Racine then signed with the Quebec Nordiques in 1976. Racine played six seasons with Quebec, registering 238 points (24 goals and 222 assists) in 578 games. Racine then joined the Calgary Flames for the 1981–82 season. Racine played his final NHL season with Calgary, playing 43 games and scoring seven goals and adding 18 assists. Racine retired after the 1982–83 season.  Racine played 12 seasons of international hockey for Canada, winning two silver medals at the World Championships (1977 and 1979). He also won a bronze medal at the 1981 World Championships.
