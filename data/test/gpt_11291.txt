Albert Kresch (born July 4, 1922)   Albert Kresch was born on July 4, 1922 in Vienna, Austria. He is an Austrian artist who has been internationally acclaimed for his unique painting techniques and often surrealistic images. His paintings are noted for their use of vivid colors and imaginative compositions.  Kresch began his artistic career in the early 1940s, working as a painter and sculptor. He later moved to Paris, France in the 1950s and began to gain recognition for his work. He has since exhibited his work throughout the world, including at the Museum of Modern Art in New York City and the Whitney Museum of American Art in New York City.  Kresch is a member of the Salzburg Academy of Fine Arts and the Royal Academy of Arts in London. He has also been awarded numerous awards, including the Grand Prize at the Venice Biennale in 1966 and the prestigious Hans Arp Medal from the Royal Academy of Arts in London in 1984.
