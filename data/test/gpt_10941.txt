Donald Gorman (born 1937 or 1938) is a Canadian musician, songwriter, and record producer.  Gorman was born in Toronto, Ontario, and started playing guitar at the age of eight. He attended York University and studied music composition under Nadia Boulanger.   Gorman became a professional musician in the early 1960s, playing guitar with various rock and blues bands. He eventually formed his own band, the Don Gorman Band, which achieved success in Canada and Europe. Gorman also produced albums for other artists. In the late 1980s, he retired from performing to focus on his record production career.  Gorman has received several awards, including two Juno Awards and two Grammy Awards. He has been inducted into the Canadian Music Hall of Fame and the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
