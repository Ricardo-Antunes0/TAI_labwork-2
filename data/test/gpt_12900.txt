A 6'6" (1.98 m) forward/center, Winn played in the National Basketball Association (NBA) for the Detroit Pistons and Philadelphia 76ers from 1941 to 1947. He was a member of the United States national team at the 1948 Olympics and the 1950 World Championship.  Winn was born in Detroit, Michigan, to Edward Lawrence Winn Sr., an insurance agent, and Thelma Winn. He attended Lawrence Tech University in Appleton, Wisconsin, where he played for the school's basketball team. He was drafted by the Detroit Pistons in the third round of the 1941 NBA draft. In his first season with the Pistons, he averaged 10.5 points per game. He was traded to the Philadelphia 76ers in 1947 and retired after playing in only nine games that season due to a knee injury.  After retiring from playing basketball, Winn worked as a vice president at Quaker Oats. He died of a heart attack at age 80 in Germantown, Maryland.
