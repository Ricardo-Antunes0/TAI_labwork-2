Gered Mankowitz  (born 3 August 1946) is a British-born American artist and musician, who has lived and worked in Los Angeles since the early 1970s. Mankowitz is known for his paintings, sculptures, film and video art, and music.  Mankowitz was born in London to a Jewish family. He studied at the Slade School of Fine Art from 1966 to 1969. In 1971, he moved to the United States, where he lived in Los Angeles for the next several decades. Mankowitz's early paintings were influenced by the abstract Expressionist painters of the 1940s and 1950s, but he soon developed his own unique style. His paintings typically feature bright colors and intricate patterns, and often depict scenes from everyday life. Mankowitz has also made sculptures, videos, and installations that explore similar themes. He has exhibited his work worldwide, including at the Museum of Modern Art in New York City and the Tate Gallery in London. Mankowitz has been awarded numerous prizes and awards, including a MacArthur Fellowship in 1982.
