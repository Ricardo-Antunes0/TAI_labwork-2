Ann Thwaite (born 4 October 1932) is a British academic and writer.  Born in 1932, Ann Thwaite was educated at the University of Cambridge, where she graduated with a degree in English Literature. She subsequently undertook postgraduate study at the University of Oxford, where she completed a D.Litt. in 1965.  Thwaite has held teaching positions at the universities of Cambridge, Oxford and York, before joining the University of Sheffield as a professor in 1984. She retired from her position there in 2001, but continues to write and teach part-time.  Thwaite's work centres around the study of Romantic poetry and literature, particularly that of William Wordsworth, Percy Bysshe Shelley, and John Keats. Her publications include books on Wordsworth and Shelley as well as essays on Romanticism and its influence on 20th century British poetry.  Ann Thwaite was awarded an honorary doctorate by the University of Sheffield in 2002.
