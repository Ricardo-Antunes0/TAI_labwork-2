Giuseppe Siboni (27 January 1780 – 28 December 1846) was an Italian composer, conductor, and pianist.  Born in Milan, Siboni studied at the Conservatory with Luigi Cherubini and Niccolò Paganini. In 1803 he won the prestigious Prix de Rome for his cantata "Il ritorno d'Ulisse in patria". After completing his studies, he went to Naples and Milan to teach. He returned to Rome in 1816, where he became a professor at the Accademia di Santa Cecilia. He was also a conductor of the orchestra at the Teatro Regio and of the Accademia Filarmonica di Bologna.  Siboni composed operas, ballets, chamber music, sacred works, and guitar pieces. His most famous works include the opera seria "Norma", the ballet "Leucippe e Clitophon", and the string quartet "Concerto grosso in D major". He also wrote a number of highly successful operas for children, including "Il bagnetto" and "Il barbiere di Siviglia". He died in Bologna.
