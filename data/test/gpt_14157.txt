The Oryx nome (Egyptian: Ma-hedj) was one of the thirty nomes of ancient Egypt. It was situated in the southern part of the country, on the east bank of the Nile.  The nome was divided into two districts: Western Oryx and Eastern Oryx. The main city within the nome was Apis (Aphs). Other important towns within the nome include Menet, Hemet, Iunu and Abu.
