Dato' Dr. Sahruddin bin Jamal (born 26 October 1949) is a Malaysian academic and diplomat. He has served as the Malaysian ambassador to the United States of America, Canada, and China, as well as Malaysia's permanent representative to the United Nations.  Born in Ipoh, Perak, Sahruddin is a graduate of the University of London with a Bachelor of Arts degree in Political Science, and a Doctor of Philosophy degree from the University of Cambridge. He also holds a Master's degree from Columbia University in International Relations and a Diploma in French from the University of London.  He began his career with the Malaysian Foreign Service in 1973. He has served in various capacities at the Malaysian Embassy in Washington, D.C., including Deputy Chief of Mission (DCM) and Ambassador (2001-2004). He was Malaysia's Permanent Representative to the United Nations from 2004 to 2007. From 2007 to 2010, he served as Malaysia's Ambassador to Canada. From 2010 to 2014, he served as Malaysia's Ambassador to China. He retired from the Malaysian Foreign Service in 2014 after 38 years of service.  He is currently a Professor at the Lee Kuan Yew School of Public Policy at National University of Singapore and a Nonresident Senior Fellow at Singapore Institute for International Affairs.
