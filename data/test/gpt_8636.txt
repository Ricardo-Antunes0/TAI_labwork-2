The Nigerian Armed Forces (NAF) are the military forces of Nigeria. The Nigerian Armed Forces are a part of the Nigerian Defence Forces, which were established on 11 October 1961. As of 2009, the Nigerian Armed Forces had a personnel strength of more than 500,000.  The Nigerian Armed Forces are divided into three branches: the Army, the Navy and the Air Force. The Nigerian Air Force is the newest branch of the Nigerian Armed Forces and was established on 1 April 1991. The Nigerian Army is the largest branch of the Nigerian Armed Forces and is responsible for land-based military operations. The Nigerian Navy is responsible for maritime operations and is composed of a fleet of over 100 vessels. The Nigerian Army has a strength of around 500,000 personnel while the Nigerian Navy has a strength of around 100,000 personnel.
