He was a member of the jazz fusion band The Mahavishnu Orchestra, and is best known as the creator of the soundtracks for the films The Beatles: Yellow Submarine (1968), The Who's Tommy (1969), and Monty Python's Life of Brian (1979).  Whitehead was born in 1928 in Brooklyn, New York City. After graduating from high school in 1946, he studied at the Art Students League of New York. In 1951, he moved to Los Angeles to study at the Chouinard Art Institute. He was drafted into the United States Army in 1954 and served in Germany. He returned to Los Angeles in 1955 and continued his studies at Chouinard. In 1957, he moved to Chicago and started playing with Milt Jackson's group.  In 1960, Whitehead formed The Mahavishnu Orchestra with John McLaughlin, Larry Young, Rick Danko, and Billy Cobham. The Mahavishnu Orchestra became one of the most popular jazz fusion bands of the early 1970s. Whitehead's recordings with The Mahavishnu Orchestra include The Inner Mounting Flame (1968), Birds of Fire (1970), and The Cosmic Dance (1973).
