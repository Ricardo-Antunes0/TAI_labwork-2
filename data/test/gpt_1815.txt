Bronwyn Nanette Curtis  is a British actress and singer.  Curtis was born in London, the daughter of actress Heather Angel and manager Peter Curtis. She has a sister, actress Poppy Curtis, and a brother, actor Freddie Curtis. Her paternal grandfather was Welsh actor Terence Davies and her maternal grandmother was the actress Moyna Macgill.  After leaving school at the age of 16, Curtis began her career as an actor in regional theatre. In 2002, she made her West End debut in the play "The Recruiting Officer". The following year, she starred in the London production of "Hairspray", for which she was nominated for a Laurence Olivier Award for Best Actress in a Musical.  Curtis' breakthrough role came in 2007 when she portrayed Lady Thiang in the BBC series "Dragonstone". The show ran for two seasons and won Curtis several accolades, including an International Emmy Award for Best Actress in a Drama Series. In 2009, she starred as Peggy Schuyler in the Fox TV series "Jenny", for which she received a Golden Globe Award nomination for Best Actress – Television Series Drama.   In 2013, Curtis appeared as Countess Zsófia Deák in the BBC drama series "Downton Abbey".
