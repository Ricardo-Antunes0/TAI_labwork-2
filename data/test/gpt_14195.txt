Märta Allertz (1628 – before 1677), often credited as Märta the Elder, was a Swedish painter, draughtsman, and etcher who was one of the most important representatives of the Dutch Golden Age of painting.  Born in 1628 in Uppsala, Märta Allertz is generally considered to be one of the most important representatives of the Dutch Golden Age of painting. She was a pupil of Johannes Vermeer and likely also worked with him on many projects. Her work is known for its delicacy and realism, as well as its religious and mythological themes. Märta Allertz died before 1677, and much of her work remains unknown.
