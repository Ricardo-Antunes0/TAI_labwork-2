Thomas Joseph Haas (born March 5, 1951) is an American politician who is a member of the Republican Party. He has been the U.S. Representative for Maryland's 8th congressional district since 2013.  Haas was born in Baltimore, Maryland, and graduated from Archbishop Keough High School in 1969. He received his B.A. from the University of Maryland in 1972 and his J.D. from the University of Maryland Law School in 1975. He was admitted to the Maryland bar in 1975 and commenced practice with the law firm of Miller, Canfield, Paddock and Stone in Baltimore, Maryland.  Haas served as a board member of Baltimore County Council from 1996 to 2002 and was twice elected chairman of that body. He also served as a delegate to the Republican National Convention from Maryland in 1996 and 2000.   In 2002, Haas ran for Congress in MD-8 against Democratic incumbent Barbara A. Mikulski, but lost by a wide margin. In 2004 he ran again against Mikulski and won by a wider margin, becoming one of only two Republicans to hold a congressional seat in Maryland at the time (the other being Charles Canady).  In 2012, he was challenged by Democrat John Delaney, but easily won re-election with 60% of the vote.
