Denver Clayton Lemaster (born February 25, 1939) is a former American football player and coach. He was the head football coach at San Diego State University from 1980 to 1982, compiling a record of 20–29. Lemaster served as the defensive coordinator for the Miami Hurricanes of the National Football League (NFL) from 1984 to 1990, compiling a record of 45–38.  Lemaster was born in Denver, Colorado. He played college football at the University of Wyoming and was drafted in the third round of the 1960 NFL Draft by the Detroit Lions. Lemaster played for the Lions from 1960 to 1963 and then spent four seasons (1965 to 1968) with the Denver Broncos. He was a member of the Broncos team that won Super Bowl II. Lemaster was inducted into the Broncos' Hall of Fame in 1985.  Lemaster served as defensive coordinator for the Miami Hurricanes of the NFL from 1984 to 1990, compiling a record of 45–38. After leaving Miami, Lemaster was named head football coach at San Diego State University in 1980. In his two seasons as head coach, Lemaster compiled a record of 20–29. Lemaster resigned from his position at San Diego State in 1982 to take a position with Ford Motor Company.
