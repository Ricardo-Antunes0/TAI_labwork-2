The year 1936 saw a number of significant radio milestones. These included the debut of commercial radio broadcasting in the United States with KDKA in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, and the first transatlantic telephone call, made by Guglielmo Marconi from Newfoundland to Cornwall, England. Other significant events include the first broadcast of a regular news program in the United States, The CBS Evening News; the first regular broadcast of a live baseball game, played between the Brooklyn Dodgers and Boston Red Sox; and the first regular broadcasts of television programming in the United States, featuring shows produced by RCA and NBC.
