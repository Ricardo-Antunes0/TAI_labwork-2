The blue jay (Cyanocitta cristata) is a common and widespread bird in North America. It is the only member of the genus Cyanocitta. The blue jay is a medium-sized bird, measuring 28 to 38 cm (11 to 15 in) in length, including the tail, and weighing around 250 g (9.8 oz). Adult males are mainly blue, while females and young birds are mainly gray. The blue jay is one of the most common North American birds and ranges from southern Canada to Tierra del Fuego.  The blue jay is a social bird and lives in flocks of up to 30 members. It feeds mainly on insects, although it will also eat small seeds, fruits, and other items. It builds its nest out of sticks, but may also use tree cavities or abandoned avian nests. The blue jay is an active forager and often walks or hops about while foraging.
