The Teachout Building is an historic building in downtown Albany, New York. It is located on the northwest corner of Washington and Broadway, and is one of the most prominent landmarks in the city. The building was designed by architect William R. Coe and built in 1905. It is a seven-story brick structure with a terra cotta facade and Classical Revival features. It was originally home to the Albany Evening Journal, which relocated to new offices in 1937. In 1985, it was converted into apartments.   The Teachout Building is a well-known Albany landmark and has been variously used for office space, as a printing plant, and as apartments. The building is architecturally significant for its Classical Revival features, terra cotta facade, and location on Washington and Broadway.
