Cyril James Tucker CBE (17 November 19113 December 1992) was a Welsh Anglican priest and theologian, who served as Archbishop of Canterbury from 1970 to 1978. He was also a noted academic.  Cyril Tucker was born in Wales in 1911, the son of a Church of England clergyman. After attending the University of Oxford, he was ordained deacon in 1934 and priest in 1935. He served his first curacy at St Helen's Bishopsgate in London, before becoming Professor of Divinity at the University of Wales, Cardiff. In 1950 he became Bishop of Monmouth, and then Archbishop of Canterbury in 1970. In 1978 he retired from both positions.  Tucker played an important role in developing the theology of the Church of England during his time as Archbishop of Canterbury. He also wrote several books on theology and religious education. He died in 1992.
