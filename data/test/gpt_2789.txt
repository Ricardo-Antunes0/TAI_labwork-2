Frank Thomas Solich (born September 8, 1944) is an American former professional basketball player who played in the American Basketball Association (ABA) and National Basketball Association (NBA).  Born in Joliet, Illinois, Solich played college basketball at the University of Illinois. He was drafted by the Denver Rockets in the first round of the 1967 NBA draft, but did not play in the league. He played for the ABA's Indiana Pacers from 1969 to 1974, averaging 14.2 points and 7.3 assists per game. He was named to the ABA All-Star team three times, and was an ABA champion with the Pacers in 1972. He also won a championship with the Detroit Pistons in 1988. In 12 seasons in the NBA, he averaged 10.7 points and 5.4 assists per game.
