The Dunmore School District is a small, rural public school district in the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania. The district serves the borough of Dunmore and parts of the borough of Greensburg. The population of the district was 2,738 as of the 2010 census.  The Dunmore School District was established in 1868. The original school was built on land donated by Colonel James Dunmore. The building served as a school until 1931, when it was destroyed by fire. A new school was built on the same site and opened in 1932.  In 2006, the Dunmore School District was ranked 279th out of 498 Pennsylvania school districts by the Pittsburgh Business Times. The ranking was based on student academic achievement as demonstrated on the PSSAs for: reading, writing, math and science. The PSSAs are given to all children in grades 3rd through 8th in public schools in Pennsylvania.
