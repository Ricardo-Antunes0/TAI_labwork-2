Co-production is a practice in the delivery of public services where both the public and private sectors are involved in the development and delivery of the service.  Co-production has been recognised as a means to improve the quality of public services, as well as to reduce costs. It often involves joint working between public and private sector partners, and can involve the sharing of resources and expertise.  Co-production is also often seen as a way to build trust between the public and private sectors, and to create a more collaborative environment. It can help to reduce duplication of effort, and to ensure that services are delivered in a cost-effective way.  There are a number of benefits associated with co-production, including:  improved quality of service reduced costs increased efficiency increased collaboration between the public and private sectors
