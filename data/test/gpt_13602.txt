Bob Giraldi (born January 17, 1939) is an American journalist, author, and commentator.  Giraldi was born in Newark, New Jersey. After graduating from high school in 1957, he began his career as a copyboy at the Newark Star-Ledger. He then worked at the Philadelphia Inquirer before joining the New York Daily News in 1967. Giraldi was the first African American to be named editorial page editor of a major U.S. newspaper. He was also the first African American to be a regular panelist on ABC's Nightline, and he has been a frequent guest on other television programs.  Giraldi has written extensively about American politics, culture, and society. His best-known books are The Unfinished Journey: Barack Obama's America (2009) and The Campaign: Obama '08 (2008). He is also the author of A Matter of Justice: The Race, the Class and the Politics of Poverty in America (2006), which was awarded the John Hope Franklin Prize for commentary from the Southern Historical Association.  In addition to his writing, Giraldi has been a frequent commentator on television and radio programs. He has also served as a senior fellow at The Century Foundation and as a visiting professor at Princeton University's Woodrow Wilson School of Public and International Affairs. Giraldi is currently a senior fellow at The Washington Institute for Near East Policy.
