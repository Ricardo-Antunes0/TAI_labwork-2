Lin Bing,  (also called Lin Ping) (born August 1949) is a Chinese composer, conductor and pianist.  Lin Bing was born in 1949 in Shanghai. He started taking piano lessons at the age of six and soon began composing. Lin Bing studied at the Shanghai Conservatory of Music, where he graduated with honours in 1975. He then went on to study at the Royal College of Music in London, where he earned his diploma in 1978.  Lin Bing has worked as a composer, conductor and pianist. He has composed orchestral and chamber music as well as solo works for piano. His works have been performed by various symphony orchestras around the world, including the Berlin Philharmonic Orchestra, the London Symphony Orchestra and the New York Philharmonic Orchestra. Lin Bing has also conducted many of his own compositions, most notably the Symphonic Poems Symphony and the Violin Concerto.  Lin Bing has received numerous awards and accolades for his work as a composer, including the prestigious First Prize at the International Tchaikovsky Competition (1978) and the Charles Ives Award (1998). He is currently a Professor at Shanghai Conservatory of Music.
