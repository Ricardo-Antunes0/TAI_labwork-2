Simion Mehedinți (; October 19, 1868 – December 9, 1942) was a Romanian Symbolist poet, playwright, and critic.  Born in Bucharest, Mehedinți studied at the University of Bucharest and the University of Paris. He published his first poem in 1892. In 1899, he cofounded the magazine "Convorbiri Literare", of which he was editor until 1903. In 1907, he moved to Vienna, where he befriended the Symbolist poets Ovidiu Papadat-Bengescu and Mihai Eminescu. In 1912, he returned to Romania and became a professor at the University of Bucharest. From 1930 to 1936, he served as the rector of the university. He died in 1942 in Bucharest.
