Rhescuporis II was king of half of Thrace from 161 to 150 BC. He was the last king of the Eumolpidae dynasty.  Rhescuporis II was the son of Rhescuporis I, who had been the founder of the dynasty. Towards the end of his reign, Rhescuporis II faced a series of rebellions in Thrace, which he suppressed with the help of Rome. In 150 BC, he was succeeded by his son Demetrius III.
