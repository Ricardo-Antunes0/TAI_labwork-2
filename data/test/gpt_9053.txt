Helinä Häkkänen-Nyholm (born 26 April 1971 in Helsinki) is a Finnish architect.  Häkkänen-Nyholm completed her undergraduate studies in Architecture at the Helsinki University of Technology in 1994, and her graduate studies in Architecture at the University of California, Berkeley in 1998.  She has worked as an assistant professor at the Helsinki University of Technology from 1998 to 2003, and as an associate professor at the Academy of Fine Arts, Helsinki from 2003 to 2007. She has been a full professor at the Helsinki University of Technology since 2007.  Häkkänen-Nyholm's practice focuses on research-based architecture and urban design. Her work has been exhibited in numerous solo and group exhibitions, including the Venice Biennale (2003), the Shanghai Biennale (2006), the Sao Paulo Bienal (2007), the Berlin Biennale (2009), and the Istanbul Biennial (2010).  Häkkänen-Nyholm is married to architect Mikko Nyholm.
