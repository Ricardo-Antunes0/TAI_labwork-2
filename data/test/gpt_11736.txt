is a Japanese shōjo manga series written and illustrated by Mitsuhiro Kishimoto. The manga tells the story of a young girl named Naruto Uzumaki who discovers she is the reincarnation of the goddess Amaterasu, and must battle her evil brother, Kaguya, in order to protect the world from destruction.  The manga was first serialized in Shueisha's Weekly Shōnen Jump magazine from October 2002 to September 2006, with a total of 620 chapters published. The individual chapters have been collected into twenty-eight tankōbon volumes. The manga was adapted into an anime series which aired on Japanese television from April 2003 to March 2006 and has been released in nine DVD box sets and thirteen Blu-ray Disc sets. The Naruto: Shippūden anime film was released in theaters in Japan on November 13, 2009. The Naruto: Shippūden anime has also been licensed for an English release by Viz Media.
