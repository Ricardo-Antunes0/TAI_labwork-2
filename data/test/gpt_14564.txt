Kapil Sharma (born 2 April 1981) is an Indian stand-up comedian, actor and television presenter. He is known for his humorous and idiosyncratic style of stand-up comedy. Kapil Sharma first came to prominence with the successful series Comedy Nights with Kapil on Comedy Central. He has since appeared in a number of Hindi comedies and also starred in the English-language romantic comedy film The Great Indian Lie (2015).  Kapil Sharma was born on 2 April 1981 in a Punjabi Sikh family in Amritsar, Punjab, India. After completing his schooling from Saint Joseph's School Amritsar, he enrolled at the National School of Drama in New Delhi, where he received his diploma in 2003. Sharma later moved to Mumbai and started working as a stand-up comedian.  In 2006, Sharma hosted the first season of Comedy Nights with Kapil on Comedy Central. The show was an instant hit and helped Sharma become one of the most popular comedians in India. In 2007, he hosted the first season of Comedy Circus on Zee TV. The show ran for two seasons and became one of the most popular reality shows in India.  In 2009, Sharma made his debut in Hindi cinema with the film Kick which became a sleeper hit. He followed this up with the comedy horror film Bhoomi which was also successful.
