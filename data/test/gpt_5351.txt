Pi Mensae (π Men), also known as Pi Mensae A, is an open cluster in the constellation Mensa. It was discovered by Pierre Méchain on September 2, 1781, and was catalogued as a star cluster by Charles Messier on November 24, 1781.  The cluster is located about 4,000 light years from Earth and is made up of roughly 100 stars. The brightest member, Pi Mensae Aa, has a apparent magnitude of 3.7.
