James Blair Steedman (July 29, 1817 – December 5, 1892) was a United States Senator from Missouri.  Steedman was born in Steubenville, Ohio in 1817 to James Blair and Nancy (Ewing) Steedman. He attended the common schools and then studied law. He was admitted to the bar in 1839 and commenced practice in Sedalia, Missouri. He served as member of the Missouri House of Representatives from 1851 to 1853.  Steedman was elected as a Republican to the Thirty-fourth and Thirty-fifth Congresses (March 4, 1855 – March 3, 1861). He was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1860 to the Thirty-sixth Congress. He resumed the practice of law in Sedalia and served as president of the Missouri State Agricultural Society from 1865 to 1868.  Steedman was elected as a Republican to the United States Senate on November 2, 1868, and served from December 5, 1868, until his death there on December 5, 1892. He was interred in Oak Hill Cemetery in Sedalia.
