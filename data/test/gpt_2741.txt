Tláhuac (; from Nahuatl) is a station on Line 3 of the Mexico City Metro. It is located in the borough of Iztapalapa in the city of Mexico City.  The station was inaugurated on 5 October 1967. It has an area of 1,200 m² and a surface of terrazzo and mosaic. The pillars that support the vaulted ceiling are black granite from the state of Michoacán. The floor is made up of marble from various parts of Mexico. There are also ceramic tiles, both Aztec and traditional images, inlaid in the floor. The walls are covered with murals by José Clemente Orozco and David Alfaro Siqueiros.
