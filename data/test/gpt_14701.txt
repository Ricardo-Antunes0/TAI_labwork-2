Uma Devi Badi (born 1965) is a contemporary artist from India.  Uma Devi Badi was born in 1965 in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. She grew up in a rural area and has been painting and sculpting since she was a child. After completing her secondary education, Badi traveled to Delhi to study art at the National School of Fine Arts. She then moved to Mumbai to continue her studies at the Sir J. J. School of Art, where she received her diploma in 1992.  Badi is known for her abstract paintings and sculptures, which explore the relationship between form and meaning. Her work often features densely layered surfaces and geometric forms, which she often references Indian mythology and spirituality. Badi has exhibited her work in India and internationally, including at the Venice Biennale in 2001 and the Gwangju Biennial in 2006. She was awarded the Padma Shri, India's fourth-highest civilian honor, in 2007.
