Vasyl Sydor ; born in Spasiv (Спасів), Chernihiv Oblast, Ukraine, on February 2, 1955; is a Ukrainian composer and conductor. Sydor began his musical studies at the Kiev Conservatory of Music where he was taught by Mykola Lysenko. He went on to study at the University of Cologne, where he studied composition with Heinz-Ulrich Schulz and conducting with Michael Tilson Thomas. Sydor's early works were influenced by minimalism and post-minimalism. He later developed a more experimental style, incorporating elements of jazz and world music into his works. Sydor has been awarded several prizes for his compositions, including the prestigious Grawemeyer Award in 1998.
