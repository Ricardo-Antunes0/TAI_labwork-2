Henry Everett "Hank" Emerson (May 28, 1925 â€“ October 10, 2010) was an American composer, musician and professor.  Born in Newton, Massachusetts, Emerson studied at the University of Michigan, where he was a member of the Delta Upsilon fraternity. He served in the United States Army during World War II and then began his career as a jazz pianist. Emerson composed music for stage, film and television, as well as classical pieces. He also founded the Boston Contemporary Music Society and was its president from 1976 to 1981. Emerson died in Boston at the age of 85.
