Ronald Charles Cey (; born February 15, 1941) is an American singer and songwriter. He achieved success as the lead singer of the 1960s rock band The Crystals.  Born in Detroit, Michigan, Cey was the son of a factory worker. The Crystals, formed in 1961, were one of the most successful rock groups of the 1960s. Their singles "He's a Rebel", "Daisy Bell" and "Then He Kissed Me" became major hits, and they appeared on several popular television shows. In 2003, Cey was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
