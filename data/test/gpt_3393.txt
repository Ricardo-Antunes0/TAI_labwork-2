NGC 495, also occasionally referred to as the Cigar Galaxy, is a spiral galaxy located in the constellation Vulpecula. The galaxy was discovered on September 22, 1784 by Charles Messier.   NGC 495 is one of the most luminous galaxies in the Local Group and has an estimated mass of 100 billion solar masses. It has an unusually high rate of star birth, with new stars being formed every day. The central regions are dominated by a supermassive black hole, with a mass of about 4 million solar masses.
