Eliashib ( ’Elyāšîḇ, "El restores") the High Priest of the Temple in Jerusalem during the reign of Herod the Great. He is mentioned in the New Testament (Hebrews 3:1-6), and according to tradition he was a descendant of Aaron.  Eliashib is first mentioned in Hebrews 3:1-6, where he is referred to as "the High Priest." According to tradition, Eliashib was a descendant of Aaron. He served as the High Priest during the reign of Herod the Great.  Eliashib is mentioned again in Acts 6:1-6, where he and his sons are killed as a result of Peter's rebuke of Herod.
