The Cheat River is a  tributary of the James River in North Carolina. It rises in the Blue Ridge Mountains of western North Carolina, and flows southeast for about 100 miles before joining the James River about 10 miles southeast of Danville, Virginia.  The Cheat River is the longest river in western North Carolina and the fifth-longest tributary of the James River. It drains an area of about 1,200 square miles, including parts of Buncombe, Haywood, Henderson, Madison, Polk, and Transylvania counties. The river has three principal tributaries: the Swannanoa, the French Broad, and the Nantahala. The Cheat is designated a Wild and Scenic River and has been designated a National Heritage Area.
