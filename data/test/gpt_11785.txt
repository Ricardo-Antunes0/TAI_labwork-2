Allan Sirois (born February 19, 1975) is a Canadian film and television actor. He is best known for his roles as François Pépin in the 2006 Canadian drama series Ça commence à faire longtemps and as Serge in the 2013 French-Canadian mockumentary series Les Visiteurs.  Allan Sirois was born in Montreal, Quebec, to a family of artists. He began his acting career at the age of nine, appearing in commercials and minor roles on Canadian television. He made his feature film debut in 1998 with a minor role in Marc-Éric Sauvé's drama film Laissez-moi vous dire quelque chose. In 2000, he starred in the short film Les Échecs de ma mère, which won the Prix Jean Vigo at the Cannes Film Festival.  Sirois gained wider recognition for his role as François Pépin on the acclaimed Canadian drama series Ça commence à faire longtemps, which aired on TVA from 2006 to 2009. In 2009, he starred as Serge in the French-Canadian mockumentary series Les Visiteurs, which became a cult hit. He has since appeared in a number of films and television shows, including the 2011 drama film L'École buissonnière and the 2017 comedy-drama series You from CBC.
