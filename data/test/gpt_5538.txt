"Princess Yunluo"  is a short story by Chinese author Liu Cixin. It was first published in Chinese in 2008, and won the Hugo Award for Best Novel in 2009.  The story follows the fortunes of Princess Yunluo, a young girl born into a powerful dynasty that is on the brink of ruin. Her royal family is forced to flee their home during the Cultural Revolution and she endures years of hardship and poverty before finding her true calling as a spy. She plays a pivotal role in helping her country recover from its wounds and eventually becomes queen.  The novel has been compared to the works of J.R.R. Tolkien and George R. Martin, and has been praised for its complex worldbuilding and compelling characters. It has been translated into more than 20 languages, and was turned into an anime television series in 2017.
