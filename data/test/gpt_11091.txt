The Terry Park Ballfield (also known as Terry Park Stadium) is a baseball field located in Terry Park in the Flushing neighborhood of Queens, New York City. It is the home field of the New York Mets of Major League Baseball. The field was formerly the home of the Brooklyn Dodgers.  The Terry Park Ballfield was built in 1914 and has a listed capacity of 10,000 spectators. It was added to the National Register of Historic Places in 1985. The field is named for former Mets player Terry Clarke.  The first game at the Terry Park Ballfield was played on April 15, 1914, when the Brooklyn Dodgers hosted the New York Giants. The final game at the Terry Park Ballfield was played on September 30, 2008, when the Mets defeated the Atlanta Braves 3-2.
