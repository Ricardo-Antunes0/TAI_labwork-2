Lewis Crum Bidamon (January 16, 1806 â€“ October 8, 1883) was a United States Senator from Missouri.  Born in Burlington, New Jersey, Bidamon attended the common schools and then studied law. He was admitted to the bar in 1827 and commenced practice in St. Louis, Missouri. He served as prosecuting attorney of St. Louis County in 1836 and 1837 and as judge of the circuit court in 1840.  Bidamon was elected as a Democrat to the Twenty-sixth United States Congress and served from March 4, 1849, to March 3, 1853. He was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1952.  Bidamon was elected as a Democrat to the United States Senate and served from March 4, 1853, to March 3, 1861. He was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1860.  Bidamon resumed the practice of law and served as U.S. consul at Liverpool, England, from 1861 to 1865. He died in St. Louis, Missouri, aged 83 years, and was interred in Bellefontaine Cemetery.
