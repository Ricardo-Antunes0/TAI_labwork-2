Maccabi Netanya F.C. (, Football Club Maccabi Netanya) is an Israeli football club based in Netanya. The club was founded in 1924 and currently plays in Liga Leumit, the top tier of Israeli football.  Maccabi Netanya has spent most of its history in the lower divisions of Israeli football, although it did make one brief appearance in the top flight, the Liga Leumit, in the 1968–69 season. The club's best league finish was eighth place in the 2013–14 season. Netanya has also made three appearances in the UEFA Cup, reaching the semi-finals of the 2002–03 edition.  Maccabi Netanya plays its home games at Netanya Stadium, which has a seating capacity of 18,000. The club's main rivals are Hapoel Netanya and ASA Tel Aviv.  Netanya Football Club was founded on 24 October 1924, at a meeting chaired by Aharon Barnea and attended by leading members of the city's Jewish community. The team's colours were chosen as blue and white stripes with a small yellow star on each side. The first game took place on 2 November 1924 against Maccabi Petah Tikva and ended in a 1–1 draw.  The early years were difficult for Netanya as they finished bottom of their division every year from 1924 until 1927 when they finished seventh.
