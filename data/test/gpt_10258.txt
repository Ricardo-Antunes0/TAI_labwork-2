Hurricane Carol was among the worst tropical cyclones in the Atlantic basin in recent history. The storm developed from a cold front off the west coast of Africa on October 18, 2005 and rapidly intensified. Carol emerged as a major hurricane on October 21 and reached major hurricane status on October 23. The hurricane attained peak winds of 175 mph (280 km/h) on October 24 before making landfall near Myrtle Beach, South Carolina the next day. Carol quickly weakened over the southeastern United States and dissipated over North Carolina on October 27. The storm caused significant damage across several states, with $8.5 billion in total damage reported. Carol was responsible for at least 34 deaths, including 21 in South Carolina.
