Theodor Kristian Avellan (, Fyodor Karlovich Avelan; (18 February 1881 â€“ 17 December 1952) was a Finnish composer and conductor.  Born in Helsinki, Avellan studied music at the Helsinki Conservatory with Alvar Aalto and Jean Sibelius. He made his conducting debut in 1914 and became chief conductor of the Finnish National Opera in 1937. Avellan also composed operas, ballets, orchestral works, choral works and songs. Among his most popular works are the symphonic poem "Kuolema" (1929), the opera "Pohjolan runo" (1935) and the ballet "The Rite of Spring" (1913).
