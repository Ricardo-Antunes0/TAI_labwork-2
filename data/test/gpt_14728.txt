Mount Mabu is a mountain in northern Kenya. At 3,896 meters (12,192 ft) high, it is the highest mountain in the country.  Mount Mabu is located in the Aberdare Range, in the Rift Valley. It is part of the Great Rift Valley system, and is situated between Mount Kenya and Mount Elgon. The mountain is part of the Aberdare National Park.  The mountain was first ascended in 1894 by an Anglo-Egyptian team led by Colonel Edmund Ironside. The peak was first climbed by a Swiss team in 1954. Mount Mabu is also home to Mount Kenya National Park's only glaciers, which are fed by meltwater from Mount Kenya's glaciers.
