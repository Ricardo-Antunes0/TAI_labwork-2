Wangdu  (Chinese: Wangdui) is a former Chinese human rights lawyer who was imprisoned for seven years on charges of subversion.  Wangdu was born in Beijing in 1974. After graduating from the Beijing University of Chemical Technology, she worked as a research scientist at the Chinese Academy of Sciences. In 2001, she became a human rights lawyer, and began to focus her attention on the issue of forced abortions and other forms of sexual violence perpetrated against women by the Chinese government.  In 2003, Wangdu was arrested on charges of subversion, and was held in pre-trial detention for six years. In 2007, she was convicted and sentenced to seven years imprisonment. She was released in 2013 after serving four years of her sentence.
