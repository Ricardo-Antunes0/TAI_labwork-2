Lydia Davis (born July 15, 1947) is an American writer, poet, and critic. She has been awarded the Pulitzer Prize, the National Book Award, and the PEN/Faulkner Award.  Davis was born in Memphis, Tennessee. She studied at Vanderbilt University and the University of Iowa. She lives in New York City.  Davis's work often explores the relationships between gender and sexuality, race and class, and history and memory. Her novels include "The Last Word on Mary," "The Return of Martin Guerre," "Olympia," "The Ministry of Utmost Happiness," and "Life After Life." Her short stories have been collected in "The Collected Stories of Lydia Davis." Davis also writes poetry, essays, and criticism.  Davis was a judge for the 2017 PEN/Jacqueline Woodson Award for Writer of Color.
