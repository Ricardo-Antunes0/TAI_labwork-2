Joseph Mario Contini (born January 29, 1957) is an American former professional baseball player. A right-handed pitcher, Contini played in Major League Baseball for the Detroit Tigers (1985–1988) and the Chicago Cubs (1989).  Contini was born in West Chester, Pennsylvania. He attended Notre Dame High School in West Chester, where he was a three-sport star in football, basketball, and baseball. He was drafted by the Detroit Tigers in the eighth round of the 1977 amateur draft.  Contini made his major league debut with the Tigers in 1985. He pitched in 95 games over two seasons, compiling a record of 7–10 with a 4.06 ERA. In 1989, he was traded to the Chicago Cubs, where he pitched for two seasons. He finished his career with the Cincinnati Reds (1991–1992) and the Cleveland Indians (1993).  Contini has since retired from baseball and resides in Michigan. He is a color commentator for the Detroit Tigers television broadcasts.
