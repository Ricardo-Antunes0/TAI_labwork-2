Bradshaw made his professional debut for Manchester United on 5 April 1955 in a league game against Birmingham City. He joined the club from amateur side Dukinfield in 1952. He played for United until his retirement in 1970, winning five league titles, three FA Cups and two European Cups. He also won 52 caps for England and scored 24 goals.  Bradshaw was an important figure at United and is regarded as one of the club's greatest ever players. He is eighth in the club's all-time goalscoring list, and was inducted into the Manchester United Hall of Fame in 2007. After his retirement he ran a football school in Manchester. He died from cancer in September 2008 at the age of 78.
