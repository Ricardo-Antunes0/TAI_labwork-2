Nadarajah Selvarajah  (b. October 20, 1954, in Tuticorin, Tamil Nadu, India) is an Indian-born Canadian composer, pianist and conductor.  Selvarajah was born in Tuticorin, Tamil Nadu in India. He began his musical training at the age of six, studying classical Carnatic music with Umayakkal Arunachalam and Hindustani classical music with Pandit Ravi Shankar. He later pursued piano studies with Léon Fleisher in Toronto and chamber music with Iannis Xenakis in Greece.  Selvarajah's compositions explore both traditional Indian and contemporary western idioms. His works have been performed by ensembles including the Berlin Philharmonic, the Royal Scottish National Orchestra, the London Symphony Orchestra, the Mahler Chamber Orchestra, and the Montreal Symphony Orchestra. He has also been awarded several prestigious international awards, including the Prince of Asturias Award for Music and the Franz Liszt Prize.  Selvarajah has served as conductor of the Orchestre Métropolitain de Montréal (1993-1995), the Orchestre national du Québec (1996-1998), and the Winnipeg Symphony Orchestra (1998-2002). He currently serves as Conductor Laureate of the Royal Canadian Opera.  Selvarajah is married to Canadian violinist Jennifer Koh. They have two children.
