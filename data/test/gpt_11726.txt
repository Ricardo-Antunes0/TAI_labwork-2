Mikael Rickfors (born 4 December 1948) is a Swedish mathematician and physicist. He is a professor at the Karolinska Institutet. Rickfors made significant contributions to the theory of partial differential equations, and is known for his work on the Rickfors equation, as well as his work on hyperbolic partial differential equations and their applications to problems in physics.  Mikael Rickfors was born in Stockholm, Sweden, on 4 December 1948. He received his undergraduate degree from Uppsala University in 1969, and his doctorate from the same institution in 1973. He began his career at the Royal Institute of Technology (KTH) in 1973, where he was appointed professor of mathematics in 1985. He moved to the Karolinska Institutet in 1992, where he currently holds the position of professor. Rickfors has been a fellow of the Royal Society since 1996, and a member of the Swedish Academy since 2003.
