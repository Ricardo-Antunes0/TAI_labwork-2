The Somberlain is the debut album by American heavy metal band Lamb of God. The album was released on June 1, 1998 through Roadrunner Records. The album features the band's original line-up: vocalist Randy Blythe, guitarists Mark Morton and Willie Adler, bassist John Campbell and drummer Shannon Larkin.  Lamb of God's first album, "The Somberlain", was released on June 1, 1998. It was recorded at Sound City Studios in Van Nuys, California with producer Rick Rubin. The album features the band's original line-up: vocalist Randy Blythe, guitarists Mark Morton and Willie Adler, bassist John Campbell and drummer Shannon Larkin.  "The Somberlain" is a heavy metal album which features fast-paced songs with aggressive vocals and complex guitar solos. The lyrics focus on themes such as religion, suicide, and murder. The album reached number 25 on the "Billboard" 200 chart.  In 2004, "The Somberlain" was reissued with two bonus tracks: "In Memory of the Deceased" and "November Rain". In 2007, the album was remastered by Andy Sneap and reissued with a bonus DVD containing the making of the album and live performances from 1998-2002.
