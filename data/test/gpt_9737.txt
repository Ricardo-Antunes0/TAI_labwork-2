Tyreke Jamir Evans (born September 19, 1989) is an American professional basketball player for the Sacramento Kings of the National Basketball Association (NBA). He played college basketball for the Memphis Grizzlies, winning Rookie of the Year honors in 2008.  Evans was selected by the Kings with the third overall pick in the 2008 NBA draft. He has also played for the Orlando Magic, New Orleans Pelicans, and Atlanta Hawks. He has led the Kings to four consecutive playoff appearances, and in 2017–18 he was named to the All-NBA Third Team.  Evans was born in Memphis, Tennessee. He attended Whitehaven High School in Memphis, where he was a two-time All-State selection and averaged 22.2 points, 7.8 rebounds and 4.6 assists per game as a senior.  Evans played one season of college basketball at Memphis, where he was named Rookie of the Year in 2008. In his freshman year, Evans averaged 17.5 points, 5.4 rebounds and 3.1 assists per game. As a sophomore in 2009–10, Evans averaged 23.4 points, 7 rebounds and 4 assists per game as Memphis made it to the Final Four before losing to Florida State. As a junior in 2010–11, Evans averaged 26 points, 7 rebounds and 4 assists per game as Memphis won the NCAA Championship with a 69–64 victory over Connecticut in the Final Four. Evans was also named Most Outstanding Player of the Final Four after averaging 25 points, 10 rebounds and 5 assists per game
