Richard Robert Volk (born March 15, 1945) is an American singer, songwriter, and musician. He is the frontman of the rock band Rick Springfield.  Volk was born in San Diego, California, and raised in the nearby town of Oceanside. He began playing guitar at age nine and formed his first band at age thirteen. After serving in the U.S. Army, Volk moved to Los Angeles and formed Rick Springfield in 1966. The band achieved international success with hits such as "I Wanna Be Your Boyfriend", "Jessie's Girl", and "Don't Talk to Strangers". Volk has also released several solo albums and written songs for other artists. He has been inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, the Songwriters Hall of Fame, and the US Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
