Andrew Charles Hilfiger is an American businessman, fashion designer, and television personality. He is the founder, chairman, and chief creative officer of Hilfiger Denim & Leather, and has been its president since 2005. He was also the co-founder of The Hilfiger Foundation, a philanthropic organization.  Hilfiger was born in Brooklyn, New York, to Charles Hilfiger and his wife Betty Jean (n√©e Edwards). His parents divorced when he was five years old and he was raised by his mother. Hilfiger attended The Hill School in Pottstown, Pennsylvania. He then attended the University of Rhode Island, where he received a B.S. in business administration in 1981.  Hilfiger began his career at the law firm of Wachtell Lipton as an associate in 1981. In 1984 he founded his own company, with $25,000 from his savings and loans from college. The company manufactured jeans for Levi Strauss & Co.. In 1995 he sold the company to American Eagle Outfitters for $1 billion.  In 2003, Hilfiger founded Hilfiger Denim & Leather with partner Tommy Hilfiger. The company designs and manufactures denim jeans, leather jackets, and women's clothing. It is headquartered in Los Angeles. In 2005 he became president of the company and its parent company, The Hilfiger Foundation.
