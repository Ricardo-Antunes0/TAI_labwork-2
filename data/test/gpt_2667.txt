Sukraraj Tropical and Infectious Disease Hospital, named after the Tamil saint Teku Perumal, is a tertiary care hospital in Chennai, India. It is one of the largest hospitals in Chennai, with a bed capacity of 1,000. The hospital has a 350-bedded Out-patient wing and a 100-bedded Intensive Care unit.  Teku Hospital was established in 1918 by the late Sri S. A. Ramasamy Iyer, the then Dewan of Madras State. The hospital commenced its first operation on 12 October 1918. The hospital is named after the Tamil saint Teku Perumal. The present management of the hospital was taken over by the Catholic Health Association of India (CHAI) in 1988. The Department of Health and Family Welfare, Government of Tamil Nadu, has been the statutory regulator of the hospital since 1988.  Teku Hospital is amongst India's premier multi-specialty hospitals and is also one of the few tertiary care hospitals in Tamil Nadu which offers a wide range of medical services including cardiology, neurology, nephrology, paediatrics, ENT, general surgery and obstetrics and gynaecology. Teku Hospital has had a distinguished record in providing quality healthcare to the people of Chennai.
