Stephen W. "Seve" Wilson (born ) is an American politician who has been the U.S. Representative for California's 33rd congressional district since 2013. A Republican, he is the first person of Irish descent to represent that district.  Wilson was born in Garden Grove, California, the son of Patricia (n√©e Hickey) and Stephen W. Wilson, who was a bank vice president. He grew up in a Catholic family and attended Notre Dame High School in Irvine, California. Wilson earned his undergraduate degree from the University of California, Santa Barbara in 2002 and a Juris Doctor from the University of Southern California School of Law in 2006.  Wilson worked as an associate at a law firm before being elected to the Orange County Board of Supervisors in 2008. He was re-elected to a second term in 2010 and served as chairman of the board's Natural Resources and Infrastructure committee. He also served on the boards of directors for the Irvine Chamber of Commerce and the Orange County Republican Party.  In November 2012, Wilson announced his candidacy for California's 33rd congressional district, which had been held by Democrat Loretta Sanchez since 2003. Wilson won the Republican primary with 56% of the vote and faced Sanchez in the general election. Wilson won with 53% of the vote, becoming the first person of Irish descent to represent that district.  Wilson serves on the House Committee on Education and the Workforce and is a member of the House Appropriations Committee. He is also a co-founder and board member for Young Conservatives
