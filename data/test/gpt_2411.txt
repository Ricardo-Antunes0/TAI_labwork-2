The Black Homestead Farm is a historic farmstead and national historic district located in Columbia Township, Columbia County, Pennsylvania. The farmhouse was built about 1800, and is a 2 1/2-story, three bay, brick dwelling on a stone foundation. It has a gable roof and is sheathed in clapboard. The property also includes a barn (c. 1850), an ice house (c. 1910), two corn cribs (c. 1870), and a smokehouse (c. 1900).  The Black Homestead Farm was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 2003.
