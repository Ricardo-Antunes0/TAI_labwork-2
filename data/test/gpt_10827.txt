In the United States military, a squad leader is a commissioned officer rank above the rank of sergeant and below the rank of captain. The squad leader is typically a junior officer, typically in the early years of their military career.  The squad leader performs the duties of a platoon leader and section leader combined into one position. They are in charge of a squad, which is composed of between eight and twelve soldiers. A squad leader is also responsible for training their squad members and ensuring that they are proficient in their military duties.
