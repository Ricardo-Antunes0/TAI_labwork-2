The Elias Boardman House is a historic house located at 209 Elm Street in Chatham, New York. It was built in 1807 and is a two-story, five bay wide Federal style brick house with a gable roof and clapboarded exterior. It features a rear wing and an octagonal cupola, both added in the early 20th century.  The Elias Boardman House was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1985.
