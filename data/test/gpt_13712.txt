Louis Henry Saban (October 13, 1921 – November 11, 2013) was an American football coach, who was the head coach of the University of Michigan from 1969 to 1976. He also served as the head coach at Michigan State from 1976 to 1985.  Saban was born in Detroit, Michigan, in 1921. He attended Cass Technical High School in Detroit and played football for the school's football team. Saban then attended the University of Michigan, where he played on the Wolverines football team and was a part of the 1940 and 1941 national championships. He graduated from Michigan in 1943 with a bachelor's degree in business administration.  Saban served in the United States Navy during World War II, reaching the rank of lieutenant commander. After the war, he became an assistant coach at his alma mater, Michigan. He then served as head coach at Ohio State from 1953 to 1959. Saban then moved to California and served as head coach at UCLA from 1960 to 1966. He then returned to Michigan and served as head coach there from 1967 to 1976. He led Michigan to a record of 53–8–2 during his tenure, including two Big Ten Conference championships (1969 and 1973).  Saban then left for Michigan State, where he served as head coach from 1976 to 1985. His tenure at MSU was shorter than his stints at UM and OSU, but he still compiled a record of 71–49–1. After leaving MSU, Saban retired from coaching and became a television broadcaster for college football games.
