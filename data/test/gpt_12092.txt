Kapoor is considered one of the most popular and influential actors of the Hindi cinema. He was awarded a Filmfare Lifetime Achievement Award in 1992.  Kapoor was born into a Hindu family in a village near Amritsar, Punjab, British India (now in Pakistan). After performing at local weddings, he made his first film appearance as an extra in the 1940 film "Andaz". In 1945, he enrolled at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art (RADA) in London, but did not graduate. Kapoor made his Hindi-language debut in the war drama "Jwar Bhata" (1948). He established himself as a leading actor with the 1949 film "Peepli Live", which was critically acclaimed and won him his first Filmfare Award for Best Actor. Kapoor's breakthrough role came with the role of an effete aristocrat in the 1957 film "Waqt".
