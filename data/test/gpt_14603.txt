Patrice Rankine is a Professor of Classics and African American Studies at Brown University. Rankine is the author of the critically acclaimed book, Citizen: An American Lyric (2002), which won the prestigious PEN/Faulkner Award. Her work has been translated into more than a dozen languages.  Rankine was born in Jackson, Mississippi, in 1964. She grew up in Gulfport and earned her B.A. from Vanderbilt University in 1986 and her Ph.D. from Yale University in 1991. She taught at Rutgers University before joining the faculty at Brown in 1994.  Rankine's writing has addressed a wide range of topics, including race and racism, gender and sexuality, and the experience of colonialism and imperialism. Her work has been praised for its daring, its virtuosity, and its ability to lyrically capture the experience of both individual experience and historical events.
