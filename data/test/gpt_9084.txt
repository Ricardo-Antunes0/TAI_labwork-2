Macarthur railway station is located on the North Shore Line in Sydney, Australia. The station serves the eastern suburbs of Sydney, including Macarthur, Forbes and Kellyville.  The North Shore Line opened between Quay Street, Randwick and Sydenham in 1887. The line was extended to Macarthur in 1914. The original station was located at the present site of the junction of the North Shore and Cumberland lines. It was renamed Macarthur in 1921.  The station is served by five trains per hour in each direction. It is also served by bus routes operated by Sydney Buses.
