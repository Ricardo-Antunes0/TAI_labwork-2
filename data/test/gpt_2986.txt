Bab's Diary is a 1917 American silent comedy film directed by D. W. Griffith and written by Griffith and Frank Harris. The film stars Lillian Gish, Charles Chaplin, and Mabel Normand.  The film follows the misadventures of young girl Bab (Lillian Gish), who keeps a journal in which she records her thoughts, feelings, and experiences. The film is full of humor and contains controversial scenes that caused controversy when it was released.
