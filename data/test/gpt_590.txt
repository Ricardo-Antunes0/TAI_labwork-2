Johannes Sløk (27 April 1916 in Frederiksberg – 31 January 1993 in København) was a Danish painter, sculptor and graphic artist. He is known for his abstract paintings, which are characterized by their use of colour and rhythmic brushstrokes.  Sløk was born in 1916 in Frederiksberg, Copenhagen, the son of a cabinetmaker. He studied at the Royal Danish Academy of Fine Arts from 1935 to 1939 and then at the École des Beaux-Arts in Paris from 1939 to 1941. After returning to Denmark he worked as a teacher at the Academy from 1948 to 1970. He was a professor at the Academy from 1970 to 1978.  Sløk's paintings are abstract, and are characterized by their use of colour and rhythmic brushstrokes. His early works were influenced by the work of Wassily Kandinsky and Paul Klee, but he later developed his own style. His art has been exhibited in many countries, including the United States, Germany, France, Italy, Japan and Denmark. He was awarded the Eckersberg Medal in 1984 and the Thorvaldsen Medal in 1992.
