The National Art Library (NAL) is a research library in Washington, D.C., United States. The library has a collection of over 2 million items, including a significant collection of American art. It is one of the most important research libraries in the United States, and is an authority on American art.  The NAL was founded in 1907 as the Library of Congress's National Art Library, and became a separate institution in 1925. It was renamed the National Art Library of the United States in 1990. The library operates two branch libraries: the John F. Kennedy Presidential Library in Boston, Massachusetts, and the National Gallery of Art Library in Washington, D.C.
