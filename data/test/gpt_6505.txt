Shamans: Siberian Spirituality and the Western Imagination is a book about shamans and shamanism by the British historian Stephen Hutton. It was published in 1998 and won the American Historical Association's prestigious Books for a Better Life Award.  Shamans are a central part of Siberian culture and spirituality, and their role in society has been widely studied by scholars. Hutton discusses the history and mythology of shamans, their practices, and the relationship between shamans and the general population. He also examines the influence of shamanism on Western thinking about religion and spirituality.
