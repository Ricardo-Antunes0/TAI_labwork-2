Euophrys omnisuperstes (the species name means standing euophrys) is a small euophrys from South America. It is a terrestrial, nocturnal frog that inhabits moist lowland forests.  The species was first described by the French naturalist Jules Bourguignon in 1857. It was initially placed in the genus Euophrys, but has since been reclassified into the new genus Omnisuperstes. The species is found in southern Brazil, Paraguay and Uruguay.
