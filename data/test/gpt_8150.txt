W. Samuel Patten is an American political scientist and the current Chancellor's Professor of Public Affairs at American University in Washington, D.C.  He is the author of over fifty books, including "The American Presidency" (2007), "The Politics of Pork" (2008), and "The Road to Power: The Political Education of George W. Bush" (2010). Patten has also written over 1,000 articles for scholarly journals and newspapers, and lectured around the world.  Patten served as the executive director of the non-partisan National Constitution Center from 2004 to 2007. He has also served on the boards of directors for PBS NewsHour, the Brookings Institution, and the Kennedy School of Government at Harvard University.  Patten was born in Los Angeles in 1950. He received his B.A. from Occidental College in 1972 and his M.A. from Stanford University in 1973. He also holds a DPhil from Oxford University, where he was a Rhodes Scholar.
