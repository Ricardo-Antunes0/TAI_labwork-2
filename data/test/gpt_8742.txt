The Sonoma TrainTown Railroad (commonly called "Sonoma Train") is a narrow gauge railroad located in Sonoma, California. It is a tourist railroad that operates passenger trains on a 1.25-mile (2.4 km) track running through the town's downtown area.  The railroad was founded in 1989 by Bob and Karen Smith as a means of promoting tourism in Sonoma. It is operated by the Smith family and has been in their possession since its inception. The railroad has six locomotives, five cars, and eight passenger cars. The railroad also has two cabooses, one of which is used for storage. The track runs through downtown Sonoma, past the City Hall and County Court buildings, and then past the Sonoma Valley Vineyards before terminating at the Santa Rosa Train Depot.
