Jeffrey S. Williams is a writer and critic known for his work in the fields of science fiction, video games, and film. He has written extensively about cinema, television, and video games, and has also been a critic and columnist for publications such as The A.V. Club, Salon, and The Washington Post. In addition to his writing, Williams has also been an occasional actor and voice actor.  Williams was born in New Jersey in 1971. He received his bachelor's degree in English from Brown University in 1993 and his master's degree in English from Wesleyan University in 1995. He taught high school English for several years before beginning his career as a writer and critic.  Williams is the author of several books, including "Final Fantasy: The Spirits Within" (2001), "Mass Effect 2" (2010), "Super Mario Odyssey" (2017), and "The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild" (2017). He also co-wrote the novelization of "Super Mario Odyssey" with Kentaro Miura. Williams has also written articles for publications such as The A.V. Club, Salon, and The Washington Post.  Williams is a frequent actor and voice actor. He has appeared in episodes of the television series "The Sopranos", "Buffy the Vampire Slayer", "Angel", "Dollhouse", and "Firefly".
