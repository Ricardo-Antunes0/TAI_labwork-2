The history of Filipino women writers is long and varied. From the ancient empires of China and Japan to the early American colonies, Filipinas have always been a part of the literary landscape.  This diverse group of writers has produced works in a wide range of genres, from historical epics to modern romance. Their works reflect the unique experiences and perspectives of Filipino women, both in the past and present.  Notable Filipino women writers include Lola Roces, Jose Corazon dela Cruz, Ninotchka Rosca, Edith Sarmiento, Noli Me Tangere, and Margarita Bonifacio. Their writing has won them prestigious awards and critical acclaim both in their home country and abroad.  Today, Filipino women continue to write prolifically in a variety of genres. Their work is often unafraid to explore controversial issues, and it is always thoughtful, insightful, and entertaining.
