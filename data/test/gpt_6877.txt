Post is the second studio album by Icelandic singer Björk, released on 18 September 1993 by One Little Indian Records. Following the critical and commercial success of her debut album, Björk decided to take a more experimental approach with Post. The album features more electronic beats and sounds than her previous work, as well as a greater use of sampling.   Post peaked at number one on the UK Albums Chart and was certified platinum by the British Phonographic Industry. It has since been certified double platinum in the United States. The album has been influential in the development of electronica and alternative music genres, and has been cited as an influence by artists such as Radiohead, The Chemical Brothers, and Massive Attack.   Björk embarked on a world tour in support of Post in 1994, which was documented on the live album Post Live. In 2002, she remixed and reworked several tracks from Post for her second studio album Homogenic (2002).
