Antoine Reed (born December 24, 1987) is an American rapper and record producer. He is best known for his single "Waves", which peaked at number 2 on the US Billboard Hot 100 chart.  Rocks was born in Houston, Texas, to a musical family. His father is the lead singer of the soul group The Reed Brothers and his mother is a singer and pianist. Antoine started rapping at the age of 10, influenced by artists such as Tupac Shakur and Biggie Smalls. He attended Lamar High School in Houston and later studied music production at the University of Texas at Austin.  In early 2013, Rocks released his first single, "Waves". The track was produced by Hit-Boy and featured guest vocals from Pharrell Williams. It became an immediate hit, topping the charts in several countries including the United States and United Kingdom. The song was nominated for Best Rap Performance at the 56th Grammy Awards but lost to Drake's "Hotline Bling". In November 2013, Rocks released his debut album, "Lift Your Own Weight", which featured guest appearances from Pharrell Williams, Chance the Rapper, Lil Wayne and Wiz Khalifa. The album reached number one in several countries including the United States and United Kingdom.
