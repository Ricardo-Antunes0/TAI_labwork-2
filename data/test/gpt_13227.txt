Flagstaff  is a city in, and the county seat of, Coconino County, Arizona, United States. The population was 60,321 at the 2010 census. Flagstaff is the principal city of the Flagstaff metropolitan area, which had a population of 154,279 in 2013. Flagstaff is located in the northeast corner of the state on the northern edge of the Grand Canyon National Park. The city is bordered by Winslow to the east and east-southeast, Williams to the south, and Snowflake to the west.   The city was founded in 1879 as a railroad town and served as a supply station for travelers on the Northern Pacific Railroad. Flagstaff became a resort town in the late 19th century with attractions such as attractions such as Bannerstone Resort, Mountainair Resort and Lake Powell Resort. The city experienced an economic decline after World War II but has been growing steadily since then and has seen new development in fields such as biotechnology and tourism.
