Georges Leclanché (October 9, 1839 – September 5, 1904) was a French chemist who discovered the law of chemical reaction rates.  Georges Leclanché was born in Paris on October 9, 1839. He studied chemistry at the University of Paris and then worked as an assistant to Augustin-Pierre Chevreul. In 1865, he became a professor at the University of Nancy and in 1894 he became the director of the chemistry laboratory at the Collège de France. Leclanché died in Paris on September 5, 1904.
