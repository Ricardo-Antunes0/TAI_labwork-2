The University of California (UC) is a public university system in the U.S. state of California. The UC system was founded on the belief that a university is not a vocational training center but a place where "the whole person" can develop their talents. The University of California has annual research and development expenditures of over $2 billion, making it one of the top ten research universities in the United States.  The University of California was founded in 1868 and comprises 23 campuses, two medical centers, and one school of law. The UC system has an annual enrollment of more than 30,000 undergraduate and graduate students. The University of California is organized into ten campuses, each with its own governing Board of Regents, Chancellors, and Presidents.
