Saint Sigfrid of Sweden  was a missionary-bishop who is venerated as a saint in the Lutheran and Catholic Churches. Born in 995, Sigfrid was canonized in 1045 and proclaimed a Doctor of the Church by Pope John XXII in 1233.  Sigfrid was born to a wealthy family in Sweden in 995. After studying at the University of Paris, he became a missionary bishop in Finland and Estonia. He also served as the Bishop of Stockholm, and died in 1045. Sigfrid is venerated as a saint both in the Lutheran and Catholic Churches. In addition, he has been recognized as a Doctor of the Church by Pope John XXII in 1233.
