The Sheriff's Son is a 1919 American silent drama film directed by Edward H. Griffith and starring Griffith, Myrna Loy and Lionel Barrymore. The screenplay by Hamlin Garland is based on the novel of the same name by Zane Grey.  The film tells the story of a young man born to a lawman who is forced to flee his home after becoming embroiled in a dispute with the townspeople. He travels to New York City, where he becomes involved in a criminal underworld and falls in love with a woman who is already married. In the end, he is able to return home and reconciles with his father.
