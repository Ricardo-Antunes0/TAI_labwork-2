Music from Van-Pires is the only studio album by the American rock band Van-Pires released in 2006.  The album was recorded at the band's own studio, The Dungeon, in Ridgewood, New Jersey. The album was produced by the band and recorded with the help of longtime collaborator and producer, Tony Visconti.  The album's sound is a departure from the heavier rock sound of their previous releases, featuring a more mellow and atmospheric sound. The album has been met with generally positive reviews, with many reviewers praising the band's new direction and the album's overall musicality.     Music from Van-Pires was nominated for Best Hard Rock Album at the 2007 Grammy Awards but lost to Black Label Society's album, Born Again.
