Ocular ischemic syndrome is the constellation of symptoms that result from a lack of blood flow to the eyes. These symptoms may include vision problems, pain, and redness. The syndrome most commonly affects people over the age of 65, but it can also occur in younger adults.  Ocular ischemic syndrome is caused by a number of different factors, including age, high blood pressure, diabetes, and atherosclerosis. The most common cause is a blockage in the blood vessels that supply the eyes. This can be caused by a number of things, such as a stroke, a blood clot, or a tumour.  The symptoms of ocular ischemic syndrome vary depending on the area of the eye that is affected. The most common symptoms are vision problems and pain. Vision problems may include difficulty seeing in bright light or seeing clearly at night. Pain may be localized to one eye or it may be felt all over the eye.  Redness and swelling are also common symptoms of ocular ischemic syndrome. Redness may be seen near the eye or it may spread throughout the entire eye. Swelling may cause discomfort when the eye is moved or when you blink.  If you experience any of these symptoms, you should see your doctor immediately. Your doctor can diagnose ocular ischemic syndrome with a simple examination and test your vision using standard methods.
