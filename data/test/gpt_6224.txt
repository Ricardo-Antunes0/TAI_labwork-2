Sarolta Geőcze de Szendrő (; 27 December 1892 – 14 September 1978) was a Hungarian actress and singer.  Geőcze was born in the city of Pécs, in the Kingdom of Hungary, on 27 December 1892. She made her stage debut at the age of nine, and appeared in numerous theatre productions before making her cinema debut in 1916. She achieved widespread success in the 1940s and 1950s, appearing in a number of notable films, including "The House by the Cemetery" (1944), "The Four Feathers" (1939), "The Devil's Advocate" (1939), "Sunny" (1947), "The Marriage of Figaro" (1948), "The Third Man" (1949), and "The Seventh Seal" (1957). Geőcze also performed frequently on radio and television, and released several albums. She died in Budapest on 14 September 1978.
