The flame-crested tanager (Loriotus cristatus) is a bird in the tanager family. It is found in the eastern and central Amazon Basin in Brazil, Bolivia, Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, and Venezuela. Its natural habitats are subtropical or tropical moist lowland forests and heavily degraded former forest.  The flame-crested tanager is a small bird with a long tail and a bright red crest. It is mainly green above with a yellow throat and breast. The males are more brightly coloured than the females. The flame-crested tanager is a shy bird that frequents subtropical or tropical moist lowland forests, where it feeds on insects and fruit.
