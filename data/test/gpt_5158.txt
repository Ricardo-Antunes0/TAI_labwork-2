The Pact of Pacification or Pacification Pact was a treaty between the United States and Spain that ended the Spanish–American War. The treaty was signed on April 26, 1898, in Washington, D.C.  The Pact of Pacification was one of the key factors that led to Spain's cession of sovereignty over Cuba and Puerto Rico to the United States in 1898. The treaty was negotiated by American Ambassador William Howard Taft and Spanish Foreign Minister Ramón Blanco y Erenia. The final document was ratified by the U.S. Senate on April 27, 1898.
