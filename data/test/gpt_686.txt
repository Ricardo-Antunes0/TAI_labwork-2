He is best known for his roles in the films The Tonys (1972), The Towering Inferno (1974), and Death of a Salesman (1978).  Smits was born in Amsterdam, Netherlands, to Gerardus Johannes Smits, a civil engineer, and Margaretha Antonia "Margo" Smits. He was the second of four children. His parents were both of Dutch descent. At the age of ten, he emigrated with his family to the United States, where they settled in Brooklyn, New York City.  Smits began his acting career in theater productions in New York City. He made his feature film debut in The Tonys (1972), which was based on the Broadway play of the same name. He went on to appear in such films as The Towering Inferno (1974), Death of a Salesman (1978), and An American Werewolf in London (1981). He also appeared in television productions, including an appearance on the soap opera Guiding Light in 1978.  Smits was married twice. His first marriage, to actress Patricia Neal, ended in divorce. He later married actress Shelley Duvall. Smits died from bladder cancer at the age of 80 in Los Angeles, California.
