Lee Myung-hee (; born 5 September 1943) is a former South Korean president and the first woman to hold the office. A career bureaucrat, she was elected as president in 2008, and was re-elected in 2012.  Lee was born in 1943 in the North Korean city of Sinuiju, which is now within the demilitarized zone separating North and South Korea. She attended a girls' high school in Pyongyang, where she became interested in politics after reading the works of Kim Il-sung. After graduating from high school, she worked as a secretary for a North Korean diplomat in Beijing. In 1969, she entered the Department of Foreign Affairs of the South Korean government, where she worked for several years before becoming head of the department's economic affairs bureau in 1985.  In 1992, Lee was appointed deputy director of the National Intelligence Service (NIS), and served in that position until 2000. In that year, she was elected to the National Assembly as a representative from Gyeonggi Province; she remained a member of parliament until her election as president.  Lee Myung-hee was elected president of South Korea on 9 December 2008, becoming the first woman to hold that office. She was re-elected on 14 December 2012 with 71% of the vote. She retired from active politics on 8 May 2017, after serving for nearly 20 years as president or chairwoman of major political parties or coalitions.
