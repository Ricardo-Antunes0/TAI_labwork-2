Hans Joachim Iwand (; 11 July 1899 – 6 October 1984) was a German mathematician, known for his work in analysis, differential geometry, and algebra.  Born in Königsberg, East Prussia, Iwand attended the universities of Göttingen and Berlin, where he received his doctorate in 1922 under the supervision of Felix Klein. From 1922 to 1926 he was a professor at the University of Giessen. He then moved to the University of Berlin, where he remained until his retirement in 1960.  Iwand was the recipient of the Max Planck Medal in 1950 and the Humboldt Prize in 1962. He also founded and was editor-in-chief of the journal "Zeitschrift für Analektische Mathematik" from 1945 to 1962.
