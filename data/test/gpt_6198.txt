Permission to Fly is the second and final studio album by British alternative rock band Radiohead. It was released on 29 October 2007 in the United Kingdom and on 4 November 2007 in the United States. The album was produced by Nigel Godrich and Radiohead, with assistance from Bryce Dessner and Patrick Carney of The Black Keys.  The album has been described by music journalists as more experimental and electronic-based than their previous work, as well as incorporating samples, strings, and other orchestral elements. Upon its release, Permission to Fly received generally positive reviews from critics, who praised its lyrical depth and experimentation. It has been certified Platinum in the United Kingdom and the United States.  Following the release of their acclaimed debut album OK Computer (1997), Radiohead experienced creative stagnation for several years. In 2003, frontman Thom Yorke began writing new songs with producer Nigel Godrich, who had worked with Radiohead on their debut album. However, Yorke felt that the material he was working on did not reflect the sound of Radiohead as a whole and decided to shelve the project.   In 2006, following the release of Hail to the Thief (their first new material in eight years), Godrich approached Yorke about revisiting Permission to Fly. Yorke agreed and began reworking some of the tracks with Godrich again.
