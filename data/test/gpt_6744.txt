Cop Killer (original Swedish title: Polism√∂rdaren) is a crime novel by the Swedish author Joakim Zander. It was first published in 2003.  The novel tells the story of Martin Beck, a police detective in Stockholm during the early 1960s. Beck is investigating a series of murders of policemen, all of whom have been shot in the head. The killer seems to be targeting officers who are trying to enforce the law.  The novel was well received by critics and won the Nordic Council's Prize for Best Crime Novel.
