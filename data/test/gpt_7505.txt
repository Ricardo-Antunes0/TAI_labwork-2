Paul Marie Indjendjet Gondjout (4 June 1912 – 30 September 1984) was a Belgian composer, conductor and pianist.  Born in Liège, Gondjout studied at the Royal Conservatory of Brussels with Nadia Boulanger and Marcel Duchamp Olivier Messiaen. He made his conducting debut in 1933 and became music director of the Antwerp Opera in 1951. In 1968 he was appointed director of the Royal Conservatory of Liège. He also taught at the conservatories of Geneva, Paris and Brussels.  Gondjout's compositions include orchestral works, chamber music, choral works, opera and ballet. He was awarded the Grand Prix de la Suisse Romande, the Prix de Rome and the Léonie-France César Award.
