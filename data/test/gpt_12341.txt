Austin Franklin Pike (October 16, 1819October 8, 1892) was an American jurist and politician who served as the 13th Governor of Arkansas from 1868 to 1871. He also served as the United States Secretary of War from 1869 to 1872.  Pike was born on October 16, 1819, in Nashville, Tennessee. He attended the University of Michigan, where he received a law degree in 1841. He then practiced law in Nashville.  Pike was elected as the 13th Governor of Arkansas in 1868. During his time as governor, Pike fought for equal rights for African Americans and opposed Reconstruction policies of the United States government. In 1870, he was appointed as the U.S. Secretary of War by Ulysses S. Grant. Pike served in this role until 1872.  After leaving office, Pike returned to Nashville and resumed his law practice. He died there on October 8, 1892.
