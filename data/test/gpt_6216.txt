Yorkshire County, Massachusetts was a county in the Commonwealth of Massachusetts. The county was created on March 4, 1793, from parts of Hampshire County and Berkshire County. York County was dissolved on February 7, 1846, and its territory was divided among Berkshire, Hampshire, and Franklin counties.  York County is located in the Pioneer Valley of western Massachusetts. The county is bordered by Berkshire County to the north, Hampshire County to the east, Franklin County to the south, and Connecticut to the west. The county seat is Northampton.  According to the U.S. Census Bureau, the county has a total area of , of which is land and (0.3%) is water.   As of the census of 2000, there were 167,856 people, 59,972 households, and 42,476 families residing in the county. The population density was 265 people per square mile (102/km²). There were 71,455 housing units at an average density of 105 per square mile (40/km²). The racial makeup of the county was 98.27% White, 0.24% Black or African American, 0.07% Native American, 0.32% Asian, 0.03% Pacific Islander, 0.92% from other races, and 0.86% from two or more races. 1.60% of the population were Hispanic or Latino of any race. 34.4% were of English ancestry according to Census 2000.
