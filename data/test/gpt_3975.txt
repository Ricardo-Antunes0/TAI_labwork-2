The 1998–99 Ranji Trophy was the 65th season of the Ranji Trophy, a first-class cricket competition in India.   West Zone won the competition, their fourth title.   The 1998–99 Ranji Trophy began on October 21, 1998 and ended on May 19, 1999. There were thirteen teams competing in the tournament. The teams were divided into two zones, West Zone and East Zone. Each team played each other twice, once at their home ground and once away. The top four teams from each zone qualified for the knock-out stage.   West Zone consisted of Maharashtra, Gujarat, Rajasthan, Andhra Pradesh and Uttar Pradesh.  East Zone consisted of Madhya Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, and Orissa.  Maharashtra won the competition with a record margin of 36 points. Gujarat finished second with 83 points, Rajasthan third with 78 points, and Uttar Pradesh fourth with 66 points.
