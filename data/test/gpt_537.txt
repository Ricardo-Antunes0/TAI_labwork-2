Zerstörergeschwader 76 (ZG 76)  was a German heavy bomber squadron of World War II. It was established in 1941 and served exclusively in the Mediterranean theater of operations, where it participated in many major campaigns.  Zerstörergeschwader 76 was part of the Kampfgeschwader (KG) 52, the second highest scoring fighter unit in the Luftwaffe during World War II. The unit claimed 1,294 aerial victories, making it one of the most successful bomber squadrons of the war. Zerstörergeschwader 76 also played a significant role in Operation Barbarossa, the German invasion of Russia. The squadron suffered heavy losses during the Battle of Kursk, however it was able to continue operations and claim a further 448 aerial victories before its eventual surrender in 1945.  Zerstörergeschwader 76 was formed on 1 October 1941 from II./Kampfgeschwader 26 (KG 26), one of the original eleven Jagdgeschwader (fighter wings) of the Luftwaffe. It served exclusively in the Mediterranean theater of operations, where its main targets were targets in Italy and North Africa. The unit participated in many major campaigns, including Operation Barbarossa, the Invasion of Sicily, Operation Husky (the Allied invasion of Sicily), and the Battle of Kursk.
