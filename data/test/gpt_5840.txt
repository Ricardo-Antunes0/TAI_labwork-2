Michael MacAuliffe, also known as Max Arthur Macauliffe, (; 12 January 1881 â€“ 16 December 1941) was a British historian and academic.  He is best known for his celebrated History of the British Empire, which was published in nine volumes between 1938 and 1954. Macauliffe also wrote extensively on medieval literature and the history of education in England.  Born in Bradford, West Yorkshire, Macauliffe was educated at Bradford Grammar School and Trinity College, Cambridge, where he read History. He took his first academic post at the University of Liverpool in 1911. In 1915 he was appointed Professor of History at Queens' College, Cambridge, a post he held until his death.  During the Second World War, Macauliffe served as a member of the British War Cabinet's Historical Committee. He died in Cambridge at the age of 57.
