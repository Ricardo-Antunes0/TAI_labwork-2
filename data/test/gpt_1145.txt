Derby Dining Center, named after reference librarian and Kentucky native, Derby Holmes, is a library-affiliated restaurant in the heart of the University of Kentucky's campus in Lexington, Kentucky. It is open to the public and serves a variety of breakfast, lunch, and dinner options.  Derby Dining Center was founded in 2008 by two University of Kentucky students, Cameron McCracken and Ryan Ferguson. The restaurant was named after reference librarian and Kentucky native, Derby Holmes. Derby Dining Center is a library-affiliated restaurant which means that all profits go back into the library. The restaurant has a variety of breakfast, lunch, and dinner options which include items such as burgers, chicken tenders, salads, pasta dishes, desserts, and coffee.
