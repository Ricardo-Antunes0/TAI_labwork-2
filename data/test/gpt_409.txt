"The Wedge" is a 1993 song by the American rock band Pearl Jam. The song was written by vocalist Eddie Vedder and guitarist Stone Gossard, and was released as the third single from the group's fourth studio album, Vitalogy.  "The Wedge" is a ballad with a slow tempo, and is written in a verse-chorus form. The lyrics explore Vedder's personal struggles with addiction and self-imposed exile. The song has been described as an "emotional journey" and "epic".  "The Wedge" reached number eight on the US "Billboard" Hot 100 chart and number one on the Mainstream Rock Tracks chart. It also reached number six on the UK Singles Chart.
