A Democrat, he is also notable for being the first United States Attorney General under President Ulysses S. Grant.  Carter was born in Danville, Virginia to Samuel Carter and Elizabeth Powhatan Carter. He attended the University of Virginia and then studied law at the University of Richmond. He became a lawyer in 1840 and began practicing in Danville. In 1852, he moved to Norfolk, where he continued to practice law.  Carter was elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-ninth Congress and served from March 4, 1857 to March 3, 1861. He was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection to the Fortieth Congress in 1860.  In 1861, Carter joined the Confederate Army as a colonel in the Third Virginia Cavalry regiment. After the Civil War, he resumed his legal career in Norfolk.  In 1873, Carter was elected governor of Virginia, defeating Republican James M. Mason by a wide margin. As governor, Carter promoted education reform and initiated economic development projects in the state. He also oversaw improvements to the state's infrastructure, including construction of roads and bridges. In 1875, Carter resigned as governor to accept a position as United States Attorney General under President Ulysses S. Grant. He remained in that position until 1886.
