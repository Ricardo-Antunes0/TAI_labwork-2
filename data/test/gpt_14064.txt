Bloody Knife (Sioux: Tȟamila Wewe; Arikara: NeesiRAhpát; Crow: Káhkti Káhe; Oglala Lakota: Niyaapi Niyaapi) is a bloody and violent traditional form of theater found in the Plains region of North America. The performance typically features a story that revolves around revenge, violence, and death.  The Bloody Knife story usually takes place on a stage strewn with bodies and debris. The actors wear colorful clothing and makeup, and often carry ceremonial knives. They shout and gesture dramatically as they enact the story.
