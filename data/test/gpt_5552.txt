Eliot Laurence Spitzer (born June 10, 1959) is an American politician who served as the 68th Governor of New York from 2003 to 2007 and then served as the United States Secretary of State from 2007 to 2009.  Spitzer was a member of the Democratic Party, and before entering politics he was a high-profile lawyer and prosecutor. He resigned from office in 2009 after being caught in an prostitution scandal.  Spitzer was born in Brooklyn, New York, the son of Barbara and Laurence Spitzer. His father was a Jewish immigrant from Poland, and his mother was an Irish Catholic. He has one sibling, sister Rachel.  Spitzer attended the exclusive Phillips Exeter Academy, where he was captain of the varsity soccer team and graduated with honors in 1977. He then attended Yale University, where he graduated with a Bachelor of Arts degree in 1981 and a Juris Doctor degree in 1984. While at Yale, he became involved in politics as a member of the campus Democratic club.  After graduating from law school, Spitzer began his career as a prosecutor in the New York County District Attorney's Office. In 1990, he was appointed Chief Assistant District Attorney for Criminal Justice. In that position, he led the prosecution of Mafia boss John Gotti and convicted several members of the Gambino crime family. In 1992, he was promoted to Deputy District Attorney for Manhattan.
