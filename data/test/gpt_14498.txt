Lewis Ronald McNeir (born December 14, 1951) is an American jazz pianist.  McNeir was born in Los Angeles, California, and began his musical studies at the age of six. He attended the University of California, Los Angeles, where he studied with Lennie Tristano and Gary Burton. In 1976, McNeir moved to New York City and quickly became a favorite of the city's jazz scene. He has since played with such notable musicians as Dizzy Gillespie, John Coltrane, McCoy Tyner, Jimmy Smith and Herbie Hancock.  McNeir has released several albums over the course of his career, including "Echoes" (1981), "The Traveler" (1983), "Ronnie McNeir" (1994), and "Reflections" (2007). He has also been featured on albums by Chick Corea, Joe Henderson, Gary Burton and Wynton Marsalis. In 2006, McNeir was awarded the DownBeat Jazz Award for Lifetime Achievement.
