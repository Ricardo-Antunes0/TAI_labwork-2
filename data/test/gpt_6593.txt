Andrew Nicholas Mackelworth (born 15 May 1963) is a British Conservative Party politician who has been the Member of Parliament (MP) for the constituency of Chipping Barnet since 2005.  Mackelworth was educated at the University of Southampton and at King's College London. He worked as a financial analyst before entering politics.  Mackelworth served as Vice Chairman of the Conservative Party from 2001 to 2003. He was also Chairman of the Conservative candidate selection committee for the London Mayoral election, 2004, and was elected MP for Chipping Barnet with a majority of 9,000 votes.  In October 2007, Mackelworth was appointed Parliamentary Private Secretary to Secretary of State for Communities and Local Government, Hazel Blears. In this capacity, he was responsible for housing policy. On 14 January 2012 he resigned his position as Parliamentary Private Secretary following allegations that he had used parliamentary expenses to pay for family holidays in Spain. Mackelworth denied any wrongdoing and later testified before the Standards Committee that he believed he had been reimbursed for the holiday by his party.  In September 2012, Mackelworth was appointed Shadow Minister for Disabled People and Carers. In this role, he campaigned on behalf of people with disabilities and carers. On 6 May 2015 Mackelworth was appointed Shadow Minister for Education by new Conservative Leader Theresa May.  Mackelworth is a member of the 1922 Committee, which is responsible for electing Conservative Party leader. He is also a member of the Work and Pensions Select Committee and the Environment
