The Mediterranean horse mackerel (Trachurus mediterraneus), also known as the common horse mackerel, is a member of the family Scombridae found in the Mediterranean Sea and the Black Sea. It is a large fish up to 1.8 m (6 ft 3 in) long and 100 kg (220 lb) in weight, with a deep red or black body and white or yellow spots on its back. The horse mackerel is an important source of protein and omega-3 fatty acids for humans and other animals.
