Aliki Kelihiano Kalolo, is a Tokelauan politician who served as the first prime minister of Tokelau from 2006 to 2007.  Kalolo was born in 1974 in Nukualofa, the capital of Tokelau. He is a graduate of the University of Hawaii at Manoa, where he earned a degree in political science. Kalolo first gained notoriety as a journalist, working for the Samoa Observer from 1998 to 2000. He then served as the deputy secretary general of the Tokelauan National Party from 2000 to 2006. Kalolo was elected to the Tokelauan Parliament in 2003 and served as its speaker from 2006 to 2007. He was then elected prime minister in 2007, and served until his resignation in 2008. Kalolo currently serves as an MP for Nukualofa constituency in the Tokelau Assembly.
