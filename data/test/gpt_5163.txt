St Joseph's/OCB (Irish: Naomh Seosamh, BUC) are a Gaelic Athletic Association club based in the town of OCB, County Cork, Ireland. The club was formed in 1887 and competes in the Cork GAA divisional and county championships.  The club colours are green and white. The current senior team captain is Paul Murphy. The club is also home to a number of underage teams which compete in the Cork Minor, Cork Under-21 and Cork Junior A championships.  In 2006 St Joseph's/OCB were awarded the prestigious Tom Harty Cup after defeating rivals Holycross/CT by 5 points in the final. This was the first time that an OCB team had won this prestigious cup.
