Isaac ben Abraham Uziel  (died 1125) was a medieval Jewish scholar and poet who wrote the commentary on the Torah known as the Sefer Ha-Ari.  Isaac Uziel was born in Worms, Germany, in about 1110. He first studied in the city of Mainz, before moving to Toulouse, where he became a disciple of the famous philosopher Moses Maimonides. Uziel then travelled to Jerusalem, where he spent the remainder of his life.  Isaac Uziel is best known for his commentary on the Torah known as the Sefer Ha-Ari. This work comprises five volumes and covers every letter of the Hebrew Bible. Uziel also wrote a number of other works, including a commentary on the Talmud and a Hebrew grammar book. He died in Jerusalem in 1125.
