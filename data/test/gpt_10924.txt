KCPQ, virtual and VHF digital channel 13, is a Fox-affiliated television station licensed to Portland, Oregon, United States. The station is owned by the Tribune Broadcasting subsidiary of the Tribune Media Company. KCPQ's studios are located on Southwest Stark Street in downtown Portland, and its transmitter is located in Hillsboro.  KCPQ signed on the air on December 18, 1961 as an ABC affiliate. It became a charter member of the Fox network on October 6, 1986. KCPQ discontinued regular programming at 11:00 p.m. on September 12, 2009 to make way for Fox's newscast "Fox 13 News at 11". The station resumed programming at midnight with a two-hour block of infomercials and other local commercials until 5:00 a.m., when it began airing Fox network programming again.  In January 2010, KCPQ announced that it would drop its affiliation with ABC and become an NBC affiliate as part of a three-way affiliation agreement between KCPQ, MyNetworkTV affiliate KCWX (channel 44) and independent station KMTT (channel 21). The switch took effect on February 17; however, KCWX continued to air ABC programming until May 15, 2010, when that station signed-off for good.
