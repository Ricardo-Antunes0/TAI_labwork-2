Václav Zelený (August 18, 1936, Prague, Czechoslovakia - November 5, 1992, Prague, Czechoslovakia) was a Czechoslovak composer and conductor.  Zelený studied at the Prague Conservatory with Josef Suk and Jiří Bartoš. He later worked as a conductor and pianist with the Czech Philharmonic Orchestra.  His compositions include operas, chamber music, symphonies, and choral works. His best-known work is the opera "The Unbearable Lightness of Being".
