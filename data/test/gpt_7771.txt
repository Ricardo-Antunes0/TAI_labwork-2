Bahman Jādhūyah/Jādūyah (also Jādhōē/Jādōē; New Persian: ), also Bahman Jadu, was a Persian poet and mystic of the Ghaznavid era.  Bahman Jaduya was born in Balkh in the early 11th century. He studied under Nasir Khusraw and later became court poet to the Ghaznavids. Jaduya is best known for his mystical poetry, which explores the relationship between humans and divine beings. His works have been praised for their elegance and simplicity.
