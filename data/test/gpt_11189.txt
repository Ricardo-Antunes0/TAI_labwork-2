William Wayne Stewart (April 12, 1928 – June 5, 2009) was an American professional baseball player and manager. He played in Major League Baseball (MLB) for the Detroit Tigers (1951–1954), Philadelphia Phillies (1955), and St. Louis Cardinals (1956–1959). He won the 1955 World Series with the Tigers. He was a minor league manager for the Houston Astros (1966–1970), Minnesota Twins (1971), and Baltimore Orioles (1972).  Stewart was born in Findlay, Ohio, and attended Findlay High School. He played college baseball at Ohio State University, where he was a teammate of future major league players Ken Kelleher and Jim Maloney. The Tigers drafted Stewart in the eighth round of the 1951 draft.  Stewart made his MLB debut with the Tigers in 1951, appearing in 54 games as a shortstop and hitting .207 with one home run and nine RBIs. In 1952, he appeared in 81 games and hit .236 with five home runs and 32 RBIs. He helped lead the Tigers to their first World Series title in 55 years when he batted .333 with two home runs and nine RBIs in the 1955 World Series. Stewart also hit .313 with two home runs and eight RBIs in the 1956 World Series.
