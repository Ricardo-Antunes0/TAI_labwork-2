Max Eastley (born 1 December 1944, Torquay, Devon) is an English singer, songwriter and musician. He is best known for his work with the punk rock band the Buzzcocks.  Eastley was born in Torquay, Devon, England, the son of a Royal Air Force serviceman and a homemaker. He was educated at the independent King's School in Torquay before studying music at the University of Southampton.  After a short spell in the military, Eastley started his career as a session musician and backing vocalist for pop and rock acts of the late 1960s and early 1970s. He first came to prominence as the singer and songwriter of the punk rock band the Buzzcocks, who formed in 1971. The band achieved international success with their singles "Love Song" (1973) and "I Don't Wanna Be a Hero" (1975), both of which reached number one in the UK charts. Eastley left the Buzzcocks in 1976 to pursue a solo career.  His first solo album, "Max Eastley", was released in 1977. Although it failed to achieve commercial success, Eastley's subsequent albums have been well-received by critics. He has also released several compilation albums and toured extensively throughout Europe, North America and Japan. In 2010, he released his first album of new material in over 20 years, "The New Max Eastley".  Eastley lives in London with his wife Katja and their two children.
