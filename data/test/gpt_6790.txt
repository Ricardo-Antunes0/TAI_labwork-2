Cullie M. Tarleton is a North Carolina-based country singer-songwriter and storyteller. Born in Hickory, North Carolina, Tarleton first began playing guitar at the age of 10. After moving to Nashville in 2006, he began writing and recording his own music. In 2009 he released his debut album "Gravity". His sophomore album "Crazy World" was released in 2013 and was nominated for a Grammy award. He has since released three more albums: "The Outsiders" (2016), "King of the Road" (2018), and "The Ballad of Cullie Tarleton" (2020).  Tarleton has toured extensively throughout the United States and Europe, and has sold over one million records. He has also been featured on albums by Jason Aldean, Miranda Lambert, and Garth Brooks. In addition to his music, Tarleton is also a storyteller and has performed at various festivals around the United States.
