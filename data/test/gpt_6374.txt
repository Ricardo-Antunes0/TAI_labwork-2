Timothy Milledge Hankinson (born February 18, 1955) is an American politician and businessman who is the 84th and current Governor of North Carolina.  Hankinson was born in Raleigh, North Carolina, the son of Doris (n√©e Hanes) and Thomas R. Hankinson. He attended the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, where he was a member of the Sigma Chi Fraternity. He graduated with a B.A. in 1977 and an M.B.A. in 1980.  Hankinson worked as a financial analyst for Arthur Andersen from 1977 to 1981. He then started his own accounting firm, Hankinson & Associates, which he ran until 1988. From 1988 to 1992, he served as deputy secretary of state under Governor James G. Martin.  In 1992, Hankinson was elected to the North Carolina General Assembly as a Democrat representing Wake County. He served in the state Senate from 1993 to 2001, becoming the Majority Leader in 1998 and serving as Speaker of the Senate from 1999 to 2001. In November 2000, he was elected Lieutenant Governor of North Carolina, serving until January 2003 when he was elected governor as a Democrat. He was re-elected in 2006 and 2010. As lieutenant governor, Hankinson served as the President of the North Carolina State Board of Education from 2002 to 2004.  As Governor of North Carolina, Hankinson has focused on improving the economy by creating jobs and raising incomes, reforming government so it is more efficient and effective, increasing access to quality education for all children
