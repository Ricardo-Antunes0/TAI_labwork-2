Hasan Kamal (born 1 January 1943) is an Indian poet, essayist and literary critic. He is the editor of the literary journal Pritish Nandy's quarterly magazine, Pratidin.  Hasan Kamal was born in Baroda, Gujarat in 1943. He completed his B.A. degree from the University of Bombay in 1965 and his M.A. from the University of Oxford in 1968. He taught at the University of Delhi from 1970 to 1992, when he retired.  Kamal's work has been published in English, Hindi, Urdu, Tamil, Telugu and Bengali. His best-known book is Ghazal: The Poems (1982), which won the Sahitya Akademi Award for English poetry in 1983. His other books include The Eye of the Storm (1990), The Argumentative Indian (1996), The Creative Process (1998), and A Season of Moths (2002).
