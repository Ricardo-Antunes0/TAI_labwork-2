The Shelton Oak, also known as Owen's Oak, is a large oak tree located in Shelton, Connecticut. It is the largest oak tree in Connecticut and the second-largest in New England.  The Shelton Oak is believed to be over 1,000 years old and is one of the few surviving examples of an oak tree that was originally native to New England. The tree is notable for its deep green leaves and large size, which has made it a popular tourist destination. The Shelton Oak is also home to a colony of American black bears, which can be seen roaming around its branches during the summer months.
