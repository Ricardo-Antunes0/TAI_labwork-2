David Salisbury Franks (1740–1793) was aide-de-camp for General George Washington and a general in the Continental Army during the American Revolution.  David Franks was born in 1740 in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. He was the son of Major General David Franks (1713–1790) and Susanna Salisbury Franks (1716–1796). His grandfather was the British diplomat Sir Francis Bernard Salisbury (1678–1751).  Franks joined the Continental Army as an ensign in 1775 and was promoted to lieutenant colonel in 1779. He fought in the Battle of Bunker Hill and was wounded at the Battle of Guilford Courthouse. He served as Washington's aide-de-camp from 1779 to 1783, and then from 1786 to 1793. He also served as a general in the Pennsylvania militia from 1779 to 1783 and from 1786 to 1793.  Franks died on January 8, 1793 in Philadelphia. He was buried in Christ Church Burial Ground.
