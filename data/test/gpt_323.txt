Madrid is served by highly developed transport infrastructure. The city has a large number of airports, including the main international airport, Madrid Barajas, and medium-sized airports for regional flights. Madrid also has a large railway system, including two main lines that run through the city centre, as well as a commuter rail system. The city has a comprehensive bus network, and there are also several riverboats and ferries that operate along the Manzanares and Tagus rivers.   Madrid is connected to other Spanish cities by a number of motorways, including the A-5, A-6 and A-7. There are also several ring roads around the city, as well as several radial roads that connect the various neighbourhoods. In addition to public transport, Madrid is also served by a large number of taxis and ride-sharing services.
