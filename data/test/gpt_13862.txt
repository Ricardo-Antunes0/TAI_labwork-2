Gnathothlibus saccoi is a moth of the family Sphingidae. It was described by Rothschild in 1903 and is known from Peru.  The wingspan is about 60Â mm. The forewings are light yellowish-ochreous, the dorsal margin more or less distinctly suffused with olive-green, the costa broadly suffused with olive-green posteriorly and with a broad olive-green band from two-thirds of the costa to the tornus, edged posteriorly with blackish. There is a slender blackish streak along the posterior margin of the band, as well as a blackish dot on the costa beyond the middle and an oblique olive-green streak from before the middle of the costa to near the apex. The hindwings are pale grey.
