Rear Admiral John Gervaise Beresford Cooke CB, DSO, RN was a Royal Navy officer who served as First Sea Lord from 1912 to 1916.  Born on 8 October 1873 in London, Cooke was educated at Eton and the Royal Naval College, Dartmouth. He entered the Royal Navy in 1892 and served in the First World War as captain of the battleship HMS Inflexible. After the war he became Deputy Chief of Staff to the Commander-in-Chief, Home Fleet. He was promoted to rear admiral in 1920 and became First Sea Lord in 1912. He retired in 1916 and died on 10 July 1936.
