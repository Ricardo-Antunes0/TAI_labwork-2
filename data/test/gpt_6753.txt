Seymour Peak is a 6,337-foot-elevation (1,932 meter) mountain summit in the Sawtooth Range in Idaho. The peak is located in the Sawtooth National Forest, east of Twin Falls and north of Stanley.  Seymour Peak is the tenth highest peak in Idaho and the third highest peak in the Sawtooth Range. The peak is named after Seymour Griggs, an early pioneer who settled in the area. The peak is part of a large area known as the Sawtooth National Recreation Area which includes many other peaks over 6,000 feet (1,800 meters) in elevation.  Seymour Peak was first climbed in 1937 by a party led by Earl W. Sawtell.
