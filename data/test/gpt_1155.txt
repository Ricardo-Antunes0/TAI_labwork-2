He also served as the Minister of Education from 2007 to 2008.  Iqbal was born in Sialkot, Punjab in 1950. After studying at Government College Sialkot, he earned a degree in philosophy from the University of Punjab in 1973. He then worked as a teacher before becoming a journalist. Iqbal became politically active in the late 1970s and early 1980s, and was one of the founders of the Pakistan Peoples Party (PPP). He served as the party's president from 1988 to 1989 and again from 1997 to 2002.  Iqbal was elected to the National Assembly of Pakistan as a PPP candidate from Constituency NA-XXX in Pakistani general election, 1990. He served as the Minister for Planning and Development in the first two cabinets of Prime Minister Benazir Bhutto. Iqbal was elected to the Senate of Pakistan in Pakistani general election, 2002, and served as the Minister for Information and Broadcasting until his resignation in 2009.  Iqbal was elected to the Prime Ministership of Pakistan on 9 June 2009, after coalition negotiations between his party (the PPP) and the Pakistan Muslim League (Nawaz) resulted in both parties holding an equal number of seats in parliament.
