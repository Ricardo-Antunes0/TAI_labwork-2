Hansen was born in Lexington, Kentucky, on February 15, 1939. He received his B.S. in physics from the University of Kentucky in 1961 and his M.S. and Ph.D. in atmospheric science from the University of Colorado at Boulder in 1964 and 1968, respectively.  Hansen began his career as a research scientist at the National Center for Atmospheric Research in Boulder, Colorado, where he specialized in global warming and climate change. He also served as a professor of atmospheric science at the University of Colorado from 1978 to 1997. Hansen was a fellow of the American Meteorological Society and the American Geophysical Union. He died on January 29, 1997, in Boulder City, Nevada, after a long battle with pancreatic cancer.
