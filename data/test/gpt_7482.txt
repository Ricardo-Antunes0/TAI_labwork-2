Seo Kang-joon (born Lee Seung-hwan on October 9, 1973) is a South Korean film director and screenwriter. He made his debut with the short film Five Years (2002).  Seo has directed a number of critically acclaimed films, such as Oldboy (2003), Sympathy for Mr. Vengeance (2003), Memories of Murder (2003), A Bittersweet Life (2005), Mother (2009), and The Handmaiden (2016). In 2015, he was awarded the Palme d'Or at the Cannes Film Festival for his directorial work on The Yellow Sea.
