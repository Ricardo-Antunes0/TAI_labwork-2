Danielle Jane Minogue (born 20 October 1971) is an Australian pop and soul singer and actress. After starting her career as a pop singer in the late 1980s, Minogue achieved worldwide success with her second album, Light Years (1991), which spawned the number-one singles "I Believe in You" and "Can't Get You Out of My Head". She has since released seven more studio albums, two compilation albums, one soundtrack album, and one video album.  Minogue has been awarded ten Brit Awards, four Grammy Awards, two Emmy Awards, a Golden Globe Award, a Indira Gandhi Award for International Peace and Solidarity, and a Star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame. She has sold over 220 million records worldwide and is the best-selling Australian artist of all time. In November 2010, she was inducted into the ARIA Hall of Fame.
