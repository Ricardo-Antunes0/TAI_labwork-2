Alice Peacock (born November 19, 1969) is an American actress, model and author.  Peacock was born in Chicago, Illinois and raised in the Near North Side community area of Chicago. She is of Irish descent. Her father, Dick Peacock, is a retired police officer from the Illinois Department of Corrections. Her mother, Joyce (née Murphy), is a retired social worker and early childhood education teacher. Peacock has two older sisters, Patti and Kathy.  Peacock began modeling at the age of eight, appearing in numerous national print ads for such brands as Sears Roebuck and JCPenney. In 1988, she appeared as the title character in the made-for-TV movie "Alice". That year, she also made her Broadway debut in the musical "The Little Mermaid" as Ariel. She won a Tony Award for Best Featured Actress in a Musical for her performance.  In 1996, Peacock starred as Rebecca Wright in the ABC television series "Rebecca". The show was cancelled after one season due to low ratings. In 2000, she starred as Dr. Remy Adamson in the Fox television series "Bones". The show ran for six seasons and won Peacock two Golden Globe Awards (one for Best Actress – Television Series Drama and one for Best Supporting Actress – Series, Miniseries or Film).
