Frederick A. Murphy is a retired American professional basketball player and head coach. Murphy played collegiately at the University of Connecticut and was drafted in the first round of the 1986 NBA Draft by the Philadelphia 76ers. He played for the 76ers, Golden State Warriors, Charlotte Hornets, and New York Knicks in his career. He was also a member of the United States men's national basketball team which won gold medals at the 1992 Barcelona Olympics and 1996 Atlanta Olympics. Murphy was named head coach of the Houston Rockets in 2009, but was fired after one season.  Murphy was born in Bridgeport, Connecticut on May 26, 1965. He attended Stonington High School where he played basketball and football. After high school, Murphy attended the University of Connecticut where he played for the Connecticut Huskies men's basketball team from 1983 to 1986. As a junior in 1985–86, Murphy led Connecticut to its first NCAA Tournament appearance since 1973 and was named First Team All-American by The Sporting News. As a senior in 1986–87, Murphy led Connecticut to its first Big East Conference championship and NCAA Tournament appearance since 1973. As a Husky, Murphy averaged 18.7 points, 5.1 rebounds and 3.4 assists per game.  Murphy was drafted by the Philadelphia 76ers with the ninth overall pick in the 1986 NBA Draft. He played for the 76ers (1986–1991), Golden State Warriors (1991–1994), Charlotte Hornets (1994–1996), and New York Knicks (1996–1999).
