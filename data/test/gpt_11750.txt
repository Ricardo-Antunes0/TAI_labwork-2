The Leopard  is a novel by Giuseppe Tomasi di Lampedusa, first published in 1958. Inspired by the story of Prince Giorgio de Medici's abduction of Catherine de' Medici, the novel tells the story of a middle-class family in Sicily during the 1930s.  The Leopard has been described as a masterpiece of Mediterranean writing and one of the most important novels of the 20th century. It is also frequently cited as an example of postcolonial literature.  The novel has been translated into more than 25 languages and has sold over 25 million copies worldwide.
