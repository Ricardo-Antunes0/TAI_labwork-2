"Should've Said No" is a song written by American singer and songwriter Keri Hilson, and produced by American record producer Dallas Austin. The song was released as the fourth and final single from Hilson's debut studio album In a Perfect World (2009).  "Should've Said No" is a dance-pop song that lyrically discusses the protagonist's regrets for decisions made in their past. The song was well received by music critics, who noted its catchiness and Hilson's vocal performance. It reached the top forty in several countries, including Australia, Canada, France, Germany, Hungary, Ireland, Italy, Netherlands, New Zealand, Norway, Sweden and the United States.   The accompanying music video for "Should've Said No" was directed by Dave Meyers and premiered on Hilson's official YouTube channel on July 21, 2010. It features Hilson dancing around a warehouse with her friends.  Keri Hilson was born in Fort Lauderdale, Florida in 1978. She started out as a backing vocalist for artists such as Usher and Ja Rule before releasing her solo debut album In a Perfect World in 2009. The album spawned four singles: "Don't Mess With My Man", "Another Sad Love Song", "Should've Said No" and "I Don't Wanna".
