Rishi Vohra is a novelist of Indian descent who writes in English. Born in London, England, he moved to India as a child and was educated in India and the United States.  Vohra's first novel, The Alchemists, was published in 2006 and won the Commonwealth Writers' Prize for Best First Book. His second novel, The Night Season, was published in 2010 and won the Arthur C. Clarke Award. His third novel, The Immortals of Meluha, was published in 2014 and won the prestigiously prestigious Indian Literary Award for Best Novel.  Vohra's work has been translated into more than twenty languages and he has been shortlisted for the Man Booker Prize and the International IMPAC Dublin Literary Award.
