Jerome Robinson (born February 22, 1997) is an American professional basketball player for the Indiana Pacers of the National Basketball Association (NBA). He played college basketball for the Arizona Wildcats.  Robinson was born in Columbus, Ohio, to Jerome Robinson Sr., a former collegiate and professional player who played in the NBA and Europe, and Lynette Robinson, a former collegiate and professional player who also played in the WNBA. His great-grandfather, Jerome Robinson, was also a professional player. Robinson attended Northland High School in Columbus.  Robinson played college basketball for the Arizona Wildcats. In his freshman season, he averaged 10.5 points and 5.0 rebounds per game. In his sophomore season, he averaged 16.3 points and 7.3 rebounds per game. In his junior season, he averaged 22.5 points and 11.0 rebounds per game. In his senior season, he averaged 28.9 points and 12.8 rebounds per game.  After going undrafted in the 2018 NBA draft, Robinson signed with the Indiana Pacers on July 6, 2018. He made his NBA debut on October 18, 2018 against the Milwaukee Bucks, recording three points and two rebounds in 15 minutes off the bench.
