555 17th Street, formerly known as the Plaza Hotel, is a historic building located in Midtown Manhattan, New York City. The building was built in 1931 as the headquarters of the General Motors Corporation. It is a 14-story limestone-clad structure with Art Deco features. The Plaza Hotel ceased operations on January 2, 2009, and the building was sold to the Related Companies on March 5, 2009.  555 17th Street is located on the northeast corner of Fifth Avenue and 17th Street in Midtown Manhattan. The building is bounded by Fifth Avenue to the north, Madison Avenue to the east, E 42nd Street to the south, and 17th Street to the west.  The Plaza Hotel was designed by McKim, Mead & White and built in 1931 as headquarters for General Motors Corporation. The building was designated a New York City landmark on October 10, 1968. The hotel ceased operations on January 2, 2009. The Related Companies purchased the building for $1 billion in March 2009.
