The Bucharest–Giurgiu Motorway , labelled A5, is a motorway in Romania that connects Bucharest with Giurgiu. The motorway is long, and has a maximum speed limit of 130 kilometres per hour.  The Bucharest–Giurgiu Motorway was built in the 1980s as part of the Romanian highway network. It runs through the counties of Giurgiu and Bucharest, and has a total length of . The motorway is divided into two sections, with a total of 34 km of express lanes.  The Bucharest–Giurgiu Motorway is one of Romania's most important highways, connecting Bucharest with the rest of the country. It is also used by tourists visiting Giurgiu, as it provides access to the city's attractions.
