Caladenia minorata, commonly known as the small-flowered caladenia orchid, is a species of orchid that is endemic to the south-west of Western Australia.  The small-flowered caladenia orchid is a terrestrial, perennial, deciduous, herb with an underground tuber and a single green leaf up to 15 cm long and 2.5 cm wide. Up to 50 flowers are arranged on a stem up to 25 cm tall. The flowers are greenish-white and have a hooded yellow labellum.   The small-flowered caladenia orchid grows in damp areas near streams and rivers.
