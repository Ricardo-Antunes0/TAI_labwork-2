Project Exploration is a not-for-profit educational program that uses an immersive virtual reality environment to explore natural and cultural heritage sites around the world.  Project Exploration was founded in 2006 by Dr. Jarrett Bell and his wife, Dr. Brooke Bell. The program is based in Wilmington, North Carolina, and uses state-of-the-art virtual reality technology to allow participants to explore sites around the world.  The program has made more than 50 trips to more than 20 countries, including visits to sites in Europe, the Middle East, Africa, Asia, and North America. In 2016, Project Exploration partnered with the National Geographic Society to create a new VR experience called "Lost Cities of Africa".
