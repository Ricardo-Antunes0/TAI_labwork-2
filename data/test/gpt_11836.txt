John Doucett (Doucette) (died November 19, 1726) was an American colonist who was the second governor of the Province of Massachusetts Bay.  Doucett was born in England around 1675. He emigrated to Boston in 1700 and became a merchant. He was elected as a member of the Boston Town Meeting in 1711 and served as its moderator in 1712. In 1716, Doucett was appointed one of the three commissioners to oversee the construction of a new colonial court house. He became the colony's second governor in 1720 and served until his death in 1726.
