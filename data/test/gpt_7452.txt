William Jasper East (September 1, 1854 - May 12, 1938) was an American lawyer and politician who served as the 34th Attorney General of the United States from 1909 to 1913.  Born in Ripley, Ohio, East earned a A.B. from the University of Michigan in 1876 and an LL.B. from Harvard Law School in 1879. He was a law clerk to Supreme Court Justice Horace Gray from 1879 to 1880 and to United States Senator James A. Garfield from 1880 to 1881. East was then a lawyer in Cleveland, Ohio, where he became a partner in the law firm of East, Gray and Loomis.  In 1909, East was nominated by President William Howard Taft to be the 34th Attorney General of the United States. He was confirmed by the United States Senate and served until 1913. During his time as Attorney General, East fought against corruption and organized crime. He also played a role in prosecuting the leaders of the 1912 assassination attempt on President William Howard Taft. East retired from active legal practice in 1914 and died in 1938 at age 83.
