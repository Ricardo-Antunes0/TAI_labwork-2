The Articles of Union of Tanganyika and Zanzibar were signed on October 26, 1961, and came into effect on December 1 of that year. The Articles of Union created a single political entity, the United Republic of Tanganyika and Zanzibar, with a president and a parliament. The president was to be the head of state, with a parliamentary system consisting of an upper house, the Senate, and a lower house, the National Assembly. The Articles also stated that English was to be the main language of government and commerce.
