Charles Joseph Krebs   (born 17 January 1945) is a British mathematician and theoretical biologist. His research is in the area of mathematical biology, specifically the theory of networks and queuing models.  Krebs was born in London, England to parents who were both teachers. He attended the University of Cambridge, where he received his Bachelor of Arts in 1966 and his Master of Arts in 1968. He then went to the University of Oxford, where he received his Doctor of Philosophy in 1972.  Krebs has been a fellow of St John's College, Cambridge since 1974, and a professor at the University of Cambridge since 1986.
