The Weaver Gymnasium, formally known as the Warren Weaver Gymnasium, is a historic gymnasium located on the campus of Rutgers University in New Brunswick, New Jersey. It was designed by the architectural firm of McKim, Mead and White and constructed in 1919. The gymnasium was named for Warren Weaver, a trustee of the university who donated $100,000 to its construction.  The Weaver Gymnasium is a three-story brick structure with limestone trim. It has a hipped roof and a central entry pavilion flanked by two Ionic columns. The interior features a gymnasium floor with seating for 1,500 spectators, as well as a dance studio and band hall. The gymnasium was designated a National Historic Landmark in 1976. It is currently used for events such as concerts and commencement ceremonies.
