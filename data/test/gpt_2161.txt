Rweyemamu was born in Iringa, in the then British Tanganyika Territory, on 28 September 1942. He attended secondary school at Makerere University, where he earned a B.Sc. degree in economics in 1965. He also earned a M.A. degree in international relations from the same institution in 1970.  Rweyemamu began his political career as a member of the Tanganyika African National Union (TANU), which he joined in 1961. He subsequently served as the party's secretary-general from 1969 to 1971 and its vice president from 1971 to 1975. In 1975, he was appointed Tanzania's ambassador to China, where he served until 1977.  Upon his return to Tanzania, Rweyemamu resumed his work with TANU and was elected to the country's National Assembly as a representative for Iringa District in 1977. He subsequently served as TANU's deputy secretary-general from 1979 to 1981 and its secretary-general from 1981 to 1987. In 1987, he was elected as the country's first president, serving until 1992.  Rweyemamu died on 10 May 1997 at the age of 58 after a long illness. He was buried at Iringa Cemetery.
