Aplysina archeri, also known as a stove-pipe orchid, is a species of orchid endemic to the United States. It is found in the Appalachian Mountains and the Ozark Plateau.  The plant is a perennial herb with a fleshy, papery stem up to 25 centimeters long. The leaves are lance-shaped, up to 12 centimeters long and 2 wide, and have a pointed tip. The flowers are borne on a tall spike up to 30 centimeters tall, and have white petals that droop over the flower tube. The fruit is a brown capsule that splits open to release the seeds.
