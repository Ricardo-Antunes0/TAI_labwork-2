Josephine Gatt Ciancio (born 1946) is a Brazilian artist and sculptor. She has exhibited her work in Brazil, Germany, Switzerland, Italy, Spain and the United States.  Ciancio was born in São Paulo, Brazil in 1946. She studied at the Escola Superior de Belas Artes (ESBA), graduating in 1971. She then enrolled at the Universidade Federal de São Paulo (UNIFESP), where she obtained a degree in sculpture in 1974.   Ciancio's sculptures are often constructed from metal, wood or plastic and are often large-scale works that incorporate elements of both abstract and figurative art. Her work has been described as "original, expressive and powerful".  Ciancio has exhibited her work throughout Brazil, Germany, Switzerland, Italy, Spain and the United States. Her work is in the collections of the Museu de Arte da Paz (MUP), the Centro Cultural Banco do Brasil (CCBB) and the Banco Nacional do Desenvolvimento Econômico e Social (BNDES). In 2010 she was awarded the Creu de Sant Jordi (St George's Cross) medal by the Generalitat de Catalunya.
