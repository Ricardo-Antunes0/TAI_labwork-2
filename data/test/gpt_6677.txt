Cornell Iral Haynes Jr. (born November 2, 1972), better known by his stage name Nelly, is an American rapper and singer. Born in St. Louis, Missouri, Nelly first became known in the mid-1990s as part of the local St. Louis hip hop scene. He subsequently signed with Universal Records and released his debut album Country Grammar in 2000. The album produced the singles "Hot in Herre" and "Dilemma". Nelly went on to become one of the world's most successful rappers, with seven consecutive platinum-certified albums and singles including 2003's single "Hot in Herre (The Perfect Storm)", which reached number one on the US "Billboard" Hot 100 chart. In 2006, he released The Neptunes Present: Nelly Furtado, a collaborative album which served as his comeback after a two-year absence.   Nelly has been nominated for numerous awards, including five Grammy Awards, two American Music Awards, and three Billboard Music Awards. In 2009, Nelly was ranked number 58 on VH1's 100 Greatest Artists of All Time. In 2011, he was ranked number 10 on Rolling Stone's list of the "50 Greatest Rappers of All Time".
