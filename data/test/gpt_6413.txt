Rosalie Dreyer (3 September 1895 – 21 June 1978) was a Belgian film actress. She appeared in more than sixty films during her career, which lasted from the silent era to the early 1970s.  Dreyer was born in Hasselt, Belgium, the daughter of Anna (née Huyghe) and Emmanuel Dreyer, a carpenter. She began her acting career at the age of fourteen, appearing in a school play. After completing her secondary education, she moved to Brussels and began working as a secretary. In 1916, she decided to give up her job and pursue an acting career.  Her first role was in the Belgian silent film "The Family Robinson". Following this, she appeared in more than sixty films over the next six decades, most notably in roles in French-language films. Her most prominent role was that of Madame Andre in the 1951 film "Les Liaisons dangereuses". Dreyer retired from acting in the early 1970s and died in 1978 at the age of eighty-four.
