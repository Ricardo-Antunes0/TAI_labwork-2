Omer Bar-Lev (; born 2 October 1953) is an Israeli historian who specializes in the history of the Holocaust. He is a professor emeritus of history at Tel Aviv University, where he also served as the dean of Arts and Humanities from 2001 to 2007.     Bar-Lev was born in Tel Aviv, Mandatory Palestine, to a Jewish family from Lithuania. He attended high school in Jerusalem and then received his BA from Hebrew University of Jerusalem in 1976. In 1978 he received his MA from Harvard University and in 1982 his doctorate from the University of Cambridge.  Bar-Lev began his academic career as a faculty member at Tel Aviv University, where he served as chair of the Department of History and Archival Studies (1988–1991) and dean of Arts and Humanities (2001–2007). In 2007, he was appointed professor emeritus at Tel Aviv University.  In 2000, Bar-Lev was one of the founding members of Voices for Peace – an Israeli NGO that opposes unilateral Israeli withdrawal from territories occupied in the 1967 Six-Day War. In 2002, he was a recipient of the Israel Prize for his book "The Inheritance: The History of the Jewish People" (1996).  In 2003, Bar-Lev became a fellow at the American Academy in Berlin. In 2006, he became a fellow at the Harvard Center for European Studies in Cambridge, Massachusetts. In 2010, he was appointed as a member of the prestigious Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey.
