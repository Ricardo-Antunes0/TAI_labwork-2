Visions fugitives, Op. 22, is a cycle of six symphonies written by the Czech composer Antonín Dvořák. It was completed in 1881 and first performed in Prague that same year. The cycle is dedicated to the memory of the composer's mother.  The six symphonies are: "Symphony No. 1", "Symphony No. 2", "Symphony No. 3", "Symphony No. 4", "Symphony No. 5", and "Symphony No. 6".
