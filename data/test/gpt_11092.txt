Amorphophallus (from Ancient Greek , "without form, shape") is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araliaceae. There are about eighty species, distributed mainly in tropical and subtropical regions of the world, with a few species found in temperate zones. They are perennial vines that grow to 30 m or more, with large, fleshy, spongy bulbs up to 2 m across.  The plants are dioecious, with separate male and female plants. The flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects. The fruit is a small, globular capsule containing several small seeds.
