Rubem Azevedo Alves (15 September 1933 – 20 January 2016) was a Brazilian football player and coach who played as a midfielder.  Born in São Paulo, Alves played for hometown club São Paulo Futebol Clube, Santos Laguna, Corinthians, Valencia CF and Atlético Madrid. He served as manager of Atlético Madrid, Botafogo and Flamengo.  Alves was a member of the Brazilian national team that won the 1958 World Cup, the 1962 FIFA World Cup and the 1970 FIFA World Cup. He also played at the 1974 World Cup. He was also part of the Brazil team that finished runner-up at the 1970 Copa América.  Alves died on 20 January 2016 in São Paulo at the age of 83 after a long illness.
