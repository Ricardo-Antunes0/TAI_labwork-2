Jeetu Ahsan or Jitu Ahsan (born Syed Jitu Ahsan) is a Pakistani singer, actor and philanthropist.  Jeetu Ahsan was born in Karachi, Sindh, Pakistan on 9 November 1976. He is the son of Syed Jitu Ahsan and Raja Begum. Jeetu Ahsan completed his schooling from Karachi Grammar School. He then moved to the United States to pursue a higher education at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA), where he graduated with a Bachelor of Arts degree in Theatre Arts in 2000.  Jeetu Ahsan made his acting debut in 2001 with a starring role in the Pakistani drama serial "Ae Dil Hai Mushkil". In 2002, Jeetu Ahsan played the lead role of Arjun in the Pakistani feature film "Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge". For his performance in this film, Jeetu Ahsan won the Best Actor award at the 2002 Lux Style Awards. He also won the Filmfare Critics Award for Best Actor for his performance in "Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge". Jeetu Ahsan's other films include "Tere Bina" (2003), "Aankhen" (2004), "Dilwale" (2005), "Lagaan" (2007), and "The Reluctant Fundamentalist" (2008).
