Dichomeris cinnabarina is a moth in the Gelechiidae family. It was described by Meyrick in 1922. It is found in India (Assam).  The wingspan is about 14Â mm. The forewings are light grey, partially suffused with dark fuscous and with the costal edge blackish towards the base. There is a small dark fuscous spot on the costa near the base, and a larger one on the dorsum before the middle, as well as three other small spots of dark fuscous suffusion, one on the fold before the middle, one midway between this and the dorsum at two-thirds, and one posteriorly. There are also two blackish dots towards the costa before the apex, and a blackish dot beyond it near the termen. The hindwings are light grey, slightly darker towards the base.
