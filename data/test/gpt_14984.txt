May Gowen Watkis (née Hilda May Gowen; born October 21, 1946) is a Canadian actress and singer. She is best known for her roles as Dr. Valerie Brown on the American television series St. Elsewhere and as Audrey Horne on the Canadian television series The X-Files.  Watkins was born in Calgary, Alberta, to parents Hilda May (née Gowen), a homemaker, and John Watkis, a garage mechanic. She has two sisters, Lorna and Lorraine. Watkins was raised in the suburb of Forest Lawn in Calgary. She began her career in the early 1970s as a singer with the progressive rock band Spirit of the Air.  In 1976, Watkis landed her first major role as Dr. Valerie Brown on the American television series St. Elsewhere. The show ran for five seasons from 1982 to 1988 and became one of the most popular shows on American television during that time period. Watkins was nominated for four Emmy Awards during her time on the show, including twice for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Drama Series (1986 and 1988).  After St. Elsewhere ended its run, Watkins took a break from acting to have children. In 1995, she made her return to acting with a role as Audrey Horne on the Canadian television series The X-Files.
