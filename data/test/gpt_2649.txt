He studied architecture under Nicholas Hawksmoor and John Vanbrugh and became an architect in his own right, working mainly in London. He is most famous for his designs for country houses in the Palladian or Neoclassical style, particularly those in the vicinity of London, such as Blenheim Palace, Chatsworth House and Wardour Castle. He also designed churches, including St John's Wood Baptist Church and All Saints' Church, Wapping. Wardour died in London on 1746.
