Benjamin Russell Driebergen (born January 1, 1983) is a Canadian professional ice hockey centre who is currently playing for HC Sochi of the Kontinental Hockey League (KHL). Driebergen was drafted by the Winnipeg Jets in the second round, 49th overall, of the 2002 NHL Entry Draft.  Driebergen has played in the National Hockey League (NHL) with the Jets, Nashville Predators, and Toronto Maple Leafs. He has also played in the American Hockey League (AHL) with the Worcester Sharks, San Antonio Rampage and Manitoba Moose.  Driebergen was born and raised in Toronto, Ontario. He played junior hockey in his home province of Ontario with the Toronto Marlboros. He was drafted by the Winnipeg Jets in the second round, 49th overall, of the 2002 NHL Entry Draft. After two seasons with the Jr. Jets, Driebergen made his NHL debut in 2005–06 with the Jets. In 2008–09 he signed a two-year contract with Nashville. In 2010–11 he was traded to Toronto along with goaltender Johan Hedberg and defenceman Matt Lashoff for forward Alexei Kovalev and defenceman Jay Harrison. Injuries limited him to just 36 games over three seasons with Toronto before being traded to Nashville on July 1, 2013 in exchange for forward David Legwand and defenceman Zach Bogosian.
