Claude Heater (October 25, 1927 â€“ May 12, 2006) was an American jazz trumpeter.  Heater was born in Memphis, Tennessee. He studied trumpet with Wendell Marshall and Roy Eldridge. He served in the United States Army during the Korean War, and played with the army band. He returned to Memphis and played with Jackie McLean and Cherry Poppin' Daddies. He moved to New York City in 1957 and played with Benny Goodman, Art Blakey, Thelonious Monk, and Coleman Hawkins. He recorded with Miles Davis on "Birth of the Cool" (1959), "Kind of Blue" (1959), and "Porgy and Bess" (1965). He later recorded albums as a leader for Blue Note Records.
