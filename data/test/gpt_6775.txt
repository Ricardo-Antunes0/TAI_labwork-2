Operation Pocket Money was the title of a British propaganda film made in 1943 to encourage people to save during the Second World War.  The film was directed by Michael Powell and Emeric Pressburger and starred Alec Guinness, David Niven, and Isobel Elsom. It was shot in black and white and featured a music score by Maurice Jarre.  The film is about two British brothers who are forced to go into hiding after their father is arrested for being a German spy. They live off the money they saved during the war and use it to buy supplies they need to survive. The brothers eventually escape from prison and return home to their families.
