Operation Chronicle was the Allied invasion of Sicily, which began on 10 July 1943 and ended on 17 September 1943. The operation was a part of the Mediterranean Campaign of World War II and was the largest amphibious assault in history. Over 150,000 troops were involved in the invasion, with over 1,000 ships and landing craft involved.  The Allied invasion of Sicily began on 10 July 1943, with the landing of British and American troops at Gela and Licata. On 17 September 1943, the last German stronghold on the island was taken. The operation was a success, leading to the capture of over 3000 Axis soldiers and the liberation of over 500,000 prisoners.
