Oleg Vladimirovich Deripaska (; born 2 January 1963) is a Russian businessman and politician who has been Chairman of the Board and Chief Executive Officer (CEO) of aluminum producer RUSAL since 2006.  Deripaska is ranked at number 10 on the Forbes World's Billionaires list with a net worth of $13.9 billion as of March 2016. He has also been ranked number 2 in Russia on the Forbes list of billionaires, and number 9 in Europe on that same list.  Deripaska was born in 1963, in the city of Nizhny Novgorod, in Volga Region, Soviet Union. After graduating from Nizhny Novgorod State University with a degree in engineering, Deripaska worked at various state-owned companies before founding RUSAL in 1993. The company went public in 1999. In 2006, Deripaska became Chairman of the Board and CEO of RUSAL.  Deripaska has been involved in a number of controversies during his career. In 2007, he was accused of bribing Russian officials to win a contract to supply weapons to the Republic of Congo; he was later cleared of all charges. In 2009, Deripaska was arrested on charges of bribery and extortion after allegedly threatening to harm one of his business rivals; he was later released on bail. In 2013, Deripaska was again accused of bribery after it was alleged that he had paid millions of dollars to Kremlin officials in order to win contracts from Russian state oil company Rosneft
