The Public Menace is a 1935 American drama film directed by Henry King and starring John Barrymore, Myrna Loy, and Douglas Fairbanks Jr. The screenplay was written by James M. Cain, based on his short story "The Public Enemy".  When newspaper reporter Paul Sheldon is falsely accused of rape, he flees the city to seek refuge with his sister in the country. There he becomes involved with a group of radicals who are planning to overthrow the government. However, when Sheldon is finally exonerated, he decides to return to the city and expose the radicals.
