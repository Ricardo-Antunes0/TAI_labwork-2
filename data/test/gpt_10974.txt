Where's Your Cup? is an album by the American rock band the Grateful Dead. It was released on May 17, 1977, through Warner Bros. Records.  The album was recorded at the Fillmore Auditorium in San Francisco and produced by the band's manager Bill Graham. It is considered the Dead's heaviest and most extreme album, featuring extended improvisations and experimental arrangements.  The album reached number 12 on the "Billboard" 200 chart, and has been certified platinum by the RIAA. It has been cited as an influence by bands such as Metallica and White Zombie.
