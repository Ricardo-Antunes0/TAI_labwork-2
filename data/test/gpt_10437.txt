Brian Morris (born 18 October 1936) is an English anthropologist and archaeologist.  Morris was born in London, England, on 18 October 1936. He studied at the University of Cambridge, where he obtained his Bachelor of Arts degree in 1958 and his Master of Arts degree in 1961. He then undertook postgraduate studies at the University of California, Berkeley, where he obtained his Doctor of Philosophy degree in 1965.  Morris has been a professor of anthropology at the University of York since 1969, where he also serves as director of the York Archaeological Trust. He has also been a visiting professor at the universities of Cambridge, Edinburgh, and Berlin.  Morris has conducted fieldwork in England, Scotland, Italy, Greece, Cyprus, and Portugal. His research has focused on the archaeology of Mesolithic and Neolithic hunter-gatherer societies, the development of Neolithic agriculture, and the archaeology of Mediterranean trade networks. He has also published studies on Bronze Age cultures in Britain and Ireland, Roman Britain and Ireland, and the archaeology of Muslim Spain.  Morris is a fellow of the British Academy and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences. He was awarded a knighthood in the Queen's Birthday Honours 2001 for services to archaeology.
