Ruthie Ann Miles (born April 21, 1983) is an American actress and singer. She is known for her roles as Jodie Farrow in the ABC drama series Lost (2004–2010), for which she was nominated for a Golden Globe Award, and as Beth Greene on the Showtime series Dexter (2013–2015).  Miles was born in Dallas, Texas, the daughter of Sonya (née Pemberton), a beautician, and Larry Miles, a truck driver. She has two younger sisters and one younger brother. Miles attended Chaparral High School in Rancho Santa Margarita, California. After graduating from high school, Miles attended the University of Southern California where she majored in theatre and performed in numerous stage productions. Miles made her feature film debut in 2002 in The Core.  Miles played Jodie Farrow on the ABC drama series Lost from 2004 to 2010. Her performance garnered her nominations for a Golden Globe Award and an Emmy Award. She starred as Beth Greene on the Showtime series Dexter from 2013 to 2015. Her performance as Beth earned her nominations for a Primetime Emmy Award and a Saturn Award. Miles stars in the CBS television pilot The Code, which began airing on September 24, 2018.
