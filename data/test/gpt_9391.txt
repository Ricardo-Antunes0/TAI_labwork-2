Asbjørn Nesheim (14 December 1906 in Trondheim – 3 December 1988 in Oslo) was a Norwegian author, playwright and theatre director.   Nesheim studied at the University of Oslo, and worked as a teacher, journalist and theatre critic before becoming a full-time author in the 1930s. He is best known for his novel trilogy "The Saga of Gunnlaugur" (1941), which tells the story of a Viking warrior's quest for revenge. Nesheim also wrote children's books, including the popular series "Rolf and the Viking" (1949).   He was a co-founder of the National Theatre in Oslo in 1945, serving as its first director. He also served as the head of the Norwegian Academy of Literature from 1957 to 1973.
