Mario Fargetta (born 19 July 1962, Lissone, Italy) is an Italian composer and pianist.  Fargetta was born in Lissone, a small town near Milan, Italy. He showed early musical talent and at the age of nine he won first prize in a local piano competition. He studied at the Milan Conservatory with Franco Donatoni and graduated in 1984.  In 1986 he won the Ferruccio Busoni International Piano Competition in Bolzano, Italy. This was followed by first prize in the Tchaikovsky Competition in Moscow in 1988 and fourth prize in the Leeds International Piano Competition in 1989.  Since then Fargetta has been widely acclaimed as one of the leading contemporary composers. His works have been performed throughout Europe, North America and Japan, and have been featured on television programmes such as "Late Night with Conan O'Brien" and "The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson". His compositions have also been featured in a number of films, including "The Talented Mr Ripley" (1999), "The Italian Job" (2003) and "Holidays", which was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay.  Fargetta currently resides in Rome, Italy.
