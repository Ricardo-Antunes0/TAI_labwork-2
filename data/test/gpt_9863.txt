Lower Kinnerton Hall, also known as Bridge House, is a Grade I listed building in the civil parish of Lower Kinnerton and the borough of Harrogate, North Yorkshire, England.  The hall was built in 1737-40 for the Kinnerton family by the architect John Carr of York. It is a two-storey, five-bay brick country house with a slate roof. The interior features a grand staircase and elaborate plasterwork. It is now used as a conference centre.  The hall was designated a Grade I listed building on 2 May 1952.
