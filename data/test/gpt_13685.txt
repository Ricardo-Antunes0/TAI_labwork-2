Edward D. Hess (born October 28, 1947) is an American historian, author, and professor emeritus at Princeton University. He is the author of several books on Tudor England, including The Tudors: The History of a Dynasty (1989), Henry VIII (1996), and Elizabeth I (2007).  Hess was born in Boston, Massachusetts, on October 28, 1947. He received his B.A. from Yale University in 1969 and his M.A. from Harvard University in 1971. He completed his Ph.D. at Yale in 1974. From 1974 to 1977 he was a research associate at the Huntington Library and Museum in San Marino, California.  From 1977 to 1978 Hess was an assistant professor at Harvard University. From 1978 to 1984 he was an associate professor at Princeton University. He became a professor emeritus at Princeton in 2002.  Hess has been a fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences since 1986, and he has been a member of the British Academy since 1997.
