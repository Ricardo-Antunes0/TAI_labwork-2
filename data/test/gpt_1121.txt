Operation Sayeed (Arabic: عملية الصياد) also known as Operation Golden Arrow, was a military operation conducted by the Egyptian military against Islamist militants in the Sinai Peninsula, in late 2013.  The operation was launched on 19 December 2013, with the stated objective of restoring security and stability to the Sinai Peninsula. It involved the deployment of Egyptian military forces into the region, as well as the participation of a number of foreign nations. The operation concluded on 7 January 2014.  The operation is notable for its high casualty rate; according to Egyptian media, 97% of the casualties were militants, while 3% were Egyptian security forces. The Egyptian government has declared it a success, citing the destruction of terrorist camps and weapons caches as well as the capture or killing of a large number of militants.
