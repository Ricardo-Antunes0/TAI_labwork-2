He was a noted expert on the history of French philosophy, and wrote extensively on the history and philosophy of Nazi Germany.  Frykman was born in Toronto, Ontario, Canada, on 19 April 1922. He received his Ph.D. from the University of Chicago in 1954. Frykman taught at UCLA for nearly fifty years, retiring in 1997. He was a distinguished professor at UCLA and held the Regents Chair in History and Philosophy. Frykman also served as President of the American Philosophical Association (APA) in 1976-77. He died on 7 February 2006 in Los Angeles, California, at the age of 85.
