The Türkentor (Turks' Gate) is a triumphal arch in the city of Helmstedt, in Lower Saxony, Germany.  Construction of the Türkentor began in 1827 and was completed in 1830. It is a popular tourist destination, and has been labelled a cultural heritage site. The arch is made of sandstone and is 22 metres high and 11 metres wide. It is decorated with a series of reliefs depicting Turkish life and culture.
