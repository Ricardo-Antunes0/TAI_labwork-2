Tigran Balayan (Armenian: Տիգրան Բալայան; born October 30, 1961) is an Armenian-American journalist and author who has been the host of the nightly news program "A.M. Report" on PBS since 1995.  Born in Yerevan, Armenia, Balayan immigrated with his family to the United States in 1978 when he was six years old. He graduated from the University of Maryland with a degree in journalism and moved to New York City, where he began his career as a reporter for WNBC-TV. In 1994, he became the host of "A.M. Report" on PBS. The program is internationally acclaimed for its in-depth reporting and Balayan has won numerous awards for his journalism, including a Peabody Award and two Edward R. Murrow Awards.  Balayan is also a columnist for The Huffington Post and a contributing editor to The Daily Beast magazine. In addition to his work as a journalist, Balayan is the author of two books, "Burning Time: A Family Memoir" and "The Last Great War: Armenia and Azerbaijan in the Shadow of World War I". He lives in New York City with his wife and two children.
