Mawlawi Hamdullah Mukhlis (مولوی حمد اللہ مخلص) (born 1939) is a distinguished Afghan jurist, who served as the 11th President of Afghanistan from 2002 to 2014.    Mukhlis was born in 1939 in the Panjshir Province of present-day Afghanistan. He earned a BA degree from Kabul University in 1963 and a LLB degree from the same institution in 1967. He was then appointed as an assistant professor at the university, where he served until 1981.  In 1981, Mukhlis was appointed a judge at the Supreme Court of Afghanistan. He served in this position until 1994, when he was elevated to the rank of Mawlawi (a rank equivalent to a judge). In 2002, Mukhlis was elected as the 11th President of Afghanistan, serving until 2014. During his presidency, Mukhlis faced several challenges, including a protracted war with the Taliban and allegations of corruption. He retired from his presidency in 2014 and currently serves as the chairman of Afghanistan's High Peace Council.
