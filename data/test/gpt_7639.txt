Borhat (in Assamese: বৰহাট) is a partially submerged village in the Karbi Anglong district of Assam, India. It is located on the left bank of the Brahmaputra River and is about 9 km from the district headquarters at Diphu.  The village has a population of around 1,500 people. The main occupation of the villagers is agriculture, with some hunting and fishing also carried out. The village has a primary school, a health centre and a few shops. The nearest railway station is at Diphu.
