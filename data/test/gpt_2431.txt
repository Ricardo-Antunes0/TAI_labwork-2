William Henry Squire, ARCM (8 August 1871 – 12 October 1954) was an Australian artist and art teacher. He was also a founding member of the Australian Society of Painters in Watercolour.  William Henry Squire was born on 8 August 1871 in Melbourne, Victoria, Australia to parents Edward Squire and Sarah Ann (née Symonds). He was educated at Melbourne Grammar School and the University of Melbourne, where he studied Arts.  Squire began his artistic career as a painter, exhibiting his work at the Victorian Artists' Society from 1895 to 1938. In 1919, he co-founded the Australian Society of Painters in Watercolour with fellow artists Frederick McCubbin and John Peter Russell. Squire served as its president from its foundation until his death in 1954.  Squire's paintings depict Australian landscapes and coastal scenes, as well as urban scenes and religious subjects. He also produced etchings, lithographs, and prints. His work has been exhibited in Australia, England, France, Italy, Japan, New Zealand, and the United States of America. Squire was awarded the Order of Australia (OAM) in 1957 for his services to art. He died on 12 October 1954 in Melbourne.
