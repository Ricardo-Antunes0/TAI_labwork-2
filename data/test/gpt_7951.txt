Kenneth D. Craig is a Canadian psychologist, specializing in the areas of cognitive neuroscience and social cognition. He is currently the Gordon McKay Professor of Psychology at the University of British Columbia.  Craig completed his undergraduate studies at the University of Toronto, and then received his PhD from McGill University. He has since held positions at the University of Montreal, McGill University, and the University of British Columbia. Craig's research has focused on the cognitive neuroscience of social cognition, including studies of memory, language, and categorization. He is also known for his work on Moral Foundations Theory, which proposes that different aspects of morality are based on different principles of reasoning.
