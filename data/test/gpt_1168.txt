Sir William Peryam (15349 October 1604) of Shalford, Surrey, was an English lawyer and politician who sat in the House of Commons from 1604 to 1614.  Peryam was the second son of Sir John Peryam and his wife Elizabeth Arundell. He was educated at Jesus College, Cambridge, where he became a fellow in 1590. He was called to the bar at Gray's Inn in 1597 and became a bencher in 1607.  Peryam entered Parliament in 1604 as Member of Parliament for Surrey. He served until 1614 when he was defeated at the election of 1614. He later served as Serjeant-at-law from 1615 to 1622 and as Recorder of Guildford from 1623 to 1629.
