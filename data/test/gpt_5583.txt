Patty Chang (born February 3, 1972 in Los Angeles, California) is an American actress and singer. Chang began her acting career in the late 1990s, appearing in minor roles on television series and in theatrical productions. She achieved wider recognition for her performance as Dr. Keiko Agena on the NBC science fiction drama series ER from 2003 to 2005. Chang has since appeared in a number of television series, including Sons of Anarchy and Hawaii Five-0, and in feature films, including the box-office successes The Hangover and The Help.  Chang was born in Los Angeles, California, to Taiwanese American parents. She was raised in Westlake Village, California, and attended Thousand Oaks High School. Chang studied at the American Conservatory Theater in San Francisco, where she earned a degree in acting. She made her theatrical debut in the ensemble cast of the off-Broadway production of The Glass Menagerie in 1998.  Chang's television debut came in a minor role on the NBC science fiction drama series ER as Dr. Keiko Agena from 2003 to 2005.
