Michele di Rocco (born 4 May 1982) is an Italian professional road bicycle racer who last rode for UCI WorldTeam Sky. A versatile climber and time trialist, he has also won stages in the Vuelta a España, Tour de France, Giro d'Italia, and the Vuelta a Andalucía.  Born in Rocca di Papa, near Rome, di Rocco started his career as a junior with the amateur team Colle delle Finestre. In 2001 he turned professional with the Italian team Jolly-Jolly. He became a regular member of the team during 2002, when he finished eleventh in the U23 time trial at the world championships. In 2003 he signed with the Italian team Saeco and won two stages in that year's Giro d'Italia. In 2004 he signed with the French team AG2R Prévoyance and finished second in the prologue of that year's Tour de France.  In 2005 di Rocco joined Team Sky. He finished second overall in the 2006 Vuelta a España and then won stage 2 of that year's Tour de France. He also won a stage of that year's Giro d'Italia. In 2007 he finished fourth overall in the Vuelta a España and then won two stages of that year's Tour de France. He also finished second overall in the 2007 Giro d'Italia.
