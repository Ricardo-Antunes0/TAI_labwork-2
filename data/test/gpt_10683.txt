Jonathan Castroviejo Nicolás (born 27 April 1987) is a Spanish professional footballer who plays as a left back for Spanish club Valencia CF and the Spain national team.  Castroviejo started his career in Atlético Madrid's youth system, and made his senior debuts with the reserves in 2006. He made his first-team debut on 23 November 2007, in a 3–1 home victory against Racing Santander. On 14 July 2009, Castroviejo signed a four-year contract with Valencia CF, for a €4 million transfer fee.  Castroviejo was a regular starter for Valencia during the 2010–11 season, as they reached the Copa del Rey final. He scored his first goal for the club on 26 October 2010, in a 2–1 home victory against Racing Santander. Castroviejo also contributed with four assists in La Liga that season.  On 1 July 2013, Castroviejo signed a five-year contract with English club Liverpool for an undisclosed fee, reported to be £20 million. He made his debut for the club on 18 August 2013, coming on as a substitute for Joe Allen in the 75th minute of Liverpool's 3–2 League Cup victory over Swansea City. He made his Premier League debut on 17 September 2013, starting in a 2–1 away win against Stoke City.
