Chingiz Azamatovich Aidarbekov  is a Kyrgyz politician who has been the Prime Minister of Kyrgyzstan since 11 March 2010. Previously, he served as Deputy Prime Minister and Minister for Regional Development from 2005 to 2010.  Chingiz Aidarbekov was born in Bishkek, Kyrgyzstan on 10 February 1967. After completing secondary school in Kyrgyzstan, he studied law at the Moscow State University from 1985 to 1988. He then worked as a lawyer in Bishkek before being elected to the National Assembly of Kyrgyzstan in 2000. Aidarbekov served as Deputy Prime Minister and Minister for Regional Development from 2005 to 2010, during which time he was responsible for development projects in the south of the country. He was re-elected to the National Assembly in 2009 and became Prime Minister on 11 March 2010.
