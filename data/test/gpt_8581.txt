Torreón  is a city and seat of the Municipality of Torreón in the Coahuila state of Mexico. The city has a population of 213,723 according to the 2010 census.  Torreón is located in the central part of the state, at an elevation of 1,989 meters above sea level. It is about 260 kilometers south-southwest of Saltillo and about 190 kilometers north-northwest of Monterrey. The city is located on the banks of the Río Grande de Coahuila.  The city was founded by José María Morelos y Pavón on November 10, 1824. It was named after King Charles IV of Spain.   The city's economy is based on agriculture, livestock, commerce and services. The main exports are grapes, maize and sorghum. There are also some industries producing electrical equipment, textiles and automobiles.   The main tourist attractions are the Cathedral, which was built between 1787 and 1812; the Palace of Lecumberri; and the archaeological site of Teopanzolco. There are also many museums in Torreón including the Museum of Fine Arts and a children's museum. The city also has a soccer stadium and a golf course.
