Nawaf Salam (; born 15 December 1953) is an Egyptian economist and academic who has been the Minister of Finance of Egypt since 14 March 2014.  Nawaf Salam was born on 15 December 1953 in Cairo, Egypt. He obtained his undergraduate degree in mathematics from Ain Shams University in 1975, and his doctorate in economics from the University of Paris-Dauphine in 1981. He served as a professor at Cairo University from 1981 to 1987, and as dean of its Faculty of Economics from 1987 to 1990.  From 1990 to 1995, Salam served as the economic advisor to then-President Hosni Mubarak. From 1995 to 1999, he served as the head of the Egyptian Central Bank. From 1999 to 2003, he served as the Minister of Planning and Economic Development in Mubarak's cabinet.  In 2003, Mubarak was overthrown in a popular uprising and Salam left government service. He became a visiting scholar at the University of California, Berkeley, and a professor at the American University in Cairo (AUC) in 2004.  In 2006, he was appointed head of the World Bank's Middle East and North Africa (MENA) region. In this role, he helped pioneer the bank's "reform agenda", which aimed to promote economic growth and stability through structural reforms. In 2013, Salam was promoted to be the bank's vice president for MENA regionwide operations.
