Jabalpur Airport  (colloquially referred to as Jabalpur Airport-I) is an airport serving the city of Jabalpur in the Indian state of Madhya Pradesh. The airport is also known as Jabalpur Airport-II or Jabalpur Air Force Station Airport. The airport has a single runway designated 09/27 with a length of 2,500 metres (8,202 ft).  The airport was commissioned in 1975 and is operated by the Airports Authority of India. It is one of the oldest airports in central India. The original terminal building still stands but it has been extended several times. A new terminal building was inaugurated on 24 November 2010.  The air traffic at Jabalpur Airport increased from 1,143,583 passenger movements in fiscal year 2013-14 to 1,527,048 passenger movements in fiscal year 2014-15. The airport handled a total of 1,711,847 passengers during the fiscal year ended 31 March 2016.   Jabalpur Airport has been selected as one of the 100 Smart Cities in India by the Ministry of Urban Development.
