Eric Clauesson (or Erik Klasson) (died 1492) was a priest, scholar and historian of the Protestant Reformation. He was born in Västerås, Sweden.   Clauesson was the son of a bailiff. He studied at the universities of Paris and Cologne, where he obtained his doctorate in 1484. He then moved to the University of Wittenberg, where he became a teacher in 1491. In 1493 he became rector at the University of Rostock.  Clauesson was one of the most important Protestant historians of his time. He wrote extensively on Martin Luther, Calvin, John Calvin and Ulrich Zwingli. His best-known work is the Historia Scholastica (History of Scholasticism), which covers the period from Augustine to the Reformation.
