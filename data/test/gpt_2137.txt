Official relations between Poland and the United States were established on November 1, 1918, when the United States recognized the Polish government in exile. The two countries have enjoyed strong bilateral relations since then, with cooperation in defense, trade, culture, and education being key pillars of their relationship. Poland is the 11th-largest economy in the world and is a major European Union member, while the United States is a leading economic and military power. The United States is also Poland's second-largest donor of development assistance.  The United States has been a strong supporter of democracy in Poland and has assisted in the country's transition to a market economy. The United States also supports Poland's membership in NATO and its efforts to join the European Union. The two countries have cooperated extensively in counterterrorism efforts, with Poland playing a leading role in the fight against terrorism in Europe.
