Centered today at the crossroads of Georgia and Alabama, Crabapple was originally settled by the Creek tribe in the early 18th century. After the American Revolution, the community was platted in 1787 and named for Major General James Wilson Crabapple, an American Revolutionary War hero. The town served as a regional trading center for agricultural products and became a crossroads for wagon roads and railroads. In the early 20th century, Crabapple experienced a growth spurt as a result of its coal and timber resources. Today, the town is home to a small but thriving population of 1,000 residents.
