Artur Arturovich Eizen (; 8 June 1927, Kiev, Russian SFSR â€“ 9 July 2001, Kiev) was a Ukrainian historian and professor at the National University of Kyiv-Mohyla Academy.  Eizen was one of the leading Soviet historians of the 20th century. He specialized in the history of Ukraine and Russia, as well as in modern European history. He also wrote extensively on the history of the Jews in Europe. Eizen was a member of the Ukrainian Academy of Sciences and Arts.  Eizen was born in Kiev on 8 June 1927 to a Jewish family. In 1944 he graduated from the secondary school in Kiev. He then studied at Moscow State University (MSU), where he obtained his degree in 1950. From 1951 to 1955 he worked as an assistant professor at MSU, and from 1955 to 1957 he was a research fellow at the Institute of History of the Academy of Sciences of the U.S.S.R. He then became an associate professor at MSU, and from 1967 to 1971 he served as head of the department of Russian and East European history. From 1971 to 1978 he served as a professor at MSU, and from 1978 until his death he held the post of chairman of the department of Russian and East European history at Kyiv-Mohyla Academy.  Eizen was a member of the Ukrainian Academy of Sciences and Arts (1991).
