Lauren Anne English (born April 22, 1989) is an American singer and songwriter. After signing with Capitol Records as a teenager, she released her debut album, I Believe in a Thing Called Love (2009). English's second album, Born to Die (2012), received widespread critical acclaim and spawned five top-ten singles. Her third album, Hateful Eight (2015), was also well-received by critics and earned her three Grammy Awards. English has sold over 30 million records worldwide.  English began her career as a child actress appearing in television shows including CBS's The Young and the Restless (1996–1999) and the WB's One Tree Hill (2003–2007). She began writing songs at age 17 and released her debut studio album, I Believe in a Thing Called Love, in 2009 under Capitol Records. The album sold over 1 million copies in the United States and received widespread critical acclaim, including being named one of the best albums of 2009 by Rolling Stone. Her second album, Born to Die, was released in 2012 and spawned five top-ten singles including the Billboard Hot 100 chart-topper "Born to Die". English's third album, Hateful Eight, was released in 2015 and was well-received by critics. It earned her three Grammy Awards including Record of the Year for the single "Get Outta My Way". English has sold over 30 million records worldwide.
