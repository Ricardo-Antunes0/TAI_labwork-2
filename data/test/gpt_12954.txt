Thomas Welles (14 January 1660) is the most famous English poet of the Restoration period. He was born in London, the son of an upholsterer. Welles began writing poetry when he was a teenager, and his first publication was in 1683. He served as a soldier in the English army during the Dutch Wars and the English Civil War, and in 1685 he published The Siege of Rhodes. Welles's best-known work is Othello, which was first performed in 1604.
