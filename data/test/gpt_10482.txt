"Canada" (also known as "Ca-na-da" or "The Land of the Maple Leaf") is a song by the Canadian rock band Rush, released as the second single from their album Moving Pictures. The song was written by Neil Peart and Geddy Lee, and produced by Rush.  "Canada" is a patriotic song which celebrates Canada's history, people, and landscape. The song is written in the key of A minor and moves at a tempo of 100 beats per minute. The lyrics deal with the theme of patriotism, with Lee saying that "Canada is where I want to be / I love this country, oh yeah".  The song first appeared on Rush's 1976 album Moving Pictures and was re-recorded for their 1982 album Signals. It was also re-recorded for the 2009 tribute album A Tribute to Rush: 40 Years of Music.
