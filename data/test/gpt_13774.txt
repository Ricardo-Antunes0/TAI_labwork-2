The Ralph M. Brown Act, located at title 18, United States Code, is a federal law that provides for the punishment of those who injure or threaten to injure any officer or employee of the United States while in the performance of his official duties. The act also creates a civil cause of action for such injuries.  The Brown Act was first enacted in 1871 and has been amended several times since its inception. It originally provided for a maximum sentence of one year in prison and a $2,000 fine for anyone who injured or threatened to injure any officer or employee of the United States while in the performance of his official duties. In 1986, the maximum sentence was increased to five years imprisonment and a $250,000 fine. The act also created a civil cause of action for such injuries.
