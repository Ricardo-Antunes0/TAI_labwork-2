Martha Alma Wall (March 22, 1910 – December 12, 1992) was an American writer and critic. She was a professor at the University of Iowa from 1951 to 1988.  Wall was born in Bridgeport, Connecticut, to Alma (née Greenleaf) and Lewis Wall. After graduating from high school in 1928, Wall studied English at Bryn Mawr College and then worked as a journalist. In 1941 she received her PhD from the University of Iowa, where she taught creative writing and criticism until 1988.   Wall is best known for her novels "The Glass Menagerie" (1944), "A Streetcar Named Desire" (1947), and "The Catcher in the Rye" (1951), all of which were Pulitzer Prize winners. Her other works include the short story collections "Three Women" (1953) and "East of Eden" (1955), the novel "The Great Gatsby" (1925), and the plays "Angels in America" (1979) and "The Glass Menagerie" (1987).   Martha Wall died in Iowa City, Iowa, aged 84.
