Jon Pernell Roberts (June 21, 1948 – March 12, 2019) was an American attorney and politician who served as the 25th Governor of Arkansas from 1997 to 2007. A Democrat, Roberts served two terms as the U.S. Attorney for the Eastern District of Arkansas before being elected governor. As governor, Roberts oversaw the largest expansion of healthcare in Arkansas history and was a vocal advocate for environmental conservation. He also presided over an economic boom in his state during his terms. In 2007, Roberts retired from politics after losing the Democratic Party's nomination for U.S. Senator to Blanche Lincoln.   Roberts was born in Little Rock, Arkansas, to Laura Elizabeth (née Pernell) and John Pernell Roberts; his father was a prominent pediatrician and surgeon who served as the first president of the Arkansas Medical Association. Roberts attended Hendrix College in Conway, Arkansas, where he received his undergraduate degree in 1969 and then attended the University of Arkansas School of Law, graduating with his juris doctor in 1973.  Roberts began his legal career as an assistant prosecutor in Pulaski County, Arkansas, before moving to the Eastern District of Arkansas in 1977 to become a federal prosecutor. In this role, Roberts became known for his work on major criminal cases, such as those involving members of the Sunbeam Motor Car Company bribery scandal and the Edwin Edwards corruption case. After serving as U.S. Attorney for six years, Roberts was elected governor of Arkansas in 1996; he won re-election in 2002 and 2006
