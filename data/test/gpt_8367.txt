He was born at Ipswich, Suffolk.  Wilson was educated at Charterhouse School and Trinity College, Cambridge, where he achieved a Blue in First Class Cricket in 1918. He played for Cambridge University from 1916 to 1919 and for Middlesex from 1920 to 1926. In 1922, he made his first-class debut for Middlesex against Somerset at Lord's. He played regularly for the RAF from 1923 to 1935, making 56 first-class appearances and scoring 1,290 runs at an average of 28.92. He also played seven county games for Middlesex between 1924 and 1927. After leaving the RAF he became a teacher at a school in Suffolk. He died at Eye in Suffolk in 1967 aged 73.
