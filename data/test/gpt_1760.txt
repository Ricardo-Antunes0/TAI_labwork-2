David Lawrence Geffen (born February 21, 1943) is an American musician, singer-songwriter, record producer, and actor. He is the founder of Geffen Records, a prolific record label which has released music by such artists as Stevie Nicks, U2, Madonna, and Michael Jackson.  Geffen was born in Los Angeles to a Jewish family. He attended Beverly Hills High School and then California Institute of Technology, where he was a member of the Phi Delta Theta fraternity. He began his music career in the early 1960s as a session musician, playing with such musicians as John Lennon and Phil Spector. In 1967, Geffen produced the first album by the rock band the Monkees.  Geffen's first major success came with the 1971 release of Led Zeppelin's fourth album, Led Zeppelin IV. He then signed Stevie Nicks to Geffen Records and produced her first two albums. Geffen also worked with Michael Jackson on five albums: Thriller (1982), Off the Wall (1979), Bad (1987), Dangerous (1991), and HIStory: Past, Present and Future Volume 1 (1995).  Geffen has been nominated for 18 Grammy Awards, winning five: Best Rock Album for Van Halen II in 1978; Best Pop Collaboration with Vocals for "I Will Always Love You" with Whitney Houston in 1992; Producer of the Year for Nirvana's Nevermind in 1991; and Producer of the Year for U2's Achtung Baby in 1997.
