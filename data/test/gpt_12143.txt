Steven Michael Kazee (born October 30, 1975) is an American actor. He is best known for his roles as Tony Soprano's bodyguard Johnny Sack on the HBO television series The Sopranos and as Det. Daniel "Danny" Walsh on the TNT crime drama series Major Crimes.  Kazee was born in Brooklyn, New York City, the son of Carol (n√©e Pickens) and Steven Kazee, a furniture mover. He has two sisters, Lisa and Dawn. Kazee grew up in the South Bronx and attended DeWitt Clinton High School. He began his acting career in 1997 at the age of 20, when he appeared in an episode of The Sopranos as a character named "John".  Kazee played Johnny Sack on The Sopranos from 1999 to 2007. He also appeared in numerous other television shows and films during that time, including ER, CSI: Crime Scene Investigation, Life as We Know It, and I Now Pronounce You Chuck and Larry. In 2008 he guest-starred on an episode of Law & Order: Criminal Intent as a psychopathic killer.  Since The Sopranos ended in 2007, Kazee has appeared mainly in films, including Role Models (2008), Burn After Reading (2008), No Country For Old Men (2007), Public Enemies (2009), Cowboys & Aliens (2011), and Lone Survivor (2013).
