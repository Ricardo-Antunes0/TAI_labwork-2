He earned his A.B. from Dartmouth College in 1948 and his Ph.D. from Harvard University in 1952.  He served in the United States Air Force during the Korean War and then became a professor at RAND Corporation, where he worked from 1957 to 1979. He then served as a senior economist for the United States Department of Labor from 1979 to 1988, and as a public policy advisor to the Office of Management and Budget from 1988 to 1992.  Mann wrote several books on economics and public policy, including "The Policy Paradox" (1981), "The Sources of Power" (1987), "Inflation and Unemployment: Causes and Consequences" (1989), "The Economic Structure of Society" (1996), and "A Plan for America" (2005). He also wrote articles for magazines such as "The New York Times", "The Wall Street Journal", and "The Washington Post".  Mann died on 2 August 2009, aged 83, in Chevy Chase, Maryland.
