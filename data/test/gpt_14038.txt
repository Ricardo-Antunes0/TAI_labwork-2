Kevin D. Prufer (born 1969 in Cleveland, Ohio) is an American biologist, currently the James V. Foley Distinguished Professor of Zoology at Oregon State University.  Prufer completed undergraduate studies at Brown University in 1991, where he was a member of the Phi Kappa Psi fraternity. He then attended graduate school at the University of California, Berkeley, where he earned his Ph.D. in 1998 under the guidance of Daniel Lieberman.  After completing his graduate studies, Prufer served as a postdoctoral fellow at the University of Cambridge before joining the faculty at Oregon State University in 1999. He has since been recognized as one of the leading scientists in his field, and has been awarded numerous honors including a Guggenheim Fellowship (2007-2009), a MacArthur Fellowship (2009), and a National Medal of Science (2011). Prufer's research focuses on evolutionary biology, and he is particularly known for his work on animal behavior and social organization.
