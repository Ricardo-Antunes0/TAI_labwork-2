Theodore Rodger Kubiak (born May 12, 1942) is a retired American football player and coach. He played college football at the University of Notre Dame, and was drafted by the Cleveland Browns in the first round of the 1966 NFL Draft. He also played for the Baltimore Colts, Minnesota Vikings, and Los Angeles Rams. Kubiak was the head coach of the Houston Texans from 1995 to 2001, and again from 2006 to 2008. He was inducted into the College Football Hall of Fame as a coach in 2009.  Kubiak was born in Chicago, Illinois. He attended St. Ignatius High School in Chicago Heights. Kubiak played college football at Notre Dame under head coach Ara Parseghian, graduating with a degree in business administration in 1965.  Kubiak was drafted by the Cleveland Browns with the ninth overall pick in the 1966 NFL Draft. He played for Cleveland from 1966 to 1968, Baltimore Colts from 1969 to 1971, Minnesota Vikings from 1972 to 1973, and Los Angeles Rams from 1974 to 1976. Kubiak was a two-time Pro Bowler (1968, 1969), and was named All-Pro in 1969.  Kubiak served as head coach of the Houston Texans from 1995 to 2001. After being fired by Houston, he became head coach of the Indianapolis Colts from 2002 to 2003, where he led them to their first playoff appearance since 1998.
