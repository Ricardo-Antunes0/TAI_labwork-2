Sir Wesley Winfield Hall (born 12 September 1942) is an English composer and conductor.  Hall was born in 1942 in London, England. He studied music at the Royal Academy of Music, where he was awarded a first prize in composition in 1966. He worked as a composer and conductor for many years before becoming Professor of Composition at the Royal College of Music in 1992. He has also been appointed Director of the International String Quartet Academy in Graz, Austria.  Hall's compositions have been performed throughout the world and have been awarded several prizes. His works include chamber music, orchestral works, choral works, and music for film and television. He has been nominated for three Grammy Awards, and has been awarded an Honorary Doctorate by the Royal Academy of Music. In December 2009, he was appointed Officer of the Order of the British Empire (OBE) for services to music.
