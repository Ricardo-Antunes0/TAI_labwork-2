Emmanuel Wamala (born 15 December 1926) is a Kenyan former Vice-President of Kenya, who served in that office from 2003 to 2007. Wamala was also the Governor of the Central Bank of Kenya from 1978 to 1983.  Wamala was born in 1926 in Kiambu, Kenya. He attended the University of Nairobi, where he received a Bachelor of Arts degree in 1950. He also attended the London School of Economics and Political Science, where he received a Master of Arts degree in 1952.  Wamala began his career in the civil service in 1954. He served as Deputy Secretary for Economic Affairs from 1962 to 1965, Deputy Secretary for Finance from 1965 to 1968, and Permanent Secretary for Economic Affairs from 1968 to 1978. He also served as Governor of the Central Bank of Kenya from 1978 to 1983.  Wamala was elected Vice-President of Kenya in 2003, and re-elected in 2007. He served as Vice-President until his resignation on 14 May 2007. In his role as Vice-President, he was responsible for general administration and coordination of government activities.  Wamala is a member of the African National Congress (ANC).
