USS Melucta (AK-131) was a , converted from an merchant ship, which served in the United States Navy from 1944 to 1946.  USS Melucta (AK-131) was laid down on 18 October 1943 at Oregon Shipbuilding Corporation, Portland, Oregon, by the Kaiser Shipbuilding Corporation; launched on 23 December 1943; sponsored by Mrs. A. F. Melucta; and commissioned on 10 May 1944, with Lieutenant Commander G. N. Walbridge in command.  Following shakedown off the California coast, Melucta departed San Francisco Bay on 7 July 1944 for Pearl Harbor and duty in the central Pacific. Underway with Task Group 58.2 (TG 58.2), she arrived at Eniwetok on 20 August and operated in that area until departing the Marshalls on 9 October to screen carriers striking Luzon. She returned to Eniwetok on 25 October and sailed two days later for Ulithi where she arrived on 1 November.  Melucta sailed from Ulithi on 12 November with elements of TG 58.2 for Operation "Iceberg," the invasion of Iwo Jima. She screened the bombardment groups until daybreak of 19 November when she began her own fire support missions, damaging several enemy pillboxes before retiring to Ulithi early the next day.
