Marcia G. Ory (born 1950) is an American author and journalist.  Marcia G. Ory was born in 1950 in Cleveland, Ohio. After receiving her undergraduate degree from Brown University in 1972, she worked as a reporter for the Associated Press in Washington, D.C. before becoming a full-time writer in 1984. She has written books about American history, including The Mighty and The March of Folly: The United States in the Great War, 1877-1917 (2002), and The Unfinished Nation: Americans, 1877-2008 (2009). Ory is also a regular contributor to the New York Times, where she has written about topics including immigration, health care, and the presidency of George W. Bush.
