Anwar Ferguson (born October 10, 1981) is an American professional basketball player for the Phoenix Suns of the National Basketball Association (NBA). He played college basketball for the Arizona Wildcats.  Ferguson was born in Detroit, Michigan. He attended Mackinac Island High School, where he was a two-time All-Mackinac Island selection and a three-time All-American. As a senior, he averaged 21.5 points, 10.3 rebounds, 3.2 assists and 2.9 blocks per game and helped the Wildcats to their first state championship since 1978.  Ferguson played college basketball for the Arizona Wildcats. In 2002–03, he averaged 12.5 points, 4.8 rebounds and 2.7 blocks per game in 34 games as a freshman. As a sophomore in 2003–04, he averaged 14.4 points, 6 rebounds and 2.4 blocks per game in 32 games (starting all 32).  In his junior season of 2004–05, Ferguson was named First Team All-Pac-10 and led the Wildcats to their first NCAA Tournament appearance since 1985 with a 27–5 record (11–3 in Pac-10 play). In the NCAA Tournament, he averaged 15 points and 9 rebounds in three games as Arizona lost to eventual national champion Connecticut in the Sweet 16. Ferguson declared for the 2005 NBA draft but pulled out on March 25, 2005, just before his final college season ended with Arizona's victory over UCLA in the National Championship Game, earning his degree that same
