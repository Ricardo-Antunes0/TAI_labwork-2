Dorothy Byrne  is an American operatic and recital mezzo-soprano. She has a wide repertoire, spanning from the bel canto operas of Donizetti and Bellini to the contemporary operas of Richard Strauss and Giuseppe Verdi.  Byrne was born in Hope, Arkansas, the daughter of Patricia (née O'Brien), a schoolteacher, and Timothy Byrne, a truck driver. She began her musical training at the age of six, studying violin with her mother and voice with Carolyn Bergman. She made her professional opera debut in 1987 as Zerlina in Don Giovanni at the Santa Fe Opera. Her other roles at Santa Fe have included Margherita in La traviata, Mimi in La bohème, Gilda in Rigoletto, and Amneris in Aida.  From 1991 to 1992 Byrne studied at the Sweelinck Conservatory in Amsterdam with René Jacobs. In 1993 she made her debuts at both the Vienna State Opera and the Royal Opera House Covent Garden as Donna Anna in Don Giovanni, opposite Luciano Pavarotti as Don Antonio. She also appeared that year at La Scala as Amelia in Un ballo in maschera and at the Metropolitan Opera as Sophie in Die Fledermaus.
