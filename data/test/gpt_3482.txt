David Richard Evan Hale (born October 26, 1951) is a retired United States Army general who served as the 22nd Chief of Staff of the United States Army from February 10, 2006, to November 4, 2007.  Hale was born in 1951 in Lebanon, New Hampshire. He graduated from the United States Military Academy in 1973, and later earned a master's degree in business administration from the University of Southern California. After completing officer training at Fort Benning, Georgia, he was commissioned a second lieutenant in the infantry.   In 1975, he was assigned to Company A, 2nd Battalion, 41st Infantry Regiment, 3rd Brigade (Separate), 25th Infantry Division. He later commanded 2/41st Infantry from 1987 to 1988 and 1/41st Infantry from 1989 to 1990. He subsequently served as deputy commander for operations for III Corps and as director for force development for VII Corps.  He was promoted to colonel in 1992 and brigadier general in 1997. From 1998 to 2000, he served as Deputy Chief of Staff for Plans and Operations at U.S. Central Command (CENTCOM). In that role, he was responsible for all aspects of CENTCOM's operational plans and budget. Hale then served as the Commanding General of the 3rd Infantry Division until his appointment as Chief of Staff of the Army on February 10, 2006.  Hale retired on November 4, 2007. He is currently a Distinguished Visiting Fellow at the Hoover Institution at Stanford University.
