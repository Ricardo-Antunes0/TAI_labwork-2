Olanrewaju Olusegun Mark Bamidele "Lanre" Oyebanjo (born 1 January 1975) is a retired Nigerian footballer who played as a forward. He has played for clubs in Nigeria, Italy, Saudi Arabia, and Qatar.  Oyebanjo started his professional career at Ilorin United in the Nigerian Premier League. He then moved to Italian Serie A club Parma, where he played for two seasons. In January 2001, Oyebanjo transferred to Saudi Premier League club Al-Ittihad Jeddah. He spent two seasons with Al-Ittihad before transferring to Qatar SC in July 2003. Oyebanjo spent three seasons with Qatar SC, before retiring in 2005.  After retiring from football, Oyebanjo started business ventures in Qatar. In 2007, he founded Lanre Football Academy which provides training facilities for young African footballers. He also owns a restaurant in Doha called The Lanre Oyebanjo Restaurant.  Oyebanjo was a member of the Nigerian national team that participated in the 1996 Summer Olympics, the 1998 World Cup, the 2002 Africa Cup of Nations, and the 2006 World Cup. He also played for the Nigeria U-23 team at the 1996 Summer Olympics and at the 1997 UNCAF Nations Cup.
