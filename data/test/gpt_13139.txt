The suiriri flycatcher (Suiriri suiriri) is a small passerine bird in the family Tyrannidae. It is found in Bolivia, Brazil, Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, and Venezuela. Its natural habitats are subtropical or tropical moist lowland forest, subtropical or tropical moist montane forest, and heavily degraded former forest.  The suiriri flycatcher is a small passerine bird with a length of about 23 cm and a weight of about 2 g. It has a black body with a white belly and distinctive red eyes. The male has a black head with a red crown and a white supercilium above the eye. The female is similar to the male but has a duller crown and no red on the side of the head. The juvenile has a brown head with some black markings. The song of the suiriri flycatcher is a sharp kee-kee-kee-kee-kee.
