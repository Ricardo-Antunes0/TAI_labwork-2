John E. Mullally (October 22, 1874 - February 2, 1961) was an American prelate of the Roman Catholic Church. He served as Bishop of Buffalo from 1933 to 1951.  Mullally was born in Buffalo, New York, to Irish immigrants. After attending local parochial schools, he entered St. Bonaventure's College (now the University at Buffalo) in 1892. He studied at the Pontifical Gregorian University in Rome, from where he was ordained to the priesthood on May 30, 1901.  Mullally served as a curate at St. Joseph's parish in Buffalo from 1901 to 1903 and then at Holy Family parish from 1903 to 1906. He then served as secretary to Bishop John J. McNulty from 1906 to 1909 and pastor of Our Lady of Sorrows parish in Buffalo from 1909 to 1911.  On September 3, 1911, Mullally was appointed auxiliary bishop of Buffalo and Titular Bishop of "Telma" by Pope Pius X. He received his episcopal consecration on the following November 8 from McNulty, with Bishops Edward J. Hennessy and Patrick Joseph Hayes serving as co-consecrators, at St. Joseph's Cathedral. As an auxiliary bishop, Mullally served as chairman of the Board of Education for Erie County and as president of the Catholic Charities Appeal Board of New York State.  On March 26, 1933, Mullally was appointed bishop of Buffalo by Pope Pius XI., receiving his episcopal consecration
