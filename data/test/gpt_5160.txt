After completing her secondary education at the Baku High School No. 1, she graduated from the Baku State University with a degree in law in 1998.  Vakilova started her journalistic career in 1998 as a reporter for the weekly news magazine "Azerbaijan". In 2000 she became the deputy editor of "Azerbaijan News" magazine. From 2002 to 2006 she worked as the editor-in-chief of the "Azerbaijan Times" newspaper. In 2006 she founded and served as the director of the Heydar Aliyev Foundation for Human Rights Education and Research.  Since 2002, Vakilova has been a member of the International Helsinki Federation for Human Rights (IHF), which is headquartered in Geneva. She has also been a member of several human rights organizations, including Amnesty International (AI), Human Rights Watch (HRW), Reporters without Borders (RSF), and Front line Defenders (FLD).   Vakilova has been involved in many human rights cases and campaigns, including those involving violations of freedom of expression, torture and ill-treatment, trafficking in persons, attacks on journalists, and discrimination against minorities.
