Sir Roger George Moore  (14 October 1927 â€“ 24 May 2017) was an English actor who appeared in more than 120 films over a fifty-year career. He was known for his roles as James Bond in the Eon Productions films, beginning with the first film in 1973. Moore also starred in the independent film The Last Emperor (1987), for which he was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Actor.  Moore was knighted in 1987 for his services to the performing arts. He was also made a Companion of Honour by Queen Elizabeth II in 2002 and a honorary citizen of seven towns in Ireland. In March 2015, he announced his retirement from acting. Moore died on 24 May 2017 at the age of 89.
