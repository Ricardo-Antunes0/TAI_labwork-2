Karen M. Bliss is an American applied economist, currently the Raymond J. Weil Professor of Economics and Business at Yale University.  Bliss received her B.S. in economics from Cornell University in 1991, and her M.A. and Ph.D. in economics from Yale University in 1994 and 1998, respectively. She has been a professor at Yale since 1998.  Her research focuses on applied microeconomics, economic development, public economics, and industrial organization. She has authored or co-authored more than 100 journal articles and book chapters, and has been the recipient of numerous awards, including the John Bates Clark Award (1999), the Joan Scott Award (2005), and the John D. Rockefeller III Prize in Economics (2015).
