The Breguet 901 Mouette  is a two-seat touring aircraft produced by the French aircraft manufacturer Breguet. It was originally designed as a two-seat sporting monoplane but later versions were developed for use as a general aviation aircraft.  The Breguet 901 Mouette was first flown in 1955 and entered production in 1956. The aircraft is a conventional high-winged monoplane with a straight tapered fuselage and a single engine in tractor configuration. It is made from welded steel tubing with bolted-together panels and has a tailwheel landing gear.  The 901 Mouette is a popular aircraft with pilots and owners worldwide and is still in production.
