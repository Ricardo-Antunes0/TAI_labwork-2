Sir Robert Southwell PRS (31 December 1635 â€“ 23 May 1699) was an English courtier and diplomat. He was the son of Sir Edward Southwell of Reigate, Surrey, and his first wife, Frances Eliot.   Southwell served in the Royalist army during the English Civil War and became a gentleman of the Privy Chamber. After the Restoration of Charles II he was appointed Ambassador to France, a post he held from 1672 to 1675. He then served as Ambassador to Spain from 1675 to 1679. He retired from public life in 1682.  Southwell was twice married: firstly to Elizabeth Villiers, with whom he had two sons and a daughter; and secondly to Katherine Killigrew, with whom he had three further sons.
