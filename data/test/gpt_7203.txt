Frank Filo (born December 28, 1971 in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania) is an American professional ice hockey defenseman who is currently playing with the HC Davos of the Swiss National League A.  Filo played major junior hockey in the Ontario Hockey League with the Sault Ste. Marie Greyhounds and the Peterborough Petes. He was drafted by the Buffalo Sabres in the third round, 79th overall, of the 1990 NHL Entry Draft. He played two seasons of college hockey at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute, where he was a team captain and led his team to an NCAA Division I championship in 1993.  He made his professional debut in the International Hockey League with the Buffalo Sabres' affiliate, the Rochester Americans. In 1995–96, he moved to Europe to play for HC Davos of the Swiss National League A. He has also played for Kladno nad Upper Silesian in the Czech Republic and HC Davos' feeder club, HC Lugano of Switzerland's Nationalliga A.  On July 1, 2006, Filo signed a one-year contract with HC Davos. On October 1, 2006, he scored his first goal as a professional player in a 3–1 victory over EV Zug.  In 2007–08, Filo led all Swiss defensemen in scoring with 41 points (14 goals and 27 assists) in 40 games.
