Yoram Gal (born January 7, 1952) is an Israeli mathematician and computer scientist.  Gal is known for his work in combinatorial optimization, with a focus on the traveling salesman problem and its variants. He has also made contributions to graph theory, machine learning, and distributed Computing.  Gal was born in Tel Aviv, Israel, on January 7, 1952. He received his bachelor's degree from the Hebrew University of Jerusalem in 1976, and his doctoral degree from the University of California, Berkeley in 1981.  Gal has been a professor at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem since 1981. He served as the head of the department of mathematics there from 1997 to 2001. Gal is a fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences, and of the American Mathematical Society.
