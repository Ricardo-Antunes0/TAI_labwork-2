Asansol is a (Tier-II) metropolitan city in the eastern Indian state of West Bengal. It is the district headquarters of Asansol district.  The city is located in the eastern part of the state, on the banks of the Asansol River, a tributary of the Ganges. It has an area of . Asansol was founded in 1786 by the British East India Company as a cantonment town and became a municipality on 1 April 1862. The city experienced rapid economic growth after the discovery of coal in the surrounding area in 1872, and has been described as India's "Coal Capital".  Asansol is an important industrial and commercial centre in eastern India, with a population of over 2 million people. The city is home to many large industrial units, including Hindalco Industries Limited, United Phosphorus Limited, SAIL (Chandigarh), ACC Limited, Birla Group and coal-fired thermal power plants like Kamala Nehru Thermal Power Station, Singrauli Power Plant and Koderma Thermal Power Station. The city has emerged as one of the leading IT hubs in eastern India with several companies such as Wipro, Infosys, TCS and Tech Mahindra having their regional offices here.  Asansol is also home to several educational institutions such as Asansol University, Jadavpur University and Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur.
