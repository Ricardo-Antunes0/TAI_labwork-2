Yosef Amit ( 1945) is a former Israeli diplomat, who served as the Permanent Representative of Israel to the United Nations from 1994 to 1999.  Born in Jerusalem in 1945, Yosef Amit served as a diplomat in various capacities for over thirty years, including postings in Cairo, Geneva and New York. In 1994 he was appointed Permanent Representative of Israel to the United Nations, where he played a leading role in the negotiation of the Oslo Accords and the establishment of the Palestinian Authority. He retired from the Israeli Foreign Service in 1999.
