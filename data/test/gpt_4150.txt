Horacio Adolfo Salgán (June 15, 1916 – August 5, 1997) was an Argentine writer, journalist, and political activist. He was part of the Generation of '40, a group of intellectuals who emerged in the 1940s.  Salgán was born in Buenos Aires to a middle-class family. He studied at the University of Buenos Aires, where he became involved in leftist politics. In 1944, he co-founded the magazine "Crítica". He later worked for "La Nación", "El País", and "El Mundo". Salgán's work often focused on social issues, and he was a vocal critic of Argentina's military dictatorship. He died in 1997.
