Aric Nesbitt (born January 25, 1980) is an American actor and musician. He is best known for his roles as Michael Scofield on the Fox television series Prison Break and its spin-off series, Breakout, as well as his role as Derek Shepherd on the AMC television series The Walking Dead.  Aric Nesbitt was born in San Diego, California to Kathy (née Kallenbach), a registered nurse, and Mark Nesbitt, an insurance agent. He has two sisters, actress Lizzy Caplan and writer/producer Maura Tierney. He attended Point Loma Nazarene University in San Diego and studied theater arts.  Nesbitt made his acting debut in the 2000 Fox series Spin City, in which he played the role of Austin Powers' sidekick Dr. Evil's son Artie. In 2003, he guest-starred on an episode of the CBS sitcom How I Met Your Mother. That same year, he had a small role in the comedy film Stuart Little 2. In 2004, he appeared in the independent film The Life Aquatic with Steve Zissou.  In 2005, Nesbitt was cast in the role of Michael Scofield on the Fox television series Prison Break. The show ran for five seasons from 2005 to 2009 and helped make him a household name.
