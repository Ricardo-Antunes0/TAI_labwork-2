The Imperial Palace Ingelheim  is an ancient palace located in the city of Ingelheim am Rhein, in the Rhein-Neckar-Kreis, in Baden-WÃ¼rttemberg, Germany.  The palace was built in the 12th century by Conrad I, Count Palatine of the Rhine. The present structure was erected between 1420 and 1430 by Count Palatine Ulrich II and his wife Elisabeth of Bavaria as a residence for their son, Duke Rupert I. It served as imperial residence for over 150 years.  The palace became a popular tourist destination after it was opened to the public in 1875. It is now a museum.
