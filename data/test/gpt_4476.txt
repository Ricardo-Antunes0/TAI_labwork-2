Lawrason was born in Northampton, Massachusetts to Captain John Lawrason and Elizabeth (Lyon) Lawrason. He attended the common schools and graduated from Dartmouth College in 1823. He studied law with Judge David Humphreys and was admitted to the bar in 1827.  He commenced practice in Poughkeepsie, New York, where he became one of the leading lawyers. He served as a member of the New York State Assembly in 1840 and 1841, and as a justice of the Supreme Court of New York from 1853 to 1857.  He was elected as a Democrat to the Forty-fifth Congress, serving from March 4, 1857 to March 3, 1859. He was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1858 to the Forty-sixth Congress.  After leaving Congress, Lawrason resumed his practice in Poughkeepsie. In 1861 he was appointed judge of the Chautauqua County Court by Governor John A. Dix. In 1865 he was elected governor on a platform supporting civil rights for African Americans, becoming the first Democrat to be elected governor of New York since George Clinton in 1792.
