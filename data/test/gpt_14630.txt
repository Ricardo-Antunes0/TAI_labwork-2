Ape House is a 2010 novel by American author Daniel Woodrell.  The novel is set in the fictional town of West Egg on Long Island in 1922. It tells the story of a young man, John Beecham, who is falsely accused of murder and must flee to avoid execution. Beecham is helped by a group of friends and fellow criminals who he meets while on the run. The novel also explores the themes of friendship, loyalty, and morality.  "Ape House" was published in 2010 and was well-received by critics. It was awarded the Pulitzer Prize for Fiction in 2011.
