Antonio Manuel Fernández (January 17, 1902 – January 3, 1986) was a Venezuelan-born writer, editor and professor. He was also a journalist, radio commentator and political activist, who spent most of his career in the United States.  Antonio M. Fernández was born in Caracas, Venezuela, on January 17, 1902. As a young man he worked as a journalist for various publications in Venezuela and Spain. In 1939 he moved to the United States, where he worked as a journalist and editor for several publications. He also became active in the American Communist Party and served as a delegate to the Third International Congress of Writers in Moscow in 1943.  Fernández began teaching at Fordham University in 1960 and retired from that position in 1978. He died on January 3, 1986.
