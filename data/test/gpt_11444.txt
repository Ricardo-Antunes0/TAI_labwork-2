Riggs Peak  is a peak rising to 9,906 feet (3,033 m) above sea level in the Sawatch Range of the Rocky Mountains of North America. The peak is located in Ward County, Colorado, east of the town of Fredonia.  Riggs Peak is named for Edward Riggs, an early prospector in the area. The peak was first ascended on July 12, 1874 by a party led by John Wesley Powell.
