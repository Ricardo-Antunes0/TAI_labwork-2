Match is a 2014 American drama film directed by Peter Landesman and written by Landesman and David Levien. It is based on the novel of the same name by Bret Easton Ellis.  The film stars Andrew Garfield, Emma Roberts, Justin Timberlake, and Mila Kunis. It tells the story of a group of young people in New York City in the 1980s who become romantically involved with one another while navigating the harsh realities of their lives.  The film premiered at the 2014 Toronto International Film Festival, where it was awarded the People's Choice Award. It was released in the United States on September 25, 2014, by IFC Films.  In 1987 New York City, a group of young people experience love and heartbreak as they navigate the harsh realities of their lives. Andy (Andrew Garfield), a college student struggling to find himself; Emma (Emma Roberts), a young woman trying to make it on her own; Justin (Justin Timberlake), a talented musician trying to make his way in the world; and Matty (Mila Kunis), an ambitious girl searching for her place in the world.
