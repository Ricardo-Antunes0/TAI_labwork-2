Nikhil Tandon is an Indian endocrinologist, medical scientist, and academic. He is the current Chancellor of the University of Delhi.  Tandon has authored over 600 scientific papers and has been awarded several honours, including the prestigious Padma Bhushan in 2001. He is also a Fellow of the Indian National Science Academy.  Nikhil Tandon was born in 1941 in a small town in the state of Uttar Pradesh, India. After completing his undergraduate studies at the University of Allahabad, he did his post-graduate study at the University of Liverpool, where he obtained his M.D. in 1967. He then undertook a residency at the National Institutes of Health (NIH) in Bethesda, Maryland, and returned to India to work as a research fellow at the All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS) in Delhi. In 1976 he was appointed Professor of Endocrinology at AIIMS and became Head of the Department of Endocrinology in 1984. In 1994 he was appointed Chancellor of the University of Delhi, a position he continues to hold.  Tandon's research focusses on endocrine diseases, particularly diabetes mellitus and obesity, and on novel approaches to their prevention and treatment. He has played a leading role in developing innovative treatments for diabetes and is widely recognized as an international expert on this subject. He is also known for his work on testosterone replacement therapy and for his contributions to our understanding of male reproductive health.
