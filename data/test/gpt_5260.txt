Steve Soboroff (born August 31, 1948) is an American television personality, journalist, and political analyst. Soboroff has been a commentator for CNN since the early 1990s, and is also a regular panelist on the cable news network's talk show "Crossfire." Soboroff is also a political analyst for MSNBC.  Soboroff was born in Brooklyn, New York, to Jewish parents. He attended Harvard College and the Columbia University Graduate School of Journalism. Soboroff's first job was as a reporter for WCBS-TV in New York City. He then moved to Washington, D.C., where he served as a congressional correspondent for ABC News.  In the early 1990s, Soboroff became a commentator for CNN. He has been a frequent panelist on "Crossfire" since its inception in 1999. Soboroff also provides political analysis for MSNBC. In 2012, he released his first book, "The Comeback: How Obama Won the White House and Redefined the Democratic Party." Soboroff lives in Washington with his wife and two children.
