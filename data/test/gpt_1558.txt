John Bradley Winslow (October 4, 1851July 13, 1910) was an American lawyer and politician who served as the 28th Governor of Maine from 1897 to 1899.  Winslow was born in Sangerfield, New Hampshire, the son of Judge Bradley Winslow and Elvira (Bradley) Winslow. He attended the common schools and then the University of New Hampshire, where he was a member of the Delta Theta Pi fraternity. He studied law with his father and was admitted to the bar in 1872.  Winslow started his legal career in his father's law office, but soon established a successful practice on his own. In 1885, he was elected to the Maine House of Representatives, where he served two terms. In 1896, he was elected Governor of Maine, serving one term until 1899. In 1902, he was appointed United States Attorney for the District of Maine by President Theodore Roosevelt. He remained in that position until his death in 1910.
