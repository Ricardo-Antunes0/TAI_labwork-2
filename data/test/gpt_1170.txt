Yoon Sung-hee (born 1973) is a South Korean artist who creates installations and sculptures that explore themes of time, memory, and the body.  Yoon's work has been exhibited at the Museum of Modern Art in New York, the Centre Pompidou in Paris, and the National Gallery of Victoria in Melbourne. She has also been awarded the Grand Prize at the 2006 Gwangju Biennial and the 2007 DAAD Artist's Award from the German Federal Ministry of Education and Research.  Born in Seoul in 1973, Yoon Sung-hee graduated from Korea University with a degree in Fine Arts. After several years of working as an assistant to various artists, she began her own career in 1998 with a solo exhibition at the Young Artists' Space in Seoul.   Since then, her work has been exhibited at museums and galleries around the world, including the Museum of Modern Art in New York (2001), Centre Pompidou in Paris (2002), National Gallery of Victoria in Melbourne (2004), and Tate Modern in London (2006). She was also awarded the Grand Prize at the 2006 Gwangju Biennial and the 2007 DAAD Artist's Award from the German Federal Ministry of Education and Research. In 2018 she will have a retrospective at Kunstmuseum Basel.   Yoon's installations and sculptures explore themes of time, memory, and the body.
