The Zamoskvoretskaya line , formerly Gorkovsko–Zamoskvoretskaya  line is a line of the Moscow Metro. It runs from the Zamoskvoretskaya station of Line 2 to the Gorkovskaya station of Line 5.  The Zamoskvoretskaya line was opened in 1935 as part of the first stage of the Moscow Metro. At the time, it was known as the Gorkovsko–Zamoskvoretskaya line. The line originally ran from the Gorkovskaya station on Line 5 to the Zamoskvoretskaya station on Line 2. In 1978, the line was extended north to the Kursky Rail Terminal, and in 2003 it was extended south to the Novokuznetskaya station.
