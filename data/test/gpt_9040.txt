Richard Mark Burton (born 16 January 1956) is an English journalist and presenter. He is a former editor of the Sunday Times, where he also served as deputy editor.  Burton was born in London, England, the son of the journalist and author Richard Burton and actress Elizabeth Taylor. He was educated at Eton College and Trinity College, Cambridge. He began his career as a sub-editor on the Sunday Times in 1978. He was appointed deputy editor in 1990 and editor from 1995 to 2000.  Burton has presented numerous programmes for the BBC, including The South Bank Show, Newsnight, and coverage of the 1997 general election. In 2002 he presented a three-part series for Channel 4 about British film directors. Burton has also written a number of books, including The Liar's Club (1993), about politicians; Shakespeare's Women (1999), about female Shakespearean actors; and Churchill: A Life (2004).
