Christopher Brennan (born 1832, date of death unknown) was an Irish sailor who is best known for his participation in the first voyage of the clipper ship SS Great Britain.  Brennan was born in 1832 in County Cork, Ireland. He was an able sailor, and by 1855 he had sailed on a number of voyages, including one to Australia. In 1860 he sailed on the clipper ship SS Great Britain, which was the first ship to use a square-rigged sail. Brennan was one of the crew members who helped make the voyage a success. He remained aboard the ship after it returned to port, and died unknown in 1892.
