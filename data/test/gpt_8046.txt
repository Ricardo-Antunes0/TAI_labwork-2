Some Girls is the 14th British and 18th American studio album by the British singer and songwriter Madonna. It was released on 14 November 2002 by Maverick Records and Warner Bros. Records, and produced by Madonna, Shep Pettibone and Patrick Leonard.  The album was met with generally mixed reviews from music critics. Some reviewers called it an improvement over her previous albums, while others criticized its lack of innovation. It debuted at number one on the US "Billboard" 200 with first-week sales of over 246,000 copies, becoming Madonna's seventh consecutive number-one album in the United States. The album also reached number one in Canada, Ireland and the United Kingdom. The album has sold over 9 million copies worldwide.  "Some Girls" is a dance-pop and R&B album that draws inspiration from 1980s pop music. Its lyrics discuss female empowerment, self-love and sexual freedom. The album's title track was a critical and commercial success, peaking at number one in thirteen countries including the US, UK, Australia and Canada. The other singles released from the album include "Don't Tell Me", "Hollywood", "Bitch I'm Madonna" and "Frozen."  Madonna began working on her 14th studio album in 2001 after finishing her world tour behind her 13th studio album "Sticky & Sweet".
