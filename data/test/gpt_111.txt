Mario Bava (31 July 1914 – 25 October 1975) was an Italian film director, screenwriter and producer. He directed 38 feature films in a career spanning more than five decades.  Born in 1914 in Rome, Bava started his career as a cinema projectionist before making his directorial debut with the low-budget film "The Girl Who Kicked the Hornet's Nest" (1953). He gained critical and popular success with the horror film "Black Sunday" (1960), which was later remade by Bava as "Satan's Slave" (1977). He also directed the science fiction thriller "Planet of the Vampires" (1965), the gothic romance "Curse of the Cat People" (1961), and the crime film "Tenebrae" (1963). In his later years, Bava directed two fantasy films, "The Witches" (1990) and "Labyrinth" (1986), before dying of a heart attack in 1975 at the age of 56.  Mario Bava was born on 31 July 1914 in Rome to Pietro, a barber, and Adelina (née Sartori) Bava. He had two sisters, Lucia and Adelina. The family moved to Naples when Bava was six years old. After completing his secondary education at a Salesian college in Naples, he started work as a cinema projectionist.  In 1934, at age 23, he wrote and directed his first short film, "The Student".
