Martha Watts (February 13, 1848 – December 9, 1924) was an American stage and film actress.  Watts was born in Troy, New York, the daughter of John Watts (1813-1888), a carpenter, and Elizabeth (née Sutherland) Watts. She began her acting career in amateur theater in 1869. She made her professional debut at the age of 22 in a production of "The Rivals". Watts became a leading actress on Broadway in the 1880s. Her most famous Broadway roles were as Lady Macbeth in William Shakespeare's play Macbeth (1881), Juliet in William Shakespeare's play Romeo and Juliet (1886), and Lady Bracknell in Oscar Wilde's play The Importance of Being Earnest (1895).  Watts made her first motion picture appearance in 1903. She appeared in more than seventy films over the next thirty years, usually playing supporting roles. Her last role was as Mrs. Miniver in Frank Capra's film version of Philip Pullman's children's novel, The Railway Children (1946). After her retirement from acting, she moved to Glendale, California, where she lived until her death at the age of eighty-eight.
