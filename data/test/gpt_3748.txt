Ernst Bach (10 May 1876 – 1 February 1957) was a German composer and pianist. He is best known for his six orchestral suites, which are among the most popular works in the classical repertoire.  Ernst Bach was born in Würzburg, Bavaria, into a musical family. His father, Johann Baptist Bach, was a cellist and organist, and his mother, Maria Theresia Bach, was a pianist. Ernst began playing the piano at the age of four, and by the age of eight he had composed his first piece of music. After studying at the Royal Conservatory of Munich with Robert Fuchs and Carl Reinecke, Bach enrolled in the University of Munich to study philosophy, but he soon changed his course of study to musicology.  Bach's early compositions were largely chamber pieces. In 1895 he wrote his first orchestral work, the suites for solo violin and string orchestra "1. Suite", which became one of his most popular works. He wrote several more suites over the next few years, as well as other orchestral works including the "Concerto for Violin and Orchestra" (1897), "Symphony No. 1 in C minor" (1898), and "Requiem" (1895).
