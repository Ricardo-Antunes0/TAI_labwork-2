The BRM P61 was a Formula One racing car that was built by the British Racing Motors (BRM) team and raced from 1961 to 1968.  The P61 was designed by Tony Southgate and first raced in the 1961 season. It was BRM's first cars with monocoque construction, and it proved to be a successful design, winning several races and finishing second in the championship behind the Ferrari 246 GTO. The P61 was replaced by the P62 in the 1963 season, but it continued to be raced until 1968.
