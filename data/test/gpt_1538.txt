The Montreal Building (Portuguese: Edifício Montreal) is a skyscraper in the financial district of Montreal, Quebec, Canada. It is the 24th-tallest building in the city and is located at 1001 La Gauchetière Street.  The Montreal Building was designed by architects Jacques Gréber and Mario Beaulieu and was completed in 1972. It is a rectangular structure clad in polished granite, with a reflective glazed façade. The building rises to a height of 251.3 metres (818 ft 3 in), making it the 24th-tallest building in Montreal. It is also the tallest building in the Quebec City metropolitan area.
