Tipner  is a residential district of the city of Dresden, in the German state of Saxony. It is located on the left bank of the Elbe river, north-west of the center of Dresden.   The name "Tipner" derives from the Slavic word "tip" meaning "point" or "ridge". The area was first settled in the 12th century by Slavic settlers. The first documented reference to Tipner dates from 1291. In 1345, Tipner was granted town rights by King Wenceslaus III.  Tipner is known for its 19th century architecture and is home to several historical buildings, including the former Tipner Palace, now a museum. Today, Tipner is a popular residential district with a population of around 10,000.
