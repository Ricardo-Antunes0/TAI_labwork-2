Wisła Mała (, "Little Vistula"; , "German: Weisse Vistula") is a river in Poland and Germany.  The Wisła Mała originates in the southern Carpathians and flows through central Poland before entering the Upper Vistula river near the city of Gdańsk. It then passes through the West Pomeranian Voivodeship, where it is called the White Vistula, before flowing into the North Sea near Szczecin.  The Wisła Mała is long and has a drainage basin of . It is the tenth longest river in Central Europe.
