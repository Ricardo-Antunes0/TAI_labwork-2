Prosto z mostu (English: Straight Out) was a Yugoslav rock band from Zagreb. They were active from 1978 to 1984.  Formed in 1978 in Zagreb, Prosto z mostu were one of the most popular Yugoslav rock bands of the early 1980s. Their distinctive sound was based on heavy, hard-rocking rhythms and catchy melodies. They released a number of successful albums throughout their career, including the gold-selling album "Zašto se ljubiš" in 1983. The band split up in 1984 but re-formed in 2000 and released a new album in 2002.
