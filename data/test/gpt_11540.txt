Keith McDermott (born September 28, 1953) is an American jazz bassist and composer.  Keith McDermott was born in Providence, Rhode Island on September 28, 1953. He began his musical career as a bassist, playing with various local jazz ensembles before moving to New York City in the early 1980s. There, he began working with such notable musicians as Donald Byrd, Joe Henderson, Cannonball Adderley, and Freddie Hubbard.  McDermott has since released numerous albums as a leader and has been featured on recordings by such prominent artists as Cassandra Wilson, John Scofield, and Roy Haynes. He also served as the bassist for the Grammy-winning jazz quartet The Tony Williams Lifetime.  In addition to his work as a musician, McDermott has also written music for both stage and screen. His compositions have been heard in productions of The Lion King, A Chorus Line, and Rent. He currently resides in New York City.
