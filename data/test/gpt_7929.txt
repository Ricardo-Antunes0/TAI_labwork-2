Better Luck Tomorrow is a 2002 American neo-noir crime thriller film written and directed by Wong Kar-wai. The film tells the story of a criminal mastermind (Tony Leung Chiu-wai) who is released from prison after serving a sentence for murder, and sets about planning his next heist.  The film's title refers to a proverb often used in East Asian cultures to encourage people to have good luck in the future. The proverb is "better luck tomorrow than today".  Better Luck Tomorrow received critical acclaim, with many critics praising its visual style and unique plot. It was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Screenplay.
