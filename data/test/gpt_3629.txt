Nowhere in Africa  is a 2001 documentary film directed by Michael Moore. It tells the story of the Eritrean War of Independence and the Eritrean Civil War. The film also examines the history of Ethiopia, Sudan, and Somalia.  The documentary was shot in various locations in Africa, including Asmara, Addis Ababa, Juba, and Khartoum. It premiered at the Toronto International Film Festival in September 2001 and was released in theaters on October 25, 2001.  Nowhere in Africa has been widely acclaimed by critics. It has a rating of 88% on Rotten Tomatoes based on 66 reviews. The critical consensus states "Michael Moore's powerful documentary offers a revealing look at Africa's complex history."   The film won the 2002 Academy Award for Best Documentary Feature.
