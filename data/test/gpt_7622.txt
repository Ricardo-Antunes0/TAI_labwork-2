Leicester Galleries was an art gallery located in Leicester, England. It was established in 1872 and closed in 1984.  Leicester Galleries was one of the largest and most important art galleries in Leicester. The gallery was founded in 1872 and opened its doors to the public the following year. Initially, the gallery exhibited a range of paintings, sculptures and drawings from the local area, but it soon became well known for its extensive collection of French Impressionist paintings. The gallery's reputation grew during the late 19th and early 20th centuries, and it became well known for hosting major exhibitions of British and European art. In 1984, Leicester Galleries closed its doors for good due to financial difficulties.  Today, the gallery's collection is housed at the University of Leicester's Art Gallery.
