The No-Gun Man is a 1924 American crime drama film directed by George Melford and starring Earle Hodgins, Myrna Loy and Alan Hale.   The film is based on the novel "The No-Gun Man" by Clarence Stedman. The story follows a WWI veteran turned private detective, John Q. Public, who investigates a series of murders in which the victims were shot with revolvers but not killed.
