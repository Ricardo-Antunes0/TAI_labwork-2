Edward Frost was born in Roxbury, Massachusetts on April 27, 1801. He studied medicine at the University of Pennsylvania and graduated in 1825. Frost then moved to Maine and started a medical practice in Windham.  Frost became involved in politics following the Mexican-American War and was elected as a Democrat to the Maine House of Representatives in 1847. He later served as the state's sixth governor from 1852 to 1854. Frost died in Portland, Maine on July 9, 1875.
