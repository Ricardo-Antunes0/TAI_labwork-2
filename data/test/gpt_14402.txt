Trychnopalpa is a monotypic moth genus in the family Geometridae. It was described by Francis Walker in 1854. Its only species is Trychnopalpa selene.  The wingspan is about 25 mm. The forewings are pale ochreous, slightly dirty white posteriorly and with a few scattered dark fuscous scales. There is a small black dot at the end of the cell and a larger one at the end of the fold near the base, both connected by an irregularly curved black line. The hindwings are pale fuscous, darker towards the apex and there is a small black spot on the costa before the apex, as well as a small black discal dot at one-third.
