Stephen Pleasonton (1776? – January 31, 1855) was an American lawyer and politician who served in the United States Congress and as Governor of California.  Pleasonton was born in Boston, Massachusetts, to James Pleasonton and Elizabeth (née Stephens) Pleasonton. He studied law and was admitted to the bar in 1801. He practiced law in Boston until 1813, when he moved to Illinois Territory, where he served as a territorial judge from 1813 to 1815.  In 1816, Pleasonton moved to Missouri Territory, where he served as the territorial secretary from 1816 to 1820. He then returned to Illinois Territory and served as a territorial delegate to the United States Congress from Illinois Territory from 1820 to 1824. During his time in Congress, he served as chairman of the Committee on Indian Affairs (1821–1824).  In 1824, Pleasonton was elected governor of California, serving until 1830. During his time as governor, he helped pass the California Constitution in 1849. Pleasonton died in Washington, D.C., on January 31, 1855.
