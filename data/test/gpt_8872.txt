Delilah Blue is the second full-length studio album by American singer-songwriter and musician John Mayer. The album was released on September 18, 2009, by Columbia Records. Recording sessions for the album took place between February and May 2009 at various locations in California and New York City.  John Mayer has stated that the album represents a change in direction for him, as it is more acoustic and "looser" than his previous work. The album was preceded by the release of the single "Your Body Is a Wonderland" on July 22, 2009.  Delilah Blue received generally positive reviews from music critics. At Metacritic, which assigns a normalized rating out of 100 to reviews from mainstream publications, the album received an average score of 78, which indicates "generally favorable reviews".  The title track was nominated for Best Rock Song at the 52nd Annual Grammy Awards.  John Mayer started his career as a prominent member of the grunge movement in the 1990s with bands like Pearl Jam and Nirvana. However, he has since relocated to a more acoustic-based sound with his 2006 album Continuum and 2009's Born and Raised. Delilah Blue is Mayer's second full-length studio album after Born and Raised, which was recorded in 2006 but not released until 2009.   Delilah Blue was preceded by the release of the single "Your Body Is a Wonderland" on July 22, 2009. The song reached number one on the US Billboard Hot 100 chart and remained there
