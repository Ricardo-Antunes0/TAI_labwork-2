Wilhelm Zaisser was born in 1893 in Stuttgart, Germany. He joined the German Army in 1912 and served in World War I as a pilot and observer. After the war he served in the Reichswehr and graduated from the General Staff School at Schlieben in 1926.  Zaisser served as chief of staff of VII Army Corps from 1930 to 1933 and as chief of staff of XVI Army Corps from 1933 to 1935. He was promoted to lieutenant general in 1935 and served as commander of XIV Army Corps from 1935 to 1937. Zaisser became chief of staff of Army Group A in 1938 and led the army group during its invasion of Poland in 1939. He was promoted to generalmajor in 1940 and served as deputy commander-in-chief of Army Group South from 1940 to 1941.  Zaisser became commander-in-chief of German Fourth Army in 1942, leading it during Operation Barbarossa. He was promoted to generaloberst in 1943 and commanded Army Group Centre during the Battle of Kursk and the Battle of Kiev. Zaisser was awarded the Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross with Oak Leaves and Swords for his leadership during these campaigns. He retired from the army in 1945 and died three years later at the age of 66.
