Crystal Mountain Resort is a resort and ski area located in the town of Crystal Mountain, Michigan, in the U.S. state of Michigan. The resort has a vertical drop of 1,050 feet (320 m), making it the tenth-highest ski resort in the United States, and is the only ski area in Michigan rated four stars by Ski Magazine. It also features a snowmaking system that produces over 400 inches (10,000 mm) of snow per season.  Crystal Mountain Resort is one of the oldest resorts in North America, having been founded in 1939. The resort has been owned by Vail Resorts since 2000. The resort was previously known as Crystal Mountain Ski Area and was renamed in 2002 after its parent company acquired Vail Resorts.
