Heartland is an unincorporated community located in northwestern Milam County, Texas, United States. It is located on the eastern edge of the Llano Estacado.  Heartland was first settled in 1876 by Joel and Addie McCarty. The community was named for Heartland, Missouri, the home of Joel McCarty's parents. A post office was opened in Heartland in 1877, and remained in operation until it was discontinued in 1953.  Heartland is located at . The community is situated along U.S. Route 183, which forms the eastern border of the community. The Llano Estacado National Forest lies to the east and the Llano Estacado Wildlife Management Area occupies much of the southern portion of the community.  As of the census of 2000, there were 188 people residing in Heartland. The population density was 97 people per square mile (37/km²). There were 372 housing units at an average density of 232 per square mile (89/km²). The racial makeup of the community was 97% White and 3% from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino of any race were 2% of the population.
