The Eagle Mine is a small, high-grade copper and gold mine located in the Copper Country of Michigan. It is owned by Hudbay Minerals Inc. and was the first American copper mine to be vertically developed. The mine was developed in the early 1990s, and produces about 100,000 ounces of copper and 1,000 ounces of gold annually.  The Eagle Mine is located in northeastern Michigan in the Copper Country, near the town of Houghton. The mine has an estimated reserves of 2.1 million ounces of copper and 1 million ounces of gold. The ore was mined using a underground method called sublevel mining. The mine is operated by Hudbay Minerals Inc., a subsidiary of Toronto-based Hudbay Minerals Inc.
