The 1956 Summer Olympics, officially known as the Games of the XVII Olympiad, were an international multi-sport event held in Melbourne, Australia from 15 April to 1 August 1956. Melbourne was the first city in the world to host a Summer Olympic Games, and the first to do so with a largely outdoor program.   The 1956 Games were the first to be televised live in color and also featured the first international Olympic football tournament. Melbourne was chosen as the host city during the 1955 International Olympic Committee (IOC) meeting in Amsterdam, Netherlands. Melbourne had previously hosted a series of exhibition games including the 1953 World Table Tennis Championships and the 1954 British Empire and Commonwealth Games.  The 1956 Olympics marked the commencement of the Golden Age of Australian sport with a total of 34 medals (24 gold, six silver and twelve bronze) won by Australian athletes. The opening ceremony, on 15 April, was staged at the Melbourne Cricket Ground and featured a performance by Dame Nellie Melba. The main Parade of Nations took place on 23 April and featured 27 nations, including all 10 African countries at that stage of independence. The Australian team won every medal contested, with Australia's greatest Olympic victory coming in the men's 4x100 metres relay where they completed a new world record time of 39.4 seconds.
