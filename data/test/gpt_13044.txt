Bernie M. Farber (born 1951) is a Canadian-American journalist, commentator, and public policy advocate. He is the founding executive director of the Institute for Public Accuracy, a nonpartisan, nonprofit organization that specializes in research and advocacy on issues of public interest.  Farber was born in 1951 in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. He received his B.A. from the University of Toronto in 1973 and his J.D. from the University of California at Berkeley in 1977.  Farber began his career as a reporter for the Canadian Broadcasting Corporation in 1976. He became a national correspondent for CBS News in 1981 and a senior correspondent for ABC News in 1988. In 1992, he joined the Washington Post as a national political correspondent and columnist. He served as Post editor from 1995 to 2000.  In 2001, Farber founded the Institute for Public Accuracy (IPA), which he currently serves as its executive director. The IPA is an independent research and educational organization that specializes in analysis of public policy issues from a progressive perspective. It has published numerous reports on such topics as climate change, energy conservation, healthcare reform, and U.S.-Israel relations.  Farber has also written extensively on U.S. politics and national security issues.
