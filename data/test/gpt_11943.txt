Steve Wieck (also credited as Stephan Wieck) (October 4, 1916 - October 27, 2006) was a Swiss-born American jazz pianist and composer.  Wieck was born in Zürich, Switzerland, and began playing the piano at age four. He attended the University of Zürich, where he studied with Arnold Schoenberg and Marcel Mule. In 1939, he moved to the United States and settled in New York City. He worked as a pianist in nightclubs and orchestras and also taught at the Juilliard School. He recorded prolifically as a solo artist and in collaboration with other musicians, including Dexter Gordon, Art Tatum, Thelonious Monk, Bud Powell, and John Coltrane.  Wieck's most celebrated recordings are the sessions he recorded with Coltrane in 1961 and 1962, which have been regarded as some of the finest recordings in jazz history. Wieck's style is characterized by improvisation and virtuosity on the keyboard. He died in 2006 at age 91.
