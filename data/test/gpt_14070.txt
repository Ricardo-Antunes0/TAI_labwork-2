Úrsula Hilaria Celia de la Caridad Cruz y Morales, (born July 2, 1922, Havana, Cuba—died May 20, 1997, Miami, Florida, U.S.), Cuban singer and actress who was one of the most popular and influential performers of her era.  Cruz’s early life was difficult. Her mother died when she was young, and she was raised by her father. Cruz began singing in nightclubs at the age of 14 and soon developed a following in Havana. In 1948 she toured Mexico and then made her first appearance on Broadway in 1950. She moved to New York City in 1952 and became a full-time performer.  Cruz became one of the most popular singers in the world during the 1960s and 1970s. Her performances captured the soul of her homeland and introduced Cuban music to a wider audience. She recorded more than 60 albums and performed in more than 50 countries.  Cruz was a powerful advocate for the rights of Cuban refugees and participated in many humanitarian projects. She received numerous awards, including three Grammy Awards (in 1963, 1965, and 1979).
