Gibbs was born in Sydney on 25 May 1918, the son of Reginald Mahoney Gibbs and his wife Nellie. He was educated at Sydney Boys High School and then studied at the University of New South Wales, graduating with a degree in engineering.  In 1938, Gibbs joined the Royal Australian Air Force (RAAF). He served in World War II as a fighter pilot with No. 74 Squadron RAAF and achieved fame as an ace, credited with nine aerial victories. In 1945 he was awarded the Distinguished Service Order (DSO) for his actions in the Battle of Berlin. He retired from the RAAF in 1948 with the rank of wing commander.  Gibbs later became chairman of a number of companies, including Gibbs Engineering and Gibbs Group Holdings. He died in Sydney on 1 October 1989.
