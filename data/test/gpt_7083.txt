Gerald Joseph "Jerry" Blavat (born July 3, 1946) is an American journalist and author. He is the former editor and vice president for news at the "Los Angeles Times", where he was a member of the Pulitzer Prize-winning staff from 1984 until his departure in 2004.   Blavat was born in Brooklyn, New York, to parents who were Russian Jews. After attending Yeshiva University in New York City, he began his journalism career at the "Journal-American" newspaper. He later worked at the "New York Daily News", where he was a reporter, assistant managing editor, and editor.  In 1984, Blavat joined the "Los Angeles Times" as its editor and vice president for news. In this role, he was a part of the Pulitzer Prize-winning staff that won the award for national reporting in 1989 for its coverage of the L.A. riots. He left the "Times" in 2004 to become editorial director of The Huffington Post, a position he held until 2006.  Since 2006, Blavat has been a senior writer at Salon.com, where he covers media and politics. He has also written for The New York Times Magazine, The New Yorker, and other publications. His book about the media industry, "The Loudest Voice in the Room: How the Press Overlooks Itself and How to Fix It", was published in May 2014.
