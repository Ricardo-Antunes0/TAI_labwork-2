Sierpc (Polish: ) is a town in eastern Poland, in the Lublin Voivodeship. It is the seat of the gmina (administrative district) called Gmina Sierpc. Sierpc lies approximately south-west of the regional capital Lublin.  The town has a population of 14,000 people. It was first mentioned in a 13th century deed as "Siertp". The town was founded by Prince Władysław Odonic and his wife Anna, daughter of Duke Mieszko III of Poland. The town was granted Magdeburg rights in 1248. In 1269, its name was changed to "Sierpc". In 1569, it was captured by the army of John Sigismund Zborowski and burned to the ground.  Sierpc is home to a major steel mill, Sierpc SA, which is one of Europe's leading steel producers. The steel plant employs about 1,500 people.
