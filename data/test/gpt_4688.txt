Born in Tehran, Toufanian was the son of a wealthy merchant. He studied at the American University of Beirut and then at the University of Paris, where he earned his doctorate in 1936. From 1936 to 1941, Toufanian served as an instructor at the University of Tehran. He then moved to the United States, where he taught at Columbia University from 1941 to 1952. In 1952, Toufanian became a professor at Princeton University. He retired from Princeton in 1980 and moved to Washington, D.C., where he died in 1996.  Toufanian is best known for his work on the Armenian Genocide, which he first documented in detail in The History of the Armenian People (1965). His other notable works include The Modern Middle East (1968), Iran: The Revolution Within (1975), and The Iranian Revolution (1979).
