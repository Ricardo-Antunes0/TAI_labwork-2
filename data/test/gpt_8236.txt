Oakland City Hall is the seat of government for the city of Oakland, California. The building is located at 1 Frank H. Ogawa Plaza in downtown Oakland, across the street from the Oakland Museum of California. The building was designed by architect John Galen Howard and was completed in 1939.  Oakland City Hall is an Art Deco building built on the site of an earlier city hall that was destroyed in the 1906 San Francisco earthquake and fire. The new building was designed by John Galen Howard, a well-known architect of the time. The exterior is made of red brick and granite, and features a large bronze statue of a woman by sculptor Adolph A. Weinman. The interior is decorated with marble, terrazzo floors, and frescoes. The building has a central atrium with a large skylight that allows natural light to flood into the building.
