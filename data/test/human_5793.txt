Skookumchuck  is a Chinook Jargon term that is in common use in British Columbia English and occurs in Pacific Northwest English.  Skookum means "strong" or "powerful", and "chuck" means water, so skookumchuck means "rapids" or "whitewater" (literally, "strong water"), or fresh, healthy water. It can mean any rapids, but in coastal usage refers to the powerful tidal rapids at the mouths of most of the major coastal inlets. Places named Skookumchuck include: Skookumchuck, British Columbia, a town in British Columbia named for the large rapids in this area on the Kootenay River.
