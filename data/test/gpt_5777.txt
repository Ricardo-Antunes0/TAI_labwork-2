Acropora indonesia is a species of acroporid coral found in the Indo-Pacific Ocean. It is also commonly known as the Indonesian Porites.  This coral is found in shallow water on reefs and at depths of 10-30 m. It can be found on both hard and soft substrates, and is tolerant of a wide range of salinities.  The larvae feed onzoobenthos, while the adult coral consumes algae and small invertebrates. The coloring of the coral varies from light to dark brown, with a few patches of white. The spatula shape of this coral gives it its common name.
