Sydney Showground Stadium (Known commercially as GIANTS Stadium) is a multi-purpose stadium in Sydney, Australia. It is located in the Sydney Olympic Park and was the main stadium of the 2000 Summer Olympics. The stadium also hosted six rugby union matches during the 2003 Rugby World Cup, as well as the 2005 Rugby World Cup Final.  The stadium has a seating capacity of 82,500 people and has been used for a variety of sports, including Australian rules football, rugby union, soccer, cricket and rugby league. It is also used for concerts and other events.  The Sydney Showground Stadium was first proposed in the early 1990s as an alternative to the Sydney Cricket Ground as the venue for the 2000 Summer Olympics. The original design by Danish architect Bjarke Ingels included a retractable roof that would have made the stadium one of the largest in the world. However, due to cost concerns, this was abandoned and a revised design was created by British architect Lord Norman Foster. The final design features a rectangular structure with a retractable roof covering 70% of its total area. The stadium was built by construction company Bilfinger Berger at a cost of A$1.5 billion and officially opened on 7 April 2000 with a match between Sydney FC and Adelaide United.
