He later served as the Deputy Commander of United States Forces Korea, from 1971 to 1973. In 1975 he became Chief of Staff, United States Army.     Clark is most notable for his command of the 1st Cavalry Division during the Battle of Ia Drang in November 1965. The battle is widely considered to be one of the most significant battles of the Vietnam War. He retired from the Army in 1975 with the rank of brigadier general.  Harold L. Clark was born in West Point, New York on September 28, 1915 and attended West Point from 1934 to 1937. He was commissioned into the infantry in 1937 and served with II Corps in India and China during World War II. After the war he served with III Corps in Germany until 1949, when he was assigned to The Pentagon as an infantry instructor at Fort Benning, Georgia.  In 1955 Clark was transferred to Armor School at Fort Knox and then to Armor School at Fort Knox and The Pentagon as an instructor. In 1959 he was transferred to Headquarters, United States Army Europe as Deputy Chief of Staff for Plans and Operations.
