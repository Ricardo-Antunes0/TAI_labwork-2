Twinkletoes is a 1926 American silent romantic comedy film directed by Frank Capra. It is considered one of the first feature-length animated cartoons. The film is also notable for its musical score by Dimitri Tiomkin.  Twinkletoes is a tale of love and laughter, set in a whimsical world of dance and music. The story is narrated by a little girl who tells the story of two rabbits who fall in love and are forced to flee their home in order to escape the jealous advances of a cat. Along the way, they are aided by a band of merry critters, including a bevy of dancing doves and an enthusiastic cricket.   The film was produced by Walt Disney Productions and released on November 13, 1926. It was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Animated Short Film but lost to "The Story of Segar and Lucy".
