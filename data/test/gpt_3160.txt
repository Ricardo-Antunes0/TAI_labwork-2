Julie Marie Nolke is a Canadian comedian, actor, and writer. She is known for her work on the CBC television series "This Hour Has 22 Minutes" and for her appearances on "The Ellen DeGeneres Show", "The Late Show with David Letterman", and "Jimmy Kimmel Live!".  Nolke was born in Calgary, Alberta, Canada, to parents of German and Danish descent. She grew up in Cochrane, Alberta and attended high school in Airdrie. After high school, she studied at the University of Victoria before moving to Toronto to pursue a career in comedy.  Nolke's first television appearance was as a regular on the CBC Television show "This Hour Has 22 Minutes" from 1998 to 2001. She then appeared on the Fox show "The Ellen DeGeneres Show" from 2003 to 2005. In 2006, she was a guest correspondent on the Comedy Central show "Loud Mouths". In 2007, she appeared on "The Late Show with David Letterman" and in 2008 she appeared on "Jimmy Kimmel Live!".  In 2009, Nolke wrote, directed and starred in her own one-woman show, "Julie Nolke: Life As I Know It". The show received positive reviews and was nominated for a Dora Mavor Moore Award for Outstanding New Canadian Play. In 2011, she starred in the Canadian comedy film "Pillow Talk".
