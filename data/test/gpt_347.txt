Cephalopholis nigripinnis, the blackfin grouper, banded-tail coral-cod, or black-and-yellow grouper, is a species of fish in the family Serranidae. It is found in the eastern Atlantic Ocean and the Mediterranean Sea. Its natural habitats are subtropical or tropical shallow seas, subtropical or tropical marine waters, and coral reefs.  The blackfin grouper is a large fish growing to a length of 75 cm (30 in). It has a long snout with a large mouth and small eyes set high on its head. The body is black with yellow bands running along its body. The fins are black with yellow bands. The male has larger fins and a more prominent yellow band on the tail than the female.
