Vossius Gymnasium is a public gymnasium in the town of Vossius, Netherlands. It is named after Nicolaus Vossius, a 17th century Dutch scholar and philosopher.  The gymnasium was founded in 1876 by the municipal council of Vossius. It originally served as a primary and secondary school. In 1903 it became a gymnasium for boys, and in 1961 it became a coeducational school.   The Vossius Gymnasium has approximately 900 students. It offers the Dutch education system, the VOSO curriculum, which is an adaptation of the American school system. The gymnasium has a variety of sports facilities, including an indoor track, an outdoor track, a swimming pool, and a football field.
