The Alliterative Morte Arthure is a 4346-line poem written by Geoffrey Chaucer in the late fourteenth century. It is a dark and ironic work, chronicling the death of King Arthur.  The poem is built around a series of alliterative rhymes, and tells the story of Arthur's final days. The poem is full of dark humour, and is well known for its complex plot and its often surreal imagery.
