Suzanne Pharr is an American organizer, political activist, and writer. She is the co-founder and director of the nonprofit organization Food & Water Watch.  Suzanne Pharr was born in San Francisco, California, on October 5, 1957. She is the daughter of a union organizer and a schoolteacher. After graduating from college, she worked as a social worker and then as an organizer for the United Farm Workers union.  In the early 1990s, Pharr co-founded Food & Water Watch with Josh Schwerin. The organization focuses on exposing abuses of food and water resources and promoting public policies that improve them. Over the years, Food & Water Watch has played a leading role in campaigns to reform food production, promote renewable energy, end subsidies for corporate agriculture, and protect public health and the environment.  In 2016, Pharr was appointed by President Barack Obama to serve on the National Commission on Environmental Justice.  Suzanne Pharr is the author of numerous books, including The Right to Feed: The History of a Human Right (2007), which was named one of the best books of 2007 by The New York Times Book Review and one of the best books of 2008 by The Washington Post.
