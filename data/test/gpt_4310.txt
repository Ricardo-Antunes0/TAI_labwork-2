{{Infobox person | name         = Maxine Doyle | image = | birthplace = | nationality = | occupation = | education = | family = | timeline = }} Maxine Doyle (born 1943) is an Irish novelist, short story writer, and journalist. She has written novels such as "The Country Girls" and "The Well of Loneliness", and has been awarded the Sunday Times-owned Sunday Independent Fiction Prize twice, as well as the Booker Prize in 1984 for her novel "The Well of Loneliness". She has also written non-fiction books on Irish life and culture, including "A Woman's Place" and "An Ghaeilgeoir's Life".
