Susan Gilbert is an American author, publisher, and journalist. She is the co-founder and editor-in-chief of the online magazine Slate, and was its chief content officer until March 2017.  Gilbert has written for "The New York Times Magazine", "The Atlantic", "The Washington Post", "Esquire", and other publications. She has also been a contributor to NPR's "All Things Considered" and "Morning Edition". Gilbert is a co-founder of the non-profit organization The First Things Project, which promotes public understanding of first principles in thought and culture.  Gilbert was born in New York City in 1965. She is the daughter of Sylvia Gilbert, a historian and writer, and Irwin Gilbert, an advertising executive. Gilbert graduated from Radcliffe College with a degree in history in 1987. After a year working as a research assistant at Harvard's Library of Congress, she moved to Washington, D.C., to work as a staff writer at "The Washington Post". Gilbert left the Post to co-found Slate in 1996 with former Post colleague David Plotz.
