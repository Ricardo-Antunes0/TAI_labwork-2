Ahmad Dhani Prasetyo (born Dhani Ahmad Prasetyo; 21 October 1964) is an Indonesian businessman, investor, and philanthropist. He is the founder and chairman of the garment company PT G-Star Indonesia.  Dhani was born in Medan, North Sumatra, Indonesia on 21 October 1964. He is of Javanese descent.   Dhani has a degree in economics from the University of Indonesia. He started his business career in 1987 as a sales representative for a textile company. In 1992, he founded his own textile company, PT G-Star Indonesia. The company went public in 1997 and grew rapidly, becoming one of the largest garment companies in the world by 2001. Dhani has since decentralized the company and it now operates in 19 countries. He also owns a number of other businesses, including hotels and racing horses.  Dhani has been involved in charitable work throughout his career. He is a founding member of the Ahmad Dhani Foundation, which provides education and health services to poor communities around the world. He also donated $100 million to the Clinton Global Initiative in 2013 to support global initiatives aimed at fighting poverty and inequality. In 2017, he pledged $1 billion to help rebuild areas affected by the 2017 Hangzhou earthquake in China.  Dhani has been married to Indonesian actress Vivi Prasetyo since 1998. They have two children.
