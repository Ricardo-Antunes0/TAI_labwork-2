Nada Jabado,  is a Professor of Linguistics at the University of Maryland, College Park. He is best known for his work on the reconstruction of prehistoric Indo-Aryan languages, and on morphophonology and syntax of modern Indo-Aryan languages.  Nada Jabado was born in Hyderabad, India in 1966. He completed his undergraduate studies at Jawaharlal Nehru University, Delhi, and his graduate studies at the University of California, Berkeley. Jabado received his Ph.D. from UC Berkeley in 1996.  Jabado began his academic career as an assistant professor at UC Berkeley. He later moved to the University of Maryland, College Park, where he has been a professor since 2002.  Jabado's research focuses on the reconstruction of prehistoric Indo-Aryan languages and on morphophonology and syntax of modern Indo-Aryan languages. He has also authored or coauthored a number of books, including A Reconstruction of Proto-Indo-Aryan (2001), The Evolution of Old Indo-Aryan (2003), and The Syntax of Modern Indo-Aryan (2007).
