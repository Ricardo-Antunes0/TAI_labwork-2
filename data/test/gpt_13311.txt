Knut Ingolf Løfsnes (22 December 1918 – 18 January 1998) was a Norwegian historian, journalist and broadcaster.  He was born in Kristiania, the son of Ingolf Løfsnes (1875–1957) and Hilda Torvaldsen (1882–1960). He was a brother of historian Sigurd Løfsnes.  Løfsnes finished his secondary education in 1934, and studied history at the University of Oslo from 1936 to 1939. He worked as a journalist for the newspapers "Aftenposten" and "Verdens Gang" from 1940 to 1944. From 1948 to 1951 he chaired the Norwegian Press Association. He served as deputy editor of the newspaper "Den Norske Samling" from 1951 to 1954, and as its editor from 1954 until his retirement in 1978.   Løfsnes was a member of the Norwegian Academy of Science and Letters from 1959, and a fellow of the Norwegian Historical Association from 1963. He died in 1998.
