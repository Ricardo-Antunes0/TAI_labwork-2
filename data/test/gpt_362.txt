The Royal Jewelry Museum  is an institution in the United Kingdom dedicated to the history and preservation of royal jewelry, particularly that of the British monarchy. It is housed in the former private residence of Queen Victoria, who donated it to the nation in 1897.  The museum has a considerable collection of jewelry, including pieces from the Tudors and Stuarts, as well as 18th- and 19th-century pieces by contemporary Royal Jewellers. The collection also includes pieces from India, Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The museum has been open to the public since 1902.
