Margaret Garner called "Peggy" (died 1858), was an American abolitionist and women's rights activist. She was also a writer and editor, as well as a lecturer on women's rights and temperance.  Born in Massachusetts, Garner became involved in abolitionism in the 1830s. She worked with William Lloyd Garrison to publish "The Liberator", the leading abolitionist newspaper. In 1835, she helped found the American Anti-Slavery Society, and served as its president from 1835 to 1837.  Garner was also active in women's rights. She helped organize the Seneca Falls Convention, which advocated for women's rights, in 1848. She also served as the president of the National Women's Rights Party from 1850 to 1852.  After retiring from her activism, Garner wrote several books on women's rights and temperance. She died in 1858.
