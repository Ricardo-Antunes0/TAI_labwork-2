She was built in 1870 and served until 1892. She was armed with a main armament of two rifled muzzle-loaders, and also carried a number of smaller guns for defence against enemy ships. Magdala played an important role in the suppression of the Irish Rebellion of 1879, during which she captured or destroyed several rebel vessels. After serving as a training ship for some time, Magdala was sold for scrap in 1892.
