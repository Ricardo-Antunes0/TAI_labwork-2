Charles Kenney Duncan (December 7, 1911 – February 3, 1994) was an American prelate of the Roman Catholic Church who served as the fourth Archbishop of San Francisco from 1965 to 1981.  Duncan was born in San Francisco, California, to James and Bridget (née Kenney) Duncan. He attended St. Ignatius College Preparatory and then the University of San Francisco. He was ordained a priest in 1939.  From 1945 to 1965, Duncan served as vicar general of the San Francisco Archdiocese. On March 5, 1965, he was appointed the fourth Archbishop of San Francisco, succeeding John J. McNamara. During his time as archbishop, Duncan oversaw the construction of many new churches and institutions, including Cathedral of Saint Mary of the Assumption and Saint Francis de Sales Seminary. He also presided over the Vatican's investigation into allegations of sexual abuse by Catholic priests in the United States. Duncan retired from his position on October 1, 1981.  Following his retirement from the Archbishopric, Duncan resided in San Francisco until his death from heart failure at age 80. He is buried at Holy Cross Cemetery in Colma, California.
