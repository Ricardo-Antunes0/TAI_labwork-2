Robert Derrell Griffith (born December 12, 1943) is an American former professional basketball player who competed in the NBA for the Milwaukee Bucks, Boston Celtics, and Philadelphia 76ers. He was drafted by the Milwaukee Bucks with the eighth pick in the 1965 NBA draft.  Griffith played collegiately at the University of Kansas, where he was a two-time Consensus All-American and led the Jayhawks to their first national championship in 1968. His scoring average of 27.5 points per game is still a school record.  Griffith's career was cut short by a knee injury sustained in 1971. After his retirement from playing, Griffith coached for several seasons at various levels, including two stints as head coach of the Philadelphia 76ers. In 1990, he was inducted into the Basketball Hall of Fame.
