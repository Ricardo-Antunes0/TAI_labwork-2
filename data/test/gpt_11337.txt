Raja Saleem Akhtar (Punjabi, )   is a Pakistani film director, screenwriter, producer and actor. He is also the founder of the production company Film & Television Productions. Akhtar has directed more than 50 feature films, including the critically acclaimed dramas "Karachi" (2002), "Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge" (1995) and "Lagaan" (2001). He has received numerous awards and accolades, including the National Film Award for Best Director for "Karachi" and the Padma Bhushan award for his contributions to Indian cinema.  Akhtar was born in Sialkot, Pakistan in 1957. After completing his secondary education from Islamia College Lahore, he attended the University of California, Berkeley, where he studied economics. Akhtar began his career as a filmmaker in 1984, when he directed his first short film, "Aashiq Banaya Aap". His first major success came with the drama "Karachi" (2002), which was also his debut feature film as a director. The film won numerous awards, including the National Film Award for Best Director and the Screen Award for Best Film. Akhtar followed up "Karachi" with two more successful dramas—"Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge" (1995) and "Lagaan" (2001)—both of which were also awarded the National Film Award for Best Screenplay.
