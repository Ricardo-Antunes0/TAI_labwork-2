Truman Seymour (September 24, 1824 â€“ October 14, 1902) was an American politician who served as the 31st Governor of New York from 1881 to 1883 and as a U.S. Senator from 1885 to 1899.  Born in Windsor, Vermont, Seymour attended the common schools and then studied law. He was admitted to the bar in 1849 and commenced practice in Kingston, New York. He served as Kingston town attorney from 1850 to 1851.  Seymour was elected as a Democrat to the 34th United States Congress and served from March 4, 1871, until his resignation on January 3, 1875, to accept a nomination by President Ulysses S. Grant to be United States Attorney for the Northern District of New York. He served in that office from March 4, 1875, until his resignation on January 3, 1881.  He was elected as the 31st Governor of New York in November 1880 and served from January 1, 1881, until his resignation on March 4, 1883. In November 1886 he was elected as a Democrat to the United States Senate and served from March 4, 1885, until his death in Washington, D.C., October 14, 1902.
