The Oscar Taylor House is a historic house located in Bowling Green, Kentucky. It was built in 1853 for Oscar Taylor, a prominent attorney and politician in the mid-19th century. The house is a modest 1 1/2-story brick structure with a gable roof and a central chimney. It features an elaborate Greek Revival-style porch with a pediment and brackets, and decorative elements including dentils, quoins, and modillions. The house was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1976.
