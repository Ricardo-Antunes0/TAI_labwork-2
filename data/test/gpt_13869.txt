USS Davidson (FF-1045) was a  destroyer of the Fletcher-class of destroyers built for the United States Navy during World War II.  USS Davidson was laid down on 20 December 1943 by Bethlehem Steel Corporation, San Francisco, California, launched on 3 March 1944, sponsored by Mrs. Royce L. Davidson, and commissioned on 9 July 1944 with Lieutenant Commander C. T. Covington in command.  Following shakedown off the California coast, Davidson departed Pearl Harbor on 7 September 1944 for combat duty in the Pacific War. She participated in the assault and occupation of Iwo Jima from 19 February to 2 March 1945; provided escort and fire support during the Okinawa campaign from 26 March to 1 June 1945; and fought in the Battle of Okinawa from 26 April to 10 June 1945. After a brief period of escort duty in the western Pacific, she returned to Okinawa to conduct operations against the Japanese shipping evacuation from Saipan in July 1945.  Davidson completed her combat career in mid-August 1945 and departed Okinawa for the United States. She was decommissioned on 10 October 1945 at San Diego and placed in reserve. She was struck from the Naval Vessel Register on 1 May 1972 and sold for scrapping on 15 September 1973.
