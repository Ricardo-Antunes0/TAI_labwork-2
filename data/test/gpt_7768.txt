The Green Cockatoo (a.k.a. Four Dark Hours) is a 1932 Australian film directed by Reginald Le Borg and written by Lionel Rogosin.  The Green Cockatoo is a 1932 Australian film directed by Reginald Le Borg and written by Lionel Rogosin. The story follows the life of an Aboriginal man, Joey, who is imprisoned on a penal farm in the Outback after being falsely accused of murder. Joey is befriended by a cockatoo, which helps him to pass the time and regain his self-confidence. When Joey is finally released, he sets out to find the man who framed him and avenges his family's honour.
