Blaine is a city in Whatcom County, Washington, United States. The population was 2,980 at the 2010 census. It is the county seat of Whatcom County.  Blaine was officially incorporated on February 5, 1903. The city was named after U.S. Congressman James G. Blaine (1835-1925), who was the Republican nominee for President in 1884 and 1892.
