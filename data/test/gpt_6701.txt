Yakov Vladimirovich Smushkevich (Lithuanian: Jakovas Smuškevičius, ; Russian: Яков Владимирович Смушкевич, Yakov Vladi­mirovich Smushkevich; Ukrainian: Яков Валентийович Смушкевич, Yakov Valeriyovych Smushkevich) (born December 7, 1948) is a Russian businessman and politician who served as the Prime Minister of Russia from September 1999 to August 2000.  He was born in Vilnius, the capital of Lithuania. He has a degree in engineering from the Moscow Institute of Technology.  Smushkevich first came to prominence in the early 1990s, when he was appointed deputy head of Gazprom. From 1994 to 1996, he served as the head of Gazprom's subsidiaries in Ukraine. He then served as the head of Gazprom's international division until 1999.  In September 1999, Smushkevich was appointed Prime Minister of Russia by Russian President Boris Yeltsin. His tenure was marked by economic crisis and a series of financial scandals that led to his resignation in August 2000.
