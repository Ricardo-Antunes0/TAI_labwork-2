Paratene Ngata (1849? – 15 December 1924) was a Māori leader, political activist and soldier who played an important role in the development of the Māori King Movement.  Ngata was born in 1849 in the North Island town of Te Kuiti. He was the son of Te Rauparaha and Ngatatamai. Ngata was educated at Wesley College, Auckland, and at the Royal Military College, Sandhurst. He served in the New Zealand Rifle Brigade during the Waikato War of 1863-1866.  Ngata became a leader of the Māori King Movement, which aimed to establish sovereignty for Māori over their land and resources. He played an important role in the negotiations that led to the Treaty of Waitangi. Ngata also served as Minister for Native Affairs in the Second Labour Government of New Zealand (1907-1909). He died in 1924.
