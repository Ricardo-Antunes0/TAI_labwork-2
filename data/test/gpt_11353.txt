Joel Kotkin (born December 28, 1952) is an American historian and journalist. He is the author of The New Geography of Jobs, The City: A Global History, and The Next Hundred Million: America's Coming Population Challenge.  Kotkin is also a Fellow of the Royal Historical Society, and has been a visiting professor at Tufts University, Loyola Marymount University, and the University of California, Santa Barbara. He has also been a columnist for Forbes, and was a regular commentator on MSNBC's The Daily Rundown.  Kotkin was born in Philadelphia to Jewish parents who had fled Nazi Germany. Kotkin's family moved to Los Angeles when he was young and he attended public schools there. Kotkin earned his BA from Occidental College in 1974 and his PhD from UCLA in 1980. While at UCLA, Kotkin became involved with the left-wing activist group Students for a Democratic Society (SDS).  Kotkin began his career as an academic historian at UCLA, where he taught American history from 1980 to 1985. From 1985 to 1993 he worked as a senior editor at the conservative magazine National Review. From 1993 to 1998 he was a distinguished fellow at the Manhattan Institute, where he focused on issues related to urbanism and immigration.  From 1998 until his retirement in 2013, Kotkin was a senior editor at Forbes magazine.
