Richard Perry (born June 18, 1942) is an American singer-songwriter, guitarist and producer. He is best known as the founder, lead singer, and primary songwriter of the rock band Perry Como.  Perry Como was one of the most successful pop vocalists of the mid-20th century. He had fifteen Top 40 hits in the United States, including "You Must Have Been a Beautiful Baby", "I Will Remember You", "What Would You Like to Drink?", and "Catch a Falling Star". His popularity also extended to countries such as Canada and Australia, where he had several Top 10 hits. He was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 1987.  Richard Perry was born in 1942 in Canonsburg, Pennsylvania. His father was a steelworker and his mother was a homemaker. Perry grew up in the Pittsburgh area and started playing guitar at age six. In high school, he formed his first band with classmates. After graduating from high school in 1960, Perry attended Duquesne University for a year but dropped out to pursue a career in music.  Perry first recorded for RCA Victor as a solo artist in 1963. His first major success came with the release of his album "Introducing Richard Perry" in 1965. The album included the hit single "You Must Have Been a Beautiful Baby". The following year, he released his second album, "It's Now or Never".
