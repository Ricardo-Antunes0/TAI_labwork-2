Claudius Rey (2 September 1817, in Lille – 8 May 1881, in Paris) was a French painter.  Rey was born in Lille, the son of a sculptor. After studying at the Lycée Condorcet and the École des Beaux-Arts in Paris, he worked as an illustrator and caricaturist before turning to painting. He exhibited at the Salon from 1848 to 1859, and in 1862 was made an Officer of the Legion of Honour. He was also a member of the Société Nationale des Beaux-Arts.
