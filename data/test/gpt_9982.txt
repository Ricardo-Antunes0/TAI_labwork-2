Kitagawa was born in Los Angeles, California, on December 25, 1892 to Japanese immigrant parents. He attended the University of Southern California and then the Pennsylvania Academy of Fine Arts, where he studied with Arthur Wesley Dow. After World War I he moved to Paris, where he worked with Pablo Picasso and Georges Braque. In 1921 he returned to the United States, settling in New York City. He became known for his abstract expressionism and his depictions of urban life and nature. He died in Los Angeles on October 10, 1986.
