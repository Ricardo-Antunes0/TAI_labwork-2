Cristoforo Frangipani (Christoph Frankopan) (, ; 1482-1542) was an Italian painter of the Renaissance.  Born in 1482 in Milan, Cristoforo Frankopan trained in the workshop of the Milanese painter Ambrogio Lorenzetti. In 1505 he moved to Rome where he became a follower of Raphael. He painted a fresco in the Sistine Chapel (1508-1512). In 1514 he moved to Naples, where he was active until his death. Among his students were Girolamo della Francesca and Pietro Perugino.
