He is best known as the anchor of the ABC Evening News from 1978 to 2005, and for his work as the moderator of the first U.S. presidential debate in 1992. Chancellor was also a correspondent for ABC News during the Persian Gulf War and for other global events.  John Chancellor was born on July 14, 1927, in New York City to Jewish parents. He attended Hamilton College in Clinton, New York, where he was a member of the Delta Kappa Epsilon fraternity. After graduating from Hamilton in 1949, he served in the United States Army as a radio and television announcer during the Korean War.  Chancellor began his journalism career at WHN in Utica, New York, where he worked from 1951 to 1954. He then moved to WKBW-TV in Buffalo, New York, where he became its news director in 1954. He left WKBW-TV in 1959 to become the news director at WNBC-TV in New York City. In 1965, Chancellor moved to ABC News as its senior vice president for news and public affairs.  Chancellor became ABC's evening anchor on September 13, 1978, succeeding Douglas Edwards as co-anchor of the network's flagship nightly news program, "ABC Evening News".
