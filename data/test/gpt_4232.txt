The Philadelphia Family Court Building also known as the Thomas W. Lamb Building, is an historic building located in Philadelphia. It was designed by Irwin T. Catharine and constructed in 1933. It is a six-story terra cotta and limestone building in the Art Deco style. It was added to the National Register of Historic Places in 1984.  The Philadelphia Family Court Building is located at 615 North Broad Street in the city's Center City district. It is a six-story terra cotta and limestone building in the Art Deco style. The exterior features a classical colonnade with balustrades, flanked by two wings that extend to North Broad and Spring Streets. The interior features marble floors, terrazzo walls, and brass fixtures.
