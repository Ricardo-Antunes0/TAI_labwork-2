Zostera muelleri  is a southern hemisphere aquatic plant found in bogs, lakes and rivers. It is also known as common reed, broad-leaved reed, water pennywort, or swamp pennywort.  Zostera muelleri is a perennial plant that grows up to 60 cm long. The leaves are fleshy, broad and flat, with a characteristic heart-shaped indentation near the base. The flowers are small, white and clustered at the top of the stem. The fruit is a brown pod.
