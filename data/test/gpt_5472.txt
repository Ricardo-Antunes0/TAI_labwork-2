Janet (Jan) Corinne Brown (born June 23, 1953) is an American singer, songwriter, and actress. She is best known for her work in the 1970s as a member of the folk rock duo The Carpenters.  Brown was born in Los Angeles, California. She began her music career in the early 1970s as a member of the folk rock duo The Carpenters. With The Carpenters, Brown achieved widespread success with such hits as "I Will Always Love You", "Superstar", and "We've Only Just Begun". Brown left the group in 1978 to pursue a solo career. She released two albums, 1981's self-titled effort and 1983's Heart Like a Wheel. Brown retired from music in 1984 and has since remained out of the public eye.  In 2007, Brown released a new album, entitled I Believe in You.
