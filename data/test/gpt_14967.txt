This article presents lists of the literary events and publications in 1862.  1862 was a significant year in literature, with several noteworthy events and publications. The year began with the publication of Charles Dickens' "A Tale of Two Cities", which was met with great acclaim and is still widely read today. Other notable works released in 1862 include Emily Dickinson's "The Stranger", Walt Whitman's "Leaves of Grass", and Nathaniel Hawthorne's "The Scarlet Letter". Additionally, the Civil War continued to rage in America, with reports of battles and bloodshed appearing in newspapers and literature throughout the year.
