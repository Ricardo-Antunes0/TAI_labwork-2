Nils Patrik Johansson (born 14 October 1967) is a Swedish singer, songwriter and actor. Johansson has been signed to the record label EMI since 1991 and has released eight studio albums. He is also known for his work in the musicals Mamma Mia! and Chess.  Johansson was born in Västerås, Sweden. He started his music career in the early 1990s as the frontman of the pop-rock band Cherry Vanilla, which released two albums before splitting up in 1995. In 1996, he formed the rock band A-Teens with childhood friend Per Gessle. The band released three albums before splitting up in 2002. In 2001, Johansson starred in the musical Mamma Mia! at the Stockholm Globe Theatre, which became an international success and won him several awards, including a Laurence Olivier Award for Best Actor in a Musical. He reprised his role in the 2008 film version of Mamma Mia! and in the 2017 sequel Mamma Mia! Here We Go Again. In 2012, he starred as Count Fosco in Chess at the Royal Albert Hall in London.
