Borrer was born in Henfield, Sussex on 13 January 1747 to a working-class family. After studying at the Royal Academy of Medicine in London he became a physician in 1778. Borrer is most famous for his work on the structure of the human skull, which he published in several volumes between 1790 and 1810. He also made significant contributions to the study of medical jurisprudence and pathology. Borrer died in London on 11 October 1812.
