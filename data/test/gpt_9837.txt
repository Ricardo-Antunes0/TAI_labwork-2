Robert William Upton (February 3, 1884April 28, 1948) was an American chemist and academic who was a professor of chemistry at the University of California, Berkeley.  Upton was born in Brooklyn, New York to a family of physicians. He received his undergraduate education at Cornell University and then completed his graduate work at the University of Berlin. He returned to the United States and became a naval officer before joining the faculty of the University of California, Berkeley in 1921. Upton's research focused on the chemistry of carbon compounds, particularly coal gas and coke. He also made important contributions to the study of nitrogen compounds, organometallic chemistry, and physical chemistry. Upton died in 1948 at the age of 66 after a long illness.
