He is also known for his outspokenness and for his efforts to improve the economy of Malaysia during his time as Prime Minister.  Born in Kampung Medan, Perak on 8 December 1924, Lim Keng Yaik was educated at the Victoria Institution and the University of London. He became a doctor in 1954 and practised medicine until he entered politics in 1961.  Lim served as Deputy Minister for Education from 1961 to 1963 and Deputy Minister for Health from 1963 to 1965. In 1965, he was appointed Deputy Minister for Agriculture. In 1967, he became the Minister for Agriculture and Fisheries.  In 1971, Lim became the Minister for Interior. In 1976, he was appointed Deputy Prime Minister and Minister for Development. In 1981, he succeeded Abdul Rahman Yaacob as the third Prime Minister of Malaysia. During his time as Prime Minister, Lim made efforts to improve the economy of Malaysia and to reduce corruption within government. He also played a role in negotiations that resulted in Malaysia's recognition by the United Nations in 1984. Lim retired from politics in 1986 and later died on 25 January 2004 at the age of 80.
