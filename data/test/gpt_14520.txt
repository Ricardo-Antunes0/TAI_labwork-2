Aston Croall (born 10 July 1984) is an English cricketer. He is a right-handed batsman and a right-arm medium-fast bowler.  Croall has played for Lancashire, Northamptonshire, Worcestershire and Hampshire in first-class cricket. He has also played for the England A team. Croall made his debut for Lancashire in the 2002 County Championship against Staffordshire. From 2002 to 2005, he played twenty-six matches for Lancashire, scoring 559 runs at an average of 18.48, with a high score of 77 not out.  Croall joined Northamptonshire in 2006. He made his debut for the county against Middlesex. From 2006 to 2007, he played twenty-seven matches for Northamptonshire, scoring 721 runs at an average of 26.40, with a high score of 101 not out. He also took nine wickets at an average of 37.00, with a best bowling performance of 4/98 against Warwickshire.  Croall joined Worcestershire in 2008. He made his debut for the county against Oxford University. From 2008 to 2009, he played twenty-four matches for Worcestershire, scoring 792 runs at an average of 27.88, with a high score of 101 not out. He also took ten wickets at an average of 34.43, with a best bowling performance of 3/36 against Durham University.
