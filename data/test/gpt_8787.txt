Nick Leslau, born , is an English singer, songwriter, and multi-instrumentalist. He is the lead singer and principal songwriter of the band Nick Cave and The Bad Seeds.  Leslau was born in London, England, on 14 November 1969. He was raised in Wimbledon, South London, and attended The Kings College School in Wimbledon.  Leslau formed Nick Cave and the Bad Seeds with his schoolfriend and drummer Mick Harvey in 1986. The band's debut album, "Murder Ballads", was released in 1990. The album was certified gold by the British Phonographic Industry (BPI) and produced the singles "The Mercy Seat" and "Red Right Hand". The band's second album, "The Boatman's Call", was released in 1992. The album was certified platinum by the BPI and produced the singles "Into My Arms" and "Down by the Water".  In 1993, Leslau appeared as a guest vocalist on the Guns N' Roses track "I'm In Love With My Life". In 1995, he appeared as a guest vocalist on the Faith No More track "Epic". Leslau also appeared on the soundtrack to the 1996 film "The Night We Never Met".  Leslau's third album, "The Weeping Song", was released in 1997.
