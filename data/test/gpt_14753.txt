Prometheus Bound is an oil painting by Flemish painter Peter Paul Rubens, produced in 1610-1611. It is housed in the Rubens Hall of the Royal Museum of Fine Arts Antwerp.  Prometheus Bound is a painting about Prometheus, the Titan who stole fire from the gods and gave it to man. The painting is divided into three sections, the first showing Prometheus bound to a rock while an eagle eats his liver. The second section shows Prometheus being released from the rock and taking fire from the gods to give to man. The third section shows Prometheus being punished by being chained to a rock and having an eagle tear at his liver.
