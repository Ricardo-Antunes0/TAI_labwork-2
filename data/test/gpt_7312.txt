Louis Barrabé (15 March 1895 – 13 January 1978) was a French composer, conductor and teacher.  Louis Barrabé was born in Paris on 15 March 1895. He studied at the Conservatoire de Paris, where he won the First Prize in Composition in 1917. He also studied conducting with Armand Salle and composition with Nadia Boulanger.  Barrabé began his career as a composer of theatre music, and his first opera, "Alceste", was performed in 1928. He went on to compose several more operas, including "L'amour de Loin" (1937), "Thérèse" (1947) and "Saint-Saëns" (1949). He also wrote several symphonies, including the orchestral suite "L'île Enchantée" (1945), the symphonic poem "Le Jardin des Lilas" (1947), and the symphonic suite "Les Noces" (1953).  Barrabé became a professor at the Conservatoire de Paris in 1930, and remained there until his retirement in 1966. He was also a professor at the University of California at Berkeley from 1951 to 1955.  Barrabé died on 13 January 1978.
