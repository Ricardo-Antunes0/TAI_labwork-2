Derwent Ings is a Site of Special Scientific Interest located in the county of Cumbria, England. The site is situated on the eastern bank of the River Derwent, 3.5 miles north of Keswick, and covers an area of 6.6 hectares.  Derwent Ings is a Site of Special Scientific Interest because it contains a variety of habitats which are rare or otherwise special in England. These habitats include a wide range of grasslands, heaths and mires, as well as several ponds and lakes. The site also has a number of significant bird populations, including nationally important populations of marsh harriers and golden plovers.
