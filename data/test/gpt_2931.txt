Dinidu Asanka Marage (born 24 September 1981) is a Sri Lankan cricketer who has played for the Sri Lanka national cricket team in Tests, ODIs and T20Is. He made his Test debut in October 2003 against Zimbabwe and has since played 78 matches, with a highest score of 97 not out against South Africa in January 2013. He also played in the 2007 and 2011 World Twenty20 tournaments.  Marage was born in Colombo to parents from the town of Badulla. He completed his schooling at St. Sebastian's College, Colombo before studying at the University of Western Australia, where he played for the Western Australia cricket team in the Australian National University One Day Cup. Marage made his first-class debut for Western Australia in December 2001, and played four first-class matches in 2002 before taking a break from cricket to complete his studies. He made his return to first-class cricket for Western Australia in December 2004, playing a further 18 first-class matches over the next two years.  Marage made his ODI debut for Sri Lanka in November 2004 against Zimbabwe, and has since played 107 matches, with a highest score of 97 not out against South Africa in January 2013. He also played in the 2007 and 2011 World Twenty20 tournaments. In December 2009, Marage was named as captain of Sri Lanka's Twenty20 team for the 2010 World Twenty20 tournament, but he was replaced as captain shortly before the tournament began by Mahela Jayawardene.
