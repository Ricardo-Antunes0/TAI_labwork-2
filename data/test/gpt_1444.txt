Tigres del Licey (English: Licey Tigers) is a professional baseball team based in León, Guanajuato, Mexico. The team competes in the Mexican League and is a member of the Liga de Beisbol Profesional (LBP).  The Tigres were founded in 1939 as the Leon Tigers and joined the Mexican League in 1944. They reached their greatest success in the 1970s, when they won three league championships and two Caribbean Series. In 1990, they moved to San Diego and became the San Diego Padres' Triple-A affiliate. The team returned to Mexico in 2003 and has since played in León.  The Tigres play their home games at Estadio León Raúl Rojas, which has a capacity of 7,000.
