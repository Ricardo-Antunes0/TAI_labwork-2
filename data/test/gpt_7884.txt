Sahana Udupa is a media anthropologist. She is the author of the critically acclaimed book Media Effects: A Social Theory of Media.  Udupa's work examines how media effects social and cultural phenomena, with a focus on the intersections of race, class, gender, sexuality, and national identity. Her book is seen as foundational in the field of media effects studies.  Udupa was born in Karnataka, India in 1971. She earned her B.A. from the University of Mysore in 1995 and her M.A. from Temple University in 1998. She completed her Ph.D. at New York University in 2006.
