Credit card hijacking is a form of credit card fraud in which a thief obtains unauthorized access to a victim's credit card account information, typically by stealing the victim's credit card number or authentication code.  Credit card hijacking occurs when a thief obtains unauthorized access to a victim's credit card account information, typically by stealing the victim's credit card number or authentication code. The thief then uses the stolen information to make purchases or withdraw cash from an ATM or bank account.   Credit card hijacking is a form of credit card fraud that can be costly for both the victim and the perpetrator. Victims can end up owing large amounts of money to the banks and other creditors who were victimized, and thieves can face criminal charges, including theft and identity theft.
