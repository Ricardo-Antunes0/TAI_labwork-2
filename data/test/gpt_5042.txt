10 to Midnight is a 1983 American slasher film directed by Wes Craven and written by John D. MacDonald. The film follows a group of teenagers who are stalked and killed by a masked killer after spending the night at a deserted carnival.  Released on October 10, 1983, 10 to Midnight was Wes Craven's directorial debut and the first film in the "Scream" series. The film received mixed reviews from critics, but has since developed a cult following. It was followed by four sequels: Scream 2 (1991), Scream 3 (1997), Scream 4 (2011), and Scream Queens (2015).  The film is set at a carnival during the night where a group of teenagers spend the night. The killer, who is masked and dressed in black, begins to stalk and kill them one by one. The teenagers try to figure out who the killer is before they are killed.
