The Democratic Unionist Party (DUP) is a conservative political party in the United Kingdom. It is one of the two major parties in Northern Ireland, alongside the Ulster Unionist Party. The DUP was founded on 10 October 1971 by Ian Paisley and Peter Robinson.  The DUP is a Protestant party and strongly opposes Irish republicanism and the Good Friday Agreement. The party has ten MPs in the House of Commons, making it the fourth largest party in the UK. The DUP is a member of the Conservative Party, and has been part of every coalition government since 2010.  The DUP's main policies are reducing taxation, reducing government spending, reducing immigration, and maintaining Northern Ireland's neutrality in both international affairs and the European Union.
