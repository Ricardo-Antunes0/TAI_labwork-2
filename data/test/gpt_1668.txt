J.D. Ryan was a Montana rancher and businessman who was the Republican nominee for United States Senate in 1994.  J.D. Ryan was born on April 2, 1942 in Helena, Montana. After graduating from high school, he worked as a cattleman on his family's ranch in Montana. In the early 1970s, Ryan began his own business, which manufactured custom-made cowboy boots and saddles. He also became involved in politics, serving as a county commissioner in Montana and as a member of the Montana House of Representatives. In 1984, Ryan was elected to the United States House of Representatives from Montana's at-large congressional district. He served in the House from 1985 to 1993, and was the chairman of the House Budget Committee during the last two years of his term.  In 1994, Ryan ran for the open United States Senate seat in Montana. He faced incumbent Democratic Senator Max Baucus, and won by a wide margin. Ryan served one term in the Senate from 1995 to 2003, and was an influential voice on fiscal issues during that time. He retired from active politics in 2003 and returned to his ranch in Montana. J.D. Ryan died on November 4, 2016 at the age of 78 after a long illness.
