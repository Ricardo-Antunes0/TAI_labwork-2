He also served as the Minister of Education from 1981 to 1989 and the Minister of Industry, Mining and Trade from 1989 to 1993.  Ghalibafian was born in 1935 in Tehran. He received a degree in engineering from Sharif University of Technology in 1957. Ghalibafian started his career as an engineer with the oil company British Petroleum in 1958. In 1978, he became the president of Iran's Olympic Committee.  In 1981, Ghalibafian was appointed Minister of Education under the administration of Prime Minister Mohammad Khatami. In 1989, he was appointed Minister of Industry, Mining and Trade under the administration of Prime Minister Mir-Hossein Mousavi. Ghalibafian was considered a reformist minister and helped revive Iran's economy during this time.  In 2003, Ghalibafian was elected president of Iran after defeating incumbent Mohammad Khatami in a landslide election. Ghalibafian's presidency was marked by tensions with the United States, which accused him of human rights abuses. He resigned from office on 17 June 2009 following protests against his government.  Ghalibafian died on 12 July 2016 at the age of 88 after a long illness.
