Malcom Mussen Lamoh is a Malaysian politician who has been the Member of the Parliament of Malaysia for the Kuching seat since 2019.  Malcom Mussen Lamoh was born on 26 March 1974 in Kampar, Perak, Malaysia. He is the eldest son of former Perak Chief Minister and Deputy Prime Minister, M. S. Lamoh. His father was also a Member of Parliament from the same seat from 1969 to 1981, and again from 1987 to 2001.   Malcom Mussen Lamoh attended Sekolah Menengah Kebangsaan Kampar (SMK) before proceeding to the University of Malaya where he obtained a Bachelor of Arts (B.A.) degree in 1995. He later earned a Master of Arts (M.A.) degree in Political Science from the University of Melbourne in Australia in 2001.  Malcom Mussen Lamoh began his career as a research officer with think tank, Institute for Democracy and Economic Affairs (IDEAS), before serving as an assistant editor with Malay daily newspaper Harian Metro from 2003 to 2004. He then became an assistant editor with The Star newspaper before being appointed as the news director for Sarawak Report, a Sarawak-based independent news outlet, in February 2006.
