Giovanni Francini (; born 3 August 1963) is an Italian conductor.  Born in Milan, Francini studied at the Milan Conservatory with Franco Ferrara and Franco Faccio. He made his debut as a conductor with the Teatro Regio di Parma in 1988, and from 1992 to 1993 he was assistant conductor of the Teatro Regio di Torino. He became principal conductor of the Teatro Regio di Parma in 1994, and held the post until 2003. From 2003 to 2009 he was principal conductor of the San Francisco Symphony Orchestra. He has also served as music director of the Royal Scottish National Orchestra (1998–2001), the Munich Philharmonic (2003–2009), and the Royal Opera House, London (2009–2012).  Francini has recorded many operas, including La traviata, Don Giovanni, La sonnambula, Tosca, Madama Butterfly, L'elisir d'amore, Cavalleria rusticana, Pagliacci and La Bohème.
