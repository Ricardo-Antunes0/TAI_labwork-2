Xenophontos monastery  is an Orthodox Christian monastery in the town of Kastoria, in the province of Ioannina, Greece. It is dedicated to Saint Xenophontos, a fourth-century monk who is venerated as a saint.   The monastery was founded in the early 14th century by monks from the Monastery of Saint John the Theologian on Mount Athos. It was damaged during the Turkish invasion of Greece in 1821, but was restored between 1827 and 1830 by Bishop Germanos of Patras. In 1923, during the Greco-Turkish War, it was damaged again and closed for a time.   In 1974, it was again damaged by fire and restored between 1975 and 1976 by Archbishop Iakovos of Athens with the financial support of Queen Elizabeth II. Today, it houses about fifty monks.
