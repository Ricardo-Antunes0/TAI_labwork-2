The Williamson County Jail, located at 105 South Houston Street in Georgetown, Texas, is the largest municipal jail in the United States. Opened in 1984, it has a designed capacity of 2,000 inmates.  The Williamson County Jail is home to the Texas Department of Criminal Justice's (TDCJ) Central Unit, which is responsible for supervising all TDCJ institutions statewide. The Williamson County Jail also houses the TDCJ's Regional Security Office (RSO), which provides security for TDCJ facilities throughout central and eastern Texas.
