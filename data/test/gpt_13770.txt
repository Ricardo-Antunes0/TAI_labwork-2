As One (; lit. "Korea") is a 2016 South Korean drama film directed by Lee Chang-dong. It was screened in the Un Certain Regard section at the 2016 Cannes Film Festival.  Ji-wook is a loner who works as a security guard at a music hall. One day, he is assigned to guard composer Yoon-hee, who is in the middle of a performance. Ji-wook befriends Yoon-hee and they start to fall in love with each other. However, their relationship is complicated by the fact that they are from two different social classes and the fact that Ji-wook is still mourning the death of his wife.   As One received mixed reviews from critics. On Rotten Tomatoes, the film has a rating of 58% based on reviews from 24 critics, with an average rating of 5/10.  The Times called it "a melancholic, beautifully shot work about isolation and love" while The Telegraph's Robbie Collin awarded it 4 out of 5 stars, calling it "an impressively accomplished and affecting film".
