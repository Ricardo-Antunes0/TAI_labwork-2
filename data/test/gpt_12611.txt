Richard Marin Scrushy (born August 1952) is an American businessman, who is the founder and former CEO of the healthcare services company AmeriHealth Caritas.  Scrushy was born in 1952 in Columbus, Mississippi. He received a Bachelor of Science degree from Tulane University in 1974. He also received an M.B.A. from the University of Michigan in 1977.  Scrushy founded AmeriHealth Caritas in 1983. The company provides healthcare services to low-income and uninsured people in the United States. In 2006, Forbes listed AmeriHealth Caritas as the sixth largest private healthcare provider in the United States, with annual revenue of $5.1 billion. Scrushy was named the CEO of AmeriHealth Caritas in 1994 and served in that role until his retirement in 2007. He was succeeded by James Hackett Jr.  Scrushy is a member of the National Academy of Medicine and a fellow of the American College of Surgeons. He has also been awarded honorary degrees from Southern Methodist University, Tulane University, and Xavier University.
