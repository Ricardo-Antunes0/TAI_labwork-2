Oxynoemacheilus anatolicus, the Burdur loach, is a species of fish in the family Cyprinidae. It is found in the eastern Mediterranean and Black Sea drainages. It prefers cool, fast-flowing waters with gravel and cobbles.  The Burdur loach is a small fish, up to 18 cm long. It has a slender body with a pointed snout and large eyes. The colour is variable but usually dirty grey or olive green, with black spots on the head and body. The fins are dark, with a white band around the edge. The loach feeds on small invertebrates and crustaceans, spawning in late spring or early summer.
