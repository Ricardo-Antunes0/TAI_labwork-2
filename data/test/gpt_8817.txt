Jhonen C. Vasquez (; born September 1, 1966) is an American comics writer, artist and film director. Vasquez is best known for his work on the comic book series Homestuck, which he co-created with Andrew Hussie.  Vasquez was born in Glendale, California, to Juan Vasquez and Linda Vasquez. He has two sisters, Jana and Janelle. He is of Mexican and Irish descent. Vasquez attended Claremont McKenna College in Claremont, California, where he studied political science and economics. He dropped out after one semester to pursue a career in animation and comics.   Vasquez's early work involved illustrating comic books such as "Batman: The Animated Series" and "The Sandman". Vasquez's first solo project was the short-lived comic book series "Johnny the Homicidal Maniac", which was published by Fantagraphics Books in 1994. The series was later collected into a trade paperback titled "Johnny the Homicidal Maniac: Special Edition" (2000).  In 1996, Vasquez began working with writer Andrew Hussie on the webcomic "Homestuck", which chronicled the adventures of a group of aliens who come to Earth to play video games and subsequently become embroiled in human society. The series ran for 10 years before concluding in March 2015. During that time, Vasquez also directed two episodes of the web series "Hussie & Dave: Megalo Box".
