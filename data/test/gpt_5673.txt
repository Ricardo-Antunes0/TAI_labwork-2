Madison Waterworks, also known as Nichols Station, is a historic waterworks located at Madison in Madison County, New York. It was built about 1837, and is a 2-story, gable roofed stone building on a raised basement. It measures 40 feet by 60 feet (12.19 m by 18.29 m).  The Madison Waterworks were built in 1837 by the Nichols family to provide drinking water to the burgeoning town of Madison. The Nichols Station served as the waterworks until 1848, when it was replaced by a larger facility. The building underwent a restoration in the early 1990s and now houses the Madison Historical Society.
