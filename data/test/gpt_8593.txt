John Barry Humphries  (born 17 February 1951) is an Australian actor, comedian, singer, and writer. He is best known for his work in television and film as a performer and voice artist. Humphries has also written scripts for television shows, including The Young Ones and Yes Minister.  Humphries was born in Sydney, Australia, the son of Evelyn (n√©e Meehan), a seamstress, and Barry Humphries Sr., a bus driver. His father was Irish and his mother was Australian. Humphries attended Newtown High School and studied drama at the University of New South Wales but dropped out to work in local touring companies.  In 1978, Humphries started appearing on Australian television shows such as The Mavis Bewley Show and The Mike Walsh Show. He first gained international recognition as the co-host of the 1980s British game show The Big Breakfast. In 1986, he starred as Ding Dongs creator Harry Enfield in the BBC sitcom The Young Ones. He also appeared in the Australian TV series The Adventures of Barry McKenzie which aired from 1988 to 1990 and in the US sitcom Yes Minister from 1992 to 1993.  Humphries has starred in a number of feature films, including Moonstruck (1987), Buster (1988), My Lucky Stars (1991), Charade (1993), The Cat's Meow (2001), and Rat Race (2002). In 2006, he starred in the role of Emperor Napoleon in Tim Burton's production of Sweeney Todd: The Demon Barber of Fleet Street
