The sand cat (Felis margarita), also known as the margarita cat or simply margarita, is a small wildcat of the family Felidae. It is found in the deserts of North Africa and the Middle East.  The sand cat is a small wildcat of the family Felidae. It is found in the deserts of North Africa and the Middle East. The sand cat has a long slender body with a short tail, and a long slender neck. It has a long pointed nose and rounded ears. The coat is sand coloured, but can vary to be almost any colour, including black, brown, grey, white and yellow. The sand cat is active during the day, but spends most of its time in burrows or under rocks. It feeds on small animals, such as rodents and lizards, as well as insects.
