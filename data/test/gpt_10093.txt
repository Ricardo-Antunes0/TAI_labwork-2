Matthew Alton Cartwright (born May 1, 1961) is an American actor and comedian. He is known for his roles on the sitcoms Ally McBeal (1997–2001) and The Office (2005–2013).  Cartwright was born in Glendale, California, to Arlene (née Vollmer), a registered nurse, and Gary Cartwright, a Marine Corps officer who later became a police officer. He has two sisters, Gina and Lisa. Cartwright grew up in Rocklin, California.  Cartwright attended Placer High School in Auburn, California and then studied theater at the University of California, Davis. After graduation, he moved to Los Angeles to pursue an acting career.  Cartwright's first role was as a cast member on the Fox sitcom Ally McBeal (1997–2001). He also had recurring roles on the NBC series The West Wing (1999–2000) and the ABC series Lost (2004). In 2005, he starred as Regional Manager Dwight Schrute on the NBC sitcom The Office. The show ran for nine seasons until 2013. In 2013, Cartwright guest-starred on the Fox sitcom New Girl as Coach Taylor. In 2014, he co-starred in the CBS sitcom Mom as Pete Campbell's father-in-law.  Cartwright is married to actress Jennifer Aniston; they have one child together.
