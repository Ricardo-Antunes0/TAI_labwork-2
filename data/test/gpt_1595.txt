Nicholas Alexander Folk (born November 5, 1984) is an American football quarterback for the Kansas City Chiefs of the National Football League (NFL). He was drafted by the Chiefs in the sixth round of the 2006 NFL Draft. Folk has also played for the Arizona Cardinals, Philadelphia Eagles, and Tampa Bay Buccaneers.  Folk attended Brookhaven High School in Jackson, Mississippi and played for the school's football team as a junior and senior. He was a three-time all-state selection, leading his team to consecutive state championships as a senior, and was also named to the All-America team by "USA Today". Folk was also a two-time first-team all-conference player and was rated as the third-best quarterback in the nation by Scout.com.  Folk played college football at Oklahoma State University, where he was a two-time starting quarterback for the Cowboys. He led Oklahoma State to an appearance in the BCS National Championship game against Florida State University, but was unsuccessful in leading his team to a victory. Folk finished his college career with a record of 33–10–1 as a starter, including a 24–3 mark in Big 12 Conference play.  Folk was drafted by the Chiefs in the sixth round of the 2006 NFL Draft. After not being considered for the starting quarterback position by head coach Herman Edwards early in his rookie season, Folk won the job after Edwards was fired midway through the 2007 season.
