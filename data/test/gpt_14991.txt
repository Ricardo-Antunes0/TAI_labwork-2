He was born in 1971 in Ibaraki Prefecture and is best known for his work on the manga series "Hunter x Hunter" and its anime adaptations.  Koji Kabuto debuted as a manga artist in the magazine "Weekly Shōnen Magazine" in 1994 with the short story "Mysterious Visitor". He went on to serialize the manga series "Hunter x Hunter" in "Shōnen Jump" from 1998 to 2004, followed by a series of one-shot manga titled "The Adventures of HxH: Greed Island" between 2004 and 2006. The anime adaptations of "Hunter x Hunter" were produced by Nippon Animation from 1999 to 2004 and by Madhouse from 2005 to 2012.  In addition to his work on "Hunter x Hunter", Kabuto has also written a number of manga series, including the supernatural horror series "Kuroshitsuji" and the science fiction series "Galaxy Railways". In 2010, he collaborated with Makoto Shinkai on the film adaptation of Shinkai's animated feature film "Your Name."
