Bell is a typeface that was designed by Edward Johnston in 1876. Johnston named the typeface after his friend, the Scottish bell-founder John Bell. Johnston designed Bell as a display typeface for the printing company Caslon. The font was first released in 1876 and has been used in printed materials ever since. Bell is also one of the most popular typefaces for digital applications, such as webfonts and mobile applications.
