The Gabriel Richard Building, also known as the Richard Building, is a historic commercial building in downtown Omaha, Nebraska. It was built in 1908 and is a prominent example of Beaux Arts architecture. It was named for Gabriel Richard, a Omaha businessman and politician.  The Gabriel Richard Building is located in the heart of Omaha's business district, on the south side of Broadway near Douglas Street. It is a large three-story brick building, with a terra cotta facade and a classical roofline. The building has a symmetrical front facade, with two large arched windows and an elaborate entrance loggia. The interior features marble floors and walls, ornate plasterwork, and a grand central atrium. The Gabriel Richard Building was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1982.
