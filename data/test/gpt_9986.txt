The Inta Corrective Labor Camp (Intalag) (, literally "Inside the Camp") is a concentration camp located in the Suwałki Region of north-central Poland, operated by the Ministry of Interior since 2002. It is one of three concentration camps in Poland, the other two being Stutthof concentration camp and Auschwitz concentration camp.  The Intalag was established in 2002 to replace the former Wadowice Labor Camp, which had been closed in 2001. The Intalag is located about west of the city of Suwałki and about south of the regional capital Toruń. The camp has an area of and a population of 7,500 inmates, including approximately 1,500 prisoners who are HIV positive.
