Sue Anschutz-Rodgers (born 1936) is an American businesswoman, philanthropist, and socialite who is the widow of Phil Anschutz, co-founder of the Los Angeles Lakers.  Sue Anschutz-Rodgers was born in 1936 in Los Angeles, California, the daughter of businessman Philip Anschutz and his wife, actress Judy Rodgers. She is the sister of business executive David Anschutz. She married Phil Anschutz in 1962 and they had two children together: Phil Jr. (born 1965) and Lisa (born 1967). The couple divorced in 1978.  In 1981, Sue Anschutz-Rodgers married businessman and film producer Jerry Bruckheimer. They had one child together, Chad (born 1983). The couple divorced in 1990.  Since her husband's death in 2002, Sue Anschutz-Rodgers has been a major benefactor of the arts and charitable causes. She has donated millions of dollars to a number of causes, including support for AIDS research and education, homeless shelters and cancer research. In 2006 she was awarded an honorary doctorate from Brown University.
