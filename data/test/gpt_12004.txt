Marvel's Daredevil, or simply Daredevil, is an American superhero television series developed by F. Gary Gray and Drew Goddard for Marvel Television, based on the Marvel Comics character of the same name. The series stars Charlie Cox, Vincent D'Onofrio, Deborah Ann Woll, Elden Henson, Rosario Dawson, and Jon Bernthal. It is set in the fictional city of New York City, and follows Matt Murdock (Cox), a blind lawyer who fights crime using his superhuman senses and martial arts training.  The series was first announced in March 2014, with Goddard attached as showrunner and Gray executive producing. Filming began in October 2014 in New York City. Daredevil was released on Netflix on April 10, 2016. The first season received positive reviews from critics, with many noting Cox's performance as the standout. A second season was released on March 18, 2018.  Daredevil is a Marvel Comics character who first appeared in 1964 in The Amazing Spider-Man #129. He is a costumed vigilante who fights crime using his superhuman senses and martial arts training. Daredevil has been adapted to television several times, most notably as a starring role in the 2003 television series starring Ben Affleck. In 2013, Marvel Television developed a new series for Netflix with Drew Goddard as showrunner and Charlie Cox as Daredevil.
