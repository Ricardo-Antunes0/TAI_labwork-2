Joseph Saulter is an American musician, actor, and singer. Saulter was born in Louisville, Kentucky, the son of actress and singer Joanne Woodward and actor Gene Saulter. He began his career as a child actor in the late 1950s and early 1960s appearing in several television series, including The Andy Griffith Show, The Beverly Hillbillies, Gunsmoke, and Peyton Place. Saulter's first major role came in the 1967 film The Graduate, for which he was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor. He later appeared in several successful films, including Easy Rider (1969), American Graffiti (1973), The Towering Inferno (1974), and Apocalypse Now (1979). Saulter also had notable roles in the television series M*A*S*H, Quincy, M.E., and Wings. In 1982, he released his solo album Joseph Saulter.
