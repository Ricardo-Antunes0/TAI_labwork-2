Diamond is the debut Japanese studio album by 4Minute. It was released on June 9, 2009, by J Records and distributed by Avex Trax.  The album was produced by Japanese singer-songwriter and record producer Tetsuya Komuro, who had worked with the group previously on their 2007 single "Blue Moon". The album's lyrical content is about youth, love, and relationships.  Diamond charted at number one on the Oricon Albums Chart, selling over 265,000 copies in its first week. It has since been certified platinum by the Recording Industry Association of Japan (RIAJ). "Diamond" received positive reviews from music critics, who praised Komuro's production and 4Minute's vocal abilities. The album was nominated for Best Japanese Album at the 2010 Japan Gold Disc Awards.  4Minute was formed in 2006 by three members of girl group J Soul Brothers: Seungri, Junsu, and Daehyun. They released their debut single "Blue Moon" in 2007 and followed it up with the double A-side single "Forever"/"Good Luck". Tetsuya Komuro, a well-known Japanese singer-songwriter and record producer, worked with the group on their single "Diamond" and produced their debut album "Diamond".  "Diamond" is a pop album with elements of R&B and soul. The group's vocals are often compared to those of American singers Britney Spears and Christina Aguilera.
