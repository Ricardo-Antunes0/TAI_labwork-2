The Nathaniel Hempstead House, also known as the Hempstead-Nathaniel House, is a historic house at 210 Washington Street in the West Village of Manhattan, New York City.  The house was built in 1783 for Nathaniel Hempstead, a merchant and politician who served as the Mayor of New York City in 1792. It is a fine example of Federal period architecture, with a symmetrical facade and brick veneer. The property was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1970.
