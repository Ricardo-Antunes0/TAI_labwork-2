Rudolf Lothar [rú:dolf ló:tar] (born Rudolf Lothar Schönerer, October 8, 1884 – May 1, 1945) was an Austro-Hungarian general who commanded the XIV Panzer Corps in World War II. He is also known for his role in the April 9, 1945 assassination of Adolf Hitler.  Rudolf Lothar was born in Vienna, Austria-Hungary on October 8, 1884. He was the son of a wealthy industrialist and politician, and joined the Austro-Hungarian Army in 1904. Lotharserved in World War I and attained the rank of captain. In 1938, he was appointed commander of the XIV Panzer Corps.  On April 9, 1945, Lothar led a group of Nazis who assassinated Adolf Hitler at his headquarters in Berlin. He was later captured by the Allies and executed by hanging on May 1, 1945.
