Geordie  are a British rock band, formed in Newcastle upon Tyne in 1980. The band has released six studio albums, two compilation albums, one live album and one extended play.  The band was formed in Newcastle upon Tyne in 1980 by Gary Clements (vocals, guitar), Phil Collen (guitar), Alan Hunter (bass) and Mick Hutton (drums). After playing local clubs and festivals, the band signed to EMI Records and released their first album, "First Impression", in 1981. The album reached number eight on the UK Albums Chart and spawned the hit single "Love In Vain".  The follow-up album, "Toys In The Attic", was released in 1982 and reached number two on the UK Albums Chart. The singles "Heat Of The Moment" and "Rock 'N' Roll Kids" both reached number one on the UK Singles Chart. Geordie went on to tour worldwide and release three more albums – "Big City Nights" (1983), "Soldier" (1985) and "Out Of Order" (1987) – before splitting up in 1989. Clements, Collen and Hunter subsequently formed Slipknot.  Geordie reformed in 2003 and released their sixth studio album, "Hits", that year. The album charted at number 47 on the UK Albums Chart.
