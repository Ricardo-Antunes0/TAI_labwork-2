Michel Léveillé is a Canadian ice hockey player.  Léveillé was born in Montreal, Quebec, on February 17, 1988. He played major junior ice hockey with the Halifax Mooseheads of the Quebec Major Junior Hockey League (QMJHL). He was drafted by the New York Islanders in the sixth round (162nd overall) of the 2005 NHL Entry Draft. Léveillé made his professional debut with the Islanders' American Hockey League (AHL) affiliate, the Bridgeport Sound Tigers, in 2005–06. He was traded to the Toronto Maple Leafs on January 8, 2007, in exchange for future considerations. Léveillé scored his first NHL goal on October 3, 2007, against goaltender Pascal Leclaire of the Montreal Canadiens.  Léveillé was a key player on the Maple Leafs' 2007 Stanley Cup championship team. He scored two goals and five assists in 20 playoff games. Léveillé finished the 2007–08 season with 10 goals and 27 assists for 37 points in 81 games.  Léveillé suffered a concussion in a game against the Detroit Red Wings on December 5, 2009.   Léveillé signed as a free agent with HC Sochi of the Kontinental Hockey League (KHL) on July 1, 2010.
