The Marcus Wallenberg Prize is the highest honour bestowed by the Swedish Academy, the country's highest cultural institution. The Prize was first awarded in 1948 and is awarded annually to a person who has made an outstanding contribution to humanity through art, literature, science or humanitarian work.  Marcus Wallenberg (1888-1946) was a Swedish businessman and philanthropist who saved thousands of Jews during World War II. He is considered one of Sweden's most important figures of the 20th century.
