Album Number Two is the second studio album by American singer and songwriter Taylor Swift. It was released on October 27, 2006, by Big Machine Records. The album was produced by Swift and Nathan Chapman.  The album's title is a reference to the Beatles' album Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band. Swift wrote all of the album's songs with the intention of creating an "epic" record. She worked on the album for more than two years and produced it with help from several collaborators.  "Album Number Two" has a more mature sound than its predecessor, 2006's "Taylor Swift". It contains lyrical themes of love, loss, and heartbreak. The album debuted at number one on the US Billboard 200 chart and has sold over 5 million copies in the United States. It has been certified platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA). "Album Number Two" received generally positive reviews from music critics, who praised its production and lyrical content.  Swift embarked on a world tour in support of "Album Number Two" in 2007. The tour grossed over $125 million and received critical acclaim. In December 2016, she announced her sixth studio album, "Reputation", which was released on November 10, 2017.
