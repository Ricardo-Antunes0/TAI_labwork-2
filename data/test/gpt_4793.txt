Puig de Massanella is the second highest mountain peak in the Pyrenees, after Aneto. It is situated in the comarca of Alt Empordà, in Catalonia, Spain.  Puig de Massanella is a pyramidal peak of granite and quartzite, measuring 2,848 m above sea level. It is located about 10 km north-northwest of the village of Puigcerdà, within the Alt Empordà National Park. The summit of Puig de Massanella is accessible by a steep and narrow path which leads to its north face. The peak is topped by an ancient sanctuary dedicated to St. Michael the Archangel, which was destroyed by the French in 1809.
