Colleen Mae Ballinger (born November 21, 1986) is an American actress, singer and dancer. She is known for her roles as Candace Cameron Bure in the Nickelodeon sitcom Bunsen Honeydew and its spin-off, Drake & Josh, as well as her breakout performance as Effie White in the Broadway revival of The Color Purple. Ballinger has also starred in the films The Internship (2013), The Fault in Our Stars (2014) and Set It Up (2011).  Ballinger was born in Naperville, Illinois, to a family of actors. She has two older sisters. Ballinger's mother is an actress and singer, and her father is a retired military officer and restaurant owner. Ballinger began dancing at the age of three and started acting in school plays at the age of six. When she was twelve, she won a role on the Nickelodeon show Drake & Josh. Ballinger was subsequently cast in the role of Candace Cameron Bure on Bunsen Honeydew, which aired from 2004 to 2008. In 2013, Ballinger reprised her role as Candace on Drake & Josh spin-off, Drake & Josh: International House.   In 2014, Ballinger starred as Hazel Grace Lancaster in the film The Fault in Our Stars alongside Shailene Woodley and Ansel Elgort.
