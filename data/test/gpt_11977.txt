Anne Clarissa Eden, Countess of Avon (; born 1 March 1955) is a British author and journalist. Born in London, she was educated at the University of Oxford, where she read Philosophy, Politics and Economics.  Eden began her career as a journalist with the "Sunday Times" in 1978. She moved to "The Observer" in 1984, where she was political editor from 1988 to 1995. She has since written for "The Guardian", "The Independent", "The New York Times", "Le Monde" and other publications.  Eden's first novel, "The House of Mirth", was published in 1997 and won the Somerset Maugham Award. Her second novel, "The Zookeeper's Wife", was published in 2005 and won the Orange Prize for Fiction. Her third novel, "Burnt Norton", was published in 2009 and won the James Tait Black Memorial Prize. Her fourth novel, "Persuasion", was published in 2012 and won the Costa Book of the Year Award.  Eden is married to the historian Simon Schama, with whom she has two children.
