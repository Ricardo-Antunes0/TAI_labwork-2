The Aurora Subdivision or Aurora Sub is a subdivision of the City of Aurora, Colorado. It is located in the north-central part of the city, bounded by 16th Street on the south, York Street on the west, Cottage Grove Avenue on the north, and Quebec Street on the east. The subdivision contains a mix of single-family homes and apartment buildings.  The Aurora Subdivision was developed in the early 1950s. At that time, it was one of the first subdivisions in Aurora. The subdivision is named after the Aurora Borealis, which can be seen from many of its streets.
