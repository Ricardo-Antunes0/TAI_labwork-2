Francis Joseph "Frank" Scaturro (born July 26, 1947) is a retired American professional baseball player and manager. He played in Major League Baseball (MLB) for the Baltimore Orioles, Cleveland Indians, and Chicago White Sox from 1969 to 1985. A switch hitter, he was a three-time All-Star and won two Gold Glove Awards.  Scaturro was known as a patient hitter who made good use of all parts of the strike zone. He finished his MLB career with a .262 batting average, 3,059 hits, 1,062 runs batted in (RBIs), and 49 home runs. After retiring as a player, he served as the Orioles' bench coach from 1989 to 1992 and as their manager from 1993 to 1995. He was then the Cleveland Indians' hitting coach from 1996 to 1998 before becoming the White Sox' manager in 1999.  In 2002, Scaturro became the first manager in MLB history to be fired after his team had won its division championship. He was replaced by Terry Francona of the Boston Red Sox.  Scaturro was born on July 26, 1947, in Mount Carmel, Pennsylvania. He attended Mount Carmel High School where he played baseball and basketball. He signed with the Baltimore Orioles as an amateur free agent in 1966 and made his debut with the team that season. During his MLB career, Scaturro played for the Orioles (1969–1985), Cleveland Indians (1986–1988), Chicago White Sox (1989–1995), and Seattle Mariners
