Edward Jeffrey Irving Ardizzone,  (16 October 1932 – 2 December 2002) was an American composer, arranger, conductor and music educator.  Born in New York City, Ardizzone studied at the Juilliard School of Music with Aaron Copland and Leonard Bernstein. He then moved to London, where he worked as an arranger and conductor with the Royal Philharmonic Orchestra. In the 1970s he founded the Ardizzone Chamber Music Series and served as its director until his death.  Ardizzone's compositions include orchestral works, chamber music, choral works, and solo pieces. His best-known work is the opera "The Consul", which was first performed in 1974. He also wrote songs for performers such as Barbra Streisand and Plácido Domingo.
