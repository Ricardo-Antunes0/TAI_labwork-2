Susana Lanteri (8 May 1935 – 5 October 2016) was an Italian actress, singer and dancer. Lanteri was born in Rome, the daughter of the film director Luchino Visconti and his wife, the actress Isotta Nogara. She made her acting debut at the age of 16 in the film Il gattopardo (1958), and became well known for her roles in films such as La Dolce Vita (1960), The Damned (1961), Fellini's 8½ (1963), The Leopard (1963), Death in Venice (1971), and Apocalypse Now (1979). She also starred in several successful musicals, including Gianni Schicchi (1962), La Cage aux Folles (1978), and My Night at Maud's (1982). Lanteri retired from acting in the early 1990s, but resumed her career in 2003. She died on 5 October 2016, aged 85.
