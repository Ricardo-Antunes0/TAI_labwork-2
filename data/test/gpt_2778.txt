Thayer David (born David Thayer Hersey; March 20, 1919 â€“ December 7, 2006) was an American novelist and screenwriter.  David was born in Boston, Massachusetts to Alfred Hersey, a physician and surgeon, and his wife, the former Dorothea Kent. He was educated at the Hotchkiss School and then at Harvard College. After serving in the United States Navy during World War II, David began writing fiction. His first novel, "The Hill" (1956), won the National Book Award. David's other novels include "The Far Side of the World" (1961), "The Catcher in the Rye" (1951), and "Death of a Salesman" (1949). He also wrote screenplays for such films as "On The Beach" (1959), "The Towering Inferno" (1974), and "The English Patient" (1996).   David lived in Westport, Connecticut, until his death there at age 87.
