The salamuri  is a Georgian, recorder-like instrument with six strings. It has a pear-shaped body made of wood or bone, and a curved neck. The strings are played with the fingers or a plectrum.  The salamuri is a member of the luri family of instruments, which also includes the duduk and the kuduk. The name salamuri is derived from the Georgian word "sala" meaning "gigantic", and "muri" meaning "wind". The salamuri is used in Georgian traditional music and is also sometimes used in contemporary music.
