Cincius, whose praenomen was likely Lucius and nomen Lucius, was a senator of the Roman Republic. He was consul in 208 BC.  Cincius may have been a member of the gens Cincius.  Cincius is mentioned in the Fasti Capitolini as one of the senators who offered prayers when the temple of Jupiter Optimus Maximus was dedicated in 204 BC. He also served as praetor in 191 BC and as consul in 208 BC.
