Benjamin Thomas "Tony" Atkins (August 26, 1968 – January 7, 2006) was an American singer, songwriter, and record producer.  Atkins was born in New York City. He started his career as a backing vocalist for the rock band Journey. After a few years he began to write and record his own material. His debut album, "Life", was released in 1995. The album peaked at number 13 on the "Billboard" 200 and spawned two singles, "Only the Strong Survive" and "Who's Crying Now". Atkins later released two more albums, "Unleashed" (1998) and "Soul Survivor" (2001). He died from pancreatic cancer in 2006 at the age of 42.
