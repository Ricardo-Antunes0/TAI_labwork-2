The Wadden Sea ( ; ;  Dutch: Waddenzee) is a body of water in the Netherlands, Germany and Denmark.   It is the largest coastal plain on the mainland of Europe, covering an area of 1,351 km2 (537 sq mi). The coastline is largely sandy and flat, with a few small hills. It has a mean depth of only 12 m (39 ft). The water is very clean and clear, with a temperature range of 10–18 °C (50–64 °F). The main rivers flowing into the Wadden Sea are the Meuse, Weser, Elbe and Rhine.
