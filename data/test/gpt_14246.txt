Edward Joseph Daly (November 22, 1922 â€“ May 14, 1991) was an American politician who served as the 41st Governor of Colorado from 1971 to 1979 and as a U.S. Senator from 1979 to 1981.  Daly was born in Chicago, Illinois, on November 22, 1922, the son of Edward Joseph Daly, Sr., a carpenter and laborer, and the former Margaret Mahon. He grew up in the Near West Side neighborhood of Chicago. Daly attended St. Ignatius College Preparatory School and then Notre Dame University in Notre Dame, Indiana, where he played football under legendary coach Frank Leahy.   He served in the United States Air Force during World War II, then returned to Notre Dame and graduated with a B.A. in political science in 1948. He married the former Patricia Anne Cullinan on May 17, 1949. Daly then moved to Colorado Springs where he began his political career by serving as a city councilman from 1952 to 1954. In 1968, he was elected governor of Colorado on a platform of reform, becoming the first Democrat to be elected governor since Robert W. Teller had been elected in 1938. He was re-elected in 1970 and 1978. Daly left office after being defeated for re-election by Republican Dick Lamm in 1980.  Daly served one term in the U.S. Senate before returning to private business pursuits and later resumed his political career as Chairman of the Democratic National Committee from 1987 to 1991.
