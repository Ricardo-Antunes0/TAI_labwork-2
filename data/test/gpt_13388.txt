He studied at the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM) where he obtained a degree in Music Theory and Composition in 1973.  He then went on to study at the University of Utah, where he obtained an M.A. in 1975 and a D.M.A. in 1976, both in Composition. He also studied with Luigi Nono at the Accademia Nazionale di Santa Cecilia in Rome, Italy from 1975 to 1977.  After returning to Mexico, Rosal taught composition at UNAM from 1978 to 1999, and served as Director of the UNAM Institute for Contemporary Arts from 1999 to 2003.  He died from leukemia on 22 March 2006 in Mexico City at the age of 59.  Rosal's compositions are highly experimental, drawing on a wide range of styles including classical, contemporary, electronic, and popular music. He has been called "one of Mexico's most important composers" and his work has been performed around the world.
