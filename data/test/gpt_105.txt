Born in Cologne, Meyer studied at the University of Bonn and then at the University of Berlin, where he became a professor in 1868. He was a member of the Prussian Academy of Sciences and the Royal Academy of London.  Meyer is most famous for his work on Homer, in particular his editions of the Iliad and the Odyssey. He also wrote on Latin grammar and poetry.
