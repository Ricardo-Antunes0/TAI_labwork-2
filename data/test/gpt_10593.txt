Igloo House is a heritage-listed dwelling located at 110-116 Northumberland Street, in the inner city Sydney suburb of The Rocks, in the City of Sydney local government area of New South Wales, Australia. It was designed by James Barnet and built from 1876 to 1877. It is also known as Igloo House (1876-1977) and The Rocks Igloo. It was added to the New South Wales State Heritage Register on 2 April 1999.  Igloo House is a substantial, two storey, weatherboard dwelling with a hipped roof and a gabled front verandah. The building is laid out in a U-shape with an entrance on the eastern side and a kitchen at the rear. The interior has large rooms with double doors opening onto the verandah and high ceilings.
