Edwin Ume-Ezeoke was born in 1935 in Oguta, Nigeria. He received a B.A. from the University of Ibadan in 1957 and a M.A. from the University of London in 1961. He also holds a D.Phil. from Oxford University.  From 1966 to 1969, Ume-Ezeoke served as a Foreign Service Officer with the Nigerian Diplomatic Service. From 1969 to 1971, he served as Deputy Director for the Nigerian Office of Economic and Social Affairs. From 1971 to 1977, he served as Nigeria's Ambassador to Egypt, Ethiopia, and Somalia.  Ume-Ezeoke became Secretary-General of the United Nations on February 5, 2003, succeeding Kofi Annan. He served until his retirement on November 30, 2007. In this capacity, he presided over many landmark events including the Iraq War and Hurricane Katrina.  He died on February 5, 2009 at the age of 75 after a long illness.
