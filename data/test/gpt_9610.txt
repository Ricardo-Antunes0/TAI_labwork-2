He began writing plays while still in school, and joined the Egyptian Writers Union in 1938. He served as its secretary-general from 1968 to 1970.  Elzein is best known for his plays depicting the struggles of the Egyptian people against the British occupation of Egypt during World War II. His most famous play, "The Return", was written in 1965 and won the prestigious Al-Fajr Award in 1966. The play was later made into a successful movie by the same name, which was released in 1978.  Elzein also wrote political commentary for newspapers and magazines. He served on the editorial boards of several publications, including "Al-Ahram" and "Al-Masry al-Youm".  Mohyeldin Elzein died on October 9, 2002 at the age of 88.
