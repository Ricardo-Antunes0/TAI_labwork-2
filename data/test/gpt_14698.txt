After studying at the University of Minnesota, he worked as a jazz guitarist in the early 1950s with the Woody Herman Orchestra and with Lloyd Glenn and His Orchestra. He led his own groups from the late 1950s until his death in 1988.  Fortune composed music for films and television, including "The Apartment" (1960), "The Nun's Story" (1959), and "The Towering Inferno" (1974). He also wrote arrangements for the jazz saxophonist Dexter Gordon.
