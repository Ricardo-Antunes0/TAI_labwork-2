Norah Simpson (5 July 1895 – 19 February 1969) was an Australian artist, sculptor and writer.  Born in 1895 in Adelaide, South Australia, Norah Simpson studied at the South Australian School of Art from 1913 to 1915. She moved to Melbourne in 1916 to study at the Victorian School of Arts and Crafts under Arthur Streeton. In 1919 she travelled to Europe, where she studied at the Académie Julian in Paris and the Royal Academy of Arts in London.  Simpson returned to Melbourne in 1920 and established a studio in Richmond. She exhibited her work at the National Gallery of Victoria, the Melbourne Institute of Contemporary Art and other galleries, and was awarded several prizes, including the Charles Keulman Prize in 1930. In 1935 she was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society of Victoria.  Simpson died in 1969 in Melbourne. Her works are held by various galleries and museums, including the National Gallery of Victoria, the Art Gallery of New South Wales and the Tasmanian Museum and Art Gallery.
