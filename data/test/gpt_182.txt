Viva Hate is the debut solo studio album by English singer-songwriter Morrissey. It was released on 2 April 1987 by record label Smiths Records.  The album was recorded between 1986 and 1987, following the dissolution of the band The Smiths. Viva Hate was Morrissey's first album with new band members, replacing guitarist Johnny Marr and bassist Andy Rourke with Bernard Butler and Mike Joyce, respectively.  The album is a mixture of pop, rock, and indie music, and lyrically it explores themes of racism, homophobia, and self-hatred. Upon its release, Viva Hate received widespread critical acclaim; it has since been cited as one of the greatest debut albums of all time. It has sold over 1 million copies worldwide.
