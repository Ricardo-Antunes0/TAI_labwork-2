Zhao Shiyan (; 13 April 1901 - 16 December 1991) was a Chinese Communist revolutionary and politician. He served as the Chairman of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC) from 1958 to 1969.  Zhao Shiyan was born in a peasant family in the town of Zhaoji, Henan province, on 13 April 1901. After graduating from high school, he worked as a teacher in his hometown. In 1925, he joined the Chinese Communist Party (CCP). He rose through the ranks of the CCP and became a member of its Central Committee in 1937.  In 1938, Zhao Shiyan was appointed head of the propaganda department of the Guangdong provincial government. In 1958, Zhao Shiyan was elected Chairman of the CPPCC, serving until his retirement in 1969. During his tenure as Chairman, Zhao Shiyan played an important role in consolidating power within the CCP and improving relations with other countries in East Asia. He died on 16 December 1991 at the age of 87.
