She was the daughter of the barrister and Liberal politician Sir Edward Osborne and his wife Edith Bernal. She attended the Camberwell School of Art from 1924 to 1926, studying under Henry Tonks.  Blake began her career as a sculptor in the 1930s, exhibiting her work at the Royal Academy, London and at the Venice Biennale. She also wrote poetry and published two novels, "The Journey" (1948) and "The Well" (1962).  In 1951 she married the painter Anthony Blunt. They had two children, a son and a daughter. The couple divorced in 1965. In 1981 she married the photographer David Elliott. She died in 1988 in London.
