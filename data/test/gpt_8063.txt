The Thomas Iron Company was a major American industrial company founded in 1875 by Thomas Edison and his brother-in-law, George Westinghouse.  The company made a wide variety of products, including railroad rails and locomotives, electric light bulbs, and air conditioning systems. In 1901, it merged with the American Steel Company to form the U.S. Steel Corporation. The company became a public company in 1907. It was acquired by Bethlehem Steel in 1980 and ceased operations in 2002.
