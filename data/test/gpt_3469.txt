The Sædinge Runestone or DR 217 is a runestone located in Sædinge, Sweden. It is classified as being in the Rundata classification system as Runestone S12. The inscription on the stone is a runic text that tells of King Harald Finehair's victory over the Geats at the Battle of Hjörungavágr.  The inscription on the Sædinge Runestone was carved in the late 10th century by a Viking chieftain named Ragnvaldr. The inscription tells of King Harald Finehair's victory over the Geats at the Battle of Hjörungavágr. The runestone was originally located in the parish of Sädinge, but was moved to its current location in 1841.
