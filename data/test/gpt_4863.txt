Jane Dee Hull (; August 8, 1935 – May 8, 2013) was an American actress and singer who starred in the 1960s sitcom The Dick Van Dyke Show.  Hull was born in Los Angeles, California, the daughter of Dorothy (née Koop), a schoolteacher, and Harry Hull, an electrician. After her parents divorced, she was raised by her mother. She graduated from Fremont High School in Fremont, California. Hull studied at the American Academy of Dramatic Arts in New York City.  Hull made her Broadway debut in 1959 in the play "The Girls Against the Boys". She later appeared in the Broadway productions of "The World of Suzie Wong" (1963), "The Odd Couple" (1968), and "Promises, Promises" (1971).  Hull made her television debut on the CBS series "The Dick Van Dyke Show" in 1963. She played Jane Van Dyke, the wife of Van Dyke's character Dick Van Dyke. The show ran for six seasons from 1963 to 1968. Hull also appeared on "The Carol Burnett Show", "The Ed Sullivan Show", and "The Tonight Show Starring Johnny Carson".
