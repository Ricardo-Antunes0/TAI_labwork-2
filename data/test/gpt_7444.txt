Santos "Sandy" Alomar Conde Sr. (; ; October 10, 1943 – March 17, 2002) was an American baseball player, manager and executive. He played in Major League Baseball as a catcher for the Cleveland Indians (1967–1970), Baltimore Orioles (1970–1972), Toronto Blue Jays (1972), Philadelphia Phillies (1973), and Chicago White Sox (1973). He was elected to the Baseball Hall of Fame in 2006.  Alomar was born in San Pedro de Macoris, Dominican Republic, to Santos Alomar, Sr. and Flor Melendez. In 1960, he joined his family in the United States. Alomar attended Bishop Neumann High School in Philadelphia, where he played baseball and basketball. After graduating from high school, Alomar attended the University of Miami on a basketball scholarship and played for the Miami Hurricanes men's basketball team from 1962 to 1965.  Alomar was drafted by the Cleveland Indians in the first round of the 1965 Major League Baseball draft. He made his major league debut with the Indians on April 6, 1967. He spent four seasons with Cleveland before being traded to the Baltimore Orioles on January 12, 1970 for Jim Beattie and $50,000. After one season with Baltimore, Alomar was traded to the Toronto Blue Jays on November 10, 1971 for cash and Jerry Hairston Jr..
