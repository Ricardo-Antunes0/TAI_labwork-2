Beat Zehnder (born 9 January 1966) is a German football manager and a former player. He is currently the manager of Bayern Munich II.  Zehnder started his career in the lower leagues with Borussia Neunkirchen, before moving to the top flight with Bayer Leverkusen in 1990. He played for Bayer Leverkusen, Werder Bremen, Schalke 04, Hannover 96 and Bayern Munich, winning the German championship with Bayern in 1996 and the UEFA Champions League in 1998. Zehnder retired from playing professional football in 2001.  Zehnder began his coaching career with Bayer Leverkusen's reserve team in 2002, before being appointed head coach of the under-19 team in 2003. Zehnder resigned from his role as head coach of Germany on 15 November 2007 after a disappointing Euro 2008 qualifying campaign.
