Pam's Delight is an English cultivar of the apple. It is a cross between the Newtown Pippin and the Braeburn apple.  The Pam's Delight apple was bred in the UK by Norman Martin of the Martin Brothers Nursery in Newtown, Powys, Wales in the early 1990s. The tree was selected for its resistance to scab and a good flavor. The tree was released in 1995 and has been commercially available since 2000.  The Pam's Delight apple is a medium-sized apple with a yellow-green skin and a red or orange flesh. The flavor is tart and sweet with a slightly syrupy texture. It is highly rated for its crisp, juicy, and sweet-tart flavor.
