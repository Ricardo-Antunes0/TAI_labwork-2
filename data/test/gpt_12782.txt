He also served as the Chairman of the African Union from 2005 to 2007.  Mwikuta was born in Mwanza, Tanganyika in 1941. He attended local schools before studying at Makerere University, graduating with a degree in political science in 1966. He then worked as a teacher before becoming active in politics.  Mwikuta first became involved in politics when he joined the Tanganyika African National Union (TANU), serving as its treasurer from 1967 to 1970. He later became deputy secretary-general of TANU, serving from 1974 to 1976. In 1980, he became the party's president.  In March 1992, Mwikuta was appointed Prime Minister of Tanzania by President Julius Nyerere following the resignation of Ali Mohamed Shein. He served in this position until 1995, when he was replaced by Benjamin Mkapa.   In 2000, Mwikuta was appointed Chairman of the African Union (AU), replacing Jean Ping. He served in this position until 2007, when he was replaced by Nkosazana Dlamini-Zuma.   Mwikuta died on 3 December 2017 at the age of 80 after a long illness.
