HMS Carysfort was a  light cruiser of the Royal Navy, launched on 25 January 1914. Commissioned in May 1914, she served during World War I in the North Sea and the Mediterranean. She was torpedoed and sunk on 22 November 1917 by the German submarine U-21. Carysfort was the first British warship to be sunk by a U-boat.  HMS Carysfort was laid down at the Laird Shipbuilding Company, Birkenhead, on 25 January 1914 and launched on 25 January 1914. She was commissioned into the Royal Navy on 5 May 1914.
