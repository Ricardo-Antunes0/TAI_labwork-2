The AIM-97 Seekbat or XAIM-97A Seek Bat is an American ground-launched cruise missile designed to be used by the United States Air Force. The Seekbat is produced by Boeing Defense, Space and Security.  The AIM-97 Seekbat was first flown in 1990 and entered service in 1995. The Seekbat is a two-stage, solid-fueled missile with a range of about 250 kilometers (155 miles) and a speed of about Mach 2. It can be carried by the A-10 Thunderbolt II and F-15E Strike Eagle aircraft.
