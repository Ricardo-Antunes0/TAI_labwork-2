The Byzantine economy was among the most advanced in the world during the Middle Ages. Its economic system was characterized by a complex network of markets, guilds, and merchant associations. The Byzantine economy was based on the principles of private property and free enterprise. Foreign merchants were welcomed and were able to trade freely with Constantinople, despite the presence of strong monopolies.
