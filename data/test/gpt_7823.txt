He served as Premier of China from 1949 to 1955.  Wan Fulin was born in Wuxi, Jiangsu province, on 20 November 1880. After studying at Nanking University and the University of Wuhan, he worked as a teacher before joining the revolutionary movement in 1911. He became a member of the Chinese Communist Party in 1920 and soon rose through its ranks, becoming its general secretary in 1943. As party leader, Wan Fulin helped to consolidate Mao Zedong's power within the CCP and advance the party's agenda of socialism through communism. He served as Premier of China from 1949 to 1955, presiding over significant economic development and social progress during this time. Wan Fulin died on 10 October 1951 at the age of 77 after a long illness.
