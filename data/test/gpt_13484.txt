Jon Adams is an American folk musician, singer, songwriter, and guitarist. He is best known as the lead vocalist and guitarist for the punk rock band the Dwarves. Born in Santa Cruz, California, Adams began playing guitar at a young age, influenced by folk and punk rock artists such as Bob Dylan and Joe Strummer. He formed the Dwarves in 1986 with drummer Brendan Canty and bassist Dave Pirner, and released their debut album, This Is Not a Test, in 1989. Adams' lyrics are often political in nature, dealing with subjects such as social injustice, war, and environmentalism. He has released seven studio albums with the Dwarves, as well as two solo albums. In addition to his work with the Dwarves, Adams has played guitar on albums by such artists as The Black Keys, The Decemberists, and The Flaming Lips.
