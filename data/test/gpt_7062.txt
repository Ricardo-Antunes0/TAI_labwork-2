St Columb Canal sometimes referred to as the Grand Canal is a 163-mile-long waterway in Ireland that runs from Lough Neagh in the north to St John's Channel in the south.  The Grand Canal was designed by the Irish engineer and architect James Gandon in 1825 and it was completed in 1829. It is the longest canal in Ireland and it is also the oldest. The canal runs through counties Donegal, Cavan, Monaghan and Galway. The canal has a number of locks and it also has several aqueducts. The canal was used for transportation of goods and people. Today, the canal is used for leisure activities such as boating, fishing, swimming and walking.
