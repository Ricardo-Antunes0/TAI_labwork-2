Tim Wittman is a retired American soccer player who played professionally in the North American Soccer League, Major League Soccer, and the Canadian Professional Soccer League.  Wittman attended the University of Virginia, where he played on the men's soccer team from 1990 to 1993. He was named to the All-Atlantic Coast Conference first team in 1992 and 1993. In 1994, Wittman signed with the Miami Fusion of the North American Soccer League. He spent two seasons with the Fusion before moving to MLS club D.C. United in 1996. Wittman played six seasons with D.C. United, helping the club to two MLS Cup titles in 1997 and 1999. In 2001, Wittman signed with Vancouver Whitecaps FC of the Canadian Professional Soccer League. He spent three seasons with Vancouver, helping the club to two CSL championships in 2002 and 2003. Wittman retired from professional soccer following the 2004 season. In total, Wittman made 176 appearances in MLS, NASL, and CSL competition. He also made six appearances for the United States national team, scoring one goal.
