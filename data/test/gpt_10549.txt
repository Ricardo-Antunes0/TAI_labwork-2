Sir Michael Richard Bond FRSA FRSE FRCPsych FSSC(UK) FRCPath DPH (Hon) (born 5 October 1944) is a British physician who specializes in psychiatry and neurology. He is a Distinguished Professor of Psychiatry at Imperial College London, and is the chairman of the King's Fund. He has been president of the Royal College of Psychiatrists since 2002.  Born in 1944 in Dartford, Kent, Bond was educated at the University of London, where he received his MBBS in 1966. He then undertook postgraduate training in psychiatry at the Maudsley Hospital and at the National Institute of Mental Health in London. In 1973, Bond was awarded a PhD from the University of London for work on schizophrenia.  Bond began his career as a doctor in North London. In 1978, he moved to the Royal Free Hospital in Hampstead, London, where he worked for ten years. In 1992, Bond moved to Imperial College London, where he has since been a Distinguished Professor of Psychiatry. He also holds a position as Chairman of the King's Fund.  Bond has been president of the Royal College of Psychiatrists since 2002. In this role, he has made significant contributions to mental health policy and practice in the UK. He was knighted in 2001 for services to psychiatry. Bond has also served on numerous government committees and advisory groups, including those that developed the Mental Health Act 1983 and the Mental Health Act 2007.
