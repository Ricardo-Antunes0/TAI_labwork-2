Streptanthus glandulosus is a species of flowering plant in the mustard family known by the common name glandular streptanthus. It is endemic to California, where it is known from the eastern slopes of the Sierra Nevada and adjacent mountains.  This annual herb grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with a robust stem and branching networks of spreading green leaves. The inflorescence is a cluster of several small white or pink flowers. The fruit is an inflated capsule filled with small black seeds.   This plant grows in moist, shaded locations on the ground or in crevices in rocks. There are few other plants in its habitat, and it is threatened by urban development and mining.
