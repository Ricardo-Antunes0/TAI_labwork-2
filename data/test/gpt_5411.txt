Wolf-Udo Ettel (26 February 1921 â€“ 17 January 2010) was a German historian and political scientist, who specialized in the history of the Cold War.  Ettel was born in Magdeburg, Germany. He studied history and political science at the University of Bonn and at the University of Heidelberg. He received his doctorate from Heidelberg in 1948.   From 1951 to 1953, he was a research fellow at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey. From 1953 to 1955, he was an assistant professor at the University of Bonn. From 1955 to 1957, he was an associate professor at the University of Bonn. From 1957 to 1959, he was a professor at the University of Hamburg. From 1959 to 1971, he was a professor at the University of Heidelberg.   Ettel served as president of the German Historical Association from 1968 to 1972 and as vice-president of the International History Association from 1978 to 1979. In 1975, he received an honorary doctorate from the University of Hamburg.   Ettel died on 17 January 2010 in Heidelberg.
