The New-England Courant (also spelled New England Chronicle) was a newspaper published in Boston, Massachusetts, from 1712 to 1877. The Courant was the first American daily newspaper, and the first paper published in the United States.  The Courant was founded on December 4, 1712, by James Franklin. It began as a weekly publication, and became a daily newspaper in 1792. The Courant had a significant impact on American history and politics, especially during the American Revolution. Several of the Founding Fathers, such as Benjamin Franklin, John Adams, and Thomas Jefferson, were subscribers to the Courant. The Courant also played a significant role in the development of American law.  The Courant ceased publication on March 3, 1877.
