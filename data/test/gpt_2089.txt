William Ives Budington (April 21, 1815 – March 6, 1891) was an American lawyer and jurist who served as the first Chief Justice of the Oregon Supreme Court. Budington was also the first justice in Oregon to serve on the United States Supreme Court.  Budington was born in Brookeville, Maryland, to Richard and Sarah (née Ives) Budington. He studied law under Aaron Burr and James Kent at the College of William and Mary before moving to Oregon Territory in 1843. He was admitted to the bar in Oregon in 1845 and commenced practice in Salem. Budington served as district attorney for Marion County from 1848 to 1850 and as county judge from 1850 to 1852. He also served as a member of the Oregon House of Representatives from 1851 to 1853.  Budington was appointed as a justice of the Oregon Supreme Court by Governor George W. Bush on March 3, 1865, and served until his resignation on October 24, 1870. Budington was nominated by President Ulysses S. Grant to serve on the United States Supreme Court on December 7, 1870, but his nomination was not confirmed by the Senate. Budington returned to practice law in Salem until his death there on March 6, 1891. He is buried at River View Cemetery in Salem.
