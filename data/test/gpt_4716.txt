9 Puppis is a binary star system in the northern constellation of Puppis. The primary component, A1V, is a blue-white main sequence dwarf star that has a average apparent magnitude of 4.92. It is about 227 light years from Earth.  9 Puppis was first discovered by the French astronomer Pierre Méchain on August 12, 1781. It was named by him after his sister, Caroline Méchain. The system is also known as HD 916414 and AC +24° 9 Puppis.   The secondary component, B9V, is a white dwarf with an apparent magnitude of 12.5. It is about 13 light years from Earth.
