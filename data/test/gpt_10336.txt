Runyon was born in New York City to Irish immigrants. As a young man, he worked as a reporter for the New York Herald and later became a crime reporter for the New York World. He began writing short stories in the 1840s and achieved major success with his novella The Legend of Sleepy Hollow (1857), which is often cited as the first example of American Gothic literature. Runyon wrote more than two dozen novels and hundreds of short stories, many of which are still popular today. His works have been adapted for television and film numerous times.  Runyon was married three times and had six children. He died in Manhattan at the age of 78.
