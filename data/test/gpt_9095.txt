Born in Cargill, County Kildare, Picton joined the army in 1776 and served in the American War of Independence. He served in Portugal and Spain in the late 18th century, before being made a lieutenant-general in 1814. Picton served as Governor of Madras from 1806 to 1809 and as Governor of Quebec from 1811 to 1815. He was also Member of Parliament for East Looe from 1812 to 1830.
