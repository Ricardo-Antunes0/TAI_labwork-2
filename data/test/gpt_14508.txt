Yi Seong-deuk (; 1775 - 27 November 1851) was a Korean painter of the Joseon Dynasty. He is considered one of the most representative painters of the "Yi dynasty" of painting, which refers to the period from 1801 to 1851.  Yi Seong-deuk was born in 1775 in Gyeongsang Province, during the reign of King Jeongjo. He became a court painter in 1801 and served as an official artist for the royal family until his death in 1851.  He is best known for his paintings of Buddhist subjects, which were often commissioned by members of the royal family. He also produced a number of portraits and landscapes.
