He worked as a clerk in a draper's shop before becoming a journalist. He published his first volume of poetry, "The Druids", in 1820. Coulter wrote mainly light verse, but he is best known for his plays, including "The Bear" (1824), "The Castle" (1827), and "The Mayor of Casterbridge" (1837). He died in 1843.
