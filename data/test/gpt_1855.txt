Shree K. Nayar is an engineer and former Chairman of the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). Nayar is also a recipient of the Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civilian honour.  Nayar was born in 1932 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He obtained his bachelor's degree in engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology in 1955 and his master's degree from the University of Michigan in 1959. He worked as an engineer with the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) from 1962 until his appointment as its Chairman in 1991. Nayar served as the ISRO's Director-General from 1996 to 2001. He retired from ISRO in 2003. Nayar has authored several books on space technology and directed a number of documentaries on space exploration and satellite communication.
