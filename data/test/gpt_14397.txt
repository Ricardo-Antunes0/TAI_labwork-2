The Snow Bowl is the nickname of the NCAA Division I-A football game played on December 31, 1985, in Pasadena, California. The game pitted the #1 USC Trojans (9–0) against the #2 Michigan Wolverines (8–1). It was the final game of the 1985 season and USC's 27th consecutive victory.  Michigan was attempting to become the first team to ever win two consecutive national championships, while USC was seeking its 8th national championship. The game was close throughout, with both teams scoring late in the fourth quarter. With seconds remaining and the Wolverines having a chance to win on a field goal, quarterback Ron Turner threw an interception to cornerback Darrell Green, sealing the victory for USC.
