Haberbusch i Schiele was a (now defunct) Austrian art magazine that was published between 1926 and 1938. It was one of the most important and influential art magazines of its time.  Haberbusch i Schiele was founded by Fritz Haberbusch and Gustav Schiele in 1926. The magazine was based in Vienna and was one of the most important and influential art magazines of its time. It was notable for its progressive approach to art, which championed the work of modern artists such as Wassily Kandinsky, Paul Klee, and Otto Dix. Haberbusch i Schiele also published essays on art and criticism, as well as features on prominent artists and their works. The magazine ceased publication in 1938 after being banned by the Nazi regime.
