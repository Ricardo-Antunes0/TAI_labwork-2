Maggie Lindsy Haberman (born October 30, 1973) is an American journalist and the chief political correspondent for The New York Times. Haberman has been with The Times since January 2001, and has reported on national politics, the White House, Congress, and the Trump administration.  Haberman was born in Los Angeles. She grew up in Bethesda, Maryland, and graduated from Georgetown University in 1996 with a B.A. in political science and international relations.  Haberman worked as a reporter for the "Los Angeles Times" from 1998 to 2001, before joining The New York Times as its first political correspondent. She has covered national politics, the White House, Congress, and the Trump administration. She has also covered the 2008 and 2012 U.S. presidential elections extensively. Haberman is a regular guest on various media outlets to discuss politics and current events.  In February 2018, Haberman was one of several reporters who received an advance copy of the controversial Trump-Russia dossier compiled by former British intelligence officer Christopher Steele. Haberman published an story detailing allegations in the dossier on March 16, 2018.
