He began studying the piano at the age of five, and performed his first jazz solo at the age of twelve. He then studied at the Victorian College of the Arts, where he won first prize in the 1967 H V Mitchell Student Piano Competition.  In 1969, Fodi moved to Sydney to take up a position as pianist with the Sydney Symphony Orchestra. He later formed his own trio with bassist Phil Manzanera and drummer Tony Williams, which played throughout Australia and internationally. In 1974, he was awarded a Commonwealth Arts Council Fellowship to study at the University of California, Berkeley. While in California, he worked with Jerry Garcia, John Lee Hooker, and Herbie Hancock.   Fodi returned to Australia in 1976 and resumed his career with the Sydney Symphony Orchestra. In 1984 he co-founded the jazz group Brotherhood of Breath with John Butler, which performed until 1996. He also continued to work as a session musician, appearing on albums by artists such as Diana Krall, Celine Dion, and Neil Young. Fodi died from a heart attack in 2007 at the age of 66.
