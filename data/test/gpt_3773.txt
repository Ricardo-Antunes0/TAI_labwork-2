Michael Kenneth Mann (born February 5, 1943) is an American film director, producer, and screenwriter. He has directed such films as The Last of the Mohicans (1992), Heat (1995), and Collateral (2004). Mann also wrote the screenplay for Heat, and directed its sequel, Collateral.  Mann was born in Los Angeles, California. He studied film at the University of Southern California School of Cinematic Arts. He began his career as a screenwriter, working on scripts for the television series Miami Vice and Crime Story. Mann's directorial debut was The Last of the Mohicans (1992), which was critically acclaimed and won him a Golden Globe Award for Best Director. His next film, Heat (1995), was even more successful, winning four Academy Awards including Best Director and Best Picture. Mann followed it with two other successful films, Collateral (2004) and The Insider (2007). In 2010, he directed Public Enemies, a crime drama based on the story of John Dillinger.
