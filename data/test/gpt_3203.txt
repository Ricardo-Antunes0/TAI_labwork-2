Cuthbert Euan Charles Rabagliati, , (1 January 1920 – 10 December 2003) was a Canadian pianist and conductor.  Rabagliati was born in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. He studied piano with his father, Cuthbert Rabagliati, and composition with Nadia Boulanger. He made his concert debut at the age of fifteen in Montreal, and played with such leading orchestras as the Montreal Symphony, the Philadelphia Orchestra, the Boston Symphony Orchestra and the Vienna Philharmonic.  He made his conducting debut with the Montreal Symphony in 1951. In 1966 he founded the Orchestre de Quebec, which he led for thirty-six years. In 1976 he founded Les Arts Florissants, an orchestra devoted to contemporary classical music. He served as its music director until 1995.  Rabagliati was awarded the Order of Canada in 1978 and the première prize at the Tchaikovsky Competition in 1980. He was also awarded a Grammy Award for Best Classical Album for his recording of Prokofiev's Piano Concerto No. 3 with the Montreal Symphony (1980).  Rabagliati died in 2003 at the age of 86 after a long illness.
