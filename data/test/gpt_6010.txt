Sue Scott is a British sociologist and author who is known for her work on social exclusion and social mobility.  Sue Scott was born in Coventry, England in 1964. She received her B.A. from the University of Warwick in 1986, and her M.A. and Ph.D. from the University of Cambridge in 1989 and 1993, respectively.  Scott has been a lecturer at the University of Cambridge since 1993, and a professor since 2006. She has also been a visiting professor at the University of Tokyo, the University of California at Berkeley, and the Université de Montréal.  Scott's work has focused on social exclusion and social mobility, particularly in Britain and Europe. She is the author of The Excluded (1996), Social Exclusion (1998), Mobility (2002), and The Social Construction of Opportunity (2011).
