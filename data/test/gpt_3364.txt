"Casino Boogie" is a song by English rock band the Who, released as a single in 1969. Written by Pete Townshend, the song is about a man who is addicted to gambling.  The Who originally recorded "Casino Boogie" for their debut album, the Who's First Album, in March 1969. The track was not included on the album, but was instead released as a single in December of that year. The song reached number nine on the UK Singles Chart. The song was later included on the band's second album, Tommy, in 1970.
