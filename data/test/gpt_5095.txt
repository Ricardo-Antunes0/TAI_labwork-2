Duncan McKenzie (born 10 June 1950) is a Scottish historian and academic.  McKenzie was born in Glasgow, Scotland, the son of Jessie McKenzie (n√©e McCallum) and Joseph Duncan McKenzie. He was educated at the Royal High School of Edinburgh, where he was headboy, and at Trinity College, Cambridge, where he graduated with a First in History in 1973.  After a year of postgraduate study at the University of California, Berkeley, McKenzie returned to Cambridge University as a research fellow in history. He then spent two years at the University of Edinburgh, before moving to the University of Manchester in 1979 as a lecturer in modern Scottish history. He became a professor at Manchester in 1987 and was director of its Centre for Scottish History from 1992 to 1996.  McKenzie has written extensively on Scottish history and his books include "The Scottish Nation" (1985), "The Making of the Modern Scottish State" (1992), "Scotland's Empire: The United Kingdom and the World, 1714-1837" (1997), "Scotland's War of Independence" (1998), "An Era of Revolution: Scotland 1745-1789" (2005), and "The Radical Enlightenment: Philosophy and the Making of Modernity 1667-1733" (2011). He has also written articles for journals such as "History", "Past & Present", and "The British Journal for Eighteenth-Century Studies".
