Kokshetau (meaning Blueish Mountain in Kazakh, ; [koksɪtɑu] ()) is a city in the Kokshetau Region of southern Kazakhstan. It has a population of about 119,000 people.  Kokshetau is located at an altitude of 1,640 meters above sea level on the banks of the Kokshetau River. The city is served by Kokshetau Airport. The region around Kokshetau is rich in mineral resources.  The city was founded in 1871 as a settlement on the site of an earlier Kazakh nomadic camp. It served as the administrative center of the Kokchetau District until it was reassigned to the Kokshetau Region in 1998.
