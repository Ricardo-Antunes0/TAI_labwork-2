Harald Olav Aabrekk (born 22 February 1956) is a Norwegian businessperson and politician for the Conservative Party. He has served as a deputy representative to the Norwegian Parliament from Hordaland during the terms 2005–2009 and 2013–2017.  Aabrekk grew up in the town of Bergen, where he finished his secondary education in 1974. He then studied business administration at the University of Bergen, graduating with a degree in 1979. He worked his way up through various positions in the oil industry, starting with a job as a drilling engineer with Norsk Hydro in 1982. In 1992, he started his own company, AAB Resources, which developed offshore oil and gas fields.  Aabrekk was first elected to the Norwegian Parliament from Hordaland in the 2005 election, and was re-elected in 2009. He served as Deputy Leader of the Conservative Party from 2006 to 2007. In 2013, he was elected to serve as a full representative for Hordaland in the Norwegian Parliament, replacing Odd Reidar Humlen. He was re-elected in 2017.
