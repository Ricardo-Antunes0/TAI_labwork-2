Rashoderick "Shock" Linwood (born October 13, 1993) is an American professional basketball player for the Dallas Mavericks of the National Basketball Association (NBA). He played college basketball for the Kentucky Wildcats.  Linwood was born in Lexington, Kentucky. He attended Lexington High School, where he was a three-time All-State selection and helped lead the school to its first state championship in 24 years. In his junior year, Linwood averaged 26.5 points, 10 rebounds and 5 assists while leading his team to a 27–3 record and the Class AAA state title. As a senior, he averaged 26.8 points, 10.9 rebounds and 7.7 assists while leading Lexington to a 32–1 record and its second straight state championship.  Linwood played one season of college basketball at Kentucky, where he averaged 14.8 points, 6.2 rebounds and 3.1 assists per game as a freshman in 2012–13 before suffering a season-ending foot injury. As a sophomore in 2013–14, he averaged 17.4 points, 6.8 rebounds and 2.6 assists per game as the Wildcats finished second in the SEC regular season and made it to the Final Four before losing to eventual champion North Carolina. As a junior in 2014–15, Linwood averaged 20.3 points, 7.5 rebounds and 2.9 assists per game as Kentucky finished third in the SEC regular season and won its first SEC tournament championship since 1985 before losing in the NCAA Tournament Final to Duke.
