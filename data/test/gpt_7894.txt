Four Afghan Steeds (Chinese: 爱乌罕四骏图) is a painting by the Chinese painter Wu Guanzhong, completed in 1486. It is now in the collection of the National Palace Museum, Taipei.  The painting measures 183.5 cm by 137 cm and is considered a masterpiece of the late Ming dynasty. It is one of Wu's best known works and his most well-known painting. The horse on the right is white, while the horse on the left is black. The horses are depicted running free and are among Wu's most famous pieces of work.
