Glanusk Park  is a country estate located in the town of Springdale in the province of Alberta, Canada.  Glanusk Park is situated on a 326-acre (130 ha) estate on the east side of Springdale, overlooking the Bow River. The estate was first settled by the Glanusk family in 1910. The estate was acquired by the National Capital Commission in 1938 and is currently administered by Parks Canada.  The estate is home to a variety of habitats including prairie, aspen and birch forests, wetlands, meadows and ravines. The property is also home to a number of rare and endangered species of plants and animals, including the black-footed ferret, pallid bat and pine marten.   The estate is open to the public from May to September.
