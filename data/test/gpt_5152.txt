Case & Draper was a photographic studio founded in 1892 by Edward Case and Homer Draper. The studio specialized in portraiture and promotional material. The studio closed in 1969.  Case & Draper was one of the earliest photographic studios in the United States. The studio specialized in portraiture and promotional material, and was active from 1892 to 1969. The studio's most famous client was Ford Motor Company, who used the studio to produce images of its executives and products. The studio also produced images for such companies as Coca-Cola and Kodak.
