Solma is a village development committee in Rautahat District in the Narayani Zone of south-eastern Nepal. At the time of the 1991 Nepal census it had a population of 4,853 people living in 812 individual households.  Solma village development committee is located at a distance of 5 km from Sankhuwasabha town and 10 km from Rautahat district headquarters. It is bounded by the Rautahat district to the north, east, and south and by the Narayani Zone to the west. The village has an area of 1.5 km2 and has a population density of 135 persons per km2. There are a total of 812 individual households in the village.  The main occupation of the people in Solma is agriculture. There are a total of 308 hectares of land in the village which is used for farming purposes. The main crops grown in Solma are paddy, maize, wheat, and barley. There are also some vegetables and fruits grown in the village. The villagers also earn a living through wage labour. There are a total of 903 persons working in wage labour in Solma village development committee. The major sources of livelihood for the villagers are agriculture, wage labour, and business activities.
