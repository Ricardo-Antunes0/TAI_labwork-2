Du Zeng (died 319) was a Chinese scholar, poet and philosopher. He was a native of Wuling County, Henan province. He was a disciple of the philosopher Xunzi and is considered one of the most important figures in the development of Chinese philosophy.  Du Zeng is best known for his work on ethics, which has been influential in East Asian thought. His most famous work, the Zengzi, is a collection of essays on morality and philosophy. Du Zeng also authored a number of poetry collections.
