Born in London, Georgina Sweet began her career as a child performer in vaudeville. She made her Broadway debut in 1897, and achieved great success in stage and film appearances throughout the early to middle part of the 20th century. Her most notable roles were in the films Madame X (1934), The Bells of St. Mary's (1935), and The Little Foxes (1941).  Georgina Sweet was married three times, first to actor Harry Beaumont from 1897 to 1906, then to film producer Joseph M. Schenck from 1906 to 1933, and finally to composer Arthur Freed from 1933 until her death.
