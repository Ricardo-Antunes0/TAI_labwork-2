Madron  is a civil parish and town located in the Vale of Glamorgan, South Wales. The population was 1,947 at the 2011 census.  The town is situated on the A4 Bristol-Cardiff motorway, about west of Cardiff and from Swansea. It is served by a dual carriageway bypass which opened in 1982. The town has a railway station on the Newport to Cardiff Line, which also serves the nearby city of Newport. Madron has a small shopping centre and a gym.  The name Madron is Welsh for "the meadow" or "the clearing in the forest". The settlement was originally known as Newbridge, but was renamed in 1883 in honour of the Marquess of Bute, who had recently acquired an estate there.   Madron is part of the electoral ward called Butetown. This ward has a population of 10,527 according to the 2011 census.
