The Principality or, from 1253, Kingdom of Galicia–Volhynia was a Ruthenian–Ukrainian polity that emerged in the late 12th century and lasted until its dissolution in 1772. The Galicia–Volhynia kingdom originated from the union of the two Ruthenian principalities of Galicia and Volhynia.  The Galician–Volhynian kingdom formed in the aftermath of the disintegration of the Kievan Rus' and its division into separate principalities. This occurred in the late 12th century, following a period of upheaval and civil war. The original nucleus of the Galician–Volhynian kingdom was in present-day western Ukraine, especially around L'viv and Ivano-Frankivsk. Initially, Galicia–Volhynia competed with other Ruthenian states for hegemony over Rus' lands. However, by the 1310s, Galicia–Volhynia had emerged as one of the most powerful states in southern Rus'.  The kingdom reached its peak in the early 1320s, when it occupied an extensive territory stretching from central Poland to the Black Sea coast. However, by the 1330s, Galicia–Volhynia was faced with a series of challenges to its hegemony. First, there was a resurgence of Russian power under Vladimir I Monomakh.
