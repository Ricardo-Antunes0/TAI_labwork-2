Hal Niedzviecki (born January 9, 1971) is an American journalist, author and broadcaster. He is the co-founder and editor of the website Slate. Niedzviecki was born in Chicago, Illinois. He attended Yale University, where he was a member of the Scroll and Key Society. After graduating in 1993, Niedzviecki worked as a reporter for the "Chicago Tribune". He also wrote for "GQ" and "Slate". In 2002, Niedzviecki founded Slate, an online magazine covering politics, culture, and economics. The website has won numerous awards, including two National Magazine Awards. Niedzviecki also hosts the weekly TV show "Political Junkie" on PBS.
