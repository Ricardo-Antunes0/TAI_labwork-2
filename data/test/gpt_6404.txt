Harris A. Lewin, an American biologist, is known for his work in the fields of social psychology, developmental psychology, and psychobiology. Lewin was born in Brooklyn, New York, on October 11, 1907. He received his undergraduate degree from City College of New York in 1927 and his doctorate from Columbia University in 1933. Lewin served as a research associate at the University of California at Berkeley from 1933 to 1937 and at the University of Chicago from 1937 to 1939. He joined the faculty at the University of Maine in 1939 and remained there until his retirement in 1978. Lewin was elected to the National Academy of Sciences in 1963 and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences in 1977. He died on December 25, 1994.
