Biduanita Negara Kartina Dahari (12 November 1941 â€“ 7 January 2002) was a Malaysian author, poet, painter and musician.  She was born in Kuala Terengganu, Terengganu to an Indian father, Biduanita Ramanathan, and a Malay mother, Madam Zarina Mohd Kassim. She was educated at the Malay College Kuala Terengganu and the University of Queensland, Australia. After earning her undergraduate degree in 1964, she returned to Malaysia and worked as a lecturer at the University of Malaya until 1969.  Kartina Dahari's first novel, "Anak Gadis" ("The Girl's Son"), was published in 1971. Her novel "Bintang Biru dan Bintang Merah" ("The Blue and Red Star") was published in 1977. She also wrote poetry, children's literature, plays and musical scores.  She married Malaysian architect Mohd Nadzri Ismail in 1967; they had two children. In 1978 she divorced Mohd Nadzri Ismail and married Javanese academic Abdullah Dahari. They divorced in 1984. Kartina Dahari died of cancer on 7 January 2002 in Kuala Lumpur at the age of 62.
