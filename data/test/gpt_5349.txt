She began her professional acting career as a child in telenovelas before making her feature film debut in the independent drama film The Faculty (1998).  Ramirez's breakthrough role came in the teenage comedy-drama 13 Going on 30 (2004), for which she received widespread critical acclaim and was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Actress. She subsequently appeared in several commercially successful films, including Monster-in-Law (2005), Fast & Furious (2006), and The Break-Up (2008). Ramirez achieved further critical recognition for her performances in the dramas My Sister's Keeper (2009) and The Help (2011), both of which received Golden Globe nominations.  Ramirez subsequently appeared in two critically acclaimed films, the science fiction film Ex Machina (2015) and the crime drama Sicario (2015). She has also starred in the independent films Like Crazy (2011) and Don't Breathe (2016).
