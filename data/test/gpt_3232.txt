Adam Freeland (born 7 August 1973) is a British journalist and author. He is the London-based correspondent for The Guardian, covering British politics and international affairs.  Freeland was educated at Westminster School, an independent boys' school in London, and read Politics, Philosophy and Economics at Magdalen College, Oxford.  He joined The Guardian in 1998 as a political correspondent. He has also worked for the Financial Times, Reuters and the BBC. In 2009, he was appointed deputy editor of The Guardian's online edition.  Freeland has written a number of books on British politics, including The Outsiders: Eight Unconventional Leaders Who Made Britain Great (2011) and Britannia Unchained: How the British Are rediscovering their identity (2014). He has also written articles for The New York Times, The Economist, and Time magazine.
