He was educated at Charterhouse School and then Balliol College, Oxford, where he obtained a first-class honours degree in history. He then undertook postgraduate study at the University of California, Berkeley.  Brecknock worked as a research fellow at All Souls College, Oxford, from 1962 to 1965, and then as a lecturer at the University of Bristol from 1965 to 1967. He then became a lecturer at the University of Cambridge, where he remained until his retirement in 1999.  Brecknock's major historical works include "The Norman Conquest" (1977), "The Aristocracy in England" (1981), "The English Revolution" (1989), and "England Under King George I" (1994). He also wrote articles for various historical journals and edited volumes on topics including the Tudors and the Stuarts.  Brecknock died in Cambridge on 4 March 2007.
