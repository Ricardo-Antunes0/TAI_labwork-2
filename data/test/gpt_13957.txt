The Chicago Boys were a group of economists, businessmen and government officials who were instrumental in the economic and political modernization of Chile during the 1960s. Under the guidance of President Salvador Allende, the Chicago Boys helped to implement sweeping reforms that transformed Chile into a prosperous and democratic nation. These reforms included privatizing state-owned industries, liberalizing trade, and increasing access to education and healthcare. The Chicago Boys are credited with helping to make Chile one of Latin America's most stable and prosperous countries.
