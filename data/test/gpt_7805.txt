Nguyễn Văn Thiệu (; 5 April 1923 – 14 October 2012) was a Vietnamese political leader who served as Prime Minister of Vietnam from 1965 to 1968 and from 1979 to 1981.  Nguyễn Văn Thiệu was born on 5 April 1923 in the town of Quảng Trị in central Vietnam. He completed his secondary education at the Lycée Français de Hanoi in 1941 and then entered the University of Hanoi, where he studied law. He joined the French Indochina Army in 1945 and fought against the Viet Minh during the First Indochina War. After the war, he served as deputy provincial governor (1949–1951) and then provincial governor (1951–1954) of Quảng Nam province.  In 1956, Thiệu was elected to the National Assembly as a member of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam (DRV) party. He served as Minister of Finance from February to May 1958, during the first term of Prime Minister Nguyễn Khánh. In October 1958, he was elected Speaker of the National Assembly. In 1960, he became Minister of Foreign Affairs, serving until 1965 when he was elected Prime Minister. During his time as Prime Minister, Thiệu played a key role in negotiating Vietnam's entry into the United Nations and in strengthening ties with socialist countries. In 1979, he was elected President of Vietnam
