Antonio Margarito Montiel (born March 18, 1978) is an American professional boxer who competed from 2000 to 2012. He held the WBC light middleweight title from 2004 to 2006 and the WBA light middleweight title from 2009 to 2012. In February 2013, Margarito was suspended for nine months for testing positive for a banned substance.  Margarito was born in Torrance, California, to Mexican parents. He turned professional in 2000 and won the International Boxing Federation (IBF) light middleweight title in 2002 with a unanimous decision over José Luis Castillo. Margarito also won the vacant WBC light middleweight title in 2004 by outpointing Carlos Baldomir. Margarito defended his title twice, against mandatory challenger Jermain Taylor and then against whom he had originally fought for the IBF belt, Erik Morales. Margarito lost both fights by knockout. In 2006 he moved up to the middleweight division and won the WBA light middleweight title by knocking out Juan Manuel Márquez in the tenth round. Margarito successfully defended his title twice against Luiz Antonio Barrera, who was ranked number two in the IBF at the time of their fight, and then against Miguel Cotto, who had just won the WBO super lightweight championship. On October 7, 2009, Margarito defeated Ireland's Erislandy Lara via unanimous decision to win the WBA's interim light middleweight title. On November 12, 2009, he regained his full WBA light middleweight title
