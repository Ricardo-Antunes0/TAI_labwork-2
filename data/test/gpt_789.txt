The University of Waterloo (commonly referred to as Waterloo) is a public research university in Waterloo, Ontario, Canada. It was founded in 1857 as the Waterloo Collegiate Institute and became an international university in 1971. With more than 26,000 students from over 150 countries, Waterloo is one of Canada's largest and most diversified post-secondary institutions.  Waterloo is known for its innovative research and teaching in engineering, computing, business, management, law, science, and health sciences. The university has produced many notable alumni including Bill Gates, Paul Allen, and David Chalmers. Waterloo also has active alumni associations with more than 300 groups across Canada and around the world.
