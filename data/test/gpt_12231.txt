The Victorian Scottish Regiment (VSR) was an infantry regiment of the British Army, raised in 1881. It served in the Second Boer War and World War I, before being disbanded in 1922. The regiment was reformed in 2006 as a volunteer infantry regiment.  The Victorian Scottish Regiment was formed on 1 October 1881 at the Royal Military Academy Sandhurst, from the cadet corps of the Highland Light Infantry and the 2nd Battalion of the Seaforth Highlanders. The regiment served in the Second Boer War from 1899 to 1902 and played a major role in the Battle of Colenso. It then saw service in World War I from 1914 to 1918. In 1917, it was given its own identity, becoming the Victorian Scottish Regiment (VSR). The regiment was disbanded on 30 September 1922.  The regiment was reformed on 1 April 2006 as a volunteer infantry regiment. The unit is based at Catterick Garrison and has seen service in Iraq and Afghanistan.
