WLNA is the callsign of an AM radio station broadcasting on the frequency of 1090 kHz in the Washington, D.C., Metropolitan Area. The station is owned and operated by iHeartMedia, Inc.  WLNA was first licensed in 1922 as WRC and was one of the earliest radio stations in the Washington, D.C., area. In the early days, WRC was a daytime-only station. It changed its call letters to WLNA in 1947 and began broadcasting at night. In 1957, WLNA became an affiliate of the ABC Radio Network and remained with that network until 1974, when it became an affiliate of the CBS Radio Network. From 1974 to 1996, WLNA was an affiliate of the Mutual Broadcasting System.   In 1996, WLNA switched to the Fox Sports Radio Network and remained with that network until 2006, when it became an affiliate of the ESPN Radio Network. In 2009, WLNA became an affiliate of the Cumulus Media Networks talk radio network.
