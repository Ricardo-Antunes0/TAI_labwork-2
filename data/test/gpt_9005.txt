Michel Ancel (born 29 March 1972) is a French video game designer, director, and producer. He is best known for his work on the video games Rayman, Beyond Good & Evil, andAssassin's Creed. In 1995, he became the lead programmer on the company's first game, Jumanji: The Journey of Man. In 1997, he founded the company Revolution Software with fellow Ubisoft employees David Ancel and Christophe Balestra.  Ancel's first video game project as sole director was Beyond Good & Evil (2003), which received critical acclaim and sold over two million copies worldwide. He directed the sequel, Beyond Good & Evil 2 (2007), which was equally well received and sold over three million copies. Assassin's Creed (2007) is one of the best-selling video games ever released and has been credited with revitalizing the action-adventure genre.
