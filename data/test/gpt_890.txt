Donald Montgomery (May 3, 1848 – May 10, 1927) was an American educator and administrator. He served as the fourth president of the University of Michigan from 1887 to 1891 and was also the first chancellor of the University of Wisconsin–Madison.  Donald Montgomery was born in Oswego, New York on May 3, 1848. He attended the local district school and then the Oswego Academy before enrolling at Union College in 1867. He transferred to the University of Michigan in 1870 and completed his undergraduate studies in 1873.  Montgomery began his teaching career at Michigan in 1873. He became a full professor of mathematics in 1877 and served as dean of the faculty from 1888 to 1889. He also served as acting president of Michigan from 1887 to 1888.  Montgomery became the fourth president of the University of Michigan in 1887. During his tenure, he oversaw a number of important expansions and improvements to the university. In 1889, he inaugurated the University of Michigan Press and oversaw its growth into one of America's leading academic publishers. Montgomery also oversaw the creation of the University's graduate schools, including the School of Medicine and Public Health (1889), School of Law (1892), School of Music (1895), School of Social Work (1914), College of Engineering (1916), and College of Agricultural Sciences (1921).
