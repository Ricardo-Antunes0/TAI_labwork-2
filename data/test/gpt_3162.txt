Melvin and Howard (stylized as Melvin (and) Howard) is an American television sitcom created by Dan Savage and Jeff Greenstein. The series aired on HBO from November 10, 1996, to May 19, 1998.  The show follows the lives of Melvin (Jeff Greenstein) and Howard (Dan Savage), two gay men who live together in a suburban Chicago apartment. The show often features topical humor and references to various LGBT issues of the time.  Despite critical acclaim, the series was cancelled after only five seasons. However, its online popularity led to a revival on Netflix in 2015, which concluded on May 19, 2018.
