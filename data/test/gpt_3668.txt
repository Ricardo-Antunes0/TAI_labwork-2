Michael O'Sullivan (March 4, 1934 – July 24, 2007) was an American actor, primarily known for his roles in the 1970s and 1980s. He also had a successful career in stage and television.  O'Sullivan was born in 1934 in the Bronx, New York City, the son of Irish immigrants. After attending Catholic school, he studied drama at the Neighborhood Playhouse School of the Theatre. He made his film debut in the 1960 film "The Prime of Miss Jean Brodie".  O'Sullivan's breakthrough role came in the 1970 film "The French Lieutenant's Woman", for which he was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Actor. He subsequently appeared in such films as "The Deer Hunter", "The Godfather Part II", "The Godfather Part III" and "Apocalypse Now". O'Sullivan starred as Detective Jimmy Malone on the popular television series "Hill Street Blues" from 1981 to 1987.  In 1989, O'Sullivan appeared as The Master in Stanley Kubrick's science fiction film "Eyes Wide Shut". He also had a role in the 1999 film "The Horse Whisperer".  O'Sullivan died of bladder cancer at his home in Los Angeles on July 24, 2007, aged 74.
