Choe Je-u, who used the pen name Juche, was a North Korean writer and journalist. He is best known for his novel The Flower Girl, which tells the story of a woman's life in North Korea during the late 1940s.  Born in 1924 in the Yanji prefecture of North Korea, Choe Je-u studied at the Kim Il-sung University before working as a journalist for the Korean Central News Agency. In 1954 he published his first novel, The Flower Girl, which tells the story of a woman's life in North Korea during the late 1940s. The book was banned shortly after its publication and Choe Je-u fled to China, where he continued to write until his death in 1998.
