Helmut Lent (13 June 1918 – 7 October 2009) was a German general who served as chief of staff of the Bundeswehr from 1964 to 1969.  Lent was born in Berlin, Germany, on 13 June 1918. He attended the Royal Military Academy of Berlin and the War College of the Armed Forces Staff in Berlin. He also attended the Infantry School at Fort Benning, Georgia.   Lent served with the 7th Infantry Division in World War II. He became deputy commander of the 3rd Infantry Division in 1951 and, two years later, commanded the division during its deployment to Korea. In 1959 he became commander of VII Corps stationed in West Germany. From 1964 to 1969 he served as chief of staff of the Bundeswehr, a post he held during the development of NATO's military strategy.   Lent retired from active duty in 1969 and was promoted to Generalleutnant. He died on 7 October 2009 at the age of 96.
