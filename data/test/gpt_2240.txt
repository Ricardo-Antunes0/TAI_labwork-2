Thurman Lee Thomas (born May 16, 1966) is an American former professional basketball player who played in the National Basketball Association (NBA). He was drafted by the Chicago Bulls in the first round of the 1988 NBA draft. A 7'0" power forward/center, Thomas played for six franchises in his 14-year NBA career, most notably the Bulls, Cleveland Cavaliers, Sacramento Kings, and Dallas Mavericks. He retired in 2003 after playing for the Mavericks.  Thomas was born in Chattanooga, Tennessee and attended Sequoyah High School. He played college basketball at Southern Illinois University Carbondale before being drafted by the Chicago Bulls. During his NBA career, Thomas was known for his physical play and strong rebounding abilities. He also had a prolific scoring average, averaging 19.6 points per game over his career.  After retiring from the NBA, Thomas started a basketball camp in Richardson, Texas and became an assistant coach with the Mavericks. In 2016, he published his autobiography, "The Dream: My Life as a Professional Basketball Player".
