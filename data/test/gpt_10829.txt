Accident by Design is a 1950 detective novel by American author Raymond Chandler. It is the last novel in the celebrated Chandler detective series begun with The Big Sleep.  The novel is set in the fictional city of San Francisco in 1949 and revolves around the investigation of a fatal automobile accident. The novel's title refers to the theory that some accidents are intentionally caused, either as part of a planned murder or as part of a scheme to commit insurance fraud.  The story was adapted as a film of the same name, released in 1981.
