Danny Needham  (May 20, 1867 - September 1, 1941) was a Canadian-born American film actor and director. He appeared in over 130 films between 1911 and 1941.  Born in Victoria, British Columbia, Needham started his acting career in vaudeville. He made his screen debut in 1911 in the film The Hazards of Helen. He continued to appear in films throughout the 1910s and 1920s, most notably in roles in The Miracle Man (1923), The Ghost of Tom Joad (1925), and Scarface (1932). He also directed one film, The Life of Emile Zola (1937).  Needham retired from acting in 1941 but continued to work as a director until his death in 1941.
