Collar rot is a bacterial disease of cattle caused by the bacterium Mycoplasma mycoides. The disease is highly contagious and often fatal, particularly in young animals. The symptoms of collar rot include fever, anorexia, and weight loss. The lesions that develop on the neck and shoulders are often swollen and discolored, and may eventually lead to death. Collar rot is most commonly seen in young, healthy animals, and is a common cause of death in dairy cattle. There are currently no effective vaccines or treatments for collar rot, but research into the causes and prevention of the disease is ongoing.
