Keith J. Bulluck (born April 4, 1977) is an American professional basketball player. He currently plays for the Los Angeles Lakers of the National Basketball Association (NBA).  Bulluck played college basketball at the University of North Carolina. He was selected by the Memphis Grizzlies with the first overall pick in the 1998 NBA draft. He has also played for the Atlanta Hawks, New York Knicks, Houston Rockets, and Boston Celtics. Bulluck has won three NBA championships with the Lakers (2010, 2012, and 2013), two with Atlanta (2004 and 2005), and one with Boston (2008). He is also a two-time runner-up in the NBA Finals, losing to the Miami Heat in 2006 and to the Dallas Mavericks in 2011.  Keith Bulluck was born on April 4th 1977 in Charlotte, North Carolina, USA. Bulluck attended East Mecklenburg High School in Charlotte where he starred on the basketball team. Bulluck led his school to two state championships and was named North Carolina Player of the Year as a senior in 1996.   Bulluck attended the University of North Carolina where he played college basketball for coach Dean Smith's Tar Heels. As a freshman in 1995–96, Bulluck averaged 10.1 points and 5.2 rebounds per game while shooting 52% from the field and 38% from 3-point range. In his sophomore season of 1996–97, Bulluck averaged 18.3 points and 7.4 rebounds per game and led UNC to their first NCAA Tournament appearance since 1982.
