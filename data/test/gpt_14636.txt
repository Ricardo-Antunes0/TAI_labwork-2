María Eugenia Llamas Andresco (19 February 1944 – 1 November 2006) was a Spanish actress and singer.  Born in Madrid, Llamas began her career as a theatrical actress and singer. She achieved international recognition for her work in the musicals "Casa de los Enamorados" (1977) and "The King and I" (1978), for which she won a Tony Award for Best Featured Actress in a Musical. She later starred in films such as "The Ruling Class" (1983), "A Little Princess" (1986), "The Guest" (1985), and "The Adventures of Priscilla, Queen of the Desert" (1994).  Llamas was also notable for her work on television, appearing in series such as "El Señor de Los Anillos" (1981), "La casa de Bernarda Alba" (1984), "Hermanos en el combate" (1990), and "Volverte a ver" (1997). She died of cancer at the age of 62.
