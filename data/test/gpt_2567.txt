Guram Nikolaishvili (; born 3 November 1952) is a Georgian politician who has served as the Prime Minister of Georgia since 28 April 2016. He previously served as the Minister of Defense from 2012 to 2016.  Nikolaishvili was born in Tbilisi, Georgia, on 3 November 1952. He graduated from the Tbilisi State University with a degree in law in 1977. He then worked as a lawyer until 1984, when he was elected to the Parliament of Georgia. Between 1990 and 1992, Nikolaishvili served as the Chairman of the Parliament's Committee on Defense and Security. In 1992 he was appointed Minister of Internal Affairs by President Zviad Gamsakhurdia, serving until 1996. From 1996 to 2003, Nikolaishvili served as the Minister of Justice. In 2003 he was elected to the presidency of Georgia, and re-elected in 2007.  In 2012, Nikolaishvili was appointed Minister of Defense by Georgian Prime Minister Irakli Garibashvili. He retained this position following the 2014 parliamentary election and subsequent cabinet reshuffle. In 2016, Nikolaishvili was elected to serve as Prime Minister of Georgia.
