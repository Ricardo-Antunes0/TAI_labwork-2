Samuel Sidney Harrell (January 18, 1838 – September 10, 1903) was an American jurist and statesman who served as the eleventh Governor of Alabama from 1884 to 1892.  Harrell was born in Lincolnton, North Carolina, to Leroy and Elizabeth (née Sidney) Harrell. He attended the common schools and then read law to be admitted to the bar in 1861. He practiced law in Phenix City, Alabama, from 1861 to 1868. He served as a member of the Alabama House of Representatives from 1872 to 1876, and as the state's Attorney General from 1876 to 1880.  Harrell was elected as a Democrat to the United States Senate in 1880, serving from 1881 to 1887. He was chairman of the Committee on Railroads and Canals during the Fifty-fourth Congress. After leaving the Senate, Harrell resumed his legal practice in Montgomery, Alabama.  Harrell was nominated by President Grover Cleveland to be Secretary of State in 1889, but he was not confirmed by the Senate. He thereafter resumed his legal practice in Montgomery. In 1893, Harrell was appointed by President Harrison as a member of the Interstate Commerce Commission. He served on that commission until his death in 1903. Harrell is buried in Montgomery's Elm Hill Cemetery.
