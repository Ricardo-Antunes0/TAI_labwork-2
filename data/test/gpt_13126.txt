Ourehouare (died 1698) was a native American war chief and explorer in the early 18th century. He is best known for leading a group of about 150 men on an expedition into present-day Minnesota in 1701, becoming the first Europeans to explore that area.   Born in what is now upstate New York, Ourehouare became a war chief in the area around what is now northwestern Michigan. In 1701, he led a group of about 150 men on an expedition into present-day Minnesota. The group explored what is now the Red River Valley and passed through the area that is now Fargo, North Dakota. Ourehouare and his men were the first Europeans to explore that area.  Ourehouare died in 1698. He was buried near present-day Forest River, Minnesota.
