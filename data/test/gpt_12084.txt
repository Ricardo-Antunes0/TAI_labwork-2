James Graham Ballard (15 November 193019 April 2009) was an English author of science fiction, horror, and thrillers. He is considered one of the most important and influential writers of the post-war British literary scene. Ballard was born in Larkhall, Lanarkshire, Scotland, and emigrated to Canada with his family in 1935. He began writing professionally in the late 1950s, publishing his first novel, The Drowned World, in 1961. Ballard's best-known works include the science fiction novels The Drowned World and The Atrocity Exhibition, the horror novel The Ballard Papers, and the thriller novel Empire of the Sun. He also wrote a book of short stories called The Wind from Nowhere. Ballard's work frequently features characters struggling against overwhelming forces, often featuring ecological or social catastrophes. His work has been praised for its originality and for its exploration of post-apocalyptic landscapes and ideas.
