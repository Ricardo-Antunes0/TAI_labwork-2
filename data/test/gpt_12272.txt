Sayaka Kishimoto was born in the Japanese city of Osaka on February 15, 1966. After studying at the Osaka Municipal Art School, she began working as a freelance artist in the early 1990s. In 1992, she began working on the manga series Naruto, which quickly became one of Japan's most popular comics. She continued to work on the series until its conclusion in 2007, creating over 500 episodes and 25 volumes.  In addition to her work on Naruto, Kishimoto has also produced a number of other manga and anime projects, including Claymore, Wolf's Rain and Baccano!. She has also been involved in countless art exhibitions around the world, including a retrospective at the Tokyo Metropolitan Museum of Art in 2006.  Sayaka Kishimoto died of pancreatic cancer on July 13, 2013 at the age of 50.
