"Angel" is a song recorded by the British pop band Eurythmics. Written by group member David A. Stewart and produced by Stewart and Colin Thurston, it was released in November 1983 as the lead single from the group's seventh studio album, Touch.  "Angel" is a ballad about a love that is forbidden. The song received positive reviews from music critics, who praised Stewart's vocals and Thurston's production. It became the group's third top-ten hit in the United States, peaking at number six on the "Billboard" Hot 100 chart. The song also reached number one in Canada and the United Kingdom.
