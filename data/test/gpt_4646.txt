Steven Siegel (born 1953) is an American lawyer and former prosecutor who is currently a partner at the law firm of Debevoise & Plimpton.  Siegel was born in New York City in 1953. He received his undergraduate degree from Yale College in 1975 and his law degree from Harvard Law School in 1979. After graduation, he clerked for Judge Laurence Silberman of the United States Court of Appeals for the District of Columbia Circuit.  Siegel began his career as a prosecutor in the Manhattan District Attorney's Office, where he was a member of the Major Crimes Unit. In 1985, he became head of the unit, and later served as its deputy chief. In 1993, he was appointed district attorney for Manhattan, and served until 1996.  After leaving office, Siegel joined Debevoise & Plimpton as a lawyer in its white-collar crime division. He has been a partner at the firm since 2002.  Siegel is a member of the American Bar Association, the Federalist Society, and the New York County Lawyers' Association. He is also a member of the board of directors of The New York Restoration Project, which helps homeless people get off the streets and into housing.  Siegel has been married to Diane since 1978. They have two children.
