eHealth Ontario is the agency tasked with developing and implementing statewide electronic health information and resources for patients and their caregivers.  eHealth Ontario was created in 2007 as part of the Ontario Health Insurance Plan (OHIP) Reforms. The agency was created as a result of recommendations made by the Standing Committee on Social Development in its report, A Framework for Electronic Health Information.   The mandate of eHealth Ontario is to improve the quality of life for Ontarians by developing and implementing statewide electronic health information and resources. This includes work in the areas of quality improvement, research, public education, and innovation.   eHealth Ontario has a staff of over 100 people and operates from offices in Toronto, Ottawa, and London.
