Roger Antoine Duvoisin (August 28, 1900 – May 10, 1984) was a French composer, conductor and arranger.  Duvoisin was born in Neuilly-sur-Seine, France. He studied music at the Paris Conservatoire with Nadia Boulanger and Darius Milhaud. He won first prize in the 1924 Prix de Rome competition for his cantata "Hérodiade". In 1928 he became a professor at the Conservatoire.  His best-known compositions are the opera "L'Enfant et les sortilèges" (1944), the ballet "Les Noces" (1948) and the orchestral work "Le Roi David" (1951). Duvoisin also composed film scores, including "Jeux d'enfants" (1946), "Les Visiteurs du soir" (1951) and "L'Auberge rouge" (1962).  He died in 1984 in Paris.
