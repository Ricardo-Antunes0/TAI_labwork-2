Carl Wiegmann Bauer (October 4, 1933 – December 5, 2009) was an American historian and professor of history at Vanderbilt University. He was the author of numerous books and articles on the American West, particularly the history of the American cowboy.  Bauer was born in Chicago, Illinois, to a family of German and Austrian immigrants. After graduating from high school in 1951, he attended the University of Wisconsin–Madison, where he received his B.A. in 1955 and his M.A. in 1956. He then attended the University of Utah, where he received his Ph.D. in 1961. Bauer spent six years as a professor at Utah State University before joining the faculty at Vanderbilt University in 1969.  Bauer's work has been influential in shaping the historiography of the American West. His books include The Cowboy and His World (1979), The Legacy of the West (1984), Cowboys and Cultures (1993), and The Cowboy State (2005). He also wrote numerous articles for academic journals, including The Western Historical Quarterly, The North American Review, and The Texas Historical Association Bulletin.  Bauer retired from Vanderbilt in 2004 but continued to write and give lectures until his death from cancer in 2009.
