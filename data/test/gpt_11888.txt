Adrienne Louise Clarkson  (; née Poy; born October 17, 1961) is a Canadian actress and singer. She is currently the host of the Canadian reality television show "The Amazing Race Canada". Clarkson has received numerous awards, including four Golden Globe Awards, three Emmy Awards, and two Screen Actors Guild Awards.  Clarkson was born in Guelph, Ontario, the daughter of Barbara (née Poy), a homemaker, and Harry Clarkson, a former engineer. Her paternal grandparents were of Scottish descent. Clarkson has two elder sisters, Lucille (born 1951) and Lynn (born 1953). Clarkson attended Guelph Collegiate Institute and then the University of Western Ontario, where she obtained a BA in English literature in 1985.  Clarkson began her career in the early 1980s as a model. She made her acting debut in an independent film called "Road Trip" (1981), and had her first lead role in the television movie "Storm Warning" (1982). Her breakthrough came in the 1989 film "My Beautiful Laundrette", for which she received critical acclaim and several award nominations, including an Academy Award nomination for Best Actress. In 1991, she starred as Sarah Manning in the successful romantic drama series "Downtown Abbey". The show ran for four seasons before finishing in 2003.
