William E. Watson was born in New York City in 1922. Watson studied at the City College of New York and the Columbia University School of Journalism. He served in the U.S. Army during World War II where he became a war correspondent. Watson worked for the Associated Press and the New York Herald Tribune before becoming a senior writer for Newsweek in 1961. He was a contributing editor for the magazine until his retirement in 1992. Watson won a Pulitzer Prize for his reporting on the Vietnam War.
