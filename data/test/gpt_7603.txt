Nathaniel Brassey was born c. 1697 at Roxford, Essex, the son of John Brassey and his wife Sarah Popham. He began life as a gentleman farmer but by 1722 he had become a Tory MP for Essex, having bought the manor of Halstead in that county in 1721. Brassey served as Treasurer of the Navy from 1727 to 1730 and as Joint Secretary at War from 1730 to 1732. In 1754 he was elected MP for Bridgwater but died the following year aged 69.  Brassey married Martha Poulett, daughter of Sir George Poulett, 1st Baronet, in 1725. They had four sons and two daughters:  Nathaniel Brassey was buried at Halstead on 28 March 1765. His son George succeeded him in the parliamentary seat.
