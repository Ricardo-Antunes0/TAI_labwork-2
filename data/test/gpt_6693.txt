Barton Paul Levenson (born May 9, 1960) is an American cognitive neuroscientist who specializes in the study of attention and its disorders. He is currently the Blanche W. Bruce Professor of Cognitive Neuroscience at the University of California, Berkeley.  Levenson was born in Los Angeles, California, to Jewish parents. He attended public schools in the city before enrolling at Stanford University, where he earned his undergraduate degree in 1982, and then completed his graduate studies at Harvard University in 1985.  After completing his graduate studies, Levenson joined the faculty at UC Berkeley, where he has been a full professor since 2006. His research focuses on the biology of attention and its disorders, with a particular emphasis on the role that attention plays in cognitive processing and memory. He has also published extensively on the development of cognitive neuroscience theory and methodologies, as well as on theoretical issues associated with the understanding of human cognition.  Levenson is a fellow of both the American Academy of Arts and Sciences and the Society for Experimental Biology. In 2009, he was awarded the prestigious Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz Prize by the Deutsche Forschungsgemeinschaft.
