Speedy Boarding (foaled 10 February 2012) is a British Thoroughbred racehorse and sire. He was the leading two-year-old in Britain in 2013, winning three of his six races including the Dewhurst Stakes and the King George VI and Queen Elizabeth Stakes. In the following year he won the 2000 Guineas and The Derby before being retired to stud.  Speedy Boarding is a bay horse with a white sock on his left hind foot bred by Sheikh Mohammed's Godolphin organisation. He was sired by Galileo, who won ten races including the Epsom Derby and Prix de l'Arc de Triomphe. Speedy Boarding's dam, Arazi, was a successful sprinter who finished fourth in the 1000 metres at the 2004 Summer Olympics. As a descendant of the broodmare Ardross, she is also related to several major winners including Darley Champion Two Year Old Male Horse, Sadler's Wells Gold Cup winner Parade (GB) and Melbourne Cup winner Zenyatta (USA).  Speedy Boarding was sent into training with Saeed bin Suroor at Newmarket, Suffolk.  On his racing debut, Speedy Boarding contested a maiden race over six furlongs at Newmarket Racecourse on 5 May 2013 and finished second to the colt Marmelade. On 4 July he started 1/5 favourite for a six-furlong maiden at Goodwood Racecourse but finished third behind the French colt Vieux Lion d'Or
