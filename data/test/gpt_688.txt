Grimmelshausen-Preis is a literary prize in Germany, awarded annually for an outstanding work of German-language fiction. The prize is named after the 17th century German author Friedrich Christian Grimmelshausen.  The Grimmelshausen-Preis was first awarded in 1950, and has been given annually since then to a German-language novel. The prize is administered by the Stiftung Deutsche Kulturforschung (German Cultural Research Foundation), and is worth EUR 25,000.
