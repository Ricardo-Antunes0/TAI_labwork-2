Darragh Kenny is an Irish equestrian who has competed in the Olympics and the World Equestrian Games.  Darragh Kenny was born on 14 November 1983 in Ballinasloe, County Galway, Ireland. He started riding when he was just four years old and progressed through the ranks of equestrianism, winning a number of Irish titles before progressing to international competition. Kenny first competed at the 2000 Summer Olympics in Sydney, Australia, where he finished twenty-fourth in the team competition. Four years later, at the 2004 Summer Olympics in Athens, Greece, Kenny again finished twenty-fourth in the team competition. He then competed at the 2008 Summer Olympics in Beijing, China, where he finished eleventh in the individual competition.  Kenny has also competed at the World Equestrian Games, first appearing at the 2002 World Equestrian Games in Aachen, Germany. He finished tenth with Ireland in that competition. He returned to compete at the 2006 World Equestrian Games in Tryon, North Carolina, where he finished eleventh with Ireland. At the 2010 World Equestrian Games in Normandy, France, Kenny finished sixth with Ireland.  Kenny currently resides in Dublin, Ireland.
