Han Myeong-sook (born March 24, 1944;  died October 2, 2017) was a South Korean classical pianist.  Han Myeong-sook was born on March 24, 1944 in Seoul, South Korea. She started her music studies at the age of five with her mother, who was a pianist. Han Myeong-sook debuted as a soloist in 1963 with the Seoul Symphony Orchestra. In 1973 she founded the Han Myeong-sook Piano Competition, which has been held annually since then. Han Myeong-sook has performed throughout the world, including concerts in Japan, Germany, Italy, the United States, and South Korea. She has also recorded extensively for various labels. In 1990 she won the prestigious Japan Prize for her outstanding career as a classical pianist. Han Myeong-sook died on October 2, 2017 at the age of 75.
