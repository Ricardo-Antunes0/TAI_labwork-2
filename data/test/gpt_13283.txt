Anseriformes is an order of birds   The order Anseriformes includes all the waterfowl and shorebirds, which are typically distinguished by their long necks, legs, and bills. They are the most diverse group of birds in terms of number of species. They are found on all continents except Antarctica and they are the only group of birds that can fly. The order contains six families: Anatidae (ducks), Brachyceridae (shorebirds), Ciconiidae (waders), Pelecaniformes (pelicans), Anseriformes (waterfowl), and Phalacrocoracidae ( Cormorants).
