He was the founder of the Canadian Pacific Railway and the first Prime Minister of Canada, serving from 1871 to 1873.  Born in Forres, Scotland, Gibson came to Canada in 1849 and founded a pulp and paper mill in Sarnia, Ontario. He became a prominent businessman and politician, serving as president of the Board of Trade of Ontario and president of the Toronto Board of Trade. In 1871, he was elected to the House of Commons of Canada as a Conservative MP for Essex North and served as Prime Minister during the final months of Confederation. In 1873, he was appointed Lieutenant-Governor of Ontario.  Gibson retired from politics in 1880 and died in Toronto at the age of 71. He is buried in Mount Pleasant Cemetery in Toronto.
