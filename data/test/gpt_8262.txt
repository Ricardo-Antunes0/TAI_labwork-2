Albert Wayne "Boots" Hollingsworth  (February 25, 1919 â€“ July 3, 1986) was an American professional baseball player. He played in Major League Baseball from 1941 to 1954 for the Philadelphia Phillies, Detroit Tigers, Baltimore Orioles, and Boston Red Sox.  Hollingsworth was a left-handed batter and pitcher. He was born in Ellijay, Georgia, and died in Lawrenceville, Georgia.  Hollingsworth was selected by the Phillies in the eighth round of the 1941 amateur draft. He made his professional debut with the Class A Williamsport Bills in 1941 and went on to play with the Class AA Reading Phillies, Class AAA Scranton/Wilkes-Barre IronPigs and the Philadelphia Phillies. He appeared in a total of 356 games over parts of six seasons and had a career batting average of .248 with 54 home runs and 248 RBIs. Hollingsworth also pitched in 63 games over four seasons and had a career earned run average of 4.16.  Following his playing career, Hollingsworth served as a manager in the Phillies' farm system from 1957 to 1959 and again from 1964 to 1966.
