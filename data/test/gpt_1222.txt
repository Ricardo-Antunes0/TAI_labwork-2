The Panathenaea (or Panathenaia) was a multi-day religious festival in honor of the goddess Athena, held every four years in Athens, Greece. It was the largest and most important religious event in the city. The festival was celebrated from 4 to 6 November.  The festival consisted of a series of athletic competitions and concerts, in which the best athletes and singers competed. There were also public processions in honour of Athena, with participants wearing special costumes and carrying sacrificial offerings. The festival was also known for its dramatic plays, which were performed in the Theater of Dionysus.
