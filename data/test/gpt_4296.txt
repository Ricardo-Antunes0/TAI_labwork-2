Wade Island is located in the Susquehanna River in York County, Pennsylvania. The island is about long and wide. It has a total area of about .    Wade Island was formed by the Susquehanna River during the last ice age. The island is part of the historic Wade-Winkler line, which marked the division between the northern and southern sections of Pennsylvania.     The first European settlers arrived on Wade Island around 1760. There were several mills on the island during the 19th century, but most of them closed down in the early 20th century. The island is now mostly used for recreational purposes.
