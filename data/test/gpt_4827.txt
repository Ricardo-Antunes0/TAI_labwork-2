Vjekoslav Karas (19 May 1821 - 5 January 1884) was a Croatian writer and journalist. He was the founder and editor of the magazine "Narodna objava" (National News), later renamed "Nacional" (National), the first Croatian newspaper.   Karas was born in the village of Gornji Kraljevec, near Karlovac, in 1821. After completing his elementary education in his home town, he studied at the Franciscan monastery in Zagreb. In 1846 he became editor of the magazine "Zmaj" (Dragon), which he founded. In 1852 he became editor of "Narodna objava". He served as minister of education in the government of Josip Jelačić. In 1881 he became a senator.
