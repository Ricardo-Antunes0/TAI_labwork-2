Alice Mitchell Rivlin (born Georgianna Alice Mitchell; January 3, 1951) is an American economist and diplomat who has been the senior senator from Maryland since 2007. Rivlin was formerly the director of the Congressional Budget Office (CBO) from 2003 to 2007.  Rivlin was born in Washington, D.C., the daughter of Ruth (n√©e Mitchell), a homemaker, and Mitchell Rivlin, a clothing salesman. She graduated from George Washington University in 1973 with a B.A. in economics. Rivlin then earned an M.P.P. from Harvard University's Kennedy School of Government in 1978.  After working as a congressional staffer and an economist at the Federal Reserve Bank of New York, Rivlin became director of the Congressional Budget Office in 2003. There, she directed the CBO's evaluation of the 2002 Medicare Prescription Drug, Improvement, and Modernization Act and its impact on federal budget deficits and health care costs. Rivlin also played a key role in developing CBO's report on the Bush Administration's proposed Social Security reform package.  Rivlin left CBO in 2007 to become a senior fellow at the Brookings Institution, where she worked on issues related to health care reform, tax policy, budget deficits, and fiscal responsibility. In 2010 she was appointed by President Barack Obama to chair the National Commission on Fiscal Responsibility and Reform, which issued a report advocating for major changes to U.S. fiscal policy.
