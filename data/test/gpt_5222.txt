"40", also known as "40 (How Long)", is a song by American rapper Kanye West. The song was released on June 18, 2006 as the lead single from his third studio album, Late Registration. The song features vocals from West, Jay-Z, and Ludacris. "40" is built around the sample of Archie Bell and The Drells' 1966 hit single "Here I Go (Again)", and its lyrics explore West's feelings of independence and recklessness at the age of 40.  "40" received acclaim from music critics, who lauded its production and West's lyrical content. It was nominated for Best Rap Song at the 2007 Grammy Awards and won in that category at the 2008 MTV Video Music Awards. The song has been featured in several popular television shows, including the second and fourth seasons of HBO's series Entourage, and the first and second seasons of Fox's series New Girl. In February 2012, "40" was voted as the greatest hip-hop song of all time by MTV UK.  Background Kanye West was working on his third studio album Late Registration in a Los Angeles studio with producers such as Timbaland, Missy Elliott, and The Neptunes. One of the tracks that West had completed was "40".
