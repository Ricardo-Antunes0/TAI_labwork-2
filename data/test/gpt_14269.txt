Zion Lateef Williamson (born July 6, 2000) is an American professional basketball player for the Boston Celtics of the National Basketball Association (NBA). Williamson was drafted by the Celtics with the first overall pick in the 2018 NBA draft.  Williamson attended Hillcrest High School in Sacramento, California. As a senior, he averaged 27.9 points, 10.7 rebounds, 4.5 assists and 2.3 blocks per game on 54 percent shooting from the field, 41 percent shooting from three-point range and 78 percent from the free throw line to lead Hillcrest to their fourth consecutive state championship. He was also named California's Mr. Basketball and was a McDonald's All-American.  Williamson played one season of college basketball for Duke University before being drafted by the Celtics. In his lone season with Duke, he averaged 26.2 points, 10.5 rebounds and 4.8 assists per game on 48 percent shooting from the field and 36 percent shooting from three-point range as a freshman and sophomore, respectively. Williamson was named to the All-ACC First Team as a sophomore and was a finalist for the Naismith College Player of the Year Award.  Williamson is considered one of the best players in college basketball history and is considered one of the most versatile players in NBA history due to his ability to score inside and outside, pass effectively, defend multiple positions and shoot from anywhere on the court.
