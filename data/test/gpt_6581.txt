John David McAdoo (April 4, 1824 – March 27, 1898) was an American football player, coach, and general in the Union Army during the American Civil War. He served as the head coach at the United States Military Academy at West Point for one season in 1869. As a senior officer in the Union Army, he participated in many engagements throughout the war, most notably the Battle of Gettysburg. McAdoo was posthumously awarded the Medal of Honor for his actions at Gettysburg.  McAdoo was born on April 4, 1824 in West Chester, Pennsylvania to John McAdoo and Maria Jane (née Young) McAdoo. He attended the United States Military Academy at West Point from 1846 to 1849, where he played football and was captain of the cadet corps. He graduated second in his class in 1849 and was commissioned a second lieutenant in the 2nd U.S. Cavalry. McAdoo served with his regiment throughout California, Oregon Territory, and Washington Territory before resigning his commission on July 30, 1854 to become a coach at West Point.  In 1869, McAdoo was appointed head coach at West Point, where he led the Cadets to a 3–2–1 record and was named United States Military Academy's first captain of football.
