Ali Jawad (born 12 January 1989) is an English professional footballer who plays for Championship club Reading as a midfielder.  Born in Reading, Jawad began his career with the club's youth system, and made his first-team debut in the 2006–07 season. He spent three seasons on loan at Stockport County, before joining Cheltenham Town on a permanent basis in 2009. He spent two seasons at Cheltenham, before signing for Burton Albion in 2012. He spent two seasons with Burton, before joining Bristol City in 2014. He spent two seasons with Bristol City, before joining Reading in 2016.  Jawad has represented England at under-19, under-20 and under-21 level.  Ali Jawad was born on 12 January 1989 in Reading, Berkshire to Jamaican parents who had moved to England to escape the violence of the Jamaican Revolution. Jawad attended The John Fisher School, a secondary school in Reading, and played for local side Ashbourne United before joining the Reading youth system at the age of eight. He made his first-team debut for Reading as a substitute in the 2006–07 season. After impressing during the club's pre-season tour of North America, Jawad was loaned out to Stockport County for the 2007–08 season. He made 43 appearances for Stockport County, scoring five goals.
