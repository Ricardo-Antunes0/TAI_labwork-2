The Bruneau – Jarbidge Rivers Wilderness is a unique and ecologically important area of the Rocky Mountains located in southern British Columbia, Canada. The wilderness is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including grizzly bears, wolves, mountain goats, elk, and black bears. The remote and rugged landscape also supports a variety of plant and animal species that are not typically found in more heavily populated areas.  The wilderness was designated as a protected area in 1991, and is now managed by the BC Ministry of Environment. The rugged terrain and diverse environment make the Bruneau – Jarbidge Rivers Wilderness an ideal place to experience the natural beauty of the Rocky Mountains. The wilderness is accessible by foot or horseback, and provides opportunities for camping, hiking, hunting, fishing, and skiing.
