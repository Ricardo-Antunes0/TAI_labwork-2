He became a successful merchant and rancher, and was elected to the Kansas State Legislature in 1874. Franks was a strong supporter of education, and helped to establish Winfield College in 1886. He died in 1901, and is buried at the Winfield Cemetery.  The Charles Franks House is a two-story brick residence with a hipped roof. It has a front porch with Tuscan columns, and an gabled roofline. The house was built in 1887, and is reflective of the Victorian style. It is listed on the National Register of Historic Places, and is currently used as a museum.
