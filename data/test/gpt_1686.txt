Douglas John Buffone (June 27, 1944 â€“ December 16, 1985) was an American singer-songwriter and musician.  Buffone was born in The Bronx, New York City and attended the Juilliard School of Music, where he studied with Leonard Bernstein. Buffone's first album, "Doug Buffone", was released in 1970. He is perhaps best known for his song "Walking on Sunshine" which was a hit in the United Kingdom in 1979 and reached number one on the US Billboard Hot 100 chart. The song won a Grammy Award for Best Song Written for a Motion Picture or Television. Buffone also recorded the song "The Last Waltz" with Bob Dylan, Emmylou Harris and other members of the Band. Buffone died of a heart attack at the age of 42.
