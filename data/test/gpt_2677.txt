Herbert Arthur Popley (born c. 1879) was an English chemist and physicist who worked on the theory of molecules.  Popley was born on c. 1879 in Lancashire, England. He received his B.A. from the University of Cambridge in 1903 and his M.A. from the University of Cambridge in 1907. He then began working at the Cavendish Laboratory at the University of Cambridge, where he became a fellow in 1911 and a professor in 1919. In 1922, he moved to the University of Chicago, where he became the first professor of chemistry and physics at the university. He remained there until his retirement in 1957. Popley was also a fellow of both the Royal Society and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences. He died on October 10, 1967 in Chicago
