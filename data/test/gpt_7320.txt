Barbara Jean "Bobbie" Spencer, RN is a Canadian singer and actress. Born in Toronto, Ontario, she is the daughter of singer-songwriter Jean (née Sinclair) and actor Donald Spencer.  Spencer began her music career as a teenager, recording and releasing two independent albums. She made her television debut in the Canadian drama series "Heartland" in 2000. She subsequently appeared in the films "The Santa Clause 2" (2002) and "The Princess Diaries 2: Royal Engagement" (2004). In 2005, she released her first mainstream album, which spawned the hit singles "I Believe in a Thing Called Love" and "Thank You for Being a Friend". Her second album, "More Than Words", was released in 2007.  Spencer has since starred in various films and television shows, including the roles of Elle Woods in the Disney Channel sitcom "Charmed" (2006–08), Samantha Jones in the NBC soap opera "The West Wing" (2003–10), and Brooke Logan on the ABC Family drama series "Pretty Little Liars" (2011–present). She has also made appearances on Broadway, including performances in the musicals "The Producers" (2005), "Spider-Man: Turn Off the Dark" (2010), and "The Band's Visit" (2015).  Spencer was honoured with a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame at 6667 Hollywood Boulevard in Hollywood, California on February 10, 2016.
