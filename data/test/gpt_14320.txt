Admiralty Inlet is a strait in the northern coast of Vancouver Island in British Columbia, Canada. It separates the mainland of Vancouver Island from the Gulf Islands. The strait is about long and wide, and has a maximum depth of . The strait is navigable for vessels up to and up to wide.  The strait is named after the British Admiralty, which administered Vancouver Island during the late 19th century. The strait was used by the Royal Navy as a route between Victoria and Esquimalt during the First World War, and by the United States Navy during World War II as a route between Seattle and San Francisco. Today the strait is used mainly for shipping traffic.
