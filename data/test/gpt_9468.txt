Harry Demopoulos is a researcher in the field of artificial intelligence and machine learning who has made significant contributions to the field. He has authored or coauthored over 100 papers, book chapters, and conference presentations on a wide range of AI topics, including natural language processing,deep learning, reinforcement learning, and virtual assistants. He is also the co-founder of Demopoulos and Associates, a research company that specializes in AI and machine learning.  Demopoulos was born in Athens, Greece, in 1963. He received his bachelor's degree in mathematics from Brown University in 1985 and his master's degree in artificial intelligence from the University of California, Berkeley, in 1988. He joined the faculty of Carnegie Mellon University in 1988 as an assistant professor of computer science. In 1997 he moved to the University of Maryland, College Park, where he served as the university's associate dean for research for six years. In 2011 he was appointed the Charles E. Young Professor at Stanford University, where he remains as a professor of computer science and director of the Stanford Artificial Intelligence Laboratory.
