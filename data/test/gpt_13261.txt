The subspecies is genetically distinct from other cutthroat trout found in the region and has been designated as a species by the United States Fish and Wildlife Service.  The Yellowstone cutthroat trout is a small, brightly colored fish that can reach a length of 72 inches (182 cm). The fish has a greenish-gold body with a black stripe down its back. The males have larger heads and jaws than the females, and they also have a distinctive yellow stripe on their throats. The Yellowstone cutthroat trout is an important species for the ecosystem of the park, feeding on small fish and aquatic insects.
