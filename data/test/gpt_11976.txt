Christopher Adam Daughtry (born December 26, 1979) is an American singer, songwriter and actor. Born and raised in Louisville, Kentucky, he began his career in the late 1990s as the lead singer of the rock band Daughtry. After leaving the band in 2001, Daughtry established himself as a solo artist, releasing his debut album, Just What I Needed, in 2002. The album reached number one on the Billboard 200 chart and was certified triple platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA). His follow-up album, Breakthrough, was released in 2005 and also topped the charts; it was certified quadruple platinum by the RIAA. Daughtry's fourth album, 2006's Here I Stand, was also successful commercially. Its first single, "I Want to Know What Love Is", reached number one on the Billboard Hot 100 chart and became his first number-one single in the United Kingdom. His fifth album, 2009's Breakthrough II: The Unreleased Songs, was his first release since leaving Sony Music Entertainment in 2009. He has since released two more albums through independent labels: Imperfectly Imperfect (2010) and Nothing Left to Lose (2013).
