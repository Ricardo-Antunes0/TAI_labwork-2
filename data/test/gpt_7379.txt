She is best known for her novels Iseult of the Speckled Band and Ulysses, which were published in the 1940s and 1950s, respectively. Joyce wrote in both English and Italian, and her work has been translated into many languages.  Joyce was born in Trieste, then part of Austria-Hungary, to an Anglo-Irish family. After the breakup of Austria-Hungary in 1918, her family moved to Rome, where she grew up. She began writing while working as a journalist and later as a librarian. Her first novel, Iseult of the Speckled Band, was published in 1939. It became a bestseller and was awarded the prestigious Strega Prize. Her other novels include Ulysses (1955), Finnegans Wake (1939), and a volume of short stories titled Dubliners (1941). Joyce died in Rome in 1991.
