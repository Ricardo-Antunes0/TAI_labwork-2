General Mohammed Asif Safi (1923 — July 8, 2002) was a Pakistani general and politician who served as the Prime Minister of Pakistan from November 25, 1969 to March 3, 1971. He also served as the Chief Minister of Punjab from 1958 to 1969. Safi was a longtime member of the conservative Muslim League and was one of the architects of Pakistan's policy of secularism.  Safi was born in 1923 in the town of Safiabad in the Bolan District of Balochistan province. He completed his early education at a local school before attending the Royal Military Academy Sandhurst. He subsequently served in the British Indian Army, rising to the rank of brigadier general. In 1958, he joined the Pakistani National Army and served in various positions before becoming the Chief Minister of Punjab in 1969. In 1971, Safi became the Prime Minister of Pakistan after winning a landslide victory in parliamentary elections. He resigned from office less than a year later following a military coup d'état which overthrew his government. Safi died in 2002 at the age of 80.
