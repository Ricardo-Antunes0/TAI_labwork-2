Aron Qabyşūly Edigeev (; 31 January 1953 – 15 January 2016) was a Kyrgyz politician and diplomat who served as the Prime Minister of Kyrgyzstan from 2006 to 2011. He also served as the Foreign Minister from 2002 to 2006.  Aron Atabek was born in 1953 in the Kara-Kul Valley of Osh Province, Kazakh SSR, Soviet Union to a family of Kyrgyz nomads. He graduated from the Kyrgyz State University with a degree in law.  Aron Atabek started his career as a lawyer in the Osh Province branch of the Supreme Court of the Kyrgyz SSR in 1973. He later served as Deputy Prosecutor General of the Kyrgyz SSR from 1990 to 1992, and Deputy Chairman of the State Committee on Migration and Refugees from 1992 to 1996.  Aron Atabek was elected to the National Assembly of Kyrgyzstan in March 1998 as a member of the Liberal Democratic Party (LDP). He served as chairman of the Foreign Affairs Committee from 1998 to 2001, and as deputy prime minister for economic development from 2001 to 2002. In February 2002, he was appointed Foreign Minister by President Askar Akayev.  Aron Atabek was re-elected to the National Assembly in March 2006, and appointed Prime Minister by President Kurmanbek Bakiyev on 5 May 2006.
