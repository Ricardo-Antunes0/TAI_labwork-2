Daniel Kharitonov (Russian: Даниил Харитонов) (born 22 January 1971) is a Russian professional football coach and a former player. He is currently the head coach of FC Krasnodar.  Kharitonov made his professional debut in 1988 in the Soviet Second League with FC Krylia Sovetov Samara. In 1992, he moved to FC Dynamo Moscow, where he played until 1995. In 1995, he joined FC Spartak Moscow, where he played until 1998. In 1998, he moved to FC Rostov. In 1999, he joined FC Lokomotiv Moscow. He played for Lokomotiv until 2003. In 2003, he moved to FC Krylia Sovetov Samara. He played for Krylia Sovetov until 2006.  In 2006, Kharitonov signed with FC Zenit Saint Petersburg. He played for Zenit until 2009. In 2009, he signed with FC Rostov. He played for Rostov until 2013. In 2013, he signed with FC Krasnodar. He has been with Krasnodar since 2013.  Kharitonov made his debut for the Russia national football team in 1995 and has since earned over 50 caps, scoring three goals. He was a member of the Russian team that finished third at the 2000 European Championship and fourth at the 2004 European Championship.
