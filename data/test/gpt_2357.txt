Typhoon Hester was a severe typhoon that struck the Philippines during February 1952. The storm attained its peak intensity on February 20, with winds of 160 mph (260 km/h). Hester killed at least 171 people and caused $24 million in damages.  Typhoon Hester formed from a tropical depression on February 10, 1952, over the western Pacific Ocean. The system rapidly intensified into a severe typhoon on February 13, and reached its peak intensity on February 20. While passing through the Philippines, Hester killed 171 people and caused $24 million in damages. The storm weakened after striking the country, and dissipated on February 24 over the South China Sea.
