USS Truxtun (DDG-103) is an  currently active United States Navy destroyer. She was named for Commodore John Rodgers Truxtun, who distinguished himself in the American Revolution and the War of 1812.  USS Truxtun is the tenth destroyer of her class and was laid down by Bath Iron Works Corporation in Bath, Maine, on 25 October 1984. She was launched on 10 January 1986 and commissioned on 6 October 1988.  After completing her initial training, Truxtun deployed to the Mediterranean Sea in 1989 to join the United States Sixth Fleet. She participated in Operation Praying Mantis, a naval campaign in support of Operation Desert Storm that ended in February 1991. After returning to her homeport at Naval Base Kitsap-Bangor, Washington, Truxtun participated in several exercises and visited ports around the world before returning home in 1995.  In 1996, Truxtun deployed to the Mediterranean again as part of Operation Southern Watch. The ship conducted numerous patrols and exercises before returning to Naval Base Kitsap-Bangor in July 1997. In 1998, Truxtun participated in Operation Desert Fox and conducted numerous patrols off the coast of Iraq. Following the end of Desert Fox, Truxtun returned to her homeport where she underwent an extended overhaul that lasted until 2000.  Truxtun deployed to the Persian Gulf region in early 2001 as part of Operation Enduring Freedom.
