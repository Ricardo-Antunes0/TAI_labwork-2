Visby  is an urban area in Gotland, Sweden. The town has a population of about 25,000 and is the seat of Visby Municipality.  Visby is an old town on the east coast of Gotland. The town was founded in the 11th century by settlers from Uppland. It was originally a fishing village, but later developed into a prosperous trading town due to its location on the Baltic Sea trade route. Today, Visby is an important tourist destination, thanks to its well-preserved medieval centre and its natural surroundings.
