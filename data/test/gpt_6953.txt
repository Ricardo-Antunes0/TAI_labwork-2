Régis Boyer (25 June 1932 – 16 October 2017) was a French actor.  Boyer was born in 1932 in the Paris suburb of Bagnolet. He studied at the Lycée Pierre Corneille before beginning his acting career in the early 1950s. He made his film debut in 1956 in the role of an altar boy in the Jean-Luc Godard drama "Weekend". He went on to appear in a number of acclaimed films, including "The 400 Blows" (1959), "The Wages of Fear" (1953), "Les Enfants du Paradis" (1945), "La Piscine" (1963), and "L'Étranger" (1967).  Boyer won the César Award for Best Actor for his performance as a sadistic prison warden in the 1981 film "La Reine Margot". He also won the Best Actor Award at the 1984 Cannes Film Festival for his performance as an alcoholic aristocrat in Michel Deville's drama "Last Days of Disco". In 2002, Boyer was made an Officer of the Legion of Honour.  Boyer died on 16 October 2017, aged 84, following a long illness.
