The Kalinago, also known as the Island Caribs, are a people who live in the Greater Antilles, specifically on the island of St. Kitts and Nevis. They are the most distantly related to the Carib people, who are found in the Lesser Antilles. The Kalinago are believed to have migrated from South America sometime in the first millennium AD.  The Kalinago are a small people, with a population of only around 4,000. They are primarily subsistence farmers, growing sugarcane, corn, and bananas. They also hunt and fish. The Kalinago language is a member of the Arawakan family of languages, and is closely related to the other languages spoken on St. Kitts and Nevis, such as English and French.
