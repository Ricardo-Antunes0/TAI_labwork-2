Viscount Davidson, of Little Gaddesden in the County of Hertford, is a British Conservative politician. He has been the Member of Parliament for South East Hertfordshire since 1997.  Davidson was educated at Eton College and Christ Church, Oxford, where he graduated with a first class honours degree in Politics. He then studied law at the University of London.  After completing his legal training, Davidson worked as a barrister. He became a QC in 1988. In 1997, Davidson was elected to the House of Commons as MP for South East Hertfordshire. He served as Parliamentary Private Secretary to Michael Portillo from 2001 to 2002 and to William Hague from 2002 to 2005. Davidson was Vice Chairman of the Conservative Party from 2005 to 2006.  Davidson was appointed Minister for Defence Personnel, Welfare and Veterans in May 2006, and served until the Conservative Party lost the 2010 general election. After the election, Davidson served as Minister for Immigration and Citizenship from 2010 to 2012. In September 2012, he was appointed Minister for Infrastructure and Transport. In this role he led the coalition's negotiations on the UK's membership of the European Union.   In October 2016, Davidson resigned as Minister for Infrastructure and Transport after reports emerged that he had an extramarital affair with a journalist whilst his wife was pregnant. He was replaced by Robert Goodwill
