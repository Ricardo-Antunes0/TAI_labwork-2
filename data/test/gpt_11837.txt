The Rumbula massacre is a collective term used to describe the killings of 8,000 Jews in Rumbula, Latvia, during the Holocaust. The massacre took place between November 14 and 16, 1941.  The Rumbula massacre was one of the largest mass killings of Jews during the Holocaust. It occurred in Rumbula, a small town in Latvia, between November 14 and 16, 1941. The victims were 8,000 people—men, women, and children—who were killed by Germans and Latvian collaborators. The massacre was part of a larger Nazi effort to exterminate the Jewish population of Latvia.
