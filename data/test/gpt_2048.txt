Francisco Velásquez (17??-18??) was a Dominican painter.  Velásquez was born in Santo Domingo, the eldest son of Francisco Velásquez and Catalina de la Torre. He began his training in painting with his father, then studied in Madrid with José de Goya. He returned to Hispaniola in 1768 and settled in Santo Domingo. There he became court painter to King Charles III of Spain and painted some of the most important portraits of the monarch, including one of himself which is now in the Prado Museum. He also produced religious paintings, including a Crucifixion (now in the Museo Nacional de Arte, Santo Domingo).  Velásquez died in Santo Domingo in 18??.
