Regent Street is a major shopping street in the West End of London. It runs from Piccadilly Circus to Burlington Gardens, and is lined with high-end stores.  Regent Street has been a retail destination since the 18th century, and its prestigious status was reinforced by King George IV who opened the first bona fide department store on the street, Grosvenor House, in 1827. Today, Regent Street is home to some of the world's most famous luxury stores, including Harrods, Selfridges and Browns.
