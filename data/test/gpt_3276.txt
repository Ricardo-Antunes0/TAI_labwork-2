Wills was born in 1922 in Vernon, Texas. He received his bachelor's degree from Baylor University and his law degree from the University of Texas at Austin. He served in the United States Army during World War II and then entered the practice of law. Wills was elected to the Texas House of Representatives in 1956 and to the Texas Senate in 1960. In 1964, he was elected governor of Texas and served until his resignation due to his election to Vice President in 1973. He was a delegate to the Democratic National Convention in 1968 and 1972. Wills returned to private practice after his term as Vice President ended. He died in 2003 at the age of 84.
