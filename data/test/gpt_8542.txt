Joaquín Almunia Amann (born 17 June 1948) is a Spanish lawyer and politician who served as the fifth Vice President of the European Commission from January 2007 to December 2014. Previously, he was the Minister of Justice from 1996 to 2004 in the first government of José María Aznar.   Almunia was born in Madrid. He studied law at the University of Madrid, where he graduated with a degree in law in 1970. From 1970 to 1975, he worked as a law clerk in the office of the then-Minister of Justice, Manuel Fraga Iribarne. In 1975, he became an adviser to Fraga in his capacity as Minister of Justice. He became a member of the Law Faculty at the University of Salamanca in 1978.   From 1979 to 1983, Almunia was a professor at the same university. He served as Secretary-General of the Spanish Socialist Workers' Party (PSOE) during the late 1970s and early 1980s. In 1984, he was elected to the Congress of Deputies for Valencia Province, and was re-elected in 1988, 1993 and 2000.   In 1995, Almunia was appointed Minister for Justice in José María Aznar's first government. As Minister for Justice, Almunia presided over major changes to Spain's legal system, including the introduction of a new Civil Code and changes to criminal procedure laws. He also presided over several inquiries into corruption within Spanish public institutions, notably those relating to public contracts awarded by his Ministry.
