Gloria Daisy Wekker (born June 13, 1950) is an American singer, songwriter and actress. She is best known for her 1975 hit single "I Will Always Love You" which topped the "Billboard" Hot 100 chart for seven weeks.  Wekker was born in Lake Worth, Florida, the daughter of Daisy Mae (n√©e Hill), a teacher, and Calvin Wekker, a musician. She began her music career as a backing singer with the pop group The Buckinghams. In 1975, Wekker recorded the ballad "I Will Always Love You" with producer Phil Spector. The single became a number one hit on the "Billboard" Hot 100 chart and stayed there for seven weeks. It also reached number one in the United Kingdom, Australia, and New Zealand.  Wekker's other hits include the singles "You're My Heartbeat" (1977), "I Believe in You" (1978), and "When I Fall in Love" (1981). She has won four Grammy Awards: Best Female Pop Vocal Performance for "I Will Always Love You", Best Pop Instrumental Performance for "The Look of Love", Best Female Country Vocal Performance for "Good Ole Boys Like Me", and Best Country Solo Performance for "The Cowboy in Me".  Wekker retired from performing in the early 1990s but made a comeback in 2003 to perform at the 50th anniversary celebration of The Beatles' album "Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band".
