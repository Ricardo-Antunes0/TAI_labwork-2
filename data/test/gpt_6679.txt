Günther Schack (12 November 1917 – 14 December 1996) was a German physicist and astrophysicist.  Schack was born in 1917 in Berlin, Germany. He received his doctorate from the University of Berlin in 1940, and began working at the Kaiser Wilhelm Institute for Physics in Berlin the same year. He later worked at the Max Planck Institute for Nuclear Physics and at the University of Tübingen.  Schack is best known for his work on the theory of stellar evolution, including work on the nucleosynthesis of heavy elements in stars and the structure and evolution of planetary systems. He also developed models to explain the microwave background radiation. Schack died in 1996.
