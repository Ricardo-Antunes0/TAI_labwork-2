Scott A. Brison  (born May 10, 1959) is a Canadian politician who was the Liberal Party of Canada Leader from 2006 to 2009 and the 25th Prime Minister of Canada from 2008 to 2011. He previously served as the Minister of Finance, Minister of Foreign Affairs and Minister of Intergovernmental Affairs in the cabinet of Prime Minister Paul Martin.  Brison was born in Toronto, Ontario. He studied at McGill University and Harvard Law School before entering public service as a law clerk to Justice Bertha Wilson of the Supreme Court of Canada. He then worked as an associate at the firm McCarthy Tétrault, where he became a partner in 1991.  Brison entered federal politics in 1993, when he was appointed to the Senate by then-Prime Minister Jean Chrétien. He served as Chair of the Standing Senate Committee on National Security and Defence from 2002 to 2006.  Brison was elected leader of the Liberal Party on March 26, 2006, defeating former Prime Minister Paul Martin in a tense race. As Liberal Party leader, Brison implemented a number of ambitious policies, including his signature initiative—the Canada Health Act—which improved access to health care for all Canadians. Brison was re-elected as party leader on May 14, 2007, defeating former Ontario Premier Dalton McGuinty.  In October 2008, following Martin's retirement as Liberal Party leader and Prime Minister, Brison was appointed by Martin to replace him as Canada's 25th Prime Minister.
