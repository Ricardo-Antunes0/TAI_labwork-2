Dennis Cutler Blair (born February 4, 1947) is a retired United States Army general who served as the 27th Commander of U.S. Central Command (CENTCOM) from July 2006 to June 2009. He was replaced by General James Mattis.  Blair was born in Columbus, Ohio, and graduated from the United States Military Academy in 1969, where he was a member of the "Bulldog" Brigade. He served as an infantry officer in South Vietnam, where he was wounded multiple times, and later as a brigade commander in the 101st Airborne Division during Operation Desert Storm. He has also served as the Deputy Chief of Staff for Intelligence, G-2 (Intelligence), and Deputy Commander for Special Operations Command.  Blair's military awards and decorations include the Defense Superior Service Medal, Legion of Merit with oak leaf cluster, Bronze Star Medal with three oak leaf clusters, Joint Service Commendation Medal with two oak leaf clusters, and Combat Infantryman Badge.
