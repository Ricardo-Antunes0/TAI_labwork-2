Igor Aleksandrovich Kravchuk (; born September 13, 1949) is a Ukrainian politician who has been the Prime Minister of Ukraine since December 8, 2006. Previously, he was the Chairman of the Verkhovna Rada (parliament) from 1994 to 1998 and again from March 17, 2000 to December 8, 2006.  Igor Kravchuk was born in the city of Kharkiv, Soviet Union. After graduating from high school in 1967, he worked as an engineer at a state-owned factory in Kharkiv. He joined the Communist Party of Ukraine in 1970 and worked his way up through its ranks. In 1988 he became head of the city administration in Kharkiv.  Kravchuk became a Member of Parliament in elections in November 1990, and served as Deputy Speaker of the Verkhovna Rada from January to November 1994. He was then elected Chairman of the Verkhovna Rada on March 17, 2000, and held that post until his resignation on December 8, 2006.  As Prime Minister, Kravchuk's priorities were stabilization of the Ukrainian economy after years of corruption and mismanagement under his predecessor Viktor Yushchenko, and implementation of constitutional reform necessary for Ukraine to join the European Union. He also promoted closer ties with Russia and negotiated a cease-fire agreement between pro-Russian separatists in eastern Ukraine and government forces.
