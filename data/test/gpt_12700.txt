Robert Kayser (born 15 January 1805 in Vienna, Austria-Hungary; died 18 February 1875 in Vienna) was an Austrian painter and lithographer.  Robert Kayser was born on 15 January 1805 in Vienna, Austria-Hungary. He was the son of Johann Kayser and his second wife, Anna Theresia Weber. Robert attended the Academy of Fine Arts in Vienna, where he studied under Johann Nepomuk Hummel and Joseph Anton Koch. He first exhibited at the age of twenty-one, with a painting entitled "The Death of Cleopatra".  Robert Kayser became a well-known painter and lithographer in his own right, primarily painting religious subjects. He died on 18 February 1875 in Vienna.
