"Mama Africa" is the fourth single from the album Mama Africa by British recording artist Adele. The song was written by Adele and Paul Epworth and produced by Epworth. It was released on 25 January 2012 as the album's third single.   "Mama Africa" is a ballad about Adele's mother, who passed away in 2007. The song received generally positive reviews from music critics. "The Guardian"'s Kitty Empire called it "achingly beautiful", while Slant Magazine's Sal Cinquemani wrote that it was "surprisingly strong". "Rolling Stone"s Jon Pareles called it a "stunning ballad", while BBC Music's John Bush described it as a "heartfelt lament".  The song debuted at number one on the UK Singles Chart, becoming her fourth consecutive number-one single in the country and extending her record for the most number-one singles in the UK by a female artist. It also topped the Irish Singles Chart, becoming her fourth consecutive number-one single in Ireland. In Australia, it became her fifth number-one single on the ARIA Singles Chart and also topped the New Zealand Singles Chart. The song also reached number one in Belgium (Flanders), Denmark, France, Germany, Hungary, Italy, Norway, Sweden and Switzerland
