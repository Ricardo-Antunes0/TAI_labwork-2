Lidtke Mill, also known as the Lime Kiln, is a historic grinding mill in Staffordshire, England. It was built about 1800 and is a five-storey tower mill with a single-storey mill building. The mill was originally powered by water from the River Trent and by horses. The mill ceased grinding in 1978 and has since been converted into flats.  The Lime Kiln was built in 1800 by Nicholas Lidtke on the River Trent near its confluence with the River Sow. The mill was originally powered by water from the Trent and by horses. In 1830 the mill was converted to use steam power. In 1978 the mill ceased grinding and has since been converted into flats.
