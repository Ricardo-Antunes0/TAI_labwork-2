A starets (; fem. ) is an elder or spiritual teacher in Eastern Orthodox Christianity, who is also often a monk.  Eastern Orthodox Christians believe that there is one God in three persons, namely the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit. To learn more about the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit, people turn to their spiritual teachers or starets. A starets is an elder or spiritual teacher who has been through a rigorous process of training and can offer guidance on how to grow closer to God.
