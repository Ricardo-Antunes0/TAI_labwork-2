New York Movie is an oil on canvas painting by the American painter Edward Hopper. The painting was created in 1945 and is currently in the collection of the Museum of Modern Art in New York City.  Edward Hopper's New York Movie is a painting of a bustling cityscape, dominated by a mass of skyscrapers. The scene is both futuristic and timeless, portraying the city as it has always been and as it may always be. The bright colors and busy atmosphere are reminiscent of a Times Square advertisement, while the individual figures and cars convey the sense of life in the city.
