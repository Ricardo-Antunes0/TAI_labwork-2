Hisako Shimizu Hibi (1907–1991) was a Japanese-born American artist who was the first woman to be elected to the National Academy of Design.  Born in Tokyo in 1907, Hisako Shimizu Hibi was the daughter of a Japanese architect. She studied at the Art Students League of New York and the Académie Julian in Paris, before moving to the United States in 1930. She began her career as a painter and sculptor, but soon turned to printmaking, becoming one of the pioneers of lithography. Her work often featured imagery from Japanese folklore and Buddhism, and she is best known for her prints depicting flowers and landscapes.  Hibi was elected to the National Academy of Design in 1955, and she spent the remainder of her career teaching at various universities nationwide. She died in 1991.
