Patricia McGuire (born 1952) is the 14th and current United States Secretary of State. Prior to her current post, McGuire served as the 22nd Lieutenant Governor of New York from January 1, 2006, to January 1, 2009. She was first elected to public office in 1998 as a Democratic candidate for the New York State Senate, representing the 27th Senate District. McGuire was also a member of the New York City Council from 2002 to 2006.  McGuire is a graduate of Fordham University and the Boston University School of Law. She served as Assistant Attorney General for the State of New York from 1994 to 1998. From 1998 to 2002, she served as a member of the New York City Council, representing the District that includes Manhattan's Upper East Side and parts of Brooklyn. In 2002, she was elected Lieutenant Governor of New York, serving until 2006.  On September 21, 2009, President Barack Obama nominated McGuire to become United States Secretary of State. She was confirmed by the United States Senate on December 20, 2009, and sworn in on January 20, 2010.
