Born in Glasgow, McNicol attended Glasgow University before becoming a journalist with the "Glasgow Herald" in 1954. He joined the Labour Party in 1956 and became active in local politics, serving on Glasgow City Council from 1967 to 1974 and as its leader from 1972 to 1974.   McNicol was elected to Parliament as the MP for Glasgow Pollok in February 1974, becoming Parliamentary Private Secretary to James Callaghan, the then Prime Minister. He was appointed Home Secretary in 1979, serving until 1981, when he was sacked by Margaret Thatcher. McNicol then served as Chancellor of the Exchequer from 1981 to 1983.  McNicol retired from Parliament at the 1983 general election and became a consultant. He died in 2014 aged 83.
