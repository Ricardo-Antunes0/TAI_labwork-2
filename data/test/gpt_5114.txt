William West Sr. FLS (22 February 1848 â€“ 11 December 1924) was a British botanist who specialised in the study of ferns.   West was born in Bow, London, the son of a gardener. He studied at the Royal Botanic Gardens, Kew, from 1868 to 1872, and then at the University of Edinburgh, where he received his doctorate in 1875. From 1876 to 1879 he was assistant curator at the Royal Botanic Gardens, Kew. In 1879 he became lecturer on botany at the University of Liverpool and in 1890 professor of botany and director of the Liverpool Botanic Garden. He retired in 1912.  West died in Liverpool in 1924. His son William West Jr. (1878-1949) also became a botanist.
