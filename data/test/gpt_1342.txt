Robert Hugh Ferrell (May 8, 1921 – August 4, 2009) was an American mathematician and professor of mathematics at the University of Utah. Ferrell was known for his work in analytic number theory and algebra.  Ferrell was born in 1921 in Salt Lake City, Utah. After receiving his undergraduate degree from the University of Utah in 1942, Ferrell served in the United States Navy during World War II. He returned to the University of Utah to complete his graduate studies, receiving his Ph.D. in 1948.  Ferrell began his teaching career at the University of Utah in 1948, where he served as a professor of mathematics until his retirement in 1992. He was a fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences and the Mathematical Association of America. Ferrell died on August 4, 2009 in Salt Lake City at the age of 87.
