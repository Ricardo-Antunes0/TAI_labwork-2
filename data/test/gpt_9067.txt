Clinostomum marginatum is a species of parasitic alveolates in the phylum Apicomplexa. It is a fluke that infects the pulmonary artery of vertebrates, causing a serious blood infection. The parasite was first described in 1882 by Charles Joseph Minot and was named after the Belgian anatomist Auguste Marguerite Marguerite Clinchy.
