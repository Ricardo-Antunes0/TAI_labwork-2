The Battle of Abensberg took place on 13 May 1799 during the French Revolutionary Wars. A French army under Jean Victor Marie Moreau attacked a combined Austrian and Bavarian force led by Archduke Charles of Austria. The Austrians were forced to retreat and the French won a strategic victory.  The Battle of Abensberg was one of the few victories achieved by the French military during the French Revolutionary Wars. It occurred as part of the War of the Second Coalition, part of a larger conflict called the Napoleonic Wars. The French army attacked an Austrian army led by Archduke Charles, Duke of Teschen.
