"Moneytalks" is a song written by Malcolm McLaren and Noel Gallagher. It was originally recorded by the English punk rock band The Sex Pistols as the B-side to their single "Anarchy in the UK".  The song is about the evils of capitalism and consumerism, and has been described as a "propaganda anthem".   Moneytalks was first released as a single in 1978, and was later included on The Sex Pistols' 1979 album Never Mind the Bollocks, Here's the Sex Pistols. The song reached number nine on the UK Singles Chart. In 2004, it was voted number 10 in Channel 4's 100 Greatest Singles of All Time.
