Changdeokgung (Hangul: 창덕궁, Hanja: 昌德宮; literally, "The Palace of Peace and Harmony") is a palace in Seoul, South Korea. It is the main royal residence of the Korean Monarchy and the largest palace in the country. The palace complex also includes the National Theater of Korea, Changdeokgung Palace Museum, and Gyeongbokgung Palace.  Changdeokgung was originally built in 1414 and enlarged several times since then. The present structure was completed in 1876 under the reign of King Gojong. The palace has been the official residence of the Korean Monarchy since 1916. The current monarch, King Sejong III, resides at Changdeokgung Palace along with his consort Queen Solmokhwa.  The Changdeokgung Palace Complex is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
