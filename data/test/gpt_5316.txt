Iztok Osojnik (born 27 July 1951) is a Slovenian historian and academic.  Iztok Osojnik was born in Ljubljana, Slovenia, on 27 July 1951. He received his undergraduate degree from the University of Ljubljana in 1973, and his doctorate from the same institution in 1978.   From 1978 to 1981, Osojnik was a postdoctoral fellow at the University of California, Los Angeles. From 1981 to 1987, he was a professor of history at the University of Ljubljana. He has been a full professor of history at the University of Ljubljana since 1987.   Osojnik has been a member of the Slovenian Academy of Sciences and Arts since 1990 and its president since 2003. He has also been a member of the Croatian Academy of Sciences and Arts since 1997.   Osojnik's main research interests are medieval history and historiography, modern European history, and Slovene national history. He has written extensively on these topics, publishing several books and articles on medieval history, historiography, modern European history, and Slovene national history. His most famous book is "Medieval Slovenia: A Socio-Historical Study" (1989), which has been translated into several languages.
