Joseph William Singer is an American legal scholar and jurist who has been a professor at Yale Law School since 1965. He is the co-author, with Jerome Frank, of The Law of Torts (4th ed. 2008), and the author of numerous articles on tort law, contracts, property law, criminal law, and constitutional law.  Born in Cleveland, Ohio, on March 22, 1926, Singer graduated from Yale College in 1948 and Yale Law School in 1953. He was an editor-in-chief of the Yale Law Journal from 1959 to 1963 and was a lecturer at Yale Law School from 1954 to 1965. He was a law clerk to Judge Learned Hand of the United States Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit from 1953 to 1954.  Singer has served as a member of the American Law Institute (1962–1965), the Connecticut Bar Association (1966), the American Academy of Arts and Sciences (1987), the faculty of the Harvard University Program on International Business Law (1995–1996), and the faculty of The New York University School of Law (1996–1999). He is a fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences and the American College of Trial Lawyers.  Singer was appointed to the United States District Court for the District of Connecticut by President Jimmy Carter on May 20, 1979, and was nominated to serve on the United States Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit by President Ronald Reagan on October 15, 1985. He was confirmed by the United States Senate on December 5, 1985
