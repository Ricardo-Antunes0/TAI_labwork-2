Mr William Collins is a fictional character in the novel, Great Expectations, by Charles Dickens. He is the narrator's friend and one of the main protagonists.  William Collins is the son of a wealthy English gentleman, Mr. Collins. William is a brilliant young man, but he is also arrogant and vain. He falls in love with Miss Pross, an impoverished girl who lives in a workhouse. He is determined to win her affection, even if it means insulting her or using rough treatment.  Eventually, William realizes that he loves Miss Pross for her own sake, not for her money or social status. He proposes marriage to her and she accepts. They are married soon after and live happily ever after.
