Raúl del Cristi Gómez Jattin (31 May 1928 – 12 October 2006) was a Mexican musician, singer and composer. He is considered one of the most important and influential figures in the history of Mexican popular music.  Born in Mexicali, Baja California, Gómez Jattin started his musical career as a teenager in the 1950s, playing in local cantinas and nightclubs. In the early 1960s he formed his own band, Los Tres Reyes, which became one of Mexico's most popular ensembles. Over the next two decades he recorded dozens of albums and charted with hits such as "Cuando me quieras", "No me digas que no vale la pena", "El día que te fuiste" and "Aún no termino de creer". He also collaborated with other leading Mexican artists, including Pedro Infante, Lucero and José José.  In 2001 Gómez Jattin was awarded the National Prize for Popular Arts. He died in 2006 at the age of 80 after a long illness.
