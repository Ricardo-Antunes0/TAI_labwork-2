Sarah Ann Douglas (born January 25, 1944) is an American actress. She has received three Golden Globe Awards, two Emmy Awards, and a BAFTA Award.  Douglas was born in Chicago, Illinois, the daughter of Elizabeth (n√©e Pollard), a teacher, and Charles Edward Douglas, an advertising executive. Douglas has English, Irish, Scottish, and German ancestry. When she was five years old, her family moved to Santa Monica, California. Douglas attended Santa Monica High School. After graduating from high school in 1962, she attended the University of California, Berkeley and studied philosophy. She dropped out after a year to pursue an acting career.  Douglas made her acting debut in the Off-Broadway play "Who's Afraid of Virginia Woolf?" in 1967. She won a Golden Globe Award for Best New Star for her performance in the same play. Douglas then appeared on television in roles such as that of Dr. Lisa Cuddy on "The West Wing" from 1999 to 2006.
