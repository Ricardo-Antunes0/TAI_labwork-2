Melquíades Rafael Ruiz Martínez (born October 23, 1978 in Santo Domingo, Dominican Republic) is a retired professional baseball outfielder.  Martínez played for the Milwaukee Brewers, Colorado Rockies, and Detroit Tigers of Major League Baseball. He batted and threw right-handed.  Martínez was signed by the Milwaukee Brewers as an amateur free agent in 1998. He made his MLB debut with the Brewers on May 8, 2001. He played for the Rockies from 2003 to 2006, and then played for the Tigers from 2007 to 2010. He batted .263 with 107 home runs and 502 RBI in 1,345 games. In 2011, he played for the Yokohama Baystars of Japan's Nippon Professional Baseball.   Martínez retired in 2011 after spending time with both the Baystars and the Mexican League's Leones del Caracas. In 2012, he served as a player-coach for the Tigres de Quintana Roo in Mexico's Liga Baseball Americana.   In 2013, Martínez became a batting coach for the Bridgeport Bluefish of the Atlantic League of Professional Baseball. In 2018, he joined the Tigres de la Ligua of the Venezuelan Professional Baseball League as their hitting coach.
