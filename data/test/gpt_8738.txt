Iskar (, ; also transliterated as Iskur, Ishkur, and Ishkur) is a town and district in the autonomous province of Kurdistan in northern Iraq. The town has a population of around 21,000 people.  Iskar is located on the banks of the Iskar River, about north of the provincial capital Erbil. Historically, Iskar was an important agricultural center, producing wheat, barley, and other crops. The town also has a zinc smelter and a cement factory.  The Iskar district was formed in 2003 as a result of the division of the Arbil province. The town of Iskar was designated as the district seat.
