Marsha Coleman-Adebayo is an American former senior executive in banking and finance. She served as the chief executive officer of BB&T Corporation from 2007 to 2010, and then as its chairman until resigning in 2014.  Coleman-Adebayo was born in Fort Lauderdale, Florida, in 1961. She received her bachelor's degree from Stanford University in 1983 and her MBA from the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania in 1987.  Coleman-Adebayo began her career at BB&T in 1987 as a financial analyst. She was named vice president and controller in 1993, and became president and CEO in 2007. During her tenure, BB&T grew from a regional bank with $14 billion in assets to a national powerhouse with more than $250 billion in assets. In 2010, Coleman-Adebayo was named chairman of BB&T Corporation, a position she held until resigning in 2014.  Coleman-Adebayo is currently a member of the board of directors of JPMorgan Chase & Co., Wells Fargo & Co., and First Horizon National Corporation. She also serves on the boards of directors for the National Bank for Economic Development and the American Bankers Association. Coleman-Adebayo is also a co-founder of impact investment firm Starboard Value LP.
