The Constitution of Bhutan (Dzongkha: འབྲུག་གི་རྩ་ཁྲིམས་ཆེན་མོ་; Wylie: zhing kyi bum pa'i dzong) is the supreme law of Bhutan. It was adopted on 5 June 2008, and came into force on 17 July 2008. The Constitution is the first written constitution in the world to be based on the philosophy of Gross National Happiness.  The Constitution establishes a parliamentary system with an upper house, the National Council, and a lower house, the National Assembly. The head of state is the King who is represented by the Prime Minister. The Constitution also establishes civil rights and liberties, including freedom of speech, freedom of assembly, and freedom of religion. The Constitution prohibits discrimination based on race, sex, or religion.
