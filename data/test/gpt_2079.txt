The Connaught Medical Research Laboratories was a research facility in Galway, Ireland. It was founded in 1917 by Sir William Connaught, 1st Baronet, who was then the Secretary of State for War.   Connaught Laboratories was one of the largest and most important biomedical research organisations in the world and it is now part of GlaxoSmithKline. The laboratories were involved in a wide range of medical research projects, including work on viruses, cancer and heart disease.
