Thomas Edward Hassan (born ) is an American computer scientist, known for his work in artificial intelligence and machine learning.  Hassan was born in Arlington, Virginia, on February 20, 1966. He received a B.S. in Computer Science from the University of Virginia in 1988 and an M.S. and D.S. in Computer Science from Stanford University in 1990 and 1991, respectively.  He was a member of the faculty at the University of Maryland from 1991 to 2002, where he was the Chair of the Department of Computer Science and Co-Director of the Institute for Artificial Intelligence. He then became a Professor at the University of California, Berkeley where he is now a Distinguished Professor in the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences.  Hassan's research focuses on artificial intelligence, machine learning, and natural language processing. He has published over 350 papers in these fields and has been cited over 8,000 times according to Google Scholar. He has been awarded over $40 million in grants from funding agencies such as the National Science Foundation, DARPA, and Microsoft Research.  In 2016, he was elected as a Fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences "for contributions to artificial intelligence and machine learning".
