Betsey Ross Wright (born July 4, 1943) is an American politician who served as the 24th United States Secretary of State from 2009 to 2013. Prior to that, she was the 65th Governor of Connecticut from 1999 to 2007.  Wright is a Democrat who formerly served as the Chair of the Democratic National Committee (DNC). She played a prominent role in the Obama presidential campaigns of 2008 and 2012, serving as the campaign's national spokesperson and as a top strategist. Wright was also a key figure in the administration's response to the 2012 Benghazi attacks and in negotiations over the Affordable Care Act.  Wright was born in Chicago, Illinois, and raised in Danbury, Connecticut. After graduating from high school, Wright attended Wellesley College, where she majored in political science. She then earned her J.D. from Yale Law School.  Wright worked as a lawyer before entering politics. In 1994, she was elected as the first female mayor of Stamford, Connecticut. In 1998, Wright was elected Governor of Connecticut, becoming the first woman to serve as governor of a U.S. state. As governor, Wright focused on improving education and economic opportunities for Connecticut residents, championed health care reform, and increased access to renewable energy resources.  In 2009, Wright was appointed U.S. Secretary of State by then-President Barack Obama after serving as chair of his transition team.
