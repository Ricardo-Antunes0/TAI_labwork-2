Hrafnsmál (Old Norse: ; "raven song") is a poem from the Poetic Edda, composed in the 10th century by Snorri Sturluson. The poem is composed in a skaldic style and tells the story of a hero who defeats a giant.  The poem is about Hrólfr Kraki, a viking chieftain who challenges a giant, Þjálfi, to a duel. Þjálfi accepts and the two meet at Hrafnsmál Pass. Hrólfr Kraki kills Þjálfi with his bare hands and reclaims his kingdom. The poem celebrates Hrólfr Kraki's strength and bravery.
