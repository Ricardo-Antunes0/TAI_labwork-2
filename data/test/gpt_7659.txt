Frank M. Gibson (born 1916 or 1917) is an American composer, conductor, and music educator.  Gibson was born in Kansas City, Missouri, and studied at the University of Kansas and the Juilliard School. He served in the United States Army during World War II, where he became a music instructor. After the war, he worked as a composer and conductor for television and film. Gibson died in 2018 at the age of 98.
