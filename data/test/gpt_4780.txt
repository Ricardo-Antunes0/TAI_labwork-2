Enschede is the main railway station in the city of Enschede, in the Dutch province of Overijssel. The station has 14 platforms, making it the second busiest railway station in the country after Rotterdam Centraal. The main entrance is on the east side of the station.     Enschede railway station is operated by Nederlandse Spoorwegen (NS). The station is also a major hub for Arriva Netherlands. The station building was designed by Willem Marinus Dudok and built between 1868 and 1871. It was listed as a national heritage site in 1988.
