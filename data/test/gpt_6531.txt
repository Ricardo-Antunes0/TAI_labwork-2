Sékou Kourouma (1956 – 18 April 2020) was a Burkinabé politician who served as President of Burkina Faso from 17 July 2014 to 18 April 2020. Previously he had been Prime Minister of Burkina Faso from 22 October 2009 to 18 April 2020.  Kourouma was born in 1956 in the town of Bobo-Dioulasso in the north-east of Burkina Faso. He has a degree in law from the University of Ouagadougou.  Kourouma first became involved in politics in the early 1990s, when he served as Secretary-General of the Rally for Democracy and Progress (RDP), an opposition party. He was subsequently elected to the National Assembly as a RDP member in 1997, and served as Deputy Prime Minister under Blaise Compaoré from 2000 to 2002.  In October 2009, following Compaoré's election as President of Burkina Faso, Kourouma was appointed Prime Minister. He led a government that was generally supportive of Compaoré's rule, and played an important role in the transition period following Compaoré's ousting in a military coup in September 2012.  On 17 July 2014, Kourouma was elected President of Burkina Faso by popular vote, defeating former Prime Minister Jean-Michel Blaise Compaoré. As President, Kourouma continued to support Compaoré's former regime and maintained tight control over the military
