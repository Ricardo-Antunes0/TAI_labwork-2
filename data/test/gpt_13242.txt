Dave was born in Bombay (now Mumbai), India, to an Anglo-Indian father, Harry Dave, and an Indian mother, Kamala Dave. She began her performing career as a child, appearing in several stage productions with her family. In 1954 she moved to England with her family and began taking singing lessons.  Dave's first television appearance was in the children's programme "Tiny Toon Adventures" in 1961. She made her professional singing debut the following year with the London Symphony Orchestra at the Royal Albert Hall. She appeared in numerous British musicals and soap operas during the 1960s and 1970s, including "The Avengers", "The New Adventures of Robin Hood", "Crossroads" and "The City". In 1973 she recorded her only album, "Eva Dave".  In 1978 Dave married producer Tony Visconti and retired from performing. She later appeared in a number of television documentaries and made occasional appearances at music festivals. Dave died from cancer in 2003 at the age of 71.
