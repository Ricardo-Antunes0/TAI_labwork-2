Dorothy Emmerson is an American actress and singer. She is known for her work in the Broadway revival of Cabaret, for which she won the Tony Award for Best Actress in a Musical.  Emmerson was born in Cambridge, Massachusetts, to Katherine (née Garvey), an artist and teacher, and John Emmerson, a financial analyst and engineer. She has two sisters, Emily and Julia.  Emmerson first attended the Cambridge School of Weston before transferring to the prestigious New England Conservatory of Music in Boston, where she studied music theory and performance. After graduating from NECM in 2002 with a BFA in music theater, Emmerson moved to New York City to begin her acting career.   Emmerson made her Broadway debut in 2003 as Sally Bowles in the revival of Cabaret starring Alan Cumming. She won the Tony Award for Best Actress in a Musical for her performance.   In 2007, Emmerson starred as Elle Woods in the Broadway revival of Legally Blonde starring Reese Witherspoon. The production was nominated for nine Tony Awards, including Best Revival of a Musical.   In 2013, Emmerson starred as Fantine in Tom Hooper's stage adaptation of Les Misérables at London's Olivier Theatre.
