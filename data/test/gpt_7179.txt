Terry Ruane (born 21 November 1946 in Limerick, Ireland) is a former Irish rugby union player and coach. He played as a lock and captained the Ireland national team from 1976 to 1980. Ruane was also head coach of the Ireland national rugby union team from 1984 to 1986.  Ruane played rugby for Limerick in the 1966-67 and 1967-68 seasons before moving to England to play for Leicester Tigers. He won two Heineken Cup titles with Leicester in 1971 and 1973. Ruane also played for the British and Irish Lions on their 1974 tour of New Zealand.  After retiring from playing, Ruane became a coach, first with Leicester Tigers and later with Munster. He was head coach of Ireland from 1984 to 1986, leading them to the 1986 Rugby World Cup semi-finals. He was also appointed head coach of the Irish national side in 2003, but resigned after just two months in the role.
