Soe Win (; ; born 1 March 1951) is a Burmese military general who was the Commander-in-Chief of the Tatmadaw (the Myanmar Armed Forces) from September 2017 until his resignation on 10 December 2017.  Soe Win was born in March 1951 in Shwebo, Sagaing Region. He joined the military in 1969 and rose through the ranks. He served as the Deputy Commander-in-Chief of the Myanmar Army from 2007 to 2009. In September 2017, he was appointed Commander-in-Chief of the Tatmadaw.  Soe Win has been criticized for his role in the suppression of pro-democracy protests in Myanmar in 2007 and 2008. In November 2017, he was accused by Human Rights Watch of ordering soldiers to massacre Rohingya Muslims in Myanmar's Rakhine State. On 10 December 2017, he resigned from his position as Commander-in-Chief of the Tatmadaw.
