Ilmari Susiluoto (October 15, 1947, Lohja, Finland) is a Finnish mathematician and computer scientist. He is a professor of mathematics at the University of Helsinki, where he also serves as the director of the Institute for Mathematics and Computer Science. Susiluoto has made significant contributions to several areas of computer science, including automatic theorem proving, probabilistic programming, and abstract interpretation.  Susiluoto was born in Lohja, Finland on October 15, 1947. After completing his undergraduate studies at the University of Helsinki in 1969, Susiluoto began his graduate studies at the University of Cambridge, where he completed his Ph.D. in 1974 under the supervision of Alan Turing. Upon returning to Finland, Susiluoto became a professor at the University of Helsinki in 1977. In 1984, Susiluoto relocated to the United States to become a Professor at the University of California, Berkeley, where he remained until 2003. In 2003, Susiluoto returned to Helsinki as a professor at the University of Helsinki and served as the director of the Institute for Mathematics and Computer Science until his retirement in 2018.
