Lubert Stryer (born March 2, 1938, in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania) is an American biochemist and the current William R. Kenan, Jr. Professor of Biochemistry at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill where he also served as the Director of the Kenan-Flagler Center for Biomedical Research from 2002-2013.  Lubert Stryer is a biochemist who has made significant contributions to our understanding of the structure and function of proteins. His work has helped us to better understand how proteins interact with other molecules and to figure out how they are able to carry out their various functions. Dr. Stryer has also developed methods for studying protein structure and function in vitro, which has helped us to develop more effective treatments for diseases such as cancer. He is a member of the National Academy of Sciences and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.
