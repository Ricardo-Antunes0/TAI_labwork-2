Al-Hadi Ali (1345/46 – September 6, 1432) was a Persian historian, religious figure, and vizier of the Mamluk Sultanate of Egypt. He is considered one of the leading experts on Islamic history.  Al-Hadi Ali was born in 1345/46 in Cairo, then part of the Mamluk Sultanate of Egypt. He began his education at an early age and soon became interested in history and religion. In 1362/63, he started to work as a secretary to the Mamluk governor of Mecca. In 1375/76, he was appointed vizier of Damascus and served in that position until his death in 1432.  Al-Hadi Ali was a prolific historian and wrote extensively on Islamic history, theology, and philosophy. His most important works are the Kitāb al-Tanbīh (Book of Precepts), a comprehensive guide to Islamic law, and the Tārīkh al-Islām (History of Islam), which is one of the most comprehensive histories of Islam ever written.
