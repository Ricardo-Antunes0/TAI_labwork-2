He is best known for his work on the development of the first practical microwave oven, and for his work on the development of high-power microwave sources and antennas.  Yang Gensi was born in 1922 in Qingdao, Shandong province. After graduating from high school, he entered Tsinghua University in 1941. During World War II, he served in the Imperial Japanese Army as a military engineer. After the war, he returned to Tsinghua University and completed his undergraduate studies in 1948. In 1951, he obtained his PhD from Tsinghua University with a thesis on microwave technology.  From 1951 to 1966, Yang Gensi worked at the National Radio Research Laboratory of China (NRRL), where he developed the first practical microwave oven and extensively worked on high-power microwave sources and antennas. In 1966, he moved to Beijing Institute of Technology (BIT), where he served as a professor until his retirement in 1991.  Yang Gensi was awarded several prestigious awards during his lifetime, including the State Science and Technology Award (1981), the National Natural Science Award (1986), and the Chinese Academy of Sciences' prestigious Caius Prize (1993). He died on June 18, 2007 at the age of 86.
