Peter Glantz is an American director of the Natural Resources Defense Council (NRDC). He is also a senior fellow at the Center for American Progress.  Glantz was born in Cleveland, Ohio, in 1954. He received his B.A. from Brown University in 1977 and his J.D. from Yale Law School in 1980. From 1980 to 1984, he worked as a law clerk to Judge Arthur W. Goldberg of the United States Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit.  From 1984 to 1987, Glantz was a staff attorney at the Natural Resources Defense Council. From 1987 to 1991, he was a senior attorney at the NRDC. From 1991 to 1995, he was the director of the NRDC's International Program.  Since 1995, Glantz has been a senior fellow at the Center for American Progress, where he is the director of the Energy and Environment Program and a member of the CAP's Environment and Economics Task Force. Glantz also served as a special advisor to Vice President Al Gore on climate change from 2001 to 2003, and as a member of Gore's Presidential Inaugural Committee in January 2001.  In 2004, Glantz was awarded an honorary doctorate from Brown University. In 2009, he received an honorary degree from City University of New York School of Law.  Glantz is an outspoken critic of hydraulic fracturing (fracking), which he has described as "the most important environmental issue we face."
