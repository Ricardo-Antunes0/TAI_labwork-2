Steve Ludlam is a defense industry engineer and inventor, who is known for his work in the fields of stealth technology and thermal management systems. Ludlam has also made contributions to the design of aircraft engines and missile systems.  Ludlam was born on October 10, 1960, in Perth, Western Australia. He received his B.Eng. degree from the University of Western Australia in 1982 and his M.S. degree from the Royal Military College of Canada in 1984. Ludlam worked for General Electric for five years before joining Northrop Grumman in 1988. He has held various engineering positions with Northrop Grumman, including director of aircraft engine development, vice president of design and engineering, and president of the company's Defense Systems Group. In 2002, Ludlam was appointed vice president and chief scientist at Raytheon Missile Systems. He left Raytheon in 2006 to become a professor at the University of Southern California's Viterbi School of Engineering. Ludlam is also a Fellow of the American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics (AIAA) and the Royal Aeronautical Society (RAeS).
