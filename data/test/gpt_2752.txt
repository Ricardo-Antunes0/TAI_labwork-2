Nantwich railway station serves the town of Nantwich, Cheshire, England. The station is on the Crewe to Manchester Line and is north of Manchester Piccadilly.  The station was opened by the London and North Western Railway on 1 September 1846 as Nantwich Town. It was renamed Nantwich railway station on 1 December 1847. The station was nationalised into British Railways on 1 January 1948. In 2015, the station was the subject of a campaign to reopen it as a heritage railway.   In 2018, the station was renamed Nantwich North after being merged with Nantwich South.   The typical off-peak service is three trains per hour to Manchester Piccadilly and one train per hour to Crewe. There is also an hourly service to Wrexham General.
