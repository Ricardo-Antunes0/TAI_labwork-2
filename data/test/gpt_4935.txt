Ryan was born in Dorchester, Boston, Massachusetts, to Irish Catholic parents. He attended Boston Latin School and then Boston College, where he received his B.A. in 1951. He then attended Boston University School of Law, where he was graduated with his J.D. in 1955. Ryan began his legal career as a law clerk for Judge Arthur Garrity of the U.S. District Court for the District of Massachusetts.  In 1958, Ryan was elected as a Democrat to the Massachusetts House of Representatives, representing Dorchester County. In 1962, he was elected to the Massachusetts Senate, serving until his election as governor in 1974. While in the Senate, Ryan chaired the Committee on Labor and Public Welfare and the Committee on Rules and Administration.  As governor, Ryan presided over a period of economic growth and improved public health conditions in Massachusetts. He made welfare reform a top priority, signing into law reforms that eliminated Aid to Families with Dependent Children (AFDC), replaced it with a new program called Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF), and created work requirements for people receiving benefits.
