Erik Solheim (born 18 January 1955) is a Norwegian business magnate, environmentalist and politician. He is the CEO of the energy company Norsk Hydro, and is a former Minister of Trade and Industry in the Norwegian government.  Born in Oslo, Solheim is the son of Arnulf Solheim, a Conservative Party politician who served as Minister of Labour in the second cabinet Stoltenberg. He studied economics at the University of Oslo and graduated in 1978. He then worked for his father's company Norsk Hydro from 1978 to 1986, when he became CEO. From 1990 to 1997 he was chairman of the board of NORAD (Norwegian Aerospace Defense Command).  Solheim was a member of Oslo City Council from 1988 to 1991 and from 1995 to 2001. He served as a deputy representative to the Norwegian Parliament from Oslo during the term 1989–1993. He was elected as a member of parliament for Akershus during the term 1993–1997, serving as Minister of Trade and Industry from 1996 to 1997.  From 2002 to 2004 he served as president of The Norwegian Enterprise Agency. From 2004 to 2007 he chaired The Green Growth Business Forum (VG Børsen). In 2007 he founded The Green Investment Bank which he chaired until its dissolution in 2013.  Solheim has been a board member of several environmental organizations, including WWF Norway, The Nature Conservancy Norway and Naturvernforbundet. He is also chairman of The Climate Foundation, which he co-founded in 2007.
