Gyula Pados (; born 2 April 1969) is a Hungarian football manager and former player.  Pados played professionally for Ferencváros, Videoton and Újpest between 1986 and 1999. He made his international debut for Hungary in a 1990 FIFA World Cup qualifying match against Turkey and earned a total of 63 caps, scoring 10 goals. He represented his country at the 1998, 2002 and 2006 World Cups.  Pados began his managerial career with Videoton in 2001, helping them to two Hungarian League titles and one Hungarian Cup. He was appointed manager of Újpest in 2008, leading them to the 2009–10 Magyar Kupa title. In July 2010, he was appointed manager of the Hungary national team, but resigned from his position after just nine months in charge.
