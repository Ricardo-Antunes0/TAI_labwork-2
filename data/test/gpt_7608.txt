Mohammad Obaid Kamal (born 4 September 1972) is a Pakistani politician who has been the Prime Minister of Pakistan since 11 August 2013, after being appointed by the President of Pakistan. He is the first Pakistani to hold the office.  Kamal was born in 1972 in Sialkot, Punjab, Pakistan. He is a graduate of the University of Oxford where he obtained a degree in Modern History. He also holds an MBA from Harvard Business School.  He began his career as a banker at the National Bank of Pakistan, rising to become its Vice President in charge of corporate and investment banking. In 2001, he became the chairman of the board of directors at United Bank Limited (UBL).  In 2008, Kamal was elected as a Member of the National Assembly from Constituency NA-122 (Sialkot-I) as a candidate of the Pakistan Peoples Party (PPP). He served as Minister for Planning and Development from June 2008 to March 2010 in the cabinet of Prime Minister Yousaf Raza Gilani.   In October 2012, Kamal was appointed as Advisor to Prime Minister Yousaf Raza Gilani on Foreign Affairs. On 11 August 2013, he was appointed Prime Minister after Gilani resigned due to a constitutional crisis over his nomination for a third term.   Kamal is married and has two children.
