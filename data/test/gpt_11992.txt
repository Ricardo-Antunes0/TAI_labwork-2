Norman Haire, born Norman Zions (21 January 1924 – 10 October 2010), was an English actor who was known for his roles in British cinema and on Broadway. He was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Actor for his performance in the 1978 film The Deer Hunter.  Haire was born in Liverpool, Lancashire, the son of Jewish immigrants, Sadie (née Schwartz) and Emanuel Zions. His family moved to London when he was three years old. After attending a local primary school and a secondary school in Whitechapel, he began training at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art in 1943. Haire made his stage debut in 1945 at the Old Vic Theatre, appearing as Lennie Small in a production of George Bernard Shaw's "Androcles and the Lion".  Haire's film debut came in the 1948 British film "The Lavender Hill Mob". He appeared in many films throughout the 1950s and 1960s, including appearances as Stephen Dedalus in Stanley Kubrick's seminal "A Clockwork Orange" (1971), as well as roles in such films as "The Italian Job" (1969), "The Great Escape" (1963), "Saturday Night Fever" (1977), and "The Boys from Brazil" (1978).  Haire achieved international recognition for his performance as Captain Nathan Jameson in Peter Bogdanovich's acclaimed 1978 film The Deer Hunter.
