The 1990 All-American Bowl was an American college football bowl game played on December 31, 1990 at Sun Devil Stadium in Tempe, Arizona. The game was the fifteenth edition of the All-American Bowl and featured the West Coast Conference (WCC) champion UCLA Bruins (10–2) against the Big Eight Conference (Big 8) champion Kansas State Wildcats (10–2).  UCLA was recognized as a co-national champion with Florida State after both teams finished with identical 10–2 records. UCLA was selected to represent the Western Division in the Fiesta Bowl, while Kansas State was selected to represent the Eastern Division in the Orange Bowl.   UCLA won 34–14, behind running back Marcus Allen's two touchdowns and 204 yards on 36 carries. Quarterback Rick Neuheisel completed 16 of 33 passes for 202 yards and one touchdown.
