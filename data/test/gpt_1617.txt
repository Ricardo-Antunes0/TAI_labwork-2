The gens Caelia was a plebeian family at Rome. Gaius Caelius was the first of this gens to be known to history.  The Caelii were a plebeian family at Rome. Gaius Caelius was the first of this gens to be known to history. He was tribune of the people in 233 BC, and he is mentioned in the same year in connection with two suits brought by the plebeians against the patrician Cornelius Scipio Nasica. In 222 BC he was consul with Terentius Varro, and he is mentioned as one of the conspirators who planned to kill the consul, but his plot was discovered and he was executed.
