The Jefferson Barracks Bridge, officially the Jefferson Barracks Memorial Bridge, is a bridge in Saint Louis, Missouri that spans the Mississippi River. It is named after Thomas Jefferson, the third President of the United States.   The bridge is located in the eastern section of the city and connects Jefferson Barracks with Tower Grove Park. The bridge opened on November 10, 1921 and cost $4.5 million to construct.
