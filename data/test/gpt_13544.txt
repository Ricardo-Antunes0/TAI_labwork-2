Richard Elovich (born 1954) is a social scientist and historian who specializes in historical demography and social networks. He is the Luis A. Moreno Professor of Sociology at the University of California, Irvine.  Elovich earned his Ph.D. from the University of Michigan in 1983, and has been at UC Irvine since 1988. He is a fellow of the American Sociological Association, and a past president of the American Historical Association.  He has written or edited nine books, including The New Social Networks: An Introduction (1996), which was named one of the best books of 1996 by The New York Times Book Review and Choice magazine, and co-edited with James Henslin, Networks and Communities: Social Structure and Interaction (1998). He has also published more than 150 articles in professional journals, including the American Journal of Sociology, Social Science Quarterly, and Social Networks.
