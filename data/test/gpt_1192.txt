He initially trained with his father, but then studied at the Royal Academy of Art in Amsterdam from 1753 to 1760. He became a member of the Haarlem Guild of St. Luke in 1761 and was elected a member of the Royal Academy of Arts in 1768. He was a friend of Johannes Vermeer and participated in the painting of several of Vermeer's works, including The Music Lesson (1764) and Girl with a Pearl Earring (1765).
