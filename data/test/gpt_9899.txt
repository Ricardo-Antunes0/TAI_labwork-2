Joseph Conrad Étienne Dion (August 11, 1918 – October 28, 1995) was a Canadian singer and actor. He was known for his powerful voice and for his renditions of classic French and Italian songs.  Born in Montreal, Quebec, Dion began his career in show business as a teenager, performing in local venues and making his debut on Canadian radio in 1939. After moving to New York City in 1948, he signed with Columbia Records and released his first album the following year. His success continued with subsequent albums released throughout the 1950s and 1960s, including such classics as "My Way", "Les Belles Belles", "All By Myself", and "A Song for You". He achieved international fame in 1965 with the release of the album "Dion", which sold over 25 million copies worldwide. Dion's subsequent albums continued to sell well, and he became one of the most popular singers of the late 1960s and early 1970s.  In the later part of his career, Dion became known for his humanitarian work. He performed numerous benefit concerts throughout the world, including a series of shows to help rebuild Haiti following its devastating earthquake in 2010. He received numerous awards throughout his career, including five Grammy Awards, five Golden Globe Awards, an Academy Award nomination for Best Actor, and a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.
