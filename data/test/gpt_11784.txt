Joseph Francis Fagan (12 March 1921 – 9 December 1991) was an Irish Fine Gael politician who served as Minister for Education in the governments of Charles Haughey and Liam Cosgrave.  Born in Tralee, County Kerry, Fagan was educated at the Christian Brothers School in Tralee and St. Patrick's College, Maynooth. He taught mathematics at Blackrock College from 1941 to 1945. He was elected to Dáil Éireann as a Fine Gael Teachta Dála (TD) for the Kerry South constituency at the 1948 general election. He remained a TD until he retired from politics at the 1981 general election.  Fagan served as Minister for Education in the governments of Charles Haughey and Liam Cosgrave. In this role, he introduced free secondary education for all in Ireland, increased spending on education, and improved teacher training. He also established the National Institute of Educational Research. Fagan was a controversial figure within Irish politics, frequently accused of corruption by his opponents. He retired from politics at the 1981 general election, after which he became a director of several companies. Fagan died in 1991 at the age of 71.
