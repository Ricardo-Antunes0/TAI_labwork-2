Bryheem Hancock (born March 1, 1980 in Troy, Michigan) is an American basketball player who last played for the Memphis Grizzlies of the National Basketball Association (NBA). He is 6 feet 9 inches tall and plays center.  Hancock attended Troy High School, where he was a three-time All-State selection and was a member of the National Honor Society. He played college basketball at Michigan State University, where he was a two-time First Team All-Big Ten selection and helped the Spartans reach the NCAA Tournament in his senior year.  Hancock was drafted by the Philadelphia 76ers with the tenth overall pick in the 2000 NBA Draft. He spent four seasons with the 76ers, before being traded to the Miami Heat in 2006. He then spent two seasons with the Heat before signing with the Grizzlies in 2009. Hancock has also played for the Utah Jazz, New Orleans Hornets, and Detroit Pistons. In 2012, he signed with Turkish club Kayserispor. Hancock has appeared in more than 800 NBA games and averaged 10.5 points and 7.4 rebounds per game.  Hancock is married to LaToya Sedrick and they have two children.
