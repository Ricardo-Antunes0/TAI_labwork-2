The Fort de Condé, or Fort Pille, is a fortification located in the commune of Condé-sur-Aisne, in the department of Nord, in northern France. The fort was built between 1812 and 1814 to protect the important crossing of the Aisne River.   The fort is an octagonal structure with a total perimeter of 7.5 kilometers. It is made out of brick and has a height of 15 meters. The fort has six bastions and is protected by a ditch and a moat. The fort also has two cannons that can fire 105 mm shells. Today, the fort is used as a military museum.
