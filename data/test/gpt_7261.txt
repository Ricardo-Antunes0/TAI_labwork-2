Lois Galgay Reckitt (born December 31, 1944) is an American businesswoman who is the president and CEO of Reckitt Benckiser, a multinational consumer goods company. Reckitt Benckiser was founded in 1879 and is headquartered in London.  Reckitt Benckiser is the world’s leading housewares, health and beauty company with products available in more than 180 countries. Reckitt Benckiser owns the Duracell battery brand, including Duracell lithium-ion, nickel-cadmium and alkaline batteries. The company also manufactures Lysol disinfectants, Air Wick air fresheners, and other cleaning products.   Reckitt Benckiser was founded in 1879 by two brothers, Charles and James Reckitt. The company started as a small pharmacy in London. In the early days, the company made products such as soap, candles and bleach. Today, Reckitt Benckiser manufactures a wide range of household goods, including air fresheners, disinfectants and kitchen supplies. The company has a global presence with operations in more than 180 countries.   Reckitt Benckiser is ranked number 30 on the Fortune 500 list of the largest United States companies. In 2016, the company earned £11.5 billion ($16 billion) in revenue. Lois Galgay Reckitt is the president and CEO of Reckitt Benckiser, which has a global market share of 11 percent.
