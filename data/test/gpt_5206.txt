Pierre Nicolas Le Chéron d'Incarville (21 August 1736 – 21 June 1814) was a French general and statesman. After serving in the French military, he became a member of the National Constituent Assembly and helped write the Constitution of 1793. He served as Minister of War in 1794, and then as Foreign Minister from 1795 to 1800.  In 1802, he was made Duke of Incarville and Peer of France. In 1805, he became President of the Council of Five Hundred. He retired from public life in 1814 and died the following year.
