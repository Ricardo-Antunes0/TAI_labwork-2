Troy Kenneth Aikman (born November 21, 1966) is a former American football quarterback and current television commentator. He played for the Dallas Cowboys in the National Football League (NFL) for 18 seasons, from 1988 to 2001. He was a three-time Super Bowl champion (1993, 1995, 1996), and led the Cowboys to their first championship in franchise history in 1995. Aikman was also the NFL Most Valuable Player (MVP) in 1993 and 1996.  Aikman was born in Tulsa, Oklahoma, and attended Jenks High School in Jenks, Oklahoma. Aikman played college football at Oklahoma State University. As a sophomore in 1985, he led the Cowboys to their only Big Eight Conference title. As a senior in 1986, he set an NCAA record with 4,883 passing yards, and led Oklahoma State to the playoffs for the first time in school history. Aikman was drafted by the Dallas Cowboys with the second overall pick of the 1988 NFL Draft.  Aikman became one of only four quarterbacks in NFL history to win three Super Bowls (the others are Tom Brady, Peyton Manning, and Ben Roethlisberger). He also ranks third on the NFL's all-time passing list with 46,127 yards and fourth with 249 touchdown passes. In 1994 he became only the second player in league history (after Dan Marino) to win both MVP and Super Bowl MVP honors in the same season. Aikman was inducted into the Pro Football Hall of Fame in February 2018.
