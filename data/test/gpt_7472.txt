Bradley Kent Stevens (born October 22, 1976) is an American basketball coach who is the head coach of the Boston Celtics of the National Basketball Association (NBA). Stevens previously served as head coach of Butler University from 2011 to 2014, and he led the Bulldogs to the NCAA Tournament in each of his final three seasons.  Stevens played college basketball for the Butler Bulldogs, where he was a four-year starter and two-time captain. He was drafted by the Minnesota Timberwolves in the first round of the 2000 NBA Draft, but did not play in the league. Stevens spent four seasons as an assistant coach at Butler before being hired as head coach in 2011.  In his first season, Stevens led Butler to a 21–11 record and an appearance in the 2012 NCAA Tournament. The Bulldogs subsequently made three consecutive appearances (2013–2015) in the NCAA Tournament, culminating with a berth in the 2015 Final Four. Stevens was also named National Coach of the Year by Sporting News and NABC Coach of the Year in 2015.  On May 7, 2014, Stevens was introduced as head coach of the Boston Celtics. In his first season with Boston, he led them to a 48–34 record and a berth in the playoffs. The Celtics then defeated the Atlanta Hawks 4–2 in the Eastern Conference Finals, before losing to Cleveland Cavaliers 4–3 in the NBA Finals.
