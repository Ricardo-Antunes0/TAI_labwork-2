Mohammad Abdullah Abulhasan (born January 12, 1943) is an Bangladeshi-born American mathematician and mathematical physicist. He is a professor of mathematics at the University of California, Berkeley.  Abulhasan was born in Dhaka, Bangladesh to a family of modest means. After completing his undergraduate studies in mathematics at the University of Dhaka, he moved to the United States to continue his studies at the University of California, Berkeley. After earning his doctorate in 1970, Abulhasan began his career as a professor of mathematics at Berkeley. He has made significant contributions to a wide range of fields in mathematics, including probability theory, algebraic geometry, and mathematical physics. He has also been deeply involved in the development of the field of algorithmic information theory.  Abulhasan is a member of the National Academy of Sciences and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences. In 2006, he was awarded the Fields Medal by the International Mathematical Union.
