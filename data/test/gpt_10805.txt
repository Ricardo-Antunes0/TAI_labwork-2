Peter Terson (born Peter Patterson; 16 February 1937) is an English actor. He is known for his roles in the television series Midsomer Murders, The Bill and Coronation Street.  Peter Terson was born on 16 February 1937 in Sheffield, South Yorkshire, England to Harold Patterson, a glove fitter, and his wife Nora (n√©e Richards). He attended Sedbergh School and trained at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art (RADA), graduating in 1961.  Terson made his acting debut in 1962 in a production of Loot at the Sheffield Playhouse. He played the role of Stanislaus in the London production of Eugene O'Neill's play Mourning Becomes Electra opposite Vanessa Redgrave and Michael Pennington.  In 1963, Terson joined the cast of the television series Midsomer Murders as Detective Chief Inspector Tom Barnaby. He continued to appear in the series until its conclusion in 1995, playing a total of 156 episodes. In addition to Midsomer Murders, Terson also appeared in the films The Man with Two Faces (1967), The Wicker Man (1973), and The Three Musketeers (1993).  From 1995 to 2003, Terson played the role of Roy Cropper on the British soap opera Coronation Street. He left the show after eleven years to pursue other acting opportunities.
