Clarence Elmer "Bud" Pinkston (February 1, 1900 – November 17, 1977) was an American football player and coach. He played college football for the University of Michigan from 1920 to 1923 and served as the head football coach at Michigan State University from 1924 to 1926, compiling a record of 10–4.  Pinkston was born in White Sulphur Springs, West Virginia. He attended the University of Michigan where he played for the Michigan Wolverines football team from 1920 to 1923. He was selected as a first-team player on the 1920 All-American football team and was also selected as the captain of the 1923 team.  Pinkston served as the head football coach at Michigan State University from 1924 to 1926, compiling a record of 10–4. He also served as the athletic director at Michigan State from 1937 to 1949.
