Basic Instinct is a 1992 neo-noir erotic thriller film directed by Michael Douglas and starring Sharon Stone and Michael Douglas. The screenplay by James Dearden is based on the novel of the same name by Ian Rankin.  The film tells the story of Catherine Tramell (Stone), a successful San Francisco attorney, and her relationship with detective Alex DeRenzy (Douglas). When DeRenzy is assigned to investigate the murder of her husband, Tramell realizes that he is also investigating her own past. The film culminates with a showdown between Tramell and DeRenzy, during which she reveals her true identity as a serial killer.  Basic Instinct has been controversial since its release. Some critics praised the film's depiction of sexual violence, while others found it offensive and exploitative. The film was a box office success, grossing over $260 million worldwide. It received several awards, including two Golden Globe Awards for Best Motion Picture – Drama and Best Director – Motion Picture for Michael Douglas.
