The Pokekea Megalithic Site is a megalithic site located in the village of Pokekea on the island of Kauaʻi in Hawaiʻi. The site consists of a series of large megaliths, several of which are up to 20 feet (6 m) tall and weighing up to 10 tons (9 t). The megaliths were built between 1000 and 2000 years ago, and are thought to have been used for religious ceremonies or as burial sites.
