Dale Roger Coogan (August 14, 1930 – May 10, 2014) was an American comedian, actor and writer. He was best known for his work on television as a performer on The Dick Cavett Show, Saturday Night Live, and Coogan's World.  He also wrote and starred in the films The Big Lebowski, Dead Man Walking and There's Something About Mary. He has also been a frequent guest on talk shows and variety shows.  Coogan was born in Evanston, Illinois, to Esther (née Levy) and Harry Coogan, who was a salesman. His parents were Jewish immigrants from Hungary. He was raised in Wilmette, Illinois. He attended Skokie Junior High School and then Skokie High School, where he was president of his senior class.  In 1955, Coogan enrolled at Northwestern University but dropped out after two years to join the Second City comedy troupe in Chicago. There he became friends with John Belushi and Dan Aykroyd, who would later appear on Saturday Night Live with him. After a successful stint at Second City, Coogan moved to New York City to join the cast of The Dick Cavett Show in 1969.   Coogan's most famous television role came in 1975 when he joined the cast of Saturday Night Live as a featured player.
