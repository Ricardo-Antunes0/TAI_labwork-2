Guillermo Vilas (; born 17 August 1952) is a retired Chilean football player and manager.  He played for Colo-Colo in the Chilean Primera División for 18 years, winning five national championships and two Copa América titles. He also played for clubs in Argentina, Spain and Italy, where he won the European Cup with Barcelona in 1992. Vilas was capped 149 times by Chile and scored 26 goals. He was also manager of Chile from 2006 to 2008.  Born in Viña del Mar, Vilas started his career with local side Colo-Colo, making his debut in the Segunda División on 10 March 1970 in a 1–0 home win over Deportes Iquique. He went on to become one of Colo Colo's most successful players, helping them win five national championships and two Copa América titles between 1973 and 1984.  In May 1984, Vilas transferred to Spanish club Barcelona for a fee of $2 million. He made his La Liga debut on 16 August 1984 in a 2–2 away draw against Atlético Madrid, and finished his first season with 19 goals in 36 appearances. The following year he helped Barcelona win their fourth European Cup with a 2–1 victory over Benfica at Wembley Stadium. Vilas finished the tournament as the tournament's top scorer with six goals.
