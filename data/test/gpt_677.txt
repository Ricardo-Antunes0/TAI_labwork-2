Guatemala City , locally known as Guatemala or C Guatemala City , is the capital and largest city of Guatemala. The city has an estimated population of 1,093,533 as of 2016. It is also the most populous city in Central America and the 14th most populous city in the world.  As a developing city, Guatemala City has seen rapid growth in recent decades, expanding rapidly from a population of just under half a million in 1960 to over 1.5 million by 1985. However, the recent past has been marked by significant social and political unrest, with sporadic outbreaks of violence continuing into the present. As such, Guatemala City continues to face significant challenges in terms of development, safety, and governance.  The city is located on the eastern bank of the Usumacinta River in Central America, at an elevation of 2,600 meters (8,530 feet). The metropolitan area extends beyond the administrative boundaries of the city, including municipalities such as San Pedro Sula and Retalhuleu.
