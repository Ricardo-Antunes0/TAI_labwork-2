Mount Owen  is the second highest peak in the Wind River Range, located in the U.S. state of Wyoming. At 14,259 feet (4,392 meters), it is the 54th highest peak in the United States and the sixth highest peak in Wyoming. The mountain is located in the Bridger-Teton National Forest and is part of a group of mountains known collectively as The Twelve Apostles.  Mount Owen was first climbed on July 16, 1872 by a party led by David L. Wheeler.
