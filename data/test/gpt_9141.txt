The Moog Source is a monophonic Z80 sampler that was produced by Moog Inc. in 1984. It is a rare and expensive instrument, and its sound has been compared to that of the Serge Modular synthesizer.  The Moog Source was designed as a portable sampler for music production and performance. It has 16 voice polyphony, 12 drum tracks, 8 bass tracks, 4 percussion tracks, and 2 stereo audio outputs. It also includes a built-in vocoder and a sequencer with 16 steps.  The Moog Source has been used by artists including Aphex Twin, Deadmau5, and Tangerine Dream.
