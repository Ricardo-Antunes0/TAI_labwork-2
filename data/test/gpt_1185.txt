Dana Hudkins Crawford (born 1931) is an American poet and memoirist. She is the author of three volumes of poetry, a memoir, and a novel.  Crawford was born in 1931 in Cleveland, Ohio. Her parents were both teachers, and she attended college at the University of Michigan. After graduating in 1953, she moved to New York City, where she worked as a secretary and waitress. She began writing poetry in the early 1960s, and her first book, A Gathering of Old Things, was published in 1972. Crawford has been the recipient of a Guggenheim Fellowship, a National Endowment for the Arts Fellowship, and a Lannan Foundation Grant. She has also been awarded fellowships from the Rockefeller and Ford foundations.
