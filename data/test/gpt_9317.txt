Jerome Carrington (born December 25, 1982), better known by his stage name Rome Cee, is an American rapper and record producer from the Bronx, New York. he is the founder of the record label imprint Sainthood Music.  Cee began rapping and producing in 2001, releasing his first mixtape, "The Real Rome Cee", that same year. In 2003, he released his first full-length album, "Long Live The King". He has since released three more albums—"Trap Muzik", "Rome Is God" and "Four Sevens"—and two extended plays, "The Return" and "The Third Quarter". In 2009, he released his fourth album, "The Great American Depression", which reached number 1 on the Billboard Top Rap Albums chart.  Cee has collaborated with a variety of artists, including Kanye West, ASAP Rocky, Ludacris and Jadakiss. He has also performed at several festivals, including Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival, Lollapalooza and South by Southwest. In 2015, he was a part of the inaugural lineup for Governors Ball Music Festival.
