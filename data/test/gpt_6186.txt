Janua linguarum reserata (English: The Door of Languages Reserved for Research) is a book by the Swiss linguist Jakob Grimm, published in 1854. It is a compilation of Grimm's own observations and research on languages around the world, as well as observations from others.  The book covers over 1,000 languages from around the world, with sections on phonology, morphology, syntax, lexicon, and grammar. It is considered to be one of the most important sources of information on the languages of the world.
