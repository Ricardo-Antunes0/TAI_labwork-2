Vinod G. Khandare, PVSM, AVSM, SM is an Indian military officer and a former head of the Indian Army's Special Forces Command. He has also served as director general of the Research and Analysis Wing (RAW) and as the Chief of the Army Staff.  Khandare was born on 1 November 1952 in Mumbai, Maharashtra, India. He is a graduate of the Indian Military Academy, Dehradun, and holds a Master's degree in Defence Studies from King's College London. He also holds a Diploma in Defence Management from Cranfield University.  Khandare began his military career as a commissioned officer in the Indian Army in 1974. He has served in several command and staff positions, including commanding officer of the Assam Rifles Regiment and joint commander of the 1st Mechanised Infantry Division. He also served as director general of RAW, and as the Chief of Army Staff from 2007 to 2008.  In 2009, Khandare was awarded the Padma Vibhushan, India's second-highest civilian honour.
