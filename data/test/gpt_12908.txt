Aldo Trionfo (12 December 1921 – 6 January 2006) was an Italian actor, film director and screenwriter. He was born in Catania, Sicily. Trionfo started his career as a stage actor, appearing in various plays before making his film debut in the 1950 drama La donna è bella (The Woman Is Beautiful). He won the Best Actor award at the Venice Film Festival for his performance in L'amore è una cosa seria (Love Is a Serious Business) in 1960. He later directed and starred in a number of successful films, including Il sorpasso (1966), Il grande amore (1979), and La battaglia di Legnano (1981). He also wrote screenplays for several of his films. Trionfo died in 2006 at the age of 83.
