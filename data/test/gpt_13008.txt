John Bush Shinn III (February 17, 1935October 2, 1992) was an American politician who served as the 41st Vice President of the United States from 1981 to 1989, under the presidency of Ronald Reagan.  Bush was born in Dallas, Texas. After attending Phillips Academy in Andover, Massachusetts, he graduated from Yale University in 1957 with a Bachelor of Arts degree. Bush later earned a Juris Doctor from Yale Law School in 1961. He served as a staff lawyer for the Senate Committee on Finance from 1961 to 1963. Bush then began his political career as a member of the Texas State Senate from 1963 to 1967. He was elected to the House of Representatives from Texas's 7th congressional district in 1967, and served until 1981. He was elected Vice President of the United States in 1980, and reelected in 1984 and 1988. Bush left office in 1989 due to his father's death. He returned to Houston, Texas and resumed the practice of law.  Bush died at age 54 on October 2, 1992 after a long battle with leukemia. He is buried at the Bush Family Cemetery in Greenwich, Connecticut.
