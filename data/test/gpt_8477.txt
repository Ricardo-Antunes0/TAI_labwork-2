Double Act is a children's novel by British author Enid Blyton. It was first published in the United Kingdom by Doubleday on 2 October 1938, and in the United States by Dodd, Mead & Company on 4 October 1938.  The novel is about two best friends, Emily and Fred, who are always together. When Emily's parents move to America, she is sad to lose her friend, but soon finds out that she can still text him and email him. When Fred's parents move to Australia, he is sad to lose his friend, but soon finds out that he can still talk to her online.   The novel has been adapted many times for different media. A radio play was broadcast on BBC Radio 4 in 1990, and a stage adaptation was produced in London in 2007.
