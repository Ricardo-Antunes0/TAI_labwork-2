The Anukhva church  is a ruined Armenian Apostolic church located in the village of Anukhva, in the Ararat Province of Armenia. The church is dated to the 7th century AD, and is one of the oldest surviving churches in Armenia.  The Anukhva church was built in the late 7th century AD by an unknown architect on an elevated spot in the village of Anukhva, near the Araxes River. The church is a single-nave basilica with a cruciform plan. The walls are composed of large blocks of ashlar stone, and the roof is made of large tiled tiles.   The Anukhva church is currently in a state of disrepair, and its roof has collapsed. It is currently open to visitors only during the summer months.
