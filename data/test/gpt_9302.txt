James Hamilton Jr. (May 8, 1786 – October 10, 1836) was a United States Representative from Maryland.  Born in Frederick County, Maryland, Hamilton attended the common schools and graduated from Princeton University in 1805. He studied law and was admitted to the bar in 1809. He commenced practice in Frederick, Maryland and served as member of the State house of delegates in 1814.  Hamilton served as member of the State senate in 1815 and 1816. He was elected as a Federalist to the Fifteenth, Sixteenth and Seventeenth Congresses (March 4, 1817 – March 3, 1821). He was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1920 to the Eighteenth Congress. He resumed the practice of law in Frederick, Maryland and died there October 10, 1836. He was interred in Greenmount Cemetery.
