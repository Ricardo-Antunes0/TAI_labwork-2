Max von Widnmann (ennobled as Maximilian Ritter von Widnmann-Montreal on 9 July 1868) was a German general during World War I and World War II. He was a recipient of the Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross with Oak Leaves and Swords.  Max von Widnmann was born in 1868 in the city of Heidelberg. He attended the Military Academy at Berlin, graduating in 1888 as a lieutenant. Widnmann served with distinction in various staff and command positions, including during World War I as Chief of Staff to General Paul von Hindenburg and General Erich Ludendorff.  In 1938, Widnmann was appointed Inspector General of the Wehrmacht and Commander in Chief of Army Group Center. In this capacity, he directed the invasion of Poland and the subsequent campaigns against Czechoslovakia, France, Belgium and the Netherlands.
