Lucius Aurunculeius Cotta (died 54 BC) was a Roman politician who served as consul in 51 BC. He was the son of the consul Gaius Aurunculeius Cotta.  Cotta served as tribune of the people in 50 BC and was elected consul for that year. He served until the end of the year, when he was defeated in the elections for praetor. In 49 BC he was elected curule aedile. He served until his death in 54 BC.  Cotta had two sons, Gaius Aurunculeius Cotta and Lucius Aurunculeius Cotta, both of whom became consuls.
