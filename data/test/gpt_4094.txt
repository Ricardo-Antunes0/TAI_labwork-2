Baron Abercromby, of Aboukir and of Tullibody in the County of Kincardine, is a title in the Peerage of Scotland. It was created on 29 April 1783 for the soldier and politician Sir John Abercromby, 2nd Baronet. He had previously served as Member of Parliament for Kincardine Burghs. The Abercromby Baronetcy, of Tullibody in the County of Kincardine, had been created in the Baronetage of Nova Scotia on 12 November 1692 for James Abercromby. The second Baronet was the son of Sir James Abercromby, 1st Baronet. The third Baronet represented Dunbartonshire in the House of Commons. The fourth Baronet sat as Member of Parliament for Dunbartonshire East. The fifth Baronet represented Dunbartonshire West in the House of Commons. The sixth Baronet was a Conservative MP and served as Secretary of State for Scotland from 1979 to 1983. The seventh Baronet is also a Conservative MP and served as Secretary of State for Defence from 2003 to 2007. He is the son of the eighth Baronet, who succeeded his father in 1978. As of 2014 the titles are held by the ninth Baronet, who succeeded his father in 1978.   The family seat is Tullibody House, near Kirriemuir, Angus.
