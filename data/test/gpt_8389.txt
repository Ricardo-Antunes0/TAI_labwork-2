Neil Abercrombie (born June 26, 1938) is an American author and game designer. He is known for his works in the "Dungeons & Dragons" fantasy role-playing game and for his work on the "Shadowrun" game line. Abercrombie also designed the card game "Tempest".  Abercrombie was born in 1938, in Seattle, Washington. He graduated from the University of Washington with a degree in English literature. Abercrombie began writing during his undergraduate years, and has since written over twenty novels, including the best-selling "The Blade Itself" series. He also wrote the "Shardworld" trilogy, which was nominated for a Hugo Award. Abercrombie is a co-creator of the "Dungeons & Dragons" role-playing game, and he served as its lead designer from 1978 to 1987.  Abercrombie has also worked on several video games, including the "Shadowrun" game line and the "Tempest" card game. He has been nominated for several awards, including two Hugo Awards and a Nebula Award. In 2017, Abercrombie was diagnosed with cancer and began a treatment plan.
