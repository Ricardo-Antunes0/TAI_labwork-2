Abu Ja'far Ashinas (; died 17 or 18 October 953) was the Abbasid Caliph in Baghdad from 942 to 953.  Abu Ja'far Ashinas was born in Samarra into the Ashina Dynasty, a noble family of Iranian origin. He was the son of Abu al-Qasim Muhammad and grandson of Abu Muslim al-Ashina. He succeeded his father as governor of Iraq in 942 and, after defeating a revolt by some of his relatives, became caliph in 953. He attempted to stamp out corruption and revive the economy, but was largely unsuccessful. He died in either 17 or 18 October 953.
