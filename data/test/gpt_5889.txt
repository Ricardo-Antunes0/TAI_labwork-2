Tsutomu Kawabuchi (; 16 May 1925 â€“ 9 November 2003) was a Japanese architect and critic, known for his modernist designs.  Born in Osaka, Kawabuchi graduated from Osaka University of Arts in 1945. He worked as an assistant to Kenzo Tange from 1945 to 1947, and then worked as an assistant to Tange's successor, Arata Isozaki from 1947 to 1952. From 1952 to 1957, he was a professor at the Tokyo National University of Fine Arts and Music. In 1957, he formed the office of Tsutomu Kawabuchi with Associates.  Kawabuchi pioneered the use of glass in architecture and designed many landmark buildings in Japan, including the Isetan Department Store in Shinjuku (1962), the Tokyo International Forum (1971), and the Kashiwa Reactor Building (1981). He was awarded the Praemium Imperiale by the Italian government in 1985.  Kawabuchi died of a heart attack in 2003 at the age of 80.
