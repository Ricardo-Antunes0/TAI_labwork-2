Pablo Acosta Villarreal, commonly referred to as PABLO, is a Mexican actor, singer and model.  Born in Mexico City to a father of Spanish and Mexican heritage, Acosta Villarreal began his acting career at the age of twelve in the telenovela La Madrastra. In 2001, he starred in the Mexican feature film El Cantante, for which he received critical acclaim. He later achieved international recognition for his role as Gustavo Arellano in the telenovela Rebelde and for his portrayal of Enrique in Alejandro González Iñárritu's biographical film Amores Perros.  In 2009, Acosta Villarreal released his first album, Sólo una vez. The following year, he appeared as himself in the comedy film Borat: Cultural Learnings of America for Make Benefit Glorious Nation of Kazakhstan. In 2012, he starred as José in the drama film The Act of Killing and as Daniel Ortega in the political thriller film Cartel Land. In 2014, he starred in the crime thriller film Sicario and in the historical drama film The Revenant.
