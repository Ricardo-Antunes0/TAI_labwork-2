He has received critical acclaim for his acting skills, especially for his portrayals of villains.  Sivakumar was born in 1947 in Palakkad, Kerala to Padmanabhan Nair and Parvathy. He did his schooling from St. John's Higher Secondary School, Palakkad. After completing his graduation from Mar Thoma College, Palakkad, he started his career as an actor in the 1970s. He made his debut in the Tamil film industry with "Tholi Prema Katha" (1976). He then started directing and producing films in the 1980s. His breakthrough came with "Ningalude Naal" (1987), which won him the National Film Award for Best Feature Film in Tamil. He went on to achieve further success with "Kaliyil Kattu" (1988), "Vaali" (1989), and "Ponnar Shankar" (1991). Sivakumar also starred in several successful Malayalam films including "Chandralekha" (1981), "Manorama" (1983), "Oru Pathirikaaran Oru Rajathaniyil" (1985), and "Vismaya Kanavu" (1987).
