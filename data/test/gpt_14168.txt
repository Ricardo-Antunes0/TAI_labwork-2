The Southern Sudan Association (SSA) was a non-governmental organisation (NGO) founded in 1989 to promote the independence of Southern Sudan.  The Southern Sudan Association was founded in 1989 by a group of Sudanese intellectuals with the aim of promoting the independence of Southern Sudan. The organisation played a leading role in the negotiations that led to the signing of the Comprehensive Peace Agreement in 2005, and it continues to work on behalf of Southern Sudanese refugees and internally displaced persons.
