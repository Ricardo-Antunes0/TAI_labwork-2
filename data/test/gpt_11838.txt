Paul Michael Romer (born November 6, 1955) is an American economist. He is currently the A. W. Mellon Professor of Economics at the New York University School of Arts and Science, and a fellow of the American Economic Association, the Econometric Society, and the Society for Political Economy.  Romer is known for his work on economic growth, Romer's paradox, and technological progress. He is also known for his advocacy of increased government spending on research and development.
