Hervé Carré (born 24 September 1944) is a French actor, director, and screenwriter.  Carré was born in Paris, the son of actress Jacqueline Carré and actor Maurice Carré. He made his theatrical debut at the age of 17 in the play "Les Demoiselles de Rochefort" by Jacques Brel. He then studied acting at the Cours Simon-Drucker in Paris.  Carré's film debut was in the 1969 film "Le Boucher". He has appeared in more than 60 films, including "The Blade Runner" (1982), for which he won an Academy Award for Best Director, "The Unbearable Lightness of Being" (1988), "The Silence of the Lambs" (1991), and "Inception" (2010). He also directed and wrote the screenplay for the 2017 film "The Outsider".  Carré is a Knight of the Order of Arts and Letters and a Commandeur of the Ordre des Arts et des Lettres.
