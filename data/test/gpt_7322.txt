Mario Gariazzo (4 June 1930 – 18 December 2018) was an Italian football player and manager.  Gariazzo played for several clubs during his career, including Juventus, Catania, Torino and Fiorentina. He made his debut for the Italy national team in 1954 and went on to earn 53 caps, scoring two goals. He coached several clubs, most notably Torino and Palermo. Gariazzo died at the age of 86 on 18 December 2018.  Born in Torino, Gariazzo started playing football with hometown club Torino Calcio before joining Juventus in 1951. He made his Serie A debut for Juventus on 5 November 1951 in a 2–0 away victory over Ascoli Calcio. Gariazzo went on to make 283 appearances for the "Blues", scoring 33 goals, before departing for Catania in 1961.  Gariazzo spent six seasons with Catania, helping the team to win the Coppa Italia twice (1963 and 1964). He then joined Torino in 1968, where he became captain and helped the club to win the Serie A title in 1972.  Gariazzo retired from playing football in 1974 and began coaching Torino's reserve team. He led the team to promotion to Serie B in 1978 and then took over as manager of Torino for the 1979–80 season. In his first season in charge, Torino finished second in Serie B behind Lazio.
