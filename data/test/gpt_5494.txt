Goddard attended Brooklyn Technical High School and then received a B.S. from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute in 1935. He worked for several years at the Douglas Aircraft Company before joining the MIT Aeronautical Laboratory in 1939 as a research assistant in the wind tunnel group. In 1942 he became head of the wind tunnel group, and in 1945 he became head of the aircraft design division at MIT.  In 1948 Goddard left MIT to become research manager of the General Electric Aircraft Division, and in 1952 he became vice president of that division. In 1955 he moved to the Sikorsky Aircraft Corporation as vice president and director of research, where he remained until his retirement in 1972.  Goddard was a member of the National Academy of Sciences, the American Academy of Arts and Sciences, and the American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics. He was also a recipient of the National Medal of Science.
