Nicole Leeper Piquero is an American criminologist who currently serves as the Jay P. Lefkowitz Professor of Criminology and Criminal Justice at the University of Michigan. She is also a fellow at the National Academy of Sciences, and a director of the National Center for Victims of Crime.  Piquero's research focuses on crime and justice, with a particular focus on the intersection of race and crime. Her work has been cited in both the scholarly and popular press, and she has been featured in documentaries about crime and justice.  Piquero was born in Boston, Massachusetts, in 1970. She received her B.A. from Brown University in 1992, and her M.A. and Ph.D. from Columbia University in 1995 and 1999, respectively.  Piquero began her academic career at Rutgers University, where she was a research associate under the tutelage of James Q. Wilson. She then served as a professor at the State University of New York at Buffalo, before joining the faculty at the University of Michigan in 2007.  Piquero has been awarded several prestigious awards, including a MacArthur Fellowship in 2002, a Guggenheim Fellowship in 2004, and a Fulbright scholarship in 2002. In 2013, she was elected to the National Academy of Sciences.
