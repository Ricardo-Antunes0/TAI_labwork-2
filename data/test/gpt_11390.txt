Arturo Santos Hernández (born July 9, 1987) is a Mexican singer and actor. He is best known as the former lead singer of the Mexican pop group Mini Charly Manson.  Born in Mexicali, Baja California, Hernández began his musical career in 2002 as the lead vocalist of the pop group Mini Charly Manson. The group achieved moderate success in Mexico and South America, but their biggest success came in 2007 with the release of their self-titled debut album, which spawned the singles "Por Qué Te Quiero" and "Eres mi Vida". Following the release of their second album, "Inmortal", in 2009, Hernández left Mini Charly Manson to pursue a solo career.  In 2012, Hernández was cast in the Mexican telenovela "Amar y Vivir", playing the role of Rodrigo. The show was cancelled after one season, but Hernández has since reprised his role in the spin-off series "Rodrigo y Gabriela".
