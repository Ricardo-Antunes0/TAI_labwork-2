He studied at the Montreal School of Fine Arts from 1933 to 1937, and then at the Ontario College of Art from 1937 to 1939.   He worked as a lithographer for the Royal Canadian Mounted Police from 1941 to 1946, and then became a full-time artist. His paintings typically depict scenes from rural Canada, and he has been called "the father of landscape painting in Canada". He was awarded the Order of Canada in 1981, and the Governor General's Award in Visual and Media Arts in 1988.  Commanda died in 1995 at the age of 78.
