Cyprian was a Byzantine military leader who played an important role in the defense of the eastern frontier against raids by the Arabs in the early 7th century.  Cyprian was born in the early 7th century, probably in Constantinople. He served as a commander on the eastern frontier against raids by the Arabs from 693 to 698, and again from 710 to 715. In 698, he led a successful campaign against the Arabs near Melitene. After this, he was appointed governor of Palestine and Egypt. He died in 728.
