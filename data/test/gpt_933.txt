New World Scene is artwork thought to be created in the late 15th century to early 16th century. It is an early example of Mannerism in painting, and shows the influence of the Flemish painters, most notably Rembrandt. The painting features a group of people in a landscape setting, with some figures prominently featuring facial features that are strongly reminiscent of those by Rembrandt.
