Charles Stuart Gregson (29 May 1817 - 24 December 1881) was a Scottish physician and Liberal politician who sat in the House of Commons from 1868 to 1874.  Gregson was born in Glasgow on 29 May 1817, the son of Thomas Gregson and his wife Charlotte. He was educated at the Glasgow Academy and then at the University of Glasgow, where he studied medicine. He became a doctor in 1841.  Gregson lived in Edinburgh and became a member of the Royal College of Physicians of Edinburgh in 1854. He was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society of Edinburgh in 1861.  Gregson was elected as Member of Parliament for Edinburgh South at the 1868 general election, defeating the Conservative candidate John Thomson by 1,444 votes. He held the seat until his defeat at the 1874 general election by the Conservative candidate James Gladstone.  Gregson died on 24 December 1881, aged 74.
