Beautiful Memories is a 1976 vinyl album by American singer Barbra Streisand. The album was released on April 29, 1976, by Columbia Records. Streisand co-produced the album with John Hammond and Neil Sedaka.  Streisand's first album in six years, it yielded five charting singles on the "Billboard" Hot 100: "People," "The Way You Look Tonight," "A Love Like That (Can't Be Broken)," "Evergreen (Love Theme from A Star Is Born)", and "You're Never Too Old (To Dance Again)."   The album received mixed reviews upon its release, with some reviewers praising her return to pop standards and others finding her vocals weak and unconvincing. Despite this, Beautiful Memories reached number one on the US "Billboard" 200 chart and has since been certified platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA). In 2001, the album was ranked number 487 on "Rolling Stone" magazine's list of the 500 Greatest Albums of All Time.
