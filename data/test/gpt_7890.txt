Salvatore Augustine Giunta (; born January 21, 1953) is an Italian-American attorney and politician who served as the 63rd Mayor of New York City from 2002 to 2013.  From 1978 to 2002, Giunta was a federal prosecutor in the United States Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York. He was later elected to the New York City Council in 2001, and became Mayor in 2002 upon the resignation of Michael Bloomberg. During his tenure, Giunta presided over the city's largest public-private partnership program, which generated billions of dollars for city projects. He left office in 2013 after being term-limited out of office.  Giunta was born in Staten Island, New York, to an Italian-American family. He attended James Madison High School in Staten Island, and then Fordham University School of Law. After graduating from law school, Giunta worked as a prosecutor in the United States Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York. In 1978, he was appointed to a position on the U.S. Attorney's Advisory Committee on Federal Criminal Justice Policy by Edward M. Kennedy, then United States Attorney for the Southern District of New York.  In 2001, Giunta was elected to the New York City Council representing Staten Island's 2nd district. He became Deputy Majority Leader in 2003 and then Majority Leader in 2006.
