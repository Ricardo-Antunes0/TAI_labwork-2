, sometimes mistakenly referred to as Nebu Tatsuguchi, (born November 5, 1951) is a Japanese musician, composer and audio engineer. He is the founder and leader of the free-jazz group Yellow Magic Orchestra. In addition to his work with YMO, Tatsuguchi has also collaborated with composers such as Ryuichi Sakamoto, Vangelis and John Zorn.  Born in Tokyo, Tatsuguchi started his musical career as a drummer in a rock band in the early 1970s. It was not until he formed the free-jazz group Yellow Magic Orchestra in 1978 that he achieved international recognition. The band's eclectic mix of jazz, funk, classical and electronic music has earned them a dedicated following both in Japan and abroad. Over the years, YMO has released over twenty albums, including collaborations with such luminaries as David Bowie, Brian Eno, Steve Winwood and Phil Collins. In 2009, Tatsuguchi received the Japan Record Award for his contribution to Japanese music.
