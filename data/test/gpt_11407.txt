Joël Cloarec (born 5 February 1966) is a French actor and director.  Born in the town of Saint-Étienne, in the Loire Valley, Cloarec began his career as a stage actor at the age of fifteen. He then moved to Paris to study at the National School of Dramatic Art, from which he graduated in 1991.  Cloarec's screen debut came in 1993 with a minor role in Gilles de Rais' film adaptation of Bertolt Brecht's play The Threepenny Opera. He went on to appear in several films, including Luc Besson's The Professional (1998) and Bertrand Tavernier's La Femme d'Alain Resnais (2001), before landing a leading role in Jacques Audiard's crime film The Beat That My Heart Skipped (2003). Cloarec later starred in Jean-Pierre Jeunet's Delicatessen (2007), Abbas Kiarostami's A Separation (2011), and Yorgos Lanthimos' The Lobster (2015), for which he was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Actor.  Since 2016, Cloarec has been directing films, including the acclaimed drama Monsieur Lazhar (2016) and the coming-of-age film White Material (2018).
