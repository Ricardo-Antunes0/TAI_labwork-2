49 Librae is the Flamsteed designation for the binary star system 49 Librae, located in the handle of the Sickle constellations. The brighter star, component A, is a white dwarf approximately the size of the Earth, while the fainter component, component B, is a red dwarf.  The system is located about 240 light years from Earth in the constellation of Libra. It was first observed by English astronomer James Dunlop in 1826 and has since been studied by many astronomers.
