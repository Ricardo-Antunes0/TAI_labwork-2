Henrik Bernard Oldenland aka Heinrich Bernhard Oldenland (31 October 1849 - 3 March 1912) was a Norwegian physician and medical researcher.  He was born in Kristiania (now Oslo), Norway. He graduated from the University of Oslo in 1872, and practiced medicine in Kristiania from 1872 to 1896. He then moved to Berlin, Germany, where he worked as a physician until his death.  He is best known for his work on tuberculosis, especially the study of its causes and treatment. He was also a noted scholar of German literature and history.
