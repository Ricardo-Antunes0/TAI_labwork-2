Harold Schlosberg (January 3, 1904 – August 5, 1982) was an American lawyer and politician who served as the 34th Governor of New York from 1954 to 1962. He also served in the United States House of Representatives from 1945 to 1949 and was a United States Senator from 1951 to 1960.  Born in Brooklyn, New York, Schlosberg graduated from Stuyvesant High School in 1922 and then attended Columbia Law School, where he was a member of the Delta Kappa Epsilon fraternity. He was admitted to the bar in 1927 and commenced practice in Brooklyn.  Schlosberg served as assistant district attorney of Kings County, New York, from 1934 to 1942, and then as district attorney of that same county from 1942 to 1945. In 1944, he was elected to the U.S. House of Representatives as a Democrat and served there until 1949.  In 1950, Schlosberg was elected to the Senate, where he served until 1960. During his time in the Senate, he chaired the Committee on Rules and Administration (1954–1955) and the Committee on Labor and Public Welfare (1959–1960).  After leaving the Senate, Schlosberg resumed the practice of law in Brooklyn. He died there on August 5, 1982.
