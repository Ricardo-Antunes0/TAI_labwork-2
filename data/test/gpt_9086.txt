The Manhattan Center is a building in New York City. It is located on the west side of Manhattan, between Sixth and Seventh Avenues, between West 53rd and 54th Streets. The building was designed by architect William Van Alen and constructed in 1930. The building is 22 stories tall, with a basement and an attic. The building is clad in polished granite and limestone, with terra cotta details. It has a copper cornice and a projecting triple-tiered pylon at the roofline.
