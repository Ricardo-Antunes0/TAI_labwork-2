Jack Gibbons (born 2 March 1962) is a Northern Irish former professional association football manager and player.  Born in Belfast, Gibbons played as a midfielder for Scottish club Celtic and English clubs Everton and Manchester United. He also had spells with Spanish clubs Real Zaragoza and Valencia, as well as Greek side Panathinaikos. Gibbons was appointed manager of Fulham in 2002, but was sacked after just four months. He later managed Blackpool, Sheffield United and Sunderland before his dismissal from Sunderland in October 2014.  Gibbons has been diagnosed with cancer and is currently undergoing treatment.
