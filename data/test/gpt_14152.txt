Stephen Burgess Evans (born April 1, 1963), also known as Steve Evans, is an American author and screenwriter.  Evans was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. He graduated from the University of Pennsylvania with a degree in English Literature. After graduating, Evans worked as a journalist for "The Philadelphia Inquirer" and "The Baltimore Sun".  Evans has written several novels, including "The Lost Symbol" (2009), "The Language of Thorns" (2010), "City of Bones" (2011), and "Shadow of the Moon" (2015). His books have been published by Doubleday, HarperCollins, and Random House.  Evans wrote the screenplay for the 2011 film adaptation of "The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo", which was directed by David Fincher and starred Daniel Craig and Rooney Mara. Evans also wrote the screenplay for the 2016 film adaptation of "The Girl on the Train", which was directed by Tate Taylor and starred Emily Blunt and Justin Theroux.
