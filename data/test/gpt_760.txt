Born in Los Angeles, Stowell began his musical career as a member of the R&B vocal group the Remarks in the early 1960s. He then became a successful solo artist, scoring several popular hits in the 1970s and 1980s, including "I Believe in You" (1973), "I'll Always Love You" (1975), "I Can't Stop Loving You" (1978), and "The Wanderer" (1979).  Stowell's death at age 72 was due to complications from a stroke. He was survived by his wife, Della Reese, and two daughters.
