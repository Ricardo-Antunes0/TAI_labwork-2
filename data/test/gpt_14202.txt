Michael Dean Boggs (born November 8, 1978) is an American musician, singer and songwriter who has been a member of the bands Death Cab for Cutie and The Paper Kites.  Boggs was born in San Francisco, California, to a musical family. His father, Dean Boggs, is an acoustic guitarist and music professor, and his mother, Deborah Boggs, is a harpist. He started playing the guitar at the age of five. He attended the San Francisco Conservatory of Music, where he studied classical guitar under Yung-Hsuan Lee.  Boggs formed Death Cab for Cutie with Ben Gibbard and Issac Brock in 1998. The band released its debut album "Transatlanticism" in 2000. The album produced two singles, "The New Year" and "Soul Meets Body". In 2002, Boggs co-wrote and recorded the soundtrack for the film "The Catcher in the Rye".  In 2006, Boggs left Death Cab for Cutie to focus on his own projects. He formed The Paper Kites with drummer John Roderick and bassist Nick Harmer. The band released its debut album "The Great Eastern" in 2010. In 2013, Boggs released the solo album "Michael Boggs".
