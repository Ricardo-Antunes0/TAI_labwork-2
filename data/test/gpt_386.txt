Mbabaram or Mbaba…Ωam, often referred to as the Basarwa of the Eastern Congo, are a Bantu-speaking people inhabiting the eastern part of the Democratic Republic of the Congo.  The Mbabaram live in the forested mountains of North Kivu and are well known for their ivory carving and metalworking. They are also famous for their buffalo hunting, which is an important part of their culture. The Mbabaram speak a Bantu language and belong to the Luba-Katanga branch of the Bantu languages.
