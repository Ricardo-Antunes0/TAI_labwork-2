David L. Snyder (born September 22, 1944) is an American politician who served as the 42nd Governor of Michigan from 1999 to 2007, and is a Republican. He was also Chairman of the Republican National Committee from 2005 to 2009.  Born in Detroit, Snyder graduated from Groves High School in 1962. He earned a B.A. from Wayne State University in 1966 and a J.D. from the University of Michigan Law School in 1970.  Snyder served in the Michigan House of Representatives from 1973 to 1979 and was Majority Leader in 1978 and 1979. He was elected Governor of Michigan in 1998, defeating incumbent Democrat Jennifer Granholm by 64% to 34%. Snyder served two terms, leaving office in 2007 with a 61% approval rating.  In 2003, Snyder was elected Chairman of the Republican National Committee (RNC). He served until 2009 when he resigned to take over as president and CEO of Snyder Companies, a family-owned business with interests in construction, real estate development, farming, trucking, insurance and banking.  In May 2013, Snyder announced his candidacy for Governor of Michigan in the 2014 election; he won the Republican primary election on August 7th, 2014. Snyder was defeated by Democratic candidate Gretchen Whitmer by 10 points in the general election on November 4th, 2014.
