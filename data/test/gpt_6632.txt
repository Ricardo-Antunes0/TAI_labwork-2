Freesound is a collaborative repository of CC-BY licensed sound recordings, originally founded in 2001. Freesound is made up of over 16 million files and receives contributions from people all over the world.  Freesound was founded in 2001 by Cutter David A. Black, with the aim of making a repository of CC-BY licensed sound recordings. The project quickly gained a following, and by 2005 it had over 16 million files. In 2013, Freesound partnered with SoundCloud to allow users to upload and share their sounds with the world.  Today, Freesound continues to be a popular source for sound recordings, with over 330,000 new files added each week. Freesound is used by musicians and sound designers all over the world, and its collection of sounds is constantly growing.
