"Last Kiss" is a song released by American rock band Kiss in 1983. The song was written by the band's lead singer, Gene Simmons, and produced by the band's lead guitarist, Ace Frehley.  "Last Kiss" is a ballad about a farewell kiss. The song was voted number nine on VH1's 100 Greatest Songs of Rock and Roll.  "Last Kiss" was released as the second single from the band's 1983 album Creatures of the Night. The song reached number one on the U.S. Billboard Hot 100 chart, making it Kiss' sixth U.S. number one single. It also reached number one in Canada and in Australia. The song became certified gold by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA) for sales of over 500,000 copies in the United States.   The song has been covered by a variety of artists, including Barbra Streisand, Whitney Houston, Celine Dion, and Ashlee Simpson.
