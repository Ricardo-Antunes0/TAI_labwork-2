Baron Thierry de Gruben (born 17 November 1951) is a Belgian businessman and philanthopist. He is the chairman and chief executive officer (CEO) of the Ardian Group, a company he founded in 1987.  De Gruben was born in 1951 in Brussels, Belgium. He studied business administration at the Catholic University of Leuven, graduating with a degree in 1975.  He started his career as a Société anonyme trader in 1975, working for Banque Bruxelles Lambert. In 1987, he founded the Ardian Group, which became one of Belgium's largest private companies. He has been chairman and CEO of the company since its inception.  De Gruben is chairman of the board of directors of the Ardian Foundation, which he founded in 1999 to promote philanthropy and social responsibility. The foundation has awarded more than €60 million in grants to organizations working to improve quality of life for people around the world.  De Gruben is a member of the Royal Academy of Belgium, the Belgian Institute of International Affairs, and the European Academy of Sciences and Arts. He has also been awarded honorary citizenship by several cities around the world, including Bucharest, Sofia, and Lviv.  In 2018, de Gruben was named one of Belgium's greatest living entrepreneurs by La Libre Belgique.
