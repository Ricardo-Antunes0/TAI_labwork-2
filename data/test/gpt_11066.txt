Jan Foss (1 December 1938 - 12 January 2018) was a Norwegian biochemist.  Foss was born in Oslo, Norway. He received his undergraduate degree from the University of Oslo in 1962, and his doctorate from the University of Massachusetts Amherst in 1967. He then became a postdoctoral fellow at the University of California, Berkeley, where he worked with Stanley Cohen on the structure of ribonuclease Z. In 1971 he moved to the University of Oslo as an associate professor. He became a full professor in 1984 and served as rector of the University of Oslo from 1994 to 1997.  Foss is known for his research on ribonucleases, which have been used to study genetic diseases. He was also involved in research on the genetic control of protein synthesis. In 2002, he was awarded the Fridtjof Nansen Medal. Foss died in January 2018 at the age of 80.
