Anthony William "Tony" Marx (born February 28, 1945 in Bournemouth, England) is a British Marxist philosopher and academic.  Marx attended the University of Sussex, where he obtained a Bachelor of Arts degree in 1966 and a Master of Arts degree in 1967. He then attended the University of Cambridge, where he obtained a Doctor of Philosophy degree in 1971.  Marx has been a professor at the University of Essex since 1971. In 2002, he was appointed Professor of Philosophy at the University of East London. He also served as Director of Studies at the University of Essex from 2000 to 2002. In addition, Marx has been a visiting professor at various universities, including the University of Freiburg in Germany, the Universidad Complutense de Madrid in Spain, and Beijing Normal University in China.  Marx is the author or editor of thirteen books, including "Philosophy and Revolution", "The Revolutionary Idea", "Humanism and Marxism", "Marx's Theory of History", "The Poverty of Philosophy", "On Historical Materialism", "Contradictions and Theories: Studies in Marxist Philosophy", "Philosophy and Politics: A Critical Introduction", and "On Religion". He has also published more than seventy articles in academic journals, including "Philosophical Review", "Journal of Political Philosophy", "Ethics", and "European Journal for Philosophy of Religion".
