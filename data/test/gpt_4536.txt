Joshua Stevenson Baker (born December 25, 1986) is an American professional basketball player for the Houston Rockets of the National Basketball Association (NBA). He played college basketball for the University of Kentucky.  Baker was drafted by the Philadelphia 76ers with the 18th overall pick in the 2006 NBA draft. He played for the 76ers, Memphis Grizzlies, and Oklahoma City Thunder before being traded to the Rockets in 2017. He has also played for the Chinese National Team.  Born and raised in Lexington, Kentucky, Baker attended Lexington Catholic High School. As a junior, he averaged 20.4 points and 8.6 rebounds per game and helped lead his team to a 27-4 record and a Kentucky Class AAA state championship. As a senior, he averaged 25.0 points, 10.8 rebounds, 3.2 assists, 1.8 blocks and 1.2 steals per game while helping lead Lexington Catholic to a 26-5 record and another state championship. He was named Kentucky's Mr. Basketball as a senior and was also a McDonald's All-American and a Parade All-American.  Baker began his college career at Memphis where he averaged 14.9 points and 5.7 rebounds per game as a freshman before breaking his hand in February 2007. He transferred to Kentucky the following year and averaged 16.3 points, 6.7 rebounds and 2 assists per game while leading his team to the NCAA Men's Division I Basketball Championship Game where they lost to Duke 79-70.
