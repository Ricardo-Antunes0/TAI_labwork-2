Tamara Thorne (born 20 November 1957 in Los Angeles) is an American actress, singer and writer.  Thorne began her acting career in the late 1970s, appearing in various television roles before making her debut in the feature film Jagged Edge (1985). She went on to receive critical acclaim for her work in such films as Thelma & Louise (1991), Batman Returns (1992), and Single White Female (1992). Her most successful role to date has been as Harley Quinn in the DC Extended Universe, starting with Batman v Superman: Dawn of Justice (2016) and continuing with Suicide Squad (2016), Wonder Woman (2017), Justice League (2017), and Aquaman (2018).  Thorne has also written several books, including a memoir entitled Crazy Beautiful: My Life As an Actress, Writer and Drug Addict (2004); a novel entitled Devoted: A Novel (2009); and a children's book entitled I'm Not Scared: A Book About Being Different (2011).
