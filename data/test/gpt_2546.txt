In the Battle of Mohrungen on 25 May 1813, a combined French and Bavarian army led by Emperor Napoleon I decisively defeated a Austro-Russian force under Field Marshal Mikhail Kutuzov.  The Battle of Mohrungen was fought on the plain of Mohrungen near Magdeburg, Germany. The Austrians, under Field Marshal Mikhail Kutuzov, were defending a position on the east bank of the Elbe River against a French army under Emperor Napoleon I. The battle was a decisive victory for the French, who captured more than 4,000 prisoners and inflicted 6,000 casualties on their opponents.
