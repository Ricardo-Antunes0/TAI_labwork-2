Till Lindemann (; born 4 January 1963) is a German singer, songwriter and musician. He is the lead singer and main songwriter of the rock band Rammstein.  Lindemann was born in Pforzheim, Germany. He started his musical career in the early 1980s as a member of the band Rheingold, before beginning his solo career in the early 1990s. In 1992, Lindemann formed the band Rammstein with Richard Kruspe, Till Lindemann's former bassist in Rheingold. The band achieved international success with their first album, Reise, Ein Abenteuer (1994), which was certified triple platinum in Germany and charted in several countries. Their second album, Mutter (1996), was also successful and received a Grammy Award nomination for Best Rock Album. Their third album, Amerika (2001), was their first to chart in the United States and their fourth album, Rosenrot (2005), received widespread critical acclaim and became their first number-one album in Germany. Their fifth album, Zoolander (2008), was a commercial success worldwide, selling over 10 million copies.
