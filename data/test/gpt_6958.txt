Viscount Mersey, of Toxteth in the County of Liverpool, is a title in the Peerage of the United Kingdom. It was created on 2 February 1892 for the Conservative politician and businessman George Spencer-Churchill, fourth son of the 4th Duke of Marlborough. The title became extinct on his death in 1944.  The Spencer-Churchill family descends from John Churchill, 1st Duke of Marlborough, an English general who served in the War of the Spanish Succession and later fought in the Anglo-Dutch Wars. His son John Spencer-Churchill, 2nd Duke of Marlborough, was a political figure who served as Prime Minister of Great Britain from 1807 to 1809 and from 1828 to 1830. His grandson George Spencer-Churchill, 3rd Duke of Marlborough, was a general and politician who served as Secretary at War under Lord Melbourne and as Prime Minister from 1858 to 1874. His son Edward Spencer-Churchill, 4th Duke of Marlborough, inherited the dukedom but died without sons in 1892, when he was created Viscount Mersey.
