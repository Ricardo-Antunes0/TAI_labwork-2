Adolf Mauritz Fonahn (15 June 1873 – 20 November 1944) was a German composer and pianist of the late Romantic and early Modern periods.  Fonahn was born in Cologne, the son of a singer. He studied at the Cologne Conservatory under Heinrich Schütz and Robert Schumann, graduating in 1898. From 1898 to 1900 he studied with Johannes Brahms in Vienna. In 1902 he won first prize at the Klavierwoche in Vienna, and that same year he had his first opera, Das Liebesverbot, staged in Berlin. In 1907 his song cycle Der Jüngling von Orleans was performed at the Bayreuth Festspielhaus. In 1910 he became a professor at the Berlin Conservatory, where he remained until his death.
