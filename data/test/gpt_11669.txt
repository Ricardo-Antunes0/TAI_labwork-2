Käte Niederkirchner (born Käte Appel or Käte Niederkirchner Appel on February 5, 1933 in Munich, Germany) is a German actress and film director.  Niederkirchner was born the daughter of the actor Gustav Appel and the actress Elisabeth Niederkirchner. She made her stage debut in 1952 at the Bavarian State Opera in the role of Rosalinde in Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart's Die Zauberflöte. After appearing in a number of television productions, she made her film debut in 1966 with the role of Lisbeth in Hans-Jürgen Syberberg's Berlin Alexanderplatz. She won the German Film Award for Best Actress for her performance as Hanna Schmitz in Volker Schlöndorff's 1979 film The Tin Drum. In 1984, she directed her first feature film, a crime drama titled Käthe Kollwitz. In addition to her acting and directing careers, Niederkirchner has also written and directed plays.
