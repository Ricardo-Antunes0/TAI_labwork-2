Chou Chun-hsun (Taiwanese POJ:  Chiu Ch√πn-hun; born October 8, 1948) is a Taiwanese politician who has been the President of the Republic of China since 2008. He is also the chairman of the Democratic Progressive Party.  Born in Tainan, Chou received his early education at Tainan Municipal Elementary School and graduated from National Taiwan University with a degree in political science in 1971. He then worked as a research fellow at Academia Sinica, before entering politics in 1976.   Chou first served as deputy director of the Central Policy Research Office from 1983 to 1986 and secretary-general of the Kuomintang from 1986 to 1992. In 1992, he was elected to the Legislative Yuan, serving until 1998. He then served as minister without portfolio in the KMT administration of President Chen Shui-bian from 1998 to 2000.   In 2000, Chou joined the newly founded Democratic Progressive Party and was elected to the National Assembly that year. He served as chairman of the DPPS until 2004, when he was elected president of the party. In 2008, he was elected president of the Republic of China, defeating Kuomintang nominee Ma Ying-jeou by a wide margin.
