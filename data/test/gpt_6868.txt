Die Another Day is a 2002 spy film directed by Lee Tamahori and produced by Michael G. Wilson and Barbara Broccoli. It is a sequel to the 1997 film Goldeneye, and is the fourth film in the James Bond series. The screenplay was written by Neal Purvis, Robert Wade and Michael G. Wilson. The film stars Pierce Brosnan as James Bond, Sophie Marceau as Vesper Lynd, David Niven as M, Judi Dench as M's secretary Miss Moneypenny and Sean Bean as 007's adversary Hugo Drax.  In Die Another Day, Bond investigates Drax's nuclear plant in Madagascar and learns that he has been using it to produce weapons of mass destruction. After destroying the plant, Bond pursues Drax to London, where he fights him in a high-speed car chase through the city. In the end, Bond kills Drax by throwing him out of a helicopter into the Thames River.
