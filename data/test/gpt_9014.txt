Georgios Papoulias (; 19 May 1927, Athens, Greece â€“ 1 October 2006, Athens) was a Greek mathematician, who worked on partial differential equations, variational calculus and mathematical physics.  Georgios Papoulias was born on May 19, 1927 in Athens, Greece. He obtained his undergraduate degree from the University of Athens in 1950 and his PhD from the University of Cambridge in 1954 under the supervision of John von Neumann. Papoulias then worked at the University of California, Berkeley from 1954 to 1957 as a post-doctoral fellow with von Neumann.  In 1957, Papoulias moved to the University of Paris where he became a professor in 1968 and remained until his retirement in 1998. He was a fellow of both the American Academy of Arts and Sciences and the Royal Society of London. He died on October 1, 2006 in Athens.
