Hurricane Danny was the first major hurricane of the 2015 Atlantic hurricane season. Forming on July 12, the storm slowly intensified, reaching Category 1 status on July 16. Danny then began a rapid intensification on July 17, reaching Category 5 status that day. The storm peaked with winds of 175 mph (280 km/h) on July 18 before making landfall near McClellanville, South Carolina as a powerful Category 4 hurricane. Danny rapidly weakened over the Carolinas and was downgraded to a tropical storm on July 20. However, the storm re-strengthened and reached Category 3 status again on July 23 before making another landfall near the mouth of the Mississippi River as a major hurricane. Danny quickly weakened over land and was downgraded to a tropical depression on July 25. The storm dissipated over Texas later that day.  Hurricane Danny was one of the most powerful hurricanes to hit the United States in over a decade. It caused significant damage throughout its path, particularly in South Carolina and Mississippi. At least 21 people were killed in total, making it one of the deadliest hurricanes in US history.
