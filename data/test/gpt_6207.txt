The Robinvale railway line is a freight railway line in Victoria, Australia. The line links the Port of Melbourne with the town of Robinvale.   The line was opened in 1879 as part of the Victorian Railways' broad gauge network. In 1923, the line was converted to standard gauge. After being closed for over thirty years, the line was reopened in 1993 as part of the Victorian Railways' network of tourist railways. Today, the line operates as a heritage railway.
