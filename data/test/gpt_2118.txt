Helmut Andreas Paul Grieshaber or HAP Grieshaber (born 12 February 1948) is a German economist, politician and member of the Christian Democratic Union.  Grieshaber was born in Gera, Thuringia in 1948. After completing his Abitur in 1966 he studied Economics at the University of Freiburg and the University of Mannheim. In 1971 he received his doctorate from the University of Mannheim, with a thesis on the economics of agricultural technology. From 1971 to 1978 he was an assistant professor at the University of Mannheim, and from 1978 to 1981 he was a professor at the Free University of Berlin. He has been a professor at the University of Kiel since 1981.  From 1984 to 1987 Grieshaber was Minister for Economic Cooperation and Development in the Federal Republic of Germany (FRG). From 1987 to 1990 he was Federal Minister for Research and Technology in the FRG. From 1990 to 1994 he was Federal Minister for Environmental Affairs in the FRG. From 1994 to 1998 he was Federal Minister for Economic Cooperation and Development in the new German government.
