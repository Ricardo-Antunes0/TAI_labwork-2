Troy Blakely (December 18, 1949  February 2, 2019) was an American singer and songwriter who had a successful career in the 1970s and 1980s. He is best known for his work as the lead singer of the rock band Troy Blakely and the Blue Notes.  Blakely was born in Cleveland, Ohio, to parents Walter and Ernestine Blakely. He began playing the drums at age four and became interested in singing after hearing groups such as The Supremes and The Temptations. He formed the Troy Blakely and the Blue Notes in 1968 with his friend Ronnie Winters. The band released two albums before disbanding in 1971.  In 1973, Blakely formed the funk band The Troys with bassist Pee Wee Crayton, keyboardist Glen Payne, and drummer Rick DeLuca. The group released two albums before disbanding in 1978. In 1979, Blakely joined the funk group Parliament-Funkadelic as their lead singer and remained with them until 1985. During his time with Parliament-Funkadelic, Blakely released four albums, including 1985's "Troy's Trip".  In 1988, Blakely formed the rock band Troy Blakely and the Soul Searchers with bassist Gerald Butler and drummer Tyrone Woods. The group released two albums before disbanding in 1991.  Blakely died of cancer on February 2, 2019 at the age of 71.
