Jack Morris Sherman (January 18, 1956 – August 16, 2003) was an American professional baseball player who played in Major League Baseball (MLB) for the Detroit Tigers, Los Angeles Dodgers, and Minnesota Twins. He was a two-time All-Star and won the American League (AL) Cy Young Award in 1988.  Sherman was born in Montgomery, Alabama, and attended Auburn University. He was drafted by the Detroit Tigers in the first round of the 1978 amateur draft. After playing in the minors for four seasons, Sherman made his MLB debut with the Tigers in 1984. He became one of the most dominant pitchers in baseball during his 14-year career, winning 211 games while losing only 88. In 1988, Sherman won the AL Cy Young Award after leading the league with a 2.27 ERA and finishing second in voting to Seattle Mariners pitcher Randy Johnson. Sherman also led the Tigers to their first World Series championship in 22 years in 1988.  After his retirement as a player, Sherman served as a pitching coach for the Dodgers and Twins from 2000 to 2003. He died from lung cancer at age 50 in 2003.
