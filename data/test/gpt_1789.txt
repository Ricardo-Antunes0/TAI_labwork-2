Emilio Botín-Sanz de Sautuola García de los Reyes (born 1 March 1942) is a Spanish banker and businessman. He is the Chairman and Chief Executive Officer (CEO) of Santander Group, the largest banking group in Spain, and the third largest in Europe.  Botín was born in 1942 in Madrid, Spain, to José Botín y García-Sanz de Sautuola and Doña María de los Reyes y Botín. He attended the Instituto Cervantes in Madrid, where he studied business administration. Botín first worked for his father's company, Banco Santander, before he joined La Caixa Bank as a director in 1975. In 1988, he became the CEO of La Caixa and held that position until 2000. In 2001, he founded Banco Santander Vizcaya Argentaria (BSV), which merged with Banco Santander in 2004. He served as the chairman of BSV until 2006.  Botín was appointed chairman of Santander Group in 2006 and CEO in 2007. He has been the chairman of the board of directors at Santander since 2009. He has also been a member of the boards of directors of Telefónica since 2006, Acciona since 2007, Iberdrola since 2011, General Electric since 2013, AIG from 2013 to 2014, and Prisa from 2014 to 2015.  Botín married journalist Pilar Rubio on 3 July 1977; they have two children.
