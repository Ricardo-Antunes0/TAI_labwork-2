Minar-i Chakari is a pillar made of pink sandstone located in the center of the city of Kabul, Afghanistan. The tower was built in 1497 by Timur and stands at 116.5 meters tall.   The pillar is a popular tourist destination and is also known as the "Lion's Tower" because of its resemblance to a lion's head. The tower is decorated with mosaics, terracotta tiles, and floral designs.
