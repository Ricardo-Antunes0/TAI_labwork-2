David Fyodorovich Tukhmanov PAR (, was born on September 25, 1948 in Leningrad, USSR) is a Russian composer and conductor.  Tukhmanov was born into a musical family. His father was a violinist, his mother a pianist, and he has two sisters. He began his musical studies at the age of six at the Leningrad Conservatory under the tutelage of Lev Oborin. He continued his studies at the Moscow Conservatory with Lev Arsenyev and Nikolai Myaskovsky, winning First Prize in 1971.  From 1971 to 1974, Tukhmanov served as Assistant Conductor of the Leningrad Philharmonic Orchestra. He then became Conductor of the Tallinn Symphony Orchestra (1974-1983), Principal Conductor of the Russian Chamber Orchestra (1983-1991), and Chief Conductor of the Mariinsky Theatre Orchestra (1991-2000).  Since 2000, Tukhmanov has been Conductor laureate of the Belarusian National Opera and Ballet Theatre. In 2003 he was awarded the title of People's Artist of Russia.  Tukhmanov's compositions include operas, ballets, symphonies, choral works, and chamber music. His most famous opera is "The Love for Three Oranges" (premiered in 1984).
