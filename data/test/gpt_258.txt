Maria Michi (24 May 1921 – 7 March 1978) was an Italian singer, actress and model. She is best known as the lead singer of the vocal trio Michi & The Delfonics.  Born in Rome, Michi began her career as a model and appeared in several films before landing a role in the Mario Mattoli comedy film "Il ladro di bambini" (1955). She then formed the trio Michi & The Delfonics with her husband Renato Caruso and bassist Pino Daniele. The group scored success with their recordings of traditional Italian pop songs, including their debut hit "Si può fare" (1958). Michi featured on several other singles before retiring from the music industry in 1972. She later appeared in a number of Italian television dramas and made occasional appearances on Italian talk shows.  Michi died of cancer in 1978 at the age of 55. Her death was widely mourned in Italy and she was posthumously awarded a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.
