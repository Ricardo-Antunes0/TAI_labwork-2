MII is a professional analog recording videocassette format that was developed in the early 1990s by Sony and Philips. It was intended as a successor to the Betamax format, but was not widely adopted.  MII is an analog format with a capacity of up to 1 hour and 30 minutes per tape. It uses half-inch tape cassettes with a capacity of between 64 and 128 MB. The tapes are recorded in stereo using the AC-3 encoding format.
