Todd Edward Burns (born July 6, 1963) is an American comedian, actor, and writer.  Burns was raised in Bellevue, Washington and attended Bellevue High School. He then attended the University of Oregon, where he was a member of the Delta Chi Fraternity. He transferred to the University of Washington, where he studied political science.  Burns began his stand-up career in the early 1990s in Seattle. He appeared as a guest on "The Conan O'Brien Show" and "The Late Show with David Letterman", and released his first comedy album, "Boys Club", in 1993. He moved to Los Angeles in 1995 and released his second album, "Complexities of Truth".  In 1998, Burns starred in and wrote the screenplay for the film "The Brothers McMullen", which was directed by Jonathan Levine. The film received critical acclaim and won the Grand Jury Prize at the Sundance Film Festival. Burns also appeared in the film, playing the role of McMullen's brother.  In 2002, Burns starred in and wrote the screenplay for the film "The Big Lebowski", which was directed by Joel and Ethan Coen. The film received critical acclaim and won six Academy Awards, including Best Picture. Burns also appeared in the film, playing the role of Jeffrey Lebowski.  Burns has been married to actress Julie Bowen since 1998. They have two children together.
