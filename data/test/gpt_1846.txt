Mark Korda is an Australian businessman and film producer.  He is the co-founder, with his brother and business partner, Michael Korda, of the London-based production company Working Title Films. He produced the films The Thief of Bagdad (1940), From Here to Eternity (1953), and Lawrence of Arabia (1962). He also produced the television miniseries A Tale of Two Cities (1984).  Korda was born in Melbourne, Australia on October 3, 1912. After studying at Melbourne University, he started his own film distribution company in 1935. In 1946, he founded Working Title Films with his brother Michael. The company became internationally successful and produced many critically acclaimed films, including The Elephant Man (1980), The Third Man (1949), and Chaplin (1992). He retired from active film production in 1984 but remains a consultant producer for Working Title Films.
