Jay Wexler (born April 12, 1969) is an American film and television producer, director and writer.  Wexler was born in New York City and raised in Los Angeles. He began his career as a production assistant on the Fox series "Party of Five", before moving up the ladder to become a producer on that show and later on "Baywatch". He then became a writer/producer on the CBS series "Nash Bridges" before creating and directing the WB series "The Guardian".  Wexler's feature film credits include "Saving Private Ryan" (1998), "The Insider" (1999), "The Village" (2004), and "The Lucky One" (2012). His television credits include "Party of Five", "Nash Bridges", "24", "Chuck", and "The Mindy Project".
