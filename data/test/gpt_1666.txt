Frank Scoblete (born 1947) is an American artist and sculptor known for his large-scale public art installations.  Frank Scoblete was born in 1947 in New York City. After studying at the School of the Museum of Fine Arts, Boston, he received his BFA in 1969. In the early 1970s, he began working as a sculptor and installation artist. His first major public works were the "Hexagon" (1982) and "Circle" (1983) sculptures installed in Philadelphia's LOVE Park. Scoblete's work has been exhibited at the Museum of Modern Art, the Whitney Museum of American Art, and the Guggenheim Museum, among others. He is currently a professor at Yale School of Art.
