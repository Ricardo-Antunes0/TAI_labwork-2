Stavros Karampelas is a Greek politician and Member of the Hellenic Parliament for the Panhellenic Socialist Movement.  Karampelas was born on December 7, 1963 in the town of Komotini, in the province of Aetolia-Acarnania, Greece. He studied political science at the University of Athens and graduated in 1987. He then worked as a journalist for the newspaper "Eleftherotypia".  Karampelas was first elected to the Hellenic Parliament in 1996 as a member of the New Democracy Party. He served as Deputy Minister for Social Policy from 2004 to 2007 and as Minister for Regional Development from 2007 to 2009. From 2009 to 2011, he was Minister for Maritime Affairs and Fisheries. In February 2011, he resigned from his position as Minister of Maritime Affairs and Fisheries after several corruption allegations were made against him.  In May 2011, Karampelas founded the Panhellenic Socialist Movement (PASOK). The PASOK is a centrist political party that has been in government with the New Democracy Party since May 2012. Karampelas has been re-elected to the Hellenic Parliament multiple times since 2011.
