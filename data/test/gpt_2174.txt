Mark Kasprzyk (born 1977), stage name M. Rivers, is a Polish-born British singer, songwriter and musician. He is the lead singer of the electronic music duo Mark Ronson & The Business Intl.   Rivers was born in Warsaw, Poland in 1977 to a Polish father and an English mother. He moved to the United Kingdom with his family at the age of six. After attending a Catholic boarding school in England, he studied music technology at the University of Westminster.  Rivers began his musical career as the lead singer of London-based electro-pop outfit The Black Keys, which he formed in 2001 with Dan Auerbach and Patrick Carney. The duo released its debut album, "Brothers", in 2002 and went on to release three more albums before disbanding in 2006. Rivers subsequently pursued a solo career, releasing his debut album, "The Miseducation of M. Rivers", in 2007. The album reached number 10 on the UK Albums Chart and spawned the singles "Lose My Breath" and "River".  Rivers has collaborated with a variety of artists over the course of his career, including Sir Paul McCartney, Usher, Tove Lo, Nile Rodgers, Pharrell Williams and Snoop Dogg. He has also appeared as a guest judge on the eighth season of American Idol and performed at several music festivals around the world, including Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival and Glastonbury Festival.
