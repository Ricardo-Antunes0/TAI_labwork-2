Jacek Olczak (born 1965) is a Polish film director and screenwriter.  Olczak's films are often characterised by their intense atmosphere and distinctive visual style, which has been praised by critics. He has developed a reputation for films with strong narrative arcs and complex, multi-layered storylines. His most well-known film is the psychological thriller The Pianist (2002), which was awarded the Palme d'Or at the 2002 Cannes Film Festival.
