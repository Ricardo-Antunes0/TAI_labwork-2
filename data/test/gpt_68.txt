Ericameria parryi (syn. Chrysothamnus parryi) is a species of flowering plant in the aster family. It is endemic to Ecuador and known from the Galápagos Islands.  This species is a shrub or small tree growing to a height of 2–5 m tall. The leaves are oblong, long and broad, with serrated margins. The flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of branches, and are yellow or greenish-yellow with a darker band on the petals. The fruit is a curved pod 2–3 cm long containing several seeds.
