David Cohen (November 13, 1914 – October 18, 1998) was a Canadian politician. He was a Liberal Member of Parliament for the riding of Windsor—St. Clair from 1962 to 1988.  Cohen was born in Windsor, Ontario and educated at the University of Toronto and Osgoode Hall Law School of York University. He served in the Canadian Army during World War II and then practiced law in Windsor. In 1962, he was elected to the House of Commons as a Liberal representing Windsor—St. Clair. He served as Parliamentary Secretary to the Minister of Revenue and Deputy Minister of National Health and Welfare from 1971 to 1974 and as Parliamentary Secretary to the Minister of Finance from 1974 to 1977. Cohen was also a member of the Standing Committee on Transport and Communications and the Standing Committee on Industry and Commerce. He retired from Parliament in 1988.
