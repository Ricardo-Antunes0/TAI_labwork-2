Malcolm X Day is an American holiday celebrated on February 14th. The holiday was first observed in the city of New York, where it was originally called "X-Day". The holiday is dedicated to Malcolm X, a black American Muslim leader and activist who was assassinated in 1965.  Malcolm X Day is celebrated in remembrance of Malcolm X's work as a civil rights activist and his impact on American society. His speeches and writings are remembered for their militant anti-racist rhetoric and advocacy for black self-reliance. Malcolm X is also credited with helping to create the Nation of Islam, a controversial religious movement which has been described as black supremacist.
