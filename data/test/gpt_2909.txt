Kakatiya Kala Thoranam (also called Warangal Gate) is one of the twelve gates of the city of Warangal in the Indian state of Telangana. The gate, which is also known as Kakatiya Kala Thoranam Mahal, is a 16th-century structure built by the Kakatiya dynasty.  The gate was built in 1598 by the Kakatiya ruler, Balarama Varma, in memory of his father, Emperor Krishnadevaraya. The gate is located in the city's old core area and is a major tourist attraction. It is also used as a ceremonial gateway to the city. The gate is decorated with sculptures and reliefs depicting scenes from the Hindu epic Mahabharata.
