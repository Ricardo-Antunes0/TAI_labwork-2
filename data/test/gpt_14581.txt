Professor Wong Yuk-shan, SBS, BBS, JP (; born January 10, 1937), is a Chinese-born Taiwanese educator, social scientist and politician. He served as the Minister of Education from 2000 to 2002 and as the Vice President of the Republic of China from 2002 to 2004.  Wong was born in 1937 in Wenzhou, Zhejiang Province, China. He received his B.A. from National Taiwan University in 1959, his M.A. from the University of Michigan in 1962, and his Ph.D. from Cornell University in 1966.  Wong began his teaching career at National Taiwan University in 1962 and later served as the Dean of the Faculty of Social Science from 1986 to 1990. He then served as the Minister of Education from 2000 to 2002 and as the Vice President of the Republic of China from 2002 to 2004. Wong has been a professor at National Chiao Tung University since 1990 and has also been a visiting professor at universities in North America, Europe and Asia.
