Tasa Konević Apostolović (; d. 1916) was a Serbian Orthodox monk and writer.  Tasa Konević was born into a noble family in the village of Apostolovići near Kalinovik, in present-day Montenegro. In 1875 he entered the Monastery of Saint Sava in Belgrade and took the monastic name Tasa. He developed a reputation for piety and learning, and in 1892 was made abbot of the monastery. Konević also served as librarian to Patriarch Aleksandar Karadjordjević.  Konević wrote numerous religious and historical texts, including a biography of Saint Sava. He also wrote poetry, some of which has been praised for its evocative language. Konević died in 1916 at the age of 78.
