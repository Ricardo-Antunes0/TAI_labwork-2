Colonel Frederick Cecil "Derek" de Saram, OBE, DSO, MC, was a British Army officer who became a successful regimental commander in the Rhodesian Bush War.  Frederick de Saram was born on 12 March 1932 in Salisbury, Southern Rhodesia. He was educated at Wellington College and the Royal Military Academy, Sandhurst.  De Saram served in the Royal Scots Greys before being commissioned into the British Army in 1953. He served with the Royal Scots Greys and then with the 1st Battalion The Queen's Own Cameron Highlanders before being posted to the 2nd Battalion The King's Royal Rifle Corps in 1957.   De Saram transferred to the infantry in 1959 and served with the 2nd Battalion The King's Royal Rifle Corps and then with the 4th Battalion The Parachute Regiment before being posted to the 1st Battalion The Royal Ulster Rifles in 1964.   De Saram became a regimental commander in the Rhodesian Bush War, serving with the 1st Battalion The Royal Ulster Rifles and then with the 4th/5th Special Air Service Regiment. He retired from the Army in 1978.   De Saram was made an Officer of the Order of the British Empire (OBE) in 1978 and a Member of the Order of Zimbabwe (MZ) in 1980. He died on 14 November 2010 at the age of 80.
