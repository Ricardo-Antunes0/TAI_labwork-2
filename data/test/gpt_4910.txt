The shy heathwren (Hylacola cauta) is a small passerine bird of the heath family. It is found in Europe and central Asia.  The shy heathwren is a small bird, about 20 cm long. It is mainly black, but has a white throat and chest, and a white streak through the eye. It has a delicate appearance, with a short tail and small wings. The song is a soft, high-pitched whistle.  The shy heathwren breeds in central and eastern Europe, and winters in Africa, Arabia, and India. It is usually seen in open areas with some vegetation, but can also be found in urban areas. It feeds on insects and other small animals.
