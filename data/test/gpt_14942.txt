Willie Mays Aikens (born October 14, 1954) is an American former professional baseball player. He played as an outfielder in Major League Baseball (MLB) for the San Francisco Giants, Philadelphia Phillies, and Toronto Blue Jays from 1974 to 1998. He is the all-time leader in home runs (755), runs batted in (2,154), and total bases (3,391) by a left-handed hitter in MLB history. Aikens was inducted into the Baseball Hall of Fame in 2002.  Aikens was born in Mobile, Alabama, and played baseball for the Mobile Bears of the Gulf Coast League. He was drafted by the Giants in the fourth round of the 1974 MLB draft and made his debut with them that season. He became a regular player for them over the next decade, becoming a two-time All-Star and winning three Gold Glove Awards. In 1998, Aikens was traded to Toronto and finished his career with the Blue Jays. He announced his retirement from professional baseball in 2001.  Aikens has been involved in several business ventures after retiring from baseball. In 2003, he founded Willie Aikens Enterprises, a hospitality company which operates Willie's Barbecue restaurant chains in Texas and Florida. In 2006, he founded Willie Aikens Foundation, a charitable organization which promotes literacy and education.
