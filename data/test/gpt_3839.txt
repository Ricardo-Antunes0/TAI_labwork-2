Polish Requiem (original Polish title:  Requiem; Polish pronunciation: [reËˆkwim]; English title: Polish Requiem) is a composition for organ, chorus and orchestra by the Polish composer Karol Szymanowski. The work was finished in 1911 and first performed in 1912.  The Polish Requiem has been described as one of the most powerful pieces of secular music ever written. It is also one of the most frequently performed works for organ, with over 1,000 performances recorded to date. The work is widely considered to be one of the finest examples of classical organ music.
