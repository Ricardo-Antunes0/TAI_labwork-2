Shane Rose (born 24 April 1973 in Perth, Western Australia) is an Australian singer, songwriter, and guitarist.  Shane Rose was born in Perth, Western Australia. He was raised in the small town of Wickepin, about 150 kilometres north-west of Perth. Shane Rose has been a musician all his life and started playing the guitar at the age of 10. He learned to play by listening to his older brother's records and playing along to them. Shane rose to prominence in the early 1990s as the lead guitarist and backing vocalist for the Australian rock band Karnivool.  In 1998, Shane released his solo debut album, "Shane Rose". The album spawned two hit singles, "Fire" and "I'm Not Alone". Shane rose to international prominence with his performances on both "The Tonight Show with Jay Leno" and "The Late Show with David Letterman". In 2001, Shane released his second album, "The Best of Shane Rose". The album spawned two more hit singles, " Whiskey" and "Don't Say Goodbye".  In 2006, Shane released his third album, "The Soul of Shane Rose". The album was nominated for a ARIA Award for Best Pop Release. In 2008, Shane released his fourth album, "The Reckoning", which spawned the hit single "The Last Goodbye". Shane has toured extensively throughout Australia and internationally.
