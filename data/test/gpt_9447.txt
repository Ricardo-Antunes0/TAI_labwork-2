Heaven Without People; ) is the debut studio album by American deathcore band DevilDriver. It was released on November 5, 2006 through Relapse Records.  The album was recorded at Sound City Studios in Van Nuys, California with producer Jason Suecof. The album's sound has been compared to that of Slipknot, Deicide and Lamb of God.  The album peaked at number 151 on the "Billboard" 200 chart. It has sold over 100,000 copies worldwide.  "Heaven Without People" received mixed reviews from music critics. At Metacritic, which assigns a normalized rating out of 100 to reviews from mainstream critics, the album has a score of 57, indicating "mixed or average reviews".   AllMusic reviewer John Bush awarded the album 3 out of 5 stars and stated that "while there are definite standouts on this release, such as the blistering 'Killing Fields' and the catchy 'Torn to Pieces,' there's not much that really grabs you and sucks you in."   In a more positive review, Sputnikmusic's Evanescence-lover gave the album an 8 out of 10 and wrote "DevilDriver have created an impressively cohesive debut that is sure to please fans of all types of metal."
