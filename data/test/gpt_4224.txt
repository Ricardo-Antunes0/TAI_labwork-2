Columbia is a U.S. city in and the county seat of Hinds County, Mississippi. The population was 27,521 at the 2010 census.  Columbia is part of the Jackson metropolitan area. It is located in south-central Mississippi on the eastern bank of the Pearl River, approximately south of Jackson and north of Vicksburg.  Columbia is a center for trade and commerce in south-central Mississippi. The city is home to the largest concentration of businesses in Hinds County, and it is an agricultural hub, with cotton, soybeans, and poultry being the leading agricultural products. The city's economy is also based on education, health care, and tourism.  The city was founded in 1824 by Major John Coffee Hays and Major George Graham as "The New Columbia." It was named for South Carolina's capital city of Columbia. Columbia was incorporated as a city in 1828.  The first courthouse was built in 1839. The current courthouse was built in 1917 and designed by noted American architect Walter Gropius. Columbia became a center for civil rights activity during the 1960s and 1970s with organizations such as the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP) holding meetings and protests there. In 1991, the National Football League's Kansas City Chiefs moved to the city from Truman Sports Complex in Kansas City; they were renamed the Chiefs after their initial name, the Kansas City Chiefs.
