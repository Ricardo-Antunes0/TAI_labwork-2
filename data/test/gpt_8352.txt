The Freshmen were among the most popular blue-eyed soul groups of the late 1960s and early 1970s, enjoying chart and album success with singles such as "Let's Hear It for the Boy" and "Ain't That a Shame". They were inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2018.  The Freshmen were formed in 1965 in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania by brothers Jeffrey and Ronald Phillips, along with cousins Melvin "Monty" Bogan and Eddie Kendricks. The group initially consisted of Jeffrey Phillips on lead vocals and acoustic guitar, Ronald Phillips on lead vocals and electric guitar, Melvin Bogan on tenor saxophone and baritone saxophone, Eddie Kendricks on bass guitar, and Ronnie Crawford on drums.  The Freshmen signed with Atlantic Records in 1966, releasing their self-titled debut album that year. The album was produced by Jerry Wexler and featured the hits "Let's Hear It for the Boy" and "Ain't That a Shame". The group toured extensively throughout the US and Canada during the next two years, building a large following.  In 1968, The Freshmen released their second album, entitled "Fresh", which featured the hit single "I Can't Get Next to You". The album reached number two on the US charts and was also successful overseas.
