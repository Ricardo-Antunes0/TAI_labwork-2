Bird Homestead, also known as the Bouton-Bird-Erikson Homestead, is a historic home and farm complex located in Rye, Westchester County, New York. It is owned by the city of Rye and was purchased in 2009. The property is situated on Blind Brook estuary, off the Long Island Sound. The property is adjacent to the Rye Meeting House.  The main part of the house was built in 1835, and is a two-story, three-bay wide frame building in the Greek Revival style.  It sits on a brick foundation and has a low-pitched, side gable roof.  It features a one-story, full-width, front porch.  Also on the property are a contributing two-story barn built in the 1880s and a long, one-story outbuilding. It was added to the National Register of Historic Places in 2010. It is operated along with the adjacent Rye Meeting House, by the not-for-profit Committee to Save the Bird Homestead.
