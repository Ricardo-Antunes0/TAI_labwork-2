Sophar Rangoon was born in 1822 in the village of Myaing, present-day Bago Region, Myanmar. He was orphaned at an early age and became a ward of the local courtier, Kyaw Kyaw Tun. After learning martial arts from Kyaw Kyaw Tun, Rangoon entered the Royal Military Academy in 1842 and graduated in 1846. He served in various military posts in Upper Burma before being made brigadier general in 1859.   Rangoon played a leading role in the suppression of rebellions in the late 1860s and early 1870s. In 1871 he became commander-in-chief of the army and played an important role in the restoration of Pagan rule. He died on 22 January 1890 at the age of 62.
