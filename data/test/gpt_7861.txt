Karl Ernst Thomas de Maizière (; born 7 February 1956 in Heidelberg) is a German politician who served as the 11th Minister of the Interior from 2009 to 2013. He is a member of the Christian Democratic Union (CDU).  Born into a Protestant family, de Maizière studied law at the University of Heidelberg and the University of Freiburg. After completing his legal training, he worked as a lawyer in Heidelberg and then in Bonn.  De Maizière entered politics in 1987, when he was elected to the Bundestag for the CDU. He served as a deputy from 1987 to 1998, and then as Minister of State for Federal Affairs from 1998 to 2002. In 2002, he was appointed Minister of the Interior by Chancellor Gerhard Schröder, serving until 2009.  In 2009, de Maizière was appointed Minister of the Interior by newly elected Chancellor Angela Merkel. As interior minister, he was responsible for policing and border control, as well as for Germany's immigration policy. In 2013, he resigned from his position after being implicated in a political scandal surrounding the leaking of documents relating to surveillance by the NSA.  Since leaving office, de Maizière has been a senior fellow at the German Institute for International and Security Affairs (SWP). In 2017, he was appointed Secretary General of the CDU parliamentary group in the Bundestag.
