Jean Marquès-Rivière (1903–2000) was a French journalist, historian, and politician.  Born in 1903 in Paris, Marquès-Rivière began his career as a journalist in the 1930s. He served as the chief editor of the French newspaper "L'Humanité" from 1952 to 1979, during which time he also served as the newspaper's director of information. He was a member of the French National Assembly from 1958 to 1962, and from 1965 to 1968 he was the president of the National Assembly's General Commission on Press and Propaganda. He was also a professor at the University of Paris II from 1968 to 1989. Marquès-Rivière died in 2000.
