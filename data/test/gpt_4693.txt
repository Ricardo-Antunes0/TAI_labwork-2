Arthur John Hartman (July 14, 1888 – January 9, 1967) was an American psychologist and dean of the University of Utah from 1945 to 1954.  Hartman was born in Salt Lake City, Utah to John Wesley Hartman and Sarah Elizabeth (née Elam) Hartman. Hartman was educated at the University of Utah, where he obtained a B.A. in 1912 and an M.A. in 1913. He then studied at the University of Berlin, where he received his doctorate in 1916.  Hartman returned to the United States and became a faculty member at the University of Utah in 1916. He served as dean of the university's College of Liberal Arts from 1945 to 1954, during which time he also served as president of the Association for the Study of Social Problems from 1951 to 1952. Hartman died in 1967 at the age of 84.
