Roberto Calderoli (born 18 April 1956) is an Italian conservative politician and member of the Senate of the Republic since 2006.  Calderoli was born in Turin, Piedmont. He is a lawyer by profession. Calderoli was first elected to the Italian Chamber of Deputies in the 1994 general election, representing the Italian Lega Nord. He was re-elected in 1998,2000 and 2006. Calderoli served as Minister of Education from 1998 to 2001 under Silvio Berlusconi's first government.  In February 2009, Calderoli was accused of racism after he said on television that "immigrants from non-European countries must be forced to leave Italy" and "they should not be let into our schools". Calderoli later said he was joking and that he did not endorse any racist views. In June 2009, he was acquitted of charges of inciting racial hatred.
