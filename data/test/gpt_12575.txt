The Zettabyte Era is an era in computing that refers to the point at which 1 zettabyte (1021 bytes) of data is stored on digital media. The term was coined in 2007 by IBM researcher Gennaro Giovannini. This milestone was reached in late 2012 when a hard drive manufactured by Toshiba reached a capacity of 1 trillion bytes. Since 2007, there has been exponential growth in data storage capacity, with new technologies such as 3D printing and quantum computing increasing the potential for storing ever-more data.
