Berlin Coal Carrier is a black and white silent film released in 1918. The film was directed by D.W. Griffith and stars Lillian Gish, Griffith, and Walter Huston.  Berlin Coal Carrier tells the story of a young woman who is forced to work on a coal ship in order to support her family. The crew of the ship is made up of rough and tough men who treat the woman poorly, but she manages to stick together with them and eventually falls in love with one of the men on the ship.
