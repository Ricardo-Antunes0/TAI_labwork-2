Paitchau (also Gunung Paitchau, or Paitchao, or Paitchaou) is a mountain in West Java, Indonesia. It is located in the province of Central Java, about 120 kilometers southwest of the city of Yogyakarta. With an elevation of 2,642 meters above sea level, it is one of the highest mountains in Java. The mountain is known for its scenery and natural resources, including gold and silver deposits.  Paitchau is a popular tourist destination, with several trails leading to the summit. The mountain is also used for agriculture, with plantations covering much of its slopes. Paitchau has been the site of several archaeological discoveries, including remains of ancient Hindu temples.
