The Humphrey Sommers House is a pre-Revolutionary period house in Westborough, Massachusetts. It was built c. 1710 by Humphrey Sommers, a successful farmer and landowner who served as selectman and town treasurer. The house is a two-story wood frame structure, with a gable roof and clapboard siding. It features a central chimney and two side chimneys, and is surrounded by a low stone wall. The house was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1984.
