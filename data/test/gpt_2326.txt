Abu Bakr al-Siddiq (; 27 October 57323 August 634) was the first of the six major companions of Muhammad who became the first caliph, or successor to Muhammad.  Abu Bakr was born into a wealthy Quraysh family in Mecca in 573 CE. He became a Muslim during the time of Muhammad in the early 7th century CE. After the death of Muhammad, Abu Bakr became the leader of the Muslim community and was responsible for making decisions on religious matters. He also became known as the "father of Islamic law". Abu Bakr was also one of Muhammad's most trusted companions.
