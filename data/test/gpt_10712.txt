Omnibus was an Italian language weekly illustrated magazine published in Milan, Italy, from 1965 to 1978. It was the first illustrated magazine in Europe devoted to comics and cartoons.   Omnibus was founded in 1965 by the artist and writer Maurizio De Felice as a supplement to the newspaper Corriere della Sera. The magazine featured comic strips, cartoons, and humorous articles. In 1978 it merged with the satirical magazine L'Espresso to form L'Espresso-Omnibus.   The magazine was influential in the development of Italian comics and animation. It was also notable for its editorial policy of promoting political and social satire.
