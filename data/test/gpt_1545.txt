Mother Mary Aloysia Hardey, R.S.C.J., (Piscataway, Maryland, 28 May 1839 â€“ London, England, 6 November 1897) was an American Catholic religious educator and the founder of the Sisters of Charity of the Incarnate Word.  Aloysia Hardey was born in Piscataway, Maryland to a farming family. She entered the Sisters of Charity of the Incarnate Word on 7 September 1863 in Baltimore, Maryland. She taught at several schools in Baltimore before founding a school for girls in London, England in 1892. Aloysia Hardey became known as "the foundress of the British branch of the Sisters of Charity".  Aloysia Hardey died in London, England on 6 November 1897.
