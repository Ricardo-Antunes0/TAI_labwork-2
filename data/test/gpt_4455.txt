Richard Lyndell Stroup (died November 18, 2021) was an American politician who served as the 22nd Lieutenant Governor of Arkansas from 2014 to 2021.  Stroup was born in Fort Smith, Arkansas, on November 18, 1961. He earned a Bachelor of Science degree in business administration from the University of Arkansas in 1983. He then worked as a business executive before entering politics.  Stroup was elected lieutenant governor in 2014, as the running mate of then-governor Asa Hutchinson. During his time in office, Stroup served as the presiding officer of the Arkansas Senate and as president pro tempore of the Arkansas House of Representatives. He also chaired the Governors' Task Force on Economic Development and served as a member of the board of directors for the Arkanasian Dental Association.  Stroup announced his intention to resign from office effective January 10, 2021, after being diagnosed with pancreatic cancer. He died on November 18, 2021, just months before his term would have ended.
