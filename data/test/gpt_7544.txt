Chris Hillman is an American musician and songwriter. He has released four solo albums, one with The Byrds and three with The Desert Rose Band. He has also released two compilation albums and fourteen singles.  Hillman was born in Los Angeles, California, on December 3, 1945. He began his career as a member of the Byrds, with which he recorded five studio albums between 1965 and 1968. After leaving the Byrds, Hillman formed The Desert Rose Band with veterans of the country music scene. The band released three albums between 1973 and 1975 before breaking up. In 1984, Hillman formed a new Desert Rose Band with singer/guitarist John Doe and bassist Larry McCallum. This incarnation of the band released two albums before disbanding in 1987.  Since 1987, Hillman has released four solo albums: 1990's Country Soul, 1992's Waiting for the Moon to Shine, 1995's Road Goes On Forever and 1998's Live at the Greek Theater. He has also released two compilation albums: 1990's Greatest Hits and 2001's Legends. He has also released fourteen singles, including four Number One hits: "Back in Your Arms", "Don't Let Go", "Jessie's Girl" and "I'm Lonesome Today".  Hillman is a member of the Board of Governors for the Academy of Country Music. In 2001, he was inducted into the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame as a member of The Byrds.
