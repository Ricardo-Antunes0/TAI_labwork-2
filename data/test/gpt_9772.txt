Arjun Lal Jingar is an Indian politician who is a member of the Indian National Congress party. He has been a Member of the Rajya Sabha, the upper house of the Indian Parliament, since May 2014. Previously he was a Member of the Madhya Pradesh Legislative Assembly from 2003 to 2007 and from 2009 to 2014.  Arjun Lal Jingar was born on 17 January 1964 in a Jat family in Bhopal, Madhya Pradesh. He completed his graduation from Mahatma Gandhi College, Bhopal in 1983 and did his post graduation from Jawaharlal Nehru University in 1988. He started his political career as an activist with the Youth Congress. He was elected to the Madhya Pradesh Legislative Assembly as a candidate of the Indian National Congress party from the Jabalpur constituency in 2003. He served as the Minister of State for Tourism and Culture in the government of Shivraj Singh Chouhan until he resigned in 2007 over differences with Chouhan on policy matters.  He was re-elected to the Madhya Pradesh Legislative Assembly from Jabalpur in 2009 and served as Minister of State for Heavy Industries and Public Enterprises until he resigned in 2014 to take up a Rajya Sabha seat. In May 2014, he was appointed Minister of State for Petroleum and Natural Gas. In this role, he played a leading role in negotiations to secure India's entry into the Nuclear Suppliers Group.
