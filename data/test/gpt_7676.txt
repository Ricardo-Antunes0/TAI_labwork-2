From 1931 to 1935 he taught at the Royal College of Art, where he became Head of the School of Painting and Sculpture. In 1945 he was appointed Director of the Sir John Cass School of Art at Liverpool, a post he held until his retirement in 1971.  Winer's work is characterised by its simplification and abstraction, as well as its use of strong colours. He exhibited regularly throughout his career, and his works are in many public collections, including the Victoria and Albert Museum, the Tate Gallery, and the Museo Nacional de Bellas Artes in Mexico City. He was made a Member of the Order of Merit in 1979. He died in 1994.
