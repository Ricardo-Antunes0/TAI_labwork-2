Marian Elizabeth Hobson Jeanneret,  (née Hobson; 8 January 1918 – 3 March 2003) was an English historian and academic.  Born in London, Jeanneret was educated at the University of Oxford, where she earned her Bachelor of Arts in 1939 and her Master of Arts in 1942. She then worked as a research fellow at Somerville College, Oxford, where she completed her Doctor of Philosophy in 1948.  Jeanneret began her teaching career at Somerville College, Oxford, where she served as a lecturer from 1948 to 1951. From 1951 to 1953, she served as a professor at the University of Edinburgh. She then returned to Oxford, where she served as a professor from 1953 to 1971. Jeanneret also served as the vice-principal (academic) of the University of Oxford from 1970 to 1973.  Jeanneret is noted for her work on medieval history. Her notable works include "The English Church in the Middle Ages" (1956), "The Growth of Parliamentary Democracy" (1961), and "The Elizabethan World" (1978). She was also a Fellow of the British Academy and the Royal Historical Society. In 1963, Jeanneret was awarded an honorary doctorate by the University of Edinburgh.  Jeanneret died on 3 March 2003 in Oxfordshire, England.
