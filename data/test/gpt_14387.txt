Elaine Margaret Sadler  is an Australian actress and singer. She started her performing career as a child, appearing in stage productions such as "The Piano Lesson" and "The Sound of Music". She attended Cranbourne Secondary College before studying at the Victorian College of the Arts, from which she graduated in 1995 with a Bachelor of Fine Arts.  Sadler's television debut came in 1994 with the Australian soap opera "Neighbours". She played character Gina Burrows from 1995 to 1997. In 1998, she appeared in the short-lived soap opera "Heartbreak High". In 1999, she began playing Rachel Kinski on the Australian drama series "Rush". The show ran for two seasons before being cancelled in 2001.   In 2002, Sadler appeared as Katarina Witt on the Australian drama series "Kings". The show ran for two seasons before being cancelled in 2004. That same year, she appeared as Mona Simpson on the Australian drama series "The Slap". The show ran for two seasons before being cancelled in 2006.   In 2007, Sadler starred as Tracy Turnblad in the Australian musical drama film adaptation of John Waters' 1978 film "Hairspray", which was directed by Adam Shankman and produced by Andrew Lloyd Webber and David Geffen.
