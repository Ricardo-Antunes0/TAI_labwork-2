Poker is a family of card games that involve hands of five cards, with either two players or a single player against another. The game is played with a standard deck of 52 cards.  Poker was descended from a Spanish game called poque, which was brought to America in the 16th century. In modern poker, the object is to win as much money as possible by making better hands than the other player. The game can be played for money or for entertainment.  Poker is widely considered to be a dangerous game. Many people who play poker become addicted to the excitement and dopamine released during gameplay. There have been incidents of people losing large sums of money due to poor play or gambling addiction.
