Gail Gibbons is an American writer and critic.  Gail Gibbons was born in Evanston, Illinois, in 1952. She received her B.A. from Northwestern University in 1974 and her M.F.A. from the University of Iowa in 1977. Gibbons is the author of four novels: The Last Great American Dream (1988), A Fine Romance (1991), The Mercy Seat (1994), and The Unnamed (1999). Gibbons has also written criticism for magazines and newspapers, including the New York Times, Harper's, and the Washington Post. Gibbons has taught at the University of Iowa, Brown University, and the University of California at Irvine. She currently lives in Washington, D.C., with her husband, the novelist Michael Chabon.
