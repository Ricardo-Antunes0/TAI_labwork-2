The Ural Federal University named after the great Russian scientist and statesman N. I. Vernadsky (UFU) is a federal university located in the city of Ural, Russia, with campuses in the cities of Yekaterinburg and Chelyabinsk. It was founded in 1991 and began operations in 1992.  UFU is the only federal university in Russia that was founded specifically to meet the needs of the Urals region. The main focus of the University is to provide quality education, research and development in the fields of natural sciences, mathematics, engineering and technology, economics, law, humanities and social sciences. UFU also offers unique opportunities for international study. The University has a strong research base with nearly 500 professors and researchers from 80 countries working on over 200 projects.
