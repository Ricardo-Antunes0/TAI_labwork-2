Enrique Chaffardet (December 5, 1907 – April 14, 1978) was a French painter and sculptor.  Born in Paris, Chaffardet studied at the École Nationale Supérieure des Beaux-Arts under Maurice Denis. He exhibited his work at the Salon d'Automne in 1928 and the Salon de Paris in 1929. Chaffardet joined the Group of Ten in 1930 and participated in the third dimension of painting at the Salon des Indépendants in 1936. He also exhibited at the Venice Biennale in 1940.  Chaffardet's work is best known for its bold use of color and its sculptural qualities. His paintings typically depict urban scenes with a strong sense of movement. His sculpture often features human figures with elongated limbs and exaggerated heads.
