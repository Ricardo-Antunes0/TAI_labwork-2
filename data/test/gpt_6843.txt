Beat Street is the sixth studio album by the British rock band the Rolling Stones. It was released on 8 November 1972 by Rolling Stones Records in the United Kingdom and by ABKCO Records in the United States. Recorded at Olympic Studios in London between January and March 1972, the album marked a departure from the band's earlier blues-oriented work, incorporating elements of funk, soul, and psychedelia.  Critical reception to Beat Street was mixed. Some reviewers praised its experimental sound, while others dismissed it as unenjoyable and derivative. The album has since been largely forgotten, selling fewer than 100,000 copies in the United States during its original release.  In 2003, Rolling Stone magazine ranked Beat Street number 592 on their list of "The 500 Greatest Albums of All Time".
