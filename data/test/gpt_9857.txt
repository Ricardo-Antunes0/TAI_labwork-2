John Daniel Boon (January 8, 1817 – December 30, 1905) was a U.S. Representative from Missouri.  Born near Lexington, Missouri, Boon attended the common schools and Jefferson College, at Canandaigua, New York. He studied law. He was admitted to the bar in 1839 and commenced practice in Lexington. He served as member of the State constitutional convention in 1848. He moved to St. Louis, Missouri, in 1856 and continued the practice of law. He served as member of the State house of representatives in 1865 and 1866 and as speaker of the house in 1865.  Boon was elected as a Republican to the Forty-third Congress (March 4, 1873 – March 3, 1875). He was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1874 to the Forty-fourth Congress. He resumed the practice of law in St. Louis, Missouri and served as president of the board of education there from 1886 to 1895. He died there December 30, 1905 and was interred in Bellefontaine Cemetery.
