John D. Towle (d. 1887) was an American educator and historian. He was the first president of Towle College, now Towson University.  Towle was born in 1832 in Stockbridge, Massachusetts. He attended the common schools and then studied at Dartmouth College and at the University of Pennsylvania. He taught school for several years before becoming a school superintendent in Maryland.  In 1872, Towle became the president of Towle College, which had been founded in 1871. Under his leadership, the school grew rapidly and achieved great success. Towle died in 1887, after serving as president for only five years, and is buried in the college cemetery.
