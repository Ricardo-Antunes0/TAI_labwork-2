He was born in Amsterdam, the son of Pieter Serrarens and Anna Geertruida van der Donck. After studying at the University of Amsterdam, he obtained a doctorate in mathematics from the University of Cambridge in 1954. He then worked at the University of California, Berkeley and the University of Oxford before being appointed professor at the University of Groningen in 1966. Serrarens retired from his professorship in 1983.  Serrarens is known for his work in mathematical physics, especially on the theory of nonlinear partial differential equations and the theory of harmonic oscillators. In 1968 he was awarded the Fields Medal, one of the most prestigious prizes in mathematics. He was also a member of the Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences since 1959. He died on 23 December 2015 at the age of 92.
