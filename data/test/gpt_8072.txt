Jay R. Traver (1894–1974) was a University of Michigan professor, historian, and author.  Traver was born in 1894 in Battle Creek, Michigan. He received his B.A. in 1915 from the University of Michigan and his M.A. in 1916. He received his Ph.D from the University of Michigan in 1921.  Traver began his teaching career at the University of Michigan in 1921 as an instructor in history. In 1934 he was promoted to assistant professor and in 1941 he was promoted to associate professor. He became a full professor at the University of Michigan in 1951 and retired in 1970.  During his career at the University of Michigan, Traver served as the chair of the history department and as chairman of the Department of History and Political Science. He also served as the editor-in-chief of the "Michigan Quarterly Review" from 1955 to 1962 and as president of the American Historical Association from 1963 to 1964.
