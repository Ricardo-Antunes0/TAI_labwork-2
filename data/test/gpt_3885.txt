Pekina is a town and locality in the central-western region of Western Australia. It is located within the Shire of Menzies, about 560 kilometres (350 mi) from Perth, the state's capital. At the 2006 census, Pekina had a population of 339.  The town is situated in a fertile area and is known for its production of fruit and vegetables. The town also has a bakery, drapery and grocery store, hotel and pub. There is also a primary school and a medical clinic. The Pekina railway station is served by Transwa coaches twice weekly to Geraldton and Kalgoorlie.
