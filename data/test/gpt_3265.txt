Matthew Loveland Dennis (February 11, 1914 – December 8, 1988) was an American character actor, comedian and singer. He is best known for his work on television, where he was a regular on the situation comedy Rowan and Martin's Laugh-In from 1967 to 1975.  Dennis was born in Brooklyn, New York, to John Dennis, an advertising executive, and his wife, the former Lillian (née Loveland). After attending Columbia University in New York City, Dennis began his career performing at nightclubs and theaters in the 1930s. He made his film debut in 1942 in the comedy-drama The Gay Divorcee.  Dennis's breakthrough came in 1967 when he joined the cast of Rowan and Martin's Laugh-In, a television show that brought him national prominence. He remained with the show until its cancellation in 1975. During that time, he also appeared on such sitcoms as The Bob Newhart Show and Happy Days.  After Laugh-In ended, Dennis continued to act in films and television shows, but he became more involved in stand-up comedy. He toured throughout the United States for several years before retiring in 1981.  Dennis was married three times: to actress Betsy Drake (1944–1946); to singer Phyllis Diller (1967–1975); and to model/actress Bebe Buell (1980–1988).
