Chewelah  is a city in Stevens County, Washington, United States. The population was 2,157 at the 2010 census.  Chewelah was founded in 1902 and was named for Chief Chewelah of the Nisqually tribe. The city is located on U.S. Route 12 and the Chewelah River.  Chewelah is the home of the Stevens County Fair and Expo, which is one of the largest county fairs in Washington. The fairgrounds are home to a variety of events, including a livestock show, carnival, and flea market.  The city is also home to Chewelah High School, which was established in 1923. The school is known for its wrestling program, which has produced many successful athletes.
