The FS Class 737 is a high-speed train type operated by Italian State Railways, sharing components with the Spanish AVE Class 102. It was developed as a replacement for the ageing Ferrovie dello Stato (Italian State Railways, FS) Class E430 Frecciarossa high-speed train. The first two trains were delivered in 2007 and entered service in 2008.  The class is composed of six carriages: four first class and two second class compartments. The trains are powered by two Siemens S60A3+ Pulse diesel engines, providing a maximum speed of 300 km/h (186 mph).
