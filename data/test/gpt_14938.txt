Sourav Chandidas Ganguly (; natively spelled as Ganguly, born 12 January 1971) is an Indian former cricketer who played for the Indian cricket team in Tests, ODIs and Twenty20s. He played as a wicket-keeper-batsman and was a dependable lower-order batsman. A right-handed batsman, he was known for his aggressive batting style, as well as his fielding ability in the covers.  Sourav Ganguly made his Test debut in 1996 against Zimbabwe and went on to play 146 matches, scoring 7,426 runs with a highest score of 149 not out. He was a part of the Indian team that won the ICC World Cup in 2003 and became the first captain to be named captain of the ICC World XI. He retired from international cricket in December 2007 after playing a final match against Australia.
