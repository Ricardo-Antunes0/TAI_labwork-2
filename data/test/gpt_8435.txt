Burmese (, MLCTS: mranmabhasa, IPA: ) is a language spoken in Myanmar (formerly Burma), and is the national language of Myanmar. It is also spoken as a second language in Thailand, Laos, and India.  Burmese is a tonal language and there are six tones: high, middle, low, rising, falling, and neutral. The basic word order in Burmese is subject–verb–object. There is a strong grammatical gender system and a three-part verb conjugation. Burmese has a rich verbal system with numerous particles.  Burmese is an agglutinative language and has a large number of loanwords from other languages including English, Thai, Mon, and Shan. There are also many indigenous Burmese words. Some of the most important literary works in Burmese were written in the 13th century by the poet Mrauk U.
