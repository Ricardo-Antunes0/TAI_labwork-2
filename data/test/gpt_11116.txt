"Missile Gap" is a 2006 English language novel by Canadian author Margaret Atwood. The book is the sequel to her award-winning 1985 novel The Handmaid's Tale, and tells the story of Offred, a member of the Handmaids in the Republic of Gilead, and her niece Moira.  The novel was announced as a selection for the 2007 Toronto International Film Festival on 10 November 2006. The film adaptation, directed by Bruce Miller and starring Elisabeth Moss, was released in Canada on 25 June 2017.  The novel has been praised for its dystopian vision and its exploration of themes such as feminism, religion, and the role of women in society.
