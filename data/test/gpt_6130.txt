The Magic Mountain  is a novel by German author Thomas Mann, first published in 1924. It tells the story of Hans Castorp, a young man who is sent to a sanatorium on the Swiss Alps for tuberculosis treatment. While there, he becomes friends with the other patients and learns about life, love, and death.  Mann's novel has been praised for its exploration of the human condition and its depiction of life at a sanatorium. It has also been called one of the greatest novels of the 20th century.
