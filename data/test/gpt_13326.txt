Wrocław  is a city in southwestern Poland, the largest city in the Lower Silesian Voivodeship and the seventh largest city in Poland. It was founded by the Teutonic Knights in 1241 and became a major commercial center at the heart of the Protestant Mittel-Europa. Due to its religious and cultural diversity, Wrocław has been dubbed a "cultural capital of Poland".   Wrocław is situated on the Oder River, in the historic Lower Silesian region. The city is also located on national highways No. 8 and No. 9, and railway lines connecting it with Katowice, Kraków, Wrocław, Prague and Vienna. With a population of approximately 650,000 people, Wrocław is one of the most densely populated cities in Poland.
