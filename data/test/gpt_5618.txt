The Stony Brook Seawolves are the athletic teams that represent Stony Brook University in the National Collegiate Athletic Association (NCAA) Division I athletics. The Seawolves compete as members of the America East Conference (AEC).  The Seawolves mascot is Wally the Walrus and their colors are green and white. The Seawolves have a rich athletic history, having won or shared more than 100 NCAA championships. The Seawolves have appeared in the NCAA Men's Division I Basketball Championship 27 times, most recently in 2017.  The Seawolves teams have been sponsored by Pepsi since 1982 and have had several other major sponsors throughout their history. In 2007, Stony Brook became the first Division I university in the United States to receive a grant from Nike for a new indoor practice facility. The facility, which was completed in 2009, was designed by HOK Sport and has been called one of the most advanced basketball practice facilities in the country.  Stony Brook University was founded in 1911 as Stony Brook School of Marine Science and Technology. In 1961, it changed its name to State University of New York at Stony Brook and began awarding degrees in arts and sciences. In 1966, it became a division of the University of California system, and in 1971 it became an independent institution again.
