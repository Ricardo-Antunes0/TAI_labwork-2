The Battle of Hingakākā (sometimes written Hiringakaka) was fought on January 17, 1813, during the Napoleonic Wars, by a combined force of British and American troops against an army of Māori warriors led by Te Rauparaha. The battle was inconclusive, with both sides claiming victory.  The Battle of Hingakaka was fought on January 17, 1813, during the Napoleonic Wars, by a combined force of British and American troops against an army of Māori warriors led by Te Rauparaha. The battle was inconclusive, with both sides claiming victory. The British and Americans had landed on the north-west coast of New Zealand in late 1812 in an attempt to capture Auckland from the Māori. Te Rauparaha had assembled a force of about 1,000 men to oppose them and he led them in a determined attack. Despite outnumbering the British and American forces two to one, the battle was inconclusive and ended in a stalemate.
