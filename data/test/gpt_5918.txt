Amar Haldipur is a noted music director and composer in the Bollywood film industry. He has composed music for over 350 films and has won several awards, including the Filmfare Award for Best Music Director twice.  Haldipur was born on 13 September 1952 in Haldipur, a small town in the state of Uttar Pradesh, India. He started his musical training at the age of six, learning to play the harmonium and the tabla. He later trained under noted music directors like Rajesh Roshan and Laxmikant-Pyarelal.  After completing his formal musical education, Haldipur started working as an assistant to Roshan, composing background scores for a number of films including Deewaar (1975), Dushman (1977), and Kabhi Khushi Kabhie Gham (1981). He composed his first full-length score for the film Baazi (1984), which won him critical acclaim and established him as a leading music director in Bollywood.  Haldipur's subsequent scores include Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge (1995), Kuch Kuch Hota Hai (1998), Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge 2 (2004), 3 Idiots (2009), Kick (2010), and Happy New Year (2014).
