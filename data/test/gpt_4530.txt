Leatherheads is a 2008 American sports comedy-drama film directed by Peter Farrelly and written by Farrelly and Bob Nelson. The film stars George Clooney, Tim Robbins, John Goodman, and Helen Mirren.  The film is about the 1951 Harvard University football team led by All-American quarterback Everett "Pete" DiMaggio, who is trying to lead his team to the Ivy League championship game against Yale. The film tells the story of the team's relationships with one another and with their opponents.  "Leatherheads" was shot on location in Albany, Boston, Burlington, Connecticut, New Haven, Philadelphia, and New York City.  "Leatherheads" was a box office success, grossing over $130 million worldwide. The DVD and Blu-ray Disc sales have also been successful.
