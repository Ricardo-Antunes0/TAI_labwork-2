Robert John Pearce (born 24 February 1946) is an English actor who has worked in television, film and theatre. He is perhaps best known for his roles as Det. Sgt. Andy Martin in the television series Midsomer Murders and John Cleese's sidekick in the sitcom Fawlty Towers.  Pearce was born in Northampton, England, the son of Lillian (née Morris) and Reginald Pearce, a civil servant. He was educated at Northampton School for Boys and Queens' College, Cambridge. After leaving Cambridge, he worked in advertising before beginning his acting career in the late 1970s.  Pearce's first television appearance was in the 1978 BBC2 comedy-drama series The New Avengers, playing a role that was later reprised in the 1981 spin-off series Pennies From Heaven. He then appeared as a prison officer in the 1981–82 British crime drama series The Bill, and as PC Steve McDonald in the 1984–85 British crime drama series Police Surgeon.  In 1990, Pearce starred as Det. Sgt. Andy Martin in the successful ITV police drama Midsomer Murders. The show ran for nine series and became one of Britain's most popular programmes. Pearce also appeared as Dr David Hunter in the 1993 ITV medical drama series Heartbeat and as journalist Malcolm Muggeridge in the 1998 BBC1 historical drama series The Second World War: A History In Pictures.
