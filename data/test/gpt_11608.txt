Air Commodore Richard Charles Montagu Pink CBE (born 23 May 1927) is a retired Royal Air Force officer who served as Commander-in-Chief of the British Air Forces in Europe (C-in-C BAFFE) from 1984 to 1986.  Pink served in the RAF for over thirty years, during which time he held a number of senior command and staff appointments. He was made Air Commodore in 1973 and Commander-in-Chief of the British Air Forces in Europe in 1984. In this role, he was responsible for the defence of Western Europe against potential Soviet aggression. Following his retirement from the RAF in 1986, Pink became a senior adviser to the government on defence matters.  Pink was born on 23 May 1927 in Kingston upon Thames, Surrey. He was educated at Eton College and Trinity College, Cambridge, where he became a member of the Foot Guards. After serving in the Royal Artillery for two years, Pink joined the RAF in 1947. He served as a pilot with No. 54 Squadron RAF during the Korean War and then served with No. 33 Squadron RAF until he was made an air commodore in 1973.  In 1984, Pink was made Commander-in-Chief of the British Air Forces in Europe (C-in-C BAFFE). During his time in this role, he was responsible for the defence of Western Europe against potential Soviet aggression. Pink retired from the RAF in 1986 and subsequently became a senior adviser to the government on defence matters. He died on 13 November 2017
