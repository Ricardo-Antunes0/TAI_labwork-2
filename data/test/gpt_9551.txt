Crossfire Hurricane is a 2012 documentary film directed by Barbara Kopple. The film tells the story of the Great Miami Hurricane of 1935, one of the deadliest natural disasters in American history.  The Great Miami Hurricane, also known as the Crossfire Hurricane, was a devastating natural disaster that struck the Miami area in October 1935. The hurricane killed more than 800 people and caused $200 million in damage.  The film tells the story of the storm from its origins in the Caribbean Sea to its landfall on Miami Beach. The film also explores the efforts made to respond to the disaster, and the human cost of such a powerful storm.
