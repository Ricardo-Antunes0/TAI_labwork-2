Nikolai Borisovich Kulikov (Russian: Куликов, Николай Борисович; October 10, 1913 – June 27, 1941) was a Soviet military officer who was shot by the Germans during the Battle of Kiev in 1941.  Kulikov was born in the town of Orsha in Vitebsk Governorate, Russia, on October 10, 1913. He graduated from the Moscow Military School of Engineering in 1934 and from the Frunze Military Academy in 1937.  He served with the Soviet Army from 1937 to 1941, during which time he participated in the 1939 invasion of Poland and the 1940 invasion of Finland. Kulikov was awarded the Order of Lenin for his actions during the war.  Kulikov was assigned to the 6th Guards Army during Operation Barbarossa, the German invasion of Russia, in 1941. On June 27, 1941, Kulikov was shot by German forces while fighting near Kiev. He was posthumously awarded the title Hero of Soviet Union and promoted to major general.
