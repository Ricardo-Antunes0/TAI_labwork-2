Several vessels have been named Grenada Packet:  - The first ship to bear this name was a brigantine that sailed from Liverpool, England to Saint Kitts in 1784.   - A packet boat built in 1847 at the Swan Hunter shipyard in Newcastle upon Tyne, England was chartered by the Royal Mail to transport mail between Grenada and Saint Lucia.   - The "Grenada Packet" was a steel-hulled, sidewheel packet steamer that operated on the New York-Philadelphia route from 1897 until 1907.   - The "Grenada Packet" was a wooden three-masted schooner that sailed from New York to Saint Vincent and the Grenadines in 1931.
