Grishneshwar Jyotirlinga Temple, sometimes referred to as the "Shiva temple of Gujarat", is a Hindu temple dedicated to the god Shiva located in the town of Gir Somnath, in Gir Somnath district, Gujarat, India.  The temple is dated to the 10th century CE and is a prominent example of the Chalukya architecture. The presiding deity is Shiva, as represented by the lingam, and his consort Parvati is also worshiped here. The temple is considered to be one of the twelve Jyotirlingas. The temple complex also includes a "jagannath" (temple of the lord of the world), two small temples dedicated to Ganesha and Kartikeya, and a large tank. There are also several smaller shrines within the complex.  The temple is open from 6am to 12 pm and 4 pm to 8 pm on all days except Mondays and Fridays, when it opens at 6 am and closes at 7 pm. Admission is free for all visitors.
