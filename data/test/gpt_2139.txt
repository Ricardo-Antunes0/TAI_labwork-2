Louis-Adélard Senécal (July 10, 1829 – October 10, 1902) was a French painter, sculptor and medalist. He was born in Paris.  Senécal was born in the Rue Saint-Jacques, Paris, on July 10, 1829. He was the son of a sculptor named François-Xavier Senécal and the niece of the painter François-Joseph Krier. From 1848 to 1850 he studied at the École des Beaux-Arts under Jacques-Louis David and Jean-Auguste-Dominique Ingres.  In 1850, he won a scholarship to study in Italy, where he stayed for two years. On his return to France, he exhibited at the Salon for the first time in 1852 with a painting called "The Return of Proserpine". He quickly became one of the most popular painters at the Salon, and his work often depicted scenes from classical or biblical mythology.  Senécal also became well known for his sculptures, which were often displayed in public places such as squares and parks. He was made a member of the Legion of Honor in 1892. Senécal died in Paris on October 10, 1902.
