Paradeisos is an archaeological site located in the municipality of Oinos, on the northeast coast of the island of Euboea, in Greece. It is a large sanctuary, and its remains date back to the late Mycenaean period. The site was first excavated in 1870 by the German archaeologist August Schliemann.  Paradeisos is one of the most important Mycenaean sites in Greece. It was discovered in 1870 by August Schliemann and contains a large sanctuary, as well as tombs and an unexcavated area. The remains date back to the late Mycenaean period, and include a number of well-preserved Linear B tablets. The site was first excavated by Schliemann, who found numerous artefacts, including two pieces of gold jewellery, a wine jar, and a number of tablets written in Linear B script.
