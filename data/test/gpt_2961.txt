Pierre Nzé (born 1939) is a Congolese artist who has become well known for his paintings of urban landscapes and people in the Democratic Republic of Congo.  Pierre Nzé was born in 1939 in Pointe-Noire, the capital of the Congo. He studied at the University of Lubumbashi, graduating with a degree in arts in 1965. He worked as a teacher before becoming a full-time artist in 1983.  Nzé's paintings depict scenes from the everyday lives of Congolese people, most notably the cityscape and the street scene. He often uses bright colours and detailed renderings to depict his subjects with realism.  Pierre Nzé has exhibited his work throughout Africa and Europe, and has been awarded several prizes, including the prestigious Prix de Rome in 1984. He is currently based in Kinshasa, the capital of the DRC.
