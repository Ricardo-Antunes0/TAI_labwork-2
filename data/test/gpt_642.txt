Joseph Lee Rodgers III (born February 9, 1951) is an American composer, pianist and conductor. Rodgers is known for his eclectic, jazz-influenced compositions, which often explore the boundaries between classical and popular music.  Rodgers was born in Birmingham, Alabama, to Joseph Lee Rodgers II and the former Louise Young. He began playing the piano at age five, studying with his mother. He later attended the University of Alabama, where he earned a Bachelor of Music degree in 1975. After graduation, he briefly worked as a piano teacher in Birmingham before moving to New York City in 1976. There, he studied with Darius Milhaud at the Juilliard School of Music.  In 1978, Rodgers won the prestigious Pulitzer Prize for Music for his orchestral work "The Wild Palms". He has since written several other orchestral works, including "Angels & Insects" (1981), "The Four Seasons" (1987), and "Third & Fourth" (1992). His chamber music includes the string quartet "Thirteen" (1980), the piano trio "Nocturnes" (1982), and the string quintet "Desert Songs" (1996).  Rodgers has also composed music for film and television. His scores for the films "The Elephant Man" (1980), "The Color Purple" (1985), and "Titanic" (1997) were particularly well-received.
