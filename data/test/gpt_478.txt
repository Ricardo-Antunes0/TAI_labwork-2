Major General Andrew James Molan,  (born 14 November 1956) is a retired Australian Army officer who served as Chief of the Defence Force from 1 July 2015 to 30 June 2017.  Molan was born in Melbourne, Victoria, and attended Scotch College, Melbourne. He completed an Arts/Law degree at the University of Melbourne in 1978 and was admitted to the Victorian Bar the same year. He joined the Australian Army in 1979 and served in a number of senior positions, including Commander of the 3rd Brigade, 1st Division; Commandant of the Royal Military College, Duntroon; and Deputy Chief of Army. He has also served as Director General Land Operations (2004–2005), Deputy Chief of Joint Operations (2005–2007), and Commander Australian Defence College (2007–2010).  In 2010, Molan was appointed Vice Chief of the Defence Force, taking over from Lieutenant General David Morrison. In July 2015 he was promoted to Chief of the Defence Force, succeeding General David Hurley. During his time as Chief of Defence Force, Molan oversaw a number of defence reform initiatives, including a plan to reduce Australia's military footprint by 50 per cent by 2035. On 30 June 2017, Molan retired from his position after almost 25 years service.
