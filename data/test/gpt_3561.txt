Brian Julian Warry Stonehouse MBE (29 August 1957 – 10 November 2006) was an English footballer who played as a defender.  Stonehouse started his career with Everton, where he made 319 appearances in all competitions between 1978 and 1984. He then moved to Norwich City, where he made 228 appearances over two spells. He also had a spell with Sheffield United, before retiring in 1992.  After retiring from football, Stonehouse worked as a coach with Norwich City's academy. He died in 2006 after a long battle with cancer.  Born in Liverpool, Merseyside, England, Stonehouse joined Everton as an apprentice in 1977 and made his first-team debut aged just 18 in a 1–1 draw against Newcastle United on 5 October 1978. He quickly established himself as a first-team regular, and went on to make 319 appearances for the club between 1978 and 1984. He was part of the Everton team that won the FA Cup in 1981 and reached the semi-finals of the UEFA Cup in 1983. During his time at Goodison Park, he formed an influential defensive partnership with fellow England international Gary Stevens.  Following Everton's relegation from the top flight in 1984, Stonehouse transferred to Norwich City for £175,000. He made 228 appearances for the Canaries over two spells, helping them to win promotion back to the Premier League in 1988 and reach the Football League Cup final in 1990.
