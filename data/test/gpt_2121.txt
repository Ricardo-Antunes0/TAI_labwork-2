Sean McVay (born January 24, 1986) is an American football coach and former player who is the head coach of the Los Angeles Rams of the National Football League (NFL). McVay was previously the offensive coordinator for the Washington Redskins, where he helped lead the team to an NFC East Division title and a berth in Super Bowl LIII.  McVay was born in 1986 in Santa Clarita, California. He played college football at the University of Southern California (USC), where he was a two-time All-American and won the Biletnikoff Award as the nation's best receiver in 2009. He also played for the Kansas City Chiefs and the St. Louis Rams in the NFL.
