The Global New Car Assessment Programme (Global NCAP) is a not-for-profit organisation that was founded in 1988. Global NCAP research and testing is carried out on new cars sold in the European Union, Japan and the United States.  The organisation's stated aim is to improve road safety by conducting independent tests of new cars and providing objective data to consumers, legislators and the automotive industry. Global NCAP has been credited with helping to make the world's roads one of the safest places to be.  As of September 2018, Global NCAP has conducted more than 36,000 tests and issued more than 8,500 ratings worldwide.
