Jose “Poli” Garcia (born in San Diego, California, on January 16, 1975) is a retired American professional soccer player who played as a forward.  Garcia played college soccer at the University of San Diego and was drafted in the first round (15th overall) of the 1996 MLS SuperDraft by the Kansas City Wizards. He played two seasons with the Wizards before signing with Mexican side Puebla in 1998. He spent two seasons with Puebla, winning the Mexican League championship in 2000.  He returned to MLS in 2002 when he signed with the Tampa Bay Mutiny. He scored 11 goals in his first season with the Mutiny and was named to the 2002 MLS All-Star Team. The Mutiny finished second in the Eastern Conference and made it to the 2002 MLS Cup Final, where they lost 3-2 to Chicago Fire.  Garcia then signed with English club Middlesbrough where he won promotion to the Premier League in his first season. He made only one appearance for Middlesbrough before joining Scottish side Dundee United on a free transfer in 2004. He spent three seasons with Dundee United before retiring in 2007.  Garcia has also appeared for the U.S. national team, making his debut in a friendly against Spain on November 13, 2000. He has also appeared at the 2002 World Cup and 2006 World Cup.
