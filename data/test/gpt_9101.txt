Golbadan Baji  was a concubine (and later wife) of the Mughal emperor, Akbar. She was of Rajput origin and had been married off to Akbar as part of a political alliance between her family and the Mughals.  Golbadan Baji was a powerful and influential woman in the court of the Mughal emperor, Akbar. She was known for her beauty and intelligence, and she played an important role in influencing Akbar's policies. After Akbar's death, Golbadan Baji became the Empress Dowager of India. She participated in many political affairs during her time as Empress Dowager, and she helped to maintain the stability of the Mughal Empire during times of unrest. Golbadan Baji died in 1605 AD, and she is remembered as one of the most powerful women in Mughal history.
