Birnbaum was born in 1926 in New York City to Jewish parents. After graduating from high school, he served in the United States Navy during World War II. After the war, he studied at the City College of New York, where he received his bachelor's degree in 1949. He then attended Columbia University, where he received his Ph.D. in 1955.  Birnbaum began his career as a research psychologist at the National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH). He became the head of NIMH's Division of Experimental Psychology in 1967, and remained in that position until 1978. In 1978, he was appointed the seventeenth president of Hunter College, a position he held until 1984. During his tenure as president, Birnbaum worked to improve Hunter's finances and enrollment. He also oversaw construction of the college's new Main Campus building and the establishment of its Department of Psychology.  Birnbaum died on November 16, 2016 at the age of 92.
