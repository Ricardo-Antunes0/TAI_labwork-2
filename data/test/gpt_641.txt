Christopher Michael Holt (born September 18, 1971) is an American former professional pitcher who played in Major League Baseball (MLB) for the Cincinnati Reds, Texas Rangers, and Detroit Tigers. Holt was drafted by the Reds in the 1st round of the 1991 amateur draft.  Holt attended the University of Southern California (USC), where he played college baseball for the USC Trojans. He made his MLB debut with the Reds in 1993, and spent most of his career with Cincinnati, playing in 977 games over 12 seasons. He also played for the Rangers and Tigers. In 2002, Holt became one of only four players in MLB history to throw a no-hitter and have a save in the same game.  After his playing career ended, Holt became a pitching coach with the Reds and Rangers. He has also worked as a broadcaster for Fox Sports Midwest and Fox Sports Southwest.
