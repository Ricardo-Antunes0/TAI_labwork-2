Carol Zhao (Chinese:赵一羽; born June 20, 1995) is an American actress and singer. She is known for her roles as Mei-Ling on the ABC drama series China Beach (2000–2002) and its spin-off China Beach: The New Generation (2004–2006), as well as for her performance in the Broadway musical Rent (2005).  Zhao was born in Newark, New Jersey to Taiwanese parents. She moved to Los Angeles with her family when she was a toddler. Zhao began her acting career at the age of eight, appearing in commercials and on television shows. She made her feature film debut in the 2000 drama film China Beach, portraying the title character, Mei-Ling. Zhao also appeared in the television series China Beach: The New Generation, which followed the cast of the original series after they left the military and moved to California. Zhao won a Young Artist Award for her role in China Beach: The New Generation.  Zhao made her Broadway debut in Rent, playing Mimi Joestar. Her performance earned her a Tony Award nomination for Best Performance by an Actress in a Leading Role in a Musical. Zhao reprised her role in the 2007 film adaptation of Rent. In 2009, she starred as Li Ling in the independent film The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo, which was directed by David Fincher and produced by Joel Silver.
