A Hakka walled village  is a type of traditional Chinese village that is typically located in the mountainous regions of southern China, especially in the provinces of Guangdong, Hainan and Hunan. They are also found in Taiwan.  The Hakka people are a Chinese ethnic group who reside primarily in the southern province of Guangdong. The Hakka people are also found in Taiwan, where they form one of the largest minority groups. The Hakka are noted for their agricultural practices and their distinctive dialect of Chinese. Hakka villages typically consist of a walled center surrounded by an open courtyard. This arrangement is thought to protect the village from attack and to facilitate trade and communication between the village residents.
