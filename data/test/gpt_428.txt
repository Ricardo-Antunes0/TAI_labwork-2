Time is the ninth studio album by the Finnish symphonic metal band ELO, released in 1984. It was the band's last album with lead singer/songwriter Jari-Pekka Koivu, and marked a significant change in the band's sound, incorporating more synthesizers into their music.  After four successful albums, including the platinum-selling album Eldorado, ELO parted ways with lead singer Jari-Pekka Koivu and replaced him with former vocalist Roy Thomas Baker. Time marked a significant change in the band's sound, incorporating more synthesizers into their music. The album was not as commercially successful as the band's previous work, but has since gained a cult following.  Time was recorded at Finnvox studios in Helsinki, Finland between February and May 1984. The album was produced by the band and Roy Thomas Baker.   The track "Queen of the Night" is based on the opera La Traviata by Giuseppe Verdi.
