Nilanjan Chatterjee is a Bloomberg Distinguished Professor of Economics at New York University and a senior fellow at the Peterson Institute for International Economics. He is also a research associate at the National Bureau of Economic Research.  Nilanjan Chatterjee was born in Calcutta, India in 1951. He earned his B.A. from the University of Calcutta in 1971 and his M.A. from Columbia University in 1974. He earned his Ph.D. from Yale University in 1978.  Chatterjee has been a professor of economics at New York University since 1984 and a distinguished professor since 2006. He is also a senior fellow at the Peterson Institute for International Economics and a research associate at the National Bureau of Economic Research.  His research focuses on economic development, growth, and public economics, with a particular focus on market failures and institutional economics. He has published more than 150 papers in academic journals and has been featured in several documentaries, including The Great Indian Dream (2003), India's Economic Reforms (2007), The Price of Revolution (2011), and The Next Billion: India's Path to Prosperity (2014).  Chatterjee was awarded the prestigious Croucher medal by the London School of Economics in 2003 and the Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in 2005. In 2011, he was awarded the prestigious John Bates Clark Award by the American Economic Association for his contributions to economic theory and policy analysis.
