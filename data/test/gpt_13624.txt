Never Touched Me is a 1919 American silent drama film directed by D.W. Griffith and starring Mary Pickford and Griffith.  The film tells the story of a young woman who is forced into an arranged marriage with a man she does not love. She is desperate to escape and finds support from her friends and family. The film was groundbreaking for its time, depicting a strong female character who is not afraid to stand up for herself.
