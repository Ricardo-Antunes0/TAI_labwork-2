David Parker Craig  (23 December 1919 – 25 May 1997) was an American philosopher who is especially known for his work on ethics, philosophy of religion, and philosophy of mind. He also wrote extensively on theories of knowledge and language.  Craig was born in Omaha, Nebraska, and educated at the University of Nebraska at Lincoln, where he received his B.A. in 1940 and his M.A. in 1941. He then studied under John Dewey at the University of Chicago, receiving his Ph.D. in 1948. After teaching at the University of Wisconsin–Madison from 1948 to 1958, Craig became a professor at Princeton University, where he remained until his retirement in 1988.  Craig was awarded the honorary degree of D.Litt from the University of Oxford in 1984 and the A.M. degree from Yale University in 1988. He was also awarded an honorary doctorate by the University of Copenhagen in 1992.  Craig died on May 25, 1997, at the age of 80
