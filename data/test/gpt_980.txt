Lý Tự Trọng (20 October 1914 in the city of Hanoi – 8 March 1988 in the city of Paris) was a Vietnamese writer, thinker and activist.  Lý Tự Trọng was born on October 20th 1914 in the city of Hanoi, Vietnam. He was educated in France and he returned to Vietnam in 1946. He worked as a journalist, and he wrote books on philosophy and politics. He was also an organizer of the Vietminh movement, and he served as the chairman of the Central Committee of the Vietminh from 1952 to 1954. After the war, he lived in Paris and he died on March 8th 1988.
