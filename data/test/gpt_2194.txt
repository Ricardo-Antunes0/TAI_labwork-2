"Radio Lover" is a murder ballad by American rock band The Cure. It is the opening track on their fourth album, Seventeen Seconds.  The song was written by vocalist Robert Smith and guitarist Simon Gallup, and produced by the band. "Radio Lover" is a slow-paced ballad with a melancholic lyrical content. It is heavily inspired by the romance of FM radio and its ability to transport listeners to different parts of the world.  The song was first performed live on August 29, 1984 at The Pit in Southampton, England. The song reached number 11 on the UK Singles Chart and number 12 on the US "Billboard" Hot 100.
