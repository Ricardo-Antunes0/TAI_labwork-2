Hetty Balkenende (also spelled Hettie, born 1939) is a Dutch politician who served as the Prime Minister of the Netherlands from 2002 to 2010.  Balkenende was born in The Hague in 1939. After completing her secondary education, she studied law at the University of Amsterdam. She worked as a lawyer before entering politics.  Balkenende was first elected to the Dutch Parliament in 1977, and served as a member of the Labour Party until she became Prime Minister in 2002. She was re-elected in 2006 and 2010.  As Prime Minister, Balkenende was responsible for a number of major policies, including the Netherlands' participation in the Iraq War, which she opposed, and the introduction of the euro currency. She is also known for her work on social welfare reform, which aimed to reduce poverty and social inequality in the Netherlands. Balkenende resigned as Prime Minister after her party lost the 2010 general election to the right-wing Freedom Party of Geert Wilders. Balkenende has since retired from politics.
