Léon Fiévez (30 April 1855 – 27 October 1920) was a French Radical politician. He served as Prime Minister of France from 1906 to 1909.  Fiévez was born in Montpellier, France into a family of modest means. He became an attorney in 1875 and began his political career by joining the Democratic Republican Alliance (ADR). Fiévez served as Mayor of Montpellier from 1892 to 1902. In 1902, he was elected to the National Assembly, where he served as Minister of Justice from 1904 to 1906. Fiévez was elected Prime Minister of France in 1906, and he served until 1909. During his time as Prime Minister, Fiévez enacted a number of social reforms, including a law providing for universal suffrage and a law granting women the right to vote. Fiévez died in Montpellier at the age of 71.
