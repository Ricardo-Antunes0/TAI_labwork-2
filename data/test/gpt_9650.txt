The northern sunfish (Lepomis peltastes) is a species of fish in the family Lepomidae. It is found in Atlantic Canada, the northeastern United States, and Quebec.  The northern sunfish is a small fish, growing to a length of about 18 cm. It has a pointed snout and protruding lower jaw, and its scales are smooth. The northern sunfish is usually green or brownish-green, but can also be yellow or reddish-brown. It has a conspicuous black stripe down its back. The northern sunfish is an ambush predator that feeds on aquatic invertebrates and small fish.
