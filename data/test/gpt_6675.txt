The 37th Armor is an armor (tank) regiment of the United States Army. It was first organized in 1917 as the 37th Infantry Regiment, a part of the National Guard. In 1933, it was reorganized as the 37th Armor Regiment and assigned to the 9th Armored Division. The regiment saw service in World War II and the Korean War. In 1968, it was reorganized as a Saint Louis-based heavy armor battalion and assigned to the 1st Armored Division.  The 37th Armor Regiment is headquartered at Fort Leonard Wood, Missouri, and has a battalion stationed at Fort Bliss, Texas. It is a Saint Louis-based unit that falls under the command of III Corps.
