Robert Dennis Crumb (; born August 30, 1944) is an American artist, writer, and musician. He is known for his comics work, particularly the Zap Comix series, the R. Crumb Archives series, and his album covers.  Crumb was born in San Francisco, California, to middle-class parents. He began drawing at an early age and developed a strong interest in jazz. In the late 1960s, he moved to New York City and began creating underground comics with co-workers such as Al Jaffee, Harvey Pekar, and Gilbert Shelton. In 1973 he published his first underground comic book, Cheap Thrills.  In the 1980s Crumb began producing mainstream comics including Keep on Truckin' (1981) and Music for Chameleons (1986). He has also written children's books and directed several feature films. He currently resides in the San Francisco Bay Area.
