Pierre Hujoel (traditional Chinese: 胡月) is a Belgian artist who is best known for his installation art. He was born in Liège in 1963. Hujoel studied at the Royal Academy of Fine Arts of Belgium from 1985 to 1991 and then at the Université de Liège from 1991 to 1994. His first solo exhibition was at the KASK in Ghent in 1995. He has since exhibited extensively throughout Europe and North America, most notably at the Museum of Modern Art in New York (2000), the Centre Pompidou in Paris (2002), and the Tate Britain in London (2005). In 2008, Hujoel was appointed a Knight of the Order of Leopold by King Albert II of Belgium.
