The Toulouse Mass is a polyphonic 14th-century mass composed in southern France. The composer is unknown, but the work is thought to have been created for the Notre Dame de Toulouse cathedral. The mass is characterised by its polyphonic structure and its use of dissonance, and has been praised for its unique sound.
