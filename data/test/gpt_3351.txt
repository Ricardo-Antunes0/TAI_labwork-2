Lyman Knute Swenson (23 October 1892 – 6 December 1975) was an American painter and sculptor.  Born in Minneapolis, Minnesota, Swenson studied at the Minneapolis School of Fine Arts and the Académie Julian in Paris. He was a co-founder of the American Academy of Arts and Letters, and served as its president from 1946 to 1954. He won the National Medal of Arts in 1958.  Swenson is best known for his paintings and sculptures of American landscapes and figures, which are often characterized by their serene beauty and simplicity. His work has been exhibited at the Museum of Modern Art, the Whitney Museum of American Art, and the Smithsonian Institution's National Gallery of American Art, among other venues.
