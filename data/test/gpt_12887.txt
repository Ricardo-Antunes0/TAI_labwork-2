He emigrated to the United States in 1875 and settled in Chicago. He became a naturalized American citizen in 1884.  Coughlin began his political career as an alderman on the city council of Chicago. He was elected to a second term in 1895 and to a third term in 1901. In 1907, he was elected mayor of Chicago, serving until 1906.  After leaving office as mayor, Coughlin resumed his career as an alderman on the city council. He served for another two terms, from 1909 to 1911 and 1913 to 1915.  Coughlin died in 1926 at the age of 76. He was buried at Mount Carmel Cemetery in Hillside, Illinois.
