David Pearson Wright (born 1953) is an American philosopher, who has written extensively on issues in ethics and political philosophy.  Wright was born in Chicago, Illinois, in 1953. He received his undergraduate degree from the University of Notre Dame in 1975, and his graduate degree from the University of Texas at Austin in 1979. Wright has been a professor at the University of Texas at Austin since 1979.  Wright's work in ethics and political philosophy has focused on questions such as the nature of good and evil, the justification of political authority, and the relationship between reason and tradition. He has also written extensively on issues in epistemology and philosophy of language.  Wright has been a recipient of several fellowships, including the Guggenheim Fellowship (1979), the John Simon Guggenheim Memorial Fellowship (1985), and the ACLS Fellowship (2002). He was also a fellow at the Center for Advanced Study in Theoretical Physics in 2003.
