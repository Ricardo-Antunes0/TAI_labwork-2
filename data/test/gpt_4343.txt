Pavel Ivanovich Belyayev (; 26 June 1925 â€“ 10 December 2006) was a Russian cosmonaut, who was the first person to die in space.  Born in 1925 in the town of Sverdlovsk, Belyayev became a pilot in the Soviet Air Force in 1950 and soon became a test pilot. In 1963 he was selected as one of the six cosmonauts for the Soviet manned spaceflight program. He participated in four missions, first as pilot of Soyuz 1, Soyuz 2, Soyuz 3 and Soyuz 4, before his death in 2006. He was the first person to die in space.
