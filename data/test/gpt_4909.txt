Seiichi Sugano (菅野誠一 17 December 1939 – 30 October 2007) was a Japanese composer and conductor.  Sugano was born in Tokyo on December 17, 1939. After graduating from Tokyo National University of Fine Arts and Music, Sugano began his career as a composer, working with the NHK Symphony Orchestra. In 1969 he founded the Sugano String Quartet, with which he has since toured throughout Japan and abroad. He has also composed music for film and television, most notably the soundtracks to the films "Tokyo Sonata" (1987), "In the Realm of the Senses" (1993), and "The Twilight Samurai" (2003). In 1996 Sugano was awarded the prestigious Japan Academy Prize for Music. He died of a heart attack on October 30, 2007 at the age of 68.
