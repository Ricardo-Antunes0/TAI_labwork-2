William D. Beavis is professor and GFZ Potsdam Research associate at the Potsdam Institute for Climate Impact Research. He is also a research scientist at the German Aerospace Center (DLR) and the Federal Institute for Geosciences and Natural Resources (BMBF).  William Beavis was born on June 14, 1966 in Lubbock, Texas. Beavis attended Texas Tech University, where he earned a B.S. in atmospheric science in 1988. After graduation, he worked as a research meteorologist at the National Weather Service in Lubbock before earning his M.S. in meteorology from the University of Oklahoma in 1991. In 1994, he earned his Ph.D. in atmospheric science from Oklahoma State University.  Beavis began his career at the Potsdam Institute for Climate Impact Research (PIK) in 1996 as a postdoctoral researcher under the supervision of Hans Joachim Schellnhuber. He was promoted to assistant professor at PIK in 2002 and became a full professor there in 2006. At PIK, Beavis has been involved in a wide range of climate research projects, including studies of global climate change, the role of clouds and aerosols in climate change, regional climate modeling, and impacts of climate change on human health and agriculture.  Beavis has also been involved in numerous collaborations with researchers from around the world.
