Gergely Homonnay (24 August 1969 – 1 March 2006) was a Hungarian singer, songwriter and actor. He was best known for his work as a member of the Hungarian pop group Visztas.  Homonnay was born in Budapest, Hungary, on 24 August 1969. He began his music career in the early 1990s, when he joined the group Visztas. The group achieved international success with their singles "Két férfi" (1994) and "Nem fogom elmenni" (1996). In 1997, Homonnay left Visztas to pursue a solo career. He released two albums, "Élet vagy halál" (2000) and "Új élet" (2002), before dying from an AIDS-related illness at the age of 34 in 2006.
