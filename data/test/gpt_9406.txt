Jan Nicolaas Dreyer (born 9 September 1976) is a Dutch film director, screenwriter and producer. He has directed films such as The Passion of the Christ (2004), The Boy in the Striped Pajamas (2008), and The Grand Budapest Hotel (2014).  Dreyer was born in Amsterdam, Netherlands. He studied film at the University of Amsterdam, where he worked as an assistant director on the short films Amsterdam (2002) and The Third Man (2006). Dreyer's debut feature film, The Passion of the Christ, was a critical and commercial success, winning numerous awards including an Academy Award for Best Director.
