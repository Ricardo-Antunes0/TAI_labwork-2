The Tägermoos is an area of 1.54 km2 in the municipality of Gstaad, in the canton of Bern, Switzerland.  The Tägermoos is a meadow located in the municipality of Gstaad, in the canton of Bern. The meadow is bounded to the north by the valley of the Aare river, to the east by the valley of the Saane river, and to the south by the Schiltal valley. The Tägermoos is part of a nature reserve that was established in 1984.  The meadow is home to a variety of wild flowers, including gentian, common cinquefoil, blue-eyed grass, and lesser celandine. The meadow is also home to a number of small mammals, including hares and voles.
