Patrick Paul Billingsley (May 3, 1925 – December 31, 2015) was an American actor, singer, and producer. He is best known for his roles as Dr. Mark Sloan on the ABC medical drama series General Hospital from 1963 to 1976, and as Pete Martell on the NBC soap opera Days of Our Lives from 1984 to 1991.  Patrick Billingsley was born in Los Angeles, California, to parents Lillian (née Luce; 1892–1978) and John Billingsley (1894–1978), a theatrical manager and agent. His paternal grandparents were English immigrants. He attended Santa Monica High School, where he played football and was a member of the varsity track team.  Billingsley began his acting career in the early 1950s, appearing in small roles on such television programs as The Philco Television Playhouse and Studio One. He made his feature film debut in 1957 with a small role in The Defector. In 1959, he had a role in the television film Peyton Place, and subsequently appeared on such programs as The Twilight Zone, Alfred Hitchcock Presents, Gunsmoke, Ben Casey, 77 Sunset Strip, and The Alfred Hitchcock Hour.  In 1963, Billingsley landed the role of Dr. Mark Sloan on the ABC medical drama series General Hospital. He played the role until 1976. From 1984 to 1991, he portrayed Pete Martell on the NBC soap opera Days of Our Lives. In 1981 he made a guest appearance on CBS's The New Dick Van Dyke Show
