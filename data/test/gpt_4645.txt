The discography of American singer Diana Ross, also known as Diana Ross & The Supremes, consists of twenty-nine studio albums, one live album, one compilation album, fifty-six singles, thirty-six music videos, and forty-four awards.  Ross began her recording career in the early 1960s as a member of the Supremes alongside founding members Florence Ballard and Mary Wilson. After signing with Motown Records in 1967, the group achieved success with Ross as the lead singer. The Supremes became one of the most successful pop groups of all time, selling over 200 million records worldwide. Following the departure of Wilson in 1970, Ross released a string of successful solo albums before reuniting with the Supremes in 1975. The group disbanded in 1977 but Ross continued to release solo albums until her retirement in 1998.  Since her retirement, Ross has made occasional appearances on stage and television. She has also been involved in charity work and has been awarded several honorary degrees. In 2007, she was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
