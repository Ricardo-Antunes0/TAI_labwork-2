Carol Nagy Jacklin (1939 - 2011) was an American artist who worked primarily in fiber art.  Born in 1939 in Cleveland, Ohio, Nagy Jacklin studied at the Cleveland Institute of Art, where she received her BFA in 1962. She then moved to New York City, where she continued her education at the School of Visual Arts, earning her MFA in 1965. Nagy Jacklin's early work was in mixed media, but she soon shifted to fiber art, which proved to be a more versatile medium for her expression. She worked primarily with wool and silk fibers, often incorporating natural dyes and other materials into her work. Nagy Jacklin exhibited her work throughout the United States and Europe, winning awards and accolades along the way. She died in 2011 at the age of 74.
