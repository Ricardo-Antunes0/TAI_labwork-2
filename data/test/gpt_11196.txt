Julius Marvin Page (born February 26, 1982) is an American football cornerback who is a free agent. He was drafted by the Tampa Bay Buccaneers in the seventh round of the 2004 NFL Draft. He played college football at Michigan.  Julius Page attended Bishop McNamara Catholic High School in Orland Park, Illinois. He was a two-time all-state selection and helped McNamara to a state championship as a senior.  Page played collegiately at Michigan where he was a two-time All-Big Ten selection and finished his career with 187 tackles, 15 interceptions, five fumble recoveries and four sacks. As a junior in 2002, he was named to the All-Big Ten Freshman team and finished second on the team with 73 tackles. As a senior in 2003, he was named first-team All-American and finished fourth in voting for the Heisman Trophy.  Page was drafted by the Tampa Bay Buccaneers in the seventh round of the 2004 NFL Draft. He signed with the team on May 13, 2004. He played six games for the Buccaneers during the 2004 season and recorded one tackle before being waived on November 17, 2004. Page was re-signed to the team's practice squad on November 18, 2004 and was promoted to the active roster on December 4, 2004. He played in all 16 games for the Buccaneers during the 2005 season and recorded 49 tackles, one sack and one interception before being released on September 3, 2006.
