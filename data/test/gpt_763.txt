Zapp is the debut studio album by American funk artist Zapp, released on July 21, 1975, byRCA Records. Recorded in early 1974 at the Record Plant in Hollywood, the album was produced by John Silver and arranged and conducted by George Duke.  Zapp is considered a landmark funk album, influenced by the likes of James Brown and Sly and the Family Stone. It spawned two Billboard Hot 100 singles: "I Want Your Love" (US #8) and "The Latest Craze" (US #25). The album has been certified platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA).  In 2003, "Zapp" was ranked number 357 on Rolling Stone's list of the 500 greatest albums of all time.
