Llanwenarth House is a small country house, situated in the village of Llanwenarth, about 2 miles (3 km) west of Carmarthen, in Carmarthenshire, Wales. It is a Grade II* listed building.  The house was originally built in the late 18th century by the Lloyd family and was originally known as Llwyn-y-Garth. The Lloyd family were a wealthy and prominent local family who owned coal mines and limekilns in the area. The house passed through several hands before being purchased by Sir Hugh Grosvenor, 1st Baronet in 1893. Sir Hugh was the son of the 2nd Duke of Westminster and he inherited Llanwenarth House from his mother. Sir Hugh refurbished the house and added a new wing which was designed by architect George Edmund Street. The Grosvenor family still own Llanwenarth House and it is currently used as a country residence.
