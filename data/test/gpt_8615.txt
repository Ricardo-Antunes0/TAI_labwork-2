Count Hippolyte François Jaubert (28 October 1798 – 10 February 1874) was a French jurist and politician. He served as the 12th Prime Minister of France from 1848 to 1849.  Hippolyte François Jaubert was born in Versailles on 28 October 1798. He was the son of François Jaubert, a lawyer and politician who served as the deputy for the department of Alpes-de-Haute-Provence during the French Revolution. Hippolyte François Jaubert studied at the Lycée Louis-le-Grand, before attending the University of Paris, where he obtained his law degree in 1818.  After completing his legal studies, Jaubert became a lawyer in his father's practice. He also began to engage in politics, serving as a deputy for Alpes-de-Haute-Provence from 1824 to 1830. In 1830, he was appointed Secretary of State for Foreign Affairs under Charles X. Jaubert served in this position until the July Revolution of 1830, which led to the abdication of Charles X and the establishment of the July Monarchy under Louis Philippe I.  Jaubert continued to serve in various government positions during the reign of Louis Philippe I. He became Minister of Justice in 1840, and served in this position until 1848, when he was appointed Prime Minister by Louis Philippe I.
