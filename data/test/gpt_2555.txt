Phil Mulloy (born 29 August 1948) is a British musician and songwriter. Mulloy was a founder member of the British progressive rock band Yes, and has been the band's lead guitarist since its inception in 1970. He has also released solo albums and worked with a variety of other bands and projects.  Mulloy was born in Cambridge, England on 29 August 1948. He began playing guitar at an early age, influenced by his father, a jazz musician. Mulloy formed Yes in 1970 with singer Jon Anderson, bassist Rick Wakeman, and drummer Bill Bruford. The band achieved international success with its progressive rock music, and released nine studio albums before disbanding in 1998. Mulloy has since released two solo albums and collaborated with a variety of artists. He currently tours with the Phil Mulloy Band.
