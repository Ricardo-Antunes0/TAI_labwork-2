British Steel is the sixth studio album by the British heavy metal band Iron Maiden. Released on 22 October 1990, it was their first album with new lead singer Bruce Dickinson and their last with bassist Steve Harris.  The album peaked at number 2 on the UK Albums Chart, behind Paul McCartney's 1990 album Flowers in the Dirt. It has been certified Platinum by the British Phonographic Industry.  The album was recorded over a period of eighteen months, with production handled by Dave Evans and Martin Birch. "British Steel" is noted for its heavier sound and incorporation of elements of thrash metal into Iron Maiden's signature heavy metal style. The album's singles "Run to the Hills" and "The Number of the Beast" became major hits in the UK and worldwide, respectively.
