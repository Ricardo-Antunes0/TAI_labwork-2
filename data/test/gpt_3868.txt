Gilles Larrain (Dec 5, 1938) is a French film director, screenwriter, and producer. He has directed more than fifty films since the early 1970s, including La Pointe Courte (1975), Une Semaine de Bonheur (1977), and Paris, Texas (1984).  Larrain was born in Saint-Nazaire, Loire-Atlantique, France. He studied at the University of Paris and at the University of Vienna. Before making his film debut in the early 1970s, he wrote scripts for French television. Larrain's films are characterised by their skillful use of long takes and non-actors.
