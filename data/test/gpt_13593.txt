Herbert Edward Cobb (August 6, 1904 – May 7, 1984) was an American actor, director and screenwriter. He was known for his roles in films such as Casablanca (1942), for which he received his only Academy Award nomination, and The Man Who Shot Liberty Valance (1962).  Cobb was born in Dallas, Texas, the son of Lillie (née Reeves) and Herbert Edward Cobb. He was educated at the University of Texas at Austin, where he studied architecture. After leaving college, Cobb moved to Hollywood to pursue a career in acting. He made his debut in the Broadway production of The Threepenny Opera in 1932. Cobb's first major role came with the release of Casablanca in 1942, for which he received his only nomination for the Academy Award for Best Actor. He appeared in films such as The Misfits (1946), High Noon (1952), and El Dorado (1960). Cobb also directed two films, The Informer (1935) and The Hanged Man (1938). He retired from acting in the early 1970s and died in 1984 at the age of 83.
