Rui Naiwei (; born December 28, 1963) is a Chinese artist, sculptor and architect.  Naiwei was born in 1963 in Wuhan, Hubei Province, China. He graduated from the Central Academy of Fine Arts (CAFA) in Beijing in 1986. Naiwei has exhibited at the Venice Biennale, the SÃ£o Paulo Biennial, and the Gwangju Biennale. In 2013, he was the first Chinese artist to be awarded the prestigious Praemium Imperiale by the Italian government. Naiwei is also a professor at CAFA.  Naiwei is considered one of the most important contemporary Chinese artists. His art often features delicate sculptures and abstract designs that explore themes of entropy, temporality and memory. He has been compared to contemporaries such as Yayoi Kusama and Zhang Huan.
