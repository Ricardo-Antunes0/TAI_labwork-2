Malcolm Sathiyanathan Adiseshiah (18 April 1910 – 21 January 1988) was a Sri Lankan-born British economist and academic who is most well known for his work in the field of international trade.  He was the first Professor of Economics at the University of Sussex, where he served from 1951 until his retirement in 1970. He also served as President of the British Economic Association from 1972 to 1973. He was a member of the Royal Economic Society, the Academy of Social Sciences and the British Academy.  Born in Sri Lanka, Malcolm Adiseshiah moved to Britain with his family at the age of five. He attended University College London (UCL), where he obtained his B.A. in economics in 1930, and then undertook postgraduate studies at Cambridge University, obtaining his M.A. and Ph.D. in 1933 and 1936, respectively.  After completing his academic studies, Malcolm Adiseshiah began his professional career as a lecturer at University College London in 1936. In 1941 he transferred to the University of Sussex, where he served as Professor of Economics from 1951 until his retirement in 1970. During this time he also served as President of the British Economic Association from 1972 to 1973.  Malcolm Adiseshiah was a prolific writer and has published several books on economic topics, including Trade and Development (1961), The Economics of Imports (1963), The Theory of International Trade (1965), and The Theory of International Investment (1972).
