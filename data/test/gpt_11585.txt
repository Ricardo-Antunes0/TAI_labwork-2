Young Sherlocks is a 1922 American silent comedy film directed by and starring Buster Keaton. The film is also known as The Three Ages of Buster Keaton.  The film is a darkly comic tale of a group of young men who band together to foil a scheme by their older, more experienced counterparts. Keaton stars as Joe, the leader of the group, who tries to keep his younger friends in line while they navigate the perils of early adulthood.  The film was a commercial and critical success, and is considered one of Keaton's best films. It has been cited as an influence on later generations of filmmakers, including Martin Scorsese and Wes Anderson.
