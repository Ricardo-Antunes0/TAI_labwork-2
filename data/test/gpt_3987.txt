The UCF Knights  are the athletic teams that represent the University of Central Florida in Orlando, Florida. The Knights compete in Division I of the National Collegiate Athletic Association (NCAA) and are members of the American Athletic Conference (AAC).  The UCF Knights began intercollegiate athletics in 1972 as a member of the Sunshine State Conference (SSC). In 1978, the Knights became a charter member of the Mid-American Conference (MAC). The Knights competed in the MAC until 1991 when they joined the American Athletic Conference. Since joining the AAC, UCF has won six conference championships and made ten appearances in the NCAA Division I Men's Basketball Tournament.  The UCF Knights men's basketball team has been perennial contenders in both the AAC and NCAA tournaments. The team has appeared in the NCAA Tournament nine times, including five consecutive appearances from 2006 to 2010. The Knights have also appeared in four consecutive AAC Tournament Championships, most recently in 2016. The men's basketball program also boasts two National Players of the Year, Toney Douglas in 2006 and Shaquille O'Neal in 2007.
