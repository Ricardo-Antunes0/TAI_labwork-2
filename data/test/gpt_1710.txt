Beatrice Pauline Hewitt (1873-1956) was a British artist and sculptor. Her sculptures are noted for their expressive use of modelling, surface texture, and light. She was also a painter, who worked in a naturalistic style.  Beatrice Pauline Hewitt was born in 1873 in London, England to an artistic family. Her father was the painter James Hewitt and her mother was the sculptor Fanny Hewitt. She began her artistic career as a sculptor, but later transitioned to painting. She is best known for her expressive sculptures that use modelling, surface texture, and light to create unique images. She died in 1956 in London.
