Akdamut, or Akdamus or Akdamut Milin, or simply Akdamut, is an ancient Egyptian word meaning "the abysmal god".  Akdamut is one of the most important and mysterious gods in ancient Egyptian religion. In ancient Egyptian religion, he was the god of the underworld and the protector of the pharaoh. He was also considered a god of chaos, destruction and resurrection. The ancient Egyptians believed that he was the son of Ra and the goddess Neith, and that he would one day bring about the end of the world.  Akdamut was originally a god of the desert, but he later became associated with death and the underworld. He was often represented as a man with a serpent coiled around his waist.
