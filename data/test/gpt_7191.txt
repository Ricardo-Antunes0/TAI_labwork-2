Austen was the son of Rear-Admiral Sir Francis Austen and his first wife Henrietta, daughter of Admiral Sir George Cockburn. He joined the Royal Navy in 1815 and served in the Napoleonic Wars. He became commanding officer of the frigate HMS "Lively" in 1839 and captain of the frigate HMS "Cumberland" in 1840. He was knighted in 1852.  In politics, Austen was a Tory MP for Totnes from 1852 to 1857 and for Weymouth from 1857 to 1865. He became First Naval Lord in 1865, serving until his retirement in 1868.
