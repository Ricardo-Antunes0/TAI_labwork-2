Acacia mearnsii, commonly known as black wattle, is a tree or shrub belonging to the genus Acacia of the subfamily Phyllanthoideae. It is native to arid areas in Australia, where it occurs in a wide range of habitats including open woodland, sand dunes and heath.  The black wattle is a medium-sized tree or shrub growing to a height of 2 metres or more. The branches are covered in short, black hairs which are often sticky to the touch. The leaves are arranged in opposite pairs, the leaflets measuring 5–10 cm long and 1–2 cm wide. The flowers are yellow, red or white, and are produced in spikes along the branches from late winter to early summer. The fruit is a pod containing several small seeds.
