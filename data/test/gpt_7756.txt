Ilija Despot (Zaostrog, 20 July 1885 – Belgrade, 10 October 1939) was a Serbian general and politician.  Born in the town of Zaostrog, Ilija Despot grew up in the Principality of Serbia. He attended the Military School in Belgrade and graduated in 1908.  Despot fought in World War I as a captain and was wounded at the Battle of Kolubara. He later served as a colonel in the army of the Kingdom of Serbs, Croats and Slovenes. In 1922 he was promoted to general and served as the military governor of Kosovo and Vojvodina.  In 1930, Despot was appointed Minister for War in the government of Nikola Pašić. He served in this position until his death from a heart attack three years later.
