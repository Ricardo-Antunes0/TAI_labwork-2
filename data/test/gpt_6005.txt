Ronald Nicholas Andruff (born July 10, 1953) is a retired American professional golfer who played on the PGA Tour in the 1970s and 1980s. He was inducted into the World Golf Hall of Fame in 1998.  Andruff was born in Tulsa, Oklahoma, and raised in Scottsdale, Arizona. He attended Chaparral High School, where he was a three-time All-State selection in golf and football.  Andruff attended the University of Arizona, where he played for the Wildcats' golf team. He won the 1971 NCAA Championship, and was runner-up in 1972.  Andruff turned professional in 1973. He won three PGA Tour events that year, including the John Deere Classic. He also finished second at the Masters Tournament and fourth at the U.S. Open. Andruff added another three PGA Tour titles in 1974, 1975, and 1976.  Andruff struggled to maintain his place on the PGA Tour during the late 1970s and early 1980s, but he did finish runner-up twice at The Masters (in 1978 and 1981). Andruff also played on the Champions Tour from 1998 to 2001.  Andruff retired from professional golf in 1984. He now works as a golf commentator for CBS Sports Network and Fox Sports 1.
