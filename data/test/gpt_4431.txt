Jim Whitesell (born December 27, 1959) is an American singer and songwriter. He is best known as the frontman of the punk rock band The Urinals.  Whitesell was born in West Palm Beach, Florida, and attended St. Mary's High School in Delray Beach, Florida. He formed The Urinals in 1980 with high school friends Randy Blythe (guitar) and Jerry Gaskill (drums). The band released its debut album, Dumb Luck, in 1981 on the independent label Homestead Records. The album was distributed by major label Sire Records in 1982.  The Urinals released four more albums before breaking up in 1987. Whitesell later played guitar for the hardcore punk band Black Flag from 1991 to 1994 and recorded two albums with them. He also played guitar for the pop-punk band Blink-182 from 1998 to 2001 and recorded two albums with them.  Since 2002, Whitesell has been a solo artist. His self-titled debut album was released that year. His second album, Blues Before Sunrise, was released in 2004 and features performances by Bob Weir, John Mayer, Willie Nelson, and John Mayall. His third album, Jim Whitesell III, was released in 2008 and features performances by Brent Mydland, Warren Haynes, and Janis Joplin. His fourth album, Love & Hate & Sex & Drugs & Rock 'N' Roll was released in 2013 and was produced by T-Bone Burnett.
