Joli Quentin Kansil (born Joel Dennis Gaines on October 25, 1972) is an American actor, voice actor and stand-up comedian.  Kansil was born in New Orleans, Louisiana, the son of a singer/songwriter mother and an engineer father. After moving to Tampa, Florida, he attended Jesuit High School before moving on to the University of South Florida majoring in drama.  Kansil began his professional acting career as a stage actor with the Tampa Bay Theatre Company before making his television debut in 1995 with a guest appearance on the sitcom Sister, Sister. He made his feature film debut in 1999 with a small role in the comedy-drama My Best Friend's Wedding. Kansil's breakthrough role came in 2003 when he was cast as the title character in the WB series Devious Maids. The show ran for four seasons and Kansil received critical acclaim for his performance as D'Apollonio "Dapper" Donato, a scheming but lovable Italian-American maid. He has since appeared in several television series and films including 2009's horror film Saw III, the 2010 crime thriller film The Town and the 2013 action film Olympus Has Fallen.  Kansil is also known for his voice acting work which includes providing the voice of Goofy in Disney's 2010 animated feature film Mickey Mouse Clubhouse and voicing various roles in Adult Swim's animated series Robot Chicken.
