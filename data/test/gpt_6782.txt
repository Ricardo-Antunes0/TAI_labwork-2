Eucalyptus torquata, commonly known as coral gum, is a species of eucalyptus tree found in eastern Australia. It grows to a height of around 25 metres and has a smooth, red-brown trunk. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a blunt end, and are around 10 cm long. The flowers are blue or white, and are pollinated by bees. The gum is used for timber, pulp, paper and oil production.
