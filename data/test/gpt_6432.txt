John Lee Lee (11 December 1802 â€“ 30 December 1869) was an American lawyer and politician who served as the Governor of Louisiana from 1849 to 1851.  Lee was born in Washington, D.C., to General John Augustine Lee and Anne Elizabeth Lees-McRae. He attended the University of Virginia, where he was a member of the Phi Beta Kappa Society and the Delta Theta Pi Fraternity. He then studied law and was admitted to the bar in 1825.  Lee practiced law in New Orleans, and was elected district attorney for Orleans Parish in 1836. He served in the Louisiana House of Representatives from 1840 to 1842, and in the Louisiana State Senate from 1844 to 1848. In 1849, he was elected governor of Louisiana, serving until 1851.  Lee married Martha Jefferson Taliaferro on 9 October 1830. They had four children: John Augustine Lee (born 1830), Anne Elizabeth Lee (born 1832), Martha Jefferson Lee (born 1835), and George Washington Lee (born 1840). Martha Jefferson Lee married Confederate General Pierre G. Troup on 2 November 1865.  Lee died in New Orleans at the age of seventy-six. He is buried at Saint Louis Cemetery No. 1 in New Orleans.
