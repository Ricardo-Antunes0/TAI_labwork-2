Bedford–Stuyvesant , colloquially known as Bed–Stuy, is a neighborhood in the New York City borough of Brooklyn. It is located south of Downtown Brooklyn, east of Greenpoint, and north of Bushwick. Bedford–Stuyvesant is known for its poverty, crime, and high population density.  Bedford–Stuyvesant is one of the largest and most densely populated neighborhoods in the United States. As of the 2010 United States Census, it had a population of 279,162. It was founded in 1654 as part of the Dutch colony of New Netherland and was originally named Breuckelen. It was named after Henry Frederick, Duke of Bedford, and his son-in-law Stuyvesant, both admirals in the Dutch navy. The neighborhood's history is marked by violence and instability; it has been called "the most dangerous place in America" more than once.  Bedford–Stuyvesant is currently undergoing a large redevelopment process that has been underway since the 1990s. The goal is to improve conditions for residents while also creating new jobs and businesses. Major projects include a new police precinct, community centers, and schools.
