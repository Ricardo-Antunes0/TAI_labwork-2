A maraca , sometimes called rumba shaker, is a percussion instrument originating from the Americas. It is played by shaking the instrument to produce a rhythm.  The maraca is a percussion instrument originating from the Americas. It is played by shaking the instrument to produce a rhythm. It has a long, cylindrical body made of wood or metal, with a small drum head at one end and a larger one at the other. The drumhead is struck with mallets or fingers, producing sound.
