Sujan Sakhi is a 1994 Bangladeshi film directed by Ali Akbar Khan. The film is about a young man, Sujan, who falls in love with a girl, Sumi, but is rejected by her family. In an effort to win her over, he takes a job as a bonded laborer on a plantation run by her family.   The film was shot on location in the Sundarbans. It was screened at the 1994 Cannes Film Festival and won the Prize of the Ecumenical Jury.
