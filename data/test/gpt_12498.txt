Jérôme Dumas , better known as Junior Caldera, was a French artist, musician and composer.  Born in Marseille on January 28, 1928, Dumas was the son of a carpenter. He began his musical education at the age of six, studying the violin and later the piano. He also began painting at an early age, and by the time he was eighteen he had exhibited his work in a number of exhibitions.  In 1947 Dumas moved to Paris to study at the École des Beaux-Arts. He began working as a session musician and composer for several French film studios, and in 1955 he released his first album, which featured covers of American pop songs. In 1959 he released his first album of original material, which was well received by critics. He continued to release albums throughout the 1960s and 1970s, occasionally touring Europe. In 1984 he published his autobiography, Je ne suis qu'un singer.  Dumas died on October 11, 1998, in Paris at the age of 78.
