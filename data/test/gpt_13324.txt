Alison Rosalind Bracknell (12 July 1964 – 4 November 2001), better known as Leah Bracknell, was an English actress and singer.  Leah Bracknell was born in Hammersmith, London, the daughter of Rosalind (née Leigh-Pemberton) and Rodney Bracknell, a film producer. She was educated at the Dragon School in Oxford and then at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art.  Bracknell made her stage debut in 1984 in the original West End production of "The Devils". She went on to appear in many British television programmes and films, including "The West Wing", "Boys from the Blackstuff", "Murder on the Orient Express", "Chariots of Fire", "Heartbeat" and "Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone".  In 1996, Bracknell married actor Andrew Sachs. The couple had two children. Leah Bracknell died of ovarian cancer on 4 November 2001 at the age of 38.
