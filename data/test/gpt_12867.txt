Markland Wood is a residential neighbourhood in the city of Toronto, Ontario, Canada. It is located in the western section of the city, bounded by Bathurst Street to the west, Dupont Street to the north, Sheppard Avenue to the east, and Keele Street to the south.  Markland Wood was first developed in the early 1900s as a streetcar suburb. The neighbourhood features single-family homes on wide streets lined with trees. The community is home to a number of schools and recreation facilities, including a soccer field and a park.  The neighbourhood has seen a recent increase in development with new housing units and condominiums being built. There is also a growing population of immigrants and refugees who have made Markland Wood their home.
