Tony Rossi (born December 11, 1943) is a former Major League Baseball player who played for the Cleveland Indians from 1968 to 1970. He batted and threw right-handed.  Rossi was born in Cleveland, Ohio. He attended Western Reserve University, where he played college baseball for the Western Reserve Red Hawks. Rossi was drafted by the Cleveland Indians in the first round of the 1966 amateur draft. He made his major league debut with the Indians in 1968, and played for them until 1970. In 1969, he led American League rookies in batting average (.282), home runs (14), and RBIs (55). Rossi was also selected to play in the 1969 All-Star Game.  Rossi later played for the Texas Rangers and the Philadelphia Phillies. He retired from baseball after playing in only two games with the Phillies in 1971. Rossi later worked as a baseball coach at several schools, including Western Reserve, Cleveland State University, Youngstown State University, and Kent State University.     Tony Rossi is currently an assistant coach at Kent State University where he coordinates their hitting program
