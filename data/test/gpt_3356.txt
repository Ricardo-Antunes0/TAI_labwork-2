Mikko Kyösti Heiniö (born 18 May 1948) is a Finnish historian, currently a professor at the University of Helsinki. He is known for his work on Finnish medieval history, and in particular his book, A History of Finland, which has been translated into English, German, French and Spanish.  Mikko Heiniö was born on 18 May 1948 in Helsinki, Finland. He received his undergraduate degree from the University of Helsinki in 1972 and his doctorate from the University of Jyväskylä in 1980. From 1980 to 1983 he was a research fellow at the Finnish National Research Institute for History. From 1983 to 1989 he was a professor at the University of Turku. He became a professor at the University of Helsinki in 1989.  Heiniö's work focuses on Finnish medieval history, particularly the period from the 11th century to the 13th century. His book, A History of Finland, was published in 1999 and has been translated into English, German, French and Spanish. Heiniö has also written articles for various journals and newspapers, including Helsingin Sanomat and Suomen Kuvalehti.
