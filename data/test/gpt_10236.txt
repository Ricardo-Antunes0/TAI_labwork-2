Leigh Brackett was born in Los Angeles, California, on December 7, 1915. She was educated at the University of Southern California and later worked as a secretary. She married screenwriter Walter Wanger in 1942.  Brackett's first professional writing job was for a short story for the anthology For Whom the Bell Tolls. She then wrote scripts for a number of low-budget films, including Storm Over the Caribbean (1948), Sahara (1949), and The Big Sleep (1946), which won her an Oscar nomination.  Brackett's work on The Wicker Man (1973) won her another Oscar nomination. Her final film was The Godfather (1972).  She died of cancer on November 11, 1998.
