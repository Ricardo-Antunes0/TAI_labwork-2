Lampong Sheanghah (LS), a small village with a population of about 1,000 people located in the Darlac district of southern Laos, is one of the most isolated villages in Laos. It is located at an altitude of 2,590 metres above sea level and is surrounded by dense forest.  Lampong Sheanghah was originally settled by Mon people who migrated from Yunnan province in China. The village is known for its handicrafts, such as baskets, mats, and textiles. The local economy is based on subsistence farming and logging. There are no paved roads in the village and access to health care, education, or other basic services is limited.   The inhabitants of Lampong Sheanghah are fiercely independent and fiercely proud of their culture and traditions. They are also known for their hospitality and warm hearts.
