Lara Kramer is a Canadian dancer and choreographer.  Lara Kramer was born and raised in Toronto, Canada. She began her dance training at the age of five, studying ballet and contemporary dance. After high school, Kramer studied at the Royal Conservatory of Music in Toronto, where she received a Bachelor of Music in Dance.  Kramer began her career as a dancer with the National Ballet of Canada (NBC), where she was a member of the company's corps de ballet from 2002 to 2005. In 2006, she joined the New York City Ballet (NYCB) as a dancer and assistant choreographer. While at NYCB, Kramer created and choreographed several works for the company, including "Les Liaisons Dangereuses" (2007), "The Nutcracker" (2008), and "Tchaikovsky Pas de deux" (2009).  In 2010, Kramer left NYCB to establish her own dance company, Lara Kramer Dance. The company has since produced several works, including "The Sleeping Beauty" (2011), "Don Quixote" (2012), and "Swan Lake" (2013).
