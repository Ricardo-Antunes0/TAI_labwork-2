Peter Balakian, born June 13, 1951, is an American composer, pianist and conductor. He is the founder and artistic director of the Los Angeles-based ensemble New World Symphony. Balakian has composed over 150 works, including nine symphonies, six concertos, five operas, and numerous other orchestral, choral and chamber pieces. His music has been performed by ensembles around the world, including the Berlin Philharmonic, London Symphony Orchestra, Royal Philharmonic Orchestra, Los Angeles Philharmonic Orchestra, Boston Symphony Orchestra, Chicago Symphony Orchestra, Philadelphia Orchestra and New York Philharmonic.  Born in Beirut to Armenian parents who had fled Turkey during the 1920s famine, Balakian began his musical studies at the age of five with his mother. He later studied at the Manhattan School of Music under Robert Craft and at Tanglewood under Leonard Bernstein. In 1975 he won first prize in the prestigious Tanglewood International Piano Competition.  From 1975 to 1979 Balakian was the principal pianist of the Cincinnati Symphony Orchestra. He later moved to Los Angeles where he founded New World Symphony in 1981. The ensemble has since become one of America's most successful contemporary music ensembles and has made several recordings on Nonesuch Records. Balakian has also served as artistic director of both the Aspen Music Festival and the Seattle Symphony Orchestral Institute. In 2006 he was awarded an honorary doctorate from the University of Cincinnati.
