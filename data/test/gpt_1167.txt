Jon Nese is a Teaching Professor and Associate Dean of Academic Programs at The College of William and Mary. Dr. Nese has been teaching at the college since 1984, and is currently the Associate Dean in charge of Academic Programs.  Dr. Nese's research focuses on instructional technology, assessment, and student success. He has authored or co-authored more than 50 peer-reviewed articles and book chapters on these topics. Dr. Nese also serves as the editor of "ACADEMIC PROGRAMS: A Journal of Theory and Practice" and as a member of the editorial boards of "The Journal of Higher Education" and "The Review of Educational Research."  Dr. Nese has been awarded several fellowships and grants for his research, including a Fulbright Fellowship to study at the University of Konstanz in Germany, a National Science Foundation CAREER Award to study at the University of Edinburgh in Scotland, and a Virginia Commission on Higher Education Faculty Development Award to study at Yale University in New Haven, Connecticut.  He is also a certified professional instructor (CPE), having earned his certification through the National Council for Accreditation of Teacher Education (NCATE).
