Judah ibn Verga (Hebrew: יהודה אבן וירגה) (c. 1135 – c. 1190) was a Hebrew poet of the Middle Ages.  Judah ibn Verga was born in Córdoba, Andalusia, in the early 11th century. He is best known for his poetry, which is written in Hebrew and Arabo-Andalusian dialects. His most famous work is the epic poem "Orlando Furioso", which tells the story of Orlando, a nobleman who travels through Europe fighting against the Saracens. Judah ibn Verga also wrote other poems, including "The Song of Songs" and "The Legend of Good and Bad Women". He died in Córdoba in 1190.
