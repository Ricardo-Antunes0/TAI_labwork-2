Scott Adam Rice (born September 21, 1981) is an American former professional baseball pitcher. He played in Major League Baseball (MLB) for the Los Angeles Dodgers, Kansas City Royals, Toronto Blue Jays, and Chicago White Sox.  Rice attended Hinsdale Central High School in Hinsdale, Illinois. He was drafted by the Dodgers in the first round of the 2000 MLB Draft. He made his MLB debut with the Dodgers in 2006. The following year, he was traded to the Royals. In 2009, Rice was traded to the Blue Jays. The Blue Jays traded him to the White Sox in 2012. With the White Sox, Rice won the 2013 American League Division Series MVP Award after leading them to a championship victory over the Detroit Tigers. Rice signed a one-year contract with the Dodgers for the 2014 season, but he was released before appearing in a game. He signed a minor league contract with the Texas Rangers in 2015 and retired from professional baseball at the end of that season.  Rice has been married to wife Alison since 2006. They have two children together, son Nolan and daughter Nora.
