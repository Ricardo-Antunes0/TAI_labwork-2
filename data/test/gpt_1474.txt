Jheanelle Wilkins (born May 29, 1988) is an American football linebacker who is currently a free agent. She was drafted by the Washington Redskins in the fourth round of the 2010 NFL Draft. She played college football at the University of South Carolina.  Wilkins was born in Sumter, South Carolina. She attended Mt. Zion Christian Academy in Rock Hill, South Carolina and played high school football for the Mount Zion Lions.  Wilkins played college football at the University of South Carolina. As a freshman in 2006, she started all 11 games and led the Gamecocks with 104 tackles. As a sophomore in 2007, she started all 12 games and led the Gamecocks with 164 tackles and 5 interceptions. As a junior in 2008, she started all 12 games and was second on the team with 128 tackles. Wilkins was a unanimous first-team All-American as a senior in 2009.  Wilkins was drafted by the Washington Redskins in the fourth round of the 2010 NFL Draft. Wilkins signed with the Redskins on July 25, 2010. On September 3, 2010, she made her NFL debut against the Dallas Cowboys and recorded two tackles. In Week 11 against the New York Giants, she recorded her first career sack, which helped lead to a Redskins victory. In Week 16 against the Philadelphia Eagles, Wilkins recorded her first career interception off of Eagles quarterback Michael Vick.
