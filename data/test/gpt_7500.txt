No Other is the fourth solo studio album by English singer and songwriter David Bowie, released on 8 July 1977 by RCA Records.  The album was recorded in London and produced by Bowie and Tony Visconti. It was recorded weeks after the release of Bowie's third album, "Station to Station".  "No Other" was well received by critics, who praised its dark and atmospheric music. It reached number six on the UK Albums Chart and number nine in the US. The album has been certified Silver by the British Phonographic Industry and Platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America.  In 2006, "No Other" was reissued on CD with bonus tracks and a new booklet by Cherry Red Records. In 2013, "No Other" was reissued as a deluxe edition including a remastered version of the album, bonus tracks, and a new booklet.
