André Beauneveu (born c. 1335 in Valenciennes, died 1406 in Paris) was a French jurist and politician. He served as the Chancellor of France from 1392 to 1402.  André Beauneveu was born in Valenciennes around 1335, the son of a notary. He studied law at the University of Paris, and became a lawyer in his hometown. He served as the Chancellor of France from 1392 to 1402. In this position, he helped to reorganize the French government and improve the economy. Beauneveu died in Paris in 1406.
