Curtis Williamson  (January2, 1867 April18, 1944) was an American labor organizer and co-founder of the American Federation of Labor.  Williamson was born in 1867 in Massachusetts. He emigrated to the United States in 1882, and worked as a printer and compositor. In 1886, Williamson became an organizer for the Typographical Union of America. He served as treasurer for the union from 1900 to 1906, and as its president from 1906 to 1912.  In 1912, Williamson co-founded the American Federation of Labor with Samuel Gompers. He served as its first secretary-treasurer, and later as its president from 1916 to 1921. In 1935, Williamson was awarded the Medal of Honor by President Franklin D. Roosevelt for his work on behalf of labor. He died in 1944.
