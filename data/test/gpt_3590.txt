Rebecca Kapitire Ndjoze-Ojo (born 18 March 1956) is a Nigerian academic and politician who has been the Governor of the Central Bank of Nigeria since 25 October 2017.  Ndjoze-Ojo was born in Kogi State on 18 March 1956. She completed her secondary education at the Holy Cross Convent School, Kogi in 1972 and then studied economics at the University of Ibadan, graduating with a Bachelor of Science (BSc) degree in 1978. She then undertook postgraduate studies at the London School of Economics, completing a Master of Science (MSc) degree in economic policy in 1984.  Ndjoze-Ojo began her career as a lecturer at the University of Lagos, where she held various positions, including that of Associate Professor, from 1984 until 1992. She then served as the Deputy Vice-Chancellor for Academic Affairs at the Federal University of Technology Owerri from 1992 until 1995.  In 1995, Ndjoze-Ojo was appointed as the Minister of State for Planning and Development in Nigeria's First Republic government. She remained in that role until 1999, when she was appointed Minister for Women's Affairs. In 2003, she became Minister for Youth and Sports, a position she held until 2007.  Ndjoze-Ojo was elected to the Nigerian National Assembly as an Ebony Alliance candidate for Ondo State constituency in 2007, serving until 2011. She subsequently served as the Minister for Environment and Natural Resources from 2011 until 2015.
