Jeff Friedman is an American poet and translator.  Jeff Friedman was born in Brooklyn, New York City, on July 8, 1964. He received his B.A. from Brown University and his M.F.A. from the University of Iowa Writers' Workshop.  Friedman's first book of poetry, The Book of Jokes, was published by Counterpoint in 2002. His second book, The Book of Games, was published by Farrar, Straus and Giroux in 2006. His poems have been published in a number of literary journals, including Poetry, The New Yorker, Harper's Magazine, The Paris Review, and The Southern Review. His translations are published by Farrar, Straus and Giroux and Random House.  Friedman is the recipient of a Guggenheim Fellowship (1999), a Whiting Award (2006), a Lannan Foundation Award (2007), a National Endowment for the Arts Fellowship (2010), and a fellowship from the Academy of American Poets (2011). He has taught at Columbia University and Rutgers University-Newark.
