Mihai Ghimpu (born 19 November 1951) is a Romanian politician who has been Prime Minister of Romania since 26 October 2004. He is also the President of the National Liberal Party, which he has led since 1996.  Ghimpu was born in Brăila, and studied at the University of Bucharest Faculty of Law. He became a lawyer in 1975, and worked as a public prosecutor from 1978 to 1990. Ghimpu served as Deputy Minister of Justice from 1990 to 1992, and as Minister of Justice from 1992 to 1996.  He was elected president of the National Liberal Party in 1996, and was re-elected in 2000 and 2004. As president, Ghimpu presided over the party's merger with the Union for Democratic Reform in 2001, creating the National Liberal Union. He was also a member of the European Parliament from 1999 to 2004.  Ghimpu was appointed Prime Minister by President Traian Băsescu on 26 October 2004, replacing Adrian Năstase. He retained this position until 14 November 2005, when he resigned due to differences with Băsescu over constitutional reform proposals. Ghimpu returned to the prime ministership on 26 October 2007 after being nominated by Băsescu and being endorsed by the National Liberal Party's assembly. He was re-elected as Prime Minister in December 2009 with a majority of 64% of the vote. In May 2014, Ghimpu announced his intention to resign as Prime Minister after his party lost its majority
