DeVaughn Walter Nixon (born July 8, 1983) is an American football quarterback for the Washington Redskins of the National Football League (NFL). He was drafted by the Redskins in the fourth round of the 2006 NFL Draft. He played college football at Michigan.  Nixon has also been a member of the Indianapolis Colts, Baltimore Ravens, and Minnesota Vikings. He has also been a member of the United States national team.  Nixon was born in Detroit, Michigan, to Pamela Nixon and former United States President Richard Nixon. His father, who was also a quarterback in the NFL, played for the Dallas Cowboys and San Diego Chargers. Nixon has two older brothers – Eric and Tucker – and a younger sister, Tricia.  Nixon attended De La Salle Collegiate Institute in Concord Township, Michigan, where he played football and basketball as a junior and senior. In football, he was an All-State selection as a senior quarterback and helped lead De La Salle to the state semifinals as a junior.  Nixon attended Michigan University, where he played for the Michigan Wolverines football team from 2001 to 2004.
