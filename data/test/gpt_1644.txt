Erwin, formerly named Duke, is a town in Moore County, North Carolina, United States. The population was 2,269 at the 2010 census. It is part of the Winston-Salem, North Carolina Metropolitan Statistical Area.  Erwin is located in southern Moore County at (35.647609, -79.977657). The town is bordered to the south by the town of Mount Airy and to the east by the town of Siler City. U.S. Route 70 passes through Erwin as Main Street and leads east to Greensboro and west to Goldsboro.  According to the United States Census Bureau, Erwin has a total area of , all of it land.  As of the census of 2000, there were 2,346 people, 809 households, and 602 families residing in the town. The population density was 563.4 people per square mile (217.4/km²). There were 853 housing units at an average density of 184.4 per square mile (70.8/km²). The racial makeup of the town was 96.40% White, 0.36% African American, 0.36% Native American, 0.92% Asian, 0.12% from other races, and 1.98% from two or more races  Hispanic or Latino of any race were 1.68% of the population.  There were 809 households out of which 31.5% had children under the age of 18
