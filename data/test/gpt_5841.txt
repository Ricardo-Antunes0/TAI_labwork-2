Michael Denzil Xavier Portillo (born 26 May 1952) is a British Conservative Party politician who served as the Member of Parliament (MP) for Enfield Southgate from 1997 to 2005. He then served as the Secretary of State for Trade and Industry from 2005 to 2006.  Portillo was born in London, the son of Olga (n√©e Burda) and Denzil Portillo. He was educated at North London Collegiate School and Jesus College, Cambridge, where he read history.  In 1974, Portillo became a lecturer at the University of Essex. He was elected MP for Enfield Southgate in the 1997 general election, and held the seat until his retirement at the 2005 general election. From 1997 to 2001, he was Parliamentary Private Secretary to Kenneth Clarke, Secretary of State for Education and Employment.   In 2001, Portillo was appointed Shadow Minister for Transport. In 2003, he became Shadow Minister for Infrastructure and Housing. When Iain Duncan Smith resigned as Conservative Party Leader on 20 February 2006, Portillo was nominated to succeed him. He was appointed Secretary of State for Trade and Industry on 24 February 2006.  Portillo announced his retirement from Parliament at the General Election on 7 May 2005, after being defeated by Labour's David Blunkett in Enfield Southgate.  Portillo has been married to Rachel McArthur since 1984; they have one daughter and two sons.
