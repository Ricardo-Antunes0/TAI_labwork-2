Polina Olegovna Anikeeva (born 1982) is a Russian artist who lives and works in Saint Petersburg. She is known for her paintings and sculptures of everyday objects, often depicting commonplace objects in unexpectedly whimsical or grotesque ways.  Anikeeva was born in Saint Petersburg, Russia, in 1982. She studied at the Saint Petersburg State University of Arts from 2001 to 2005, graduating with a degree in painting.  Anikeeva's art is often unconventional, incorporating elements of pop culture and kitsch into her paintings and sculptures of everyday objects. Her work has been exhibited at museums and galleries around the world, including the Museum of Modern Art in New York City, the Centre Pompidou in Paris, the Museum of Contemporary Art in Los Angeles, and the Mori Art Museum in Tokyo. In 2018 she was awarded the prestigious Turner Prize.
