He was the first Japanese player to be named Asian Footballer of the Year in 1996.  Baba was known as a midfield playmaker and is widely regarded as one of the best Japanese footballers of all time. He played at international level for Japan from 1992 to 2002, including at the 1998 FIFA World Cup and the 2002 FIFA World Cup. In total, he made 85 appearances for Japan and scored 14 goals. He was also a member of the Japanese national team that finished third at the 1996 AFC Asian Cup.  After his playing career ended, Baba became manager of Shimizu S-Pulse in 2005. He left the club in 2007 and became manager of Kashima Antlers in 2008. He left Kashima Antlers in 2011 and became manager of Vissel Kobe in 2013. In January 2016, he left Vissel Kobe after two seasons and was appointed manager of Sagan Tosu. He resigned from his position in September 2018.
