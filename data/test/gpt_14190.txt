Kristjan Kitsing (born December 11, 1990) is a Canadian professional ice hockey centre who is currently playing with HC Davos of the Swiss National League (NLA). Kitsing was selected in the first round, 16th overall, by the Winnipeg Jets in the 2008 NHL Entry Draft.  Kitsing played major junior hockey for the Portland Winterhawks of the Western Hockey League (WHL). He recorded 104 points in 69 games in his final season with the Winterhawks, leading all rookies in scoring and earning WHL Rookie of the Year honours. Kitsing was also selected to play for Team Canada at the World Junior Championships, where he recorded five goals and seven assists in seven games.  After completing his junior career, Kitsing signed a three-year contract with Winnipeg on July 2, 2011. He made his NHL debut on October 5, 2011 against the Nashville Predators, recording an assist in a 3-2 victory. He scored his first NHL goal on November 7, 2011 against Curtis Joseph of the Toronto Maple Leafs.  Injury plagued Kitsing during his first two seasons with Winnipeg. In 2013–14, he appeared in just 20 games and recorded one goal and one assist. In 2014–15, he only appeared in 17 games due to another injury and was waived by Winnipeg on February 25, 2015.  Kitsing signed a two-year contract with HC Davos of the NLA on July 1, 2015.
