Shitalanatha was the tenth tirthankara of the Jain religion, and the last of the major tirthankaras. He was born in the present-day Vindhya Pradesh state of India in an era that is traditionally said to have been characterized by great social and political turmoil.  Shitalanatha is credited with restoring peace and order to a chaotic world, and he is venerated as a healer and teacher of righteousness. He is also known for his profound understanding of the philosophy and practice of Jainism, which he passed on to his disciples.  Shitalanatha is one of the most popular tirthankaras in Jainism, and pilgrims visit his shrine throughout India to seek his blessings.
