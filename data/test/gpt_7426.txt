The Barabati Stadium is an Indian sports stadium located in the city of Cuttack, Odisha. It is currently used mostly for cricket matches, and is the home ground of the Odisha cricket team. The stadium has a capacity of 20,000 people.  The stadium was first built in 1935, and was originally known as the Cuttack Stadium. In 1992, the stadium was renamed in honour of the Indian revolutionary and freedom fighter Balabati Saha, who played a significant role in the struggle for Indian independence. In 1999, the stadium underwent renovation, which included the installation of new seating and floodlights. The renovation was completed in 2000.  The Barabati Stadium has played host to several important cricket matches, including the 1999 ICC World Cup Qualifier, the 2001 Odisha State Cricket Championship, and the 2008 Odisha State Cricket League. The stadium is also used for concerts and other events.
