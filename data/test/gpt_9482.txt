Murari Sharma (Hindi: मुरारी शर्मा), (1 January 1912 – 16 December 1969) was an Indian film actor who appeared in over 350 films in Hindi and Urdu. He is considered one of the most popular and enduring actors of Hindi cinema.  Born in 1912 in a Hindu upper-caste family in Amritsar, Punjab, Sharma began his career as a stage actor before making his film debut in 1940. He initially achieved success in supporting roles, but later became a leading actor with appearances in classic films such as "Awaara" (1955), "Pyar Ki Jeet" (1957), "Do Bigha Zamin" (1958), and "Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge" (1995). He was awarded the Padma Bhushan, the third-highest civilian honour in India, in 1967. He died 16 December 1969 in Mumbai at the age of 57.
