Matthew Theron Ruff (born September 8, 1965) is an American actor, comedian, and writer. He is known for his role as Mike Baxter on the NBC sitcom Will & Grace, for which he won two Golden Globe Awards, a Primetime Emmy Award, and a Screen Actors Guild Award.  Ruff was born in Clinton, Iowa, the son of Barbara (n√©e Theron), a secretary, and Donald Ruff, a truck driver. He has two sisters. He attended Southeast Community School in Clinton before transferring to West Des Moines Area Community College. Ruff turned to comedy after dropping out of college and began working at a local comedy club.  In 1992, Ruff appeared as a regular on the short-lived sitcom The Ben Stiller Show. In 1993, he starred in the feature film Clueless with Alicia Silverstone and Paul Rudd. In 1995, Ruff starred in the cult hit Re-Animator with Stuart Gordon and Jeffrey Combs.  In 1997, Ruff starred opposite Debra Winger in the romantic comedy My Life With Fran. The following year he co-starred with Catherine Zeta-Jones in the legal drama The Practice and earned an Emmy nomination for his performance. In 2000, Ruff portrayed Dale Gribble on the Fox sitcom King of the Hill for six seasons.
