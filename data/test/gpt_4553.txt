Shantanu Sengupta is an Indian cell biologist and a National Professor at the National Institute of Biomedical Technology. He has also served as the Director of the National Institute of Immunology and Cell Science, Chairman of the Scientific Advisory Council to the Prime Minister of India, and Member of the Indian Academy of Sciences.  Shantanu Sengupta was born on November 18, 1947 in Kolkata, India. He completed his undergraduate studies at Presidency College, Kolkata in 1967 and obtained his Master's degree from Calcutta University in 1969. In 1974, he completed his Ph.D. at the University of California, Los Angeles under the guidance of Edward C. Smith.  From 1974 to 1979, Sengupta was a post-doctoral fellow at the University of California, San Francisco. From 1979 to 1987, he was an Assistant Professor at Presidency College, Kolkata. In 1987, he moved to the National Institute of Biomedical Technology (NIBT), where he served as a professor until his retirement in 2011.
