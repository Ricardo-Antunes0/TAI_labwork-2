Martin Snítil (born 20 November 1978) is a Slovak football player who last played for FK DAC 1904 Dunajská Streda.  Snítil started his career in his home-town club ŠK Slovan Bratislava, before transferring to Hungarian side Vasas SC in the winter of 2004. He made his debut for Vasas on 24 October 2004 in a 3–0 home win against FC Sopron. Snítil played a total of 120 league games for Vasas, scoring 11 goals.  In February 2007, Snítil transferred to German Bundesliga side FC Schalke 04. He made his Schalke debut on 21 February 2007 in a 2–1 away loss against VfB Stuttgart. Snitil played 15 Bundesliga games for Schalke, scoring one goal.  After Schalke's relegation from the Bundesliga at the end of the 2007–08 season, Snítil was released by the club. He then signed for Rangers on a two-year contract in July 2008. He made his Rangers debut on 26 July 2008, coming on as a substitute for Kenny Miller in a 1–1 draw against Hearts. Snítil made only two appearances for Rangers, both as a substitute, before being released by the club at the end of the season.  In January 2009, Snítil signed for Slovak Premier League side FK DAC 1904 Dunajská Streda. He made his DAC 1904 debut on 18 January 2009
