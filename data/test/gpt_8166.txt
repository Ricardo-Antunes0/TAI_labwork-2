Eucalyptus mitchelliana, commonly known as Buffalo sallee, Mitchell's eucalyptus, or Mitchell's peppermint, is a tree or shrub of the genus Eucalyptus in the Myrtaceae family. It is native to eastern Australia and Tasmania.  The tree typically grows to a height of and has smooth grey bark. The leaves are arranged alternately, are long and wide, and have a sharp point on the end. The flowers are white and arranged in clusters around the branches. The fruit is a legume pod which is long and wide.  The tree is used for timber, its oil which is used in cosmetics, varnishes, and as a flavouring agent in food, and its seeds which are used for animal feed.
