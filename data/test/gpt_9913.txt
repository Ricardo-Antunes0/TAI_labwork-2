Kamionka  is a town in Lubartów County, Lublin Voivodeship, in eastern Poland. It is the seat of the gmina (administrative district) called Gmina Kamionka. The town has a population of 2,500.  Kamionka is located in the Lesser Poland region, about east of the regional capital Lublin. The town is situated on the left bank of the Lubartów River, on the border of historic Lesser Poland and Galicia.  The town has a long history, dating back to the 13th century. It was first mentioned in 1222 as a possession of the Knights Templar. In 1327 it was granted to Jan Dziwisz Czarniecki, one of the most powerful magnates of the Polish Kingdom. In 1383–1384 it was controlled by Casimir III of Poland. From 1492 until 1772 it was part of Austrian Habsburg Monarchy. In 1772 it passed to Kingdom of Prussia.  In 1807 Kamionka became part of Russian Empire. The town was badly damaged during World War I and World War II. After World War II Kamionka became part of Poland again.   The economy is based on agriculture and small industry. There are also some light industry and service sectors present.
