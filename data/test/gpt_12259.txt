Joseph Norbert Brinkman (born April 9, 1944) is an American football coach and former player who is currently the defensive coordinator for the Cincinnati Bengals of the National Football League (NFL). He was the head coach of the Pittsburgh Steelers from 2000 to 2003, compiling a record of 21–41. Brinkman played football for Ohio State University from 1963 to 1966 and was a first-team All-Big Ten Conference selection as a senior.  Brinkman began his coaching career as an assistant coach at Ohio State under Woody Hayes from 1967 to 1969. He then served as the defensive coordinator at Purdue University from 1970 to 1973. He was named head coach of the Pittsburgh Steelers in 2000, and led the team to two playoff appearances in his three seasons as head coach. In 2003, he was fired after posting a record of 21–41 in three seasons. He returned to coaching in 2006, serving as the defensive coordinator for the New York Giants before being named interim head coach of the Cincinnati Bengals in 2014. He was named permanent head coach later that year and has led the team to two playoff appearances since then.
