Major General Fred Livingood Walker (June 11, 1892 – January 8, 1978) was an American soldier and a recipient of the Medal of Honor, the highest and most prestigious military award for valor in combat.  Fred Livingood Walker was born on June 11, 1892 in Neosho, Missouri, to Lonnie and Martha (née Simpson) Walker. He attended high school in Neosho and then attended the United States Military Academy at West Point from 1912 to 1916. He graduated from West Point in June 1916 and was commissioned a second lieutenant in the Infantry.  Walker served with the American Expeditionary Forces during World War I. He was awarded the Medal of Honor for his actions during the Battle of Saint-Mihiel on September 12, 1918. After the war, he served with the occupation forces in Germany.  In 1923, Walker married Frances Evelyn Herrington. They had two children. Walker retired from the Army in 1946 with the rank of major general. He died on January 8, 1978 in Arlington, Virginia.
