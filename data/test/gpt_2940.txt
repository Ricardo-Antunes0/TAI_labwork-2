Gary Douglas Beer (born 10 October 1941), also known by his stage name, Gary Beer, is an English singer, songwriter, and musician. He is the lead singer and one of the founding members of the 1960s pop group The Tornados.  Gary Beer was born in 1941 in Paddington, London to Jewish parents who were refugees from the Nazi regime in Germany. His father was a tailor and his mother a homemaker. As a child, he was interested in music and played the saxophone in school orchestra. When he was 16, he met The Tornados singer Tony Sheridan at a club in London and they formed a band together. The Tornados had several hit singles including "Telstar" and "Walking on Sunshine". In 1969, The Tornados split up and Beer started a solo career. He has since released several albums and toured extensively around the world.
