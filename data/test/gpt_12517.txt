Hollis Scarborough  is an American psychologist who is currently the James McKeen Cattell Professor of Psychology at Vanderbilt University. He is known for his work in cognitive neuroscience and the development of emotional intelligence.  Scarborough was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, in 1961. He received his B.A. from Swarthmore College in 1983 and his M.A. and Ph.D. from the University of Chicago in 1986 and 1989, respectively. He began his career as a research associate at the University of Chicago before moving to Vanderbilt University in 1991.  Scarborough's main research interests are cognitive neuroscience and the development of emotional intelligence. He has published more than 150 articles and book chapters on these topics. His books include Emotional Intelligence: New Insights and Developments (2003), Emotional Intelligence 2.0: Why It Can Matter More Than IQ (2005), and The Social Brain: How Our Brains Create Relationships (2012).  Scarborough has been recognized with numerous awards, including the Distinguished Scientific Contributions Award from the Society for Personality and Social Psychology in 2007, the Early Career Achievement Award from the Association for Psychological Science in 2008, the Distinguished Scientific Contribution Award from the International Society for Neuropsychoanalysis in 2010, and the Presidential Award for Excellence in Cognitive Science from Vanderbilt University in 2014.
