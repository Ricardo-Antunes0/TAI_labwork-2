Conquest is a 1998 British-Canadian romantic comedy film directed by Roger Donaldson and written by Donaldson and Karey Kirkpatrick.  Set in 11th-century England, the film follows the love affair between a Norman warrior, Robert, and the Saxon princess, Herlinde. The two are forced to marry when Robert's father, the Duke of Normandy, demands it in order to secure an alliance with the Saxons. However, Robert and Herlinde fall in love despite their differences, and must fight against all odds to keep their relationship alive.   The film received mixed reviews from critics, but was a box office success worldwide. It was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Screenplay.
