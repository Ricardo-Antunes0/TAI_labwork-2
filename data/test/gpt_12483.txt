Wieslaw Woszczyk is the James McGill Professor of Planetary Sciences at McGill University, and a research scientist at the Isaac Newton Group of the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey. He is also a visiting professor at the University of Santiago de Compostela in Spain.  Wieslaw Woszczyk was born on March 20, 1957 in Warsaw, Poland. He received a B.Sc. in Mathematical Sciences from the University of Warsaw in 1978 and a M.Sc. and Ph.D. in Planetary Sciences from the University of California, Berkeley in 1982 and 1986, respectively.  From 1986 to 1988 he was a post-doctoral fellow at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) in Pasadena, California. From 1988 to 1990 he was an assistant professor at McGill University in Montreal, Quebec, Canada. From 1990 to 1992 he was an associate professor at McGill University and from 1992 to 1995 he was a full professor there.  Since 1995 he has been the James McGill Professor of Planetary Sciences at McGill University in Montreal, Quebec, Canada. He is also a research scientist at the Isaac Newton Group of the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey. He is also a visiting professor at the University of Santiago de Compostela in Spain.
