Timothy James Farron (born 27 May 1970) is a British politician who has been the Leader of the Liberal Democrats since 2015. A member of the Church of England, Farron served as the Member of Parliament (MP) for Westmorland and Lonsdale from 2005 to 2015.  Timothy James Farron was born on 27 May 1970 in Preston, Lancashire, England. He was educated at the University of York, where he gained a BA in Politics and Economics. Farron then worked as a Research Assistant for the Liberal Democrats in their early years before being elected to Parliament in the 2005 general election as MP for Westmorland and Lonsdale.  Farron served as Minister of State at the Department for Education from 2010 to 2011, and Minister for Rural Affairs from 2011 to 2012. He also served as Deputy Leader of the Liberal Democrats from 2013 to 2015. Farron was elected Leader of the Liberal Democrats on 15 May 2015, following Nick Clegg's resignation after the 2015 general election.
