Cullen Andrew Jones (born February 29, 1984) is an American actor, comedian, writer and producer. He is known for his roles as Tim on the Fox sitcom New Girl and as Ben on the NBC sitcom Community. He also starred as FBI Special Agent Dale Cooper in the CBS drama series Twin Peaks.  Jones was born in Los Angeles, California, to Andy Jones, an advertising executive, and his wife, actress Heather Graham. He has two sisters and a brother. His paternal grandfather was African American and his maternal grandfather was English. Jones attended The Buckley School in Sherman Oaks, California. He then attended the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA), where he studied English literature.  Jones began his career in 2001 as a cast member of the MTV sketch comedy show The State. In 2003, he starred in the short-lived NBC comedy-drama series Undeclared as a character named Tim. In 2004, he appeared in the film adaptation of the Broadway play The 25th Annual Putnam County Spelling Bee and starred opposite Alicia Keys in the music video for her song "Fallin". In 2005, he portrayed Mark Twain in a production of How to Succeed in Business Without Really Trying for Playwrights Horizons in New York City.  In 2007, Jones co-created and starred in the Fox sitcom New Girl alongside Zooey Deschanel and Jake Johnson. The show ran for six seasons before being cancelled in 2015.
