He was a professor at the University of British Columbia where he founded and was the director of the Institute for Theoretical Physics.  Dey was born on 14 February 1962 in Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, India. He was educated at the Banaras Hindu University and obtained his B.Sc. in mathematics in 1984 and his M.Sc. in 1985. He then moved to Canada, where he obtained his Ph.D. from the University of Toronto in 1991 under the supervision of Norman Levinson.  Dey was a professor at the University of British Columbia where he founded and was the director of the Institute for Theoretical Physics (ITP). Dey's research focused on mathematical physics, with a particular focus on problems in quantum field theory and string theory. In 2006, he became a fellow of the Royal Society of Canada. He died on 4 January 2014 after a long battle with cancer.
