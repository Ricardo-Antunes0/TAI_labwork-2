The gens Numisia was a family at Rome.  The Numisii were a Roman family of equestrian rank. The first of the family to appear in history is Publius Numisius, who was consul in 444 BC. The Numisii were prominent in the civil and military life of Rome, and several members held the offices of pontifex maximus and praetor. The most famous member of the gens was Publius Numisius Scaurus, who was tribune of the people in 367 BC and consul in 339 BC.
