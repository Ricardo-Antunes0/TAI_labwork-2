Josef Škvorecký  (; September 27, 1924 – February 2, 2006) was a Czech philosopher and linguist.  Škvorecký was born in Prague, Czechoslovakia, into a Jewish family. He emigrated to the United States in 1961, and became a naturalized American citizen in 1970. Škvorecký taught at the University of California, Berkeley from 1971 until his retirement in 1996. He was also a visiting professor at the University of Paris II from 1982 until his death.  Škvorecký is best known for his work on semantics and grammar, but he also wrote extensively on philosophy and aesthetics. His most important philosophical work is "Semantics and Pragmatics" (1981), which has been influential in the fields of pragmatics and linguistic philosophy. His most famous book on aesthetics is "The Aesthetic Dimension" (1985).  Škvorecký died of leukemia at the age of 80 in 2006.
