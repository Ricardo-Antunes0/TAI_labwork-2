Julian Pender Hume (born 3 March 1960) is a British philosopher and historian of philosophy. He is a professor of philosophy at the University of Cambridge, and director of the Hume Project.  Hume is best known for his work on moral philosophy, political philosophy, and philosophical history. He has also written on natural theology, economics, and psychology.  Hume was born in 1960 in Edinburgh, Scotland. He was educated at the Royal High School in Edinburgh and at Balliol College, Oxford, where he studied philosophy, graduating with a Bachelor of Arts (BA) degree in 1981. He then studied at the University of Cambridge, where he received his Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) degree in 1989.  Hume has been a professor of philosophy at the University of Cambridge since 1990. From 1998 to 2000 he was a fellow at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey. In 2005 he was made a Fellow of the British Academy (FBA).
