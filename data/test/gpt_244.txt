Station for Two   is a novel by J.D. Salinger, published in 1951. It is the first of three novels in the Glass family series.   The novel tells the story of Holden Caulfield, a 17-year-old teenager from New York City who is discharged from an institution for juvenile delinquents and travels around the United States. Holden is a troubled youth who has spent most of his life being shuttled between various institutions. On his journey, Holden meets a number of people, including a traveling salesman named Buddy Glass and a girl named Phoebe who he falls in love with.
