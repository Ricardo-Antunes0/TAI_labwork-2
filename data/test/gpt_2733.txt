Dunfermline  is a town and former royal burgh in Fife, Scotland. At the 2001 Census, its population was 17,043. It is the administrative centre of the district of Dunfermline and Menstrie.  Dunfermline is known for its Royal Burgh status and its history. The town has a rich cultural heritage, with many notable buildings and attractions including the Town House, the Basilica of St Andrews and the Dunfermline War Memorial. The area has a long sporting history, with Dunfermline Athletic Club being one of the oldest football clubs in the world.  Dunfermline is also home to Fife's only university, Robert Gordon University.
