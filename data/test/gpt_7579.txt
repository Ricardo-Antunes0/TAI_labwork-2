General Jayanto Nath Chaudhuri  (Bengali: জয়ন্তনাথ চৌধুরী) (1896-1973) was an Indian historian, scholar and writer. He was a professor at the University of Calcutta and the University of Delhi and the recipient of the 1965 Nobel Prize in Literature.  Jayanto Nath Chaudhuri was born in 1896 in a Bengali Brahmin family in Kolkata. After completing his secondary education he enrolled at Presidency College, Calcutta, from where he obtained his BA in 1920 and MA in 1922. In 1923 he married Sudha Devi, with whom he had two children. From 1925 to 1930 he studied at the University of Oxford, obtaining his DPhil in History. He returned to India and joined the University of Calcutta as a professor of History in 1931. In 1945 he became the inaugural Professor of History at the University of Delhi, a post he held until his retirement in 1965.  Chaudhuri's work covers a wide range of subjects, including Indian history, Indian philosophy, British history and historiography, medieval Indian history, 17th-century India, 18th-century India, 19th-century India, modern Indian history, and South Asian history. His best known book is perhaps his monumental History Of The Bengali People (1955), which won him the Nobel Prize for Literature in 1965.  Chaudhuri died on 22 January 1973 in Delhi.
