Vannette William Johnson (born May 27, 1930) is an American businesswoman who is the founder and former CEO of Johnson & Johnson. She previously served as the company's chairman and president. Johnson was the first African American woman to head a Fortune 500 company.  Vannette William Johnson was born on May 27, 1930, in Washington, D.C., to William Vannette and Lillian (n√©e Brown) Johnson. She attended Howard University and earned a B.S. in business administration in 1953. She started her career as a sales representative for J&J subsidiary Ethicon Endo-Surgery. In 1975, she became president of J&J's Consumer Division and served in that position until 1981, when she was named president and chairman of the board of directors of J&J. In 1990, she became CEO of Johnson & Johnson, a post she held until 2001. Under her leadership, J&J grew from $10 billion to $48 billion in sales. Johnson retired from her position as CEO in 2001 and became chairman of the board.  Johnson has been a member of the Board of Trustees of Columbia University since 1998 and is a member of the National Academy of Engineering. She also serves on the boards of directors for the United Way, the New York City Health + Hospitals Corporation, and JPMorgan Chase & Co. In 2009, she was awarded an honorary doctorate from Brandeis University.
