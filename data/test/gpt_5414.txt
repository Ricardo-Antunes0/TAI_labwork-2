Danny Stanton (September 24, 1896 â€“ May 2, 1967) was an American jazz trumpeter.  Born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, Stanton played with Bix Beiderbecke and Benny Goodman before moving to New York City in the 1920s. He played with Charlie Barnet, Duke Ellington, and Coleman Hawkins before settling in Los Angeles in the 1940s. He recorded with Charlie Parker and Dizzy Gillespie, and toured with Benny Goodman and Lionel Hampton. In the 1960s he played with Art Pepper and Thelonious Monk. He died in Los Angeles.
