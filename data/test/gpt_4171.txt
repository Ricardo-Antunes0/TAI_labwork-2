The Nakajima Ki-34 was a Japanese light bomber and fighter aircraft designed by Nakajima. With its distinctive twin-boom undercarriage, the Ki-34 was one of the most famous Japanese aircraft of World War II.  The Ki-34 was used by the Imperial Japanese Army Air Force and Navy during World War II. It was also exported to China and Finland, where it served with various air forces until the late 1950s. The Ki-34 remained in service with a number of countries for many years after the war.
