French wine is produced all throughout France, with a wide variety available both in price and quality. The most well-known types of French wine are the red wines such as Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, and Cabernet Franc, as well as the whites such as Chardonnay, Sauvignon Blanc, and Semillon.   French wine has a long history dating back to the time of the Roman Empire. The Gauls were known to enjoy wine and even export it to Rome. Wine production in France soon became an important part of the economy, and many of the world's most famous French wineries were founded in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Today, French wine is exported all over the world and is considered among the best in quality.
