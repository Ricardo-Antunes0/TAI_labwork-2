Antoine-Charles Taschereau (October 26, 1797 – June 9, 1874) was a French general and statesman who served as Prime Minister of France from 1829 to 1834. He also served in the Napoleonic Wars, serving in the Imperial Russian Army and later rising to the rank of Marshal of France.  Born into a wealthy bourgeois family in Bordeaux, Taschereau began his military career in the French Army in 1815. He fought in the Napoleonic Wars on the side of Napoleon I, becoming a general in 1823. After Napoleon's fall, Taschereau joined the Bourbon Restoration government of Louis XVIII as Minister of War. He helped to suppress the July Revolution of 1830 and served as Prime Minister from 1829 to 1834. In this role, he enacted sweeping social reforms, including the abolition of slavery. He died in 1874 after years of illness.
