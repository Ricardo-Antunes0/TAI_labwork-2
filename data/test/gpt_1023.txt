Sisimiut , formerly known as Holsteinsborg, is a town in the Qaasuitsup municipality in northwestern Greenland.   Sisimiut was founded in 1784 by the Danish missionary Hans Egede, and was originally named Holsteinsborg in his honor. The town became known as Sisimiut in 1951, after the nearby Sisimiut Bay was explored by American and Danish researchers. The population of Sisimiut was 2,971 as of 2010.  Sisimiut is the administrative center of the Qaasuitsup municipality, and it is also home to the University of Greenland. The town is situated on the shores of Sisimiut Bay on the northeastern coast of Greenland, northeast of the Arctic Circle.
