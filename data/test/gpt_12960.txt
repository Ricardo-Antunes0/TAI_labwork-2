Sohni Mahiwal or  Suhni Mehar  (Urdu: سوئی نہیل‎) is an Indo-Pakistani singer and songwriter, who has been performing since the early 1990s.  Mahiwal was born in Karachi, Sindh, Pakistan on January 10, 1968. She is the daughter of renowned Punjabi singer-songwriter Maqbool Mahiwal and Begum Parveen. After completing her schooling from Karachi's prestigious La Martinière Girls School in 1986, Mahiwal went on to study music at the University of California, Los Angeles, where she earned a degree in music composition in 1991.  Mahiwal's musical career began in the early 1990s when she co-founded the band Nusrat Fateh Ali Khan with her father. The band released their debut album, Khuda Ke Liye, in 1992. The album was a critical and commercial success and established Mahiwal as one of the leading voices of Pakistani pop music.   Since then, Mahiwal has recorded several solo albums and collaborated with a range of international artists including Carlos Santana, India Arie, Aamer Rahman and Arif Mardin. She has also appeared in several films including Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge (1995), Dil To Pagal Hai (1998) and Aankhen (2003). In 2006, she performed at the opening ceremony of the 2006 FIFA World Cup in Germany.
