John Archer (died October 13, 1998) was an American basketball player who played in the National Basketball Association (NBA). He was drafted by the Philadelphia 76ers with the third overall pick of the 1977 NBA draft.  Archer attended the University of Houston, where he played college basketball for the Houston Cougars. He was a two-time All-American and won the Naismith College Player of the Year Award in 1978. He was also a member of the United States national basketball team and competed in the FIBA World Championship in 1978 and 1980.  Archer played for the Philadelphia 76ers, Milwaukee Bucks and Seattle SuperSonics during his NBA career. He won an NBA titles with Philadelphia in 1982 and made an All-Star appearance with Milwaukee in 1984. In 1988, he was traded to Seattle and finished his career with the SuperSonics. Archer was inducted into the Philadelphia 76ers' Hall of Fame in 1998. He died of heart failure on October 13, 1998 at the age of 47.
