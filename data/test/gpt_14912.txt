Christoph Eschenbach (; born 20 February 1940) is a German composer, conductor and music teacher.  Christoph Eschenbach was born in 1940 in Königsberg, East Prussia. He studied composition at the University of Music and Performing Arts in Vienna with Bruno Maderna and Karlheinz Stockhausen, receiving his diploma in 1964. During the same period he also continued his studies with Luigi Nono, earning a degree in composition in 1966.  In 1968 he became Conductor of the Staatstheater Nürnberg. In 1974 he moved to the Bavarian State Opera House, where he remained until 1988. During that time he also served as Principal Conductor of the Munich Philharmonic from 1978 to 1988. In 1988 he became Chief Conductor and Music Director of the Berlin State Opera, a post he held until 2001. Since 2002 he has been Honorary Conductor of the Berlin Philharmonic.  Eschenbach's compositions have been widely performed, especially during his time as Chief Conductor of the Berlin State Opera. His works have been recorded many times, including a complete cycle of Der Ring des Nibelungen on CD (DG 474 563-2). He has also written works for stage and film.  In 2006 Eschenbach was given an honorary doctorate by the University of Music and Performing Arts in Vienna.
