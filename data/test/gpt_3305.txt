It is derived from the Hebrew word emmanu-el, meaning "God is with us". It has been used by various people throughout history. The name Emanuel has been most notably used by two kings of Israel: King Emanuel I (1569-1574) and King Emanuel II (1859-1886). Other notable bearers of the name include American pastor and author Emanuel A.M. Eller (1810-1892) and Israeli Prime Minister Ehud Barak (1948-2013).
