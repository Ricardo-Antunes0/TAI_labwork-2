David B. Doty (born 1950) is an American psychologist who has made significant contributions to the field of cognitive neuroscience. He is currently Professor of Psychology at the University of California, Santa Barbara.  Doty's work has focused on the mechanisms and consequences of cognitive restructuring, or the process by which people change their thinking in response to new information. His research has shown that cognitive restructuring is a fundamental mechanism by which people learn and remember information, and that it is an important tool for problem solving.  Doty's work has also been influential in the development of cognitive neuroscience as a field. He is the founder and current editor-in-chief of the journal "Cognitive Neuroscience". He has also served as president of the Cognitive Neuroscience Society and as vice-president of the American Psychological Association.
