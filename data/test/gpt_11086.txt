Meridian (BH 250) is a bronze sculpture by British artist Peter Heppell. It is located in the Hepworth Wakefield Museum in Wakefield, West Yorkshire, England.  The sculpture, created in 1985 and cast in 1989, comprises a large bronze disc on a circular pedestal, measuring 40 feet (12 m) in diameter and weighing 120 tons. The sculpture is composed of two concentric circles of bronze, each 18 feet (5.5 m) in diameter and 3 feet (1 m) deep. The inner circle represents the Earth's orbit around the sun, while the outer circle represents the moon's orbit around Earth. The sculpture was commissioned by the Hepworth Wakefield Foundation and dedicated on 20 October 1989.
