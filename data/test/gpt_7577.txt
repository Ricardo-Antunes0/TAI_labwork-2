John Garber (November 9, 1833 – December 15, 1912) was an American painter and printmaker. He is known for his depictions of rural American life, as well as his illustrations of children's books.  Garber was born in Utica, New York, the son of Lucretia (née Peck) and William Garber. He studied art at the School of the National Academy of Design in New York City from 1854 to 1856, and then moved to Washington, D.C., to study at the studio of Samuel F. B. Morse. He returned to New York in 1859 and opened a studio on Broadway.  In 1862, Garber married Caroline Woodbury and they had two daughters. He exhibited at the National Academy of Design from 1859 to 1865 and from 1871 to 1905, and at the Philadelphia Academy of Fine Arts from 1861 to 1865. In 1903, he moved to West Newton, Massachusetts.  Garber died in West Newton, Massachusetts, in 1912. His papers are held by the Archives of American Art at The Smithsonian Institution in Washington, D.C.
