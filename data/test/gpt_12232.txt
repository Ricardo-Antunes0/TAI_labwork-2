Thamizhavel Go. Sarangapani, (, 19 April 1903 – 12 October 1973) was an Indian Tamil philosopher, scholar, and author. He is considered to be one of the most important and influential thinkers in the history of Indian philosophy.  Born on 19 April 1903 in Tirunelveli, Tamil Nadu, Thamizhavel G. Sarangapani was the son of Goverdhanan and Parvathi. He completed his primary education from Srimathi Ammal's school in Tirunelveli before studying at the Government Arts College, Tirunelveli, where he obtained a B.A. degree in 1924. The same year, he moved to Madras to study at the Madras Christian College (MCC), where he obtained an M.A. degree in 1926. After completing his studies at MCC, Sarangapani travelled to Europe to continue his studies at the University of Freiburg (1927–1928), the University of Geneva (1928–1929), and the University of Rome (1929–1930). He returned to India in 1930 and began teaching at MCC.  Sarangapani retired from active teaching in 1969 and died on 12 October 1973.
