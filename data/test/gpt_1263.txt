Ricardo Moyano is an Argentinian musician and songwriter, who rose to prominence in the late 1990s as frontman and main songwriter for the Argentine rock band La Ley. He has also been a member of the bands Rata Blanca, Los Fabulosos Cadillacs, and Toto Cutugno.  Moyano has been nominated for four Grammy Awards, including Best New Artist in 1998 and Best Latin Rock Album in 2002. He has sold over 20 million records worldwide.  Ricardo Moyano was born in Avellaneda, Buenos Aires, Argentina on November 25th, 1967. At the age of 16, he formed the band La Ley with his high school friends Diego Torneo and Gustavo Santaolalla. The band quickly gained a following in Argentina with their energetic live shows and catchy indie rock tunes. In 1992 they were signed to BMG Music Argentina and released their self-titled debut album that year.   The following year saw the release of their second album, El Poder de la Música. The album was a critical and commercial success and produced some of La Ley's most popular songs, including "Porque Te Quiero" and "Déjame Que Te Ame".
