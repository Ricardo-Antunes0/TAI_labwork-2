Core & Satellite Portfolio Management is an approach to portfolio management and analysis that emphasizes the importance of core holdings and the importance of deploying resources efficiently across a spectrum of investment options, or "satellites." The goal is to create a portfolio that provides sustainable growth and diversification while minimizing risk. Core holdings are those that have a higher degree of intrinsic value, while satellites are those that provide additional opportunities for growth and diversification.  Core & Satellite Portfolio Management was developed in the late 1990s by two professors at Brown University, Jeremy Siegel and John Liew. The approach has been widely adopted by financial institutions and investment firms around the world.
