Graham's Magazine was a nineteenth-century periodical based in the United Kingdom. It was founded by James Graham in 1827, and reached its peak circulation of over 130,000 in 1840. The magazine ceased publication in 1855.  Graham's Magazine was one of the most popular periodicals of its era, with a circulation of over 130,000 in 1840. Its political stance was Liberal-radical, and it championed causes such as abolitionism, free trade, and education reform.
