He was the Sterling Professor of Political Science at Yale University and a member of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.  Born in Toronto, Ontario, Panitch attended McGill University in Montreal, where he studied political science. He then went to the University of Chicago, where he received his MA and PhD degrees in political science.  Panitch's early work was in Marxist theory. He is best known for his work on social democracy, Marxism, and electoral politics, as well as his writings on labour history and theory. He has also published articles on a variety of other topics, including education, globalization, and feminism.  In 2002, Panitch was awarded the J.S. Mill Award by the Canadian Political Science Association for his outstanding contributions to political science. In 2007, he became a fellow of the Royal Society of Canada.  Panitch died at his home in New Haven, Connecticut, aged 71.
