Banksia wonganensis is a large shrub endemic to the Wongan Hills in the Gold Coast hinterland of Queensland, Australia. It typically grows to a height of 2–5 m, and has a spreading habit. The leaves are small, linear, and 0.5–2 cm long, with a serrated margin. The flowers are pink and arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. The fruit is a globular red drupe.
