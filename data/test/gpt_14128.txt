Anthony Michael Brumley (born April 9, 1963) is an American former professional baseball infielder. He played in Major League Baseball (MLB) for the Houston Astros, Chicago White Sox, New York Yankees, and Minnesota Twins.  Brumley attended the University of Arkansas where he played college baseball for the Arkansas Razorbacks. He was drafted by the Astros in the fourth round of the 1985 amateur draft. He made his MLB debut with the Astros in 1986 and spent his entire career with them. He was a member of the Astros' National League championship teams in 1995 and 2005. He also won a Gold Glove Award in 1995. Brumley finished his career with a .282 batting average, 990 hits, and 213 home runs in 1,824 games.  After his retirement as a player, Brumley served as an assistant coach for the White Sox from 2006 to 2008 and then for the Yankees from 2009 to 2013. He was most recently a coach for the Twins from 2014 to 2016.
