Yeghishe Charents House Museum ( (Yehishe Charents'i Hovsep)) is an Armenian house-museum in the Yerevan neighbourhood of Charents. It was founded in 1965 by Yeghishe Charents and his wife Nune Charents.  Yeghishe Charents (1896-1984) was an Armenian artist and sculptor. He was born in the village of Aghdam, now in the Yerevan Province of Armenia. He studied at the Tbilisi State Academy of Fine Arts between 1915 and 1918, then moved to Paris, where he studied at the Académie Julian and at the École des Beaux-Arts. He returned to Armenia in 1926 and became a member of the Association of Artists of Armenia. Yeghishe Charents was also a recipient of several awards, including the State Prize of Armenia (1948), the Order of Lenin (1958), and the Order of Friendship of Peoples (1973).  Yeghishe Charents House Museum is filled with examples of Charents' work as an artist and sculptor. The museum also includes a library and classrooms for art students.
