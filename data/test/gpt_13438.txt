Black's Beach is a secluded section of coastline located on the western side of Vancouver, British Columbia. The beach is named after Captain George Black, a prominent figure in the early days of British Columbia.  Black's Beach is a narrow strip of sand running for about 1 kilometer along the coast of False Creek. The beach is flanked by cliffs and is difficult to access, making it a popular spot for climbers and kayakers. The seclusion and privacy make it a popular spot for lovers. The beach is also a popular spot for fishing.
