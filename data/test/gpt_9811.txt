Michael Roland Powers (; born November 19, 1955) is an American author, journalist, diplomat, and senior foreign policy advisor to U.S. President Barack Obama. He previously served as the 51st United States Ambassador to the United Nations from 2009 to 2013. Powers was born in Chicago, Illinois. After graduating from Yale University in 1977 with a degree in political science, he became a reporter for The New Republic magazine. Powers served as Deputy National Security Advisor for International Security Affairs under Presidents Bill Clinton and George W. Bush and was appointed as United States Ambassador to the United Nations by Obama on December 6, 2009. Powers retired from the U.S. Foreign Service on August 31, 2013.   Michael Powers is an experienced diplomat with more than three decades of experience in U.S. foreign policy-making and international relations, including service as Deputy National Security Advisor for International Security Affairs during both the Clinton and Bush administrations. A veteran reporter and editor, he has written extensively on U.S. foreign policy, international relations and national security issues, including seven books on those topics (see below). He has also served as a senior foreign policy advisor to U.S. presidents Bill Clinton and Barack Obama since leaving the U.S. Foreign Service in 2013 and has been a regular commentator on international affairs for various media outlets since then.
