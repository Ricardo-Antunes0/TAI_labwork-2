Sadik Harchaoui (born 4 March 1973, in Béjaïa) is a French-Algerian journalist and author. He is the founder and editor-in-chief of the online magazine Jeune Afrique.  He is the son of Algerian immigrants and was born in Béjaïa. He completed his secondary education at the Lycée Français de Béjaïa and then studied law at the University of Orléans.  Harchaoui worked as a lawyer before becoming a journalist in 1997. He began his career writing for the magazine L'Opinion, before founding the online magazine Jeune Afrique in 2002. Harchaoui has written for numerous publications, including Le Monde, Libération, El País and Le Nouvel Observateur. He has also been a guest commentator on French television networks such as France 2, TF1 and C8.  Harchaoui is a Chevalier des Arts et des Lettres, and a member of the Académie française. In 2014, he was named one of the 100 most influential people in the world by Time magazine.
