Willie D. Burton is an African American historian, author, and professor. Burton is known for his work in African American history, the history of Louisiana, and the history of the United States.  Burton was born in Baton Rouge, Louisiana in 1949. He received his bachelor's degree from Louisiana State University in 1971 and his doctorate from Harvard University in 1978. He began his teaching career at Morehouse College in Atlanta, Georgia in 1978 and has since held various academic positions at several universities, most recently at Howard University.  Burton's work has focused on the history of African Americans and the history of Louisiana. He has written books on both topics, as well as articles for newspapers and magazines. He has also appeared on television and radio programs discussing these topics. Burton is currently a Professor of History at Howard University.
