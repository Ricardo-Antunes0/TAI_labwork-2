Grace H. Knapp (21 November 1870 – 9 September 1959) was an American physician and medical educator.  Grace Knapp was born on 21 November 1870 in Westfield, New York, the daughter of Dr. James P. Knapp and Frances A. (née Proctor). She received her undergraduate degree from Mount Holyoke College in 1894 and her medical degree from the University of Chicago in 1897.  Knapp began her career as a resident physician at the Chicago Women's Hospital in 1897. She remained at the hospital for six years before joining the faculty there as an instructor in 1899. During her time at Chicago, Knapp also served as the associate director of women's health services at the hospital from 1905 to 1907. In 1911, she moved to New York City and became a professor of obstetrics and gynecology at Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons. At Columbia, Knapp also served as the dean of women from 1922 to 1938.  Knapp was also active in medical education. She served as the president of the American Medical Association's Section on Obstetrics and Gynecology from 1928 to 1929, and she was also a member of both the American Academy of Pediatrics and the American College of Physicians. Grace Knapp retired from Columbia in 1938 and died on 9 September 1959 in New York City.
