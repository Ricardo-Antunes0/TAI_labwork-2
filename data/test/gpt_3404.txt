Charles Lawrence Persip (July 26, 1929 – November 2, 1967) was an American football player and coach. He played college football at the University of Notre Dame and was drafted in the first round of the 1950 NFL Draft by the Detroit Lions. Persip played professional football as a halfback for the Lions (1950–1953) and Baltimore Colts (1954–1956). He later coached the Lions (1957–1959), Baltimore Colts (1960), and Philadelphia Eagles (1961–1963).  Persip was killed in a car accident while coaching for the Eagles in 1967. He was only 38 years old.
