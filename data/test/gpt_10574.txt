Edward James Blake (December 23, 1925 â€“ October 10, 2006) was an American drummer, composer, and recording artist. He is principally known as the drummer for the rock band The Velvet Underground.  Blake was born in Los Angeles, California, on December 23, 1925. He played drums in local bands in his youth and joined The Velvet Underground in 1965. Blake played on most of the group's recordings and contributed to their only album to be certified platinum, "The Velvet Underground & the Velvet Underground & Nico". He left the band in 1973 and continued to play drums sporadically until his death in 2006.  Blake was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 1986 and the Grammy Hall of Fame in 1993. He received a Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award in 2002. In 2003, Blake was honored with a National Medal of Arts from President George W. Bush.
