American country music singer Scotty McCreery has released six studio albums, one live album, two compilation albums, and thirty-six singles. He has won three Grammy Awards and three Academy of Country Music Awards.  Scotty McCreery was born on July 6, 1988 in Lafayette, Louisiana. He started singing at an early age and was encouraged by his father to pursue a career in music. In 2006, McCreery released his first album, "I'm Just a Country Boy", which peaked at number two on the Billboard country albums chart. The album produced two singles, "One Night" and "I Will Always Love You". In 2007, he released his second album, "Passion", which reached number one on the Billboard country albums chart and produced four singles: "I Will Be There", "The Trouble with Girls", "What About Now", and "TheRIPT." In 2009, he released his third album, "Golden". The album reached number one on the Billboard country albums chart and produced five singles: "Saturday Night", "I Believe in a Thing Called Love", "History of a Broken Heart (featuring Tim McGraw)", "You & Me (featuring Miranda Lambert)", and "Rain Is A Good Thing".
