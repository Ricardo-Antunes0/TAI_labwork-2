Parzival is a medieval romance by the German poet Wolfram von Eschenbach. It is one of the best-known and most widely read German romances, and has been adapted for the stage and screen many times.  Parzival, the son of a nobleman in the Holy Roman Empire, is framed for murder and sentenced to death by a corrupt judge. He escapes from prison and seeks revenge on his accusers. He meets a damsel, loves her, and then rescues her from a dragon. In the end, Parzival is recognized as a true hero and rewarded with marriage to the damsel.
