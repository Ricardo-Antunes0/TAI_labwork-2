New Zealand - Turkey relations are the bilateral relations between New Zealand and Turkey. The two countries have a long history of diplomatic and trade relations, with both countries being members of the United Nations, the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development, the Pacific Islands Forum, and the Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation forum.  Turkey - New Zealand relations are warm and friendly. The two countries have co-operated closely in regional and global forums, with strong economic links. Turkish investors have been instrumental in developing New Zealand's economy, while New Zealanders have helped to expand Turkish tourism.  The relationship between Turkey and New Zealand is based on shared interests in democracy, human rights, trade, security, culture, and education. Joint efforts are being made to enhance bilateral ties through bilateral dialogues and projects.
