George Zoritch  (born Yuri Zorich; Russian: Георгий Зорич) is a Russian-born American film director, screenwriter and producer. He is best known for his work as a screenwriter on the blockbuster films Armageddon and 300.  Zoritch was born in Moscow, Russia, to a Jewish family. He immigrated to the United States with his family at the age of three, and grew up in New York City. After studying film production at New York University, he worked as a production assistant on several low-budget films.  Zoritch's first writing credit was for the 1997 film The Siege of Firebase Gloria, which was directed by his then-colleague Shane Carruth. From there, he wrote and directed the short film The Lost Son (2002), which won the Grand Jury Prize at the Sundance Film Festival, and the feature film Armageddon (1998), which was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Screenplay.  Zoritch has also produced films such as The Blair Witch Project, Half Nelson and 300. He is currently working on two new projects: an adaptation of the novel Beowulf and an untitled action movie set in space.
