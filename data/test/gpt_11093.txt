Clarence Dickinson "Doc" Long, Jr. (December 11, 1882 – January 17, 1966) was an American football player and physician. He played college football at the University of Michigan from 1903 to 1905 and was captain of the 1905 team. He was elected to the College Football Hall of Fame in 1954. Long served as the head football coach at the University of Michigan from 1906 to 1907 and at the University of Illinois from 1908 to 1909, compiling a career record of 14–5.
