Jack Eddington was a member of the British Astronomical Society and a Fellow of the Royal Society.  Eddington was born in 1882 in Cambridge, England. He studied at Gonville and Caius College, Cambridge, and earned his B.A. in 1904 and his M.A. in 1905. He then worked as an assistant lecturer at the Gonville and Caius College until 1907, when he became a lecturer at the University of Cambridge.  In 1918, Eddington was appointed professor of mathematics at the University of Manchester, where he remained until his retirement in 1943. He served as president of the British Astronomical Society in 1931 and 1932, and was also a member of the Royal Society.  Eddington's major accomplishments include his work on the theory of black holes, his discovery of the Eddington limit, and his development of the general theory of relativity. He died in 1944.
