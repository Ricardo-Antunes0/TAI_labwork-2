Kangaroo is a 1923 novel by D.H. Lawrence.  Kangaroo is a story of Australia, told from the point of view of an Englishman, Charles Ryder. The novel is divided into three parts: the Outback, the city, and London. In the Outback, Ryder experiences the harshness and unpredictability of life on a cattle station. In the city, he deals with the temptations of success and love. In London, he tries to find his place in society and rediscover his own identity.  The novel has been praised for its realistic portrayal of Australian life and its exploration of the characters' motivations. However, it has also been criticized for its explicit and graphic descriptions of sexual activity.
