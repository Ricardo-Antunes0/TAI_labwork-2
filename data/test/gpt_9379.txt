Burton Point is an Iron Age promontory fort on the east coast of Cheshire, England. It was occupied from the 1st century BC to the 5th century AD. The fort is one of a group of forts and promontories along the eastern coast of Cheshire, collectively known as the Cheshire Lines.   The fort is made up of an outer bank and inner rampart. The rampart has been demolished in places, but the bank is still visible. There are two entrances into the fort, one on the east side and one on the west side. There is also a well in the fort. The fort is about north-east of Winsford and about south-west of Nantwich.
