Caroline M. Solomon is an American academic who specializes in medieval history and literature.  She received her B.A. from Vassar College in 1985 and her M.A. and Ph.D. from the University of Michigan in 1989 and 1993, respectively. She joined the faculty of the University of Michigan in 1993, where she currently serves as the John L. Loeb Associate Professor of History and Literature in the School of Literature, Arts, and Sciences. Her research focuses on medieval English literature and culture, with a particular emphasis on Chaucer, Langland, and Shakespeare. She has also published work on Geoffrey Chaucer's poetry,  John Gower's Confessio Amantis,  and William Langland's Piers Plowman.  Solomon has been awarded fellowships from the Guggenheim Foundation (1996-1997), the American Council of Learned Societies (ACLS) (2003), and the National Endowment for the Humanities (NEH) (2010).
