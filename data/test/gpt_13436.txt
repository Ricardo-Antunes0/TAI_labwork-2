Sir Kenneth Mathieson Dalglish  (born 4 January 1946) is a Scottish former footballer, who played as a striker. He played for Liverpool, Manchester United, Newcastle United, Glasgow Rangers, and Celtic. Dalglish won eleven league titles and three FA Cups with Liverpool, and was voted the European Footballer of the Year in 1985.  Dalglish began his career at Liverpool, making his first-team debut in 1964. He was an integral part of the side that won the 1966–67 league championship and the 1970–71 European Cup. He moved to Manchester United in 1974 and helped the club to win the 1976–77 league championship and the 1977–78 European Cup. He joined Newcastle United in 1981 and helped them to win the 1983–84 league championship. Dalglish joined Glasgow Rangers in 1985 and helped them to win the Scottish Premier Division title. He retired from professional football in 1992 after playing for Celtic. Dalglish was inducted into the English Football Hall of Fame in 2005. He was also awarded an honorary doctorate from Heriot-Watt University in 2006.  Sir Kenneth Mathieson Dalglish was born on 4 January 1946 in Glasgow, Scotland. He started his career at Liverpool playing for their youth teams before making his first-team debut for the Reds in 1964.
