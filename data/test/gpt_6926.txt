Kanalia  is a village in Thessaly, Greece. It is part of the municipality of Giannitsa. According to the 2011 census, the population of the village is 182 people.  The village Kanalia is located in the southeast of Thessaly, about 10 km from Giannitsa. It is a small and isolated settlement, built on a small hill that dominates the surrounding plain. The village has an agricultural character and mainly depends on agriculture and livestock.  The village was first mentioned in written sources in the early 19th century, when it was inhabited by Ottoman Turks who were expelled by the Greek army in 1822.   Kanalia has a small community centre that houses a library, a gymnasium, and a kindergarten. The nearest major town is Giannitsa, which is about 10 km away.
