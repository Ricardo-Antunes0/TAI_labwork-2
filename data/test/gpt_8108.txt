(also sometimes credited 三国連太郎; ; January 20, 1898 – March 16, 1968) was a Japanese actor, singer, and dramatist. He is considered one of the most significant Japanese actors of the 20th century.  Born in Tokyo, Mikuni had a difficult childhood. His father died when he was young and his mother abandoned him to become a Buddhist nun. He was raised by his older sister. As a result of this difficult upbringing, Mikuni developed an intense hatred for adults. He began his career as an actor in silent movies in 1924. He soon became known for his dramatic roles in films such as "The Tale of Genji" (1926), "The Life of Oharu" (1927), and "Sansho the Bailiff" (1929). In 1951, he won the Best Actor Award at the Cannes Film Festival for his portrayal of Kōbō Daishi in the film "Kobanashi". Mikuni also played important roles on stage, notably in the play "Madame Butterfly" (1954).  Mikuni was married four times. He had two daughters with his first wife, Yūko Ogawa. After Ogawa's death, he married actress Kyōko Kishida in 1957. They had a daughter together, Mika Kishida, but divorced in 1966.
