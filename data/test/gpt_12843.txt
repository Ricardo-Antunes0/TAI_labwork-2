Charles Laberge (October 21, 1827 – August 4, 1912) was a Canadian artist and lithographer.  Charles Laberge was born in Quebec City, Canada on October 21, 1827. He was the son of Joseph Laberge and Marguerite Favreau.  Laberge began his artistic career as a lithographer, working for various publishing houses in Quebec City and Montreal. He is most famous for his illustrations for the children's book series "Les Aventures de Tintin" by Hergé. He also created the illustrations for the book "Le Petit Saint-Bernard" by Émile Zola.  Laberge died in 1912 in Montreal.
