Bonniebrook Homestead is a historic homestead site located in the town of Plymouth, Massachusetts. The main house was built in 1752 by Benjamin Church, a prominent Plymouth settler, and is one of the most notable examples of colonial architecture in the town. The property also includes a number of outbuildings, including a kitchen house, smokehouse, and barn.  Bonniebrook Homestead was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1989. The property is now owned by the Trustees of Reservations, who manage it as a historic site.
