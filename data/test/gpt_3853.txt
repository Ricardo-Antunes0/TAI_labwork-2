Gérard Henri Grisey (; ; 17 June 1921 – 25 July 1997) was a French singer, songwriter, and actor. He was considered one of the most influential French singers of the 20th century, with a vocal range from baritone to tenor.  Born in Paris, Grisey attended the Lycée Carnot and then the Lycée Louis-le-Grand. He began his musical career as a member of the group Les Fourmis Sainte-Marie in 1941. After World War II, Grisey toured Europe with Les Fourmis Sainte-Marie and then formed his own vocal trio. In 1954, he had his first international hit with the song "Mon P'tit Papa".  Grisey's first solo album, Le Disque d'Or (1957), was a critical and popular success. He followed it up with several more successful albums over the next two decades, including Électrique (1965), L'Homme qui voulait être aimé (1967), and Au Hasard Balthazar (1972). His final album, L'Ombre (1994), was released just one month before his death.  Throughout his career, Grisey won numerous awards, including five Grammys. He also received a Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Motion Picture Musical or Comedy for his role in La Vie en rose (1985).
