KingsIsle Entertainment, Inc. is an American video game publisher and developer based in New York City. The company was founded in 2003 by Bruce Straley, Jonathan Smith, and Robert Khoo.  KingsIsle Entertainment develops and publishes video games for personal computers, handheld gaming systems, and mobile devices. The company's most successful products include the "Baldur's Gate" series, "Dragon Age" series, "Dark Souls" series, "The Witcher" series, and the "Fallout" series. The company has also developed cooperative online video games such as "Dragon Age: Inquisition", "Destiny", and "The Division".  KingsIsle Entertainment has won several awards for its video games, including Best PC Game of the Year at the 2009 Academy of Interactive Arts & Sciences Awards, and Best Role-Playing Game of the Year at the 2010 Golden Joystick Awards. In 2013, the company was acquired by Take-Two Interactive.
