Randy Holden  (born July 2, 1945) is an American singer, songwriter and record producer. Holden has been a member of the country music duo The Statler Brothers, with his brother Bob. He also had a successful solo career, and has written or co-written many country hits.  Holden was born in Lubbock, Texas in 1945. After graduating from high school, he served in the U.S. Marine Corps and then studied music at Texas Tech University, where he met his future Statler Brother Bob. In 1971, the two formed the country music duo The Statler Brothers and had their first hit that year with "The Wanderer". They went on to produce many charted singles over the next few decades, including "I'm So Lonesome I Could Cry", "The Gambler", "You Ain't Seen Nothing Yet" and "Ain't That a Shame". Holden also had a successful solo career during this time; his biggest hits include "The Best of Me", "I'm Leavin' Tonight" and "All I Could Do Was Cry". He retired from performing in the late 1990s but has since returned to recording and performing.
