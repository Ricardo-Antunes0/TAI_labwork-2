The House of Wisdom , also known as the Bibliotheca Alexandrina or the Alexandrian Library, is one of the oldest and most famous libraries in the world. It was founded in 330 BC by Ptolemy I Soter, one of the three great Macedonian kings who ruled Egypt during antiquity. The library was moved to its present location by Caliph Omar in 640 AD.  The library is a large collection of manuscripts and books on various subjects, located in the Egyptian capital Cairo. It is one of the main repositories of ancient manuscripts and a research center for Islamic studies. It is also renowned for its rare manuscripts on Jewish law, poetry and history.
