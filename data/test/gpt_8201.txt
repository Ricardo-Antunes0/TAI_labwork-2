He studied at Brown University, from which he graduated in 1774. He then studied law with his father and was admitted to the bar in 1777.  Olney practiced law in Providence until 1789, when he became a judge of the Rhode Island Superior Court. He served on the court until 1802, when he was appointed associate justice of the United States Supreme Court, serving until his death in 1812.  Olney was a supporter of Thomas Jefferson and the Democratic-Republican Party. He is best known for his opinion in "Marbury v. Madison", which upheld the power of the judicial branch to issue writs of mandamus and injunction.
