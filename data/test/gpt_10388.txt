Danio is a genus of small freshwater fish in the family Cyprinidae. It contains about 10 species, all of which are native to Southeast Asia and China, with a few species also found in Nepal and India.  The Danio genus includes small, predatory fish that inhabit slow-moving water ecosystems. They are generally shy and difficult to catch, preferring to dart away when approached. They are valued as aquarium fish and are also used for food.
