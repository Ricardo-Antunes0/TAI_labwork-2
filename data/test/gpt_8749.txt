George A. "Fred" Wright is the Associate Director for Research at the National Park Service's National Museum of American History. Wright has been with the National Park Service since 1988 and has worked in a number of capacities including research historian, curator, and program manager.  Wright has authored or co-authored a number of books and articles on the history of the National Park Service and its impact on American society. He has also served as a consultant to various television programs and documentaries on the National Park Service. In 2013, he was awarded the Frederick Law Olmsted Award for distinguished service to American landscape preservation.
