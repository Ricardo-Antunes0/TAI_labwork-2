The Bakersfield Drillers was the initial moniker of an American professional baseball team based in Bakersfield, California. The Drillers played in the Pacific Coast League from 1962 to 1967.  The Drillers were a charter member of the PCL and played their first season at Municipal Stadium. The team moved to Rabobank Stadium in 1968 and finished the season with a record of 79–83. In 1969, the Drillers moved to the new Kern County Stadium and finished with a record of 83–59. The team folded after the 1967 season.
