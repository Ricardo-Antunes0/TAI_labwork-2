Virginia Maksymowicz (born February 19, 1952) is an American composer, pianist, and music educator. Maksymowicz is known for her music composed for the stage, including her opera Amahl and the Night Visitors (1990), and her orchestral works including the Piano Concerto (1994).  Virginia Maksymowicz was born on February 19, 1952, in New York City. She began her musical studies at the age of six, studying piano with Shirley Geissler. Maksymowicz went on to study composition at the University of Southern California with Iannis Xenakis and Donald Martino. She also studied with Luigi Nono at the Accademia Nazionale di Santa Cecilia in Rome.  Maksymowicz has been composer-in-residence at Yale University, the University of Iowa, and Princeton University. She has also served as an associate professor of composition at Yale University and the New England Conservatory of Music.  Maksymowicz's notable works include the opera Amahl and the Night Visitors (1990), which was commissioned by the Houston Grand Opera; the Piano Concerto (1994), which was commissioned by the Boston Symphony Orchestra; and the orchestral works The Watcher (1999) and The Life of a Great Composer (2002). She has also written chamber music, vocal works, and electronic music.  Maksymowicz is married to composer Christopher Rouse. They have two daughters.
