Kwame Harris (born March 15, 1982) is an American politician who is the junior United States Senator from Maryland, serving since January 3, 2019. A member of the Democratic Party, Harris represents Maryland's Fourth Congressional District, which encompasses portions of Baltimore and its suburbs.  Harris was born in Baltimore, Maryland. Raised in the city's Sandtown-Winchester neighborhood, Harris attended public schools before graduating from high school at age 17. He then attended Howard University where he earned a B.A. in political science and African-American studies in 2003.  After graduation, Harris worked as a community organizer for organizations such as ACORN and Project Vote before being elected to the Baltimore City Council in 2006 at the age of 25. During his time on the council, Harris was responsible for making Baltimore one of the most affordable cities in America through his work on budget reforms and public investment.  In 2012, Harris was elected to the U.S. Senate to replace Barbara Mikulski, becoming Maryland's first black U.S. senator since Reconstruction. During his time in the Senate, Harris has focused on issues such as health care reform, criminal justice reform, and economic development initiatives. In April 2019, Harris announced his candidacy for Governor of Maryland in the 2020 election. He is currently polling second behind incumbent Larry Hogan.
