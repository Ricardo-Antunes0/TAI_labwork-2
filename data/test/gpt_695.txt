Black Beauty is an album by the English rock band the Beatles, released on vinyl and audio cassette on 20 May 1968 in the United Kingdom, and on CD on 15 November 1995 in the United States.  The album was recorded over a period of three weeks at EMI's Abbey Road Studios in London between 23 April and 12 May 1968, during the height of the Beatles' popularity. It was their nineteenth album and eighth studio album for EMI. Black Beauty was their first album to be produced by George Martin, who had succeeded Pete Best as the Beatles' producer.  The album reached number one in Britain and number two in the United States, where it was certified gold by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA) on 7 July 1968. It has been certified platinum by the RIAA on 18 January 2002.  Black Beauty is generally considered to be one of the best albums by the Beatles, and has been included in several lists of the greatest albums of all time. The album has been reissued numerous times, most recently on CD and digital download in 2013, as part of the "Past Masters" series.
