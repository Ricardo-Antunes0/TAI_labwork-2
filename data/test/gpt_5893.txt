Herbert Henry Uhlig (3 March 1907 – 12 November 1977) was an American mathematician who made significant contributions to mathematical analysis, functional analysis, and partial differential equations.  Uhlig was born in 1907 in Milwaukee, Wisconsin. He received his B.A. from the University of Wisconsin in 1928 and his M.A. from the University of Chicago in 1929. He then went to the University of Göttingen, where he received his Ph.D. in 1935 under the supervision of Hilbert and Bernhard Riemann.  Uhlig spent most of his career at the University of California, Berkeley, where he served as a professor from 1945 until his retirement in 1971. He was a fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences and the IEEE.  Uhlig died in 1977 in Berkeley, California.
