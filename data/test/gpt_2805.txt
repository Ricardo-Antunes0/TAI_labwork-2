Gregory of Huntingdon (fl. 1290) was a medieval English philosopher and theologian.  Gregory was born in the town of Huntingdon, in the English county of Huntingdon, around 1240. He was a student at Cambridge University, where he obtained his bachelor's degree in 1268 and his master's degree in 1273. He then became a lecturer at the University of Cambridge, where he remained until his death.  Gregory is best known for his work "The Six Books of Revelation", which he wrote between 1290 and 1310. He is also known for his writings on philosophy and theology, which aimed to reconcile Christianity with Aristotelian thought.
