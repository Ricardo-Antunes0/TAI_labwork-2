Jessica K. Hodgins is an American roboticist who specializes in humanoid robotics and humanoids in general. She is currently a professor at Carnegie Mellon University, where she also directs the humanoid robotics lab.  Hodgins has authored or co-authored more than 100 publications in the field of humanoid robotics, with particular emphasis on locomotion and manipulation. She is also a member of the IEEE and ACM.  In 2006, Hodgins received a MacArthur Fellowship, which is awarded annually to individuals who make significant contributions to creative arts, science, or mathematics.
