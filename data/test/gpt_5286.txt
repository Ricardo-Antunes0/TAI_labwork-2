The Grenada National Museum is a museum in Saint George, Grenada. It is located on the site of the former Royal Governor's Palace, which was destroyed by a fire in 1975. The museum was founded in 1978 and has since grown to encompass a collection of over 25,000 objects. The museum's main focus is on Grenada's pre-colonial and colonial history, with a particular emphasis on art and archaeology.   The museum also has a library and research facilities. It is open Monday to Saturday 9am to 5pm, and Sunday 1pm to 4pm.
