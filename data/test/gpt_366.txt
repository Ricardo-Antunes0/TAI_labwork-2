Salina Hayat Ivy (born 5 June 1966) is a British actress and model. She is known for her roles in the films Bend It Like Beckham, The Bodyguard, and X-Men: First Class.  Ivy was born in London, England, the daughter of Nazanin (n√©e Khodabandeh), an Iranian actress, and Richard Hayat, a British actor and musician. She has an older brother, Rufus. She attended Bedales School in Hampshire and then the Guildhall School of Music and Drama in London.  Ivy's career began with small roles in television shows such as BBC1's "Casualty" and "Heartbeat" before she was cast as Julie Walters' daughter in the BBC2 sitcom "Walters". She also had a recurring role on the Channel 4 soap opera "Brookside". Her breakthrough role came in the film Bend It Like Beckham (2002), for which she was nominated for a BAFTA Award for Best Actress. Since then, she has appeared in a number of high-profile films, including The Bodyguard (1996), X-Men: First Class (2011), and Kingsman: The Secret Service (2014).  In 2016, Ivy published her autobiography, "Beautiful Chaos".
