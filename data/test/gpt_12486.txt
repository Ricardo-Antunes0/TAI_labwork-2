Kristian Williams (born 1974) is an American artist and musician. He is best known for his large-scale, public sculptures that often explore themes of mortality and the human condition.  Kristian Williams was born in 1974 in Lynchburg, Virginia. He received his BFA from the Maryland Institute College of Art in 1999 and his MFA from the Rhode Island School of Design in 2004. Williams has exhibited work in venues across the United States, Europe, and Asia, including the Museum of Modern Art, the Whitney Museum of American Art, and the Tate Modern. He has been awarded fellowships from the Guggenheim Foundation, the MacArthur Foundation, and the Pollock-Krasner Foundation. In 2017 he was awarded an honorary doctorate from Brown University.
