Armadillidium phalacronum is a large European species of freshwater snail with a gill and an operculum, a gastropod mollusk in the family Hydrobiidae. It is found in France, Italy, Switzerland, Austria, Germany and Hungary.  The maximum recorded shell length is 110 mm. It feeds on plants such as reed canary grass ("Phalaris arundinacea"), common reed ("Phragmites australis"), willowherb ("Epilobium angustifolium"), smooth meadowsweet ("Filipendula ulmaria"), common hogweed ("Heracleum sphondylium") and water mint ("Mentha aquatica").
