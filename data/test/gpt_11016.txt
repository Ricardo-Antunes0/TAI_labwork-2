Néria Lúcio Buzatto (born January 13, 1980) is a Brazilian actress and singer.  Buzatto was born in São Paulo, Brazil, to parents of Italian descent. She began her career as a model and actress, and was cast in the role of Clarice in the Brazilian telenovela "Clarice" (2002), for which she received a nomination for Best Actress at the 2003 Brazilian TV Awards. She has since appeared in several successful Brazilian TV and theatrical productions. Buzatto is also known for her roles in the 2008 Spanish-language film "Tiempo de amor", 2009's "Segundo Sol", and the 2010 Brazilian romantic comedy "O Que Ela Tem". In 2007, she released her debut album, "Néria Lúcio Buzatto".
