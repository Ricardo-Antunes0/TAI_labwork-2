Nikolsk  is a town and the administrative center of Nikolsk Oblast, Russia, located on the left bank of the Volga River south of Vologda, the administrative center of the oblast. Population:   The town was first mentioned in chronicles in 1247 as a part of a feud between the Princes of Moscow and Rurik Rostislavovich. In 1313, it was granted town status. It became part of the Grand Duchy of Moscow in 1485. In 1777, it was granted to Prince Grigory Potemkin. From 1917 to 1924, it was part of the Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic. It became an autonomous oblast in 1924.
