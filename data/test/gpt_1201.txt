The Atlantic sturgeon (Acipenser oxyrinchus oxyrinchus) is a species of sturgeon that inhabits the Atlantic Ocean, including the Great Lakes and their tributaries. The Atlantic sturgeon is the largest of the four sturgeons species in North America, reaching lengths of up to 3.9 m (12.8 ft).  The Atlantic sturgeon is a large fish that can weigh up to 450 kg (1,000 lb). It has a deep red or black body with a distinctive white stripe down its side. The Atlantic sturgeon is a predator that feeds on fish, crustaceans, and other aquatic creatures.
