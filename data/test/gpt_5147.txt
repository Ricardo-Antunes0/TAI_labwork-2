Lucinda "Lucy" Sanders (born 1954) is the first female to serve as the Mayor of Atlanta, Georgia, serving from 2014 to 2019. She is also the first African American to hold the position.  Sanders was born in Atlanta, Georgia in 1954. She earned a B.A. from Spelman College in 1976 and an M.P.A. from the University of Georgia in 1985. She worked as a policy analyst for the city of Atlanta from 1985 to 1991, and served as city controller from 1991 to 1998. From 1998 to 2004, she was the executive director of the Atlanta Housing Authority. Sanders was elected mayor of Atlanta in November 2014, defeating incumbent Kasim Reed by more than 20 percent of the vote. She was re-elected in November 2018 with more than 70 percent of the vote.
