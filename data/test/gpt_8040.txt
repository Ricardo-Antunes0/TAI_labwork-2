Zhou Yu (; born 23 January 1989) is a Chinese sprint canoer who has competed since the late 2000s. He has won nine medals at the World Championships (4 golds, 4 silver, and 1 bronze), as well as thirteen medals at the Asian Games (5 gold, 4 silver, and 3 bronze).  Zhou Yu was born in 1989 in Jiangsu Province. He started canoeing at the age of six and joined the national team in 2007. He made his international debut at the 2008 Summer Olympics in Beijing, where he finished fourth in the K-2 1000 m event. Zhou improved to win his first gold medal at the 2009 World Canoe Sprint Championships in Szeged, Hungary with a time of 2:02.079 minutes. In 2012, Zhou captured his third world title with a time of 2:00.811 minutes in the K-2 1000 m event at the World Canoe Sprint Championships in Szeged.  At the 2014 Asian Games in Incheon, South Korea, Zhou Yu won his fifth gold medal with a time of 2:04.810 minutes in the K-4 1000 m event. He also won silver medal with a time of 2:05.597 minutes in the K-4 1000 m event at the 2017 Asian Games
