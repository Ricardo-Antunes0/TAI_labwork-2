Serge Blanc (31 December 1929 – 29 November 2003) was a violinist who had an immense impact on the playing of the violin. He was born in Lyon, France, into a musical family. Blanc studied with Ysaÿe, Rostropovich and Leonard Bernstein, and made his debut at the age of 16.   Blanc's virtuosity was legendary; he could play any piece by Paganini or Beethoven with ease and breathtaking speed. He became one of the most popular violinists in the world, performing with many of the greatest orchestras. He also made many recordings, both for classical labels and for popular albums. Blanc died at the age of 72 after a long battle with cancer.
