USS Georgia (BB-15) was a United States Navy pre-dreadnought battleship. She was the second ship of the United States Navy to be named in honor of the US state of Georgia.  USS Georgia was laid down on 14 October 1907 at the Fore River Shipbuilding Company in Quincy, Massachusetts, launched on 2 June 1909, sponsored by Mrs. W. B. Talbott, and commissioned on 16 March 1911, Captain George S. Brown in command.  Georgia completed her trials off Cape Hatteras on 10 July 1911 and joined the Atlantic Fleet. She joined the European Squadron in November and remained with it until February 1912 when she returned to the United States and resumed training operations off Cape Hatteras.  On 27 May 1912, Georgia departed for World War I as part of the American Expeditionary Force. She arrived in France on 9 July and joined the 3rd Battle Squadron of the Grand Fleet. She participated in numerous engagements during the war, including the Battle of Heligoland Bight on 24 August 1914; the Battle of Dogger Bank on 15 January 1915; and the Battle of Jutland on 31 May 1916.  Georgia was decommissioned on 16 January 1919 at Philadelphia and placed in reserve. She was transferred to Brazil on 5 December 1922 and renamed Riachuelo.
