The original Maroon Creek Bridge is a historic bridge located in the city of Indianapolis, Indiana. It was built in 1891 and is a three-span Warren pony truss bridge with a total length of 1,848 feet. The bridge is listed on the National Register of Historic Places and is a contributing property to the Central Near East Side District.  The Maroon Creek Bridge is significant for its role in the city's transportation network. It was one of the first bridges built over Maroon Creek, which was an important transportation route into the city. The bridge has also been important for its architectural significance. It is one of the few surviving examples of a Pony Truss bridge in the city and has been noted for its design and construction.
