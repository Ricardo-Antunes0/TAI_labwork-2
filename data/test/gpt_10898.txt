Vijayawada (formerly Bezawada) is the second largest city in the Indian state of Andhra Pradesh after Hyderabad. It is located on the banks of the Krishna River, in the southeast corner of the state. Vijayawada is administered by a municipal corporation.  Vijayawada is an important commercial and industrial center in Andhra Pradesh, and is one of the fastest growing cities in India. It is also a major tourist destination, with many attractions including temples, beaches and gardens. The city has a population of 1,711,000 (2011 census).  The name Vijayawada is derived from "Vijaya", the mythical hero of the epic Ramayana. The city was founded in 1536 by Harihara Raya II, who named it after his brother Vira Jaya Raya, who was also the founder of Golkonda.  Vijayawada was ruled by the Vijayanagara Empire from 1336 to 1565. After the empire's decline, it came under the control of the Bahmani Sultanate, followed by Musunuri Nayaks and then the Mughals. The French colonial empire took control of it in 1763. In 1806, during the Anglo-French conflict over territories in India, British forces captured Vijayawada after a three-month siege.
