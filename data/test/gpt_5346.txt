General Sir Albert Houtum-Schindler  (born 24 October 1881, date of death unknown) was a senior British Army officer who played a significant role in World War II as the Commanding Officer (CO) of the British Army's 6th Airborne Division. He is best known for his command of Operation Plunder, the successful airborne assault on the German oil refinery at Ploie»ôti in August 1944.  Born into a wealthy Dutch family, Schindler showed an early interest in a military career. After attending the Royal Military Academy, Woolwich, he was commissioned as a second lieutenant in the Royal Engineers in 1903. He served in South Africa during the Second Boer War and was promoted to lieutenant in 1907. In 1912, he was transferred to the Royal Flying Corps and served during World War I with No. 22 Squadron RFC.  Schindler remained in the RAF after the war and was promoted to captain in 1922. He served on staff at the Imperial Defence College and thereafter held a series of staff posts within the British military. In 1938, Schindler was promoted to major general and appointed Director of Military Training at the War Office. He became known as 'the General who never was' after his repeated requests for higher command were rejected by London.  In May 1941, Schindler was appointed CO of the 6th Airborne Division and led it during Operation Husky, the Allied invasion of Sicily.
