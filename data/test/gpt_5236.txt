Jan Václav Josef Frierenberger (German: Johann Wenzel Frierenberger, Czech: Václav Jan Josef Frierenberger, 2 February 1892 – 15 December 1978) was a Czechoslovak mathematician, astronomer and physicist.  Frierenberger was born in Tábor, then part of Austria-Hungary. In 1922 he became a professor at Charles University in Prague. He made important contributions to differential geometry, topology and mathematical physics. He also developed a novel method for solving Partial Differential Equations.  Frierenberger was awarded the Czechoslovak State Prize in 1954 and the Wolf Prize in mathematics in 1956.
