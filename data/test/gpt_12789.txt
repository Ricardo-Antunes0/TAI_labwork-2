Ranganathaswamy Temple  is an ancient and venerated Hindu temple dedicated to the god Ranganathaswamy, located in the city of Bangalore, Karnataka, India. The temple is considered one of the most important pilgrimage sites for Hindus and is one of the two most visited temples in Karnataka (the other being the Banaras Hindu University).  The temple is one of the oldest in Southern India, and is said to date back to the early 6th century CE. It is one of the 108 "ashtadhatu" temples dedicated to various Hindu deities. The temple complex covers an area of over 58 acres (23 hectares), and includes a number of shrines and buildings, including a "garbhagriha", an inner sanctum housing the deity, a "mandapa", and several smaller shrines. The temple is open from 6:00am to 12:00pm and 4:00pm to 8:00pm every day. The annual festival held at the templecomplex attracts millions of devotees from across India and abroad.  Ranganathaswamy Temple was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1987.
