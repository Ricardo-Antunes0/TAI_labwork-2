Sandra Marie Farmer-Patrick (née Farmer, born 18 December 1957) is a Canadian poet, author and academic.  Sandra Farmer-Patrick was born on 18 December 1957 in Sainte-Marie, Quebec, Canada. She received a B.A. from the University of British Columbia in 1979 and an M.A. from the University of Victoria in 1982. She has been a professor of English at the Université du Québec à Montréal since 1987 and was named a Canada Research Chair in Creative Writing in 2004.  Farmer-Patrick's poetry has been published in magazines and anthologies both in Canada and abroad, notably "Prairie Schooner", "The Threepenny Review", "Chicago Review", "The Kenyon Review", and "Poetry". Her first book of poetry, "The Book of Hours", was published in 1993. Her most recent book is "What We Leave Behind" (2010).  Her work has been nominated for the Governor General's Award for Poetry, the Griffin Poetry Prize, and the Prix Aurora Award. In 2010 she was elected to the Royal Society of Canada.
