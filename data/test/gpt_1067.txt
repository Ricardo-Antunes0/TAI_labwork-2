Mihail Gheorghiu Bujor (November 8, 1881 – October 17, 1964) was a Romanian Communist politician and leader of the Romanian Workers' Party (Partidul Muncii Române, PMR). He served as the sixth Prime Minister of Romania from 1944 to 1946 and from 1952 to 1956.  Born in Iași, Bujor joined the Social Democratic Labor Party in 1903. He became a member of its Central Committee in 1918, and was elected to the Romanian Parliament in the 1918 general election. Bujor became a member of the newly founded Communist Party of Romania in 1923. He served as its secretary-general from 1930 to 1931, and as its president from 1940 to 1942.  Bujor was arrested by the authorities during World War II, but was released in 1943 after he pledged allegiance to King Michael I and joined the National Liberation Front. He became leader of the PMR in 1944, and served as Prime Minister from 1944 to 1946. In 1952, he was elected President of Romania, serving until his death in 1964.
