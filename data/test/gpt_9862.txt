Claude de Kemoularia (March 30, 1922 – February 1, 2002) was a French writer, film critic and essayist.  He was born in Paris. After studying at the University of Paris, he worked as a journalist for magazines such as "Cahiers du cinéma", "Le Temps" and "L'Express". He also wrote screenplays, including the script for the Jean-Luc Godard film "The Young Girls of Rochefort" (1960).  Kemoularia's first book, a study of André Bazin, was published in 1958. He collaborated with François Truffaut on the screenplay for "The 400 Blows" (1959). In the early 1960s he became known as a film critic for "Le Nouvel Observateur". He also wrote essays and criticism for other journals, including "Le Monde", "Les Lettres françaises" and "Le Nouvel Observateur".  Kemoularia died in 2002.
