Ruggero Mastroianni (7 November 1929 – 9 October 1984) was an Italian actor and film director. He appeared in more than 100 films in a career spanning more than five decades. He was renowned for his deep, resonant voice, chiseled features, and dark, dramatic looks.  Mastroianni was born in the town of Monfalcone, in the province of Gorizia, Italy. His father, Antonio Mastroianni, was a blacksmith and his mother, Ida (née Bussotti), was a seamstress. Ruggero Mastroianni began working in his father's shop when he was 14 years old. After World War II, Mastroianni started his career as an actor in film and on stage. In 1950, he won a Nastro d'Argento award for Best Actor for his performance in the film La Dolce Vita. In 1953, Mastroianni appeared in the commercially successful drama film Paisa. His breakthrough as an actor came with the role of Fortebraccio in the 1954 film Roman Holiday directed by William Wyler. Mastroianni received international attention for his performance as the title character in Federico Fellini's 1957 film La Dolce Vita. For this role he won a BAFTA Award for Best Actor and a Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Motion Picture Drama.   In 1960, Mastroianni starred opposite Claudia Cardinale in the romantic drama film Il Gattopardo
