The Moore-Mayo House is a historic house located at 13 Moore Street in Concord, New Hampshire. The house was built c. 1792-93 by John Mayo and his wife Hannah Moore, and is one of the earliest surviving buildings in Concord. The house is a 2-1/2 story wood frame structure, with a gable roof, clapboard siding, and a granite foundation. It has two entrances, one on the front facade and the other on the side, and is decorated with Federal style detailing, including a dentilled cornice and trim. The house was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1983.
