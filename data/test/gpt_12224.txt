Cynthia Weber Farah Haines (born 1949) is an American author, journalist, and civil rights activist. She is the author of three books: Rage Becomes Her: The Life and Times of Madeline Albright; My Life As an Allegory: A Memoir; and The View from the Top: A Memoir of Life in the White House. She has also written for The New York Times, Washington Post, Los Angeles Times, and Time magazine.  Cynthia Farah was born in 1949 in Cleveland, Ohio, to a Lebanese immigrant father and a Jewish mother. She attended the University of Pennsylvania and received a degree in history. In 1976, she moved to Washington, D.C., to work as a reporter for The Washington Post. In 1981, she became a White House correspondent for Time magazine, where she covered Presidents Ronald Reagan and George H.W. Bush. In 1991, she left Time to write her first book, Rage Becomes Her: The Life and Times of Madeline Albright.  Farah is the founder and president of the Institute on Race and Poverty (IRP), a research organization based in Washington D.C. She has also been a visiting professor at Georgetown University and Columbia University. Farah was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Barack Obama in 2013.
