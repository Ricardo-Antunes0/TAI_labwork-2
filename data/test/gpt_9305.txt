Kumar Tuhin is a career diplomat from the Indian Foreign Service. He has served as the Ambassador of India to the United States of America, the Republic of Korea, and the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia.  Kumar Tuhin was born in Shimla, Himachal Pradesh, India on December 2, 1966. He earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in political science from the University of Delhi in 1990. He then attended the Fletcher School of Law and Diplomacy at Tufts University, where he earned a Master of Arts degree in international relations in 1993.  Tuhin joined the Indian Foreign Service in 1993. He has served as the Deputy Director General for North America at the Ministry of External Affairs (MOFA) from 2006 to 2007, and as India's Ambassador to the United States of America from 2007 to 2011. From 2011 to 2013, he served as India's Ambassador to the Republic of Korea. From 2013 to 2016, he served as India's Ambassador to the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia.   Tuhin is a member of the Board of Trustees at The Fletcher School of Law and Diplomacy at Tufts University. He is also a member of The Indira Gandhi National Centre for Arts and Culture, The Asia Society's Council on Foreign Relations (CFR), and The Leadership Conference on Civil and Human Rights (LCCR).  Kumar Tuhin is married to Neera Tuhin. They have two children.
