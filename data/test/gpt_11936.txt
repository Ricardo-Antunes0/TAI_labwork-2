Ethical marketing refers to the application of principles of ethics and morality to marketing practices. It is a term that has been in use since the early 1990s, but did not gain widespread usage until the late 2000s.  There is no one definition of ethical marketing, as it is a relatively new term with a growing body of literature. Generally speaking, ethical marketing refers to practices that adhere to universal principles of ethics and morality, such as honesty, truthfulness, fairness, and respect for human rights. It also encompasses principles that are specific to the marketing industry, such as the promotion of sustainable development, social responsibility, and consumer protection.  The goal of ethical marketing is to create positive customer relationships by complying with ethical principles. This can help businesses build trust and credibility with their customers, while potentially reducing the risk of customer backlash. In addition, ethical marketing can lead to increased sales by aligning business goals with those of its customers.  There is no single answer to the question of how to ethically market products. Each company and product will require its own unique approach in order to comply with all applicable ethical principles while still meeting customer needs.
