Carl H. June (born 1953) is an American mathematician and professor at the University of Wyoming in Laramie, Wyoming. He specializes in partial differential equations and their applications, with a focus on the mathematical analysis of shock waves.  June was born in 1953 in Laramie, Wyoming. He received his undergraduate degree from the University of Wyoming in 1975, and his Ph.D. from the University of Utah in 1979.  June has been a professor at the University of Wyoming since 1981. His research focuses on partial differential equations and their applications, with a particular focus on the mathematical analysis of shock waves. He has authored or co-authored more than 150 journal articles and book chapters, and has been cited more than 3,000 times. In addition, he has served on the editorial boards of several journals, including "Journal of Mathematical Sciences", "Progress in Differential Equations", and "The Mathematical Gazette".  June was elected to the National Academy of Sciences in 2013.
