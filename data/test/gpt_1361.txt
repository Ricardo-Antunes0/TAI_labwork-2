Stott was educated at Westminster School and then read theology at King's College, Cambridge, where he was a fellow of Trinity College from 1952 until his retirement in 1988. He was then appointed a CBE in 1989.  Stott's main interests were in systematic theology and biblical interpretation. He wrote extensively on the theology of Paul and the New Testament, as well as on contemporary theological issues such as justification by faith alone, the atonement and the ministry of reconciliation. He was also known for his work on preaching and evangelism, and for his writings on ethics and history.  Stott died aged 85 on 21 March 2013.
