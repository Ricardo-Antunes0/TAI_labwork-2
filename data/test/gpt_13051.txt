The Cheyenne River (; "Good River"), also known as the Little Big Horn River, is a river in the United States. It is a tributary of the Yellowstone River, approximately long. The river is located in Wyoming and Montana.  The Cheyenne River originates in the Wind River Range in Wyoming and flows east-northeast through eastern Wyoming and central Montana to join the Yellowstone River near the town of Livingston, Montana. The Cheyenne River has been dammed twice: first by the United States Bureau of Reclamation in 1944 to form Lake Yellowstone, and second by Lincoln County in 1978 to form Lake Oahe. The lower section of the river between these two reservoirs is designated as a Wild and Scenic River.
