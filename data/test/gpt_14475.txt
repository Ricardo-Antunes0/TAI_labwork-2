It was used to determine the hours, days, months and years.  The Hydraulis of Dion is a type of water clock, invented in the 3rd century BC by the engineer Heron of Alexandria. It consisted of a number of bronze or marble disks mounted on a frame. The disks had concentric circles inscribed on them, and could be rotated to show the time.
