Arthur Tremayne (23 Feb. 1701â€“1796), of Sydenham, Kent, was an English landowner, politician and colonial administrator.  Tremayne was the son of Sir Arthur Tremayne, 1st Baronet and his wife, Anne, daughter of Sir John Luttrell, 3rd Baronet. He was educated at Eton and Merton College, Oxford. In 1731 he succeeded his father as baronet.  In 1745 Tremayne was elected to the House of Commons for Kent County; he held the seat until his death. In 1761 he was appointed Surveyor-General of Customs for England and Wales; he served in that office until 1768. He also served as Lord Lieutenant of Kent from 1767 to 1768 and from 1772 to 1775.  Tremayne died in London on 5 January 1796, aged 86. He had married Lady Anne Stanhope, eldest daughter of Charles Stanhope, 4th Earl of Harrington, in 1739. They had two sons and three daughters.
