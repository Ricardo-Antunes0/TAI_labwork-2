Pardis Mahdavi is an American scholar and author of Persian and Iranian Studies.  Mahdavi was born in Tehran, Iran in 1951. She received her undergraduate degree from the University of California, Berkeley and her graduate degree from the University of Pennsylvania. Mahdavi has been a professor of Persian and Iranian Studies at the University of California, Los Angeles since 1985.  Mahdavi's work focuses on Persian and Iranian poetry, folklore, history, and culture. Her most notable book is The Book of Beauty: Letters from a Poet to His Daughter (1994), which is a collection of letters written by Iranian poet Rumi to his daughter. Mahdavi has also published articles on Persian literature and history, as well as translations of Persian poetry.
