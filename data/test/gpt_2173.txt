Clift was recognized as one of the leading actors of his era, and was often called the "King of Hollywood".  Born in Somerville, New Jersey, Clift moved with his family to Albany, New York, at an early age. He began his acting career in the late 1850s in regional theater. He made his Broadway debut in 1867 and achieved international recognition the following year with a performance as Hamlet. In 1875, he starred in William Wyler's historic production of Ben-Hur. Clift played leading roles in several successful productions, including The Last Tycoon (1937), for which he received his first Academy Award nomination for Best Actor. Throughout his career, he appeared in more than sixty films. He died from a heart attack in 1908 at the age of 69.
