MacKerricher State Park is a state park in the town of Stockbridge, Massachusetts, United States. The park is named for Colonel John MacKerricher, an American Civil War general who served as Governor of New York.  The park is located on the east side of Stockbridge, on the north side of Main Street. It is bounded on the west by the town line with Pocasset and on the north by the town line with Lenox. The park is mostly open fields and woodlands, with a few small lakes and streams. There are a number of hiking trails in the park, as well as a playground, a picnic area, and a nature center.
