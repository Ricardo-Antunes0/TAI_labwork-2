Alex O. Solis (born March 25, 1964) is an American economist who is currently a senior economist at the Federal Reserve Bank of San Francisco. He was previously the vice president and chief economist of the Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis, where he served from July 2007 to February 2013. Solis has also been a research associate at the National Bureau of Economic Research, a professor of economics at Washington University in St. Louis, and a visiting professor at the London School of Economics.  Solis has authored or co-authored more than 150 articles and book chapters on topics such as monetary policy, economic growth, and employment. He is also the co-editor (with Alberto Alesina) of "Macroeconomics: Principles and Policy", which has been described as "the standard reference work in the field."  Born in Paterson, New Jersey, Solis received his B.A. in economics from Yale University in 1986 and his M.A. and Ph.D. in economics from Stanford University in 1990 and 1993 respectively.
