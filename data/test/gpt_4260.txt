Bulbophyllum gracillimum, commonly known as the wispy orchid, is a species of orchid that is endemic to the south-west of Western Australia.  The wispy orchid is a terrestrial, perennial, deciduous, herb with an underground tuber and a single leaf long and wide. Up to fifteen flowers long and wide are borne on a flowering stem tall. The flowers are blue-green with white stripes and are pollinated by wasps.  The wispy orchid grows in sand and clay soils near rivers and creeks.
