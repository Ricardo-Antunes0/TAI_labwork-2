The Penang Masonic Temple is a significant example of late 19th century colonial architecture and is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The temple is located on George Street, adjacent to the Penang State Legislative Assembly Building and the Government House.  The Penang Masonic Temple is composed of two wings, each wing housing a separate assembly hall and lecture theatre. The building features Corinthian columns, marble floors, and a cupola atop the roof. The interior of the temple features a mural painted by French artist Paul Gauguin. The Penang Masonic Temple has been used for various purposes over the years, including housing the Penang State Museum from 1955 to 1975 and serving as the headquarters of the Freemasons in Malaysia from 1975 to 1998. In 1998, the building was converted into an hotel by Genting Group.
