Carbrain /kar 'bren/  is a neighbourhood in east Belfast, Northern Ireland. It is situated in the east of the city, adjacent to the Lisburn Road and Andersonstown Road.  The area is bounded by the Lisburn Road to the north, Andersonstown Road to the south, and the Sandy Row/New Lodge Road interface to the west. The Carbrain name derives from a nearby car dealership which opened in the 1960s.  In recent years, Carbrain has become increasingly popular as an entertainment district, with a number of nightclubs and bars located there. It has also become a popular residential area for young people and families.
