Tau Librae, Latinized from Ï„ Librae, is the Bayer designation for a star in the constellation Libra. It is situated about midway between the brighter stars Alpha and Beta Librae.  Tau Librae is a blue-white primary star that is about twice the size of the sun and has about the same mass. It is radiating about 119 times the solar luminosity from its outer atmosphere at an effective temperature of 4,000 kelvins.
