Wesley William Roemer (born October 7, 1986) is an American professional soccer player who most recently played as a midfielder for Orlando City SC in Major League Soccer.  Wes Roemer was born in Fort Lauderdale, Florida, to parents Wesley and Cindy (n√©e Nelson) Roemer. He has two younger sisters, Kaitlyn and Brooke. His father played college football at the University of Akron. Wes Roemer attended Stonington High School, where he was a three-sport star in soccer, basketball, and track & field. In soccer, he won two state championships with the Stonington High School boys' soccer team. Roemer also played for the U.S. under-17 national team and was a member of the United States U-20 national team that finished fourth at the 2003 FIFA World Youth Championship.  After high school, Roemer played for Seton Hall University from 2004 to 2006. While there, he was a two-time Atlantic 10 Conference Player of the Year and led Seton Hall to the 2006 NCAA Tournament where they lost in the first round to Clemson University. Roemer also played for United Soccer League side Miami FC in 2007 and for USL First Division side Charleston Battery in 2008.  Prior to signing with Orlando City SC on March 1, 2013, Roemer spent time with Danish club Randers FC and Scottish club Heart of Midlothian on loan during the 2012 MLS season.
