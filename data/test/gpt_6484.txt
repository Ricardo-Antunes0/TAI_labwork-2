The Piper's Price is a 1917 silent drama film directed by D. W. Griffith and produced by the Triangle Film Company. It was one of Griffith's most ambitious and expensive productions and is considered one of the most important achievements in American cinema.  The story is set in the fictional town of Kingsbridge, Massachusetts, during the American Revolution. The film follows a group of people whose lives are changed by a mysterious stranger named Samuel who arrives from out of town with information about a hidden treasure. The townsfolk compete to find the treasure, with each person trying to outbid the others. The film culminates with a dramatic showdown between the would-be treasure seekers and Samuel himself.  The Piper's Price was critically acclaimed upon its release, and has been cited as one of Griffith's greatest achievements. It has been cited as an early influence on Hollywood cinema, and is often cited as one of the greatest films ever made.
