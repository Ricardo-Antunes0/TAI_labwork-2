Cedric Prakash, (born 3 November 1951), is a British-born Canadian pharmacologist and academic who is currently a Professor of Pharmacology at the University of Toronto. He is known for his work on the pharmacology and toxicology of monosodium glutamate (MSG) and its effects on human health.  Cedric Prakash was born in 1951 in London, England. He received his BSc (Hons) from the University of London in 1973, and his PhD from the University of Cambridge in 1978. After postdoctoral studies at Harvard University, he joined the Faculty of Medicine at the University of Toronto in 1980, where he has been a Professor of Pharmacology since 1998.  Prakash's research focuses on the pharmacology and toxicology of monosodium glutamate (MSG), specifically its effects on human health. He has published over 340 journal articles and book chapters, as well as two books on MSG: "Monosodium Glutamate: Taste, Health and Safety Issues" (2002) and "The Health Risks of Monosodium Glutamate" (2007). He is also a member of the Royal Society of Canada and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.  In 2006, Prakash was awarded the Order of Canada for "his contributions to pharmacology and toxicology, particularly for his work on monosodium glutamate."
