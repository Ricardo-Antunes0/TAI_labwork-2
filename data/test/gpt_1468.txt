In 1876 he became a magistrate, and from 1892 to 1916 he was Master of the Rolls. He was also a Conservative MP for Northampton from 1880 to 1885 and for Seaford from 1886 to 1916. Clarke died in London.  Clarke's best-known works are the novels "The Time Machine" (1895), "The War of the Worlds" (1898), and "The Invisible Man" (1897). He also wrote plays, including "The Bells" (1893), "The Daughter of Time" (1904), and "The Marquis of Blandford" (1909).
