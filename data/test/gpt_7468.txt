Elite overproduction is a concept developed by French economist Jean-Baptiste Say in the early 1800s. Say argued that overproduction, or too much production of goods for economic reasons, could lead to economic depressions.  Elite overproduction is a concept that was developed by French economist Jean-Baptiste Say in the early 1800s. Say argued that overproduction, or too much production of goods for economic reasons, could lead to economic depressions. This theory has been used by economists to explain why market economies undergo cycles of boom and bust.
