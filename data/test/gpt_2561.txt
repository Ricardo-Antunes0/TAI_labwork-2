Koyilandy (; formerly known in English as Quilon) is a city on the Coromandel Coast of the Bay of Bengal, in the state of Tamil Nadu, India. It is the administrative headquarters of Koyilandy District. As of 2011, the population of Koyilandy was around 176,000.  Koyilandy is known for its pearl industry, its silk industry and for its tea gardens. The city is also home to the University of Madras and a number of colleges and universities.  The earliest references to Koyilandy date back to the 6th century AD. The city was ruled by the Chola dynasty from the 12th century until it was conquered by the Delhi Sultanate in the 14th century. It was subsequently ruled by the Madurai Sultanate and then the British Raj. In 1949, Koyilandy became a part of India after independence.
