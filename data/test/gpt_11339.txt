John Richard Markell (born March 10, 1956) is an American politician who currently serves as the 78th Governor of Oregon, in office since 2015. A Democrat, he previously served as a United States Senator from 2013 to 2015.  Born in Omaha, Nebraska, Markell earned a B.A. from the University of Oregon in 1978 and an M.B.A. from the Harvard Business School in 1983. He worked for several years as a management consultant before becoming a political consultant and lobbyist in 1990.  Markell was elected to the Oregon House of Representatives in 1994 and served two terms before being elected to the U.S. Senate in 2002, defeating incumbent Gordon Smith with 55% of the vote. He was re-elected in 2006 with 54% of the vote and served two terms, leaving office in 2015.  Markell is considered a centrist Democrat who is pro-choice, pro-gun control, supports legalizing marijuana, and supports environmental protection measures like reducing greenhouse gas emissions. He has been critical of Donald Trump's presidency, stating that he "represents a danger to our country and our way of life." In November 2017, Markell announced his candidacy for Governor of Oregon in the 2020 election.
