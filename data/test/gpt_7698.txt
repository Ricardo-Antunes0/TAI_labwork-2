Sugarloaf is an unincorporated community residential area located in Santa Barbara County, California. The population was 1,032 at the 2010 census.  Sugarloaf is located at (34.018682, -119.014092).  According to the United States Census Bureau, the community has a total area of 0.5 square miles (1.3 km²), all of it land.  As of the census of 2000, there were 1,003 people, 344 households, and 276 families residing in the community. The population density was 1,759.9 people per square mile (692.9/km²). There were 366 housing units at an average density of 775.2 per square mile (298.5/km²). The racial makeup of the community was 91.48% White, 2.97% African American, 0.11% Native American, 0.11% Asian, 2.41% from other races, and 2.06% from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino of any race were 7.68% of the population.  There were 344 households out of which 36.8% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 49.7% were married couples living together, 11.2% had a female householder with no husband present, and 33.0% were non-families. 26.4% of all households were made up of individuals and 9.8% had someone living alone who was 65 years of
