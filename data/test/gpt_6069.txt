Sullivan was born in the town of Charleston, New York, on July 23, 1862. He graduated from Columbia Law School in 1884 and practiced law in Buffalo until he was elected to the New York State Senate in 1902. In 1909, he was elected governor, defeating Democrat William Jennings Bryan by a wide margin. Sullivan was re-elected in 1911, but due to his support of the military during World War I he did not serve out his full term. He died in 1922.
