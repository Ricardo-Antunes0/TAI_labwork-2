Isaac Rosenfeld (March 10, 1918, Chicago - February 1, 2003, Los Angeles) was an American composer, conductor, and music educator.  Rosenfeld was born in Chicago to a Jewish family. He began his musical studies at the age of 12 with Emanuel Feuermann and then at the University of Chicago where he studied with Otto Luening and Nadia Boulanger. He served in the United States Army during World War II and then attended the University of California, Berkeley where he studied with Virgil Thomson and Darius Milhaud. Rosenfeld later became a faculty member at the Juilliard School where he taught composition until his retirement in 1986.  Rosenfeld's compositions include orchestral works, operas, chamber music, vocal works, and film scores. His most well-known works include the opera "The Diary of Anne Frank" (1959), the orchestral work "Concerto for Orchestra" (1966), and the cantata "A Child Is Born" (1978). He was awarded the Pulitzer Prize for Music in 1978 for his cantata "A Child Is Born".
