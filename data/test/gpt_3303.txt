Born in Cincinnati, Ohio, Glenn began her career as a stage actress and singer in the 1920s. She is best known for her roles in the Broadway musicals "Parisian Nights" (1931), "The Baker's Wife" (1935), and "Strike Up the Band" (1937).  During World War II, Glenn served as a United States Army Nurse Corps officer in the European theater. After the war, she resumed her career in stage and television productions. She also appeared in several motion pictures, including "The Picture of Dorian Gray" (1945), "The Unfinished Dance" (1945), and "The Nun's Story" (1959).  Glenn died at the age of 92 in Greenwich Village, New York City.
