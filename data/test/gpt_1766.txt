The NCR Book Award for Non-Fiction, established in 1988, is a prestigious award given annually by the National Council of Review.  The NCR Book Award for Non-Fiction celebrates the best in nonfiction writing, honoring books that explore any and all aspects of life. Previous winners have included such iconic authors as Toni Morrison, Doris Kearns Goodwin, and Bill Bryson, as well as groundbreaking thinkers such as Noam Chomsky and Edward O. Wilson.  The NCR Book Award for Non-Fiction is open to any book published in any language during the calendar year and covers a wide range of topics, from history to nature to biography. The judges are experts in the field of nonfiction, and they consider not only literary quality but also innovation, impact, and originality when awarding the prize.
