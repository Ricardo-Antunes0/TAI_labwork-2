Dakan (Destiny) is a 1997 French/Guinean drama film directed by Abdellatif Kechiche. It is loosely based on the novel of the same name by Mahamane Boumedienne.  The film tells the story of a young African man, Baba, who moves to Paris in search of a better life. Dakan explores the complex and often difficult relationships between Baba, his family and friends, and the wider community in which they live. The film won the Palme d'Or at the 1997 Cannes Film Festival.   Dakan was released theatrically in France on 16 October 1997. It was later released in the UK on 14 November 1997 and in the US on 12 December 1997.   The film received positive reviews from critics and has been cited as one of the best French films of all time. It has been awarded multiple awards, including Best Film at the 1998 Cannes Film Festival and Best Director at the 1998 Guineas Film Festival.
