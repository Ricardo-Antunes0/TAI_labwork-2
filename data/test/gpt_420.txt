Fitzgerald was born in Boston, Massachusetts, to Michael and Catherine Fitzgerald. He studied at Boston College and the Pontifical Gregorian University, Rome. He was ordained a priest in 1917. From 1923 to 1941 he served as a professor of theology at Boston College. He served as pastor of the Church of the Annunciation in Newton from 1923 to 1941. He was named the fourth Archbishop of Boston on January 10, 1941. He resigned on October 16, 1965.
