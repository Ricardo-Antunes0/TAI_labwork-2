The settlement is approximately 2.5 metres below sea level and was occupied from about 150,000 to 120,000 years ago. The settlement was first discovered in 1816 by a local farmer and it was first excavated in 1826 by James Prinsep. The settlement has been extensively excavated since then and has revealed evidence of human occupation dating back to the early Mesolithic period. The settlement is also home to a number of endangered plant species, including the marsh fritillary butterfly.
