Dún Laoghaire–Rathdown  is a county in Ireland. It is part of the Dublin Region in the province of Leinster, and is one of three successor counties to County Dublin, which was disestablished in 1994. It is named after the former borough of Dún Laoghaire and the barony of Rathdown. Dún Laoghaire–Rathdown County Council is the local authority for the county. The population of the county was 218,018 at the time of the 2016 census. Geography and subdivisions Dún Laoghaire–Rathdown is bordered to the east by the Irish Sea, to the north by the city of Dublin, to the west by the county of South Dublin and to the south by County Wicklow. It is one of four local government areas that comprise the Dublin Region, with the city of Dublin, Fingal, and South Dublin. Located to the south-east of the capital city of Dublin, the county town of Dún Laoghaire–Rathdown is Dún Laoghaire. Since 2015, the county lies within the Eastern & Midland Regional Authority, one of three such subdivisions of the state. University College Dublin and Dún Laoghaire Institute of Art, Design and Technology are located in the county.
