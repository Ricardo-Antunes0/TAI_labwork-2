Domhnall Ua Buachalla (; ; 3 February 1481 – 21 January 1556) was an Irish nobleman and leading figure in the Tudor dynasty.  Domhnall was the eldest son of Domhnall Ua Buachalla, 1st Earl of Westmeath and Lady Margaret Fitzgerald. He was born at Riverstown Castle, near Mullingar, County Westmeath.  At the age of twenty-one, Domhnall was appointed Lord Deputy of Ireland under the rule of his father. In this role he played a prominent part in the Tudor conquest of Ireland, and was responsible for the foundation of Dublin Castle. He also participated in the Battle of Sussex (1547) and the Battle of Pinkhampton (1548).  In 1553, Domhnall became Lord President of Munster, a post he held until his death. He was also Lord Lieutenant of Leinster from 1550 to 1553.  Domhnall married Lady Catherine Grey (1488–1543), daughter of Henry Grey, 1st Duke of Suffolk and Lady Catherine Neville.
