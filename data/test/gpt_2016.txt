Erin H. Kimmerle is an American forensic anthropologist and professor at the University of North Texas. She specializes in the study of human remains, particularly those involved in homicide.  Kimmerle's research has focused on the development of methods for estimating age and sex from skeletal remains, the application of anthropological theory to criminal investigations, and the interpretation of archaeological and forensic data. She has also published extensively on aspects of forensic anthropology and archaeology.  Kimmerle was named a Fellow of the American Academy of Forensic Sciences in 2009, and she has served as president of the National Association for Research in Crime and Delinquency (NARCCD) and the Society for Human Identification (SHI). She is also a member of the American Academy of Pediatrics, the American Association for the Advancement of Science, and the Royal Anthropological Institute.
