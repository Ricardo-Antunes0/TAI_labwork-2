Anita Doron (born June 3, 1974) is an Israeli author and journalist. She is the author of several books, including the 2006 bestseller, The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo. Doron was born in Tel Aviv to a Jewish family. She attended the Hebrew Reali School in Tel Aviv and then studied political science at Tel Aviv University. Doron began her career as a journalist at Haaretz, where she worked from 1998 to 2002. She then moved to Sweden, where she worked as a foreign correspondent for the daily Svenska Dagbladet from 2002 to 2005. In 2006, Doron published her first book, The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo, which became a international bestseller. The book was adapted into a successful film released in 2009. Doron has since written two more books based on the Dragon Tattoo series: The Girl Who Kicked the Hornet's Nest and The Girl Who Played with Fire.
