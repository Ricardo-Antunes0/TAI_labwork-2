Amarsinh Bhilabhai Chaudhary was born on 31 July 1941 in a family of lawyers in Vadodara, Gujarat. He completed his schooling from Cathedral and John Connon School, Vadodara and then went to the University of Bombay to study law. He was called to the Bar from the Inner Temple in 1965. After practising for a few years in Bombay, he moved to Delhi in 1969 and started his career as a lawyer with the Supreme Court. He became a judge of the Delhi High Court in 1986, and later became a judge of the Supreme Court of India. He served as Chief Justice of India from 2007 to 2012. He died on 15 February 2017, aged 80.
