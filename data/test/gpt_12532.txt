He began studying music as a child and by the age of 15 he had composed several pieces. In 1863, Davis moved to the United States and began working as a conductor in Detroit. In 1868, he moved to New York City and began working as a conductor for the Metropolitan Opera. He remained with the Metropolitan Opera for the rest of his life.  Davis was awarded the Pulitzer Prize for Music in 1904 for his composition "The Dream of Gerontius". He died in 1911 and was buried in Mount Hebron Cemetery in Flushing, New York.
