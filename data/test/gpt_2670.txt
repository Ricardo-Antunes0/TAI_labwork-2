Patrik Antonius (born 13 December 1980) is a Swedish professional footballer who plays as a midfielder for English club Fulham.  Born in Malmö, Antonius started his career with Malmö FF, before moving to IFK Göteborg in 2001. He spent six seasons at the club, helping them to two Allsvenskan titles and two Svenska Cupen finals. In 2008, Antonius signed for FC Barcelona for a fee of £8 million. He made his La Liga debut in a 2–1 away win over Racing Santander and went on to make 24 appearances for the Catalans, scoring one goal.  In January 2011, Antonius signed for Chelsea on a six-and-a-half-year contract. He made his Premier League debut in a 3–1 home win over Tottenham Hotspur and went on to make 44 appearances for the club, scoring seven goals. In January 2014, Antonius joined Fulham on an initial one-and-a-half-year deal with the option of a further year. He made 29 appearances for Fulham during the 2013–14 season as they finished in ninth place in the Premier League. On 28 February 2017, Antonius scored his first goal for Fulham in a 2–1 home victory over Bournemouth.
