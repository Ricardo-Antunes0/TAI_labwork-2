Amynthas mekongianus, the Mekong worm or Mekong giant worm, is a large, predatory worm that inhabits the Mekong River basin. It can grow up to 2.5 meters long and weigh up to 100 kilograms.  The Mekong giant worm is the largest known worm and one of the largest invertebrates in the world. It is found in the lower reaches of the Mekong River in Laos, Cambodia, Vietnam, and Thailand. It feeds mainly on fish, but has been known to consume other creatures, including small mammals and birds. The worm is considered a dangerous animal and is hunted for its meat and skin.
