Sylvi Inkeri Anttila ( Metsämies; 29 November 1923 – 15 January 2007) was a Finnish artist and author. Anttila is best known for his paintings, which depict the natural environment and Finnish rural life.  Sylvi Inkeri Anttila was born in 1923 in the small town of Rautalampi in the Kuopio region of Finland. After completing his compulsory military service in 1945, he enrolled at the University of Helsinki, where he studied painting under Yrjö Kallio and Eero Saarinen. In 1951 Anttila moved to Paris, where he remained for the next decade. He returned to Finland in 1967 and settled in Ivalo.  Anttila's paintings depict the natural environment and Finnish rural life. He also wrote books on painting and art history, as well as poetry. He died in Ivalo in 2007 at the age of 84.
