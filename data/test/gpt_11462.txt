Constantin Tabarcea (8 February 1937 – 14 September 2013) was a Romanian jurist and politician, who served as the fifth President of Romania from 1989 to 1992.  Born in Iasi, Tabarcea studied at the University of Bucharest, where he obtained a law degree in 1960. He then worked as a lawyer before being appointed as a judge on the Romanian Court of Appeals in 1977. He was also a professor at the University of Bucharest Law School from 1981 to 1984.  Tabarcea entered politics in 1985, when he was elected to the Romanian Senate. He became Minister of Justice in 1989, and later served as Minister of National Defense from 1990 to 1992. Tabarcea was elected president following the death of Nicolae Ceauşescu in December 1989, and served until his resignation in January 1992. He later served as the chairman of the Democratic Liberal Party (PDL). Tabarcea died in 2013 at the age of 80.
