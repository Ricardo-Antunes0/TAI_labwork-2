The Mount Shasta Wilderness is a          505,940-acre (2,070 km2) wilderness area in the Shasta-Trinity National Forest in Northern California. It is the largest wilderness area in the United States east of the Rocky Mountains. The wilderness is managed by the US Forest Service.  The Mount Shasta Wilderness was established on September 2, 1984, as the first National Wilderness Area in California. The forest that contains it was first acquired by the US government in 1853 and the area around Mount Shasta was designated a national forest in 1893.
