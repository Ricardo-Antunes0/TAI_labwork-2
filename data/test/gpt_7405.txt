Lee Sheriden (born Roger Pritchard; 11 April 1947) is an English singer and songwriter, best known for his work in the 1970s as the lead singer and principal songwriter of the rock band Sheriden.  Sheriden was born in Westminster, London, England, on 11 April 1947. He attended Abingdon School and then studied at the Royal Academy of Music, from which he graduated with a degree in music in 1971.  Sheriden first came to prominence as the lead singer and songwriter of the rock band Sheriden, which formed in 1968. The band released two albums, "Somewhere Else" (1970) and "Sheriden" (1971), before splitting up in 1973. Sheriden then embarked on a solo career, releasing several albums over the next few years. He achieved modest success with his songs "Love Lies Bleeding" (1976) and "I Don't Want To Go Home" (1978), both of which charted in the UK. In 1987, he released his final album, "Lonely Nights".  Sheriden has since retired from music and has focused on writing and painting. He currently lives in France.
