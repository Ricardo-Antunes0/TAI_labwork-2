Sir Nicholas Bayard Dill (28 December 1905 â€“ 9 September 1979) was an English historian.  Dill was born in London, the son of Sir Herbert Dill, a historian and Liberal Party politician who served as First Lord of the Admiralty in the Conservative governments of Stanley Baldwin and Neville Chamberlain. He was educated at Eton and then Trinity College, Cambridge, where he graduated with a second-class degree in 1927. He then studied at the College of Europe, during which time he met his wife, the historian Barbara Wootton.  Dill began his career as a tutor at Eton from 1927 to 1932. He then became a lecturer at Cambridge, where he remained until 1957. From 1957 to 1961 he was Commonwealth Fellow at King's College, London and from 1961 to 1966 he was Master of Trinity College, Cambridge.  Dill wrote many books on British history, including "The Birth of the Modern World" (1937), "The United Kingdom since 1707" (1948), "The Making of the Middle Class" (1953), and "British History: A New Perspective" (1965). He also wrote extensively on European history and was a member of the editorial board of "The Journal of Modern History".  Dill was knighted in 1959. He died in 1979 at the age of 78.
