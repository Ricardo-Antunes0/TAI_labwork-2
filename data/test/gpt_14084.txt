Paul William "Bear" Bryant (September 11, 1913 – June 8, 1984) was an American football coach and athletic director. He was the head football coach at the University of Alabama from 1944 to 1957, and at the University of Texas from 1958 to 1982. Bryant is a member of the College Football Hall of Fame as well as the National Football Foundation Hall of Fame. He was also a member of the American Football Coaches Association Hall of Fame and the Texas Sports Hall of Fame.  Bryant was born in a small town in northeast Alabama, near Tuscumbia. He attended high school in Tuscumbia, where he played football and basketball under coach Calvary Christian School's legendary coach Harry Stuhldreher. Bryant also played baseball and ran track and field at Tuscumbia High School. After graduating from high school, Bryant attended Samford University in Birmingham, Alabama, where he played football for the Bulldogs under head coach Bob Zuppke.  Bryant never playedprofessional football in his life due to World War II. After the war, he returned to Samford, this time playing for head coach Bear Bryant and becoming a two-time All-American halfback. As a senior in 1946, Bryant led Samford to an undefeated season and the Southern Conference championship game against Auburn University; however, Auburn defeated Samford 21–0.
