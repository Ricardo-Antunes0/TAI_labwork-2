Haji Alakbar Mosque  was an Azerbaijani mosque in Istanbul, Turkey. It was built in 1785 by Haji Alakbar, a wealthy merchant from Tabriz, Iran. The mosque was badly damaged during the Great Fire of Istanbul in 1764 and rebuilt in 1822.   The mosque is situated in the Üsküdar district of Istanbul, on the Asian side of the Bosphorus Strait facing Üsküdar Park. The mosque is a popular tourist destination and is one of the most photographed mosques in Istanbul.
