Vicente Bacallar y Sanna, 1st Marquess of Mancera (; born Vicente Rodríguez Bacallar y Sanna on March 2, 1901 – July 17, 1990) was a Spanish-born American actor and filmmaker. He appeared in more than 100 films in a career spanning more than sixty years. He is considered one of the greatest and most influential actors of his era, and one of the greatest actors of all time.  Bacallar was born in the province of Alicante, Spain, to Vicente Rodríguez Bacallar, a civil engineer, and his wife Mercedes Sanna. He emigrated to the United States with his family at the age of six, settling in New York City. After attending the School of Industrial Art (now known as the School of Visual Arts), he began his acting career in Broadway productions. He first gained critical recognition for his work in the 1941 play "The Painted Veil", for which he won a Tony Award. In 1942 he starred in "Call Me Madam", for which he received his second Tony Award nomination. Bacallar played Michael Corleone in the 1953 Academy Award-winning film adaptation of Mario Puzo's novel "The Godfather". His performance as Don Vito Corleone was widely acknowledged as one of the great screen performances of all time.
