Gadani  is a coastal town of Thar Desert in the Sindh province of Pakistan. It is located on the coast of Arabian Sea, about 190 kilometers from the city of Karachi and about 150 kilometers from the town of Sukkur.  The Gadani people are a Sindhi tribe who trace their origins to the ancient kingdom of Gandhara. They are Sunni Muslims and are predominantly agriculturalists. The Gadani are also cattle herders and fishermen.  The Gadani area has an ancient history, with evidence of human habitation dating back to the Neolithic period. The Gadani people played an important role in the early history of Sindh, and were one of the few tribes to resist Muslim conquest in the seventh century. After being defeated by the Muslims, they eventually assimilated into mainstream Muslim society.  Gadani is a small town with a population of about 50,000 people. It is known for its fishing industry, which is dominated by tuna and sardines. There is also a small textile industry in Gadani. The main sources of income for the town are agriculture and tourism.
