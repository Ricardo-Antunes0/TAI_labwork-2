Casino Raiders is a 1989 Hong Kong action film directed by John Woo and produced by Michael De Luca. The film stars Jackie Chan, Sammo Hung, and Wilson Yip.  Inspired by the 1966 James Bond film Thunderball, the story follows a team of criminals who attempt to rob a casino in Macau. The film features elaborate action sequences and stunts, including one scene in which Chan performs a backflip off a building.  Casino Raiders was commercially successful, grossing $141 million worldwide. It received critical acclaim, with particular praise for its visual effects and Chan's performance. It has been cited as an influence on later Asian action films such as Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon and The Raid: Redemption.
