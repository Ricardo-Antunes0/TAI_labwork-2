The Mars Room is a 2018 novel by Emma Donoghue. It tells the story of Mary, a woman who is falsely accused of murder and must defend herself in a high-profile trial.  The novel was praised by critics for its tense and suspenseful plot, its atmospheric writing, and Donoghue's effective use of flashbacks. It was also noted for its depictions of mental illness and its exploration of the ethics of trial representation.  Mary is a woman who is falsely accused of murder. She must defend herself in a high-profile trial, and must navigate the difficult waters of a criminal justice system that she does not understand. The Mars Room is an atmospheric and tense story that explores the ethics of trial representation, mental illness, and the power of memory.
