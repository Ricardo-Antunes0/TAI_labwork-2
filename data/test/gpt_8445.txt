A. Karunanidhi ( Tamil : கருணாநிதி) was an Indian political leader who served as the Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu from May 1967 to March 1970 and again from May 1977 to September 1980. He was also a Member of the Parliament of India from 1952 to 1984. He served as the President of the Tamil Nadu Congress Committee from 1962 to 1984.  Karunanidhi was born on 16 October 1911 in Thiruvannamalai, a district in southern Tamil Nadu. He completed his schooling from Poonamallee High School and then studied law at Madras Law College. After completing his law studies, Karunanidhi began practising law in Madras.  Karunanidhi was first elected to the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly as an Independent candidate from Pallipalayam constituency in 1952, winning a by-election held after the death of the incumbent M. Karunanidhi. He initially served as Deputy Speaker of the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly, before becoming Minister for Labour and Social Welfare in 1955. In 1960, he was re-elected to the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly as an Indian National Congress candidate from Kanyakumari constituency, defeating the Communist Party of India candidate Muthuvel Karunanidhi.  Karunanidhi was appointed Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu on 11 May 1967, after winning the election held that year with a landslide majority. He served as Chief Minister until 30 March 1970, when he was replaced by E. V. K. Ram
