Eugène Edouard Bernard Lacomblé (26 October 1896 – 17 December 1978) was a French composer, violist and conductor.  Lacomblé was born in Paris, the son of composer and violinist Alexandre Lacomblé and his wife Marie-Eugène (née de la Châtelaine). He studied at the Paris Conservatoire with Nadia Boulanger, winning first prize in 1916. He also studied at the University of Berlin with Max Reger.  Lacomblé's compositional style is tonal, lyrical and expressive, and he often employed folk melodies in his works. He also held teaching posts at the Conservatoire de Paris and the University of Geneva. He died in Paris.
