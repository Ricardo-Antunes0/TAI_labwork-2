Bruno Pieters (born 17 June 1975 in Turnhout) is a Belgian former professional footballer who played as a midfielder.  Pieters started playing football with Racing Genk, before moving to Dutch side PSV Eindhoven in 1995. He would spend six seasons at the club, making over 150 appearances. Pieters then signed for FC Barcelona in 1999, and spent four seasons with the Spanish side. He helped them win the La Liga championship in 2001 and the Copa del Rey in 2002. Pieters then moved to Fulham in England, where he spent two seasons before retiring in 2004.  Since retiring, Pieters has worked as a football analyst for RTBF and Sport Vlaanderen. He also played for Belgian side KFC Oostende in the Belgian Second Division during the 2006-07 season.
