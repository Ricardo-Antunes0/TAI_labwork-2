He is best known for directing the films "Seven Samurai" and "The Silence of the Lambs".  Toyoda was born in 1933 in Utsunomiya City, Tochigi Prefecture, Japan. After graduating from Waseda University in 1957, Toyoda started his movie career as an assistant director on the film "The Naked City". He directed his first feature film, "Seven Samurai", in 1954. The film was a critical and commercial success and established Toyoda as a leading filmmaker. Toyoda continued to directs critically acclaimed films such as "The Hidden Fortress", "Throne of Blood", and "The Silence of the Lambs". In 2008, he was awarded the Japan Academy Prize for Lifetime Achievement. He died on January 21, 2016 at the age of 88.
