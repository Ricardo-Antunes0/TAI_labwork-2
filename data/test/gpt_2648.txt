Pamela Susan Courson (December 22, 1946 â€“ July 8, 2009) was an American actress and voice artist.  Born in Fort Worth, Texas, Courson began her theatrical career in the late 1960s, appearing in regional theater before making her Broadway debut in the 1971 play "Lenny". She later appeared in the films "The Owl and the Pussycat" (1970), "Car Wash" (1976), "The Great Santini" (1979), "All That Jazz" (1979), "The Morning After" (1986), and "Mississippi Burning" (1988).  In 1993, Courson starred as Veronica Sawyer's mother in the CBS sitcom "Veronica's Closet". She reprised the role for a 1996 spin-off series, "Wonder Woman", and voiced characters on both series. Courson also appeared in guest roles on television programs such as "Law & Order", "Cheers", and "Murder, She Wrote".  Courson died of cancer at her home in Newport Coast, California, at the age of 70.
