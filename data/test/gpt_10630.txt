David Vadim was born in Ukraine and was a Russian film director, screenwriter, and producer. He directed over thirty films in a career spanning more than fifty years. His films have been noted for their artistry and often feature erotic or controversial content.  David Vadim was born in the Ukrainian town of Zhytomyr on October 16, 1925. He was the son of a furniture maker and began his film career as an assistant director on Soviet films in the early 1950s. He made his directorial debut with the 1965 film, Blowup, which was noted for its innovative photography and use of sound.  Vadim's subsequent films include Story of a Love Affair (1968), Barbarella (1968), The Night Porter (1974), Emanuelle and the Last Cannibals (1977), and Valley of the Dolls (1970). His 1971 film, The Last Waltz, was nominated for Best Picture at the Academy Awards and won Best Screenplay.  Vadim's later films include Chantal (1992), Crime and Punishment (1996), Saint-Tropez (1998), Europa Europa (2002), and My Week with Marilyn (2011). He died on October 10, 2016 at the age of ninety-four.
