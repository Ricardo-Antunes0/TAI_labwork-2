Jeffrey D. "J.D." Gordon is an American writer and journalist. He is the author of the novel The Devil's Chessboard: Allen Dulles, the CIA, and the Rise of America's Secret Government and the co-author of The Iranian Threat: The Real Story of Iran's Revolutionary Guards and Their Global Agenda.  Gordon was born in Boston, Massachusetts, in 1954. He attended Yale University, where he was a member of the Scroll and Key Society and the Delta Kappa Epsilon fraternity. He graduated in 1976 with a degree in political science.  Gordon began his career as an investigative reporter for The Wall Street Journal. In 1984, he moved to The Washington Post, where he served as a national security correspondent and bureau chief. He was also a deputy editor at The Post.  Gordon is the author of five books:   The Devil's Chessboard: Allen Dulles, the CIA, and the Rise of America's Secret Government  The Iranian Threat: The Real Story of Iran's Revolutionary Guards and Their Global Agenda  The Lone Ranger and Tonto Fistfight in Heaven: John Wayne, Western Movies, and the Mythology of the American West  The Outlaw Banker: A True Story of Corporate Greed, Political Corruption, and the Money Laundering that Destroyed One Financial Dynasty  The Big Stick: How Four Decades of U.S. Diplomacy Created Modern China
