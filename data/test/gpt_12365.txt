Colonel Harold Carmichael Wylly, CB (18 January 1868 – 26 September 1954) was an Australian soldier, politician and diplomat who served as the fifth Governor-General of Australia.  Born in Sydney, New South Wales, Wylly was the son of Colonel Carmichael Wylly, a distinguished soldier and administrator in the Australian Imperial Force, and his wife Marianne (née Carmichael). He was educated at The King's School, Parramatta and then at the University of Melbourne, where he graduated with a Bachelor of Arts in 1892.  Wylly joined the Victorian Military Forces in 1892 and was commissioned as a lieutenant in the Victorian Regiment of Infantry. In 1896 he was posted to the North Australian Colonies and served on the staff of the military commander there until 1898. He returned to Victoria and served on the staff of the Victorian Military District until 1902.   In 1902 Wylly was appointed military secretary to the Governor-General of Australia, Sir George Pearce. Wylly held this position for six years, during which time he played a major role in organising military campaigns against the Boer Republics during the Second Boer War. He was promoted to captain in 1907 and transferred to New South Wales Command in 1909. In 1911 Wylly became secretary to the Governor-General of New South Wales, where he remained until 1914.
