Edward Norman Baillieu  (born 31 July 1954) is a former Australian politician who served as the Premier of Victoria from 2008 to 2014, and Leader of the Liberal Party of Victoria from 2005 to 2014.  Born in Melbourne, Baillieu was educated at Wesley College, Melbourne and the University of Melbourne, where he graduated with a Bachelor of Arts and a Bachelor of Laws. He worked as a lawyer before entering politics.  Baillieu first became involved in politics as a volunteer for the Liberal Party in the early 1980s. He was elected to the Victorian Legislative Assembly in 1992 as the member for Northern Victoria. Baillieu served as Minister for Transport from 1998 to 2002 and Minister for Housing from 2002 to 2006.  In March 2006, Baillieu was elected Leader of the Liberal Party, defeating incumbent John Brumby. As leader, Baillieu led the Liberals to victory at the Victorian state election later that year, winning 51 seats out of 80 in the Legislative Assembly. As Premier, Baillieu presided over Victoria during the global financial crisis and subsequent Victorian recession. In November 2014, after ten years as Premier, Baillieu announced his resignation from politics.  Baillieu has been married to Lynne Baillieu since 1978; they have four children.
