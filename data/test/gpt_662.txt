A motion in limine is a formal request by a party to exclude evidence or argument from a trial or hearing. A motion in limine is often used to protect the rights of a party against undue prejudice. The main purpose of a motion in limine is to prevent the opposing party from using evidence that would be prejudicial to the party requesting the exclusion. For example, if a company wants to prevent its competitors from presenting testimony about its shady business practices, it might file a motion in limine requesting that such testimony be excluded from the trial.
