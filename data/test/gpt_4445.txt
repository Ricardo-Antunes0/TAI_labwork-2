Anthony George Charles (born 27 June 1900 in Birmingham, England - died 1999) was a British jazz clarinetist and composer.  Dixie Brown was born Anthony George Charles in Birmingham, England and started playing the clarinet at the age of six. He studied at the Royal Academy of Music in London, where he graduated with first class honours. He then studied with Jules Massenet in Paris and became a noted clarinetist and composer.  He played with many of the leading British bands of the 1920s and 1930s, including the Ted Heath Band, Benny Goodman's Orchestra, and the Glenn Miller Orchestra. He also toured America and played with bands including Benny Goodman's Orchestra, Artie Shaw's Orchestra, and Duke Ellington's Orchestra.  He retired from performing in the early 1960s, but continued to compose music until his death in 1999. His compositions include "The Entertainer" (1936), "Blues After Hours" (1940), "The Bossa Nova Waltz" (1949), and "Prelude to a Kiss" (1952).
