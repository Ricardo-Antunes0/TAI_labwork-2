The Cisrhenian Republic  was a client state of the Ottoman Empire. It was declared on April 15, 1828 and lasted until its downfall on October 10, 1829. The republic was proclaimed in an effort to quash a revolt by the Greek population of Ioannina against Ottoman rule.  The Cisrhenian Republic was established in 1828 after a revolt against Ottoman rule by the Greek population of Ioannina. The republic was proclaimed by Ali Pasha, the governor of Thessaly, in an effort to quash the revolt. The Cisrhenian Republic consisted of four provinces: Ioannina, Arta, Larissa, and Trikala. The capital of the republic was Ioannina.
