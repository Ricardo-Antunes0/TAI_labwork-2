Muhammad Mumith Ahmed (; born 18 January 1984), better known by his stage name Mumzy Stranger, is a Bangladeshi-born British rapper and songwriter. He is best known for his single "Mumzy Stranger" which topped the UK Singles Chart in 2016.  Muhammad Mumith Ahmed was born on 18 January 1984 in Dhaka, Bangladesh. Ahmed moved to England with his family when he was five years old. He started rapping at the age of 15 and released his first mixtape, "Bangladesh Boyz", in 2006.  In 2009, Ahmed signed to XL Recordings and released his debut album, "Expectations". The album reached number nine on the UK Albums Chart and spawned the singles "Trip", "Permanent", and "Hood". In 2013, he released his second album, "The Miseducation of Mumzy Stranger", which charted at number seven in the UK. The album's lead single, "Mumzy Stranger", topped the UK Singles Chart in 2016.  Ahmed has toured internationally supporting acts such as Drake, Skepta, and Stormzy. In 2018, he released the single "Rain".
