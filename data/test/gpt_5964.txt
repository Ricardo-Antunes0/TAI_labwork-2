Keren Pevzner (, nee Kira Gennadievna Konovalova; born 18 January 1982) is an Israeli actress and singer.  Pevzner was born in Tel Aviv to a Jewish family. Her father, Gennadiy Konovalov, is a Russian-born Israeli journalist and playwright, and her mother, Kira Gennadievna Konovalova, is an Israeli actress. She has two sisters, both of whom are also actresses: Liria Konovalova and Alona Konovalova.  Pevzner began her acting career at the age of nine, appearing in short films and commercials. She made her feature film debut in the 2001 drama "A Tale of Love and Darkness". She has since appeared in numerous Israeli films and television series, as well as international productions such as "The Mummy Returns" (2001), "The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy" (2005), "Pirates of Caribbean: Dead Man's Chest" (2006), "Star Trek" (2009), "The Internship" (2013), and "Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 2" (2017). In 2009, she won the award for Best New Actress at the Jerusalem Film Festival for her role in the film "The Mummy Returns".  In 2018, Pevzner starred in the Netflix series "The Crown". The show was praised by critics for its depiction of Queen Elizabeth II's early years. Pevzner was also nominated for a Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead
