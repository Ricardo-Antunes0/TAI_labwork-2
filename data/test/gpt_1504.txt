Rory Stuart (born January 9, 1956) is a Canadian-born American actor, voice actor, and comedian. He is best known for his roles as BoJack Horseman on the Netflix series BoJack Horseman and its predecessor, BoJack Horseman: A Christmas Special.  Stuart was born in Toronto, Canada, to American parents. He has an older sister and a younger brother. After attending the University of British Columbia, Stuart moved to Chicago to pursue a career in acting. He began his career performing in sketch comedy shows and improvisational comedy troupes before landing his first major role as David Spade's sidekick in the 1996 HBO sitcom The Larry Sanders Show. Stuart also appeared in episodes of Arrested Development, Curb Your Enthusiasm, and The Office.  In 2010, Stuart guest-starred as himself on the final episode of Arrested Development. The following year, he reprised his role as David Spade's sidekick in the film adaptation of The Larry Sanders Show. In 2012, Stuart starred opposite Will Ferrell in the romantic comedy Old School. The same year, he also appeared in the independent comedy film Hit & Run alongside Kevin Smith and Jason Bateman.  In 2013, Stuart joined the cast of the Netflix series BoJack Horseman as BoJack Horseman's father, Mr. Peanutbutter.
