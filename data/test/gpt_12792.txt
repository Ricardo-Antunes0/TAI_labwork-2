Hamdi Quran is a member of the Quranic family, considered one of the most important books of the Islamic tradition.  The Hamdi Quran is named for Hamdi ibn al-Zubayr, who inherited it in 702 CE from his father, al-Zubayr ibn al-Awwam. It is the only known copy of the Quran that is illustrated with paintings and calligraphy.  The book is a collection of hadiths, which are reports of the Prophet Muhammad's sayings and deeds. It is also a commentary on the Quran, and contains explanations of its verses.
