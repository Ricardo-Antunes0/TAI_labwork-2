Hail Mary  is a 1985 French-language drama film written and directed by Jean-Pierre Melville. The film stars Isabelle Huppert, Rene Auberjonois, and Jean-Pierre Léaud.  The film tells the story of a woman, Mary, who is in a coma and is in a near-death state. She has a son, Joseph, who is visiting her. A priest, Father Quignon (Léaud), tries to comfort Joseph and tell him what to do when Mary dies.   As Mary's condition worsens, Joseph starts to doubt whether she will die. He starts to pray to the Virgin Mary for help. The Virgin Mary hears his prayers and helps Mary to recover.   The film ends with Joseph and the priest going back home to tell his mother that she is awake and well.
