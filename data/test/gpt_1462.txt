Can Dündar (, born 16 June 1961) is a Turkish journalist, who has worked for the Cumhuriyet daily since 1985. He was jailed from 10 February 2015 to 7 May 2016 on terrorism charges.  Dündar is noted for his reporting on the Gulen movement, which he has been investigating since 2013. He was also convicted of espionage in relation to this investigation in December 2018 and sentenced to five years and eight months imprisonment.   Dündar was born in 1961 in Istanbul, Turkey. He graduated with a degree in political science from Istanbul University, and began working as a reporter for the Cumhuriyet daily in 1985. He became known for his investigations into the Turkish government's relationship with the Islamic preacher Fethullah Gülen, which led to his imprisonment from 10 February 2015 to 7 May 2016 on terrorism charges. Dündar was convicted of espionage related to this investigation in December 2018 and sentenced to 5 years and 8 months imprisonment.
