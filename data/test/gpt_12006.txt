Born in Seoul into an aristocratic family, Cho Byung-Hwa studied at the Seoul National University of Arts from 1939 to 1942, under the guidance of Yoon Sang-Woo. After leaving university, he worked as a composer for the Korean Broadcasting System and later the Korea National Broadcasting Corporation. In 1966, he became professor at Sogang University and served in that position until his retirement in 1990.  Cho's works encompass a wide range of styles, including traditional Korean court music, orchestral and chamber works, choral and electronic music. He has been awarded several prestigious awards, including the Grand Prize of the Berlin Music Festival in 1967, the Koussei Prize in 1985 and the Order of Cultural Merit in 1990.
