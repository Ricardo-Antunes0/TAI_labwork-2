Troy Trevor Tulowitzki (born October 10, 1984), nicknamed "Troy Boom" is an American professional baseball shortstop for the Colorado Rockies of Major League Baseball (MLB). He has also played for the Toronto Blue Jays, Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim, and San Diego Padres. Tulowitzki has won three MVP Awards, two Silver Slugger Awards, and two Gold Gloves. He led all American League shortstops in assists in 2016.  Troy Tulowitzki was born in Boulder, Colorado, to Troy and Brenda Tulowitzki. His father played minor league baseball for the Tampa Bay Devil Rays and Milwaukee Brewers, and his mother was a competitive swimmer. Tulowitzki has two older sisters, Tiffany and Tessa. As a child, Tulowitzki was a fan of the Toronto Blue Jays, and he attended a few games at Exhibition Stadium as a youth.  Tulowitzki attended Regis Jesuit High School in Aurora, Colorado. He was not heavily recruited out of high school but chose to attend Regis Jesuit because he felt it would be a good fit for him academically; he also felt the coaching staff had a good reputation. Tulowitzki played college baseball at the University of Florida, where he was a standout player for the Florida Gators baseball team. As a freshman in 2002, he was named to the All-SEC Freshman Team.
