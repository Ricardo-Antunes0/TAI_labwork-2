Elsa Andrea Elisabeth Björkman-Goldschmidt (1888–1982) was a Swedish writer, playwright and screenwriter, and one of the most important figures in the development of the Swedish film industry.  Born in Stockholm, Björkman-Goldschmidt was educated at Stockholm University and worked as a teacher before turning to writing. Her first novel, The Adventures of Auguste Dupin (1911), was published to great acclaim and she went on to write plays, short stories and novels. Her best known work is the novel Anna Karenina (1927), which was made into a celebrated film by Soviet director Sergei Eisenstein in 1937.  After the war, Björkman-Goldschmidt moved to France and then to England, where she died in 1982. She was awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1955.
