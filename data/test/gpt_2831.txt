Richard Edmond Bennett (born 1946) is a professor of sociology at the University of California, Irvine. He studies the sociology of race and Ethnicity, and has written extensively on the topic.  Bennett was born in 1946 in Richmond, California. He received his B.A. from UCLA in 1968 and his M.A. from UC Riverside in 1971. He then received his Ph.D. from UC Irvine in 1976.  Bennett has been a professor of sociology at the University of California, Irvine since 1976. He has also been a visiting professor at Stanford University (1996), the University of Paris (1997), and Columbia University (1998).  Bennett's research focuses on the sociology of race and Ethnicity. He has written extensively on the topic, including books such as White America's Conscience: The Unspoken Truth About Racial Discrimination (1998), co-authored with Randall Robinson, and The New American Dilemma: Immigration and Race in the Twenty-First Century (2003), co-authored with George J. Annas.  Bennett is also a member of the American Sociological Association, the Sociology Department at UC Irvine, and the American Academy of Political and Social Science.
