The discography of Mel B, an English singer and songwriter, consists of six studio albums, one compilation album, twenty-three singles, and ten promotional singles.  Mel B was born in Hammersmith, London, to a Jamaican father and an English mother. She began her music career in the late 1990s as a backing singer for English boy band The Beatmasters. She later released her first solo single, "I'm Not a Girl, Not Yet a Woman", in 2001. Her debut album, "No More Tears (Enough Is Enough)", was released in 2003 and achieved commercial success. In 2006, she starred in the reality television show "The X Factor", which helped to launch the careers of contestants including Joe McElderry and Little Mix. She later guest starred on the British show "Top Boy" and released her second album, "Exile", in 2007. In 2009, she released her third album, "Point of No Return", which became her first album to be certified platinum in the United Kingdom. Her fourth album, "Stripped", was released in 2011 and reached number one on the UK Albums Chart. In 2013, she released her fifth album, "Brave". The album received widespread critical acclaim and won B two Grammy Awards: Best Pop Solo Performance for the song "Till I Collapse" and Best Music Video for the song "We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together".
