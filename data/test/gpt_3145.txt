The National Anticorruption Directorate , formerly National Anti-Corruption Bureau (NAB) is a Philippine government agency that investigates and prosecutes corruption in the Philippines. The agency was created on January 30, 2006, through Republic Act No. 9485, also known as the Anticorruption Law of 2006.  The National Anticorruption Directorate is a national agency under the Department of Justice that investigates and prosecutes corruption in the Philippines. It was created on January 30, 2006, through Republic Act No. 9485, better known as the Anticorruption Law of 2006. The National Anticorruption Directorate is charged with conducting investigations into allegations of corruption involving public officials and their associates, as well as recovering any ill-gotten wealth that has been acquired through corrupt practices.  The National Anticorruption Directorate is headed by a Director who is appointed by the President with the advice and consent of the Senate. The current Director is Roelito Andaya Jr.
