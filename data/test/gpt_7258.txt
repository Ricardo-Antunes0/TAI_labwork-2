Paul R. Frommer (; born September 17, 1940) is an American travel writer, journalist and author who specializes in European travel. He is the author of more than 30 guidebooks to Europe, Asia, the Middle East and Latin America.  Frommer was born in Boston, Massachusetts, the son of Elaine (n√©e Kohn) and Harry Frommer, a lawyer. He was educated at Tufts University, graduating in 1962 with a degree in political science. Frommer then served in the United States Army as a first lieutenant. After his discharge in 1966, he worked as a foreign correspondent for the "Boston Globe" before becoming a freelance writer in 1971.  In 1973 Frommer became the Europe correspondent for " Conde Nast Traveler ", a position he held until 1978. In 1978 he published his first guidebook, "The Best of Europe", which became a bestseller.  Frommer's work has won many awards, including two National Book Awards (for "The Best of Europe" and "Spain"). He was inducted into the International Travel Hall of Fame in 2001. In 2012 he was awarded an honorary doctorate by Tufts University.  Frommer currently resides on Long Island with his wife, Kathy Kohn-Frommer
