The Pharaoh's Daughter , is a ballet based on the story of Joseph and his beloved wife, Pharaoh's daughter, Rachele. The ballet was choreographed by Kenneth MacMillan and premiered at the Royal Opera House, London on 7 April 1973.   The ballet tells the story of Joseph and his beautiful but rebellious wife Rachele. When famine threatens their country, Pharaoh makes a fateful decision to allow Joseph to bring food from Egypt's vast storehouses to save his people. Along the way, Joseph must contend with Rachele's anger and jealousy over his preferential treatment from her father. Ultimately, the two are forced to come to terms with their love for one another and fight for their survival together.
