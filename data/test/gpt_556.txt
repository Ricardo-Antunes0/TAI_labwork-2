Mihael Mišo Montiljo (16 May 1928 – 2 December 2001) was a Slovenian writer, philosopher, and essayist.  Montiljo was born in Ljubljana in 1928. After finishing grammar school in 1947, he studied philosophy and literature at the University of Ljubljana, where he graduated in 1951. He then worked as a high school teacher in Ljubljana and started writing professionally in 1957.  Montiljo's first novel, "Kmetija na Dolgu" (The Farm on the Hill), was published in 1962. His most famous work is the novel "Zgodbe o Pivku" (Tales of Peter), which was published in 1969 and won the Prešeren Award. The book tells the story of a group of friends from pre-war Slovenia who are forced to flee their home country during World War II and end up living in exile in Italy.  Montiljo also wrote essays, short stories, and poetry. He died in Ljubljana in 2001.
