Andy Michner (born October 27, 1968, Ann Arbor, Michigan) is an American musician, singer and songwriter. He is the frontman and principal songwriter of the rock band Switchfoot, who has released six studio albums. Michner also plays guitar, keyboards and drums in the band. In 1990, he met Jon Foreman and Chad Butler while they were both attending college in San Diego. The three of them started playing together in a band called Daylight.  In 1992, Michner moved to Sacramento, California to join Foreman and Butler in their newly formed band Christian Alternative. They released two EPs before signing to a major label and releasing their debut album, "The Beautiful Letdown", in 1995. The album peaked at number 39 on the "Billboard" 200 chart and received mixed reviews from critics. In 1996, Michner left Christian Alternative to focus on Switchfoot full-time.  Switchfoot's second album, "The Beautiful Letdown", was released in 1996 and spawned the singles "Meant to Live" and "Dare You to Move". The album peaked at number 39 on the "Billboard" 200 chart and received mixed reviews from critics. In 2002, Switchfoot released their third album, "The Beautiful Letdown II".
