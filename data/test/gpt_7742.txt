Keegan Connor Tracy (born December 3, 1971) is an American actress and model. She began her acting career in the early 1990s, appearing in television series such as Northern Exposure, Walker, Texas Ranger and The West Wing. In 2002, she won a Golden Globe Award for her performance in the film Crash. Tracy has since appeared in films such as National Treasure, 10 Things I Hate About You, and The Internship. She voiced the title character of the animated film Barbie Dreamhouse Adventures.  Born in Los Angeles, California, Keegan Connor Tracy is the daughter of actor Tracy Letts and actress Jane Fonda. Tracy has two sisters, Daphne and Vanessa. Her father is of Welsh and Irish descent and her mother is of Japanese descent. Tracy grew up in Sherman Oaks, California, attending Notre Dame High School. She began her modeling career at the age of 16 and later appeared in campaigns for Calvin Klein, Lululemon Athletica, Nike and cover models for magazines such as Elle and Cosmopolitan.  In 2002, Tracy won a Golden Globe Award for her performance in the film Crash. She also received critical acclaim for her roles in 10 Things I Hate About You (1999), National Treasure (2000), New York Minute (2001) and Mean Girls (2004). In 2007, she starred alongside Tim Robbins in the drama The Darjeeling Limited. In 2009, she starred in the comedy-drama Love & Other Drugs opposite Robert De Niro.
