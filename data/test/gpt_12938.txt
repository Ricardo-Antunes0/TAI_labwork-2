Blinky the Clown (June 30, 1921 – February 21, 2016), better known by his stage name Russell Scott, was an American comedian, actor, and voice actor.  Scott was born in West Helena, Arkansas, the son of Lula (née Hill) and Samuel G. Scott. His father was a sharecropper and later worked as a mechanic and chauffeur. As a child, Scott attended segregated schools in Arkansas before moving with his family to San Diego, California in the 1940s. After high school, Scott enlisted in the United States Navy and served during World War II. He was discharged in 1946 and began his performing career in nightclubs and theaters in San Diego.  In 1951, Scott moved to Los Angeles and began appearing on television shows. He achieved national prominence as the host of the variety show "The Russell Scott Show" from 1965 to 1971. He also appeared in several films, including "The Munsters" (1964), "The Odd Couple" (1968), "The Love Boat" (1977), and "Airplane!" (1980). In 2006, he published his memoir, "My Life As an Impersonator: From Stage to Screen".  Scott died of natural causes at his home in Los Angeles at the age of 94.
