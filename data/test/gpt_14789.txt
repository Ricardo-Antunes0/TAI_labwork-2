Paul Benedict Sunderland (born March 29, 1952) is a Canadian-American actor, voice actor, and comedian. He is known for his roles as Geoffrey Chaucer in the television series The Canterbury Tales (1986–1999), as Dr. Mark Sloan on the NBC medical drama series ER (1994–2009), and as Tim "The Tool Man" Taylor on the ABC sitcom Home Improvement (1991–1999).  Sunderland was born in Toronto, Ontario, Canada, the son of Janice (née Hart) and Benedict Sunderland, an advertising executive. He has English and Scottish ancestry. After attending Upper Canada College and the National Theatre School of Canada, he began his acting career in the late 1970s with small roles in television shows such as The Littlest Hobo, The Streets of San Francisco and Battlestar Galactica.  In 1986, he landed the role of Geoffrey Chaucer on the television series The Canterbury Tales. For his portrayal of Chaucer, Sunderland won three Emmy Awards (including Outstanding Lead Actor in a Drama Series), a Golden Globe Award and a Screen Actors Guild Award. He reprised his role as Chaucer on the spin-off series Canterbury Tales: The Legend of Robin Hood (1998) and Canterbury Tales: Seasons 1 & 2 (2005). In 1994, he starred as Dr. Mark Sloan on the NBC medical drama series ER.
