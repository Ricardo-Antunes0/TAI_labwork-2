Keikavus  was the ruler of the province of Kei in the early Viking Age.   Keikavus was a powerful Norseman who ruled over the province of Kei in the early Viking Age. He is known from a few sources, including a law code from the 980s that bears his name, and a few inscriptions. He is thought to have been a son of Ragnvaldr Loðbrók and a brother of Óláfr Tryggvason. Keikavus is thought to have died in 986, after reigning for around twenty years.
