The 335th Air Refueling Wing is an inactive United States Air Force unit. Its last assignment was with the 341st Bombardment Wing, stationed at Castle Air Force Base, California.  The wing was first activated in 1943 as the 335th Bombardment Group at Gowen Field, Idaho. The group served in combat in the European Theater of Operations and earned a Distinguished Unit Citation for its actions in support of Operation Overlord. After the war, the group served in the occupation force in Germany and inactivated in 1946.  The group was reactivated as the 335th Air Refueling Wing at Castle Air Force Base, California, in 1952. The wing provided air refueling support to United States Air Forces in Europe during the Cold War. In 1991, the wing was inactivated due to budget reductions.  The 335th Air Refueling Wing was reactivated at Castle Air Force Base, California on 1 October 1952 as part of Strategic Air Command's deterrent force. The wing operated Boeing KC-97 Stratotankers and Boeing VC-137A ValuJetliners providing air refueling support to United States Air Forces in Europe (USAFE) during the Cold War. In 1991, with the dissolution of SAC and reduction of USAF Europe's tanker fleet, the wing was inactivated due to budget reductions.
