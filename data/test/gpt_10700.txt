Epidendrum calanthum is a terrestrial reed-stemmed Epidendrum from the Orchid family. It is endemic to the Mount Lofty Ranges of South Australia.  This species has a single erect stem reaching up to 30 cm in height and is covered in soft, woolly hairs. The stem is greenish-brown with a purple tinge and has white spots on the midrib and petals. The flowers are white, pink or purple and are up to 10 cm across. They are borne on a long stalk that curves downwards.  This species grows in wet areas near streams or waterfalls and is found in the eastern part of the Mount Lofty Ranges. It is threatened by habitat loss due to logging and development.
