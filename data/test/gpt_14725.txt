Robert Patrick Lalonde (born March 27, 1951) is a Canadian former professional ice hockey player who played 18 seasons in the National Hockey League (NHL) for the Montreal Canadiens, Philadelphia Flyers, and Vancouver Canucks. He was drafted by the Canadiens in the first round, fourth overall, in the 1970 NHL Amateur Draft.  Lalonde is considered one of the best offensive forwards in Canadiens history and is recognized as one of the most entertaining players to watch on ice. His career high in points was 97 set during the 1977–78 season. He also scored a record 102 goals in a season with the Flyers in 1980–81. He was inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame in 1998.
