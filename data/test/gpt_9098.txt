Amberley (Māori: Kōwai) is a town located in the Waikato Region of New Zealand's North Island. At the 2006 census, Amberley had a population of 7,552. The town is located near the centre of the Waikato Plains, about halfway between Hamilton and Tauranga.  Amberley is a rural town with a small commercial centre. It is also home to a regional university, Waikato University. The town has a number of primary and secondary schools, as well as a hospital and several shops.  Amberley is known for its natural environment, including its lakes and forests. The town is also popular for its golf courses and horse riding.
