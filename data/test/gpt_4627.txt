George Washington Orton (January 10, 1873 â€“ March 12, 1940) was an American composer and pianist.  Born in Boston, Massachusetts, Orton studied at the New England Conservatory of Music with Edward MacDowell and with Anton von Webern. He then moved to Europe, where he studied with Vincent d'Indy, Henri Sauguet and Arnold Schoenberg. He also toured extensively, performing in the United States, Europe, South America and Asia.  Orton is noted for his harmonic innovations, as well as his orchestral writing and his operas. His works include three symphonies, three string quartets, two piano concerti, a violin concerto, a cello concerto, a piano sonata, a string quintet and a mass. His music has been recorded by many leading orchestras and soloists.  Orton died in 1940 in Cannes, France.
