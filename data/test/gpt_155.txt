Rose Valley is a small, historic borough located in the northwest corner of Lancaster County, Pennsylvania, United States. The population was 1,462 at the 2000 census.  Rose Valley is located at (40.858734, -76.560973).  According to the United States Census Bureau, the borough has a total area of , all of it land.  As of the census of 2000, there were 1,462 people, 593 households, and 384 families residing in the borough. The population density was 2,274.4 people per square mile (899.1/km²). There were 621 housing units at an average density of 892.8 per square mile (341.5/km²). The racial makeup of the borough was 99.27% White, 0.07% African American, 0.07% Native American, 0.17% Asian, 0.07% from other races, and 0.53% from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino of any race were 0.53% of the population.  There were 593 households out of which 38.2% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 61.3% were married couples living together, 9.7% had a female householder with no husband present, and 25.6% were non-families. 23.9% of all households were made up of individuals and 11.2% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older
