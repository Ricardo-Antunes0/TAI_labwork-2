Air Vice Marshal John Oliver Andrews,  born in Melbourne, Australia on 4 September 1928,  was a senior Australian air officer who served in the Royal Australian Air Force (RAAF) for over thirty years.   Andrews began his military career in the RAAF as a pilot in 1948, and during his time in the military he held a number of senior positions, including Commander of No. 6 Operational Command and Air Vice Marshal. He retired from the RAAF in 1983. After retirement from the military, Andrews continued to serve as a board member for several companies and organizations, and he also served as president of the RAAF Association. He died on 1 December 2014.
