Smith Falls, at 63 feet (19 m), is the tallest waterfall in the province of Ontario, Canada. It is located in Smith Falls Provincial Park, approximately 16 kilometres (10 mi) southeast of Peterborough.  Smith Falls is composed of two waterfalls, Upper and Lower Smith Falls. The Upper Smith Falls drops 84 feet (26 m) into a narrow gorge and has a drop of 50 feet (15 m). The Lower Smith Falls is composed of three smaller waterfalls and has a total drop of just 23 feet (7 m).      The falls were first discovered by European settlers in 1793. The name "Smith Falls" is derived from one of the first European families to reside in the area: the Smiths.
