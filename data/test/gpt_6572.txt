Kjeld Kirk Kristiansen (; born 27 December 1955) is a Danish business executive, philanthropist and investor. He is the founder, CEO and chairman of the investment company Danske Invest and a member of the board of directors at companies such as Carlsberg, Danfoss and A.P. Moller-Maersk.  Kristiansen was born in 1955 in Taastrup, Denmark. He studied business administration at the University of Copenhagen, graduating with a degree in 1978.  In 1978, Kristiansen founded his own investment company, Danske Invest. Moller-Maersk from 2006 to 2013. He has been awarded an Honorary Doctorate from Heriot-Watt University in 2006 and an Honorary Doctorate from Roskilde University in 2016.  Kristiansen is also a philanthropist, having donated more than DKK 1 billion (US$157 million) to various causes throughout his career. These include funding research into spinal cord injuries and dementia, supporting arts initiatives and supporting young entrepreneurs.
