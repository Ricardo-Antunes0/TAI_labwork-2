Jacob Townsend (born 20 June 1993) is a professional footballer who plays as a midfielder for Championship club Birmingham City.  Born in Wolverhampton, Townsend joined Birmingham City's academy at the age of 10. He made his first-team debut in a 2–1 defeat to Bristol City in the League Cup on 23 October 2011, and made a further two appearances during the 2011–12 season. He made his Premier League debut on 21 November 2012, coming on as a substitute for Nikola Žigić in a 3–1 victory over Newcastle United. He made his first start in a 2–1 win over Fulham on 2 December 2012. Townsend was loaned to League One club Rotherham United for the 2013–14 season, where he made 21 appearances.  Townsend made his return to the Birmingham first team in the 2014–15 season, making 15 appearances. He was an unused substitute for England in their 5–1 victory over Sweden at the UEFA Euro 2016 qualifiers on 8 October 2015. Townsend made 33 appearances in the 2015–16 season, helping Birmingham to promotion to the Premier League. He started all 38 of Birmingham's matches in their first season back in the top flight, scoring three goals, including two goals in a 5–2 win over Everton on 17 January 2017. Townsend signed a new contract with Birmingham City in May 2017 which will keep him at the club until 2021.  Townsend has represented England at under-16, under-17 and under-18 level.
