Marjolein Tambayong, better known by her stage name Rima Melati, is a Malaysian singer and actress. She is known for her work in the Malaysian film industry, most notably for her roles in the films The Promise and A Reason to Live.  Rima was born in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia on July 24, 1985 to a family of artists. She started her musical career as a child, singing in church and in local talent shows. After completing her secondary education at Sekolah Menengah Kebangsaan Bukit Jalil, she studied at the Drama Centre London, graduating with a Bachelor of Arts in 2005. Upon returning to Malaysia, she began working in the entertainment industry as an actress and singer.  After making her acting debut with a minor role in the 2004 film The Promise, Rima appeared in several Malaysian films throughout the next few years, notably playing supporting roles in A Reason to Live (2006), The Game Plan (2007) and One Fine Day (2009). Her breakthrough performance came in 2010 with the role of Aishah in the film adaptation of Mohd Zainal Abidin's novel The Promise, for which she received critical acclaim and was nominated for Best Actress at the Malaysian Film Awards. Rima followed up this success with roles in several other films throughout the next few years, including playing lead roles in A Better Tomorrow II (2012), The Drop (2014) and The Turning Point (2015).
