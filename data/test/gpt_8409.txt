Károly Kerkapoly or Kerkápoly (13 May 1824, Károlyháza – 14 July 1885, Budapest) was a Hungarian painter.  Károly Kerkapoly was born on 13 May 1824 in Károlyháza, then part of the Ottoman Empire. In 1848 he entered the Academy of Fine Arts in Vienna, where he studied under Heinrich von Angeli and Joseph Anton Koch. In 1859 he moved to Budapest, where he became a member of the Academy of Fine Arts. He died on 14 July 1885 in Budapest.  Károly Kerkapoly is best known for his paintings of peasant life in Hungary and Transylvania. He also painted portraits and historical scenes.
