Lucy Kérimée Gutteridge (born 28 November 1956) is an English author and journalist.  Born in London, Gutteridge attended the University of Oxford, where she was a Rhodes Scholar. She later worked as a journalist for the "Sunday Times" and "The Observer". Gutteridge has written nine novels, including the Booker Prize-winning novel "Life of Pi" (2011). She has also written non-fiction books about architecture and travel. Gutteridge currently lives in London.  Gutteridge's debut novel, "The Physick Book of Deliverance Dane", was published in 1999. It won the Somerset Maugham Award and the Orange Prize for Fiction. Her second novel, "The Sense of an Ending", was published in 2007 and won the Guardian Fiction Prize and the James Tait Black Memorial Prize. Her third novel, "Life of Pi", was published in 2011 and won the Man Booker Prize, the National Book Critics Circle Award, and the Pulitzer Prize.
