The Manchester Yankees was the name of a professional baseball team that played in the International League from 1978 to 1984.  The team was originally known as the New York Yankees of the International League. The Yankees moved to Manchester, New Hampshire, in 1978 and became the Manchester Yankees. The team played at the new Yankee Stadium in Manchester, which opened in April 1978. The Yankees finished first in the league in 1979 and 1980 and second in 1981 and 1982. In 1983, they finished fourth and were eliminated from the playoffs. The team moved to Hartford, Connecticut, in 1984 and became the Hartford Colonials.
