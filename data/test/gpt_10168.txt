Universalism was a Russian anarcho-communist organization that operated from the early 1910s to 1926. The group was founded in Kiev by Yevgeny Zamyatin and Ivan Klimentov, and initially consisted of both Russians and Jews. Universalism espoused a philosophy of individual liberty and pacifism, and opposed all forms of hierarchy and government. The organization was banned in 1926, and most of its members were arrested or killed during the Stalinist Purges.
