He was the founder and president of the ABC television network from its inception in 1948 until his retirement in 1981.  Born in New York City, Goldenson was the son of a tailor. He began his career in radio, working for CBS before founding ABC. He was responsible for popularizing the use of color television on American television and helped to make ABC the dominant network in the US. He also served as the chairman of the Board of Directors of General Motors from 1966 to 1981. Goldenson was awarded an honorary degree from Bard College in 1978. He died at age 94 in Palm Beach, Florida.
