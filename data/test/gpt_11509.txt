Brian Theodore Tyler (born May 8, 1972) is an American singer and songwriter. He is the frontman and primary songwriter for the rock band Bon Jovi. Tyler has released twelve studio albums with Bon Jovi, including the album Keep the Faith (2001), which was certified Diamond by the RIAA. Two of Tyler's greatest hits are "Livin' on a Prayer" and "You Give Love a Bad Name".  Born in Sayreville, New Jersey, Tyler began his music career as the lead vocalist of the hard rock band Bon Jovi. The band achieved worldwide success with their first two albums, Slippery When Wet (1986) and New Jersey (1988). In 2001, Bon Jovi released their album Keep the Faith, which was certified Diamond by the RIAA. Tyler is also known for his collaborations with other artists, most notably "I'll Sleep When I'm Dead" with Slash from Guns N' Roses, "Lay Your Hands on Me" with David Bryan from Bon Jovi, and "Who Says You Can't Go Home" with Cher from the movie Wild Wild West.
