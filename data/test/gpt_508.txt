The 1981 Intercontinental Cup was an association football match contested between Spain's Real Madrid and Brazil's Santos. The match was played at the Santiago Bernabéu Stadium in Madrid, Spain on 28 May 1981. The winner of the match went on to win the Intercontinental Cup, which was Real Madrid's third title.  The 1981 Intercontinental Cup was a match that pitted two of the most successful clubs in world football against each other. Real Madrid had won the European Cup in 1974 and 1976, as well as the Spanish Cup in 1977, while Santos had won the Intercontinental Cup in 1970 and the Brazilian Championship in 1978. The two sides met at the Santiago Bernabéu Stadium in Madrid, Spain on 28 May 1981 with Santos leading 1–0 after a goal by Jairzinho. However, Real Madrid would come back to win the match 2–1 thanks to goals from Fernando Hierro and Amancio. This result gave Real Madrid their third title, with Santos claiming their second title.
