Linda Medlar was born in 1949 in Dallas, Texas. She began dancing at the age of three, and by the time she was eleven she had joined the Dallas Ballet. She was a principal dancer with the American Ballet Theatre from 1979 to 1997. Medlar has been called "one of the great ballerinas of our time."  Medlar has received numerous awards, including a Laurence Olivier Award for Outstanding Achievement in Dance, a Tony Award for Best Dance Performance, a George Abbott Award, and an Emmy Award. She has also received honorary degrees from Yale University and Carnegie Mellon University.  Medlar retired from the American Ballet Theatre in 1997. Since then she has continued to dance with various companies, most notably with The Vienna State Opera Ballet and the Royal Danish Ballet. In 2006 she created her own company, Linda Medlar Dance Company, which tours worldwide.
