Gulf Publishing Company is an international publishing company. It was founded in 1974 by Pervaiz Iqbal, a Pakistani-born American businessman.  The company publishes books in English and Arabic, and has a library of more than 250,000 titles. It has offices in Washington, D.C., London, Cairo, Riyadh, and Karachi.  Gulf Publishing Company has published a range of books on subjects such as politics, history, business, Islam, and culture. Some of the company's best-known books include The Gulf War: A International Report (with David Haines), The Taliban: Militant Islam, Oil and Fundamentalism in Central Asia (with Ahmed Rashid), and The Arab Awakening: Unfinished Revolution (with Michael Eisenstadt).
