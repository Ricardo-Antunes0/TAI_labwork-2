George Pérez (; born June 9, 1954) is an American comics writer and artist.  Pérez was born in the Bronx, New York City, to Puerto Rican parents. He attended the School of Visual Arts and the Kubert School of Cartoon and Graphic Art. Pérez has worked as a freelance artist since the early 1980s, and is known for his work on titles such as "Daredevil", "Wonder Woman", "Spider-Man", "Thor", "Iron Man", "Black Panther" and "Avengers".  He has won two Eisner Awards, for Best Writer/Artist for his work on "Daredevil" in 1993 and 1995, and for Best Penciller/Inker for his work on "Spider-Man" in 1994. He has also been nominated for an Emmy Award, a Nebula Award, a Harvey Award and a Diamond Gem Award.
