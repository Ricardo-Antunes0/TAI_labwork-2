Sir Gilbert Denys (c. 1350â€“1422) of Siston, Somerset, was an English lawyer and politician.  Gilbert Denys was born in about 1350, the eldest son of Sir John Denys of Siston, Somerset, by his wife Joan de Beauchamp. He was educated at the University of Oxford, where he took the degrees of B.A. and M.A. He became a barrister in 1378 and served as Recorder of Taunton from 1388 to 1406.  In 1406 Denys was elected Member of Parliament for Somerset, and served until his death in 1422. Denys served as a Justice of the Peace for Somerset from 1411 to 1412 and for Dorset from 1412 to 1422. He was also Lord Warden of the Cinque Ports from 1413 to 1422.  Denys died on 25 October 1422, aged about seventy-four, at Siston Castle. He was buried at All Saints' Church, Bridgwater.
