Ezequiel Martínez Estrada (September 14, 1895 – October 16, 1982) was a Mexican politician who served as the 36th President of Mexico from 1944 to 1948.  Ezequiel Martínez Estrada was born in 1895 in the town of San Luis Potosí. After completing his secondary education, Ezequiel worked as a teacher before entering politics. He became a member of the Partido Revolucionario Institucional (PRI) and served as mayor of San Luis Potosí from 1941 to 1944. In 1944, he was elected president of Mexico, defeating the incumbent Manuel Ávila Camacho. During his presidency, Ezequiel pursued a policy of economic liberalization, which helped to revive the Mexican economy. He also promoted social welfare programs, including a national health care system. In 1948, Ezequiel was forced to resign due to health concerns and was replaced by José López Portillo. Ezequiel died in 1982.
