Saint Malchus of Syria (or Malchus of Melitene) (died 347) was a Christian martyr, who is venerated as a saint by the Catholic and Orthodox Churches.  Saint Malchus was born in the town of Melitene in the kingdom of Palaestina Prima, present-day Syria. He was an officer in the Roman army and fought against the Christians. After his conversion to Christianity, he refused to abandon his faith and was beheaded on charges of being a traitor to the state.  The Syriac Orthodox Church celebrates his feast day on July 22. The Catholic Church celebrates him on July 25.
