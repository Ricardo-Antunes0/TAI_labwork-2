Raymond George Riles (born June 1, 1950) is an American prelate of the Catholic Church. He has been the Bishop of Dallas since 2003.  Riles was born in Mobile, Alabama, to George and Dorothy (n√©e Riles) Riles. He received his early education at St. Paul's School in Mobile and then attended the University of South Alabama, from which he received a Bachelor of Arts degree in 1972. He then attended the Pontifical Lateran University in Rome, where he obtained a licentiate in sacred theology in 1975.  Riles was ordained to the priesthood on May 8, 1976, by Bishop John F. O'Connor of Mobile. After a year of pastoral work in Mobile, he served as professor of systematic theology at the Catholic Theological Union in Chicago from 1977 to 1979. He then served as pastor of Saint Alphonsus Parish in Saint Louis from 1979 to 1982, and of Saint Bernard Parish in Kansas City from 1982 to 1985.  Riles was named Auxiliary Bishop of Saint Louis on August 29, 1985. He was consecrated on November 20 of that year by Cardinal Joseph Bernardin. As an auxiliary bishop, Riles served as vicar general for the Archdiocese of Saint Louis and chancellor for Catholic schools within the archdiocese. In this role he was responsible for the administration of Catholic Charities and the St. Louis University School of Medicine and Health Sciences.
