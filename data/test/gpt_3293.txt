Guy of Thouars (died 13 April 1213) was a medieval French nobleman and military leader.   Guy of Thouars was the son of Aymer de Thouars, Lord of Villeneuve-l√®s-Avignon, and Jeanne d'Auvergne. He succeeded his father in 1201. He fought in the Albigensian Crusade and the Eighth Crusade. In 1205 he was captured by the English and ransomed the next year. In 1208, he participated in the Battle of Bouvines, where he was captured by the English again. He was released in 1209 and resumed his crusade. In 1211, he participated in the Siege of Jerusalem, where he was killed during the assault on the Saracen fortress of Ascalon.
