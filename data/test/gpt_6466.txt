He served as Secretary of State for War from 1969 to 1970, and as Secretary of State for Scotland from 1970 to 1974.  McNaughton was born in 1916 in Glasgow, Scotland. He was educated at Glasgow Academy and the Royal Military College, Sandhurst. He was commissioned into the Scots Guards in 1938. He fought in the Second World War, serving in North Africa and Italy. After the war he was appointed Commanding Officer of the 1st Battalion Scots Guards, which he led during the Malayan Emergency.  After retiring from the army in 1968, McNaughton became a civil servant. He served as Secretary of State for War from 1969 to 1970, and as Secretary of State for Scotland from 1970 to 1974. In 1974 he was made a Companion of the Order of Bath. McNaughton died in 2002 aged 88.
