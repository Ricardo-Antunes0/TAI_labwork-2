Dacca or Dhaka is the capital and largest city of Bangladesh. It is the southernmost city of the country, located on the Meghna River. The city has a population of about 15 million people, making it the eighth most populous city in the world. Dacca was founded in 1605 by Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb as a provincial headquarters. It became the largest city in Bengal after Calcutta and then was made the capital of East Pakistan during its independence from British rule in 1947. After Partition, Dacca became capital of the newly created nation of Bangladesh.  Dacca is a centre for traditional handicrafts, such as shawlmaking, brocade-making and quilting. It has significant furniture and textile industries and is home to Bangladesh's national museum, which houses a large collection of antiquities from both ancient and medieval periods. The city also has significant educational institutions, including universities and colleges. It is also home to several important archaeological sites, including the ruins of the Sher Shah Suri's palace and the 16th-century Moghul architecture of Shah Jahan's Taj Mahal complex.
