John Henry Okullu (1929, Ramba Village, Asembo District, Uganda - October 21, 1966) was an Ugandan author, artist and poet.  Henry Okullu was born in Ramba Village in Asembo District in 1929. He was educated at Kyambogo Primary School and Ngora Teachers' Training College. He then studied at Makerere University where he obtained a BA degree in English Literature in 1953. He then travelled to England where he obtained an MA degree in English Literature from the University of Birmingham in 1957.  Henry Okullu began his literary career in 1956 with the publication of his first book, "The Leopard's Tail". This was followed by "The White Queen" (1957), "The Gilded Cage" (1959), "The Blue Lagoon" (1960), and "The Golden Cage" (1962). His works have been translated into many languages and he has been acclaimed as one of Uganda's leading authors. He died on October 21, 1966 after a long battle with leukemia.
