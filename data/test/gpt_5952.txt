Sara Thornton (born 1954/1955) is a British woman who was convicted of the manslaughter of her two young sons in 1995. She has consistently maintained her innocence and has campaigned for a retrial.  Thornton was born in 1954 or 1955 in the United Kingdom to Scottish parents. She married and had two sons in the early 1990s. In 1995, Thornton's husband died suddenly, leaving her struggling with financial and emotional problems. On 3 December 1995, Thornton killed her two sons by throwing them off a bridge into a river below. Thornton has consistently maintained her innocence and has campaigned for a retrial. In March 2016, it was announced that Thornton had been granted conditional release from prison after serving 18 years of her sentence.
