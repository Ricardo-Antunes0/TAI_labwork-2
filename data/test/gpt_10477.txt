San Nicolas is one of the sixteen districts of Manila, Philippines. It is bounded on the north by the districts of Malate and Ermita, on the east by the districts of Sta. Cruz and Paco, on the south by the district of Santa Mesa, and on the west by Quezon City.   The district is named after Saint Nicolas of Myra, a 4th century Christian saint and bishop of Myra in Lycia who is venerated as a patron saint of sailors and children.  San Nicolas was inaugurated as a district on November 18, 1936. It is composed of barrios that used to be part of Ermita and Malate districts. The first president of San Nicolas was Benigno Ramos.
