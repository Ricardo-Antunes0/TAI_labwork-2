Joseph McNulty (1840 or 1841 - 1909) was an Irish-born American architect. He is best known for his design of the Brooklyn Bridge.  Joseph McNulty was born in 1840 or 1841 in Ireland, the son of James and Catherine McNulty. He came to the United States in 1854 and settled in Brooklyn, New York.  McNulty began his career as a draughtsman for architects Edward L. Warren and George W. Fuller. In 1873, he founded his own firm, McNulty and McNulty, which lasted until 1909. He designed many notable buildings in Brooklyn, including the Brooklyn Bridge (1883), the Brooklyn County Jail (1887), and the St. John's Evangelical Lutheran Church (1895).  McNulty died in 1909.
