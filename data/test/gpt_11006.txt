William Brangham (1968) is an American journalist, author, and commentator. He is the co-founder and editor-in-chief of National Review Online, a conservative magazine.  Brangham was born in 1968 in New York City. He received his B.A. from the University of Virginia in 1990, and his J.D. from Yale Law School in 1993.  Brangham began his career as a law clerk to Judge Richard Posner of the Seventh Circuit Court of Appeals. He then worked as a judicial assistant to Judge Michael Luttig of the Federal District Court for the Eastern District of Arkansas.  In 1995, Brangham joined the staff of The Weekly Standard, where he served as a deputy editor for two years. In 1999, he left The Weekly Standard to become a reporter for NewsMax Media, where he served as a national correspondent for two years.  In 2002, Brangham founded National Review Online, serving as its editor-in-chief until 2013. During his tenure at National Review Online, Brangham helped to rebuild the magazine into an influential conservative voice online. He has also written for The Wall Street Journal, The New York Times, The Washington Post, and other publications.  Brangham is currently a senior fellow at the Hoover Institution at Stanford University and a visiting professor at the Arthur L. Carter Journalism Institute at Long Island University School of Journalism and Mass Communication.
