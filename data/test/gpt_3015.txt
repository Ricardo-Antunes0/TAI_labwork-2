Caryl Mack Parker (born in Abilene, Texas) is an American artist who has worked in a variety of media, including painting, sculpture, photography, and installation.  Parker was born in Abilene, Texas in 1951. She received her BFA from the University of Texas at Austin in 1975, and her MFA from Yale University in 1978. Parker has exhibited widely throughout the United States and internationally, and her work is in the collections of the Museum of Modern Art, the Whitney Museum of American Art, and the Tate Gallery. She was a recipient of the Guggenheim Fellowship in 1995 and the Pollock-Krasner Foundation Award in 1999.
