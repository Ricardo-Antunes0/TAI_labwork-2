Michael McGreevey (born February 7, 1948) is an American politician who was the 45th Governor of New Jersey from 1999 to 2003. McGreevey resigned in 2004 after being charged with corruption.  McGreevey was born in Paterson, New Jersey, the son of Catherine and Joseph McGreevey. He grew up in Linden, New Jersey, and attended Saint Peter's Preparatory School in Newark, New Jersey. McGreevey then attended The College of William and Mary in Williamsburg, Virginia, where he earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in Political Science in 1970.  After college, McGreevey worked as a reporter for the "Newark Star-Ledger" newspaper before entering politics. He served as an aide to U.S. Senator Bill Bradley from 1971 to 1977. In 1978, he was appointed Deputy Director of the New Jersey Department of Community Affairs by Democratic Governor James Florio. McGreevey was elected Essex County Executive in 1987 and served two terms, from 1989 to 1995.  In March 1999, McGreevey was elected Governor of New Jersey, defeating Republican nominee Christine Todd Whitman by a wide margin. During his tenure as governor, McGreevey made national headlines for his involvement with a prostitution scandal and subsequent resignation in 2004. In 2006, he pleaded guilty to federal charges of fraud and conspiracy related to his role in the scandal and was sentenced to 18 months in prison. He was released early due to good behavior and currently resides in Livingston, New Jersey.
