Levine is known for his work on ethics, metaphysics, and philosophy of religion.  Levine was born in Brooklyn, New York City on February 12, 1937. He received his B.A. from Cornell University in 1959 and his M.A. and Ph.D. from Yale University in 1962 and 1965, respectively. Levine began his career as a Professor of Philosophy at the University of California, Irvine from 1965 to 1989, where he served as Chair of the Philosophy Department from 1981 to 1985. From 1989 to 1993 he served as President of the American Philosophical Association. Levine died on March 2, 2018 at the age of 80 after a long battle with cancer.
