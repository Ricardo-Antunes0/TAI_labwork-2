Restenneth Priory was a monastic house of the Cistercian Order in the parish of Restenneth, in the county of Cornwall, England.  The house was founded in 1163 by Walter de Stapledon, 2nd Earl of York and Count of Boulogne and his first wife, Adeliza, daughter of Walter de Lacy, Earl of Lincoln. The priory was dissolved in 1539 during the Dissolution of the Monasteries.
