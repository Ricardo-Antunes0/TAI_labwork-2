Thore Langfeldt (born 30 September 1943) is a Norwegian author, playwright and journalist. He was born in Oslo.   Langfeldt was educated at the University of Oslo, where he studied political science. He worked as a journalist for the Norwegian Broadcasting Corporation from 1968 to 1971, and then as editor of the newspaper Aftenposten from 1971 to 1978. He served as the Norwegian Minister of Culture from 1978 to 1981. Langfeldt has written several novels, short stories and plays. His most famous work is the novel Flamingo Road (1985), which was made into a successful film in 1987.
