Gnaeus Gellius ( half of 2nd centuryBC) was a Roman author, who wrote mostly in Latin. He is most famous for his work on Roman literature, which includes a biography of Pliny the Younger, and a series of Attic Nights.  Gnaeus Gellius was born in Rome in the early 2nd century BC. After studying at the University of Athens, he returned to Rome and began writing, producing works on Roman literature, including a biography of Pliny the Younger, and a series of Attic Nights. He died around 120 AD.
