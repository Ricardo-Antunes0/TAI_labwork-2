Patrick Ian Woodcock (born April 27, 1977) is a Canadian professional ice hockey defenceman who is currently an unrestricted free agent. He most recently played with HC Davos in the National League A (NLA) of Switzerland.  Woodcock was drafted by the Ottawa Senators in the first round, 16th overall, of the 1995 NHL Entry Draft. He has also played for the Detroit Red Wings, Columbus Blue Jackets, Philadelphia Flyers and Calgary Flames. Woodcock was a member of Team Canada at the 2002 Winter Olympics and the 2006 Winter Olympics.  Patrick Woodcock was born on April 27, 1977 in Timmins, Ontario. He is a graduate of the University of Wisconsin–Madison where he played for the Wisconsin Badgers men's ice hockey team from 1997 to 2000. In his senior season, Woodcock was a First-Team All-American and helped lead Wisconsin to the Frozen Four appearance.  Woodcock played for Ottawa Senators from 1995 to 2000 and then moved to Detroit Red Wings where he spent three seasons from 2001 to 2003. He then moved to Columbus Blue Jackets where he spent two seasons from 2004 to 2006. After leaving Columbus, Woodcock signed with Philadelphia Flyers for two seasons from 2007 to 2009. He then signed with Calgary Flames for one season in 2010–11 before moving back to Switzerland in 2012 with HC Davos. Woodcock has also played for Team Canada at the 2002 Winter Olympics and the 2006 Winter Olympics.
