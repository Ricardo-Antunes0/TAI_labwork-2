Marie Claire Ross (born 1975 or 1976) is a Canadian writer and journalist.  Ross was born in 1975 or 1976 in Toronto, Ontario. She grew up in the city's east end. She studied English literature at the University of Toronto, but did not complete her degree.  Ross began her career as a journalist at "The Globe and Mail" in 1998. She later became a senior writer for "Marie Claire". In 2006, she published her first book, "The Girls' Guide to Getting What You Want", which was a New York Times Bestseller. Her second book, "The Rules: Time-Tested Secrets For Capturing The Heart of Mr. Right" was published in 2009 and also became a New York Times Bestseller.  In 2012, Ross published her third book, "The Life-Changing Magic of Tidying Up". The book has been translated into more than 20 languages and has been on the New York Times Bestseller list for over six months.  Ross is also a regular panelist on "Canada's Next Top Model".
