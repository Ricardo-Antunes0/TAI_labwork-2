Stephen Larigaudelle Dubuisson  (born Étienne de la Vallière Larigaudelle, on 21 October 1951 in Cayenne) is a French anthropologist and primatologist who has been working in the field of primate behaviour for over 25 years.  He is a Professor at the University of Poitiers, where he has been head of the Department of Anthropology from 1995 to 2007. He also currently holds the Chair of Primatology at the National Museum of Natural History (MNHN) in Paris.  Dubuisson's research focuses on the study of human and non-human primates, with a particular interest in their social behaviour and communication. He has published more than 200 articles and book chapters on these topics, and his work has been cited more than 4,000 times.  He was elected as a Fellow of the Royal Society (FRS) in 2001, and he was awarded the Medal of the University of Poitiers in 2007. He was also named a Chevalier de la Légion d'honneur in 2009.
