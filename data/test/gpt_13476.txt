Count Imre Zichy de Zich et Vásonkeő (; 22 January 1792 – 21 October 1868) was a Hungarian statesman and jurist who served twice as Prime Minister of Hungary, from 1848 to 1849 and from 1854 to 1856.  Zichy was born in Kassa (now Kolozsvár, Romania), then part of the Austrian Empire. After studying law at the University of Vienna, he returned to Hungary and practiced law in Pest. Zichy served as Minister of Justice in the first government of Count István Széchenyi between 1839 and 1840, and again in the second government of Count István Széchenyi between 1848 and 1849. He also served as Governor of Transylvania between 1850 and 1853.  Zichy became Prime Minister for the second time in 1854, after serving as Minister of Finance in the first cabinet of Count István Esterházy. His second term lasted until 1856. He also served as Speaker of the House of Magnates between 1861 and 1865. He retired from public life in 1865. Zichy died at his country estate, Zichy Palace, in Budapest.
