Udagawa Yōan was born in 1821 in the city of Kyoto, then part of the shogunate of Japan. After studying at the prestigious Shōgakkō School and the University of Tokyo, he became a professor at the latter institution in 1854. Udagawa Yōan is best known for his translations of Plato, Aristotle, and Descartes, as well as his philosophical writings. He also made important contributions to Japanese thought on moral philosophy and aesthetics.
