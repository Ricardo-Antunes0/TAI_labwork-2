The Roundup Central School is a historic school in Roundup, Montana. It was built in 1909 and is a three-story, rectangular, brick building with a steeply pitched roof. It has a gable roof and a projecting central tower with a pyramidal roof. The school was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1982. The school was built in 1909 as a one-room schoolhouse on land that was donated by the family of Frank W. Weed. The school served the community for nearly fifty years, until it closed in 1957 due to declining enrollment. In 1982, the school was listed on the National Register of Historic Places.
