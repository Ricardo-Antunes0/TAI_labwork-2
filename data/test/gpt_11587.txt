Artashes Abeghyan (also Abeghian) ( 1 January 1965 – 10 May 2005) was an Armenian sculptor.  Born in Yerevan, Armenia, Abeghyan is best known for his monumental bronze sculptures of animals and human beings, as well as his public art projects. He exhibited widely throughout the world, including at the Venice Biennale, the São Paulo Art Biennial, and the Whitney Biennial. Abeghyan was also a recipient of numerous awards, including the Golden Lion Award at the Venice Biennale in 1996, and the prestigious National Artist of Armenia award in 2001. He died in Yerevan at the age of 41 from a heart attack.  Abeghyan was born in 1965 in Yerevan, Armenia. After completing his secondary education at the Etchmiadzin State University, he began studying sculpture at the Tohokian Academy of Fine Arts in 1985. In 1989 he graduated from the academy with honours and moved to Moscow to continue his studies at Moscow State University of Fine Arts. There he met sculptor Zurab Tsereteli, who helped him find an audience outside of Armenia.  Abeghyan's early work focused on bronze castings of animals and human beings. He exhibited his first sculptures at the Moscow Biennale in 1994. The following year he participated in the Venice Biennale, where his sculpture "The Watch" won a Golden Lion Award.
