The Patz Brothers House, is located in East Hanover Township, Morris County, New Jersey. The house was built in 1790 and is a two-story, five bay, Georgian style wood frame house. It has a hipped roof and features a full-width front porch. The house was added to the National Register of Historic Places on October 11, 1978.  The Patz Brothers were a family of shoemakers who operated a shop in East Hanover Township from the early 1700s until the early 1800s. The brothers are likely responsible for the construction of the house which is one of the oldest surviving structures in Hanover Township. The house is also one of the few remaining Georgian style houses in Morris County.
