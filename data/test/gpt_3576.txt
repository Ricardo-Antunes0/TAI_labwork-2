He became Chief of the General Staff in 1957 and served in this position until his retirement in 1967.  Born in Melbourne, Victoria on 1 October 1897, Michel joined the Australian Army in 1915 and served in World War I as a lieutenant with the 14th Battalion AIF. He was promoted to captain in 1919 and served with the 3rd Division during the 1920s. He transferred to the 2nd Division in 1930 and was made a major in 1934. In 1937 he was promoted to lieutenant colonel and appointed commander of the 2/7th Battalion. He served with the 2/7th during World War II, including campaigns in New Guinea, Borneo and North-West Pacific. He was made a field marshal in 1944.  Michel returned to Australia after World War II and served as Chief of Staff of the Australian Army from 1957 to 1967. He retired from the army on 31 December 1967. Michel died on 23 July 1986 aged 88 years.
