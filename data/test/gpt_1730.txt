Schilder-boeck is a book written by the Dutch artist Piet Mondrian in 1918. It is a collection of lithographs and paintings depicting geometric abstractions, primarily squares and rectangles. The book has been considered a seminal work in the development of abstract art, and has been credited with having helped to revive the European art movement during the early 20th century.
