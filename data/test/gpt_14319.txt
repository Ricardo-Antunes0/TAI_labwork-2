Amjad Zia (born in Sialkot Cantt) is a Pakistani poet, novelist and playwright. He is the author of "The Book of Shadows" (1996), "The Great Game" (2003), and "The Traveller's Rest" (2010).  Zia was born in Sialkot Cantt, in the Punjab province of Pakistan in 1953. After completing his early education at local schools, he attended the Government College, Sialkot. He later earned a Master's degree in English Literature from the University of the Punjab.  Zia started writing poetry in the early 1970s. His first collection of poems, "Amber", was published in 1974. He followed this with "The Moon and I" (1977), a volume of poems inspired by the life and works of Rabindranath Tagore.  Zia's first novel, "The Book of Shadows", was published in 1996. The book tells the story of two friends – a Muslim and a Hindu – who are struggling to reconcile their different world views. It was awarded the prestigious Sahitya Akademi Award in 1997.  Zia's second novel, "The Great Game", was published in 2003. It is a thriller set against the backdrop of the 1971 Indo-Pakistani War of Independence and tells the story of a young Englishwoman who becomes involved in the conflict.
