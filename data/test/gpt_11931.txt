Zacharias Lewala (fl. 1908) was a Namibian pastor. He was born in the village of Kudus, in the Otjozondjupa Region of Namibia. He studied to become a pastor at the Evangelical Theological Seminary of America in Evanston, Illinois. He served as a pastor in the village of Ondangwa in the Caprivi Region of Namibia from 1908 until his death in 1924.  Lewala is best known for his work as a missionary in the Caprivi Region of Namibia. In 1912, he founded the Ondangwa Mission, which became one of the largest and most successful Protestant missions in Namibia. He also served as president of the South-West Africa Mission from 1919 to 1923. Lewala was a prolific writer and author of several books about his work as a missionary in Namibia. He is also considered to be one of the founders of modern Protestantism in Namibia.
