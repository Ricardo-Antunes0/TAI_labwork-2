The Moses Kent House is a historic house at 9 Main Street in Winthrop, Maine. It was built in 1792 and is a well-preserved example of Federal period architecture. Its main block is three bays wide, with a single-story wing on the right side. The main entrance is framed by pilasters and a transom window, and it is topped by a simple pediment. The interior features original woodwork, plasterwork, and trim. It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1978.
