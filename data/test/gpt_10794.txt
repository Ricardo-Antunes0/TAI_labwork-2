The Tongan megapode (Megapodius pritchardii) is a megapode found in the islands of Tonga and Samoa. It was originally described by the British naturalist Alfred Russel Wallace in 1858. It is the only extant species in the genus Megapodius.  The Tongan megapode is a large, ground-dwelling bird with a long neck, a short tail, and large feet. It is mainly brown with some black markings on its head, neck, and wings. The Tongan megapode feeds on insects and is capable of walking on two legs or using its wings to fly.
