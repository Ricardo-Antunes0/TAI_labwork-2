Solidago missouriensis is a species of flowering plant in the buckwheat family known by the common name Missouri goldenrod.  It is native to the eastern United States, where it is found in a wide range of habitats from dry woods and prairies to moist woodlands and riverbanks. It is a perennial herb growing up to one meter tall, with slender stems lined with leaves up to 20 centimeters long. The inflorescence is a cluster of up to 12 bright yellow flowers surrounded by woolly bracts.
