He is best known for his work on emotions, including the concept of emotion contagion.  Cacioppo was born in Chicago, Illinois on June 12, 1951. He earned a B.A. in psychology from Oberlin College in 1973 and a Ph.D. in social psychology from the University of Michigan in 1978.  Cacioppo began his career as an assistant professor at the University of Chicago in 1978, and became a full professor there in 1987. He served as the chair of the Department of Psychology at the University of Chicago from 2001 to 2003. In 2002, he was named one of the inaugural fellows of the American Association for the Advancement of Science.  Cacioppo died on January 25, 2013 after a long battle with cancer.
