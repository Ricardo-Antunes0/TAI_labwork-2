James Vincent McNichol III (born July 2, 1951) is an American singer, songwriter, and musician. He was the lead singer of the rock band the Eagles from 1971 to 1975, and has since had a successful solo career.  McNichol was born in San Francisco, California. He formed the Eagles in 1971 with Don Henley, Bernie Leadon, Randy Meisner and Glenn Frey. The band achieved international success with their first album, "Eagles", which sold over 20 million copies. They followed up with four more successful albums before splitting in 1975.  Since then, McNichol has had a successful solo career, most notably with the platinum-selling albums "I Can't Tell You Why" (1978), "Take It to the Limit" (1979), "The Last Great American Band" (1980), and "Back Home" (1987). He has also released several movie soundtrack albums, and has been involved in philanthropic work. In 2009, McNichol was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame as a member of the Eagles.
