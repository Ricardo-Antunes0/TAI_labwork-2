"Ten" is a song co-written and recorded by American singer and songwriter Katy Perry for her third studio album, Teenage Dream (2010). It was written by Perry, Max Martin, and Dr. Luke and produced by the latter two. "Ten" was released as the album's lead single on October 24, 2010, by Capitol Records.  The song is a mid-tempo ballad about the narrator's feelings for a past love, who has since moved on. The lyrics are written in the form of a letter to the former partner. "Ten" received generally positive reviews from music critics, who praised Perry's vocals and the song's composition. It became Perry's fourth consecutive number-one single in the United States, where it became the best-selling digital track of 2010. Internationally, "Ten" topped charts in Australia, Canada, Ireland, New Zealand, Norway, Sweden and the United Kingdom.  The accompanying music video was directed by Dave Meyers and features Perry portraying a femme fatale. It received positive reviews from critics for its visuals and portrayal of Perry's performance. The video won several awards accolades including Video of the Year at the 53rd Grammy Awards. In December 2010, "Ten" was nominated for Best Pop Video at the 52nd Annual Grammy Awards but lost to "I Gotta Feeling" by The Black Eyed Peas.
