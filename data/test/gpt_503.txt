Malayalam, the lingua franca of the Indian state of Kerala, is a classical language with a rich literary tradition. Malayalam literature, which has its roots in the Classical Period of Malayalam history, has been shaped by the syncretic traditions of Hinduism, Islam and Christianity. Major literary figures of Malayalam include Vaikundar Kovilakom, Thunchaththu Ezhuthachan, Kunjunni Marthanda Varma, and Ulloor S. Parameswaran. The language has been used to write novels, short stories, poems, plays and songs.
