He studied at the University of Amsterdam, where he received his doctorate in 1881. From 1882 to 1889 he was a professor of botany at the University of Groningen. In 1889 he emigrated to the United States, where he became a professor at Yale University. In 1914 he returned to the Netherlands, where he retired in 1932. He died on 8 October 1945 in The Hague.  Knobel was a leading figure in the development of taxonomy, and is best remembered for his work on the classification of plants and for his descriptions of new taxa. His major work, "The Plant World" (1914), is still widely used as a reference work on plant taxonomy. He also made important contributions to plant physiology, morphology, and cytology.
