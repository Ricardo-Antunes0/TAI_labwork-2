Mary Esther Murtfeldt (6 August 1839, New York City â€“ 21 January 1912, Paris) was an American sculptor.  Murtfeldt was born in New York City to German immigrant parents. After studying at the Art Students League of New York with John La Farge and Frederic Remington, she moved to Paris in 1865 and became a leading sculptor of the Franco-Prussian War period. Murtfeldt exhibited at the Salon from 1867 to 1907, and at the Paris Exposition of 1878. Her works are in the collections of the Metropolitan Museum of Art, the Museum of Fine Arts, Boston, and the Detroit Institute of Arts.
