Edgar Hilsenrath (April 2, 1926 – December 17, 2007) was an American journalist and commentator. He was the founder and editor-in-chief of the conservative Wall Street Journal editorial page, serving in that role from 1967 to 2003.  Hilsenrath was born in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, and attended the University of Wisconsin–Madison. He served in the United States Army during the Korean War. After the war, he worked for the Associated Press and then for The Washington Post.  Hilsenrath joined the Wall Street Journal in 1967 as its editorial page editor. He became its publisher in 1998, a position he held until his retirement in 2003. As publisher, Hilsenrath helped guide the Journal through a period of rapid growth and transformation. Under his leadership, the Journal won a number of prestigious journalism awards, including a Pulitzer Prize in 2002 for its coverage of the September 11 attacks.  Hilsenrath died at his home in Potomac, Maryland, aged 83.
