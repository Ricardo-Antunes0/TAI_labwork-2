Mark Wilf (born 1962) is an American businessman and the president and CEO of the Minneapolis-based Target Corporation.  Wilf was born in 1962 in Minneapolis, Minnesota, to Ann and Robert Wilf. He graduated from Southwest High School in 1980 and from the University of Minnesota with a degree in business administration in 1984. He began his career at Target as a sales representative in 1984, and was promoted to district manager in 1987. He became president and CEO of Target Corporation in January 2002, succeeding Gregg Steinhafel. In that role, he led Target through its merger with Minnesota-based QVC in 2000, its acquisition of Boston's J.C. Penney in 2008, and its spinoff of the Canadian department store chain Zellers in 2013. Wilf retired from Target Corporation effective January 1, 2019.   Wilf is married to his wife, Lori, and they have two children. He is a member of the Board of Directors of the Minneapolis Institute of Arts, the Board of Trustees of Augsburg College, and the Board of Directors of Planned Parenthood Federation of America.
