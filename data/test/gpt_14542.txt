Franz von Löher (15 October 1818, Paderborn, Westphalia – 15 October 1883, Paderborn), was a German zoologist and biologist. He was the founder of the modern systematics of zoology.  Löher studied at the universities of Göttingen and Bonn, where he received his doctorate in 1843. He then worked as a teacher at the gymnasium in Paderborn, where he developed his interest in zoology. In 1851, he became an associate professor at the University of Göttingen. In 1854, he became a full professor at Göttingen.  Löher is best known for his work on the classification of animals. He developed the modern systematics of zoology, which is still used today. He also made significant contributions to the study of anatomy, physiology, and behavior.
