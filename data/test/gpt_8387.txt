The Lambeth Bible was created in Lambeth Palace, London, in the 12th century. It is one of the most important sources of medieval English illumination and is one of the few surviving examples of a complete Bible illuminated by a single scribe. The manuscript is especially notable for its extensive use of gold and silver leaf, as well as for the high quality of its illustrations.  The Lambeth Bible was donated to the British Library in 1753 by Archbishop Thomas Becket. Today, it is one of the Library's most popular items and is regularly exhibited around the world.
