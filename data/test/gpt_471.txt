He was the head of the Ramakrishna Mission, a major spiritual organization founded by Ramakrishna in 1887.  Krishnaswamy Ramiah was born in a Hindu family in the town of Tiruvannamalai in Tamil Nadu, to an agricultural family. He studied at a local school and then at a college in Tiruchirapalli. He joined the Madras Christian College as a student and graduated with a B.A. in 1912. He then studied at the University of Madras and graduated with an M.A. in 1914. In 1916 he became a teacher at his old school, and later that year he married Sarojini Naidu, with whom he had three children.  In 1917, Ramiah started working for the Ramakrishna Mission as an assistant priest. He later became the head of the mission's educational institutions, serving in that capacity until 1952. During this time he wrote many textbooks on Vedanta and Hindu philosophy for use by the students of the mission's schools. In 1952 he retired from his position and moved to Doon Valley, where he spent the rest of his life writing religious texts and giving lectures on Hindu philosophy and mysticism.  Krishnaswamy Ramiah died on 15 January 1982 at the age of 92. His funeral procession was attended by millions of people from all over India and abroad
