Rajiv Vijay Raghavan (born 29 May 1958) is an Indian film actor who has appeared in around 350 films in a career spanning almost four decades. He is best known for his work in the Tamil language film industry.  Rajiv Vijay Raghavan was born on 29 May 1958 in Chennai, Tamil Nadu, India. He has appeared in around 350 films in a career spanning almost four decades. He started his career in the 1970s, appearing in small roles in Tamil films. He rose to prominence with his performance as Raja in the 1981 Tamil film "Namma Aalu". He has since appeared in a wide range of films, including mainstream Hindi and English language productions. His most significant work in the Tamil language film industry has been in lead roles, most notably in the commercially successful films "Kanavu" (1991) and "Kuruvilla Thirumagal" (1996). He has also appeared in a number of successful Tamil-language romantic comedies.  Rajiv Vijay Raghavan has received numerous accolades throughout his career, including four National Film Awards and three Filmfare Awards. He was awarded the Padma Bhushan honour by the Government of India in 2002. In 2007, he was awarded an honorary doctorate by the University of Melbourne.
