Flavio Pimenta Alves  (born 30 November 1967) is a Portuguese retired professional footballer who played as a central defender.  Alves was born in Vila Real, Porto District. After starting out with hometown's lowly S.C. Olhanense, he moved to FC Porto in 1988 and made his debut in the Primeira Liga on 8 October 1988 in a 2–1 away win against Vitória de Guimarães. He went on to play 341 matches and score six goals for the " Dragões", winning three national championships (1992, 1994, 1997) and one Supertaça Cândido de Oliveira (1994).  After his contract expired at Porto, Alves signed for Racing Club de Portugal in the summer of 1998. He helped the Madeira side to promotion to the top flight in his first season, but retired at the end of the 2000–01 campaign with totals of 226 matches and six goals in all competitions.  Alves started his coaching career as assistant manager at FC Porto's U-19 team, before being appointed head coach of the B-team in 2009. He led them to promotion to the second division in his first season, but was sacked after two months due to poor results.   In November 2010, Alves was appointed head coach of C.D. Feirense, but was sacked four months later after failing to win any of their five games in charge.
