Martin E. Huld is an American linguist who has made significant contributions to the theory of morphology. He is also known for his work on the history of English grammar and for his studies of African American English.  Huld was born in Minnesota in 1943. He received his undergraduate degree from the University of Minnesota in 1965 and his graduate degree from the University of California, Berkeley in 1969.  Huld began his career as a professor at Yale University, where he remained until he retired in 2006. Since then, he has been a professor at Brown University.  Huld's research focuses on the theory of morphology, the history of English grammar, and African American English. He has written several books, including The Oxford Guide to English Grammar (2001), Morphology: A Functional Perspective (2002), and African American English: The History and Development of a New Language (2007).
