Daniel-Henry Kahnweiler (25 June 1884 – 11 November 1967) was a French art critic and historian. He was a founder of the modern art movement Nouveau Réalisme.  Kahnweiler was born in 1884 in Strasbourg, the son of a Jewish banker. After studying law, he turned to art criticism in 1907. He became known as a champion of the work of the Cubist painters Pablo Picasso and Georges Braque, and helped to spread their popularity. In 1923 he co-founded the Parisian magazine "Le Cubiste". Kahnweiler was also an influential historian of art, writing extensively on artists including Michelangelo, Leonardo da Vinci, Rembrandt, and Paul Cézanne. He died in 1967.
