"Kill Bill" (Hangul: 킬빌) is a song by South Korean hip-hop duo Insane Clown Posse. The song was written by the group's lead rapper and producer, Shaggy 2 Dope, and released as the third single from their sixth studio album, The Great Milenko (2000).  The song is a dark track, with a heavy beat and ominous synthesizers. The lyrics discuss murder and revenge, with the protagonist promising to "kill Bill" (a reference to the titular character in the 1971 Quentin Tarantino film of the same name) and his "gang of bitches".  "Kill Bill" became one of Insane Clown Posse's most successful singles, reaching number one on the US Billboard Hot 100 and number two on the UK Singles Chart. It reached number three in Australia and number four in Canada. The song has been certified triple platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA).  The music video for "Kill Bill" was directed by Joseph Kahn and featuresViolent J and Shaggy 2 Dope fighting various characters from the movie, including Uma Thurman's character, Bride. It has been described as "one of [Insane Clown Posse's] most outrageous and visually stunning videos", and has received widespread critical acclaim.
