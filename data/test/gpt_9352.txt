Five Golden Flowers (Chinese: 五朶金花; pinyin: Wǔ zhěn jīnhuā) are five flowers that are associated with the Chinese deity of happiness, joy, and prosperity, Guan Yu. The flowers are: chrysanthemum, orchid, snake's head fern, mother-of-pearl shell, and ginger.  The five flowers are often associated with celebrations, such as birthdays and anniversaries. In China, the flowers are often given as presents to friends and family.
