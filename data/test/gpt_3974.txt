Daniel Seth Loeb (born December 18, 1961) is an American businessman and philanthropist. Loeb is the founder, chairman, and CEO of Loeb & Loeb LLP, a law firm.  Loeb was born in New York City to Jewish parents. He attended The Dalton School and then Harvard College, where he was a member of the varsity tennis team and president of the Harvard Crimson. After graduating from Harvard, Loeb clerked for Judge Irving Kaufman on the United States Court of Appeals for the District of Columbia Circuit. He then entered private practice at the law firm of Shearman & Sterling. In 1994, Loeb co-founded the hedge fund Third Point LLC with Allan Dershowitz and Daniel S. Loeb. Third Point has since become one of the world's largest independent investment firms.  Loeb is a member of the Council on Foreign Relations and serves on its Board of Directors. He also served on President Obama's Economic Recovery Advisory Board from 2009 to 2010. In December 2013, he was appointed to serve as a non-voting trustee on the Museum of Modern Art's board of trustees.  Loeb is married to Susan Loomis Loeb; they have four children.
