Saint Vladimir's is a former Ukrainian Catholic seminary in the city of Kiev, now a university. It was founded in 1881 by Saint Vladimir (Volodymyr) of Kiev and became the first Ukrainian Catholic seminary in the world.  Saint Vladimir's is one of the oldest and most respected universities in Ukraine, with a long and rich history. The seminary was founded in 1881 as the first Ukrainian Catholic seminary in the world and has since grown into one of the country's leading universities. Today, Saint Vladimir's offers a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programmes, with strong emphasis on humanities and social sciences. The campus is located in the heart of Kiev, close to major tourist attractions and business districts.
