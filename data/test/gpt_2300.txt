Hills House is a 17th-century residence located in Denham, Buckinghamshire, England. It is a Grade II* listed building.  The house was built in 1612 by Sir Thomas Roe, 1st Baronet and is a two-storey Jacobean style mansion with a hipped roof. The entrance front has a pedimented portico with Ionic columns and a balustrade. There are three floors of windows, the lower two of which are arched. The house is surrounded by gardens and parkland.   The estate was purchased by the Denham family in 1711 and became the home of the first Viscount Denham. The house was used as a guesthouse for members of the Royal Family during the 1930s. It became a Grade II* listed building in 1951. It is now used as offices and accommodation.
