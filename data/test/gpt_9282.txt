He played first-class cricket for Middlesex between 1903 and 1921, and umpired 106 Test matches between 1921 and 1946.   Born in Westminster, London, Vereker was the son of George Vereker, a clerk in the Indian Civil Service. He was educated at Eton College and Trinity College, Cambridge. He made his first-class debut for Middlesex against Derbyshire in 1903. He scored 2 runs in the first innings and 1 not out in the second, as Middlesex lost by an innings margin. In 1904 he made his List A debut for Middlesex against Northamptonshire. Batting first, he scored 29 not out from the middle order, helping Middlesex to victory by an innings and 25 runs. The following year he made his only appearance for the Marylebone Cricket Club against the touring Australians. In 1905 he made his first-class debut for Cambridge University against Oxford University. Playing as an opening batsman, he scored 59 not out from the lower order, helping Cambridge to victory by an innings and 144 runs. He also played for Cambridgeshire in 1906 and 1907. In 1908 he played for Hampshire against Surrey at The Oval.
