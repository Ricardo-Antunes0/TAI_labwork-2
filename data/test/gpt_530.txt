Chris Kelsey (born June 5, 1961) is an American journalist who has been a foreign correspondent for The Associated Press since 1987. Kelsey is currently the AP's chief Asia correspondent.  Kelsey's reporting has taken him to more than 50 countries, including war zones and conflict-ridden regions such as the Balkans, the Middle East, Afghanistan, and Sri Lanka. He has covered major world events such as the Gulf War and the 2003 invasion of Iraq.  He has won numerous awards for his work as a journalist, including the Overseas Press Club Award for Best International Reporting in 2006 for his coverage of the Sri Lankan civil war.  Kelsey has also written two books about his experiences as a foreign correspondent: "A Dangerous Place: A Journey into War-Torn Sri Lanka" (2008) and "The Siege: A Story of Modern Day Baghdad" (2012).
