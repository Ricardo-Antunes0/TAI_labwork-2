Ricardo Gullón Fernández (30/31 August 1908 – 1 February 1989) was a Spanish football player and coach.  Born in Barcelona, Gullón played as a defender and midfielder for FC Barcelona, Real Madrid, Athletic Bilbao, Valencia CF and Atlético Madrid. He also played for the Spain national team and was part of the team that won the 1930 World Cup.  After retiring as a player, Gullón became a coach, leading Valencia CF to the Liga championship in 1957 and Real Madrid to the European Cup Winners' Cup in 1959. He also served as manager of Atlético Madrid and Barcelona.
