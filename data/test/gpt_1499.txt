Dungan ( or ) is a Sinitic language spoken in the Xinjiang Uyghur Autonomous Region, by the Dungan people. It is also spoken in parts of neighbouring Kyrgyzstan and Tajikistan. There are about 10 million speakers of Dungan, making it the seventh most spoken language in China.  The Dungan language is closely related to other Sinitic languages, such as Chinese and Mongolian. It is sometimes considered a dialect of Chinese, but it has its own unique grammar and vocabulary. The Dungan language has been heavily influenced by Russian and other Turkic languages over the years.
