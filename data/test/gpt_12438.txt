The Cochecton–Damascus Bridge, sometimes called the Cochecton suspension bridge, is a suspension bridge that spans the Susquehanna River in Cochecton, Pennsylvania, United States. It is the world's longest single-span suspension bridge with a main span of 1,260 feet (390 m). The bridge opened to traffic on October 4, 1924.  The Cochecton–Damascus Bridge was designed by the engineering firm of Yarrow and Murray and was built by the American Bridge Company. The construction cost was $1.5 million. The toll for a car was 25 cents at the time it opened. The original designers were Charles Alton Yarrow and his son, Arthur Murray Yarrow.  The Cochecton–Damascus Bridge has a main span of 1,260 feet (390 m), making it the world's longest single-span suspension bridge. It is also the world's heaviest single-span suspension bridge, weighing in at 26 million pounds (12 million kg).   The Cochecton–Damascus Bridge has two towers that are each 125 feet (38 m) high and support a main cable that is 4,500 feet (1,400 m) long. The towers are made of concrete and have a total weight of 240 million pounds (109 million kg). The main cable is made of steel cables that are 23 inches (58 cm) in diameter and have a weight of 950 million pounds (410 million kg).
