Middle Korean is the stage in the history of Korean language between the early Middle Ages and modern day. It is the period in which Korean was no longer a spoken language, but rather a written one. This period saw major changes in the Korean language, with many new words and meanings being introduced. Middle Korean is also known for its extensive borrowing from Chinese, which can be seen in the vocabulary and grammar of this period.
