Khodi Feiz (born 1963) is an industrial designer and artist who is known for his innovative use of material and fabrication techniques in his work.  Khodi Feiz was born in 1963 in Tehran, Iran. He received his bachelor's degree in industrial design from Mohajerin University of Art and Design in Tehran, Iran, in 1987. In 1992, he received his master's degree from the Royal College of Art in London, England.  From 1992 to 1994, Feiz was a research fellow at the Royal College of Art. From 1994 to 1996, he was a research associate at the University of Technology Sydney, Australia. In 1996, he moved to New York City and began working as an independent designer.  Feiz has exhibited his work internationally, including at the Museum of Modern Art in New York City; the Victoria and Albert Museum in London; the Centre Pompidou in Paris; and the Museum of Fine Arts Houston. He has also exhibited at the Cooper-Hewitt National Design Museum in New York City; the San Francisco Museum of Modern Art; and the Beijing Contemporary Art Centre.  Feiz's work is characterized by its innovative use of materials and fabrication techniques. He has developed unique methods for creating three-dimensional structures from thin sheets of metal or plastic, which he often intricately assembles into complex forms.
