Babak Bayat (,13 June 1946 â€“ 26 October 2004) was an Iranian novelist, short story writer, journalist and film director. He was born in Tehran.  Bayat was the author of fourteen novels, most famously "The Thief" (1994), which won the prestigious PEN Award in 1995. He also wrote a number of short stories and several plays.  Bayat was a journalist for the daily newspaper "Shargh" from 1978 to 1984, and then served as deputy editor of "Etemad", Iran's leading reformist daily newspaper, from 1985 to 1992. As a film director, he made two feature films, "The Mummy's Curse" (1999) and "The Children of Heaven" (2003), both of which received critical acclaim. He also directed several shorts films.  Bayat died in 2004 of liver cancer at the age of 60.
