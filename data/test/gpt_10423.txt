Joanne Whalley (born 25 August 1961), credited as Joanne Whalley, is an English actress, best known for her roles in the ITV soap opera Coronation Street and the BBC television series Midsomer Murders.  Whalley was born in London, England, the daughter of actress June Whitfield and footballer Archie Whalley. She has two sisters: actress Hayley Whalley and singer-songwriter Emma Whitfield. Her father was killed in a car accident when she was 14 years old. She trained at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art (RADA), graduating in 1983.  Whalley's first professional stage appearance was in a London production of the musical Ain't Misbehavin' in 1984. She made her television debut in 1985 with a guest appearance on the BBC1 drama series Motorman. From 1985 to 1989, she starred as Jenny Colgan in the British soap opera Coronation Street, which made her a household name. She left the show to appear as Gilly Cooper on the ITV mystery series Midsomer Murders from 1990 to 1995.  Since then, Whalley has appeared in numerous films and television productions, including The Borrower (1996), Dead Man's Shoes (1998), Gosford Park (2001), The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, The Witch and The Wardrobe (2005), Doctor Who (2005), and Death at a Funeral (2007).
