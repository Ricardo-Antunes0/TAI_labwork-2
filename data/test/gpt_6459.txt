Herbert Thomas "Tom" Reiner Jr. (September 21, 1922 – May 6, 2009) was an American actor and comedian. He is best known for his role as Howard Stern's father on the Howard Stern Show, for which he won a Daytime Emmy Award in 1986.  Reiner was born in Newark, New Jersey, the son of Herbert Reiner Sr., a dentist, and his wife, Thelma (née Goldstein). He attended the University of Pennsylvania, where he played football and was a member of the Quakers.  Reiner started his career in the late 1940s as a stand-up comic. He landed his first significant role on Broadway in the play No Time For Sergeants (1949). From there he went on to appear in films such as The Apartment (1960), The Saint (1962), The Odd Couple (1968), and One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest (1975), for which he won a Golden Globe Award. In 1986, Reiner won an Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series for his portrayal of Howard Stern's father on the Howard Stern Show.  Reiner retired from acting in 1993. He resided in Westport, Connecticut until his death from natural causes at age 89 on May 6, 2009.
