Samuel Mathew Piantadosi (April 1946 - 4 July 2006) was an American jazz saxophonist.  Piantadosi was born in Springfield, Massachusetts. He played with the saxophonists Lee Konitz, Jimmy Giuffre, and Stan Getz early in his career before moving to Europe in the late 1960s. He returned to the United States in the early 1970s and played with such contemporaries as Donald Byrd, Sonny Rollins, Freddie Hubbard, and Weather Report. He also collaborated with such artists as Chick Corea, Herbie Hancock, and Gary Burton.  Piantadosi died from a heart attack in 2006 at the age of 60.
