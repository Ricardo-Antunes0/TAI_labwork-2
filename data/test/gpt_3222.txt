Christopher Ballew (born May 28, 1965) is an American musician, singer, songwriter and record producer. He is best known as the founder, lead vocalist and rhythm guitarist of the alternative rock band The Replacements.  Ballew was born in Bloomington, Minnesota, to Betty (n√©e Standridge), a teacher and guidance counselor, and John Ballew, a construction worker. He has two older sisters. Ballew started playing guitar at the age of 12 and formed his first band with schoolmates at the age of 14. In 1985, he moved to Minneapolis to join the band The Gits. After The Gits disbanded in 1987, Ballew formed The Replacements with Bob Stinson (guitar), Tommy Stinson (bass) and Chris Mars (drums).  The Replacements achieved commercial success with their fourth album "Let It Be" (1987), which produced the singles "Can't Hardly Wait" and "I Don't Want To Know". Their subsequent albums "Tim" (1991) and "Pleased To Meet Me" (1995) received mixed reviews but still sold well. The band broke up in 1991 but reformed in 2003 for a short tour. In 2006, they released their first album of new material in 17 years, "Don't Tell a Soul".
