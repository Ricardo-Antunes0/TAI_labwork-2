Born in New York City, Rusher attended Yale University, where he was a member of the secret society Skull and Bones. He served in the United States Army during World War II, reaching the rank of captain. After the war, he worked as a journalist for "The New Republic" and then as a columnist for "National Review". He also wrote books on conservatism and libertarianism. Rusher was a fellow at the Hoover Institution at Stanford University and at the Cato Institute. He died in 2015 at the age of 92.
