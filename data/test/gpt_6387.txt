Milivoje Trbić , known as Vojče (Војче) in his homeland, was a Montenegrin general and politician. He served as the Prime Minister of Montenegro from 1991 to 1992, during the rule of Slobodan Milošević.  Born in 1927 in Berane, Trbić attended the Military School in Belgrade. He joined the Yugoslav Army in 1948, and fought in the Korean War. He became a colonel in 1967 and a brigadier general in 1971. Trbić served as the Minister of Defense from 1982 to 1986.  Trbić was elected to the National Assembly of Yugoslavia from Berane in the 1983 election. He served as the Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Defense of Montenegro from 1991 to 1992, during the rule of Slobodan Milošević.  After Milošević's ouster in October 1992, Trbić served as the Prime Minister of Montenegro from November 1992 to May 1993. He was later appointed Ambassador to Sweden by President Milošević.  Trbić died on 17 February 2016 at the age of 88.
