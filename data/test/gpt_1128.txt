Johannes Eick (born 22 April 1964) is a German historian who specializes in medieval history, mainly the Carolingian period.  Eick was born in Berlin, Germany. After studying history at the universities of Freiburg and Munich, he worked as a research assistant at the University of Paderborn from 1988 to 1991, and as a Research Fellow at King's College London from 1991 to 1995. He was then a lecturer at the universities of Münster and Erfurt from 1995 to 2003, and became an associate professor at the University of Erfurt in 2003. From 2006 to 2010 he was a full professor at the University of Würzburg. Since 2010 he has been a professor of medieval history at the University of Mainz.  Eick's research focuses on the Carolingian era, specifically on the reigns of Charlemagne (742–814) and Louis the Pious (814–840). He has also written on religious history, dynasty politics, and medieval society. Eick is a member of the German Historical Society, the Royal Historical Society, and the American Historical Association.  Eick has also written several historical novels set in late antiquity and the early Middle Ages. The first book in this series, "Die Macht der Könige" (The Power of Kings), was published in 2009. The second book, "Das Vermächtnis des Königs" (The Legacy of the King), was published in 2012.
