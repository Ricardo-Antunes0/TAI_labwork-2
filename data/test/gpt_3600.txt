Roy Edmund Steckel (October 17, 1887 â€“ August 12, 1970) was an American economist who is principally noted for his work on economic growth theory and economic development.  Born in Springfield, Illinois, Steckel received his undergraduate degree from the University of Illinois in 1908 and his Ph.D. from the University of Cambridge in 1912. He taught at the University of California, Berkeley from 1912 to 1945, where he was chairman of the department of economics from 1942 to 1945. He then served as president of the Carnegie Institution of Washington from 1945 to 1953. He was a Research Associate at the National Bureau of Economic Research from 1933 to 1955, and a Research Professor at Stanford University from 1955 to 1970.  Steckel was awarded the Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences in 1969 "for his pioneering work on economic growth theory and economic development".
