Donald E. Stewart (24 January 193028 April 2016) was an American economist and academic who specialized in monetary economics, public finance, and business cycles.  Donald E. Stewart was born on January 24, 1930 in Lowell, Massachusetts. He received his B.A. from Brown University in 1952 and his M.A. from the University of California, Berkeley in 1953. Stewart earned his Ph.D. from the University of Michigan in 1957.  Stewart began his career as an instructor at Brown University in 1952. He then served as an assistant professor at the University of California, Berkeley from 1957 to 1960 and as a professor at the University of Michigan from 1960 to 1971. From 1971 to 1977, Stewart served as the president of Northeastern University in Boston. He then returned to the University of Michigan as a professor emeritus until his death on April 28, 2016.  During his career, Stewart published several books and articles on monetary economics, public finance, and business cycles. He was also a Fellow of the American Economic Association and the Institute of Economic Affairs.
