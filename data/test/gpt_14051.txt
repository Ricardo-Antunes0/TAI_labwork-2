Pichhore is a town and a nagar panchayat in Shivpuri district in the Indian state of Madhya Pradesh. It is located on the banks of the Betwa River.  Pichhore is one of the oldest and most important towns in Shivpuri district. The town is located on the banks of the Betwa River, about 150 kilometers north-west of Bhopal. Pichhore is also on the main rout from Bhopal to Indore and Ujjain. The town has a population of about 16,000.  The town was known as "Pithora" in ancient times. Pichhore is mentioned in the Mahabharata as one of the five towns that supported Duryodhana against Yudhisthira. Pichhore later became an important centre of trade and commerce. The town was ruled by Rajputs for many centuries.  The present day town is built around a fort built by Maharaja Jai Singh II in 1727. The fort has been turned into an art gallery.  The old part of the town is clustered around a marketplace that has been restored and now houses a number of shops and restaurants. There are also several temples and mosques in the old part of the town. The new part of the town is built around a shopping complex, which includes multiple cinemas and a large supermarket.
