Vyatcheslav Kagan-Paley, also known as Slava ("Слава" in Russian) is a Russian-born composer, conductor, and pianist. He is best known for his compositions for the theatre, including his Tony-nominated score for the play Angels in America.  Kagan-Paley was born in Moscow, Russia on September 25, 1954. He began studying music at the age of six, and later studied at the Moscow Conservatory with faculty including Dmitri Shostakovich and Yuri Temirkanov. He made his conducting debut at the age of nineteen, and has since worked with many of the world's leading orchestras, including the London Symphony Orchestra, the Chicago Symphony Orchestra, and the Vienna Philharmonic.  His compositions have been performed around the world, including a performance of his work Angels in America at the Royal National Theatre in London which won him a Tony Award in 1996. In 2006, he was awarded an honorary doctorate from King's College London.  Kagan-Paley has two children: a daughter named Anna and a son named Ivan. He currently resides in New York City.
