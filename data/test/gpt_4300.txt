The Panic of 1796–1797 was a series of economic panics in Western Europe that started in late 1796 and lasted until late 1797. The causes of the panic were complex, with speculation on market prices, debt levels, and land values all playing a role. The panic led to a number of bankruptcies and the ruin of many individuals and businesses.
