The Ventures are an American instrumental rock and roll band formed in 1958 in Fort Lauderdale, Florida. The band was originally composed of bassist and vocalist Bob Babbitt, guitarist Michael Nesmith, drummer Jerry Van Dyke, and trumpeter Don Wilson.  The Ventures were influential in the development of surf and rock 'n' roll music and are often credited as the first instrumental rock group. They are also well known for their hit songs "Walk Don't Run" and "The Wanderer". In 2008, they were inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
