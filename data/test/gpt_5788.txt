Charles Umlauf (July 17, 1911 â€“ November 4, 1987) was an American composer, pianist and conductor.  Born in Buffalo, New York, Umlauf studied at the Buffalo Academy of Music and with Arthur Goldmark. He made his debut as a composer in 1930 with the piano concerto "Sonata". He also wrote orchestral works, chamber music, and songs. His best-known works include the opera "The Sound of Music" and the song cycle "Lulu".  Umlauf served as music director of the Houston Symphony from 1949 to 1966 and the Philadelphia Orchestra from 1966 to 1972. He was awarded a Grammy Award for his recording of Beethoven's Ninth Symphony with the Philadelphia Orchestra. He died in Philadelphia of heart failure.
