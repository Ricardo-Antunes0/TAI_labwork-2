Don or the Don is the nickname of American basketball player LeBron James. The nickname was given to James by then-Marquette assistant coach Rick Majerus.  LeBron James was born July 8, 1984, in Akron, Ohio. He attended St. Vincent-St. Mary High School in Akron, where he was a standout player in both basketball and football. James originally committed to play football at the University of Miami, but changed his mind and opted to play basketball for the Miami Hurricanes. After one season at Miami, James was drafted first overall by the Cleveland Cavaliers in the 2003 NBA Draft.  James has been one of the most dominant players in the NBA for nearly a decade. He has won multiple championships with the Cavaliers and Miami Heat, and is currently a member of the Los Angeles Lakers. He is also a two-time Olympic gold medalist, having played for Team USA in the 2008 and 2012 games.
