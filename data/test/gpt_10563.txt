The Iril River is a river that flows through Ireland. It is located in County Galway, and flows into the Atlantic Ocean at Galway Bay. The Iril River is the longest river in County Galway.  The Iril River is a vital waterway for the people of County Galway. It supplies drinking water and hydroelectric power to the area, and is also used for fishing and tourism. The river has a rich history, and was important to the Irish people during the early days of their country.
