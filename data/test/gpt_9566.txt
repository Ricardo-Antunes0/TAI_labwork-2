The Ironclad Board was an advisory board made up of business and political leaders in the United States during the early years of World War I. The board was created in October 1914, and its purpose was to advise President Woodrow Wilson on economic and military matters related to the war.  The board consisted of seventeen members, including eleven business leaders and six political leaders. Among the business leaders were Andrew Carnegie, J.P. Morgan, and William Jennings Bryan. The political leaders included Woodrow Wilson, Robert Lansing, and William Howard Taft.
