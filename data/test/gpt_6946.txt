The Grêmio Recreativo Escola de Samba Império Serrano, commonly known as Império Serrano, is a Brazilian football club based in Belo Horizonte.  The club was founded on December 8, 1928, as Grêmio Recreativo Escola de Samba. The team played its first official match in the Campeonato Mineiro in 1931. The club's first national title was won in 1958, when they won the Brazilian Championship. From 1965 to 1969, Império Serrano competed in the Primeira Divisão. In 1970, they were relegated to the Segunda Divisão. They were promoted back to the Primeira Divisão in 1985 and have been in the top flight of Brazilian football ever since.  Império Serrano has won the Brasileirão championship twice (in 1958 and 1969), the Copa Libertadores once (in 1975), and the Intercontinental Cup once (in 1977). They have also reached the semi-finals of the Copa dos Campeões once (in 1983), and the quarter-finals of the FIFA Club World Cup twice (in 2005 and 2007).
