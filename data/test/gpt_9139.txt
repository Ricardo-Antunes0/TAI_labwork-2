The Honourable Zoë Elizabeth Tryon (born 1 July 1972) is a Canadian businesswoman and former actress. She is the co-founder of the fashion company, Zoë Tryon Limited.  Born in Toronto, Ontario, Tryon is the daughter of Nancy (née Watson), an art teacher, and Gordon Tryon, a businessman. She has two sisters, Georgina and Celia.   Tryon trained at the National Theatre School of Canada in Montreal, Quebec, graduating in 1993. She began her acting career in stage productions before making her screen debut in the Canadian TV series "The Newsroom" (2000). Her feature film debut came in the Canadian film "The Sweet Hereafter" (1997), for which she received a Genie Award nomination for Best Actress.  In 2001, she was cast as Vanessa Kensington in the ABC television series "Dynasty". The show ran for five seasons from 2001 to 2005. After "Dynasty", she appeared in films such as "Baggage Claim" (1998), "The Patriot" (2000), "The Shipping News" (2001), and "Monster-in-Law" (2005). She also guest-starred on television series such as "Boston Legal", "CSI: Crime Scene Investigation", "Grey's Anatomy", and "Once Upon a Time".
