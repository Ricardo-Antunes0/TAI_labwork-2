The Remington–Keene is an early bolt-action rifle designed by American firearms designer and manufacturer Hiram Remington and independently designed by Eliphalet Remington. The Remington–Keene was first manufactured in 1875.   The Remington–Keene is a lightweight rifle built on the Remington Model 8 design. It has a 30-inch barrel with a brass receiver, screw-in chokes, and an overall length of 47.5 inches. The rifle was marketed as a sporting rifle and competed with the Winchester Model 1876.
