Alfred Haywood (born August 6, 1948) is an American author, artist, and political activist. He is best known for his poetry and prose writings, which focus on Black history and culture.  Haywood was born in 1948 in Harlan County, Kentucky, to a sharecropper family. He moved to New York City in the early 1970s and became active in the Black Arts Movement. In the early 1980s, he relocated to West Berlin and began writing about Afro-German relations. After the fall of the Berlin Wall, he returned to the United States and has since been a prolific writer and activist.  Haywood has been a vocal critic of capitalism and U.S. foreign policy. He has also written about ecology, gender issues, and American imperialism. He currently lives in Oakland, California.
