Laila al-Atrash (Arabic: ليلى الأطرش; died 17 February 2009) was a Palestinian poet and literary critic.  Born in the city of Ramla in central Palestine, al-Atrash studied at the American University of Beirut, where she earned her BA in Literature and Arabic in 1978. After spending several years travelling and writing, she returned to Beirut, where she worked as a literary critic for the Arabic daily Al-Hayat. In 1993, she was awarded the al-Khalili Prize for her poetry collection "Bitter Flowers". She also wrote a number of critical studies on Arabic poetry and authors.  Laila al-Atrash died on 17 February 2009 in Beirut after a long illness. She was 59 years old.
