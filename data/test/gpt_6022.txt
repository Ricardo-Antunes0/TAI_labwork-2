Pressed Steel Company Limited was a British steel making company.  The company was founded in 1884, as the Pressed Steel Company of Great Britain by Henry Bessemer. It operated as a subsidiary of the British Steel Corporation from its formation in 1971, until its privatisation in 1987.  Pressed Steel Company Limited produced a wide range of steel products, including carbon and alloy steels, stainless steels and special alloys. The company had around 9,000 employees at its peak.  In 1987, Pressed Steel Company Limited was privatised and became a constituent of the British Steel Corporation. The company went into receivership in 1992 and was sold to JB Hunt in 1995. In 2003, the business was sold to Nucor Corporation. Nucor announced its intention to sell the business to ArcelorMittal in August 2014, but the sale did not take place.
