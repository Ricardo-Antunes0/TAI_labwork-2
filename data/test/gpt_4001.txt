Linshcosteus is a genus of assassin bugs in the family Reduviidae. It contains about 10 species, all found in southern China, Vietnam, and Thailand.  The genus Linshcosteus was erected by Wang & Chen in 1995 to house the assassin bugs found in southern China, Vietnam, and Thailand. The species are small to medium-sized predators that feed primarily on other insects. They have longish antennae and flattened bodies.
