Cúcuta , officially San José de Cúcuta, is the capital of the department of Cundinamarca in western Colombia. It is located on the Magdalena River, at an elevation of 1,530 meters above sea level.  The city has a population of around 525,000 people, making it the eleventh-largest city in Colombia. Cúcuta is the commercial and administrative center for the department of Cundinamarca and is also the main center for trade in agricultural products. It is also home to several universities and a military academy.  Cúcuta was founded on October 10, 1537 by Gonzalo Jiménez de Quesada and was named "San José de Cúcuta" after Saint Joseph. The city was recognized as the provincial capital in 1853.
