Mark Alfred Carleton (Ohio, United States 7 October 1958) is an American economist who is the Robert W. Woodruff Professor of Economics and Business at Emory University.  He is known for his work in economic development, including his work on entrepreneurship and growth theory, as well as for his research on the economics of education and labor markets. He has also published work on taxation and public finance.  Carleton was born in Ohio in 1958. He earned a bachelor's degree from the University of Michigan in 1980, and a doctorate from the University of Chicago in 1984.  Carleton began his career at the Federal Reserve Bank of Chicago, where he worked from 1984 to 1989. He then served as a visiting professor at the Université Libre de Bruxelles (1989-1990), a visiting scholar at the National Bureau of Economic Research (1990-1991), and a senior fellow at the Hoover Institution at Stanford University (1991-1994). From 1994 to 1998, he was a professor of economics at Brown University. In 1998, he moved to Emory University, where he currently serves as the Robert W. Woodruff Professor of Economics and Business. He has also held visiting appointments at INSEAD (2000), the European Central Bank (2002), Universidad Autónoma de Madrid (2005), and the London School of Economics (2006).
