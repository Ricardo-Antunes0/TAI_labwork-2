Lille Cathedral, the Basilica of Notre Dame de Lille, is a Roman Catholic cathedral in Lille, France. It is the seat of the Bishop of Lille. The present cathedral was built between 1220 and 1260, during the reign of Louis IX of France. It was dedicated to the Virgin Mary.  The Basilica of Notre Dame de Lille is a Romanesque-Gothic cathedral. It has been listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1981. The cathedral is one of the most visited tourist attractions in France, receiving over two million visitors each year.
