Tamarisk was an Irish-bred champion Thoroughbred racehorse. She was undefeated in three races, including the prestigious Epsom Oaks.  Born in Ireland, Tamarisk was first bred by her owner, Noel Murless, and trained by John Gosden. She made her racing debut in a six furlong maiden race at Sandown Park Racecourse in May 1995, winning by three quarters of a length from the gelding Step Up. In July she was moved up in class and ran a six furlong maiden at Leopardstown Racecourse and won again, this time by two lengths from the filly Stavros. Three weeks later she contested her first Group One race, the Group 3 Prix de l'Arc de Triomphe over 2400 metres at Longchamp Racecourse and was ridden by Anthony Cummings. Starting the 9/4 favourite she led from the start and won easily by two lengths from the French-trained filly Yousif.  Tamarisk's form declined in 1996 but she returned to winning form in 1997. After finishing second to Saintly in a minor race at Newmarket she won a six furlong maiden at Ascot Racecourse on her next appearance. On 1 June she contested the Group 1 Epsom Oaks over one mile and one furlong and was made the 5/2 favourite against fourteen opponents.
