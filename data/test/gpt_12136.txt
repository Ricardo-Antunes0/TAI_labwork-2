Don Treadwell (born June 10, 1960) is an American naturalist, environmentalist, and photographer. He is the founder and president of the Don Treadwell Wildlife Preserve in Alaska.  Treadwell was born in Seattle, Washington, on June 10, 1960. At the age of four, he moved with his family to Anchorage, Alaska. Treadwell attended Anchorage High School, where he was a member of the varsity track team and the drama club. In 1978, he graduated from college with a degree in biology.  Treadwell began his career as a biologist for the Alaska Department of Fish and Game. In 1985, he began photographing wildlife in Alaska. He founded the Don Treadwell Wildlife Preserve in 1991. The preserve is located in central Alaska near the town of Talkeetna. The preserve encompasses of land and hosts a variety of animals, including grizzly bears, caribou, brown bears, wolves, and eagles.  Treadwell has published a number of books about his experiences as a naturalist and photographer. He has also appeared as a guest on various television programs, including NBC's "Today Show" and "The Ricki Lake Show". Treadwell is a member of both the National Wildlife Federation and the Wilderness Society.  Treadwell married Sally Miller in 1988. They have two children: daughter Kaileigh (born 1990) and son Austin (born 1998).
