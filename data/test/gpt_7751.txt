"Opening Night" is the seventeenth episode of the third season of the American musical comedy-drama television series Glee, and the sixty-ninth episode overall. Written by Ryan Murphy and directed by co-creator Brad Falchuk, it aired on Fox in the United States on October 28, 2009.  The series follows the high school musical crew of the fictitious William McKinley High School in Lima, Ohio, and their social lives and relationships. This episode features the show's first major crossover with another television series, when New Directions' resident bad boy Finn (Cory Monteith) visits McKinley to take part in its production of "Spring Awakening".  Opening Night was watched by 8.29 million American viewers and received mixed reviews from critics. Some praised the incorporation of "Spring Awakening", while others criticized its lack of originality.  New Directions' resident bad boy Finn (Cory Monteith) visits William McKinley High School to take part in its production of "Spring Awakening". Finn is immediately drawn to Rachel (Lea Michele), but she is not interested in him due to his reputation. At a party after Opening Night, Finn and Kurt (Chris Colfer) get into a fight that ends with Kurt being expelled. Finn then tells Rachel he likes her, but she does not believe him.
