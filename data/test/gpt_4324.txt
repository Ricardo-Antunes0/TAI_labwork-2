"Cross Road Blues" (also known as "Crossroads") is a blues song written by Robert Johnson in 1938. It is considered one of the most important blues songs and one of the most influential recordings in the history of blues.  Cross Road Blues is a 12-bar blues song with a simple structure consisting of a AAB pattern, with the A section consisting of four bars, the B section consisting of two bars, and the C section consisting of one bar. The song is in E♭ major and is based on the chord progression A–D–G–B. The lyrics are about a man who wants to leave his home town and travel to seek his fortune elsewhere, but is forced to stay because he has no money.
