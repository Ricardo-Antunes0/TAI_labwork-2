Kenneth ("Ken") John Lorraway (6 February 1956 â€“ 3 December 2018) was an American novelist and short story writer, known for his mysteries.  Lorraway was born in Boston, Massachusetts. He attended Phillips Academy in Andover, Massachusetts, graduating in 1974. He then studied at the University of Virginia, where he received an M.F.A. in 1978. Lorraway taught writing at the University of Missouri from 1978 to 1990 and at Towson State University from 1990 to 1998.  Lorraway's first novel, "The Outfit", was published in 1992 and won the Edgar Award for Best Novel. His subsequent novels include "The Replacement Killers" (1999), "The Black Box" (2002), "The Drop" (2004), "The Burning Room" (2007), "Devil's Pool" (2011), and "The Uninvited" (2015). Lorraway's mystery series featuring Boston private investigator Harry Bosch focused on a complex web of relationships between characters on both sides of the law. The series was adapted into a television series starring Michael Connelly and Rachel McAdams, which debuted in 2009 on NBC and ended in 2016. Lorraway wrote two companion novels to the television series, "Made to Kill" (2010) and "Blood Work" (2013).  Lorraway died on 3 December 2018 at the age of 60 after a long battle with pancreatic cancer.
