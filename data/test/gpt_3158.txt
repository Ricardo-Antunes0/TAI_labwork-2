The Cozzens House Hotel, later known as the Carlton House Hotel, was a grand hotel on the Strand in London. It was built in 1827 by George Cozzens and was originally known as the Carlton House.  The hotel became famous for its ballroom, which hosted many of the most prestigious society events of the day. The hotel also had a casino, a theatre, and a restaurant. It closed in 1939 after years of decline and was demolished in 1958.
