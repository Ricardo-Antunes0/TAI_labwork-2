The Declaration of Sports (also known as the Saint-Simon Declaration) is a declaration written by French philosopher, thinker, and political economist Auguste Comte in 1851. The declaration calls for the establishment of an international Olympic Games.  The Declaration of Sports is a declaration written by French philosopher, thinker, and political economist Auguste Comte in 1851. The declaration calls for the establishment of an international Olympic Games. It was first presented at the First International Congress of Sport in Paris that year.
