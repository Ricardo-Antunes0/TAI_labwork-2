Rodoljub "Roćko" Čolaković (; 7 June 1900 – 24 October 1944) was a Croatian general who played a major role in the Axis occupation of Yugoslavia during World War II. After the war, he was sentenced to death by the Yugoslavian communist government, but he escaped to Argentina and died in obscurity.  Čolaković was born in 1900 in the town of Vukovar, then part of the Austro-Hungarian Empire. He graduated from the Military School in Zagreb in 1923 and served with distinction during World War II, notably as an artillery officer during the Battle of the River Sutjište. Čolaković played a major role in the Axis occupation of Yugoslavia, serving as Deputy Chief of Staff for Military Affairs and commander of the Croatian Military District. He was also briefly Minister of Defence in 1941.  After the war, Čolaković was tried by Yugoslavia's communist government and sentenced to death for his role in the occupation. He escaped to Argentina, where he died in obscurity in 1964.
