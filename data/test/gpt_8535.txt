{{Infobox artist | name           | image = [[File:Fidelia-Bridges.jpg|200px]]  Fidelia Bridges (born 1933) is an American painter, sculptor, and printmaker. She is best known for her installation art, often using materials such as paper and thread to create installations that explore the boundaries between sculpture, painting, and architecture.  Bridges was born in 1933 in Providence, Rhode Island. After receiving her BFA from the Rhode Island School of Design in 1957, she studied at the Yale School of Art from 1957 to 1959. In 1960 she received a grant from the Pollock-Krasner Foundation to study sculpture at the Staatliche Kunstschule in Dresden, Germany. Upon her return to the United States, she began working as an assistant to Robert Motherwell at the Whitney Museum of American Art from 1961 to 1963. In 1969 she was awarded a Guggenheim Fellowship, which allowed her to spend two years traveling in Europe and Africa.  Bridges is most well known for her installation art, which often explores the boundaries between sculpture, painting, and architecture. Her work has been exhibited at the Museum of Modern Art in New York City, the Tate Gallery in London, and the National Gallery of Canada in Ottawa.
