Terry Cryer (24 June 1934 – 15 January 2018) was an English actor, best known for his roles as Arthur Daley in the television series M*A*S*H, and in the films Some Like It Hot and The Crying Game.  Cryer was born in 1934 in Bury, Lancashire, the son of Marguerite (née O'Brien) and Reginald Cryer. His father was an inspector with the Lancashire Fire Brigade. Cryer attended Bury Grammar School before serving his National Service with the Royal Air Force. After leaving the RAF, he studied at RADA.  Cryer made his professional stage debut in 1957 at the Old Vic Theatre in a production of "The Entertainer". He then appeared in productions at London's Royal Court Theatre and at Stratford-upon-Avon's Royal Shakespeare Festival before landing his first television role, as Sergeant Jim McCoy in the medical drama M*A*S*H. Cryer remained with M*A*S*H for eleven seasons from 1972 to 1982. He also appeared in several films during this time, including Some Like It Hot (1959), The Apartment (1960), The Three Musketeers (1961), The Nun's Story (1965), The Towering Inferno (1974), and The Crying Game (1992).
