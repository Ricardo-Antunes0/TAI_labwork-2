Rick Emerson (born March 7, 1973), formerly of The Rick Emerson Band, is an American singer-songwriter, musician and record producer.  Emerson was born in Baltimore, Maryland. He initially pursued a career as a musician, playing in various bands before forming the Rick Emerson Band in 1997. The band released two albums before splitting up in 2002. Emerson then began to focus on his solo career, releasing two albums and touring extensively before signing to Atlantic Records in 2010. His debut album for the label, "The Lost Highway", was released in 2012 and reached number 14 on the "Billboard" 200 chart. Emerson's second album, "Last Days Here", was released in 2016 and reached number 4 on the Billboard 200 chart.  Emerson has won a Grammy Award for Best Contemporary Folk Album for his work on the album "The Weight of These Wings" (2013). He was also nominated for a Grammy Award for Best New Artist in 2001.
