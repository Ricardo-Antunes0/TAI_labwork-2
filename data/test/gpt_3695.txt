Boisdale is a town in the Central Highlands region of Victoria, Australia. The town is located in the Shire of Central Gippsland, 120 kilometres south-east of the state capital Melbourne and 50 kilometres north-west of the town of Morwell. At the 2006 census, Boisdale had a population of 2,872.  Boisdale was founded in 1882 as a railway siding on the Morwell to Bairnsdale railway line and was initially known as Boisdale Junction. The town was renamed in honour of Sir Archibald Boisdale, Governor of Victoria from 1881 to 1885. The town's main economic activity is forestry. The town has a primary school, a post office, a hotel, several cafes and restaurants and a small shopping centre.
