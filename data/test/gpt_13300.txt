Thomas William Osborne (born February 23, 1937) is a former American football player who was the head coach of the Nebraska Cornhuskers football team from 1981 to 1994. Osborne is also a former player and assistant coach at the collegiate level.  Tom Osborne was born on February 23, 1937, in Columbus, Ohio. He attended Columbus North High School and played college football at the University of Nebraska. Osborne was a two-time consensus All-American and was inducted into the College Football Hall of Fame in 1986. He won the Maxwell Award as the best player in college football in 1962 and 1964. Osborne was drafted by the Washington Redskins in the first round of the 1965 NFL Draft, but did not play in the NFL. He later played for the Kansas City Chiefs and Oakland Raiders of the American Football League (AFL).  Osborne began his coaching career as an assistant coach at his alma mater, Nebraska, under Bob Devaney from 1966 to 1976. Osborne then served as head coach at Miami (Ohio) from 1977 to 1980 and at Stanford from 1981 to 1994. In 1995, he became an assistant coach for the Tampa Bay Buccaneers of the National Football League (NFL). Osborne retired from coaching following the 1998 season.  Osborne is also a businessman. He founded Tom Osborne Companies, which manufactures roofing materials, in 1996.
