Marshal of Reno is a 1944 American Western film directed by George Marshall and starring Gene Autry, Roy Rogers, and Dale Evans. The film is about a lawman who must save the town of Reno from a group of outlaws.  The film is set in the Old West and features music and scenes of horseback riding. It was released by Paramount Pictures.  In Reno, Nevada, a group of outlaws led by Curly Bill Anderson has been terrorizing the townspeople. Sheriff Luke Brody asks Marshal Tom Blake to help him take down the gang. Blake is a stern but fair lawman who is known for his skill with a gun. With the help of his deputy, Pete, Blake sets out to track down the outlaws and bring them to justice.
