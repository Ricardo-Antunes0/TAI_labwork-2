Strata is an American rock band from Los Angeles, California. Formed in 2004, the band consists of vocalist and guitarist Matt Smith, bassist and backing vocalist Davey Brown, drummer Anthony Vallejo and keyboardist Jesse Lacey.  Strata's self-titled debut album was released in 2006 through independent label Solid State Records. The album was produced by David Bendeth (System of a Down, Avenged Sevenfold) and received acclaim from music critics. After touring in support of the album, Strata signed to Century Media Records and released their second album, "The Great Dissolution", in 2009. The album was noted for its heavier sound and increased use of keyboards. The band toured in support of the album throughout 2009 and 2010 before releasing their third album, "The Human Abstract", in 2012. The album was again noted for its heavier sound and garnered the band their first major label record deal with A&M Records.   In 2013, Strata parted ways with A&M Records and released their fourth album, "Inception", through independent label Solid State Records. The album was met with mixed reviews from music critics but was well received by fans. In 2016, Strata announced they were breaking up after a decade of touring and recording.   Since 2007, Strata has been known for their energetic live performances which have been praised by fans and critics alike.
