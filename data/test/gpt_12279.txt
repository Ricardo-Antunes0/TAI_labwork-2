Bruce is a town and a locality in the Mid North region of South Australia. At the 2006 census, Bruce had a population of 737.  Bruce was founded in 1853 as a railway siding on the Adelaide-Port Augusta railway line. The town became an important wheat-growing area in the late 19th century, and was subsequently developed as a service centre for the surrounding agricultural district. It is now a small mainly residential town with a few commercial businesses.  The town is located on the Bruce Highway, about south of the regional capital of Port Pirie. The district surrounding Bruce is known for its fertile soils and extensive pastoral and agricultural production. The town is also home to the Mid North Regional Museum and the Bruce Historical Museum.
