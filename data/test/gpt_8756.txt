He is most well-known for his work on the Cognitive Therapy Analysis of Thinking and Feeling (CTATF) model.  Lottman was born in Brooklyn, New York to a Jewish family. After high school, he served in the United States Army during World War II. After the war, he attended the City College of New York, where he received his B.A. in 1949. He then attended the University of Chicago, where he received his M.A. in 1951 and his Ph.D. in 1955.  Lottman began his career as a psychoanalyst at the University of Chicago before switching to CBT in the early 1970s. He was a professor at UCLA from 1973 until his death in 2006. Throughout his career, Lottman published more than 250 papers and books on CBT and other psychological topics.
