Anuradha Kapur is an Indian theatre director and actor. She is the founder and Artistic Director of the Anuradha Kapur Theatre Workshop, which is based in Mumbai, India.  Born on 21 October 1966 in Mumbai, Kapur started her career as an actor in theatre productions. Her first play, "Agni", was produced by the National School of Drama (NSD) in 1992. Kapur later founded the Anuradha Kapur Theatre Workshop, which is based in Mumbai. The theatre workshop has produced several critically acclaimed plays, including "The Maids" (2004), "The Glass Menagerie" (2005), "The Merchant of Venice" (2009), and "Rosencrantz and Guildenstern Are Dead" (2012). Kapur has also played significant roles in Indian cinema, including in the films "A Wednesday" (2009) and "Tanu Weds Manu" (2015).
