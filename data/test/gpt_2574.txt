Huntington is a city in Baker County, Oregon, United States. The population was 2,058 at the 2010 census.  Huntington was originally called "Union" and under the name of that community for about a year, before it was renamed Huntington in 1881 after General Henry L. Huntington. The city's first post office was established in 1881.  The city is located at the junction of Oregon Route 199 and Oregon Route 219, about south of Grants Pass and east of Medford.
