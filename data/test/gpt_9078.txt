Marlin Tyrell Jackson (born June 30, 1983) is an American football linebacker for the Tennessee Titans of the National Football League (NFL). He was drafted by the Titans in the third round of the 2006 NFL Draft. He played college football at Oregon.  Jackson has also been a member of the Indianapolis Colts, New Orleans Saints, and Tampa Bay Buccaneers. Jackson has also been a member of the United States Olympic Team.  Jackson attended Wilson High School in Wilson, North Carolina. He was a standout linebacker and wide receiver for the Warriors, recording 256 tackles (158 solo) and 13 sacks during his senior season. He was also selected as a first-team all-state player and was named to the Charlotte Observer's All-Southern Team.  Jackson attended Oregon, where he played for the Oregon Ducks football team from 2001 to 2004. He finished his career with 368 tackles (237 solo), 11 interceptions, 49 passes defended, two forced fumbles and one fumble recovery. He was a two-time All-Pac-10 selection and a first-team All-American in 2004. Jackson is also Oregon's all-time leader in total tackles (1,811), solo tackles (1,311), interceptions (39), passes defended (77), forced fumbles (16), and fumble recoveries (14).
