Margaret Tracey (born March 28, 1967) is an American author and journalist.  Tracey was born in Boston, Massachusetts. After graduating from college, she worked as a journalist for "The Boston Globe" for eight years. She then moved to New York City and worked as a magazine editor for "O, The Oprah Magazine" for two years. Tracey then became a freelance writer and has written for "The New York Times Magazine", "Vanity Fair", "The Atlantic", and "Elle".  Tracey's first book, "Bee Season: A Novel", was published in 2002. Her second book, "The Great American Novel: A Genuinely Ambiguous History", was published in 2007. Her third book, "The Well-Tempered City: A Novel", was published in 2016.
