George Halford's Orchestra was a professional symphony orchestra based in England. It was founded by George Halford in 1936 and played until his death in 1978. The orchestra made many recordings, most of which are available on CD.   The orchestra was noted for its performances of British and European classical music, as well as its recordings of popular music. It toured extensively throughout Europe and the United States, making appearances at major venues such as the Royal Albert Hall and Carnegie Hall.   In 2002, the orchestra was merged with the London Symphony Orchestra to form the London Symphony Orchestra and Chorus.
