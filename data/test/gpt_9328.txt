The Documentary is the debut studio album by American rapper Kanye West. It was released on May 21, 2002, by Roc-A-Fella Records and Def Jam Recordings. Recording sessions for the album took place during 2001 and 2002 at various studios in New York City and Los Angeles. The album features guest appearances from Jay-Z, Common, Mos Def, Fugees member Wyclef Jean, and Bon Iver frontman Justin Vernon.  The album has been cited as a key influence on the hip hop genre, along with West's later work in fashion design and architecture. Upon its release, The Documentary received mostly positive reviews from music critics, who praised its experimental production and West's lyrical delivery. The album has sold over two million copies worldwide.
