James Credle (born February 7, 1945) is an American composer, conductor and music educator. He is the Charles Ives Professor of Music at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.  Credle was born in Evanston, Illinois, United States. He studied at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, where he earned his Bachelor of Music in 1967 and his Master of Music in 1968. Credle has composed a large body of music, including orchestral works, chamber music, choral works, solo works for piano and orchestra, and electronic music. His chamber music includes three string quartets (1973, 1978, 1984), two piano trios (1971, 1974), a cello sonata (1975), and a piano quintet (1983).
