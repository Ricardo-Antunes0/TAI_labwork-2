Esther Mae Scott (March 25, 1893 – October 16, 1981) was an American actress, singer and dancer. She appeared in more than 100 films and television shows during a career that began in the 1920s.  Scott was born in Springfield, Missouri, the daughter of John and Lizzie Scott. Her father was a carpenter and cabinetmaker. She started her theatrical career in vaudeville and then began appearing in Broadway productions. Her first major film role was in the 1937 drama "The Life of Emile Zola". In the 1940s she starred in several popular comedies, including "Too Many Girls" (1940), "The Uninvited" (1942), "The Lady Eve" (1942), "It's a Wonderful Life" (1946), and "My Favorite Wife" (1940).  Scott retired from acting in the early 1960s but continued to perform as a singer and dancer. She died at age 86 in Los Angeles.
