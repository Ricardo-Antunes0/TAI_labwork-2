"Freaky Deaky" is a song recorded by the American rock band Kiss. It was written by Gene Simmons, Paul Stanley and Ace Frehley and released in 1976 as the third single from the band's album Destroyer.  "Freaky Deaky" is a heavy metal song with a bassline and guitar work that features a drum solo in the middle of the song. The song tells the story of a man who is obsessed with a woman and how he tries to win her over. The song has been noted by music critics for its complex composition.  "Freaky Deaky" was successful on the charts, reaching number nine on the "Billboard" Hot 100 chart and number two on the Modern Rock Tracks chart. The song also reached number one on the Canadian RPM charts. The song was nominated for a Grammy Award for Best Hard Rock Performance, but lost to Black Sabbath's "Iron Man".
