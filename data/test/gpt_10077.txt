Prodelaca myodes is a moth in the family Crambidae. It was described by Turner in 1908. It is found in Australia, where it has been recorded from New South Wales.  The wingspan is about 20Â mm. The forewings are uniform light ochreous-yellow, with a small black spot just before the middle and a larger one at three-fourths, as well as a series of blackish dots around the costa and termen. There is also a blackish dot on the costa before the apex. The hindwings are light ochreous-yellow, with a small black dot at the base and two more just beyond the anal angle.
