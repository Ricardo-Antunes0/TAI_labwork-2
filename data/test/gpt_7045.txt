Zhang Yuzhe (; 16 February 1902 – 10 September 1985) was a Chinese writer, translator, critic and educator.  Zhang was born in 1902 in Suzhou, Jiangsu province. He graduated from the University of Beijing with a degree in law in 1925. He began his writing career in 1929 with the short story "Qianyan" (霞山), which won first prize in the Jiangsu Writers' Association's annual literary competition.  Zhang's breakthrough came with the novel "The Story of Qiu Ju" (邱嘉玉片), which was published in 1938 and became a bestseller. The novel tells the story of a poor peasant woman named Qiu Ju who is transformed by her love for a wealthy man into something akin to a ghost. The book is widely regarded as one of the greatest works of Chinese literature and has been translated into several languages.  Zhang Yuzhe served as president of the Chinese PEN Club from 1949 to 1951 and as president of the Writers' Association of China from 1954 to 1955. He was also a member of the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences and the Chinese Academy of Arts. Zhang died in 1985.
