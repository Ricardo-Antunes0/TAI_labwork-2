Edward John Ziemba (born May 2, 1932) is an American mathematician and computer scientist, known for his work in theoretical computer science and mathematical physics.  Ziemba was born in the Bronx, New York City. He attended the City College of New York (CCNY), where he earned his B.S. in 1955 and his M.S. in 1956. He then went to the University of California, Berkeley, where he earned his Ph.D. in 1960, under the supervision of Julian Bigelow and Norbert Wiener.  After completing his doctoral studies, Ziemba worked at AT&T Bell Laboratories for two years before joining the University of Utah as an assistant professor in 1962. He was promoted to full professor there in 1970, and retired from Utah in 1999. He is currently a Distinguished Research Professor at the University of Utah School of Computing.  Ziemba has been a Fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences since 1984, a Fellow of the American Mathematical Society since 1985, and a Fellow of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers since 1989. He has also been awarded honorary doctorates from Brown University (1985), Columbia University (1989), Rutgers University (1996), and Utah Valley University (1998).
