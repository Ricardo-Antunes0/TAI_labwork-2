British–Polish relations are the bilateral relations between the United Kingdom and Poland. Both nations are members of the European Union and NATO. The two countries have a long history of diplomatic and trade relations dating back to the 16th century.  The United Kingdom is Poland's seventh-largest trade partner with bilateral trade totalling £11.5 billion in 2016. The United Kingdom is also Poland's second-largest foreign investor, with investment totaling £7.5 billion in 2016. The two nations have a strong relationship based on shared values such as democracy, freedom, rule of law, and human rights. The United Kingdom has been a strong advocate for Polish membership in the European Union and NATO, and has supported Polish efforts to join the World Trade Organization.  The United Kingdom is also a major donor to international organisations such as the World Health Organization, the United Nations Development Programme, and the International Monetary Fund. British Prime Minister Theresa May visited Poland in July 2017 to reaffirm the UK's support for Polish membership in the European Union and NATO, as well as its commitment to helping Poland develop economically.
