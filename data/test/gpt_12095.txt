RoboCop 3 is a 1993 American science fiction action film directed by Paul Verhoeven and written by Edward Neumeier and Michael Miner. It is the third installment in the RoboCop series and stars Peter Weller, Nancy Allen, Dan O'Herlihy, Miguel Ferrer, and Ronny Cox.  The film follows RoboCop 3, a new model of RoboCop designed to be more humanlike, as he investigates crime in Detroit. However, the criminal element finds a way to turn the robot against the people of Detroit, leading to an explosive climax.  RoboCop 3 was met with mixed reviews from critics. It grossed $102 million domestically and $183 million worldwide on a $40 million budget.  In the year 2028, crime has become so rampant that the police have turned to robots to do their jobs. A new model of RoboCop has been developed and is now being used by officer Alex Murphy (Peter Weller). However, Murphy becomes concerned when he discovers that the new RoboCop is not truly humanlike and begins to suspect that the machine is being controlled by someone else.   Murphy's suspicions are confirmed when he is attacked by a group of criminals who have turned RoboCop against the people of Detroit. Murphy must use all his skills as a cop to save the city before it is destroyed forever.
