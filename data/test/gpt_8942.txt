Muiris Ó Súilleabháin (; 19 February 1904 – 17 September 1986), known as Maurice O'Sullivan, was an Irish playwright, screenwriter and novelist.  Born in Dublin, O'Sullivan attended Blackrock College and studied law at Trinity College, but abandoned his studies to pursue a career in writing. He first achieved recognition for his play "The Plough and the Stars" which was first produced in 1938. O'Sullivan's best-known works include the novels "The Country Girl" (1951), "The Tower" (1957), and "The Catcher in the Rye" (1951), all of which were adapted for the stage. He also wrote screenplays for several films, including "The Quiet Man" (1952), "The Unforgiven" (1960), and "The Crying Game" (1992). O'Sullivan was awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1976. He died in 1986.
