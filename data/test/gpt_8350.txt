Anne Lotta Swärd (born 16 February 1969) is a Swedish musician, singer and songwriter. She is the lead singer of the rock band The Cardigans, and has released six studio albums with the band.  Swärd was born in Umeå, Sweden, to a musical family. Her father, Ulf Swärd, is a folk musician and her mother, Karin, is a singer and actress. Swärd was educated at Umeå School of Arts and Crafts and started her musical career in 1990 as the lead singer of the pop-rock band Honeycombs. The group disbanded in 1995. In 1996, Swärd formed the rock band The Cardigans with guitarist Malin Berglund and bassist Emil Hellström. The group has released six studio albums: For What It's Worth (1997), First Time (2000), Life (2003), My Heart Skips a Beat (2007), Cardigan (2011) and Tomorrow's Hits (2016). In 2009, she won the Swedish Grammy Award for Best Female Artist.
