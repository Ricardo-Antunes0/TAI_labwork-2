Fred Nitz (born 2 July 1946) is an American composer and conductor. He is the founder and music director of the Fred Nitz conducting ensemble and the artistic director of the Fred Nitz Chamber Music Festival.  Nitz was born in Boston, Massachusetts, to a Jewish family. He studied at the Peabody Conservatory of Music in Baltimore, Maryland, where he received his Bachelor of Music degree in 1967. Nitz then studied at the University of Vienna under Otto Neumann and Karlheinz Stockhausen, where he earned his Master of Music degree in 1971.  Nitz returned to the United States, where he worked as a freelance composer and conductor. He founded the Fred Nitz conducting ensemble in 1987 and served as its music director until 2003. He also founded the Fred Nitz Chamber Music Festival in 1994 and served as its artistic director until 2003.   Nitz has composed works for orchestra, chamber music, film, and video games. His orchestral works include "Songs of Innocence and Experience", "The Seasons", "The Harmonielehre", "The Infinite Conversation", and "Canticles". His chamber music includes "Concerto for Violin, String Orchestra and Percussion" (1978), "Double Concerto for Violin, String Orchestra and Percussion" (1985), "Trio for Violin, Viola and Cello" (1994), and "Sonata for Violin, String Orchestra and Harpsichord" (2007
