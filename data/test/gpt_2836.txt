Chatham University is a private university in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, United States. It was founded in 1854 as the Pittsburgh Academy of the Fine Arts and was renamed Chatham College in 1892. The university has approximately 3,500 students and offers bachelor's, master's and doctoral degrees in a range of disciplines.  Chatham University is ranked as a Tier 1 institution by the US News & World Report. The Carnegie Foundation for the Advancement of Teaching ranks Chatham University as one of the top 25 liberal arts colleges in the United States. Chatham is also a member of the Association of American Universities.
