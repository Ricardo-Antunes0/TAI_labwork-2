The Kensington Soldiers' Monument is a historic war memorial located in Kensington Market, Toronto, Ontario, Canada. Dedicated on November 11, 1921, it is jointly administered by the City of Toronto and the Kensington Market Business Improvement Area.  The monument is composed of a granite base surmounted by a bronze statue of a mounted soldier commemorating the men of the Canadian Expeditionary Force who died in World War I. The monument was designed by Toronto architect Alfred Bettman and executed by the Whyte & Mackay sculptors.  The monument was dedicated on November 11, 1921, to honour the memory of the 1,688 Canadian soldiers who died in the First World War. It was unveiled by Sir Robert Borden, then Prime Minister of Canada.
