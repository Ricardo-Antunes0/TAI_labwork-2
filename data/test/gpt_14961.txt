Guddal Church  is a parish church located in the town of Guddal, in the Diocese of Jharkhand, India. The church is dedicated to the patron saint of Jharkhand, Saint Bartholomew.  The present church was built in 1883 by the missionary Reverend William Carey. The foundation stone was laid by the then Governor General of India, Lord Ripon on 6 August 1883. The church was consecrated by the then Bishop of Calcutta, William Carey on 10 September 1884.  The church has a seating capacity of about 1000 people. It is also used for special occasions such as marriages and christenings.
