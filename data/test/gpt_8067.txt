The following events occurred in April 1961:  The Cuban Missile Crisis began on April 12, 1961, when the Soviet Union placed nuclear missiles in Cuba. The United States responded with a naval blockade of Cuba. The crisis ended on October 28, 1962, after the Soviet Union agreed to remove the missiles and dismantle the Cuban missile program.  The Berlin Crisis began on November 9, 1961, when the Soviet Union demanded that East Berlin be given full independence and access to the West. West Berlin, which was under Western control, was not willing to give up its status as an autonomous city. The crisis ended on September 12, 1963, when the two Germanys agreed to a treaty that divided Berlin into two zones.
