John Robert Risher Jr. (September 23, 1938 – December 2, 2015) was an American businessman who became the chairman and chief executive officer (CEO) of McDonald's Corporation.  Risher was born in 1938 in San Bernardino, California, to John Robert Risher Sr. and his wife, the former Gertrude Esther (née Moskowitz). He attended San Bernardino Valley College and the University of Utah, where he received a B.S. in business administration in 1960.  Risher joined McDonald's in 1961 as a restaurant manager. He became president of McDonald's USA in 1978 and CEO in 1984. During his tenure, he modernized the company by introducing franchising, introducing new menu items such as the McChicken sandwich and Happy Meals, and making changes to the company's business model. He also oversaw the successful turnaround of McDonald's Japan during his tenure as CEO from 1988 to 1992. In 1996, Risher was awarded an honorary Doctor of Laws degree from Utah Valley University.  Risher retired from his position as CEO on July 1, 2000. He remained chairman of the board until 2003 and served on the company's board of directors until his death. He was also a member of the President's Council on Jobs and Competitiveness (2001–2003).  Risher died at his home in Napa County, California, on December 2, 2015, aged 80.
