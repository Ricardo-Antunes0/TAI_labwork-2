The Gospel of Matthias is a  gospel text probably composed in the mid-1st century CE. Its text reflects the beliefs of the early Jesus movement and is a precursor to the canonical gospels.  The Gospel of Matthias is written in a simple and straightforward style, with few of the elaborations and mythological elements found in other gospels. It is believed to have been composed in Aramaic, and it depicts Jesus as a teacher who preaches repentance and salvation through faith in God.
