Born in Soweto, Gigaba attended the prestigious Kingsway School in Johannesburg, where he was headboy. He then went on to study law at the University of South Africa, where he became involved in student politics and was elected to the executive of the university's student representative council. He also worked as a research associate at the University of Johannesburg's Centre for Constitutional and Development Studies, where he wrote a thesis on the role of trade unions in post-apartheid South Africa.  Gigaba's legal career began in 1997, when he was appointed as a junior advocate with the Pretoria-based firm Dali Tambo & Company. He later became a senior advocate with the same firm, and was appointed as a deputy attorney general in 2004. In 2006, he was appointed as the Minister of Home Affairs, serving until 2009.  In 2009, Gigaba was elected as the Mayor of Johannesburg, defeating incumbent Amos Masondo by just over 1% of the vote. During his time as mayor, Gigaba intensified efforts to combat crime and corruption in Johannesburg, while also introducing new initiatives such as free Wi-Fi access in all public areas and improved public transport.
