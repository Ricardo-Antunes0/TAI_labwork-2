He was one of the Eight Elders of the Communist Party of China.  Wang Guoquan was born in 1911 in Fenyang, Anhui province. He joined the Communist Party of China in 1931 and fought in the Chinese Civil War, serving as a political commissar and battalion commander. After the war, he served as the Deputy Governor of Jilin province from 1949 to 1952. He then served as Deputy Governor of Henan province from 1952 to 1954, and Deputy Director of the Political Department of the Central Military Commission from 1954 to 1955. He was then promoted to Director of the Political Department of the Central Military Commission, a position he held until 1965.  In 1965, Wang Guoquan was appointed Deputy Commander of the PLA, a position he held until 1976. During his time as Deputy Commander, Wang Guoquan played an important role in overseeing military operations in the Sino-Vietnamese War, the Cultural Revolution, and the Vietnam War. He died on July 18, 2004.
