Antonio Manuel Lima Dias (February 22, 1944 – December 20, 2009) was a Portuguese-born Brazilian bishop of the Roman Catholic Church.  Dias was born in Lisbon, Portugal, to Manuel Dias and Maria Lima. He emigrated to Brazil with his family at the age of five and grew up in São Paulo. He studied at the Pontifical Catholic University of São Paulo, from where he obtained a licentiate in philosophy. He was ordained to the priesthood on April 16, 1969.  Dias served as secretary to the Archbishop of São Paulo from 1971 to 1974 and then as director of religious education for the archdiocese from 1974 to 1976. He then served as pastor of the parish of Santo Amaro da Purificação from 1976 to 1980. Dias was named auxiliary bishop of São Paulo on February 12, 1980 and was consecrated on April 16 of that year. He served as bishop of São Paulo until his death.
