He served in both the Korean and Vietnam War, as well as in numerous other assignments.  Ardisana was born in 1924 in Blainville, Quebec, Canada. He attended the United States Military Academy at West Point, New York and graduated with the Class of 1947. After commissioning as a second lieutenant, he served in the infantry during the Korean War. He later served in Vietnam, where he was wounded twice. After his return to duty, he served as an operations officer and then as a battalion commander in the 101st Airborne Division (Airmobile). He later served as a staff officer at the Pentagon and then as the commanding general of Fort Bragg. He retired from the Army in 1978.  After his retirement from the Army, Ardisana became a resident fellow at the Hoover Institution at Stanford University. He died on February 21, 1985.
