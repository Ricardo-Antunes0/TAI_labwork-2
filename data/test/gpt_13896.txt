USS Kingman (APB-47) was a self-propelled barracks boat acquired by the United States Navy for use as a training vessel. She was named for Kingman, Arizona.  Kingman was constructed in 1944 as a self-propelled barracks boat by the Zenith Shipbuilding Corporation in Duluth, Minnesota. The vessel was acquired by the United States Navy in 1945 and assigned to the Naval Training Center at Great Lakes, Illinois. She served as a training vessel until 1959, when she was transferred to the Naval Base Ventura County at Port Hueneme, California. She was sold for scrap in 1982.
