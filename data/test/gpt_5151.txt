Donna Bailey is an American politician and a member of the Democratic Party. She has served in the United States House of Representatives since 2013, representing Texas's 23rd congressional district.  Bailey was born in Houston, Texas, to parents who worked in education. She attended the University of Houston, where she earned a degree in political science and international relations. After college, Bailey worked as a staffer for then-state senator John Whitmire and then for U.S. Representative Gene Green.  In 2011, Bailey won a seat on the Houston City Council, serving until her election to Congress in 2013. During her time on the City Council, Bailey chaired the powerful Budget and Fiscal Affairs Committee and was an outspoken advocate for economic development and affordable housing.  In Congress, Bailey has been an outspoken advocate for environmental justice and affordable housing. She has also been a leader on health care reform, serving on the Committee on Energy and Commerce and the Select Committee on Benghazi.
