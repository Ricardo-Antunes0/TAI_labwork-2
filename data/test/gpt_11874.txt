Dennis L. Jenkins is a research archaeologist, author, and lecturer who specializes in Mesoamerican archaeology and archaeology of the Gulf Coast region. He is currently a Research Associate at the University of Alabama at Birmingham. Jenkins has also served as an adjunct professor at the University of South Alabama, Lynn University, and the University of West Florida.  Jenkins has written and edited several books on Mesoamerican archaeology, including "The Pre-Columbian World: Ancient Mesoamerica" (1995), "2000 B.C.: The New World" (1998), and "Ancient Maya Civilization" (2003). He has also published articles in journals such as "Mesoamerican Archaeology", "Journal of Field Archaeology", and "PLOS One".  Jenkins was born in Birmingham, Alabama, in 1961. He received his bachelor's degree from Auburn University in 1983 and his master's degree from the University of Tennessee in 1984. He received his doctorate from the University of Alabama in 1992.
