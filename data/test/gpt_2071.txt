Njáls saga (modern Icelandic pronunciation: ) (also known as Njáls saga hins Breiðablik, Njáls saga of the White Horse or The Saga of the White Horse) is a long Icelandic saga dating from the 14th century. It tells the story of Niels, a young man who is brought up by an uncle after his mother's death and then becomes embroiled in a series of adventures.  The saga is notable for its intricate and often violent plot, its colourful descriptions of medieval Iceland, and the vivid characterisations of its protagonists. It has been called "the Icelandic Iliad".
