The Ollis-class ferries are a trio of passenger ferries built in the United Kingdom in the late 1990s. The vessels have a design based on the earlier Britannia-class ferries, but with updated interior facilities and a new livery.  The Ollis-class ferries were built by Swan Hunter at Wallsend, Tyne and Wear for Caledonian MacBrayne. Launched in 1999, they were the first new ferries to be built in the United Kingdom for over twenty years. The vessels entered service on the Hebridean ferry routes in 2000.
