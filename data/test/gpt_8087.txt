Kayvan Najarian is an Iranian-American scientist, who is currently a managing director at investment bank Susquehanna International Group. Najarian has also served as the president and CEO of Research Affiliates, a hedge fund he co-founded in 1994.  Najarian was born in Tehran, Iran, in 1961. He received his B.S. in chemical engineering from the University of Tehran in 1984, and his M.S. and Ph.D. in chemical engineering from the University of California, Berkeley, in 1987 and 1991, respectively.  Najarian worked at Dow Corning Corporation from 1987 to 1994, where he was a research scientist and manager of research and development projects. In 1994, Najarian co-founded Research Affiliates LLC, a hedge fund. He served as its president and CEO until 2003, when he was replaced by Anthony Scaramucci, who later became the president of the Trump Administration's Export Council.  From 2003 to 2006, Najarian served as a senior vice president at Lehman Brothers Holdings Inc., where he was responsible for managing Lehman Brothers' global equities business. From 2006 to 2009, Najarian served as an executive vice president at Citadel Investment Group LLC., a robo-advisor firm.  In 2009, Najarian joined the board of directors of Square Inc., a mobile payments company.
