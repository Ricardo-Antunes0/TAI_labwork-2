Linnea Carlson Ehri, Ph.D. is an American cognitive scientist, currently the James McKeen Cattell Associate Professor of Psychological and Brain Sciences at Vanderbilt University. She is known for her work on cognitive neuroscience and for her development of the Theory of Planned Behavior.  Ehri was born in Detroit, Michigan, in 1971. She received her B.S. from the University of Minnesota in 1991, and her Ph.D. from the University of California, Berkeley, in 1997. She was a postdoctoral fellow at the University of Utah from 1997 to 1999, and then a research associate at the University of California, Davis, from 1999 to 2002.  Ehri has been a faculty member at Vanderbilt since 2002. Her research focuses on cognitive neuroscience and the theory of planned behavior. She has published several books, including The Cognitive Neuropsychology of Memory (2008), Applying Cognitive Psychology to Business (2012), and When Things Go Wrong: Making Sense of Randomness (2013).
