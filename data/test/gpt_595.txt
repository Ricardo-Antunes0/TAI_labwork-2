Solanum chacoense is a species of wild potato found in the Argentine provinces of Chaco and Formosa. The plant is a perennial herb that grows up to 1 meter tall. The leaves are ovate-oblong, with a wavy margin, and are covered in short hairs. The flowers are white, with a yellow center, and are arranged in racemes. The fruit is a red or black berry that is about 2 centimeters in diameter.
