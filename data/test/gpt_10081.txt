Edward Mansvelt or Mansfield (fl. 1659-1666) was an English lawyer and politician who sat in the House of Commons between 1659 and 1666.  Mansvelt was the son of Sir John Mansfield of Clopton, Yorkshire and his wife Frances, daughter of George Booth of Shugborough. He was admitted at Gray's Inn in 1641 and became a bencher in 1654. In 1659, he was elected Member of Parliament for Nottinghamshire. He was re-elected MP for Nottinghamshire in 1661 and for York in 1665. Mansvelt died in 1666 aged about 45.
