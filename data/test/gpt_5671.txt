Rennard James Strickland  was  a Canadian professional ice hockey player who played in the National Hockey League (NHL) for the Quebec Nordiques, Edmonton Oilers, and Vancouver Canucks. He was selected by the Nordiques in the first round, fourth overall, of the 1984 NHL Entry Draft.  Strickland won the Stanley Cup with the Oilers in 1987, and also played in the NHL All-Star Game that season. He is also a two-time winner of the Lady Byng Memorial Trophy as the league's most gentlemanly player. Strickland played his entire career with Edmonton.  After retiring as a player, Strickland served as an assistant coach with Anaheim Ducks and Detroit Red Wings before becoming head coach of the Manitoba Moose of the American Hockey League (AHL) in 2010. Strickland was fired from his position with Manitoba after just four games in 2012.
