Martin Allen Pasetta (June 16, 1932 – October 1, 2009) was an American professional baseball player. A left-handed pitcher, he played in Major League Baseball for the Detroit Tigers (1957), Chicago White Sox (1958), and Philadelphia Phillies (1959).  Pasetta was born in Brooklyn, New York, and attended Brooklyn Technical High School. He signed with the Tigers as an amateur free agent in 1952, and made his major league debut with them in 1957. He finished his career with the Phillies, appearing in 148 games over three seasons. Pasetta was a member of the American League All-Star team in 1958 and 1959. He also won a World Series championship with the Phillies in 1964.  After his playing career ended, Pasetta became a pitching coach for the Detroit Tigers (1965–1967) and Chicago White Sox (1968–1969). He later served as a scout for the Baltimore Orioles (1970–1982) and Philadelphia Phillies (1983–1989). Pasetta died at his home in South River, New Jersey, aged 80.
