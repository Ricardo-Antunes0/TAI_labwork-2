Abū'l-Najm Badr ibn ʿAbdallāh al-Jamālī al-Mustanṣirī, better known as Badr al-Jamālī, was a Muslim theologian, jurist, and political theorist who lived in the 11th century. He was one of the most influential Islamic thinkers of his time.  Badr al-Jamali was born in Baghdad in 1085 CE to a family of jurists. He began his studies in Islamic theology and jurisprudence at an early age and soon became one of the leading religious scholars of his time. He also became well-known for his political thought, which focused on the need for a strong government in order to maintain Islamic law and protect the rights of its citizens. Badr al-Jamali died in 1137 CE.
