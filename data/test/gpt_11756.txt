The Battle of Sulukh was fought between Armenian and Ottoman forces in the Sulukh River Valley in the present-day southeastern Turkey on 3-4 August 1914. It was the first engagement of the World War I Armenian Genocide. The Ottomans massacred over 20,000 Armenians in the battle, while an equal number were killed by Armenian fighters.
