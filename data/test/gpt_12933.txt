The 1955 NBA draft was the ninth annual draft of the National Basketball Association. The draft was held on June 21, 1955 at the New York State Theater in New York City. The draft was televised by NBC.  The draft consisted of 14 teams, with the first three picks in each round receiving a first-round draft pick, a second-round draft pick, and a third-round draft pick. The remaining picks were then traded between the teams.  The Philadelphia Warriors selected Wilt Chamberlain with the first overall pick, followed by the Minneapolis Lakers selecting Pat Riley with the second overall pick and the Syracuse Nationals selecting Bob McAdoo with the third overall pick.
