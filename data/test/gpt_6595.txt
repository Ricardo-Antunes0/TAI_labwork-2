Jake E. Lee (born Jakey Lou Williams, October 3, 1937) is an American musician, singer, songwriter and record producer, who is best known as the lead guitarist for the rock band The Eagles from 1971 to 1980. He has also been a member of John Fogerty's Creedence Clearwater Revival and the Allman Brothers Band.  Born in Shreveport, Louisiana, Lee moved to San Francisco in the early 1960s to become a musician. He was a founding member of the rock band The Eagles, which was formed in 1971. The Eagles achieved international success with their first album, "Eagles", which sold over 30 million copies. They went on to have several more successful albums, including "Hotel California", "The Long Run", and "The Very Best of the Eagles". In 1980, the band disbanded due to creative differences.  Lee has also been a member of John Fogerty's Creedence Clearwater Revival and the Allman Brothers Band. He has released several solo albums and has worked with a range of other artists, including Aretha Franklin, Joe Walsh, B.B. King, and Taj Mahal. He has been inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame twice, as a member of The Eagles and as a solo artist.
