He was the recipient of three National Film Awards, eight Filmfare Awards and two Padma Bhushan awards.  Khanna was born on 6 October 1946 in a Punjabi Sikh family in Amritsar, Punjab, British India to Gurdial Singh Khanna and Surinder Kaur. After completing his schooling from Government Senior Secondary School Amritsar and University of Punjab, he began his acting career in 1969 with the Hindi film "Chandni". He achieved international recognition with the thriller film "Awaara" (1976), for which he received the Golden Globe Award for Best Foreign Actor.   Khanna's subsequent roles in films such as "Deewaar" (1975), "Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge" (1995), "Kuch Kuch Hota Hai" (1998), "Kahaani" (2006) and "Raees" (2017) made him one of the most popular and respected actors in Hindi cinema. In 2007, he portrayed the Hindu god Rama in the epic Ramayana television series, which was a critical and ratings success.
