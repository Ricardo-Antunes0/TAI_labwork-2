Yıldırım Tuğrul Türkeş (born 1 December 1954) is a Turkish politician who has been the Prime Minister of Turkey since August 2014. Previously he was the Minister of Interior from 2003 to 2014.  Tuğrul Türkeş was born on 1 December 1954 in Gaziantep, Turkey. He completed his primary and secondary education in Gaziantep. He then studied at the Ankara University Faculty of Political Sciences and graduated with a degree in political science in 1978.  Tuğrul Türkeş began his career as a member of the Felicity Party, which he left in 1984 to join the National Salvation Party (MHP). He served as MHP's deputy chairman from 1984 to 1988, and was elected as the party's chairman in 1988. In 1991, he left the MHP to join the newly formed Republican People's Party (CHP).  In 1993, Tuğrul Türkeş was appointed Minister of the Interior by Prime Minister Necmettin Erbakan. He served in this role until 1996, when he was appointed Deputy Prime Minister and Minister for Regional Development. In 1998, Tuğrul Türkeş was appointed Deputy Prime Minister and Minister for Foreign Affairs. In 2003, he was appointed Minister of Interior by Prime Minister Recep Tayyip Erdoğan. He served in this role until 2014, when he was appointed Prime Minister by Erdoğan.  Tuğrul Türkeş is married and has two children.
