Camp Polk was a former army camp in Polk County, Oregon, United States. The camp was located near the town of Camp Douglas. It operated from 1903 to 1907, and then from 1909 to 1913.  The camp was named for U.S. Senator James K. Polk of Tennessee. The camp was used to house soldiers who were stationed at Fort Douglas in downtown Portland. The soldiers were responsible for maintaining the property and grounds of the fort.
