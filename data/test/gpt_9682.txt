Nick Stabile  (born March 4, 1971) is an American comedian, actor, and writer. He is known for his work on The Tonight Show with Jay Leno, Late Night with Conan O'Brien, as well as his own eponymous Nick Stabile Show on Comedy Central.  Stabile was born in Canoga Park, California, to an Italian-American family. He attended college at Pepperdine University and then moved to New York City to pursue a career in stand-up comedy. After a few years of performing in clubs around the city, Stabile landed a regular spot on The Tonight Show with Jay Leno in 1998. He remained with the show until 2003, when he left to join Late Night with Conan O'Brien. Stabile also appeared on The Daily Show with Jon Stewart and became one of the show's most popular guests.  In 2006, Stabile created and starred in his own television series, the Nick Stabile Show, which aired on Comedy Central for two seasons. In 2009, he published his memoir, True Story: A Memoir of My Life As a Stand-Up comic. Stabile currently resides in Los Angeles.
