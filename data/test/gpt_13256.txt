Freddie King (September 3, 1934December 28, 1976) was an American blues guitarist and singer. He was one of the most influential blues guitarists of the 1960s and 1970s, and is considered one of the greatest electric blues guitarists of all time.  Freddie King was born in Montgomery, Alabama on September 3, 1934. He learned to play the blues guitar from his father, a musician with a wide repertoire who also taught King how to play the harmonica. In 1955, at the age of 16, King moved to Los Angeles, California and started playing in local clubs. He soon became known for his powerful blues guitar playing and soulful singing.  In the early 1960s, King collaborated with various blues musicians, including B.B. King and Muddy Waters. His 1965 album, "Freddie King", was a major hit and established him as one of the most influential electric blues guitarists of his era. In 1968, he released his most successful album to date, "Born Under a Bad Sign", which was certified platinum by the RIAA.  King suffered from alcoholism and drug abuse throughout his life, and died in 1976 at the age of 42. However, his music continues to be popular among blues fans worldwide.
