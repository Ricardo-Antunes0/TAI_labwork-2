Karin Schaupp (born 1972) is a German-born artist who lives and works in London. Schaupp's practice spans painting, sculpture, video, and performance.  Karin Schaupp was born in 1972 in Hamburg, Germany. After graduating from the Academy of Fine Arts, Hamburg in 1996, she moved to London, where she has lived and worked since. Schaupp's practice spans painting, sculpture, video, and performance. Her work often investigates the boundaries between the physical and the digital worlds and explores the ethical implications of surveillance technologies. Recent exhibitions include The Invisible Committee at Serpentine Gallery (London), We Are Legion at Centre Pompidou (Paris), and Exposure: New Photography from the 21st Century at MUMOK (Graz).
