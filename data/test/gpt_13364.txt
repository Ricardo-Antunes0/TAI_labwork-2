Sir Richard Richards  (5 November 1752 â€“ 18 October 1828) was an English judge.  Richards was born in London the son of James Richards, a solicitor. He was educated at Charterhouse School and Christ Church, Oxford, where he read Law. He became a barrister in 1776 and a bencher of the Inner Temple in 1793.   Richards was appointed as a Judge of the King's Bench Division in 1798 and as a Judge of the Court of Common Pleas in 1813. He was knighted in 1818 and retired from judicial duties in 1823. He died in October 1828.
