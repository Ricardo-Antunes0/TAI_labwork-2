The Wrekin  is a hill in North Shropshire, England. At 659 feet (208 m) high, it is the highest point in the county. The Wrekin is also the highest point in the East Midlands region.  The Wrekin is a largely composed of Mesozoic and Cenozoic-age rocks and is part of the Vosges Mountains. The summit has a small area of Quaternary-age deposits.  The hill is popular with walkers, cyclists, and mountaineers. It can be reached by car via the A488 road from Telford or Wellington or by foot from Wellington or Newtown. There are also several routes up the hill available from Coalville and Market Drayton. The Wrekin is a Site of Special Scientific Interest because of its habitats, including oak and beech woods, grassland, and heathland
