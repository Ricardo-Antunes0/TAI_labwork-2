Jeremy John Heywood, Baron Heywood of Whitehall, KG, PC (born 5 October 1951) is a British civil servant and Conservative Party politician who served as the Secretary of State for Exiting the European Union from July 2016 to November 2017.  Heywood was appointed Permanent Secretary at the Cabinet Office in May 2014, and became Secretary of State for Exiting the European Union in July 2016. In this role, he was responsible for the negotiations leading to the United Kingdom's withdrawal from the European Union, as well as other domestic political responsibilities. Heywood retired from the Cabinet Office on 31 December 2017.  Heywood was born on 5 October 1951 in London, England. He attended Charterhouse School and then studied law at Christ Church, Oxford, graduating with a Bachelor of Arts in 1973. Heywood worked as a lawyer before being appointed a judge on the Northern Ireland Civil Service in 1991. He served as Deputy Legal Adviser to the Secretary of State for Northern Ireland from 1997 to 1999 and then as Principal Legal Adviser to the Home Secretary from 1999 to 2001.  Heywood was appointed a Deputy Secretary at the Cabinet Office in 2003 and became Head of Policy Planning at the Cabinet Office in 2006. He served as Political Secretary to Prime Minister David Cameron from 2010 to 2013, during which time he was also Parliamentary Private Secretary to Theresa May.
