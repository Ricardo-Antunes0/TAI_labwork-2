Dave Puddington (born ) is a former English professional rugby league footballer who played in the 1960s, 1970s and 1980s. A Great Britain international representative forward, he played at club level for Wigan, Leeds, Hull KR and Wakefield Trinity, as a , or , i.e. number 8 or 10, 11 or 12, during the era of contested scrums.  Dave Puddington's birth was registered in Bolton, Lancashire, England. He died aged 71 on Saturday 1 October 2016 at the Royal Bolton Hospital in Bolton, Greater Manchester, England after a long illness.  Dave Puddington was born in Bolton, Lancashire on Wednesday 3 October 1945 to Stanley and Dorothy Puddington. He attended Bolton Central School and played rugby union for the school and district team before turning to rugby league with Wigan in 1962. He made his first-team début against Salford on Saturday 14 January 1963 and went on to make 192 appearances for the Wigan club over 13 seasons from 1962 until 1978. In total, he scored 16 tries for Wigan – joint fourth-most in the club's history – and won two League Championship medals (1974–75 and 1976–77) and one Challenge Cup (1975).  Dave Puddington made his international début for Great Britain against Australia at Central Park, Wigan on Saturday 21 November 1971.
