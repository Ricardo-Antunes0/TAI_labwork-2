The Kohl Center is an arena and convention center in Milwaukee, Wisconsin. It is named for businessman and philanthropist William S. Kohl, Jr., who donated $63 million for its construction in 1988. It is located on the campus of the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee.  The Kohl Center was designed by Cesar Pelli and opened on October 4, 1988. The arena has a capacity of 18,562 for basketball and 17,128 for ice hockey. It has hosted many major events, including the NCAA Men's Division I Basketball Championship, the NCAA Women's Division I Basketball Championship, the NHL Western Conference Finals, and the NBA All-Star Game. The Kohl Center was also home to the Milwaukee Bucks of the NBA from 1988 to 2018.   The Kohl Center has undergone several renovations since its opening.
