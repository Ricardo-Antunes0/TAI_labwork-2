Michael Hout (born May 14, 1950) is an American composer and pianist.  Michael Hout was born in Cleveland, Ohio, on May 14, 1950. He received his undergraduate degree from Oberlin College in 1971, and his Masters and Doctorate degrees from the University of Michigan in 1974 and 1978, respectively.  Hout has been a professor of music at the University of California at Berkeley since 1978. He has also served as Composer-in-Residence at the San Francisco Conservatory of Music (1984-1985), the Aspen Music Festival (1986), and the Tanglewood Music Center (1991). He was the winner of the Gershwin Prize for New American Music in 1993.  Hout's orchestral works include two cycles of symphonies, a string quartet, and a piano concerto. His chamber works include two piano trios, a string quintet, and a piano quartet. His vocal works include an opera, a oratorio, and three cantatas.
