Jeni (J.A.) Mawter (born 1959) is an English artist, who creates intricate and often highly coloured paintings. She is known for her often fantastical or surreal depictions of landscapes and scenes from everyday life.  Mawter was born in 1959 in Leicester, England. She studied painting at the University of Leicester, where she graduated with a Bachelor of Arts in 1981. She then moved to London, where she continued her studies at the Royal College of Art, graduating with a Master of Arts in 1984.  Mawter has exhibited her work throughout the United Kingdom and internationally, including at the Royal Academy of Arts in London, the Museum of Modern Art in New York City, and the Centre Pompidou in Paris. Her work has been featured in numerous magazine and newspaper articles, and she has been awarded several awards, including a British Council scholarship in 1984 and a French government grant in 1988.  Jeni Mawter currently lives and works in London.
