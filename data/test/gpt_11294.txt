After serving as a minister in the United States, Quimby moved to London, where he served as the pastor of the African Methodist Episcopal Zion Church. He also founded the London School of Economics and Political Science.  Quimby is best known for his work as an abolitionist. He was a key figure in the Underground Railroad, helping slaves escape to freedom in the north. He also played a role in the development of the African Methodist Episcopal Zion Church, which became one of the leading black churches in America. Quimby is also well known for his work as a political leader. He served as the secretary of the Anti-Slavery Society and was a vocal opponent of slavery throughout his life.
