Hibbertia pustulata is a species of flowering plant in the family Dilleniaceae and is endemic to the Blue Mountains area in New South Wales. It is a small, low-lying shrub with more or less glabrous foliage, more or less linear leaves, and yellow flowers usually arranged singly on the ends of stems, the flowers with four stamens on one side of two glabrous carpels. Description Hibbertia pustulata is a small shrub that typically to a height of up to  long and has more or less glabrous foliage. The leaves are more or less linear, mostly  long,  wide on a petiole  long and with the edges rolled under. The flowers are arranged singly on the ends of branches with a triangular bract  long at the base. The five sepals are joined at the base, the outer sepal lobes  long and  wide, the inner sepal lobes  wide. The five petals are yellow, egg-shaped with the narrower end towards the base with a notch at the tip,  long and there are four stamens fused at the base on one side of the two carpels, each carpel with four to six ovules. Flowering mainly occurs from August to October.
