Anthony Walsh is an American criminologist and professor at the University of Maryland, College Park. He specializes in criminology and public policy, and his research has focused on law enforcement, criminal justice reform, and the intersection of race and crime. He is also the founding editor-in-chief of The Crime Report, a quarterly journal published by the University of Maryland.  Walsh was born in Boston, Massachusetts in 1965. He received his B.A. from Tufts University in 1987 and his M.A. from Boston University in 1989. He received his Ph.D. from the University of Maryland in 1993.  Walsh began his academic career as an assistant professor at the University of Maryland in 1995. He became a full professor there in 2006, and he served as the chair of the criminology department from 2009 to 2012. In 2013, Walsh was named the vice chancellor for research at the University of Maryland, College Park. He remains a professor at that institution.  Walsh's research has focused on law enforcement, criminal justice reform, and the intersection of race and crime. His work has been published in journals such as The Crime Report, Criminology, Justice Quarterly, Journal of Criminal Law and Criminology, and Social Problems. Walsh has also written two books: Street-Level Drug Enforcement: A New Paradigm for Policing (2009) and Race Matters: The Unspoken Contours of Racial Inequality (2012).
