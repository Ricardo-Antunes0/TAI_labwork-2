The Elbe–Weser waterway  or Elbe–Weser shipping route is a waterway in Germany and Austria. The waterway connects the Elbe River in Germany with the Weser River in northern Germany, and forms part of the border between these two countries. The waterway is about long and has a maximum width of .  The Elbe–Weser shipping route is one of the busiest waterways in Europe. It is used for shipping goods between eastern and western Germany, as well as between northern and southern Germany. The route also serves as a shortcut for ships travelling between the Atlantic Ocean and the Mediterranean Sea. The waterway is also important for recreational boating.
