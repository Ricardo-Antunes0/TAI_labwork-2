Myrna Herzog (born in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil) is a Brazilian-born American film, stage and television actress. Herzog has appeared in a variety of films, including the Academy Award-nominated drama The Squid and the Whale (2005), for which she was nominated for the BAFTA Award for Best Actress in a British Film. She also appeared in the films The Road (2009) and Foxcatcher (2014), both of which received critical acclaim. Herzog was nominated for the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Drama Series for her performance in the HBO series Boardwalk Empire (2010–14).  Herzog was born in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, to Jewish parents. Her father, Arno Herzog, was a psychoanalyst and professor at the Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro. Her mother, Isabelle Herzog (née Levinson), was a painter and sculptor who has exhibited her work throughout Brazil and Europe. Myrna Herzog has two sisters, actress Paula Herzog and musician Tania Herzog. She attended the Chapada Diamantina School and then studied at New York University's Tisch School of the Arts, graduating with an MFA in 1998.  Herzog began her acting career in 1998 with an appearance on the Brazilian telenovela "Amor à Vida".
