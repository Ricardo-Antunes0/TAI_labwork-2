The Aérospatiale Gazelle (company designations SA 340, SA 341, SA 342, SA 343 and SA 344) is a French-built medium-lift helicopter developed by the manufacturer Aérospatiale.  The Gazelle was designed in the late 1960s as a successor to the Aérospatiale Alouette III and first flew on 30 September 1969. The Gazelle entered service with the French Army in 1972 and with the French Air Force in 1973. The Gazelle has been exported to over 20 countries.
