Betty Mona Desmond Ayers (née FitzGerald; 19 November 1922 – 12 November 2002) was an Anglo-Irish actress who appeared in films and on television.  Duffy Ayers was born in Dublin, Ireland, the daughter of actress Mona Desmond and her second husband, actor FitzGerald. She made her film debut in "The Seventh Veil" (1957), and worked steadily in British films and television throughout the 1960s and 1970s. She appeared in the television series "Randall and Hopkirk (Deceased)" (1970), "The Sweeney" (1971), "The Professionals" (1978), "The Bill" (1993) and "Midsomer Murders" (1997). Ayers also appeared in the films "The Damned United" (1969), "The Omen" (1976), "The Boys from Brazil" (1978) and "Murder on the Orient Express" (1974).
