Edward Liveing (8 February 1832 – 2 March 1913) was an English historian, antiquarian and numismatist.  Born in London, Edward Liveing was the son of George Liveing, a successful cloth merchant. He was educated at Eton and Trinity College, Cambridge, where he graduated with a BA in 1855 and an MA in 1858. He began his career as a schoolmaster before becoming a Fellow of Trinity College in 1859.   In 1864, he became the Regius Professor of History at Cambridge and held that position until his retirement in 1909. During his tenure at Cambridge, he also served as President of the Royal Historical Society (1883–1884), President of the Numismatic Society (1889), and Vice-President of the British Academy (1906).  He died in 1913.
