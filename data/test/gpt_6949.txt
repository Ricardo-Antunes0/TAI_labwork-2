Deborah Sinclair is a Canadian social worker, author, and television presenter. She is known for her work on the CBC Television show Canada Reads, as well as her memoirs, The Glass Castle and Wild at Heart.  Sinclair was born in Sudbury, Ontario, in 1951. She attended the University of Toronto, where she received a Bachelor of Arts in social work in 1973. Sinclair began her career as a social worker in Toronto, but later moved to Vancouver to work on a series of television programs about addiction and mental health. She also wrote a series of memoirs, The Glass Castle (2005) and Wild at Heart (2009), which were both successful and critically acclaimed. In 2013, Sinclair returned to Toronto to host the CBC Television show Canada Reads.
