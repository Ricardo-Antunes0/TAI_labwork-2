Jimmie C. Lee (born March 27, 1937) is an American country singer and songwriter. He has released more than 20 albums, and has charted more than 30 singles on the Billboard country charts, including five number ones.  Jimmie Lee is also known for his work as a session musician, playing guitar, mandolin and dobro in Nashville with artists such as Emmylou Harris, Dolly Parton, Conway Twitty, and Porter Wagoner. He has produced or co-produced albums for such country stars as Ricky Skaggs, Reba McEntire and Brooks & Dunn.  Lee was inducted into the Country Music Hall of Fame in 1998.
