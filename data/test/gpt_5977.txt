Ditchley Park is a country house near Chipping Norton in Oxfordshire, England. It was designed by the architect George Edmund Street in the Gothic style for the wealthy brewer and politician Thomas Pitt-Rivers. Construction started in 1868 and was completed in 1875.  Ditchley Park is best known as the home of the Pitt Rivers Museum, which contains a large collection of Egyptian artefacts and antiquities. It is also used for music concerts and other events.
