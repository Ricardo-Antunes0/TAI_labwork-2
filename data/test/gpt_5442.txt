Theodore M. Brantley (or Brantly) (February 12, 1828 â€“ May 25, 1898) was an American historian and professor at Harvard University.  Brantley was born in Greensboro, North Carolina, to a slaveholder. He attended the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, where he was a member of the Phi Beta Kappa Society and the Alpha chapter of the Delta Kappa Epsilon fraternity. He then studied law, but abandoned it to study history at Harvard under William Roscoe Thayer. He received his A.M. in 1853 and his Ph.D. in 1858.  Brantley became an instructor at Harvard in 1856 and an associate professor in 1872. He served as president of the American Historical Association in 1881-82. He died in 1898 after a long illness and was buried at Mount Auburn Cemetery in Cambridge, Massachusetts.
