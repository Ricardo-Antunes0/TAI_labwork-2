Jarvis was born in San Francisco, California, to Arnold and Lillian Jarvis. He attended St. Ignatius High School in San Francisco before enrolling at the University of California, Berkeley, where he was a member of the Alpha Tau Omega fraternity. After graduating from Berkeley in 1925, Jarvis worked as a reporter for the "San Francisco Examiner".  In 1931, Jarvis married his wife, Helen Gahagan Douglas. The Jarvises had two children: daughter Helen Douglas Jarvis (born 1932) and son Howard Arnold Jarvis II (born 1935).  Jarvis entered politics in 1937 when he was elected to the State Assembly as a Democrat from the 12th District. In 1941, he was elected to the State Senate from the 8th District and served two terms until he was elected governor in 1951. As governor, Jarvis promoted increased funding for education and public works projects and reduced taxes. He also presided over major improvements to state transportation systems, including the construction of the Golden Gate Bridge and the development of Interstate 80.  Following his term as governor, Jarvis served one term in the U.S. House of Representatives before returning to private life. He died on December 12, 2003 at the age of 98 in Woodside, California.
