Mon Niye Kachakachi was a Bengali language daily newspaper in India. It was first published from Calcutta on 15 August 1916.  The newspaper was started by Dr. Subhas Chandra Bose and other revolutionaries of the Indian independence movement. "Mon Niye Kachakachi" was the mouthpiece of the Indian National Congress. In 1937, during the British Raj, it was closed down by the British authorities.
