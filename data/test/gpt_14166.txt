Bellastraea aurea, common name the golden smallshell, is a species of small mollusc in the family Columbellidae.  The golden smallshell is a slender, golden shelled mollusc. It has a smooth, shiny surface with a few indistinct darker markings. The shell measures about 2-3 cm in length and 1.5 cm in width. The shell is undecorated except for a single row of short, stout teeth near the front. This species is found throughout much of the eastern coast of North America, from Nova Scotia to Florida and west to Texas. It prefers warm coastal habitats with sandy or muddy substrates.
