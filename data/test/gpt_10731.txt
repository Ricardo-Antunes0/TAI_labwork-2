Edwin Meese III (born December 2, 1931) is an American lawyer, jurist, and politician who served as the 32nd Attorney General of the United States from 1985 to 1989, and as U.S. Secretary of Education from 1987 to 1989. Meese served as a Republican in the United States Senate from 1989 to 2013.  Edwin Meese III was born in Sacramento, California, to Edwin Meese II, a U.S. Attorney for the Western District of California, and Edith (n√©e Kappelhoff) Meese. He has a sister, Marianne (born 1933), and a brother, John (born 1935). He attended St. Ignatius High School in San Francisco. He received his B.A. from the University of Notre Dame in 1953 and his J.D. from the University of California, Davis in 1957.  After working as an assistant U.S. attorney in Sacramento from 1957 to 1961, Meese was an associate deputy attorney general under Robert Bork from 1961 to 1965. In 1965, he became an attorney with the law firm of Jones Day and served as general counsel for the Office of Legal Counsel from 1971 to 1973 under President Richard Nixon and as acting Attorney General from 1973 to 1974 under Attorney General Elliot Richardson.
