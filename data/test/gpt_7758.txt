Steven Spooner is an American pianist, and composer. He has released four albums as a solo artist, and has collaborated with a number of other musicians, including John Zorn, Thurston Moore, Bill Frisell, and Paul Motian.  Steven Spooner was born in 1961 in West Hartford, Connecticut. He studied at the University of Chicago, where he received his B.M. in 1982 and his M.M. in 1984. He also studied at the University of Music and Performing Arts Vienna, where he obtained his Diploma in 1989.  Spooner's career as a pianist began in the early 1980s, when he played with the improvisation group The Necks. He released his first solo album, "Distant Echoes", in 1988. Since then, he has released four more albums: "Fragments" (1995), "The Garden" (1998), "The Mirror" (2002), and "In Retrospect" (2005).  Spooner has also collaborated with a number of other musicians, including John Zorn, Thurston Moore, Bill Frisell, and Paul Motian. He has also performed with ensembles such as the Arditti Quartet and the David Rothenberg Ensemble.  Spooner currently lives in New York City.
