Samuel Orcutt (1824–1893) was an American historian and political theorist. He is best known for his work on the history of American democracy, and for his influential articulation of the concept of Progressive Democracy.  Orcutt was born in 1824 in Charlestown, Massachusetts. He graduated from Brown University in 1847 and taught at Brown and Columbia universities before joining the faculty of Harvard University in 1871. He served as president of the American Historical Association in 1885–86. He died in 1893.
