DeShawn Stevenson (born April 3, 1981) is an American professional basketball player who is currently playing for the Memphis Grizzlies of the National Basketball Association (NBA). He played college basketball for the University of Connecticut.  Stevenson was born in Hartford, Connecticut. He attended St. John's School in Danbury, Connecticut, and then transferred to Huntington North High School in Huntington, New York, where he was a three-year starter and led his team to a state championships as a senior.  Stevenson attended the University of Connecticut, where he played for coach Jim Calhoun's UConn Huskies men's basketball team from 2001 to 2004. As a freshman in 2001–02, Stevenson was a key player on UConn's run to the NCAA Championship Game. As a sophomore in 2002–03, he averaged 10.9 points and 5.5 rebounds per game and was named the Most Improved Player of the Big East Conference. As a junior in 2003–04, he averaged 14.7 points, 6.1 rebounds and 1.8 blocks per game and was named an All-Big East First Team selection. As a senior in 2004–05, Stevenson averaged 18.4 points, 7.6 rebounds and 2.0 blocks per game and was named an All-American First Team selection by "The Sporting News" and an All-Big East Second Team selection by the Associated Press.  On May 26, 2005, Stevenson signed with the Philadelphia 76ers as an undrafted free agent after going undrafted in the 2005 NBA
