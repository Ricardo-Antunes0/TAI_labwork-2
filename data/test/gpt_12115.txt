Wooden's Legacy is a public artwork by American artist Michael Heizer located in the Central Park in New York City. The sculpture is made of over 1,000 wooden poles, each measuring four feet (1.2 m) in diameter and weighing around 500 pounds (227 kg). It was installed in the park in 1994.  The sculpture is named after its creator, Michael Heizer, and his father, the late Edward Heizer. The elder Heizer was an important figure in the history of American sculpture.
