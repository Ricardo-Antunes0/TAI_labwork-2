Ivan Ksenofontovich Ksenofontov (Russian: Иван Ксенофонтович Ксенофонтов; 18 December [O.S. 5 December] 1881 – 10 March 1966) was a Russian film director, screenwriter and actor, who is considered one of the most important figures in the history of Russian cinema. Ksenofontov's films often explore the conflict between human emotions and social conventions. He was a prominent member of the Russian avant-garde and is often referred to as one of its most important directors.  Ivan Ksenofontov was born on 18 December 1881 in Nizhny Novgorod, Russia, then part of the Russian Empire. His father, Ksenofont Mikhaylovich Ksenofontov, was a railway engineer and his mother, Anna Ivanovna (née Gorbunova), came from a wealthy peasant family.  The young Ksenofontov began his education at home before entering the Nizhny Novgorod School of Painting, Sculpture and Architecture in 1898. A year later he transferred to the Saint Petersburg School of Painting, Sculpture and Architecture where he studied under Ivan Michailovich Savinov and Vladimir Stasov.  In 1904 Ksenofontov married Olga Khokhlova and they had two daughters, Irina (born 1905) and Ludmila (born 1908).
