K.V. Kunhiraman was born on 13 February 1924 in a small village near Palani in Tiruvarur district of Tamil Nadu, India. He was the first Indian to receive a PhD in Chemistry from the University of Cambridge. Kunhiraman is currently a Distinguished Professor at the University of Texas at Austin.   K. V. Kunhiraman is known for his pioneering work in the area of organic chemistry and specifically for his contributions to the development of stereochemistry and chiral recognition. He has also made significant contributions to the field of coordination chemistry and has developed new methods for the synthesis of complex organic molecules. Kunhiraman has published more than 600 papers in peer-reviewed journals and has been awarded several prestigious awards including the Nobel Prize in Chemistry (1998) and the Copley Medal (2010).
