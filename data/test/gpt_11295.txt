Kingstonian Football Club is an English semi-professional football club based in Kingston, Greater London. The club was formed in 1881 and plays in the National League South, the sixth tier of English football.  Kingstonian played its first competitive match in the FA Cup against Corinthian Casuals on 1 December 1882. The club progressed to the third round proper where they were beaten 5–0 by Stalybridge Celtic. In the 1883–84 season, Kingstonian reached the first round of the FA Cup for the first time, where they were drawn against Wolverhampton Wanderers. The match finished 2–2 after a replay and Kingstonian went on to lose 3–1 in the second replay. In 1888, Kingstonian reached the FA Cup semi-finals for the first time where they lost 2–1 to Preston North End. The following season, they reached the semi-finals again, this time losing 3–2 to Wolverhampton Wanderers.  In 1891, Kingstonian became founder members of the Southern League which was formed as a division two league. The club finished as runners-up in its first season in the Southern League and were promoted to division one. In 1895, Kingstonian won the Southern League championship and were promoted to division one of the Football League. However, after only one season in the Football League, Kingstonian were relegated back to the Southern League.
