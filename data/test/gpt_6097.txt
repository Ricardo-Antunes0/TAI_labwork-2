in•ter a•li•a is the fourth studio album by the American rock band Radiohead, released on September 15, 2008 through Interscope Records. The album was produced by Nigel Godrich and recorded in several studios in England and France between 2006 and 2008.  Radiohead began work on "In•ter a•li•a" in 2006, after the release of their third album "Hail to the Thief". The band had been working on material for a follow-up to "Hail to the Thief" for several years and toured in support of the album. Recording sessions for "In•ter a•li•a" took place at various locations in England and France, including Godrich's mansion Ellington House, Abbey Road Studios, and AIR Studios.  "In•ter a•li•a" incorporates various influences, including electronica, world music, and dubstep. The album garnered positive reviews from critics, who praised its experimental sound and Thom Yorke's vocals. It debuted at number one on the UK Albums Chart and reached the top ten in many other countries. It has been certified platinum by the British Phonographic Industry (BPI) and has sold over two million copies worldwide.  Thom Yorke and his fellow Radiohead members have released four studio albums since their formation in 1985: "Pablo Honey" (1992), "The Bends" (1995), "OK Computer" (1997), and "Hail to the Thief" (2007).
