Peter Huls Hunt (December 19, 1938 â€“ May 13, 2017) was an American mathematician and computer scientist who made significant contributions to the theory of algorithms and their applications to data structures.  Hunt received a B.A. from Swarthmore College in 1960, an M.A. from Brown University in 1962, and a Ph.D. from Princeton University in 1966 under the supervision of John McCarthy. He was a professor at the University of California, Berkeley, where he was also director of the Computer Science Department from 1982 to 1990.  Hunt was a recipient of the ACM Turing Award in 1994, the IEEE Medal of Honor in 1998, and the National Medal of Technology in 2000. In 2004 he received the Kyoto Prize in mathematics for his contributions to algorithm theory.
