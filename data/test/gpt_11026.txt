Erythema migrans or erythema chronicum migrans is a skin condition caused by the human Epstein-Barr virus. EBV is a member of the herpesviruses family and causes a variety of viral illnesses in both humans and other animals. Erythema migrans is the most common manifestation of EBV infection and is typically a pruritic, erythematous, vesicular skin eruption that develops on any part of the body. The most common sites are the torso, neck, arms, and legs. The eruption may be solitary or multiple and can last for weeks to months. Often, there is a predilection for areas that are frequently exposed to the sun or wind.
