Anurag Agrawal (born 1972) is an American ecologist and professor at Cornell University. He is known for his work on community ecology, landscape ecology, and climate change.  Agrawal was born in 1972 in the United States to Indian parents. He grew up in India before moving to the United States to study environmental science at the University of California, Berkeley. After completing his undergraduate degree, he moved to Cornell University to continue his studies, where he achieved a doctorate in 2004.  Agrawal's research focuses on community ecology, landscape ecology, and climate change. He has published extensively on these topics, and has been recognised for his work by receiving several awards, including the MacArthur Fellowship in 2006 and the American Association for the Advancement of Science Award for Outstanding Contributions to Science in 2010. Agrawal is currently a professor at Cornell University.
