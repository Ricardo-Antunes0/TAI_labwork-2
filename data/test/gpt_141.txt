Lionel G. Job (born January 23, 1942) is an American economist, who is currently a professor of economics at Stanford University. He is also a research associate at the National Bureau of Economic Research and a member of the American Economic Association, the European Economic Association, and the Institute of Mathematical Economics.  Job obtained his B.A. from Brown University in 1964, and his M.A. and Ph.D. from Princeton University in 1967 and 1972, respectively. He was a faculty member at Massachusetts Institute of Technology from 1972 to 1986, when he moved to Stanford University.  Job's research has been in general equilibrium theory and macroeconomics, with a focus on labor markets, taxation, and financial markets. He has written three books: The Theory of Job Search (1977), Unemployment and Inflation (1983), and The Role of Monetary Policy (2002). He also edited two collections of essays: Essays in Honor of Baruch Spinoza (1985) and Essays on the State of Economics (1998).
