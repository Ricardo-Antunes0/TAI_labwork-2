He was the son of a pharmacist and studied medicine at the University of Freiburg, where he was a member of the "Freiburger Arbeiter-Vereinigung". After graduation he worked as a doctor at a hospital in Essen. In 1906 he became a professor at the University of Hamburg, where he remained until his death. He is best known for his work on skin diseases, particularly vitiligo.
