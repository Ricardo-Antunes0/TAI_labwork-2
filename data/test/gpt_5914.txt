Khomas is one of the fourteen regions of Ethiopia. It is located in the Southern Nations, Nationalities and Peoples' Region. The region has an area of 1,835,107 square kilometers and a population of about 9.5 million as of 2006.  The region is bordered on the south by the SNNPR, on the west by Harari Region, on the north by Oromia Region, and on the east by Amhara Region. The major towns in Khomas include Metema, Silt'e, Wollo, and Magdala. The region has a variety of natural resources, including oil, gas, and mineral resources.  The region was formed in 1995 from parts of Harari and Oromia Regions. The predominant languages spoken in Khomas are Oromiffa and Amharic.
