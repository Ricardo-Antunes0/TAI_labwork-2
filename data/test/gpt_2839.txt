Matthew Scarlett (born 5 June 1979) is an English actor and singer. He is the son of actor Sir Anthony Hopkins and actress Diana Scarwid.  Scarlett was born in London, England, the son of actors Sir Anthony Hopkins and Diana Scarwid. He was educated at Charterhouse School and the University of Cambridge, where he studied English literature.  Scarlett began his career as a model, appearing in campaigns for Burberry, Dolce & Gabbana, and Gucci. He made his acting debut in 2003 with a small role in the television series Midsomer Murders. His breakthrough role came in 2005 when he was cast as Alfred Pennyworth in the British film Batman Begins, which led to roles in the sequels Batman Begins â€“ Dark Night and The Dark Knight.  In 2008, Scarlett starred as King George II in the historical drama The Young Victoria opposite Emily Blunt. That same year, he appeared in the critically acclaimed independent film I'm Not There starring Heath Ledger. In 2009, he starred opposite Sienna Miller in the psychological thriller A Dangerous Method and voiced Mickey Mouse in Disney's animated feature The Princess and The Frog. In 2010, Scarlett played Guy de Lusignan in Kingdom of Heaven, for which he was nominated for a BAFTA Award for Best Actor. The following year, he starred as antihero Maximus Decimus Meridius in Ridley Scott's epic blockbuster Gladiator.
