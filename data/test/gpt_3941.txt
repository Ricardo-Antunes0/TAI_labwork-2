The Dallas Black Giants were professional and Negro American baseball team that competed in the Negro American League from 1915 to 1948.  The Dallas Black Giants were one of the most successful teams in Negro League history, winning eight league championships (1915, 1916, 1918, 1919, 1920, 1921, 1924, and 1948). The Black Giants also won four Western Division titles (1921, 1922, 1923, and 1924) and two Southern Division titles (1923 and 1924). They were also runners-up in the league's inaugural championship season of 1915.  The Black Giants played their home games at Texas League Park in Dallas. The team was managed by Oscar Charleston from 1915 to 1923 and by Buck O'Neil from 1924 to 1948.
