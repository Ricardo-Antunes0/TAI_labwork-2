James Alan Hetfield (born August 3, 1963) is an American musician, singer, and songwriter who is the lead vocalist and rhythm guitarist for the metal band Metallica. With fellow bandmate Lars Ulrich, he is one of the two original members of Metallica.  Born in Downey, California, Hetfield began playing guitar at age 12. After being rejected from several junior colleges, Hetfield attended Pasadena City College in Pasadena, California where he met Ulrich. The two formed Metallica in 1981 and released their debut album, Kill 'Em All, in 1983. The band achieved widespread success with their subsequent albums and became one of the world's most influential heavy metal bands. Metallica have released ten studio albums and have sold over 150 million records worldwide. Hetfield has also worked with a number of other artists, including Lenny Kravitz, Rammstein, and Skid Row. In 2005, he was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
