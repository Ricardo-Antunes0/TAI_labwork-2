Lorraine McKenna ( Giroux; born December 22, 1971) is a Canadian singer-songwriter and musician. McKenna has released five studio albums and two EPs, as well as several singles and music videos.  McKenna began her musical career in the early 1990s, when she was a member of the Montreal-based alt-country band The Dears. The band released two albums before splitting up in 1998. McKenna then formed the alternative country band the Mavericks with her then-husband, drummer Brant Bjork. The Mavericks released two albums before disbanding in 2006.  Since 2006, McKenna has released five studio albums: "Born to Die" (2007), "The Wind Cries Mary" (2008), "Heartworn" (2011), "High Water" (2015), and "A Deeper Understanding" (2018). She has also released two EPs: "Keep Me Close" (2010) and "Take Me with You" (2013). McKenna has received several awards, including four Juno Awards and a Grammy Award nomination.
