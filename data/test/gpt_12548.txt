"Bitter Sweet Symphony" is a song recorded by American singer Whitney Houston and German producer Rolf Lübbersmann. The song was written by Houston, Lübbersmann, and Peter Zizzo. It was released as the second single from Houston's seventh studio album I Look to You (1990).  "Bitter Sweet Symphony" is a ballad about a former love that is still remembered fondly. The lyrics speak of the hope that the former love still carries within them. The song was well received by music critics who praised its emotional depth. "Bitter Sweet Symphony" became one of Houston's most successful singles, topping the charts in numerous countries and becoming her fourth number-one single in the United States. The song has been covered by numerous artists, most notably by Barbra Streisand who released a version which reached the top five on the "Billboard" Hot 100.
