Khordeh Avesta, meaning 'little, or lesser, or secondary Avesta', is a collection of texts and traditions related to the Zoroastrian religion. Composed in the late first millennium BC or early first millennium AD, it comprises four books, the Gathas, Yasna, Visperad, and Vendidad.  The Khordeh Avesta is the second most important scripture of the Zoroastrian faith, after the holy book Avesta. It is a collection of hymns and prayers, with a focus on ritual and liturgy. The text also includes teachings on morality, cosmology, and history.
