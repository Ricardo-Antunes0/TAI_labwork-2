Lady Carla (8 March 1993 â€“ 2012) was a British Thoroughbred racehorse and broodmare. She was owned and trained by Sir Michael Stoute and raced in the United Kingdom and France.  Lady Carla was sired by Galileo, a leading British stallion who won the Champion Stakes and the King George VI and Queen Elizabeth Stakes. Her dam, Lady Aurelia, was a proven performer who won twelve races in Britain, including the Nassau Stakes and the Group One Matriarch Stakes.  Lady Carla made her racecourse debut in a maiden race over six furlongs at Newmarket Racecourse in May 2003. She started the 7/4 favourite but was beaten fourteen lengths by the colt Nijinsky.  In her next start Lady Carla contested the Group Three Duchess of York Stakes over seven furlongs at York Racecourse and finished second to the French-trained filly Mona Lisa. On her final appearance of the year she finished third behind Jazil and Mona Lisa in the Group Two Pretty Polly Stakes over six furlongs at Ascot Racecourse.  Lady Carla began her four-year-old season by finishing third behind Nijinsky and Mona Lisa in the Group One Princess of Wales's Stakes over one mile at Royal Ascot.
