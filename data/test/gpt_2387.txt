Jack Gregory Downing (October 21, 1940June 27, 1998) was an American politician who served as a United States Representative from California from 1983 to 1997. A Republican, he served as House Majority Whip in the 104th Congress.  Downing was born in Glendale, California, to Jack and Lillian Downing. He attended Glendale Union High School, and then the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA). He served in the United States Marine Corps from 1961 to 1963. Downing worked for Hughes Aircraft Company from 1963 to 1967, and then for the California Department of Motor Vehicles from 1967 to 1973. He then began a business career, working as a marketing executive for several companies before founding his own company in 1983.  Downing was first elected to the House of Representatives in 1982, defeating incumbent Democrat Phil Burton by a wide margin. He was re-elected five more times with comfortable margins before retiring in 1997. During his congressional career, Downing served on many committees, including the House Appropriations Committee, the House Judiciary Committee, and the House Energy and Commerce Committee. In 1992 he ran unsuccessfully for the Republican Party's nomination for Governor of California.  Downing died of heart failure at his home in Rancho Mirage, California, at the age of 56. He was interred at Forest Lawn Memorial Park in Glendale.
