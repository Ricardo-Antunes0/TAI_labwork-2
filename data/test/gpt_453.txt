Grailey Hewett "Grady" Higginbotham (December 31, 1892 – October 9, 1951) was an American football player and coach. Higginbotham played college football for the University of Michigan from 1913 to 1916 and professionally for the Chicago Cardinals in 1917 and the Canton Bulldogs of the National Football League (NFL) in 1918. He served as the head football coach at George Washington University in 1922, compiling a record of 6–5. Higginbotham was also the head basketball coach at George Washington from 1922 to 1923, tallying a mark of 13–9.  Higginbotham was born in Bellefontaine, Ohio, in 1892. He attended Bloomfield Hills High School in Bloomfield Hills, Michigan, where he played football and basketball under coach Harry Kipke. Higginbotham enrolled at the University of Michigan in 1912 and played for the Michigan Wolverines football team from 1913 to 1916. As a freshman, Higginbotham was a member of Michigan's "Big Ten Conference championship" team that compiled a 6–1 record and outscored its opponents by a combined total of 215 to 28. He was also selected as an All-American by Walter Camp and "The New York Times".
