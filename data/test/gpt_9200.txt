Eugene Alexander Sittig (October 1, 1847 – January 2, 1921) was an American historian and educator who specialized in the history of the United States.  Sittig was born in Allegheny County, Pennsylvania, to a family of German immigrants. After completing preparatory studies in Pittsburgh, he entered Princeton University in 1868, where he studied history and political science. In 1872 he graduated with a degree in history.  In 1874 Sittig began teaching at the new University of Kentucky, where he remained for the rest of his career. He became a full professor in 1889 and served as dean of the faculty from 1907 to 1908. He was also chairman of the department of history from 1907 to 1915 and president of the American Historical Association from 1911 to 1912.  Sittig's most important work was his six-volume History of the United States (1903–1915). The first volume, subtitled The Colonial Period, covers the colonial period from 1492 to 1763. The second volume, subtitled The National Period, covers the period from 1763 to 1877. The third volume, subtitled The Progressive Era, covers the period from 1877 to 1917. The fourth volume, subtitled The War Years and Reconstruction, covers the period from 1917 to 1921. The fifth and final volume, subtitled The New Deal and World War II, covers the period from 1921 to 1945.  Sittig was also an active teacher and lecturer.
