Alex Benjamin Novikoff  was a Russian-born American historian and author. He is best known for his work on Russian history, particularly the history of the Russian Empire, the Soviet Union, and Russia's relations with the West.  Born in Moscow in 1941, Novikoff moved to the United States with his family in 1961. After graduating from Harvard University with a degree in history in 1964, Novikoff taught at Brown University and then at Cornell University before joining Georgetown University as a professor of Russian history in 1982. Novikoff retired from Georgetown in 2007.  Novikoff has written several books on Russian history, including The Russian Revolution (1970), The Soviet Union (1983), and The Russian Empire (1988). He has also written articles for magazines and newspapers, including The New York Times, The Washington Post, and The Wall Street Journal.  Novikoff was awarded the National Humanities Medal in 2006 by President George W. Bush.
