Libyan literature has its roots in Antiquity, with significant contributions made by Islamic scholars. In the modern era, Libyan writers have written in a variety of languages, including Arabic, English, French, Italian, and Spanish.   Libyan literature has been shaped by a number of factors, including the country's tumultuous history, its location on the Mediterranean Sea, and its diverse population. Today, Libyan writers continue to produce works that explore the country's culture and history.
