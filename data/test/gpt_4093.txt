The Gitanjali Express is a superfast express train belonging to Indian Railways that runs between Howrah and Mumbai in India. The train has a maximum speed of 160 kmph and covers a distance of approximately 870 km in just 17 hours and 15 minutes. It is hauled by a WAP-4 electric locomotive from both the Howrah Junction and Mumbai Central stations.  The Gitanjali Express was introduced in October 2009 as an effort to improve the punctuality of trains on the Howrah-Mumbai route. The train initially ran as an overnight train, but was later extended to daytime running. In July 2017, ICF Railway announced that the Gitanjali Express would be converted into a fully air-conditioned train.
