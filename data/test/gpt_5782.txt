The name Macedonia is used in a number of ways in the English language.     The most common meaning is the geographical region, which includes the country of Macedonia and parts of Greece and Serbia.     In historical contexts, the name refers to a region that was part of the Byzantine Empire and later the Ottoman Empire.     In religious contexts, the name refers to the Christian church in Macedonia.     The term Macedonians is used in a number of ways, but most commonly it refers to people who identify themselves as ethnic Macedonians or who are from the geographical region of Macedonia.
