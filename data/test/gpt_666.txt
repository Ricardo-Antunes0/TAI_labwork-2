The Jewish Cemetery in Worms or Heiliger Friedhof ( holy cemetery) is the oldest Jewish cemetery in the world. It was founded in the 12th century, and today it is one of the largest and most important Jewish cemeteries in Europe. The cemetery is located in the old part of Worms, on an island in the River Nahe.  The Jewish Cemetery in Worms was founded in the 12th century. At its peak, it was one of the largest and most important Jewish cemeteries in Europe. Today, it is one of the few remaining cemeteries from that period in Germany. The cemetery contains more than 3500 graves, many of which are well-preserved.
