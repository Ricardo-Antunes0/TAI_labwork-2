Al-Risala al-Qushayriyya fi 'Ilm al-Tasawwuf , mostly known as the Risala of al-Qushayri, is a medieval Arabic text on Sufi mysticism.  The Risala was composed by al-Qushayri (d. 874), who was one of the most renowned Sufi masters of his time. The work is divided into six sections, each dealing with a specific aspect of mysticism.  The Risala is an essential text for students of Sufism and has been influential in the development of Islamic mysticism. It offers a comprehensive overview of the mystical path and provides instruction on various techniques for achieving mystical union with God.
