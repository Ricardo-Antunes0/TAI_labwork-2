Maciej Lewenstein (born September 21, 1955 in Warsaw) is a Polish composer, pianist, and conductor. He has written music for stage, chamber and orchestral works, as well as film and television soundtracks.  Lewenstein began his musical studies at the Warsaw School of Music where he was taught by Wojciech Kilar and Andrzej Panufnik. He later studied at the Royal Academy of Music in London, where he was awarded a diploma in 1977.  Lewenstein has worked as a professor of composition at the Warsaw Academy of Music since 1984. He is also the artistic director of the Warsaw Autumn Festival and the Warsaw Spring Festival.  Lewenstein has received several awards including the Fryderyk Chopin Award (1994), the Zbigniew Preisner Award (2004), and the Krakow Honorary Award (2006). In 2009 he was awarded an Honorary Doctorate from Jagiellonian University in Krakow.
