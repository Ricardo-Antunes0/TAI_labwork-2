John Colbatch (1664â€“11 February 1748), sometimes Colbach, was an English physician who is considered the father of modern obstetrics.  Colbatch was born in 1664 in Wenlock, Shropshire, the son of a gentleman farmer. He attended Wadham College, Oxford, graduating with a BA in 1687 and an MD in 1692. He served briefly as a naval surgeon before becoming a physician in London.  In 1709, Colbatch married Elizabeth Sharpe. Together they had six children, three of whom died in infancy. Elizabeth died in 1724.  In 1725, Colbatch married Sarah Butland. Together they had eight children, five of whom survived to adulthood. Sarah died in 1756.  Colbatch died on 11 February 1748 at the age of eighty-six.
