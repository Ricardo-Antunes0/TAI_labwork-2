Anton Henry "Tony" Momsen, Jr. (January 29, 1948 – May 12, 1993) was an American musician, singer, and songwriter. He was the lead singer and frontman of the rock band Mamas & the Papas.  Tony Momsen was born in Culver City, California, to Anton Momsen and Marguerite (née O'Brien) Momsen. His father was an actor and singer who performed with bands such as The Pajama Game and The Young Americans. His mother was a model and actress. Tony had two older sisters, Gail and Pamela.  Momsen began his musical career as the lead singer and frontman of the rock band Mamas & the Papas. The group achieved success in the late 1960s with songs such as "Don't Let Me Be Misunderstood", "California Dreamin'", and "I Saw Her Standing There". In 1971, Momsen left the group to pursue a solo career. He released two albums, "Big Girls Don't Cry" (1971) and "FM" (1972). In 1978, he reunited with Mamas & the Papas for a tour. He died of AIDS-related complications in 1993 at age 46.
