Simone-Marie Plé-Caussade (14 August 1897, Paris – 16 September 1984, Paris) was a French philosopher and sociologist. After studying at the Sorbonne and the École normale supérieure, she became a professor at the Sorbonne in the 1930s. She was also a visiting professor at universities in the United States, England and Ireland.  Plé-Caussade's major works include "L'existence des personnes" (1935), "L'homme et son milieu" (1941), "Le devoir de la réflexion" (1953), and "Les sources de la morale" (1958). She also wrote essays on topics such as education, death, art, and religion.
