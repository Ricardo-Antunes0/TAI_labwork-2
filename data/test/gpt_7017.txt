"Real Girl" is a song written by Diane Warren and recorded by American singer Madonna. It was released as the lead single from her seventh studio album, Hard Candy (2008). The song was produced by Warren and The-Dream, with additional production from StarGate.  "Real Girl" is a dance-pop and electro-R&B song that lyrically speaks about a woman who is in love with someone who does not love her back. The song received positive reviews from music critics, who praised its production and Madonna's vocals. It reached the top ten in numerous countries, including Australia, Belgium, Canada, France, Germany, Ireland, the Netherlands, New Zealand, Norway, Sweden and the United Kingdom. "Real Girl" was certified platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA) in 2008 for sales of over 1 million units.  The accompanying music video for "Real Girl" was directed by David Fincher and shot in Los Angeles over three days in March 2008. It portrays Madonna as a woman who has been replaced by a lookalike version of herself. The video has been noted for its exploration of the Hollywood image machine and for its references to Madge's career and personal life at the time of its release.
