Taqī ad-Dīn ʾAḥmad ibn ʿAbd al-Ḥalīm ibn ʿAbd al-Karīm ibn Taymiyyah (Arabic: طاقي الدين ʾأحمد بن عبد الله بن عبد الكريم بن تيمية‎) (c. 628-724) was an Islamic theologian and philosopher. He was born in the city of Taymiyyah in the province of Basra in present-day Iraq. After studying at the local schools, Ibn Taymiyyah traveled to Damascus, where he studied under the prominent Sunni scholar, Abu al-Hasan al-Basri. Ibn Taymiyyah soon emerged as one of the most influential members of Basri's circle of disciples.  Ibn Taymiyyah's career as a religious scholar was cut short when he was imprisoned by the Umayyad Caliphate on charges of heresy. Ibn Taymiyyah spent the next decade in prison, where he developed and expanded his philosophical teachings. After his release from prison in 717, Ibn Taymiyyah returned to Damascus and continued to preach his unique brand of Muslim theology. He died in 724 at the age of eighty-one.
