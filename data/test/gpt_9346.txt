George Edmond Manley (born September 17, 1965) is a Jamaican-Canadian politician who is the Member of Parliament for the riding of Kingston and the Islands. He is a member of the New Democratic Party.  Manley was born in Kingston, Jamaica, to parents who were both educators. He moved to Canada at the age of eight, and completed high school in Toronto. Manley then studied political science at York University, where he was a member of the Delta Upsilon fraternity. He also worked as a youth worker in Toronto's inner city.  Manley first became involved in politics as a student at York University. In 1988, he was an organizer for the New Democratic Party's Toronto Youth Forum. In 1990, he was elected to the York University Board of Governors.  Manley was first elected to Parliament in the 2006 federal election, defeating Liberal incumbent Anna Gainey by over 6,000 votes in the riding of Kingston and the Islands. He served as Critic for Democratic Institutions and Human Rights from 2006 to 2008, and served as Deputy House Leader from 2008 to 2011. In 2011, he was appointed Parliamentary Secretary to Minister of National Defence Peter MacKay, and served in that position until his defeat in the 2015 election.  In October 2017, Manley announced his candidacy for Leader of the Official Opposition during the next NDP leadership election. He withdrew his candidacy on January 24th 2018 after garnering only 1% support in a leadership review vote amongst NDP members..
