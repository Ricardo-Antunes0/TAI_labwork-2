"Nothing Is Promised" is a song recorded by Canadian singer-songwriter Carly Rae Jepsen. It was released as the lead single from her second studio album, E•MO•TION (2015). The song was written by Jepsen and Max Martin, with production handled by the latter. "Nothing Is Promised" is a synthpop and electropop song that features heavy use of synthesizers and drum machines. Lyrically, it is a love song that speaks to the idea that nothing is guaranteed in life.  "Nothing Is Promised" was well received by music critics, who praised its catchy melody and Jepsen's vocals. The song reached number one on the Canadian Hot 100 and the US Billboard Hot 100, becoming her second number-one single in the United States after "Call Me Maybe" (2012). It also peaked at number two in Australia, Ireland, New Zealand and the United Kingdom. The accompanying music video for "Nothing Is Promised" was directed by Dave Meyers and features Jepsen singing in a white room with various CGI elements.
