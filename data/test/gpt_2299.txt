Eight ships of the Royal Navy have borne the name HMS Royal George. The most recent, Royal George, was decommissioned in 2009.  The first HMS Royal George was a 50-gun fourth rate ship of the line built in 1755 at Chatham Dockyard and launched on 26 May 1755. She participated in the Battle of Quiberon Bay in 1759 and the Battle of Cape Saint Vincent in 1797. She was sold in 1816.  The second HMS Royal George was a 74-gun third rate ship of the line built at Deptford Dockyard and launched on 10 October 1759. She took part in the Battle of Camperdown on 11 October 1801 and the Battle of Copenhagen on 2 April 1801. She was sold in 1814.  The third HMS Royal George was a 74-gun third rate ship of the line built at Woolwich Dockyard and launched on 4 November 1760. She took part in the Battle of Toulon on 10 July 1793 and the Battle of Malaga on 14 September 1811. She was sold in 1816.  The fourth HMS Royal George was a 74-gun third rate ship of the line built at Plymouth Dockyard and launched on 21 December 1765.
