The Bodhisattvacaryāvatāra or Bodhicaryāvatāra (; Tibetan: བྱང་ཆུབ་སེམས་དཔའི་སྤྱོད་པ་ལ་འཇུག་པ་, Wylie: bsdus pa caryab yid kyi phreng ba) is a Buddhist tantra text attributed to the Indian sage Nāgārjuna.  The text is a philosophical and meditative work on the nature of reality and its relationship to the practitioner's mind. It is one of the most important sources of Mahayana Buddhist philosophy, and has been influential in the development of various Mahayana Buddhist traditions, including Chan Buddhism and Tibetan Buddhism.
