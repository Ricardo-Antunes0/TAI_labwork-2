Mingering Mike (born 1950) is a fictitious character in the novel, The Great Gatsby, by F. Scott Fitzgerald.  Mingering Mike is a small-time con man who is a friend of Gatsby and a frequent visitor to his home. Mike is never seen in the company of anyone of consequence, and his only purpose in life seems to be to pass the time and chat with Gatsby. Despite this, Gatsby seems to be very fond of him.  One day, Gatsby invites Mike over for a drink, but he never arrives. Gatsby becomes worried and goes to look for him, but he cannot find him anywhere. Finally, he learns that Mike has been arrested and sent to prison.
