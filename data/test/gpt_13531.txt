Pamela Jane Cornell (; born October 25, 1965) is an American actress and singer.  Born in Los Angeles, California, Cornell studied at the American Conservatory Theater in San Francisco before making her Broadway debut in the 1987 revival of "The Robber Bridegroom". She went on to receive Tony Award nominations for her roles in "The House of Blue Leaves" (1995), "A Little Night Music" (1998), and "The Price" (2002). She has also appeared in films such as "The Village" (2004), "Frost/Nixon" (2008), "The Help" (2011), and "Sing" (2016).  Cornell has released two solo albums, "Heartbeats" (1994) and "Anywhere I Lay My Head" (2000). She has also participated in several musicals, including "Kiss Me, Kate", "Into the Woods", and "Carousel". In 2018, she will star in the title role of the Broadway revival of Bernadette Peters' play "The Girls".
