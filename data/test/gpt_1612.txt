"Boondocks" is a song co-written and recorded by American hip hop artist Tupac Shakur. The song was released as the second single from his fifth album, Me Against the World.  "Boondocks" is a revenge song, featuring Shakur rapping about the hardships he endured growing up in the low-income housing projects in Baltimore, Maryland. The song was generally well received by music critics, who praised its poetic lyrics and dark tone.  The song reached number 26 on the "Billboard" Hot 100 chart in 1993 and number 3 on the Hot Rap Singles chart. It has been certified gold by the RIAA.
