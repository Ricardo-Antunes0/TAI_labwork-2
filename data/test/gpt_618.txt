Michael Joseph Alstott (born December 21, 1973), nicknamed "The Hammer", is a former American football running back. He played ten seasons in the National Football League (NFL) for the Tampa Bay Buccaneers, Carolina Panthers, Indianapolis Colts, and New Orleans Saints. He was drafted by the Buccaneers in the first round of the 1997 NFL Draft. He played college football at the University of Florida.  Alstott was a three-time Pro Bowler and two-time All-Pro. He also won two Super Bowls with the Bucs (2002 and 2005), and one with the Colts (2008). He is the second player in NFL history to score a postseason touchdown in each of his first four seasons. Alstott was also a member of the United States national team, winning a gold medal in the 2000 Summer Olympics.  Alstott was inducted into the Tampa Bay Buccaneers Hall of Fame in 2009 and the University of Florida Athletic Hall of Fame in 2012. He was also named to the NFL's All-Decade Team for the 2000s. In 2018, he was named to the Pro Football Hall of Fame.  Michael Joseph Alstott was born on December 21, 1973, in Pensacola, Florida, to Barbara and Michael Alstott. As a youngster, Alstott played football as an offensive lineman for Escambia County High School in Pensacola before switching to running back during his junior year. He attended University of Florida where he played for coach Urban Meyer's Florida Gators football team
