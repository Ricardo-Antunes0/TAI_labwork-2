Swans are birds of the family Anatidae. There are 16 extant species of swans, which are classified in four genera. The swan's natural habitat is temperate and subarctic waters, mainly in the Northern Hemisphere.  The black-necked swan is the most common species of swan in the world, found in all the continents except Antarctica. The white-fronted swan is the most widespread species in the Americas, and the mute swan is the most widespread in Europe. The red-breasted swan is found in Asia and Australasia, and the trumpeter Swan is found in North America.
