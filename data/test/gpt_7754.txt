Li Ying (李瑛) (died 737), né Li Bian (李辯), was a prince of the Chinese dynasty Tang Dynasty, who briefly served as a chancellor during the reign of Emperor Xuanzong.  Li Ying was born in about 692, during the reign of Emperor Gaozong. He was the son of Li Bian the Prince of Xiang, a nephew of Emperor Gaozong's wife Empress Wu (later known as Wu Zetian), and a cousin of Emperor Gaozong's wife Empress Huichang. When Li Ying was young, he attended the military academy Shouchun (壽春; in modern Shijiazhuang, Hebei). In 710, he passed the imperial examinations and became a general.  In 716, when Emperor Xuanzong was Prince of Qi and had just seized power from his father Emperor Gaozong, he made Li Ying an imperial censor with the title "Tong Zhongshu Menxia Pingzhangshi" (同中書門下平章事). In 719, Li Ying was made the deputy minister of defense (兵部侍郎) and then minister of justice (刑部尚書). He served in those positions for about two years.
