Michael Walter Michel (born August 4, 1954) is an American actor, voice actor, and comedian. He is known for his work on the animated television series The Simpsons, voicing Chief Wiggum, as well as providing the voice of Dr. Hibbert in the adult animated series The Simpsons Movie.  Michel was born in Toledo, Ohio. He attended the University of Notre Dame in Indiana, where he received a B.A. in Theatre Arts. After graduating from Notre Dame, Michel moved to Los Angeles to pursue an acting career.  Michel's professional acting debut came in the early 1980s, when he appeared in a number of television roles, including a recurring role on the soap opera General Hospital. In 1984, Michel landed his first starring role on the animated sitcom The Simpsons, where he played Chief Wiggum for six seasons. He also provided the voice of Dr. Hibbert in the series' first three seasons and reprised this role in The Simpsons Movie (2007).  Since departing from The Simpsons in 2000, Michel has guest-starred on numerous television shows, including ER and Monk. He has also appeared in several movies, including Wayne's World 2 (1992), Austin Powers: International Man of Mystery (1997), and Role Models (2008). In 2010, Michel released his first comedy album, I'm Sorry (But I Don't Love You).
