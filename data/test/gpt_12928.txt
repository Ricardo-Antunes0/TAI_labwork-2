Anne Marie Lofaso (born October 23, 1965) is an American literary critic, essayist and educator. She is the Charles W. Eliot University Professor of English at Harvard University, where she also serves as the director of the Harvard Writing Center.  Lofaso was born in Springfield, Massachusetts, to a family of Italian descent. She attended the Philips Exeter Academy in New Hampshire and then studied literature at Yale University, where she was a member of the Phi Beta Kappa Society and the Skull and Bones secret society. She then received her Ph.D. from Yale in 1992, with a dissertation on Toni Morrison's "Beloved".  Lofaso began her academic career as an instructor at Princeton University before moving to Harvard in 1998. At Harvard, she has been a Charles W. Eliot University Professor of English since 2007 and also serves as the director of the Harvard Writing Center. Her work has primarily focused on American literature and literary criticism, with a particular emphasis on African-American literature and writing.  Lofaso has received several awards and accolades throughout her career, including the Guggenheim Fellowship (1998), a MacArthur Fellowship (1999), the National Book Award for Criticism (2001), and the John Ciardi Prize (2008). In addition, she has been named one of "The 25 Most Influential People in American Letters" by "The Wall Street Journal" in 2012, one of "The 100 Most Influential People in American Education" by "The Washington Post" in 2013
