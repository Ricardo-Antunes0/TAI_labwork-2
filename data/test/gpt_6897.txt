Vogt (March 10, 1932 in Cleveland, Ohio â€“ August 2, 2013 in Cleveland) was an American chemist who specialized in the study of molecular recognition and the development of new methods for the identification and characterization of biomolecules.  Vogt earned his B.S. from Case Institute of Technology in 53 and his M.S. from Yale University in 55. He then completed his Ph.D. at Yale University in 1959 under the direction of Donald C. Glaser with a thesis on the structure-activity relationships of anthracene derivatives as potential anticancer agents.  After completing his doctoral work, Vogt was hired as an assistant professor at Case Western Reserve University where he remained for eight years before joining the faculty at Ohio State University in 1966 where he served as chairman of the chemistry department from 1978 to 1984. He was also vice president for research at Ohio State from 1984 to 1988 and served as president of the American Chemical Society from 1988 to 1989.  Peter K. Vogt died on August 2, 2013 after a long illness.
