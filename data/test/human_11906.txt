The tumulus was designated a National Historic Site of Japan in 1936. It is the largest of a cluster of kofun, including the Ajiyoshi Hakusan Jinja Kofun and the Gotosho Kofun, which together with the Ajiyoshi Kasugayama Kofun located about 500 meters to the northwest, forms the Ajiyoshi Kofun Cluster. Overview The Ajiyoshi Futagoyama Kofun is a , which is shaped like a keyhole, having one square end and one circular end, when viewed from above. With a length of 96 meters and a height of six meters, it is estimated to have been constructed in the middle of the 6th century AD. The tumulus was constructed in two tiers, and is orientated 30 degrees to the northwest. The tumulus had a structure on the northeast side, possibly a ceremonial platform, and was originally surrounded by  a shield-shaped moat.
