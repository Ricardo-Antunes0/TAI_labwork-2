Jeffrey A. Krell is an openly gay civil rights attorney and educator who is the legal director of the Southern Poverty Law Center. Krell has litigated a wide range of cases, including suits against the Ku Klux Klan, the American Nazi Party, and the Westboro Baptist Church.  Born in Syracuse, New York, Krell earned his undergraduate degree from Fordham University in 1986 and his law degree from Yale Law School in 1990. After brief stints working as a prosecutor and a public defender, he joined the SPLC in 1994 as a litigation attorney. He has since become one of the organization's most influential lawyers, playing a leading role in its many high-profile civil rights cases.  Krell has been particularly active in fighting hate groups and their leaders. He has represented the SPLC in numerous lawsuits against white supremacists, neo-Nazis, and other hate groups, including successful lawsuits against the Ku Klux Klan and the American Nazi Party. In 2007, Krell won a $3 million judgment against the Westboro Baptist Church after the church picketed a funeral of a gay teenager killed in a car accident.  In addition to his work on behalf of the SPLC, Krell has written extensively on civil rights issues. He is the author of several books on constitutional law and civil rights history, including Fighting Hate: The History of the Southern Poverty Law Center (2008) and Righteous Resistance: The Story of Martin Luther King Jr.'s Civil Rights Campaign (2012).
