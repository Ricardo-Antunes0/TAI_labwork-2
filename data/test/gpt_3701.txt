Saint-Georges (sometimes unofficially called Saint-Georges-de-l'Oyapock) is a town and commune, the capital of the French overseas department of French Guiana, in South America.  Saint-Georges is situated on the Oyapock River, at an altitude of about , and has a population of about 25,000. The town is known for its luxurious hotels and casinos as well as its popular market.  The town was founded in 1690 by French colonists and was first called Oyapock. It was renamed Saint-Georges in 1777 in honor of Louis XVI. The town was captured by the British during the Napoleonic Wars, but was returned to France in 1814.  Saint-Georges is home to a number of important historical sites, including the former bishop's palace, Fort de Saint-Georges, the 18th century Saint-Georges Cathedral, now a national monument, and the Museum of Colonial Art.
