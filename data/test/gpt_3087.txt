Nunberg is best known for his contributions to the philosophy of language and political theory, in particular his work on counterfactuals and the use of metaphors in politics.  Nunberg was born in 1945 in Boston, Massachusetts. He received his B.A. from Harvard University in 1967, and his M.A. and Ph.D. from Columbia University in 1970 and 1975, respectively.  Nunberg taught at the University of California at Berkeley from 1975 to 1991, where he became a full professor in 1988. He then moved to New York University, where he served as a professor until his death from cancer on August 11, 2015.
