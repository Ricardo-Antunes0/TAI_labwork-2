Franck Piccard (born 17 September 1965) is a French astronaut, engineer and businessman. He is the first astronaut to have flown in both the Soyuz TMA-13 and TMA-14 spacecraft.  Piccard was born in Lausanne, Switzerland, on 17 September 1965. He completed his secondary education at the Collège Jean-Baptiste Colbert in Neuchâtel, Switzerland, and his undergraduate studies at the University of Geneva, where he received a degree in civil engineering in 1988.  Piccard has logged over 2,000 hours of flying time, including more than 1,500 hours as a pilot. He has completed over 150 flights, including 51 suborbital flights and 12 orbital missions. Piccard has also logged over 5,000 hours in space. He has been involved in many space missions and has held several positions within the European Space Agency (ESA).  Piccard was selected as an astronaut candidate by ESA in 1998. He completed his training at the European Astronaut Centre (EAC) in Cologne, Germany, and joined ESA's astronaut corps in 2002. He has been involved in a number of ESA missions: he served as pilot on ESA's first mission to the International Space Station (ISS), Soyuz TMA-1 (2005), Soyuz TMA-2 (2006), Soyuz TMA-3 (2007), and Soyuz TMA-4 (2008).
