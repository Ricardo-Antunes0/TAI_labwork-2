Dendrobium lobbii, commonly known as the straggly orchid, is a species of orchid native to eastern and southeastern Australia.  The straggly orchid is a terrestrial, perennial, deciduous, sympatric orchid that grows in colonies. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette around the stem and are ovate to elliptic in shape with a pointed tip. The flowers are white to pale pink, have a narrowbanded green stripe running down the center of the petals, and are up to 15 cm long.
