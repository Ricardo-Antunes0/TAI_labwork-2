Logan Lamont Thomas (born July 1, 1991) is an American football tight end for the Arizona Cardinals of the National Football League (NFL). He played college football at Clemson.  Thomas was drafted by the Cardinals in the fifth round of the 2013 NFL Draft. He has also been a member of the Tampa Bay Buccaneers, Miami Dolphins, and Oakland Raiders.  Born in Charlotte, North Carolina, Thomas attended Charlotte Catholic High School. As a senior, he caught 69 passes for 1,069 yards and 12 touchdowns. He also added 474 yards and 5 touchdowns on 39 kickoff returns.   Thomas played college football at Clemson. As a junior in 2010, he started all twelve games and caught 49 passes for 538 yards and four touchdowns. As a senior in 2011, he started all thirteen games and caught 78 passes for 1,142 yards and ten touchdowns. He was named a first-team All-American by "Sports Illustrated" and a second-team All-American by "The Sporting News". He also won the John Mackey Award as the nation's top tight end. Thomas was also a finalist for the Johnny Unitas Golden Arm Award as the nation's top senior quarterback.  Thomas was drafted by the Cardinals in the fifth round of the 2013 NFL Draft. On September 3, 2016, he was waived by the Cardinals with an injury settlement.   On September 5, 2016, Thomas was signed to the Buccaneers' practice squad. He was promoted to the active roster on December 8, 2016
