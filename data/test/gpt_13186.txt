The Tyndale Monument  is a tower in the city of Norwich, in Norfolk, England. It is named after William Tyndale, who was executed in 1536 for printing the Bible in the English language.   The tower was built in 1884 as a memorial to Tyndale, and is 137 feet (43 m) high. It is made of Portland stone and has an octagonal turret at the top. The monument is a Grade I listed building.
