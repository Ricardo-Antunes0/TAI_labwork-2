Earl Zebedee Hooker (January 15, 1930 â€“ December 24, 2009) was an American jazz pianist and composer.  Hooker was born in the Bronx, New York City, the son of a postal worker. As a teenager he played in local juke joints, and started working as a session musician in the early 1950s. He played with Joe Henderson, Art Blakey, and Thelonious Monk, among others. Hooker was a member of the Miles Davis Quintet from 1963 to 1968, and recorded "Round About Midnight" with them. He also recorded numerous albums as a leader.  Hooker suffered from Parkinson's disease for many years and died in 2009 at the age of 80.
