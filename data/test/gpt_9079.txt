The Western Xia or the Xi Xia was a major Chinese dynasty that ruled northwest China from 1038 to 1227. It was founded by the Tangut emperor Taizu, who overthrew the Later Liang dynasty.  The Western Xia empire was a short-lived imperial regime that was founded by the Tangut emperor Taizu, who overthrew the Later Liang dynasty in 1038. The Western Xia empire lasted for only seventy years, from 1038 to 1227. The Western Xia was a small and weak state that was unable to resist the Mongol invasions of the early 12th century. After the fall of the Western Xia, most of its territory was absorbed by the neighbouring Song dynasty.
