The Nevada presidential caucuses were an electoral process by which citizens of the U.S. state of Nevada voted for delegates to the Democratic National Convention in 2016. The process was open to all registered Democrats, regardless of their voting residency in the state.  The caucuses took place on February 20, 2016, and were the first step in the presidential nomination process for the Democratic Party. Delegates were allocated based on each candidate's percentage of the vote, with Hillary Clinton receiving 34% of the vote and Bernie Sanders receiving 62%.
