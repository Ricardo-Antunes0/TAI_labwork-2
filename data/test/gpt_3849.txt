She is best known for her children's book, The Tale of the Very Old Woman Who Swallowed a Dragon, which was published in 1970.  Nathalia Crane was born on 11 August 1913 in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. She graduated from the University of Pennsylvania in 1936 with a degree in journalism. She started her career as a reporter for the Philadelphia Evening Bulletin. Crane later became an assistant editor at Scribner's Magazine.  Crane wrote her first children's book, The Tale of the Very Old Woman Who Swallowed a Dragon, in 1970. The book was published by Simon & Schuster and became a New York Times bestseller. Crane later wrote three sequels to the book: The Tale of the Brave Little Girl Who Fought a Monster (1972), The Tale of the Scary Sleepover (1974), and The Tale of the Kitten Who Saved Christmas (1977). All four books were published by Simon & Schuster.  Crane died on 21 December 1991 in Manhattan, New York City.
