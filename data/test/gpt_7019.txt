The Girard Public Library, a Carnegie library, is a historic, municipally-owned building located at Girard in Erie County, Pennsylvania, United States. The library was designed by the architectural firm of McKim, Mead and White and built in 1897. It is a two-story, five bay by four bay, red brick building in the Beaux-Arts style. It features a square tower on the front facade and a carved stone entranceway with Ionic columns. It was added to the National Register of Historic Places in 1978.
