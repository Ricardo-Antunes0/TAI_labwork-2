Robert Vance Bruce (December 19, 1923 in Detroit, Michigan â€“ July 30, 2007 in Grosse Pointe Woods, Michigan) was a United States Circuit Judge of the United States Court of Appeals for the Sixth Circuit.  Bruce received a Bachelor of Arts degree from the University of Michigan in 1944 and a Juris Doctor from Yale Law School in 1949. He served in the United States Army as an infantryman during World War II, where he received the Purple Heart. Bruce was a law clerk to Judge John Minor Wisdom of the United States District Court for the Eastern District of Michigan from 1949 to 1950. He was an assistant district attorney for Wayne County, Michigan from 1950 to 1952. He was in private practice in Detroit from 1952 to 1968.  Bruce was nominated by President Lyndon B. Johnson on March 7, 1968, to a new seat on the United States Court of Appeals for the Sixth Circuit created by 92 Stat. 1629. He was confirmed by the United States Senate on May 12, 1968, and received his commission on May 13, 1968. He served as Chief Judge from 1987 to 1995. His service terminated on July 30, 2007, due to his death in Grosse Pointe Woods at the age of 85.
