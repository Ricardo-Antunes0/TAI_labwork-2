Natalya Gomez is a professor, researcher, cryosphere scientist, and author of the book "The Cryosphere: A New Global History." Gomez has also published articles in "Geography" and "Environment and Development" and is a co-author of the book "Arctic Climate Change: Impacts on Human Security."  Born in Mexico City, Gomez earned her undergraduate degree in geography from the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM) in 1999. She then completed her graduate studies at the University of California, Irvine, where she earned her Master's degree in geography in 2002 and her PhD in cryosphere science in 2006.  Gomez has been a professor of geography at the National Autonomous University of Mexico since 2006. She has also been a visiting professor at the University of Alaska Fairbanks, the University of Oslo, and Universidad Nacional Autonoma de Mexico.  Gomez is the director of the Center for Cryosphere Research at UNAM. She is also a research associate at the Centre for International Forestry Research (CIFOR), an adjunct associate professor at the Scripps Institution of Oceanography, and a member of the editorial board for "Geography Compass."
