The Dunedin Volcano is an extensively eroded stratovolcano located in the Otago region of New Zealand. The volcano is situated near the town of Dunedin and has a basal diameter of about 8 kilometres. The most recent eruption occurred around 1700 AD and produced Plinian eruptions.  The Dunedin Volcano is a popular tourist destination due to its striking landscape, as well as its proximity to the city of Dunedin. It has been the site of numerous eruptions and is considered to be a potentially hazardous volcanoe. The volcano is monitored by the Department of Conservation.
