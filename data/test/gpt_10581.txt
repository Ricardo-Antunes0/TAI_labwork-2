Crawshay was born on 20 June 1892 in London, England, the son of Captain Hugh Crawshay and his wife Mabel Isabella Cartland. He was educated at Eton College and joined the Royal Air Force on 26 May 1915. He served during World War I as a pilot with No. 1 Squadron RFC and was awarded the Military Cross in 1917.  He served in the Second World War as a captain in No. 41 Squadron RAF, flying Bristol Blenheims during Operation Overlord in Normandy in June 1944. On 27 February 1945 he shot down two German Fw 190s over Amiens. He was killed in action a few minutes later, when his aircraft was shot down by a second Fw 190. He is buried in Reichswald Forest War Cemetery, Germany.  Crawshay was posthumously awarded the George Cross for his "gallantry and distinguished service" during World War II. The award was made on 7 December 1945 by King George VI at Buckingham Palace, London, England.
