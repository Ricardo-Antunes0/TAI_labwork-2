Motograter was an American heavy metal band formed in 1987. The band was initially signed to Caroline Records, but broke up in 1990. They reformed in 2004 and released their first album in 17 years, entitled "Vengeance Is Mine", in 2006. The band has since disbanded.  Motograter was formed in 1987 in Huntington Beach, California by vocalist/guitarist Chad Gray, drummer Rob Affuso, bassist Joey Vera and guitarist Phil Cappelli. After releasing an EP and a full-length album on Caroline Records, the band broke up in 1990. The five members went their separate ways until 2004, when they reunited and recorded their first album in 17 years, "Vengeance Is Mine". The album was released on Roadrunner Records and charted at number 44 on the "Billboard" 200. The band toured extensively in support of the album and played at several major festivals, including Ozzfest and Download Festival. In 2006, Motograter disbanded again.  In 2009, Gray formed a new Motograter with drummer Casey Scruggs (ex-Death), bassist Mike Angelakos (ex-Death) and guitarist Jeff Loomis (ex- Death). This lineup recorded the self-titled album in 2010 and toured extensively throughout North America.
