Frank Clayton Matthews (born December 31, 1947) is an American country music singer and songwriter.  Matthews was born in Saginaw, Michigan. He first came to prominence as the lead singer of the bluegrass group The Dillards, which he formed in 1972 with his brother-in-law and future country music star Jerry Douglas. The Dillards released four albums before disbanding in 1978. Matthews then started a solo career, releasing his debut album in 1979. He has since released 16 albums, most of which have been certified gold or higher by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA). He has also had several number one singles on the Billboard Hot Country Singles & Tracks charts, including "I Can't Stop Loving You" and "When I Fall In Love". Matthews has also won several awards, including a Grammy Award for Best Country Vocal Performance by a Male in 1990.
