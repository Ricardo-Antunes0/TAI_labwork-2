The  is the guide to kanji that is published by the Japanese Ministry of Education. The Jōyō kanji list was completed in 1946 and revised in 1954.  The Jōyō kanji list is a set of 1,945 characters that are considered to be the most useful and commonly used kanji in contemporary Japanese. The characters are arranged in 128 blocks, each of which is classified according to one of the seven radicals.
