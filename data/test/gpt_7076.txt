Jason Perry (born 29 December 1969) is an English singer and songwriter. He has released six studio albums and two compilation albums, as well as eighteen singles and two promotional singles.  Perry was born in Bournemouth, England, to a musical family. His father, Gary Perry, is a former lead singer of the 1960s pop group The Four Preps, and his mother, Julie Perry, is a singer and actress. He began his career as a solo artist in the early 1990s, and released his debut album, "Jason Perry", in 1995. The album charted at number thirty-seven on the UK Albums Chart. After the release of the album, Perry toured extensively throughout Europe and North America.  In 1998, Perry released his second album, "Small World". The album charted at number seventeen on the UK Albums Chart and spawned two hit singles—"Don't Worry" and "I Believe in You". After the release of the album, Perry embarked on a tour of Europe and North America.  In 2000, Perry released his third album, "Love Songs". The album charted at number nineteen on the UK Albums Chart and spawned two hit singles—"I'll Be There" and "Love Changes Everything". After the release of the album, Perry embarked on a tour of Europe and North America.  In 2002, Perry released his fourth album, "One Night Only".
