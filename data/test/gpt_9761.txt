The Vatnsmýrin Nature Reserve (, "water marsh") is a nature reserve in the southwest of Iceland. It covers an area of 2,209.5 ha and is part of the Vatnajökull National Park. The nature reserve was established in 1975 and it is managed by the Icelandic Nature Conservation Agency.  The nature reserve is situated in the southwestern part of Iceland, on the banks of the river Vatnajökull. It covers an area of 2,209.5 ha and is part of the Vatnajökull National Park. The nature reserve is home to a rich variety of plant and animal life, including Arctic terns, eider ducks, otters, lynxes, wolverines and wild horses.
