James Otto Birr (August 22, 1916 – September 9, 2002) was an American football player and coach. He served as the head football coach at the University of Utah from 1951 to 1956, compiling a record of 18–26–2. Birr was also the head basketball coach at Utah from 1947 to 1959, tallying a record of 376–191. Birr was inducted into the College Football Hall of Fame as a player in 1976.  Jim Birr played football for the University of Utah from 1937 to 1940. He played in three bowl games, including the 1942 Rose Bowl where he was named the Most Valuable Player.  After playing football at Utah, Birr served as an assistant coach at his alma mater from 1941 to 1949. He then became the head football coach at Weber State College in 1950 and led the Wildcats to an 8–1 record and a berth in the Missouri Valley Conference championship game.  Birr moved to Brigham Young University in 1951 and led the Cougars to an 8–1 record before being hired at Utah. In his first season at Utah, Birr led the team to a 5–4 record. The following year, Utah went 7–2 and finished third in the Pacific-8 Conference. In 1954, Birr's second season at Utah, the team went 9–2 and won the Pacific-8 Conference title.
