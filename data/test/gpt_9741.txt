Prem Anand (Odia: ପ୍ରେମ ଆନନ୍ଦ) is an Odia film actor who has appeared in over 250 films in a career spanning over five decades. He has been described as the "Odia film industry's most prolific and popular actor". He is considered one of the most respected and well-known actors of Odia cinema, and has won several awards, including the National Film Award for Best Actor for his performance in Nayak (1991).  Born into a poor family in Cuttack, Anand started his career as a stage actor and worked his way up to feature films. He made his acting debut in the 1953 film Puri Jagannath. He has starred in a wide range of genres, including dramas, comedies, action films, and love stories. He is known for his versatile acting skills and for his role in Nayak, which won him the National Film Award for Best Actor. Anand has also been involved in several social causes, including work towards the rights of Indigenous people and promotion of environmental conservation. He is currently retired from acting and lives in Cuttack with his wife.
