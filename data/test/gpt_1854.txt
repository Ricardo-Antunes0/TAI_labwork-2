Sally P. C. Njie or N'Jie (30 September 1967 – 6 September 2016) was a Cameroonian journalist and author.  Njie was born in Yaoundé, Cameroon, on 30 September 1967. She earned her undergraduate degree from the University of Bamenda and her master's degree from the University of Montpellier.  Njie worked as a journalist for several newspapers before founding her own paper, "Le Réveil" in 1997. She also served as the editor-in-chief of the newspaper until her death.  Njie's writing has focused on Cameroon's political and social landscape. She is the author of two books, "L'Empire des dieux" (2000) and "Le Monde sans Dieu" (2005).  Njie was diagnosed with leukemia in October 2015. She died on 6 September 2016 at the age of 50.
