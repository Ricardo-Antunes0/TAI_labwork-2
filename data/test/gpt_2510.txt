The Battle of Viadangos or Fontedangos (Fonte de Angos or Viadangos de la Corona) was fought on February 13, 1212, between the forces of Alfonso X of Castile and the nobles of the Kingdom of Aragon, commanded by James I of Aragon.  The battle was fought near Viadangos de la Corona, a village in the province of Zaragoza in Aragon. The Castilian army was led by Fernando III, Count of Flanders and James I of Aragon, while the Aragonese army was led by James II. The battle resulted in a Castilian victory, with Alfonso X claiming victory over James I.
