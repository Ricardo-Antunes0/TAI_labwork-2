Stephen Charles Balboni (; born January 16, 1952) is an American singer, songwriter, and record producer. He is best known as the lead singer and front man of the rock band Bon Jovi.  Born in Neptune City, New Jersey, Balboni grew up in Sayreville, New Jersey. He started his career as a lead singer with the band Slippery Slopes in 1970. The following year, he co-founded Bon Jovi with guitarist Richie Sambora and drummer Tico Torres. The band achieved international success with their debut album "Bon Jovi" (1977), which yielded the singles "Kiss Me Always", "Livin' on a Prayer", and "Wanted Dead or Alive". After several successful albums throughout the 1980s, Bon Jovi achieved their first No. 1 single in the United States with the ballad "You Give Love a Bad Name" (1987). The band's 1990 album "Keep the Faith" became their second No. 1 album in the United States. Their fourth album, "New Jersey" ( 1995), was their first No. 1 album in Australia and their fifth album, "These Days" (1998), was their first No. 1 album in the UK. In 2007, Balboni was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame as a member of Bon Jovi.
