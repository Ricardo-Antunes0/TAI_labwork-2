Dehradun  is the capital, largest city and the most populous city of Uttarakhand state in northern India. It is also the administrative headquarters of Dehradun district. According to the 2011 census, Dehradun had a population of 479,819, making it the sixth most populous city in India. Dehradun is situated on the banks of the River Teesta and has been a major religious and cultural centre in Uttarakhand since ancient times. The city has been the political, commercial and cultural centre of the region for centuries.  Dehradun is known for its natural beauty, including its vast forests, hills, lakes and rivers. The city is home to a number of universities and colleges, as well as a number of temples and archaeological sites. Dehradun is also known for its "chai" culture, which refers to the popular tea stalls found throughout the city.
