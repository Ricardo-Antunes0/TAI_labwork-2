She was the first woman to serve as director of the Museum of Modern Art (MoMA), where she served from 1934 to 1966.  Brandeis was born in 1885 in Boston, Massachusetts, to Jewish immigrants from Austria-Hungary. After studying at Boston Architectural College and the School of the Museum of Fine Arts, she worked as an art administrator at the Pennsylvania Academy of the Fine Arts and at the Metropolitan Museum of Art. In 1934, she was appointed director of MoMA, a post she held until her retirement in 1966. During her tenure, MoMA became a leading center for modern art, and Brandeis was widely recognized as one of its most influential directors. She died in 1975.
