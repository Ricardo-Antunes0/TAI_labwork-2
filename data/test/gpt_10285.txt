Sir Richard Vesey Hamilton  (28 May 1788 â€“ 21 December 1857) was a Scottish physician, naturalist, and explorer. He was born in Helensburgh, Dunbartonshire, the son of Alexander Hamilton and Rachel Vesey.  Hamilton was educated at the University of Glasgow and the University of Edinburgh. In 1814 he became a fellow of the Royal College of Physicians of Edinburgh. In 1816 he was elected president of the Royal College of Surgeons of Edinburgh.  In 1820 Hamilton set out on an expedition to explore the Amazon River. He reached its mouth in 1822, becoming the first European to do so. He also travelled up the Orinoco River. In 1830 he published an account of his travels, including a description of the Venezuelan rainforest.  In 1836 Hamilton was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society. He died in Edinburgh aged 79.  Hamilton married Caroline Smith in 1822. They had two children: Alexander Smith Hamilton (1823-1883) and Caroline Hamilton (1826-1909).
