Randolph Lewis Braham (December 20, 1922 – January 24, 2003) was an American economist and professor who specialized in public policy. He was the founder and director of the Center for Public Policy at the University of Virginia.  Braham was born in Richmond, Virginia, to Lewis Randolph Braham and Thelma (née Powell) Braham. He attended The Choate School in Wallingford, Connecticut, and graduated from Harvard University in 1943. He then served in the United States Army during World War II, earning a commission as a second lieutenant. After the war he returned to Harvard, where he received his doctorate in economics in 1948.  Braham began his academic career at Yale University, where he became assistant professor of economics in 1949 and full professor in 1959. In 1965 he moved to the University of Virginia, where he remained until his retirement in 1998. At Virginia he founded and directed the Center for Public Policy, which became one of the leading research centers in public policy.  Braham was a member of the National Academy of Sciences and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences. He served as president of the American Economic Association (1975), and as vice president of The Brookings Institution (1967-1968). He also served as chairman of the President's Council on Economic Advisors (1981-1982).  Randolph L. Braham died on January 24, 2003, at age 82.
