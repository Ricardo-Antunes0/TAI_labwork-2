Archencyrtus is an extinct genus of wasp in the family Vespidae. It was endemic to North America, where it is known from the middle Eocene to the early Oligocene.  Archencyrtus was a small wasp, about 1.5 cm long. Its body was covered in short, bristly hairs, and its wings were small and stubby. It was a aerial hunter, feeding on other insects in the air.
