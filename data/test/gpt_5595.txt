Kendal was born Margaret Shafto Kendal in London, the daughter of a theatrical agent. At the age of seven, she appeared in a small role in the play "The King's Speech". She made her stage debut in 1929 at the age of twelve, appearing alongside her father in a production of "The Merchant of Venice".  Kendal's first starring role came in 1932, when she appeared in an adaptation of Dickens' "A Tale of Two Cities" at London's Old Vic. She went on to appear in many plays and films throughout her career, including roles in "The Picture of Dorian Gray" (1945), "The Unsinkable Molly Brown" (1958), and "Oliver!" (1968). She also appeared frequently on British television, beginning with a performance on the BBC's wartime service "Wartime Theatre" in 1940.  Dame Madge Kendal was made a Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire in 1965 for services to drama. In 1987, she received a Lifetime Achievement Award from the British Academy of Film and Television Arts (BAFTA). She died three years later, aged 86.
