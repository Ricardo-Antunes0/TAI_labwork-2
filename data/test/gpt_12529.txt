Hoàng Xuân Sính (born September 8, 1933) is a Vietnamese singer.  Hoàng Xuân Sính was born on September 8, 1933, in Đà Nẵng, Vietnam. He started his musical career in the early 1950s, performing with the "Vietnamese Folk Choir" and later with the "Nguyen Cao Minh Choir". In 1957 he joined the "National Orchestra of Vietnam", and in 1963 he formed his own group, the "Hoàng Xuân Sính Combo".  Sính's most famous recordings date from the late 1960s and early 1970s, during which time he released a number of albums including "Người Việt Nam Của Tuổi Trăng" (1968), "Điệu Tiên Đêm" (1969), and "Tuổi Háo Vũ" (1971). In 1972 he won the Golden Bell Award for Best Singer.  Sính retired from music in the early 1980s, but has since made a comeback and resumed performing. He has also released a number of albums of traditional Vietnamese music.
