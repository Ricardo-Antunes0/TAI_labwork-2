The Black Family is a professional wrestling promotion founded by Jerry "The King" Lawler in Memphis, Tennessee in 1978. The company was originally called The Memphis Wrestling Association and Jerry Lawler was the booker. The promotion was renamed The Black Family when it began touring nationally. The Black Family is known for its hardcore wrestling matches, which often feature blood and gore.  The Black Family's flagship show is Memphis Wrestling Championship Wrestling (MWCW), which has been airing since 1978. The promotion also produces weekly television shows, including Memphis Wrestling TV and Memphis Championship Wrestling TV II, both of which have been airing since 1984. The company also produces a monthly magazine, Memphis Championship Wrestling Magazine.
