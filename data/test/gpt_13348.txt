Boronia repanda, commonly known as the granite boronia, is a species of flowering plant in the borage family, Boraginaceae.  Boronia repanda is a ligneous evergreen shrub or small tree growing to a height of up to 3 metres (10 ft). The leaves are elliptical, 10–25 cm (4–10 in) long and wide, with serrated margins. The flowers are arranged singly or in groups of up to six on short stalks at the ends of the branches, each flower having five yellow petals and five green sepals. The fruit is a brown capsule about 2 cm long.  The species is endemic to eastern Australia, where it is found in coastal and hilly areas from Queensland south to New South Wales and Victoria. It is tolerant of moderate dryness and is often found growing among granite outcrops. It is classified as vulnerable by the Australian Government Department of Sustainability and Environment.
