Dan Caruso (born July 1, 1963) is an American actor, comedian, and musician. He has appeared in over 200 films and television shows since 1983.  Born in New York City, Caruso is best known for his work in the television series "My Name Is Earl" and its spin-off "Everybody Hates Chris", as well as for his starring role in the 2004 film "The United States of Leland". He has also appeared in such films as "The Godfather Part II", "A Few Good Men", "The Princess Diaries", "The Hangover", and "Kiss Kiss Bang Bang".  In addition to his acting work, Caruso has released three comedy albums: "Big Trouble in Little China", "Not Enough Fish" and "Daddy's Home". He is also a musician, having played bass guitar in the bands The Blue Meanies and The Wack Packers.
