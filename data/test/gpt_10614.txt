Atherton is a town in the Metropolitan Borough of Salford, in Greater Manchester, England. It is situated 8 miles (13Â km) east of Manchester city centre, on the banks of the River Irwell. The population of the town was 18,859 at the 2011 census.  Atherton was founded in 1795 as a mill town on the banks of the River Irwell. It became an industrial community and a centre for cotton spinning and weaving. The textile industry declined in the late 20th century but Atherton remains an important commercial and residential town. The town has a history of footballing excellence with local clubs including Salford City Reds (1887), Atherton United (1905), and Salford City Ladies (1911).  The town is served by Atherton railway station and its primary shopping area is around Market Street.
