The Intergovernmental Oceanographic Commission of UNESCO (IOC/UNESCO) is an intergovernmental organization with a membership of 191 countries. It was established on 16 November 1972, as the International Commission for the Protection of the Marine Environment of the International Council of Scientific Unions, with a mandate to promote the understanding and conservation of marine environments.   The IOC is divided into five Regional commissions: Africa, Americas, Asia-Pacific, Europe and the Mediterranean, each with a specific mandate. The commissions are responsible for carrying out their respective mandates in accordance with the decisions of the governing council. The governing council is composed of representatives from all five regional commissions.   The IOC Secretariat is located in Paris, France.
