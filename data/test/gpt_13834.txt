Claude Vigée (born Claude Strauss; 3 January 1764 – 25 September 1842) was a French painter who became famous for his paintings of Classical and religious subjects.  Claude Vigée was born in Paris, the son of a sculptor. He began his artistic training as a sculptor, but switched to painting in 1785. He won the Prix de Rome in 1790, and completed his studies at the French Academy in Rome in 1795. He returned to Paris and began to exhibit his work, and in 1802 he became a member of the Institut de France. He also served as an official painter to Napoleon I.  Vigée painted many religious and classical subjects, as well as portraits. His most famous works are the paintings of the Cathedral of Notre Dame (1809–1810) and the Arc de Triomphe (1836). He died in 1842.
