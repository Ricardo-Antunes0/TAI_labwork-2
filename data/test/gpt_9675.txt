Jaime Lynn Herrera Beutler ( ; born January 25, 1984) is an American politician and businesswoman who is the current U.S. Representative for Washington's 3rd congressional district. She is a member of the Republican Party, and previously served as a member of the Washington State House of Representatives from 2008 to 2013.  Herrera Beutler was born in Seattle, Washington, to Jaime Herrera and John Beutler. She has two sisters and one brother. Herrera Beutler attended North Seattle Elementary School and Roosevelt High School in Seattle. She then studied at the University of Washington, graduating with a degree in political science.  She worked as a legislative aide for former State Representative Dave Ullrich from 2002 to 2004. Herrera Beutler then served as communications director for OneAmerica Votes, a voter registration organization, from 2004 to 2006.  Herrera Beutler was elected to the Washington State House of Representatives in 2006, representing the 42nd legislative district. During her time in office, she served as vice chair of the House Education Committee and as a member of the House Commerce, Justice, and Transportation Committee. In 2013, she was elected to the U.S. House of Representatives, defeating incumbent Democrat Jay Inslee in a close race.  In Congress, Herrera Beutler has been a member of the Republican Policy Committee and the Republican Steering Committee.
