Charles McArthur (May 1844 – 3 July 1916) was an Australian politician and a member of the Legislative Assembly of Queensland.  McArthur was born in 1844 at Lambton, Tasmania, the son of Alexander McArthur and his wife Isabella (née Miller). He migrated to Queensland with his parents in 1853, and worked as a farm labourer before becoming a wool buyer and commission agent. In 1875, he married Eliza Jane Gourlay, with whom he had five children. In 1889, he was elected to the Queensland Legislative Assembly as the member for Mackay; he held the seat until his death in 1916. McArthur was a strong supporter of labour rights, and was twice chairman of the Parliamentary Labor Party. He died in 1916 after a long illness, and was buried at Indooroopilly General Cemetery.
