The Fierce Dispute is a 1929 novel by W. Somerset Maugham. It tells the story of two friends, one French and one English, who drift apart over a period of many years as the French becomes more involved in left-wing politics and the English becomes more conservative.  The novel is set during the interwar period and is primarily a character study of the two men. It has been praised for its evocation of French life and culture, and for its depiction of the changing social attitudes of the time.
