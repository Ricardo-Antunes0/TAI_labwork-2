Asser was born in Kidderminster, Worcestershire, England on 31 October 1837. He was educated at Eton College and the Royal Military Academy, Woolwich.  He served in the Crimean War as a lieutenant with the Royal Engineers, and was present at the Siege of Sevastopol. In 1859 he was appointed commanding officer of the 3rd Battalion of the Royal Engineers. He served in the Second Boer War as a major with the 7th (Royal Irish) Dragoon Guards and took part in the Relief of Kimberley. He was promoted to lieutenant-colonel in 1900 and colonel in 1905.  He retired from active service on 30 June 1910. He died on 15 January 1911 at his home in Kingston upon Thames, Surrey.
