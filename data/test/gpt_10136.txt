Shaquille Harrison (born October 6, 1993) is an American professional basketball player for the Cleveland Cavaliers of the National Basketball Association (NBA). He played college basketball for Louisiana State University (LSU).  Harrison was born in Baton Rouge, Louisiana. He is the son of Shaquille O'Neal and Shaunie O'Neal. He has two older sisters, Shareese and Shaniqua. Harrison attended Baton Rouge Magnet High School, where he was a three-time All-State selection and helped lead his team to a state championship game appearance in his senior year.  Harrison originally committed to play college basketball for the LSU Tigers, but then changed his commitment to the Arizona Wildcats shortly before the 2010–11 season. After one season with the Wildcats, he declared for the 2011 NBA draft and was drafted by the Miami Heat with the sixth overall pick. However, he was traded to the Cleveland Cavaliers on draft night.  In his first season with Cleveland, Harrison started all 82 games and averaged 11.4 points, 5.4 rebounds and 1.9 assists per game. In 2014–15, he averaged 18.1 points, 7.8 rebounds and 2.3 assists per game as an All-Star starter for the Cavaliers. He was named to the 2016 Eastern Conference All-Star team and led Cleveland to their first championship in 52 years with a Game 7 victory over the Golden State Warriors in the 2016 NBA Finals. On July 10, 2017, Harrison signed a four-year $100 million contract extension
