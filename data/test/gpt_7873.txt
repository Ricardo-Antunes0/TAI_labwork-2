John Lewis, a Baptist minister who eventually became headmaster of Eton College. Lewis was educated at Eton and Christ Church, Oxford. He began his career as a teacher before becoming a journalist and publisher. In 1853 he became editor of the "British Quarterly Review".  Lewis was also a philanthropist, founding the London School of Economics (LSE) in 1895 and serving as its first president. He also founded the Lewis Foundation to provide scholarships to students from disadvantaged backgrounds. Lewis died in 1876.
