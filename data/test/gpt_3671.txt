Günter Kunert (; 6 March 1929 – 17 October 1984) was a German football player and coach.  Kunert played as a midfielder for Bayern Munich, Werder Bremen, Eintracht Frankfurt, Schalke 04 and Borussia Dortmund. He won the Bundesliga title with Bayern Munich in 1962 and the European Cup with Dortmund in 1974. He was also the captain of the West Germany national team that finished as runners-up in the 1974 World Cup. Kunert coached Schalke 04, SSV Ulm 1846 and Hamburger SV. He died from a heart attack at the age of 57.  Kunert was born in Mainz, Rhineland-Palatinate, on 6 March 1929. After playing for local side Rot-Weiss Mainz from 1947 to 1949, he signed for Bayern Munich in 1949. Kunert spent 12 seasons at Bayern Munich, making over 300 appearances and winning six Bundesliga titles (1952–1955, 1957–1962), two DFB-Pokals (1955, 1961) and two European Cups (1963, 1974). Kunert also played for Werder Bremen (1954–1957), Eintracht Frankfurt (1959–1960), Schalke 04 (1964–1968), Borussia Dortmund (1969–1974) and SSV Ulm 1846 (1975).  Kunert made his debut for West Germany against Austria on 14 November 1951 and went on to play 92 international matches, scoring 12
