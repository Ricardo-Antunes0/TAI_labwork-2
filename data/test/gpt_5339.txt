He lived in the United States from 1978 to 1985, and then returned to China. Chen's writing career began in the 1980s with short stories and essays. He became known for his novels, most notably "The Boy Who Harnessed the Wind" (2013), which was adapted into a film of the same name. Chen also translated several English-language works, including "The Catcher in the Rye" and "The Grapes of Wrath". He died of cancer in 2016.
