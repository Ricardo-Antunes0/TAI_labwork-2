Dhives Akuru or Divehi Akuru (island letters), also known as the Dhivehi language, is a sociolect of the Dhivehi language spoken in the Maldives. It is a prestige dialect, used by educated Maldivians. Dhives Akuru is mutually intelligible with the standard Maldivian language, but has some distinctive features. Mutual intelligibility between Dhives Akuru and the standard Maldivian language is high, but there are some distinctive features which set it apart from the other dialects. Notably, Dhives Akuru has more loanwords from Arabic and Persian than any other dialect of the Maldivian language.
