Bioko Norte (; Spanish for "North Bioko'') is an island in Equatorial Guinea, lying off the coast of Gabon. The island has an area of . It has a population of about 81,000 people.  The island is administratively divided into two municipalities: Mbandaka and Otrobanda. The port of Mbandaka is the main economic and transport centre on the island.  The main agricultural products are palm oil, coffee, cotton, and peanuts. The island has a number of small mining companies, including one producing uranium. There is also some fishing activity.  The island is served by Mbandaka Airport.
