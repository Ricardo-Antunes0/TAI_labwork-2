Ohrbach's was a moderate-priced department store with locations in Buffalo and Detroit, Michigan.  The Ohrbach's chain was founded in 1887 by Max Ohrbach in Buffalo, New York. The store was a moderate-priced department store with locations in Buffalo and Detroit, Michigan. In 1936, the chain was acquired by the Lazarus family of Detroit, Michigan. In 1969, the chain was sold to Kinney Shoes Corporation and ceased operations that same year.   The store is most noted for its contributions to fashion, design, and merchandising. It was one of the first retailers to feature designer names such as Balenciaga, Christian Dior, and Yves Saint Laurent in its clothing lines. In addition, Ohrbach's was known for its innovative window displays and its integrated marketing campaigns that targeted both women and men.
