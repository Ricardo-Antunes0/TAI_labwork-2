Wigston railway station has been the name of a railway station in the town of Wigston, Leicestershire, England since 1847. The station is on the Leicester to Loughborough Line and is operated by East Midlands Trains.  The station opened on 1 July 1847 as part of the Grand Junction Railway. The station was renamed Wigston in 1885. The Great Central Railway opened a branch from here to Beaumont in 1899. The line closed to passengers on 9 January 1966 and to goods on 7 May 1966. The line remained open for freight until 8 February 1980.
