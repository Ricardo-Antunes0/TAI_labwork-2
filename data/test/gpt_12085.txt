Woyzeck is a 2000 musical with music by Peter Schickele and a book and lyrics by David Ives. It is based on the 1843 novella of the same name by German author Heinrich von Kleist.   The show tells the story of Woyzeck, a fictional character whose life spirals out of control after he is accused of murdering his sweetheart, Marie. Forced to flee from justice, Woyzeck becomes a wanderer, living on the margins of society. Along the way, he meets a host of strange characters, each of whom contributes to his downfall.   The musical has been critically acclaimed, winning awards including four Tony Awards (including Best Musical), two Drama Desk Awards, and three Outer Critics Circle Awards. It has been performed around the world, including at New York's Broadway Theatre and London's West End.
