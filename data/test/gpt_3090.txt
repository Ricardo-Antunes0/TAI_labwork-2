Rex Woods (21 July 1903 – 18 November 1987) was an Australian artist.  Rex Woods was born on 21 July 1903 in Melbourne, Victoria, Australia. He studied at the National Gallery of Victoria School of Art from 1922 to 1926 under Arthur Streeton, and at the Royal College of Art in London from 1926 to 1929. Woods first exhibited at the Melbourne Art Gallery in 1927. He moved to Europe in 1930, and lived in France, Italy, and England before returning to Australia in 1941. Woods died on 18 November 1987 in Melbourne, Victoria.  Rex Woods was a major figure in Australian art during the 1930s and 1940s, and his work is representative of the Australian modernist tradition. Woods was influenced by European modernism, and his works typically feature abstracted forms and bright colours. He is recognised for his paintings of Melbourne cityscapes and landscapes, as well as for his sculpture.
