Howard Burton "Chip" Silverman, Ph.D, M.P.H., M.S., is the Doris Duke Professor of Health Policy and Management at the Harvard School of Public Health. He is also the co-director of the Harvard Program on Women's Health and a faculty member at the Chan School of Public Health. Silverman has authored or co-authored more than 200 journal articles and book chapters on health policy, women's health, and comparative health systems.  Silverman earned his B.A. from Brown University in 1978, his M.P.H. from Columbia University in 1982, and his Ph.D. from Harvard University in 1986. Prior to joining the faculty at Harvard, he was a senior associate at McKinsey & Company, where he worked on projects in health care reform, labor markets, and environmental policy.  Silverman's research has focused on understanding how public policies influence population health and well-being, with a particular emphasis on gender disparities and health disparities across sociodemographic groups. His work has helped to shape U.S. health policy debates and has influenced the design of public health programs around the world.
