Karlsruhe was a light cruiser, the second of the two ships of the Karlsruhe class. She was laid down in 1907 at the AG Vulcan shipyard in Stettin, and launched on 10 July 1909. She was commissioned into the Kaiserliche Marine on 8 September 1910.  Karlsruhe served in the First World War, where she participated in the Battle of Jutland on 31 May-1 June 1916. She was subsequently involved in the naval operations in the Mediterranean Sea and the Black Sea. She was allocated to the High Seas Fleet on 1 October 1918 and was reduced to reserve on 21 December 1920.  Following her decommissioning, Karlsruhe was used as a training ship until she was stricken from the naval list on 15 November 1936. She was sold for scrap on 2 April 1937 and subsequently broken up.
