Bradley Sinopoli (born April 14, 1988) is a Canadian football wide receiver for the Ottawa Redblacks of the Canadian Football League (CFL). He played college football at Western Michigan. He was signed by the Buffalo Bills as an undrafted free agent in 2010.  Sinopoli has also been a member of the Oakland Raiders, Edmonton Eskimos, and Toronto Argonauts.  Sinopoli was born in Sudbury, Ontario. He attended Notre Dame High School in Welland, where he played football and basketball. Sinopoli then attended Western Michigan University, where he played college football. As a junior in 2008, Sinopoli had 79 receptions for 1,264 yards and 11 touchdowns. As a senior in 2009, he had 97 receptions for 1,345 yards and six touchdowns.  After going undrafted in the 2010 NFL Draft, Sinopoli was signed by the Buffalo Bills as an undrafted free agent. He made his professional debut on September 5, 2010 against the Miami Dolphins. In 2012, Sinopoli was traded to the Oakland Raiders for a seventh-round draft pick in 2013. In 2014, Sinopoli was traded to Edmonton Eskimos for a sixth-round draft pick in 2015. In 2016, Sinopoli was traded to Toronto Argonauts for quarterback Zach Collaros and a fourth-round draft pick in 2017. On February 9, 2018, Sinopoli was released by the Toronto Argonauts.
