Madhavan Pillai Ramakrishna Kurup was an Indian physicist who made significant contributions to the fields of physics, mathematics and astrophysics. A prolific writer, Kurup authored over 300 articles and six books. He was awarded the Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civilian honour, in 2007.  Kurup was born on 5 July 1931 in the village of Kuttippuram in the present-day Tanjore district of Tamil Nadu, India. After completing his undergraduate studies at Madras University, he obtained his Ph.D. from the University of Chicago in 1953 under the guidance of Nobel Laureate William Fowler. Kurup then worked at various institutions in India before joining the Indian Institute of Science in Bangalore as a staff scientist in 1964. He became head of the department of physics at IISc in 1985 and served as its director from 1990 to 2001. Kurup was also a visiting professor at numerous universities across the world. He died on 15 December 2016 at the age of 88.
