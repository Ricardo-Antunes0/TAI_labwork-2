Andre Waismann (Portuguese: André Waisman)  is a Brazilian physician who is a Professor of Medicine at the University of Pennsylvania, where he is also the Director of the Perelman School of Medicine's Division of Cardiovascular Medicine.  Waismann was born in São Paulo, Brazil, on October 17, 1956. He graduated from the University of São Paulo with a medical degree in 1980 and subsequently completed a residency in internal medicine at the University of São Paulo. He then completed a fellowship in cardiology at Johns Hopkins Hospital in Baltimore, Maryland.  Waismann joined the faculty at Penn in 1995 and became the Director of the Division of Cardiovascular Medicine in 2009. He is a member of the American Academy of Cardiology and the American College of Cardiology.  Waismann's research focuses on heart failure and cardiovascular disease. He is particularly interested in developing new treatments for these conditions and has published more than 800 papers in peer-reviewed journals. He has also been involved in several clinical trials, most notably the REACT trial, which was designed to evaluate the effectiveness of resynchronization therapy for patients with heart failure who are not candidates for surgery.
