Let the Road is the debut studio album by American singer-songwriter Taylor Swift. It was released on October 27, 2005, by Big Machine Records. The album was produced by Swift and Nathan Chapman with assistance from Davey Johnstone and Shellback.  The album has received generally positive reviews from music critics, who praised its production and Swift's vocals. It has sold over three million copies in the United States and over six million copies worldwide.  In late 2004, Taylor Swift signed a record deal with Big Machine Records. She began work on her debut album in early 2005 with production help from Nathan Chapman, Davey Johnstone, and Shellback. "Let the Road" was released on October 27, 2005, by Big Machine Records. The album received generally positive reviews from music critics. At Metacritic, which assigns a normalized rating out of 100 to reviews from mainstream publications, the album received an average score of 76, which indicates "generally favorable reviews". Stephen Thomas Erlewine of AllMusic noted that "while she doesn't quite have the voice of an Angel Olsen or the songwriting chops of Neko Case yet", her "songs are often impressively well-crafted". He also complimented the production which he deemed "rich and textured".  "Let the Road" debuted at number four on the US Billboard 200 chart with first-week sales of 154,000 copies.
