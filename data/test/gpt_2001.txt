Hitesh Sukhlal Mehta (born 1959) is a Canadian-born Indian film director, producer and screenwriter. He is the founder and CEO of the production company Bluegrass Films.  Born in Montreal, Quebec, Mehta moved to India with his family at the age of five. After attending the National School of Drama in New Delhi, he started his career as an assistant director on films such as "Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge" (1995) and "Kuch Kuch Hota Hai" (1998), before making his directorial debut with the comedy-drama "Paanch" (2003). For his work on "Paanch", he received the National Film Award for Best Feature Film in Hindi.  Mehta's subsequent films include the romantic drama "The Life of Pi" (2012), the crime thriller "No One Killed Jessica" (2013), the historical drama "Queen" (2015), and the comedy-drama "Father" (2018). He has also produced films such as "The Dirty Picture" (2007) and "Bhaag Milkha Bhaag" (2011).  Mehta has been married to actress Farah Khan since 1992.
