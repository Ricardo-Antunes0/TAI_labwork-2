Optymistychna (: meaning "optimistic", also known as the "Optymist's Cave") is a cave located in the Lipnica Forest near Jasna Gora, southern Poland. The cave was formed about 20,000 years ago during the last Ice Age. During the warmer periods of the last glacial period, animals such as mammoths, bison, horses and reindeer used to live in the Lipnica Forest.     The cave is mostly known for its paintings, created by prehistoric artists about 18,000 years ago. The paintings depict animals such as horses, bison and mammoths, as well as geometric designs.
