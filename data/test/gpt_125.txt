–Samuel Alston "Sam" Beatty (April 23, 1923 – September 10, 2006) was an American actor and film producer. He appeared in more than ninety films and television shows from the 1940s through the 1990s, and is best known for his work in the Western film genre.  Beatty was born in Los Angeles, California, the son of Mabel (née Alston) and Samuel A. Beatty Sr., a traveling salesman. He had a brother, Sidney. Beatty's father died when he was young, leaving the family in poverty. Beatty's mother worked as a file clerk at Universal Pictures to support her sons. In 1944, aged 18, Beatty enlisted in the United States Marine Corps and served in the Pacific theater during World War II. After returning to Los Angeles, he began his acting career with a role in the Broadway play "Orpheus Descending" in 1947.  Beatty made his screen debut in the B-movie "Gunfight at the O.K. Corral" (1957), and achieved prominence with his roles in several Westerns, including "The Great Escape" (1963), "The Wild Bunch" (1969), "The Shootist" (1976), and "Butch Cassidy and the Sundance Kid" (1969).
