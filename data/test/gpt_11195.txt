Dmitry Sharifyanovich Karimov (born October 23, 1978) is the current President of Uzbekistan, having held the office since 2006. He is also the Chairman of the Central Asian Republics Economic Union.  Karimov was born in Navoi, Uzbekistan, on October 23, 1978. He completed his secondary education at the Fergana Secondary School in 1997 and subsequently studied at Moscow State Institute of International Relations (MGIMO), graduating with a degree in international relations in 2001.  Karimov began his career as a researcher at the Institute of World Economy and International Relations (IMEMO) in Moscow. In 2002, he joined the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Uzbekistan as a diplomat, serving as an assistant to the Director General for European and American Affairs from 2006 to 2007. He then served as Deputy Minister for Foreign Affairs from 2007 to 2008.  In August 2006, Karimov was appointed acting president of Uzbekistan after President Islam Karimov underwent surgery for an aneurysm. He was confirmed as president by the Uzbek Parliament in December 2006 and took office on January 1, 2007. As president, Karimov has faced significant human rights abuses by the Uzbek government including censorship and restricted freedom of speech and assembly.
