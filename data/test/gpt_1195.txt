Hakone Gardens is an  traditional Japanese garden located in the city of Hakone, in the Kanagawa Prefecture of Japan. The garden is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.  Hakone Gardens was created by the shogun Tokugawa Ieyasu in 1604 as a place to take relaxation and enjoy the view of Mount Fuji. The gardens occupy an area of and have over 1,000 species of plants, including a number of rare plants. The garden has been open to the public since 1872.   The garden has been renovated numerous times, most recently in 2003. The gardens are open from April to November.
