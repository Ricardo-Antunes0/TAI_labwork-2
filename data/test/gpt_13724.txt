Stanley James Hallett (October 6, 1930 – November 21, 2007) was an American actor. He is perhaps best known for his role as Dr. Mark Sloan on the ABC medical drama series "Grey's Anatomy".  Hallett was born in Hollywood, California, the son of Loretta (née Beatty) and Charles Hallett. He graduated from Beverly Hills High School in 1948. After serving in the United States Army, Hallett attended the University of Southern California and then the American Academy of Dramatic Arts, graduating in 1954.  Hallett made his Broadway debut in 1957 in a production of "The Prime of Miss Jean Brodie". He appeared in several productions on Broadway before landing his first major television role as Dr. Mark Sloan on "Grey's Anatomy" in 2003. He reprised his role for all six seasons of the show's run.  Hallett appeared in several feature films over the years, including "The Fortune Cookie" (1966), "Midnight Cowboy" (1969), "Alice Doesn't Live Here Anymore" (1974), "Murphy's Law" (1985), and "Revenge of the Nerds" (1987). In 1996, he guest-starred on an episode of "X-Files".  Hallett married actress Marsha Mason in 1958; they had two daughters before divorcing in 1972.
