Gujarat  is a state on the western coast of India. The state is bordered by Maharashtra to the north, Rajasthan to the east, Madhya Pradesh to the south and the Arabian Sea to the west. With a population of over 100 million, it is the sixth most populous state in India. The state has an area of 1,752,005 kmÂ² and a population of 1,267,799,000 as of 2011. Gujarati is the official language of Gujarat and is spoken by around 98% of the population.  The state was formed on 1 November 1956 from the merger of the Princely State of Gujarat with parts of Rajastan and Madhya Bharat. The state is named after Gujarat's second ruler Maha Vir Singh who ruled from 1719 to 1743. Gujarat is one of the most industrialized states in India, with major sectors including textiles, chemicals, pharmaceuticals and engineering. The state has been a center for trading for centuries and contains some of the oldest trading ports in India. Gujarat is also home to some of India's most prestigious educational institutions, including Mahatma Gandhi University and Sardar Patel University.
