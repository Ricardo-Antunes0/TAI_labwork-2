Frank Neubert (28 September 1915 – 13 November 1984) was a German mathematician who worked on algebra and calculus.  Neubert was born in Lübeck, Germany, on 28 September 1915. He studied mathematics at the University of Göttingen and received his doctorate in 1939. He then worked at the University of Berlin, where he became a professor in 1958. Neubert died in Berlin on 13 November 1984.  Neubert's major works include the book "Algebra" (1958), which is considered a standard reference on the subject, and "Calculus I" (1959), which is often used as a first course in the subject.
