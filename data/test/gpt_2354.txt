Deborah Chow is a Canadian filmmaker, television producer, and actress. She is best known for her work in the television series MDA: Medical Detectives of Atlanta and her directorial debut, the feature film Big Eyes.  Chow was born in Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada to immigrant parents from Hong Kong. She attended the University of British Columbia and studied film. She started her career as a production assistant on the Canadian series The Professionals and went on to work in various roles on Canadian television series including The Border, Murdoch Mysteries, and Da Vinci's Inquest. In 2003, Chow moved to the United States to work on the first season of MDA: Medical Detectives of Atlanta. She became co-creator and showrunner for the show during its second season and remained an executive producer through its four seasons.  In 2012, Chow directed her feature film debut, Big Eyes, which premiered at the 2012 Toronto International Film Festival. The film received positive reviews from critics and won several awards including the Golden Globe Award for Best Motion Picture – Musical or Comedy and the BAFTA Award for Best Film Not in the English Language. Chow is also a recipient of a Directors Guild of America Award nomination for Outstanding Directing – Long Form.
