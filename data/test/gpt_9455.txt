Gustáv Slamečka (born 5 June 1959) is a Czech conductor. He is the Artistic Director of the Prague Symphony Orchestra and Principal Conductor of the Czech Philharmonic.  Born in Prague, Slamečka began his musical studies at the age of 10, studying violin with Vlastimil Brod and conducting with Jiří Bělohlávek. He later studied with Václav Neuman and Ivan Fischer at the Academy of Music in Prague, where he earned his diploma in 1984.  In 1985, Slamečka was appointed chief conductor of the Brno Philharmonic, a post he held until 1995. From 1995 to 1998, he was music director of the Dresden Staatskapelle. In 1998, he was appointed Chief Conductor and Artistic Director of the Prague Symphony Orchestra, a post he has held since 2002.  Slamečka has made numerous recordings for labels such as Deutsche Grammophon, Decca, and Naxos. He has also collaborated extensively with other prominent conductors, including Riccardo Chailly, Sir Simon Rattle, Gianandrea Noseda, and Valery Gergiev.  In January 2018 it was announced that Slamečka had been appointed Principal Conductor of the Czech Philharmonic effective from 1 March 2018.
