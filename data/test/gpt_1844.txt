Jenő Buzánszky (4 May 1925 – 11 September 2009) was a Hungarian conductor, violist, and composer.  Born in Budapest, Buzánszky studied at the Franz Liszt Academy of Music, where he was a pupil of László Lajtha. He made his conducting debut in 1948 with the Hungarian Radio Symphony Orchestra. He worked as a conductor with many orchestras throughout Europe and the Americas, including the Berlin Philharmonic Orchestra, the Vienna Philharmonic Orchestra, the London Symphony Orchestra, and the New York Philharmonic. In 1962 he founded the Budapest Symphony Orchestra. He also composed music for stage and film, including the orchestral piece "Requiem". He died in 2009 at the age of 86.
