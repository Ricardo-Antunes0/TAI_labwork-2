Bokolmayo (Boqolmayo in Somali, Bokolmayo English) is a town in the Mogadishu region of Somalia. It is located in the Lower Shabelle province, about 8 kilometres southwest of the provincial capital, Hargeisa. The town has a population of about 10,000 people.  Bokolmayo was founded in the early 19th century by the Ajuran Sultanate. The town served as a regional centre for commerce and trade. In the late 19th century, it came under British control and became a centre for education. In the early 20th century, it came under Italian occupation and subsequently became a stronghold for the Somali National Army during the Somali Civil War.  The town is home to a number of mosques and schools. It is also known for its livestock market, which is one of the largest in Somalia. The town is served by a number of small businesses, including traders in livestock and agricultural products.
