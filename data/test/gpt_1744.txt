Roberts was born in 1942 in Huntsville, Alabama, and attended public schools there. He earned his B.S. from the University of Alabama in 1964 and his J.D. from the University of Alabama School of Law in 1967.  Roberts practiced law in his native Huntsville from 1967 to 1984, when he was appointed as a United States District Judge for the Northern District of Alabama by President Reagan. He served as a judge for 14 years, until his appointment as Secretary of Defense.   Roberts resigned from his judicial duties on October 20, 2000, due to health concerns stemming from a stroke he had earlier that year. He died on October 20, 2000, at the age of 58, in Madison, Alabama.
