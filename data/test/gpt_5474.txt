The Real Kids are an American rock band from Los Angeles, California. The band was formed in 1994 by brothers and longtime friends, Josh and Jesse Echols.  The Real Kids have released six full-length albums, two EPs, and one compilation album. The band's most successful album to date is their 2001 release, "The Real Kids". The album was certified platinum by the RIAA and has sold over 220,000 copies.  The Real Kids have toured extensively throughout the United States and Europe, selling out venues such as the Hollywood Bowl and London's Brixton Academy. In 2006, The Real Kids were nominated for a Grammy Award for Best Rock Album for "The Real Kids". The band lost to the Foo Fighters in the category.   In 2009, Jesse Echols founded the record label 88 Fingers Records which released the band's sixth album, "Dear Life".
