Janet McAdams (born 1957) is an American actress, best known for her roles in the television series The Office and in the films The Notebook and Doubt.  McAdams was born in Athens, Ohio, to a Protestant family. She is of Scots-Irish and English descent. When she was ten, her family moved to Knoxville, Tennessee, where she attended the University of Tennessee at Chattanooga. She then moved to New York City to pursue an acting career.  In 1984, McAdams made her Broadway debut in The House of Blue Leaves. She also appeared in the Broadway productions of Crimes of the Heart (1990), The Little Foxes (1991), and Death of a Salesman (1992).  McAdams' first television role was as secretary Jan Levinson on the NBC sitcom The Office from 2003 to 2005. Her portrayal of the uptight but determined office worker won her critical acclaim and several award nominations, including an Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Comedy Series in 2005. McAdams reprised her role as Jan Levinson for two seasons of the spin-off show The Office: United States of Tara from 2009 to 2011.  Since 2006, McAdams has starred as Beth Miller in the ABC family drama series The Good Wife. For her performance as Beth Miller, McAdams has been nominated for three Primetime Emmy Awards, winning one in 2009.
