Furnace Brook Parkway is a historic parkway in the town of Furnace Brook, New York. The 2.5 mile long parkway is a linear park that traverses the town from north to south. It was designed by noted landscape architect Frederick Law Olmsted and opened in 1892. The parkway is a well-preserved example of early 20th century park design and features a number of well-maintained historic structures, including a stone arch bridge over Furnace Brook, a number of formal gardens, and a large deer park.  Furnace Brook Parkway was designated a National Historic Landmark in 1990. The parkway is maintained by the town of Furnace Brook and is open to visitors year-round.
