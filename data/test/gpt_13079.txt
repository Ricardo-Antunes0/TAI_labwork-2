He moved to the Thirteen Colonies with his parents in 1750, settling in the Province of New York. He studied law with John Jay and was admitted to the bar in 1768. He practiced law in Canandaigua, New York.  Adams was elected as a Democratic-Republican to the United States Congress, serving from March 4, 1793, until his death on March 22, 1813. He was a delegate to the New York State constitutional convention of 1801. He was buried in Canandaigua Cemetery.
