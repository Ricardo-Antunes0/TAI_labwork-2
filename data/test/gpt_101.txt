Julius Sello Malema (born 3 March 1981) is a South African politician who has been the president of the African National Congress (ANC) since 18 December 2013. Malema became Deputy President of the ANC in December 2012 and served in that position until he was elected President of the ANC on 18 December 2013.  Malema grew up in a poor family in Soweto. He first became politically active at the age of 16, when he joined the National Union of Youth (NUY) and began protesting against apartheid. After leaving high school, Malema became involved with the ANC Youth League, serving as its treasurer from 2002 to 2005. He was also a member of the African National Congress's national executive committee from 2005 to 2009.  In 2009, Malema was sentenced to five years' imprisonment for assault and defamation after attacking then-ANC MP Chris Hani with a tyre iron during an anti-government protest. He was released in February 2011 after serving three years of his sentence.  Malema played a leading role in the Marikana massacre, in which 34 striking platinum miners were killed by security forces on 16 August 2012. The following day, Malema led a march through Soweto in protest at the killings; he was later convicted of incitement to violence and given a four-year suspended sentence.
