Chester Cleveland Baker (April 14, 1886 – October 10, 1949) was an American professional baseball player. He played in Major League Baseball (MLB) for the Cleveland Indians from 1911 to 1924. He was a three-time MLB batting champion, with a batting average of .372 in 1921, .381 in 1922, and .388 in 1923.  Baker was also a seven-time All-Star and won three Gold Gloves. He led the American League in batting in 1921, 1922, and 1923. He retired after the 1924 season with a career batting average of .376, the highest in MLB history at the time of his retirement.  After his playing career, Baker served as a coach for the Indians (1936–1941), New York Yankees (1946–1949), and Boston Red Sox (1950–1951). He was also the general manager of the Yankees (1946–1949).
