Margaret Florey is an Australian linguist whose research focuses on the syntax and morphology of Australian aboriginal languages.  Margaret Florey was born in Melbourne, Victoria in 1956. She completed her B.A. at the University of Melbourne in 1978 and her M.A. at the University of California, Berkeley in 1981. She then undertook post-doctoral research at the University of Queensland, where she worked until 1988.  Florey has been a professor of linguistics at the University of Technology, Sydney since 1988, and has also held visiting positions at the University of California, Santa Cruz and the Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics in Germany. Her research focuses on the syntax and morphology of Australian aboriginal languages, and she has published extensively on this topic. She is also a member of the Australian Academy of Sciences and the Royal Society of New South Wales.
