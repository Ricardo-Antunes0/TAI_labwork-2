Richard Yates was born in Boston, Massachusetts, on June 6, 1921. He attended Bowdoin College and then the University of Chicago, where he studied under the renowned critic Lionel Trilling. After serving in World War II, Yates worked as a journalist and then as a teacher before publishing his first book in 1958. His novels include Revolutionary Road (1962), which won the National Book Award, and The Executioner's Song (1979), which was awarded the Pulitzer Prize. He died in 1976.
