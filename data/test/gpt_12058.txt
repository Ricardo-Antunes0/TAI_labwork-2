Jean-Marie Eveillard (born 1940) is a French art critic, historian, and curator. He is the founder and director of the Musée d'Orsay in Paris.   Eveillard was born in 1940 in Lyon, France. He studied history at the University of Lyon and then earned his degree at the École des Beaux-Arts in Paris. After a period of teaching at the École des Beaux-Arts, he became a critic for "Le Figaro" magazine in 1968. In 1984, he founded the Musée d'Orsay with Pierre Bergé. He has been its director since its opening in 1986.  Eveillard has written extensively on art history and has been particularly noted for his criticism of postmodernism. He has also published books on artists such as Léonard de Vinci, Claude Monet, and Paul Cézanne. In 2006, he was made a Chevalier de la Légion d'Honneur.
