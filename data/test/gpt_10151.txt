Hugh Joseph Chisholm I (; May 2, 1911 – December 10, 1988) was an American philosopher, legal theorist, and political scientist. He is best known for his work in the philosophy of law, political philosophy, and social theory.  Chisholm was born in Buffalo, New York, the son of Margaret (née Higgins) and John Chisholm. He received his B.A. from the University of Buffalo in 1933 and his J.D. from the University of Chicago Law School in 1936. He was an assistant professor at Chicago Law School from 1938 to 1940, then an instructor there from 1940 to 1951. He became a full professor at Chicago Law School in 1951 and served as its dean from 1960 to 1968.  He was a visiting professor at the University of Edinburgh from 1951 to 1952, at the University of California, Berkeley from 1954 to 1955, and at Columbia University from 1957 to 1958. He served as a trustee of the University of Chicago from 1965 to 1968 and as its president from 1968 to 1974. He also served on the boards of directors of both Ford Motor Company and Sears Roebuck & Company.  Chisholm died in Chicago at the age of 78.  Chisholm's philosophical work concerns legal theory, political philosophy, and social theory.
