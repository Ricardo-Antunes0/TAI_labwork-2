The Tyrolean State Theatre in Innsbruck      The Tyrolean State Theatre is a historic theatre in Innsbruck, Austria. It was designed by the architect Friedrich von Schmidt and built between 1872 and 1875. The theatre has a capacity of 1,858 seats.  The Tyrolean State Theatre was originally built as a private theatre by the brewer Georg von Habsburg-Lothringen. In 1895 it was bought by the city of Innsbruck and renamed the Innsbruck State Theatre. The theatre was extensively renovated between 1996 and 1999 by the architect Armin Hofmann.
