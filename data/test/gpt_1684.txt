The National Softball Association (NSA) “is a 501(c)(3) not-for-profit organization headquartered in Lawrence, Kansas, and founded in 1984. The NSA is the national governing body for softball in the United States.”  The NSA has over 1,500 member clubs located in all 50 states and in numerous Canadian provinces. These clubs are responsible for the administration and promotion of softball at the local, regional, and national levels. In addition to its governance responsibilities, the NSA provides support services such as training programs, equipment and apparel sales, and league management services. The NSA also conducts research on softball issues and promotes the sport through its media outlets, including its annual publication, Softball America.
