Donald Vivian Bennett (May 9, 1915 â€“ February 22, 2004) was a United States Circuit Judge of the United States Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit.  Bennett was born on May 9, 1915 in San Francisco, California. He received a Bachelor of Arts degree from the University of California, Berkeley in 1937 and a Juris Doctor from Stanford Law School in 1940. He was in private practice in San Francisco from 1940 to 1948. He was an Assistant United States Attorney for the Northern District of California from 1948 to 1951. He was a judge on the Superior Court of California, County of Marin from 1951 to 1957.  Bennett was nominated by President Dwight D. Eisenhower on July 10, 1957 to a seat on the United States Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit vacated by Judge William T. Rucker. He was confirmed by the Senate on August 2, 1957 and received his commission on August 3, 1957. He served as Chief Judge from 1971 to 1977. Bennett served as a member of the Judicial Conference of the United States from 1971 to 1975 and as its Chairman from 1975 to 1977. He retired from the bench on February 22, 2004.
