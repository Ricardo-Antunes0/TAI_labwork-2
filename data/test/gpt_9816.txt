Mindanao  is the second-largest island in the Philippines after Luzon. It is located in the southwestern part of the country, and is bounded on the north by the Celebes Sea, on the east by the South China Sea, on the south by Mindanao Strait, and on the west by Palawan. The island is divided into three provinces: Bukidnon, Compostela Valley, and Muslim Mindanao. The major cities are Zamboanga del Norte, Davao City, General Santos City and Cagayan de Oro.  Mindanao is an agricultural and mining province. It is home to many ecological wonders such as Mount Apo, Lake Lanao, Mount Kitanglad and Mount Pulag. The province has a rich cultural heritage with a number of indigenous groups such as the Maranao, Igbo-Ukwu, Isneg and Bajau. The province also has a number of tourist destinations such as Masiu National Park, Bunawan Resort and Siargao Island.
