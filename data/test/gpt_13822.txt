Sir Murray Gordon Halberg  (born 7 September 1939) is a Canadian-born British biochemist and academic. He is currently the Pro-Vice-Chancellor for Research and Innovation at the University of Cambridge, a position he has held since January 2018.  Halberg was educated at the University of Toronto, where he earned his B.Sc. in 1961 and his M.Sc. in 1963. He then undertook postgraduate studies at the University of Cambridge, where he obtained his D.Phil. in 1966.  Halberg has been a professor of biochemistry at the University of Cambridge since 1967, and served as its Pro-Vice-Chancellor for Research and Innovation from 2013 to 2018. In January 2018, he was appointed to the newly created position of Pro-Vice-Chancellor for Research and Enterprise.  Halberg has published extensively in biochemistry, most notably on the mechanism of protein synthesis and on the role of lipids in cell signalling. He was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society in 1997, and a Fellow of the Academy of Medical Sciences in 2002.
