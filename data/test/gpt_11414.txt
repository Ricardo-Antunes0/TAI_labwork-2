He was born in Hamilton, Upper Canada, the son of Simon Patten, a United Empire Loyalist, and Frances Nelson, both of Scottish descent. Patten attended Upper Canada College and then the University of Toronto where he obtained his law degree in 1874. He was called to the Ontario bar in 1875.  Patten practised law in Hamilton until 1892 when he moved to Toronto. He served as solicitor for York County from 1892 to 1896 and for Peel County from 1896 to 1898. In 1898, he was appointed deputy attorney general for Ontario. Patten was elected to the House of Commons of Canada as a Liberal-Conservative candidate in the riding of York North in the Canadian federal election of 1900. He was re-elected in 1904 and 1908. Patten served as Minister without Portfolio in the government of Sir John A. Macdonald from 1911 to 1912. He retired from politics in 1916. Patten died in Toronto at the age of 68.
