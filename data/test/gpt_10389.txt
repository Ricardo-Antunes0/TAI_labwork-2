Pond Meeting House is a historic Quaker meeting house in Chester, Pennsylvania. The Meeting House was built in 1741 and is a three-story, gable-roofed, stone building with a front porch. It is the oldest surviving Quaker meeting house in the United States.  The Pond Meeting House is currently used as a museum and is open to the public on select weekends. It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1978.
