Rhapsody is an operetta in 2 Acts, by Victor Herbert. It was first performed on December 11, 1898, at the Metropolitan Opera in New York City. The libretto was written by Arthur Hammerstein II and the music by Herbert.  Rhapsody tells the story of a love affair between a young man and woman. The young man is a talented musician and the woman is an opera singer. The opera is set against the backdrop of a European royal court.
