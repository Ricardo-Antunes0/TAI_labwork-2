Youssef Mouihbi (, born 1 April 1985 in Rabat) is a Moroccan footballer who currently plays for Raja Casablanca.  Youssef Mouihbi was born in Rabat on 1 April 1985. He started playing football with local side USM Alger, before moving to Spain to join Atlético Madrid. Mouihbi made his La Liga debut on 11 September 2002 in a 2–1 away defeat against Real Zaragoza, and scored his first goal for the club on 18 October 2002 in a 3–2 home victory over Racing Santander.  Mouihbi left Atlético Madrid in 2004 and signed for FC Barcelona, where he spent two seasons. He made his Barcelona debut on 11 August 2004 in a 0–0 away draw against Valencia CF, and scored his first goal for the club on 17 September 2004 in a 3–1 home victory over Racing Santander.  Mouihbi also played for Real Zaragoza, Valencia CF, Racing Santander, FC Barcelona and Real Betis. He made his international debut for Morocco on 4 February 2003 in a 2–1 away loss against Gabon, and played at the 2004 African Cup of Nations. Mouihbi was part of the Moroccan team that reached the final of the 2007 AFC Championship qualification tournament, but they were defeated by Qatar.  In 2009, Mouihbi joined Raja Casablanca.
