The Clutha were a traditional Scottish band. The group was formed in Glasgow in 1965 by brothers Jimmy and Robert Burns. They were one of the most popular traditional Scottish bands of their era.  The Clutha are best known for their hit single "Auld Lang Syne" (1976), which reached number one on the UK Singles Chart. The group also enjoyed success with other singles including "The Flowers of Scotland", "The Lass of Ayr" and "The Whistle That Dies". The Clutha disbanded in 1982.
