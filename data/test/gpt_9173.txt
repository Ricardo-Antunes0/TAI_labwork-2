Colin Maurice Nicholson  (21 June 1936 – 7 October 2016) was an English film and stage actor.  Nicholson began his career in the late 1950s, appearing in minor television roles before making his feature film debut in the 1961 comedy-drama "A Taste of Honey". In the early 1970s, he became a leading man in British films, with roles in "The Hindenburg Disaster" (1975), "Nicholson's Nick" (1977), and "The Towering Inferno" (1974). He achieved international recognition for his performance as Roy Batty in the 1976 Ridley Scott film "Blade Runner", which made him one of the most highly regarded actors of his generation.  Nicholson's subsequent films included "The Shining" (1980), for which he received critical acclaim, and "Rain Man" (1988), for which he was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Actor. In 1992, Nicholson starred as Inspector Javert in Andrew Lloyd Webber's stage production of "Les Misérables", for which he won a Tony Award. He reprised the role in the 1998 film adaptation, earning another Academy Award nomination. In 2002, Nicholson starred in Ron Howard's drama "Angels and Demons", for which he was nominated for a Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Motion Picture Drama. He starred as Max DeMille in the 2003 biopic "The Great White Hope", and as Batman in Christopher Nolan's 2005 blockbuster "The Dark Knight".
