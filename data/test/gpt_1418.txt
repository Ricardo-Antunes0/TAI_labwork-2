Welle-Strand was born in Christiania (now Oslo), Norway. He finished his secondary education in 1904 and then studied medicine at the University of Oslo, graduating in 1912. He then worked as a doctor in Drammen, before moving to Kristiania (now Oslo) in 1919. Here he became head physician at the National Hospital for Diseases of the Chest, from 1925 to 1936. He also served as a professor at the University of Oslo from 1928 to 1936. In 1936 he became director of the National Cancer Institute in Oslo, a post he held until his retirement in 1948.  During World War II, Welle-Strand served as Director General of Health Services in Norway, from 1940 to 1945. He was also a member of the Norwegian Nobel Committee from 1940 to 1948. In 1945 he was made an honorary member of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.  Welle-Strand was awarded the Riksakademiens Fredrikke Medal in 1950, and the King's Medal of Merit in 1952. He was also a member of the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences.   Welle-Strand died in Oslo on 10 October 1957.
