Rosemary Ann Butler (born April 6, 1947) is an American actress, singer, and author. She is best known for her role as Sue Snell on the ABC sitcom The Brady Bunch. In addition to her television work, Butler has starred in several motion pictures, including Love Story (1970), The Great Gatsby (1974), The Amityville Horror (2005), and Dolphin Tale 2 (2014).  Butler was born in Los Angeles, California, the daughter of actors Ann Blyth and Leslie Butler. She is of Scottish and Irish descent. Her parents divorced when she was young, and she was raised by her mother. After graduating from high school in 1965, she studied at the University of Southern California before beginning her acting career.  Butler's first role was on the ABC sitcom The Brady Bunch in 1969. She played Sue Snell, the youngest sister of Maureen McCormick's character Marcia Brady. The show ran for six seasons before ending in 1974. Following The Brady Bunch, Butler starred in several motion pictures, including Love Story (1970), The Great Gatsby (1974), The Amityville Horror (2005), and Dolphin Tale 2 (2014).
