Quentin Richard Stephen Letts (born 6 February 1962) is an English journalist and author, specialising in politics and the media.  He has been the political editor of the "Daily Mail" since 2002, and is a member of the Press Complaints Commission. He has also written for "The Observer", "The Spectator" and "The Sunday Times".  Born in Manchester, Letts was educated at the Dragon School, a state secondary school in Oxford. He then studied history at Jesus College, Oxford, where he was president of the Oxford Union.  Following university, Letts worked as a researcher for the Conservative MP Robert Kilroy-Silk. He then joined "The Daily Mirror", where he became political editor in 1992. He moved to "The Daily Mail" in 2002.  In 2006, Letts published a biography of Tony Blair entitled "". In it, he criticised Blair's actions during the Iraq War and his relationship with George W. Bush. The book was met with largely positive reviews from critics.  In 2009, Letts published "A Government of Wolves: The Emerging American Police State". The book criticises the increasing corporatisation of the United States police force and argues that this has led to increased levels of police brutality and abuse towards citizens. It was met with mixed reviews from critics.  In 2012, Letts published "", a history of Britain during the 2010s. It was met with generally positive reviews from critics.
