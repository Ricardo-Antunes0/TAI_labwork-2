Vladimir Vladimirovich Tsyganko (; ; also Țâganco, Tțiganco, and Tsygancko; ; born 2 October 1958) is a retired Russian professional football player and coach.  Tsyganko made his professional debut in the Soviet Second League in 1975 for FC Dynamo Moscow. He played over 350 games in the Soviet First League, the Russian Premier League, and the UEFA Champions League with Dynamo Moscow, Spartak Moscow, CSKA Moscow, Lokomotiv Moscow, FC Barcelona, Krasnodar Krai and FC Rostov. He also played for the Russian national team.  Between 2002 and 2006, he was manager of FC Rostov. In 2007, he became manager of FC Terek Grozny. In 2009, he became manager of FC Anzhi Makhachkala. On 7 July 2013, he was sacked by FC Anzhi Makhachkala after a poor run of results. On 5 September 2014, he was sacked by FC Krylia Sovetov Samara after a poor run of results.     On 14 December 2014, he was appointed as manager of FC Rostov. He left Rostov on 21 December 2016 after a poor run of results.  On 22 December 2016, he was appointed as manager of FC Krylia Sovetov Samara.
