Francisco Elías Riquelme (26 June 1890, in Montevideo – 12 October 1976) was a Uruguayan footballer who played as a midfielder. He is considered one of the greatest players in Uruguayan football history. Riquelme is considered one of the most complete midfielders of all time, with a strong passing ability, as well as great dribbling skills.  Riquelme made his professional debut with River Plate in 1911, and became one of the club's most dominant players. He helped River Plate win the Copa Libertadores in 1922 and 1926 and the Inter-American Cup in 1924. In 1928, he joined Boca Juniors, where he won four league titles and the Copa Argentina in 1930. He retired in 1936 after playing for Newell's Old Boys. Riquelme was also a successful international player, winning three Copa América titles with Uruguay (1927, 1931 and 1935). He was inducted into the Uruguay Football Hall of Fame in 1967.
