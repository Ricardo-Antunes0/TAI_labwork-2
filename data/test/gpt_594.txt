Mount Newcomb is a 13,422-foot-elevation (4,091 meter) mountain in the Newcomb Mountains of the White Mountains of New Hampshire, United States. It is the highest mountain in the White Mountains.   The mountain is located within the Presidential Range, southwest of Mount Washington and northeast of Mount Monroe. The summit can be reached by a strenuous, 4.5 mile (7.2 km) hike from the Mount Washington Auto Road. There are three privately owned campsites on the mountain, as well as an open summit area with views in all directions. The Appalachian Trail crosses the summit at 3.5 miles (5.6 km) from the Auto Road, and there are several other trails that lead to the summit.
