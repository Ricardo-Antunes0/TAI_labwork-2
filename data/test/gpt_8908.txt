Wright was born in Brooklyn, New York, to Jewish parents. She received her undergraduate degree from Barnard College in 1948, and her doctorate from Columbia University in 1955. Wright began her career as a research psychologist at the Rand Corporation, where she worked from 1957 to 1961. She then became a professor of psychology at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, where she served as chairman of the department from 1978 to 1984. In 1984, she moved to Rutgers University, where she served as the university's first female provost until her retirement in 1997.  Wright was a fellow of the American Psychological Association and the Society of Psychoanalytic Dialogues. In 1993, she received the Presidential Award for Distinguished Contributions to Psychology from President Bill Clinton. She died at her home in Princeton, New Jersey, aged 84.
