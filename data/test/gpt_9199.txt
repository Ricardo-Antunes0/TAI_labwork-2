Roger De Vlaeminck (; born 24 August 1946) is a Belgian former professional road bicycle racer who competed as a professional from 1967 to 1984. He was the winner of the Tour de France three times, in 1974, 1977 and 1978. De Vlaeminck also won the Vuelta a España twice, in 1974 and 1977.  De Vlaeminck was one of the greatest classics specialists of all time, winning all six of the Monument races (the Tour of Flanders, Paris–Roubaix, Liège–Bastogne–Liège, Amstel Gold Race, La Flèche Wallonne and Ardennes Classics). He also won five stages in the Tour de France and three stages in the Vuelta a España. De Vlaeminck was nicknamed "Le Manxman" because of his island home off the coast of Scotland.
