Corona (Spanish for "Crown") is a city in Riverside County, California, United States. As of the 2010 census, the population was 201,079, making it the fifth largest city in California. Corona is also the home of Riverside International Airport, which is one of the largest airports in the United States.  The city was founded in 1870 and was named after the crown jewel of the Spanish empire. The city was originally called Temescal after a nearby hill that Coronados explorer Juan Rodríguez Cabrillo thought may have been the hill where Jesus was crucified. The city was later renamed Corona after Fernando VII, king of Spain at the time.  Corona is located at 33°11′N 117°24′W﻿ / ﻿33.2164°N 117.4606°W﻿ / 33.2164; -117.4606 (33.2165,-117.4759), in southwestern Riverside County on the eastern banks of the Santa Ana River. It is bordered by Moreno Valley to the north, Lake Elsinore to the east and southeast, Norco to the south and west and Corona Hills to the northwest.
