Edward Weston Carpender ( – May 16, 1877) was an American painter who was a master of the Hudson River School.  Edward Weston was born in 1855 in Mount Holly, New Jersey. His father was a lawyer and his mother was an accomplished painter. As a child, Weston was very interested in art and showed an early talent for it. He studied at the National Academy of Design in New York City under the instruction of John Singer Sargent and Thomas Eakins. In 1877, Weston traveled to Europe to study painting under Jean-Baptiste-Camille Corot at the French Academy of Painting and Sculpture in Paris. Upon his return to the United States, he settled in Greenwich Village in New York City and became one of the leading painters of the Hudson River School. Weston died in 1877 in Saint-Étienne-du-Rouvray, France, at the age of 49.
