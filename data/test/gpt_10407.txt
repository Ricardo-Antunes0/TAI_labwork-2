A Gaiety Girl is an English musical with a book and lyrics by George and Ira Gershwin and music by Harold Arlen. The show was first produced on Broadway in 1934 and ran for 1,318 performances. It was revived in 1951 and 1975.  "A Gaiety Girl" tells the story of Nellie O'Neill, an Irish immigrant who comes to New York City in search of a new life. Forced to work as a chorus girl in a seedy theater, she is determined to make something of herself. With the help of her friends, Nellie rises to the top of the theater world and finds love along the way.
