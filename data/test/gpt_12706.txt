Kamalesh Maitra (28 April 1928 – 22 April 2009) was an Indian mathematician and a professor at the University of Delhi. Kamalesh Maitra is known for his work in algebra, number theory, and analysis. In particular, he is known for his solutions to the Riemann hypothesis and the Weil conjectures.  Kamalesh Maitra was born in 1928 in a small village in Uttar Pradesh, India. He received his undergraduate degree in mathematics from Allahabad University in 1948 and his doctorate from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in 1959. He joined the faculty of the University of Delhi as a professor in 1965. He retired from the university in 1998 and died on 22 April 2009.  Kamalesh Maitra's research focused on algebra, number theory, and analysis. His work included solutions to the Riemann hypothesis and the Weil conjectures. He also developed methods for solving systems of linear equations.
