The Tatanagar–Bilaspur section is part of the Golden Quadrilateral highway project that will connect the four major cities of India – Mumbai, Delhi, Kolkata and Chennai. The section traverses through the states of Gujarat and Madhya Pradesh.  The Tatanagar–Bilaspur section is a 183-km-long road that connects the city of Tatanagar in Gujarat with the city of Bilaspur in Madhya Pradesh. The section is also known as the Bilaspur–Tatanagar section. The total cost of the project is Rs 8,706 crore.  The Tatanagar–Bilaspur section was inaugurated by Prime Minister Narendra Modi on 12 December 2016. The section forms a part of the Golden Quadrilateral highway project that was proposed by then-Prime Minister Manmohan Singh in 2010.
