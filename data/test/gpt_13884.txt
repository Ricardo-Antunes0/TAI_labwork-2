The Burmese Border loach, angelicus loach or bonytongue loach (A. angelicus) is a species of ray-finned fish in the family Cyprinidae.  The Burmese Border loach is a small, slender fish that can reach a length of 24 cm. It has a long, slender body with a pointed snout and protruding eyes. The coloration is variable, but usually it is light green with scattered dark spots or blotches. The belly is white and the scales are small and smooth. The Burmese Border loach is found in fast-flowing streams and rivers in Myanmar and Thailand. It feeds on insects, algae, and crustaceans.
