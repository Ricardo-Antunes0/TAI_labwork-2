The  Children Act 1948 was an act of the United Kingdom Parliament that created the welfare state for children in the United Kingdom. The act made provision for a range of social services, including housing, health care, education, social security and food. It also created new courts to deal with child welfare cases and established the National Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Children (NSPCC).  The act was introduced by Labour MP Leo Abbot and it received royal assent on 28 July 1948.
