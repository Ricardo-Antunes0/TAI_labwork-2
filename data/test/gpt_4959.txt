The mugger crocodile (Crocodylus palustris) is a large crocodile that is endemic to the muddy rivers and estuaries of the Australian mainland. It is the largest extant crocodile and the heaviest extant crocodile.  The mugger crocodile is a large, powerful and aggressive animal that can grow up to 3.3 metres long and weigh up to 460 kilograms. It has a broad head with prominent ridges above the eyes and a snout that is short but very broad. The mugger crocodile has a green or brown body covered with small, scattered spots of darker colour. The underparts are whitish, and the tail is long and stout. The mugger crocodile feeds mainly on large prey, including fish, turtles and mammals. It is capable of swimming powerfully and rapidly through water up to 1 metre deep.
