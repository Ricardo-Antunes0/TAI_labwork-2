Robert S. Rivkin (born December 6, 1960) is an American lawyer who served as the United States Attorney for the Southern District of New York from 2001 to 2003.  Rivkin was born in 1960 in Brooklyn, New York. He is a graduate of Yale Law School, where he was an editor of the "Yale Law Journal" and received his bachelor's degree from Princeton University in 1982. After law school, Rivkin clerked for Judge J. Michael Luttig on the United States Court of Appeals for the Fourth Circuit.  Rivkin practiced law in Washington, D.C., before returning to New York City in 1989 to join the law firm of Wachtell, Lipton, Rosen & Katz. In 2001, President George W. Bush nominated Rivkin to be the United States Attorney for the Southern District of New York. Rivkin was confirmed by the United States Senate and served until 2003. During his time as US Attorney, Rivkin led investigations into organized crime and corruption in both Manhattan and Brooklyn.  Rivkin returned to Wachtell, Lipton, Rosen & Katz in 2003 and has since focused his practice on criminal defense and white-collar criminal investigations. He also serves as a director at the investment firm Allen & Company LLC. Rivkin married Elisabeth Acker in 2005 and they have two daughters.
