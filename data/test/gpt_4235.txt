Maxine Minniver (also Blake, Donovan and Kinsella) is a British author. She is the author of six novels, three collections of short stories, and a non-fiction book.  Minniver was born in London on 4 October 1956. She was educated at the University of East Anglia, where she studied English Literature. Minniver's first novel, "The Cutting Edge", was published in 1987 and won the Somerset Maugham Award. Her novel "The Language of Flowers" was published in 1995 and won the Whitbread Prize. "Beloved" was published in 2008 and won the Orange Prize for Fiction.  Minniver's non-fiction book "The Girl with the Dragoon Tattoo" was published in 2009 and became a bestseller in Britain. It was longlisted for the 2010 Man Booker Prize and won the Scottish Book Awards Book of the Year prize. In 2012 she published her sixth novel, "The Throwback". Minniver currently lives in London.
