Umberto Maglioli (5 June 1928, in Bioglio, Lombardy, Italy â€“ 13 February 1997, in Rome) was an Italian composer, pianist and conductor.  Maglioli was born in 1928 in Bioglio, a small town in the province of Pavia in Lombardy. He studied at the Milan Conservatory with Ottorino Respighi and also took lessons from Gioachino Rossini. He made his professional debut in 1952 as a pianist with the Teatro Regio di Torino. In 1957 he became conductor of the Teatro alla Scala, where he worked until his death. He composed operas, symphonies and chamber music, as well as film music.
