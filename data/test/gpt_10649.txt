Bruce Ferber is an American novelist and short story writer. Ferber's novels are set in the fictional town of West Egg on Long Island in 1922.  Ferber was born in Brooklyn, New York, on February 16, 1925. After graduating from high school, he studied at Vassar College, where he graduated in 1947. He then worked as a journalist for "The New York Times", "The New Yorker", and "Esquire". His first novel, "The Dream Life of Eddie Bauer", was published in 1959 and won the 1960 National Book Award. Ferber's most famous novel is "The Great Gatsby", which was published in 1974 and was awarded the Pulitzer Prize that year. Ferber also wrote the screenplay for the film version of "The Great Gatsby" that was released in 1974.  Ferber has been married three times and has three children. He currently resides in West Egg, Long Island, where he continues to write novels and short stories.
