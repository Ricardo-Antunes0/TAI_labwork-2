Nenad Medić (Serbian Cyrillic: Ненад Медић, born December 11, 1965) is a Serbian-Montenegrin computer scientist, known for his work on automatic theorem proving and program verification.  Nenad Medić was born in Belgrade, Yugoslavia, on December 11, 1965. He obtained his undergraduate degree in mathematics from the University of Belgrade in 1989 and his PhD in computer science from the University of California, Berkeley in 1994.  After postdoctoral research at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) and the University of Wisconsin–Madison, he became a faculty member at the University of Utah in 1997. He has been a Full Professor there since 2002.  He is also a Adjunct Professor at the Université catholique de Louvain (Belgium), where he served as Chair of the Department of Computer Science from 2006 to 2009.  Medić's research interests include automatic theorem proving and program verification. He has made seminal contributions to these areas, developing practical techniques for verifying complex software systems. His work has had significant impact on the field of software engineering, and he has been recognized with numerous awards and honours, including the ACM SIGPLAN Notation Prize in 2001 and the IEEE Computer Society Distinguished Service Award in 2007. In 2013 he was elected to the National Academy of Sciences (NAS).
