Raman Hrabarenka (Belarusian: Раман Грабарэнка, born 24 March 1987) is a Belarusian professional footballer who plays for FC BATE Borisov as a defender.  Hrabarenka started his career with FC Minsk in the Belarusian Premier League. He moved to Scottish side Rangers on loan in January 2009, making his debut in a 2–0 defeat to Hibernian. He made five appearances for Rangers before returning to Minsk at the end of the season.  Hrabarenka joined Ukrainian club FC Metalurh Donetsk on a three-year contract in July 2011. He made his debut for Metalurh Donetsk in a 1–1 draw with Arsenal Kyiv on 17 July 2011. Hrabarenka was part of the Metalurh Donetsk side that won the Ukrainian Cup in 2013. Hrabarenka left Metalurh Donetsk in January 2016 after six years with the club.  He signed for Russian club FC Krylia Sovetov Samara on a two-and-a-half year contract in January 2016. He made his debut for Krylia Sovetov Samara in a 3–2 victory over FC Terek Grozny on 20 January 2016. Hrabarenka left Krylia Sovetov Samara at the end of the 2017 season after six years with the club.
