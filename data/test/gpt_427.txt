The Emeryk Hutten-Czapski Museum (Also the Czapski Museum; Polish: Muzeum Emeryka Huttena-Czapskiego) is a museum located in the city of Pozna≈Ñ, western Poland. It was founded in 1924 and named after the Polish general and statesman Emeryk Hutten-Czapski.    The museum has a collection of paintings, sculptures, furniture, porcelain, jewellery and weapons. The main collection is devoted to the history of Poland and the history of art in the country. It also has a significant collection of prints and drawings. The museum also has a large numismatic collection.
