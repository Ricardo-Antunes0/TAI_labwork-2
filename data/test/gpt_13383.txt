Alicia Paz is an artist based in Mexico City. She is known for her innovative painting and sculpture, her critical writing, and her activism on behalf of social justice.  Alicia Paz was born in Mexico City on March 6, 1948. She is a descendant of the Aztec poet, Cristóbal Colón. Paz received her early education at the Colegio de la Frontera Sur in Ciudad Juárez, Chihuahua. In 1968 she enrolled in the Escuela Nacional de Bellas Artes (ENBA) in Mexico City, where she studied with Guillermo Kahlo and David Alfaro Siqueiros.  Paz's work has been exhibited internationally, and she has received numerous awards including the National Prize for Arts and Sciences (1981), the Cervantes Prize (1993), the Premio Casa de las Américas (1995), and the Premio Fonoteca del Instituto Nacional de Bellas Artes (1998). She is a member of the Salón de la Plástica Mexicana and the Academia de la Historia de México.
