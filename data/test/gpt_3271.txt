Maarat Saidnaya, or Maaret Saidnaya  is a city located in the Al-Baath General Governorate of Syria. It is a suburb of Damascus, located about 10 kilometers northeast of the capital.  The city was named after Sheikh Said al-Din al-Baathi, an Islamic scholar and jurist who lived in the 14th century. The population was estimated to be around 190,000 in 2004.  Maarat Saidnaya has an elevation of about 1,170 meters above sea level. The city is surrounded by mountains on all sides and is situated in a fertile plain. It has a hot desert climate with very hot summers and mild winters.
