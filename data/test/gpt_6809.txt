Kathleen Hallisey Rubins (born October 14, 1978) is an American actress and singer-songwriter. She is known for her roles as Meredith Grey on the ABC medical drama Grey's Anatomy, Jenna Maroney on the NBC comedy series 30 Rock, and Diane Lockhart on the CBS legal drama The Good Wife. Rubins released her debut album, Are You There, Vodka? It's Me, Chelsea, in 2012.  Kathleen Hallisey Rubins was born in 1978 in Northridge, California. She is the daughter of Janice (n√©e Hallisey), a teacher and speech pathologist, and Gary Rubins, a telecommunications executive. Rubins has two younger sisters. After attending Northridge High School and Santa Monica College, Rubins started her career as a cast member of the Fox sitcom That '70s Show (1998-2002). She also had a recurring role on the WB series 7th Heaven (1999-2000) and appeared in several episodes of Veronica Mars (2002-2004).  Rubins was cast as Meredith Grey on the ABC medical drama Grey's Anatomy (2005-2015), for which she received two Primetime Emmy Award nominations. She starred in the films How to Lose a Guy in 10 Days (2003) and Just Go With It (2010), and guest-starred on many television series, including ER, CSI: Crime Scene Investigation, House, Bones, Alias, Boston Legal, The Good Wife, Parks and Recreation and Brooklyn Nine-Nine.
