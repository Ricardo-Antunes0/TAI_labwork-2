Barbara M. Knickerbocker Beskind (born 1924) is an American business executive and philanthropist.  Beskind was born in 1924 in Newark, New Jersey to a Holocaust survivor, Eugenie (n√©e Gutman), and Morris Knickerbocker, a real estate developer. Beskind attended the Ethical Culture Fieldston School in Bronx, New York. She received her bachelor's degree from Barnard College in 1944 and her master's degree from Columbia University in 1946.  Beskind began her career as an accountant with Price Waterhouse in 1946. She became partner in the firm in 1962 and was named president and CEO in 1977. During her tenure at Price Waterhouse, she was responsible for the company's growth from an accounting firm to a global business services company. Beskind served as chairman of the board of directors of Price Waterhouse from 1978 to 1986 and as chairman of the board of directors of American Express from 1986 to 1992.  Beskind is a trustee of the Museum of Modern Art and a member of the boards of trustees of both The Wharton School and Barnard College. She is also a member of the Council on Foreign Relations and the American Enterprise Institute. In 1993, Beskind was awarded an honorary doctorate by Barnard College.
