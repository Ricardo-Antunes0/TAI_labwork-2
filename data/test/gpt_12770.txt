Margaret Anne Stanley, OBE FMedSci is a British virologist who has made significant contributions to the understanding of human virus diseases.  Stanley was born in 1934 in London, England. She attended University College London where she obtained her BSc in 1954 and her MD in 1960. She completed her postgraduate training at the London School of Hygiene & Tropical Medicine, where she obtained her PhD in 1967.  Stanley began her career as a research fellow at the University of London. She then became a lecturer at the University of Edinburgh, where she remained until 1982. In 1982, she was appointed as a professor at the University of Birmingham, where she remained until her retirement in 2003.  Stanley has made significant contributions to the understanding of human virus diseases. Her work has focused on the development of vaccines for human influenza viruses, including the creation and testing of new vaccine candidates. She is also known for her work on hepatitis C and on the genetic basis of human disease. Stanley was elected an honorary fellow of the Royal College of Physicians in 1981 and an honorary fellow of the Royal Society in 1998. She was also awarded the OBE in 1998 for her contributions to science.
