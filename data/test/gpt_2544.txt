Shirley Schaub Wilson (September 26, 1925 – September 1, 2008) was an American actress and singer.  Shirley Wilson was born in Los Angeles, California, the daughter of Virginia (née Schaub) and Max Wilson. She attended high school at the Convent of the Sacred Heart in Los Angeles. Wilson made her stage debut in 1944 in a production of "The Music Man".  Wilson's film debut came in the 1945 film "The Hunchback of Notre Dame" as Esmeralda. She appeared in such films as "The Madwoman of Chaillot", "The Bells of St. Mary's", "The Nun's Story", "The Best Years of Our Lives", "Wilson", and "The Defiant Ones".  In 1968, Wilson won a Tony Award for her performance in the Broadway production of "The Three Sisters". She also received a Golden Globe Award nomination for her performance. In addition to her stage work, Wilson appeared in numerous television productions, including "The Defenders", "Hannibal", and "Murder, She Wrote".  Wilson married actor Richard Kiley on October 9, 1951; the couple divorced in 1966. She later married music producer and composer Harry Warren on September 1, 1970. Wilson died from cancer on September 1, 2008, aged 87.
