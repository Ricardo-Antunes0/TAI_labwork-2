Jenny Dalton-Hill (born March 5, 1974) is an American singer-songwriter, record producer, and musician. She has released four studio albums: "Jenny Dalton" (2000), "The Beginning" (2002), "Wildflower" (2005), and "Set the Fire" (2008).  Dalton was born in Boston, Massachusetts. She began her career as a folk singer-songwriter, releasing her debut album, "Jenny Dalton", in 2000. The album received critical acclaim and earned Dalton a nomination for the Grammy Award for Best New Artist. Dalton followed up her debut album with the 2002 release "The Beginning". The album was more pop-oriented and produced three top ten singles on the US charts. In 2005, Dalton released her third album, "Wildflower". The album peaked at number two on the US Billboard 200 and spawned two number one singles on the Billboard Hot 100: "A Thousand Miles" and "Good Days". In 2008, Dalton released her fourth album, "Set the Fire". The album peaked at number three on the US Billboard 200 and spawned two number one singles on the Billboard Hot 100: "I'll Stand" and "Beautiful Mess".
