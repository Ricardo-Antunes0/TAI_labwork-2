Jane Eyre is a musical drama with a book by Alan Jay Lerner and music by Frederick Loewe. The story is loosely based on the novel by Charlotte Bronte, and tells the story of Jane Eyre (played by Rumer Willis), an orphan who is taken in by her uncle, Mr. Rochester (played by Michael York), and falls in love with him.  The musical opened on Broadway in 1967 and ran for 1,812 performances. It won three Tony Awards, including Best Musical. A film adaptation of the musical was released in 1975, and won two Academy Awards, including Best Picture. A stage adaptation of the film premiered at the Royal National Theatre in London in 2018.
