Thato Rantao Mwosa is a Botswana-American writer-director, and founder of the production company, Depth Filmworks.  Thato Rantao Mwosa was born on January 3rd, 1988 in Gaborone, Botswana to parents of Setswana descent. After completing his secondary education at a local secondary school, Mwosa moved to the United States in 2006 to study film at the University of Southern California. In 2009, he graduated with a degree in Film and Television Production.   Since 2008, Mwosa has been working as a writer and director in the United States. His films have been screened at various film festivals around the world, including: the Amsterdam International Film Festival (2009), the Marrakesh International Film Festival (2011), and the San Francisco International Film Festival (2013). In 2014, he was awarded the prestigious jury prize at the Sundance Film Festival for his short film 'The Rider'.   In 2017, Mwosa founded Depth Filmworks, a production company that specializes in independent films. He is also a member of the Directors Guild of America (DGA).
