FabricLive 48 is the fourth album by Bristol-based post-rock band Filthy Friends. Recorded at several locations across Bristol, England, in 2008 and 2009, it was their second album and their first release on the label Warp.  The album is a diverse and eclectic mix of post-rock, electronica and ambient music. Themes of isolation, alienation and loss are explored throughout the album.  The album was well received by critics, with many praising the band's unique sound and atmospheric compositions. It has been cited as an influence by several post-rock bands, including Mogwai and Godspeed You! Black Emperor.
