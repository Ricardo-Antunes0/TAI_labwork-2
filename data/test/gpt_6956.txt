Kuttabul is a rural town and locality in the Lockyer Valley Region, Queensland, Australia. It is located on the banks of the Brisbane River, about north-west of the state capital, Brisbane. At the 2011 census, Kuttabul had a population of 1,832.  The name Kuttabul is derived from the Aboriginal word for "meeting place". The town was first settled in 1876 by a party of Aborigines who had been transported from Moreton Bay to work on the sugarcane plantations in the district. The town was gazetted as a town on 5 September 1885.  The town is served by Kuttabul railway station on the Sunshine Coast Line.
