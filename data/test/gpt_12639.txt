Elizabeth Humfrey, known professionally as Betsy (stylized as betsy), is an American singer and songwriter.  Betsy was born in Detroit, Michigan on October 6, 1981 to parents Lisa Humfrey (n√©e Campbell) and Rick Humfrey. She has a younger brother named Jeremias. Growing up, Betsy attended Western Hills Elementary School and then Lawrence Technological University. After graduating with a degree in musical technology, Betsy moved to Chicago to pursue a music career.  In 2006, Betsy released her self-titled debut EP through independent record label Red Balloon Records. The album peaked at number 37 on the "Billboard" Independent Albums chart. In 2007, she was featured on the track "The Way You Love Me" from the album "Unforgettable" by Canadian singer Nelly Furtado. In 2008, Betsy signed to Interscope Records and released her second EP, "Betsy". The EP peaked at number 69 on the "Billboard" Independent Albums chart.  In 2009, Betsy released her first full-length album, "Fearless". The album peaked at number 46 on the "Billboard" 200 chart and was certified gold by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA). The lead single from the album, "What Would You Do?", peaked at number six on the "Billboard" Hot 100 chart.
