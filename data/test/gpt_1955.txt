Maria Toorpakai Wazir (; ; born November 25, 1942) is a Bangladeshi politician and diplomat who has been the Foreign Minister of Bangladesh since December 2014. Previously she served as the country's Permanent Representative to the United Nations from December 2007 to December 2014.  Wazir was born in Jalpaiguri, West Bengal, India. She earned a bachelor's degree in political science from Lady Shri Ram College for Women, University of Delhi in 1967. She earned a master's degree in international relations from the Fletcher School of Law and Diplomacy at Tufts University in 1971.  Wazir began her career as a diplomat with the Ministry of External Affairs of India. From 1977 to 1979, she served as the Indian ambassador to Kuwait. From 1979 to 1981, she was the Indian ambassador to Bahrain. From 1981 to 1985, she was the Indian ambassador to Somalia. From 1985 to 1989, she was the Indian ambassador to Sudan. From 1989 to 1991, she was the Indian ambassador to Qatar. From 1991 to 1993, she was the Indian foreign secretary.  Wazir served as the Ambassador of Bangladesh to France from 1993 to 1995. From 1995 to 1997, she was the Director General of Protocol and Consular Affairs at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Bangladesh. From 1997 until her appointment as Permanent Representative to the United Nations in 2007, she served as Director General of Protocol at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Bangladesh.  Wazir was nominated by then-President Sheikh Hasina for her position
