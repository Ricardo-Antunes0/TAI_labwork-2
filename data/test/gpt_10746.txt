Sushil Datta, also known as Sushil Datta Sharma, is an Indian-born entrepreneur, investor and philanthropist. He is the co-founder and CEO of Genpact, a software company which he founded in 2001.  Datta was born in Ahmedabad, India in 1967. He earned his bachelor's degree from the University of California, Berkeley in 1989 and his MBA from the Harvard Business School in 1992. Datta moved to the United States to start his business career and worked as a management consultant for several years.  In 2001, Datta co-founded Genpact with Vineet Nayar. The company became one of the leading providers of outsourced software development services. In 2007, Genpact was acquired by Accenture for $2.5 billion. Datta remains the CEO of the company.  Datta has also been involved in a number of philanthropic projects. He is a founding member of the IDFC Foundation, a charity which helps low-income children in India access education and health care. He has also funded scholarships for students from low-income backgrounds to attend universities around the world and donated money to help homeless people in Mumbai find shelter. Datta is also a member of the advisory board for the Ashoka Foundation, a non-profit organization which helps promote social justice around the world.  Datta is married to Reena Datta and they have two children.
