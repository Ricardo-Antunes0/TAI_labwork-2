Bertha von Hillern (4 August 1853, Trier, Rhineland-Palatinate – 11 July 1922, Cologne) was a German composer and pianist.  Born in Trier, Bertha von Hillern was the daughter of a lawyer. She began her musical education at home with her father and later studied composition with Robert Schumann in Düsseldorf. In 1877 she married the conductor Carl Maria von Weber and moved to Vienna, where she became a well-known composer. She collaborated with Weber on several operas and became his permanent assistant after his death in 1897. Von Hillern also composed orchestral works, chamber music, songs, and ballets. She died in Cologne
