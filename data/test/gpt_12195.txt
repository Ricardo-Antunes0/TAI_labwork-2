Achyranthes aspera (common  names:  chaff-flower, rough spotted orchid) is a species of orchid endemic to southern and southeastern Queensland in Australia.   The chaff-flower orchid is a terrestrial, perennial, deciduous, herb with an underground tuber and between three and six glossy green leaves. Up to twenty greenish-white flowers with red spots are borne on a tall stem. The flowers are pollinated by bees.   The chaff-flower orchid grows in dry open woodland and heath.
