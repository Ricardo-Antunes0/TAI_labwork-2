He is best known for his work as a correspondent for the Reuters news agency during World War II and the early years of the Cold War.  Reuter was born in Montreal, Quebec, to German-Jewish parents. He studied at McGill University and Osgoode Hall Law School before beginning his career as a journalist in 1916. He served in the Canadian Army during World War I and later worked as a reporter for several provincial newspapers.  In 1936, Reuter joined Reuters news agency as its first foreign correspondent. During the 1930s and 1940s, he became one of the leading investigative journalists in the world, reporting on topics such as Nazi Germany, Stalin's Russia, and the atomic bomb development program.  In 1954, Reuter was awarded the Order of Canada for his contributions to journalism. He died in 1968 at the age of 78.
