The Rosenheim–Kufstein railway (German: Bahnstrecke Rosenheim–Kufstein) is a rail line in the Austrian Alps.  Opened in 1892, it connects the Bavarian town of Rosenheim with the Styrian town of Kufstein. It is operated by ÖBB.  The line runs through the scenic Kufsteiner Tauern National Park and crosses the Ötztal Alps at an altitude of 2,864 metres (9,554 ft). The total length of the line is 42.5 kilometres (26.7 mi).
