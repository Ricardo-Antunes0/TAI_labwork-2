The Eugene C. Eppley Foundation was founded in 1982 in memory of Eugene C. Eppley, an American businessman and philanthropist. The foundation focuses on environmental and social issues and has made grants in support of a number of causes, including conservation, education, health care, and the arts.   Eppley was born in 1908 in Los Angeles, California and died in 1998. He was a self-made millionaire who made his fortune through the ownership and operation of several businesses, including a lumber company and a trucking company. He was also a member of the Knights of Columbus and the American Red Cross. Eppley was a passionate supporter of environmentalism and social justice and was active in many philanthropic organizations. He was particularly interested in promoting education and charitable causes affecting disadvantaged communities.  The Eugene C. Eppley Foundation is headquartered in Los Angeles and has offices in Boston, Chicago, Dallas, Denver, Detroit, Houston, Indianapolis, Kansas City, Las Vegas, Miami, Milwaukee, New York City, Orlando, Philadelphia, Phoenix, San Francisco Bay Area (including Oakland), Seattle/Tacoma, and Washington D.C.
