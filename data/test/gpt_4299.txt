Jogen Chowdhury (born 19 February 1939) is a Bangladeshi economist and civil servant who has served in a number of government ministries. He was the Economic Affairs Adviser to the Prime Minister of Bangladesh from 1987 to 1990, and the Chairman of the Board of Governors of the Asian Development Bank from 1990 to 1993.  Chowdhury was born on 19 February 1939 in Tangail, East Pakistan (now Bangladesh). He received his Bachelor's degree from the University of Dhaka in 1961, and his Master's degree from the London School of Economics in 1963. He later received his Doctorate from the University of London in 1971.  Chowdhury began his government service as a lecturer at the University of Dhaka in 1965. He later served as an official at the Ministry of Planning and Development from 1965 to 1967, and at the Ministry of Finance from 1967 to 1971. He was then appointed Economic Affairs Adviser to the Prime Minister of Bangladesh in 1987, and served in that position until 1990. Chowdhury later served as Chairman of the Board of Governors of the Asian Development Bank from 1990 to 1993. He has also served on several government committees, including those responsible for economic planning, development finance, and trade promotion.
