Quehanna Wild Area  is a wildlife conservation area located in northeastern Pennsylvania. The area covers 2,027 acres and is managed by the Pennsylvania Game Commission.   The Quehanna Wild Area was established in 1932 as the Quehanna Game Preserve. The land was acquired from the heirs of Anthony Drexel, a Philadelphia financier and philanthropist who had amassed a large estate in northeastern Pennsylvania. The preserve was renamed the Quehanna Wild Area in 1985 in honor of the Quehanna River, which forms its northern border.   The Quehanna Wild Area is home to a variety of wildlife, including white-tailed deer, black bear, wild turkey, and bald eagles. The area is also popular for hiking and camping.
