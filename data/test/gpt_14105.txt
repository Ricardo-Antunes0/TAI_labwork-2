The Alexander Thoirs House is the oldest surviving house in the city of Zurich, Switzerland. It is located at Bahnhofstrasse 2, in the Swiss city's historic district of Innere Stadt.   The house was built in 1712 by Alexander Thoirs, a wealthy merchant and investor. It is a typical Swiss timber-frame house, with a single storey and a gable roof. The facade features a large front door and two windows on each side. The interior features an elaborate wood-carved staircase, as well as numerous other decorative features.   The house was added to the Swiss National Inventory of Cultural Property in 1978.
