Konopište is a village in the Municipality of Kriva Palanka in the Republic of Macedonia. It is located on the banks of the Konopište River, southeast of Kriva Palanka. Konopište has a population of 424 people.  Konopište was first mentioned in written sources in 1346. The village was burned down by the Ottoman Turks in 1578, but was rebuilt. It became part of Yugoslavia in 1918. Konopište was destroyed again by the Turkish army in 1916, and rebuilt again.   The village is known for its wine.
