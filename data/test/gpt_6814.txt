Jorge Armando Arce Armenta (; born July 3, 1978) is a Mexican professional baseball third baseman for the San Francisco Giants of Major League Baseball (MLB). He previously played for the Anaheim Angels, Chicago White Sox, Seattle Mariners, Kansas City Royals and St. Louis Cardinals.  Arce was signed as an amateur free agent by the Angels in 1998. He made his MLB debut with the Angels in 2001 and has since played for Anaheim, Chicago, Seattle, Kansas City, and St. Louis. Arce has won six Gold Glove Awards (2003–2007, 2011), four Silver Slugger Awards (2004–2007, 2011), and one Platinum Glove Award (2003). He has led the American League in hits twice (2004 and 2006), stolen bases twice (2005 and 2006), and runs scored twice (2006 and 2007). Arce is a five-time All-Star selection and was voted to the 2007 All-Star Game as a reserve.  On July 30, 2017, Arce signed a three-year contract with the Giants.
