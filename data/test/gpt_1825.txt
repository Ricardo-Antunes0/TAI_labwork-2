Ravayat-e Fath , variously translated as The Reign of the Father, The Rule of the Father, The Reign of the Prophet, or The Islamic Empire, is an 18th century Persian epic poem by Amir Khusraw. It is one of the longest poems in classical Persian and is often considered to be one of the greatest masterpieces in Persian literature.  The poem tells the story of Amir Khusraw's rise to power as a military general and then as the ruler of a vast empire. It is filled with descriptions of battles and triumphs, as well as descriptions of Amir Khusraw's interactions with his subjects and his advisors.
