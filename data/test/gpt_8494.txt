The red-capped lark, Calandrella cinerea, is a passerine bird in the lark family found in open areas such as heaths and moors in the northern hemisphere.  The red-capped lark is a small, plump bird with a short tail and short wings. It has a red cap and mantle, and a yellow throat and belly. It is similar to the golden lark, but is smaller and has a more distinct red cap. The red-capped lark is insectivorous, eating small insects, spiders, and other invertebrates.
