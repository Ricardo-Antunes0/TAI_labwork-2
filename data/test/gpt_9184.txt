He became a fellow of Trinity in 1810 and a lecturer in 1816. In 1825 he was appointed Regius Professor of Greek at Cambridge, a post he held until his death.  Bosworth's principal works were editions of Homer, of Thucydides and Xenophon, and of the Odes of Horace. He also edited the works of Demosthenes and Theocritus. He was knighted in 1852.
