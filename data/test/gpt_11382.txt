Mychal George Thompson (born January 30, 1955) is a retired American professional basketball player who played in the National Basketball Association (NBA) for the Denver Nuggets, Portland Trail Blazers, Los Angeles Lakers and Seattle SuperSonics. Thompson was a seven-time All-Star and three-time champion, winning back-to-back championships with the Lakers in 2002 and 2003. He also won two gold medals as a member of the United States men's national basketball team, one at the 1984 Summer Olympics and one at the 1996 Summer Olympics.  Thompson attended John F. Kennedy High School in Los Angeles, where he led his team to the city championship as a senior. He then played college basketball at Louisiana State University (LSU), where he was a two-time consensus first-team All-American and led the Tigers to the Final Four in 1978.
