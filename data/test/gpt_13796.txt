Tom Duncan is an American entrepreneur and investor. He has been a co-founder and CEO of several technology companies, most notably Thrive Global, which he sold to Unilever in 2017 for over $1 billion.  Duncan was born in Kansas City, Missouri, and raised in the suburbs of Chicago. He received his bachelor's degree from the University of Notre Dame in 2002 and his MBA from the Kellogg School of Management at Northwestern University in 2007.  Duncan's first venture was a software company he started with two friends while he was a student at the University of Notre Dame. The company, called EchoSign, was acquired by Adobe in 2005. Duncan later co-founded Thrive Global with fellow Notre Dame alum Joel Simkhai. The company was acquired by Unilever in 2017 for over $1 billion.  Duncan is also an investor and advisor to several technology startups, including Homejoy, Zenefits, and Postmates.
