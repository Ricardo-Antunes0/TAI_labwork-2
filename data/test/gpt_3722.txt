Colonel Wilbur J. Peterkin (February 13, 1904 – November 9, 1984) was a United States Army officer and a recipient of the Medal of Honor.  Wilbur J. Peterkin was born on February 13, 1904, in Chicago, Illinois. He was educated at the University of Michigan and the United States Military Academy where he was commissioned a Second lieutenant in the United States Army in 1925.  During World War II, Lieutenant Colonel Peterkin served with the 7th Infantry Regiment, 3rd Infantry Division. He participated in the Battle of the Bulge, the Battle of Normandy, and the liberation of Paris. He was awarded the Medal of Honor for his actions at Saint-Lô during Operation Cobra on July 25, 1944.  After the war, Lieutenant Colonel Peterkin served as aide to General Omar N. Bradley and as Commanding General of Fort Bragg. He retired from the Army in 1961.  Lieutenant Colonel Peterkin died on November 9, 1984, in Arlington County, Virginia. He was buried at Arlington National Cemetery.
