Harcharan Singh was born in 1914 in village Harcharan, district Ludhiana, Punjab, British India (now in Punjab, Pakistan). He completed his early education at local schools and then went on to study at Government College, Ludhiana. Singh then did his post-graduate studies at the University of the Punjab, where he obtained a MA and PhD in English Literature.  After completing his studies, Singh began his teaching career at the Government College, Ludhiana. In 1968 he was transferred to the University of the Punjab, where he remained until 1981. During this time he also served as the university's Vice-Chancellor from 1978 to 1981.   Singh's literary career began in the late 1940s with publication of a collection of short stories, entitled "Talti" (meaning "The Touch"). His first novel, "Betaab" (1954), tells the story of two families from different parts of Punjab who are forced to migrate to Delhi after Partition. Singh's later novels include "Dharti" (1975), "Nihal" (1981), and "Anmol Ratna" (1991). All three novels were awarded the Sahitya Akademi Award.
