Christian Blaize Hackenberg (born February 14, 1995) is an American football quarterback for the New York Jets of the National Football League (NFL). He played college football at Penn State.  Hackenberg was drafted by the Jets with the 46th overall pick in the 2016 NFL Draft. He has also been a member of the Cleveland Browns, San Francisco 49ers, and Oakland Raiders.  Hackenberg was born in Red Lion, Pennsylvania, to parents Randy and Kelli Hackenberg. He has two younger brothers, Ryker and Ryan. Hackenberg attended Central Bucks West High School in Whitehall Township, Pennsylvania, where he played quarterback for the Bucks varsity football team. In his junior year, he led Central Bucks West to the PIAA Class AA state championship game, where they lost to eventual champions Downingtown East 49-35.  In his senior year, Hackenberg completed 289 of 506 passes for 4,361 yards and 41 touchdowns with only six interceptions. He also rushed for 1,069 yards and 13 touchdowns on 136 carries. He was named first-team all-state as a senior and was also selected as the Pennsylvania Gatorade Player of the Year. In addition to playing football, Hackenberg also competed in track & field and lacrosse during high school.  Hackenberg originally committed to play football for Penn State but switched to play for Rutgers following an official visit to the school.
