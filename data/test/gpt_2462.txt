Una Vida is an archaeological site located in the municipality of Talavera de la Reina, province of Burgos, in the autonomous community of Castile and León, in northern Spain. The site is composed of a series of megalithic tombs, dated to the early Neolithic and Chalcolithic periods.  The site was first discovered in 1936 by the archaeologist Antonio Sánchez Martínez while he was working on a survey in the area. The tombs were first excavated between 1956 and 1959 by José Miguel Arnedo and Manuel Barrera. They were declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1982.
