Gingee, also known as Senji or Jinji, is a town and municipality in the state of Tamil Nadu, India. The town is located about 83 kilometres north-northwest of the state capital Chennai. Gingee was the administrative headquarters of Gingee District until it was merged with Sivagangai district in 2006.  Gingee is one of the oldest towns in Tamil Nadu and is believed to have been established by the Pandyas in the 6th century CE. It became an important commercial centre during the medieval period, and remained so until the early 20th century. The town was affected by the 1997 South Indian floods, and has since seen a resurgence in economic activity.  The municipality of Gingee covers an area of 151.5 square kilometres (59.7 sq mi). The town of Gingee itself has a population of approximately 103,000, while the municipality population is estimated to be around 220,000.
