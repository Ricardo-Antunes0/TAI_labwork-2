Roger L. Green (born June 23, 1949) is an American computer scientist who is currently a Distinguished University Professor at Arizona State University. He is known for his work in artificial intelligence, machine learning, and natural language processing.  Green was born in Salt Lake City, Utah, on June 23, 1949. He received a B.S. from Brigham Young University in 1971 and a M.S. from the University of Utah in 1973. He received his Ph.D. from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 1977 under the supervision of Marvin Minsky.  From 1977 to 1984, Green was a professor at the University of California, Berkeley; at Stanford University from 1984 to 1990; and at the University of Utah from 1990 to 1997. He then moved to Arizona State University, where he is now a Distinguished University Professor.  Green has been a member of the National Academy of Sciences since 1992 and a member of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences since 1998. He is also a Fellow of the Association for Computing Machinery and the American Association for Artificial Intelligence.
