"Point It Out"  is a 1969 soul/gospel-infused rhythm and blues song by the American group The Impressions.  The song was written by the Impressions' lead singer Eddie Kendricks, and produced by the group's bassist, Melvin Franklin. It was released as the first single from the group's eponymous fifth album. "Point It Out" reached number one on the "Billboard" Hot 100 chart in early 1970, and remained there for six weeks. The song also topped the R&B chart and reached number two on the country music chart. The song has been certified gold by the RIAA.  The song is about Kendricks' love for a woman who is not interested in him. He sings about how he wants to show her "point it out" (show her how he feels) and make her his girl.  The song has been covered by numerous artists, including Ray Charles, Aretha Franklin, Patti LaBelle, and Whitney Houston.
