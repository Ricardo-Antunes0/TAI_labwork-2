Harleysville National Corporation (NASDAQ:HNBC) was a Harleysville, Pennsylvania-based diversified manufacturing company. The company specialized in the production of fabricated metal products, including headers, manifolds, and other components for the automotive, commercial truck, and aircraft industries. The company also manufactured a range of engine and transmission parts.  In early 2010, Harleyville National Corporation announced that it had agreed to be purchased by a consortium of investors led by Cerberus Capital Management LP for $1.4 billion. The deal was completed in March 2010. Following the acquisition, Cerberus Capital Management LP assumed full ownership of the company and its operating subsidiaries.  Harleysville National Corporation was founded in 1896 as the Harleysville Iron Works. The company grew rapidly during the early 1900s, becoming one of the largest manufacturers of fabricated metal products in the United States. In the early 1990s, Harleysville National Corporation began to decline due to competition from foreign manufacturers and a shift in the automotive market to lighter weight vehicle components. In 2003, the company filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection.
