Moore also composed the music for the films The Pirates of Penzance and The Santa Clause.  Moore was born in 1924 in Manchester, England. He started playing the piano at the age of four and composed his first piece of music at the age of nine. He attended Harrow School where he studied composition with Arnold Bax and Olivier Messiaen. In 1947, he co-founded the London Contemporary Music Group, which later became the Composers' Guild of Great Britain.  Moore's work on The Simpsons began in 1989 and ended in 1997. He wrote over 600 songs for the show, including " Bart's Comet ", " The Yellow Submarine", and " Marge vs. the Monorail ". He also composed the music for fourteen feature films, including The Pirates of Penzance (1948), The Santa Clause (1994), and Arthur Christmas (2013). Moore was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Original Score for The Santa Clause.  Moore died on 1 January 2009 at the age of eighty-six.
