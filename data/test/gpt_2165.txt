Ali Ahmed Aldairy  is a Bahraini-born Canadian soccer player who currently plays for Vancouver Whitecaps FC in Major League Soccer. He has also previously played for Toronto FC, Seattle Sounders FC, and Montreal Impact. Aldairy has represented Bahrain at international level, playing for the country at the 2010 and 2014 World Cups.  Aldairy was born in Bahrain to an Egyptian father and a Bahraini mother. He moved to Canada with his family when he was two years old and grew up in the city of Vaughan, Ontario. Aldairy played youth soccer for Vaughan Soccer Club and then joined the Vaughan Azzurri academy.  Aldairy made his debut for Toronto FC on November 28, 2009 in a 2-1 victory over Chicago Fire. He scored his first MLS goal on May 17, 2010 in a 3-1 win over Columbus Crew SC. Aldairy finished the 2010 season with six goals in 24 appearances.  In 201012, Aldairy signed with Seattle Sounders FC and helped the club reach the MLS Cup playoffs. He scored two goals in Seattle's 4-2 victory over San Jose Earthquakes in the quarterfinals but was sidelined by an injury in the semifinals against Real Salt Lake. Aldairy finished the 2012 season with six goals in 26 appearances.  Aldairy signed with Montreal Impact on January 22, 2013 and helped the club reach the CONCACAF Champions League semifinals.
