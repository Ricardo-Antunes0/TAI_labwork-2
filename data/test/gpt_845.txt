Victorian literature refers to English literature during the reign of Queen Victoria (1837-1901). The term is often used to refer specifically to the Victorian era's dominant literary form, the novel. It spans a wide range of genres and styles, from 19th-century realistic fiction to historical fiction, children's literature, and Gothic fiction.  Victorian literature has been characterised as a period of stability and prosperity, when England was at the height of its power and Empire. Major writers of the period include Charles Dickens, Charlotte Bronte, Emily Bronte, and William Makepeace Thackeray. The era saw a flowering of classical learning, with a renewed interest in English literature and poetry. In addition, the Victorian period saw the rise of the novel as an important form of literature, with authors such as Charles Dickens and Jane Austen leading the way.
