The Tenma goddesses are twelve guardian deities of the Yamato region in Japan. The story of how they came to be is said to be a mythological tale of the founding of the Yamato dynasty.  The origins of the Tenma goddesses are said to date back to before the establishment of the Yamato dynasty. They were originally twelve spirits who protected the people and land of the Yamato region. Over time, they came to be worshipped as separate goddesses, and their temples became important centers of religious activity in the region. Today, their shrines remain popular tourist destinations in Japan, and their cults continue to play a significant role in Japanese culture.
