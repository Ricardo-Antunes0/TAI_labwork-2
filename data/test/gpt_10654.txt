Saptashrungi or Saptashringi (Marathi: सप्तश्रृंगी, ) is a Sanskrit word meaning "seven treasures". It is also the name of a Buddhist scripture which consists of seven subjects: the nature of reality, the spiritual path, the virtues, meditation, mantras, and wisdom.  The Saptashrungi is one of the most important texts in Buddhism. It is also one of the oldest Buddhist scriptures. The text is thought to have been composed in the 1st century CE.
