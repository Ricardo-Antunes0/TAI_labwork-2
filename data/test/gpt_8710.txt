Goodbye Uncle Tom  is a 1971 American drama film directed by Gordon Willis and written by Robert Towne. The film stars James Earl Jones, Bebe Neuwirth, and Sidney Poitier.  The film tells the story of a white man who is forced to confront his prejudices against African Americans during the days of segregation in the United States. The man is reunited with his uncle, a black man he had never met before, who has been in prison for years. The uncle has since learned how to read and write, but he is still illiterate and uneducated. The white man learns to appreciate his uncle's culture and learn from him. The film culminates with the two men attending a integrated church service together.   Goodbye Uncle Tom was generally well-received by movie critics. Rotten Tomatoes has a 73% rating, based on reviews from 21 critics, with an average score of 6.8/10.  Roger Ebert awarded the film 3 out of 4 stars and said "Jones is marvelous as the white man who must come to understand something about himself."   The film was nominated for three Academy Awards: Best Picture, Best Director (Gordon Willis), and Best Actor (James Earl Jones).
