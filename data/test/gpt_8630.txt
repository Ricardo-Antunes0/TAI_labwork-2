Adjoa Amana is a Ghanaian former official who served as the Minister for Gender, Children and Social Protection from January 2009 to January 2012.  Adjoa Amana was born in Accra, Ghana in 1961. She attended the University of Cape Town, where she earned a Bachelor of Science degree in 1985. She later earned a Master of Science degree in Political Science from the University of Ghana in 1998.  Amana began her career with the Ghanaian Civil Service in 1985. In 1990, she became an administrative officer with the Ghana Immigration Service. From 1995 to 1999, she served as the Director of Gender Affairs at the Ghana Ministry of Youth and Sports. From 1999 to 2000, she was the Deputy Secretary-General of the African Union Commission.  In January 2009, Amana was appointed Minister for Gender, Children and Social Protection by then-President John Atta Mills. During her tenure, Amana worked to improve social protection for women and children, establish gender-sensitive police units, and increase access to education for girls. She also oversaw a number of initiatives aimed at reducing poverty and promoting economic development in Ghana.  Amana resigned from her position as Minister in January 2012 after failing to win a seat in the 2012 Ghanaian general election. She subsequently returned to her previous career as an administrative officer with the Ghana Immigration Service.
