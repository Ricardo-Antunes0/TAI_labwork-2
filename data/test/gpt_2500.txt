UniCredit Bank Austria AG, better known as Bank Austria, is a large Austrian banking institution. The bank was founded in 1818 and has been headquartered in Vienna since 1885. Bank Austria is one of the six largest banks in Austria and has a total assets of €173.1 billion as of December 31, 2017.  Bank Austria traces its roots back to 1818 when the "Vienna Bank" was founded by a group of wealthy Viennese merchants. The bank's first major acquisition came in 1855 when it acquired the "Niederösterreichische Creditanstalt". In 1886, Bank Austria moved its headquarters from Vienna to its present location in central Vienna.  Bank Austria has played a significant role in Austro-Hungarian and Austrian banking history. In 1910, the bank became the first institution in Austria to offer a savings account to consumers. Over the course of the next several decades, Bank Austria acquired a number of smaller banks and financial institutions.  In 1995, Bank Austria merged with the "Austrian Creditanstalt" and the "Vienna City Credit Bank". The combined bank became known as UniCredit Bank Austria AG. As part of the merger, UniCredit Bank Austria acquired a number of subsidiaries, including Banque de France (Austria) S.A., Creditanstalt für Wirtschaft und Recht GmbH, and Kreditanstalt für Wiederaufbau GmbH.
