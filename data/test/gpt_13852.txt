Hui Aloha ʻĀina  were two Hawaiian patriotic organizations, one founded in 1872 by Kamehameha III and the other in 1893 by Lunalilo.  The first Hui Aloha ʻĀina was founded on November 12, 1872, by King Kamehameha III. The organization was designed to unify the Hawaiian people and promote Hawaiian culture. The main goals of the Hui Aloha ʻĀina were to protect the islands and their culture, and to promote education.  The second Hui Aloha ʻĀina was founded on November 15, 1893, by Lunalilo. The main goal of the organization was to preserve Hawaiian culture. The organization also promoted education, especially in the field of music.
