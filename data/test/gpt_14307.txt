Joydeep Karmakar (born 7 September 1979) is an Indian-born British cricketer. He is a right-handed batsman and right-arm medium pace bowler who has played for England, the Pune Warriors India in the Indian Premier League and for Hampshire in county cricket.  Karmakar was born in Mumbai, Maharashtra, and played his early cricket for Mumbai Indians. He made his first-class debut for Rajasthan in 1999, and made a significant impact, scoring 775 runs at an average of 36.62. He made his England debut in 2002 against Sri Lanka, and made five further first-class appearances that year. In 2003 he played two One Day Internationals for England against New Zealand. In 2004 he was awarded his first full contract by Hampshire.  Karmakar was a key player for Hampshire during their time in Division Two of the County Championship. He scored 1,821 runs at an average of 38.57, with a highest score of 214 not out against Middlesex. He also took 106 wickets at an average of 34.35, with a best performance of 7/92 against Northamptonshire. He was named as the Cricket Writers' Club's Cricketer of the Year for 2006.  In 2007 Karmakar moved to the Pune Warriors India team, and he played three IPL seasons for them before returning to Hampshire in 2011.
