Renato Zveteremich (Trieste, Italy, 1893 - Milan, Italy, 1973) was an Italian painter.  Zveteremich was born in Trieste in 1893. He studied at the Academy of Fine Arts in Trieste under Luigi Russolo and Luchino Visconti. In 1921, he moved to Milan and became a member of the Associazione Nazionale Arte Futuriste.  Zveteremich's work is characterised by its technological approach to painting, which often features abstract shapes and geometric patterns. He is best known for his paintings of planes and skyscrapers, which depict the dynamism and energy of modern life. Zveteremich died in Milan in 1973.
