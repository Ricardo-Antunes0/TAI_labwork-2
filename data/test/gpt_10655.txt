Mary Ann Mobley (February 17, 1937 – May 10, 2016) was an American singer, songwriter and actress.  Mobley was born in Beaumont, Texas, the daughter of Vera (née Nelson) and Earl Mobley. She began her career as a singer with the gospel group The Imperials in the early 1960s.  In 1968, Mobley signed a recording contract with Warner Bros. Records and released her debut album "Just a Little Bit" that year. Mobley's breakthrough came in 1969 with the hit single "Don't Call Me Baby". The song reached number one on the "Billboard" Hot 100 chart and stayed there for six weeks. The follow-up single "Love Letters" also reached number one on the Hot 100 chart, staying there for six weeks as well. The albums "Just a Little Bit" and "Love Letters" both went platinum.  Mobley starred in the 1972 film "Lady Sings the Blues", which was based on the autobiographical novel of the same name by Billie Holiday. She also appeared in the 1975 film "The Wiz". Mobley released her final album in 1979 before retiring from music.  Mobley died on May 10, 2016, at her home in Nashville, Tennessee, after a long illness. She was 81 years old.
