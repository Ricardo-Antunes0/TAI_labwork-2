Pyramidella panamensis is a species of sea snail, a marine gastropod mollusk in the family Pyramidellidae, the pyrams and their allies.  The shell grows to a height of 3.5 mm. The small, white shell has a depressed conical shape. The spire is acute. The six whorls are slightly concave. They are marked by strong axial ribs, which are crossed by numerous finer spiral cords. The aperture is oblong-ovate. The outer lip is thin, showing the external sculpture within. The columella is slightly nodulous, and the siphonal canal is short and straight.   This species occurs in the Pacific Ocean off Panama.
