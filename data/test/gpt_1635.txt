Arlen Ingolf Erdahl  (born February 27, 1948) is a Norwegian-born American businessman, film producer, and former politician. He is the co-founder and CEO of Paramount Pictures Corporation. Erdahl was born in Oslo, Norway, the son of actress Liv Ullmann and film producer Ingolf Erich Erdahl.  Erdahl was educated at the University of California, Berkeley, where he received his B.A. in 1970. He then moved to Los Angeles and started his career as a production assistant on the movie The Paper Chase (1973). In 1975, he founded his own production company, Erdal Productions. In 1982, he co-founded Paramount Pictures with Gordon Getty and Jonathan Meledandri. Erdahl served as Paramount's chairman from 1996 to 2001.  He is a member of the Motion Picture Association of America (MPAA) and the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences (AMPAS). He has also been a member of the board of governors of The Santa Fe Institute since 2002. He established the Arlen Erdahl Foundation in 1999 to promote environmental causes. He has also served on the boards of several other philanthropic organizations including The Nature Conservancy, The Children's Hospital Los Angeles, and The Art Fund/LA.  In October 2009, Erdahl was appointed co-chairman of President Obama's Task Force for American Jobs and Economic Growth, which was created to provide recommendations on how to create jobs in America.
