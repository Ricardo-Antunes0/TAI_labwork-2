William C. "Bud" Pierce (born October 3, 1953) is a retired American professional basketball player and coach. He played collegiately at the University of Kansas and was selected by the Boston Celtics in the first round of the 1975 NBA Draft. A 6' 7" forward/center, Pierce played in nine NBA seasons from 1975 to 1983, averaging 20.9 points, 8.4 rebounds and 2.5 assists per game. He won a championship with the Celtics in 1981 and was named MVP of the 1984 NBA Finals. After his playing career ended, Pierce coached several teams, including the Clippers (2004–2006), Boston Celtics (2007–2008), Seattle SuperSonics (2009–2010), and Washington Wizards (2011).  Pierce was diagnosed with prostate cancer in 2012 and announced his retirement from coaching shortly after. He currently resides in Los Angeles with his wife, former model Linda Rambis Pierce.
