Feliciano Vierra "Flash" Tavares Sr. (October 29, 1941 – September 5, 2013) was a Brazilian professional wrestler, better known by his ring name, Feliciano.  Tavares is best known for his appearances in the World Wrestling Federation (WWF), where he was a three-time World Champion, as well as the National Wrestling Alliance (NWA), where he held the NWA World Heavyweight Championship twice. He is also a two-time International Wrestling Federation (IWF) Heavyweight Champion. Tavares was inducted into the WWE Hall of Fame in 2004.  Tavares was born in 1941 in the city of Cruzeiro do Sul, in Brazil. When he was six years old, his family moved to São Paulo, and Tavares began training to become a professional wrestler. He made his professional wrestling debut in 1958, and won his first championship within two years.   In 1965, Tavares left Brazil for the United States to compete in the National Wrestling Alliance (NWA). He initially competed under the name "Feliciano". In 1968, he became a three-time NWA World Heavyweight Champion. Tavares also won the NWA United States Heavyweight Championship and the NWA Southern Heavyweight Championship during his time in the United States.  In 1975, Tavares returned to Brazil to compete in the Universal Wrestling Association (UWA). He won the UWA World Heavyweight Championship that year.
