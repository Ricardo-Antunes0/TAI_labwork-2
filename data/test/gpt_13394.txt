Eric Arthur Heiden (born June 14, 1958) is an American journalist, author and lecturer. He is the co-founder and former editor-in-chief of the online news and commentary website Slate, which he left in 2013.  Heiden was born in Chicago, Illinois. After graduating from Dartmouth College in 1980, Heiden worked as a reporter for "The Wall Street Journal" in New York City. In 1984, he moved to Washington D.C., where he became a senior writer for "The National Journal".  In 1997, Heiden co-founded Slate with Joss Whedon and Bruce Meyer. The site has since become one of the leading liberal news and opinion websites in the United States. In 2013, Heiden left Slate to become a visiting professor at Georgetown University's School of Foreign Service.  Heiden is the author of five books: "The Accidental President", "The Final Days", "Hitler's Willing Executioners", "The Nazi Connection: The Extortion of Dr. Klaus Barbie and the Ordeal of Pierre Laval" and "The War Against Truth: How Official Propaganda Led America into Wars Based on False Premises".  Heiden has also written for "The New York Times", "The Atlantic", "Newsweek", " Harper's ", and other publications.
