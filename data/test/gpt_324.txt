Vettakkoru Makan  is a Hindu deity who is believed to be the god of food and nourishment. He is depicted as a bearded man wearing a dhoti and a kurta.  The cult of Vettakkoru Makan emerged in Tamil Nadu in the late medieval period. He is considered the god of food and sustenance, and is worshiped in homes to ensure a bountiful supply of food. Offerings of rice, milk, and curd are common, and prayers are often made for good health and long life. Vettakkoru Makan is also invoked during rites of passage, such as weddings and childbirth.
