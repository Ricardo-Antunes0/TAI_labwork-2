Björn Kraus (born 1969) is a German artist who is best known for his large-scale installations and public artworks.  Kraus was born in 1969 in the city of Münster, Westphalia, Germany. He studied art at the Academy of Fine Arts in Münster from 1988 to 1994. After graduation, he moved to Berlin, where he continued his studies at the Berlin University of the Arts (BFA) from 1994 to 1997.  Kraus's work has been exhibited internationally since the early 1990s. His first major exhibition was held at the Kunstverein Hamburg in 1996. His most recent major exhibition was held at the Palazzo Reale in Milan, Italy from May to September 2016.  Kraus's installations and public artworks often feature large-scale structures made out of welded metal plates or sheets that are often brightly coloured and adorned with elaborate designs. His work has been described as "impressive" and "stunning", and has been praised for its "eccentricity and originality".
