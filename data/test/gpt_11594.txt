Umberto D’Ancona (9 May 1896 – 24 September 1984) was an Italian journalist, author, and politician who served as Prime Minister four times between 1922 and 1943. He also served as Minister of Foreign Affairs in two governments, first under Benito Mussolini and then under Pietro Badoglio.  D’Ancona was born in Rome to a humble family of modest means. After completing his schooling he began his journalistic career at the age of 21, working for the conservative newspaper Il Popolo d’Italia. He rapidly became one of Italy’s most prominent journalists, writing articles on a variety of topics.  In 1922, D’Ancona was elected to the Italian Parliament as a member of the National Fascist Party. He soon became a member of Mussolini’s inner circle, serving as Minister of Posts and Telecommunications in 1925 and as Deputy Prime Minister in 1926. In 1929, he was appointed Foreign Minister by Mussolini, a position he held until 1943. During his time as Foreign Minister, D’Ancona played a pivotal role in forging Italy’s alliance with Nazi Germany and helping to consolidate Mussolini’s power.  After the Italian defeat in World War II, D’Ancona was arrested by the Allies and imprisoned for almost two years. He returned to politics in 1947, serving as Minister of Education until 1951.
