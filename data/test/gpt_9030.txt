The Jordan River Parkway is an approximately 47-mile long parkway located in the U.S. state of Utah. The parkway traverses the east side of the Jordan River and connects the towns of West Jordan, Utah with the city of Sandy, Utah.  The creation of the parkway was first proposed in 1959 as a means to connect the growing metropolitan areas of Salt Lake City and Sandy. Construction on the parkway began in 1970 and was completed in 1974. The parkway was designated a National Recreation Area in 1975 and was added to the National Park System in 1978. The parkway is managed by the National Park Service as part of the Zion National Park Complex.  The Jordan River Parkway is a popular destination for visitors from across the United States and around the world. The parkway is home to a number of attractions, including Zion National Park, Bryce Canyon National Park, and Arches National Park. The Jordan River Parkway also provides access to numerous recreational opportunities, including hiking, biking, fishing, and kayaking.
