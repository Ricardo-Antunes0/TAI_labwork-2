Tony F. Schneider (November 11, 1917 – November 3, 2006) was an American historian and archivist. He was the first Archivist of the United States Senate, serving from 1977 to 1981.  Schneider was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, the son of Emma (née Friedman) and Harry Schneider. He received his B.A. from Villanova University in 1939 and his M.A. from the University of Pennsylvania in 1942. In 1945 he earned a Ph.D. from the University of Wisconsin–Madison where he studied under Robert H. Ferrell and Frederick C. Teggert.  Schneider served as a lieutenant commander in the United States Navy during World War II, attaining the rank of captain. After the war he joined the faculty of Temple University in Philadelphia, where he remained for more than thirty years, retiring in 1978 as professor emeritus.  In 1971 Schneider was appointed Archivist of the United States Senate, a position he held until 1981. During his tenure, he helped establish Archives II at the National Archives and Records Administration (NARA). In 1984 he was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by Ronald Reagan.  Schneider died on November 3, 2006, aged 92, in Reading, Pennsylvania.
