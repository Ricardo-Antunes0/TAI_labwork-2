The Atherton House, also known as the Atherton Mansion, is a historic residence located at 216 East Gold Street in the Gold Rush town of Atherton, California. The house was built in 1887 by lumber baron and politician George Atherton, and is a fine example of Second Empire architecture. It was added to the National Register of Historic Places on November 24, 1978.  The Atherton Mansion is a two-story woodframe structure on a concrete foundation, with a mansard roof and turrets at each corner. The exterior is finished in chicle and terracotta colors, with details in terra cotta and cast iron. The interior features carved woodwork, marble fireplaces, gilded ceilings, and stained glass windows.
