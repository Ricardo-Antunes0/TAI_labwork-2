Edward "Ed" J. Suslovic (born November 17, 1957) is an American business executive and retired professional ice hockey player. He played for the Boston Bruins, Edmonton Oilers, and Vancouver Canucks in the National Hockey League (NHL).  Suslovic was drafted by the Boston Bruins in the fourth round of the 1977 NHL Amateur Draft. He turned professional with the Bruins in 1979. He scored his first NHL goal on October 12, 1979 against Rick Wamsley of the Philadelphia Flyers. He played for Edmonton from 1981 to 1984, and Vancouver from 1985 to 1991. Suslovic won two Stanley Cups with Edmonton in 1984 and 1985. After playing one season with HC Davos of the Swiss National League A, Suslovic retired in 1992.  Suslovic served as president of Oilers Entertainment Group from 2002 to 2005. He was also president and chief operating officer of Oilers Entertainment Group's subsidiary, Oilers Entertainment Marketing Ltd., from 2003 to 2005. In 2006, he became president and chief executive officer of Bio-Rad Laboratories Inc., a biotechnology company based in Northbrook, Illinois.
