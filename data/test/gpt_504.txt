Yu-Chin Chen is an economist and researcher. He is currently a senior research fellow at the Institute of Global Economics and Policy at the University of California, Berkeley. Chen is also a research associate at the National Bureau of Economic Research, where he studies international trade and financial market regulation.  Yu-Chin Chen was born in Taipei, Taiwan in 1971. He earned his bachelor's degree in economics from the National Taiwan University in 1995 and his master's degree in economics from Yale University in 1998.  Chen began his career as an economist at the World Bank in 1998. He served as a research associate at the National Bureau of Economic Research from 2003 to 2006, and was a visiting scholar at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology from 2006 to 2007.  Between 2007 and 2010, Chen served as an assistant professor of economics at Princeton University. He joined the faculty of the University of California, Berkeley in 2010.  Chen's research focuses on international trade and financial market regulation. He has written extensively on these topics, including articles for The Quarterly Journal of Economics, The American Economic Review, and The Review of Economics and Statistics.
