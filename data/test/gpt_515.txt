The World Indigenous Games are a multisport event organized by the International Indigenous Games Association (IIGA) to celebrate the traditions, cultures and heritage of indigenous peoples. The first World Indigenous Games were held in 2009 in Mysore, India. The games are open to indigenous people from all over the world, with athletes coming from over 60 countries and territories. The games feature a variety of sports and events, including traditional games like archery and wrestling, as well as modern sports like table tennis and basketball. The aim of the World Indigenous Games is to promote cultural exchange and mutual understanding between indigenous peoples and other cultures, while also promoting indigenous sports and talents.
