Bemiston is an unincorporated community and former census-designated place in Marengo County, Alabama, United States. It had a population of 100 as of the 2010 census.  The community is located on U.S. Route 82, north-northwest of Montgomery.  Bemiston was founded in 1885 as a stop on the Alabama Central Railroad. The community was named for E. W. Bemiston, the railroad's president. Early businesses included a post office, a general store, and a cotton gin. A school was built in Bemiston in 1890 and remained open until 1981. Today, the town is home to the Bemiston Cemetery, which is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.
