Rushbeds Wood is a 56 hectare nature reserve in Rushbed in Northamptonshire, England. It is managed by the Wildlife Trusts.  Rushbeds Wood is a woodland of oak and beech, with alder and sycamore. There are many wet hollows and waterlogged areas, which make it an ideal place for birds such as woodpeckers and owls. The reserve is also home to a number of bats, including the greater horseshoe bat.
