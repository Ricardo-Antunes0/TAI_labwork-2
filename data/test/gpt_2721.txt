Lieutenant General Thomas C. Waskow is a retired United States Army officer who served as the Commander of United States Army Europe and then as the Deputy Commanding General, United States Army Training and Doctrine Command.  Thomas Waskow was born in 1957 in Washington, D.C. He is a graduate of the United States Military Academy at West Point, Class of 1979. After graduation, he served in a variety of infantry and armor assignments at Fort Benning, Georgia; Fort Stewart, Georgia; Fort Lewis, Washington; and the Pentagon.  In 1994, he became the first officer to be assigned as the Deputy Chief of Staff for Intelligence for the United States Army. In this role, he was responsible for directing all intelligence activities within USAREC Europe and also served as the senior intelligence officer for U.S. forces stationed in Germany. In 1997, he was appointed Commanding General of 1st Infantry Division (Mechanized) where he led the division during operations in Bosnia and Herzegovina and Kosovo.  In 2002, he was appointed Commander of United States Army Europe (USAREUR). During his tenure, USAREUR was responsible for providing command and control over more than 240,000 troops stationed in 17 countries throughout Central, Eastern and Southern Europe. In 2003, he was promoted to lieutenant general and became the Deputy Commanding General (Operations), United States Army Training and Doctrine Command (TRADOC).
