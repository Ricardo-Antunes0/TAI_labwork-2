Formed in London, the band consisted of guitarist, composer and vocalist Mick Anglo, bassist Martin Cooper, drummer Kenny Hames, and keyboard player and vocalist David Gilmour.   Their innovative and ground-breaking sound was a mix of virtuoso instrumental rock with psychedelic soundscapes and experimental electronics. Their debut album, "Super Numeri", was released in 1971 and was a critical success. The band toured extensively throughout Europe and the United States, but disbanded in 1974 following the release of their second album, "Invisible".  Mick Anglo went on to form the progressive rock supergroup Asia with fellow Super Numeri members Martin Cooper and Kenny Hames. David Gilmour also formed the rock band Pink Floyd with former members of King Crimson Roger Waters and Nick Mason.
