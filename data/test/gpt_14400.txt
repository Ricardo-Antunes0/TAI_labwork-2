Kentucky Pride is a 1925 American silent drama film directed by Frank Borzage and starring Barbara Stanwyck, Reginald Denny, and Lillian Gish. The film is based on the 1924 novel The Kentuckian by Owen Wister.  Kentucky Pride tells the story of a feud between two families in the fictional town of Greendale. The Stanwyck family is wealthy and arrogant, while the Denny family is poor and has little respect for the Stanwicks. The feud begins when the Dennys try to buy land from the Stanwicks, but are refused because of their inferior status. The Stanwicks then plot to drive the Dennys out of Greendale. Barbara Stanwyck stars as Rachel Stanwyck, a proud and determined woman who stands up to her family's opponents. Reginald Denny stars as her husband, John Denny, a strong and principled man who loves Rachel deeply. Lillian Gish stars as Rachel's mother, Sarah, a hardworking woman who is devoted to her daughter. The rivalry between the two families culminates in a final showdown, during which all involved are forced to confront their true selves.
