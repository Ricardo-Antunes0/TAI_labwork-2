Colsyrnola brunnea is a species of sea snail, a marine gastropod mollusk in the family Pseudomelatomidae.  The length of the shell attains 7.5 mm, its diameter 3.5 mm. The solid, turreted shell has a conical shape, its surface shining and smooth. Its color is uniform light brown, with revolving lines of white on the upper part of the whorls. The palatal fascia is conspicuous and white, as is the spiral band above it. The aperture is suboval, with a white callus inside it. The columella is straight and dark brown. The outer lip is smooth and glossy.    This marine species occurs off Florida, USA; Ascension Island; St Kitts; Antigua; Barbados; Venezuela and Colombia
