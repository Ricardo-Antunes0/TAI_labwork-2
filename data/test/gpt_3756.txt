Sonnet 141 is the informal name given to a group of sonnets by the English poet William Shakespeare. The sonnets are often dated to around 1590, although some scholars argue for a later date.   Sonnet 141 is composed of 14 lines, and it is often considered one of the most powerful and affecting sonnets in Shakespeare's corpus. The poem addresses a love that is troubled by doubt and fear. The speaker suggests that love can overcome any obstacle, even death itself.
