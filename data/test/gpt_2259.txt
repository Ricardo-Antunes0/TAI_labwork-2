The Montenero Circuit, official name: Circuito del Montenero, is a motorsport race track located in the Province of Cuneo, north-west Italy. The circuit is situated on the Montenero mountain and has a length of 4.407 km.  The Montenero Circuit was inaugurated on May 1, 1974, with a Formula Two race. It hosted its first Formula One race on May 25, 1975. It was the venue of the 1976 Italian Grand Prix, won by Jackie Stewart in a Tyrrell 009. The track continued to be used until the 1990 Formula One season. Since then, it has been mostly used for motorcycle racing, including the MotoGP and World Superbike Championships.
