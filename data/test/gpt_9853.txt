Vaccinium meridionale, agraz or Andean blueberry, is a species of blueberry found in the Andes from southern Chile to northern Argentina. It is a shrub up to 2 m tall, with blue flowers in corymbs. The fruits are ellipsoid, 5–10 cm long and 3–4 cm wide, blue when mature, and contain 1–2 seeds.  Vaccinium meridionale is a shrub up to 2 m tall, with blue flowers in corymbs.
