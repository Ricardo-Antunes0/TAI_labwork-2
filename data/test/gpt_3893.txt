Sangiovese (, also , , ) is a variety of red wine made in the region of Tuscany in central Italy. In addition to being made from the Sangiovese grape, wine made from this variety must have a minimum alcohol level of 12%. Sangiovese is often considered one of the world's most complex wines, with a range of flavors and aromas.  Sangiovese has a history that goes back centuries. It is thought that the first wine from this region was produced in the Middle Ages. Today, Sangiovese is a popular wine choice in Italy, as well as abroad. Some well-known brands of Sangiovese include Brunello di Montalcino, Castel del Monte, and Sagrantino di Montefalco.
