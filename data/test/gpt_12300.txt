Kimberly Flagg Sellers is an American statistician. She is the George D. Smith Professor in the Department of Statistics and Actuarial Science at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, where she was appointed in 2002. Sellers received her Bachelor of Science degree in mathematics from Stanford University in 1975, and her Ph.D. in mathematics from Brown University in 1979.  Sellers' research concerns the application of probability theory to problems in statistics and actuarial science. She has published several books and articles on the subject, and has been recognized with numerous awards, including a National Medal of Science in 2004.  In addition to her work at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, Sellers is a member of the National Academy of Sciences, and a fellow of the American Statistical Association, the American Academy of Arts and Sciences, and the International Statistical Institute.
