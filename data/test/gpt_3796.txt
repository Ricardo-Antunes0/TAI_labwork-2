Adele Dorothy Diamond  is a professor at the University of California, Los Angeles and a scholar in the field of feminist geography. She is also the co-founder, with Cynthia Enloe, of the Institute for Women's Studies.  Adele Diamond was born on October 5, 1948, in New York City. She received her B.A. from Barnard College in 1968 and her Ph.D. from the University of California, Berkeley, in 1973.  Diamond is currently a professor at the University of California, Los Angeles, where she also serves as associate director for Graduate Studies in the School of Education and Information Studies. She is also a scholar in the field of feminist geography and co-founder, with Cynthia Enloe, of the Institute for Women's Studies at UCLA.  Diamond's research focuses on feminist theory and feminist geography. Her book "Women and Territory: The Making of Feminist Geography" (1994) was described by Publishers Weekly as "a landmark work that will be read for many years to come." Her other books include "The Geography of Gender" (1993), "Women's Stories: A Geography of Gender" (1996), "Sexed Spaces: A Feminist Perspective on Architecture" (2001), and "The Body Politic: Power and Physique in Social Life" (2005).  Diamond has received numerous awards and honors including a Guggenheim Fellowship in 1988, a Fulbright Fellowship in 1990, an American Association of University Women Award for Distinguished Contributions
