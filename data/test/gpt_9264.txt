Fannin, Mississippi is a rural community located in northeastern Mississippi. The population was 1,751 at the 2010 census.  Fannin is located in northeast Mississippi, in Lauderdale County. The town is on the north bank of the Tallahatchie River, about east of Cleveland and west of Tupelo. U.S. Route 49 passes through Fannin, leading northwest to Columbus and southeast to Philadelphia.  According to the United States Census Bureau, the town has a total area of , of which is land and , or 2.05%, is water.  As of the census of 2000, there were 1,826 people, 712 households, and 545 families residing in the town. The population density was 363.6 people per square mile (139.3/km²). There were 849 housing units at an average density of 159.2 per square mile (61.0/km²). The racial makeup of the town was 98.21% White, 0.23% African American, 0.43% Native American, 0.06% Asian, 0.06% from other races, and 0.86% from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino of any race were 0.86% of the population.
