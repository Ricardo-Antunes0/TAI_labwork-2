The International School Sport Federation (ISF) is the governing body for school sport in the world, with over 700 member schools. The organisation offers a range of sporting opportunities for its members, including the World School Championships. The championships are held every two years and are open to athletes from any school in the world.   The first World School Championships were held in 1951 in Geneva, Switzerland. The event has since been hosted in a number of different countries, including England, Scotland, Austria, Finland, Sweden and the United States. In recent years, the championships have been held in China, Hungary and South Korea. The event currently takes place in Thailand from 7-13 December.
