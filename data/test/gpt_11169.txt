Gurbachan Singh Jagat (born 1 July 1942) is a Canadian Sikh religious leader and author. He is the president of the International Sikh Youth Federation and the vice president of the World Sikh Council.  Gurbachan Singh Jagat was born on 1 July 1942 in Amritsar, Punjab, India. He graduated from the University of British Columbia with a Bachelor of Arts in 1967 and received his Master of Divinity from Trinity College, Hartford in 1971. He immigrated to Canada in 1971.  Gurbachan Singh Jagat has served as president of the International Sikh Youth Federation (ISYF) since 1984, and as vice president since 1992. He has also been a member of the World Sikh Council (WSC) since 1988. In 1994, he was appointed an honorary life member of ISYF.  Gurbachan Singh Jagat has been an active writer and lecturer on Sikh issues since the early 1970s. His publications include articles and books on Sikh history, theology, philosophy, and social action. His latest book is "Sikhism: A Short History" (HarperOne, 2007).  Gurbachan Singh Jagat was appointed a Member of the Order of Canada in 1991 for his contributions to Sikhism and world peace.
