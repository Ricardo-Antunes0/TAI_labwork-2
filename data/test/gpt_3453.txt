Burton Kendall Wheeler (February 27, 1882January 6, 1957) was an American lawyer and politician who served as the 26th Governor of Oklahoma. He also served as the United States Secretary of Commerce under Franklin D. Roosevelt, from 1933 to 1945.  Wheeler was born in 1882 in San Francisco, California. After graduating from the University of California, Berkeley, he attended law school at Harvard University. He was admitted to the bar in 1905 and practiced law in San Francisco.  Wheeler served as the mayor of San Francisco from 1917 to 1919. He then served as the United States Attorney for the Northern District of California from 1921 to 1923. He was elected as a Democrat to the U.S. Senate in 1926 and served from 1927 to 1933.  In 1933, Wheeler was appointed by Franklin D. Roosevelt as secretary of commerce, a position he held until 1945. During his time in this position, he helped lead the effort to establish the United Nations and promoted economic recovery after World War II. Wheeler also played a role in developing U.S. antitrust policy and in negotiating the Bretton Woods Agreement, which established international monetary standards.  After leaving government service, Wheeler resumed his legal practice in San Francisco and later in Washington, D.C. He died on January 6, 1957, in Bethesda, Maryland, and was buried in Arlington National Cemetery.
