William H. Hutchison (1843 – June 17, 1911) was an American lawyer, businessman and politician who served as the 23rd Governor of Texas from 1892 to 1894. Hutchison also served in the United States House of Representatives from Texas's 2nd congressional district from 1889 to 1893.  Hutchison was born in 1843 in Rutherford County, Tennessee, the son of James Hutchison and the former Nancy Jane (née Yearby) Hutchison. He attended the University of Texas at Austin and graduated with a law degree in 1865. Hutchison then moved to Fort Worth, Texas, where he started a law practice.  In 1889, Hutchison was elected to the United States House of Representatives as a Republican from Texas's 2nd congressional district. He served in the House until 1893.  Hutchison then returned to Texas and served as the 23rd Governor of Texas from 1892 to 1894. During his tenure, he fought against corruption in state government and promoted railroad development in the state. Hutchison also served on the United States Commission on Civil Service during the administration of President Benjamin Harrison.  Hutchison died on June 17, 1911, in Fort Worth, Texas, after a long illness. He was buried in Oakwood Cemetery there.
