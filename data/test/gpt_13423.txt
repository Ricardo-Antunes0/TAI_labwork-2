Antique, officially the Province of Antique , is a province in the Philippines located in the Central Luzon region. The province has a land area of 5,403.98 square kilometers and a population of 1,527,369 as of the 2010 census.  The province comprises 16 municipalities: Anilao, Baguio City, Bontoc, Cabanatuan City, Dagupan City, Dinalupihan, El Nido, La Trinidad, Magalang, Mabalacat, Manila, Nabua, Olongapo City, Ozamiz City, San Fernando and Tarlac. It is bordered by the provinces of Aurora to the north-west; Nueva Ecija to the south-west; Pampanga to the south-east; Zambales to the east and Isabela to the north-east.  The province is named after its capital city of Antique which was originally a barrio of Baguio. The name Antique comes from the Arabic word antaqa meaning "ancient". The province's patron saint is Saint John the Baptist.   In antiquity, Antique was part of what are now present-day Zambales and Pampanga provinces. The first recorded European explorer to reach this area was Francisco de Orellana in 1542. It was not until Spanish colonial times that settlement began in earnest.
