Devonport is a city in northern Tasmania, Australia. It is the administrative centre of the City of Devonport and is located on the estuary of the River Derwent, about north-west of the state capital, Hobart. At the 2006 census, Devonport had a population of 14,972.  The city was founded in 1826 as a penal settlement and later became an important port for exporting coal, timber and other goods. The city's economy has changed significantly since the closure of the steel mills in the 1990s and today it is mainly a service and tourism centre. Tourism is a significant industry in Devonport with attractions including its beaches, marinas and wineries. The city is also home to several galleries and museums.  Devonport is one of Tasmania's most culturally diverse cities with a significant Aboriginal population. The city has been twinned with Lorient, France since 1985.
