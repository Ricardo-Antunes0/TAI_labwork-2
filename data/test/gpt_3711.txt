Compulsive behavior is defined as performing an act or series of acts without any conscious intention or reason. It is often a repetitive behavior that is difficult to stop and causes distress or problems in the individual's life. There are several types of compulsive behavior, including rituals, checking, hoarding, and skin picking.  Compulsive behavior can be caused by a variety of factors, including addiction, mental health conditions, and traumatic experiences. It is often treated with therapy and medication.
