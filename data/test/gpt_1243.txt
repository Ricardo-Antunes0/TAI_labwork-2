David Lee Meggett (born April 30, 1966) is an American former professional baseball player who played in Major League Baseball (MLB) for the Minnesota Twins, Philadelphia Phillies, and Los Angeles Dodgers. He has also played in Nippon Professional Baseball (NPB) for the Fukuoka SoftBank Hawks.  Meggett made his MLB debut with the Twins in 1990, and played for them until 2003. He then signed with the Phillies, and played for them until 2007. He was traded to the Dodgers in 2008, and played for them until 2013. In 2014, Meggett signed with the Orix Buffaloes of NPB.  Meggett was born in Minneapolis, Minnesota. He attended Robbinsdale Cooper High School in Robbinsdale, Minnesota and played college baseball at the University of Southern California (USC).  In 1990, Meggett made his MLB debut with the Minnesota Twins. That year he had a batting average of .269 and a home run total of nine. He also finished second in the American League (AL) Rookie of the Year voting behind Cecil Fielder of the Detroit Tigers. The following year, he had a batting average of .277 and a home run total of 18.  In 1992, Meggett had his best season as a major leaguer.
