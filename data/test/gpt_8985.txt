James David Graham Niven (; 1 March 1922 – 14 October 2014) was an English actor and former captain of the British Army. He was known for his roles in films such as The Man Who Would Be King (1965), My Favorite Year (1982), and An American in Paris (1951).  Niven was born in the village of Burnham Deepdale, Lancashire, England, the son of Lilian (née Sharpe) and Major David Graham Niven. His father was an officer in the British Army and later a journalist and novelist. His mother was from a family of Lancashire cotton plantation owners. Niven had a brother, Michael Niven, who became an actor and director.  Niven attended Eton College and then the Royal Military Academy Sandhurst. After training, he was commissioned into the British Army as a second lieutenant on 24 June 1942. He was promoted to lieutenant on 24 December 1945, and to captain on 30 June 1950. Niven saw service in Cyprus, Persia, North Borneo, Malaya and Singapore before being discharged on 26 July 1954.   Niven's first film appearance was in the Second World War propaganda film The Battle of Britain (1969). In 1969, he starred in the James Bond film On Her Majesty's Secret Service.
