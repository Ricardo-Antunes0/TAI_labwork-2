Peter K. Homer (born 1961) of Southwest Missouri, is a recognized authority on the history of the American West and the cowboy culture.  Peter Homer is an award-winning historian and author of twelve books on the American West, including "The Cowboy State: A History of Wyoming" and "The Myth of the Cowboy: An American Folklore History." Homer has also written extensively for journals and magazines, including "Western Historical Quarterly," "Southwest Review," "Cowboy Life," and "Range Management."  Homer was born in 1961 in Southwest Missouri. After completing undergraduate and graduate work at the University of Kansas, he taught Western history at Central Michigan University before joining the faculty of California State University, Fresno. He was named Distinguished Professor at Fresno State in 2006.  Homer lives in Fresno with his wife, Karin, and their two children.
