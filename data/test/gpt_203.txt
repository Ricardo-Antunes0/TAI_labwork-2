Daddy's Little Girls is a 2007 American psychological horror film directed by David Gordon Green and written by Vincenzo Natali. It is based on the novel of the same name by James Patterson.  The film stars Annette Bening, Drew Barrymore, Jamie Lee Curtis, and Jada Pinkett Smith. It tells the story of four women who are kidnapped by a deranged father who plans to use them as sex slaves. The four women must work together to escape and save themselves.  Daddy's Little Girls was released in theaters on October 5, 2007. The film received mixed reviews from critics, some of whom praised its suspenseful plot and performances, while others found its content disturbing and graphic. However, it was a commercial success, grossing over $137 million worldwide. A sequel, Daddy's Little Girls: The Return, was released in 2009.
