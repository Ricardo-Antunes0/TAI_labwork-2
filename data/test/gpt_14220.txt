Audri Mukhopadhyay (born 1974) is a Canadian writer. She has published three novels, a collection of short stories, and a non-fiction book on Bengali literature.  Mukhopadhyay was born in Kolkata, India in 1974. She moved to Canada with her family when she was six years old. She completed a degree in English literature from the University of British Columbia in 2002. She then worked as a journalist for several newspapers before she began writing full-time.  Mukhopadhyay's first novel, The Unbearable Lightness of Being, was published in 2008 and won the Rogers Writers' Trust Fiction Prize that year. Her second novel, The Hungry Ghosts, was published in 2013 and won the Governor General's Award for English-language fiction. Her third novel, Half of a Yellow Sun, was published in 2016 and was longlisted for the Man Booker Prize.  Mukhopadhyay has also written short stories and non-fiction books on Bengali literature. Her book on Bengali poetry, Walking with Other People: Selected Writings on Poetry, was published in 2017.
