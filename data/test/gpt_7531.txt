Lacaille 9352 (Lac 9352) is a red supergiant star located about 230 light-years from Earth in the constellation Lacerta. It has a diameter of about 120 times the radius of the Sun and is estimated to be at least 2,000 times as bright as the Sun.  Lacaille 9352 is notable for its high chromospheric activity, which has caused it to exhibit an enhanced variation in its apparent brightness over time. It is also a candidate for having a companion star.
