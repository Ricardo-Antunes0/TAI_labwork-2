Norman D. Stevens (1932 - Dec. 15, 1999) was an American historian and political scientist. He is known for his work on the history of the Republican Party.  Stevens was born in 1932 in Galesburg, Illinois. He received his undergraduate degree in history from the University of Michigan in 1954 and his PhD in political science from the same institution in 1960.  From 1960 to 1962, Stevens served as a research associate at the University of Chicago's Center for Advanced Study of Social Problems. He then became a professor at the University of Michigan, where he remained until his death in 1999. During his tenure at Michigan, Stevens was also a Distinguished Service Professor and director of the university's Center for Political Studies.  Stevens authored or co-authored more than 40 books, including The Republican Revolution: The History of an Idea (1976), The Emerging Republican Majority (1978), and The Politics of Party Division (1987). He also served as editor-in-chief of the journal American Political Science Review from 1984 to 1989.  Stevens was a member of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences, the American Historical Association, and the National Academy of Sciences. He was also a recipient of the John F Kennedy Book Award and the Harold Laski Award from the London School of Economics.
