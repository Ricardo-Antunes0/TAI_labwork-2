Space Mutiny (also known as Mutiny in Space) is a 1966 American science fiction film directed by Robert Wise and written by Gene L. Coon, based on the novel of the same name by Frederik Pohl. The film stars James Garner, Donald Pleasence, and Lloyd Bridges.  The crew of the mining vessel "Enterprise" rebel against their captain, attempting to find a new home in space.  Space Mutiny was released on June 6, 1966 in the United States. It was a critical and commercial success, grossing over $7 million at the box office. The film won three Academy Awards: Best Sound Editing, Best Sound Mixing, and Best Visual Effects.
