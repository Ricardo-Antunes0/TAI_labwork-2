Benjamin Garlike (c. 1766 â€“ 14 May 1847) was an American United States Representative from New York.  Garlike was born in 1766 in the town of Malone, New York, the son of Nicholas Garlike and Tryphena (Whitney) Garlike. He attended the common schools and graduated from Union College in 1787. He studied law, was admitted to the bar in 1789 and commenced practice in Malone.  Garlike served as justice of the peace for Otsego County from 1792 to 1800. He also served as director of the Canandaigua Academy from 1802 to 1810. In 1814, he married Hannah Pardee.  Garlike was elected as a Federalist to the Fifteenth Congress and served from March 4, 1817, to March 3, 1819. He was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1818 to the Sixteenth Congress.  Garlike resumed the practice of law in Malone and also engaged in agricultural pursuits. He died on 14 May 1847 and was interred in Malone Cemetery.
