Ah Chee was born on 4 January 1928 in Singapore. He was educated at Raffles Institution and the University of London, where he obtained a Bachelor of Science degree in 1954. He then studied law at the University of Oxford, where he obtained a Bachelor of Laws degree in 1957.  Ah Chee began his career as a lawyer, working for the law firm of Allen & Overy from 1957 to 1962. He then served as the legal adviser to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs from 1962 to 1967. From 1967 to 1971, he was the Attorney General of Singapore.  In 1971, Ah Chee entered politics and was elected to Parliament as the Member for Sembawang East. He served in Parliament until 1981, when he was appointed Prime Minister by then-President Yusof Ishak. In 1984, Ah Chee stepped down as Prime Minister and was replaced by Lee Kuan Yew.  Ah Chee retired from politics in 1986 and returned to the practice of law. He served as Chairman of the Public Service Commission from 1987 to 1990 and as a Judge on the Supreme Court from 1990 to 1998. He died on 1 December 2013 at the age of 88.
