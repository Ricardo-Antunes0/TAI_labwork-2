Jean Alexandre Vaillant (1804 – 21 March 1865) was a French general and military historian.  Vaillant was born in Montauban, Tarn-et-Garonne, the son of a tanner. He enlisted in the French army in 1821, became a sub-lieutenant in 1826, and a lieutenant in 1830. He served in Algeria from 1830 to 1835 and in the campaigns of 1836 and 1837 against Great Britain. He fought at the Battle of Fuentes de Onoro (1837), where he was severely wounded, and at the Battle of Navarino (1827).   He was made a captain in 1840 and a colonel in 1846. He served during the Second Italian War of Independence (1848–1849), distinguishing himself at the Battle of Custoza (1848). In 1850 he became chief of staff to Marshal Nicolas Oudinot, commanding the III Corps during the campaign against Austria.   In 1851 Vaillant became inspector-general of infantry, serving under Marshal Adolphe Niel. In this capacity he introduced many reforms which increased efficiency and training in the French army. In March 1852 he was made a general of division.   Vaillant fought at the Battle of Austerlitz (2 December 1805), where he commanded one of Oudinot's columns.
