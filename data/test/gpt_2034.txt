Narasimhan was born in 1891 to Narasimha Mudaliar and Sundaramma at Kanchipuram, a town in the South Indian state of Tamil Nadu. He attended the Madras Christian College and then studied law at the Madras Law College, where he became friends with Jawaharlal Nehru. Narasimham served as a lawyer before being called to the bar in 1914.  Narasimhan played an important role in the Indian independence movement, working alongside Nehru and Mahatma Gandhi. He served as secretary to the All-India Congress Committee (AICC) and later as its president. In 1946, he was elected to the Constituent Assembly of India, where he served as the chairman of the Constitutional Drafting Committee. In January 1948, Narasimhan became Minister of State for Home Affairs and remained in that position until September 1948, when he was made Deputy Prime Minister and Minister for Finance.
