Something Upstairs (also published as Something Upstairs: A Mystery) is a 1948 mystery novel by American writer Agatha Christie. The novel is set in the fictional country of Downland, and tells the story of the investigation into the death of a young woman, Mrs. Oliver.  Christie's first novel for adults, it was published in the UK by Heinemann and in the US by Dodd, Mead and Company. The novel was serialized in The Sunday Times from March 10 to April 10, 1948.  The novel was adapted for television as a three-part episode of Agatha Christie's Poirot (1990), with David Suchet reprising his role as Hercule Poirot.
