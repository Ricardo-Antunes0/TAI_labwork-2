Zhu Weizhi (; 26 May 19051999), also known as Weizhi the Great, was a Chinese statesman and general who served as the eighth Premier of the Republic of China from 1954 to 1957.  Zhu Weizhi was born in Wuchang, Hubei province in 1905. He graduated from the Wuchang Military Academy in 1923 and served in the Army of the Republic of China during the Northern Expedition. He fought in the Second Sino-Japanese War and World War II, becoming a general. After the war, he resumed his military career and served as commander of the Communist forces in Henan province.  In 1948, Zhu Weizhi became deputy secretary-general of the Communist Party of China. He served as deputy premier from 1954 to 1957 and became premier in 1957. As premier, Zhu Weizhi pursued moderate economic policies which were successful in stabilizing the economy and reducing inflation.  Zhu Weizhi died on 26 May 1999 at the age of 100.
