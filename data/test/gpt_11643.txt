Fidel Andrés Surco Cañasaca (born 30 November 1954) is a Cuban politician and diplomat. He served as the Foreign Minister of Cuba from 2008 to 2012.  Surco was born in Cañasaca, Holguín Province, on 30 November 1954. He attended the University of the Orient in Havana, where he obtained a degree in law. He also studied at the University of Paris and the University of Madrid.  In 1978, Surco began his career as a lawyer in the Ministry of Justice. From 1982 to 1986, he served as first secretary of the Communist Party of Cuba's legal department. From 1987 to 1988, Surco was a member of the Supreme Soviet of Cuba.  From 1990 to 1998, Surco served as first secretary (deputy minister) of the Communist Party's international relations department. In this capacity, he represented Cuba at various conferences and meetings organized by the United Nations General Assembly, including its Fourth World Conference on Women in Beijing in 1995. Surco also served as Cuba's envoy to the United States from 1998 to 2002.  In 2002 Surco was appointed ambassador to France. From 2006 to 2008, he served as ambassador to Spain. On 11 March 2008, he was appointed Foreign Minister of Cuba by President Raúl Castro.
