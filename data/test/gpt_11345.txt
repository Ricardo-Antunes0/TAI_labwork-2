Souley Abdoulaye (born 1965) is a Nigerien politician and the current president of the National Assembly. He was elected to the position in May 2007, defeating incumbent Abdoulaye Wade.  Souley Abdoulaye was born in 1965 in Niamey, Niger. He attended the University of Niamey, where he obtained a degree in law. Abdoulaye began his political career in 1992, when he was elected to the National Assembly as an independent representative. He subsequently served as Minister of State for Youth and Sports from 2000 to 2002, and as Minister of State for the Civil Service from 2002 to 2004.  In May 2007, Souley Abdoulaye was elected president of the National Assembly, defeating incumbent Abdoulaye Wade. As president of the National Assembly, Souley Abdoulaye is responsible for overseeing the functioning of the legislature. In this capacity, he has played a key role in efforts to address corruption and improve legislative oversight.
