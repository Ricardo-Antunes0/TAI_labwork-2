Jones was born in Detroit, Michigan, on July 19, 1936. He was the youngest of four children of James and Lula Jones. His father was a professional musician who played the cornet and drums. Jones began singing at an early age, and by the time he was eight years old he had started performing with his father in local shows.  In 1956, Jones met Ernie Banks, a fellow Detroit-area musician, at a show and the two started working together as members of a vocal group called The Four Tops. The following year, they formed The Miracles with Smokey Robinson and Bobby Rogers. (1963), "Ghetto Hit Parade" (1965), and "The Miracles Finest Hour" (1967).
