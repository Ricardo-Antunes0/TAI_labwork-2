Final Fantasy VIII is a role-playing video game developed and published by Square Enix for the PlayStation video game console. It was released in Japan in 1999, and in North America and Europe in 2000. The game is the eighth installment in the Final Fantasy series, and the second game in the series to be developed for a personal computer.  Final Fantasy VIII is set on a world called Gaia, which is being harassed by a dark force known as Sephiroth. The player controls characters Cloud Strife, Squall Leonhart, and Raijin Tidus as they join forces with other members of the party to stop Sephiroth. The game features a new battle system that allows players to control characters with different abilities to create strategic combinations, as well as an original story that expands on elements from the earlier games in the series.  Final Fantasy VIII received generally positive reviews from both critics and gamers, who praised its graphics, battle system, and story. The game has been cited as one of the best games in the series, and has been included on several lists of the greatest video games of all time.
