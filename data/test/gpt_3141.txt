Wyre was born in London, England, to a working-class family. After leaving school at 16, he began working as a textile labourer. Wyre became involved in the music scene in London in the late 1960s, playing guitar in a number of local bands. He formed The Jam in 1971 with Paul Weller, Bruce Foxton, and Rick Fenn. The Jam became one of the most popular British rock bands of the 1970s and 1980s, achieving critical and commercial success with their albums From Here to Eternity (1973), All Mod Cons (1976), and Sound Affects (1980). Wyre left The Jam in 1985 to focus on his solo career. His work as a solo artist included the albums John Wyre (1987), Shadows on the Road (1989), and Street Songs (1991). Wyre died from a heart attack at the age of 56.
