Severo-Kurilsk (; , Kashiwabara) is a town and the administrative center of Kurilsky District of Kamchatka Krai, Russia, located on the eastern coast of the Kamchatka Peninsula, opposite Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky, the largest city on Kamchatka. Population:   The town was founded in 1784 as a Russian military post and named after Count Evgeny Severo-Kurilsky. In 1896, it became the administrative center of Kurilsky Uyezd. The town was once home to a Naval Base and an Leningrad Military District headquarters. In 1958, it was renamed Severo-Kurilsk after Mikhail Gorbachev.
