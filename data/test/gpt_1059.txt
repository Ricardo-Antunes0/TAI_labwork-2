Nadine Gelineau was a Canadian-born music industry executive.  Gelineau was born in Montreal, Quebec, Canada on November 3, 1963. She received a degree in music from McGill University in 1985. After graduating, she worked as a music industry executive for labels including RCA and A&M Records.  In 1994, Gelineau became president of Mercury Records Canada. She later became president and CEO of PolyGram Canada. In 2006, she was appointed vice-chairman of the Canadian Radio Music Awards. In 2007, she was made an Officer of the Order of Canada.  Gelineau died on January 26, 2018, at the age of 61 from pancreatic cancer.
