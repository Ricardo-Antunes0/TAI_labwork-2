The museum was founded in 1884 and has an extensive collection of prints and drawings from the 15th to the 19th centuries. The collection is particularly strong in Dutch, Flemish and French prints and drawings, as well as Italian Renaissance prints. The museum also houses a large number of original copperplates and copper engravings, used to produce relief prints.   The museum is situated in the former printing works of the "Gouden Eeuw" newspaper company, which was founded in 1785.
