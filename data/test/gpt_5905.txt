The WWE 24/7 Championship is a professional wrestling championship created and promoted by the American professional wrestling promotion WWE. The championship was introduced on October 24, 2009, at the October 24, 2009 episode of WWE Raw, as a result of the brand extension that took place in June of that year. It is contested for in professional wrestling matches between wrestlers who are signed to WWE contracts and are employed by WWE as full-time employees. As of 2019, there have been 34 champions crowned.  The championship is a unifying title for all brands under the WWE banner. As such, it is not exclusive to one division or another and can be defended by any wrestler in any WWE brand. The inaugural champion was John Cena, who defeated The Miz to win the championship at Armageddon. As of 2019, the most recent champion is Daniel Bryan, who defeated AJ Styles in a three-out-of-four falls match to win the title at WrestleMania 35.
