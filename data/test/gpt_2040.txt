Perry Francis Rotella (born May 28, 1963) is an American entrepreneur and businessman. He is the co-founder and CEO of the record label Geffen Records, which he founded with David Geffen in 1984.  Rotella was born in Buffalo, New York, to Italian-American parents. He attended Canisius College in Buffalo, where he studied music business. After dropping out of Canisius, Rotella worked as a janitor at CBS Records while he tried to get his music business degree from UCLA. In 1984, he and Geffen founded Geffen Records. The label has since released albums by such artists as U2, Bruce Springsteen, Radiohead, and Madonna. In 2006, he purchased a controlling stake in EMI Music Group's Vertigo Records subsidiary.  Rotella has been involved in a number of philanthropic projects. He is a co-founder of the Los Angeles-based AIDS Healthcare Foundation (AHF), which helps to provide medical care and support to people with HIV/AIDS. In 2004, Rotella was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame as a member of U2.
