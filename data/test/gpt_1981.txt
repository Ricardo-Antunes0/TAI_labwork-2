Udo Mario Strutynski (born September 21, 1942) is a Canadian retired professional ice hockey player. He played in the National Hockey League (NHL) for the Philadelphia Flyers, Toronto Maple Leafs and Boston Bruins.  Strutynski was born in Vancouver, British Columbia. He played junior hockey for the Burnaby Bites, and then turned professional with the Flyers in 1966. He scored his first NHL goal on October 5, 1966 against Ron Hextall of the Philadelphia Flyers. Over the next 12 seasons, he played for the Flyers, Maple Leafs, and Bruins, amassing 337 points in 592 games. Strutynski was a key member of three Stanley Cup-winning teams: with the Flyers in 1974 and 1975, and with the Maple Leafs in 1984. In 1976 he was traded to the Boston Bruins, where he played until 1980. In 1980 he signed as a free agent with the California Golden Seals and retired after playing one season with them.  After retirement from hockey, Strutynski became a broadcaster for TSN in 1990 and remained there until his retirement in 2006. He was inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame in 1998.  Udo Strutynski was born on September 21st, 1942 in Vancouver, British Columbia to Heinz and Margarethe Strutynski. His father was a firefighter and gymnastics coach who also competed internationally for Germany; his mother was a dance teacher who competed for Austria.
