He attended the common schools and graduated from Union College in 1859. Ames then studied law and was admitted to the bar in 1860. He practiced law in Troy until 1866, when he enlisted in the Union Army during the American Civil War. Ames served in the Army of the Potomac during the campaigns of General Robert E. Lee and General Ulysses S. Grant, reaching the rank of captain.  After the war, Ames returned to Troy and resumed his practice. He also served as city attorney for Troy from 1871 to 1875. In 1878, he was elected as a Republican to the Iowa House of Representatives, serving two terms before being elected governor in 1880. He served two terms as governor, from 1880 to 1884 and from 1886 to 1888. Ames then served one term in the United States Senate, from 1889 to 1897.  Ames died on September 24, 1917, in Washington D.C., and was buried in Mount Hope Cemetery there. He was married to Sarah Elizabeth Nichols (1838-1915) and they had three children: Adelbert Nichols (1862-1939), Elizabeth Averill Ames (1865-1945), and Ralph Waldo Ames (1868-1942).
