Georg Schreyögg (13 August 1870 – 7 March 1933) was a Swiss painter. He is considered to be one of the most important painters of the Expressionist movement in Switzerland.  Georg Schreyögg was born on 13 August 1870 in Zürich, Switzerland. He studied at the School of Fine Arts of Zurich and then at the Royal Academy of Fine Arts of Antwerp. From 1892 to 1893 he studied in Paris at the Académie Julian.  In 1896, Schreyögg moved to Berlin, Germany, where he was a member of the Die Brücke group. He returned to Switzerland in 1903 and settled in Zürich. In 1924 he moved to Davos, Switzerland, where he died on 7 March 1933.  Schreyögg's paintings are characterized by their Expressionist style and use of strong colors. His most famous works include "Frauenkirche" (1916), "Der Sturm" (1919), and "Morgengrauen" (1921).
