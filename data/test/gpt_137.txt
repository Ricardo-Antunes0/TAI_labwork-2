The Tholos do Barro (also known as the Stonehenge of the Atlantic) is a megalithic monument located in the municipality of São Lourenço da Mata, Portugal. The structure, made of large stones, is composed of a trilithon and a series of circles that may have served as altars. The monument is considered one of the most important megalithic sites in Europe.  The Tholos do Barro was first documented in the 17th century, and its first excavations were carried out in the early 20th century. The monument was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1996.
