El-Ouali Mustapha Sayed (also known as El-Ouali Mustapha, Ouali Mustapha, and El-Ouali Mohammed; Arabic: مصطفى علي مصطفى‎‎) (born 9 July 1947) is a Moroccan politician who has been Prime Minister of Morocco since 7 November 2007. Previously he was Minister of Defense from 2003 to 2007.  Sayed was born in the city of Settat on 9 July 1947. He is a graduate of the prestigious Royal Military Academy of Morocco. He also holds a degree in political science from the University of Paris.  He began his career in the military, serving as an officer in various posts, notably as head of military intelligence. In this capacity, he played an instrumental role in suppressing the Islamist insurgency that erupted in Morocco in the 1990s.  In 1993, he was appointed Minister of Defense, a position he held until 2003. As minister, Sayed played a leading role in implementing economic and social reforms aimed at modernizing Morocco and improving the country's relations with its neighbors. He also presided over a period of significant military modernization and expansion, including the acquisition of new fighter jets and armored vehicles.  After winning the 2007 general election, Sayed was appointed Prime Minister by King Mohammed VI.
