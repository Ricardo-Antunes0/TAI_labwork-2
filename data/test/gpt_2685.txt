Patrick Aloysius Murphy (21 June 1918 – 31 January 1995) was an Irish journalist and broadcaster.  Murphy was born in Dublin, the son of a doctor. He was educated at St. Kevin's College, Dublin and the University of Cambridge, where he obtained a degree in history. After serving in the Irish Army during World War II, he began his journalistic career with the Irish Times in 1944. In 1955 he became political editor of the Sunday Tribune and in 1962 he became its editor. He retired from journalism in 1978.  Murphy was also a broadcaster, working first for the BBC and then for RTÉ. He hosted programmes such as "The Patrick Aloysius Murphy Show" on RTÉ Radio 1 from 1977 to 1985 and "The Murphy Report" on RTÉ Television from 1985 to 1994. He was made a Member of the Order of Merit in 1987.
