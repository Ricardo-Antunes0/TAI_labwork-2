Irving "Puggy" Feinstein (October 6, 1909 â€“ October 11, 2006) was an American pianist, composer, and arranger.  Born in New York City, Feinstein studied piano with William Kapell and theory with Darius Milhaud. He played in the pit orchestras of Broadway plays in the 1930s and 1940s. He began composing in the 1940s, and his best-known works are the jazz standard "Take the A Train" and the ballet score "The Nutcracker".  Feinstein also worked as a pianist, arranger, and conductor, performing with many leading jazz and classical musicians. He recorded extensively for Capitol Records between the 1950s and 1980s. In 1992, he was awarded the National Medal of Arts.  Feinstein died at his home in New York City at age 93.
