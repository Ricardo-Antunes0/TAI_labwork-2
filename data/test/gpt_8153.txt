Sukumar Ray (; 30 October 1887 â€“ 16 November 1962) was an Indian film actor who appeared in over 350 films in a career spanning more than fifty years. He is considered to be one of the most popular and respected actors of Hindi cinema.  Born in the town of Durgapur in the Krishnanagar district of West Bengal, Ray made his debut in 1912 with the movie "Sati". He achieved widespread fame for his remarkable performance as the title character in the landmark 1943 movie "Pather Panchali". For his role in this film, Ray was awarded India's first National Film Award for Best Actor. He also received the Padma Bhushan Award from the Government of India in 1954.  Ray's numerous other awards and recognitions include two Filmfare Awards, four Golden Globe Awards, two Bollywood Filmfare Awards, a Dadasaheb Phalke Award, and an honorary degree from the University of Calcutta. He has been described as "the greatest Hindi film actor" and one of the world's most popular actors. He has been credited with having helped to revive Hindi cinema after its decline during World War II.  Ray died on 16 November 1962 at the age of 72 after a long illness.
