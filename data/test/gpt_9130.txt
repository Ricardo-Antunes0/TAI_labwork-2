Erik Philip Paulsen (born May 14, 1965) is an American politician who is the U.S. Representative for Minnesota's 3rd congressional district, serving since 2009. A member of the Republican Party, he is the first representative from Minnesota to be re-elected with more than 60% of the vote since World War II.   Paulsen was born in Bloomington, Minnesota, and graduated from Bloomington High School in 1983. He then attended the University of Minnesota, where he received his B.A. in political science in 1987 and his J.D. in 1990. While at the University of Minnesota, he served as president of the College Republicans and was a member of the Phi Gamma Delta fraternity.  Paulsen worked as a lawyer for a small firm in Minneapolis from 1990 to 1994. He then served as a policy analyst for the House Republican Conference from 1994 to 1997. He was also a fellow at the Hoover Institution at Stanford University from 1997 to 1998.  In 1998, Paulsen was elected to the Minnesota House of Representatives as a Republican, representing District 58B (Bloomington and parts of southern Minneapolis). In 2002, he was elected Speaker of the House, becoming only the second Republican in history to hold that position (the first being Dennis Richardson). In 2006, Paulsen was re-elected to his House seat with 71% of the vote.
