He was a founding member of the Ethiopian Journalists Association and served as its president from 1984 to 1986. He also served as the editor-in-chief of the weekly newspaper Addis Ababa News from 1978 to 1984.  Tsegaye Gabre-Medhin was born on 17 August 1936 in the town of Aksum, in the Oromia Region of Ethiopia. He attended secondary school in his hometown before enrolling at the University of Addis Ababa in 1957 to study law. However, he abandoned his studies after only two years and instead started working as a journalist for the government-run newspaper "Ethiopian Herald".  In 1961, Gabre-Medhin became editor-in-chief of "Addis Ababa News", a weekly newspaper which he later renamed "Addis Ababa Daily News". In 1978, he became editor-in-chief of the newly founded weekly newspaper "Addis Ababa News". During his time at "Addis Ababa News", Gabre-Medhin became one of Ethiopia's most vocal critics of the ruling Derg regime.  Gabre-Medhin's outspokenness led to him being persecuted by the Derg regime. He was imprisoned on several occasions and had his assets confiscated. In 1984, he was one of the founding members of the Ethiopian Journalists Association (EJA). He served as its president from 1984 to 1986
