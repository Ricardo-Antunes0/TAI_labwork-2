Jefferson Cowie is an American historian, author, and lecturer. He is the Sterling Professor of History at Yale University. He has written extensively on American history, Indian history, and the history of the American West.  Cowie was born in Bayonne, New Jersey in 1955. He received his B.A. from Rutgers University in 1977 and his M.A. from Yale University in 1979. He completed his doctoral studies at Yale University in 1984 with a dissertation on the history of the American West.  Cowie has been a professor at Yale since 1984 and is the Sterling Professor of History. He has been the recipient of a Guggenheim Fellowship, a John Simon Guggenheim Fellowship, and a Ford Foundation Fellowship. He is also a member of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences, the American Historical Association, and the Royal Historical Society of Great Britain.  Cowie's books include The Course of Empire: The History of the American Revolution, 1763-1789 (1989), Indians on the Great Plains: A History (1995), The Fate of Liberty: Jefferson's Legacy (2002), and Empire's Workshop: Forging America's Military Supremacy (2007). His most recent book is The Unfinished Nation: A History of the United States Since 1776 (2013).
