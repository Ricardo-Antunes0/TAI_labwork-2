The Fremont-Winema National Forest of south central Oregon is one of the largest national forests in the United States. The forest covers more than 9,000 square miles and has over 450,000 acres of designated wilderness. The forest is home to a wide variety of plants and animals, including grizzly bears, black bears, elk, deer, cougars, and wolves. The forest is also a popular outdoor destination for hikers, backpackers, mountain bikers, and equestrians.
