Johnny Tremain is a 1957 film made by 20th Century Fox. The film is based on the novel of the same name by Elizabeth Gaskell. The story follows a young man, John Tremain, who is transported from 1811 to 1814 during the War of 1812. Tremain must use his wits and strength to survive in a new world where he is constantly faced with challenges.  The film was well-received by audiences and critics, and was nominated for three Academy Awards: Best Actor for Alec Guinness, Best Art Direction-Set Decoration (Black & White), and Best Sound.
