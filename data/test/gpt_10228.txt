William Craig Fugate (born May 14, 1959) is an American football executive who is the current executive vice president and general manager of the Seattle Seahawks of the National Football League (NFL). He previously served as the general manager of the Tampa Bay Buccaneers from 2009 to 2014.  Fugate was born in Cincinnati, Ohio, and attended La Salle High School in Cincinnati. He played college football at Washington State University. He began his career as a player personnel assistant for the Philadelphia Eagles before being promoted to assistant director of player personnel. He was then hired as the general manager of the Cleveland Browns in 2002. Fugate was fired by Browns owner Art Modell on December 10, 2003, after Modell decided to fire head coach Butch Davis and promote Romeo Crennel.  Fugate then served as the general manager of the Baltimore Ravens from 2004 to 2007. He was fired by Ravens owner Steve Bisciotti on January 12, 2007, after Bisciotti decided to fire head coach Brian Billick and promote John Harbaugh.  Fugate was then hired as the general manager of the Tampa Bay Buccaneers on January 15, 2009. He was fired by Buccaneers owner Malcolm Glazer on December 30, 2014.  Fugate is married to Sheryl Fugate, who is also a football executive. They have two children.
