Dmitry Arkadievich Shmidt (; born David Aronovich Schmidt on October 5, 1969) is a Russian businessman and politician who served as the Prime Minister of Russia from 2012 to 2014. Previously, he was the Deputy Prime Minister from 2008 to 2012.  Shmidt was born in Moscow, Russian SFSR, into a Jewish family. His father was a chemist and his mother was a teacher. He graduated from Moscow State University with a degree in law. He then worked as a lawyer before founding his own business in 1998.  Shmidt has been involved in politics since 2002, when he became a member of the United Russia party. In October 2008, he was appointed Deputy Prime Minister in charge of economic development issues. He became the Prime Minister of Russia on December 31, 2012, succeeding Putin's longtime friend and ally, Dmitry Medvedev. In this role, Shmidt had to deal with the aftermath of the Russian financial crisis of 2014-2015 and the subsequent annexation of Crimea by the Russian Federation. In March 2018, Shmidt announced his resignation as Prime Minister due to health reasons.  Shmidt married Tatiana Venediktova in 1999 and they have two children.
