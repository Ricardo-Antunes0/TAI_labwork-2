The Commissioner's Award is presented annually by the Professional Hockey Writers Association (PHWA) to the player who has exhibited the most skill and excellence in hockey during the regular season.  The PHWA presents the Commissioner's Award annually to the player who has exhibited the most skill and excellence in hockey during the regular season. The award was first given in 1972-73 to defenseman Bobby Orr of the Boston Bruins. The recipient is selected by a vote of the PHWA membership after consultation with league officials.
