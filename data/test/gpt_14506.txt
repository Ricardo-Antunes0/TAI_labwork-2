"Stacy's Mom" is a song by American rock band Blink-182. It was released as the lead single from the band's seventh studio album, California (2001).  "Stacy's Mom" is a pop-punk song written by bassist and vocalist Mark Hoppus and drummer Travis Barker. It is about a teenage boy who is in love with his mother. The song was inspired by Barker's own experiences with his mother.  "Stacy's Mom" peaked at number seven on the US "Billboard" Hot 100 and number one on the US Modern Rock Tracks chart. The song also charted in Australia, Canada, Finland, Ireland, the Netherlands, New Zealand, Norway, Sweden, and the UK. "Stacy's Mom" has been certified platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA).  The song was performed live for the first time on May 15, 2001 at the Warfield Theatre in San Francisco and was also included as part of Blink-182's set list for their 2002 tour. It was re-recorded for their 2009 tour and has been played live ever since. A music video was made for the song, which features a teenage boy lip syncing to the song in a bedroom.
