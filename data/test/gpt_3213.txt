Sue Jeffers (born October 3, 1956) was a Democratic member of the United States House of Representatives from New York. She served from 1997 until her resignation in 2002.  Jeffers was born in Rochester, New York on October 3, 1956. She graduated from SUNY Geneseo with a degree in political science in 1978. Jeffers worked as an aide to State Senator John DeFrancisco before being elected to the Rochester City Council in 1990. She was reelected in 1994 and served as the council's minority leader.  In 1996, Jeffers was elected to the House of Representatives as a Democrat, defeating incumbent Republican William D. Owens. She served one term and resigned in 2002, citing health reasons. Jeffers later became executive director of the New York State Democratic Committee. In this capacity, she helped lead the party's efforts to regain control of the State Senate in 2006 and win back the Governor's Mansion in 2010. In 2016, Jeffers was named President of the Women's Leadership Institute at Rochester Institute of Technology.
