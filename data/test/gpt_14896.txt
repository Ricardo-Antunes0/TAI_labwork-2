John Bloomfield (born in Winchester, Kentucky), attended the University of Kentucky, where he was a member of the Sigma Alpha Epsilon fraternity. Bloomfield played bass in a number of bands before landing a recording contract with Rick Rubin's American Recordings in 1985. He recorded two albums with Rubin and appeared on such albums as Beastie Boys' License to Ill and Neil Young's Harvest.  Bloomfield toured extensively in the late 1980s and early 1990s with bands such as Dinosaur Jr., R.E.M., and Sonic Youth, and also released two solo albums, 1987's John Bloomfield and 1990's Ancient Energy. He continued to tour sporadically throughout the 2000s, but has since retired from performing.
