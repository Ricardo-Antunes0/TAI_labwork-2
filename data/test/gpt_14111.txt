The Hải Vân Pass (, , "ocean pass") is a mountain pass in central Vietnam, connecting Quảng Trị Province with Thừa Thiên–Huế Province. It forms the eastern approach to the Huế Valley from the north.  The pass is located over an altitude of 2,845 m (9,190 ft) above mean sea level, and is used by both motor vehicles and pedestrians. The Huế-Quảng Trị Expressway (Highway 1A), running eastwards from Hanoi, passes through the pass.
