The Brotherton Library is a 1936 Grade II listed building located in the town of Brotherton, near Wigan in Greater Manchester, England. The library has a collection of over 10,000 items and offers free public access. It is also a venue for events and has a cafe.  The library was founded in 1892 and originally served as a meeting place for the local community. It began to grow in size and importance over the years and in 1936 was given Grade II listed status. The library has since undergone several renovations including a new children's area which was opened in 2007. The library also offers free internet access and hosts various events throughout the year.
