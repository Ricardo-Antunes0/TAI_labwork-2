Robotech: The Movie, also called Robotech: The New Generation, is a 1995 American-Japanese animated science fiction action film directed by Dean DeBlois and produced by Harmony Gold. It is the first feature-length animated movie based on the Japanese anime series Robotech. The film was released on August 9, 1995, in the United States and was distributed by Columbia Pictures.  The film is set in the year 2030, five years after the end of the Second Robotech War. A new threat arises when a group of aliens known as the Zentraedi land on Earth and take control of a large portion of the planet. To combat this new threat, the United Earth Government commissions a team of young pilots to pilot giant mecha and fight against the Zentraedi. The team is led by Rick Hunter, son of Rick Hunter from the original television series.  The film was well received by fans and critics alike, with many praising its animation and action scenes. It was also nominated for an Academy Award for Best Visual Effects.
