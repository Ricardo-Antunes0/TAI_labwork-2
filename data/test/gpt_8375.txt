The Vikings is a 1958 epic historical drama film directed by Andrew V. McLaglen and produced by 20th Century Fox. The screenplay by Winston Graham is based on the 1958 novel of the same name by the Scottish author James Hogg.  The film tells the story of Harald Fairhair, king of Norway, and his attempt to unite all of Scandinavia under one rule. The plot also focuses on the battles between Harald's Viking armies and those of his rivals.   The cast features Kirk Douglas as Harald Fairhair, Richard Harris as his brother Rollo, and Christopher Lee as King Ecbert of Wessex. Other well-known actors in supporting roles include Ralph Richardson, Ian Hendry, John Mills, Angela Lansbury, and Ingrid Bergman.  The Vikings was a major financial and critical success when it was released in 1958. It has since been considered a classic film in the history of cinema.
