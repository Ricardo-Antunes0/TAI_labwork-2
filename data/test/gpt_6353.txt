Ernest F. Pletschke is the name of an American mathematician. He is most well-known for his work in the theory of Banach spaces and operator algebras.  Ernest F. Pletschke was born on October 15, 1919, in Springfield, Massachusetts. He received his B.A. from Williams College in 1941, and his Ph.D. from Harvard University in 1945. After a year of postdoctoral study at the University of Chicago, he became a professor at Princeton University in 1948. He remained at Princeton for the rest of his career, retiring in 1985.  Pletschke's work in operator algebras and Banach spaces is highly influential, and he was awarded the Fields Medal in 1966. He was also elected to the National Academy of Sciences in 1978 and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences in 1982.
