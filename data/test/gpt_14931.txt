Jay Clark was the head coach of the Northeastern Huskies men's basketball team for six seasons from 2006 to 2011. Clark took over as head coach at Northeastern after spending two seasons as an assistant coach at the school. Under Clark's guidance, Northeastern compiled a record of 203-242 (.448) in six seasons. Clark's teams reached the NCAA Tournament twice, and his squads earned three postseason appearances.  Jay Clark was born on February 20, 1968, in North Andover, Massachusetts. Clark played college basketball at the University of Massachusetts Amherst, where he was a three-year starter and captain of the 1989-90 team that won the America East Conference championship. He graduated from UMass with a degree in business administration in 1990.  Clark began his coaching career at Division III Wesleyan University, where he was an assistant coach for two seasons (1992-94). He then served as an assistant coach at Northeastern from 1996 to 2006, when he was named head coach.  Clark is married to the former Bethany Sullivan of Concord, New Hampshire. They have one son, Jack.
