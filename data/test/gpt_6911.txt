Marie Gernet (1 October 1865 – 1924) was a French painter and sculptor.  Marie Gernet was born in Paris on 1 October 1865. She was the daughter of the sculptor Jean-Léon Gernet and the painter Amélie Rigaud.  Marie Gernet studied at the Académie Julian and at the École des Beaux-Arts, where she was a student of Jules Bastien-Lepage. She exhibited her work at the Salon from 1885 onwards, and was awarded a silver medal in 1889.  Marie Gernet married the painter Jacques Lipchitz in 1893. The couple moved to Berlin in 1898, where Marie Gernet became associated with the Die Brücke group of artists. She returned to Paris in 1905, and died there on 24 February 1924.
