Ben Turner (born 23 January 1984 in Sheffield, England) is a British weightlifter who has competed in the 97 kg category. He has won a total of six medals at World and European championships, including four golds. Turner is also the reigning Commonwealth champion in the 97 kg category.  Ben Turner was born on 23 January 1984 in Sheffield, England. He first took up weightlifting at the age of 16 and started competing in national championships two years later. Turner made his international debut at the 2003 World Championships in Bangkok, where he finished ninth in the 97 kg category. He improved his performance at the 2004 World Championships in Istanbul, where he won silver medal with a total lift of 298 kg. Turner then won his first gold medal at the 2005 World Championships in Montreal, Canada, with a lift of 297 kg. At the 2006 European Championships in Plovdiv, Bulgaria, he lifted 297 kg to win gold medal. At the 2007 World Championships in Tokyo, Japan, Turner lifted 296 kg to win silver medal. In 2008, Turner won gold medal with 295 kg lift at the European Championships in Reykjavik. The same year he became British champion for the first time with a total lift of 291 kg.  In 2009, Turner defended his European title with a total lift of 298 kg. He then competed at the World Championships in Beijing, China and lifted 297 kg to win bronze medal.
