Eleanor Joan 'Dusty' Miller,  (August 3, 1938 – October 25, 2015) was a Canadian politician. She served as a Member of the Legislative Assembly of New Brunswick from 1978 to 1987, and then as the province's Lieutenant Governor from 1987 to 1995.  Miller was born in Moncton, New Brunswick, the daughter of Walter and Eleanor (née McNeil) Miller. She was educated at Moncton High School and the University of New Brunswick. She married Michael F. Smith in 1963.  Miller was first elected to the New Brunswick House of Assembly as the Progressive Conservative candidate for Sackville-Bristol-Lincoln in a by-election on February 16, 1978. She was re-elected in the 1979 provincial election and served until her defeat in the 1987 provincial election.  Miller was appointed Lieutenant Governor of New Brunswick on December 20, 1987, serving until January 6, 1995. In this capacity she was responsible for overseeing all executive actions of the government. Miller also served as President of the Canadian Federation of Independent Business from 1993 to 1995.  Miller died on October 25, 2015, at the age of 80 after a long battle with cancer.
