Bonny Susan Hicks (5 January 1968 â€“ 16 December 2010) was an Australian journalist and author.  Bonny Hicks was born in 1968 in Sydney, Australia. She graduated with a degree in Journalism from the University of Technology, Sydney in 1992. Hicks worked as a reporter for "The Daily Telegraph" from 1992 to 1994, and then became a staff writer for "The Australian" from 1994 to 1997. In 1997, she began working as a freelance journalist, writing for "The Sydney Morning Herald", "The Age", "Australian Business Review", and other publications.  Hicks died of cancer on 16 December 2010 at the age of 41. She was survived by her husband, Greg Hicks; their two children,born in 2005 and 2006; her mother; and her brother.  Hicks' first book, "The Life and Death of Sophie Monk" (2007), tells the story of Sophie Monk, an Australian actress. The book was shortlisted for the 2008 Melbourne Writers' Festival Prize for New Writing. Her second book, "Underbelly: The Golden Mile" (2009), is a history of prostitution in Melbourne from the 1870s to the 1990s. It was published in Australia in 2009 and won the 2010 Prime Minister's Literary Award for Non-Fiction.
