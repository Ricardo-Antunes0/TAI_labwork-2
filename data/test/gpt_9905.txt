Kimberly Sherell Johnson is an American clinical psychologist and author. She is known for her work in the area of borderline personality disorder, and has written several books on the subject. Johnson also conducts psychotherapy sessions and lectures on borderline personality disorder.  Kimberly Sherell Johnson was born on October 9, 1954, in Jacksonville, Florida. She received her undergraduate degree from Florida State University in 1976 and her M.D. from the University of South Florida in 1980. Johnson completed her internship at Jackson Memorial Hospital in Miami, Florida, and then began her psychiatric residency at Massachusetts General Hospital in Boston, Massachusetts.  Johnson is a clinical psychologist who specializes in the treatment of borderline personality disorder (BPD). She has authored several books on the subject, including Borderline Personality Disorder: A New Perspective (1996), The Borderline Personality Problem Workbook (1998), and Understanding Borderline Personality Disorder: A Guide to Research (2002). Johnson also conducts psychotherapy sessions and lectures on BPD. In 2003, she was named one of Psychology Today's "50 Most Influential People in Psychology."  Johnson is married to Gary Johnson, the former governor of New Mexico. They have two children.
