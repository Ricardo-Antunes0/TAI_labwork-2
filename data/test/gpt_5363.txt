Telphusa syndelta is a moth of the family Gelechiidae. It is found in the United States, where it has been recorded from California.  The wingspan is about 17.5Â mm. The forewings are blackish brown, but variably sprinkled with white and with a conspicuous elongate blackish brown streak from the base of the costa through the middle of the wing, as well as a round blackish brown spot at one-fourth from the base of the costa. A similar spot is found at three-fourths, and there is a small blackish brown spot on the dorsum before the discal stigmata, as well as some scattered black scales. The hindwings are light fuscous.
