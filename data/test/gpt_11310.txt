Irvine Theodore Shillingford (born 18 April 1944) is a British historian and author.  Irvine Shillingford was born in London, England, on 18 April 1944. He graduated from the University of Cambridge in 1966 with a BA in History. He then studied at the University of Chicago, where he completed his doctoral dissertation in 1971.  Shillingford worked as a lecturer at the University of Cambridge from 1971 to 1977, and then joined the faculty at Yale University as a professor of history. He has also been a visiting professor at the University of California, Berkeley; the University of Texas at Austin; and the University of British Columbia.  Shillingford is the author of more than twenty books, including The New Imperialism (1978), The Renaissance: Studies in Art and Society (1981), The War Against Napoleon (1994), and The Age of Revolution, 1763-1848 (2003). He has also written numerous articles for journals and magazines, and has appeared as a guest on various television programs.  Shillingford is married to historian Elizabeth Shillingford. They have two children.
