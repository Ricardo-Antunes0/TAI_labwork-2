Jacob Sloat Fassett (November 13, 1853 – October 25, 1918) was an American jurist who served as the fourth Associate Justice of the United States Supreme Court from 1903 to 1910.  Fassett was born in Montpelier, Vermont, to Luman and Evaline (née Sloat) Fassett. He attended the University of Vermont, where he was a member of Phi Beta Kappa. He then studied law at the University of Michigan Law School. He was admitted to the bar in 1876 and commenced practice in Burlington, Vermont.  Fassett served as city solicitor of Burlington from 1883 to 1886, and as state's attorney for Chittenden County from 1886 to 1890. He was a member of the Vermont House of Representatives from 1889 to 1890.  Fassett was nominated by President Benjamin Harrison to the U.S. Supreme Court on December 8, 1890, to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Justice David Brewer. He was confirmed by the United States Senate on December 20, 1890, and received his commission the same day. He served on the Supreme Court until his retirement on October 25, 1910.  Fassett died in Burlington, Vermont, aged 70 years, 174 days. He is interred at Forest Lawn Cemetery in Burlington.
