Barston is a village and civil parish in the East Riding of Yorkshire, England. It is situated approximately south-west of Hull and east of the city of York. Barston is a scattered settlement with a few small shops and a parish church. The population of the civil parish at the 2011 census was 258.  The name "Barston" is thought to be derived from the Old English word "barrn" meaning a farmstead or settlement. The first record of the village dates from 1086 when it was mentioned in the Domesday Book as "Bernestune". During the Middle Ages, Barston was part of the manor of Hessle and it formed part of the hundred of Haverton. The village became part of the East Riding of Yorkshire in 1974.  The parish church, dedicated to Saint John the Baptist, was built in 1859 and has been Grade II listed since 1984. The village has a public house, the Fox & Hounds, which was built in 1892.
