Cyanothamnus rigenss, commonly known as the stiff goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial plant in the sunflower family. It is native to the eastern and central United States, from Maryland north to Quebec and west to Iowa and Nebraska.  Its leaves are opposite, stalkless, and 3-6 inches long. The flowers are yellow, with a blue center, and are borne in clusters of up to 12. The fruit is a dry capsule.
