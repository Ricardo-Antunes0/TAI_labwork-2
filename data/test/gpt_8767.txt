Ceriops australis, the yellow mangrove or smooth-fruited mangrove, is a species of mangrove in the family Rhizophoraceae. It is endemic to Queensland, Australia.  The yellow mangrove grows up to 30 metres tall and has a spread of up to 2 metres. It is distinguished from other mangroves by its smooth, yellow-green bark and its large, flat leaves which are up to 60 cm long and 20 cm wide. The flowers are white and the fruit is a yellow pod which contains a single seed.  The yellow mangrove is threatened by coastal development and waterlogging.
