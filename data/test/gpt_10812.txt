Beverly Guy-Sheftall (born June 1, 1946, in Los Angeles, California) is an American visual artist, who works primarily in video and audio. Sheftall was one of the first women to earn a degree in art from the University of California, Berkeley.  Guy-Sheftall's early video works were often appropriated or reworked excerpts from popular culture, such as John Wayne movies and sitcoms. More recently, she has begun making longer works that explore narrative and memory. Her videos are often screened together as single-channel installations, and they are often used as teaching tools in art schools. Guy-Sheftall currently lives and works in New York City.  Guy-Sheftall was born in Los Angeles in 1946 to English immigrants. She grew up in the San Fernando Valley and studied painting at the University of California, Berkeley, where she received her BFA in 1967. She then moved to New York City to study with Yoko Ono at the School of Visual Arts. Guy-Sheftall's first video work, "The Clock", was screened at the Whitney Museum of American Art in 1971. In 1973 she co-founded Video Art International with Daniel Buren and David Salle.
