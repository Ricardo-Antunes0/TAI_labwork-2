Gradimir Gojer (born 4 March 1951) is a Slovenian historian, academic and politician. He is currently a professor at the University of Ljubljana, where he also served as rector from 2004 to 2006.  Born in Ljubljana, Gojer graduated from the University of Ljubljana with a degree in history in 1973. He then completed his doctoral studies at the same institution, receiving his PhD in 1989. From 1973 to 1976, Gojer lectured at the Faculty of Arts and Sciences at the University of Ljubljana. From 1976 to 1982, he was a research associate at the Institute for Slovenian History in Ljubljana.  From 1982 to 2004, Gojer was a professor at the University of Ljubljana. In 1992, he became a full professor and served as rector from 2004 to 2006. He is currently a professor at the Department of History and Political Science and also serves as head of the Slovenian National Commission for UNESCO.  Gojer has written extensively on historical topics, including Slovenian history, Croatian history, and Yugoslav history. He has also published books on contemporary European politics and on historical theory. In 2007, he received the Order of Zeta for his contributions to Slovenian culture.
