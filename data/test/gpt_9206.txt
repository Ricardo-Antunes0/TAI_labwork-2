Syringa Park is a provincial park on Vancouver Island, in the Canadian province of British Columbia. The park is located in the municipality of Qualicum Beach, on the northeast coast of the island. The park was established in 1972 and covers an area of 443 hectares.  The park features a wide variety of natural habitats, including old growth forest, estuarine and marine habitats, and grasslands. The park is home to a number of rare and endangered species, including the southern bald eagle, Vancouver Island marmot, and Vancouver Island giant salamander.
