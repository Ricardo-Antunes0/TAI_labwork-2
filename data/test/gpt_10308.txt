Wei Jin (born October 1959) is a Chinese-born Canadian film and television actor.  Wei Jin was born in Shaoxing, Zhejiang Province, China in October 1959. He emigrated to Canada with his family at the age of three and grew up in Toronto.  After graduating from high school, Wei Jin studied at the University of Toronto, where he earned a degree in theatre. In the early 1980s, he began his acting career on stage in Toronto and Montreal.  Wei Jin has appeared in several Canadian television series, including "Street Legal", "The X-Files", "Republic of Doyle" and "Da Vinci's Inquest". He also appeared in the Canadian series "Kill My Darlings" and "The L Word".  He has also appeared in films including "Last Exit to Brooklyn", "American Psycho", "Rushmore" and "The Prestige". His most recent film appearance was in the Chinese film "Legend of the Assassin".  Wei Jin is married to actress Mandy Moore and they have two children.
