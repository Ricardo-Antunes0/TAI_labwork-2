Keala Keanaaina (born May 30, 1977) is an American singer, songwriter, and actress.  Keanaaina was born in Honolulu and raised in the Waianae Coast village of Makaha. She is of Native Hawaiian and German descent. She is the granddaughter of Samuel Keanaaina and Nani Kaheli. Her mother is of Portuguese descent. Keanaaina began her musical career at age 14 as a lead vocalist with the band Makaha Sons.  In 1995, she released her self-titled debut album, which featured the singles "I Will Always Love You" and "How Far I'll Go". The album was certified gold by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA). In 1997, she appeared as an extra on "Hawaii Five-O" and sang the theme song for the show's fourth season.  In 1998, she was a featured vocalist on "Hawaii Five-O"'s twentieth anniversary album, "20 Years of Five-O". That same year, she starred in the independent film "Breathless", alongside Dennis Quaid and Anthony Quinn. The film was poorly received and Keanaaina's performance was derided by many as wooden and uninspiring.  In 1999, she released her second album, "Our Time".
