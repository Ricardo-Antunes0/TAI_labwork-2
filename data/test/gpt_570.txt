Anthony Patrick Leslie Minford  (born 17 July 1947) is a British economist, currently Professor of Economics at the University of Surrey. He is known for his advocacy of supply-side economics and his criticism of the welfare state.  Patrick Minford was born in 1947 in Liverpool, England. He attended King's College, Cambridge, where he received his BA in 1966 and his MA in 1969. He then undertook postgraduate studies at the London School of Economics, where he received his DPhil in 1974.  Patrick Minford began his career at the London School of Economics, where he became a lecturer in economics in 1978. In 1981, he moved to the University of Sussex, where he became a professor in 1992 and held the position until he moved to the University of Surrey in 2007.  Patrick Minford's research focuses on economic theory and policy, with a particular emphasis on supply-side economics and welfare reform. He has written extensively on these topics, publishing books such as The Theory of Economic Policy (1986), The Limits to Growth (1972), and The Affluent Society (1967).  Patrick Minford has been a vocal critic of the welfare state, arguing that it is ineffective and harmful to society as a whole. He has also been a strong advocate for supply-side economics, which he believes is the best way to achieve economic growth and improve social conditions.
