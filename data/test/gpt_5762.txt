Ichneutica haedifrontella is a moth of the family Geometridae. It is found in New Zealand.  The wingspan is about 25 mm. The forewings are light ochreous-fuscous, with a few scattered black scales, and some scattered white scales throughout the wing. There are also black markings towards the base of the costa, as well as a small black discocellular spot and a larger one near the dorsum before the tornus, as well as markings along the inner margin of both wings, including a triangular black spot at three-fourths, an oblique line from beneath this to before the apex of the triangle, and two more triangular spots about one-fourth from these lines. The hindwings are fuscous-grey. Adults are on wing from November to December.
