The MPL-50 (, malaya pekhotnaya lopata-50, small-caliber machine gun 50 mm) is a 7.62Ã—51mm NATO light machine gun used by many militaries around the world. It is manufactured by Belgian arms manufacturer FN Herstal, now part of the conglomerate FN Herstal Group.  Design work on the MPL-50 began in 1969 and the weapon entered service with the Malaysian Army in the early 1980s. It has been exported to more than 20 countries and is currently in service with the armies of Afghanistan, Bahrain, Cambodia, Egypt, Indonesia, Iraq, Kuwait, Lebanon, Libya, Mauritania, Morocco, Niger, Oman, Philippines, Qatar, Saudi Arabia and Yemen.
