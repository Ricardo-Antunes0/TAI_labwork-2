John Neulinger (April 26, 1924 - June 9, 1990) was an American composer and pianist. He was born in New York City to Russian-Jewish parents. After studying at the Juilliard School of Music and Yale University, Neulinger became a faculty member at the University of Southern California, where he taught composition until his death. His most popular works include the operas Death of a Salesman and A View from the Bridge, and the piano concertos The Deer Hunter and Second Son.
