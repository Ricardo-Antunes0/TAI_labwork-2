Ernst Christian Hesse (14 April 167616 May 1759) was a German philosopher, poet, and writer. He is best known for his novel, The Glass Bead Game.  Hesse was born in Kassel into a very wealthy family. At the age of eighteen he went to the University of Marburg to study theology, but he soon became disillusioned with the church and left without taking a degree. He then travelled throughout Europe, spending time in France, Italy, and Switzerland. In 1699 he moved to Weimar, where he began to write and publish poetry. In 1726 he married Catharina Dorothea Königin of Brunswick-Lüneburg, with whom he had six children.  Hesse is best known for his novel, The Glass Bead Game (1896), which tells the story of a group of people who spend a year at a villa in Italy playing a game called "The Glass Bead Game". The novel has been translated into more than twenty languages and has sold over fifteen million copies. Hesse also wrote poetry, essays, plays, and children's stories. He died in Weimar at the age of eighty-four.
