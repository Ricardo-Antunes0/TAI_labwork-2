Phlomoides tuberosa (syn. Phlomis tuberosa) is a species of flowering plant in the daisy family known by the common name Tuberous phlomoides.  This species is endemic to California, where it is known from the Sierra Nevada and Transverse Ranges. It grows in open habitat, including grassy meadows, chaparral, and oak woodlands.  This perennial herb grows up to 50 centimeters tall and has a stem covered in soft, tuberous scales. The leaves are up to 20 centimeters long and have lance-shaped blades divided into sharply pointed lobes. The inflorescence is a cluster of up to 40 yellow disc flowers surrounded by long, woolly stalks.
