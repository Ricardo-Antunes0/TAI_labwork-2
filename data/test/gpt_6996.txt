Carl Milton White (1903 – November 8, 1988) was an American astronomer and astrophysicist who worked in the fields of stellar evolution, the sun, and the Milky Way. He was also a prolific writer, publishing over 100 articles in scientific journals.  White was born in 1903 in Lynchburg, Virginia. He received his bachelor's degree from the University of Virginia in 1925, and his master's degree from Harvard University in 1926. He then worked as a research associate at the Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics from 1926 to 1929. He moved to the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) in 1930, where he became an associate professor of astronomy in 1934. White remained at UCLA until his retirement in 1978.  White was a prolific writer, publishing over 100 articles in scientific journals. He is most famous for his work on stellar evolution and the sun. His work on the subject led to him being awarded the George Pólya Prize by the American Astronomical Society in 1967. White died on November 8, 1988.
