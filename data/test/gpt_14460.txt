David Director Friedman (born February 12, 1945) is an American economist and philosopher, who has written extensively on the philosophy of libertarianism. He is a professor of economics at George Mason University, where he also served as the university's first Director of the Mercatus Center.  David Friedman was born in Brooklyn, New York City, to a Jewish family. Friedman attended Yale University, where he earned his B.A. in Economics in 1967. He then attended the University of Chicago, where he earned his M.A. and Ph.D. in Economics in 1971 and 1976, respectively.  Friedman has been a professor of economics at George Mason University since 1978, where he also served as the university's first Director of the Mercatus Center from 1996 to 2003. In 2003, Friedman was named the inaugural James M. Buchanan Professor of Economics at George Mason University.  Friedman has written extensively on libertarianism and economic theory, and is known for his advocacy of laissez-faire economics and his criticism of government interventionism. He has also written widely on philosophy, including works on ethics and political philosophy.
