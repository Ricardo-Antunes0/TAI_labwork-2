R. J. Adams, also known as Bob Adams, is an American author, newspaper columnist and political activist.  Adams was born in Los Angeles, California in 1940, the son of Patricia (n√©e McCarthy) and Joseph P. Adams, a career officer in the United States Air Force. Adams has two brothers, Patrick and Timothy. After graduating from high school in 1958, Adams attended Stanford University but left before completing his undergraduate studies to enlist in the United States Air Force. He served as a Special Agent with the Air Force Security Service during the Vietnam War.  Adams married his first wife, Jane Elizabeth (Betty) Dewey, on November 26, 1967 in Concord, California. They divorced on January 3, 1978. On November 25, 1978 he married his second wife, Lynda Ann (McVay) Adams. They have four children: Rachel Elizabeth (born October 10, 1979), Sarah Catherine (born November 12, 1982), Joseph P. III (born March 5, 1985), and John Rufus (born December 31, 1987).  Adams began writing a weekly column for "The Oregonian" in 1976 and continued to write columns for that paper until his death on July 12, 2016 at the age of 76.  Adams was active in politics from an early age. In 1964 he campaigned for U.S. Senator Barry Goldwater of Arizona and later worked as a spokesman for the National Conservative Political Action Committee. In 1972 Adams ran unsuccessfully for the U.S. House of Representatives
