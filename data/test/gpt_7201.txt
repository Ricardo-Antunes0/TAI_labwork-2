Michael Barry (born 18 December 1975) is a British cyclist who has won three stages of the Tour de France. He also competed in the 2004 and 2012 Summer Olympics.  Born in Huddersfield, West Yorkshire, Barry started cycling at the age of 15, when he was given a bike by his father. After winning a junior national championship, he turned professional in 1998 with Team NetApp-Endura. He won his first stage of the Tour de France in 2002, and repeated the feat in 2004. In 2005 he won two stages, and finished second in the general classification. He also competed in the 2006 and 2008 Summer Olympics.  Barry joined Team Sky for the 2009 season, and won a stage of the Tour de France in his first race with them. He also finished second overall in that year's Tour de France. In 2012 he won a stage of the Tour de France and finished fourth overall, becoming only the second British rider to do so after Sir Bradley Wiggins.
