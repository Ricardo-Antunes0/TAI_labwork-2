The Duluth-Superior Lumberjacks were a professional indoor hockey team that played in the World Hockey Association from 1972 to 1975. The team was based in Duluth, Minnesota.  The Duluth-Superior Lumberjacks were one of the original six teams that formed the World Hockey Association in 1972. The Lumberjacks were led by head coach Don Cherry and played their home games at the Duluth Entertainment and Convention Center.  The Lumberjacks finished the 1972–73 season with a record of 16 wins and 54 losses, good for last place in the league. In 1973–74, the Lumberjacks improved to fifth place with a record of 33 wins and 37 losses. However, they finished last in the league again in 1974–75 with a record of 22 wins and 56 losses.
