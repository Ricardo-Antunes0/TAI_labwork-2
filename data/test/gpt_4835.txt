Executive Order 13987, officially titled Organizing and Strengthening American Intelligence, was an executive order issued by United States President Barack Obama on April 26, 2013. The order aimed to improve the effectiveness and efficiency of the United States Intelligence Community.  The order created a National Intelligence Director and a National Counterterrorism Center, aimed at improving coordination and communication between the 16 separate U.S. intelligence agencies. It also established a National Security Council Principals Committee to provide strategic direction to the Intelligence Community and appointed John Brennan as the first National Intelligence Director and James Clapper as the first National Counterterrorism Center director.  The order also directed the creation of a review board to oversee the activities of the U.S. intelligence community, and outlined measures that would be taken in response to intelligence failures or threats to national security.
