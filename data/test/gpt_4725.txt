The Great Twenty-Eight is a compilation album by American rock band Pearl Jam. It was released on November 25, 1998, by Epic Records.  The album is a collection of cover versions of songs by classic rock bands, including The Who, Led Zeppelin, and The Rolling Stones. It was produced by the band's drummer, Mike McCready, and features guest appearances by vocalists Eddie Vedder and Stone Gossard of Pearl Jam and Chris Cornell of Soundgarden.  The album was a commercial success, debuting at number two on the US "Billboard" 200 chart and selling over 6 million copies in the United States. It has been certified Platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA).  The Great Twenty-Eight received generally positive reviews from music critics. At Metacritic, which assigns a normalized rating out of 100 to reviews from mainstream publications, the album received an average score of 73, which indicates "generally favorable reviews".  AllMusic's Stephen Thomas Erlewine called the album "exhilarating" and wrote that it "shows that Pearl Jam remain one of rock's most inventive and consistently inspired bands." Robert Christgau called it "the best album ever made by any cover band" in his review for "The Village Voice".
