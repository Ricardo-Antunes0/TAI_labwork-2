The Strategikon of Kekaumenos is a work on military strategy written by Byzantine emperor Constantine VII Porphyrogenitus in the 10th century. The work is notable for its inclusion of information on siege warfare, and has been described as "the most important Byzantine military treatise ever written". The work covers a wide range of topics, including how to plan an attack, how to defend against an attack, and how to conduct sieges. It is one of the few sources of information on Byzantine siege warfare that is still extant.
