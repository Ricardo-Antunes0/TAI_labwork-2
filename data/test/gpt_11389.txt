Marie Boas Hall was born in Brooklyn, New York, on October 18, 1919. She received her B.A. from Barnard College in 1941 and her M.A. from Columbia University in 1943. She then studied at the University of Berlin under Franz Boas, receiving her Ph.D. in 1948.  After returning to the United States, Hall joined the faculty at Columbia University. She became one of the founders of cultural anthropology and served as its first director from 1963 to 1973. In 1984 she was awarded the National Humanities Medal.  Hall died on December 31, 2003, in Manhattan, New York City.
