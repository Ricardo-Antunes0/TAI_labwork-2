The Ernst Strüngmann Institute for Neuroscience in Frankfurt, Germany, is a world-leading research center for the study of the brain and nervous system. The institute has a long history of groundbreaking work in neuroscience, beginning with the Nobel Prize-winning discoveries of Karl von Frisch and Ernst Strüngmann in the 1920s. Today, the institute's scientists are engaged in a range of groundbreaking projects aimed at understanding the molecular and cellular basis of brain function.
