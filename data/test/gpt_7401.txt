Black Jackson  are an alternative rock band from the United States that formed in 2006. The band consists of lead vocalist and guitarist John Jackson, bassist and backing vocalist Bryan Harvey, drummer Johnny Kelley and keyboardist Austin James.  Black Jackson was formed in 2006 in Los Angeles, California by lead vocalist and guitarist John Jackson, bassist and backing vocalist Bryan Harvey, drummer Johnny Kelley and keyboardist Austin James. The band released their self-titled debut album in 2007 on the independent label Equal Vision Records. The album was well-received by critics, who praised the band's unique mix of alternative rock and funk influences.  The band toured extensively in support of their debut album, playing shows across the United States as well as in Mexico and Canada. In 2008, they released their second album, "Puzzle", which featured a more stripped-down approach with less funk influence. The album was well-received by critics and enjoyed increased airplay on alternative radio stations.  In 2009, Black Jackson signed to Polydor Records and released their third album, "Walking on Sunshine". The album was again well-received by critics and saw increased airplay on alternative radio stations. The band toured extensively in support of the album, including appearances at festivals such as Bonnaroo and Lollapalooza.
