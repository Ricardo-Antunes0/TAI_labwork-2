Al-Mahdi Salah ad-Din (died 1445) was a 14th century Sunni Muslim scholar, jurist and Sufi mystic. He is considered one of the most important legal theorists in Sunni Islam and a pioneer of Islamic jurisprudence.  Born in Damascus, Al-Mahdi Salah ad-Din was a descendant of the Prophet Muhammad. He studied Islamic jurisprudence under some of the most influential scholars of his time, including Uthman ibn Affan, Shafi'i, and Abu Hanifa. He also studied theology and mysticism under various teachers, including Ibn al-Arabi and Jalaluddin Rumi.  Al-Mahdi Salah ad-Din held several prominent positions during his lifetime, including judge in Aleppo and Baghdad, and head of the Fatimid Caliphate's general intelligence service. He is best known for his seminal work on Islamic law, the Muqaddimah (or "Plan"), which outlines a systematic approach to understanding Islamic law.
