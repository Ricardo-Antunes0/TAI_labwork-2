Frederick Tenney (November 26, 1871 – July 16, 1954) was an American football coach and player. He served as the head football coach at the University of Kansas from 1912 to 1915, compiling a record of 10–5–1. Tenney was also the head basketball coach at Kansas from 1912 to 1916, tallying a record of 50–9. In 1916, he left Kansas to become the head football and basketball coach at Iowa State University, where he posted a record of 21–8 in three years. Tenney later served as the athletic director at Iowa State from 1936 to 1951.  Frederick Tenney was born on November 26, 1871, in Newton, Kansas. He attended Newton High School and played football and basketball for the school's team. After graduating from high school in 1889, Tenney enrolled at Washburn College in Topeka, Kansas, where he played college football and baseball for the Washburn Tigers football team and the baseball team. Tenney graduated from Washburn College with a bachelor's degree in 1893.  Tenney began his coaching career at Newton High School in 1893. From 1893 to 1896, he served as the head football coach at Newton High School. During his two-year stint as the head football coach at Newton High School, Tenney compiled a record of 12–2–2.
