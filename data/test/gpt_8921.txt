William Howard Love (born August 14, 1957, in Baltimore, Maryland) is an American businessman, entrepreneur and philanthropist. He is the founder and chief executive officer of Love Media, a digital media company.  Love was born in Baltimore, Maryland. He attended Northwestern University, where he earned a bachelor's degree in business administration in 1979.   After completing his undergraduate studies, Love worked for a financial services company. In 1984, he founded Love Media, a digital media company that creates and distributes online content. The company has grown to be one of the largest providers of online content in the world.  Love has been involved in a number of philanthropic projects. He is the founder and chairman of the Love Foundation, which provides grants to non-profit organizations. He also supports the work of the National Geographic Society and the Smithsonian Institution. In 2006, he donated $10 million to Northwestern University to fund construction of the William H. Love Library and Museum.  Love has been married to his wife, Lisa Love, since 1988. They have two children together.
