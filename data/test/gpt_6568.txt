A left-handed batsman and right-arm bowler, he was a very successful lower-order batsman and occasional wicketkeeper.  Birtles made his first-class debut for Northamptonshire against Derbyshire at the County Ground, Northampton, on 4 April 1904. He played three further first-class matches for the county in that season, with his final appearance coming against Worcestershire at New Road, Worcester, on 20 May 1904. In his four first-class matches for the county, he scored a total of 191 runs at an average of 21.75 batting first in the lower order. With the ball, he took 7 wickets at an average of 36.33, including 3 wickets in his final first-class match.  Birtles played Minor counties cricket for Northamptonshire from 1904 to 1921, making a total of 106 appearances. He scored a total of 892 runs at an average of 18.13 and took 78 wickets at an average of 30.10, which included 3 wickets in his final Minor Counties match.  Birtles died on 25 December 1960 at the age of 81 in South Shields.
