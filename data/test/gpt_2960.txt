He also served as a Lord Justice of Appeal from 1977 to 1984.  Kenneth Younger was born in 1916, the son of Sir John Younger, 3rd Baronet, and his wife, the former Violet Maynard. He was educated at Eton College and Trinity College, Oxford, where he read law. After qualifying as a barrister, he joined the practice of his father.  In 1951, Younger was elected to Parliament as the MP for Aldershot. He held the seat until 1966, when he was appointed a Lord Justice of Appeal. He served in this position until 1984, when he retired from politics.  In retirement, Younger served as chairman of several organisations including the British Library Board and the Broadcasting Board of Governors. He also became involved in literary and historical projects, serving as president of the Friends of Bletchley Park and chairman of the Imperial War Museum Research Committee. He died on 16 October 2009 at the age of 96.
