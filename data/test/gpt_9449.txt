Hector Joseph "Hurricane" Kilrea (June 11, 1907 – February 21, 1983) was an American and Canadian professional ice hockey forward who played in the National Hockey League and World Hockey Association.  Kilrea was born in Toronto, Ontario, Canada and played his junior hockey with the Toronto Marlboros. He then played for the Kitchener Rangers of the Ontario Hockey Association before being drafted by the Montreal Canadiens in the second round of the 1929 NHL Amateur Draft. Kilrea made his NHL debut with Montreal in 1929–30 and scored 16 goals in 49 games. The following season, he was traded to the Boston Bruins and finished with 12 goals in 52 games. Kilrea then moved to the World Hockey Association (WHA) where he played for the Toronto St. Patricks and Montreal Canadiens. In his two seasons with Toronto, Kilrea recorded 57 points in 61 games. He then returned to the NHL with the Chicago Black Hawks in 1933–34 and recorded 10 goals and 31 points in 66 games. The following season, Kilrea joined the New York Rangers and recorded 14 goals and 36 points in 67 games. He retired after playing one final season for the Detroit Red Wings in 1937–38. Kilrea finished his NHL career with 184 goals and 338 points in 608 games.  Kilrea later coached hockey at Lower Canada College and was also a scout for Montreal, Boston, Chicago, Detroit, New York, Philadelphia, Vancouver and Winnipeg. He died on February 21, 1983 at the age of 78 after a long illness.
