Didier "Ecama" Marouani (born 14 July 1953, in Marseille) is a French composer and conductor. He has composed for stage, orchestra, chamber music, and choral works, as well as film scores.  Didier Marouani was born in Marseille on 14 July 1953. After studying at the Conservatoire de Paris, he won first prize in the conducting class of Bernard Herrmann in 1978. In 1981 he won the prestigious first prize at the International Karl Böhm Competition in Vienna.  From 1983 to 1987 he was music director of the Orchestre National de France, and from 1988 to 1996 he was principal conductor of the Orchestre Philharmonique de Radio France. In 1997 he became conductor laureate of the Orchestre National de France.  Since 1999 he has been director of the Lyon Opera. In 2003 he guest conducted at the Berlin Symphony Orchestra under Riccardo Muti, and in 2006 he guest conducted at the Royal Liverpool Philharmonic Orchestra under Rafael Kubelik.  Didier Marouani's compositions include operas, orchestral works, chamber music, and choral works. His most well-known opera is "L'amour de Loin" (1991), which has been performed around the world.
