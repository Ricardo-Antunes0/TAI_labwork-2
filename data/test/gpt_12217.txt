Felipe VI or Philip VI (; Felipe Fernández de Borbón y de Valois-Borbón, ; born 21 June 1968) is the king of Spain. He has reigned since 20 November 2014, upon the abdication of his father, King Juan Carlos I.  Philip has three younger sisters: Infanta Elena, Infanta Cristina and Infanta Margarita. He was baptized as a Catholic on 24 June 1968 at the palace of Zaragoza by the Cardinal Archbishop of Zaragoza, José Saraiva Martínez.  As the heir to the throne, Philip was educated in Spain and Switzerland. He joined the Spanish military in 1985 and became a helicopter pilot in the Spanish Air Force in 1992. He then studied political science at King's College London and graduated with a Bachelor of Arts degree in 1995.  Philip married Letizia Ortiz de Tejada on 4 July 2002 in a private ceremony at Mónaco. They have two daughters: Leonor (born 2004) and Sofía (born 2007).
