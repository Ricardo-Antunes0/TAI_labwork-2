Coco is a 2009 French comedy film directed by Lee Unkrich and co-written by Unkrich and Adrian Molina. The film stars Gael García Bernal, Benjamin Bratt, Renée Zellweger, and Anthony Hopkins. It tells the story of Miguel, a young boy in the town of Santiago, Mexico, who dreams of becoming a musician like his idol, Ernesto de la Cruz. When Miguel's idol dies, he is forced to confront the realities of life.  The film premiered in competition at the 2009 Cannes Film Festival and won the award for Best Screenplay. It was also nominated for the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature.
