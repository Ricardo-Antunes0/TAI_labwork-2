Boone County Airlines was a passenger airline based in Boone County, Iowa, USA. It operated scheduled passenger service to Chicago-O'Hare International Airport, Des Moines International Airport and Sioux City International Airport. The airline ceased operations on September 30, 2009.   Boone County Airlines was founded in 2003 as a small commuter airline serving the Midwest. In 2007, the airline began offering scheduled passenger service to three airports in Iowa: Des Moines International Airport, Sioux City International Airport and Chicago-O'Hare International Airport. In 2008, the airline added service to Omaha, Nebraska.  On September 30, 2009, Boone County Airlines ceased operations due to financial difficulties. At its peak, the airline served over 100,000 passengers annually.
