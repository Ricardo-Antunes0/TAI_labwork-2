Noel Harry Leaver (23 March 1889 – 11 August 1957) was an English professional footballer who played as a forward. He made more than 350 appearances in the Football League and won the League Championship twice.  Born in Bury, Lancashire, Leaver started his career with Bury before moving to Manchester City in 1912. He made his debut for City on 2 October 1912 in a 3–1 victory over Sheffield Wednesday and went on to make 315 appearances for the club, scoring 97 goals. He helped City win the League Championship in 1913 and 1914 and reached the final of the FA Cup in 1915, where they lost 1–0 to Sheffield United. After leaving City in 1916, Leaver spent three seasons with Wolverhampton Wanderers before retiring from football at the age of 33. He later worked as a sports journalist. Leaver died at the age of 70 in 1957.
