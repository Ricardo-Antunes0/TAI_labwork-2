USS Keosanqua (ATA-198) was a Maricopa-class auxiliary transport aircraft carrier of the United States Navy. She was named for the Keosanqua River in California.  Keosanqua was laid down on 6 May 1944 by the California Shipbuilding Corporation, Yard No. 3, in San Pedro, California; launched on 19 September 1944; sponsored by Mrs. Otto K. Springer; and commissioned on 25 November 1944, with Lieutenant Commander J. R. Pritchard in command.  After shakedown, Keosanqua sailed for the Pacific theatre, arriving at Pearl Harbor on 14 January 1945. For the next two months she operated out of Hawaiian waters providing air support to forces engaged in the liberation of Okinawa Gunto and the Iwo Jima operation. On 2 March she sailed for the Marianas taking part in the invasion of Guam, where she remained until 20 July. She then steamed to Eniwetok to take part in Operation "Iceberg," the massive amphibious assault against Okinawa which began on 1 April 1945. Keosanqua continued to supply air cover for the troops ashore until hostilities ended on 15 August 1945.  Following the surrender of Japan, Keosanqua proceeded to Nagasaki, Japan where she embarked veterans of the Pacific theater for transport back to Hawaii. From Nagasaki she sailed to San Francisco Bay where she disembarked her passengers on 11 October 1945 and arrived at Pearl Harbor on 18 October 1945.
