Hicklin was born in 1895 in Lynn, Massachusetts. He attended the School of the Museum of Fine Arts in Boston, where he studied with Arthur Wesley Dow and Stanley William Hayter.  Hicklin worked as an illustrator for pulp magazines during the 1920s and 1930s, before becoming a comic book artist in the 1940s. He is best known for his work on the 1950s superhero series "The Adventures of Mr. Mxyzptlk" and the 1960s "The Amazing Spider-Man". He also worked on books such as "The Cat in the Hat" and "The Grapes of Wrath".  Hicklin died in 1975.
