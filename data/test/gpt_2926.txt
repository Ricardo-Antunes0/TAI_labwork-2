Lubbock Christian University (LCU) is a private, evangelical Christian university located in Lubbock, Texas, United States. It has a total enrollment of about 3,000 students.  LCU is a member of the Council for Christian Colleges and Universities and the Texas Christian University System. The university offers undergraduate degrees in arts and sciences, business, economics, education, theology, music and nursing. It also offers graduate programs in biblical studies, church leadership and music.  LCU traces its origins to 1907 when the First Baptist Church of Lubbock established an elementary school that would later become Lubbock Junior College. In 1951, Lubbock Baptist College became a four-year institution under the leadership of Dr. J. Frank Norris. In 1968, the college changed its name to Lubbock Christian University in honor of Dr. Norris' legacy.
