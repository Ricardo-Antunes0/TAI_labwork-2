Richard Halliburton (January 9, 1900 – presumed dead June 25, 1947) was an American oilman, engineer, and entrepreneur who is best known for his development of the modern water well drilling method.  Richard Halliburton was born in Kansas City, Missouri, on January 9, 1900. He attended the University of Kansas and studied engineering. In 1922 he started his own oilfield services business.  Halliburton's most famous accomplishment was his development of the modern water well drilling method. This technique allowed oil and gas to be extracted from deep underground rock formations. It revolutionized oil production and made Halliburton a millionaire.  Halliburton died in a plane crash on June 25, 1947. He was only 49 years old. He is widely considered one of the most influential figures in American history.
