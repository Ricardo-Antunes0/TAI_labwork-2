"A Bug's Land" (stylized "a bug's land") is a 2001 American computer-animated adventure comedy film produced by Walt Disney Feature Animation and released by Walt Disney Pictures. It was directed by John Lasseter and co-directed by Lee Unkrich, and written by Michael Arndt, Lasseter, and Pete Docter. The film stars the voices of Tom Hanks, Rita Ora, Timothy Spall, Jennifer Lopez, Chris Elliott, and Tony Goldwyn.  The film follows the adventures of a young bug named Max as he travels to an alternate world where all the bugs have landed due to a mysterious force. Along the way, he meets a variety of characters who help him learn about his new world and the forces that are trying to keep it from prospering. "A Bug's Land" was released to critical acclaim and was a box office success, grossing over $821 million worldwide. It received multiple awards and nominations, including an Academy Award for Best Animated Feature.  "A Bug's Land" is the first feature-length animated film produced by Walt Disney Feature Animation since their 1995 release "The Lion King". In an interview with "The Verge", Pixar president Ed Catmull said that Disney's decision to produce an animated movie again was motivated by their desire to emulate the critical and financial success that Pixar had achieved with their films "Toy Story 3" (2010) and "Up" (2009).
