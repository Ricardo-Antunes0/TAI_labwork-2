He is best known for his command of the 1st Minnesota Volunteer Infantry Regiment during the latter conflict.  Ord was born in Hardin Township, Franklin County, Ohio, on October 18, 1818. He graduated from the United States Military Academy in 1839 and was commissioned a Second Lieutenant in the U.S. Army. Ord served in various posts before being promoted to First Lieutenant in 1853 and to Captain in 1859.  In July 1861, Ord was appointed colonel of the 1st Minnesota Volunteer Infantry Regiment, which he led during the American Civil War. During his command of the regiment, Ord fought in several battles, including Antietam, Fredericksburg and Chancellorsville. He was brevetted brigadier general for his actions at Fredericksburg and major general for his actions at Gettysburg. After the war, Ord served as superintendent of West Point from 1872 to 1875.  Ord died on December 15, 1886 in Washington, D.C. He is buried at Arlington National Cemetery.
