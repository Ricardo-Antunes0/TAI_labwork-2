Raízen (, pronounced Ha-ease-ain't) is the third studio album by the Mexican singer-songwriter Lucero. It was released on October 7, 2017, by Sony Music Latin.  The album was produced by Lucero and Gustavo Cerati, who had previously worked together on her previous album "Amar es Una Acción". The album's title is Spanish for "roots", and it is Lucero's first album without her husband, the musician Andrés Musa.  "Raízen" received generally positive reviews from music critics. At Metacritic, which assigns a normalized rating out of 100 to reviews from mainstream publications, the album has received an average score of 73, which indicates "generally favorable reviews".  The album debuted at number one on the Mexican Albums Chart and has been certified Platinum by the PROFECO.
