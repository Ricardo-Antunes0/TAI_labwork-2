Victor Haim Perera (1934 - 14 June 2017) was a Sri Lankan writer, poet and literary critic. He was the author of sixteen books, including five volumes of poetry, five novels and a collection of short stories. He has also published a number of critical essays and articles on literature and culture.  Born in 1934 in Kandana, a small town in the central highlands of Sri Lanka, Perera began writing at an early age. After completing his education at the Royal College, Colombo, he worked as a teacher before embarking on a literary career in the early 1970s. His first book, a collection of poetry titled "Bella", was published in 1973.  Perera's novels explore the themes of love, life and death. He has been widely recognised for his lyrical writing style and for his ability to capture the essence of Sri Lankan culture and history. He died on 14 June 2017 after a prolonged illness.
