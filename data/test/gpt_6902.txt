Sir Jean de Carrouges IV (c. 1330s-1410s) was a French nobleman and soldier. He served King John II of France, and was appointed Constable of France in 1391 and Governor of Languedoc in 1393. He is sometimes referred to as Jean de Carrouges the Elder.  Sir Jean de Carrouges IV was born in about 1330, the son of Sir Jean de Carrouges III and Jeanne d'Arc. He served King John II of France, and was appointed Constable of France in 1391 and Governor of Languedoc in 1393. He is sometimes referred to as Jean de Carrouges the Elder.  Jean de Carrouges died in 1410, at the age of about seventy-five.
