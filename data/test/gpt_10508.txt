Sturm und Drang (, ; usually translated as "storm and stress", "tumult and passion"), also known as the German Romantic movement, was a cultural and artistic movement in Germany in the late 18th and early 19th centuries. It began around 1790 and ended around 1830.  The name Sturm und Drang is German for "storm and stress", and is often translated as "tumult and passion". The movement is usually understood to refer to a specific group of writers, composers, artists, and thinkers who were influenced by the French Revolution, its ideals of liberty, equality, and fraternity, as well as its violence. They sought to overturn traditional values and institutions, and to create a new German culture that would be based on emotion and feeling rather than reason or restraint.  Most members of the Sturm und Drang movement were young men who were affected by the French Revolution in different ways. They were inspired by the new ideas of liberty, equality, and fraternity, as well as by the violence of the Revolution.
