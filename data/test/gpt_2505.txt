William Earl "Smoke" Blanchard (March 3, 1915 – July 21, 1991) was an American professional basketball player. He played in the National Basketball Association (NBA) for the Minneapolis Lakers from 1941 to 1946 and the Baltimore Bullets from 1947 to 1953. He was also a member of the United States men's national basketball team that competed in the 1948 Summer Olympics, and the 1952 Summer Olympics.  Blanchard played collegiately at the University of Kansas, where he helped lead the Jayhawks to a national title in 1936. He was also a member of the United States military during World War II, playing for the Army Black Knights during the 1944–45 season. After his playing career ended, Blanchard served as an NBA referee from 1954 to 1961.
