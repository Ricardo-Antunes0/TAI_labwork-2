Alexander "Sasha" Zhiroff (born April 13, 1951) is an American jazz bassist. Born in Brooklyn, Zhiroff began playing the bass at age 9. He attended the New School for Social Research in New York City and then studied at the Berklee College of Music. He has worked with many notable jazz musicians, including Miles Davis, John Coltrane, Bill Evans, Tony Williams, and Gary Burton.  Zhiroff has recorded more than 20 albums as a soloist and with various groups, and has won numerous awards including the Down Beat Jazz Award for Bassist of the Year in 1982, 1986 and 1992. In 2004 he was awarded the National Medal of Arts by President George W. Bush.
