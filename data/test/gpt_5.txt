Bridges is best known for his development of the first portable timepiece, the Balance Clock. The Balance Clock was a vibrating weight balance clock that could be carried around and used to time events. It was first demonstrated in 1733 and patented in 1734. The Balance Clock became popular and was used by many people throughout the world.  Henry Bridges died in London on February 5, 1754. He was 72 years old.
