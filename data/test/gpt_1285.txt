Richard Preston Butler Jr. (born December 3, 1974), better known by his stage name Rico Love, is an American rapper and actor. He is the half-brother of American singer Usher.  Rico Love was born in Dallas, Texas, to Richard Preston Butler Jr. and Debra Lewis. He has a half-brother, Usher Raymond IV. Love grew up in Chattanooga, Tennessee, and was educated at the private St. John's School. At the age of 15, he moved to Atlanta to pursue a musical career.  Love began his music career as a member of the rap group 3rd Gear, with whom he released one album in 1997. The following year, he formed the duo Playaz Circle with fellow Atlanta-based rapper T.I.. The duo released two albums before disbanding in 2001. Love then began to focus on his acting career, appearing in several films and television shows between 2001 and 2007. In 2009, he reunited with Playaz Circle for the group's first album in six years. That same year, he also released his debut solo album, "The Movement", which peaked at number one on the "Billboard" Top Rap Albums chart.   In 2010, Love appeared on Drake's song "Hold On We're Going Home" and contributed vocals to the song's remix featuring Lil Wayne and Rick Ross. In 2011, he appeared on Gucci Mane's single "Laid Low".
