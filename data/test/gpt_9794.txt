Peggy Antonio (2 June 1917 – 11 January 1992) was a French actress.  Born in Paris to a Spanish father and an Italian mother, Peggy Antonio began her performing career in the 1940s. She became a leading lady in French films in the 1950s and 1960s, appearing in such classics as Marcel Pagnol's "L'École des femmes" (1948), Jean-Luc Godard's "Pierrot le fou" (1965), and Louis Malle's "Le Mur" (1967). She also appeared in a number of American films, including "The Nun's Story" (1959), "The Birdcage" (1996), and Woody Allen's "Melvin and Howard" (2009).  Antonio was nominated for the César Award for Best Actress for her role in Louis Malle's "Le Mur", and she won the award for her performance in Marcel Pagnol's "L'École des femmes". She also received nominations for the Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Motion Picture Drama and the BAFTA Award for Best Actress in a Leading Role. Her other film credits include "Les Enfants du Paradis" (1945), "Quai des Orfèvres" (1947), "Trois Couleurs: Rouge" (1964), and "Les Amants du Pont-Neuf" (1980).  Antonio died of ovarian cancer at age 74.
