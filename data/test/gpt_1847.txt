Robert T. Tranquillo is an American bioengineer, who is the co-inventor of the Tranquillo Method, a cardiac resynchronization therapy (CRT) technology.  Robert Tranquillo was born in Buffalo, New York, in 1958. He earned a B.S. in biochemistry from the State University of New York at Buffalo in 1982 and an M.D. from the University of Rochester School of Medicine and Dentistry in 1986. He completed his residency at Brigham and Women's Hospital in Boston in 1990.  Tranquillo is a clinical associate professor of medicine at Johns Hopkins University School of Medicine and a member of the Institute for Clinical and Translational Sciences at Johns Hopkins Bayview Medical Center. He is also the director of the Cardiac Resynchronization Therapy Program at Johns Hopkins Hospital.  Tranquillo has published more than 200 peer-reviewed scientific articles, as well as book chapters on CRT and heart disease. He was awarded the American Heart Association's 2017 Scientific Achievement Award for his work on CRT.
