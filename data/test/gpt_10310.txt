Jiří Čart (German: Georg Czarth, Zarth, Czard, Kart; Czech: Jiří Čar; Slovak: Jaroslav Čar) (born 1952) is a Czech artist, best known for his video, performance and installation art.  Čart was born in Prague, Czechoslovakia, in 1952. He studied at the Academy of Fine Arts, Prague from 1973 to 1978.   In 1978 he moved to West Berlin, Germany, where he continued his education at the Hochschule der Künste (University of the Arts). He completed his studies in 1982 with a Diploma in Fine Arts. In 1985 he immigrated to New York City, becoming a U.S. citizen in 1992.   Since 1990 Čart has been residing in Cologne, Germany. He has exhibited extensively throughout the world, including solo exhibitions at the Museum of Modern Art (New York), the Guggenheim Museum (New York), the Fondation Cartier pour l’Art Contemporain (Paris), the Whitney Museum of American Art (New York), Kunstmuseum Basel (Switzerland), Centre Pompidou-Metz (France), and ZKM Center for Art and Media (Cologne).
