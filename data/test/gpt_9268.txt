He also served in the Massachusetts House of Representatives from 1979 to 1983.  Tuttle was born in Boston, Massachusetts and attended Boston Latin School. He then attended Harvard College, where he was a member of the Harvards Wicca Club. After completing his undergraduate studies, he went on to study law at Yale Law School, where he was a member of the Yale Law Journal. He subsequently became a law clerk to Justice Arthur Goldberg of the United States Supreme Court.  Tuttle began his career as an associate lawyer with the law firm of Sullivan & Cromwell. He then served as the Deputy Attorney General for Administration for the Commonwealth of Massachusetts from 1978 to 1979. In 1979, he was elected to the Massachusetts House of Representatives, where he served until 1983. During his time in the House, he was Chair of the Joint Committee on Public Safety and Homeland Security and a member of the Committee on Ways and Means, the Committee on Judiciary, and the Joint Committee on Printing.
