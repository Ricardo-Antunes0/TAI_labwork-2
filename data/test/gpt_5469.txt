Gregory K. Beale (born 1949 in Dallas, Texas) is an American economist and author. He is currently a senior fellow at the Cato Institute, where he has written extensively on economic policy.  Beale received his B.Sc. in economics from the University of California, Berkeley in 1971 and his M.A. and Ph.D. in economics from Yale University in 1974 and 1977, respectively.  He has been a professor of economics at George Mason University since 1977, where he also served as associate dean for academic affairs from 1989 to 1992 and dean of the faculty from 1992 to 1997. He was named a Distinguished Professor at George Mason in 2000.  Beale is the author or co-author of more than 20 books and 300 scholarly articles on a variety of economic topics, including taxation, monetary policy, trade policy, and industrial organization. He has testified before Congress on economic issues numerous times, most notably on the 2002 Bush tax cuts.  Beale has been a critic of supply-side economics and libertarianism in general, arguing that they are ineffective at achieving their purported goals and that they contribute to economic inequality.
