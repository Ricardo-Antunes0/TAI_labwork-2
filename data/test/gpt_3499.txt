Kenneth Mason Easley Jr. (born January 15, 1969) is an American former professional basketball player who played in the National Basketball Association (NBA). He played most of his career with the Orlando Magic, with whom he won an NBA championship in 1996.  Easley was born in Mobile, Alabama and attended Jerry L. Jones High School. He played college basketball for the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, where he was a two-time All-American and led the Tar Heels to the Final Four in 1991. The Orlando Magic selected Easley with the third overall pick in the 1991 NBA draft.  Easley played for six seasons with Orlando, averaging 19.7 points, 6.1 rebounds and 2.3 assists per game. In 1996, he helped lead the Magic to their first championship by averaging 18.4 points, 7.4 rebounds and 3.2 assists per game in 78 regular season games before being named the Finals MVP. After his retirement as a player, Easley served as an assistant coach for the Orlando Magic from 1998 to 2001 and was head coach from 2003 to 2006.
