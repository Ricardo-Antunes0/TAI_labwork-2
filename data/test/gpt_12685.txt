Tauranga  is a coastal metropolitan city in the Bay of Plenty Region of New Zealand. It is the largest city on the Bay of Plenty coast and the third most populous city in the country. At the 2006 census, Tauranga had a population of 161,000. The metropolitan area has a population of 285,000.  Tauranga is situated on the banks of the Tauranga River, at the southern end of the Bay of Plenty. The city is served by Tauranga Airport, which is located about 10 kilometres to the east of the city centre. The city has a well-developed transport network, including bus and rail services.  The main economic activity in Tauranga is manufacturing, particularly food processing and textiles. Other major industries include tourism, education, and health care. The city has a diverse cultural scene, with both indigenous and international organisations present. Tauranga has been ranked as one of the top ten most liveable cities in New Zealand by Monocle magazine.
