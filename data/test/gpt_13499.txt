Ratón Pass is a 7,834 ft (2,388 m) elevation pass in the Santa Catalina Mountains, on the border of Arizona and New Mexico. It connects the Santa Rita Ranger District of the Santa Rita Mountains National Monument with the Gila National Forest.   The pass is a popular tourist destination, as it offers magnificent views of the Chiricahua Mountains, San Francisco Peaks, and White Sands National Monument. The road through Ratón Pass is a two-lane paved road that is open to vehicular traffic from April to October. Winter weather can close the pass until late March or early April.   From Ratón Pass, motorists can access many recreation opportunities in the Santa Rita Mountains National Monument and Gila National Forest, including camping, hiking, mountain biking, and horseback riding.
