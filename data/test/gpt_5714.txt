Courtfield, Welsh Bicknor, Herefordshire, England is a small village situated on the River Monnow, about 5 miles south east of Monmouth. The village has a population of about 200 people and is in the ecclesiastical parish of Welsh Bicknor.  The name Courtfield is thought to derive from the Old English word 'courte' meaning a court or courtyard, and 'feld' meaning a field. The village likely grew up around an old court or manor house. The present Church of England parish church was built in 1837, and is a grade II* listed building.  The village lies within the Brecon Beacons National Park and is close to the National Forest and the Brecon Beacons National Park Authority offices. The village has a small pub, the White Hart Inn, which serves food and drinks. There is also a post office, library, hair salon and two small businesses.  Courtfield is home to Welsh Bicknor Cricket Club which plays in the Monmouthshire Cricket League. The club was formed in 1887 and has over 350 members.
