Van Rocks are very conspicuous pinnacle rocks located on the east side of the Van Houten Mountains, about 10 kilometers southeast of Cimarron, New Mexico. The Van Rocks are accessible by a well-maintained trail from the east side of the mountains.  The Van Rocks are composed of Precambrian rocks that were affected by erosion by the Rio Grande and its tributaries. The Van Rocks are composed of sandstone, shale, and limestone. They are about 150 feet high and have a base diameter of about 50 feet.
