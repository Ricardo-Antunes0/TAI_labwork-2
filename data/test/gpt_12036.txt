Without Orders is a 1936 American drama film directed by Edward Dmytryk and starring Gary Cooper, Loretta Young, and Edward G. Robinson. The screenplay was written by John M. Stahl and Lewis Milestone.  The film is set in the coal mines of West Virginia during the Great Depression. The story follows the struggles of a group of miners who resist management's attempt to impose discipline on them.   The film was well received by critics, and has been cited as an influential work in the development of the American film noir genre. It was nominated for four Academy Awards, including Best Picture and Best Director, but won only for Robinson's performance.
