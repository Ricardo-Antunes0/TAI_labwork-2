Boldre is a village and civil parish in the borough of Chichester in West Sussex, England. The population of the civil parish at the 2011 census was 211.  The village is located on the A27 road, six miles (10 km) west of Chichester and two miles (3 km) south-west of the town of Haywards Heath. The parish includes the hamlet of East Boldre and the isolated farmsteads of West Boldre and East Boldre.  The village has a small primary school and a church, both dedicated to Saint Michael. The parish also includes a public house, the Boldre Inn.  The Boldre Valley, a Site of Special Scientific Interest, runs through the parish. It is characterised by broad meadows crossed by deep ravines, and is home to a large number of rare plants and animals.
