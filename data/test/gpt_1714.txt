Damien Richard Martyn (born 21 October 1971) is an English singer, songwriter and record producer. He was the lead singer and main songwriter for the band The Libertines from 1998 to 2015. At the age of 35, he released his first solo album, Hesitation Marks, to critical acclaim.   Damien Martyn was born in Barnsley, South Yorkshire, England on 21 October 1971. He met Carl Barat and Pete Doherty while they were both attending art school in London. The three formed a band, The Libertines, in 1997 and released their debut album, Libertines, in 1999. The album was certified Silver by the British Phonographic Industry (BPI) for sales of over 50,000 copies in the United Kingdom. The band's second album, Fear of Life (2002), peaked at number two on the UK Albums Chart and spawned four UK Singles Chart top 10 hits: "Can't Stand Me Now", "Time for Mercy", "The Last Great American Rock 'n' Roll Dream" and "Street Spirit (Fade Out)".   After a hiatus from the band following the release of their fourth album, 2004's Antichrist Superstar, The Libertines reunited in 2009 and released their fifth album, Up the Bracket.
