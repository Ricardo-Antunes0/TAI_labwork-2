Rhapsody in White is the first studio album by American rock band Queen. It was released on August 27, 1975, by EMI Records.  The album was recorded over a period of two months at the Queen studio in London and was produced by Roy Thomas Baker. It peaked at number two on the UK Albums Chart and spawned four successful singles: "We Will Rock You", "Queenie Eye", "The Show Must Go On" and "We Are the Champions". The album has been certified 23x Platinum by the British Phonographic Industry (BPI) and has sold over 30 million copies worldwide.  "Rhapsody in White" is considered one of the defining albums of the 1970s rock genre, and was praised by music critics for its strong musical composition, energetic performances, and Queen's innovative use of sound effects and effects editing. The album has been cited as an influence by numerous bands and artists, including Metallica, Aerosmith, Guns N' Roses, Red Hot Chili Peppers, Iron Maiden, and Green Day.  ==Background==   Originally titled "Queen", the band's first album was recorded in late 1974 at their home studio in Shepherds Bush, London with producer Roy Thomas Baker. The group had recently completed their first UK tour and were looking to capitalize on their newfound popularity.
