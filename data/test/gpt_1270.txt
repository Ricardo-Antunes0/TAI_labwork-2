Antoine Krilone Kombouaré  (born 16 November 1965 in Aix-en-Provence) is a French football manager who is currently the manager of the Al-Jazeera team.  Kombouaré was formerly the head coach of Marseille from 2004 to 2008. He also managed Olympique de Marseille from 2009 to 2013, and Süper Lig side Fenerbahçe in 2014.  Kombouaré was born in Aix-en-Provence, France on 16 November 1965. He started his coaching career at AS Cannes in 1997, before moving to Marseille in 2004. He spent three years at the helm of Les Phocéens, leading them to two Ligue 1 titles and a UEFA Champions League berth in his final year.  Kombouaré was appointed manager of Al Jazira on 2 June 2014, succeeding Frenchman Rudi Garcia. In his debut season in charge, he led the club to a fourth-place finish in the UAE Arabian Gulf League, as well as qualification for the 2015 AFC Champions League. Kombouaré was sacked by Jazira on 9 October 2015 with the club bottom of the league table, three points from safety.
