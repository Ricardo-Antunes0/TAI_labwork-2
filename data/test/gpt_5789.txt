Lophira lanceolata, commonly known as the dwarf palm, is a palm native to the Lesser Antilles and the southern ranges of Central America.  The dwarf palm is a small, slow-growing palm that reaches a height of only 18 to 30 inches. The leaves are arranged in a rosette at the top of the stem, and the leaflets are arranged in pairs. The fruit is a small, brownish-white coconut.
