William Laws Calley Jr. (born June 8, 1944) is an American soldier who was convicted of the premeditated murder of 20 Vietnamese civilians during the Vietnam War. He was sentenced to life in prison, with no possibility of parole.  William Calley Jr. was born in Buffalo, New York, on June 8, 1944. He enlisted in the U.S. Army in November 1961 and was sent to Vietnam in March 1965. While serving as a squad leader in Company A, 3rd Battalion, 10th Marines, 3rd Marine Division, Calley participated in the My Lai massacre, which occurred on March 16, 1968. During the massacre, Calley and his men killed approximately 100 unarmed Vietnamese civilians - including women and children - in a Buddhist pagoda.  After the massacre, Calley and his men fled the scene of the crime and hid for hours before returning to their unit. Calley was subsequently charged with premeditated murder and found guilty by a U.S. military tribunal on October 24, 1971. He was sentenced to life in prison without parole.
