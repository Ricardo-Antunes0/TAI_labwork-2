Lincoln (formally the Lincoln Motor Company) is an American automobile manufacturer that was founded in 1912 by Henry Leland and William C. Durant. The company was purchased by Ford Motor Company in 1922, and passed through several other owners before being merged into Ford in 1955. Lincoln has been the subject of several films, including the eponymous 1947 film starring Marlon Brando and James Stewart, and the 2012 film directed by Daniel Day-Lewis.   Lincoln vehicles are known for their luxury, durability, and style. The company has produced various models over its history, including the Mark VIII, Continental Mark V, MKZ, Navigator, MKT, Zephyr, and the recently discontinued MKC. Lincoln produces luxury cars including the Lincoln LS, Continental Mark LT, and Navigator.
