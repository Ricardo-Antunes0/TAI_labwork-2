Muhammad Siddique Musafir (1 April 1879 - 13 January 1957) was an Indian independence activist and a leader of the Pakistan movement. Musafir was also a writer, poet, and playwright.  Muhammad Siddique Musafir was born in the town of Muzaffargarh in Punjab, British India on 1 April 1879. He studied at Government College, Lahore and attained an LLB degree from the University of London in 1903. In 1906, he married Tahira Banu and they had four children.  Musafir participated in the Indian independence movement from its early days. He was imprisoned several times for his activities, including a stint of eleven years in Cellular Jail during British rule in India. He served as president of the All-India Muslim League from 1930 to 1941 and as its chairman from 1940 to 1941. He also served as the first Prime Minister of Pakistan from August 1947 until his resignation in February 1948. He was awarded the prestigious Bharat Ratna award in 1954. Musafir died on 13 January 1957.
