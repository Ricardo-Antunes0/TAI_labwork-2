Gheorghe Vrănceanu (June 30, 1900 – April 4, 1978) was a Romanian author, playwright, and political activist. He is best known for his 1944 novel The Catcher in the Rye, which has been widely acclaimed as one of the finest novels of the 20th century.  Born in Iaşi, Vrănceanu studied jurisprudence at the University of Bucharest before working as a lawyer. He began writing in the 1940s, publishing his first novel, The Catcher in the Rye, in 1944. His other works include The Death of Mr. László (1951), The Notebook of Ionesco (1958), and Crimes and Punishments (1968). Vrănceanu was a prominent member of the Romanian Communist Party and served as its general secretary from 1952 to 1956. He was also a member of the French Communist Party and the European Parliament. He died of cancer in 1978.
