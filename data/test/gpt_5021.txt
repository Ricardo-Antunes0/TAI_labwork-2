Leprous is a rock band from Notodden, Norway, formed in 2001. Leprous is known for its unique and intricate progressive metal style. The band's latest release is the studio album The Quantum Enigma (2018).  Leprous was formed in 2001 by guitarist/vocalist Henrik Krueger, drummer Jarle Stene, and bassist Espen Aarseth. The band released its debut album, self-titled Leprous, in 2002. The band's second album, The Congregation of the Damned, was released in 2004 and featured the singles "The Great Below" and "The Unforgiving." Leprous' third album, Erebus, was released in 2006 and featured the singles "A Path Not Taken" and "The Architect." Leprous' fourth album, Malice, was released in 2009 and featured the singles "Into the Labyrinth" and "Nemo." Leprous' fifth album, Arcanum Opus, was released in 2012 and featured the singles "Eternity" and "The Veil of Maya." Leprous' sixth album, The Quantum Enigma, was released in 2018.
