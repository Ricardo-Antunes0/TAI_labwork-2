He is most notable for his 1961 single, "It's All Over Now", which reached number one on the "Billboard" Hot 100 chart.  Lenny Lyles was born in Memphis, Tennessee, United States. He began singing in church at the age of six and later sang with a local gospel quartet. In 1958, he recorded his first single, "I'll Fly Away". The following year, he signed with Federal Records and recorded his first album, "Lenny Lyles". The album was not successful and Lyles left Federal Records in 1961.  Lyles then signed with Vee-Jay Records and recorded the song "It's All Over Now". The single reached number one on the "Billboard" Hot 100 chart in 1961 and became Lyles' biggest success. Lyles toured extensively and appeared on television shows such as "The Ed Sullivan Show". He died in 1967 at the age of 27 from an accidental gunshot wound.
