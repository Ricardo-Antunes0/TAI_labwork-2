Lisa Dixon is a Professor of Psychiatry at the University of California, San Francisco. She is a Fellow of the American Psychiatric Association and the California Psychiatric Association.  Dixon's research focuses on anxiety, mood disorders, and addiction. She has authored or coauthored more than 150 journal articles and book chapters, and has been cited more than 3,000 times.  Dixon is also a clinical researcher, conducting studies on the efficacy of medications and treatments for anxiety, mood disorders, and addiction. She has served on the boards of directors for several mental health organizations, and is currently president-elect of the Society for Neuroscience.  Dixon was born in Memphis, Tennessee in 1965. She received her undergraduate degree from Vanderbilt University in 1987 and her medical degree from Harvard Medical School in 1991. She completed her residency in psychiatry at Yale University School of Medicine in 1995. After completing her residency, Dixon joined the faculty at UCSF as an assistant professor in 1995. In 2003 she was promoted to associate professor and then professor in 2007.
