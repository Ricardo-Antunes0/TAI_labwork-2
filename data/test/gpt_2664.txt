High School Caesar is a 1960 American black and white comedy film directed by Mervyn LeRoy and written by Howard Koch, who also stars in the title role. The film tells the story of a high school student who assumes the role of dictator after his school is taken over by a gang of bullies.  High School Caesar was released on December 25, 1960, by Columbia Pictures. It was nominated for two Academy Awards, Best Actor in a Leading Role (Howard Koch) and Best Writing, Screenplay Based on Material from Another Medium.  The film has been cited as an influence on the political comedy films of the 1980s and 1990s.
