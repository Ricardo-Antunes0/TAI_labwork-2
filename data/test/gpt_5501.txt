Christiane Herzog (née Krauss; (26 October 1936 – 24 October 2017) was a German opera singer. She had a wide vocal range, from mezzo-soprano to soprano, and was particularly renowned for her interpretations of the works of Wagner.  Christiane Herzog was born on 26 October 1936 in Berlin, Germany. She studied at the Hochschule für Musik in Berlin, graduating in 1960. Herzog made her operatic debut at the Staatstheater Kassel as Sieglinde in the 1965 production of Richard Wagner's Die Walküre. She appeared at major opera houses throughout Europe and North America before retiring from the stage in 2000.  Herzog died on 24 October 2017, aged 80.
