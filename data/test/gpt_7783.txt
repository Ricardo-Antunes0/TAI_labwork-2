Gino N. Jennings (born February 10, 1963) is an American former professional baseball player who was a relief pitcher in Major League Baseball (MLB) for the Philadelphia Phillies, Chicago Cubs, and Kansas City Royals. He played college baseball at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA).  Jennings was signed by the Phillies as an undrafted free agent in 1985. He made his MLB debut with the Phillies in 1988. He pitched in the majors for 13 seasons, spending most of his time with the Phillies, where he won two World Series championships (1993 and 1995). He also played for the Cubs and Royals. In 2001, Jennings was diagnosed with multiple myeloma, which caused him to miss most of the season. He retired from baseball after the 2002 season.
