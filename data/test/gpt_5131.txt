Andre Romelle Young (born February 18, 1965), better known by his stage name Dr. Dre, is an American rapper, record producer, and entrepreneur. After starting as a DJ for the hip hop group N.W.A, Dre became one of the founding members of the hip hop group Death Row Records in 1991. He has since released several solo albums and collaborated with other artists. In 1995, Dre was involved in a highly publicized feud with Tupac Shakur after allegations of sexual assault were made against him. He has since released two more albums and has been largely retired from public appearances.  Dr. Dre's musical style has been described as hard rock, funk, soul, and rap. He is credited with pioneering the subgenre west coast gangsta rap, and has influenced artists such as Eminem, Snoop Dogg, Kanye West, and 50 Cent. In 2006, he received a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.
