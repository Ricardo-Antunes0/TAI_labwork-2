Monte Circeo or Cape Circeo  is a mountain located in the province of Campobasso, region of Molise, Italy. With an elevation of 1,846 metres (6,098 feet), it is the highest peak in the Apennines.   The mountain's name is derived from the goddess Circe, who was said to have lived there in antiquity. The summit of Monte Circeo is a popular tourist destination for hikers and climbers.
