Joshua Allen Cain, (born July 31, 1976) is an American singer, songwriter and musician. He is the lead singer and guitarist of the rock band The Used, who have released five studio albums.  Cain was born in Huntington Beach, California. He has a younger brother, Shane, who is also a musician. Cain grew up listening to punk rock and alternative music, and began writing songs at the age of 15. He started his own band, Dead End Road, in 1996 with high school friends. The band released two albums before disbanding in 2000.  Cain joined The Used in 2002. The band signed to Atlantic Records and released their debut album, The Used, in 2003. The album reached number nine on the US Billboard 200 chart and was certified platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA). After touring in support of the album, Cain released a solo album, Now You're Gone... Forever, in 2004.  The Used released their second album, Believe Me, in 2005. The album reached number one on the US Billboard 200 chart and was certified double platinum by the RIAA. The album spawned four singles: "The Underground", "Chaos", "The Outsider", and "In Love with a Ghost". Cain wrote or co-wrote all of the songs on Believe Me.  The Used released their third album, Conspiracy Theory, in 2007. The album reached number three on the US Billboard 200 chart and was certified triple platinum by the RIAA.
