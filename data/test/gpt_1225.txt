Edward Murray East (October 4, 1879 – October 25, 1951) was an American psychologist and educator who was the first Dean of the School of Education at the University of Wisconsin–Madison.  Edward Murray East was born on October 4, 1879 in Milwaukee, Wisconsin. He received his bachelor's degree from the University of Wisconsin–Madison in 1902 and his doctorate of education from Harvard University in 1906. East began his career as a professor of psychology at the University of Wisconsin–Madison in 1907. He served as the school's first dean from 1923 to 1927. East later served as president of Midwestern State Teachers College in Wichita, Kansas from 1931 to 1934. He died on October 25, 1951 in Madison, Wisconsin.
