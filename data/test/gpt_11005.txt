Brigadier James Hargest,  (4 September 1891 – 24 January 1962) was a senior Australian Army officer who served in World War II and the Korean War. He is best known for his command of the 3rd Battalion, Royal Australian Regiment during the Battle of Buna–Gona.  James Hargest was born on 4 September 1891 in Wagga Wagga, New South Wales, to James and Annie Hargest. He attended Wagga Wagga High School and then the University of Sydney, where he studied law.  Hargest joined the Australian Military Forces in 1911, serving with the 5th Infantry Brigade in Egypt and then with the 2nd Australian Infantry Brigade in France during World War I. Promoted to lieutenant colonel in 1920, he served as second-in-command of the 5th Infantry Brigade from 1921 to 1922. In 1923 he was posted to London as military attaché to the British Ministry of Defence.   Hargest returned to Australia in 1925 and was appointed commander of the 3rd Battalion, Royal Australian Regiment. He served with the battalion during the 1926 Malayan Emergency and during the Second World War fought in New Guinea, where he was promoted to brigadier in 1941. After serving as commander of III Corps from 1943 to 1944, Hargest was given command of the 3rd Division, which he led during Operation Lilliput in 1945. He retired from the army in 1948 after 36 years of service.   Hargest died on 24 January 1962 at Canberra
