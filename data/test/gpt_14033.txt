Chesterville is an unincorporated community in eastern Travis County, Texas, United States. It is located along the eastern shore of Lake Travis, about four miles north of State Highway 183 and eight miles northeast of Austin.  Chesterville was founded in 1871 by a group of settlers who named the community for Chesterton, England. The community grew rapidly in the early days, with a population of over 900 by 1900. However, the Great Depression and World War II slowed growth and by 1950 the population had fallen to only 260. However, by 1990 the population had increased to 1,500 and has remained relatively stable since then.  Today, Chesterville is a small community with a few businesses and homes.
