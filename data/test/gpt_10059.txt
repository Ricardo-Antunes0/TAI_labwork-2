The Winding Gulf is a long tributary of the Mississippi River that flows through the states of Mississippi and Louisiana. The gulf is named for its winding course. The river begins in the White Mountains of eastern Alabama, and flows southward through Mississippi, where it joins the Mississippi River at Vicksburg. Downstream from Vicksburg, the Winding Gulf flows through Louisiana before emptying into the Mississippi near Gulfport. The gulf is an important waterway for shipping, and its estuary forms part of the Gulf Coast region of the United States.
