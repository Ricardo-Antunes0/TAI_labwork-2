Cedric Constantine Myton (born 1947) is a British academic and writer who specialises in the history of colonialism.  Cedric Myton was born in 1947 in Trinidad and Tobago. He attended Queen's University, Kingston, Jamaica, where he received his undergraduate degree in English Literature in 1969. Myton then attended the University of Cambridge, where he received his doctorate in 1977 for a thesis on the history of colonialism.  After completing his doctoral studies, Myton taught at the University of Toronto from 1977 to 1979 and at Yale University from 1979 to 1984. He then moved to the University of Cambridge, where he has since been a professor of colonial history. He has also served as the director of the Centre for Historical Research on Africa at Cambridge and as the editor-in-chief of the journal "History". In 2007, he was elected a Fellow of the British Academy.  Myton's writings focus largely on the history of colonialism and its aftermath. He has written several books on the subject, including "The White Man's Burden: European Colonialism in Africa" (1998), "The Loss of Empire: British Responses to decolonization" (2002), and "Colonialism and Its Aftermath: The British Experience" (2005). His latest book is "Empire: The History of an Idea" (2013).
