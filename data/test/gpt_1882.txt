These Old Broads is a 2001 American romantic comedy film directed by Peter Bogdanovich and written by Leslie Dixon. The film stars Bette Midler, Goldie Hawn, and Diane Keaton.  The film tells the story of a group of women who are friends since childhood and who reunite for a week-long cruise in the Caribbean. Midler plays Ruth, a faded music legend who has been reduced to working at a retirement home. Hawn plays Peggy, Ruth's daughter and the owner of a successful real estate business. Keaton plays Ruth's ex-wife, Bonnie, who is now married to a wealthy Republican.   The film received mixed reviews from critics but was a box office success, grossing over $130 million worldwide.
