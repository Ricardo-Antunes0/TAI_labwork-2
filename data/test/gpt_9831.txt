Born in New York City, Mandelman earned a B.A. from Barnard College in 1934 and an M.A. from Columbia University in 1937. She taught at Hunter College from 1944 to 1973, where she was named a full professor in 1965. She served as chairman of the History Department from 1974 to 1978.  Mandelman's work focused on the history of women in the United States, particularly their roles in the labor movement and in politics. Her book "Women of the Left: A History of American Feminists" (1976) was a groundbreaking work that won her a Guggenheim Fellowship in 1977. She also wrote "The Female Problem: An Historical Analysis" (1958), "The American Woman: A History" (1975), and "Revolution at Home: The Origins of the Women's Rights Movement in America" (1988).  Mandelman was awarded a Guggenheim Fellowship in 1977, a National Humanities Medal in 1985, and an honorary doctorate from Suffolk University in 1990. In 1992, she was inducted into the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.
