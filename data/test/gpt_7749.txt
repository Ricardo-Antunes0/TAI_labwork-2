Ashish Bhasin is a globally recognised media entrepreneur and investor. He is the Founding Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of Excalibur Ventures, a venture capital firm.  Bhasin has a proven track record in building successful media businesses. He founded SBWI, a digital media company, in 2000, which was acquired by AOL in 2001. Following the acquisition, Bhasin served as Vice President of Interactive Media for AOL North America. He then founded 3ABN Inc., a multi-platform media company, in 2003. The company was acquired by Clear Channel Communications in 2006.  Bhasin also has significant experience investing in early stage technology companies. He is the Founder and Managing Partner of Early Stage Partners, a venture capital firm that has invested in more than 50 startups.  Bhasin is also an active philanthropist. He is the Co-founder and Chairman of the Ashish Bhasin Foundation, which provides charitable support to organizations that address social justice issues.  Bhasin is a widely respected figure in the media industry. He has been named one of the Most Influential People in Digital Media by Crain's Chicago Business and one of the Top 25 Most Powerful People in Digital Media by Fast Company magazine.  Ashish Bhasin was born on December 23, 1966, in Mumbai, India. He holds a bachelor's degree from Brown University and an MBA from the Kellogg School of Management at Northwestern University
