Benjamin Cas Haley (born 1980; Paris, Texas) is an American artist.  Cas Haley was born in Paris, Texas in 1980. He received his undergraduate degree from Baylor University in 2002 and his graduate degree from the University of Houston in 2006. Haley has exhibited his work nationally and internationally, most notably at the Whitney Biennial in 2012 and the Dia Art Foundation in 2014.  Haley's work centres on the idea of memory and its power to shape our identity. His paintings explore the ways we remember and forget history, as well as our place within it. Haley's paintings are often populated by anonymous figures who are caught in transit between past and present, struggling to find an identity or a foothold in a world that is constantly moving forward.
