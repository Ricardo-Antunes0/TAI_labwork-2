He was a major figure in the development of the Las Vegas Strip during the 1960s and 1970s.  Tuttle was born in 1908 in Petaluma, California. He attended Stanford University, graduating with a degree in business in 1931. He then worked for his father's real estate company, Tuttle & Tuttle.  In the 1950s, Tuttle began developing land near Las Vegas. His first project was the Boulevard Hotel and Casino, which he built with partner Benny Binion. In 1961, he founded Magruder Tuttle Properties. The company became one of the largest developers in Las Vegas, responsible for projects such as the Las Vegas Hilton and UNLV's Sam Boyd Stadium. In 1974, Tuttle sold Magruder Tuttle Properties to MGM Grand Hotel and Casino Corporation.  Tuttle died in 1988. He was inducted into the Nevada Gaming Hall of Fame in 1992 and the National Association of Realtors Hall of Fame in 1993.
