Wilhelmina Wendt (8 August 1896 – 26 March 1968) was a German actress and singer who appeared in more than fifty films between 1920 and 1958. She was also a successful concert performer, appearing at major venues around the world. Wendt was born in Braunschweig, Lower Saxony, the daughter of the actor and director Otto Wendt and his wife Friederike (née Wagner). She made her stage debut in 1916 at the Deutsches Theater in Berlin and achieved prominence in 1920 with her performance in the stage production of Robert Wiene's Expressionist film The Cabinet of Dr. Caligari.
