The 597th Bombardment Squadron is an inactive United States Air Force unit. Its last assignment was with the 479th Bombardment Group, stationed at Griffiss Air Force Base, New York.  The squadron was first activated in early 1942 as the 597th Bombardment Squadron (Heavy), one of the original squadrons of the Eighth Air Force. After training in the United States, the squadron deployed to England in August 1942 and participated in the opening phases of Operation Torch, the Allied invasion of North Africa. The squadron later served in the Mediterranean theater, earning a Distinguished Unit Citation for its actions during Operation Husky, the Allied invasion of Sicily. In 1944, it moved to Italy and participated in the Battle of Anzio and Operations Avalanche and Gothic. The 597th later fought in Southern France before returning to England in 1945. After V-E Day, it demobilized and returned to the United States.  The 597th Bombardment Squadron was reactivated as an all-jet squadron at Griffiss AFB on 1 March 1992 as part of a restructuring of USAF strategic bomber forces. It was assigned to the 479th Bombardment Group, which was inactivated on 1 October 1993 when its units were consolidated into a single unit at Griffiss AFB under the 479th Wing.
