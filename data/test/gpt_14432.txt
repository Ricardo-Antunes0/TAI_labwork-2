Alcan Highway is a 2013 documentary film directed by Michael Moore. It examines the history and impact of the Alcan Highway, a 2,400-mile network of roads that connects Canadian and American territories across the Arctic.  The Alcan Highway is a 2,400-mile network of roads that connects Canadian and American territories across the Arctic. The project was begun in the 1930s as a way to transport materials for the construction of the St. Lawrence Seaway. The highway became a symbol of cooperation between Canada and the United States during the Cold War. The film examines the history and impact of the Alcan Highway, as well as its influence on regional economies.
