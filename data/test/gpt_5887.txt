Gandahati waterfall is located on the Mahendratanaya River in the state of West Bengal, India. It is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the region and is world-renowned for its beauty. The waterfall is located at a height of 753 meters and has a total length of 915 meters. The waterfall was first discovered by a local named Sankar Dev in 1827. He named it Gandahati after his home village in West Bengal. The waterfall was first commercially exploited in 1902 when a hydroelectric power plant was built on the Mahendratanaya River.
