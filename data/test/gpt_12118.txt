Christopher Paul Anthony DiStefano (born August 26, 1969) is an American musician, songwriter, and record producer. He is the founder, vocalist and sole permanent member of the rock band Marilyn Manson.  DiStefano was born in West Babylon, New York. He started his music career in the late 1980s as the singer for the glam metal band Dope. In 1991, he formed Marilyn Manson with guitarist Scott Weiland and drummer Twiggy Ramirez. The band's debut album, Portrait of an American Family, was released in 1994 and sold over 900,000 copies. After releasing three more studio albums, Manson broke up in 1998. DiStefano later formed the band Hella with former Guns N' Roses guitarist Izzy Stradlin and bassist Rob Caggiano. They released one album in 2002 before disbanding.  Since Marilyn Manson's reunion in 2012, DiStefano has released two albums with the band: Born Villain (2013) and Holy Wood (2015). He also produced the albums of Weiland's new project The Wildabouts (2016) and Eagles of Death Metal's latest album Death of a Bachelor (2018).
