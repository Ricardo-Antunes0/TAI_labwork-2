Marc Trachtenberg (born February 9, 1946) is an American film and television director, and screenwriter. He is best known for directing the films Being John Malkovich, Wonder Boys, and 10 Days in October.  Trachtenberg was born in Newark, New Jersey. He attended Brown University, where he studied mathematics and film. After graduating from Brown, he worked as an editor for Roger Corman's New World Pictures. Trachtenberg made his directorial debut with the 1984 film Being John Malkovich. The film won him the Academy Award for Best Director. Trachtenberg's other directorial efforts include Wonder Boys (2000), 10 Days in October (2002), and A Beautiful Mind (2001).
