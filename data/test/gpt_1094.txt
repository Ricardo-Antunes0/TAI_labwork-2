Huaral is a district in the Peruvian department of Lambayeque. It is located in the north-central part of the valley of the Huaral River, about 80 kilometers from the department capital, Ica. The district has an area of 1,814 square kilometers and a population of 116,521 (2005 census).  The district is located at an altitude of 2,000 meters above sea level, in the foothills of the Andes. The climate is temperate and the district has a typical Andean landscape of peaks and valleys. The principal economic activities are agriculture, livestock farming, forestry and mining. There are reserves of silver, gold and other minerals.  The district was founded on July 24, 1861 by Don Manuel García de la Torre y Orellana as a result of a petition presented by Governor Justo José de Urquiza y Santillán to the government of Peru.
