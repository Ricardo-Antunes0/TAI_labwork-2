The Tax Foundation is a Washington, D.C.-based think tank devoted to promoting sound tax policy. The Foundation's research is used by policymakers and the media to inform discussion of tax issues.  The Tax Foundation was founded in 1937 as an independent, nonpartisan research organization. It is the nation's leading source of tax policy information, with a staff of more than 100 analysts and experts.  The Tax Foundation's mission is to promote sound tax policy and to help make informed decisions about taxes. The Foundation's work focuses on five core areas: federal taxation, state and local taxation, international taxation, business taxation, and taxation of wealth.  The Tax Foundation conducts research in a number of formats, including studies of specific tax proposals and analyses of broad tax issues. The Foundation also produces reports and essays on a variety of topics, including federal taxes, state and local taxes, international taxation, business taxation, and taxation of wealth.
