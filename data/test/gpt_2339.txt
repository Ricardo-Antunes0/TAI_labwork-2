Michalis Koussis (Greek: Μιχάλης Κούσης; 10 October 1945 – 15 January 2014) was a Greek singer, songwriter, and actor. He was born in Athens, and died in Athens aged 67.     Koussis initially studied law, but then switched to music, firstly studying voice at the Athens Conservatory. He made his professional debut in 1967 with the group Afti. In 1974 he formed the group Demis Roussos with Vangelis and released two successful albums. His later solo work included collaborations with Yanni, Lenny Kravitz, and Michael Bolton.    Kousis had nine Top 40 hits in Greece and two in Italy, including "To Pagasthana" (1978), which became a popular European hit. He also had success internationally with the songs "Chariots of Fire" (1981) and "My Best Friend" (1992). He was awarded the prestigious Greek Golden Globe Award for Best Male Singer in 1984.
