He was educated at the University of Oxford, where he obtained a Bachelor of Arts degree in 1892 and a Master of Arts degree in 1893. He then studied at the University of Cambridge, where he received a Bachelor of Laws degree in 1896.  Harris began his legal career in 1896 as a clerk to the Supreme Court of New South Wales. He became an associate justice of the Supreme Court of New South Wales in 1903, and a full justice of the court in 1916. He was knighted in 1924.  Harris served as the Chancellor of the University of Sydney from 1927 to 1935. He also served as the President of the Law Society of New South Wales from 1922 to 1923, and as the President of the Australian National University from 1923 to 1934. Harris died on 3 October 1935.
