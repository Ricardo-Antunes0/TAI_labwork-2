Mary Anne Hobbs (born 16 May 1964) is an English actress. She is best known for her roles as Det. Sgt. Annie Cabbot in the BBC One police drama series, Midsomer Murders, and as Dr. Donna Hayward in the Fox mystery series, Bones. Hobbs has also had recurring roles on the BBC sitcom, Miranda, and the NBC drama series, The Blacklist.  Hobbs was born in Coventry, Warwickshire, England. She trained at the Bristol Old Vic Theatre School, graduating in 1986. Hobbs began her stage career in 1987 playing the role of Young Gertrude in a production of Shakespeare's Tragedy of Hamlet at the Royal Court Theatre. She made her television debut in 1991 appearing as a guest star in an episode of the BBC soap opera EastEnders.  Hobbs first appeared as Det. Sgt. Annie Cabbot in the BBC One police drama series, Midsomer Murders, which first aired on 1 November 1997. In 2003, Hobbs was nominated for a BAFTA Award for Best Supporting Actress for her work on Midsomer Murders. She left Midsomer Murders at the end of its tenth season on 25 May 2003 to pursue other acting opportunities.  Hobbs returned to television in 2004 when she played Dr. Donna Hayward in the Fox mystery series, Bones. She remained with Bones until its conclusion on 21 May 2013.
