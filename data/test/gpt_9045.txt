Sir William John McKell  (26 September 1849 – 16 December 1929) was an Australian politician who served as a Senator for New South Wales from 1901 to 1913. McKell was the son of William McKell and his wife Elizabeth (née Smith). He was born in Sydney, New South Wales, and educated at Scotch College, Melbourne. McKell married Louisa Alice Wright on 8 January 1885. He was knighted in 1903 and appointed a Companion of the Order of Australia in 1913. McKell died in Sydney in 1929.   Sir William John McKell was born on 26 September 1849 in Sydney, New South Wales, to William McKell and Elizabeth Smith. After completing his education at Scotch College, Melbourne, McKell worked as a civil engineer before entering politics. He first stood for office at the 1885 NSW state election, winning the seat of Liverpool from the incumbent member, Frederick Holder. McKell was re-elected at the 1886 and 1889 elections, before being appointed to the Senate in 1901 following the death of his father-in-law, Henry Parkes. In 1913 McKell was knighted and appointed a Companion of the Order of Australia for his service as Minister for External Affairs from 1910 to 1913. He died in Sydney on 16 December 1929 aged 81 years.
