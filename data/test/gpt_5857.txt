The Sonata for Violin and Piano in A major, Op. 109, by Antonín Dvořák, was written in 1892 and is one of the most popular and well-known violin sonatas. The work is scored for a solo violin and piano and is composed in three movements:  The first movement, "Allegro moderato", is in the key of A major and is based on a simple theme. The second movement, "Andante", is in D minor and explores the use of repeated notes. The third movement, "Rondo alla turca", is in the key of E-flat major and is characterized by its Turkish rhythms.
