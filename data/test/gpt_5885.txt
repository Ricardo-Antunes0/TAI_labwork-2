"Rush" is the fifth episode of the first season of the American science fiction television series "The X-Files". It was written by Glen Morgan and James Wong, and directed by Vince Gilligan. The episode first aired on the Fox network on October 3, 1993.  The show centers on the investigations of FBI special agents Fox Mulder (David Duchovny) and Dana Scully (Gillian Anderson) who work on cases that cannot be solved by the normal criminal justice system. In this episode, Mulder and Scully investigate a series of murders in which people have their hearts removed.  "Rush" received positive reviews from critics. It has been cited as one of the show's best episodes. The episode was nominated for a Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Special Visual Effects for a Series.  Mulder and Scully investigate a series of murders in which people have their hearts removed. They find that the killer is using a virus to cause heart failure, which then results in the removal of the heart.   The episode ends with Mulder finding out that the killer is his former partner, John Doggett (Douglas Elam). Doggett had been discharged from the military after serving time for murdering a fellow soldier who had raped Doggett's sister. Mulder had been investigating Doggett's involvement in the murders, but had been unable to prove anything against him. Now, Mulder realizes that Doggett is the murderer and tries to catch him before he can kill again
