German submarine U-778 was a Type VIIC U-boat built for Nazi Germany's Kriegsmarine during World War II.  U-778 was laid down on 2 September 1942 at the Flensburger Schiffbau-Gesellschaft yard in Flensburg, Germany, as yard number 927. She was launched on 21 November 1942 and commissioned under the command of "Oberleutnant zur See" Dietrich von Choltitz on 5 February 1943.  After training with the 8th U-boat Flotilla, U-778 sailed from Kiel on her first patrol on 1 May 1943. Her objective was to sink a convoy of ships travelling from North America to the United Kingdom. After a fruitless three-day search, she surfaced and torpedoed the motor vessel SS Empire Harvest off the coast of County Cork, Ireland. The U-boat then sank the ship with all hands.  U-778 next attacked a convoy of five ships travelling from Gibraltar bound for Liverpool on 4 June 1943. She sank the British freighter MV Empire Pride with all hands off the coast of Portugal.  On 22 July 1943, U-778 sank the auxiliary cruiser HMAS Perth off the coast of North Africa with all hands after a 16-hour battle.  U-778 ended her first patrol in early August 1943 by sailing into Lorient, France for repairs.
