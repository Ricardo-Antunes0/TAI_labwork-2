Severe Tropical Cyclone Niran was a very powerful cyclone that affected Bangladesh, India, and Myanmar during the month of January 2014. The storm formed on January 8 near the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, and quickly intensified into a very severe tropical cyclone. Niran made landfall in India on January 10 as a very severe cyclone with winds of 205 mph (330 km/h). Niran weakened as it moved over land, but regained strength over water and became an intense typhoon on January 12 before making landfall again in Myanmar on January 14. Niran dissipated late on January 15 near the Thai-Myanmar border.  Niran was one of the most intense tropical cyclones to affect India in recent memory. The storm caused significant damage across many parts of the country, with a death toll of at least 137 people. In addition, Niran caused widespread power outages and left millions of people homeless. In total, Niran caused $1 billion in damage (2014 USD) and left more than 1 million people homeless.
