He attended the Royal Academy of Fine Arts in Munich, where he studied under Ludwig von Knaus and Friedrich Overbeck. In 1826, he won the prestigious Prix de Rome with a painting entitled "The Martyrdom of Saint Maurice". After his return to Munich, he became instructor at the academy, serving in that capacity for almost fifty years. He also served as director of the National Gallery of Bavaria from 1852 to 1859. Michael Neher is best known for his portraits, but he also produced landscapes and historical scenes.
