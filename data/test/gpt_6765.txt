The South Downs Way is a long distance footpath that runs east-west across the South Downs in England. It is the longest National Park footpath in the UK, and is also one of the most popular. The Way starts at Winchester, passes through Eastbourne, Midhurst, Petworth and Brighton, and ends at Bognor Regis. The Way passes through some of the most beautiful countryside in England, with rolling hills, woodland and rivers. There are plenty of attractions along the Way, including several ancient villages, renowned gardens and castles.
