The McLemore Site, designated by the Smithsonian Institution in 1974, is an archaeological site located near the town of McLemore in southeastern Polk County, Texas. The site is believed to represent the site of a large 18th century Native American village.  The McLemore Site was first surveyed by Alfred V. Kidder in 1924, and again in 1936 by James A. Ford. Its importance was recognized by the Smithsonian Institution in 1974, when it was added to the National Register of Historic Places. The site has been the subject of archaeological investigations by a number of researchers, most notably Alfred V. Kidder, James A. Ford, and Gordon Willey.
