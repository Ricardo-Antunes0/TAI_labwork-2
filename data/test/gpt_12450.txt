Donald Leslie Chipp, AO (21 August 192528 July 2002) was an Australian politician.  Born in Brisbane, Queensland, Chipp was educated at St Joseph's College, Gregory Terrace and the University of Queensland, where he became a law lecturer. He was admitted to the Queensland Bar in 1952 and commenced practice in Brisbane.  Chipp first entered politics as the Liberal member for Oxley in the Queensland Legislative Assembly from 1966 until his retirement in 1992. He served as Deputy Premier and Minister for Agriculture and Fisheries under Joh Bjelke-Petersen from 1975 to 1979 and as Minister for Tourism and Recreation from 1979 to 1982. Chipp was also Leader of the Opposition from 1977 to 1982. In 1992, he was appointed an Officer of the Order of Australia (AO) for services to public administration. Chipp died in 2002 at the age of 78.
