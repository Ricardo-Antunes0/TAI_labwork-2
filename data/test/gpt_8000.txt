Kvamsøy Church  is a historic parish church on the island of Kvamsøy in Møre og Romsdal county, Norway. It is located in the village of Kvamsøy on the eastern shore of the island. The church is part of the Vågsøy parish in the Indre Møre deanery in the Diocese of Møre. The white, wooden church was built between 1848 and 1850 by an unknown architect. The church seats about 230 people.  The first recorded mention of a church on Kvamsøy is from 1186. The present church was built in three stages. The original part of the church was built between 1848 and 1850 by an unknown architect. This section is a three-aisled, wooden structure with a cruciform plan. It is decorated with Jacobus Zschokke's frescoes of "The Last Supper" and "The Ascension". The second section, which was added in 1892, is a two-story, brick building with a gable roof. The third and final section was added in 1954. This section is a one-story, aluminum frame building with a flat roof.
