Philipp Christoph Zeller (8 April 1808 – 7 October 1877) was a German historian, classical philologist, and archaeologist.  Zeller was born in Stuttgart, the son of a bookseller. He studied classics and philology at the Universities of Tübingen and Würzburg, where he was a student of Friedrich Rudolf von Ihering. In 1829 he became an assistant to the Romanist Friedrich Wilhelm Jacobi at the University of Tübingen. In 1833 he became a professor of Greek at the University of Königsberg, where he remained until his retirement in 1862.  He is best known for his work on Homer and Greek history, but he also wrote on Roman history and archaeology. His most famous work is his two volume history of Rome, published between 1854 and 1858. Zeller was a member of the Prussian Academy of Sciences in Berlin from 1855 onwards.  Zeller died in Königsberg.
