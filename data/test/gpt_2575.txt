Josef Zezulka (30 March 1912 – 13 September 2000) was a Czechoslovak film director, screenwriter and actor. He directed over sixty films between 1934 and 1988.  Zezulka was born in Moravský Krumlov, then part of the Austro-Hungarian Empire. After studying at the National Academy of Theatre and Film Arts in Prague, he began his career as a stage actor. He made his film debut in 1934, directing and writing the screenplay for the historical drama "The White Queen". In the following years, he directed mainly historical dramas and family films.  In the 1960s, Zezulka directed more films about contemporary subjects, such as "The Firemen's Ball" (1963), about a strike by firemen, and "The Children of run-down houses" (1965), about street kids. His last film was "A Question of Honor" (1988). He died in Prague at the age of 91.
