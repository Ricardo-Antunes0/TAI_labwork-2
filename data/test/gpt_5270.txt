T. B. Blackstone Memorial Library is a public library in the city of Worcester, Massachusetts. Established in 1892 and dedicated to the memory of the jurist and statesman Thomas Blackstone, it is the city's second-oldest public library.  The library is housed in a building that was erected in 1892 and designed by noted Boston architect Andrew Dickson White. The building is located at 500 Memorial Drive in Worcester, on the edge of the campus of Worcester State University. The library has a collection of over 200,000 items, including books, periodicals, newspapers, microfilms, and audio-visual materials. The library offers free access to its resources to the public.
