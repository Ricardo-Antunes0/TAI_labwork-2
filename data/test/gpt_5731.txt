"So Hard" is a song by English rock band Radiohead. It was written by frontman Thom Yorke and recorded in February 1994 at the band's Bedroom Studio in Oxfordshire.  The song was released as the fourth single from their debut album "Creep" in May 1994. It reached number eight on the UK Singles Chart and number 15 on the US "Billboard" Modern Rock Tracks chart.  "So Hard" is a ballad with a slow tempo. It lyrically talks about being addicted to drugs and how it makes life difficult. Yorke has said that the song is about his experience with heroin addiction.   The song has been described as dark and moody, with Yorke singing about feeling alone and misunderstood. BBC music critic John Harris called it "one of Radiohead's most dramatic songs".
