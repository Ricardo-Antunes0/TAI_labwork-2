Ferdinand Martin Gomez Romualdez (, born November 10, 1971) is a Mexican politician affiliated with the National Action Party. He has served as Deputy of the LVIII Legislature of the Mexican Congress representing Puebla.  Romualdez studied law at the Autonomous University of Puebla and worked as a prosecutor before being elected to the National Congress in 2006. He has been involved in several scandals, most notably the Casa Blanca affair, in which he was alleged to have received bribes from construction companies in return for allowing them to build luxurious houses in government-owned land in Puebla.
