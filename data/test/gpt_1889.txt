Joseph-Napoléon-Henri Bourassa (; September 1, 1868 – July 24, 1957) was a Canadian prime minister, farmer and journalist. He served twice as Premier of Quebec, from 1930 to 1944 and from 1948 to 1957, and was the founder of the Bourassa Government.  Bourassa was born in 1868 in Saint-Nicolas-de-Port-Joli, Quebec, the son of Michel Bourassa and Marie-Louise Geoffrion. He attended the Collège Sainte-Marie in Montreal and then studied at Laval University. After working as a journalist for various newspapers, he entered politics in 1910 as a member of the Quebec Liberal Party. He served as Minister of Agriculture and Minister of Labour under Premier Louis-Léon Trudeau from 1919 to 1920. In 1930, he became Premier of Quebec on Trudeau's return to federal politics. During his time as premier, Bourassa led the province through the Great Depression and World War II. He resigned as premier in 1944 to enter federal politics, but was re-elected in 1948 and served until 1957. Bourassa was a Member of Parliament for Chambly from 1917 to 1935 and for La Prairie from 1935 to 1957. He was also ambassador to France (1944–1946), United States (1946–1949), and Italy (1950–1951). Bourassa died in 1957 at the age of 88.
