Østhorn (until 1939 Korsvoll) is a station on the Kongsvinger Line situated in Kongsvinger Municipality, Norway. The station is served by the Sørlandet Line and is located from Oslo Central Station. It is also served by the Østfold Line.  The station was opened on 16 October 1916 as part of the Kongsvinger Line. The station was rebuilt in 1939. The original building was demolished and a new building was built. The original entrance was at the east side of the building and a new entrance was built at the west side of the building.   The station has two platforms, with an incline leading up to them from the tracks. It has a manned ticket office, automatic ticket machines, a customer service desk, a shop and a waiting room.
