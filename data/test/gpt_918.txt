Affane Cappoquin GAA is a Gaelic Athletic Association club based in Affane, County Cork, Ireland. The club fields teams in both gaelic and hurling.  Affane Cappoquin GAA was founded in 1898 and has since produced many successful athletes. The most notable members of the Affane club include former Cork player and manager Eoin English, and All-Ireland medal winner Richie Hogan. The club has also produced several successful coaches, including Tomás Ó Ceallaigh and Paddy Durcan.   Affane Cappoquin GAA currently has teams in both gaelic football and hurling. The hurling team has won the Cork Senior Hurling Championship on two occasions (1975 and 1983). The club also claimed the Cork Intermediate Hurling Championship in 1984. Affane Cappoquin GAA have also won the Cork Club Hurling Championship on a number of occasions, most recently in 2009.
