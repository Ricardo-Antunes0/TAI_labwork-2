George Whitmore Jr., (-1968) was an man of African-American descent who became a well-known American singer and actor in the 1950s and 1960s. He is best remembered for his roles in the musicals "The King and I" (1951), "The Sound of Music" (1965), and "The Birdcage" (1996).  Whitmore was born in Memphis, Tennessee, on October 10, 1926. After attending local schools, he enlisted in the United States Navy during World War II. He served in the Pacific theater and was discharged in 1946. Whitmore then studied at the American Conservatory Theater in San Francisco, where he was a member of the Group Theatre.  Whitmore made his Broadway debut in 1951 as the title character in The King and I. He went on to star in several other musicals, including The Sound of Music (1965), which won him an Academy Award for Best Actor; The Birdcage (1996), for which he received another Academy Award nomination; and A Chorus Line (1975). He also appeared in several films, including The Defiant Ones (1958), Sweet Bird of Youth (1959), The Great Race (1965), The Sandpiper (1965), and Guess Who's Coming to Dinner? (1967). Whitmore retired from acting in the early 1970s but made occasional appearances on television and in film thereafter. He died of cancer on May 1, 1968.
