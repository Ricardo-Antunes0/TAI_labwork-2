CURE International (also known as CURE, formerly known as the CARE International Foundation) is an international not-for-profit humanitarian organization that provides medical assistance in developing countries.  CURE is headquartered in London, England, and has a worldwide network of over 1,000 clinics and hospitals. The organization is best known for its work on AIDS and cancer, but it also provides assistance on a wide range of health issues. CURE has been involved in many major humanitarian disasters, including the 2004 Indian Ocean tsunami and the 2010 Haiti earthquake.  CURE was founded in 1949 as the CARE International Foundation by Virginia Apuzzo and Dr. Peter Doherty. In 1984, CARE became an independent charity.
