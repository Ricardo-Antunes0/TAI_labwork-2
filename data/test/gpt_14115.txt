"Connie" Edmund Kötscher (17 April 1909 – 1 July 1992) was an Austrian composer and conductor. He is noted for his contributions to 20th century classical music, particularly his work in the field of serialism.  Kötscher was born in Vienna, Austria, on 17 April 1909. He studied at the Academy of Music Vienna with Arnold Schoenberg and Anton Webern, and later with Max Reger. He served as a cellist in the Wiener Symphoniker from 1933 to 1941.  In 1941 he joined the Vienna Opera, where he served as assistant to Clemens Krauss and conductor from 1945 to 1952. In 1952 he became music director of the Salzburg Festival, a post he held until his retirement in 1978.  Kötscher composed many works for orchestra and chamber ensemble, including orchestral works "Concerto for Violin, Strings and Woodwinds" (1946), "Symphony No. 1" (1948), "Concerto for Violin, String Orchestra and Harpsichord" (1957), "Sonata for Violin and Piano" (1956), "Concerto for Orchestra" (1970), and "Symphony No. 2" (1972). His works have been performed by major symphony orchestras around the world, including the Berlin Philharmonic, Boston Symphony Orchestra, Chicago Symphony Orchestra, Cleveland Orchestra, Royal Concertgebouw Orchestra Amsterdam, Royal Danish Philharmonic Orchestra
