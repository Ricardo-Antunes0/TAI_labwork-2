Kaya Brüel (born 30 August 1972) is a German actress and singer. She is known for her roles in the crime-drama television series Das Teufelskreuz and the heist film Crimen, both of which she starred in together with Til Schweiger.  Brüel was born in Munich, Bavaria, to a Turkish father and a German mother. She has two older sisters. When she was four years old, her family moved to Istanbul, where she spent the next six years. In 1987, they moved back to Germany and settled in Baden-Württemberg. Brüel started her acting career in the theater, appearing in productions of such plays as The Glass Menagerie and A Streetcar Named Desire.  In 2001, Brüel made her film debut in the thriller Crimen. The following year, she starred in Das Teufelskreuz, a crime-drama television series based on the novels by Peter Hochhuth. For her performance in the series, Brüel was awarded the Bavarian TV Award for Best Actress in a Drama Series.   Brüel has since appeared in several other films, including 2006's Deutschland 83 and 2009's Liebesgeschichten von der Leipziger Straße. In 2012, she starred in the heist film Crimen alongside Til Schweiger. That same year, she released her first album, Die Unschuld der Anderen.
