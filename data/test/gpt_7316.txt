Timothy Hinman (21 July 1762 - 29 May 1825) was an American lawyer, politician, and diplomat who served as the first United States Secretary of War from 1813 to 1815.  Hinman was born in Boston, Massachusetts on 21 July 1762. He attended Harvard University, where he received his bachelor's degree in 1783 and his law degree in 1787. Hinman practiced law in Boston until 1801, when he was elected to the United States House of Representatives as a Federalist. He served in the House until 1815, when he was appointed Secretary of War by President James Madison. Hinman served in that position until his resignation in 1815. He died on 29 May 1825 in Washington, D.C.
