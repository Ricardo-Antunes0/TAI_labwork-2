Omar Hashi Aden (, Arabic: عمر هاشي عدن‎) (born December 1, 1934) is a former Jordanian diplomat and politician.   He was born in al-Salt, Jordan, on December 1, 1934. After attending the American University of Beirut and the University of Geneva, he began his career as a diplomat in the Jordanian Foreign Service in 1963. He served as Jordan's Ambassador to France from 1985 to 1990 and to the United States from 1990 to 1993. From 1993 to 1997 he served as Secretary-General of the Arab League. Aden served as Prime Minister of Jordan from 1997 to 1999. He retired from active politics in 2000.
