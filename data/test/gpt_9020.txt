"Venom (Music from the Motion Picture)", more commonly known by its former title "The Eminem Show", is the third studio album by American rapper Eminem. It was released on November 3, 2002, by Aftermath Entertainment and Interscope Records. The album features the single "Lose Yourself" which became his first number-one single on the US "Billboard" Hot 100 and also hit number one in several other countries. The album was certified 4Ã— Platinum by the RIAA.  Venom was met with generally positive reviews from music critics. At Metacritic, which assigns a normalized rating out of 100 to reviews from mainstream critics, the album has an average score of 72, based on 22 reviews.  Eminem has said that Venom is his most personal and autobiographical album to date. He revealed that much of the material on the album is based on his experiences as an African American in America. The album's themes include misogyny, addiction, and depression.  In 2003, Eminem released a follow-up album to Venom, titled Encore.
