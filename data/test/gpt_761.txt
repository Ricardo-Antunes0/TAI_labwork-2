Susan Charlotte Buchan, Baroness Tweedsmuir (née Grosvenor; 24 May 1881 – 3 October 1964) was a British author and politician. She was the first woman to hold a cabinet post in the United Kingdom, serving as Minister for War from 1933 to 1935.  Buchan was also a Member of Parliament (MP) for Stirling from 1923 to 1945. She served as Home Secretary from 1935 to 1939, during which time she enacted the Emergency Powers Act 1936, which allowed the government to rule by decree in case of a national emergency. Buchan was a Conservative MP and minister throughout her career. After retiring from Parliament, she served as president of the Royal Society of Literature from 1955 to 1963. Buchan is commemorated in the surname of her grandson, the Conservative MP Timothy Laurence Buchan.
