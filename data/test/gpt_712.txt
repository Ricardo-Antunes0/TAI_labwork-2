The Tri-State Transit Authority (TTA), which markets itself as "New York's Largest Public Transportation System," provides bus, train and paratransit service to nearly two million riders in the three counties of New York, New Jersey and Connecticut.  The Tri-State Transit Authority was founded in 1937 as the Regional Transportation Authority. It was renamed the Tri-State Transit Authority in 1966 and became a joint public authority of the three states in 1984. The Tri-State Transit Authority is headquartered in Manhattan.  The Tri-State Transit Authority operates a system of buses, trains and paratransit vehicles. Its buses serve more than 350 routes throughout the region. The Tri-State Transit Authority's commuter rail service provides service to Penn Station in Manhattan and Newark Penn Station in Newark, New Jersey. The paratransit service provides transportation to people with disabilities.
