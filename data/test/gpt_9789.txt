Rahim Moeini Kermanshahi (, February 6, 1923 â€“ March 15, 2009) was an Iranian lawyer and politician who served as the tenth Prime Minister of Iran from August 14, 1981 to September 24, 1981.  Kermanshahi was born in 1923 in Tehran. After receiving his undergraduate education in law at the University of Tehran, he began his legal career in 1947. He served as the Deputy Attorney General of Iran from 1968 to 1971, and as the Minister of Justice from 1971 to 1975. He was elected to the Iranian Parliament in 1972 and served as its Speaker from 1978 to 1981. Kermanshahi was appointed Prime Minister by the President of Iran, Mohammad Reza Pahlavi, on August 14, 1981. He resigned from office on September 24, 1981, after less than a month in office.  Kermanshahi died on March 15, 2009, in Tehran at the age of 88.
