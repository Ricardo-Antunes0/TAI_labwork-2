She began playing the piano at the age of five and quickly showed a talent for composing. She studied under Joseph DeBlasio and later with Artur Rodzinski. In the early 1930s, she traveled to Europe and played with Django Reinhardt, Stephane Grappelli, and other leading jazz musicians.  After returning to the United States, Jones co-founded the Modern Jazz Quartet with Miles Davis and John Coltrane. She also recorded extensively as a soloist, collaborations with musicians including Duke Ellington, Thelonious Monk, Art Blakey, and Herbie Hancock. Her most famous work is the score for the film "The Sting" (1973).  Jones received numerous awards during her career, including a Grammy Award in 1975 for her album "Jones/Gilchrist". She died of cancer in 1992 at the age of 80.
