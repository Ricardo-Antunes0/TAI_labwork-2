Zenyatta (foaled April 1, 2004) is a Thoroughbred racehorse who has competed in the United States and Canada. He is best known for his victory in the 2013 Kentucky Derby, making him the first horse since Affirmed in 1978 to win the race twice.  Zenyatta is a chestnut horse with a white blaze down his face and neck, and two white feet. He was bred in Kentucky by Barbara and Charles F. Haag Jr. Zenyatta was sired by Northern Dancer, who won the 1987 Kentucky Derby and the Preakness Stakes, as well as the Champion Stakes and Belmont Stakes. His dam, Street Cry, was a successful racemare who won the Hollywood Gold Cup and the Monterrey Stakes.  Zenyatta began his racing career in 2006, winning three minor races including the Golden Slipper Stakes at Woodbine Racetrack in Toronto. In 2007 he finished third in both of his starts before being retired to broodmare duty at Haag's Stallion Station near Versailles, Kentucky.  Zenyatta was retired from breeding in 2012 but returned to racing that year. His first start of 2013 was the Fountain of Youth Stakes at Gulfstream Park where he finished second behind favouite Preakness winner I'll Have Another.
