June Brooks (1924-2010) was an Oklahoma businesswoman, philanthropist, and civil rights activist.  Born in 1924 in Tulsa, Oklahoma, Brooks was the daughter of a Baptist minister. After graduating from high school, Brooks worked as a secretary before starting her own accounting firm in 1950. She later became the owner of several businesses, including a restaurant chain and a bank.  In the 1960s, Brooks began to focus on civil rights activism. She was a co-founder of the Tulsa Civil Rights Commission and served as its chairwoman for over twenty years. She also served on the boards of several organizations, including the NAACP and the Southern Christian Leadership Conference. Brooks was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Jimmy Carter in 1977.  Brooks died in 2010 at the age of 89. She was inducted into the Oklahoma Hall of Fame in 2001 and the Oklahoma Women's Hall of Fame in 2006.
