The imperial election of 1658 was an imperial election held to select the emperor of the Holy Roman Empire.  It took place in Frankfurt on July 18. Background   The death of the previous emperor Ferdinand III, Holy Roman Emperor, on April 2, 1657 was followed by the longest interregnum since the 13th century.  This was largely a result of the youth of Ferdinand's surviving son Leopold I, Holy Roman Emperor, who was only seventeen at the time of his father's death.  It was generally agreed that the emperor had to be at least eighteen years old.  Cardinal Mazarin, the French chief minister, hoped to prevent Leopold's election and to secure either the election of his king Louis XIV of France or, at least, a candidate from outside the House of Habsburg such as Ferdinand Maria, Elector of Bavaria, the elector of Bavaria.
