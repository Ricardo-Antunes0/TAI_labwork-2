Peshotanu (Avestan , Middle Persian Peshyotan, Peshotan) was a Parthian king who ruled from ca. 190 to ca. 158 BC.  Peshotanu was the son of the previous king, Artabanus V, and the brother of Artabanus VI. He succeeded his brother to the Parthian throne in 190 BC. He reigned for sixteen years, during which time he fought a series of civil wars against his brothers. In 158 BC he was assassinated by one of his own generals, and was succeeded by his son, Vologases I.
