Wild Pigeon (, also known as Someone's Fortune) is a 2006 Taiwanese drama film directed by Hou Hsiao-hsien and written by Su Min-che. It stars Shu Qi and Huang Bo in the lead roles.  The film tells the story of two cousins, one of whom is blind, who are forced to leave their home in Taiwan after their father is falsely accused of a crime he did not commit. They travel to China, where they start a new life as beggars. The film examines the themes of identity, persecution, and hope.  "Wild Pigeon" was awarded the Grand Prix at the 2006 Cannes Film Festival. It was also nominated for the Golden Globe Award for Best Foreign Language Film.
