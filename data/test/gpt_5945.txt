Jonny Kennedy (4 November 1966 â€“ 26 July 1998) was an Irish singer and songwriter. He was the son of the late U.S. President John F. Kennedy and First Lady Jacqueline Kennedy.  Kennedy began his career as a child performer and achieved international success in the 1980s with his debut album, You're Nobody 'til Somebody Loves You (1987). He released four more albums during his lifetime, including the platinum-selling Heartbeats (1992), which yielded the international hit single "I Will Always Love You". His final album, Alive and Kicking (1998), was released posthumously and reached number one in Ireland. Kennedy's music was featured in the films The Commitments (1990) and The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring (2001).  He died from a brain tumor at the age of 36.
