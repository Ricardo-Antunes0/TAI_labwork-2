Nikola Nešković (c. 1729 – 1785) was a Serbian Enlightenment writer, philosopher, linguist and ethnographer.  Nikola Nešković was born in 1729 in Novi Sad, Principality of Serbia. He was educated in the Jesuit schools of Belgrade and Sremski Karlovci, and later at the University of Vienna. In 1754 he became a professor at the University of Belgrade. He died in 1785.  Nikola Nešković was a prolific writer, producing philosophical, linguistic and ethnographical works. His most important work is "Istorija srpskoga jezika" (History of Serbian Language), which was published in three volumes from 1775 to 1779. He is also known for his translations of Montesquieu's "Spirit of the Laws" and Jean-Jacques Rousseau's "The Social Contract".
