The Ducks (formerly known as the Jeff Carter-era Anaheim Ducks) are a professional ice hockey team based in Anaheim, California. They are members of the Pacific Division of the Western Conference of the National Hockey League (NHL). The team was founded in 1993 as the Mighty Ducks of Anaheim. They have appeared in the playoffs six times, making it to the Stanley Cup Final on four occasions (1994, 2003, 2006, 2007). The Ducks reached their first Stanley Cup Final in 1994, losing to the New York Rangers. They made it back to the final in 2003 but lost to the Tampa Bay Lightning. In 2006, they defeated the Edmonton Oilers to win their first championship. The 2007 season was their most successful yet as they won their second championship by defeating the Ottawa Senators in six games.   The Ducks were founded on November 17, 1993 as an expansion team along with the Toronto Maple Leafs and Florida Panthers. The Mighty Ducks of Anaheim were owned by Disney Corporation and were coached by Randy Carlyle.
