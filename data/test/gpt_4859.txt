He played as a catcher in Major League Baseball (MLB) for the Milwaukee Braves, Baltimore Orioles, California Angels, and Toronto Blue Jays. Anderson served as the manager of the Milwaukee Brewers, Atlanta Braves, and Texas Rangers.  Anderson was born in Tulsa, Oklahoma. He attended Southeastern Oklahoma State University and played college baseball for the Eagles. He signed with the Milwaukee Braves as an amateur free agent in 1954 and made his MLB debut with the Braves in 1957. Anderson led the National League (NL) in hits twice (1959 and 1960), batting average once (1960), and home runs twice (1957 and 1960).  Anderson was traded to the Baltimore Orioles in 1964 where he spent his most successful years as a catcher. In 1970, he led the Orioles to their first ever World Series title. Anderson also helped Baltimore to win two American League (AL) titles (1966 and 1969). Anderson was elected to the Baseball Hall of Fame in 1983.  After retiring as a player, Anderson served as a coach for the Atlanta Braves from 1974 to 1976. He then became manager of the Milwaukee Brewers from 1977 to 1980. Anderson led the Brewers to consecutive division titles in 1980 and 1981 but was fired after leading them to a third-place finish in 1982.
