Mansur Rafizadeh (; 14 December 1930, Kerman, Iran â€“ 25 January 2018, Tehran) was an Iranian mathematician, physicist, and academic. He was the eleventh president of the Islamic Republic of Iran from 1981 to 1988.  Mansur Rafizadeh was born in Kerman, Iran in 1930. After completing his undergraduate studies at the University of Tehran in 1951, he went on to complete his graduate studies at the University of Chicago under the supervision of Saul Kripke. He then returned to Tehran to teach mathematics at the university.  Rafizadeh played an important role in the development of modern Iranian mathematics. He was a co-founder of the journal "Iranian Mathematical Journal" and served as its editor from its inception in 1966 until his death. He was also a founder and first editor-in-chief of "IEEE Transactions on Mathematical Sciences" (TMS), serving in that capacity from 1975 to 1988.  Rafizadeh was elected president of Iran in 1981, after serving as deputy minister of science and technology under the presidency of Mohammad Khatami. He served as president until 1988, when he was succeeded by Ali Akbar Hashemi Rafsanjani. Rafizadeh remained a prominent figure within Iranian academia and served as dean of the Faculty of Sciences at Tehran University from 1988 until his death in 2018.
