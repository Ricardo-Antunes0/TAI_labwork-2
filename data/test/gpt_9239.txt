Mohammad Javad Zarif Khansari (; ; born February 14, 1957) is an Iranian diplomat and nuclear negotiator who has been the Foreign Minister of Iran since 2013. Zarif served as Iran's Permanent Representative to the United Nations from 2005 to 2013.  Zarif was born in Tehran on February 14, 1957, to a religious family. He is of Kurdish descent. After completing his high school studies in Tehran, Zarif studied philosophy at University of Tehran and law at University of London. He also has a LLM from Harvard Law School.  Zarif started his career as a lawyer. In 1991, Zarif became the head of the department of international legal affairs at the Iranian Foreign Ministry. He also served as Iran's ambassador to Norway from 2001 to 2005.  In 2005, Zarif was appointed Iran's Permanent Representative to the United Nations by President Mahmoud Ahmadinejad. Zarif served in this position until he was named Foreign Minister in 2013 by President Hassan Rouhani following Ahmadinejad's resignation due to corruption allegations.  Zarif has been a vocal opponent of the United States' policies in the Middle East and has defended Iranian nuclear programs and its right to enrich uranium for peaceful purposes. He has also condemned Israeli actions against Palestinians and defended Iran's support for Syrian President Bashar al-Assad during the Syrian Civil War.
