The New London Custom House is a historic building located at the foot of Federal Street in Downtown New London, Connecticut. Built in 1801–02, it is the oldest surviving governmental building in the state. It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1984.  The New London Custom House is a two-story brick structure, with a gabled roof and clapboarded exterior. Its main facade is three bays wide, with a central entrance flanked by pilasters and topped by a pediment. The windows are set in round-arch openings with molded surrounds, and there is a Palladian window in the gable end. The interior features original woodwork, including paneling and staircases.  The New London Custom House was built in 1801–02 to serve as the state's first general post office and customhouse. The building was designed by Richard Upjohn, one of the leading architects of his day, and was one of his most ambitious projects. It served as the state's main post office until 1833, and as its customhouse until 1855. In 1984, it was listed on the National Register of Historic Places.
