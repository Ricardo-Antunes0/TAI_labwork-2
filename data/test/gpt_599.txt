Mendenhall is a city in Simpson County, Mississippi, United States. As of the 2010 census, the population was 2,547. The city is located in northeastern Simpson County at (33.827913, -92.870379).  According to the United States Census Bureau, the city has a total area of , all land.  As of the census of 2000, there were 2,514 people, 928 households, and 651 families residing in the city. The population density was 1,205.4 people per square mile (463.4/km²). There were 996 housing units at an average density of 456.3 per square mile (182.1/km²). The racial makeup of the city was 92.98% White, 5.11% African American, 0.14% Native American, 0.21% Asian, 0.06% Pacific Islander, 0.92% from other races, and 1.70% from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino of any race were 1.84% of the population.  There were 928 households out of which 36.2% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 53.3% were married couples living together, 17.7% had a female householder with no husband present, and 26.4% were non-families. 22.8% of all households were made up of individuals and 10.1% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age
