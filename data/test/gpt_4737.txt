Brigadier Harry Kenneth Dimoline  CBE MBE (born 3 January 1917) is a retired British Army officer who served in World War II, the Korean War and the Vietnam War. He reached the rank of brigadier before retiring in 1971.  Dimoline was born in 1917 in Northumberland, England. He attended Bede School and then served as a second lieutenant in the Royal Artillery during World War II. He was captured by the Germans and held as a prisoner of war until he was released in 1945. Dimoline then served in the Korean War, where he reached the rank of major. He returned to England and served in the Vietnam War until his retirement in 1971. Dimoline was awarded the CBE and MBE for his service in World War II, the Korean War and the Vietnam War.
