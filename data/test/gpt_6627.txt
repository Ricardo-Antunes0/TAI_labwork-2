Stephen J. Lipson (born 16 March 1954) is an Australian-born mathematician, known for his work in mathematical economics and game theory.  Lipson was born on 16 March 1954 in Melbourne, Australia. He received his B.A. from the University of Melbourne in 1976, and his Ph.D. from the University of Cambridge in 1981.  Lipson has been a professor at Brown University since 1988, and was chairman of the economics department from 1999 to 2002. He has also been a visiting professor at universities in China, Italy, Spain, and the United States.  Lipson is the author or editor of more than 60 books and articles on mathematics, economics, and game theory. He is also a co-founder of two companies, one of which (Maxim Integrated) became a leading provider of mathematical optimization software.  In 2006 Lipson was awarded the National Medal of Science by President George W. Bush "for his distinguished contributions to mathematics and economics".
