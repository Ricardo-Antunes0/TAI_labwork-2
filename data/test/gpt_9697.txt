Anatoly Andreyevich Gromyko (; 15 April 1932 – 20 October 2005) was a Soviet statesman and diplomat. He was the third Secretary General of the United Nations, serving from 1981 to 1985.  Born in the town of Ust-Ilimsk, in Siberia, Gromyko joined the Soviet Communist Party in 1943. He became a senior official in the Foreign Ministry in 1964, serving as Deputy Foreign Minister from 1969 to 1971 and Minister of Foreign Affairs from 1971 to 1975. He played a major role in Soviet policy during the Cold War, becoming one of the Soviet Union's most influential diplomats.  In 1981, Gromyko was appointed Secretary General of the United Nations, serving until 1985. In this role, he was responsible for UN affairs and acted as a mediator in conflicts such as the Iran–Iraq War and the Falklands War. He also presided over the UN Commission on Human Rights in 1984. After leaving the UN, Gromyko became a senior Russian diplomat and served as Ambassador to France from 1988 to 1991, Ambassador to Great Britain from 1991 to 1996, and Foreign Minister again from 1996 to 1998. He retired in 2000 and died ten years later at the age of 76.
