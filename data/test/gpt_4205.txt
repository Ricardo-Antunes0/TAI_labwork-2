Alexander Vasilyevich Stupin (;  February 1776 â€“ November 5, 1830) was a Russian composer, pianist and conductor.  Stupin was born in Moscow, the son of a musician. He studied at the Moscow Conservatory with Anton Rubinstein, and then with Ludwig van Beethoven. He made his debut as a pianist in 1800. From 1806 to 1809 he served as Kapellmeister to the court of Tsar Alexander I. In 1812 he became Director of the Saint Petersburg Conservatory, where he remained until his death.  Among his students were Adelina Patti, Anton Rubinstein's daughter, Nikolai Rimsky-Korsakov, and Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky.
