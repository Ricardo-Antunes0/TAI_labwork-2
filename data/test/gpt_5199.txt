He entered the Dominican order in 1198, and taught philosophy and theology at the University of Paris. He died in 1230.  De Barjols is best known for his work "Summa theologiae", a comprehensive philosophical work which covers all areas of theology. He also wrote "Quaestiones disputatae" (1215), "Tractatus de potentia et actibus animae" (1224), and "Tractatus de anima et vitae" (1230).
