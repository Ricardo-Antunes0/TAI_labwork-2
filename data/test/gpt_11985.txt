Njoo Cheong Seng (Perfected Spelling: Nyoo Cheong Seng; born November 8, 1956) is a Malaysian businessman and politician. He is the founder and chairman of the Maybank Group.  Njoo Cheong Seng was born in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia on November 8, 1956. He is the founder and chairman of the Maybank Group, which he founded in 1984 with a capital of RM1 million. He has since grown the group into one of the largest financial institutions in Southeast Asia, with assets totalling over US$50 billion. He has also been involved in a number of philanthropic initiatives, including donations to various schools and hospitals. Njoo Cheong Seng was elected to the Malaysian parliament in 2006 as a member of the Barisan Nasional (BN) party. He has since served as Deputy Minister in the Prime Minister's Department and Minister in the Ministry of Finance. He was appointed Minister of Tourism in 2013, and Minister in the Ministry of Communications and Multimedia in 2015.
