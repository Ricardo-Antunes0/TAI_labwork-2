In an inquisitorial system of law, the magistrate is a judge who presides over trials. Magistrates are typically appointed by the government or by a higher court, and often have a law degree. They may also be called judges, justices, or magistrates.  A magistrate's role in the judicial system varies depending on the country and jurisdiction. Magistrates may be responsible for hearing initial complaints or issuing warrants, conducting preliminary investigations, or presiding over trials. In some jurisdictions, they may also be responsible for resolving disputes between parties or issuing opinions on legal questions.
