Theodore Wirth (1863–1949) was instrumental in designing the first modern computer and was a professor of engineering at the University of Illinois. He was also one of the co-founders of the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey.  Born in 1863 in Zurich, Wirth received his engineering degree from the Eidgenössische Polytechnische Schule (ETH) in 1887. He worked as a professor of engineering at the University of Illinois from 1906 to 1949, where he helped to design the first modern computer, ENIAC. Wirth was also one of the co-founders of the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey. He died in 1949.
