Calvin Holmes Carter (May 19, 1829 – October 17, 1896) was an American jurist and politician who served as the 23rd Governor of Georgia from 1881 to 1883.  Carter was born in Crawford County, Georgia, to John Holmes and Nancy (née Walthall) Carter. He attended the common schools and then studied law. He was admitted to the bar in 1851 and commenced practice in Rome, Georgia. Carter served as member of the Georgia House of Representatives from 1857 to 1861, and as state senator from 1861 to 1871. He was elected governor in 1881, serving until his resignation in 1883. Carter died in Rome, Georgia, aged 77.
