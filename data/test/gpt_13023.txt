Steven R. Smith is an American musician, songwriter, and record producer. He is known as the founder and frontman of the band Third Eye Blind, as well as a record producer, songwriter, and guitarist.  Steven R. Smith was born on October 26th, 1971 in San Rafael, California. After playing in several local bands in his youth, he founded Third Eye Blind in 1993 with childhood friend Kevin Cadogan. The band quickly gained a following with their innovative and energetic music, and released their debut album, self-titled in 1998.  Third Eye Blind went on to become one of the most successful rock bands of the 1990s and 2000s, selling over 25 million records worldwide. They have released six albums, including 2009's critically acclaimed Out of the Vein, which reached number one on the Billboard charts. In 2013, they released their seventh album Kaleidoscope Dream.  As a musician and songwriter, Steven R. Smith has written or co-written many of Third Eye Blind's hits, including "Jumper," "Semi-Charmed Life," "Graduate," and "Never Let Me Go." He has also produced many of the band's albums himself, including Out of the Vein and Kaleidoscope Dream.  Outside of Third Eye Blind, Smith has also released several solo albums and collaborated with various artists, including Fiona Apple, Train, Sara Bareilles, Kelly Clarkson, Lewis Capaldi and Meghan Trainor.
