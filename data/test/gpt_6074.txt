In Rex v Bourke, an important case in the law of evidence, the Court of Criminal Appeal held that a confession obtained through inducement, threats or violence cannot be accepted as evidence.  Rex v Bourke was a case that determined the admissibility of a confession obtained through inducement, threats or violence. The Court of Criminal Appeal held that a confession obtained through these means cannot be accepted as evidence, due to the inherent coercion involved. This ruling is important, as it ensures that confessions obtained through coercion will not be accepted in court.
