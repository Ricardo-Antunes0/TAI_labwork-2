Silene spaldingii is a rare species of flowering plant in the daisy family known by the common name Spalding's catchfly.  This plant is endemic to California, where it is known from only a few occurrences in the eastern Sacramento Valley and adjacent foothills.  The plant grows a single stem up to about 40 centimeters tall from a basal rosette of leaves up to 8 centimeters long. The inflorescence is an array of small white flowers heads surrounded by bristly bracts.
