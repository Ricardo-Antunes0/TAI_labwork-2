Magora Elmira Kennedy (born Magora Ernestine Molineaux; February 14, 1926 – January 2, 2018) was an American singer and actress.  Kennedy was born in New Orleans, Louisiana, the daughter of Ernestine (née Molineaux), a jazz singer and pianist, and Jacques Kennedy, a sanitation worker and jazz trumpeter. Her paternal grandfather was the African-American actor Stepin Fetchit. She began her singing career in nightclubs in the early 1940s with her group the Mellotones before signing with Capitol Records in 1951. Her first two Capitol albums were unsuccessful, but she had her first hit single in 1953 with "Rocky Top".  In 1954, Kennedy starred in the Broadway musical "High Society" as Cilla Black's rival, Dixie Carter. The show ran for more than 2,000 performances. She then appeared in a number of films, including "The Nun's Story" (1959), "The Unsinkable Molly Brown" (1964), and "Cool Hand Luke" (1967). Kennedy retired from acting in 1985.  Kennedy was awarded a Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award in 1997. She died at her home in Los Angeles on January 2, 2018 at the age of 89.
