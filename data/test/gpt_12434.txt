German submarine U-551 was a Type VIIC U-boat of Nazi Germany's Kriegsmarine during World War II.  German submarine U-551 was laid down on 3 October 1942 at the Blohm & Voss yard in Hamburg, as yard number 810. She was launched on 6 January 1944 and commissioned under the command of "Oberleutnant zur See" Heinz-Eberhard Bey on 26 January 1944.  German submarine U-551 operated in the European theatre of World War II. She attacked shipping in the Atlantic Ocean and sank one ship. She was sunk by a British warship on 4 May 1944.
