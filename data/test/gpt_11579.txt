Caril Ann Fugate (born July 30, 1943) is an American politician who was the 49th Governor of Florida from 1999 to 2007. Fugate was also a member of the United States House of Representatives from 1993 to 2007 and served as the Republican Party's nominee for Governor of Florida in 2006.  Fugate was born in Tampa, Florida. She is a graduate of Hillsborough High School and the University of South Florida. Fugate worked as a business executive before entering politics. She served as a member of the Hillsborough County Commission from 1987 to 1993, and as its chairwoman from 1991 to 1993. Fugate also served as Mayor of Tampa from January 1, 1994 to January 1, 1999.  Fugate was elected Governor of Florida in 1998, defeating Lawton Chiles by a wide margin. As governor, Fugate enacted several conservative policies, including reductions in taxes and government spending, increases in funding for education and welfare programs, and changes to the state's health care system. In 2006, she unsuccessfully sought the Republican nomination for U.S. President against John McCain.  Fugate retired from politics after losing re-election in 2008 to Democrat Charlie Crist. She currently resides in Tampa Bay area with her husband of over 40 years, John Fugate.
