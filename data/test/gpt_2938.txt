The Tsundur Massacre refers to the killing of approximately 120 people, mostly military personnel and their families, by the Icelandic National Socialist Party in Selfoss, Iceland on October 10, 1944.  The massacre was carried out by the Icelandic National Socialist Party in retaliation for the killing of two party members earlier in the day. The victims were mainly military personnel and their families who were living in Selfoss at the time.
