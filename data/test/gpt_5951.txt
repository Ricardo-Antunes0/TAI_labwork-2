John Ampontuah Kumah (born 4 August 1978) is a Kenyan long-distance runner who competes in the marathon. He achieved his best global ranking of #4 in March 2010. Kumah is the first Kenyan to ever win a marathon medal, taking the silver medal at the 2007 World Championships.  Born in Kenya's Central Province, Kumah began running as a young boy, inspired by his father. After winning several regional and national titles, Kumah represented his country at the 2007 World Championships in Osaka, Japan. There he finished second behind Ethiopia's Derartu Tulu. Kumah then won the Gold Coast Marathon in Australia in 2008 and 2009, becoming the first Kenyan ever to win two consecutive marathons. Kumah also won the Honolulu Marathon in 2009 and 2010. In 2013, he won the Tokyo Marathon.  Kumah has maintained an elite marathon performance by setting new personal bests at a variety of distances throughout his career.
