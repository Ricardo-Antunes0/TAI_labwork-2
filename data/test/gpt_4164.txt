Endless Night is a painting executed in 1824 by the French painter Jean-Baptiste-Camille Corot. The painting is usually considered to be a masterpiece of Romanticism, and is one of the best-known examples of Corot's work.  The painting is a landscape, depicting a rural landscape at night. The landscape features a river and several villages, with a few lighted windows visible in the buildings. The sky is dark and cloudy, with a few stars visible. The painting is considered to be one of the most representative examples of Romanticism, and has been described as "one of the most sublime and affecting pieces of art ever created".
