Rogers Terrill (c. 1901 - March 1, 1976) was an American artist who is known for his paintings of the American West and his depictions of Native Americans.  Rogers Terrill was born in Kansas City, Missouri, in 1901. He studied at the University of Denver, where he received his BFA in 1924. Terrill then moved to New York City, where he worked as a commercial artist. In the 1930s, he began painting landscapes of the American West, which won him critical and popular acclaim. He died in 1976.
