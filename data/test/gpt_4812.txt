Richard Franklin Lennox Thomas Pryor Sr. (December 1, 1937 – October 25, 2005) was an American stand-up comedian, actor and author. Pryor was known for his rapid-fire delivery, satire, and dark humor.  Pryor was born in St. Louis, Missouri, the son of Margaret (née Lennox) and Richard Franklin Pryor Sr., a bus driver. He was raised in East St. Louis and attended John Burroughs High School. After dropping out of college, Pryor worked as a truck driver, stand-up comic, and actor before achieving national fame in the 1970s as a star of the NBC sketch comedy show The Dick Van Dyke Show and its spin-off program The New Dick Van Dyke Show. He then developed a persona of angry black man on stage and became one of the most controversial and iconoclastic performers in America.  Pryor's first film role was in the 1971 film The Mack. He became well known for his roles in the films Young Frankenstein (1974), Monty Python's Life of Brian (1979), and Pryor's character Bo Diddley in the 1980 movie Dune. In 1990, he starred in the successful movie Pryor directed and starred in Delirious. In 1997, he released an autobiography, I'm Not Lying: A Life of Comedy, which became a New York Times Best Seller. Pryor died from liver cancer on October 25, 2005 at age 70.
