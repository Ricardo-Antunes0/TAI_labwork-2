Freche Mädchen  is a 2008 German comedy film written and directed by Florian Henckel von Donnersmarck. It tells the story of a group of young women in their late twenties who reunite for a weekend in the country to celebrate their friend's 30th birthday.  The film was selected as the German entry for the Best Foreign Language Film at the 91st Academy Awards, but it was not nominated.  The Freche Mädchen live in Berlin and are all in their late twenties. They all grew up together, but now they are all living separate lives. The weekend they're all supposed to meet up for their friend's 30th birthday, no one can reach them by phone. They decide to drive to her house, but get lost on the way. When they finally arrive, they find out that their friend has already left for her vacation in Italy. The Freche Mädchen spend the weekend drinking, partying and chasing each other around.
