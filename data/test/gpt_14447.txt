Julia "Judy" Belle Thompson Bonds (August 27, 1928 – August 5, 2009) was an American actress, singer and television personality.  Born in Los Angeles, California, Bonds was the daughter of show business parents, Mitzi (née Geiss) and Melville Thompson. Her father was a vaudeville comic and her mother was a singer. She began her career as a child performer in stage productions and in films before making her Broadway debut in "The Boy Friend" (1953).  Bonds began her television career in the 1950s, appearing on such series as "The Phil Silvers Show", "The Lucy Show", "The Donna Reed Show" and "The Dick Van Dyke Show". In 1963, she starred as Fran Kubelik in the Broadway revival of "Damn Yankees", for which she won a Tony Award.  Bonds' film credits include roles in "The Defiant Ones" (1958), "How to Marry a Millionaire" (1953), "Imitation of Life" (1959), "A Star Is Born" (1976), "Ghost" (1990) and "Mr. & Mrs. Smith" (2005). In 1993, she received an Emmy Award nomination for her role in the television miniseries "Julia".  Bonds retired from acting in the early 1990s but returned to television appearances in later years. She died at her home in Encino, California, aged 80.
