Edwin Epps House is a Creole cottage located in the Central Business District of downtown Mobile, Alabama. It is a two-story, wood frame structure with a gable roof and front porch. It was built in 1876 by Edwin Epps, a Confederate soldier and entrepreneur who operated a grocery and general store on the first floor and rented rooms on the second floor to businesses and families. The house is now used as the Mobile County Historical Commission headquarters.
