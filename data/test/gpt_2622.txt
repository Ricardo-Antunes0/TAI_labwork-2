Edward Dominic Fenwick,  (August 19, 1768 â€“ September 21, 1851) was an American jurist and statesman who served as a U.S. Representative from New York.  Born in Somerset County, New Jersey, Fenwick attended the common schools and then studied law. He was admitted to the bar in 1792 and commenced practice in Kingston, New York. He served as district attorney for Ulster County from 1800 to 1804.  Fenwick was elected as a Federalist to the twelfth Congress, serving from March 4, 1811, to March 3, 1813. He was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1812 to the thirteenth Congress. He resumed the practice of law in Kingston.  He served as judge of the Supreme Court of the State of New York from 1820 to 1829. Fenwick died in Kingston, New York, on September 21, 1851 and was interred in the old burying ground near his home.
