Peng Chong (; March 1915 â€“ October 1988) was a Chinese painter and sculptor.  Peng was born in the town of Yongxin, Zhejiang province in 1915. He studied at the Hangzhou Academy of Fine Arts from 1934 to 1938 and then at the Beijing Academy of Painting and Sculpture from 1940 to 1945. In 1949, he travelled to Japan, where he studied under Kishin Shinoyama. He returned to China in 1951 and became a professor at the Academy of Fine Arts in Hangzhou.  Peng is best known for his paintings and sculptures of traditional Chinese figures and scenes, which are characterized by their restrained brushwork and delicate features. His work has been exhibited throughout China and abroad, most notably at the Museum of Modern Art in New York City (1964), the Tate Gallery in London (1967), and the Centre Pompidou in Paris (1973). He died in 1988.
