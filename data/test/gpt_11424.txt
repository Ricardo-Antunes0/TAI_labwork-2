Emir Music (born 1 September 1964) is a Nigerian-born singer, songwriter, producer and actor. He has sold over 30 million records worldwide and won numerous awards including six Grammy Awards, two Brit Awards, an Ivor Novello Award, a Golden Globe Award, and an Emmy Award.  He was born in Jos, Plateau State, Nigeria on 1 September 1964. At the age of five he moved to London with his family. He began his music career as a member of the group Sound Dimension. In 1990 he released his debut album "Vision Quest". The album spawned four successful singles including the international hit "Lady", which peaked at number one in over 20 countries. The album was certified triple platinum in the United States and won him two Grammy Awards.  In 2001 Emir released his second album "A Whole New World". The album featured the hit singles "Boom Boom" and "Beautiful Things". The album was certified diamond in the United States and went on to win him another Grammy Award.  In 2006 Emir released his first ever solo album "One Day I'll Fly Away". The album featured the hit single "I Believe". The album was certified platinum in the United States and went on to win him another Grammy Award.  In 2009 Emir released his third album "The X Factor". The album featured the hit singles "Love in This Club" and "Best of My Life". The album was certified platinum in the United States and went on to win him another Grammy Award.
