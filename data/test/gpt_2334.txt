Jerry Lee's Greatest! is the second studio album by American singer and songwriter Jerry Lee Lewis, released on August 15, 1956 by Sun Records. It peaked at number three on the Billboard Top LP's chart.  The album features the singles "Great Balls of Fire" and "Whole Lotta Shakin' Goin' On". It was reissued in 2003 as a two-disc set with the addition of the previously unreleased track "Frenzy".  Jerry Lee Lewis' Greatest! was recorded at Sun Studios in Memphis, Tennessee and was produced by Sam Phillips. It includes covers of older songs, including a version of "Great Balls of Fire" that is notably faster than the original recording by Jerry Lee Lewis. The album also features two newly written songs, including "Frenzy" which was written by Lewis and Buzz Cason.
