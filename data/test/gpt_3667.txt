Honduras, officially the Republic of Honduras, is a country in Central America. It is bordered by Guatemala to the west and north, El Salvador to the northwest, and Nicaragua to the east. To the south lies the Caribbean Sea. The mainland portion of Honduras has a population of around 10 million people, while the population on the islands is about 2 million.   The country is divided into 21 departments. The capital and largest city is Tegucigalpa. The official language is Spanish, although there are also indigenous languages spoken, such as Garifuna and Lenca. Honduras has a rich history and culture, with a number of important historic sites. It was colonized by Spain in the 16th century and became a republic in 1821. After a military coup in 2009, the government was replaced by a military junta led by Lieutenant Colonel Roberto Micheletti. The country is currently in a political crisis, with widespread protests and reports of human rights abuses.
