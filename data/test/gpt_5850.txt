Lake Ngardok is a lake on the Tibet-Nepal border, located in the Mahalangur National Park. It is the deepest lake in the world at a maximum depth of 2,380 metres (8,200 ft). The lake is fed by the Nardak River and has an area of about 1,500 square kilometres (580 sq mi).  Lake Ngardok is considered sacred by the Tibetan people and is also a source of drinking water for the villages close to its shores. The lake is also popular for fishing.
