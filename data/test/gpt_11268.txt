The Bosatlas is a Dutch atlas. Used as a navigation aid and as a source of information on the geography of the world, it has been published continuously since 1570. The latest edition, published in 2002, is over 6,000 pages long.  The Bosatlas is the largest and most comprehensive atlas of its kind. It is divided into sixteen sections, each covering one half of the world. The atlas is supplemented by a number of special volumes, including a world atlas and a geographical dictionary.  The Bosatlas is used by travellers, students, and researchers alike. It has also been popular among cartographers and mapmakers.
