United Nations Water (UN-Water) is an interagency organization working to achieve Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 6 by providing access to water and sanitation for all. UN-Water was created in 2015 as a merger of the World Water Council, the United Nations Development Program (UNDP) and the United Nations Environment Program (UNEP).   The organization has a goal of ensuring that everyone has access to safe, clean, affordable water and sanitation by 2030. To achieve this, UN-Water works with member states and other partners to develop and implement solutions. In addition, UN-Water funds research and innovation to develop new water technologies and strategies.   UN-Water is headquartered in Nairobi, Kenya. It has regional offices in Beijing, Cairo, Dakar, Doha, Geneva, Istanbul, Johannesburg, Madrid, Nairobi, New York City, Paris, Rome and Vienna.
