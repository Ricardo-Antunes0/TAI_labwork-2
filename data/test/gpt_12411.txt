José Espasa Anguera (1840 in La Pobla de Segura, Province of Alicante – 1915 in La Pobla de Segura) was a Spanish writer, journalist and politician.     He is considered one of the fathers of the Spanish language press.  José Espasa Anguera was born in 1840 in La Pobla de Segura, Province of Alicante. He studied law at the University of Valencia, but abandoned his studies to become a journalist. He worked for various newspapers in Valencia and Madrid before founding "El Liberal" in 1881. He was elected to the Cortes Generales in 1879 as a deputy for Alicante, and served until his death in 1915. He is best known for his novels, which portray the life of rural Andalusia during the 19th century.
