Tan Chee Khoon (; 4 March 1919 â€“ 9 February 1995) was a Singaporean poet, critic and writer. He was also known by the pen name Mr. Fong.  Tan Chee Khoon was born in 1919 in Singapore. He attended the Anglo-Chinese School (now the Anglo-Chinese School (Primary)) and later studied at the University of Malaya where he obtained his B.A. in English literature in 1945. After graduating from university, Tan worked as a lecturer at the University of Malaya before moving to Singapore in 1949 to take up a position at the National University of Singapore.  Tan's poetry is noted for its formal experimentation, its exploration of alienation and its use of Chinese vernacular language. He also published several collections of criticism and essays, as well as a novel, The Return of the Condor Heroes (1985). Tan Chee Khoon died on 9 February 1995.
