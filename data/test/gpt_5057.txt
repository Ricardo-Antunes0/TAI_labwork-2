al-Faluja  was a Palestinian Arab village located in the northern Gaza Strip. It was depopulated during the 1948 Arab-Israeli War, and its inhabitants were refugees who fled to neighbouring villages or Israel.   In 1988, al-Faluja was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is now uninhabited, but its ruins are preserved as a historic site.  Al-Faluja was located in the northwestern Gaza Strip, just east of Tel al-Sultan and north of Beit Hanoun. The village had a population of 1,500 in 1945. In 1948, al-Faluja was depopulated during the Israel War of Independence as a result of Operation Dani. Its inhabitants were refugees who fled to neighbouring villages or Israel. The only remnants of the village are its ruined homes and a few farms.
