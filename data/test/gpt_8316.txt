The Eagles are an American rock band. The band was formed in Los Angeles in 1971 by Glenn Frey, Don Henley, Bernie Leadon, and Randy Meisner. The Eagles have released eleven studio albums, three of which have been certified platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA). The band's most successful album is their 1975 self-titled debut, which has sold over 30 million copies worldwide. The Eagles have won five Grammy Awards, four American Music Awards, and an Academy Award for Best Original Song for "The Last Waltz".  The Eagles released their eleventh studio album, "The Long Road Out of Eden", in 2018.
