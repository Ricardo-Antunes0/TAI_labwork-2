He is best known for his discovery of the Kibbey effect, which is a thermodynamic effect that causes a decrease in the entropy of a system as the temperature is increased.  Kibbey was born in Westfield, New Jersey, on March 4, 1853. He received his bachelor's degree from Princeton University in 1876, and his doctorate from the University of Leipzig in 1881.  Kibbey began working at the Smithsonian Institution in 1881 as an assistant to professor A. W. Becquerel. He was promoted to professor of physics and chemistry at Johns Hopkins University in 1902, where he remained until his retirement in 1926.   Kibbey was also a fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences and the Bavarian Academy of Sciences. He died on November 27, 1927, in Baltimore, Maryland.
