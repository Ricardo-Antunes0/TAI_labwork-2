Environment Friend (foaled 19 March 1988) was an American-bred, British-trained Thoroughbred racehorse and sire. He was one of the most successful horses of his generation in Europe and was voted European Horse of the Year for 1998. After retiring from racing he became a successful breeding stallion.  Environment Friend was bred in Kentucky by Robert B. Lewis and raced in the United States. He was bought by Sheikh Mohammed's Godolphin organisation and sent to train in Britain with John Gosden.  He had his biggest success as a two-year-old in 1998 when he won five races including the 2000 Guineas and the Epsom Derby. In the following year, he won the Irish Derby, 2000 Guineas and St Leger. He retired from racing at the end of 2001 with a record of thirty-six wins from sixty-one races.  Environment Friend became a successful breeding stallion, siring many major winners including Visionaire, Best Mate, Union Rags, Distant Relative, Wootton Bassett and Al Kazeem.
