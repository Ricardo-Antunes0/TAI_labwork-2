Richard Bligh (1780-1838), chancery barrister, son of John Bligh of the Bounty, was born in 1780. He entered Gray's Inn in 1800 and was called to the bar in 1806. In 1815 he became a bencher of Gray's Inn.   In 1821 Bligh was appointed recorder of the city of London. He served in this post until 1830, when he resigned on grounds of ill-health. From 1830 until his death in 1838 he practised law at his chambers in Lincoln's Inn Fields.   Richard Bligh was a staunch Tory and served as a commissioner for inquiring into the state of the navy from 1830 to 1832 and from 1835 to 1838. He also served as treasurer of the Royal Academy from 1830 to 1840.   Bligh married, in 1810, Harriet Smith, daughter of William Smith of Branston, Norfolk. They had two sons and three daughters.
