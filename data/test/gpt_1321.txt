Loren Stokes (born November 12, 1983) is an American professional mixed martial artist who competes in the lightweight division of the Ultimate Fighting Championship. A professional competitor since 2006, he has also competed for Strikeforce, Bellator MMA and World Series of Fighting.  Born and raised in San Diego, California, Stokes first attempted MMA at the age of 19 and signed with Strikeforce in 2006. He made his promotional debut against Josh Thomson on November 17, 2006 at Strikeforce: Road to the Championship. After dominating his opponent for three rounds, Stokes was awarded a unanimous decision victory. He continued to impress in his subsequent fights, earning victories over Joe Stevenson, Jorge Masvidal and Robbie Lawler before signing with the UFC in 2013.  Stokes made his UFC debut against Danny Castillo on February 21, 2013 at UFC 146. He won the fight by unanimous decision and was quickly booked to face former lightweight champion Frankie Edgar for the title on May 25, 2013 at UFC 150. Stokes lost the fight via unanimous decision and was subsequently released from the UFC.  Stokes returned to competition in December 2014 when he faced promotional newcomer John Dodson at Bellator 158. He won the fight via TKO in the second round and was subsequently signed by the promotion. He made his promotional debut for Bellator against Phil Davis on May 3, 2015 at Bellator 172. He won the fight via knockout in the first round and was subsequently awarded a title shot against Benson Henderson for Bellator's lightweight championship on November 19
