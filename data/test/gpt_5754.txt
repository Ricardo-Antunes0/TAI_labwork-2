Fabrizio Carbone (born  in Novi Ligure, Piedmont, Italy, on September 21, 1965) is an Italian composer.  Carbone's music is based on the principle of polyphony and aleatoric techniques. He has developed a distinctive style that incorporates elements of post-minimalism, minimalism, and electroacoustic music.   His compositions have been performed by leading ensembles such as the Berlin Philharmonic, the Royal Philharmonic Orchestra, the London Symphony Orchestra, and the Orchestre National de France. In 2001 he was awarded the prestigious Prize of the City of Turin.
