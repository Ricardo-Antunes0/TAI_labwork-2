Greggory D. "Poncho" Northington (1948 – February 4, 2003) was an American musician, singer and songwriter. He was the founder and frontman of the band Poco. Northington was also known for his work as a record producer, most notably for the band Eagles.  Northington was born in 1948 in Biloxi, Mississippi. He formed Poco with guitarist David Crosby in 1975, and the band released their debut album in 1977. They went on to have success with a string of albums including "Bird on the Wire" (1980), "Peaceful Easy Feeling" (1981), "New Mornings" (1983), and "Universal Truths and Cycles" (1988). Northington produced several albums for the band, including their greatest hits compilation "Greatest Hits 1971–1991" (1991). He also collaborated with other musicians, including Richard Thompson, Emmylou Harris and Linda Ronstadt.  Northington died of a heart attack in 2003 at the age of 54.
