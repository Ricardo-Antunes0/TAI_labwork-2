Shelley Chaplin (born 4 September 1984) is an English actress who is known for her roles in The Railway Man, Dark Shadows and Frankenstein.  Shelley Chaplin was born in London, England, on 4 September 1984. She is the youngest of three children. Chaplin's father is a film producer and her mother is a psychiatric nurse. She has two older brothers. Chaplin attended the Central School of Speech and Drama in London, graduating in 2002.  Chaplin's first role was in the 2003 BBC television film The Company of Wolves. She went on to appear in several other television productions and films before landing her breakout role as Lucy North in the 2006 BBC drama series The Railway Man. Chaplin starred as Elizabeth I in the 2009 drama film Dark Shadows, for which she was nominated for an Independent Spirit Award for Best Lead Actress. In 2012, she starred as Dr. Jeannette Kwon in the horror film Frankenstein. Chaplin has also appeared in several stage productions, including The Tempest and A Midsummer Night's Dream.
