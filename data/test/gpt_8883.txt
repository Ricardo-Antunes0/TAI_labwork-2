Fat Les was a British band consisting of bassist Les Claypool, drummer Sean Kinney, guitarist Larry LaLonde and singer/guitarist Pat Smear.  The band formed in 1987 in Los Angeles and released its debut album "Featuring Les Claypool" in 1989. The album was recorded at Sound City Studios with producer Dave Mustaine. The follow-up album, "Trucker", was also recorded at Sound City and was released in 1992. The band toured extensively in support of Trucker, including a performance at the 1992 Lollapalooza festival.  In 1994, the band released its third album "Pork Soda". The album was recorded at the legendary Sweetwater Sound Studios in Austin, Texas and featured guest performances from Primus' Les Claypool and Tom Morello of Rage Against the Machine. The album spawned two singles: "Captain Claymore's Pirate Treasure" and "Mexican Hat Dance".  The band went on hiatus after touring for Pork Soda in support of its release. Les Claypool formed Frog Brigade with drummer Sean Kinney, guitarist Larry LaLonde and bassist Tim Alexander. Frog Brigade released its first album, "Pork Soda", in 2005.  Les Claypool reformed Fat Les with new members Pat Smear (vocals) and Dave Dreiwitz (guitar) in 2009 and toured extensively in support of their self-titled debut album, which was released in 2010.
