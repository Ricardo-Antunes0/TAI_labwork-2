Gustave Samuel James de Rothschild, Baron de Rothschild (1844–1924) was a French banker and philanthropist. He was the founder of the Rothschild banking dynasty.  Rothschild was born in Paris to a Jewish family. He entered the banking industry in 1868 and became a partner of his father's bank, the de Rothschild Frères. In 1892 he founded his own bank, the Banque de France, which became one of the largest in Europe. He also became a major investor in railways, coal mines, and other industrial enterprises.  Rothschild was a major benefactor of Jewish causes throughout his life. He helped create the first Jewish university in France and donated money to build synagogues and other Jewish institutions throughout Europe. He also funded medical research and charitable efforts among the poor.  Despite his wealth, Rothschild was known for his humility and sense of social justice. He was one of the founders of the French Red Cross and served as its president from 1917 to 1924. He also served as president of the World Zionist Organization from 1920 to 1924.  Rothschild died in 1924 at the age of 83. He was buried in the Père Lachaise Cemetery in Paris.
