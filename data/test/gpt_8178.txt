After studying at the Lycée Janson de Sailly and the University of Paris, he became a lawyer in 1909. Briand served in World War I as a lieutenant colonel and was wounded several times. In 1920 he was elected to the French National Assembly, where he became known for his opposition to military intervention in foreign wars and his support for economic reform. As Foreign Minister, Briand led France's efforts to conclude the Treaty of Trianon, which ended the Austro-Hungarian Empire. He also negotiated the Treaty of Lausanne, which ended World War I and established the League of Nations.
