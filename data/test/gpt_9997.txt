Professor Deborah Anne Swallow (born 27 August 1953) is a British philosopher and author. She is a professor of philosophy at the University of Oxford, a fellow of Wadham College, Oxford, and a research fellow of All Souls College, Oxford.  Swallow was born in London, England and educated at St Marylebone Grammar School for Girls, the University of Cambridge, and the University of Oxford. She was a research fellow at Balliol College, Oxford from 1987 to 1990. In 1990 she became a professor of philosophy at the University of Oxford. She has held visiting appointments at Duke University (2003), the University of California at Berkeley (2005), and the Université de Montréal (2006).  Swallow's work has focused on ethics and moral philosophy, with a particular interest in practical ethics. Her works include books on free will, moral responsibility, and practical reason. She has also written articles on various philosophical topics.  Swallow was appointed Commander of the Order of the British Empire (CBE) in the 2010 New Year Honours for services to philosophy.
