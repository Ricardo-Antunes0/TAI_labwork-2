David Lee Freedman (born 12 August 1956) is an English composer. He is known for his work in popular music, film and television.  Freedman was born in London, England, to a Jewish family. He studied composition at the Royal Academy of Music where he was awarded a B.Mus. degree in 1979. In 1984, he won the BBC Young Musician of the Year Award and the following year he was made a Fellow of the Royal College of Music.  Freedman's early works were influenced by composers such as John Cage, Philip Glass and Terry Riley. He made his debut as a composer with the opera "The American Clock" (1987), which was later staged at the Royal Opera House. Since then, Freedman has composed music for film, television and opera. His notable works include the scores for "The Piano" (1996), "The Hunchback of Notre Dame" (1996), "Titanic" (1997), "Batman & Robin" (1997), "The Lord of the Rings: The Two Towers" (2002), "King Kong" (2005), "The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, The Witch and The Wardrobe" (2006) and "Sherlock Holmes: A Game of Shadows" (2011). In 2014, Freedman composed the score for the sci-fi film "Ex Machina".
