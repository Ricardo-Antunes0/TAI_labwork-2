Sugarfoot is an American Western television series that aired on NBC from September 18, 1971, to May 16, 1973. Created by Roy Huggins, the show starred Peter Fonda as Tom Sugarfoot, a Native American cowboy and lawman in the Old West.   The series is set in the fictional town of Bridger's Pass, located in the Big Horn Mountains of Wyoming. Sugarfoot was one of the most popular series on NBC during its five-year run. The show won two Emmy Awards for Outstanding Western Series and Fonda was nominated for an Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Drama Series.
