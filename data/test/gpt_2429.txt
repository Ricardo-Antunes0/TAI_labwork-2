This article contains information about the literary events and publications of 1636.  In 1636, the year of Shakespeare's death, the English Renaissance peaked. The English playwright and poet William Shakespeare had died the previous year and the English Renaissance was in full swing. The era saw a flowering of literary talent in England, with many new authors publishing their works. Many new plays were written, including Christopher Marlowe's "Dracula" and Ben Jonson's "The Alchemist". Poets such as Andrew Marvell and John Donne published their first poems, while Edmund Spenser completed his epic poem "The Faerie Queene". The year also saw the publication of the first book of the English novel "Pride and Prejudice" by Jane Austen.
