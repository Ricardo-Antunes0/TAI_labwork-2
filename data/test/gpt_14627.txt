Mahmut Yıldırım (born 1951, Yenidal, Solhan, Bingöl Province) is a Turkish politician who has been the Prime Minister of Turkey since 2003.  He previously served as Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Finance from 1997 to 2003. He also served as the Minister of Energy from 1994 to 1997.  Born in 1951, Mahmut Yıldırım graduated from the Faculty of Economics at the University of Ankara in 1975. After working as an economist for a year, he started his political career as a member of the Republican People's Party (CHP) in 1976. He was elected to Parliament in 1980, and became Deputy Prime Minister in 1997.  Yıldırım was elected Prime Minister on 10 March 2003, after being nominated by the CHP. His first term as Prime Minister ended on 7 November 2007; he was re-elected on 6 November 2008. On 24 May 2011, he announced his resignation as Prime Minister after being re-elected for a second term on 6 November 2011. He resigned on 14 August 2013 following the May 2012 constitutional referendum and was succeeded by Ahmet Davutoğlu.
