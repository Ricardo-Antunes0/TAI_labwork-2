The Chittenden Hotel was a hotel building in downtown Burlington, Vermont. It was located on the northeast corner of Main and South Streets. The hotel was built in 1907 and closed in 1988.  The Chittenden Hotel was a Burlington landmark for over seventy years. The building was constructed in 1907 and served as the main hotel for Burlington during that time. It was known for its grandeur and its proximity to many of the city's attractions. The Chittenden Hotel closed in 1988 due to financial problems and was later demolished.
