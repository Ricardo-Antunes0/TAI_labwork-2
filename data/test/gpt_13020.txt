Jarrod Scott Saltalamacchia (; born May 2, 1983) is an American professional baseball first baseman for the Miami Marlins of Major League Baseball (MLB). He previously played for the Boston Red Sox, Kansas City Royals, and New York Yankees.  Saltalamacchia was drafted by the Red Sox in the third round of the 2002 MLB draft. He made his MLB debut with Boston in 2006, and became a regular player in 2007. Saltalamacchia was traded to the Royals in 2011, and then to the Yankees in 2012. In 2014, he won his first career Gold Glove Award. Saltalamacchia has been a member of the American League (AL) All-Star Team four times (2007, 2013, 2014, 2016). He also won an International League (IL) Player of the Year Award in 2005 with the Durham Bulls.  Saltalamacchia was born and raised in Fort Myers, Florida. He attended Lee County High School and played college baseball at Southern Methodist University (SMU). As a freshman at SMU, he set a school record with 38 home runs.
