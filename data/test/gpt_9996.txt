Russell "Slim Gray" Gibson (died January 8, 2009) was an American blues and soul singer, guitarist and songwriter.  Gibson was born in Montgomery, Alabama, in 1936, the son of a sharecropper. He began playing guitar at the age of nine and started singing at the age of fifteen. He moved to Chicago in the early 1960s and played with Muddy Waters, Howlin' Wolf and Willie Dixon. In 1972 he released his debut album, "Slim Gray's Blues", which was produced by Willie Dixon. Gibson died in 2009.
