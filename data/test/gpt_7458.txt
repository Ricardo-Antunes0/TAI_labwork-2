Ramaipur is a village in Bhadohi district, Uttar Pradesh, India. It is located about 8 km from Bhadohi town on the NH-2. Ramaipur is known for its Shiva temple, which is said to be one of the most important temples in the state.  Ramaipur was founded in the 12th century by an immigrant group from present-day Gujarat. The village is named after Rama, the Hindu god of myth and legend. The population of Ramaipur is about 2,000. The economy of Ramaipur is based on agriculture, especially wheat and sugarcane. There are also a number of small businesses in the village. The main sources of income are agriculture and employment in nearby towns.
