He is best known for his History of the Byzantine Empire, which is a significant source for the history of the Eastern Roman Empire in the late 7th and early 8th centuries.  Niketas was born in Constantinople in about 680. He was appointed governor of Armeniakon in 729 by Pope Gregory III, and held the post until 731. In 737, he was appointed governor of the theme of Thrace, and held this office until 741. His History of the Byzantine Empire covers the period from 681 to 741.
