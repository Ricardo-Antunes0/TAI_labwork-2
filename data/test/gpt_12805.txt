Anjali Monteiro is a documentary filmmaker, media artist, and activist. She is the co-director of the Media Education Project, an artist and filmmaker collective based in New York City.  Anjali Monteiro was born in Mumbai, India, in 1978. She grew up in a middle-class family and attended a Catholic convent school. She completed her undergraduate studies at the University of Mumbai, where she majored in visual arts.  In 2001, Monteiro moved to New York City to study documentary filmmaking at New York University's Tisch School of the Arts. After graduating in 2005, she worked as a creative director for a digital media company before founding the Media Education Project in 2007.  Monteiro's films explore social issues through creative media platforms such as filmmaking, photography, performance art, and interactive installations. Her work has been screened at festivals around the world, including the Sundance Film Festival, Berlin International Film Festival, and Toronto International Film Festival. In 2017, she was awarded the Order of Canada for her contributions to arts and social justice.
