Juan José Arreola Zúñiga (September 21, 1918 – February 2, 2006) was a Mexican actor and director. He is known for his roles in the telenovelas Por Amor... por Dios (1965) and La Pasionaria (1981), as well as for his work on the film El ángel de la muerte (1988).  Arreola was born in Puebla, Mexico, to a poor family. He started working at a young age, selling peanuts and candy on the street. After serving in the Mexican Army during World War II, he began his acting career in 1951. He began his television career in 1961 with a role on the series El mundo de los cantantes. He became internationally known for his roles in the telenovelas Por amor... por Dios (1965) and La pasionaria (1981). In 1988, he won an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor for his performance in El ángel de la muerte. Arreola also directed several films, including Los borrachos (1980), La madrastra (1983), and La hija del rey (1994). He died of heart failure in 2006 at the age of 88.
