"Ukridge's Dog College" is a short story written by C.S. Lewis and first published in the magazine "The Observer" in 1953.  The story is about a dog named Ukridge who goes to a dog college to learn how to be a better dog. At the college, Ukridge meets other dogs who teach him how to do things like fetch and sit. Eventually, Ukridge becomes a skilled dog and can do everything his classmates can do.
