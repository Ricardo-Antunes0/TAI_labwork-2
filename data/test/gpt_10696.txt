William "Bill" J. Crins (born 1955) is an American mathematician and physicist who has made significant contributions to the theory of partial differential equations, global optimization and potential theory.  Crins received his B.A. in mathematics from the University of California, Santa Barbara in 1977, and his Ph.D. from Yale University in 1981 under the supervision of Edwin D. Land. He has been a professor of mathematics at Brown University since 1992, and was awarded the prestigious Wolf Prize in Mathematics in 2013.
