David Klahr (born 1939) is an American mathematician and computer scientist.  He is the author of several books including "The Art of Computer Programming", "The Elements of Computing Systems", and "Computer Organization and Design". He also co-authored the textbook "Computer Science: An Introduction" with Dennis Ritchie.  Klahr was a professor at UC Berkeley from 1977 until his retirement in 2002. He won the ACM Turing Award in 1984, and the National Academy of Sciences Award in 1987.
