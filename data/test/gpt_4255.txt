Hollywood Squares (originally The Hollywood Squares) is an American television game show that aired on CBS from September 15, 1961, to May 17, 1965. The show was created by Sid Sheinberg and produced by Aaron Spelling.  The show is a panel game in which contestants try to guess the answers to questions about classic Hollywood movies. The contestant who guesses the most questions correctly wins money and a trip to Hollywood. The show ran for 139 episodes over five seasons.  Hollywood Squares was one of the first television programs to feature computer-generated graphics and set design.
