Chen Jitong (; 1851–1907), courtesy name Jingrui (景如), was a Chinese educator and writer. He served as the president of Peking University from 1902 to 1907.  Chen Jitong was born in 1851 in Taicang, Jiangsu province. After completing his education at the Taicang High School and then at the Jiangsu Provincial Normal School, he became a teacher in 1871. In 1884 he became a professor at the Jiangsu Provincial Teachers' College and in 1896 he was appointed president of the Peking University. He served in that position until his death in 1907. Chen Jitong was one of the most influential educators of his time, and he made significant contributions to Chinese education theory and practice.
