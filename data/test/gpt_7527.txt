Agni Deo Singh is an Indo-Fijian teacher, religious leader and author from the island of Viti Levu, Fiji. Singh is the founder and head of the Fiji-based Hindu organisation, the Satguru Satsang Sabha.  Agni Deo Singh was born in 1965 in the village of Talepur, on the island of Viti Levu, Fiji. He attended local schools before studying at the University of the South Pacific in Suva, Fiji.   In 1990, Singh founded the Satguru Satsang Sabha, a Hindu religious organisation based in Fiji. The Sabha provides religious services and education to Hindus in Fiji and neighbouring islands. Singh also authored several books on Hinduism. In 2006, he was awarded an honorary doctorate by Massey University in Auckland, New Zealand.   Singh has been criticised by some sections of the Fiji media for his alleged links to religious extremism and violence. However, he has also been praised for his work promoting diversity and harmony among Fiji's Hindu community. In December 2014, Singh was hospitalised after suffering a stroke. He remains active in his religious organisation and writing career.
