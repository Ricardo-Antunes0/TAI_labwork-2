The Moray Coast Railway was a heavy railway line in north-east Scotland. It connected Elgin and Inverness with Aberdeen, via Forres and Nairn. Construction of the line started in 1885 and the first section, between Inverness and Forres, opened on 25 October 1887. The entire line was opened on 1 July 1890.   The Moray Coast Railway was one of the most important railways in Scotland. It played a vital role in the development of north-east Scotland, linking the major towns and cities along its length. The line was also important for freight, carrying coal, iron ore, and other minerals to the ports of Aberdeen and Elgin. The railway was closed on 31 December 1973.
