Harold George Bryant Davenport (January 19, 1866August 8, 1943) was an American actor and stage manager. He is best remembered for his roles in several early 20th century Hollywood films, notably as the title character in The Hunchback of Notre Dame (1915), and as Mr. Potter in The Wizard of Oz (1939).  Davenport was born in San Francisco, California, the son of Francis Marion Davenport and his wife, the former Maria Louisa Bryant. He had a younger brother, Franklin Bryant Davenport. After their father's death in 1872, the boys were raised by their mother and stepfather, the Rev. Charles Edson Davenport. Davenport attended the University of California at Berkeley, where he joined the Delta Phi Epsilon fraternity.  In 1892, Davenport made his Broadway debut in "The Sign of Four". Two years later he appeared in "The Squaw Man". In 1897 he appeared in a production of Henrik Ibsen's play "Peer Gynt" at New York's Broadway Theatre. In 1902 he starred with Lillian Gish in "Gambit" at Chicago's Grand Opera House. That same year he also starred with May Robson in "The Lady Eve" at Newark, New Jersey's Clarendon Theatre.
