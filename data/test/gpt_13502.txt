Willem de Rooij (born 1969 in Beverwijk, Netherlands) is a Dutch artist who lives and works in Amsterdam.  Willem de Rooij studied at the Royal Academy of Art in The Hague from 1991 to 1995. His work often deals with the relationship between people and nature, and often employs a poetic, Symbolist approach. He has exhibited in many museums, including the Stedelijk Museum Amsterdam, the Museum of Modern Art in New York City, and the Palazzo Grassi in Venice. In 2003, he was the recipient of the Pritzker Prize.
