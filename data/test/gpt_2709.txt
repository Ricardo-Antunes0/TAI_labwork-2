Deadwood is an American Western television series created, written, and directed by David Milch. It premiered on March 22, 2004, on the cable network HBO. Deadwood is set in the fictional town of Deadwood, South Dakota, in the early 1870s during the time of the Dakota War of 1876.  The series follows the lives of a disparate group of characters in Deadwood, most notably the ambitious sheriff Seth Bullock and his deputy Al Swearengen. Other prominent characters include outlaws Wild Bill Hickok and George Hearst, saloon owner Jack Tripper, and prostitute Calamity Jane. The series was noted for its explicit language, graphic violence, and depiction of sexual content.  Deadwood was critically acclaimed and won several awards including two Primetime Emmy Awards for Outstanding Drama Series in 2005 and 2006, as well as three Golden Globe Awards for Best Television Series – Drama – including one award for Best Series (2005). The series finale aired on May 21, 2006.
