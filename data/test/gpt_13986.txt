Sir Julius Chan  (born 29 August 1952) is a Hong Kong entrepreneur, investor, and philanthropist. He is the co-founder and Chairman of the Chan Group, a conglomerate with interests in property, logistics, telecommunications, and entertainment.  Born in 1952 in Hong Kong, Chan attended the University of California, Berkeley where he received a B.A. in political science and economics in 1975. He later received an M.B.A. from the Harvard Business School in 1978.  Chan started his career as an investment banker at Salomon Brothers in 1978. In 1984, he co-founded the Chan Group with his brother Irwin Chan. The company quickly became one of Hong Kong's leading conglomerates with interests in property, logistics, telecommunications, and entertainment. In 2006, Forbes listed the Chan Group as Hong Kong's fifth largest private enterprise with a net worth of US$5.5 billion.  Chan has been involved in a number of philanthropic activities including donations to charity organizations such as Oxfam and the Starlight Children's Foundation. He has also served on the boards of several major institutions including HSBC Holdings and The University of Hong Kong. In 2003, he was knighted by Queen Elizabeth II for services to business and philanthropy.  Sir Julius Chan was born on 29 August 1952 in Hong Kong to parents of Hakka Chinese descent. He attended University of California at Berkeley where he received a BBA degree in Political Science and Economics in 1975 and an MBA from Harvard Business School in 1978.
