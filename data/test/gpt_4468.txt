Recycling codes are used to identify the materials that can be recycled from different types of waste.  The recycling codes are a system developed by the recycling industry to help identify the materials that can be recycled from different types of waste. The codes are arranged in a hierarchy, beginning with the most environmentally destructive materials and working their way down to more sustainable options.   The recycling codes are not legally binding, but they are widely used by municipalities and recycling facilities across the world. They are also featured on most recycling labels and posters.
