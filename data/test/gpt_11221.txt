He was a specialist in sprint races and won eight of them, including the 1990 Preakness Stakes and the Breeders' Cup Sprint.  Born in Lexington, Kentucky, Alphabet Soup was trained by Lou LaBarba and ridden in most of his races by jockey Jerry Bailey. He was retired to stud at the end of his racing career and had some success as a breeding stallion.  Alphabet Soup was a very versatilehorse, winning races from sprint to mile. He was also successful over dirt tracks.  Alphabet Soup was euthanized on October 10, 2007 at the age of 21 after a long battle with colic.
