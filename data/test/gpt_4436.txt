Audrey Teuki Teupuariki Tuioti Brown-Pereira (born 1975) is a New Zealand Māori artist who is known for her intricate and detailed kaumatua carvings.  Audrey Brown-Pereira was born in 1975 in Whanganui, New Zealand. She is of Māori descent, and her family is from the Ngati Porou iwi. Audrey began carving wood at an early age and studied kaumatua carving under her uncle, Pene Teupuariki Tuioti, in the 1990s.  Audrey's carvings are highly intricate and detailed, and often depict traditional Māori symbols and stories. Her work has been exhibited in New Zealand and overseas, and she has received several awards and recognition for her art. In 2013, she was appointed a Member of the New Zealand Order of Merit for services to art.
