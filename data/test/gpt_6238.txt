Anthony Dominick Benedetto (born August 3, 1926), known professionally as Tony Bennett, is an American singer, songwriter, and actor. He began his career in the late 1940s as a jazz pianist and vocalist. Bennett has been awarded five gold records and has sold over 150 million records worldwide, making him one of the world's best-selling music artists of all time.  Bennett achieved widespread fame for his performances of traditional pop songs from the 1950s and 1960s, later adding new interpretations of American pop standards and patriotic songs to his repertoire. His first #1 album was "The Complete Capitol Recordings" (1956), which featured such hit singles as "I Left My Heart in San Francisco", "The Lady Is a Tramp", and "Wonderful World". He followed this up with a series of successful albums including "Tony Bennett Sings Great Songs from the Great White Way" (1957), "Tony Bennett & His Friends" (1958), "Tony Bennett Duets" (1960), and "Tony Bennett Christmas Album" (1965). In addition, he had several successful collaborations with fellow singers such as Doris Day, Patti Page, and Ella Fitzgerald.  In 1968, Bennett recorded an album of French-language versions of popular American songs which became his first international hit. He continued to tour throughout the 1970s and 1980s, occasionally performing in Las Vegas and headlining shows at London's Royal Albert Hall.
