Kenneth Kimmins (born September 4, 1941) is an American historian who specializes in the history of science and technology.  Born in Baltimore, Maryland, Kimmins attended the University of Maryland, where he earned his B.A. in 1963 and his M.A. in 1965. He then went on to receive his Ph.D. from the University of California, Berkeley, in 1968.  Kimmins has been a professor at the University of California, Santa Barbara since 1968, where he has served as chairman of the Department of History and Science from 1989 to 1995 and as interim dean of the School of Arts and Sciences from 1998 to 1999. He has also been a visiting professor at numerous universities around the world, including the University of Cambridge (England), the Université de Montréal (Canada), and Kyoto University (Japan).  Kimmins is the author or editor of more than twenty books, including "Science at the Crossroads: Perspectives on Technology in History" (1978), "The Scientific Revolution" (1981), "Scientific American Encyclopedia: Technology" (1988), "Higgs Boson: A History" (2012), and "Inventing Science: A History of Science in American Culture" (2013). He has also written numerous articles for journals such as "Historia", " Isis", " Technology and Culture", and "Journal of American History".
