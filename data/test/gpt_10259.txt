The Caspian whipsnake is endemic to the Caspian Sea region.  The Caspian whipsnake is found in Azerbaijan, Iran, Kazakhstan, Russia and Turkmenistan. Its range includes the plains and foothills of the Caucasus Mountains as well as steppes and open grasslands on the shores of the Caspian Sea. It inhabits sandy areas near water, including estuaries and marshes. The Caspian whipsnake is a nocturnal animal that feeds on small mammals, such as rats and mice. It is capable of rapid movement, and can strike at prey up to 30 cm (12 in) long.
