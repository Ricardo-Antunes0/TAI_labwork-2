The Ohuhu clan of Umuahia north in the Eastern Region are a group of about 1,000 people. They are believed to have migrated from the Greater Ohuhu area about 100 years ago.  The Ohuhu clan trace their history back to about 100 years ago when they migrated from the Greater Ohuhu area. They speak the Ohuhu language, which is a member of the Igbo language family. The clan is divided into three sections: the Ibi, Ozuzu and Ukwu clans. The Ohuhu people are predominantly agriculturalists and traders. They are also skilled in basket-making, pottery and metalworking.
