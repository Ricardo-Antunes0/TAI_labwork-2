Common names: inland carpet python, Murray Darling carpet python  The Morelia spilota metcalfei (commonly known as the inland carpet python or Murray Darling carpet python) is a species of python found in eastern Australia. The snake is the longest of all Australian snakes, with individuals reaching lengths of over two metres (six feet). It is the only species in the genus Morelia.  The inland carpet python is endemic to eastern Australia, where it is found in the states of New South Wales, Victoria, Queensland and South Australia. It prefers semi-arid areas with plentiful vegetation, and is also found in some wetter areas. The snake is shy and elusive, and spends most of its time underground or in hiding. It feeds on small mammals and reptiles, and can live for up to 25 years.
