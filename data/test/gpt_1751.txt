Sefton Coast is a 4605.3 hectare (11379.9 acre) coastal area of North West England, stretching from the Irish Sea to the Mersey estuary.  The Sefton Coast, which includes the towns of Liverpool, Birkenhead and Bootle, is a popular tourist destination for its natural beauty and history. The area is known for its wide sandy beaches, crystal clear waters, rolling hills and woodland. There are several golf courses and resorts located on the coast, including Royal Birkdale and Crosby.  The Sefton Coast is also home to a large number of factories and businesses, including Ford's Dagenham plant, GKN's Balby plant, Honda's Liverpool factory, BMW's Bridgend plant and Airbus' Halton plant. The area has a population of around 269,000 people.
