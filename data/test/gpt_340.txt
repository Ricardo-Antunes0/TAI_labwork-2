Laura Halford (born 25 February 1996) is an English singer and songwriter. Halford first emerged on the music scene in 2012 when she was a runner-up on the UK reality singing competition The X Factor. Her debut single "Sucker" was released in November 2013, and reached number one on the UK Singles Chart. She has since released two further singles, "Better Man" and "Hands On Me".  Halford was born in Doncaster, South Yorkshire, England, to an English father and a Scottish mother. Halford began singing at a young age, and first performed publicly at the age of seven when she took part in a talent show. Halford later attended Doncaster Grammar School and then the University of Sheffield where she studied music technology. In 2012, Halford auditioned for the UK reality singing competition The X Factor and became runner-up behind eventual winner Leona Lewis.   Halford's debut single "Sucker" was released in November 2013 and reached number one on the UK Singles Chart. The song has been certified quadruple platinum by the British Phonographic Industry (BPI). Halford's follow-up singles "Better Man" and "Hands On Me" both charted within the top 20 of the UK Singles Chart. In December 2017, it was announced that Halford would be joining British boy band One Direction on their forthcoming world tour as part of their new opening act.
