Trinity Chapel, also known as St. John's Chapel, is a historic Episcopal church on the north side of Queens in New York City. It is located at the junction of Queens Boulevard and 71st Avenue, about three blocks west of the Queensboro Bridge.  The parish was founded in 1848 as Trinity Parish, with John Ogden as its first rector. Frederic Church, a Methodist church on the same block, merged with Trinity Parish in 1875. The current building was designed by Samuel Sloan and was completed in 1912. The parish has been designated a National Historic Landmark.
