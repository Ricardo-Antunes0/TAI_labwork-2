Dear Monsters is the third studio album by American rock band Foo Fighters. It was released on June 2, 2005, by RCA Records.  The album was produced by Butch Vig and Greg Kurstin, who had produced the band's two previous albums. The album is influenced by 1970s rock music, with a heavy reliance on synthesizers and drum machines. Upon release, Dear Monsters received generally favorable reviews from music critics, who praised its sound and the band's experimentation. The album reached number two on the US Billboard 200 chart and was certified platinum by the RIAA.  The album has been nominated for a Grammy Award for Best Rock Album in 2006. In support of the album, Foo Fighters embarked on a world tour in 2005. The tour extended into 2006 and ended with a performance at the Glastonbury Festival in England.
