Carl Crow (1884–1945) was a Highland, Missouri-born American painter, printmaker and sculptor. He is noted for his depictions of the American West, and for his depictions of Native Americans.  Carl Crow was born in Highland, Missouri, on October 12, 1884. He was the son of Danish immigrants Knud and Ane (Nielsen) Crow. Crow studied art at the Kansas City Art Institute (now the University of Missouri-Kansas City) and the Académie Julian in Paris, France.  Crow was a leading artist of the American West during the first half of the 20th century. He is noted for his depictions of the American landscape, particularly in his paintings of the Rocky Mountains and the Great Plains. Crow also produced numerous sculptures, including a number of works depicting Native Americans. Crow died on March 5, 1945 in California.
