The Kitab al-Bulhan , or Book of Wonders, is an Arabic book on mathematics and astronomy written in the tenth century by al-Khwarizmi.  The Kitab al-Bulhan is an important text in the history of mathematics and astronomy. It was written by al-Khwarizmi, a Muslim mathematician and astronomer, in the tenth century. The book covers a wide range of topics in mathematics and astronomy, including spherical trigonometry, lunar theory, and methods for calculating astronomical dates. The book is also notable for its treatment of algebra, which at the time was a relatively new topic.
