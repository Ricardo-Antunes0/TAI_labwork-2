The greater long-nosed bat or Mexican long-nosed bat (Nycteris anyrensis) is a bat species in the family Phyllostomidae. It is found in Mexico and Central America.  The greater long-nosed bat is the largest of the three Mexican bats. Adults measure 23 to 30 cm in length, with a wingspan of up to 45 cm. They have a broad nose and elongated ears, which are black or brown and have conspicuous white tips. Their fur is light brown to buff, with a dark brown or black-velvet dorsal stripe that extends from behind their ears to their shoulders. They feed on insects, fruit, and nectar, and are nocturnal.
