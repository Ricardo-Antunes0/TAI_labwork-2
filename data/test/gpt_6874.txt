The Brabham BT46 is a Formula One car which was designed by Gordon Murray and built by Brabham between 1966 and 1968.  The BT46 is a mid-engined, open-wheeled race car designed by British engineer Gordon Murray. It was one of the most successful Formula One cars of its era, winning 11 Grands Prix and earning multiple drivers' and constructors' championships.  The BT46's success stemmed from its innovative design, which made it the first car to use a carbon-fiber monocoque chassis. The BT46 also had a powerful, but lightweight, Renault engine.
