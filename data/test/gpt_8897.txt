The New England Patriots are a professional American football team based in Foxborough, Massachusetts. The Patriots compete in the National Football League (NFL) as a member club of the league's American Football Conference (AFC) East division. The Patriots were founded in 1960 as the Boston Patriots, and play their home games at Gillette Stadium in Foxborough, Massachusetts.  The Patriots are one of the most successful NFL franchises in terms of overall wins and losses. As of the end of the 2018 season, they have won more than 600 games and lost just under 400 games. They have also appeared in more AFC championship games (11) than any other team, and have won eight of them. The Patriots have also won five Super Bowl titles, which is second only to the Green Bay Packers' six. The Patriots are one of only two NFL teams to have won five Super Bowls in a six-year period; the other team is the Packers.  The franchise was founded by Billy Sullivan and Bob Kraft, two friends who met at Yale University. After playing together as members of the Yale Bulldogs football team, Sullivan and Kraft began scouting for a new professional football team. They found an old barn in Foxborough that would serve as the team's practice facility and called themselves the Boston Patriots.
