Sheldon tapestries are a type of British tapestry, produced at Britain's first commercial textile mills in the early 18th century. The name comes from the town of Sheldon in Nottinghamshire, where the first mills were built. The term "Sheldon tapestry" typically refers to the series of six large tapestries woven between 1724 and 1730 by Nicholas Hilliard and his assistants.
