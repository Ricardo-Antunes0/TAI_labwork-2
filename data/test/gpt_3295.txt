"The Cure" is a song recorded by the English rock band the Cure. It was released as the lead single from the group's eighth studio album, "1977", on 5 September 1977, in the United Kingdom.  The song was written by Robert Smith and released as a 7-inch single in the UK on 15 February 1977. The B-side of the single was "In Between Days". The song reached number 45 on the UK Singles Chart.   The song was later included on the band's first compilation album, "Three Imaginary Boys" (1979), and their second compilation album, "Greatest Hits" (1982). The song has also been featured on several of their live albums, including "Live at the Apollo" (1977), "Live at Leeds" (1978), "Live at Reading" (1980), and "Live at Royal Albert Hall" (2007).   The song has been covered by a number of artists over the years, including Siouxsie and the Banshees, Curve, Bauhaus, and Joy Division. The Cure performed a live version of the song for BBC Radio 1's Live Lounge in 2009.  The song was remastered and re-released as a digital download on 3 October 2009 as part of Record Store Day. This remaster featured updated sound quality and an extended remix by British electronic music producer Cauterize.
