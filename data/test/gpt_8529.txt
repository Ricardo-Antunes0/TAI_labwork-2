The Moniebogue Press was an "alternative" newspaper in Moniebogue, New York, United States, from 1978 to 1985. It was published on a monthly basis and was distributed primarily in the town of Moniebogue and the nearby villages of Hamptonburgh and Whitehall.  The Moniebogue Press was founded by Tom Robbins in 1978. The paper was initially published on a monthly basis, but increased its frequency to bimonthly in 1979. It was distributed primarily in the town of Moniebogue and the nearby villages of Hamptonburgh and Whitehall. The paper had a circulation of approximately 1,000 copies.  In 1985, the Moniebogue Press ceased publication due to financial difficulties.
