Roland Arpin (Montréal, Rosemont, 27 April 1934) is a Canadian composer and conductor.  Arpin studied music at the University of Montreal with André Brassens and Franco Donatoni. He later continued his studies in Berlin with Prof. Heinz Holliger and at the University of Cologne where he was a pupil of Hans Rosbaud. He has also been a guest professor at the universities of Cologne, Tübingen, Freiburg, and Vienna. Arpin has been the artistic director of several Canadian orchestras, including the Orchestre national de Montréal, the Orchestre symphonique de Québec, and the Edmonton Symphony Orchestra. He has won numerous awards, including the Prix Charles-Cros and the Grand Prix de la Fondation Gérard-Delannoy. In 2009 he was made an Officer of the Order of Canada.
