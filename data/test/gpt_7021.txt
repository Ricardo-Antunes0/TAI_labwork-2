Franco Mormando (born 17 August 1955) is an Italian actor, film director and screenwriter. He has appeared in more than fifty films since 1976.  Mormando was born in Piacenza, Emilia-Romagna. He is the son of a sculptor and painter, Romolo Mormando, and the brother of the actor Dario Mormando. He studied at the Accademia di Belle Arti di Parma, where he was a student of Enzo Biagi.  Mormando began his acting career on stage, appearing in productions such as Carlo Goldoni's "The Barber of Seville" and Luigi Pirandello's "Six Characters in Search of an Author". He made his cinematic debut in 1976 with the film "Ilaria e le mie compagne". In the following years he starred in a number of successful films, including "Sogno d'oro" (1978), "Il potere della notte" (1979), "I briganti" (1980), "L'uomo che amava le donne" (1981), "Il giorno della civetta" (1983), and "La citt√† delle mille ombre" (1987).  Mormando's most critically acclaimed work is probably his 1993 drama film "Il paese delle meraviglie", which won the Nastro d'argento at the Venice Film Festival.
