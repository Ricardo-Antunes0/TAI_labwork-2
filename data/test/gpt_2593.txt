Axel Honneth (; ; born 18 July 1951) is a German philosopher who specializes in social and political theory, critical theory, and philosophy of education.  Honneth was born in Frankfurt am Main, West Germany. He studied philosophy at the University of Heidelberg and at the University of Frankfurt am Main, where he received his doctorate in 1976. Between 1976 and 1978 he was a research associate at the Max Planck Institute for Philosophic Research in Frankfurt am Main. Since 1978 he has been a professor at the University of Heidelberg.  Honneth's principal works include "The Struggle for Recognition: The Moral Life of Social Classes" (1996), "The Poverty of Theory" (2000), "The Reification of Love" (2004), "Democratic Education" (2007), and "The Fragmented World: A New Social Theory" (2011).  Honneth has been a recipient of the Erasmus Prize (1999), the Hegel Prize (2008), and the Prince of Asturias Award for Progress in Humanity (2010). In 2012 he was awarded an honorary doctorate from the University of Warwick.
