Guoping Feng  is a Chinese-American neuroscientist. He is currently the Donald Bren Professor of Neuroscience at UC Irvine.  Feng was born in Shanghai, China in 1966. He received a B.S. in Biology from Peking University in 1988 and his M.D. from Harvard Medical School in 1992. He completed a residency at Massachusetts General Hospital and a post-doctoral fellowship at the University of California, San Francisco.  Feng's work focuses on understanding how the brain works and how it can be damaged by diseases such as Alzheimer's disease and Parkinson's disease. He has published over 350 papers and chapters in scientific journals, and has been awarded over $25 million in research grants. In 2012 he was elected to the National Academy of Sciences.
