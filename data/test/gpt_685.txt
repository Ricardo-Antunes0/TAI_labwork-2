Operation Nickel Grass was a strategic airlift of supplies to the Soviet Union during World War II. The operation began on December 16, 1941, and ended on May 15, 1945. The primary purpose of the airlift was to provide the Soviet Union with food, materiel, and military equipment.  Operation Nickel Grass was one of the largest airlifts in history. The cargo planes flew a total of 2,829 missions, delivering a total of 5,212,000 tons of supplies. The operation was also important in helping to prevent starvation in the Soviet Union.
