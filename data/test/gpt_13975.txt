Warsaw Water Filters, also known as Lindley's filters, are a type of water purification system invented by Scottish engineer James Lindley in 1851. The filters are made from a porous ceramic material that is impregnated with chemicals and activated by UV light. They are used to treat water for drinking, industrial use, and irrigation.
