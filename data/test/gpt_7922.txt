Henry Richard Fox Bourne (24 December 1837 – 2 April 1912) was an Englishman who became a naturalized American citizen and was the founder of the Bourne meatpacking company.  Henry Bourne was born in London on 24 December 1837 to Richard Fox Bourne, a wealthy Englishman, and his wife, Henrietta Maria (née Walker). He was the seventh of eleven children. Bourne was educated at Eton and Christ Church, Oxford, where he earned a degree in Classics in 1859.   After graduation, Bourne decided to travel for a year. He first visited Europe and then went to the United States, where he spent time in Illinois and Wisconsin. In 1861, Bourne moved to San Francisco, California, where he began working as a reporter for the "San Francisco Examiner".   In 1865, Bourne married Julia Fenton. The couple had two children: Julia Bourne (1866-1948) and Richard Fox Bourne (1868-1945).   Bourne formed a meatpacking company with his brother-in-law in 1872. The company became very successful and eventually became known as the Bourne Company. In 1906, the company merged with the American Meat Packers Association.   Henry Bourne died on 2 April 1912 in San Francisco. He was buried at Holy Cross Cemetery in Colma, California.
