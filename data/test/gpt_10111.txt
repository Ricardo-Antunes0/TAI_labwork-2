Franklin Thomas Backus (6 May 1813 – 24 November 1881) was an American jurist and politician. He served as a United States Representative from Massachusetts and as the tenth Chief Justice of the United States.  Franklin Thomas Backus was born on 6 May 1813 in Worcester, Massachusetts. He graduated from Harvard University in 1833, and read law to enter the bar in 1835. He commenced practice in Worcester.  Backus was elected as a Democrat to the Twenty-third Congress (March 4, 1861 – March 3, 1863). He was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1862 to the Twenty-fourth Congress. Backus served as a delegate to the Union National Convention in 1864.  Backus was elected as a Republican to the Thirty-fourth Congress (March 4, 1867 – March 3, 1869). He was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1868 to the Thirty-fifth Congress. Backus served as chairman of the Committee on Expenditures in the Department of State during the Thirty-sixth Congress.  Backus was appointed by President Ulysses S. Grant as Chief Justice of the United States on December 10, 1870, and he took his seat on December 17, 1870. He served until his death on 24 November 1881 in Washington D.C.. He was interred in Green-Wood Cemetery in Brooklyn, New York.
