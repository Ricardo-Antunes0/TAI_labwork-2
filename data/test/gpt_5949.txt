Herbert Edelsbrunner (born 1958) is a computer scientist and professor at the University of Cambridge.  Herbert Edelsbrunner was born in 1958 in Vienna, Austria. He obtained his B.Sc. in Mathematics from the University of Vienna in 1978 and his M.Sc. and Ph.D. degrees in Computer Science from Yale University in 1982 and 1985, respectively. From 1985 to 1987, he was a post-doctoral Fellow at the University of California, Berkeley. From 1987 to 1991, he was an Assistant Professor at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. From 1991 to 1998, he was an Associate Professor at the University of Cambridge, where he is currently a Professor. His research is in the area of computer science and its applications, with a particular emphasis on artificial intelligence and machine learning.  Edelsbrunner has been a member of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) since 1985 and of the Royal Society since 1996. He is also a Fellow of both the American Academy of Arts and Sciences (FAAR) and the British Academy (FBA).
