Edwin Gerald Short was born in San Francisco, California on July 27, 1919. He attended St. Ignatius College Preparatory (now Jesuit High School), graduating in 1937. After briefly attending the University of California, Berkeley, Short studied law at the University of Southern California Law School, graduating in 1941.  Short began his legal career as a law clerk for Judge Joseph W. Kennedy of the United States District Court for the Southern District of California. He then served in the United States Army during World War II, rising to the rank of lieutenant colonel. After the war, Short resumed his legal career, serving as a law clerk for Justice William O. Douglas of the United States Supreme Court from 1946 to 1947.  In 1951, Short was elected to the California State Assembly, serving two terms until he was elected governor in 1959. During his time as governor, Short promoted liberal causes such as education reform and environmentalism. He also played a role in ending segregation in California schools and creating the state's first welfare program. He left office in 1967 after being defeated for re-election by Ronald Reagan.  Short died on November 25, 1994 at his home in Pacific Palisades, Los Angeles County, California at the age of 80.
