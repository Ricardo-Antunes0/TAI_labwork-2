Joseph J. Himmel  (born Joseph J. Himmelstein, December 5, 1924) is an American philosopher and professor emeritus at the University of California, Berkeley. He is known for his work on ethics and political philosophy. He has also written on metaphysics and the philosophy of mind.  Himmel was born in New York City, the son of a Jewish pharmacist. He received his B.A. from City College of New York in 1944 and his M.A. from Columbia University in 1949. He served in the United States Army during World War II, attaining the rank of captain. He then attended the University of Chicago where he received his Ph.D. in philosophy in 1955.  Himmel has been a professor at the University of California, Berkeley since 1955, where he also served as chair of the philosophy department from 1970 to 1971. He has also held visiting professorships at Yale University, Princeton University, and the Hebrew University of Jerusalem. In 2001 he was awarded a Guggenheim Fellowship.  Himmel's main areas of philosophical research are ethics and political philosophy, with a special focus on the nature and justification of moral beliefs and obligations. His work has been influential in developing contemporary philosophical views on topics such as political obligation, rights, justice, desert theory, utilitarianism, and Kantianism.
