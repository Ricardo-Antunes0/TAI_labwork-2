Harold W. "Harry" Collyer (born c. 1885) was an American professional baseball player. He played in Major League Baseball (MLB) for the Philadelphia Phillies, Detroit Tigers, and Chicago White Sox between 1909 and 1918. Collyer batted and threw right-handed.  Collyer was born in 1885 in Brooklyn, New York. He attended Brooklyn Polytechnic Institute and played baseball for the Polytechnicias of Brooklyn varsity team in 1908. He signed with the Philadelphia Phillies in 1909 and made his MLB debut that season. Collyer played for the Phillies through 1918, appearing in 324 games and batting .269 with 97 RBIs. Collyer was traded to the Detroit Tigers in 1916 and to the Chicago White Sox in 1918. He played his final MLB game with the White Sox in October 1918.
