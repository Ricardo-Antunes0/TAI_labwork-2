Kerry Sanders is an American journalist. He is a senior national correspondent for CBS News, where he has worked since 1998.  Sanders was born in Knoxville, Tennessee, on July 3, 1963. He graduated from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill with a degree in government in 1985. After college, he worked as a political reporter for the "Knoxville News Sentinel" and then for the "Boston Herald".  In 1998, Sanders moved to CBS News, where he has been a senior national correspondent since. He has covered a wide range of topics, including politics, crime, education, and environment. He has also reported from overseas, including Afghanistan, Cuba, Haiti, and Iraq. In 2013, Sanders won a Peabody Award for his coverage of the 2013 Boston Marathon bombings.
