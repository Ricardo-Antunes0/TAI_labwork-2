Alfonso R. Bernard, Sr. (born August 10, 1927) is an American prelate of the Roman Catholic Church. He has been a cardinal since 1978. He served as Archbishop of Louisville from 1967 to 1978, and was elevated to the cardinalate in 1978.  Bernard was born in Louisville, Kentucky, to Alfonso and the former Rose (n√©e O'Neill) Bernard. After attending Cathedral Latin School in Louisville, he studied at St. John's Seminary in Brighton, Michigan, and at the Pontifical Gregorian University in Rome. He was ordained to the priesthood by Archbishop James Edward Quigley of Cincinnati on May 28, 1953.  After a year of pastoral work in the Diocese of Jeffersonville, Bernard became rector of St. John's Seminary in 1955. Two years later he was appointed vice president for academic affairs at the newly created Louisville seminary, St. Thomas More College. In 1959 he became secretary for education for the United States Conference of Catholic Bishops.   He was named auxiliary bishop of Louisville on October 15, 1967, and was consecrated on January 5, 1968 by Archbishop Quigley. As auxiliary bishop he served as chairman of the Board of Trustees at St. John's Seminary and as vicar general for Jefferson County. Bernard then served as president of the Association of Catholic Colleges and Universities from 1971 to 1973 and as president of the United States Catholic Conference from 1974 to 1977.
