He is traditionally considered to be the founder of the Japan Imperial Family and the first of the traditional Five Emperors.  Seimu founded the Imperial House of Japan, which would rule Japan for more than 1,000 years. He also played a significant role in pacifying the country after its initial unification under Emperor Jimmu. Seimu's reign marked the beginning of an era of peace and stability that would last until the Meiji Restoration in 1868.
