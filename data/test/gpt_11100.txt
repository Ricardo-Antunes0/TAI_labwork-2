Alwar Balasubramaniam also known as “Bala,” is a retired Indian police officer who has served in the rank of Deputy Superintendent of Police in the state of Rajasthan. He has been credited with eradicating the Naxalite movement from the state of Rajasthan.  Alwar Balasubramaniam was born on 15 October 1944 in Alwar, a city in Rajasthan. He completed his schooling from St. Joseph's Anglo-Indian School, Alwar and did his graduation from Sri Venkateswara College, Tiruchirappalli.  Balasubramaniam joined the Indian Police Service in 1967 and worked as a sub-inspector before being promoted to deputy superintendent of police in 1978. He was responsible for the anti-Naxalite operations in the state of Rajasthan. He is credited with eradicating the Naxalite movement from Rajasthan and was awarded the President's Police Medal for his work in this regard. He retired from the Indian Police Service in 1992.  Since retiring from the Indian Police Service, Balasubramaniam has served as a director with several companies. He is also a member of several civil society organisations including the Rajasthan Vikas Manch and the National Association for People's Action for Development (NAPAD).  Alwar Balasubramaniam was honoured with the Padma Bhushan award in 2009 and the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna award in 2010.
