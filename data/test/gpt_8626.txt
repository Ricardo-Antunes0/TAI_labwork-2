The Returning soldier effect is a phenomenon that occurs after a soldier has returned from military service. It is characterized by increased aggression, violence, and criminality. The effect is believed to be caused by the soldier's experience of combat, which has a profound impact on their mood and behavior.  The Returning Soldier Effect has been studied extensively, and there is no single answer as to why it occurs. Some theories suggest that the effect is caused by the stress of war, while others suggest that it is due to the soldier's homecoming experience. Whatever the cause, the Returning Soldier Effect is a significant problem in society and has been linked with a number of negative outcomes.
