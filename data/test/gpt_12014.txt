Baron Raglan, of Raglan in the County of Monmouthshire, was a British general in the First World War.  Raglan was born in 1852, the son of George Raglan, 1st Baron Raglan. He was educated at Eton and Trinity College, Cambridge. Raglan served in the Anglo-Egyptian War of 1882-1885, the Second Boer War of 1899-1902 and the Boxer Rebellion of 1900. He became General Officer Commanding British Expeditionary Force (GOC BEF) in August 1914. After initial success on the Western Front, his forces were defeated at the Battle of the Somme in 1916 and he was replaced as GOC BEF by Douglas Haig. Raglan became Commander-in-Chief, India in December 1916 and died in 1922.
