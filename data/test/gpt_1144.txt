Albert Edwin Elworthy Lee Tucker, often referred to by his initials A.E.L. (August 10, 1892 – February 28, 1970) was a United States Representative from California.  Born in Sonoma, Tucker attended the public schools of that city and the University of California, Berkeley, where he was a member of the Chi Phi Fraternity. He served in the United States Army from 1917 to 1919 and engaged in the furniture business in Sonoma from 1923 to 1930.  Tucker was elected as a Democrat to the Seventy-third Congress and to the eight succeeding Congresses (January 3, 1933 – January 3, 1947). He was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1946 to the Eighty-second Congress. He resumed his furniture business in Sonoma and died there on February 28, 1970. He is buried at Mountain View Cemetery in Sonoma.
