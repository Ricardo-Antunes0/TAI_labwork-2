Thomas Rexton Brumley (December 11, 1935 – September 18, 2009) was an American actor, known for his roles on the television series Alfred Hitchcock Presents and in the films The Jerk, The Breakfast Club, and Six Days Seven Nights.  Brumley was born in Muskegon Heights, Michigan, the son of Grace (née Rexton), a housewife, and Thomas Rexton Brumley, a tool and die maker. He attended Muskegon Heights High School. He began his career in regional theater, before making his Broadway debut in the 1968 play Incident at Vichy.  Brumley made his television debut as a guest star on Alfred Hitchcock Presents in the episode "The Mirror Crack'd" in 1963. In 1971, he starred as Professor Charles Xavier in the television series X-Men. His other notable television roles included appearances on The Waltons, Gunsmoke, Hawaii Five-O, Columbo, and Streets of San Francisco. He also had guest roles on many popular shows of the 1970s and 1980s including The Love Boat, M*A*S*H*, Cheers, Dallas, Dynasty, and Knight Rider.  Brumley's first feature film role was in The Jerk (1979), for which he was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor.
