Randall Herbert Balmer (born October 22, 1954) is an American physicist who was the 27th president of Columbia University from 2003 to 2013. Balmer is also a theoretical physicist, having conducted research in the fields of condensed matter physics, statistical mechanics, and quantum field theory.  Randall Balmer was born on October 22, 1954, in Boston, Massachusetts. He received his B.A. from Williams College in 1976 and his M.A. and Ph.D. from the University of California, Berkeley in 1979 and 1983, respectively. He began his career at the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory in 1979, where he worked as a staff physicist until joining Columbia University as a professor in 1991. Balmer served as the 27th president of Columbia University from 2003 to 2013.
