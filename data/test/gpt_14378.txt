Lloyd Polite Jr. (born January 3, 1986), better known by his stage name Lloyd, is an American singer, songwriter and record producer. He is the founder of the record label Anti Records and the release manager for the independent label Anti-Records. Lloyd has released two studio albums, "Lloyd" (2013) and "Purple Tape" (2016), as well as one extended play, "Sawdust" (2015).  Lloyd was born in Memphis, Tennessee, to Lloyd Polite Sr., a jazz bassist and composer, and Pam (n√©e Lloyd), an opera singer. He has a sister, singer/songwriter Macy Polite. Growing up in Memphis, Lloyd developed an early interest in music and began playing the trumpet at age six. He also played the drums and piano before discovering his true vocal talent during high school years when he began singing in a gospel choir.  In 2007, Lloyd co-founded the Memphis soul collective The Soulphonics with his childhood friend Jazz Jennings. The group released their self-titled debut album in 2009. The following year, Lloyd independently released his debut single "Make My Day".  In 2013, Lloyd signed to Atlantic Records subsidiary Anti Records and released his debut studio album, "Lloyd".
