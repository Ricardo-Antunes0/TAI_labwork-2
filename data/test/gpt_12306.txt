He was best known for his singles "Lean On" and "No Tears Left to Cry".  Born in Oxnard, California, Lele began rapping at the age of 14. He released his first mixtape, "Dead Presidents II", in 2009. In 2011, he released his debut album, "Lele", which received positive reviews from music critics. The album debuted at number two on the US "Billboard" 200 chart. In 2012, Lele released the single "Drillbit Taylor". The song peaked at number two on the US "Billboard" Hot 100 chart and became his first number one single in the United Kingdom. In 2013, he released the single "Who Do You Love?" The song peaked at number three on the US "Billboard" Hot 100 chart and won a Grammy Award for Best Rap Song. His second album, "Good Days", was released in 2014 and debuted at number one on the US "Billboard" 200 chart. Lele's third album, "Humble Beast", was released in 2016 and was his final album release.   Lele died of natural causes at his home in Woodland Hills, Los Angeles, California on March 20, 2016. At the time of his death, he had completed work on his fourth album.
