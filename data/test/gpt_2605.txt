Luzia Pereira de Sousa Inglês Van-Dúnem (born 2 July 1972) is a Portuguese actress, singer and television personality. She is best known for playing the title character in the Portuguese soap opera O Melhor do Que Viver and her role as Maria in the Spanish-language telenovela La Viuda de Blanca.  Luzia Inglês Van-Dúnem was born in Porto, Portugal on 2 July 1972 to parents of Portuguese and Belgian descent. She started her career as a model, appearing in several national advertising campaigns. In 1991, she made her acting debut in the Portuguese telenovela O Melhor do Que Viver, playing the role of Ana. The following year she played the lead role of Maria in the Spanish-language telenovela La Viuda de Blanca, which was broadcast in over 50 countries. Her performance earned her several accolades, including a nomination for a TV Award for Best Actress. In 1995, she released her debut album, which reached number one on the Portuguese Albums Chart. In 1997, she starred in the Portuguese drama film A Última Cruzada alongside David Silva and Teresa Peixoto. The following year she starred in the comedy film As Três Irmãs with Rui Rio and Ana Estrela. In 2000, she starred in the Portuguese drama film A Favorita with José Soares and Catarina Furtado.  In 2002, Luzia married businessman Rui Fernandes.
