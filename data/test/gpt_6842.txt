Karel Janovický (born 18 February 1930) is a Czechoslovakian philosopher and dissident.  Karel Janovický was born in 1930 in Prague, Czechoslovakia. He studied philosophy at the Charles University in Prague and then at the University of Freiburg, where he obtained his doctorate in 1963. He worked as a professor of philosophy at the Charles University from 1963 to 1988. From 1988 to 1992, he was a professor at the University of Pennsylvania. Since 1992, he has been a professor at the University of Cambridge.  Janovický has written on a wide range of topics in philosophy, including ethics, logic, metaphysics, and epistemology. He is particularly known for his work on Levinas and existentialism, which has been influential in the development of post-structuralist thought. He has also written extensively on Slavoj Žižek, with whom he has collaborated on several books.  Janovický has been a vocal critic of the Czechoslovak Socialist Republic and its government. He was one of the founders of Charter 77, an organization that worked to promote human rights in Czechoslovakia during the late 1970s and early 1980s. In 1988, he was sentenced to five years imprisonment for his involvement in Charter 77; he served two years before being released on parole.  He currently lives in Cambridge, England.
