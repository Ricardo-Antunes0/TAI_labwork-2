William Harrison Rice (October 12, 1813 – August 2, 1888) was an American Democratic Party politician who served as the 24th Governor of Indiana from 1875 to 1876.  Rice was born in Wellsville, Ohio, on October 12, 1813. He attended the common schools and graduated from Miami University in Oxford, Ohio, in 1837. He studied law and was admitted to the bar in 1840. Rice then moved to Crawfordsville, Indiana, and commenced practice.  Rice served as prosecuting attorney for Hamilton and Monroe Counties from 1840 to 1844. He also served as member of the Indiana House of Representatives from 1850 to 1854 and from 1865 to 1868. Rice was elected as a Democrat to the Forty-eighth Congress (March 4, 1873 – March 3, 1875). He was not re-elected and resumed the practice of law.  Rice was elected governor of Indiana in 1875. He served only one term, due to his own declining health. Rice died on August 2, 1888, in Crawfordsville. He is buried there in Oak Hill Cemetery.
