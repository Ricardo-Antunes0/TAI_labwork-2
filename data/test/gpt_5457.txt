Donald Wayne Foster (born 1950) is a Canadian author, journalist and researcher.  Foster was born in Toronto, Canada, in 1950. After studying journalism at Ryerson University, he began his career in journalism as a reporter for the "Toronto Star" in 1977. He has since written for a number of publications, including "The Globe and Mail", "Toronto Life", "Canadian Geographic" and "Maclean's". His books include the histories of Vancouver and Toronto, as well as "The Lost Histories of Canadian Business".  Foster has also been involved in research and writing on various historical topics. He is a senior historian with the Canadian Museum of History, where he is responsible for the museum's Canadian history section. He has also been a lecturer at Queen's University and the University of Toronto. In 2012, he was made an Officer of the Order of Canada.
