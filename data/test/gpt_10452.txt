CFPL is a radio station owned by CHUM Limited. It broadcasts at 1080 kHz on the FM band in Toronto, Ontario. The station airs a classic hits format with some talk and sports programming.  CFPL was launched on May 25, 1951 as CJCL-FM. The station was owned by the Canadian Broadcasting Corporation (CBC), and originally aired a classical music format. In the early 1960s, CJCL-FM became the first FM station in Canada to broadcast in stereo. In 1966, the CBC sold CJCL-FM to CHUM Limited. In 1980, CHUM changed the station's name to CFPL-FM.  CFPL's original studios were located at 299 Yonge Street in downtown Toronto. In 1987, CFPL moved its studios to a new building at 350 Bay Street in downtown Toronto.  On February 17, 2010, CFPL made history by becoming the first radio station in Canada to launch a digital HD Radio signal.   On October 17, 2017, CHUM Limited announced it would sell CFPL and sister stations CKVL-FM and CKZZ-FM to Bell Media for $187 million. The sale was approved by the Canadian Radio-television and Telecommunications Commission (CRTC) on December 14, 2017 and was completed on January 1, 2018.
