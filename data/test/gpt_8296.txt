68 Publishers, also called Sixty-Eight Publishers, Sixtyeight Publishers, or simply 68, was an American publishing house founded by Donald M. Grant in 1968.  68 Publishers was a small, independent, and highly selective press that published "high-quality" books in the humanities and social sciences. The press was notable for its emphasis on archival research, and for its collaborations with scholars at other universities.  The company ceased operations in 2002.
