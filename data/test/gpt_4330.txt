John Pyper-Ferguson (born February 27, 1964) is an American philosopher who specializes in ethics and political philosophy. He is the Clarence L. Smith Professor of Philosophy at Princeton University, where he also served as interim dean from 2009 to 2010.  Pyper-Ferguson was born in Boston, Massachusetts, and grew up in Arlington, Virginia. He received his B.A. from Tufts University in 1986 and his M.A. and Ph.D. from Yale University in 1989 and 1992, respectively.  Pyper-Ferguson has been a professor at Princeton since 1992, where he has also served as the Clarence L. Smith Professor of Philosophy since 2003. He has also been a visiting professor at the University of Oxford (2004), the University of Toronto (2007), and the University of Melbourne (2010). He has also been a fellow at the Centre for Human Values at Princeton University (1998-1999), the Wissenschaftskolleg zu Berlin (2003), and the Cazenovia Institute (2010).  Pyper-Ferguson's work centers on ethics and political philosophy, with a particular focus on moral responsibility, political authority, and democratic legitimacy. He has written extensively on topics such as self-defense, torture, abortion, euthanasia, climate change, pre-emptive warfare, and animal rights.
