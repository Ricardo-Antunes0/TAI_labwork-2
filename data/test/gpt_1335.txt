Nucleocytoviricota is a phylum of viruses. Members are obligate intracellular parasites that infect animal cells.  Nucleocytoviricota viruses are a diverse group of small, enveloped viruses that parasitize animal cells. They are obligate intracellular parasites and cause a wide range of diseases in mammals, including encephalitis, meningitis, and rabies. Members of this phylum are also the causative agents of several important zoonotic diseases, including Hendra fever and lyssavirus infections.
