Helen Orr "Elenor" Gordon (later McKay; 10 November 1890 – 9 January 1965) was a New Zealand artist. Born in Auckland, she studied at the Auckland School of Art from 1907 to 1911, winning first prize in the 1912 Painting and Drawing Competition. She then went to Paris to continue her studies, and attended the Académie Colarossi. Upon her return to New Zealand in 1914, she settled in Wellington and became known for her portraits and landscapes. In the 1930s she moved to Dunedin, where she continued to paint until her death.
