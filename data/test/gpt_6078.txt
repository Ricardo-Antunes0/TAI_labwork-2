Mikuláš Teich (24 July 1918 – 16 October 2015) was a Czech composer.  Born in Prague, Teich studied at the Prague Conservatory with Václav Talich and Jaroslav Seifert. He worked as a composer for the state broadcaster, Radio Prague, from 1942 until his retirement in 1984. His best-known works include the opera "The Bartered Bride" (1949), the violin concerto "Concerto for Violin and Orchestra" (1958), and the symphony "Distant Voices" (1963).
