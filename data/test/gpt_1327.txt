William G. Enloe High School, also known as Enloe or EHS, is a public high school in the city of Austin, Texas, United States. It is one of six high schools in the Austin Independent School District, and is classified as a 2A school by the UIL.  The school was built in 1956 on the site of the old Austin High School. It was designed by noted Texas architect William Pereira. The school was named for William G. Enloe, an Army aviator and Civil Rights Activist who died in a plane crash in 1948. The original building was designed to accommodate 1,000 students, but has since expanded to 1,700 students.  Enloe High School historically has had a strong academic reputation and numerous accolades, including being ranked as one of the top high schools in Texas by "The Princeton Review" in 2007 and 2010. In 2009, "USA Today" rated Enloe as one of the top 100 public high schools in the United States. In 2011, "Newsweek" ranked Enloe as one of the top 1000 schools in America.  Enloe competes in the following sports: football (6-man), cross country (9-man), track & field (men & women), basketball (men & women), soccer (men & women), tennis (women) and golf (men).
