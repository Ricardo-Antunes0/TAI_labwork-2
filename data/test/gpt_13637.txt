The 2017 Pratapgarh lynching refers to death of three people, including a man named Mohammed Akhlaq, after they were accused of consuming beef in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh.  On the evening of September 28th, 2017, three people were lynched in Pratapgarh, Uttar Pradesh, India after being accused of consuming beef. Mohammed Akhlaq, 57, his wife Ikraman Begum, 40, and son Mohammad Danish Akhlaq, 22, were all killed by a large mob that descended on their home after they were falsely accused of eating beef. The lynching has sparked outrage across India and led to calls for justice for the victims.
