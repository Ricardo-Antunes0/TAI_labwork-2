Gliese 1214 is a dim M4.5 red dwarf star located about 54 light years from Earth in the constellation of Virgo. It is the smallest and faintest of the main-sequence stars in the Gliese catalogue. With a temperature of only 1500 K and a mass only 1/15th that of the Sun, it is too small to sustain nuclear fusion and is considered a brown dwarf.
