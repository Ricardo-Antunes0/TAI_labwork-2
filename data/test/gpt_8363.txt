Bernstein was born in New York City, on January 22, 1919. After completing his undergraduate degree at the University of Chicago in 1939, Bernstein served in the United States Navy during World War II. He returned to the University of Chicago to complete his doctorate in economics in 1947.  Bernstein taught at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology from 1947 to 1971, before moving to the University of California, Berkeley. He retired from Berkeley in 1987.  Bernstein's work has been influential not only within economics but also in mathematics, statistics and game theory. In particular, he is known for his contributions to economic theory and the economics of uncertainty. His works have also been influential in the development of rational expectations theory and the theory of market crashes.  Bernstein died on October 2, 2005, at the age of 89.
