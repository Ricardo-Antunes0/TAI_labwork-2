Uthman ibn al-Huwayrith  was an Arab general and governor who played a pivotal role in the Muslim conquests of the Levant and North Africa in the 7th century. He was also a notable scholar, poet, and scientist.  Uthman ibn al-Huwayrith was born circa 656 in the Arabian city of Mecca. He rose to prominence as a general and governor during the early Muslim conquests of the Levant and North Africa, which began in the 7th century. He is especially noted for his role in defeating the Byzantine army at the Battle of Tours in 732, which helped cement Muslim control over much of Europe and North Africa. Uthman also played a significant role in introducing new scientific disciplines to Muslim society, such as mathematics and chemistry. He died in 749 at the age of 75.
