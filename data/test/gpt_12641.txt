Pierre Jadart Dumerbion or Pierre Jadart du Merbion was a French painter who was one of the most famous and influential members of the Barbizon School.  Pierre Jadart Dumerbion was born in 1826 in the small town of Merbion, Ardèche. He studied at the École des Beaux-Arts in Paris, where he was taught by Jean-Léon Gérôme and Jacques-Louis David.  Dumerbion is best known for his paintings of nature, particularly for his idyllic scenes of peasant life in the Ardèche and the Jura Mountains. He also painted seascapes, cityscapes and portraits. He died in 1881.
