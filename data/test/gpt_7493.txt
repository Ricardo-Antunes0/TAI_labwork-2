Kenneth Bae (born Pae Jun Ho; born June 12, 1975) is an American citizen and an American Christian missionary who was sentenced to 15 years in prison in North Korea on charges of espionage and attempted espionage.  Kenneth Bae was born on June 12, 1975, in South Korea, to parents who were Presbyterian missionaries. He grew up in the United States and became a naturalized U.S. citizen in 2004. In mid-2013, Bae traveled to North Korea as a part of a group of 38 tourists, most of whom were from South Korea. On December 18, 2013, he was detained at the airport in Pyongyang and subsequently charged with espionage and attempted espionage after being accused of trying to steal documents from a secret intelligence bureau. On May 12, 2014, he was sentenced to 15 years' imprisonment by the North Korean Supreme Court.   Bae has been held in various prison camps in North Korea since his arrest. In January 2017, he was reported to be in a severe state of health after spending over two years in a hard labor camp.
