Michael Arthur Raynor (born 1962) is an American composer and musician. He has composed and recorded more than thirty albums of original music, as well as soundtracks for film and video games.  Raynor was born in Chicago, Illinois, in 1962. He started playing the guitar at the age of eight, and soon began writing his own music. He attended college at Berklee College of Music in Boston, Massachusetts, where he studied with Michael Beck and Robert Fripp. After graduation, he moved to New York City and worked as a session musician.  Raynor's first album, "Dreams of a Life", was released in 1994. The album was well-received, and Raynor continued to release albums throughout the 1990s and 2000s. His most recent album, "The Human Condition", was released in 2013.
