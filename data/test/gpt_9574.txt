José Antonio Abreu Anselmi (May 7, 1939 – November 30, 2016) was a Brazilian singer. He was a popular singer in the 1970s and 1980s, known for his soft and romantic ballads.  Abreu was born in São Paulo, Brazil, on May 7, 1939. He started his career as a member of the vocal group Trio Bravo, which had several successful hits in Brazil in the 1960s and early 1970s. In 1973, he released his first solo album, which was a critical and commercial success. Abreu's popularity increased with the release of his albums throughout the 1970s and 1980s, including 1975's Cantando com Amor (Singing with Love), 1977's Saudades do Brasil (Sorrows of Brazil), 1978's O Meu Amor É Tudo (My Love Is All), and 1981's Acredito (I Believe). In 1985, he released his most successful album to date, A Gente Se Liga (People Get Connected). Abreu retired from performing in the early 1990s but made a comeback in 1999. He died from cancer on November 30, 2016 at the age of 78.
