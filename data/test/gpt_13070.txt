The Rong River, (Chinese: 榕江, tr. Róngjiāng) is a river in southern China. It is a tributary of the Pearl River, and flows through Guangdong province. The river forms part of the border between Guandong province and Yunnan province.  The Rong River has a total length of 1,100 kilometers, and its basin covers an area of 5,800 square kilometers. The Rong River rises in the Lingnan Mountains and flows through the province of Guangdong for about 400 kilometers before it enters the Pearl River near Wuchang. The upper reaches of the Rong River are known for their steep slopes, deep gorges and picturesque waterfalls.
