Friedrich Wilms (19 April 1848, in Münster, Westphalia – 12 October 1912, in Berlin) was a German physician and surgeon who is considered the father of modern otolaryngology.  Wilms was born in Münster, Westphalia, the son of a brewer. After completing his studies at the universities of Bonn and Berlin, he began work as a doctor in 1872. He became known for his work on ear, nose, and throat diseases, and founded the first otolaryngological clinic in Berlin in 1888. Wilms was also an advocate for better patient care and training, and founded the Berlin Otolaryngological Society in 1897. He died from a heart attack aged 62.
