Seamus Justin Heaney  (; 13 April 1939 â€“ 25 March 2013) was an Irish poet and laureat of the 2003 Nobel Prize in Literature.  Heaney was born in Derry, Northern Ireland, on 13 April 1939. He attended St Columb's College, Derry, and the University of Dublin. Heaney married Marie-Louise Skeffington in 1963; they had two sons. In 1970, Heaney left Ireland for the United States, where he taught at Boston University from 1971 to 1981. Heaney returned to Ireland in 1981 and became a full professor at Trinity College, Dublin.  Heaney's first collection of poems, The North, was published in 1962. His later collections include: Death of a Naturalist (1979), Beowulf (1985), The Spirit Level (1996), and Memory and Experience (1998). In 2003 Heaney was awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature "for his mastery of the forms of poetry and his powerful expressions of human experience."  Seamus Heaney died on 25 March 2013 at the age of 75 after a long illness.
