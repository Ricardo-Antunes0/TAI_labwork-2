Catch and Kill: Lies, Spies, and a Secret History of the CIA is a biography of agent Gerald R. Ford, written by Christopher A. Drew. The book was published on July 27, 2013.  Catch and Kill tells the dramatic story of Gerald R. Ford, from his early days as an intelligence officer in the CIA to his service as President of the United States. Drawing on new interviews with Ford and extensive research, Christopher A. Drew reveals the extraordinary lengths to which the Agency went to keep him under wraps-including staging a fake assassination attempt-and reveals how Ford's unique background and skills helped him become one of the Agency's most successful spies.  Award-winning journalist Drew provides a unique and definitive account of one of the most important American presidents, revealing the circumstances surrounding Ford's rise to power and his rocky tenure in office.
