Roland Trebicka (13 January 1947 – 6 October 2001) was a German-born composer and conductor.  Trebicka was born in Frankfurt, Germany. He studied music at the Hochschule für Musik in Frankfurt am Main, where his teachers included Karlheinz Stockhausen and Karl-Heinz Stockhausen. He later studied at the University of Southern California where he earned a Master of Music degree in 1977. Trebicka's early career was spent as a composer and conductor. He served as the music director of the Bavarian Radio Symphony Orchestra from 1978 to 1981 and the Munich Radio Symphony Orchestra from 1981 to 1984. From 1984 to 1995, Trebicka served as the music director of the Salzburg Festival. Trebicka died from leukemia in Salzburg, Austria, at the age of 57.
