Hampton, a rover, was a premiership player with Melbourne in 1941. He played in only six games that season but helped make the club premiers. He also won the best and fairest award for that season.  He left Melbourne at the end of the 1941 season to join Richmond, where he played 38 games over two seasons. Hampton then returned to Melbourne, where he played for two more seasons before retiring at the end of 1946. Hampton then coached Footscray for one season before retiring from coaching.  Hampton died in 1986 at the age of 78.
