He served as the Permanent Representative of Sri Lanka to the United Nations from 2009 to 2016.  Saman Chandranath Weerasinghe was born in Jaffna on 5 July 1947. After completing his primary and secondary education at Jaffna Central College, he studied at the University of Ceylon, where he obtained a Bachelor of Laws degree in 1971. He then commenced his career as a legal officer with the Colombo Bar Association.  Weerasinghe served as the Deputy Secretary (Administration) in the Ministry of Foreign Affairs from 1988 to 1989, and as the Permanent Representative of Sri Lanka to the United Nations from 2009 to 2016. During his tenure at the UN, he represented Sri Lanka at major international fora including the United Nations General Assembly, the United Nations Security Council, and the Human Rights Council. He also served as Chairman of the United Nations Development Programme's (UNDP) Advisory Board on International Cooperation in Disaster Reduction from 2014 to 2016.  Weerasinghe was awarded the Sri Lanka Armed Services Medal for gallantry in action during the Sri Lankan Civil War in 2001. He died on 31 May 2016 after a long battle with cancer.
