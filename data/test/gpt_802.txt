Terry A. Anderson (born October 27, 1947) is an American politician who served as the 38th Governor of Oklahoma from 2003 to 2007. A Republican, he was the first governor in Oklahoma history to be elected to a second term.  Anderson was born in Tulsa, Oklahoma, on October 27, 1947. He graduated from Fairview High School in 1965 and then attended the University of Tulsa, where he received his bachelor's degree in political science in 1969. Anderson then attended the University of Oklahoma College of Law, where he received his juris doctorate in 1973.  Anderson began his career as a law clerk for Judge James C. Walton of the United States District Court for the Western District of Oklahoma. He then practiced law for several years before being elected district attorney for Tulsa County, Oklahoma, in 1984. He served as district attorney until he was elected governor in 2002.  While governor, Anderson presided over a number of major accomplishments, including increasing job growth and economic stability, reforming education and criminal justice systems, and expanding healthcare availability and coverage. He also developed new strategies to combat terrorism and crime. Anderson left office after serving two terms and was succeeded by Brad Henry.  Since leaving office, Anderson has continued to work on public policy issues. In 2013, he was appointed by President Barack Obama to serve on the U.S. Commission on International Religious Freedom. In 2017, Anderson was named chairman of the board of directors for The Heritage Foundation.
