A multitude of languages are used in Canada, making it one of the most linguistically diverse countries in the world. The official languages of Canada are English and French, with both languages being used in federal and provincial government, as well as in education and business. Other languages spoken in Canada include Aboriginal languages, Arabic, Chinese, Greek, Italian, Japanese, Korean, Portuguese, Punjabi, Russian, Spanish, Ukrainian and Vietnamese.   Canada has more than 100 Aboriginal languages and dialects which are spoken by more than 1.3 million Canadians. These languages vary greatly in their morphology and syntax but share many common features including a number of phonemes that are not found in other Canadian languages. Arabic is one of the most spoken languages in Canada, with over 500 thousand residents speaking it at home. There are also significant populations of Chinese Canadians and Greek Canadians, who have contributed to the development of Canadian society and culture.
