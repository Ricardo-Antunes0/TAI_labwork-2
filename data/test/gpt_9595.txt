He is also known for his work on price stability and for his advocacy of market liberalization.  Born in 1916 in Liverpool, Lewis was educated at the Liverpool Institute High School and at the London School of Economics. After completing his undergraduate studies at LSE, he worked as a research economist at the National Coal Board from 1938 to 1944. He then served in the Royal Air Force from 1944 to 1946, during which time he attained a degree of flying officer.  Lewis returned to academia, serving as a professor at LSE from 1948 to 1963 and as Director of the Centre for Economic Policy Research from 1963 to 1979. He was appointed Governor of the Bank of England in 1979, serving until 1984. During this time, Lewis played a leading role in the implementation of monetary and fiscal policy during the early 1980s recession. Lewis remained active in public life after retiring from the Bank of England, serving as Chairman of the Committee on Economic Development from 1985 to 1992 and as President of the British Economic Association from 1991 to 2000. He died in 2001.
