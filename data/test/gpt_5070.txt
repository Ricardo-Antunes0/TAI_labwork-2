Quentin Durgens, M.P. is a Canadian dramatic poet, playwright and novelist.  Born in Montreal, Quebec, Durgens is the son of a Belgian father and a Canadian mother. He was educated at the Université de Montréal and the Université Laval. He has been married to the writer Aurore Clavet since 1981. They have two children.  Durgens has published five novels, several volumes of poetry and a play. His most famous work is the novel L'Exil (1981), which was awarded the prestigious Prix Goncourt in 1982.
