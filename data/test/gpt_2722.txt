The park was established on October 1, 1936, as a natural reserve and extended to its current size on January 13, 1991. It occupies an area of . The park is managed by the National Park Authority.  Poloniny National Park is one of the most important biosphere reserves in Poland and one of the few protected areas in Europe devoted to the conservation of mountain summits. The park contains a wide variety of habitats, including coniferous forests, meadows, rocky gorges and alpine pastures. There are several peaks over 2,000 metres (6,600 ft) in height and numerous lakes and streams. The park is home to a number of rare and endangered species of plants and animals.
