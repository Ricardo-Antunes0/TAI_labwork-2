Born in London, Rogers began his musical training at the Royal Academy of Music, where he was awarded a diploma in composition in 1957. He then studied at the University of California, Berkeley, where he received a doctorate in 1962. After returning to England, Rogers became Head of Music at the University of Essex in Colchester. He also served as Composer-in-Residence at the University of Reading from 1988 to 1990.  In 1998, Rogers was made a Fellow of the Royal Academy of Music. He died in 2014, aged 82.
