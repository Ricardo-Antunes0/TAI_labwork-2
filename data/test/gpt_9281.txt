A stress ulcer is a single or multiple healed ulcers that occur most commonly on the soles of the feet, but can also occur in other areas of the body. They are typically caused by long-term or continuous stress, such as from a job, family responsibilities, or emotional problems. They can also be caused by a physical injury, such as a burns.  Stress ulcers are very common, and can be caused by a wide variety of factors. They are usually harmless, but can occasionally become infected, leading to serious complications. Treatment usually involves rest and medications to relieve the stress that is causing the ulcer.
