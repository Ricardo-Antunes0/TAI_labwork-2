Colin Leslie McCool (9 December 1916 – 5 October 1944) was an Australian soldier, medical officer and POW during World War II.  McCool was born in Bundaberg, Queensland on 9 December 1916. He enlisted in the Australian Army in October 1940, and was posted to the 2/7th Battalion, Australian Imperial Force as a medical officer. He was captured by the Japanese in October 1944 while on leave in Hong Kong and died three months later in a Japanese prison camp. He was posthumously awarded the Medal of Honor for his actions during the Battle of Buna–Gona.  McCool was survived by his wife and two young children. His son, Colin Leslie McCool, Jr., also served in the Australian Army during the Vietnam War.
