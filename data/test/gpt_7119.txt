Rhonda Brady (born July 10, 1959 in Akron, Ohio) is an American television and radio personality.  Brady was born in Akron, Ohio and graduated from Summit High School in 1977. Brady attended the University of Akron and played on the women's basketball team. After graduating from college, Brady worked as a sales representative for a pharmaceutical company.  In 1985, Brady joined WNCN-TV in Raleigh, North Carolina, as a reporter. She later became the station's news director and then its vice president of news and public affairs. In 2002, Brady left WNCN to become the president and CEO of WTNH-TV in New Haven, Connecticut. In 2003, she moved to WPVI-TV in Philadelphia as its president and general manager. Brady left WPVI in 2006 to become the president and CEO of WKRC-TV in Cincinnati.  Brady has been a commentator for ESPN since 1997 and has also been a contributor to "The Oprah Winfrey Show", "Good Morning America", "CBS This Morning", "Fox News Sunday", and "The Today Show". She has also been a commentator for the United States Women's National Team telecasts on Fox Sports Net since 2006.  Brady was inducted into the North Carolina Sports Hall of Fame in 2001 and the Pennsylvania Broadcast Hall of Fame in 2006. In 2012, she was inducted into the Ohio Women's Hall of Fame.
