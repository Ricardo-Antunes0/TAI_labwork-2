William Read Miller (November 23, 1823November 29, 1892) was an American lawyer and politician who served as the 33rd Governor of New York, serving from 1871 to 1873.  Miller was born in Watertown, New York, the son of Judge John Miller and his wife, the former Betsey Waldron. He attended the common schools and then entered Yale College in 1843, graduating in 1847. After studying law with his father, he was admitted to the bar in 1850 and commenced practice in Watertown.  Miller was a member of the New York State Senate (27th D.) from 1857 to 1861, and was majority leader during that time. He was appointed Attorney General of New York on January 8, 1861, serving until November 3, 1861, when he resigned to enter the Union Army as colonel of the Forty-second Regiment New York State Volunteer Infantry. He served in the Army of the Potomac until December 31, 1862, when he returned to politics and resumed his duties as Attorney General.
