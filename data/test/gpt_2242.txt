Anna Laetitia Barbauld was born in London, England, in 1743 to a wealthy family. She was educated at home and then attended a convent school. After leaving school, she worked as a governess before marrying in 1769. The couple had two children before Barbauld died in 1813.  Barbauld published her first book of poems in 1773. She continued to write poetry and essays until her death in 1813. Her most famous works include "The Fable of the Bees" and "The Female Quixote".
