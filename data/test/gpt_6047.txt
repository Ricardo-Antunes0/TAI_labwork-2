Hermann Wilhelm Göring (or Goering; ; 12 August 1894 – 17 April 1945) was a German politician, military leader, and Reichsmarschall who served as the second most powerful man in Nazi Germany after Adolf Hitler from 1934 to 1945. A highly decorated veteran of World War I and World War II, Göring was a member of the Nazi Party from 1919 and the second most powerful man in Nazi Germany after Hitler from 1934 to 1938. He played a central role in the Holocaust, leading the persecution of Jews and other groups during World War II.  Born in Munich, Bavaria, Göring joined the Bavarian Army in 1914 and served during World War I. After being discharged from the army, he became a professional pilot and participated in combat missions on the Eastern Front of World War I. In 1923, he founded the first all-German airship company and became its chairman. He also served as president of the Deutscher Aero Club. In 1933, he became one of the founders of the Nazi Party and its leader two years later. As Minister of Aviation in 1933–1934, he developed and implemented Nazi Germany's aviation policies. After being appointed Reichsmarschall in 1938, he was instrumental in planning and executing World War II in Europe with aggressive warfare that resulted in millions of casualties.
