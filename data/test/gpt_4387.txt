The Diocese of Hålogaland (, historically: ) is a diocese of the Church of Norway. It covers the southeastern part of the country, including the counties of Hålogaland, Sogn og Fjordane and Møre og Romsdal. Its seat is in the city of Bergen.  The diocese was created on 1 February 1929 from parts of the Diocese of Nordland and the Diocese of Sogn og Fjordane. It lost territory to the Diocese of Arendal during the 1960s, but regained some during the 1990s.   The diocese has a population of about 210,000 people. The majority (almost two-thirds) are Norwegian citizens, with a minority (about one-third) being foreign citizens. About one-third of the population are members of the Church of Norway.
