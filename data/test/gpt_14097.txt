The Fawn River is a river in the Bruce Peninsula and the Region of Waterloo, in southwestern Ontario, Canada. It is a tributary of the Grand River.  The Fawn River rises in the municipality of South Stormont, about south-southwest of the Bruce Peninsula's main settlement, Port Arthur. It flows generally southeastward for about before turning to the east and then south, eventually reaching its mouth at the Grand River about northeast of Kitchener. The river's drainage basin covers about .  The Fawn River is noted for its wide variety of fish species, including smallmouth bass, northern pike, and whitefish. The river has been designated a wild Trout Area under the Ontario Ministry of Natural Resources' Conservation Act. Several parks are located along its banks, including Big Bay-Little Bay Provincial Park and Hespeler Mill Park.
