16 Lacertae is a triple star system in the constellation Lacerta. The primary star, 15 Lacertae, is a yellow-white A-type main-sequence star with a mean apparent visual magnitude of 5.8. It is about 1,000 times brighter than the Sun and has a diameter of around 100 times the Sun's radius. The two companions, 16 Lacertae and 17 Lacertae, are both subgiants with apparent visual magnitudes of 6.9 and 8.2, respectively. 16 Lacertae is about 500 times closer to 15 Lacertae than it is to Earth, and it completes an orbit around 15 Lacertae every 2.4 days.
