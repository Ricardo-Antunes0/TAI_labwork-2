Mort Crim (born July 31, 1935 in Brooklyn, New York) is an American jazz trumpeter and flugelhornist. He is a member of the New York Jazz Critics Circle and has been nominated for ten Grammy Awards.  Mort Crim was born in Brooklyn, New York on July 31, 1935. He began playing the trumpet at the age of nine and studied with Louis Armstrong. He played with Dizzy Gillespie in the mid-1950s and toured with Stan Getz in the 1960s.  Crim has recorded hundreds of albums as a leader and sideman, including collaborations with Art Blakey, Sonny Rollins, McCoy Tyner, Roy Haynes, Herbie Hancock, and Kenny Burrell. He has also appeared on recordings by Thelonious Monk, Charlie Parker, John Coltrane, Coleman Hawkins, and Miles Davis.  Crim was awarded the NEA Jazz Masters Award in 2002 and the Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award in 2007.
