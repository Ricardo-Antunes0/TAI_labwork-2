Emil Radok (22 May 1918 in Koloděje, Czechoslovakia – 5 November 2002 in Prague, Czech Republic) was a Czech linguist, known for his work on Slavic languages.  Radok was born in Koloděje into a Jewish family. He studied at Charles University in Prague, where he received his doctorate in 1946. From 1946 to 1958 he was a professor at the Charles University in Prague. From 1958 to 1968 he was the director of the Institute of Linguistics and Philology at Charles University.  Radok's research focused on the Slavic languages, especially Russian, Ukrainian and Belarusian. He is best known for his work on the morphology of Russian and Ukrainian. He also wrote extensively on semantics and syntax.
