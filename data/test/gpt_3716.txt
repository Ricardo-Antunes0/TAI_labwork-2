He was born and died in Mumbai, Maharashtra.  Bardolikar's father, Musaji Hafez Khan, was a renowned sarod player and his mother, Ismat Khanum, a disciple of Vilayat Khan. He started his formal music training under his father at the age of six and made his debut performance in 1944 at the age of thirteen. In 1947, he won first prize at the All-India Radio Competition for vocal music and in 1951 he won the Sangeet Natak Akademi Award for classical vocal music. He has since toured extensively throughout India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Europe and the United States of America.  Bardolikar has recorded more than seventy-five albums and has been awarded several prestigious awards, including the Sangeet Natak Akademi Award (1951), Padma Bhushan (1972), and the Padma Vibhushan (2009). He is also a recipient of the prestigious Sahitya Akademi Award (1978). In 2012 he was awarded an honorary doctorate by the University of Mumbai.
