Jason Rowbotham (born 3 January 1969) is an English former professional footballer who played in the Football League for York City, Rochdale and Halifax Town.  Rowbotham began his career as a youth player at York City, before making his debut in the Football League in 1989. He spent six seasons with York, before joining Rochdale in 1994. He spent four seasons with Rochdale, before moving to Halifax Town in 1998. He spent two seasons with Halifax, before retiring from football at the age of 30.  After retiring from football, Rowbotham began working as a coach with the York City youth team. He later became assistant manager of the club, before leaving the role in December 2014. Rowbotham was appointed manager of National League side Northwich Victoria in November 2016, but left the role two months later.
