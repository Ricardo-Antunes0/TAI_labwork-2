Sir Henry Abel Smith,  (8 March 1822 – 3 November 1892) was a British jurist and Conservative Party politician who served as the first Chief Justice of the Supreme Court of Canada.  Henry Abel Smith was born in London, England on 8 March 1822, the son of Henry Abel Smith and Charlotte (née Fox). He was educated at Eton and Trinity College, Cambridge where he earned his Bachelor of Arts degree in 1845. He then studied law at the Inner Temple and was called to the bar in 1849.  Smith began his legal career working as a barrister. In 1857, he was appointed Queen's Counsel and in 1860 he became a bencher of his Inn. In 1862, he was appointed Recorder of London.  In 1867, Smith was appointed Chief Justice of Upper Canada. He served in this position until his appointment to the Supreme Court of Canada in 1878. In 1880, he became the first Chancellor of the University of Toronto. He was also an honorary fellow of Trinity College, Cambridge.  Smith retired from the Supreme Court in 1890 and died three years later on 3 November 1892 at his home in Toronto. He was buried in Mount Pleasant Cemetery in Toronto.
