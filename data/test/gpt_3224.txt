Jean-Baptiste-Tréfflé Richard (November 23, 1856 – March 11, 1934) was a French composer and teacher.  Jean-Baptiste-Tréfflé Richard was born in Neuilly-sur-Seine, into a family of musicians. His father, François-Xavier Richard, was a violinist and teacher; his mother, Louise Tréfflé, was a pianist. As a child, Richard showed an interest in music, but he also showed an interest in drawing and painting.  Richard began his musical training at the age of six with his father. He later studied at the Paris Conservatoire with Paul de Portanciennes and Louis Vianel. He also studied composition with Gabriel Fauré and Camille Saint-Saëns.  Richard began teaching at the Conservatoire in 1899. He also served as the director of the Conservatoire's chamber music department from 1909 until his retirement in 1923. Richard died in 1934.  Richard's compositions include symphonies, string quartets, piano pieces, and songs. His most well-known works are the orchestral suite "Poésies" (1912), the opera "Faust" (1914), and the opera "Pelléas et Mélisande" (1922).
