The Chamber of Senators of Uruguay (Cámara de Senadores de Uruguay) is the upper house of the Uruguayan Parliament. It has 67 members who are elected by proportional representation to serve five-year terms.  The Chamber of Senators is a deliberative and representative body. Its main functions are to provide checks and balances on the actions of the Chamber of Deputies, to review legislation, and to provide a national forum for debate and discussion on public issues.   The Senate also has the power to investigate alleged violations of the Constitution or laws by members of the Chamber of Deputies, and can propose amendments to these same bodies.   The current president of the Senate is Jorge Larrañaga.
