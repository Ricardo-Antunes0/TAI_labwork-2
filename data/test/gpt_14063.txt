The Prescott Kid is a 1934 American Pre-Code Western film directed by George Marshall and written by Earl Baldwin and Arthur Ross. The film stars Andy Devine, Barbara Stanwyck, and Hoot Gibson.  The Prescott Kid is a western about a drifter named Tom who is taken in by a homesteader family and falls in love with their daughter. When the townspeople learn of their relationship, they push the family away, forcing Tom to choose between his love for the girl and his loyalty to them.
