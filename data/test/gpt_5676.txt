Baldemar Velásquez (born February 15, 1947) is a Mexican artist who has been painting for over four decades. Velásquez was born in the town of San Andrés Tuxtla, Veracruz, Mexico. After completing his elementary and high school education in his hometown, he studied at the Escuela Nacional de Artes Plásticas from 1967 to 1971. In 1973 he moved to Mexico City to study at the Universidad Iberoamericana, where he received his degree in painting in 1975. Velásquez then moved to Paris, France, where he continued his studies at the École Nationale Supérieure des Beaux-Arts from 1975 to 1977. In 1978 he returned to Mexico City and started teaching at the Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México. He has been a member of the Salón de la Plástica Mexicana since 1983. Velásquez has had many one-man shows throughout Mexico and abroad, and his works are in several public collections, including the Museum of Modern Art in New York City and the Musée du Louvre in Paris. He is also a member of the Instituto de Arte Contemporáneo (IAC) in Mexico City.
