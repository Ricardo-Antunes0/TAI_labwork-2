Bonaire is an unincorporated community in Houston County, in the U.S. state of Georgia. Bonaire is located off of US Route 129, northwest of Statesboro.  Bonaire was first settled in the early 1800s by refugees from the Slave Coast of West Africa. The community was originally known as "Bonair". In 1844, a post office was established at Bonair, and the community was renamed Bonaire in honor of the Dutch island of Bonaire. The community's economy was based largely on agriculture and livestock.  In the early 1900s, Bonaire's economy was based largely on tourism. The community's primary attractions were its beaches and coral reefs. Today, Bonaire is primarily a residential community.
