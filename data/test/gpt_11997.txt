Ruth Valborg Maria Gustafson née Pettersson (8 December 1904 – 3 June 1979) was a Swedish author, journalist, and screenwriter. She is best known for her novel Knut and Emma (1947), which tells the story of a young couple's sexual revolution in 1920s Sweden.  Ruth Gustafson was born in 1904 in Stockholm, Sweden to Maria Pettersson (née Gustafson) and Lars Pettersson. She attended Uppsala University where she studied sociology and literature. In 1930, she married journalist and author Sigurd Gustafson. The couple had two children, Karin and Lars. Ruth Gustafson died in 1979 in Stockholm.
