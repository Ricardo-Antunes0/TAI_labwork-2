Lingua Franca was an American magazine about English language culture and language teaching. It was founded in 1982 by John McWhorter and was published monthly until its discontinuation in 2010.  Lingua Franca was known for its informed and engaging coverage of English language culture, from language news and opinion to instructional materials, pedagogy, and technology. It was also known for its strong editorial position on the importance of language learning, as well as its commitment to innovation in the field of English language teaching. Lingua Franca's contributors included leading authorities in the field of English language education, as well as journalists, editors, and writers whose work reflected their wide-ranging interests in the language and culture.  Lingua Franca was discontinued in 2010 due to budgetary constraints.
