Dardan Molliqaj (born 25 February 1985) is an Albanian professional footballer who plays as a goalkeeper for Albanian club KS Kukësi.  Molliqaj started his career with local side Vllaznia Shkodër before moving to Italy in 2003, where he played for several Serie C1 clubs. He made his professional debut for Parma in the 2006–07 season, and went on to make over 100 appearances for the club. In January 2013, Molliqaj moved to Turkish side Trabzonspor, and he helped the club win the Turkish Super Lig championship in his first season with them. He was also named the Turkish Football Federation Player of the Year in 2013. In January 2016, Molliqaj moved to Greek side Olympiacos, and he helped the team win the Greek Super League championship in his first season with them.  Molliqaj has represented Albania at international level, and he has made over 20 appearances for the country. He made his debut for Albania in a 2010 World Cup qualification match against Macedonia, and he has since played at both the 2014 World Cup and UEFA Euro 2016 tournaments.
