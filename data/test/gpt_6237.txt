Timothy Sydney Robert Hardy  (29 October 1933 – 7 February 2006) was an English actor. He was known for his stage, film and television work.  Hardy was born in London, the son of the actress Ethel Hardy (née Sidney-Hardy) and the dramatist and author Terence Hardy. His paternal great-grandfather was Sir Arthur Thomas Hardy, 1st Baronet, a notable Victorian jurist and politician. Hardy attended Eton College, where he became a member of the theatrical society The Apostles. He then studied at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art (RADA), graduating in 1955.  Hardy made his professional stage debut in 1957 at the Old Vic, before moving to Shakespeare's Globe Theatre in 1959. In 1967 he appeared in a television adaptation of Tess of the d'Urbervilles directed by Peter Hall and starring Vanessa Redgrave. He then appeared mainly on stage in such productions as The Hardest Part by John Osborne, Othello by William Shakespeare, and Antony and Cleopatra by William Shakespeare.  Hardy's first major film role was as the title character in the 1964 film version of Henry IV Parts I and II, for which he received his first BAFTA nomination.
