The Goodere baronetcy, "of Burhope in the County of Hereford", is a title in the Baronetage of the United Kingdom. It was created on 6 October 1836 for John Goodere. The second Baronet represented Herefordshire in the House of Commons. The title became extinct on his death in 1897.   The Goodere baronetcy, of Burhope in the County of Hereford, was created on 6 October 1836 for John Goodere. He was the son of John Goodere and his wife Sarah, daughter of William Bouverie, of Burhope, Herefordshire. He served as High Sheriff of Herefordshire in 1845 and as Mayor of Hereford from 1852 to 1853. He also served as a Deputy Lieutenant for the County of Hereford and as a Deputy Lieutenant for the County Borough of Hereford. He died on 16 February 1897, aged 84 years and was buried at Holy Trinity Church, Hereford.   The heir apparent to the baronetcy is his grandson, George Goodere (born 1970).
