Kundu was born in 1930 in the city of Varanasi in Uttar Pradesh, India. He obtained his B.Sc. and M.Sc. degrees from the Banaras Hindu University,Varanasi, in 1952 and 1954, respectively. He then completed his Ph.D. at the University of Cambridge in 1957 under the supervision of Geoffrey Chew.  Kundu was a professor at the Banaras Hindu University from 1957 until his retirement in 1992. He also served as a visiting professor at the University of California, Berkeley, the University of Pittsburgh, and the University of Michigan. He was a member of the Indian National Science Academy, the Royal Society (London), and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.  Kundu was awarded the prestigious Shanti Swarup Bhatnagar Prize for Science and Technology in 1989, and the Padma Bhushan in 2001, both India's highest civilian awards. He died on 16 July 2013 at the age of 85 after a long illness.
