Sankey railway station, also known as Sankeybus Station, is located in the town of Sankey, Merseyside, England. It is on the Liverpool-Manchester Line and is served by Northern Rail.  The station was opened by the Lancashire and Yorkshire Railway on 1 March 1847 as Sankey Halt. It became a through station on the Liverpool-Manchester Line on 1 January 1855. The LYR's successor, the London and North Eastern Railway (LNER), renamed it Sankeybus Station on 1 January 1922. The station was closed for passenger services on 15 September 1960 but reopened on 2 October 1978 as part of the Beeching Axe closures.  The station has two platforms and is unstaffed. Access to the platforms is by staircases from the road below or a footbridge over the tracks. There is a small shelter on each platform and a waiting room with toilets.   The station is served by Northern Rail, which provides regular services between Liverpool and Manchester.
