Frank Jerry Kostro (born August 4, 1937) is an American former professional basketball player. He was a member of the Boston Celtics, Seattle SuperSonics and Milwaukee Bucks of the National Basketball Association (NBA) and played in the league for 13 seasons. Kostro was also a member of the United States national team, winning a gold medal at the 1964 Summer Olympics.  Kostro was born in Queens, New York. He attended Roosevelt High School in Elmhurst, Queens, where he played basketball for the school's varsity squad. Kostro then attended Northeastern University, where he played for the Northeastern Huskies men's basketball team. As a sophomore in 1959–60, he was named to the All-New England Conference first team. In his junior year, he led Northeastern to its first NCAA Tournament appearance in school history and was named the NCAAs Most Outstanding Player after averaging 21.2 points per game.  After his junior year, Kostro was drafted by the Boston Celtics in the second round (18th overall) of the 1960 NBA Draft. He played for the Celtics from 1960 to 1964 and then moved to the Seattle SuperSonics for 1965–66 and 1966–67, before joining the Milwaukee Bucks for 1968–69.
