Andreas Fugger (1394, Augsburg – 1457, Augsburg), also known as Andréas Förgert, was an influential merchant and banker in the Middle Ages. Fugger financed the exploration and colonization of new lands, made significant contributions to the development of commerce and industry, and became one of the wealthiest men in Europe.  Andreas Fugger was born in Augsburg in 1394. He started his career as a merchant, but later turned to banking. In 1425, he founded the Fugger Bank, which became one of the largest banks in Europe. He also developed a number of businesses in manufacturing and trade. He died in Augsburg in 1457.
