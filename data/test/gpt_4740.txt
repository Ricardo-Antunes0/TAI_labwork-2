Gille Escoib or Gilleasbaig of Menstrie is a 16th century Scottish nobleman and statesman. He is best known for his role in the Wars of Scottish Independence, during which he was a general in the army of King James IV.  Gilleasbaig was the son of Gilleasbuig, 6th Chief of Clan Escoib, and his wife Marjory, daughter of Sir John Gordon of Menstrie. He inherited the lordship of Menstrie from his father in 1593. He served as a general in the armies of James IV during the Wars of Scottish Independence, and was captured at the Battle of Flodden in 1513. He was later released and returned to Scotland.  Gilleasbaig died in 1537, and was buried at Menstrie Abbey.
