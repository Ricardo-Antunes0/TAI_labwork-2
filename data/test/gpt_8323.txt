Shan State, also known by the endonyms Kachin State, is a state in the north-east of Burma. It is bordered by the Chin State of Myanmar to the south and the Kachin Hills and Manipur States to the west. The state has an area of 4,848 square kilometres and a population of 1.5 million people. Shan State was once part of British India and became an autonomous state in 1948.
