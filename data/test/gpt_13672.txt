Suite algérienne in C major, Op. 60, No. 2, "L'amour est un oiseau rebelle"  The Suite algérienne is a set of six orchestral pieces written by French composer Hector Berlioz in 1829. The Suite is scored for solo cello, double bass, timpani, and strings. The work is named after the Algerian theme of its five movements.  The Suite was first performed in 1830 by the Paris Symphony Orchestra under the direction of Louis-Philippe Le Grand. The Suite's popularity ensured that subsequent performances would continue to be given even after Berlioz's death in 1869. The Suite has been recorded many times, and remains one of Berlioz's most popular works.
