Lygia Pimentel Lins (23 October 1920 – 22 June 2014), was a Brazilian photographer, sculptor, and art teacher.  Born in São Paulo, Lygia Clark began her artistic career in 1945 as a sculptor and painter. She moved to photography in the early 1960s and soon became known for her black-and-white images of people and places. Her work is considered part of the New Brazilian Photography.  Lygia Clark received several awards, including the prestigious Prêmio Camargo Guimarães in 1984 and the Praça da Liberdade Prize in 2002. In 2006, she was inducted into the National Museum of Art, Brasília. She died in Brasília at the age of 94.
