The Quill/Boven National Park comprises two separated units: the Quill National Park and the Boven National Park. The Quill National Park has an area of 490 square kilometers and the Boven National Park has an area of 1,010 square kilometers. The park is located in the Northern Territory, about 160 kilometers north-east of Darwin.  The park contains a wide range of habitats including tropical rainforest, grassland, shrubland and heath. There are a number of endemic species found in the park, including the Quill rat, the Quill frog and the Quill moth. The park is also home to a number of threatened species, including the northern quoll and the eastern bristleback lizard.
