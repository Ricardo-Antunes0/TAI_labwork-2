Singorgarh Fort, in the Damoh district of Madhya Pradesh, is a ruined fort with an impressive gate tower. The fort is located on the banks of the river Singrauli.  The Singorgarh Fort was built in the 15th century by the Chaulukya dynasty. The fort was captured by the Mughals in 1568 and remained under their control until 1659, when it was captured by the Marathas. The fort was sacked by the British in 1818 during the Second Anglo-Maratha War. After the war, it was abandoned and destroyed.  The Singorgarh Fort is located on a hilltop overlooking the river Singrauli. The fort is surrounded by a wall with three gates and an impressive gate tower. There are several temples inside the fort, as well as a series of cisterns.
