Audrey Puente (born March 3, 1970) is an American singer and actress.  Audrey Puente was born in Los Angeles, California, to Mexican parents. She began her career as an actress, appearing in minor roles in several films, including The Usual Suspects (1995) and Fearless (1996). In 1998, she released her debut album, Fee, which was produced by Prince and featured the hit single "I Believe in a Thing Called Love". The album reached number sixty-four on the Billboard 200 chart.  Puente's second album, Touch Me Again, was released in 2000 and featured the hit singles "Shape of My Heart" and "I Will Always Love You". The album reached number thirteen on the Billboard 200 chart and spawned two additional singles: "I'm Gonna Tell You a Secret" and "Ain't No Other Man". In 2002, Puente released her third album, Unapologetic Bitch, which included the singles "Foolish Games" and "Put It All on Me". The album reached number four on the Billboard 200 chart and spawned three additional singles: "Can't Stop This Thing We Started" featuring Fat Joe and R. Kelly; "I'm Outta Love" featuring 112; and "So What".  Puente's fourth album, I've Been Loving You Too Long (Not Enough), was released in 2007.
