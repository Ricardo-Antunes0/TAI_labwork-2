Halas Hall (officially the PNC Center at Halas Hall) is a multipurpose arena in the Near West Side of Chicago, Illinois, United States. Halas Hall is home to the Chicago Blackhawks of the National Hockey League (NHL). The arena also hosts concerts and other events.  Halas Hall was originally built in 1926 as the Chicago Stadium and was originally home to the Chicago Black Hawks of the National Hockey League. The Black Hawks moved to the newer United Center in 1997 and Halas Hall was renamed the PNC Center at Halas Hall. The Blackhawks moved back to Halas Hall in 2003 and currently play their home games there. The arena also hosts concerts and other events.
