Koninklijke Voetbalclub Kortrijk (often simply called KV Kortrijk, or simply Kortrijk) is a Belgian professional football club based in Kortrijk, playing in the Belgian First Division A. The club was formed in 1911 and played its first official game in 1912.  KV Kortrijk plays its home games at the Constant Vanden Stock Stadium. The stadium has a capacity of 22,000 and was built in 1969. It is named after Constant Vanden Stock, a former KV Kortrijk player and manager.  The club's traditional colours are blue and white. It has won the Belgian championship four times (1948–49, 1951–52, 1955–56, 1979–80), the Belgian Cup six times (1948–49, 1949–50, 1951–52, 1955–56, 1957–58, 1978–79), the Belgian Supercup twice (1968, 1977), and the Intertoto Cup once (1993).
