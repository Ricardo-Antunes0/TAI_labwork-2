Safdarjung Hospital is a multi-specialty hospital, and a teaching hospital in Delhi, India. It is named after the first Indian Governor-General of India, Lord William Bentinck.  The hospital was founded in 1862 by the British East India Company with an initial donation of Â£10,000 from the company's chairman, James Prinsep. The hospital was later nationalised by the Indian government in 1948. The hospital has become an iconic institution in Delhi and receives patients from all over India and abroad. It is also a major teaching hospital, with more than 750 doctors and 2,000 health professionals working at its various departments.  The hospital has a total bed capacity of 1,600 and a staff of 3,000. The hospital has a pharmacy, a bank and an outpatient department.
