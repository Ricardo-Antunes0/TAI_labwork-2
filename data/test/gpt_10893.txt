Louis-Gaston de Sonis (25 August 1825 – 2 November 1902) was a French composer.  Sonis was born in the town of Lille in Nord-Pas-de-Calais, France. He studied at the Paris Conservatoire with François-Joseph Fétis and won a first prize in 1846. He became a teacher at the conservatoire in 1855 and director of studies in 1876. He died in Paris.
