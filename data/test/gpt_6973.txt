Hangarter v. Provident Insurance Company is a case in which the Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals upheld the decision of a lower court to terminate the insurance contract of a driver who was convicted of vehicular homicide. The defendant, Jeffrey Hangarter, was driving at high speed when he hit and killed a pedestrian. The district court found that Hangarter's conviction for vehicular homicide made him an unacceptable risk to the public and terminated his insurance policy with Provident. The Ninth Circuit held that Provident had grounds to terminate Hangarter's policy because his criminal record made him an unacceptable risk to the public.
