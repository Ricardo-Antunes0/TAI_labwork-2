Paenibacillus is a genus of facultative anaerobic, Gram-positive, non-spore-forming bacteria. The genus is named after Paenibacillus fragilis, the type species. The members of the genus are able to ferment a variety of carbohydrates, including mannitol and xylitol.  The genus is distributed throughout the world, with various strains isolated from various environments. Some strains are capable of degrading toxic compounds, while others are used as probiotics in food and supplements.
