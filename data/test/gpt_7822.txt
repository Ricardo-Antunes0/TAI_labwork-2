George Ward (born 30 November 1993), better known by his stage name Cherry Valentine, is a British singer, songwriter and record producer.  Cherry Valentine was born in London, England, on 30 November 1993. He began his musical career as a teenager, recording demos and releasing a self-funded EP in 2013. The following year, he signed to Dirty Hit Records and released his debut album, "Cherry Valentine", in 2015. The album reached the top 40 in the UK and received critical acclaim, scoring 8/10 from Pitchfork and 4/5 stars from NME.  In 2016, Cherry Valentine released the single "Laugh Now Cry Later" which received airplay on BBC Radio 1 and BBC Radio 2. The song was also featured on the soundtracks of the EA Sports game "FIFA 17" and the Netflix series "Stranger Things". In 2017, he released the single "Heartbeats" which reached number one on the UK Singles Chart. The single was also featured on the soundtrack of the EA Sports game "FIFA 18".
