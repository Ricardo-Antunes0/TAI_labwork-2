Donald Frederick Charles Beck (born 21 March 1951) is a former Australian politician who was a Liberal member of the Australian House of Representatives from 1993 to 2004, and a senator for Victoria from 2004 to 2013. He was Deputy Leader of the Liberal Party of Australia from 2001 to 2003 and Leader from 2003 to 2004.  Beck was born in Melbourne, Victoria, on 21 March 1951. He was educated at Wesley College in Melbourne before studying law at the University of Melbourne. He was admitted to the Victorian Bar in 1975 and commenced practice as a lawyer in Melbourne. Beck married Lesley Mackenzie in 1978.  Beck became involved in politics in the early 1980s, when he was elected president of the Liberal Youth League. He contested the 1983 federal election as the Liberal candidate for the seat of Higgins, but was unsuccessful. In 1987, he was elected to the federal seat of Wannon, and served as parliamentary secretary to the Minister for Defence from 1988 to 1990. In 1993, Beck was elected to the House of Representatives as the Liberal Member for Warringah, defeating sitting Labor MP Bob Kerin.  In federal politics, Beck played a key role in Tony Abbott's leadership campaign in 2003. Abbott appointed Beck Deputy Leader of the Liberal Party later that year, and he became Leader following Abbott's victory at the 2003 federal election. During his time as Leader, Beck led the Liberals to victory at both the 2004 federal election and the 2007 federal election.
