Kaywin Feldman is an American archeologist and the current Associate Professor of Anthropology at the University of California, Santa Barbara.   Feldman was born in Los Angeles, California in 1975. She received her undergraduate degree from the University of California, Berkeley in 1997 and her M.A. from Brown University in 2001. Feldman then obtained her Ph.D. from Brown University in 2006 under the supervision of Nicholas P. Spanos.  After completing her doctoral studies, Feldman was a postdoctoral fellow at the University of Chicago from 2006 to 2008 and then an assistant professor at Northwestern University from 2008 to 2010. In 2010, Feldman joined the faculty at UC Santa Barbara as an associate professor.  Feldman's research focuses on the archaeology of pre-Columbian Mesoamerica and Central America, with a particular emphasis on the Maya civilization and its political and economic history. She has authored or co-authored several articles and book chapters on these topics, as well as a monograph entitled "Maya Architecture: Form, Meaning and Function" (2007).  In addition to her academic work, Feldman is also active in the field of public outreach and education. She has served on the editorial board of several academic journals, is a regular speaker at conferences and workshops on Mesoamerican archaeology, and has directed several archaeological excavations in Mesoamerica and Central America.  Feldman is married to fellow UC Santa Barbara anthropology professor David Stuart Feldman. They have two children.
