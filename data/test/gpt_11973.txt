Dirty Computer is the third studio album by Canadian singer-songwriter Justin Bieber. It was released on November 18, 2011, by Island Records. The album was produced by Bieber, Skrillex and Diplo, with additional production from Benny Blanco and Danja.  The album consists of pop and R&B songs, including the lead single "What Do You Mean?", which topped the charts in over 20 countries and became his third number one on the US Billboard Hot 100. "One Last Time" and "Never Let You Go" were also released as singles from the album. Dirty Computer received mixed reviews from music critics, who praised its production but criticized its lyrical content.  Following the release of his second studio album My World 2.0 (2010), Bieber was dropped by his record label Def Jam Recordings and management company Scooter Braun. In early 2011, he signed a recording contract with Island Records. During the same period, he began working on his third studio album with producers such as Skrillex and Diplo.  Dirty Computer was announced as its title on October 7, 2011. It was released on November 18, 2011, through Island Records. The album has a different sound from Bieber's previous two releases; according to Bieber, he wanted to explore different sounds that he hadn't experimented with before.
