Christopher Wylie (born 19 June 1989) is a British programmer and data analyst who created the data analytics firm Cambridge Analytica.  Wylie was born in London, England, to a Scottish mother and a Zimbabwean father. He has a BSc in computer science from the University of Edinburgh.  He started working for the software company Loughborough University before moving to London to work for the web-development company City+Co. In 2014, he started his own data analytics firm, Cambridge Analytica, with Alexander Nix.  Wylie was suspended from his role at Cambridge Analytica in March 2018 after reports emerged that the company had used Facebook user data without their consent.
