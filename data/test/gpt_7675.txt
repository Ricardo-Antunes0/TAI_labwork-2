He was best known for his performances in three consecutive editions of the Kentucky Derby, where he finished second in 2001, third in 2002, and winning the race in 2003.  Hernando was bred in Kentucky by Windward Estate Stables. He was sired by Northern Dancer, a leading American horse who won the Preakness Stakes and Belmont Stakes in 1978 and 1979 respectively. His dam was the impressive mare Unbridled's Song, who won twelve races including the Grade I Nunna Dei Stakes and the Grade II Whitney Handicap.  Hernando made his racing debut in a six furlong maiden race at Gulfstream Park on October 3, 1998, finishing second to the five-year-old filly Sudden Impact. He recorded his first victory in a six-furlong maiden race at Santa Anita Park on December 9.  In 1999, Hernando recorded three more victories including a win over older horses in a six furlong maiden at Gulfstream Park. On July 14 of that year he contested the Grade II Fountain of Youth Stakes at Saratoga Race Course against older horses and came second to eventual winner Rags to Riches.
