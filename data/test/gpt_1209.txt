Carl Safina (born May 23, 1955) is an American naturalist, conservationist, and author. He is the founding director of the New York City-based organization, The Safina Center for Conservation Biology.  Safina was born in Brooklyn, New York on May 23, 1955. He graduated from Boston Latin School in 1973 and from the University of California at Berkeley with a B.A. in 1976. He received his M.A. in 1978 from Harvard University and his Ph.D. in 1983 from Yale University.  Safina's work has focused on the study of animal behavior and conservation biology, with a particular emphasis on whales and dolphins. He has written extensively on these subjects, including The Dolphin Book (1988), The Whale Book (1991), and The Great Auk (2005).  Safina has also been active in promoting environmental awareness, serving on the boards of directors of numerous conservation organizations, including the World Wildlife Fund and The Nature Conservancy. In 2002 he was awarded an honorary Doctorate of Humane Letters from Brown University.
