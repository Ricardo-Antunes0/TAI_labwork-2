Ernesto Ramos Antonini  (April 24, 1898 – December 20, 1976) was an Argentine football forward who played for the Argentina national team in the 1920s. He was a member of the 1924 and 1928 Olympic teams.  Ramos Antonini was born in Buenos Aires on April 24, 1898. His father, also named Ernesto Ramos, was a railway worker. At age 11, Ramos Antonini began playing football with local clubs. He joined Club Atlético River Plate in 1916 and made his professional debut two years later. He scored 108 goals in 262 appearances with River Plate and helped the club win three league championships (1919, 1920, 1921).  Ramos Antonini also played for the Argentina national team. He made his debut in 1919 and scored two goals in six appearances during the 1920 Copa América. He played in five international games during the 1924 World Cup and scored one goal. He played in all six games of Argentina's victorious 1928 Olympic team. Ramos Antonini retired from football after playing for River Plate in 1931.  Ramos Antonini died on December 20, 1976 in Buenos Aires at age 81.
