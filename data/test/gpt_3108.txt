The 129th Rescue Wing (129 RQW) is a unit of the New York Air National Guard. The Wing is stationed at Jacob K. Javits National Guard Base, New York and is equipped with C-130 Hercules aircraft.  The 129th Rescue Wing was formed on 1 January 1963 as the 129th Fighter Squadron, one of the first squadrons of the New York Air National Guard. The squadron was equipped with F-86 Sabres and was assigned to the 108th Fighter Wing at Republic Field, Farmingdale, Long Island. In 1967, the squadron converted to the more modern F-4 Phantom II fighter aircraft and was reassigned to the 106th Fighter Wing at Griffiss Air Force Base, New York.  In 1977, the 129th began flying missions in support of United States Central Command (USCENTCOM) in Southwest Asia.
