Thomas Grove was born in about 1609, the son of Humphrey Grove of Walthamstow, Essex and his wife, Alice. He was educated at Queens' College, Cambridge, where he graduated BA in 1627 and MA in 1631. He then studied law at Lincoln's Inn, where he was called to the bar in 1636.   Grove first practised law in London, but soon moved to Worcester, where he became a successful lawyer. In 1654 he was appointed Recorder of Worcester. In 1655 he was appointed a justice of the peace for Worcestershire and in 1659 made a Freeman of Worcester. In 1660 he was elected Member of Parliament for Worcester, and held the seat until his death.   Grove was a judge on the King's Bench from 1660 to 1682. He is best known for his work on the common law, which he helped to develop. He is also remembered for his writings on law and history. Grove died in London on 27 January 1682.
