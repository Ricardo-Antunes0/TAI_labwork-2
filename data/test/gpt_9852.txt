Onofre Agustín Marimón (19 December 1923 – 24 February 2013) was a Spanish conductor.  Onofre Marimón was born in Madrid, the son of the Spanish composer and conductor Onofre Marimón and his wife, the pianist Mercedes de la Cueva. He studied at the Royal Conservatoire of Madrid, where he graduated with a degree in musicology in 1949. After completing his studies, he worked as a lecturer at the Conservatoire from 1949 to 1951.  In 1951, Marimón became assistant conductor of the RTVE Symphony Orchestra, and in 1954 he succeeded Rafael Frühbeck de Burgos as principal conductor. In 1957 he became music director of the Orquesta Sinfónica de Galicia, and in 1960 he succeeded Antonio de Ibargüen as director of the Orquesta Sinfónica Nacional de España. He served as director of both orchestras until his retirement in 1988.  Marimón conducted many of the world's leading orchestras, including the Berlin Philharmonic, London Symphony Orchestra, Philharmonia Orchestra, Rome Philharmonic Orchestra, Vienna Philharmonic Orchestra and Osaka Symphony Orchestra. He was also appointed honorary conductor of the Royal Liverpool Philharmonic Orchestra and guest conductor of the Orchestre National de France, La Scala Milan Orchestra, Royal Stockholm Philharmonic Orchestra, Shanghai Symphony Orchestra and Tokyo Metropolitan Symphony Orchestra.
