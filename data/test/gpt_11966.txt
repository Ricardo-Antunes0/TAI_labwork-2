Predrag Raković (; 10 June 191215 December 1994) was a Serbian philosopher and social scientist. He was a professor at the University of Belgrade, where he worked until his death.  Born in 1912 in Užice, Raković attended the Grammar School in his hometown and then continued his education at the University of Belgrade, where he received his doctorate in philosophy in 1937. From 1938 to 1941 he was a research associate at the Institute for Social Research in Belgrade. In 1941 he moved to the United States, where he taught at Columbia University and Princeton University. He returned to Yugoslavia in 1945 and resumed his teaching position at the University of Belgrade. He served as Dean of the Faculty of Philosophy from 1963 to 1971. Raković was a member of the Yugoslav Academy of Sciences and Arts (1945), the Serbian Academy of Science and Arts (1951), and the Yugoslav Academy of Political Sciences (1971). He died in 1994.  Raković is best known for his work in social philosophy, which is characterized by its concern with questions such as human freedom, moral responsibility, and social order. His thought has been influential both within Yugoslavia and internationally, and has been influential in the development of social theory. Some key works include "The Concept of Social Justice" (1951), "Philosophy as a Humanistic Discipline" (1963), "The Power of Ideas" (1971), and "The Sociology of Knowledge" (1973).
