The Procter & Gamble Company (P&G) is an American multinational consumer goods company that manufactures a wide range of products, including laundry detergents, toothpaste, cosmetics, and kitchen products. The company was founded in 1837 by two brothers in Cincinnati, Ohio. P&G is the world's second-largest consumer goods company measured by revenues, after Unilever.  P&G operates in more than 180 countries and employs more than 350,000 people. The company's products are sold in more than 200 countries under a wide range of brands. P&G has been named one of the World's Most Ethical Companies by the Ethics and Corporate Responsibility Institute (ECRI) every year since 2003. In 2012, P&G became the first major corporation to make a public commitment to achieving zero waste to land by 2030.
