Ramaswami Ayyar was born in 1896 in the village of Sivanur in Thanjavur district, Tamil Nadu. He completed his education at Presidency College, Madras and The Madras Law School. He started his legal career as an advocate in 1925 and served as an Additional Solicitor General of India from 1941 to 1947. He was appointed as the Chief Justice of India on 4 February 1962 and served until his retirement on 30 October 1969.  Ramaswami Ayyar was a leading figure in the Indian independence movement and played a significant role in framing the Indian Constitution. He was also one of the architects of the International Court of Justice and served as its first permanent judge from its establishment in 1946 until his retirement in 1969. Ramaswami Ayyar was awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honour, in 1974. He died on 9 November 1976 at the age of 83.
