Luambe National Park is located in the north-western corner of Zambia and is bounded by the Luambe River to the south, the Mpika River to the east, and the Luangwa River to the north. The park has an area of 1,733 square kilometers and was established in 1959.  The Luambe National Park is home to a wide variety of wildlife including lions, elephants, buffalo, leopards, hyenas, giraffes and many other animals. The park is also a popular destination for safari seekers.
