Roberto Elizondo (born December 8, 1955) is an American film and television producer and director. He is best known for his work on the horror genre, having produced and directed such films as The Haunting in Connecticut, The Amityville Horror, and The Conjuring.  Elizondo was born in Oakland, California. After graduating from college with a degree in film and television production, he began his career working as an assistant director on low-budget horror films. He then produced and directed his first feature film, The Haunting in Connecticut (2013), which received critical acclaim and grossed over $137 million worldwide. He followed this up with the horror films The Amityville Horror (2005) and The Conjuring (2013), both of which became box office successes.
