Mount Matthews is the highest peak in the Blue Ridge Mountains of North Carolina, United States. The peak is located in the Pisgah National Forest and is part of the Appalachian Mountains. It is located about northeast of Asheville.  Mount Matthews is composed of Precambrian gneiss and granite, and rises to a height of above sea level. The peak offers panoramic views of the Blue Ridge Mountains and the Great Smoky Mountains to the east, as well as the Black Mountains to the south. The summit can be accessed by a paved road from US Highway 191 or by hiking trails from nearby villages. The mountain is also popular for skiing in wintertime.
