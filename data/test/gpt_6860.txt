Flushing Town Hall is a historic Town Hall located at Flushing in Queens, New York. It was built in 1867 and is a three-story, Neoclassical Revival style brick building. It features a central clock tower and an elaborate cornice.  The Town Hall served as the seat of government for the village of Flushing until it was replaced by the current Flushing Town Hall in 1921. In 1985, it was designated a National Historic Landmark. It is currently used as administrative offices for the New York City Department of Education.
