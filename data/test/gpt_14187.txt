Eric Henry Edward Tovey (3 January 1929 – 17 September 1986) was an English comedian, actor and writer. He is probably best known for his role as Lord Littlebrook in the BBC sitcom Porridge (1971–1974), for which he was nominated for an Emmy Award.  Tovey was born in London, the son of Lilian (née Green) and Henry Tovey, a taxi driver. He was educated at Latymer Upper School and St Catharine's College, Cambridge, where he read law. He began his career as a legal clerk, but after several years he quit to perform in amateur theatricals.  Tovey made his first television appearance in 1954, appearing in an episode of the anthology series Play for Today. He got his break in the UK comedy scene in 1959 when he was cast as a guest star on the BBC1 sketch show The Frost Report. From 1959 to 1962, Tovey starred alongside David Frost and Ronnie Barker in the popular BBC sitcom Porridge. In 1971, he also starred as Lord Littlebrook in the BBC sitcom Porridge.  Tovey also appeared in several films, including The Return of the Pink Panther (1963), Bedazzled (1967), Monty Python's Flying Circus (1969–1974), The Pirates of Penzance (1948) and The Canterville Ghost (1951).
