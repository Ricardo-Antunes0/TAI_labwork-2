András Visky (born April 13, 1957) is a Hungarian-born Canadian-American conductor. He is the Artistic Director of the Atlanta Symphony Orchestra and the Conductor Laureate of the Baltimore Symphony Orchestra.  Born in Budapest, Hungary, Visky began his musical studies at the age of six, studying violin with Pál Kadosa and conducting with Zoltán Kodály. He later studied at the Conservatoire de Paris with André Previn and Pierre Boulez, earning a diploma in 1980.  Visky made his orchestral debut with the Orchestre philharmonique de Radio France in 1980, and worked with the orchestra throughout Europe before moving to North America in 1984. He began his tenure as Artistic Director of the Atlanta Symphony Orchestra in 2002, and was subsequently appointed Conductor Laureate of the Baltimore Symphony Orchestra in 2006. In addition to his work with these two orchestras, Visky also serves as Music Director of the Orchestre National de France since 2007.  Visky has received numerous awards and accolades during his career, including a Grammy Award for Best Classical Album for his recording of Tchaikovsky's Violin Concerto with Carlo Maria Giulini and the Orchestre National de France, as well as two Charles Ives Awards for Outstanding Achievement in Music. He has been described as one of the most influential and successful conductors of his generation, and has won praise for his advocacy of contemporary composers.
