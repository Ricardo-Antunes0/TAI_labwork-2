Carrozzeria Scaglietti  was an Italian automobile manufacturer, founded in the 1920s. The company was noted for its racing cars and luxury cars. After the Second World War, the company produced a range of more affordable cars. In the 1990s, it became a subsidiary of Fiat Chrysler Automobiles.  Founded by Count Scaglietti in Florence, Carrozzeria Scaglietti produced racing cars and luxury cars during the 1920s and 1930s. It was noted for its innovative design and high-quality construction. After World War II, Carrozzeria Scaglietti resumed producing more affordable vehicles, including the successful Fiat 128 and 126 sports cars. In the 1990s, it became a subsidiary of Fiat Chrysler Automobiles.
