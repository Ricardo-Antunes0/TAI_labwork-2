The Mokelumne Aqueduct is a  water conveyance system in Northern California, running from the City of Mokelumne Hill in Merced County to the Sacramento River. Completed in 1913, it is one of the longest aqueducts in use in the United States. The aqueduct is also notable for its art deco design and for its use of an early form of air conditioning.
