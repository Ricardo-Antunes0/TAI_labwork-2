Boris Safarovich Ebzeyev   is a Russian historian and political scientist who specializes in the history of the Russian Revolution.  Ebzeyev was born in Leningrad, Soviet Union, on November 11, 1929. He graduated from the Leningrad State University in 1951 with a degree in History. From 1957 to 1961 he was a research fellow at the Institute of World Economy and International Relations at the University of London. Ebzeyev then returned to Leningrad and became an assistant professor at the Leningrad State University in 1962. In 1971 he was appointed professor at the same institution and in 1980 he became a full professor.  Ebzeyev is a member of the Russian Academy of Sciences and has been president of its history division (1991-1992). He is also a member of the European Academy of Sciences and Arts, the American Academy of Arts and Sciences, and the French Academie des sciences morales et politiques. Ebzeyev has been awarded numerous prizes, including the Stalin Prize (1978),the Lenin Prize (1982),the State Prize of the Russian Federation (1991),the Order of Britain (1993),and the Order of France (1995).
