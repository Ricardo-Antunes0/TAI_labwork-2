He graduated from Occidental College in 1944 and received his Juris Doctor from the University of Southern California Law School in 1948. After serving in the United States Army during World War II, he began his legal career as a law clerk for Judge Joseph Howard of the United States Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit.  In 1951, Merriman opened his own law practice in Los Angeles. He became active in politics in the early 1960s, serving on the Los Angeles County Board of Supervisors from 1961 to 1965 and as its chairman from 1963 to 1965. In 1970, he was elected as the 31st Mayor of Los Angeles, serving until 1971 when he was elected Lieutenant Governor of California.  Merriman was elected Governor of California as a Republican in 1974. During his tenure, he advocated for fiscal conservatism and improved public safety through measures such as Proposition 13 and Proposition 7. He also enacted major social reforms such as Proposition 15 which granted state employees collective bargaining rights and Proposition 103 which raised taxes to fund welfare programs for the poor.
