PAL Airlines (formerly Provincial Airlines) is a Canadian airline headquartered in Ottawa, Ontario. The carrier operates regional and international passenger services.  PAL Airlines traces its history back to the formation of Provincial Airways in 1966. Provincial Airways was the first regional airline in Canada and operated regional services out of Ottawa and Montreal. The airline was renamed PAL Airlines in 2001. PAL Airlines flies to over 60 destinations in Canada, the United States, Mexico, Central America, the Caribbean, Europe, South America and Asia. PAL Airlines is a member of the Oneworld airline alliance.  In 2008, PAL Airlines announced it would be ceasing operations after filing for bankruptcy protection. However, the airline re-emerged in late 2009 under new ownership and restarted operations the following year. In 2012, PAL Airlines announced it would be merging with Air Travaux Quebec to form Air Canada Express. The merger was completed in 2013.
