Mahmoud Harbi Farah was born in 1921 in Hama, Syria. He studied at Damascus University where he earned a degree in law. Harbi initially worked as a lawyer before joining the Syrian Army in 1945. He rose through the ranks and served as the Chief of Staff for the Syrian Army before being appointed Deputy Prime Minister in 1966. Harbi became Prime Minister of Syria on 1 July 1976, serving until 19 September 1979. As Prime Minister, he played a leading role in the Syrian Civil War, which began in March 2011. On 29 September 1983, he was assassinated by the Islamic Jihad while in exile in Lebanon.
