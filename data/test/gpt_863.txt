Alex Job Racing is a former professional racing team that was active in Formula One from 2002 to 2003. The team was founded by Alex Job, a British racing driver who had competed in Formula One from 1991 to 2000.  The team participated in nine races of the 2002 season, but failed to score a point. For the 2003 season, the team changed its name to Brawn GP and entered only four races. However, the team achieved two podium finishes, including a second-place finish at the Belgian Grand Prix. After the season, Brawn GP merged with Jaguar Racing to create the new Jaguar F1 Team.
