The Sidama  are an ethnic group in eastern Ethiopia that number about 1 million people. They are the largest ethnic group in Semien Shewa Zone and the second largest in Amhara Region. Sidama is their main language.  The Sidama are a pastoral people who live in small villages near the shores of the Great Rift Valley. They are agriculturalists and herders, but also fish and hunt. The traditional economy is based on raising livestock, growing crops, and trading. They are traditionally known for their fine cloths, jewelry, and wood carvings.  The Sidama have a strong cultural tradition and are known for their music, dance, and story telling. They are also known for their bravery and resourcefulness in times of hardship. The Sidama are Christians, but also practice traditional beliefs such as witchcraft.
