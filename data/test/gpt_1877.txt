Michael Oliver Tighe is an American singer/songwriter, guitarist, and record producer.  Michael Tighe was born in New York City, New York on October 17, 1970. He is the son of singer/songwriter/guitarist Oliver Tighe and actress/singer Patti Smith. Tighe's first musical experience was playing guitar in his father's band, The Brooklyn Bridge.  In 1990, Michael Tighe joined the alternative rock band Hole as their second guitarist and primary songwriter. After six albums and two tours, Hole disbanded in 1998. In 2001, Michael Tighe released his solo debut album "State of Mine". The album peaked at number sixty-one on the US "Billboard" Top 200 album chart and spawned the hit single "You Don't Know What Love Is".   In 2003, Michael Tighe formed the folk-pop duo The Paper Kites with Lisa Loeb. The duo released two albums before disbanding in 2006. In 2010, Michael Tighe released his second solo album "The Unseen". The album debuted at number thirty-one on the US "Billboard" Top 200 chart and spawned the hit single "The Waiting Game".   In 2013, Michael Tighe formed the duo The Rare Birds with singer/songwriter Jenny Lewis. The duo released their self-titled debut album in September of that year.
