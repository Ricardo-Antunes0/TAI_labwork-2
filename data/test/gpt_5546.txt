William L. Haney (born June 17, 1938) is an American politician who served as the 36th Governor of Colorado from 1987 to 1991. A Republican, he was the first Hispanic governor in the United States.  Haney was born in Denver, Colorado, to parents of Irish descent. He graduated from Regis Jesuit High School in 1956 and then attended the University of Colorado, where he received his B.A. in political science in 1960. Haney then studied at the University of Notre Dame Law School, where he received his J.D. in 1964.  After graduation, Haney worked as a law clerk for Judge Jerry P. Pettit of the Colorado District Court. In 1965, he was elected county attorney for Adams County and served until 1966 when he was elected state senator for District 19, which encompasses most of Northern Colorado. He served two terms as state senator before being elected governor in 1986.  Haney was reelected to a second term as governor in 1990 and retired from public office after his term ended in 1991. He currently practices law with the firm of Hunton & Williams in Denver and is a member of the board of directors for the Rocky Mountain News Foundation and the Western Interstate Commission for Higher Education. He also serves on the advisory boards for Catholic Charities of Northern Colorado and Catholic Relief Services - Colorado Region.
