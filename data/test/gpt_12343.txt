Janet Merlo (born 1963 in Newfoundland, Canada) is a Canadian artist and sculptor. Merlo's work often explores the boundaries between sculpture, architecture, painting and installation art.  Merlo was born in 1963 in Newfoundland and Labrador, Canada. She studied at the Nova Scotia College of Art and Design in Halifax, Nova Scotia, graduating with a BFA in 1985.   Merlo's early work focused on performance and site-specific installation. Her first solo exhibition was held at the Power Plant Contemporary Art Gallery in Toronto in 1990, and her first major museum exhibition was held at the Musée d'art contemporain de Montréal in 1995. In 2003, she was awarded the Governor General's Award for Visual Arts. Merlo has been the subject of several documentary films, including Janet Merlo: Sculpture from Newfoundland (2002), Janet Merlo: The Body is Beautiful (2008) and Janet Merlo: What is Life? (2010). In 2013, she was awarded an honorary doctorate from York University.   Merlo lives and works in Toronto.
