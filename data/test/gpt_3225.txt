The Sanctuary of Our Lady of Knock, located in County Mayo, Ireland, is a shrine and pilgrimage site. The shrine is dedicated to Our Lady of Knock and is said to have been founded by Saint Patrick in the 4th century. Legend has it that Saint Patrick himself founded the shrine when he knocked on the door of a local woman who was praying to the Virgin Mary. The shrine became an important place of pilgrimage in the Middle Ages and has been visited by many notable people, including King Edward I of England and Saint John Paul II. The sanctuary is now a popular tourist destination and is also home to a small convent.
