The spotted eagle ray (Aetobatus narinari) is a ray in the family Aetobatidae. It is endemic to the Indian Ocean and the south-western Pacific Ocean.  The spotted eagle ray is a large, stout-bodied ray with a disc-shaped body and prominent spots on its back. It has a long, whip-like tail that can be up to 1.5 metres (5 feet) long. The spotted eagle ray is a predator that feeds on fish, cephalopods, and crustaceans.
