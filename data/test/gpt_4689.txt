Gilesgate is a place in County Durham, England. It is located on the A1, about south of Newcastle.  The name Gilesgate is derived from the Old English word "gildegate", meaning 'gate of the gilds'. The meaning of the place name may have been 'gate of the guilds' or 'gate of the merchants'. The first record of Gilesgate dates from AD 992. Gilesgate was a small village until the nineteenth century, when it became a suburb of Newcastle. Today, Gilesgate is a mainly residential area.  The main industries in Gilesgate are retail and service provision. There is also a small number of light manufacturing businesses in the area. The local public sector includes a primary school and a health centre. There are also several churches in Gilesgate, as well as a swimming pool and a football ground.
