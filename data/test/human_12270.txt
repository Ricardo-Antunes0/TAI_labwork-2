Inglis made his debut for Durham against the Warwickshire Second XI in the 1956 Minor Counties Championship. He played Minor counties cricket for Durham from 1956 to 1973, making 130 Minor Counties Championship appearances. He made his List A debut against Hertfordshire in the 1964 Gillette Cup. He made 7 further List A appearances, the last of which came against Essex in the 1973 Gillette Cup. In his 7 List A matches, he scored 152 runs at an average of 19.00, with a high score of 47. With the ball, he took 2 wickets at a bowling average of 41.50, with best figures of 1/23. By the time serious illness led to an early retirement from the game, Inglis had scored 6,457 runs for Durham in the Minor Counties Championship, at the time a county record. He captained Durham in 1972.
