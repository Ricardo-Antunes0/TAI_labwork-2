Georgy Timofeyevich Beregovoy (, ; 15 April 1932 – 21 November 1998) was a Russian football player and manager.  He played for FC Dynamo Moscow, FC Spartak Moscow, FC Barcelona and Real Madrid, winning the Spanish championship with Real in 1966–67. He also played three times for the Soviet Union national team, and managed them twice.  After retiring as a player, Beregovoy became a football coach. He was manager of FC Barcelona from 1985 to 1988, leading them to their first ever European Cup victory in the 1986–87 season. He then moved to AS Roma, where he won another two Serie A titles. After leaving Roma in 1992, Beregovoy had two short-lived managerial stints at Independiente and Atlético Madrid.
