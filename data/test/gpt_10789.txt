Kim Young-moo (김영무, 1944 - 26 November 2018) was a South Korean politician and diplomat. He served as the 12th Prime Minister of South Korea from 2008 to 2009, and as the Ambassador of South Korea to the United States from 2007 to 2008.  Kim Young-moo was born in 1944 in Seoul, South Korea. He graduated from Seoul National University with a B.A. in Political Science in 1966. In 1970, he earned his J.D. from Boston University School of Law.  Kim Young-moo began his career as an attorney with the Korean Legal Aid and Research Foundation in 1970. He served as the president of the foundation from 1984 to 1988. From 1988 to 1993, he served as the President of the Korea Chamber of Commerce and Industry.  Kim Young-moo entered politics in 1993 when he was elected to the National Assembly of South Korea as a member of the ruling Democratic Party of Korea (DPK). He served as Minister for Trade and Industry from 1998 to 2000 and as Minister for Finance from 2000 to 2002.  In 2002, Kim Young-moo was elected to the National Assembly again, this time as a member of the conservative New Frontier Party (NFP). He served as Minister for Planning from 2003 to 2004 and as Minister for Health and Welfare from 2004 to 2006.
