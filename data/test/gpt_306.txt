Jimmy Jax Pinchak (born James Justin Pinchak; December 5, 1933) is an American film and television director and producer. He is known for directing the 1971 film Patton, for which he won the Academy Award for Best Director.  Pinchak was born in New York City, the son of Russian Jewish immigrants. He attended Syracuse University, where he studied film and directing. Pinchak began his career as a cinematographer, working on films such as The Nun's Story (1959) and Battle of Algiers (1962). He made his directorial debut with the film Patton in 1971. Pinchak has been nominated for five Academy Awards, winning one for his direction of Patton. His other nominations were for The Godfather Part II (1974), All the President's Men (1976), Midnight Express (1978), and Driving Miss Daisy (1989).
