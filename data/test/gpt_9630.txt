Fjotolf Hansen (born 13 February 1979), better known by his stage name Anders Behring Breivik, is a Norwegian far-right extremist and mass murderer. He has admitted to killing 77 people, most of them teenagers at a summer camp on the island of Utøya on 22 July 2011.  Behring Breivik was born in Oslo, Norway, on 13 February 1979. He was raised in a middle-class family and had two sisters. Behring Breivik is a self-taught mechanic and has worked as a carpenter and construction worker.  Behring Breivik became politically active in the early 2000s, supporting the right-wing Progress Party. He also started to espouse anti-Muslim views, claiming that Islam posed a threat to Norway's culture and way of life. In 2007, he was arrested on suspicion of being involved in the arson of a youth center in Oslo. However, he was not convicted due to lack of evidence.  In early 2011, Behring Breivik began planning the bombing and shooting attacks that would take place on the day of the Norwegian parliamentary election, 8 September 2011. He stockpiled weapons and explosives at his home, and began planning his attack by reading political articles online and watching videos about far-right extremism.
