Jacob Merritt Howard (July 10, 1805April 2, 1879) was an American jurist and politician who served as the fourth United States Secretary of State (1853–1857) during the administration of President Franklin Pierce.  Jacob Howard was born in Litchfield, Connecticut on July 10, 1805. He graduated from Yale College in 1825 and studied law. He was admitted to the bar in 1827 and began a practice in Hartford, Connecticut.  Howard served as a member of the Connecticut House of Representatives (1837–1839), and as Lieutenant Governor of Connecticut (1841–1843). In 1845, he was appointed United States Attorney for the District of Connecticut. In 1847, he was elected to the United States Senate and served from March 4, 1849, until his resignation on February 8, 1853, to become Secretary of State.  Howard served as Secretary of State during the administration of Franklin Pierce and helped negotiate the Compromise of 1850. He retired from public life in 1861 and died in Hartford on April 2, 1879.
