Luis Alberto MacGregor Krieger (1918–1997) was a Chilean composer. He was born in Santiago, and died in Switzerland.  Krieger studied at the University of Chile with Alberto Ginastera and Andrés Segovia, and later at the University of Chicago with Arnold Schoenberg. He also studied privately with Nadia Boulanger and Darius Milhaud. Krieger's compositions include chamber music, orchestral works, choral works, and solo pieces. His best-known works are the opera "Ariel" and the string quartet "Concerto for String Quartet and String Orchestra".
