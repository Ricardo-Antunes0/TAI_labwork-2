Brian Hyland (born November 12, 1943) is an American singer and songwriter. He is known for his pop and rock music, and has had a successful career in both the United States and the United Kingdom. He has written and recorded songs for a variety of popular artists, including Barbra Streisand, Whitney Houston, Celine Dion, Elton John, Stevie Wonder, Rod Stewart, and Barbra Streisand.  Hyland was born in Chicago, Illinois. He began his career as a singer in the late 1960s, initially performing on the folk circuit. In 1969, he released his debut album, Brian Hyland. The album achieved modest success, but it was his follow-up album, 1971's Brian Hyland II, which brought him wider recognition. The album featured the hit singles "People Who Love Me" and "More Than You Know". In 1973, Hyland scored another hit with the song "I Will Always Love You", which became a worldwide success. Throughout the 1970s and 1980s, Hyland continued to release successful albums; among them were 1974's Brian Hyland III, 1976's Brian Hyland IV, and 1979's Brian Hyland V.  In the 1990s, Hyland began to focus on writing and recording songs for other artists. His collaborations with Barbra Streisand yielded two successful albums: 1994's Guilty (which spawned the hit single "The Way You Look Tonight") and 1998's Encore!
