Mahonia Hall is the official residence of the Governor of West Virginia. It is located in the city of Wheeling, and was completed in 1939.  Constructed in the early 1930s, Mahonia Hall served as the official residence of the Governor of West Virginia from its completion in 1939 until 1987. The building was designed by architectural firm Davis, Brody and Associates and cost $225,000 to construct. The mansion is a three-story, rectangular structure with a hipped roof and a front porch. The interior features formal living and dining rooms as well as nine bedrooms and nine bathrooms. Mahonia Hall is listed on the National Register of Historic Places and is currently used as the office of the Governor's staff.
