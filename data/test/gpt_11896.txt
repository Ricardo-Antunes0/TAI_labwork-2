Dichomeris xerodes is a moth in the family Gelechiidae. It was described by Meyrick in 1913. It is found in India (Assam).  The wingspan is about 16Â mm. The forewings are light ochreous-yellow, the costal edge blackish and the dorsal edge ochreous-yellow. There are three blackish dots on the middle of the costa, and a triangular black spot on the dorsum before the middle, as well as a corresponding dot on the tornus. There is also a small blackish spot on the apical third of the costa and three minute spots on the termen. The hindwings are light grey.
