Wojciech Tomasz Szczęsny (; born 18 April 1987) is a Polish professional footballer who plays as a goalkeeper for Serie A club Roma and the Poland national team.  Szczęsny began his career at Wisła Kraków, where he made his senior debut in 2006. In 2009, he moved to Arsenal, where he spent six years before joining Juventus in 2016. Szczęsny won the Serie A title with Juventus in 2017–18. He has also won the Coppa Italia and the UEFA Champions League with Arsenal and Juventus, respectively.  Szczęsny has represented Poland at international level since 2007. He has won 26 caps and represented his country at the 2014 World Cup and the 2018 FIFA World Cup.  Wojciech Szczęsny was born on 18 April 1987 in Kraków, Poland. He is of Polish descent. Szczęsny started playing football at the age of six for Wisła Kraków youth team.  In 2006, Szczęsny made his senior debut for Wisła Kraków in a 3–0 victory over Zawisza Bydgoszcz. He established himself as first-choice goalkeeper for Wisła during his time with the club, helping them win their first Ekstraklasa title in 2009 and reach the 2009 Polish Cup final.
