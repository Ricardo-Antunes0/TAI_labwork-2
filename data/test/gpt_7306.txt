Sir Gerald Gray Fitzmaurice  (24 October 1888 – 20 February 1962) was an Irish judge and Member of Parliament.  Born in Dublin, Fitzmaurice was the son of Maurice Fitzmaurice, 2nd Baronet and Hon. Emily Georgina Browne. He was educated at Eton and Trinity College, Cambridge, where he read law. He was called to the Irish Bar in 1912 and became a Queen's Counsel in 1931.  He was elected to the House of Commons as a Sinn Féin MP for Dublin South-East at the 1923 general election and held the seat until it was abolished in 1949. He served as Parliamentary Secretary to the Minister for External Affairs from 1924 to 1925, Parliamentary Secretary to the Minister for Justice from 1925 to 1927, and Parliamentary Secretary to the Minister for Transport from 1928 to 1929. From 1930 to 1931 he was Attorney General for Ireland.  In 1936 Fitzmaurice was appointed a Judge of the Supreme Court of Ireland and served until his retirement in 1958. He was knighted in 1945. He died in 1962 aged 78.
