Kid Boots is a musical with a book by Jerry Bock and lyrics by Sheldon Harnick. It is based on the story "The Kid" by Rudyard Kipling. The musical premiered on Broadway at the Lunt-Fontanne Theatre on November 10, 1971, and closed on February 5, 1973.  The show tells the story of a young boy who dreams of becoming a great musician. He is helped along his way by a group of eccentric mentors, including an old bootlegger and a pair of bandits. Ultimately, the boy becomes a world-famous musician.
