USS Rafael Peralta (DDG-115) is the 65th ship of the United States Navy. She was launched on October 27, 2000, and commissioned on March 3, 2002.  USS Rafael Peralta is named for Rafael Peralta (1876-1947), a Mexican-born American naval aviator who fought in World War I and World War II. He is most notable for his service as a fighter pilot during the Battle of Midway and as commander of the escort carrier USS Bunker Hill during the Battle of Leyte Gulf.  Peralta was born in Matamoros, Tamaulipas, Mexico on November 15, 1876. He enlisted in the United States Navy in 1897 and served as an aeronautical engineer in the battleship USS Maine (BB-10) during the Spanishâ€“American War. He then served as a pilot aboard several vessels before being assigned to Fighter Squadron 5 (VF-5), which he led during the Battle of Midway in June 1942. He was later promoted to captain and commanded the escort carrier USS Bunker Hill during the Battle of Leyte Gulf in October 1944. For his actions at Midway and Leyte Gulf, Peralta was awarded two Distinguished Service Medals and two Navy Crosses. He retired from the Navy in 1946 after more than 30 years of service.  USS Rafael Peralta is based at Naval Station Mayport, Florida and is assigned to Destroyer Squadron 2 (DESRON 2).
