Celia Margaret Britton, FBA (born 20 March 1957) is a British academic and historian. She is the current Vice-Chancellor of the University of Leicester.  Britton has held a number of senior academic appointments, most notably as Pro-Vice-Chancellor (Academic) at the University of Leicester from 2004 to 2007, and as Provost at the University of Dundee from 2007 to 2010. From 2010 to 2016, she served as Vice-Chancellor and President of the University of Surrey. In September 2016, Britton was appointed Vice-Chancellor and President of the University of Leicester, succeeding Paul Boyle.   Britton has written extensively on British history, particularly in the fields of constitutional history and women's history. She has also published books on the life and work of author C.S. Lewis and on Victorian cookery.
