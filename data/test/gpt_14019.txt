The Mezquital Valley  is a series of narrow, steep-sided ravines that run for about 45 kilometers (28 miles) northeast-southwest through the high country of the Mexican state of San Luis Potosí. The valley is an agricultural region and contains the towns of Mezquital, San Pedro Garza García, and El Mezquital. The valley's principal river, the Mezquital, flows through the valley. The climate of the valley is semiarid with a good deal of rainfall.
