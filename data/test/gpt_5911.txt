Sun Yi (184–203), courtesy name Shubi, was a military general of the state of Wei during the Three Kingdoms period. He was a cousin of Sun Quan, the founder of the state of Wu.  Sun Yi was born in 184, during the reign of Emperor Xian. He was a cousin of Sun Quan, the founder of the state of Wu. In 191, Sun Yi served in the campaign against Dong Zhuo. He later participated in the campaigns against Lü Bu and Zhuge Liang. In 200, he was made the military governor of Kuangyang Commandery (郭陽, in present-day eastern Shandong).  In 203, Sun Yi was killed in a battle against Cao Cao.
