Richard Godhard "Rich" Becker (born February 1, 1954) is an American entrepreneur, investor, and philanthropist. He is the founder of the software company Becker Inc., an early stage venture capital firm, and the Becker Foundation, a nonprofit organization.  Becker was born in Brooklyn, New York. He attended Yale University, where he double majored in engineering and political science. After graduation, he worked as a software engineer for Lockheed Martin. In 1984, he founded Becker Inc., a software company that developed accounting and payroll software. The company was later sold to Computer Associates.  In 1992, Becker founded the Becker Foundation, a nonprofit organization that focuses on education and social welfare. The foundation has granted more than $100 million to charity since its inception. In 2006, Becker was named one of "America's Top 100 Most Influential People" by "Forbes". In 2007, he was awarded an honorary doctorate from Yale University.
