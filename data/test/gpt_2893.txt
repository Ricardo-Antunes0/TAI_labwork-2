The Melrose Road Bridge is a two-lane, concrete arch bridge that spans the Passaic River in Passaic, New Jersey. The bridge was designed by American civil engineer Leon C. Hart and built in 1932. It is one of the few remaining bridges of its type in the United States. The bridge is located just south of Route 17 and east of Interstate 280, within a heavily populated area. The bridge has been listed on the National Register of Historic Places since 1985.
