Chaka Jara Kwahme Hodge (born 20 November 1981) is a retired English football player who played as a goalkeeper.  Hodge began his career at Charlton Athletic, making his debut in the 3–2 victory over Nottingham Forest in the Football League Cup on 7 October 1999. He went on to make over 100 appearances for Charlton, including the club's relegation from the Premier League in 2005. He then joined Leeds United, making his debut in a 1–0 defeat to Birmingham City on 14 August 2006. He made over 50 appearances for Leeds before being released at the end of the 2007–08 season. He then joined Millwall, where he made over 60 appearances before retiring in May 2013.  Hodge made his international debut for England in a 2–1 victory over Sweden on 10 November 2003. He went on to make 11 appearances for England, including the 2004 European Championship and the 2006 World Cup.
