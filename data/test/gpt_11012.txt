Shinobu Orikuchi was born on October 4, 1898, in Tokyo, Japan. He attended the Tokyo School of Fine Arts from 1918 to 1922, and then moved to New York City to study at the School of Design at Columbia University. While in America, Orikuchi became interested in the work of Wassily Kandinsky and Paul Klee.  On his return to Japan, Orikuchi began teaching at the Tokyo School of Fine Arts from 1929 to 1933. He also served as an art critic for various publications throughout his career. In 1948, he co-founded the magazine "New Art" with Tadanori Yokoyama and Norimasa Murasaki.  Orikuchi died on February 7, 1969, in Tokyo.
