Todd Jarrett is an American competitive shooter, firearms instructor, and television personality. He is best known for his work as a commentator for the National Rifle Association's (NRA) firearms training programs, as well as for his appearances on the History Channel show "American Gun".  Todd Jarrett was born in 1972 in Dallas, Texas. He began shooting at an early age, and by the time he was a teenager, he had developed a reputation as one of the best competitive shooters in the United States. In 1994, he won the prestigious USPSA World Championship Match with a score of 592 points. That same year, he also won the U.S. Open Match Championship.  In 1996, Jarrett began working as a firearms instructor for the NRA. He has since commentated on numerous NRA-sponsored shooting events, including the World Shooting Championships and the U.S. Olympic Trials. In addition to his work as a commentator, Jarrett has also appeared on the History Channel show "American Gun" and hosted his own firearms training program, "Todd Jarrett's Rapid Fire".
