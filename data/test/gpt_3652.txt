Leonard Charles Brown (born 1 October 1956) is a New Zealand politician. He is the Mayor of Auckland, serving since 18 October 2010, and was previously the Member of Parliament for Manukau City from 1999 to 2010.  Brown has been described as "a charismatic and flamboyant politician", and is often compared to another charismatic and flamboyant politician, US President Barack Obama. Brown is also known for his involvement in the business world, having been a senior executive at both Telecom New Zealand and Air New Zealand. He has been a moderate Green Party member since the early 1990s and has served as the party's spokesperson on transport, urban development, and environment.  Brown was born in 1956 in Napier, New Zealand to Lenore (née Callaghan) and Leonard Charles Brown. He attended Napier Boys High School before studying at the University of Canterbury, where he gained a Bachelor of Arts degree in 1978. After university, Brown worked as a marketing manager for Telecom New Zealand from 1978 to 1985. From 1985 to 1987, he was a marketing manager for Air New Zealand. From 1987 to 1999, Brown was a senior executive at Telecom New Zealand, including roles as general manager of telecommunications (1987–1991), managing director of Telecom (1991–1999), and chairman of Telecom Corporation (1999–2000).
