Moshe Shaked (February 21, 1945 - October 10, 2016) was an Israeli politician and jurist who served as a member of the Knesset for Likud from 2009 to 2015.  Shaked was born in 1945 in Tel Aviv, Mandatory Palestine. He studied law at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem, where he obtained his LL.B. in 1971. He worked as a lawyer for several years, before becoming a judge in the Tel Aviv District Court in 1987.  In 2003, Shaked was appointed to the Israeli Bar Association's Ethics Committee, and served on it until 2006. In 2009, he was elected to the Knesset as a candidate of Likud. He served as Deputy Speaker of the Knesset from 2013 to 2015.  In September 2015, Shaked announced that he would not run for reelection the following year, and would instead retire from politics.  Shaked died on October 10, 2016 at the age of 78
