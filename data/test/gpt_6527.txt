Linda J. Melconian is a former American politician who served as the 78th Lieutenant Governor of California from 2003 to 2007. A Democrat, she was a member of the California State Senate from 1997 to 2003.  Born in San Jose, California, Melconian earned a B.A. in political science from Stanford University in 1987 and an M.P.A. from the John F. Kennedy School of Government at Harvard University in 1990. She worked as a legislative aide for U.S. Senator Tom Harkin from 1991 to 1997 and for Governor Gray Davis from 1997 to 2003.  Melconian was elected lieutenant governor in 2003, defeating Republican nominee Tom Torlakson by a wide margin in the general election. She was re-elected in 2007 with more than 60% of the vote, becoming the first woman to be elected lieutenant governor in California and the fifth woman to serve as lieutenant governor in the United States.  After leaving office, Melconian served as executive director of Health Access California, a statewide coalition advocating for affordable health care for Californians, from 2007 to 2009. She also served on the boards of directors of Planned Parenthood Federation of America, Mercy Corps, and the Thurgood Marshall College Fund. In 2013, she was appointed chairwoman of the California Air Resources Board by Governor Jerry Brown.
