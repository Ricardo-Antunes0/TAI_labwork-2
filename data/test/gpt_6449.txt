The Midland Professional Championship is an annual professional golf competition played in the United Kingdom. It was first played in 1984 at the Old Course at St Andrews, and was originally a stroke-play event. From 1995 to 2000, it was a 54-hole event, but from 2001 onwards it has been a 72-hole event.  The Midland Professional Championship is one of the major events on the European Tour's calendar, and is also an important stop on the Challenge Tour's schedule.
