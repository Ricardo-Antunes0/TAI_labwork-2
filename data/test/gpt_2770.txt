Epano Vatheia is a Greek village in the municipality of Kastoria, in the region of West Macedonia. The village is located in the valley of the River Vatheia, 6 km from the town of Kastoria and 10 km from the town of Polykastro.   The village has a population of about 4500 people, who are mainly farmers. There is a small commercial centre with a small grocery store, a bakery, a post office, and a few cafes. There is also a small hospital. The village has two churches: an Orthodox church and a Catholic church. There are also two schools: one for primary school children and one for secondary school students.   The economy of the village is based mainly on agriculture. The main crops are wheat, barley, olives, and tomatoes. Other activities include beekeeping, sheep farming, and mining for marble and gypsum.
