Ben-My-Chree (Manx for "girl of my heart") is a small rural community in the Cariboo region of British Columbia, Canada. The population was 572 as of the 2001 census.  The settlement is situated on the banks of the Mychree River, a tributary of the Fraser River, and is adjacent to the town of Quesnel. Ben-My-Chree is accessed by Highway 97 and by air via Quesnel Airport. The community has a small post office, general store, school, church and ski hill. The local economy is based largely on agriculture, with logging and tourism also important sectors.
