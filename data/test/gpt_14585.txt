Katharina Schroth was born (February 22, 1894-April 8, 1966) in Posen, German Empire. After completing her apprenticeship in a porcelain factory, she worked as a ceramist and sculptor before emigrating to the United States in 1922. There she studied at the Pennsylvania Academy of the Fine Arts and the Yale School of Art, and opened her own studio in New York City. Schroth is chiefly remembered for her expressive ceramic sculptures of animals and people, which are characteristically rendered with warm colors and simple forms.  Katharina Schroth was born on February 22, 1894 in Posen, German Empire. After completing her apprenticeship in a porcelain factory, she worked as a ceramist and sculptor before emigrating to the United States in 1922. There she studied at the Pennsylvania Academy of the Fine Arts and the Yale School of Art, and opened her own studio in New York City. Schroth is chiefly remembered for her expressive ceramic sculptures of animals and people, which are characteristically rendered with warm colors and simple forms.  Her work has been exhibited throughout the United States and Europe, most notably at the Museum of Modern Art (1939), The Whitney Museum of American Art (1941), The Philadelphia Museum of Art (1963), The Royal Academy of Arts (1964), and The Victoria & Albert Museum (1966). She died on April 8, 1966 in New York City.
