He attended secondary school at Government College Ibadan, and studied law at the University of Ibadan.  Michika began his journalism career as a reporter for the state-owned broadcaster, Nigerian Television Authority. In 1966, he moved to Lagos to work for the Daily Times newspaper, where he became editor-in-chief in 1978. He later became publisher of the paper in 1984.  Michika was a vocal critic of corruption in Nigeria's government and society. He served as president of the Press Club of Nigeria from 1995 to 1997. In 2003, he was awarded the prestigious journalist award by the African Union. Michika died on 10 March 2009 in Lagos after a long illness.
