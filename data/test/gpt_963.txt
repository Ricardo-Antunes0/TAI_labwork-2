Born in Venice, he began his training there with his father, Pietro Tortoli. He then moved to Naples and studied under Giuseppe Bezzuoli. He returned to Venice in 1816, where he became a member of the Accademia di San Luca. In 1821 he traveled to Paris, where he studied under Jacques-Louis David. He also traveled to Rome, where he specialized in painting historico-religious subjects. He returned to Venice in 1832 and continued to work there until his death.
