Matthew D. Adler (born 1962) is the founder and CEO of iAd company, an online advertising company.  Matthew Adler was born in 1962 in New York City. After earning a degree in computer science from MIT, he started his first company, a software development consultancy, in 1984.  In 1995, he founded iAd (now part of AOL), an online advertising company, which became the fastest-growing dot-com company in history. In 2006, he sold iAd to Microsoft for $2.5 billion.  Since then, Adler has invested in a number of startups, including social network Bebo (sold to AOL for $850 million in 2008) and video sharing site Vimeo (sold to Google for $1 billion in 2012). He also co-founded mobile advertising company MoPub (sold to AppNexus for $1.2 billion in 2016).  In addition to his work in business, Adler is a vocal supporter of education reform and entrepreneurship. He is a member of the board of directors of the Rhode Island School of Design and the board of trustees of New York University's Tisch School of the Arts. He also serves on the boards of directors of Teach For America and The New America Foundation.  Adler was awarded an honorary doctorate from Brown University in 2016.
