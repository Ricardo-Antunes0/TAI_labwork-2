Willamette , from the Clackamas language of the Chinookan people, meaning "river of the whitefish"   The Willamette River is located in the U.S. state of Oregon and is the longest river in the state. It drains a basin of over 2 million acres (8,000 km2) and is the principal source of water for the city of Portland and the metropolitan area of Greater Portland. The river forms the boundary between Oregon's two most populous counties, Multnomah and Washington.
