Andrew Glaze (April 21, 1920 – February 8, 1996) was an American artist, who was noted for his abstract paintings.  Andrew Glaze was born in 1920 in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. He attended the Pennsylvania Academy of the Fine Arts in Philadelphia, studying under J.B. Neuman and Harold C. Schonberg. Glaze was drafted into the United States Army in 1943 and served in World War II as a captain in the infantry.  After the war, Glaze resumed his studies at the Academy, graduating in 1948 with a BFA. He then traveled to Europe, where he studied with famous painters such as Joan Miró and Max Ernst. Upon his return to the United States, Glaze began exhibiting his work at various galleries. In 1957, he had his first one-man exhibition at the Betty Parsons Gallery in New York City.  Glaze's work is noted for its abstract paintings, which often feature bright colors and geometric shapes. He died on February 8, 1996 in Haverhill, Massachusetts after a long illness.
