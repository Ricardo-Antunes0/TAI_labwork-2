Scott Allison Woerner (born December 18, 1958) is an American football coach and former player. He is currently the offensive coordinator and quarterbacks coach for the Kansas City Chiefs of the National Football League (NFL).  Woerner played quarterback for the University of Utah from 1977 to 1979. As a junior in 1978, he was a first-team All-American and led Utah to a Fiesta Bowl victory over Nebraska. Woerner was drafted by the Philadelphia Eagles in the second round of the 1979 NFL Draft, but opted to play in the Canadian Football League (CFL) with the Edmonton Eskimos. He played for Edmonton from 1979 to 1981 and won a Grey Cup championship in 1980. Woerner returned to the NFL with the Eagles in 1982. He played for Philadelphia until 1985 and then spent two seasons with the Tampa Bay Buccaneers before retiring.  Woerner began his coaching career as quarterbacks coach for Temple University in 1996. He was subsequently hired as quarterbacks coach and offensive coordinator at Texas A&M University from 1997 to 2000, where he helped lead the Aggies to an SEC Championship in 1998 and a BCS National Championship game appearance in 1999. Woerner was then hired as offensive coordinator and quarterbacks coach for the Arizona Cardinals in 2001, where he helped lead Arizona to an NFC Championship game appearance in 2002.
