He was the first indigenous person to be appointed a judge in New Zealand and the first Maori to become a High Court judge.  Born in 1933 in Auckland, Falconer was the son of Hari and Ngapuhi-te-Ata Falconer. He was raised on a small farm near Whakatane. Falconer attended Whakatane High School and graduated from the University of Auckland with a Bachelor of Laws in 1955. He then studied at the University of Cambridge, where he received a Master of Laws degree in 1958.  After returning to New Zealand, Falconer worked as a lawyer in Wellington. In 1965, he was appointed as an associate to the High Court of New Zealand, and in 1972 he became a full judge. He served on the Court until his retirement in 1998.  Falconer was also involved in human rights activism. He campaigned for Maori rights and for the recognition of indigenous people's rights around the world. He also advocated for the rights of prisoners and victims of crime.  Falconer died from cancer on 5 December 2009 at the age of 80.
