is a 1995 Japanese novel, a part of the Hara-Kiri series by Yukio Mishima. It tells the story of a young aristocrat's suicide, and the subsequent political turmoil it causes.  The novel is set in the fictional country of Owari during the tumultuous year 1860. The protagonist, Tatsuya, is the son of an aristocrat and a member of Owari's ruling elite. Tatsuya is in love with Kaede, the daughter of a poor samurai. When Tatsuya learns that Kaede is to be married off to another man, he decides to commit suicide in order to protect her. However, his plan backfires and instead causes widespread political upheaval.
