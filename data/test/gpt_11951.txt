Lamar "Ditney" Smith (1892 – August 13, 1963) was an American politician who served as a United States Representative from Texas from 1951 until his death in 1963. He is most noted for his work on the House Committee on Un-American Activities and for his outspoken anti-communism.  Lamar Smith was born in Hockley, Texas, in 1892. He attended the public schools of Hockley and Houston, and was graduated from the University of Texas at Austin in 1916.  Smith began his political career as a member of the Texas State Senate from 1923 to 1931. He was elected to the United States Congress in 1940, and served until his death in 1963. Smith was a member of the House Committee on Un-American Activities from its inception in 1945 until his death. He is also known for his efforts to restrict immigration into the United States, and for his opposition to Franklin D. Roosevelt's New Deal programs.  Lamar Smith was married to the former Thelma Faye Gaddy (February 13, 1896 – February 2, 1987). They had two children: Thelma Faye Smith and Lamar L. Smith, Jr.
