He studied at the School of the Museum of Fine Arts, Boston, Massachusetts from 1944 to 1947 and then at the Yale School of Art from 1947 to 1949. He began his career as a painter in the early 1950s and quickly became known for his vibrant and inventive paintings. In 1955 he moved to Paris where he continued to paint and print until his death in 2006.  Atlas is best known for his large-scale abstract paintings and prints, which often combine disparate elements into colorful and harmonious compositions. His work is noted for its fluid brushwork and imaginative use of color, as well as its innovative use of pattern.
