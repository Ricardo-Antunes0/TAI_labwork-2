Mark Hovell (21 March 1888 – 12 October 1918) was an Australian soldier and aviator who was killed in action during World War I.  Hovell was born in Melbourne, Victoria on 21 March 1888. He enlisted in the Australian Imperial Force (AIF) on 15 September 1914, and was posted to the 1st Battalion, Victorian Scottish Regiment. He saw action in Belgium, France and Germany, before being killed in action near Courcelette on 12 October 1918. Hovell was posthumously awarded the Distinguished Service Order (DSO) for his actions during the Battle of Cambrai. He is buried in Villers-Bretonneux military cemetery in France.  Hovell was married to Mabel Hovell (née Davidson) and they had two children, Mark and Joan.
