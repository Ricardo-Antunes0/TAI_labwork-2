Tempest Anderson was born in Albany, New York on December 7, 1846. After studying at the University of Michigan and Columbia University, he became a reporter for the "New York Tribune" in 1868. He wrote several novels, including "The Reef" (1887), "The Fortunes of Richard Mahony" (1892), and "25 Thousand Leagues Under the Sea" (1896). His plays include "The Power of the Press" (1892), "The Great God Brown" (1893), and "A Flag for My Father" (1901). He also wrote screenplays for several films, including "The Birth of a Nation" (1915) and "Gone with the Wind" (1939). Tempest Anderson died on August 26, 1913.
