The Ware River is a  river in the United States, located in western New Hampshire. It is a tributary of the Connecticut River, and its length is about 49 miles (79 km).  The Ware River rises in the White Mountain National Forest near the town of Gorham, and flows generally northward through the towns of Canterbury, Hampton, and Debroy, before emptying into the Connecticut River near the town of Grafton. The river has a significant impact on the economy of these communities, as it provides a significant source of water for power generation and irrigation.    The Ware River is also known for its trout fishing, which is considered to be some of the best in New England. The river has been designated as a Wild and Scenic River, and it has been designated as a "Class I" trout stream by the New Hampshire Fish and Game Department.
