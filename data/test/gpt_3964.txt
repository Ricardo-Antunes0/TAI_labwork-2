Bishop Isidore Borecky (; 1 October 1911, Budapest – 9 October 1984, Budapest) was a Hungarian prelate of the Catholic Church who served as the second Archbishop of Budapest from 1970 to 1984.  Born into a peasant family in 1911, Borecky studied at the Székesfehérvár Theological Seminary and was ordained a priest in 1937. He worked as a teacher and then served as the rector of the Seminary of Székesfehérvár from 1954 to 1966. In 1966, he was appointed Auxiliary Bishop of Esztergom-Budapest and Titular Bishop of "Sidonium" by Pope Paul VI. He was consecrated bishop on 26 October 1966.  In 1970, Borecky was appointed Archbishop of Budapest and he served in that position until his death in 1984. During his time as archbishop, he helped to revitalize the Catholic Church in Hungary and to promote interfaith dialogue. He was also instrumental in the construction of several churches in Budapest. Borecky is considered to be one of the most important Catholic bishops in Hungary and he is also considered to be one of the most influential Hungarian prelates of all time.
