The book was adapted for the stage by Elaine May and won the Tony Award for Best Play in 1973. Her short story "The Blessing" was selected for inclusion in the Best American Short Stories anthology of 1984. Ingraham's other novels include "The Egypt Game" (1978), "The Girls" (1979), and "The Last Day of June" (1993).  Ingraham was born in 1922 in Greensboro, North Carolina, to Lucille Wright and Joseph Ingraham. She attended Meredith College and earned her B.A. in 1943. She then attended the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, where she earned an M.A. in English in 1946 and a Ph.D. in English in 1953. While a student at UNC, she met her husband, Charles Wright, who would later become a professor of English there.  Ingraham began her teaching career at the University of North Carolina at Greensboro in 1954, where she remained until her retirement in 1996.
