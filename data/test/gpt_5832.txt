Alberto Pizango Chota (born August 31, 1964) is a retired Argentine footballer who played as a striker.  Pizango started his professional career with River Plate in 1981. He made over 100 league appearances for the club, scoring 41 goals. In 1990, he joined Boca Juniors. Pizango scored almost 100 goals for the club, becoming the highest scorer in their history. He also won two Argentine Championships and one Copa Libertadores title with Boca Juniors. Pizango retired from professional football in 1999.  Since retiring from playing, Pizango has worked as a football pundit foro Fox Sports. He also coaches a team of youngsters in his hometown of Avellaneda.
