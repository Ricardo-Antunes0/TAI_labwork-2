He is best known for his role as the head of the first Australian diplomatic delegation to China, and his pivotal role in establishing diplomatic relations between Australia and the People's Republic of China.  Parkes was born in Sydney, New South Wales, to Scottish parents. He attended Sydney Boys High School before studying law at the University of Sydney. After serving in the Australian Army during World War II, Parkes entered public life in 1947, becoming an MP for thesafe Labor seat of Warringah. He served as Deputy Prime Minister from 1954 to 1955 and as Minister for External Affairs from 1955 to 1963. Parkes was also Australias first ambassador to China, serving from 1958 to 1962. After leaving office, Parkes served as chairman of several corporations and as a senior adviser to the government. He died in 1995 at the age of 82.
