The Magic Barrel is a 1958 collection of short stories by Roald Dahl. It was published to wide critical and popular acclaim, becoming one of Dahl's most popular works. The title story is a humorous tale of a boy who is sent to live with his Uncle Quentin in the country, where he is forced to work in the local brewery.  The book has been praised for its humor, its memorable characters, and its skillful mix of fantasy and reality. It has also been noted for its depiction of the harshness of life in rural England during the late 1950s.
