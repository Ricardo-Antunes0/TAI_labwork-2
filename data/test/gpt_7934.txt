Nevertire is a rural village in New Brunswick, Canada. The population was 829 at the 2006 census.  Located in the heart of the Miramichi River valley, Nevertire is a quiet rural village with a population of 829 as of 2006. The village is situated at the junction of two well-maintained country roads, and is surrounded by agricultural land, woods and rivers.  The primary economic base of the village is agriculture, with a number of families raising livestock and crops. There is also a small business community that provides goods and services to the residents of the village.  The village has a number of amenities available for its residents, including a library, recreation facilities (including an outdoor pool), and a number of churches and community groups.  The village is home to the annual Miramichi Folk Festival, which attracts visitors from across New Brunswick and eastern Canada.
