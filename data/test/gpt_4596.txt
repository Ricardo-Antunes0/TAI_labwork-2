Patrick Quarcoo, popularly known as PQ, is an Australian singer-songwriter, musician and actor.  Quarcoo was born in Melbourne, Victoria, Australia on 9 November 1977. He started his music career in the late 1990s, releasing his first album, "The Way It Is" in 2001. His second album, "What About Me?" was released in 2003 and peaked at number 10 on the ARIA Albums Chart. Quarcoo has since released five further albums: "Passion", "Life", "The Jazz Sessions", "Unleash" and "Songs from the South".  In 2006, Quarcoo starred as Jesus Christ in the Australian feature film "The Great Gospel Roadshow". In 2007, he released the album "Songs from the South", which peaked at number 2 on the ARIA Albums Chart and was certified platinum. In 2009, Quarcoo starred as Rolf Harris in the Australian feature film "Rolf". That same year, he released the album "Unleash", which peaked at number 1 on the ARIA Albums Chart and was certified triple platinum. In 2010, Quarcoo starred as Harry Fisher in the Australian feature film "The Paperboy".  Quarcoo has also appeared in a number of television shows and films including "Neighbours", "Blue Heelers", "Soul Music" and "Boys". He has also performed at a number of international festivals including Coachella Valley Music
