Bertram Leonard Wolstein (February 23, 1927 — December 16, 2007) was an American entrepreneur, investor and philanthropist.  Wolstein was the co-founder and CEO of Bertram Leonard Wolstein & Co., a private equity firm, which he helped to grow into one of the largest and most successful in the world. He also founded Bertram Media, a multichannel video programming distributor, and served as its chairman and CEO. He was a major donor to Jewish causes, supporting organizations such as the United Jewish Appeal, the Hebrew University of Jerusalem and the Holocaust Museum. Wolstein was also a member of the Council on Foreign Relations.  Bertram Leonard Wolstein was born on February 23, 1927 in Brooklyn, New York to parents who were Holocaust survivors. He attended Harvard College, where he played football and earned his bachelor's degree in 1950. Wolstein then served in the United States Army for two years before becoming an investment banker at Lazard Frères. In 1965, he co-founded Bertram Leonard Wolstein & Co., which became one of the largest private equity firms in the world. The company invested in a wide range of businesses including industrial conglomerates, media companies and technology startups. In 2003, Wolstein was ranked number 11 on Forbes' list of America's richest men with a net worth of $2.7 billion.
