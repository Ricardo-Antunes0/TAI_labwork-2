Fulvio Collovati (; born 9 May 1957) is an Italian film actor, director and screenwriter.  Collovati was born in Milan, Italy. After attending the Liceo Classico San Pietro in Milan, he studied acting at the Centro Sperimentale di Cinematografia in Rome. He made his film debut in the 1976 Italian comedy film Il giorno della civetta directed by Sergio Martino. He also appeared in the films Amarcord (1987), Una notte da leoni (1990), and The Legend of 1900 (1996).  Collovati has appeared in a number of critically acclaimed films, such as The Good, the Bad and the Ugly (1966), The Conformist (1970), The Assault (1971), Mean Streets (1973), The Postman Always Rings Twice (1981), King of Comedy (1983), The Untouchables (1987), and Casino Royale (2006). He also directed and starred in the crime film Illegal Tenderness (1988).  Collovati has been married to actress Barbara Bach since 1984. They have three children: daughters Gaia and Maddalena and son Lorenzo.
