Jan Smejkal (born March 22, 1946) is a Czechoslovak journalist, author, and human rights activist who was imprisoned in the former Czechoslovakia for his political activities.  Smejkal was born in Prague, Czechoslovakia in 1946. He graduated from the Charles University in Prague with a degree in law.  Smejkal started working as a journalist in the 1960s. He was a correspondent for several Czechoslovak newspapers and magazines, including "Mladá fronta Dnes", "Lidové noviny", and "Korunk". In 1981, he became the editor-in-chief of "Karta".  Smejkal became involved in the human rights movement in the early 1970s. He was one of the founders of the Czech Helsinki Committee, and he served as its first chairman. He also served as the chairman of the Czech branch of Amnesty International.  In 1982, Smejkal was arrested and charged with espionage for belonging to the Communist Party of Czechoslovakia. He was sentenced to 10 years in prison, but was released after five years on parole.  Smejkal resumed his journalism career after his release from prison. In 1993, he became the editor-in-chief of "Respekt". He resigned from that position in 1996 after resistance from the management over his editorial decisions concerning coverage of human rights abuses in Slovakia.
