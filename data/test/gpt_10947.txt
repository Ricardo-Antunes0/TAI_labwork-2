James Franco is an American actor, filmmaker, and artist. He has received critical acclaim for his work in both comedy and drama, and has been nominated for several awards, including two Academy Awards.  Franco began his acting career in the early 2000s, appearing in television series such as "Freaks and Geeks" and "General Hospital". His first significant role came in 2004, when he starred in the short film "Elite". After appearing in several smaller films and television roles, Franco made his feature film debut in 2007 with the comedy-drama "Palo Alto". He gained wider recognition for his performance in the 2010 psychological thriller "The Place Beyond the Pines", for which he was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Actor. In 2013, he starred in the drama film "Half of a Yellow Sun", which was also nominated for the Academy Award for Best Picture.  Franco's work as an actor has garnered him numerous accolades, including two Academy Awards, three Golden Globe Awards, two Screen Actors Guild Awards, and one BAFTA Award. He has been outspoken about his political views, particularly on social media.
