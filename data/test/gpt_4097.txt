The Hallaton Helmet is a decorated iron helmet dating from the early Middle Ages. It was found near Hallaton in Leicestershire, England, in 1859 and is now in the British Museum. The helmet has a flat top with a sharply pointed front, and is decorated with a series of overlapping circles and arcs, as well as a band of interlocking rings around the circumference. The helmet is dated to around AD 700-800 and may have been made for a king or high-ranking nobleman.
