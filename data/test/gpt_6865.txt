The Jacob Hoffstetter House is a former home in Jefferson City, Missouri, United States, that is now a museum. It was built in 1846 for Jacob Hoffstetter, a German immigrant who became a successful merchant and banker. The house is a well-preserved example of Greek Revival architecture, and is one of the few surviving buildings from Jefferson City's early days as a capital city.  The Jacob Hoffstetter House is located at 401 North 7th Street in Jefferson City. It was added to the National Register of Historic Places in 1978. The museum which occupies the house is open Tuesday through Saturday from 9:00am to 4:00pm, and on Sundays from 1:00pm to 4:00pm. Admission is free.
