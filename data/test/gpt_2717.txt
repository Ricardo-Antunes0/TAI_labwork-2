Ronnie Silas Dawson Jr. (born May 19, 1966) is a former Major League Baseball outfielder. He played for the Boston Red Sox, Montreal Expos, and Cincinnati Reds from 1988 to 2002. Dawson batted and threw right-handed.  Dawson was born in Montgomery, Alabama. He attended Alabama A&M University, where he played college baseball for the Aggies. He was drafted by the Boston Red Sox in the 11th round of the 1988 Major League Baseball Draft.  Dawson made his Major League debut with the Red Sox on July 2, 1988. He appeared in 71 games that season, batting .236 with five home runs and 22 RBIs. Dawson improved his batting average in 1989, batting .263 with nine home runs and 51 RBIs. In 1990, Dawson hit .287 with 17 home runs and 83 RBIs. His performance earned him a spot on the 1990 AL All-Star team. Dawson hit .269 with 26 home runs and 106 RBIs in 1991. He also won a Gold Glove Award that season.  Dawson became a fixture in the Red Sox lineup in 1992. That year, he hit .295 with 31 home runs and 129 RBIs. He was named to the 1993 AL All-Star team and won his second Gold Glove Award that season. Dawson also led the league in slugging percentage that year (.629). In 1995, Dawson hit .293 with 24 home runs and 102 RBIs while helping lead the Red Sox to their first World Series
