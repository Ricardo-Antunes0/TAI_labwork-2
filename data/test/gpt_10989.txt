Han Geng (Chinese: 韩庚; Pinyin: Hán Gēng; born December 3, 1964) is a Chinese retired professional basketball player. He played at the power forward and center positions.  Han Geng was born in 1964 in Wuhan, Hubei Province, China. He started playing basketball when he was just six years old. In 1980, Han joined the Wuhan Sports School and two years later he joined the Wuhan Institute of Physical Education. He played for the Chinese national team from 1983 to 1993. In 1992, he moved to the United States and played for the Fort Wayne Pistons of the National Basketball Association (NBA). He was selected to play in the 1993 NBA All-Star Game. After two seasons with Fort Wayne, he moved to the Vancouver Grizzlies in 1995. He played for Vancouver until 1999. In 2000, he moved to the Memphis Grizzlies and played for them until 2003. Han then signed with the Shanghai Sharks of the Chinese Basketball Association (CBA). He retired from professional basketball in 2005.  Han Geng has won three CBA titles with Shanghai Sharks (2000, 2001, 2002). He also won a bronze medal with China at the 1992 Summer Olympics.
