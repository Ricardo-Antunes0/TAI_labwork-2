The Layer Pyramid (known locally in Arabic as the Jabal al-Akhdar) is a mountain in the Arabia Desert, located east of Riyadh, Saudi Arabia. At 5,868 meters above sea level, it is the highest peak in Saudi Arabia and the sixth highest peak in the Arabian Peninsula.  The mountain is composed of sedimentary rocks, including sandstone, shale, and limestone. The summit is made up of granite and basalt. The climate on the mountain is arid and desertlike. There are no permanent streams or rivers on the mountain, and precipitation is rare.  There is little vegetation on the mountain. The only plants that grow there are cacti and bushes. The Layer Pyramid is a popular destination for hikers and climbers.
