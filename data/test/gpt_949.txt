The Minuteman Bikeway is a 10-mile (16-kilometre) bicycle path that runs through Lexington, Massachusetts. The bikeway is named for the American Revolutionary War Minuteman, a volunteer soldier who guarded the colonial borders.  The Minuteman Bikeway was constructed in two phases, with the first phase opening in 2009 and the second phase opening in 2013. The bikeway is a linear park-like path that runs through downtown Lexington. The path is designed for cyclists, pedestrians, and joggers, and features a variety of amenities such as bike lanes, benches, and shelters. The bikeway is popular with residents and visitors to Lexington, and has been cited as a key factor in the city's growth and revitalization.
