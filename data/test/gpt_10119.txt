The Green Party  is a centre-left political party in Norway. It was founded on 6 April 1992 and has been part of the governing coalition since the 2013 general election.  The Green Party advocates for environmentalism, social justice, and progressive taxation. The party has been particularly vocal in its opposition to the exploitation of natural resources and the development of oil and gas fields in the Norwegian Arctic. The party also promotes public transport, renewable energy, and energy efficiency.  The Green Party has had several successful candidates in national elections, most notably Jens Stoltenberg, who served as Prime Minister of Norway from 1997 to 2013. In May 2017, the Green Party announced that it would be withdrawing from the governing coalition effective immediately.
