Cecile Jospé ( Weiner; August 15, 1928 – May 3, 2018) was a French actress, singer and writer.  Jospé was born in Paris to a Jewish family. She studied at the Lycée Français de Rome and then made her stage debut in 1949 in the revue "Les Deux Magots". In 1957 she made her film debut in the role of Françoise in the Jacques Demy directed film "Les Demoiselles de Rochefort". Jospé became well known for her work in comedy films, appearing in such classics as "The Nun's Story" (1959), "The Ladykillers" (1955), "Bedtime Story" (1981) and "The Bird with the Crystal Plumage" (1970). She also appeared in a number of successful television dramas including "Le Cercle Rouge" (1969-1971), "Les Liaisons Dangereuses" (1987-1989) and "Dirty Rotten Scoundrels" (1988-1991). Jospé also wrote several books including memoirs, an autobiography and a novel. She was made an Officer of the Legion of Honour in 2005.  Jospé died on May 3, 2018, at the age of 90.
