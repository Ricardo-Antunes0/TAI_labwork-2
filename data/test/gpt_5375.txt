Anna Moroni (1613–1675) was an Italian educator.   She was the daughter of a canon of the Cathedral of Siena. In 1638, she married Francesco Moroni, a civil servant and the superintendent of public instruction in Tuscany. Together, they had two daughters.   Anna Moroni was one of the founders of the Accademia dei Nobili delle Scienze di Firenze, and served as its first president (1668–1669). She also served as a judge on the Accademia della Crusca. Moroni is most famous for her work "L'arte d'istruire", which was published in 1672.
