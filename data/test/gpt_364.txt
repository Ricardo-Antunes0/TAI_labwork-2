The Methodist Episcopal Church (MEC) was the first Protestant denomination to be established in the United States. The first MEC church was founded in 1784 in New York City, and today the MEC has more than 2,200 congregations across the United States and more than 1.5 million members. The MEC is a Christian denomination, and its faith is expressed in its teachings and practices. The MEC is known for its social gospel mission, which seeks to address social issues such as poverty, human trafficking, and climate change.
