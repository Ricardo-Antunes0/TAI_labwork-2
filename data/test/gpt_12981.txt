Roy Lee Johnson (born December 31, 1938) is an American politician who served as the 38th Governor of Texas from 1987 to 1991. He was a member of the Republican Party.  Johnson was born in Tyler, Texas, and attended public schools in Tyler and Austin. He graduated from the University of Texas at Austin with a B.A. in history in 1960. He served in the United States Marine Corps from 1960 to 1966, achieving the rank of captain. He then worked as a sales representative for IBM before beginning his political career as a city councilman in Austin from 1971 to 1973.  Johnson was elected the 34th lieutenant governor of Texas in 1982, and was reelected in 1984. He was elected the 38th governor of Texas in November 1986, defeating Democratic nominee Bill Hobby by more than 20 percentage points. As governor, Johnson enacted a number of conservative policies, including reductions in taxes, spending cuts, and reforms to the state education system. He also played a significant role in efforts to lure the Houston Oilers to relocate to Dallas, and he signed into law the bill that created The University of Texas at Dallas.  In 1991, Johnson was defeated by Ann Richards in the gubernatorial election. He subsequently retired from politics and returned to IBM full-time. In 1998, he was inducted into the Texas State Historical Association Hall of Fame. Johnson currently resides in Austin, Texas.
