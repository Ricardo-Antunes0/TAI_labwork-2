Del Rey (born December 22, 1959) is an American singer, songwriter, and record producer. She has released six studio albums and three compilations, as well as several singles and video albums. Born in New York City, Del Rey began her recording career in the early 1980s as a backing vocalist for punk band The Cramps. Her first solo album, titled Gothic Lolita, was released in 1984. It was followed by a series of unsuccessful releases that began with the platinum-selling Born to Die (2011).  In 2013, she signed a recording contract with industry giant Interscope Records. Her debut album for the label, Lust for Life, was released in 2015 to critical acclaim and became her first number-one album on the United States Billboard 200 chart. The album received nominations for Best Pop Vocal Album at the 56th Annual Grammy Awards and Best Rock Album at the 57th Annual Grammy Awards. In 2018, she released the critically acclaimed album Honeymoon.
