Bret "Hollywood" Freeman (born September 15, 1966) is an American actor and comedian who has appeared in films such as "The Mask" and "The Flintstones". Freeman has appeared as a guest star on television shows such as "The Office", "Parks and Recreation", and "Girls". He has also released two comedy albums, 2009's I'm Not Here to Make Friends and 2015's Let's Be Friends. While attending Bowling Green State University, Freeman began performing stand-up comedy at night clubs around Toledo. He then transferred to the University of Michigan where he studied drama.  Freeman's first film role was in the 1994 cult classic The Mask, in which he played one of the lead roles of Stanley Ipkiss. In 1997, he starred alongside John Goodman and Rosie O'Donnell in the hit comedy film The Flintstones. Since then, he has starred in films such as The Incredible Hulk (2008), No Strings Attached (2009), The Change-Up (2011), How to Train Your Dragon 2 (2014), and The Fault in Our Stars (2014). He also had a recurring role on the NBC sitcom Parks and Recreation from 2009 to 2013.
