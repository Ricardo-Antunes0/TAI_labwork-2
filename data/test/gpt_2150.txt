Jeffrey W. Frame is an American atmospheric scientist, who has made important contributions to our understanding of the Earth's climate.  Jeffrey Frame was born in Oklahoma City, Oklahoma, on December 2, 1945. He received his B.S. from the University of Oklahoma in 1967 and his M.S. from the University of Colorado at Boulder in 1969. He completed his Ph.D. at the University of California, Irvine in 1974, under the supervision of Harry Teague.  Frame has held various teaching positions at various universities, including the University of Colorado (where he was a professor from 1978 to 1990), Yale University (1991-1996), and the University of California, Irvine (since 1997). He was a visiting professor at the University of Geneva in 1998-1999 and a consultant to the United Nations Environmental Programme (UNEP) between 2000 and 2003.  Frame's research focuses on atmospheric science, with particular emphasis on the analysis of climate data. He has made important contributions to our understanding of the Earth's climate, most notably his work on the Clausius-Clapeyron equation and onthe feedbacks between the atmosphere and Earth's surface
