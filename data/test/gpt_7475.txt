Mark Lee Mayer  is scientist emeritus at the University of California, Berkeley, where he was a professor of earth and planetary science.  Mayer is best known for his work on the geology and geochemistry of the Earth's mantle and for his studies of the evolution of the Earth's climate. He also pioneered the use of gas chromatography to study the chemistry of rocks, and he was a leader in developing techniques for extracting minerals from deep crustal rocks.  Mayer has authored or co-authored more than 300 scientific papers, and he has made several important contributions to the understanding of plate tectonics, climate change, and the evolution of Earth's interior. He is also the author of two books: The Earth: A New View from Space (1980) and The Making of a Planet: The History of Earth, Life, and Mankind (1996).
