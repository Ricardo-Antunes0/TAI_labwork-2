Earl Clifford Snyder (born May 6, 1976) is an American former professional baseball player and current coach. Snyder played in Major League Baseball as a catcher for the Boston Red Sox and Detroit Tigers from 1998 to 2006.  Snyder attended Wake Forest University, where he played college baseball for the Demon Deacons. He was drafted by the Boston Red Sox in the second round of the 1998 amateur draft. He made his major league debut with Boston in 1998. Snyder batted .247 with 46 doubles, 11 home runs, and 63 RBI in 184 games over six seasons with the Red Sox. He was traded to the Detroit Tigers in 2006, and played for them until he was released after the 2007 season. Snyder then retired from professional baseball.  Snyder began his coaching career in 2007 as a catcher/coach for the Tigres de la Universidad Autónoma de Nuevo León of the Mexican League. In 2009, Snyder became a coach for the Boston Red Sox organization, working with their Rookie-level Gulf Coast League team. He returned to coaching in 2013 when he was hired as the catching coach for the Rochester Red Wings of the Class A-Advanced International League. In 2016, Snyder became an assistant coach with the Detroit Tigers organization.
