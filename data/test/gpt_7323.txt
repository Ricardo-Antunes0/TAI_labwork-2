Bruce Ray Christensen (born February 22, 1948) is an American businessman, investor, and philanthropist. He is the founder and president of the software company Oracle Corporation. He was ranked as the 27th richest person in the world with a net worth of US$27.5 billion as of March 2018.  Christensen was born in Detroit, Michigan, to Danish immigrants Helge Christensen and Ruth Christensen. He attended the University of Michigan, where he received a B.S. in mathematics in 1969. He then attended Stanford University, where he received an M.B.A. in business administration in 1971.  Christensen started his career at IBM as a software engineer in 1972. In 1977, he founded software company Oracle Corporation with $250,000 of his own money. Oracle became one of the world's leading software companies, with annual revenues of over US$40 billion as of 2016. He has been the company's president since its inception.  Christensen has been a major donor to philanthropic causes, including education and health care programs. He has also been a major proponent of entrepreneurship and innovation, which he has described as "the engine that will make America great again". In 2018, he was named one of "The World's 100 Most Influential People" by TIME magazine.
