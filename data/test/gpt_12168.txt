Francis Bacon (28 October 1909 – 28 April 1992) was an English artist who worked in a variety of media, including painting, sculpture, installation, poetry and fiction. He was noted for his abstract compositions and the use of shock effects in his paintings.  Bacon was born in London to an upper-middle-class family. He attended Eton College and the University of Oxford, where he studied philosophy. After serving in World War II, Bacon began to exhibit his work in the late 1940s. His first major exhibition was at the Whitechapel Gallery in 1952. He later moved to a number of other galleries around the world, including the Yale Center for British Art, the Royal Academy of Arts in London, and the Musée d'Orsay in Paris.  Bacon died at age 80 in 1992. He is widely considered one of the most influential artists of the 20th century. His work has been featured in numerous museum exhibitions and has sold for millions of pounds.
