George Scott Romney (born June 7, 1941) is an American businessman, politician and activist who served as the 47th Governor of Massachusetts from 2003 to 2007 and was a candidate for the Republican nomination for President of the United States in 2008.  Romney was born in Detroit, Michigan, to George Romney, who was the co-founder of Bain Capital, and Helen Ann Romney. He is the second of five children. Romney attended Cranbrook School in Bloomfield Hills, Michigan, and then studied at Stanford University, where he earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in 1962. After college, he worked for Bain Capital from 1963 to 1969. He then served as president of Bain Capital from 1970 to 1984.  In 2002, Romney was elected Governor of Massachusetts, becoming the first Mormon to serve as governor of a U.S. state. While governor, he enacted measures such as Medicaid reform, financial regulation reform and a public health insurance option in Massachusetts. He also promoted private sector job growth and helped steer the state to a balanced budget by cutting spending while raising taxes on individuals and businesses.  In 2008, Romney announced his candidacy for the Republican nomination for President of the United States. He finished in second place behind John McCain in the primaries and lost to Barack Obama in the general election. In 2012, Romney ran for re-election as Governor of Massachusetts but was defeated by Democrat Deval Patrick.
