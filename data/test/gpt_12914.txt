Jan Sadharan Express trains are series of express trains operated by Indian Railways. Introduced in 1976, these trains are named after the eminent Indian physicist and Nobel Laureate, Jan Sadharan.  The Jan Sadharan Express trains are a high-speed rail service that runs on the busy Delhi to Kolkata route. The trains have a top speed of 250 km/h. They are equipped with modern amenities such as air-conditioning, Wi-Fi and LCD screens allowing passengers to stay connected while on board. The trains are also equipped with CCTV cameras for added security.
