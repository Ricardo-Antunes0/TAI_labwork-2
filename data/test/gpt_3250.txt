Fleischer is best known as the director of the animated features The Lion King (1994), Toy Story (1995), and Toy Story 2 (1999).  Richard Fleischer was born in 1916 in the Bronx, New York City, to Russian Jewish parents. He was raised on the Lower East Side of Manhattan. Fleischer started his filmmaking career in the early 1940s as a second unit director on several low-budget B-movies. He made his first feature-length film, The Gang's All Here, in 1951. In the 1960s, he directed some of the most acclaimed and popular films of the decade, including The Boy Who Cried Wolf (1961), The Many Loves of Dobie Gillis (1963), and Batman (1966).  In the 1970s and 1980s, Fleischer directed several unsuccessful sequels to his earlier successes, including Son of Dracula (1974), The Golden Girls (1985), and Beetlejuice (1988). However, he regained critical and commercial success with The Lion King (1994) and Toy Story (1995), which were both nominated for Academy Awards. Toy Story 2 (1999) won both an Academy Award for Best Animated Feature and a BAFTA Award for Best Film Not in the English Language.
