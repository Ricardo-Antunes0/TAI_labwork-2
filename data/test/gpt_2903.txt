McMillan's early plays were produced at the Steppenwolf Theatre Company in Chicago, where she was a founding member of the company's writing program. Her first play, "Waiting for Guffman", was produced in 1996 and won the Obie Award for Best New Play. In 1998, McMillan's second play, "The Cripple of Inishmaan", was produced Off-Broadway at Playwrights Horizons. Her third play, "The Unnamed", was produced in 2002 at the Royal National Theatre in London.  McMillan has also written screenplays, including the Sundance Film Festival Award-winning "The Notorious Bettie Page" (2005), which was directed by Lars von Trier and starred Connie Nielsen and Jamie Bell. Her fourth screenplay, "Widows", was directed by Steve McQueen and premiered at the 2018 Cannes Film Festival.
