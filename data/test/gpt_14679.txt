Saori Haruguchi was born on November 4, 1985 in Matsusaka, Mie Prefecture, Japan. She is a graduate of Matsusaka Women's University.  Haruguchi has a sister, Ayaka Haruguchi, who is also a swimmer.  Haruguchi made her international debut at the 2001 World Aquatics Championships in Barcelona, Spain, where she finished seventh in the 200-meter breaststroke. At the 2002 Asian Games in Busan, South Korea, she won a silver medal in the 200 m breaststroke behind teammate Nozomi Ikeda.  At the 2004 Summer Olympics in Athens, Greece, Haruguchi qualified for the 200 m breaststroke by finishing second at the Japanese Olympic Trials. She competed against two-time defending champion and world record holder Dara Torres of the United States, as well as fellow Japanese teammates Ikeda and Risa Watanabe. Haruguchi emerged victorious with a time of 2:06.10 minutes, just 0.12 seconds behind Torres. Her silver medal marked Japan's first-ever Olympic medal in swimming.   Since 2004, Haruguchi has retired from competition and works as an assistant coach for her former teammate Ikeda at Fukuoka University of Technology.
