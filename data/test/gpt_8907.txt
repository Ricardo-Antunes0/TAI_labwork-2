The Duisburg-Dortmund Railway is an important and heavily used railway in the Ruhr area in North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany. It is a double track line with a main line of 18.5 kilometres and a branch line of 9.1 kilometres.  The Duisburg-Dortmund Railway was opened on 1 May 1872 by the Rhenish Midland Railway Company (RMV). The railway was built to connect the coal and steel industries of the Ruhr area with the railway network of the Rhine Empire. The line was extended to Dortmund on 1 October 1905 and to Essen on 14 January 1909. The Duisburg-Dortmund Railway was nationalized on 1 July 1940 and became part of the Deutsche Bundesbahn.  Today, the Duisburg-Dortmund Railway is an important transport link between the Ruhr area and central Germany. It is used for passenger and freight traffic. The line is also used for freight transport between Dortmund and Essen. The Duisburg-Dortmund Railway is part of the Rhine-Ruhr S-Bahn network.
