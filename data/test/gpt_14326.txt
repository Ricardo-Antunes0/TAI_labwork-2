Salix glauca is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae. It is found in Europe and Asia, and is naturalized in North America.  The genus Salix comprises about 130-140 species of deciduous trees and shrubs, most of which are native to temperate Eurasia and North Africa. Salix is the only genus in the family Salicaceae.  Salix glauca is a small, slender tree or shrub with a smooth, gray bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, 2-3 inches long and broad, with serrated margins. The flowers are white or pale pink, borne singly or in pairs on long stalks above the leaves. The fruit is a capsule containing two seeds.
