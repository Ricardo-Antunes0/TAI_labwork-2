The Serb National Council (, Srpsko narodno vijeÄ‡e, SNV) is the highest representative body of Serbs in Bosnia and Herzegovina.   The Serb National Council was established in 1992 as a successor to the Serbian National Council, which had functioned as the governing body of Serbia and Montenegro prior to their independence in 1991. The Serb National Council is headquartered in Sarajevo.  The Serb National Council is composed of forty-seven representatives, elected by proportional representation using the largest remainder method with a 5% threshold.
