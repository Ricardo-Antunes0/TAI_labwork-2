The Indian silverbill or white-throated munia (Euodice chrysolopha) is a small passerine bird. It is the only member of the genus Euodice and is found in the Indian subcontinent.  The Indian silverbill is a small passerine bird, measuring 16 to 18 cm in length and weighing around 25 g. The sexes are similar, but the female has a more extensively barred tail. The head is black with a white throat and a white band across the breast. The wings and tail are black, while the rest of the plumage is light grey or white. The Indian silverbill is found in open country, including parks and gardens, as well as grassy scrubland. It feeds on insects, especially termites and ants, but also eats seeds and fruits.
