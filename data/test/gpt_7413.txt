Moray Golf Club is situated in Lossiemouth, Moray, Scotland. It was designed by Old Tom Morris and opened in 1875. The club has an 18-hole course with a par of 72.   The course is well known for its difficult layout and is considered one of the most challenging in Scotland. The course is also popular with professional golfers and has hosted several major championships, including the Open Championship in 1902, 1936, 1949 and 1977.
