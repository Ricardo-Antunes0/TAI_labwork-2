The Point Defiance Bypass (officially the Lakeview Bypass) is a freeway bypass around Point Defiance, Washington. It opened to traffic on October 10, 1991 and has a total length of .  The bypass allows for traffic to flow in both directions between the Port of Tacoma and the Seattle area. The bypass also provides access to the I-5 corridor, which runs through Washington and Oregon. The Lakeview Bypass has been criticized for its high rates of accidents and its lack of pedestrian, bicycle, and transit access.
