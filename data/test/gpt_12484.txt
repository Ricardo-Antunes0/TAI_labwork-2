Ioan Borcea (January 30, 1879–July 30, 1936) was a Romanian historian and philologist.  Borcea was born in Iași, Romania, and attended the University of Bucharest, where he obtained his doctorate in 1902. He then became a professor at the university, where he taught Romanian history and philology. He also served as the dean of the faculty of letters. Borcea was a member of the Romanian Academy. He died in 1936.  Borcea is best known for his work on Romanian history, which focused on the Middle Ages and the Renaissance. He also wrote on Romanian literature and mythology. His notable works include "Istoria românilor" (1915), "Istoria literaturii române" (1922), "Legea istoriei" (1927), and "Ioan Slavici" (1937).
