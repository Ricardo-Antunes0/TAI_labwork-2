Clyde Norman Wilson (born 11 June 1941) is a retired Anglican bishop in the United States. He was the eleventh bishop of the Episcopal Diocese of Newark, New Jersey, from 2000 to 2011.  Wilson was born in 1941 in Stroud, Gloucestershire, England. He attended King's College, Cambridge, where he studied history and theology. He was ordained in 1966 and served as a priest in England and Wales. From 1983 to 2000, Wilson served as the rector of Christ Church Cathedral in Newark, New Jersey. He was elected bishop of Newark in 2000 and served until his retirement in 2011.  Wilson has been outspoken on social issues, notably on the need for gun control and gay rights. He is a member of the Council on Foreign Relations. In 2012, he published a memoir entitled "A Life Worth Living: Reflections on Finding Faith and Finding Myself".
