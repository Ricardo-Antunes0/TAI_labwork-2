Varden Conservation Area is a Pennsylvania state park located in Greene County. The park is situated in the Allegheny Mountains and encompasses of land.  The park was established in 1924 and is named for Colonel Edwin A. Varden, a Pennsylvania state legislator who championed conservation efforts. The park features a variety of natural features, including mountains, streams, and valleys. The park is home to a variety of wildlife, including deer, trout, black bears, and elk.   The park is open from dawn to dusk year-round. Visitors may access the park by car or foot. There are several hiking trails that lead through the park.
