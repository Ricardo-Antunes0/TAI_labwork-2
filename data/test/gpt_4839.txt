Born in Kona on the Big Island of Hawaii, George Naope began his singing career as a teenaged member of a group of local boys who would gather at night and sing traditional Hawaiian songs. Naope quickly rose to prominence as one of the most successful and influential singers in Hawaiian music history, performing throughout the islands and mainland United States. He is best known for his 1961 single "Pua O Waiana", which became one of the best-selling singles in Hawaiian history.  Naope also became an outspoken advocate for Hawaiian music and culture, helping to preserve and promote the music through his recordings, performances, and writing. He was also a major figure in the development of Hawaiian modern dance, serving as director of the Kalahari Dance Theatre from 1969 to 1979. Naope died in 2000 at the age of 74 after a long illness.
