Buckland is a village and civil parish in the Cotswold Hills in Gloucestershire, England. The population of the civil parish at the 2011 census was 531. The village is located 2 miles (3 km) north-west of Cirencester and 3 miles (5 km) south-east of Cheltenham.  Buckland was recorded in the Domesday Book as "Bocland". The name is thought to come from the Old English "boc" meaning a ox, and "land" meaning a landowner's estate. Buckland was part of the manor of Gloucester.  The parish includes the settlements of Buckland, Charlton Kings, and Stroud Green. Buckland has a pub, The George, and a small shop. There is also a cricket ground and a playground.  The Church of England parish church of All Saints dates from the 12th century. It has been designated as a Grade I listed building. The churchyard contains an Anglo-Saxon cross shaft and bowl.
