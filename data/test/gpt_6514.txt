He earned his BA in Zoology from the University of Utah in 1972, and his MA and PhD from the University of California, Davis, in 1976 and 1979, respectively. Fowler became a professor of zoology at Brigham Young University (BYU) in 1985, where he served as chair of the department from 2000 to 2003. He also served on the executive committee of the Association for Tropical Biology and Conservation, the editorial board of "BioScience", and as a trustee for the American Museum of Natural History.  Fowler was known for his work on amphibians, reptiles, birds, and mammals in the tropics. His research focused on understanding how human activities and climate change were affecting these animals and their habitats.
