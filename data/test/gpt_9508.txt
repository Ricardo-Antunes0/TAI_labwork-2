Frederick "Fritz" Augustus Heinze  (December 5, 1887 – February 9, 1966) was a German-born American chemist who developed the theory of chemical bonding. He is best known for his development of the Heinze equation, which describes the intermolecular forces between molecules.  Heinze was born in Paderborn, Germany on December 5, 1887. He studied chemistry at the universities of Göttingen and Munich, receiving his doctorate from the latter in 1912. He worked at the Ludwig Maximilian University of Munich as a professor until 1933, when he emigrated to the United States. Heinze became a naturalized American citizen in 1939. He held faculty positions at several American universities, including the University of California, Berkeley and Princeton University. He retired from Princeton in 1964.  Heinze died on February 9, 1966 in Princeton, New Jersey.
