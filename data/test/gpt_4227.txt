The Christmas Wife is a 1988 American made-for-television romantic drama film directed by Peter Bogdanovich and starring Faye Dunaway and Richard Dreyfuss.  The film is based on the novel of the same name by Nicholas Sparks.  After a car accident leaves her widowed, Linda Sheffield (Faye Dunaway) moves back home to help her mother run the family business. Linda soon meets Neil Harris (Richard Dreyfuss), a widower who has recently moved in next door. Linda is initially resistant to Neil's charms, but they begin to develop a mutual attraction. Linda eventually agrees to marry him, but only if he can annul his previous marriage. Neil is unwilling to do so, but he eventually agrees to divorce his wife. They are married on Christmas Day 1988.
