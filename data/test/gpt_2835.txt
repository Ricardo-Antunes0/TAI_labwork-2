The 28th (Māori) Battalion, more commonly known as the Māori Battalion, was a New Zealand infantry battalion of the New Zealand Army. Formed in 1916 as part of the New Zealand Expeditionary Force (NZEF), it served on the Western Front during World War I and the Sinai and Palestine Campaign of World War II. During World War II, the battalion was deployed to North Africa, Italy, Greece and Burma. In 1947, it was amalgamated with the 12th (Māori) Battalion to form the 2nd Māori Battalion.  The Māori Battalion was formed on 11 December 1916 as part of the New Zealand Expeditionary Force (NZEF). The battalion served on the Western Front during World War I and took part in many significant battles including The Battle of Flers–Courcelette, The Battle of Thiepval and The Battle of Polygon Wood.  During World War II, the Māori Battalion was deployed to North Africa, Italy, Greece and Burma. In 1947, it was amalgamated with the 12th (Māori) Battalion to form the 2nd Māori Battalion. The 2nd Māori Battalion fought in the Malayan Emergency, Indonesia–Malaysia Confrontation, Korean War and Vietnam War before being disbanded in 1984.
