The Final Frontier is the fifteenth studio album by British progressive rock band Yes. It was released on 12 September 1972, by Atlantic Records.  The album was recorded at the same time as their previous album, "Fragile". The title track, which was the first song written for the album, was released as a single and became their first top 10 hit in the United States.  "The Final Frontier" was certified platinum by the RIAA. The album has been reissued several times and has sold over 2 million copies worldwide.  In late 1971, Yes were finishing up work on their tenth album, "Fragile". Guitarist Trevor Rabin had started working on a new song called "The Final Frontier" and singer Jon Anderson felt that it could be the band's next big hit. They decided to include it on their next album, even though they didn't have any other songs written yet.   "The Final Frontier" was recorded at Atlantic Studios in New York City between December 1971 and March 1972 with producer Rick Wakeman (who had also produced "Fragile"). The title track was the first song written for the album and was released as a single in early 1972. It reached number ten on the "Billboard" Hot 100 chart and number one on the Adult Contemporary chart. The song also became their first top 10 hit in the United States.
