Obafemi Devin Ayanbadejo (; born March 5, 1969) is a former American football cornerback who played fourteen seasons in the National Football League (NFL). He was drafted by the Baltimore Ravens with the eleventh pick in the 1990 NFL Draft. He played college football for the University of Maryland.  Ayanbadejo has been a member of the Baltimore Ravens, Houston Texans, and Oakland Raiders. He has also been a member of the United States national team.  Ayanbadejo was born in Houston, Texas, to Nigerian parents. He attended Lamar High School in Houston and played football for the Lamar Cardinals. As a junior in 1988, he was an All-State selection and led his team to a state championship. Ayanbadejo accepted a scholarship to attend the University of Maryland, where he played football for coach Jerry Claiborne's Maryland Terrapins football team from 1988 to 1991. He was a three-time All-American (1991, 1992, 1993), two-time first-team All-Big Ten (1990, 1991), and was twice voted as an Associated Press first-team All-American (1991, 1992). Ayanbadejo was also a two-time winner of the Jim Thorpe Award (1990, 1991).  Ayanbadejo was drafted by the Baltimore Ravens with the eleventh pick in the 1990 NFL Draft.
