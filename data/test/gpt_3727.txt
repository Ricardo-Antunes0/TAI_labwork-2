The Wardwell-Trickey Double House is a historic house in the Uptown section of Nashville, Tennessee that was designed by architect Willis Wardwell and built in 1911. It is a two-story, Tudor Revival style brick house with a hipped roof and a projecting gabled wing. It features decorative brickwork, wooden trim, and a slate roof.  The Wardwell-Trickey Double House was built for C.C. Trickey, a Vanderbilt heir who became one of Nashville's leading businessmen. The house is now used as offices for the Tennessee Historical Commission.
