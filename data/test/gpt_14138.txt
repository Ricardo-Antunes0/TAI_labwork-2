Alastair Blair Worden, FBA (born 12 January 1947) is a British historian who specialises in the history of Britain and the British Empire. He has been Professor of Modern History at Cambridge University since 1994.  Worden was educated at Eton College and then at Trinity College, Cambridge, where he was awarded a First in Classics in 1969. He then undertook postgraduate studies at the University of California, Berkeley (1970-1971), and at the University of London (1974). After a period as an Assistant Lecturer at Trinity College, Cambridge (1974-1977), he was made a Fellow of Trinity in 1977.  In 1978, Worden moved to the University of Oxford, where he was Reader in Modern History until 1994. During this time he also served as Director of the Centre for Historical Research (1984-1994) and as Vice-Principal (1988-1994). In 1994 he moved to Cambridge University, where he has been Professor of Modern History since that time.  Worden has written extensively on British history, particularly the history of British imperialism and the history of the British Empire. His work has been highly influential, and he has been described by some as "the most highly respected British historian of his generation". He has also written extensively on European history, particularly French history.  Worden was elected a Fellow of the British Academy in 1997. In 2004, he received an Honorary Doctorate from Queen's University Belfast.
