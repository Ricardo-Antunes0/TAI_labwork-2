Its plumage is mainly black with a red crown and nape, a red band across the chest and black wings with a red bar across the primary feathers. The underparts are white with black margins. The male has a conspicuous yellow bill, while the female has a duller bill. The sexes are similar, but the juvenile has more white on its underparts.  The rufous-bellied helmetshrike is a shy bird, staying in the depths of the forest where it hunts small animals such as rodents and insects. It nests in a tree cavity, laying two eggs which are incubated by the female for around 20 days.
