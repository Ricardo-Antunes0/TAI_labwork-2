Kfar Giladi (, lit. Giladi Village,) is a kibbutz in northern Israel. Located on the eastern slopes of the Giladi Mountains, it falls under the jurisdiction of Hof Ashkelon Regional Council. In it had a population of .  Kfar Giladi was founded in 1957 by a group of young immigrants from Yemen. Over its years of existence, the kibbutz has undergone many changes, including a name change to Giladi in 2001 in commemoration of Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu's grandfather, who had helped found the kibbutz. Today, Kfar Giladi is a thriving agricultural community with a strong emphasis on environmentalism. The kibbutz has several industrial and commercial enterprises, as well as a number of educational institutions.
