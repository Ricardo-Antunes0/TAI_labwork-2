Nigel John Bartle Illingworth (born 23 November 1939) is an English actor and voice actor, who has worked in theatre, radio, television and film. He is known for his roles as Chief Inspector Morse in the television series Morse, Dr. Watson in the BBC's Sherlock, and Lord Summerisle in the ITV series Game of Thrones.  Illingworth was born in Manchester, England, the son of Lilian (n√©e Bartle) and Nigel Illingworth, a civil servant. He was educated at Fettes College and University College London (BA Hons), where he studied law. After graduating from university, he trained at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art (RADA).  Illingworth's first professional appearance was as Lennie Small in the West End production of Of Mice and Men in 1959. He made his television debut in 1967 with a supporting role in the BBC's series The New Avengers. In 1969, he starred as Henry DeTamble in the BBC's adaptation of Evelyn Waugh's Decline and Fall of the British Empire. In 1976, Illingworth played the role of Sherlock Holmes' Dr. Watson opposite Jeremy Brett's Arthur Conan Doyle in the BBC's acclaimed series Sherlock. Illingworth also appeared as a guest star on various other British TV series throughout the 1970s and 1980s, including Midsomer Murders and The Sweeney.
