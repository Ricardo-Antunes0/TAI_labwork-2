He was one of the leading figures in Abstract Expressionism and one of the most renowned painters of the 20th century.  Born in Thessaloniki, Greece, Constantine John Alexopoulos moved with his family to the United States in 1912. He studied at the Pennsylvania Academy of Fine Arts, where he was mentored by Robert Motherwell and Jackson Pollock. After World War II, he moved to New York City and began working with Max Weberman and Joseph Kosuth. In 1954, he moved to Westport, Connecticut, where he continued painting until his death in 1981.
