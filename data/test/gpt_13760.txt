General Jean-François André Sordet (17 May 1852 – 18 January 1906) was a French general who served in the Franco-Prussian War, the Second Spanish War of Independence, and World War I.  Sordet was born in Saint-Dié-des-Vosges, Alsace on 17 May 1852. He entered the French military academy at Saint Cyr in 1872 and became a second lieutenant in the 3rd Chasseurs à Cheval Regiment in 1875. He served in the Franco-Prussian War of 1870/71 as a lieutenant in the 6th Chasseurs à Cheval Regiment. He fought at Sedan, Gravelotte, and Wörth. After the war he was made a captain and returned to Saint Cyr to teach cavalry tactics.  In 1884 he became an instructor at the École de Guerre, and two years later was transferred to the 2nd Dragoon Regiment as an aide-de-camp to General Louis Dubois. In 1889 he became a professor at the École Supérieure de Guerre. In 1893 he was made a colonel and served as chief of staff to General Louis Joffre during the Second Spanish War of Independence. He distinguished himself at the Battle of Cardena and was made a brigadier general for his actions.  During World War I he commanded the 4th Cavalry Division on the Western Front. He was promoted to major general for his performance and later appointed commander of the 4th Army Corps
