Fredi Jesús González (born January 28, 1964) is a Mexican singer and actor. He is best known for his work in telenovelas, including La Séptima Estación and Mujeres Asesinas.  Fredi González was born on January 28, 1964, in Monterrey, Nuevo León, Mexico. He first gained fame as a singer in the early 1990s, when he participated in the Mexican national singing competition La Voz del Norte. He then moved to Los Angeles to pursue a career in acting, appearing in television shows such as LA Law and Melrose Place. González returned to Mexico in 1997 and released his debut album, Una Historia de Amor. In 2003, he starred in the telenovela La Séptima Estación, for which he received his first nomination for Best Actor at the Televisa Awards. He has since appeared in several other telenovelas and telenovela spin-offs, including Mujeres Asesinas (2005), Hoy por hoy (2007), Amor en Seco (2009), and Mujeres Al Aire (2013). In 2013, he starred in the film El Señor de los Cielos.
