He played professionally in the National Basketball Association (NBA) for the Boston Celtics, Philadelphia 76ers, and Milwaukee Bucks. Patterson is one of just four players in NBA history to score 10,000 points and grab 5,000 rebounds in his career.  Patterson was a three-time All-Star and two-time recipient of the NBA Most Valuable Player Award. He was also named to the NBA 50th Anniversary All-Time Team. He currently ranks third in league history in points scored (27,837), fourth in assists (5,192), fifth in rebounds (11,033), and sixth in minutes played (38,387). In 2008, he was inducted into the Basketball Hall of Fame.  Born in Augusta, Georgia, Patterson attended Central High School in Augusta before playing collegiately at Georgia Tech. He was drafted by the Celtics with the second overall pick of the 1951 NBA draft and spent his entire 11-year career with them. He won an NBA championship with Boston in 1959 and was a member of the United States national basketball team that won the gold medal at the 1960 Summer Olympics. Patterson retired from playing in 1967 and served as head coach of the Bucks for two seasons before being fired following a first-round playoff loss to Philadelphia. He then served as an assistant coach for three more seasons with Boston before retiring for good in 1972.  Patterson died on September 6, 2016
