Hygiea (minor-planet designation: 10 Hygiea) is a small, rocky, non-metal asteroid. It is classified as a C-type asteroid and is located in the central region of the asteroid belt, between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter. It was discovered on October 24, 1877, by German astronomer Max Wolf at Heidelberg Observatory.  Hygiea is named after the goddess Hygieia, who was guardian of health and well-being.
