Daviesia mimosoides, commonly known as blunt-leaf bitter-pea, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae. It is found only in eastern Australia and New Zealand. The specific epithet mimosoides is from the Ancient Greek word mimos, meaning "mimosa".  Daviesia mimosoides is a perennial herb up to 150 cm tall, with a smooth stem and large leaves. The flowers are solitary or in small groups at the ends of the branches, pink or purple, with a blunt tip. The fruits are purple pods about 2 cm long.
