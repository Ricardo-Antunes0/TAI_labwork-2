Born in New York City, McLaughlin received his B.A. from Yale University in 1938 and his Ph.D. from Princeton University in 1943. He served in the United States Army during World War II, where he developed the first econometric models for forecasting troop movements. After the war he worked at the RAND Corporation, where he developed the first econometric models for economic forecasting. He also served as associate editor of the Journal of Political Economy from 1965 to 1972.  McLaughlin was elected a Fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences in 1969 and a Fellow of the Econometric Society in 1973. He died in Cambridge, Massachusetts at the age of 71.
