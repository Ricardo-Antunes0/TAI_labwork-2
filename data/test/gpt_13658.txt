Ziyuan National Geopark  is a park located in the east of Sichuan Province, China. The park covers an area of 1,090 square kilometers. The park was established in 1992 and has since been managed by the State Forestry Administration.  The Ziyuan Geopark is situated in the eastern part of Sichuan Province, in the heart of the Ziyuan River Valley. The park covers an area of 1,090 square kilometers, with a majority of its terrain lying within the Chengdu Plain and the nearby hills and mountains. The park features a wide variety of geological features, including Paleozoic and Mesozoic rocks, sedimentary deposits, metamorphic rocks, and granite domes and hills. There are also a number of lakes and rivers within the park, including the Ziyuan River and Jiuzhaigou Lake.
