Boronia alata, also known as winged boronia, is a species of boronia native to eastern Australia. The species is a shrub or small tree growing to 2–3 m tall, with fragrant white flowers in late winter and early spring. The leaves are elliptical, 10–15 cm long and 5–7 cm wide, and are green on the upper surface and brown on the underside. The flowers are white with a red center, and are borne in clusters of up to 10 flowers.
