Karen Joy Shaw is an American microbiologist and virologist. She is the Michael S. Bloomberg Professor of Microbiology and Immunology at the Johns Hopkins University School of Medicine, and a Howard Hughes Medical Institute Investigator.  Shaw was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania on October 8, 1955. She earned her undergraduate degree from Swarthmore College in 1975 and her Ph.D. from the University of Pennsylvania in 1979.  After working as a postdoctoral fellow at the Rockefeller University, Shaw became an assistant professor at the University of California, Irvine in 1987. She moved to Johns Hopkins University in 1994 and was promoted to the Michael S. Bloomberg Professor of Microbiology and Immunology in 2005.   Shaw's research focuses on viruses and their interactions with human cells. She has made significant contributions to understanding how viruses cause diseases, and has developed novel ways to prevent them from causing harm. Shaw has also developed new ways to treat viral infections, including some that are not currently available to patients.   Shaw has received numerous awards and accolades for her work, including the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 2009 jointly with Robert C. Gallo for their work on AIDS Virus Discovery. Shaw is also a member of the National Academy of Sciences and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.
