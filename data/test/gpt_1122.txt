Bibigul Akhmetkyzy Tulegenova (, Bibıgül Ahmetqyzy Tölegenova; born 22 January 1984) is a Kazakhstani professional tennis player. Tulegenova has won one singles title on the ITF Women's Circuit and reached the third round of the 2007 Australian Open.  Tulegenova was born in the town of Kayseri, in Turkey, to Kazakh parents. She has one sister and a brother. Tulegenova moved to Kazakhstan with her family when she was three years old. Her parents are both doctors. Tulegenova began playing tennis at the age of six and turned professional in 2003.  Tulegenova played her first WTA tournament at the age of 16, qualifying for the $25,000 ITF event in Ankara, Turkey. She lost in the first round to eventual champion Vera Zvonareva. Tulegenova made her WTA debut at the 2002 Kremlin Cup, where she lost in the first round to defending champion Amélie Mauresmo.  Tulegenova's breakthrough year came in 2003 when she won her first WTA singles title at the $50,000 event in Tashkent, Uzbekistan. She also won two doubles titles that year, at tournaments in Kuala Lumpur and Bucharest, partnering Elena Dementieva. Tulegenova reached her first WTA semifinal that year at the $25,000 event in Tashkent, losing to Mauresmo.  Tulegenova's 2004 season was hampered by injuries
