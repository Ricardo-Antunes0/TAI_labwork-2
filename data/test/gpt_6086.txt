The Cullman Historic District is a historic district in Cullman, Alabama. It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1976.  The Cullman Historic District is a historic district located in Cullman, Alabama. The district is roughly bounded by West Cullman Street on the south, North Second Street on the east, South Main Street on the north, and East Cullman Street on the west. The district includes 197 contributing buildings and two contributing structures. The buildings are predominantly residential in character, with a few commercial and institutional buildings scattered throughout the district. The majority of the buildings are single-family dwellings, but there are also a few duplexes and multi-family dwellings included in the district. The architectural styles represented in the district are Classical Revival, Neo-Classical Revival, and Craftsman. The most popular period of construction for residences in the district is the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
