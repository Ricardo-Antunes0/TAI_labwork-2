Partible paternity or shared paternity is a form of paternity where the father is not the only possible father of a child.  Partible paternity is a form of paternity where the father is not the only possible father of a child. It can occur when two or more men are involved in a child's conception, when one man impregnates multiple women simultaneously, or when a man believes he is the father but cannot be certain. In some cases, partible paternity can be resolved through DNA testing.
