Robert Lachmann (28 November 1892 – 8 October 1968) was a Swiss mathematician and physicist.  Lachmann was born in Zürich, Switzerland. He obtained his doctorate from the University of Zurich in 1918 under the supervision of Max Born. From 1919 to 1922 he was an instructor at ETH Zurich. In 1922 he became an assistant professor at Yale University, where he remained until his retirement in 1955.   Lachmann's work in mathematics is mainly in the area of Partial differential equations and their applications to physics. He is known for his work on the Lachmann–Plesset equation and on the theory of diffeomorphisms.
