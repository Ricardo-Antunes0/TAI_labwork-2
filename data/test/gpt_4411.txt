He was a member of the Liberal Party, and was Deputy Leader of the Opposition from 1986 to 1991.  Lawson was born in Ballarat, Victoria, to Jim and Daphne Lawson. He studied at the University of Melbourne, graduating with a Bachelor of Arts in 1960. He then worked as a journalist for "The Argus" newspaper before entering politics in 1970 as the Liberal member for Morwell in the Victorian Legislative Assembly. He served as Minister for Lands and Forestry from 1972 to 1974, Minister for Primary Industry from 1974 to 1975, and Minister for Housing from 1975 to 1978.  In 1978, Lawson was appointed Deputy Leader of the Liberal Party and Shadow Treasurer. He became Leader of the Liberal Party in 1982, and Premier of Victoria in December 1982. During his time as Premier, Lawson oversaw extensive economic reform, including the privatisation of several state-owned businesses. Lawson also introduced legislation designed to reduce crime rates in Victoria, and he presided over Victoria's successful bid to host the 1994 Commonwealth Games.  After serving two terms as Premier, Lawson resigned as Premier in 1991 due to ill health. He remained a member of parliament until his retirement at the 2001 Victorian state election. Lawson died on 22 September 2018 at the age of 85 after a long battle with Alzheimer's Disease.
