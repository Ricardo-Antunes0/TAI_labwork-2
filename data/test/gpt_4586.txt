Elizabeth Thacher Kent (September 22, 1868 – May 22, 1938) was an American author and editor.  Kent was born in Albany, New York, the daughter of Elizabeth (née Thacher) and Dr. John Kent. She attended the Convent of the Sacred Heart in New York City and then studied at Oberlin College, graduating in 1890. From 1892 to 1897 she taught at the convent school in Albany. In 1897 she married Dr. George S. Kent, with whom she had two children.  In 1901 she began working as an editor for The Century Magazine, moving on to become its managing editor in 1911 and its editor-in-chief from 1913 until her death. She also wrote several books, including The Century Book of Verse (1911), A Book of New American Verse (1920), and The Poetry of Democracy (1927).
