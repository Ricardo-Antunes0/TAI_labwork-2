James Kakalios (born December 27, 1958) is an American astronomer and science popularizer. He is an associate professor at the University of Massachusetts Amherst, known for his work on the history of astronomy, comets, and space exploration.  Kakalios earned his undergraduate degree from the University of Michigan in 1980, and his doctorate from Harvard University in 1984. He was a postdoctoral fellow at the University of California, Berkeley from 1984 to 1985, and then a research associate at the Smithsonian Astrophysical Observatory from 1985 to 1988. He joined the faculty at the University of Massachusetts Amherst in 1988.  Kakalios has written or co-authored more than 300 papers and articles, including six books: "The History of Comets" (1992), "The Solar System" (1996), "The Physics of Star Trek" (1998), "The Astronomy of Time" (2002), "Beyond the Cosmos: A New Vision for Discovering Nature" (2008), and "Origins: How the Universe Began" (2012). He has also made more than 100 television appearances, including a 2000 episode of "The Daily Show with Jon Stewart", and has been featured on radio programs such as "Fresh Air" and "Science Friday". In 2013, he was awarded the Carl Sagan Medal by the SETI Institute.  Kakalios is married to astrophysicist Elisabeth Roussel-Kakalios.
