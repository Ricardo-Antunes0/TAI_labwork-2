Denise Marie Quiñones August (born 9 September 1984) is an American singer, songwriter and actress. She is the daughter of Mexican-American singer Gloria Estefan and Cuban singer Emilio Estefan.  Quiñones was born in Miami, Florida, the daughter of Gloria Estefan and Emilio Estefan. She has two brothers, A. J. Quiñones and Omar Quiñones, and a sister, Emily Quiñones. Her father is Cuban while her mother is Mexican-American. Raised in Miami, she attended the prestigious arts school, the American Academy of Dramatic Arts.  In 2002, at the age of 18, Quiñones released her debut album "Denise". The album reached number 54 on the "Billboard" Top Latin Albums chart. In 2003 she released her second album "Nueva Era", which also reached number 54 on the Top Latin Albums chart. In 2004 she released her third album "Era". The album reached number eight on the "Billboard" Top Latin Albums chart and number one on the Latin Pop Albums chart. She has also recorded songs for various soundtracks including "The Sisterhood of the Traveling Pants 2" (2004), "A Mighty Wind" (2003), "The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King" (2003), and "El Cantante" (2006).
