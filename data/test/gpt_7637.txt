Virginia Military Institute (VMI) is a public, military-focused university located in Lexington, Virginia, United States. Founded in 1839, it is the oldest and largest school of its kind in the United States.  The university has an enrollment of about 2,500 cadets and employs about 1,000 faculty and staff. VMI offers more than 60 undergraduate and graduate programs, as well as a variety of post-graduate programs and military-related courses. VMI is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools (SACS) and the National Association of Schools of Music (NASM).  The Institute's motto is "Semper Fidelis" or "Always Faithful." The institution has a long history of contributing to the defense of the United States. It played a pivotal role in training officers for the Union Army during the American Civil War, as well as officers for World War I and II. In addition, VMI graduates have served in every U.S. military conflict since its inception.
