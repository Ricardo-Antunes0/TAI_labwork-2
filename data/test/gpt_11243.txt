The El Leoncito Astronomical Complex (Spanish: Complejo Astronómico El Leoncito) is an astronomical observatory in the municipality of San Miguel, Leon, in the autonomous community of Cantabria, northern Spain.  The observatory is located on an extinct volcano, within the Leoncito National Park. It is operated by the Instituto de Astrofísica de Cantabria (IAC), and has an active telescope and a permanent exhibition on astronomy.  The complex was opened to the public in 1988. The main instrument is an 8-metre telescope with a mirror diameter of 3.2 metres. It is used for research on astronomical phenomena such as star formation, planetary nebulae, supernovae and galaxies.
