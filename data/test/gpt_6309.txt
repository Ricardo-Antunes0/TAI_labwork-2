The politics of the Republic of Peru are dominated by a two-party system, with the Peruvian National Congress representing the center-left and the Peruvian National Unity Party (PUNU) representing the center-right. The president, who is head of state and government, is directly elected by universal suffrage for a four-year term. The Peruvian Congress has 128 members, elected for a four-year term in single-seat constituencies using the d'Hondt method of proportional representation. The Judiciary is independent. Peru is a member of the United Nations, the World Bank, the International Monetary Fund, and the Organization of American States.
