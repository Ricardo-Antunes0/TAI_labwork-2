Lalitaditya alias Muktapida (IAST: Lalitāditya Muktāpīḍa; r. c. CE 763-824) was a prominent king of the Western Kshatrapas in the 8th century. He is chiefly remembered for his campaigns against the Pratihara Empire, which resulted in its disintegration and the victory of Buddhism in India.  Lalitaditya Muktapida was the son of the last great king of the Western Kshatrapas, Dharmapala, and a princess of the Sauhara dynasty. He succeeded his father on the throne in CE 763 and faced several rebellions within his kingdom during his long reign. In CE 810, he fought a great war against the Pratiharas, which resulted in their disintegration and the victory of Buddhism in India. Lalitaditya Muktapida died in CE 824 at the age of seventy-seven.
