The northern boobook (Ninox japonica) belongs to the family of birds commonly known as boobies. It is a medium-sized bird, around 30 cm in length with a wingspan of around 60 cm. The northern boobook is found in the temperate and subarctic regions of the Northern Hemisphere, where it subsists on small prey such as mice and voles.  The northern boobook is a shy and largely nocturnal bird. It favours dense forested areas, but will also occurs in open woodland and scrubland. It builds its nests in tree cavities or crevices, typically laying 4-6 eggs.
