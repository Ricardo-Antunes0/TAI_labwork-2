Nancy Coats-Ashley was the first female lawyer in the United States. She practiced law in Buffalo, New York, in the late 1800s.  Nancy Coats-Ashley was born in 1837 in Rochester, New York. After completing her undergraduate studies at Rochester Female Seminary, she studied law with a local attorney. She was admitted to the bar in 1868 and began practicing law in Buffalo, New York.  Coats-Ashley was an important figure in the legal community of Buffalo. She served as president of the Buffalo Bar Association and as vice president of the American Bar Association. She also served on the board of directors for several charities and organizations, including the Young Women's Christian Association (YWCA) and the Western New York Women's Club.  Coats-Ashley died in 1917. She was inducted into the Buffalo Women's Hall of Fame in 1984 and the National Women's Hall of Fame in 1987.
