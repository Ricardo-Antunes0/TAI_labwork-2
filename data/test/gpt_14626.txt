Elizabeth "Libby" Beaman (1844-1934), also known by her married name, Libby Van Vleck, was an American astronomer and member of the United States National Academy of Sciences.  Born in 1844 in Hudson, New York, Beaman attended the Female Seminary at Hudson and graduated from Wesleyan University in 1868. She began her career as an assistant to the Harvard College observatory, but left to become an assistant astronomer at the United States Naval Observatory in 1871. In 1877 she became an associate astronomer at the Naval Observatory and served as its director from 1904 to 1934. Beaman was also a professor at Smith College from 1901 to 1934 and at Barnard College from 1907 to 1934.  Beaman was a prolific writer, publishing more than 50 scientific papers and books. She was awarded the Clark Medal by the Royal Astronomical Society in 1912 and the Annie Jump Cannon Award by the American Astronomical Society in 1928. Beaman died in 1934 at her home in New York City.
