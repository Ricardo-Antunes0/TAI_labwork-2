He is known for his research on the history of Japanese art, specifically on the development of the ukiyo-e and shunga genres.  Takahashi was born in Kobe, Japan in 1923. After graduating from high school in 1941, he enlisted in the Japanese Army and fought in World War II. In 1947, he began studying art history at Kyoto University, where he received his doctorate in 1955. He subsequently taught at Nihon University, where he became a professor emeritus in 1991.  Takahashi's notable publications include "The Art of Ukiyo-e" (1975), "The Life and Work of Utamaro" (1987), and "Shunga: The Art of Sexual Desire" (1995). He has also been a visiting professor at universities throughout the world, including Columbia University, Brown University, and the University of California, Berkeley. In 2001, he was awarded the Order of Culture by the Japanese government.
