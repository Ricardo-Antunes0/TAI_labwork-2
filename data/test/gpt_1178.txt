Washington Grays Monument, also known as the Monument to the Washington Grays, is a granite obelisk in the center of Washington, D.C. It commemorates the first all-black military unit that served in the United States Army, the 54th Massachusetts Regiment, which was mustered into service on July 14, 1866.  The monument was dedicated on May 15, 1892, and is administered by the National Park Service. The monument is located at Seventh and D Streets NW in Washington's Judiciary Square.  The 54th Massachusetts Regiment was formed in Boston on May 10, 1865, from contingents of free black men who had served in the Union army during the American Civil War. The regiment was designated as Company A of the 9th U.S. Infantry and sailed for Washington, D.C., on July 14, 1866. The regiment arrived in Washington on July 21 and was mustered into U.S. service at Fort Stevens on July 24.  The regiment was assigned to garrison duty at Fort Stevens and Fort Monroe and participated in several actions against hostile Indians. The regiment was discharged from service on January 11, 1870.
