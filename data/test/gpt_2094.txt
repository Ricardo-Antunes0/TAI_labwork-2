Otto Schenk (born 12 June 1930, in Düsseldorf) is a German conductor and musicologist.  Schenk studied musicology, conducting and composition at the University of Munich, where his teachers included Antonio Pappano and Wolfgang Sawallisch. He made his conducting debut with the Münchner Rundfunk-Sinfonieorchester in 1957, and his first opera appearance was as conductor of Luchino Visconti's "L'italiana in Algeri" at the Bavarian State Opera in 1960. He became chief conductor of the Deutsche Oper am Rhein in Cologne in 1978, and held the post until his retirement in 2001.  His recording of the complete operas of Richard Wagner, conducted by him between 1978 and 1984, was awarded the Deutscher Schallplattenpreis. He has been honorary conductor of the Berliner Symphoniker since 2003.  He has written extensively on music and has been a frequent guest broadcaster. In 1992 he received the Bundesverdienstkreuz (Federal Cross of Merit).
