Janakinath Bose (Bengali: জানকীনাথ বসু) (also Janaki, born January 14, 1894 – August 18, 1945) was an Indian scientist and educator who is best known for his work in the field of electrical and electronic engineering. He was also a noted physicist, mathematician and inventor.  Bose was born in Calcutta (now Kolkata), in the British Indian Empire (now in India). He received his early education at home, before attending Presidency College at Calcutta. In 1925, he received a degree in physics from the University of Calcutta. He then attended the University of Cambridge, where he received a doctorate in mathematics in 1932.  After completing his doctorate, Bose returned to India to take up a position as a professor at Presidency College. In 1936, he was appointed director of the Department of Electrical Engineering at the University of Calcutta. He served in that position until his death in 1945.  Bose is best known for his work in the field of electrical and electronic engineering. Among his notable achievements are the development of Bose–Einstein statistics and the Bose–Einstein condensate. He is also credited with coining the terms "photon" and "quantum".  Bose was also a noted physicist, mathematician and inventor. Among his notable achievements are the development of an early form of radio transmission technology, and the invention of a passive Radio Frequency amplifier.
