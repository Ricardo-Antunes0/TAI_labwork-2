"C'est l'amour" is a popular song recorded by French singer Eddy Mitchell in 1967. Written by Serge Gainsbourg and written with the lyricist Jean-Paul Sartre, it was originally released as the B-side of Mitchell's hit single "Tout doux".  Mitchell's version became a hit in France, where it reached number one on the singles chart. It also reached number one in Belgium, Denmark, Finland, Germany, Italy, Luxembourg, the Netherlands, Norway, Portugal, Spain and Sweden. The song was also a success in the United States where it reached number 21 on the "Billboard" Hot 100 and number two on the Easy Listening chart. It has been covered by a number of artists including Joe Cocker, Barbra Streisand and Celine Dion.
