Kim Seong-hyeon (; born 17 September 1998) is a South Korean professional golfer.  Kim was born in South Korea and started playing golf at the age of six. He won the U-14 Korea Junior Golf Championship in 2013, and the U-18 Korea Junior Golf Championship in 2015. He became the first player from South Korea to win a professional tournament when he won the 2016 Japan Open on the Asian Tour.  In February 2017, Kim won his first major championship when he won the PGA Tour event The Players Championship. He was only the second Korean golfer to win on the PGA Tour, after Park In-bee won the 1985 Buick Classic.  Kim is also a member of South Korea's international team, and has played in the Walker Cup twice.
