Saguenay–Lac-Saint-Jean  is a region in Quebec, Canada. It is located in the north-central part of the province, on the border of Quebec and Ontario. Saguenay–Lac-Saint-Jean covers an area of 3,851 square kilometres and has a population of 1,325,320 as of 2006.   The region is dominated by the Lac Saint-Jean and the Saguenay rivers. The region also has a number of lakes, such as Lac Saint-Jean, Lac Mégantic, and Lac Pélékanos. There are also several mountain ranges in the region, including the Laurentian Mountains and the Monts Royaux. The region is well known for its winter sports, including skiing and snowboarding.
