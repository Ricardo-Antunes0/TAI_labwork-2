Anne Henderson is a museum educator known for her work with children and school programs at The National Gallery of Art. She also worked at the Southern Methodist University at the Meadows Museum and is currently the Director of Education and Community Engagement at the Frist Art Museum. Early life and education  Anne Henderson was born in Pampa, Texas, a small town in the Texas panhandle. She grew up going to museums in the area, such as the White Deer Museum and the Panhandle Plains Historical Museum. She graduated with a B.A. in English and a minor in Art History from Baker University in Baldwin City, Kansas in 1982. Anne Henderson was later accepted at the University of Texas at Austin and graduated with a M.A. in Art History in 1985. At the University of Texas at Austin, Henderson began working as a docent at the Archer M. Huntington Art Gallery, which was later renamed the Jack S. Blanton Museum of Art.
