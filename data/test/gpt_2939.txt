Ken Hawley  (born Kenneth Wybert Hawley, December 16, 1949) is an American lawyer who served as the 66th Attorney General of Missouri from January 2009 to January 2017. He is a Republican and was re-elected in 2014.  Hawley was born in St. Louis, Missouri, and attended Parkway North High School. He then attended the University of Missouri, where he earned a B.A. in political science in 1971 and an M.A. in 1974. After college, Hawley served in the United States Navy from 1972 to 1974. He then entered law school at the University of Missouriâ€“Kansas City and graduated with his J.D. in 1976.  Hawley practiced law in Kansas City, Missouri from 1976 to 1987. He was a judicial law clerk for Judge Roy Blount of the United States Court of Appeals for the Eighth Circuit from 1977 to 1978 and for Judge John Minor Wisdom of the United States Court of Appeals for the Tenth Circuit from 1978 to 1979.  In 1987, Hawley became a deputy attorney general under Missouri Attorney General William L. Webster, serving until Webster resigned to become president of the World Bank in 1993. Webster appointed Hawley as Missouri's 66th Attorney General on January 8, 2009, after defeating Democratic State Representative Rick Brattin in the 2008 general election.  During his tenure as Attorney General, Hawley initiated or defended numerous lawsuits against major corporations, including Monsanto Company, Toyota Motor Corporation, Ford Motor Company and ConocoPhillips Company.
