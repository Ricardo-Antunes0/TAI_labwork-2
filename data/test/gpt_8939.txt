Neil Robertson (born 11 February 1982) is a professional poker player from Australia.  Robertson has cashed in major poker tournaments, including a third-place finish in the World Series of Poker (WSOP) $10,000 buy-in event in 2006. He has also won several smaller events. Robertson's most notable win came at the 2009 Aussie Millions tournament, where he defeated two-time WSOP champion Phil Ivey in the final table to take home A$3,918,812.  Robertson has also competed on the international stage, making two appearances at the World Poker Tour (WPT). His best result came at the 2006 WPT Borgata Poker Classic, where he finished runner-up to eventual champion Chris Moneymaker.
