Henry "Harry" Collinge Speakman (19 January 1864 – 24 September 1932) was an English football player, manager and scout.  Born in Blackburn, Lancashire, Speakman played for Blackburn Rovers and Newton Heath before moving to Manchester United in 1885. He made over 250 appearances for the club and won the Football League First Division title in 1898–99. He also won the FA Cup in 1887 and 1892, and was runner-up in the Championship in 1901. He later managed United, Bolton Wanderers and Manchester City.  As a scout, Speakman helped to discover players such as Denis Law, George Best and Steve Bruce. He died in 1932 at the age of 73 after a long illness.
