Jean-Joseph Rabearivelo (4 March 1901 or 1903 – 14 January 1978) was a Malagasy economist, politician and diplomat.  Born in the town of Ambohimanga (then part of the French colony of Madagascar), Rabearivelo studied at the Sorbonne and the Paris School of Economics. He served in various posts in the colonial government before becoming Minister of Finance in 1955. He was also Ambassador to France from 1963 to 1966 and to Belgium from 1967 to 1971.   Rabearivelo was a member of the National Assembly from 1956 to 1962, and again from 1965 to 1973. He was also a member of the Constitutional Council from 1960 to 1966, and President of the Urban Community of Antananarivo from 1972 to 1974. Rabearivelo died in 1978 at the age of 73.
