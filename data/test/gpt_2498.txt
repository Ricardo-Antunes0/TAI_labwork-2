Cordel Hyde (born 20 August 1973) is a Jamaican former soccer player who played as a midfielder.  Hyde played professionally in the United States and England, most notably for the Major League Soccer club D.C. United, where he helped the team to win the 2004 MLS Cup. He also played for clubs such as Crystal Palace, Queens Park Rangers, Blackburn Rovers and Swindon Town. Hyde also represented Jamaica at international level, appearing in FIFA World Cup 2002 and 2006.  Born in Kingston, Jamaica, Cordel Hyde began playing soccer at a young age with his local team Kingston FC. He then attended Montego Bay Secondary School, where he starred in both the football and basketball teams. After graduating from high school, Hyde traveled to the United States to play college soccer at Saint Louis University.  While playing for Saint Louis University, Hyde caught the attention of D.C. United head coach Bruce Arena. The team signed Hyde in 1996 and he quickly became a mainstay in the midfield. In his four seasons with D.C., Hyde helped the team to win two Lamar Hunt U.S. Open Cups (2000 and 2003), as well as the MLS Cup in 2004. After leaving D.C., Hyde spent three seasons with Crystal Palace, where he helped them reach the FA Cup semi-finals in 2006.
