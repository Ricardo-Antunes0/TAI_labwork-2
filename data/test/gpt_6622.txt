USS Hoggatt Bay (CVE-75) was the twenty-first ship of the United States Navy named for Rear Admiral James Hoggatt Bay.  The lead ship of her class, "Hoggatt Bay" was commissioned on 1 September 1942, with Captain Lawrence W. Laning in command. She was attached to the Pacific Fleet until early 1944 when she departed for the Marianas and then the Philippines. After participating in the Battle of the Philippine Sea in early 1944, "Hoggatt Bay" was transferred to Okinawa where she screened minesweepers until the end of the war.  After the war, "Hoggatt Bay" served as a training vessel for naval reservists in California until decommissioned on 10 July 1946. She was sold for scrap on 17 September 1948.
