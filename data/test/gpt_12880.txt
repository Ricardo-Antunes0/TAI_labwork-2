The Thirteen Hallows is the first novel in the Harry Potter series and was published on July 16, 1997. The novel is written by J.K. Rowling and follows the adventures of young wizard Harry Potter as he tries to prevent the death of Lord Voldemort, a dark wizard who intends to conquer the wizarding world.  The Thirteen Hallows is a dark and suspenseful novel that tells the story of Harry Potter's quest to find the seven magical artifacts known as the Hallows, which are said to be able to defeat Lord Voldemort. The novel also introduces a new cast of characters, including Ron Weasley, Hermione Granger, and Draco Malfoy; and reveals the secrets of Harry's family history.  The Thirteen Hallows has been praised for its richly imagined setting, its compelling plot, and its engaging characters. It has been acclaimed as one of the greatest novels in history and is widely considered to be one of the most popular books in the Harry Potter series.
