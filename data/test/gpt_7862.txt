American singer-songwriter and actress JoJo has been active in the music industry since the early 2000s. She has released five studio albums, two compilation albums, and thirty-six singles. JoJo has achieved success in the United States, Canada, Europe, and Japan.  JoJo was born Josephine Johnson on October 25, 1978, in Chicago, Illinois. She is of African American and Spanish descent. At the age of four, she started taking singing and dance classes and soon began performing in local talent shows. In 1997, JoJo auditioned for the reality television show "Star Search" and won the competition. She signed a recording contract with RCA Records and released her debut album "The High Road" in 1999. The album reached number four on the US Billboard 200 chart and produced three singles: "Dollhouse", "Too Little Too Late" and "All I Have".  In 2001, JoJo released her second album "The Essential JoJo". The album spawned three singles: "Leave (Get Out)", "Touch My Body" and "Blessed". The album also reached number two on the US Billboard 200 chart. In 2002, JoJo starred as Aida in the Broadway musical production of "Aida". The same year, she released her third album "Wonderland", which spawned two singles: "I Believe in a Thing Called Love" and "Be My Baby".
