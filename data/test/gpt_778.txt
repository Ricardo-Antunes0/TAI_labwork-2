Evans was born at Pontardawe, Glamorgan, England, the son of a coal miner. He made his first-class debut for Glamorgan against Oxford University in 1937 and went on to play for the county until 1954, captaining them in 1955. Evans also played Minor Counties cricket for Monmouthshire from 1947 to 1951 and for Herefordshire from 1952 to 1955. He made his Test debut against South Africa at Durban in 1948 and claimed his first wicket, that of Graeme Pollock. Evans was a regular player for Wales from the early 1950s and captained his country from 1952 to 1961. In all, he played seventy-six Tests, taking 611 wickets with a best performance of 7 for 106 against Australia at Sydney in 1957. Evans also scored 2,711 runs with a highest score of 196 not out against South Africa at Perth in 1956. He also held eighteen catches in Tests. Evans retired from international cricket after the 1961 Ashes series victory over England. After his retirement from playing, Evans served as captain of Glamorgan County Cricket Club from 1962 to 1970 and as president of the Welsh Cricket Union from 1971 to 1978. He died aged 70 at his home in Barry, Vale of Glamorgan, Wales.
