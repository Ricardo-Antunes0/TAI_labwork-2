Rebekah Mary Brooks (; born 27 May 1969) is a British journalist and former editor of the now defunct News of the World. She was the Chief Executive of News International from 2003 to 2011, when she resigned amid the phone hacking scandal.  Brooks was born in 1969 in London, England to Jewish parents. She attended the independent Queens' School, Bushey, Hertfordshire, followed by New College, Oxford, where she read Philosophy, Politics and Economics.  Brooks began her career as a researcher at the Conservative Research Department in 1992. She joined the Sunday Times in 1996 as assistant editor and became deputy editor in 1998. In 2003 she was appointed editor of the News of the World, becoming Chief Executive in 2011.  Brooks was arrested on 14 July 2013 on charges of conspiracy to pervert the course of justice and corruption over phone hacking at the News of the World. She was released on bail on 17 July 2013 and subsequently resigned from her post as CEO of News International. On 27 July 2016, Brooks was found guilty of all charges against her, including conspiracy to pervert the course of justice and corruption over phone hacking.
