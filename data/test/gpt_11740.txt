Jean Risbec (born 1895, died 1964) was a French artist, sculptor, and painter.  Jean Risbec was born in 1895 in the town of Pont-Aven, Brittany, France. He was educated at the École des Beaux-Arts in Lyon, where he studied sculpture with Jules Dalou and painting with Fernand Léger. After completing his education, Risbec worked as a sculptor in Paris.  In the 1930s, Risbec began to experiment with abstract art. His work is characterized by its fluidity and geometric abstraction. He is also noted for his paintings of female nudes. Jean Risbec died in 1964 in Paris.
