"Wang Liulang" , also translated as "Sixth Prince of the Southern Tang Dynasty" or "Prince of the Southern Tang"), born c. 936, was the sixth son of Emperor Taizu of Song and grandson of Emperor Taizong of Tang. He was considered a capable general and won several major victories against his Northern Song rivals. However, after he lost a major battle against the Muslim Khitan Empire in 1006, he was removed from his military posts and forced into retirement. He later became a monk at a temple in Chang'an.
