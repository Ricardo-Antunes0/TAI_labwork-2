Sándor Péter Kocsis (, ; 21 September 1914 – 3 October 1996) was a Hungarian politician who served as Prime Minister of Hungary from April to October 1956, and as Minister of Defence from 1951 to 1956.  Kocsis was born in Kisvárda, Kingdom of Hungary (present-day Zala County, Hungary). He worked as an electrician before he joined the Communist Party of Hungary in 1937. He became a member of the Hungarian Parliament in 1945 and served as Minister of Agriculture from 1948 to 1950. In 1951, he was appointed Minister of Defence. When the Soviet Union invaded Hungary on 4 November 1956, Kocsis was the first Prime Minister to resign from his post. He was later arrested and imprisoned in the Soviet Union. He returned to Hungary in 1989 and died in 1996.
