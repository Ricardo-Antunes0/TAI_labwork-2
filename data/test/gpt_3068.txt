L'Année philologique (The Philological Year) is an annual academic journal published in France. It covers the field of philology, with articles in all linguistic disciplines. The journal is published by the École normale supérieure and the CNRS.  The journal was founded in 1894 as "Annales de l'École normale supérieure". In 1936 it was renamed "L'Année philologique".   The journal is divided into four sections: "Actes de congrès" (congresses proceedings), "Bibliographie" (bibliography), "Revues" (reviews), and "Textes écrits" (written texts).
