M'Balia Camara was born in 1929 in Conakry, French Guinea. She grew up during a time of political and social change in French West Africa, and became an active member of the independence movement. In 1952 she won the Prix Goncourt for her novel L'Enfant noir. The following year she was appointed to the French Academy. She died in a car accident in 1955 at the age of 24.
