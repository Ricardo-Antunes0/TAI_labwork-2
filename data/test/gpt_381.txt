John Warren Kaiser (born October 6, 1926) is an American business magnate and philanthropist who is the founder, chairman, and CEO of Kaiser Permanente. He has also served as the chairman of the board of directors for both Ford Motor Company and The Walt Disney Company.  Kaiser was born in Los Angeles, California, to Jewish parents. He attended UCLA where he earned a bachelor's degree in business administration in 1948. After serving in the United States Navy from 1945 to 1947, Kaiser started his own health insurance company, Kaiser Permanente, in 1952. He has been the company's CEO since 1970.   Kaiser is noted for his work in health care reform and for his philanthropy, which includes donations to cancer research, education, and environmental causes. He has been awarded two honorary degrees from UCLA and a Presidential Medal of Freedom from George W. Bush.
