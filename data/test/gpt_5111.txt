Alanas Chošnau (born July 11, 1974) is a Lithuanian singer, songwriter, and musician. He is the lead vocalist, songwriter and main driving force in the alternative rock band Žvydas.  Alanas Chošnau was born in Kaunas, Lithuania on July 11, 1974. He started his musical career as a drummer in the metalcore band Nailonai in 1992. In 1995 he formed the alternative rock band Žvydas with his friends Šarūnas Bačkauskas and Dominykas Baranauskas. The band released their debut album, "Naujienos", in 1998. The album was well received by critics and sold out within a few weeks. The following year Žvydas released their second album, "Vaizdo". The album was even more successful than the first and featured the hit single "Dievo mūsų". In 2003 Žvydas released their third album, "Prieštaraujantys". The album was less popular than the previous two but featured the hit single "Nesupratėjimas".  In 2006 Žvydas disbanded after Alanas Chošnau decided to focus on his solo career.
