He studied piano with Louis Moreau Gottschalk and composition with Darius Milhaud at the Paris Conservatory. In 1934 he won the first prize in the International Music Competition of Florence. He became a naturalized United States citizen in 1941.  Benge's compositions include orchestral works, chamber music, choral works, and solo piano works. His best-known works are the orchestral suites "Four American Folk Songs" (1951), "Symphony No. 1" (1956), and "Symphony No. 2" (1967).
