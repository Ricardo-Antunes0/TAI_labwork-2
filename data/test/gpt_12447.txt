Daniel S. ("Danny") Nevins (born March 18, 1972) is an American journalist, who has been a foreign correspondent for The New York Times since 2003.  Nevins was born in Boston, Massachusetts, to a Jewish family. He is the son of Joyce Nevins and the late Daniel Nevins, a Pulitzer Prize-winning former reporter and editor at The Boston Globe. He graduated from the University of Pennsylvania in 1994 with a degree in history.  Nevins began his career as a reporter at The Philadelphia Inquirer in 1994. He then moved to The Boston Globe, where he was a city editor from 1998 to 2003. From 2003 to 2005, he was a correspondent for The New York Times based in Baghdad, Iraq. In 2006, he was a member of the Times' staff in Jerusalem. Nevins has also been based in Cairo and Moscow.
