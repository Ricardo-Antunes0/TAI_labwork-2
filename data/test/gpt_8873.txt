Talal Derki (Arabic: طلال ديركي Ṭālāl Dīrkī) (born January 4, 1975) is a Lebanese-German film actor and director.  Derki was born in Beirut, Lebanon to a Lebanese father and a German mother. Derki studied at the Lee Strasberg Theatre and Film Institute in New York City. He has also studied acting under the tutelage of Daniel Day-Lewis and Al Pacino.  Derki is best known for his leading role in the 2007 German drama film The Pianist. He also appeared in the Oscar-nominated films The Lives of Others (2006), Ang Lee's Lust, Caution (2011) and Blue Is the Warmest Color (2013). He is also known for his appearances in television series such as Tatort, Borgen and WDR 2's Polizeiruf 110.
