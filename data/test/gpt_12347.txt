Athens Township is a civil township of Ingham County in the U.S. state of Michigan. The population was 5,326 at the 2010 census. The municipality is located in the western part of Ingham County and is bounded by I-94 to the east, Lansing to the north and west, Mount Pleasant to the south, and Chelsea Township to the southwest.  The township was organized in 1837 and named after Athens, Greece. The town's early economy was based on agriculture and timber production. The township has since developed into a suburban community with a mix of single-family homes, commercial areas, and light industrial facilities. As of the census of 2000, there were 5,634 people, 2,439 households, and 1,746 families residing in the township. The population density was 154.0 per square mile (59.5/km²). There were 2,674 housing units at an average density of 68.9 per square mile (26.7/km²). The racial makeup of the township was 97.47% White, 0.30% African American, 0.61% Native American, 0.27% Asian, 0.02% Pacific Islander, 1.12% from other races, and 1.86% from two or more races.
