Lado Gudiashvili (; 30 March 1896 – 16 October 1967) was a Georgian academic and statesman who served as the first Prime Minister of Georgia (1946–1947) and as the country's second president (1947–1948).  Lado Gudiashvili was born in the village of Khulo in eastern Georgia on 30 March 1896. He graduated from the Tbilisi State University in 1921 with a degree in jurisprudence. Gudiashvili worked as a lawyer before joining the Democratic Republic of Georgia's government in 1935 as a deputy minister of justice. He served as Georgia's minister of education from 1943 to 1946, during which time he helped to develop Georgian education.  Gudiashvili was elected to parliament in 1946, and became Prime Minister two years later. He served until 1948, when he was replaced by Egnate Tsiskaradze. Gudiashvili later served as president of Georgia from 1947 to 1948. He retired from politics in 1952 and died 16 October 1967 in Tbilisi.
