The 10th millennium BC spanned the years 9,000 to 8,000 BC. It was a time of great technological and cultural progress, with the emergence of new forms of civilization and the development of agriculture, art, and writing.  During the 10th millennium BC, the world underwent a series of dramatic changes that led to the development of new civilizations. This period saw the emergence of new forms of agriculture and animal husbandry, as well as the beginnings of writing and art. Technological advances included the development of wheeled vehicles, metalworking techniques, and advanced weapons.  The 10th millennium BC was a time of great change and progress for humanity. Thanks to developments in technology, agriculture, and other fields, humanity began to build more complex societies that would later flourish in the 11th and 12th centuries BC.
