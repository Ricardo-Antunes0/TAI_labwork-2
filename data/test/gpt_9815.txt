Panir Ibragimov (28 December 1926 â€“ 28 October 1976) was a Soviet and Tajikistani artist, who worked in painting, sculpture, and film.  Ibragimov was born in 1926 in the Tajik SSR. He studied at the Tajik Academy of Fine Arts in Dushanbe from 1947 to 1951. He then moved to Moscow, where he continued his studies at the Vkhutemas Art School from 1951 to 1954. In 1955 he became a member of the Union of Artists of the Soviet Union.  In the 1960s and 1970s Ibragimov worked on a number of films. His most famous work is probably the film adaptation of Ibragimov's novel, The Stone Guest (1963). He died in 1976.
