Daniel Mecséry de Tsoor (29 September 1759 – 25 December 1837) was a Hungarian nobleman and statesman who served as Prime Minister of the Kingdom of Hungary from 1811 to 1813. He also served as Minister of War from 1790 to 1794 and from 1811 to 1813.  Mecséry was born in Tárkány, present-day Slovakia. He was the son of Count Mecséry János de Tsoor (1723–92) and his wife, Countess Erzsébet Bencze (1728–1805). His father served as the Governor of Transylvania and Ban of Croatia.  Mecséry entered the army at an early age and fought in the Seven Years' War (1756–63). Afterward, he became an officer in the Royal Hungarian Army. In 1790, he was appointed Minister of War and held this position until 1794.  In 1800, Mecséry was elected to the Diet of Hungary and he served as its Speaker from 1804 to 1811. In 1809, he was appointed Prime Minister of Hungary and he served in that position until 1813. During his time as Prime Minister, Mecséry presided over a period of significant reform in Hungary.
