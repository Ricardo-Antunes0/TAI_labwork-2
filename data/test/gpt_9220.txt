Jeannette Chong-Aruldoss  is a Singaporean lawyer and politician. She is currently a Member of Parliament for Aljunied GRC, and Minister in the Prime Minister's Office. Jeannette Chong-Aruldoss studied law at the University of Singapore, where she graduated with a Bachelor of Laws (LLB) degree in 1985. She then went on to study law at the London School of Economics and Political Science, where she earned a Master of Laws (LLM) degree in 1987.  After completing her legal studies, Jeannette Chong-Aruldoss began her career as a lawyer. She worked for a number of law firms before founding her own law firm, Chong-Aruldoss Law Corporation, in 1995.  Jeannette Chong-Aruldoss has been involved in politics since she was young. She first became involved in politics when she joined the National Solidarity Party (NSP), which was then a student political organisation, in 1980.  In 1991, Jeannette Chong-Aruldoss was elected to the Parliament of Singapore as a member of the NSP. She served as the party's deputy leader from 1991 to 1994, and later became its leader in 1994.
