Kem Sokha (; born 27 June 1953) is a Cambodian politician who served as the Prime Minister of Cambodia from 2008 to 2012. He has been a member of the Cambodian People's Party (CPP) since 1979.  Kem Sokha was born on 27 June 1953 in Prey Veng Province, into a peasant family. He completed his secondary education at the Sisowath High School in Phnom Penh in 1971. Kem Sokha then studied law at the Royal University of Phnom Penh and graduated with a degree in 1973.  Kem Sokha began his political career in 1979, when he joined the Cambodian People's Party (CPP). He served as secretary-general of the CPP from 1987 to 1997, and was elected to the National Assembly of Cambodia in 1993. Kem Sokha was appointed Minister of Planning and Investment in 1998, and was also made Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Public Works in that year. In 2002, Kem Sokha was named Minister of Defense.  In 2008, Kem Sokha was elected Prime Minister of Cambodia by the National Assembly. He served in that position until 2012, when he was replaced by Hun Sen. Kem Sokha then served as President of the National Assembly from 2013 to 2018.  Kem Sokha is married and has three children.
