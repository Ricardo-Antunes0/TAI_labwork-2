He attended the University of Vienna where he studied mathematics and physics. In 1931, he moved to the United States and completed his medical degree at the University of Chicago in 1934. He then began his training in psychoanalysis with Freud's son, Max Eitingon.  Bolender began his psychoanalysis career at the Menninger Clinic in Topeka, Kansas. He later became a professor of psychiatry at the University of Pittsburgh and then the University of California, Los Angeles. He also served as president of the American Psychoanalytic Association (APA) from 1967 to 1969. He died in Los Angeles on October 6, 1984.  Bolender's writings include books on psychoanalysis and psychiatry, as well as poetry and short stories. His works have been translated into several languages.
