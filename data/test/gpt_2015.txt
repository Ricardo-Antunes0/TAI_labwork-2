Richard James David "Dickie" Attwood (born 4 October 1948) is a British conductor, pianist, and composer. He is currently the Music Director of the London Symphony Orchestra and Principal Conductor of the Guangzhou Symphony Orchestra.  Attwood was born in London, England. He attended Harrow School and Trinity College, Cambridge, where he studied music. He began his musical career as a pianist, performing with the London Symphony Orchestra under Sir Colin Davis and with the English Chamber Orchestra under Nicholas Kenyon. He also worked as a conductor, serving as music director of the Royal Liverpool Philharmonic Orchestra from 1985 to 1992 and of the Rotterdam Philharmonic from 1992 to 1995.  In 1995, Attwood became the Principal Conductor of the Guangzhou Symphony Orchestra. In 2006, he was named Music Director of the London Symphony Orchestra. In 2012, he was awarded an honorary doctorate by Trinity College, Cambridge.
