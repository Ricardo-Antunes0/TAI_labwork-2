"Lamentis" is the third episode of the first season of HBO's television series Game of Thrones, and the show's seventeenth episode. It was written by David Benioff and D. B. Weiss and directed by Miguel Sapochnik.  The episode introduces the White Walkers, who are a threat to the Seven Kingdoms.  Queen Margaery Tyrell (Natalie Dormer) learns about her husband Joffrey's (Jack Gleeson) true nature when he forces her to watch a video of his father, King Tommen I (Dean-Charles Chapman) being executed. Cersei Lannister (Lena Headey) is alarmed when she realises that Joffrey has been reading from a book of incestuous prophecies meant for her and her children. The High Sparrow (Jonathan Pryce), the religious leader of King's Landing, orders the arrest of Loras Tyrell (Finn Jones) for homosexual practices with other men in the castle. Margaery intervenes on Loras' behalf, but Joffrey orders him executed instead.  Tyrion Lannister (Peter Dinklage) goes to Casterly Rock to visit his father Jaime Lannister (Nikolaj Coster-Waldau). Jaime is not happy to see Tyrion, but agrees to train him in swordfighting.
