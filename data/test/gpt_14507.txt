Henriette Marie Françoise L'Hardy (9 december 1768–30 december 1847) was a French novelist, poet and playwright.  Born in Paris, Henriette L'Hardy was the daughter of a minor administrator in the royal household. She started writing at an early age, publishing her first novel, "Le Capitaine Paul", at the age of nineteen. She achieved international success with her novels "Le Roi s'amuse" (1819), "La Reine Margot" (1830) and "Pélagie" (1836). Her plays are also popular, including "Le Médecin malgré lui" (1840), "La Dame aux camélias" (1848) and "Un Valet de Chambre" (1852).  Henriette L'Hardy died in 1847 in Paris.
