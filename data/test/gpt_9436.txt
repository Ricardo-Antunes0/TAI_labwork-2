The Minnesota River  is a tributary of the Mississippi River in the U.S. state of Minnesota.  The Minnesota River begins in northeastern Minnesota and flows southeast for about 185 miles (300 km), to its confluence with the Mississippi River near Hastings, Minnesota. The river's watershed encompasses parts of six counties and has a total area of 2,531 square miles (6,070 km²).  The river is known for its wide variety of aquatic life, including bald eagles, white-tailed deer, and otters. The river also provides drinking water to more than a million people in Minnesota and Wisconsin.
