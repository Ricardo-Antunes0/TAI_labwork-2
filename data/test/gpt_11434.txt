Gabriel Strobl (3 November 1846 in Unzmarkt, Styria, Austria-Hungary – 7 October 1917 in Vienna, Austria) was an Austrian painter and printmaker.  Gabriel Strobl was born in Unzmarkt, Styria, Austria-Hungary on 3 November 1846. He studied at the Academy of Fine Arts Vienna with Theodor von Heffel and Joseph Kügelgen. In 1875 he became a member of the Vienna Secession. He died in Vienna on 7 October 1917.  Gabriel Strobl's paintings are characterised by their focus on the human form, and he often used a naturalistic approach to depict his subjects. His prints are also notable for their stylised depictions of flowers and landscapes.
