He was educated at the Kakatiya College, Warangal, and at the University of Madras, where he obtained a Bachelor of Arts degree in 1886 and a Master of Arts degree in 1887. He then studied at the University of Cambridge, where he obtained a Doctor of Philosophy degree in 1901.  After returning to India, Sastry became a lecturer at the University of Mysore, where he served from 1903 to 1907. In 1907 he moved to the University of Madras as an assistant professor, and he became a professor there in 1912. He served as vice-chancellor of the University of Madras from 1923 to 1924.  Sastry died in Madras on 12 December 1935. He was survived by his wife and two children.  Sastry's philosophical work is focused on the philosophy of religion.
