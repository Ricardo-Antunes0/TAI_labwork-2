The Swiss Civilian Service is a Swiss government agency responsible for providing a wide range of civilian services to the population. The agency employs more than 80,000 people, including postal workers, drivers, air traffic controllers, and tax inspectors.  The Swiss Civilian Service was established in 1848 as the Directorate General for Civil Affairs. It was renamed the Swiss Civil Service in 1972 and reorganized into six directorates in 1993. The agency is headquartered in Bern.     The Swiss Civil Service provides a wide range of civilian services to the population.
