Madrid, 1987 is a 2011 Spanish drama film directed by Fernando León de Arana. The screenplay was written by Fernando León de Arana and Javier Cercas. It stars Penélope Cruz, Javier Bardem, and Adrien Brody.  In 1987, Madrid is in the grip of an economic crisis that has left the city's inhabitants polarized. Manuel (Javier Bardem), a successful banker, must choose between his love for his wife (Penélope Cruz) and his duty to his company. Alberto (Adrien Brody), a young artist, is struggling to make a living while living in a squalid flat with his mother (Pilar López de Ayala). As the economic conditions worsen, the characters find themselves struggling to maintain their grip on sanity.
