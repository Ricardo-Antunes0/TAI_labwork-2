John Angell James (6 June 1785 – 2 June 1862) was an English lawyer and politician who served as the first Lord Mayor of London in 1837.  Angell was born in London, the son of James Angell and his wife, Elizabeth (née Cooke). He was educated at Eton College and Trinity College, Cambridge, where he graduated with a BA in 1808 and an MA in 1810. He was called to the bar at the Inner Temple in 1812.  Angell practised law in London, and entered politics as a member of the Whig Party. He served as Solicitor General for England and Wales from 1830 to 1834, and as Attorney General for England and Wales from 1835 to 1841. He also served as Lord Mayor of London from 1837 to 1838.  Angell retired from politics in 1840, and died in 1862. He was buried at Highgate Cemetery.
