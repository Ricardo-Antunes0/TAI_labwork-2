Georges Dionne is a full Professor of French and Francophone Studies at the University of Ottawa, Canada. He has also been a visiting professor at universities in France, Belgium, Luxembourg, Switzerland, and Italy.  Georges Dionne is the author of over thirty books on French literature, film, and culture, as well as articles on various subjects. His most recent book is "Cinema Québécois: A History" (University of Toronto Press, 2013). He also edited "The Cambridge History of French Literature" (Cambridge University Press, 1998), and "Pierre Reverdy: A Life" (University of Chicago Press, 2007).  Georges Dionne has received numerous honours and awards including the Léonard de Vinci Prize for French-Canadian Literature (1994), the Prix Maisonneuve-Bourassa (1998), the Prix Félix-Leclerc (2002), the Jules Verne Prize (2007), and the Commander of the Order of Arts and Letters (2012). In 2017 he was made an Officer of the Order of Canada.
