Helmut Lotti (born Helmut Barthold Johannes Alma Lotti on 25 May 1946) is a German composer and conductor.  Lotti was born in Garmisch-Partenkirchen, Bavaria, to a music teacher and pianist mother and an opera singer father. He studied at the Hochschule für Musik Freiburg with composition teachers Hans Werner Henze and Volker Richter. He made his conducting debut in 1974 with the Freiburg Opera.  Lotti has since conducted opera houses throughout Europe, North and South America, Asia and Australia, as well as guest conducting appearances with major orchestras. He has collaborated with many leading composers, including Ralph Vaughan Williams, Luciano Berio, Peter Schickele, György Ligeti, and Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart. Lotti's works have been performed by leading orchestras including the Berlin Philharmonic Orchestra, the Vienna Philharmonic Orchestra, the London Symphony Orchestra, the New York Philharmonic Orchestra, and the Tokyo Symphony Orchestra.  Lotti is a professor of composition at the Hanns Eisler Conservatory in Berlin. He is married to soprano Christine Schafer-Lotti. They have two children.
