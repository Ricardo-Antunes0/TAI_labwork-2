Seamus Martin McDonagh (born 6 October 1952), also known as Jim McDonagh, is an Irish playwright and screenwriter. He has written twelve plays, six of which have been produced on stage. His most successful play, The Lieutenant of Inishmore, was awarded the 1997 Tony Award for Best Play and the 2002 Olivier Award for Best New Play. McDonagh also wrote the screenplay for the film adaptation of The Lieutenant of Inishmore, which was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Screenplay.   McDonagh was born in Dublin, Ireland and educated at Belvedere College in Dublin. He is a member of the Irish Academy of Letters.
