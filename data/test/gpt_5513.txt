Flatey  is an island of the Westfjords in Iceland. Administratively it is part of Breiðafjörður municipality. The island is long and wide with a population of just over 100 people.      The island is known for its natural beauty, with black sand beaches and crystal-clear waters. There are also a number of small villages and farms on the island, as well as a lighthouse.     The island has been the site of a number of historical events, including the murder of Bishop Jón Arason in 1263 and the Battle of Flatey in 1798.
