Daniel's, opened in late 1975, was one of the earliest Italian restaurants in the city of Philadelphia. The restaurant was located on Carpenter Street near 12th and Carpenter Streets in North Philadelphia.  Daniel's was known for its fresh Italian food, including pizza, pasta, and salads. The restaurant also served wine, beer, and cocktails. In addition to its Italian fare, Daniel's offered a variety of Americanized dishes such as chicken Caesar salad and buffalo chicken dip.  The restaurant closed its doors in 2006 after more than 40 years in business.
