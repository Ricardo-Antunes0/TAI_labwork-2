Tanisha Marie Williams is an American botanist, biologist and plant physiologist. She is currently a professor at the University of California, Davis.  Williams was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, in 1967. She received her B.S. in botany from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in 1990, and her M.S. and Ph.D. from the University of California, Davis, in 1992 and 1996, respectively.  Williams has been a professor at the University of California, Davis since 1996. She is currently a professor in the Department of Plant Sciences and the Center for Plant Biology. Williams' research focuses on plant physiology and biochemistry, with an emphasis on photosynthesis, water relations, and gas exchange. She has published more than 200 papers and book chapters, most notably on photosynthesis and water relations in plants. Williams was elected to the American Academy of Arts and Sciences in 2012.
