Mohamed Faisal is a Maldivian film actor, director and producer.  Mohamed Faisal was born in 1984 in the Maldives. He began his career as an actor in the early 2000s, appearing in several local films. In 2006, he made his directorial debut with the short film "Hangimu Hithimu".  In 2009, Faisal starred in the romantic comedy "Kaashif". The same year, he received acclaim for his performance as an autistic boy in the drama "Iruvar". He followed this up with roles in the crime thriller "Aadhal Enna" and the historical drama "Thaa".   Faisal has since appeared in several successful films, including the action thriller "Kalimantan" (2012), the romantic comedy "Billa" (2013), and the family drama "Fathiya" (2015). He is also known for his roles in television dramas, including the mystery series "Kaafila" and the period drama "Thaalaa".   Faisal is a member of the Maldivian Film Producers' Association and has served as its president. In 2016, he founded the Mohamed Faisal Film Institute to promote filmmaking in the Maldives.
