Barry Strachan Gardiner (born 10 March 1957) is a British Labour Party politician who has been the Member of Parliament (MP) for Brent North since 1997. He is currently the Deputy Leader of the Labour Party.  Gardiner was born in Huddersfield, West Yorkshire, to Scottish parents. He attended the independent Huddersfield School and studied law at the University of Hull. After working as a solicitor, he became an MP in the 1997 general election, defeating the Conservative MP Neil Hamilton by 1,811 votes. He served on the Treasury Select Committee from 1998 to 2001 and on the Home Affairs Select Committee from 2001 to 2005.  In 2003, Gardiner was one of thirteen MPs to sign a letter to General David Richards, then Chief of the Defence Staff, urging him to investigate allegations of Army misconduct in Iraq. In 2006 he was one of forty Labour MPs to support an amendment to the House of Lords Reform Bill which would have reduced the power of the upper house.  Gardiner is married with two children.
