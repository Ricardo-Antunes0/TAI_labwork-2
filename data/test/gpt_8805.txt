Abdurrahim Abdulhafiz El-Keib, PhD, (; also transcribed Abdurrahim el-Kebi, Abdurrahim el-Kebî, Abdurrahim el-Kabî, Abdurrahim el-Qeibi, and Abdurrahim al-Qeibi) is an Egyptian academic and political activist who has been imprisoned since 2014 on charges of belonging to the Muslim Brotherhood.  El-Keib was born in 1970 in the Nile Delta city of Mansoura. He received a PhD from the University of Edinburgh in 2000, and has since worked as a lecturer at Alexandria University. He has also served as the president of the Cairo Institute for Human Rights Studies.  El-Keib was arrested on 14 April 2014, along with 10 other members of the Muslim Brotherhood, on charges of belonging to a terrorist organization. The following month he was charged with conspiring to overthrow the government. He was initially held at Tora Prison, but was transferred to Cairo Military Prison in May 2014. On 30 January 2017, he was sentenced to five years in prison.
