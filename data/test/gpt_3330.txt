Jesse White (born Jesse Marc Weidenfeld; January 10, 1967) is an American actor.  White was born in Los Angeles, California, the son of Judy (n√©e Weidenfeld), a psychotherapist and art historian, and Marc White, a musician and businessman. He is of German and English descent. White has two sisters, Jennifer and Rebecca. When White was four years old, his family moved to England, where he attended the independent Westminster School in London.  White began his acting career at the age of 17 appearing in a television commercial for Pepsi. He made his theatrical debut in the London production of Neil Simon's play "The Sunshine Boys" in 1988. White's first major role came in the 1991 film "The Fisher King", for which he received critical acclaim and was nominated for a Golden Globe Award for Best Actor - Motion Picture Musical or Comedy. Other notable roles include: "The House of Mirth" (1999), "The Big Lebowski" (1998), "The Shawshank Redemption" (1994), "Braveheart" (1995), "Amistad" (1997), "The Green Mile" (1999), "A Beautiful Mind" (2001) and "The Help" (2011).  White has also appeared in several mainstream films such as "War of the Worlds" (2005), "Kiss Kiss Bang Bang" (2005), "A Good Day to Die Hard" (2013) and "" (2017).
