Ninoslav Krstić (July 10, 1946, Niševac - March 7, 2003, Belgrade) was a Serbian poet and essayist.  Krstić was born in Niševac, Serbia, on July 10, 1946. He attended the University of Belgrade Faculty of Philosophy, where he received his doctorate in 1977. Krstić taught philosophy at the University of Belgrade from 1981 to 1998. He also served as the president of the Association of Serbian Writers from 1992 to 1998.  Krstić's literary work centers on poetry and essays. His first collection of poems, "Dnevnik" (1975), won the prestigious Prešeren Award. The book was followed by "Život" (1978), "Pisma o ljubavi" (1981), and "Lepota života" (1986). Krstić's works have been translated into several languages and have been widely praised by critics.  Krstić died in 2003 in Belgrade following a long illness. He was 52 years old.
