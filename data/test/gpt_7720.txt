The Rachel Papers is a 1989 British mystery film directed by Peter Medak and starring Samantha Eggar, David Thewlis and Rupert Graves. It is based on the novel of the same name by Patricia Highsmith.  The Rachel Papers is a mystery film set in 1950s London about Rachel Weisz, an American woman employed by a British publishing house, who comes into possession of a manuscript that she believes is the lost work of an English writer named Maurice Blanchard. The manuscript appears to be full of encrypted messages and references to a murder that took place in 1954. Weisz is aided in her investigation by her editor, George Fenton, and by the police detective in charge of the case, Inspector Thackeray.
