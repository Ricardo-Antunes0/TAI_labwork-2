Ali Ahmad Jalali (Pashto/) is an Afghan political analyst and commentator. He is a visiting fellow at the Brookings Institution. He has been a commentator on Afghan politics for the BBC World Service, and is a regular contributor to the Huffington Post.  Born in Kandahar in 1969, Jalali completed his secondary education in Kabul before studying political science at Kabul University. He then went on to work as an analyst for the Afghan Ministry of Interior from 1998 to 2003. In 2003, he fled Afghanistan and settled in the United Kingdom, where he continued his work as an analyst.  In 2006, Jalali returned to Afghanistan and became a senior political analyst for the UN Assistance Mission in Afghanistan (UNAMA). In this capacity, he helped oversee the drafting of the Afghan Constitution of 2009. From 2009 to 2011, Jalali served as the deputy head of UNAMA's Political Affairs Department.  Jalali has been a frequent commentator on Afghan politics for the BBC World Service, and is a regular contributor to the Huffington Post. He also writes regularly for The Daily Beast, The New York Times, and The Washington Post.
