David Cornsilk (Cherokee Nation and United Keetoowah Band of Cherokee Indians, born October 14, 1934) is an American actor, singer and musician. He has appeared in more than fifty films and television shows since the late 1950s. He was a cast member of the Emmy Award winning 1970s and 1980s variety show The David Corn Show.   Cornsilk was born in Tahlequah, Oklahoma, to Chief Cornsilk and his wife, the former Lois White Eagle. He attended the University of Oklahoma, where he was a member of the Kappa Alpha Order fraternity and played football under coach Tom Osborne. After graduation, he served two years in the United States Air Force before making his film debut in The Outsider (1961). He also appeared in such films as The War Eagles (1963), The Haunting (1963), and The Getaway (1969).  Cornsilk's television appearances include roles on The Big Valley (1965), The Virginian (1962-1971), Gunsmoke (1955-1975), and Alfred Hitchcock Presents (1961-1972). He starred as David Whitaker on the syndicated show The David Corn Show from 1973 to 1984. In 1987, he won a Daytime Emmy Award for Outstanding Performer in a Children's Program for his portrayal of Chief Joseph on the PBS show Indian Ink.
