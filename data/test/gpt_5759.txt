Diana Marion Walford  (née Norton, born 2 February 1961) is a British former actress, model and television presenter.  Born in London, Walford began her career as a model, appearing in campaigns for designers such as Jean Muir and Howard Miller. In 1984, she made her acting debut in the BBC Television film The Adventures of Baron Munchausen. Her breakthrough came two years later with the role of Tracy Beaker in the BBC sitcom Tracy Beaker. She continued to appear in television programmes throughout the 1990s, including roles in Bergerac (1993), Casualty (1995–1997) and Bergerac: The Series (1998), before retiring from acting in 2001.  Since retirement, Walford has been a regular panellist on the ITV game show Who Wants to Be a Millionaire? (2002–present), and has also appeared as a guest presenter on several UK television shows, including Live With Regis and Kelly (2002), The Weakest Link (2003), Top Gear (2008), The X Factor (2010) and Strictly Come Dancing (2015). In 2018, she was appointed an ambassador for the campaign to prevent young people from smoking.
