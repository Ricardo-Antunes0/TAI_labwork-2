Gallavants is an American 1984 animated musical comedy film produced by Walt Disney Feature Animation and released by Walt Disney Pictures. The film follows the adventures of three vaudeville performers, The Gallant Trio, as they travel around the world performing their unique brand of entertainment.  The film was directed by Don Bluth and Gary Goldman, and features the voices of John Astin, Dick Van Dyke, and Mary Steenburgen. It was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Original Song for "The Way You Look Tonight".  The story is set in 1910s-1920s Paris and follows the adventures of three vaudeville performers: Aloysius Gallant (John Astin), his wife Berthe (Mary Steenburgen), and their manager Auguste (Dick Van Dyke). The trio travels to various locations around Europe, including Vienna, Budapest, and Moscow, before finally making their way to Broadway in New York City. Along the way, they encounter a variety of colorful characters and perform various stunts and magic tricks.  The film was released to generally positive reviews from critics. It has since been added to the United States National Film Registry.
