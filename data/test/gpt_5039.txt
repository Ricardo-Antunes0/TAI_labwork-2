The Jiashen Incident , also known as the Changchun Incident, was a bloody conflict that took place in Changchun, Jilin province, China from February to March 1934. The conflict was initiated by the Communists when they attempted to take control of the city after the fall of the Nationalist government in Beijing.  The conflict resulted in numerous casualties on both sides, with estimates of over 3,000 fatalities on the Communist side and over 10,000 fatalities on the Nationalist side. The Jiashen Incident is considered one of the bloodiest conflicts in Chinese history.
