Kenneth Brian Edmonds (born April 10, 1959), better known by his stage name Babyface, is an American singer, songwriter, and record producer. He achieved his first success as a member of the R&B group The Commodores in the late 1970s and early 1980s, before launching a successful solo career in the early 1990s.  Edmonds has sold over 100 million records worldwide and has won several awards including three Grammy Awards, two American Music Awards, and two Soul Train Awards. He has also been nominated for a Golden Globe Award and an Academy Award. In 2003, he was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
