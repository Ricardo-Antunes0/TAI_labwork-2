Milind Dattatraya Gunjal (born 4 April 1959) is an Indian politician and social activist who has been a member of the Indian National Congress since 1984. He was a Member of Parliament from Raipur in Chhattisgarh from 2004 to 2009, and from 2013 to 2014.  Gunjal was born in Raipur, Chhattisgarh on 4 April 1959. After completing his schooling from Mahatma Gandhi School, Raipur, Gunjal studied at the Faculty of Commerce, University of Delhi. He started his political career as an organiser for the Youth Congress in 1980. In 1984, he was elected to the Indian National Congress (INC) from Raipur district as its member of parliament (MP). He served as an MP from 2004 to 2009. In 2009, Gunjal was appointed Minister of State for Home Affairs in the United Progressive Alliance (UPA) government led by Prime Minister Manmohan Singh. In 2013, he was appointed Minister of State for Rural Development in the government of Prime Minister Narendra Modi.  Gunjal is known for his work on social justice and empowerment of women. He has also been involved in legislative initiatives such as the National Rural Employment Guarantee Act, which aims to provide 100 days of employment per year to rural households, and the Right to Information Act, which makes information available to the public on government activities. Gunjal has also been vocal about corruption and entrenched poverty in India.
