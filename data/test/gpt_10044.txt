Major General Celia Jane Harvey,  (born June 20, 1928, in Fredericton, New Brunswick) is a retired Canadian military officer who served in the Canadian Army from 1951 to 1975. She was the first woman to command a Canadian division and the first woman to be appointed as a lieutenant general in the Canadian Armed Forces.  Harvey was born in Fredericton, New Brunswick. She studied at the Royal Military College of Canada and graduated with a commission as an officer in the Canadian Army in 1951. She served with distinction during the Korean War, where she was awarded the United Nations Service Medal and the Korean Service Medal. In 1965, she was appointed commanding officer of 1 Canadian Infantry Brigade. She later served as deputy commander of Land Force Central Area and as commander of 4 Canadian Infantry Division. In 1975, she retired from the army with the rank of major general.  After retiring from the army, Harvey continued to serve her country in a number of roles. In 1978, she was appointed director-general of strategic planning for the Department of National Defence. She also served as president of the Royal Military College of Canada from 1978 to 1981 and was made an honorary doctor of laws by Queen's University in 1982. In 1984, she was appointed commissioner for Aboriginal peoples for Ontario and Quebec. In 1992, she was appointed honorary colonel of The Queens Own Cameron Highlanders of Ottawa.  In 2006, Harvey received the Order of Canada for her contributions to military service and her work as a civilian leader.
