Mary L. Smith (born August 28, 1962) is an American author and journalist.  Smith was born in Lexington, Kentucky, to a family of educators. She graduated from the University of Kentucky in 1984 with a B.A. in English. Smith then entered the University of Missouri School of Journalism, where she earned her M.A. in 1989.  Smith began her career as a reporter for the "Lexington Herald-Leader" in 1984. She later worked as a national correspondent for "The Washington Post" and as a staff writer for "The New York Times". Smith has also been a contributing editor at "The Atlantic Monthly", a speechwriter for President Bill Clinton, and a fellow at the Harvard Kennedy School's Institute for Politics and Public Policy. Smith has written two books, "The Mary Smith Reader" (1996) and "Sons of Thunder: The Life and Times of J.R.R. Tolkien" (2003). Smith currently teaches at the University of Southern California's Annenberg School for Communication and Journalism.
