James Robert Short (born 7 December 1936) is a former Australian politician who was a Labor Senator for New South Wales from 1984 to 1999. He served as Deputy Prime Minister in the Hawke–Keating Labor government from 1991 to 1993.  Short was born in Brisbane, Queensland, on 7 December 1936. He attended the University of Queensland, where he received a Bachelor of Arts degree in 1958 and a Bachelor of Laws degree in 1960. He worked as a lawyer before becoming a Labor politician.     Short was first elected to the New South Wales Legislative Assembly in 1974 as the member for Sydney-Kingsford. He served as Minister for Industrial Relations from 1981 to 1984, and Deputy Premier and Minister for Transport and Communications from 1984 to 1991 in the Hawke–Keating Labor government. In 1991, Short was appointed Minister for Foreign Affairs in the Keating government, serving until 1993. He retired from parliament at the 1999 election.
