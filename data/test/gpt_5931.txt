The Naǵyz Ak Zhol (English: True Bright Path) is a Tibetan Buddhist monastery in the town of Naǵyz in the Ürümqi municipality, Xinjiang Uyghur Autonomous Region, China.  Founded in 1966 by the 17th Karmapa, the monastery is one of the largest and most important Tibetan Buddhist monasteries in China. It is also one of the few Tibetan Buddhist monasteries that has been able to maintain its original monastic structure and practices. The monastery's library contains more than 10,000 volumes and its art collection includes more than a thousand paintings and sculptures.  The monastery's main activities are the study and propagation of Buddhism, the training of monks and nuns, and the running of its hospital. The monastery also operates a school for children from surrounding villages.
