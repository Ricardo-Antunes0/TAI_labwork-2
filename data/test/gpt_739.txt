The Eichsfeld ( or ; English: Oak-field) is a natural region in the south-central part of Thuringia, Germany. It is a plateau with an altitude of 100 to 300 metres above sea level and covers an area of 255 km². The Eichsfeld is bordered by the Saale-Unstrut and the Thuringian Forest to the east, the Harz to the north, and the Bavarian Forest to the west. The landscape is characterised by broad rolling hills and small valleys that are crisscrossed by small streams. The soils are mainly loam and clay. The main towns in the Eichsfeld are Hildburghausen, Weißenfels, Bad Hersfeld, Eisenach, Jena, Meiningen, Erfurt and Würzburg.
