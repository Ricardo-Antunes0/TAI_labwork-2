The Lembaga Kebudajaan Rakjat (EYD: Lembaga Kebudayaan Rakjat) is a government-owned enterprise engaged in the production and marketing of palm oil. The company was founded in 1964 and is based in Palembang, Sumatra.  Lembaga Kebudajaan Rakjat is the largest producer and marketer of palm oil in Indonesia. The company's operations are divided into three divisions: production, marketing, and research. The company has a workforce of over 2,800 people and produces around 850,000 metric tons of palm oil annually. Lembaga Kebudayaan Rakjat is a member of the Malaysian Palm Oil Association (MPOA).  Lembaga Kebudayaan Rakjat was founded in 1964 as a state-owned enterprise under the name Perumahan Rakjat Selatan. The company's name was changed to Lembaga Kebudayaan Rakjat (LKR) in 1987. In 1998, LKR became a public company listed on the Indonesian Stock Exchange (IDX: LKR).  In 2002, LKR merged with PT PALM OIL INDONESIA to form PT PALM OIL LKR. In 2006, LKR acquired 100% ownership of PT PALM OIL INDONESIA. In 2009, LKR changed its name to Lembaga Kebudayaan Rakjat (EYD: Lembaga Kebudayaan Rakjat).
