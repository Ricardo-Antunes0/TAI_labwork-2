Chief Tibiriçá (died 1562) baptized as Martim de Sousa was a Portuguese explorer who led an expedition to explore the interior of Brazil in 1554.  Chief Tibiriçá was born in Portugal around 1510. He is best known for leading an expedition to explore the interior of Brazil in 1554. The expedition consisted of about 60 men, including several Portuguese and Spanish captains. Tibiriçá and his men explored the upper tributaries of the Amazon River and reached as far east as the Tapajós River. They also explored the Rio Negro and its tributaries.  Tibiriçá died in 1562. His remains were not discovered until 1934, when they were found buried beneath a rock in the Tapajós River.
