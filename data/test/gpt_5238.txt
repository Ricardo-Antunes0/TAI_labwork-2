Wallace McCutcheon Sr. was born on June 18, 1858, in New York City, to Scots-Irish immigrants. He began his artistic career as a lithographer and illustrator, working for magazines and newspapers. He became known for his realistic portrayals of life in the city, which he often depicted from a low perspective to emphasize the cramped and chaotic atmosphere of urban life.  McCutcheon died on October 16, 1924, in New York City. His work is exhibited in the Museum of Modern Art and the Brooklyn Museum.
