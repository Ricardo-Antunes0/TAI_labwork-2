David Douglas Gustafson (born October 13, 1956) is an American musician, singer and songwriter. He is best known as the lead singer and primary songwriter of the rock band The Eagles.  Gustafson was born in Wilmington, Delaware, the son of Peggy (n√©e Douglas) and David Gustafson, a truck driver. He has two younger sisters, Karen and Lori. Gustafson attended Woodbridge High School in Woodbridge, Virginia, where he was a member of the school's marching band and played basketball and baseball.  Gustafson's first musical experience was as a member of the marching band at Woodbridge High School. After graduating from high school, he moved to Los Angeles to try to become a musician. He first worked as a musician in various cover bands before forming The Eagles in 1976 with Don Felder, Bernie Leadon and Glenn Frey.  The Eagles were one of the biggest rock bands of the 1980s. Their albums "Hotel California" (1976), "The Long Run" (1978), "Eagles" (1980), "The Very Best Of The Eagles" (1982), "Hell Freezes Over" (1984) and "Reunion" (1998) all reached number one on the Billboard charts. The band won five Grammy Awards throughout their career, including three for Best Rock Album ("Hotel California", "The Long Run", "Eagles") and one for Best Pop Performance by a Duo or Group with Vocals ("Hotel California").
