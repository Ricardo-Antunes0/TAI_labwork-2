Joara was a large Native American settlement, located in present-day Greenville County, North Carolina. It was one of the largest and most important Native American settlements in the region, and was a major trading center for the local Lenape people.  Joara was first settled in the early 17th century by the Lenape people. It became an important trading center for the local Lenape people, and was home to a large population of Lenape people. The settlement was later destroyed by the English during the Anglo-Lenape Wars in the early 18th century.
