The Philadelphia Eagles are a professional American football team based in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. The Eagles compete in the National Football League (NFL) as a member club of the league's National Football Conference (NFC) East division. The Eagles were founded in 1933 as a replacement for the bankrupt Frankford Yellow Jackets, and played their first season in 1934. The Eagles played their home games at Franklin Field until the 1970 season, when they moved to the newly constructed Philadelphia Eagles Stadium at the University of Pennsylvania.  The Eagles have won three NFL championships (1948, 1949, and 1960), five NFC Championship Games (1949, 1955, 1957, 1959, and 1960), and one Super Bowl (1981). The team has also appeared in nine divisional playoffs and have had nine head coaches since their inception. The team currently has a winning record and is currently fourth in the NFC East division.
