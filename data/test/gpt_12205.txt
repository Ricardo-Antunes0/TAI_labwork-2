Paul Hockenos (born 1963) is a Berlin-based artist and writer. He is the founder of the Hockenos Foundation, a Berlin-based arts organization, and the author of several books, including "The Impossible Object: An Essay on the Work of Paul Hockenos" (2008).  Hockenos was born in Hamburg, Germany, in 1963. He studied at the Kunstakademie Düsseldorf, where he earned his diploma in 1988. In 1990 he moved to Berlin, where he has since been based.  Hockenos's work focuses on the process of painting and its relationship to architecture. He has exhibited his paintings and drawings in Germany, Austria, Switzerland, Italy, Spain, Belgium, The Netherlands and France. His work has also been shown at the Museum of Modern Art in New York City (2005), the Centre Georges Pompidou in Paris (2000), and the National Gallery of Australia in Canberra (2002).  Hockenos is a founding member of the Association of German Art Critics and is currently a columnist for Die Zeit magazine. He has also written for The New York Times Magazine, The Guardian, Frieze magazine and other publications. In 2009 he was a recipient of the prestigious Heinrich Böll Foundation Award.
