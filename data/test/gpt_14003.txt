Leonardo Lago Faedo (born May 13, 1960) is an American professional basketball coach. He is the head coach of the Israeli team Maccabi Tel Aviv.     Faedo played college basketball at North Carolina State University and was drafted by the Boston Celtics in the first round of the 1982 NBA draft. He played for the Celtics and Philadelphia 76ers in his seven-year professional career. Faedo later served as an assistant coach for the Celtics and Portland Trail Blazers. In 2007, he was named head coach of Maccabi Tel Aviv, where he has led the team to four Israeli League championships and one Euroleague championship.     Faedo was born in Wilmington, North Carolina to a Jewish family. He attended North Carolina State University, where he played for the Wolfpack from 1979 to 1981. He was drafted by the Boston Celtics with the sixth overall pick in the 1982 NBA draft. Faedo played for the Celtics from 1982 to 1986, and then signed with the Philadelphia 76ers in 1987. He played for Philadelphia until 1990, when he was traded to the Portland Trail Blazers. Faedo retired from playing in 1991, after playing in 78 games and averaging 5.8 points per game.     After retiring from playing, Faedo served as an assistant coach with the Celtics from 1991 to 1995, and then with Portland from 1996 to 1999.
