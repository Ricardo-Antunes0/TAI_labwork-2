Maxim Shmyrev (born 30 November 1971) is a Russian professional football coach and a former player.  Shmyrev is a graduate of the Moscow State Institute of Physical Education, Sport, and Tourism.  He played for FC Torpedo-BelAZ Zhodino, FC Krylia Sovetov Samara, FC Krylia Sovetov Samara II, FC Terek Grozny and FC Rostov.  Shmyrev coached FC Terek Grozny, FC Rostov, FC Krylia Sovetov Samara, FC Krylia Sovetov Samara II and FC Kaliningrad. He was also an assistant coach with the Russia national under-21 football team from 2004 to 2007. In December 2009, he was appointed as the head coach of PFC CSKA Moscow. On 9 May 2012, he resigned from his post after a poor run of results in the Russian Premier League.
