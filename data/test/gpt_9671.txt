Rodney Allan Atkins  (born March 28, 1954) is an American singer-songwriter, guitarist, and record producer. Atkins has been a solo artist since the late 1970s, and has had several successful albums. He is best known as the frontman of the rock band Atkins, which he formed in 1978.  Atkins was born in Houston, Texas. He started playing guitar at the age of 12, and formed his first band at the age of 18. After moving to Los Angeles in 1975, he was signed to a recording contract by A&M Records. In 1978, he released his debut album, Rodney Atkins. The album was well received by critics and sold over a million copies. In 1984, he released his second album, Rodney Atkins II. The album was even more successful than the first, selling over two million copies. Between 1978 and 1985, Atkins recorded five more albums. In 1986, he disbanded Atkins due to creative differences with the other members of the band.  Since his breakup with Atkins, he has continued to make music as a solo artist. His most recent album was released in 2009.
