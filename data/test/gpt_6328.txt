The Bellanca 28-90 Flash was an American single-engined, low wing, strut-braced, monoplane aircraft designed and built by the Bellanca Aircraft Corporation in the 1930s.  The 28-90 was a conventional low wing monoplane with a fixed tailwheel landing gear and a single engine in tractor configuration. It had a wingspan of 28 feet 9 inches (8.7 m) and a height of 10 feet 2 inches (3.0 m). The aircraft was powered by a Lycoming O-145 engine, providing 330 horsepower (243 kW).
