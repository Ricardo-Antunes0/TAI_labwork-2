Judy Illes, , PHD, FRSC, FCAHS, (born October 10, 1946) is a Canadian artist who has been a key figure in the development of electronic music. She was one of the first synthesists to use computers to create her music, and her work has been influential in the development of electroacoustic music.  Iles was born in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. She studied at York University and the University of Toronto, where she earned a doctorate in art history and philosophy in 1984. That same year, she founded the Electronic Music Studio at the University of Toronto, which became one of Canada's leading centres for electronic music production. Iles has collaborated with a number of leading contemporary composers, including Steve Reich, Philip Glass, and Michael Nyman.  Iles's work has been exhibited at venues around the world, including the Museum of Modern Art in New York City, the Centre Pompidou in Paris, and the Serralves Museum in Porto. In 2006, she was awarded the Order of Canada for her contributions to Canadian art.
