Efton M. James (September 1, 1890 – October 26, 1976) was an American politician and jurist who served as the eleventh Governor of Oklahoma from 1937 to 1943. He also served in the United States Army during World War I and in the United States Navy during World War II.  James was born in Afton, Oklahoma, on September 1, 1890, to James and the former Mattie (née McClendon) James. He attended public school in Afton and then enrolled at the University of Oklahoma, where he was a member of the Phi Delta Theta fraternity. He studied law at the University of Oklahoma School of Law and was admitted to the bar in 1916.  James began his legal career in Afton, where he practiced law with his father. He later became a district judge in Oklahoma County. In 1931, James was elected as a Democrat to the United States Senate, serving until his resignation in 1936 to become Governor of Oklahoma.  During his time as Governor, James promoted economic development and improved public education in Oklahoma. He also oversaw the construction of new highways and bridges throughout the state. James resigned from office in 1943 to return to private practice.  James died on October 26, 1976, in Afton. He is buried at Hillcrest Memorial Park in Afton.
