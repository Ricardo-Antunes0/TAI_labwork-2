Ifri Oudadane is an archaeological site in Tunisia. It is located in the Oudadane valley, southeast of Kasserine, at an altitude of about 2,000 meters. It was first excavated in 1968 by Jacques Potin and Jean-Pierre Vernant.  Ifri Oudadane is a necropolis of the Phoenician period. The site has yielded evidence of a well-developed culture of the period, including pottery, terracotta figurines, cylinder seals, and glass beads. There are also remains of a palace and an amphitheater.
