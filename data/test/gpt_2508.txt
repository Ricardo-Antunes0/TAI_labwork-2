Lesley McAra is Chair of Penology at the London School of Economics and Political Science and a Fellow of the British Academy. She is also a member of the European Academy of Sciences and Arts and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.  Lesley McAra is an expert on crime and punishment, with particular expertise in penology. She has written extensively on topics such as parole, sentencing, rehabilitation, reintegration into society, and the use of evidence in criminal justice systems. McAra has also served as Chair of the UK Sentencing Council (2008-2010) and as a member of the Judicial Studies Board at the UK Ministry of Justice.  McAra has been recognised for her work in penology by being awarded the prestigious John Simon Guggenheim Memorial Fellowship in 2013. She has also been awarded honorary degrees from several universities, including Queen's University Belfast, Edge Hill University, and the University of Edinburgh.
