The Three Robbers (Die drei Räuber) is a 1954 West German crime film directed by Jürgen Prochnow and starring Heinz Rühmann, Sybille Schmitz and Gerti Reith. The film is based on the novel of the same name by Georges Simenon.   In the small town of Pont-à-Mousson in France, three criminals—the old man Buteau, the young man Arsène Lupin and the woman Lola—plan a daring heist. They have a list of wealthy targets, but will they be able to pull it off?
