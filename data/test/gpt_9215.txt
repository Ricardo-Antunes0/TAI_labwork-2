Armstrong was born in 1839 in Geneseo, New York. He attended Canandaigua Academy and then the United States Military Academy at West Point, graduating in 1859. He was commissioned a brevet second lieutenant in the infantry and served in the American Civil War. After the war he worked as a reporter for the "New York Herald" and then the "New York Tribune". In 1881 he became editor of the "New York Evening Post".  Armstrong served as Governor of New Mexico Territory from 1889 to 1891. He died on July 2, 1914.
