Wethersfield is a village and a civil parish in the Epping Forest district of Essex, England.  The village is located approximately south-west of Epping and north-east of Chipping Barnet. According to the 2011 Census the population of the civil parish was 6,725.  The parish includes the villages of Wethersfield Green, Wethersfield Heath and Wethersfield Village. The manor was given to William the Conqueror by King Edward the Confessor. The Church of England parish church of Saint John the Evangelist is Norman in origin and was built between 1170 and 1190. The village also has a Methodist chapel and a Roman Catholic church.  Wethersfield is home to Wethersfield School, a co-educational independent school with over 1,000 pupils, including around 100 boarders. The school was founded in 1572 by Richard Busby and remained an all-boys school until 1976 when it became co-educational.
