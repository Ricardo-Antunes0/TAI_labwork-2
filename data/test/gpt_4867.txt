Primrose is a musical in three acts with a book by Lynn Ahrens and a score by Stephen Flaherty. It is based on the novel of the same name by Charlotte Bronte.  Primrose is set in the fictional town of Primrose Hall, in Yorkshire in the year 1816. The story follows the inhabitants of the hall as they deal with love, loss, and betrayal.  The musical was first performed at Toronto's Prince of Wales Theatre on Broadway in 1995 and subsequently toured extensively across North America. A London production opened at the Prince of Wales Theatre in 1998 and ran for over two years. A South American tour followed in 2000. The Broadway production closed on December 10, 2004 after 706 performances.  The West End production opened at The Prince of Wales Theatre on October 4, 2006 and closed on January 2, 2010 after 2,814 performances. It was nominated for seven Olivier Awards including Best New Musical and Best Director (Lynn Ahrens). A UK tour followed in 2009.  A Japanese production opened at the Nippon Budokan on November 26, 2010 and ran for 12 performances. A Spanish-language version premiered at the Teatro de la Zarzuela in Madrid on January 22, 2013 and ran for 9 performances.
