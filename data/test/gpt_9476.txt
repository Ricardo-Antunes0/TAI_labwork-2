Archibald Trojan Steele (25 June 1903 Toronto, Ontario – 28 January 1986) was a Canadian psychiatrist who developed a theory of personality based on the work of Carl Jung.  Steele was born in Toronto, Ontario, the son of Archibald Steele and Lillian (née French). He attended Upper Canada College before studying medicine at the University of Toronto. He completed his training at the University of London and the University of Vienna.  Steele worked as a physician in Toronto before becoming a professor at the University of Toronto in 1951. He retired from teaching in 1975.  Steele's principal research interest was in personality theory and he is best known for his work on the concept of the archetypes. His book, The Nature of Personal Reality, was published in 1961.
