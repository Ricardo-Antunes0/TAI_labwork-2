The red-billed firefinch or  Senegal firefinch is a small passerine bird in the finch family Fringillidae. It is found in Senegal, Guinea-Bissau, Ivory Coast, and Ghana. Its natural habitats are subtropical or tropical moist lowland forests and heavily degraded former forest.  The red-billed firefinch is a small bird, measuring 26–30 cm in length and weighing 45–60 g. It has a small head with a black cap and a red bill. The upperparts are olive-green with a black V on the throat, and the underparts are white. The sexes are similar, but the female is duller than the male. The red-billed firefinch is very active and lively, hopping about and searching for insects and other small prey.
