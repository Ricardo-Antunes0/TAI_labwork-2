NHK World-Japan (also known simply as NHK) is a Japanese national public broadcaster headquartered in Tokyo. The broadcaster provides programming in 47 languages to audiences around the world.  NHK was founded on October 5, 1919 as the Japan Broadcasting Station, and became a full-fledged national public broadcaster on October 1, 1957. NHK operates two television networks and several radio networks, and is a major provider of educational programming. NHK also maintains extensive archives and produces documentaries.
