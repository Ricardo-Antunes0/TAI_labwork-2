Thomas Haden Church (born Thomas Richard McMillen; February 8, 1967) is an American actor and director. He is known for his roles in the films The Usual Suspects (1995), Pleasantville (1998), and Donnie Darko (2001), for which he was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor. He later moved to Austin, Texas to study at the University of Texas at Austin, where he was a member of the Alpha Tau Omega fraternity.  Church began his acting career in theater productions while still a student at the University of Texas at Austin. After graduating in 1987, he moved to New York City to pursue an acting career. He made his Broadway debut in 1987 in a production of David Mamet's Sexual Perversity: A New Play. In 1992, he won an Obie Award for his performance in David Mamet's Oleanna.  Church's first major film role came in 1995 when he was cast as The Usual Suspects' Keyser Soze. For this role, he received an Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actor.
