Samuel Calvin (February 2, 1840 – April 6, 1912) was a Canadian geologist and mineralogist who specialized in the study of igneous and metamorphic rocks. He was also a professor at the University of Toronto.  Calvin was born in 1840 in Rockland County, New York, to Calvin and Ruth (Baker) Calvin. He attended the United States Military Academy at West Point, graduating in 1859. He then served as a captain in the U.S. Army for two years before studying geology at Yale University. He received his doctorate from Yale in 1865.  Calvin began his career as a geologist with the Geological Survey of Canada in 1865. He was promoted to head the survey's mineralogy section in 1881 and became its director in 1892. He retired from the survey in 1903 but continued to teach at the University of Toronto until his death in 1912.  Calvin was a member of numerous scientific societies, including the Geological Society of America, London Geological Society, American Philosophical Society, Quebec Academy of Sciences, and Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences. He was also editor-in-chief of "The Canadian Mineralogist" from 1881 to 1903.  In 1875, Calvin married Elizabeth Atherton (1846–1907). They had two daughters: Mildred Calvin (1876–1960) and Muriel Calvin (1878–1976). Mildred married Arthur Twining Hadley; Muriel married Rufus Hadley
