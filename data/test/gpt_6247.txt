Pahlaj Nihalani is an Indian film producer. He is the founder and CEO of Nihalani Productions, a film production company. He has been involved in the production of Hindi, English and regional Indian films.  Nihalani was born in Mumbai, Maharashtra, India. He graduated with a B.A. in English from the University of Mumbai in 1984.  Nihalani began his career as a journalist with the "Hindustan Times" in 1984. In the early 1990s he started his own film production company, Nihalani Productions. In 2007, he founded the National Film Development Corporation of India (NFDC), a government-owned film finance and production company.  Nihalani has been involved in the production of Hindi, English and regional Indian films. He has produced films such as "Bombay Talkies" (1995), "Sarfaraz" (2003), "Lagaan" (2001), "Maqbool" (2003), "Dabangg" (2010) and "Shuddhi" (2015). He has also been involved in the development of films such as "Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge" (1999), "Rang De Basanti" (2006) and "Bang Bang! (2014).
