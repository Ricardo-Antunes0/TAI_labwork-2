Thomas Huckle Weller (June 15, 1915 – December 5, 1967) was an American actor and singer. He is best remembered for his roles in the films The Three Musketeers (1951), The Robe (1953), and The Ten Commandments (1956).  Weller was born in Los Angeles, California, the son of Myrtle (née Thomas) and Thomas Weller, a carpenter. After attending Brigham Young University, he began his acting career in regional theater and made his Broadway debut in 1939 in the play Take My Life, Please. He then appeared in several Broadway productions before landing his first major film role in The Three Musketeers (1951). He followed this with roles in The Robe (1953), and The Ten Commandments (1956), both of which were critical and commercial successes. Weller also appeared in a number of television movies during the 1950s and 1960s. He died from a heart attack in 1967 at the age of 48.
