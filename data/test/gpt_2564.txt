Kornelije Stanković (, ; 23 August 1831 – 10 September 1892) was a Serbian composer and pianist. He is considered one of the most important composers of the Romantic era in Serbia.  Kornelije Stanković was born on 23 August 1831 in Belgrade, Principality of Serbia to a family of musicians. He began his musical studies with his father, Nikola Stanković, a pianist and music teacher, and later continued his education in Vienna with Franz Liszt and Ludwig van Beethoven. Upon his return to Serbia in 1856, he became a professor of music at the Belgrade Academy of Arts. In 1861, he founded the Belgrade Philharmonic Society, which became one of the leading orchestras in Serbia. He died on 10 September 1892 in Belgrade.  Kornelije Stanković is best known for his compositions for orchestra and piano. His works include symphonies, chamber music, operas, and songs. He is also credited with developing the Serbian national romantic style of music. His most famous works include the symphonic poem "The Battle of Kosovo" (1877), the opera "Demeter" (1878), and the piano concerto "No. 1" (1879).
