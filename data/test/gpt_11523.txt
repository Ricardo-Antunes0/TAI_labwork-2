Malcolm Greenidge (born July 7, 1974), known professionally as E.D.I. Mean, is an American rapper from the Queens borough of New York City. He is best known for his work with the group Rawkus and for his solo debut album, "E.D.I. Mean".  Malcolm Greenidge was born in the Queens borough of New York City on July 7, 1974. He grew up in the Bedford-Stuyvesant neighborhood and began rapping at the age of fifteen. He formed the group Rawkus with fellow rappers Mr. Lif and Kool G Rap in 1991. The trio released their debut album, "Return of the Looters", in 1993. E.D.I. Mean's solo debut album, "E.D.I. Mean", was released in 1995 and featured guest appearances from Mos Def, Talib Kweli, and Q-Tip. The album was critically acclaimed and helped to introduce Greenidge to a wider audience.   Greenidge has continued to release music throughout the years, including albums such as "Malcolm X" (2002), "The Great American Rap Album" (2003), and "The Malcolm X Story" (2005). In 2006, he joined forces with producer DJ Premier to release the album "All I Need Is You".
