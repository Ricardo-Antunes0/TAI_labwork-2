Stephanie "Steph" Davis (born November 4, 1973) is an American actress and fashion model. Davis is best known for her roles as Whitney Brown in the CBS sitcom How I Met Your Mother and as Lois Lane in the CW superhero series, Supergirl.  Davis was born in Fort Lauderdale, Florida, the daughter of Beverly (n√©e Turner), a homemaker, and Ronald Davis, an automotive engineer. She has two sisters, Kimberly and Jennifer, and a brother, Ryan. Davis' family is of African-American and white descent. She grew up in Palmetto Bay, a suburb of Fort Lauderdale.  Davis attended The Kewanee School in Kewanee, Illinois for high school before attending the University of Southern California (USC). While at USC, she studied drama and worked at a clothing store. Davis then moved to New York City to pursue an acting career.  Davis' acting career began with small roles on television series such as "NYPD Blue" and "The West Wing" before landing her breakthrough role as Whitney Brown on the CBS sitcom How I Met Your Mother. In 2009, she starred as Lois Lane in the CW superhero series Supergirl.
