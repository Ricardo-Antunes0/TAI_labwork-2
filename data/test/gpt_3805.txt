Albena Petrovic-Vratchanska  (born 20 October 1965) is a retired Montenegrin professional tennis player. She reached a career-high singles ranking of World No. 11 in October 1990.  Petrovic-Vratchanska began her tennis career in 1984, playing for Yugoslavia. In 1988 she moved to the United States, and won her first WTA Tour title at the 1989 Montgomery Bell Classic. The following year she reached the semifinals of the Australian Open and won her first Grand Slam singles title at the 1990 French Open. She also made the quarterfinals of Wimbledon that year.  In 1991 Petrovic-Vratchanska won two WTA Tour titles in succession: the Barcelona Open and the Italian Open. In 1992 she won her third Grand Slam singles title at the US Open, defeating Steffi Graf in the final. She also reached the semifinals of both the French Open and Wimbledon that year. Petrovic-Vratchanska retired from professional tennis in 1995. Her career prize money was over US$5 million.  In 2000, Petrovic-Vratchanska was inducted into the ITF Women's Tennis Hall of Fame.
