"Hallelujah Money" is a song by British singer-songwriter and musician Leonard Cohen. It was written in 1981 and released as the third single from his album Various Positions. The song is about a man who has lost everything, but still clings to his faith in God.  "Hallelujah Money" became well known after it was used in the 1997 film The Full Monty. It reached number one on the Billboard Hot 100 chart in the United States, and stayed there for three weeks. The song also charted in a number of other countries, including Australia, Canada, France, Germany, Ireland, Italy, Norway, Spain and the United Kingdom.  The song has been covered by a number of artists, including Celine Dion, Jeff Buckley and Bonnie Tyler.
