Yegor Nikolaevich Solyankin (; 21 April 190126 August 1975) was a Soviet Russian painter, sculptor, architectural designer, and art educator. He is considered one of the most important representatives of the Leningrad School of Painting, Sculpture and Architecture.  Born into a peasant family in the village of Novonikolaevka in Kursk Governorate, Solyankin began his artistic training at the age of sixteen at the Kursk Art School. In 1923 he enrolled at the Leningrad Institute of Painting, Sculpture and Architecture, where he studied under Leonid Pasternak and Vladimir Tatlin. In 1925 he graduated with honors and was awarded the Stalin Prize.  Solyankin first exhibited his work in 1924 at the Leningrad Union of Artists exhibition "First Stage of Socialist Realism". From 1926 to 1929 he taught at the Leningrad Institute of Painting, Sculpture and Architecture. From 1930 to 1953 he was a professor at the St Petersburg Academy of Fine Arts.
