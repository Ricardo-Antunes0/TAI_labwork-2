Ian Date (born c. 1960) is an Australian artist who lives and works in Melbourne. Date's practice spans painting, sculpture, installation, photography, performance and video.  Date was born in 1960 in Melbourne. He studied at the University of Melbourne, where he received a Bachelor of Arts (Hons) in 1982. He then went to the Slade School of Fine Art, University of London, where he received a Master of Fine Arts in 1986.  Date has exhibited his work in Australia, Europe, Asia and North America. His major exhibitions include the 2002 Biennale of Sydney (with Lisa Milne), the 2003 Venice Biennale (with Hilary Charlesworth), the 2004 Guggenheim Museum Bilbao (with Brian Patterson), the 2005 Auckland Art Gallery (with Donnet Chau) and the 2006 Whitney Biennial (with Alex Katz).  Date's art deals with themes of memory and history. He has said that "memory is a site where we negotiate our relationship to the past and make sense of our lives."
