He is best known as the founder and president of the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA), serving in that post from 1954 to 1989.  Wexler was born in New York City, the son of Russian Jewish immigrants. He began his music industry career as a secretary at Columbia Records, where he rose to be head of A&R. With the help of Columbia president Goddard Lieberson, Wexler helped create the RIAA in 1952, which helped standardize and promote the sales and popularity of recorded music. Wexler also served as chairman of the National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences from 1981 to 1989. He was awarded the Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award in 1989.  Wexler died of natural causes at his home in Los Angeles at the age of 98.
