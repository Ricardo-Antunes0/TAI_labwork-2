Eudora Bumstead (, Stone; August 26, 1860 – October 10, 1926) was an American author. She is best known for her humorous stories and essays, which appeared in The New Yorker magazine from 1907 to 1925.  Bumstead was born in 1860 in Salina, Kansas, the daughter of Caroline Stone (née Sprague) and Edwin Stone. She was educated at the Mount Holyoke Female Seminary in South Hadley, Massachusetts and then at the University of California, Berkeley.  Bumstead married naturalist George Palmer Putnam in 1885. They had one child, a daughter named Caroline (1887-1980). Bumstead died on October 10, 1926, in Petersham, Massachusetts.
