Ashfield is a town in Franklin County, Massachusetts, United States. At the 2010 census, the population was 6,471.  The town is located in the northeast corner of Franklin County, bordered by Hampden County to the east and Hampshire County to the south. The town center is located at the intersection of U.S. Route 20 and Massachusetts Route 135, about southeast of Gardner. Ashfield is home to Ashfield State Hospital, formerly known as the Franklin State Hospital for the Insane.  The town was first settled in 1769 as part of the purchase of land from the Shawmut Indians by Stephen Higginson and his son-in-law Ebenezer Wheelwright. The town was incorporated in 1798. The name "Ashfield" may be derived from an early settler's surname or from a location called "Ashe Field". A post office called Ashfield has been in operation since 1795.
