The Connecticut Lakes are a group of lakes located in the U.S. state of Connecticut. They are part of the Farmington Valley region and are fed by the Mohawk River. The lakes are accessible by road from the town of Farmington and by boat from the towns of Durham and Woodbury.  The Connecticut Lakes are home to a variety of wildlife including white-tailed deer, black bear, and wild turkey. The lakes also contain several hundred species of plants, including blueberries, raspberries, and wild garlic. In addition to their recreational value, the Connecticut Lakes are important for their water supply.
