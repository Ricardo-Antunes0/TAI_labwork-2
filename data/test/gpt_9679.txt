The Karoliina Rantamäki Award  is an annual award given to a Finnish author who has made an outstanding contribution to the Finnish language. The award was first given in 1986.   Karoliina Rantamäki (1931–1996) was a Finnish author, poet, and translator. She is best known for her novels, including the best-selling novel Ajattelen itseni (1978), which tells the story of a Helsinki woman in the years leading up to World War II.
