The imperial election of 1658 was an election of the Holy Roman Emperor held by the College of Electors on 23 February 1658.  The election was the fifth since the death of Emperor Ferdinand II in 1657. It took place at Frankfurt am Main and resulted in a victory for Frederick IV, Elector Palatine. Frederick had been supported by his brother, King Philip IV of Spain, who was also the leading candidate of the Catholic League.
