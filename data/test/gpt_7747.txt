David Holmes (March 10, 1769August 20, 1832) was an American politician who served as the sixth Governor of Massachusetts.  Born in Pittsfield, Massachusetts, Holmes graduated from Yale College in 1789 and then studied law. He was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Pittsfield. In 1793, he served as a member of the Berkshire County Board of Supervisors. In 1800, he was elected to the Massachusetts House of Representatives, where he served two terms. He was also a member of the Massachusetts Governor's Council during the administrations of Governors John Hancock and Elbridge Gerry.  In 1809, Holmes was elected to the United States Senate, where he served two terms. He retired from the Senate in 1813. Holmes returned to his law practice in Pittsfield and also served as president of the First National Bank of Pittsfield. He died in Pittsfield and was interred in Oak Hill Cemetery.
