Melvin James Berman (14 January 1915 – 20 October 2000) was an American law professor and legal scholar. He is best known for his work in the area of constitutional law.  Berman was born in Brooklyn, New York, to Jewish parents, Harry and Rose Berman. After graduating from Brooklyn College in 1935, Berman attended Columbia Law School, where he was a member of the Alpha Delta Phi fraternity. After graduating from Columbia in 1939, Berman clerked for Judge Learned Hand of the United States Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit.  Berman began his teaching career at the University of Pennsylvania Law School in 1945. He moved to Yale Law School in 1949, where he became a full professor in 1962. Berman served as dean of Yale Law School from 1966 to 1972.  Berman was a fellow at the American Academy of Arts and Sciences and a member of the American Academy of Political and Social Science. He also served as president of the American Bar Association (1968–1969), and as vice president for academic affairs at Columbia University (1977–1978).  Berman died on 20 October 2000 at his home in New Haven, Connecticut, aged 89.
