Trichobilharzia regenti is a neuropathogenic parasitic flatworm that causes trichobilharzia infection. It is found in the human intestine and is believed to cause neurotrichiasis, a condition in which hair follicles become inflamed and swell, leading to hair loss. The parasite can also cause severe eye infection, muscle pain, and even death. Currently there is no known cure for trichobilharzia infection, and treatment is limited to prevention.
