Extremely Severe Cyclonic Storm Phailin  was a powerful and deadly tropical cyclone that affected the Andaman and Nicobar Islands in India on 25 November 2016. The storm was the first major cyclone of the 2016 North Indian Ocean cyclone season.  On 25 November 2016, Phailin made landfall on the Andaman and Nicobar Islands as a very severe cyclonic storm with wind speeds of 230 km/h (145 mph). Widespread damage ensued, with at least ten fatalities reported. In addition to the fatalities, Phailin caused extensive damage to infrastructure and homes. The cost of damage is estimated at $1.5 billion (2016 USD).
