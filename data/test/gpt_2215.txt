Hebryzelmis (Ancient Greek: Εὐρύζελμις, Ἑβρύζελμις, Ἑβροζέλμης, Εὐρύτελμις) is a genus of moths of the family Noctuidae.   The genus Hebryzelmis is composed of about 18 species distributed across the Palearctic ecozone. They are found in dry habitats such as open woodlands, heaths and moorlands. They are nocturnal and attracted to light.   Hebryzelmis are medium-sized moths with wingspan up to 40 mm. They have a pointed abdomen and a variable colour pattern, often with a contrasting band on the forewing costa. The hindwings are brown or blackish. The larvae feed on various grasses and flowers.
