Michael Juey McCray II (born August 3, 1990) is an American football cornerback for the Baltimore Ravens of the National Football League (NFL). He played college football at the University of Maryland, where he was a two-time All-American and won the Butkus Award as the nation's top linebacker.  McCray was drafted by the Ravens in the fifth round of the 2012 NFL Draft. He has also played for the Denver Broncos and New York Giants.  McCray was born in Washington, D.C., to Michael Juey McCray I, an attorney, and Sharonda McCray, a school teacher. He has two sisters, Kierra and Aja.   McCray attended Mount Vernon High School in Alexandria, Virginia. As a junior, he registered 85 tackles and 10 interceptions while helping lead his team to a state championship. As a senior, he had 138 tackles and five interceptions while helping his team finish second in the state behind Northern Virginia powerhouse Oakton High School.   McCray accepted an athletic scholarship to attend the University of Maryland, where he played for coach Randy Edsall's Maryland Terrapins football team from 2008 to 2011. As a sophomore in 2009, he started all 13 games at strong safety and led the team with 81 tackles. In 2010, he started all 13 games at strong safety and led Maryland with 116 tackles, including 10 for loss and four interceptions.
