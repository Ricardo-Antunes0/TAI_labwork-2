The Roper River is a large perennial river located in the North Carolina Piedmont. The Roper River is formed by the confluence of the Powell and White Oak rivers. The river flows east for approximately 83 miles before merging with the Pee Dee River near Lumberton. The Roper River has a drainage area of over 7,000 square miles.  The Roper River is a significant source of water for both human and animal consumption, as well as for industrial use. The river also provides recreational opportunities for fishing, boating, swimming, and floating. The Roper River is part of the Neuse and Trent rivers watersheds, and it is a major tributary to the Cape Fear River.
