Christopher J. Chambers  (born August 12, 1969) is an American actor, comedian, and writer.  Chambers was born in Worcester, Massachusetts. He is the son of Jeannette (n√©e Humphrey), a secretary, and Edward Chambers, a building inspector. Chambers was raised in Chelmsford, Massachusetts. He has two sisters, Nancy and Lisa.  Chambers attended Chelmsford High School, where he was a member of the wrestling team and the varsity soccer team. He later transferred to the University of Massachusetts Amherst, where he majored in English and minored in theater.  Chambers began his comedy career performing at the UMass Amherst Improv Club. He also began performing stand-up on the road, including appearances at the Montreal Comedy Festival and the Just for Laughs festival in Montreal.  In 1995, Chambers moved to Los Angeles to pursue a career in acting. His first television appearance was as a cast member on the short-lived sitcom "The Ben Stiller Show". His first major role came in 2000, when he played Doug Stamper on the Showtime series "House of Cards". Chambers also starred in the films "The Ten" (2010), "Green Lantern" (2011), "The Help" (2011), and "Ted" (2012).
