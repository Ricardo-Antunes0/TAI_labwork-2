"United Breaks Guitars" is a trio of musicians from Seattle, Washington, United States, who formed in 1999 and released their debut album, "The Unauthorized Biography of an American Band", in 2001. The band's second album, "The Consequence of Sound", was released in 2003. After a five-year hiatus, the band released their third album, "Tell Me How You Really Feel", in 2007. In 2009, United Breaks Guitars contributed the song "Man on the Moon" to the soundtrack of the video game Rock Band 3. In 2013, the band released their fourth album, "Electronica", which peaked at number 60 on the Billboard 200.    United Breaks Guitars was founded in 1999 by vocalist and guitarist Tobin Sprout, drummer Aaron Gittleman and bassist Ryan Ries. The band's debut album, "The Unauthorized Biography of an American Band", was released in 2001. The album was produced by John Feldmann of Green Day and featured guest appearances from members of Seattle bands like The Presidents of the United States of America and The New Amsterdams.
