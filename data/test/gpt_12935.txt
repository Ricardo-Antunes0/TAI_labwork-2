Julie V. Lund (born c. 1959) is an American historian and the Sterling Professor of History at Yale University. She specializes in eighteenth- and nineteenth-century British history, with a focus on the Atlantic world.  Lund was born in 1959 in Boston, Massachusetts. She received her B.A. from Tufts University in 1981 and her Ph.D. from Harvard University in 1987. She has been a professor at Yale University since 1991.  Lund's research focuses on eighteenth- and nineteenth-century British history, with a focus on the Atlantic world. Her work has appeared in various academic journals, including "The Historical Journal", "The American Historical Review", "The Journal of British Studies", and "The Oxford History of the British Empire".  She is the author of several books, including "The Creation of the British Empire, 1784-1815" (Oxford University Press, 1998), "The Great Nation: A History of Britain from 1714 to 1837" (Oxford University Press, 2006), and "A Tale of Two Revolutions: Britain and France in the Eighteenth Century" (Oxford University Press, 2009).
