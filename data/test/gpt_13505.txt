Boris Rõtov (; 20 August 1937 – 14 June 2007) was a Russian composer.  Rõtov was born in Leningrad, Soviet Union, on 20 August 1937. He began composing at an early age, and studied at the Leningrad Conservatory with Evgeny Mravinsky. He composed film music for such films as "War and Peace" (1985), "The Return of Sherlock Holmes" (1985), "Stalker" (1979), "The Spy Who Loved Me" (1977), and "Ivan's Childhood" (1982).
