Moore was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania on November 21, 1869. He received his undergraduate degree from the University of Pennsylvania in 1894 and his M.A. in 1896. In 1896, he became a research associate at the University of Pennsylvania's Moore School of Social Work, where he developed his anthropological theories. In 1898, he founded the Philadelphia Anthropological Society. In 1923, he became the first president of the International Council on Humanistic Psychology. Moore served as president of both the American Association for the Advancement of Science and the American Psychological Association. He died on September 20, 1938 in Philadelphia.
