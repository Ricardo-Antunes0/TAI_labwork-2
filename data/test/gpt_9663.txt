The eared nightjars are a small group of nocturnal birds in the family Cathartidae. They are found in much of the Old World tropics and subtropics.  The eared nightjar is a small, stocky bird with a short tail and large ears. It has a black head, neck, and body, and a white bar across its chest. The wings are black with white tips. The male has a red face and breast, while the female is mostly black. The eared nightjar is omnivorous, eating insects, fruits, and small rodents.
