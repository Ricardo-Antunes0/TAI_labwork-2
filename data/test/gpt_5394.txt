Huffine was born on August 25, 1889, in Cleveland, Ohio. He received his bachelor's degree from the University of Michigan in 1912 and his master's degree from Columbia University in 1914. Huffine began his career as an engineer at the United States Weather Bureau in 1914. In 1922, he became head of the department of heating, ventilation, and air conditioning at General Electric. In 1933, Huffine founded the Huffine Corporation, which developed and manufactured air conditioning systems. He retired from the company in 1957. Huffine died on January 12, 1980, in Scarsdale, New York.
