Karmsund is a strait located in Rogaland county, Norway. It separates the mainland of Rogaland from the Karmsundsfjorden, an inlet of the Barents Sea. The strait is about long and wide, and has a depth of . The Karmsund Bridge connects the mainland of Rogaland to the island of Karmsund in the fjord.  The strait is a popular tourist destination, with several hotels, restaurants and attractions situated there. The main tourist attraction is the Karmsund Bridge, which is Norway's longest bridge. The bridge was opened on 1 November 1966 and cost about 1.5 billion Norwegian kroner (NOK) to build.
