Clayton Riley Lusk (December 21, 1872 in Lusk, Wyoming â€“ October 12, 1951 in Los Angeles, California) was a U.S. Representative from Wyoming.  Clayton R. Lusk was born on December 21, 1872 in Lusk, Wyoming Territory. He attended the common schools and the University of Wyoming at Laramie. He studied law, was admitted to the bar in 1896 and commenced practice in Lusk. He served as county attorney of Carbon County from 1902 to 1904.  Lusk was elected as a Republican to the Sixty-fifth and to the three succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1917 - March 3, 1921). He was an unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1920 to the Sixty-sixth Congress. He resumed the practice of law in Los Angeles, California.  Lusk died on October 12, 1951 in Los Angeles, California and was interred in Forest Lawn Memorial Park Cemetery.
