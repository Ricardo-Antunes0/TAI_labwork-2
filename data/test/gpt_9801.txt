Eiler Theodor Lehn Schiøler (30 October 1874 – 12 November 1951) was a Norwegian sculptor.  Schiøler was born in Christiania (now Oslo), Norway, the son of Theodor Lehn and Elise Schiøler. He was a brother of painter Odd Lehn Schiøler and artist Elisabeth Schiøler. He completed his education at the School of Fine Arts in Copenhagen, Denmark in 1898.  Schiøler first exhibited at the Royal Academy of Fine Arts in Copenhagen in 1898 with the sculpture "Nina". From 1905 to 1916 he was a professor at the School of Fine Arts in Oslo, where he also served as dean from 1930 to 1939. He was decorated Knight, First Class of the Order of St. Olav in 1939. He died in 1951.
