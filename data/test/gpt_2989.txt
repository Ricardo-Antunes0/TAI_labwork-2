Anthony Perosh (born 5 October 1972) is an Australian former professional rugby league footballer of the 1990s and 2000s. He played for the Sydney Roosters, Penrith Panthers and Newcastle Knights in the National Rugby League (NRL).  Perosh was born in Sydney, New South Wales, Australia. He is of Aboriginal and Irish descent.  Perosh played for the Sydney Roosters from 1991 to 1999, appearing in 130 games and scoring 60 tries. He was part of the Roosters' 1995 premiership team. He joined Penrith Panthers in 2000, playing for them until 2003. In 2004, Perosh joined Newcastle Knights. He played for the Knights until 2007, his last season playing professional rugby league.  Perosh retired from professional rugby league at the age of 34 to focus on a career in business. In 2009, he was appointed CEO of Gold Coast Titans Football Club. In 2013, he left this position to become Chief Operating Officer of the St George Illawarra Dragons NRL club.
