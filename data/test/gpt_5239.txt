By Bizarre Hands is the first collection of short stories by American author Kurt Vonnegut. It was first published in 1952 by The Dial Press.   The book comprises nine short stories, all of which deal with the themes of science, technology, and the human condition. Vonnegut's writing is characterized by its satirical and dark humor, as well as its exploration of the absurd.   The book has been praised for its clever writing and its exploration of the human condition. However, it has also been criticised for its datedness and its lack of originality.
