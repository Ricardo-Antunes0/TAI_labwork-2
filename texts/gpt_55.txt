The Harry Potter series, authored by British writer J.K. Rowling, comprises seven captivating fantasy novels. These books follow the adventures of a young wizard named Harry Potter, along with his companions Hermione Granger and Ron Weasley, as they navigate their schooling at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. Central to the storyline is Harry's ongoing struggle against the dark wizard Lord Voldemort, who seeks immortality and domination over both magical and non-magical beings.Originally published by Bloomsbury in the United Kingdom and Scholastic Press in the United States, the series offers a rich tapestry of genres, including fantasy, drama, and coming-of-age fiction. Through its exploration of themes such as prejudice, corruption, and mortality, the world of Harry Potter resonates deeply with readers of all ages, cementing its status as a modern literary classic.Since the debut of the first novel, "Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone," in 1997, the series has garnered widespread acclaim and commercial success, selling over 600 million copies worldwide and translated into 85 languages. The books set numerous records, with the final installment achieving staggering sales figures within just twenty-four hours of its release.The popularity of the Harry Potter franchise extends beyond literature, with Warner Bros. Pictures adapting the original seven books into an eight-part film series. The franchise's total value soared to $25 billion, fueling the creation of derivative works, stage productions like "Harry Potter and the Cursed Child," and spin-off films such as "Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them."Additionally, themed attractions, collectively known as The Wizarding World of Harry Potter, have been established at Universal Destinations & Experiences amusement parks worldwide, allowing fans to immerse themselves further into Rowling's magical universe.
