Emily Jean Stone, known as Emma Stone, born on November 6, 1988, is an accomplished American actress and producer. Her career boasts numerous prestigious awards, including two Academy Awards, two British Academy Film Awards, and two Golden Globe Awards. In 2017, she topped the charts as the highest-paid actress globally and was recognized by Time magazine as one of the 100 most influential individuals worldwide. Originally from Scottsdale, Arizona, Stone's acting journey began at a young age when she participated in a theater production of The Wind in the Willows in 2000. She later moved to Los Angeles as a teenager, making her television debut on the reality show In Search of the New Partridge Family in 2004. Despite the show not being picked up, Stone's talent shone through, leading to appearances in a string of successful teen comedies like Superbad (2007), Zombieland (2009), and her breakout role in Easy A (2010). Following these successes, Stone continued to impress audiences with her versatility, starring in acclaimed films such as Crazy, Stupid, Love (2011), The Help (2011), and gaining widespread recognition as Gwen Stacy in The Amazing Spider-Man (2012) and its sequel. Notably, she earned Oscar nominations for her roles in Birdman (2014) and The Favourite (2018) before clinching two Academy Awards for Best Actress, notably for her performances in La La Land (2016) and Poor Things (2023). Stone's talent extends beyond the silver screen. She portrayed tennis legend Billie Jean King in Battle of the Sexes (2017) and the titular character in Cruella (2021).
