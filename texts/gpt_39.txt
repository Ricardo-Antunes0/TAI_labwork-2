Dakota Mayi Johnson, born on October 4, 1989, is an accomplished American actress. Born to the renowned actors Don Johnson and Melanie Griffith, she entered the film industry at the age of ten with a minor role in Crazy in Alabama (1999), helmed by her then-stepfather Antonio Banderas and featuring her mother. Following her high school graduation, she pursued acting in Los Angeles, landing a minor part in The Social Network (2010). However, her breakthrough came when she portrayed Anastasia Steele in the popular Fifty Shades film series (2015â€“2018). Recognized for her talent, she earned a BAFTA Rising Star Award nomination in 2016 and secured a spot in Forbes' 30 Under 30 list. Johnson's career flourished with diverse roles in films such as the crime drama Black Mass (2015), the drama A Bigger Splash (2015), the romantic comedy How to Be Single (2016), the horror film Suspiria (2018), the thriller Bad Times at the El Royale (2018), the coming-of-age story The Peanut Butter Falcon (2019), the psychological drama The Lost Daughter (2021), and the romantic drama Cha Cha Real Smooth (2022). Notably, she produced the latter through her company TeaTime Pictures. Recently, Johnson has taken on the titular role in Madame Web (2024), further solidifying her reputation as a versatile and accomplished actress.
