Christopher Anthony John Martin, born on March 2, 1977, is a versatile English artist known for his roles as a singer, songwriter, musician, producer, and philanthropist. His claim to fame stems from being the lead vocalist, pianist, rhythm guitarist, and co-founder of the renowned rock ensemble, Coldplay. Hailing from Exeter, Devon, Martin's journey began at University College London, where he, alongside Jonny Buckland, Guy Berryman, and Will Champion, laid the foundation of Coldplay. The band catapulted to global recognition with the release of the iconic track "Yellow" in 2000, garnering widespread acclaim for albums like "A Rush of Blood to the Head" (2002), "Viva la Vida or Death and All His Friends" (2008), among others. His contributions have earned him seven Grammy Awards and nine Brit Awards as part of Coldplay. With over 100 million albums sold worldwide as of 2021, Coldplay stands as the most successful musical group of the 21st century. Martin's influence extends beyond music, earning him a spot on Debrett's 2017 list of the most influential personalities in the United Kingdom.
